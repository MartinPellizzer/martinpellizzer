Poa matris-occidentalis subsp. matris-occidentalis

Poa matris-occidentalis subsp.

matris-occidentalis, commonly known as the western matron grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed florets.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
