Pycnandra sessilifolia

Pycnandra sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pycnandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, sessile leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
