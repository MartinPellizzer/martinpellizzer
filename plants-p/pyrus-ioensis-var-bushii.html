Pyrus ioensis var. bushii

Pyrus ioensis var. bushii, commonly known as the Bushii pear, is a rare variant of the Io pear native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellow-green flowers that appear in early spring. The fruit is typically small, with a thin skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
