Phreatia calcarata

Phreatia calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often growing on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Phreatia calcarata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
