Phyllanthus sylvaticus

Phyllanthus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wild madder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in woodland and shrubland habitats. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly toothed margin.

The roots of Phyllanthus sylvaticus have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.
