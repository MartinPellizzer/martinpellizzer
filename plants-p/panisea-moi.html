Panisea moi

Panisea moi, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the pale blooms. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for evening gardens and floral arrangements.
