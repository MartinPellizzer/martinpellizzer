Poa flava

Poa flava, commonly known as yellow meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, often reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are covered in fine hairs.

Poa flava is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
