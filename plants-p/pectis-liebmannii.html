Pectis liebmannii

Pectis liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Pectis liebmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
