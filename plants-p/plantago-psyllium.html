Plantago psyllium

Plantago psyllium, commonly known as psyllium husk, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a mucilaginous substance when its seeds are soaked in water, which is used in various traditional and modern applications. The seeds are rich in soluble fiber and are often used in herbal remedies and as a dietary supplement.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is cultivated for its health benefits and industrial uses.
