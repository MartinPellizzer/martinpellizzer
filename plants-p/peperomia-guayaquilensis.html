Peperomia guayaquilensis

Peperomia guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Guayaquil peperomia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant typically features small, clustered flowers that are often green or red in color, depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Peperomia guayaquilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
