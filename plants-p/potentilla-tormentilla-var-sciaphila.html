Potentilla tormentilla var. sciaphila

Potentilla tormentilla var. sciaphila, commonly known as the shade-loving cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dark green color.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate low light conditions.
