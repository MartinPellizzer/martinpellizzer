Periploca laevigata subsp. angustifolia

Periploca laevigata subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf periploca, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
