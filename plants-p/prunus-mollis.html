Prunus mollis

Prunus mollis, commonly known as the soft cherry or sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy when young and become smooth as they mature. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruits are small, round cherries that are sweet and edible, often used in culinary applications.

Prunus mollis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
