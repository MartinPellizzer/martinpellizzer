Pemphis setosa

Pemphis setosa, commonly known as the hairy pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pemphis setosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
