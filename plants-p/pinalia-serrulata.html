Pinalia serrulata

Pinalia serrulata, commonly known as the serrated pinyon, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of cone-like structures that contain edible seeds. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, relying on wind for pollination.

The seeds are a valuable food source for various wildlife species in its ecosystem.
