Pyrus aucuparia var. typica

Pyrus aucuparia var. typica, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, apple-like berries that ripen to a red or yellow color in late summer. It is often used in hedgerows and as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and seasonal beauty.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
