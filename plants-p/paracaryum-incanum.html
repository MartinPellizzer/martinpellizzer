Paracaryum incanum

Paracaryum incanum, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy inflorescences that give it a distinctive, cloud-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, silvery down that contributes to its common name.

Paracaryum incanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
