Phalaenopsis zebrina var. lilacina

Phalaenopsis zebrina var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-tinged moth orchid, is a hybrid orchid with distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a silvery-blue hue. The plant produces pendulous flowers in shades of pink, white, and lilac, with a unique striped pattern on the petals. It is a tropical orchid that thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
