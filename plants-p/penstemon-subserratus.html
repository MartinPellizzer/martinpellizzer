Penstemon subserratus

Penstemon subserratus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon subserratus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
