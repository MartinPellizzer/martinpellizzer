Pterocarpus indicus

Pterocarpus indicus, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its hard, durable timber used in furniture and musical instruments.
