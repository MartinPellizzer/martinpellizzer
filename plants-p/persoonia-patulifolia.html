Persoonia patulifolia

Persoonia patulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pear-fruit, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and its distinctive, urn-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The leaves are broad, elliptical, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft appearance. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain numerous seeds.

Persoonia patulifolia is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
