Pterocarpus polyanthus

Pterocarpus polyanthus, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Java rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its rich color and durability, though it is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
