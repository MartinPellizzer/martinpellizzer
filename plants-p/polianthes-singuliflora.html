Polianthes singuliflora

Polianthes singuliflora, commonly known as the singular-flowered amaryllis, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, single flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Polianthes singuliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper conditions.
