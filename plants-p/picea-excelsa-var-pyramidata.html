Picea excelsa var. pyramidata

Picea excelsa var. pyramidata, commonly known as the Pyramidal White Spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its needles are typically four to five millimeters long and have a slightly resinous scent.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
