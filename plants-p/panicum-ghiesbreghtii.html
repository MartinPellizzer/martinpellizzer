Panicum ghiesbreghtii

Panicum ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the Ghiesbreght's panicgrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental potential and ecological adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
