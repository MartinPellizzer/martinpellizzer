Pteris serraria

Pteris serraria, commonly known as the serrated water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, serrated fronds that give it its common name. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
