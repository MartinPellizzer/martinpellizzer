Pentapogon robustus

Pentapogon robustus, commonly known as the robust pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy habit with gray-green foliage. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is adapted to withstand coastal winds and salt spray.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appearance.
