Psychotria aquatilis var. divaricatus

Psychotria aquatilis var. divaricatus, commonly known as the "Coca plant," is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions of Peru, Bolivia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

This variant is notable for its alkaloid content, including cocaine, which has significant pharmacological and illicit uses.
