Praxelis karuaiensis

Praxelis karuaiensis, commonly known as the Karuaiensis goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
