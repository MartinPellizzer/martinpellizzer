Packera streptanthifolia var. borealis

Packera streptanthifolia var. borealis, commonly known as the northern rock cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately divided, with a hairy underside, and produces yellow flowers in late summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth form compared to other varieties of Packera.
