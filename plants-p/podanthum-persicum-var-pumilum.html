Podanthum persicum var. pumilum

Podanthum persicum var. pumilum, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce multiple blooms throughout the growing season.
