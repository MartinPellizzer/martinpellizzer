Platydesma campanulatum var. pubescens

Platydesma campanulatum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its bell-shaped, hairy flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to certain regions of Europe. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with a tubular corolla that opens into a bell shape.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.
