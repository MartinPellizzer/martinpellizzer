Phelipanche cernua subsp. sinaica

Phelipanche cernua subsp.

sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of North Africa and the Middle East. It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers typically in shades of blue, violet, or white.

The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and branching.

It is also known for its potential as a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and seed dispersal capabilities.
