Pastinaca opaca

Pastinaca opaca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved scorzonera, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is fibrous, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky soils.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
