Phreatia caudiflora

Phreatia caudiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, relying on its aerial roots for nutrient absorption. Its flowers, which are often violet or white, are adapted for pollination by insects and birds.

Phreatia caudiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique growth habits.
