Primula matthioli subsp. himalaica

Primula matthioli subsp.

himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas at high elevations. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
