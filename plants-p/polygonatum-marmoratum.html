Polygonatum marmoratum

Polygonatum marmoratum, commonly known as the marble-stemmed Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, marble-like patterning on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polygonatum marmoratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and ecological benefits.
