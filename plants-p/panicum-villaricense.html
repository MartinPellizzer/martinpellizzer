Panicum villaricense

Panicum villaricense, commonly known as the Mexican grass or dwarf panicum, is a species of grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
