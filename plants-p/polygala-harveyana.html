Polygala harveyana

Polygala harveyana, commonly known as the Harvey's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala harveyana is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.
