Potentilla varnensis

Potentilla varnensis, commonly known as the Varnish potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and it has a shallow root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Potentilla varnensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
