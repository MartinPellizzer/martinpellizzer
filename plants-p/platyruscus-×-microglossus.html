Platyruscus × microglossus

Platyruscus × microglossus, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a fascinating botanical hybrid that results from the cross between Platyruscus filiformis and Microglossum affine.

This plant is typically found in wetland and marsh environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces striking, often fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow or brownish in color, with intricate patterns. The plant is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
