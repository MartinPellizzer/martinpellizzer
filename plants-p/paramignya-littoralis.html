Paramignya littoralis

Paramignya littoralis, commonly known as the coastal tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in saline and humid environments, making it well adapted to coastal ecosystems. Its fruits are small, woody pods containing edible seeds.

Paramignya littoralis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
