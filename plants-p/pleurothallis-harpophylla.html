Pleurothallis harpophylla

Pleurothallis harpophylla, commonly known as the harpophylla orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, linear leaves and a compact inflorescence. The flowers are typically pale green to white with a distinctive yellow or orange lip, which is a key feature in its pollination strategy. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
