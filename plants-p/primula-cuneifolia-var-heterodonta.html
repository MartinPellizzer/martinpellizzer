Primula cuneifolia var. heterodonta

Primula cuneifolia var. heterodonta, commonly known as the heterodonta primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
