Phlomis brevilabris

Phlomis brevilabris, commonly known as narrow-lipped mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features tall, erect stems covered with soft, woolly hair, and it produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
