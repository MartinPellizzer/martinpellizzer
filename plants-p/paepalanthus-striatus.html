Paepalanthus striatus

Paepalanthus striatus, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and often have a zigzag appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
