Perezia volcanensis

Perezia volcanensis, commonly known as the volcanic perezia, is a rare flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and striking, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, volcanic soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish. Its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant resilience.

Due to its limited habitat, Perezia volcanensis is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
