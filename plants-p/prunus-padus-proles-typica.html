Prunus padus proles typica

Prunus padus proles typica, commonly known as the common bird cherry.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
