Parsonsia flavescens

Parsonsia flavescens, commonly known as the golden parsnip, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture.

Parsonsia flavescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
