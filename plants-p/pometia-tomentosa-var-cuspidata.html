Pometia tomentosa var. cuspidata

Pometia tomentosa var. cuspidata, commonly known as the hairy pometia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
