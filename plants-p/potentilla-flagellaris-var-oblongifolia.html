Potentilla flagellaris var. oblongifolia

Potentilla flagellaris var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
