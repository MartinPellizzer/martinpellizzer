Prunus sachalinensis f. orbicularis

Prunus sachalinensis f. orbicularis, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry, is a unique variant of the Sakhalin cherry tree.

It is characterized by its orbicular, or nearly round, leaves, which distinguish it from the typical ovate leaves of the species. This plant is native to the Far East, including regions of Russia and China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The flowers are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to the plant.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in garden settings.
