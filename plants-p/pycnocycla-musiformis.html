Pycnocycla musiformis

Pycnocycla musiformis, commonly known as the musiform spurge, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a distinctive, upright stem that is often covered in a thick layer of white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, textured surface.

Pycnocycla musiformis is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.
