Pedicularis stenocorys subsp. melanotricha

Pedicularis stenocorys subsp.

melanotricha, commonly known as the black-hairied lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.
