Prepusa dibotrya

Prepusa dibotrya, commonly known as the two-petaled orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically have two distinct petals, giving it its common name. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it receives indirect sunlight. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, contributing to its overall elegant appearance.

Prepusa dibotrya is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
