Potentilla schultzii

Potentilla schultzii, commonly known as Schultze's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves composed of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Potentilla schultzii is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
