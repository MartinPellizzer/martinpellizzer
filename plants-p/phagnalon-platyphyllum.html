Phagnalon platyphyllum

Phagnalon platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. Native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or marginal soils.

Its morphology is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
