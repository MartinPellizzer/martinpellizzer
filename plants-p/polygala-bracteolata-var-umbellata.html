Polygala bracteolata var. umbellata

Polygala bracteolata var. umbellata, commonly known as the umbellate milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white, umbel-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptical in shape.

The flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive structure that attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
