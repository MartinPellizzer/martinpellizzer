Polypodium naviculare

Polypodium naviculare, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, thriving in humid environments. The plant has a rhizomatous structure with fronds that are usually pinnate and have a distinctive, narrow, boat-shaped appearance. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Polypodium naviculare is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
