Pteroglossa acalcarata

Pteroglossa acalcarata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The unique spurred structure on its flowers is a notable botanical feature, aiding in pollination by specific insect species.
