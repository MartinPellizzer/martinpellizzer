Piptadenia niopo

Piptadenia niopo, commonly known as the Brazilian bottle tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by long, pod-like fruits containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
