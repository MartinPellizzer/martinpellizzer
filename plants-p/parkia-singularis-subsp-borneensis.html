Parkia singularis subsp. borneensis

Parkia singularis subsp.

borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Borneo. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing flattened, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in agroforestry systems.
