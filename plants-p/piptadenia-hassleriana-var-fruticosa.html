Piptadenia hassleriana var. fruticosa

Piptadenia hassleriana var. fruticosa, commonly known as the yellow tamarind tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a valued species in horticulture and traditional medicine.
