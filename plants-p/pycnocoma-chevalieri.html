Pycnocoma chevalieri

Pycnocoma chevalieri, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves and produces a cluster of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a short growing season and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
