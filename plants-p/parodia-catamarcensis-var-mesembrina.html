Parodia catamarcensis var. mesembrina

Parodia catamarcensis var. mesembrina, commonly known as the Mesemblina Cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Argentina.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem with a greenish-gray color and a distinctive rib structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often borne on short, erect stems. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
