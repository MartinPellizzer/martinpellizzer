Polygala angustifolia

Polygala angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its common name, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Polygala angustifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
