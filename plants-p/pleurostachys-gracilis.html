Pleurostachys gracilis

Pleurostachys gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a grassy scent.

Pleurostachys gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
