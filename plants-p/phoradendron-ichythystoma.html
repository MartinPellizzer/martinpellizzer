Phoradendron ichythystoma

Phoradendron ichythystoma, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often used in holiday traditions. It is found in various regions of the Americas, particularly in tropical and subtropical areas.

Its parasitic nature and unique reproductive strategies make it an important component of its ecological community.
