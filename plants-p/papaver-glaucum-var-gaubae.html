Papaver glaucum var. gaubae

Papaver glaucum var. gaubae, commonly known as the Gaubae poppy, is a variant of the blue poppy native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. The plant features a short, sturdy stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
