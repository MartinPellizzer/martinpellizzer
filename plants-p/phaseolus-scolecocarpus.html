Phaseolus scolecocarpus

Phaseolus scolecocarpus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that contain seeds used for food and agriculture. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in warm, temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated in many parts of the world.
