Palmerella debilis var. serrata

Palmerella debilis var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated palmerella, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and spiny, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds.

Palmerella debilis var. serrata is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush communities.
