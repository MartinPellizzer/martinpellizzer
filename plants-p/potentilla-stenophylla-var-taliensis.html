Potentilla stenophylla var. taliensis

Potentilla stenophylla var. taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at elevations above 2,000 meters.
