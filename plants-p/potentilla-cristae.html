Potentilla cristae

Potentilla cristae, commonly known as the crested potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled or wavy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
