Ptilotus exiliflorus

Ptilotus exiliflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is often found in sandy soils and is an important component of coastal and inland grasslands.
