Pandanus arapepe

Pandanus arapepe, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, palm-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for making thatch.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
