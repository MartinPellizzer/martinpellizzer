Phillyrea ilicifolia

Phillyrea ilicifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, leathery, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, black berries. Phillyrea ilicifolia is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

It has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
