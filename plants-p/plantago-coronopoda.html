Plantago coronopoda

Plantago coronopoda, commonly known as the crowned plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which is often covered with a crown-like structure of bracts. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago coronopoda have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
