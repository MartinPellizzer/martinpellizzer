Piper marivelesanum

Piper marivelesanum, commonly known as the Philippine pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed, berry-like structure that turns red when mature.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
