Phanera menispermacea

Phanera menispermacea, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The botanical features of Phanera menispermacea make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
