Piper oblancifolium

Piper oblancifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. These berries are typically harvested when they turn red and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Piperaceae family.
