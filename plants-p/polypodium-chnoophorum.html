Polypodium chnoophorum

Polypodium chnoophorum, commonly known as the broad-leafed wall fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Polypodium chnoophorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
