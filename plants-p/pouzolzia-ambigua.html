Pouzolzia ambigua

Pouzolzia ambigua, commonly known as the common pouzolzia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. Its small, opposite leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly serrated margin.

Pouzolzia ambigua produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by small, three-lobed berries.
