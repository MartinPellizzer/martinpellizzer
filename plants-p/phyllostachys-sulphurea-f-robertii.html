Phyllostachys sulphurea f. robertii

Phyllostachys sulphurea f. robertii, commonly known as the golden bamboo or yellow bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to golden-green culms, which are often more vibrant than those of the parent species. This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes. The plant typically grows in clumps, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

It is a hardy plant that is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for bamboo enthusiasts.
