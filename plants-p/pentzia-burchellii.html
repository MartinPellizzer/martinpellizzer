Pentzia burchellii

Pentzia burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs that help reduce water loss.

The Burchell's pincushion is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
