Pastinaca polakii

Pastinaca polakii, commonly known as the Polish burdock, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in composite flower heads, and it produces long, slender seeds enclosed in a bristly bur.

The plant is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
