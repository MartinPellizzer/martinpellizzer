Phyllostegia waianaeensis

Phyllostegia waianaeensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian stiltgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
