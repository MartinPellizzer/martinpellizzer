Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp. rubra

Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine, soft down.

The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
