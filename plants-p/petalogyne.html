Petalogyne

Petalogyne, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, winding stems above the water's surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate. Petalogyne is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures, often representing purity and enlightenment.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
