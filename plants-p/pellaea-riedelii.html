Pellaea riedelii

Pellaea riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's spleenwort, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of fronds. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, often on cliffs or in mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, serrated margin.

Pellaea riedelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
