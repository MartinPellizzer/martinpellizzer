Pedilanthus pavonis

Pedilanthus pavonis, commonly known as the pavon plant or the peacock plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often surrounded by showy, petal-like structures. It is typically cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish tones depending on the variety.
