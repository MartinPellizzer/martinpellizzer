Polypodium vulgare subsp. cambricum

Polypodium vulgare subsp.

cambricum, commonly known as the British maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to the temperate regions of Europe. It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds with a distinctive, glossy appearance.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
