Philadelphus pumilus var. ovatus

Philadelphus pumilus var. ovatus, commonly known as the dwarf golden spire, is a compact shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its low-growing habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for small garden spaces.
