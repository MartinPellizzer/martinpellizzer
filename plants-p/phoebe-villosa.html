Phoebe villosa

Phoebe villosa, commonly known as the hairy phoebe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. Phoebe villosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
