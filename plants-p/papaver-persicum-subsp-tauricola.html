Papaver persicum subsp. tauricola

Papaver persicum subsp.

tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a dark central blotch.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are stout and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
