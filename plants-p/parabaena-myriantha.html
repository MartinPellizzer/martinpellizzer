Parabaena myriantha

Parabaena myriantha, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of violet-blue flowers during the wet season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are upright and branched. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in undergrowth of tropical forests.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
