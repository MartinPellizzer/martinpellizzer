Pogonarthria refracta

Pogonarthria refracta, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense tufts.

Pogonarthria refracta is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
