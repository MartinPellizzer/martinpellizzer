Primula pedemontana

Primula pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
