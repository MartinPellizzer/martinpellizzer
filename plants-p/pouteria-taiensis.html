Pouteria taiensis

Pouteria taiensis, commonly known as the Tai wild persimmon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is used in local cuisines.

It also has potential medicinal uses due to its high content of bioactive compounds.
