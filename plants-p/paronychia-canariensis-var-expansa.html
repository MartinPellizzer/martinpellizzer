Paronychia canariensis var. expansa

Paronychia canariensis var. expansa, commonly known as the Canary Island heath, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in Mediterranean climates.
