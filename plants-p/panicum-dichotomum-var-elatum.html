Panicum dichotomum var. elatum

Panicum dichotomum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinct, grass-like texture, and it is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
