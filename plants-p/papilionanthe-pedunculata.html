Papilionanthe pedunculata

Papilionanthe pedunculata, commonly known as the golden wing orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a slender stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking orange to yellow flowers with a unique wing-like structure on the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
