Premna calycina

Premna calycina, commonly known as the white-flowered rubber vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its stems are often hairy and can become woody with age, while its roots are fibrous and used in traditional medicine.

Premna calycina is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant flowers.
