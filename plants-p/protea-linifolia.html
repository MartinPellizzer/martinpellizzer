Protea linifolia

Protea linifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pale pink to white flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves that give it its common name and help reduce water loss in its native arid environment. It is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa.

Protea linifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
