Pleurothallis paraniesseniae

Pleurothallis paraniesseniae, commonly known as the Paraniessen's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocky surfaces. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with prominent yellow lips. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
