Pimenta hispaniolensis

Pimenta hispaniolensis, commonly known as the Hispaniola pepper tree, is a species native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is a small evergreen tree belonging to the Myrtaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, red, edible fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, while the fruit is valued for its spicy flavor and culinary uses.
