Penaea sarcocolla

Penaea sarcocolla, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture.

Penaea sarcocolla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
