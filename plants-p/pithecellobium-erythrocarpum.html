Pithecellobium erythrocarpum

Pithecellobium erythrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Its root system has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable for soil enrichment in agroforestry systems.
