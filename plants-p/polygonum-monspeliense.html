Polygonum monspeliense

Polygonum monspeliense, commonly known as burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage that turns bright orange in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has alternate, simple, and ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Polygonum monspeliense is typically found in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and wetlands.
