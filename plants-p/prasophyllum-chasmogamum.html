Prasophyllum chasmogamum

Prasophyllum chasmogamum, commonly known as the black leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single spike of pale green to brown flowers, which are often mistaken for fungi due to their appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

Prasophyllum chasmogamum is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
