Philodendron auriculatum

Philodendron auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive ear-like protrusion on the leaf blade. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
