Pelargonium insularis

Pelargonium insularis, commonly known as the Island geranium, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

Pelargonium insularis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
