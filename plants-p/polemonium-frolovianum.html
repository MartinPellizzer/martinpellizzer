Polemonium frolovianum

Polemonium frolovianum, commonly known as Frolov's Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

Polemonium frolovianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
