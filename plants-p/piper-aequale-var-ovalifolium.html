Piper aequale var. ovalifolium

Piper aequale var. ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
