Prunus communis proles depressa

Prunus communis proles depressa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact shrub native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, tart cherries that are typically harvested in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for hedging and ornamental planting.
