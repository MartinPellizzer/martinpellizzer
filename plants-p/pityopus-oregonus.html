Pityopus oregonus

Pityopus oregonus, commonly known as the Oregon groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

Pityopus oregonus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in ecological restoration projects.
