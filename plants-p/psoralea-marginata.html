Psoralea marginata

Psoralea marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense spikes. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Psoralea marginata is adapted to arid conditions and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules.
