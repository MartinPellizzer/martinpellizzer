Polybotrya foeniculacea

Polybotrya foeniculacea, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features aromatic, feathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its rhizomes are extensively used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its unique morphology and chemical composition.
