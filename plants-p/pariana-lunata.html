Pariana lunata

Pariana lunata, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
