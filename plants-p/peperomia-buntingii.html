Peperomia buntingii

Peperomia buntingii, commonly known as the Bunting's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red or purple, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for container cultivation.
