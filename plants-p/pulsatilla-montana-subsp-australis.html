Pulsatilla montana subsp. australis

Pulsatilla montana subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
