Prunus cerasifera var. luteocordata

Prunus cerasifera var. luteocordata, commonly known as the yellow-leafed cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a visually appealing addition to landscapes.
