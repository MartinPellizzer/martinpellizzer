Photinia integrifolia var. flavidiflora

Photinia integrifolia var. flavidiflora, commonly known as the goldenflower photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is relatively easy to grow and is suitable for gardens and landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
