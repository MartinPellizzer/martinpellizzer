Polymnia caroliniana

Polymnia caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The Carolina bluebell is an important early nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
