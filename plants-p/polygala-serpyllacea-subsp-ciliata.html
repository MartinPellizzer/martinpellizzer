Polygala serpyllacea subsp. ciliata

Polygala serpyllacea subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

It is a perennial herb that blooms in early summer and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
