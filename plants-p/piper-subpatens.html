Piper subpatens

Piper subpatens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper or mountain pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in areas with humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers that develop into small, three-lobed berries. It is often found growing in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
