Plectranthus stachyoides

Plectranthus stachyoides, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a popular choice for both garden beds and indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
