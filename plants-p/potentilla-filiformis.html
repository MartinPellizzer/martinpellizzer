Potentilla filiformis

Potentilla filiformis, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous or tundra environments. Potentilla filiformis is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of high-altitude ecosystems.
