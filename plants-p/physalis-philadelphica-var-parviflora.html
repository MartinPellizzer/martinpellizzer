Physalis philadelphica var. parviflora

Physalis philadelphica var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features hairy stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically borne on long petioles. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into inflated, papery fruits resembling tiny lanterns. These fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a tangy, sweet flavor.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
