Pulsatilla × devinensis

Pulsatilla × devinensis, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals.

Pulsatilla × devinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
