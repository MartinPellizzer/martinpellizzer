Prunus serrulata f. amabilis

Prunus serrulata f. amabilis, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese weeping cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species, native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, rounded form and cascading branches that bear delicate pink flowers in early spring. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental tree is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
