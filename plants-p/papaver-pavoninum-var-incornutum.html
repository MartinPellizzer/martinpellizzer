Papaver pavoninum var. incornutum

Papaver pavoninum var. incornutum, commonly known as the hornless poppy, is a variant of the pavoninum poppy species.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in rocky or arid environments. Its flowers are typically smaller and less showy compared to the standard pavoninum variety, with a more subdued appearance.

The leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its resilience in harsh conditions.
