Pelargonium scabroide

Pelargonium scabroide, commonly known as the roughleaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Pelargonium scabroide is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
