Pilocereus repandus

Pilocereus repandus, commonly known as the moon cactus or night bloomer, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical or cylindrical stem, which is often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces showy, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink, opening at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Pilocereus repandus is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ornamental value.
