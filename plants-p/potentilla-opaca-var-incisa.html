Potentilla opaca var. incisa

Potentilla opaca var. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
