Persicaria serrulata var. asiatica

Persicaria serrulata var. asiatica, commonly known as Asian sawtooth knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish-purple in color and are covered with fine, serrated hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped with sharp, toothed edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
