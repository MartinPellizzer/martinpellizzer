Parodianthus

Parodianthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, and white. The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the colorful blooms. Parodianthus species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their beauty and ease of care.

They thrive in well-drained soil and require moderate light to flourish.
