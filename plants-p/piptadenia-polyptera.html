Piptadenia polyptera

Piptadenia polyptera, commonly known as the Brazilian tamarind, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnate, and its distinctive yellowish-orange flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces long, woody pods that contain flat, edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment in agricultural systems.
