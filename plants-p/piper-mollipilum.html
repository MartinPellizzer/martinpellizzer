Piper mollipilum

Piper mollipilum, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its finely pubescent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Piper mollipilum is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
