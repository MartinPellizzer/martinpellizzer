Pollia macrobracteata

Pollia macrobracteata, commonly known as the giant plectranthus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that resemble petals, which are often mistaken for actual flowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. Its leaves are broad, waxy, and often have a bluish or purple hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Pollia macrobracteata is valued in horticulture for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
