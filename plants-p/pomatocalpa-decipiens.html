Pomatocalpa decipiens

Pomatocalpa decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that mimic the appearance of other plants to attract pollinators. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a single or few racemes of flowers. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
