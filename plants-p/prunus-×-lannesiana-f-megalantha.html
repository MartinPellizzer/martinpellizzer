Prunus × lannesiana f. megalantha

Prunus × lannesiana f. megalantha, commonly known as the giant Japanese apricot, is a unique hybrid plant that exhibits large, showy flowers.

It is characterized by its elongated, branched stems and can grow quite tall, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the fruit is a small, hairy drupe that matures in late summer.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its floral display.
