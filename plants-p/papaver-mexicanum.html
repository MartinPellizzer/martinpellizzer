Papaver mexicanum

Papaver mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Papaver mexicanum is a member of the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species such as the opium poppy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
