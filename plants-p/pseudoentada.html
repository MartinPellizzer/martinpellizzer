Pseudoentada

Pseudoentada, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, round seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
