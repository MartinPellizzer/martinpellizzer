Pellaea dura var. holstii

Pellaea dura var. holstii, commonly known as the Holst's tough fern, is a variant of the hardy Pellaea dura species.

It is a perennial fern native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant features a compact, clump-forming growth habit with dark green, leathery fronds that are densely packed. Its rhizomes are robust, allowing it to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
