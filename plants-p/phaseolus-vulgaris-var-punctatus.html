Phaseolus vulgaris var. punctatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. punctatus, commonly known as the dotted bean, is a variant of the common bean characterized by its distinctive mottled or spotted foliage and pods.

This plant is native to the Americas and thrives in warm, temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces edible beans that are typically used in culinary applications similar to other Phaseolus species. The plant's botanical features include compound leaves with stipules and flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

Its cultivation is often associated with traditional farming practices in regions where it is native.
