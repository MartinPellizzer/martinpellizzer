Pleurothallis hirsutula

Pleurothallis hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features delicate, hairy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique morphology and adaptability to humid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
