Puccinellia poecilantha

Puccinellia poecilantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in saline soils and is an important component of salt marsh ecosystems.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
