Pulsatilla vulgaris var. germanica

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. germanica, commonly known as the German pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, and it produces a distinctive, milky sap.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
