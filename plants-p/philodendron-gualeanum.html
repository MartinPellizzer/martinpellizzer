Philodendron gualeanum

Philodendron gualeanum, commonly known as the Gualeanum philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron gualeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
