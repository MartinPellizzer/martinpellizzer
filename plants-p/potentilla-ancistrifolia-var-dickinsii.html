Potentilla ancistrifolia var. dickinsii

Potentilla ancistrifolia var. dickinsii, commonly known as the Dickinsii cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and slightly serrated. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
