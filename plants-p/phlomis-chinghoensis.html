Phlomis chinghoensis

Phlomis chinghoensis, commonly known as the Chingho wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
