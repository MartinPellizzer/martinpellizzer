Pierardia pubera

Pierardia pubera, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert plains. The plant features a low-growing rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in early spring, followed by small, greenish-brown fruits that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
