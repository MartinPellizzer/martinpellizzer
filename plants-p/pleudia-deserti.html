Pleudia deserti

Pleudia deserti, commonly known as the desert pleurothallid, is a rare orchid species native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale purple. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through specialized root systems and water-storing tissues. Its flowers typically bloom during the rainy season, showcasing a unique adaptation to its desert habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pleudia deserti is considered a vulnerable species.
