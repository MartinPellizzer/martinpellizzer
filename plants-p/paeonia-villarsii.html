Paeonia villarsii

Paeonia villarsii, commonly known as the Armenian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the family Paeoniaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers. The plant typically produces compound leaves with serrated edges and blooms in late spring to early summer with flowers ranging from pink to white. Paeonia villarsii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after species in both horticulture and phytotherapy.
