Prunus tenella var. tenella

Prunus tenella var. tenella, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that change color with the seasons, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact size and striking foliage make it a favorite for landscape design.
