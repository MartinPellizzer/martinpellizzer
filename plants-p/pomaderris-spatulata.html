Pomaderris spatulata

Pomaderris spatulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, white flowers in spring. It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.

The fruit is small and edible, though not commonly consumed by humans.
