Psychotria bulilimontis var. aestuarii

Psychotria bulilimontis var. aestuarii, commonly known as the coastal psychotria, is a flowering plant native to the humid coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests near the coast.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
