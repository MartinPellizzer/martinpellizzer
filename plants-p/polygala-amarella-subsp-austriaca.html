Polygala amarella subsp. austriaca

Polygala amarella subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian milkvetch, is a member of the Polygalaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to Central Europe. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces small, dry fruits containing seeds.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
