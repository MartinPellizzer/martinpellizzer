Potamogeton × longifolius

Potamogeton × longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs naturally in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, giving it a distinctive appearance in water bodies. The plant typically grows in shallow waters and can form dense vegetation, which provides habitat for various aquatic organisms. It reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
