Phlomis ocymifolia

Phlomis ocymifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plumevetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often fuzzy. The plant produces showy, pale purple to pink flower clusters in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
