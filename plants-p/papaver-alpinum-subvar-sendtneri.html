Papaver alpinum subvar. sendtneri

Papaver alpinum subvar. sendtneri, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a variant of the Alpine poppy species native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically in alpine meadows and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces a capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
