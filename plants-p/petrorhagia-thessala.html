Petrorhagia thessala

Petrorhagia thessala, commonly known as the Greek rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, woolly leaves. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
