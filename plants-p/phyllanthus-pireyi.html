Phyllanthus pireyi

Phyllanthus pireyi, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it susceptible to drought but resilient in disturbed environments.
