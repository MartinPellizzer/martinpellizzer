Phragmites nakaianus

Phragmites nakaianus, commonly known as the New Zealand reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense growth and hollow, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-brown spikelets that are clustered at the top of the stems. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, allowing it to spread rapidly and dominate wetland habitats.

Phragmites nakaianus plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species.
