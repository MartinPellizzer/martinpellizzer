Premna racemosa

Premna racemosa, commonly known as Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

Premna racemosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
