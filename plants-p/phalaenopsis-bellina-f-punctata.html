Phalaenopsis bellina f. punctata

Phalaenopsis bellina f. punctata, commonly known as the speckled phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its striking, speckled blooms.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features long, lance-shaped leaves and produces flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with distinctive spots or markings. It is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The botanical name reflects its origin from the species Phalaenopsis bellina, with 'punctata' referring to the speckled patterns on its flowers.
