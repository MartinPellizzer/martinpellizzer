Pogonia macrantha

Pogonia macrantha, commonly known as the showy pogonia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.

The species is known for its fragrant blooms and is considered a rare and endangered plant in many regions.
