Potentilla impolita subsp. magyarica

Potentilla impolita subsp.

magyarica, commonly known as the Hungarian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has alternate, pinnately compound leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
