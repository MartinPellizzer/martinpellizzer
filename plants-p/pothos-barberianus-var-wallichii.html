Pothos barberianus var. wallichii

Pothos barberianus var. wallichii, commonly known as the silver pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with shades of green, white, and silver, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
