Pterolepis parnassiifolia

Pterolepis parnassiifolia, commonly known as the Parnassian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a violet or purple tint. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a silvery or grayish underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pterolepis parnassiifolia is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in alpine gardens.
