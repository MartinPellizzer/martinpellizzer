Polygonatum verticillatum

Polygonatum verticillatum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring. Its berries are dark blue and edible, though often not consumed due to their bitter taste.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
