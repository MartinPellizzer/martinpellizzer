Piper patulum var. cordifolium

Piper patulum var. cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into elliptical, red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.
