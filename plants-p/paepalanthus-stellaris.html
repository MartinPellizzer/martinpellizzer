Paepalanthus stellaris

Paepalanthus stellaris, commonly known as the star cactus, is a unique plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a type of cactus belonging to the Cactaceae family, characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its distinctive appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid or semi-arid environments.
