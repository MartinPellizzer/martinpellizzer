Plectranthus caespitosus

Plectranthus caespitosus, commonly known as creeping fig or African violet, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, blooming in clusters. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its compact growth habit makes it an attractive choice for ground cover or container gardening.
