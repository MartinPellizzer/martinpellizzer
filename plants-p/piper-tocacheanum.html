Piper tocacheanum

Piper tocacheanum, commonly known as the Tocache pepper or the Amazonian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
