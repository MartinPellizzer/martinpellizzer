Parkia oppositifolia

Parkia oppositifolia, commonly known as the velvet bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces pod-like fruits containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in tropical ecosystems.
