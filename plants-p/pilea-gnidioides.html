Pilea gnidioides

Pilea gnidioides, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea gnidioides thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
