Pleurothallis handroi

Pleurothallis handroi, commonly known as the "white lady orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree branches without parasitizing them. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis handroi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
