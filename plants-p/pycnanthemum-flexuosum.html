Pycnanthemum flexuosum

Pycnanthemum flexuosum, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming effects.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
