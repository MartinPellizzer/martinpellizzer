Passiflora macrocarpa

Passiflora macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant granadilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a hairy texture.

Passiflora macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
