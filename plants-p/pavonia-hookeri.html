Pavonia hookeri

Pavonia hookeri, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
