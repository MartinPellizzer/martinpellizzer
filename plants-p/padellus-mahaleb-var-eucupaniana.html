Padellus mahaleb var. eucupaniana

Padellus mahaleb var. eucupaniana, commonly known as the Mahaleb cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfume and culinary applications.
