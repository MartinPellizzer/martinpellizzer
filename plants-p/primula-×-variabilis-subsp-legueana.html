Primula × variabilis subsp. legueana

Primula × variabilis subsp.

legueana, commonly known as the variable primrose, is a hybrid species within the Primulaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. It has ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
