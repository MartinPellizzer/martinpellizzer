Polygala oxyptera var. majus

Polygala oxyptera var. majus, commonly known as the large-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
