Paspalum brasiliense

Paspalum brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it thrive in various soil conditions.

Paspalum brasiliense is often found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along riverbanks, making it an adaptable species in its native range.
