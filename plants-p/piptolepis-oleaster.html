Piptolepis oleaster

Piptolepis oleaster, commonly known as the wild olive, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, drupe that turns from green to black as it matures.

Piptolepis oleaster is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
