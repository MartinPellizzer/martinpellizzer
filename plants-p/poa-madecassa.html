Poa madecassa

Poa madecassa, commonly known as the Madagascan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, Poa madecassa is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
