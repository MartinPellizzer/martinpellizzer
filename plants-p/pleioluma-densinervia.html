Pleioluma densinervia

Pleioluma densinervia, commonly known as the dense-veined orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and dense vein patterns in its leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Pleioluma densinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
