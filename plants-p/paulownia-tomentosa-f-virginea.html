Paulownia tomentosa f. virginea

Paulownia tomentosa f. virginea, commonly known as the velvetleaf paulownia, is a cultivar of the Paulownia tree characterized by its distinctive velvety, grayish-green foliage.

It is a deciduous tree native to Asia, specifically China, and is valued for its rapid growth and ornamental appeal. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a soft, fuzzy texture, contributing to its unique appearance. It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring, which are usually pale purple or white.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a source of lightweight timber.
