Paspalum trichoides

Paspalum trichoides, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, soft blades and tufted growth habit, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its seed heads are slender and often appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Paspalum trichoides is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
