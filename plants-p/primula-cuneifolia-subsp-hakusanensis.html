Primula cuneifolia subsp. hakusanensis

Primula cuneifolia subsp.

hakusanensis, commonly known as the Hakusan primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan. It features rosettes of basal, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and dark green.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
