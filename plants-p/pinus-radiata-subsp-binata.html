Pinus radiata subsp. binata

Pinus radiata subsp.

binata, commonly known as the silver pine, is a subspecies native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in pairs.

The cones of this species are usually pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The subspecies is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
