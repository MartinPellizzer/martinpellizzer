Phalaris trabutiana

Phalaris trabutiana, commonly known as the Trabut's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Phalaris trabutiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
