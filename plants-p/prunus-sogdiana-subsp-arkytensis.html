Prunus sogdiana subsp. arkytensis

Prunus sogdiana subsp.

arkytensis, commonly known as the Sogdian cherry, is a subspecies native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Tajikistan and Afghanistan. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
