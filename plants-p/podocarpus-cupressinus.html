Podocarpus cupressinus

Podocarpus cupressinus, commonly known as the Japanese false cypress, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It has a compact, dense growth habit with scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

Podocarpus cupressinus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for various garden settings.
