Potentilla verna var. adamsiana

Potentilla verna var. adamsiana, commonly known as the early potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, woody stems and dark green, trifoliate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
