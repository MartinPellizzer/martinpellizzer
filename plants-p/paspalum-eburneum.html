Paspalum eburneum

Paspalum eburneum, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and fluffy, seed-bearing heads that appear in late summer. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can be beneficial for erosion control.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
