Pulsatilla cernua var. cernua

Pulsatilla cernua var. cernua, commonly known as the nodding pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, basal leaves that form a rosette and its distinctive, nodding, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy, often toxic flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
