Phanera pyrrhoclada

Phanera pyrrhoclada, commonly known as the red-stemmed phanera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its distinctive red-barked stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are often aromatic and have a slightly rough texture.

Phanera pyrrhoclada is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
