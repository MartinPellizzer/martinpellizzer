Phaseolus acutifolius var. latifolius

Phaseolus acutifolius var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf common bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and erect growth habit. This plant typically produces small, pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.

The plant is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in traditional agriculture.
