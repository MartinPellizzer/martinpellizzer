Pavonia rosea

Pavonia rosea, commonly known as the rose mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red blooms that appear in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pavonia rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
