Pouteria pancheri

Pouteria pancheri, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its edible seeds, which are encased in a hard, woody shell and are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season.

Pouteria pancheri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
