Panicum fuscorubens

Panicum fuscorubens, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, fluffy seed heads that are often pink to reddish in color. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental use. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a reddish tinge that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Panicum fuscorubens is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
