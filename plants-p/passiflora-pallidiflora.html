Passiflora pallidiflora

Passiflora pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, delicate flowers with a mix of blue, white, and purple hues. The flowers have a unique structure with a crown of filaments and a central reproductive chamber. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
