Phaseolus purpusii

Phaseolus purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican bean or Purpus's bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions.
