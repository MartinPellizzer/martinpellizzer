Polygonum nodosum

Polygonum nodosum, commonly known as prickly knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces erect, branched stems that are often covered in stiff, prickly hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have toothed margins. Small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides.
