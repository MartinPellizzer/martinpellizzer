Primula esquirolii

Primula esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the spring.

Primula esquirolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
