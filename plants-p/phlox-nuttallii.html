Phlox nuttallii

Phlox nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Nuttall's phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
