Panax scutellarioides

Panax scutellarioides, commonly known as the yellow ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its root system is often harvested for its medicinal properties, although it is less commercially valuable than other ginseng species.
