Pteris × nayariana

Pteris × nayariana, commonly known as the crested water fern, is a hybrid fern species resulting from the cross between Pteris cretica and Pteris vittata.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled fronds that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in damp forests. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a serrated margin and a dark green, glossy surface.

Pteris × nayariana is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in water gardens or as a decorative plant in shaded areas.
