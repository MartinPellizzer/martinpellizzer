Pachylobus igaganga

Pachylobus igaganga, commonly known as the African wild bean, is a climbing legume native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish, and it produces edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
