Puccinellia distans subsp. borealis

Puccinellia distans subsp.

borealis, commonly known as Arctic grass or northern glassworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in tundra and coastal environments.

Its adaptations to cold climates make it an important component of Arctic plant communities.
