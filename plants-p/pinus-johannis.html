Pinus johannis

Pinus johannis, commonly known as the Johann's pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The cones of Pinus johannis are woody and cylindrical, containing edible seeds that are dispersed by wind. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
