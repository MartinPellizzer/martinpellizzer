Piper veredianum

Piper veredianum, commonly known as the Veredianum pepper or the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is also considered a problematic species in ecological contexts.
