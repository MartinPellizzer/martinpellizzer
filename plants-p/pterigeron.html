Pterigeron

Pterigeron, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in a spreading habit, forming dense mats that can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Pterigeron species are often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects due to their hardiness and adaptability.
