Phanera fulva

Phanera fulva, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Phanera fulva is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy foliage and floral display.
