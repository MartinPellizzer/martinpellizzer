Paederota bonarota var. major

Paederota bonarota var. major, commonly known as the greater red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically pink to purple in color, and its small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its role in soil nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop for livestock.
