Peperomia chambesyana

Peperomia chambesyana, commonly known as the Chambesyana pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, which are typically clustered in spikes. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Peperomia chambesyana prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
