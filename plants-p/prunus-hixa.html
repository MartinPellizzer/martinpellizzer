Prunus hixa

Prunus hixa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its fruit is a small, hard drupe that turns red when mature.

Prunus hixa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its beautiful floral display.
