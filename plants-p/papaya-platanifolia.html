Papaya platanifolia

Papaya platanifolia, commonly known as the giant papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruit that ripens quickly. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is rich in enzymes like papain, which is used in food processing and medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental leaves, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
