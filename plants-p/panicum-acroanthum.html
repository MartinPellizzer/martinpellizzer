Panicum acroanthum

Panicum acroanthum, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
