Polygala amarella var. cantalica

Polygala amarella var. cantalica, commonly known as the Cantalica milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
