Pholidia longifolia

Pholidia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Pholidia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
