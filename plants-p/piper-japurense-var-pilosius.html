Piper japurense var. pilosius

Piper japurense var. pilosius, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
