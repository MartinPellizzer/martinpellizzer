Panicum fuscinode

Panicum fuscinode, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its coarse, reddish-brown foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often pale yellow to reddish in color.

Panicum fuscinode is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
