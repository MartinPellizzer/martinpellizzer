Plectranthus pichompae

Plectranthus pichompae, commonly known as the golden plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
