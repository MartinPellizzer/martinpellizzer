Polygala vulgaris var. ciliata

Polygala vulgaris var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems with opposite, trifoliate leaves that have ciliate margins, giving it its scientific name. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
