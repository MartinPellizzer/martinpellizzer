Psidium formosum

Psidium formosum, commonly known as the Taiwan guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for both fruit production and landscaping purposes.
