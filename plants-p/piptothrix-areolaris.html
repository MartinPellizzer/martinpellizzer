Piptothrix areolaris

Piptothrix areolaris, commonly known as the areolar pincushion cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that form a spherical or cushion-like shape. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stems, adapting to arid environments.

Piptothrix areolaris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
