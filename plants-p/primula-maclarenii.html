Primula maclarenii

Primula maclarenii, commonly known as the Maclaren's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating.

Primula maclarenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
