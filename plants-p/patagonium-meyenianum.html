Patagonium meyenianum

Patagonium meyenianum, commonly known as the Andean balsam, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate climates at high altitudes. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
