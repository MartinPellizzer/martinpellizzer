Panicum sanguinale var. reimarioides

Panicum sanguinale var. reimarioides, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its erect, cylindrical inflorescences that bear dense clusters of small, red or brown spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and the plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant color and architectural form.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
