Pfaffia elata

Pfaffia elata, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind. Pfaffia elata is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its purported adaptogenic and energizing effects.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
