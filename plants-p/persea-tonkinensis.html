Persea tonkinensis

Persea tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its bark is thick and cork-like, and it thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
