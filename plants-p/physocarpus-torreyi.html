Physocarpus torreyi

Physocarpus torreyi, commonly known as the smooth ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Physocarpus torreyi is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
