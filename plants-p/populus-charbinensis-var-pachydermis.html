Populus charbinensis var. pachydermis

Populus charbinensis var. pachydermis, commonly known as the thick-barked poplar, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark that provides protection against environmental stressors. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soils, preferring temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth margin and a pale underside.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
