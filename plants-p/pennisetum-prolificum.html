Pennisetum prolificum

Pennisetum prolificum, commonly known as purple top millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive purple-tinted foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces fluffy, light green to brown seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Pennisetum prolificum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
