Pachycladon fastigiatum

Pachycladon fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. The plant produces small, cylindrical flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, which appear in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in full sun.

The species is often used in native gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
