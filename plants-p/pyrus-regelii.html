Pyrus regelii

Pyrus regelii, commonly known as the Regel's pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits that are similar to pears.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.
