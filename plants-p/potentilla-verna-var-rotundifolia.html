Potentilla verna var. rotundifolia

Potentilla verna var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are typically arranged in groups of five, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
