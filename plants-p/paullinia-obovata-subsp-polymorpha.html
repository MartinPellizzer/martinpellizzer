Paullinia obovata subsp. polymorpha

Paullinia obovata subsp.

polymorpha, commonly known as the Brazilian soursop, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height.

The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to obovate leaves with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a large, spiny, green berry that ripens to yellow or orange.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
