Paraspalathus capitata

Paraspalathus capitata, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is enclosed in a hard, woody casing. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
