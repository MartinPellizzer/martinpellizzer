Plantago aegyptica

Plantago aegyptica, commonly known as Egyptian plantain, is a perennial herb native to Africa, the Middle East, and southern Europe.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces greenish-white flowers in a dense spike, which are borne on a tall, slender stem. It thrives in a variety of soils but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is also known for its ability to colonize disturbed areas.
