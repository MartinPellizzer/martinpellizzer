Panicum miliaceum var. anthracinum

Panicum miliaceum var. anthracinum, commonly known as red millet, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown inflorescence.

It is native to warm temperate and tropical regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that mature into hard, edible seeds. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious grain.
