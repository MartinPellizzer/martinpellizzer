Psiadia lithospermifolia

Psiadia lithospermifolia, commonly known as the stone root daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
