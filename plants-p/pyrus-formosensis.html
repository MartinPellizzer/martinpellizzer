Pyrus formosensis

Pyrus formosensis, commonly known as the Formosan pear, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers in spring. The tree produces edible pears that are typically small, sweet, and have a crisp texture. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
