Patagonium canescens

Patagonium canescens, commonly known as the Patagonian hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems produce small, greenish-white inflorescences, and it is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Patagonium canescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
