Prunus cerasus f. variegata

Prunus cerasus f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cherry or variegated sour cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and green patterns on the leaves. The plant produces small, sour cherries that are typically used for cooking rather than eating fresh. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual interest.
