Pinus haageana

Pinus haageana, commonly known as the Haage's pine, is a species of conifer native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife. Its needles are long and slender, growing in bundles of five, and its bark is thick and fissured.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the mountainous regions where it is found.
