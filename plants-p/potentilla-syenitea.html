Potentilla syenitea

Potentilla syenitea, commonly known as the Syenite Cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Potentilla syenitea is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine environments.
