Panicum lancifolium

Panicum lancifolium, commonly known as eastern dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are linear and have a sharp, pointed tip. The plant produces slender, flowering stalks that bear small, fluffy panicles, which are often white or pale green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Panicum lancifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ecological benefits.
