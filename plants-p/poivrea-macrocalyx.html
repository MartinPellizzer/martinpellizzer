Poivrea macrocalyx

Poivrea macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Poivrea macrocalyx is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
