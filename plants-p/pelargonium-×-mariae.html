Pelargonium × mariae

Pelargonium × mariae, commonly known as the marie geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it thrives in well-drained soil and sunny conditions. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Pelargonium × mariae is also popular in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
