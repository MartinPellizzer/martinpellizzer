Philodendron validinervium

Philodendron validinervium, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with prominent red or orange veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by orange berries, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.

Philodendron validinervium is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
