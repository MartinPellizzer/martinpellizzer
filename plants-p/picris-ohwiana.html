Picris ohwiana

Picris ohwiana, commonly known as the Indian goatweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant features slender, branching stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Picris ohwiana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
