Phyllanthus hookeri

Phyllanthus hookeri, commonly known as the Indian star gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, star-shaped yellow flowers and red, fleshy berries that resemble small gooseberries. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Phyllanthus hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
