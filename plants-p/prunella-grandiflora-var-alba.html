Prunella grandiflora var. alba

Prunella grandiflora var. alba, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 30 cm tall, featuring opposite, hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white, bilaterally symmetric flowers. The flowers are typically arranged in whorls along the stems, and the plant blooms from late spring to early autumn. It prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands, stream banks, and gardens.

The plant is known for its medicinal uses and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
