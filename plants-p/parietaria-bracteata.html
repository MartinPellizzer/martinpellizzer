Parietaria bracteata

Parietaria bracteata, commonly known as the common wall rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences. Parietaria bracteata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to environmental stress.

It is also noted for its role in supporting local pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
