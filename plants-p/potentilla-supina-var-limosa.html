Potentilla supina var. limosa

Potentilla supina var. limosa, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features wiry, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early to mid-summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval to elliptic, with serrated edges and a grayish-green color.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands.
