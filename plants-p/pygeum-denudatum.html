Pygeum denudatum

Pygeum denudatum, commonly known as the naked African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
