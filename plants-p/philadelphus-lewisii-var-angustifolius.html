Philadelphus lewisii var. angustifolius

Philadelphus lewisii var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with lance-shaped, serrated leaflets that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
