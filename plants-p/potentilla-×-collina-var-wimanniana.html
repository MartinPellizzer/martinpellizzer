Potentilla × collina var. wimanniana

Potentilla × collina var. wimanniana, commonly known as the Wimann's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring dense, upright growth with soft, hairy stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine plantings.
