Platanthera integrilabia

Platanthera integrilabia, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its striking, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, greenish-brown pseudobulbs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture and light conditions.
