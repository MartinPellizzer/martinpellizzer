Potentilla rupestris var. saxicola

Potentilla rupestris var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and rocky environments.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its compact growth habit makes it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for rocky or gravelly landscapes.
