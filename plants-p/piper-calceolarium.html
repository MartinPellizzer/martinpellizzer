Piper calceolarium

Piper calceolarium, commonly known as the "moonflower pepper," is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain aromatic seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
