Phyllanthus consanguineus

Phyllanthus consanguineus, commonly known as the Indian kidney bean or false gourd, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
