Poa macrocalyx var. tianschanica

Poa macrocalyx var. tianschanica, commonly known as the Tian Shan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contributes to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
