Primula tosaensis f. brachycarpa

Primula tosaensis f. brachycarpa, commonly known as the Tosa primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically oval and hairy, while the flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is notable for its shorter fruiting stems compared to the typical form, giving it a distinctive appearance.
