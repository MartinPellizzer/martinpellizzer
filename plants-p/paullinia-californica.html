Paullinia californica

Paullinia californica, commonly known as the California sarsaparilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Saururaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into yellow, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
