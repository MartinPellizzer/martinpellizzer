Phleum mesopotamiae

Phleum mesopotamiae, commonly known as Mesopotamian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. The plant has slender, tufted culms and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and usually covered with fine hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
