Piper subglabrum

Piper subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds.

The botanical features of Piper subglabrum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
