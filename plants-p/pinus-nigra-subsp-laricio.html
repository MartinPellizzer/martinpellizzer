Pinus nigra subsp. laricio

Pinus nigra subsp.

laricio, commonly known as the Greek black pine, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark bark and needle-like leaves, which are typically longer than those of other pine species.

The cones of this plant are woody and can persist on the tree for several years. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional landscapes.
