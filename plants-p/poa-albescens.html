Poa albescens

Poa albescens, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Its flowering stems are slender and often bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

Poa albescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
