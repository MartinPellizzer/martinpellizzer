Pygeum atrovillosum

Pygeum atrovillosum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
