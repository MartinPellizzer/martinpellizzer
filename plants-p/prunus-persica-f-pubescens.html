Prunus persica f. pubescens

Prunus persica f. pubescens, commonly known as the fuzzy peach, is a botanical variant of the peach tree characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence on the fruit's surface.

This trait is a result of genetic variation and contributes to the fruit's unique texture and appearance. The plant retains the typical growth habits of Prunus persica, including a deciduous nature and a preference for temperate climates. Its flowers are similar to those of the standard peach, featuring five petals and numerous stamens.

The fruit, while less commercially cultivated than the common peach, is valued for its ornamental qualities and unique sensory characteristics.
