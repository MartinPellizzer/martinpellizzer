Piper argyrophyllum var. walkeri

Piper argyrophyllum var. walkeri, commonly known as the silver-leafed pepper plant, is a variant of the argyrophyllum species within the Piper genus.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white variegation on its leaves, which contrasts with its dark green foliage. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. It produces small, clustered white flowers followed by red, peppery fruits.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and can also be used in traditional herbal practices.
