Purdiaea stenopetala var. stereosepala

Purdiaea stenopetala var. stereosepala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled purdiaea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular. Its sepals are fused at the base, a distinguishing feature of the varietal name "stereosepala.".

It is primarily found in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its native habitat.
