Primula dryadifolia subsp. congestifolia

Primula dryadifolia subsp.

congestifolia, commonly known as the clustered primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are oval to lance-shaped, often with a slight serration along the edges. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
