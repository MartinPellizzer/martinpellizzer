Pithecellobium paucipinnatum

Pithecellobium paucipinnatum, commonly known as the small-leaf burr legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and savanna habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its potential medicinal properties.
