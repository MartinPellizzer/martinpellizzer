Paspalum foliosum

Paspalum foliosum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It is often found in areas with high humidity and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

Paspalum foliosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
