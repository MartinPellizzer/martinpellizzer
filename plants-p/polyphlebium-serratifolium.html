Polyphlebium serratifolium

Polyphlebium serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated leaf fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its fronds that are deeply lobed and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.
