Piper cercidiphyllum

Piper cercidiphyllum, commonly known as the Chinese pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
