Phaseolus vulgaris var. albidus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. albidus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white pod and seed color, distinguishing it from the typical red or brown kidney beans. This plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. The leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces erect, bushy growth.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is used in various culinary and agricultural contexts.
