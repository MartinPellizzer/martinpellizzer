Primula firmipes subsp. firmipes

Primula firmipes subsp.

firmipes, commonly known as the firm-footed primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant features basal rosettes of ovate, hairy leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive, spurred corolla structure.
