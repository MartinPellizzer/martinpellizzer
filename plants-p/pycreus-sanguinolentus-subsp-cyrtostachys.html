Pycreus sanguinolentus subsp. cyrtostachys

Pycreus sanguinolentus subsp.

cyrtostachys, commonly known as the crimson-veined orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its vibrant red veins contrasting against its pale green foliage.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species in horticulture.
