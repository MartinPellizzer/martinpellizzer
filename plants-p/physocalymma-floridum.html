Physocalymma floridum

Physocalymma floridum, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Physocalymma floridum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
