Prunus mutabilis f. subsessilis

Prunus mutabilis f. subsessilis, commonly known as the white flowering cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers that appear in early spring. The subsessilis form exhibits shorter pedicels compared to the typical form, giving the flowers a more compact appearance. This cultivar is often valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of East Asia.
