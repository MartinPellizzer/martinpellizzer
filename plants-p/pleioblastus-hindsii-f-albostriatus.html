Pleioblastus hindsii f. albostriatus

Pleioblastus hindsii f. albostriatus, commonly known as the white-striped bamboo, is a cultivar of the bamboo genus Pleioblastus.

It is characterized by its striking white stripes on dark green leaves, creating a visually appealing contrast. This variety is native to China and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
