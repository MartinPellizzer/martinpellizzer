Prunus spinosa var. parvifolia

Prunus spinosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length. The plant produces small, dark purple berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in supporting biodiversity.
