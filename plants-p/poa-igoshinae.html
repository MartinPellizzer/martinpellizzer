Poa igoshinae

Poa igoshinae, commonly known as the Japanese bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, soft blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or shaded soils. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually erect and slender.

Poa igoshinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
