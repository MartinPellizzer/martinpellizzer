Piptocoma hypochlora

Piptocoma hypochlora, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
