Perideridia erythrorhiza

Perideridia erythrorhiza, commonly known as red-root sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in sagebrush steppe and other arid environments. The plant has a distinctive red-colored root system, which is a key identifying feature. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring.

Perideridia erythrorhiza is adapted to harsh conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
