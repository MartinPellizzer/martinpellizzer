Pfaffia luzuliflora var. elliptica

Pfaffia luzuliflora var. elliptica, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
