Pyrus heryana

Pyrus heryana, commonly known as the Heryan's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central Asia and is characterized by its deciduous nature and ornamental value. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with smooth, dark-barked stems and alternate, simple leaves. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible fruits that are similar to pears.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
