Pilea latifolia

Pilea latifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it an excellent choice for indoor gardening.
