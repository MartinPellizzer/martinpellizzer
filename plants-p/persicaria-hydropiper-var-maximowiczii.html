Persicaria hydropiper var. maximowiczii

Persicaria hydropiper var. maximowiczii, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a distinctive scent when crushed, which is due to the presence of essential oils. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and streambanks.

Its leaves are oval-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.
