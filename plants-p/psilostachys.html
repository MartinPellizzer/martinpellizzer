Psilostachys

Psilostachys, commonly known as the Chinese grass or psilostachy, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous and native to East Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia. They are characterized by their slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Psilostachys species often thrive in moist, shaded environments and are sometimes cultivated for their ornamental value.

The genus is of interest to botanists due to its ecological roles and potential for horticultural use.
