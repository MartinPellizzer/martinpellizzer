Pelargonium quercinum

Pelargonium quercinum, commonly known as the oakleaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium quercinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
