Polygala hilariana

Polygala hilariana, commonly known as the Madagascar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and coastal habitats, exhibiting a low, compact growth form. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, while its fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

Polygala hilariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
