Potamogeton longipetiolatus

Potamogeton longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often longer than they are wide, with long petioles that help it float just below the water surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spikes, typically emerging from the water during the summer months. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving rivers.
