Pittosporum tenuifolium var. fasciculatum

Pittosporum tenuifolium var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
