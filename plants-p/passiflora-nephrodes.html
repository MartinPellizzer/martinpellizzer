Passiflora nephrodes

Passiflora nephrodes, commonly known as the kidneyleaf passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped leaves and striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and white petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its growth habit includes both climbing and shrub forms, and it thrives in humid, warm climates.

The botanical features of Passiflora nephrodes make it a valuable subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
