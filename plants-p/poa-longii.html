Poa longii

Poa longii, commonly known as the longleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its fine texture and delicate appearance.

Poa longii is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
