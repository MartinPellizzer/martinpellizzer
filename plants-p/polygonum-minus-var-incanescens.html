Polygonum minus var. incanescens

Polygonum minus var. incanescens, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features upright stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a pale underside and a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
