Passiflora foetida var. parvifolia

Passiflora foetida var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its fruit is a rough, spiny capsule that contains inedible, toxic seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
