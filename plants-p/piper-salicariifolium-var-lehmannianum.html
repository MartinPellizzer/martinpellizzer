Piper salicariifolium var. lehmannianum

Piper salicariifolium var. lehmannianum, commonly known as the Lehmann's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed berry that turns dark purple when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
