Podocarpus sweetii

Podocarpus sweetii, commonly known as the sweet podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically opposite, narrow, and lanceolate, with a leathery texture. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
