Penstemon virgatus

Penstemon virgatus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically reaches heights of 2 to 6 feet and produces lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Penstemon virgatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
