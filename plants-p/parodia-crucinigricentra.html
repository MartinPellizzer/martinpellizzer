Parodia crucinigricentra

Parodia crucinigricentra, commonly known as the Zebra Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that are covered in clusters of small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and xeriscaping.
