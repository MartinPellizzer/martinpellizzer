Pycnocoma reticulata

Pycnocoma reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined pycnocoma, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, and its flowers are yellow to orange in color.

Pycnocoma reticulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
