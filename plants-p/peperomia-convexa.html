Peperomia convexa

Peperomia convexa, commonly known as the Shiny Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, and often variegated leaves that are glossy and slightly curled, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and minimal water needs.

Peperomia convexa is prized for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
