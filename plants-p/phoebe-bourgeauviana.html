Phoebe bourgeauviana

Phoebe bourgeauviana, commonly known as the Florida phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
