Patagonium miraflorense

Patagonium miraflorense, commonly known as the Andean flameflower, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
