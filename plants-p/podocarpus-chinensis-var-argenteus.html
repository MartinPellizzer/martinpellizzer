Podocarpus chinensis var. argenteus

Podocarpus chinensis var. argenteus, commonly known as the silver podocarp, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a slow-growing evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish powdery coating. The leaves are typically arranged in whorls and can vary in size depending on light exposure. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain its seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
