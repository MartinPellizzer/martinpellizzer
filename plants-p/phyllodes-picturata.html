Phyllodes picturata

Phyllodes picturata, commonly known as the painted wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique, flattened, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to its native habitat. Phyllodes picturata is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
