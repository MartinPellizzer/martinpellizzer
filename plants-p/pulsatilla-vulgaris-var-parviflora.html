Pulsatilla vulgaris var. parviflora

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its early spring blooming period, producing showy, showy, purple-pink flowers that resemble those of the common pasqueflower but are smaller in size. The plant has a short, hairy stem that supports clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Ranunculaceae family, valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
