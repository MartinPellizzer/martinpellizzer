Pleurothallis rhaphidopus

Pleurothallis rhaphidopus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis rhaphidopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
