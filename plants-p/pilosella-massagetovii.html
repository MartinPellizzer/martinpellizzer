Pilosella massagetovii

Pilosella massagetovii, commonly known as the Massagetov's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounded by a central disk of tubular flowers, giving it a delicate, fluffy appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow, grass-like, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Pilosella massagetovii thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
