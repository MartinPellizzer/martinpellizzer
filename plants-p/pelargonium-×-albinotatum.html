Pelargonium × albinotatum

Pelargonium × albinotatum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × odoratum.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring combinations of white, yellow, and green on the leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium × albinotatum is a popular ornamental plant used in gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
