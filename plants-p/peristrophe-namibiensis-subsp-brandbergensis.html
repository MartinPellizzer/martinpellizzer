Peristrophe namibiensis subsp. brandbergensis

Peristrophe namibiensis subsp.

brandbergensis, commonly known as the Brandberg grass, is a perennial grass species native to Namibia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
