Pyrus caeruleomontana

Pyrus caeruleomontana, commonly known as the blue mountain pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Tien Shan and Pamir Mountains. The plant is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a small, apple-like berry that ripens to a deep blue or purple color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in horticulture.
