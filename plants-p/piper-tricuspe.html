Piper tricuspe

Piper tricuspe, commonly known as the three-lobed pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are followed by small, round berries that contain the aromatic seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
