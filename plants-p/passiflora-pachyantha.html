Passiflora pachyantha

Passiflora pachyantha, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically purple or red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and often have a waxy coating.

Passiflora pachyantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
