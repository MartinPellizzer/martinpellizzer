Psidium crenatum

Psidium crenatum, commonly known as the Strawberry Guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
