Primula clusiana

Primula clusiana, commonly known as the Clusian primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments, often in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Primula clusiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
