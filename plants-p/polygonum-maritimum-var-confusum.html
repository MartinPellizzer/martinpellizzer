Polygonum maritimum var. confusum

Polygonum maritimum var. confusum, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in coastal dunes and tidal zones. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and its economic importance in herbal medicine and nutrition.
