Pitcheria galactioides var. parvifolia

Pitcheria galactioides var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It features distinctive, pale green to yellowish stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in umbel-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variant is notable for its role in supporting pollinators and its ecological importance in native habitats.
