Pieris rubrovenia

Pieris rubrovenia, commonly known as the red-veined Japanese laurel, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking red veins. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms, it is often cultivated for landscaping purposes.
