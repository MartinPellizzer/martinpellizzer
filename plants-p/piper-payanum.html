Piper payanum

Piper payanum, commonly known as the Payanum pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit, a small berry, contains the aromatic compounds responsible for its distinctive flavor.

Piper payanum is often grown for its culinary and medicinal uses due to its pungent and spicy properties.
