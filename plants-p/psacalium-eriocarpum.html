Psacalium eriocarpum

Psacalium eriocarpum, commonly known as the woolly senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by hairy, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Psacalium eriocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
