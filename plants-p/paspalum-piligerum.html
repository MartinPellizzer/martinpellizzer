Paspalum piligerum

Paspalum piligerum, commonly known as the rough paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along waterways.

Paspalum piligerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
