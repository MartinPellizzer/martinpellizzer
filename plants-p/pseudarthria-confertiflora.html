Pseudarthria confertiflora

Pseudarthria confertiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered pseudarthria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
