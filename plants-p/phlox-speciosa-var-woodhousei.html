Phlox speciosa var. woodhousei

Phlox speciosa var. woodhousei, commonly known as the Woodhouse's garden phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from pink to lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
