Papaver argemone var. neglectum

Papaver argemone var. neglectum, commonly known as the common poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, showy flowers with bright orange petals and a distinctive central disk, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Its seeds are small and hardy, allowing it to persist in various environments.
