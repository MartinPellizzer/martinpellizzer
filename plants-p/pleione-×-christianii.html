Pleione × christianii

Pleione × christianii, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom during the winter months, giving it its common name. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its cool, high-altitude habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or red with yellow lips, and they emit a faint fragrance.

Pleione × christianii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its seasonal blooms.
