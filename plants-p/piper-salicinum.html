Piper salicinum

Piper salicinum, commonly known as the Chinese pepper vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its aerial stems are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Piper salicinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb various structures.
