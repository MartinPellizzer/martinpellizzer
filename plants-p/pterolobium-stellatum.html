Pterolobium stellatum

Pterolobium stellatum, commonly known as the star-stemmed legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with woody stems and trifoliate leaves. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes, and it produces small, hairy pods containing lens-shaped seeds.

Pterolobium stellatum is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
