Polypodium pilipes

Polypodium pilipes, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

Polypodium pilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
