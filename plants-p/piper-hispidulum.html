Piper hispidulum

Piper hispidulum, commonly known as the rough pepper or hispid hollyhock, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
