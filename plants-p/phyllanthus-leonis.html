Phyllanthus leonis

Phyllanthus leonis, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Australia, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
