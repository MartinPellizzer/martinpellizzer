Polemonium caeruleum var. subalpinum

Polemonium caeruleum var. subalpinum, commonly known as the alpine bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
