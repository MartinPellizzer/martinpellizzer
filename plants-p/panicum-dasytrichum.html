Panicum dasytrichum

Panicum dasytrichum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces spikelets that are often arranged in a panicle, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in wetlands and along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
