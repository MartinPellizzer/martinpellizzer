Potentilla × tynieckii

Potentilla × tynieckii, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish-brown in color. Potentilla × tynieckii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
