Pygeum parviflorum var. genuinum

Pygeum parviflorum var. genuinum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It features smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
