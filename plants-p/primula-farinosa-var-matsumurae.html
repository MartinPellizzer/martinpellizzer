Primula farinosa var. matsumurae

Primula farinosa var. matsumurae, commonly known as the Matsumura's primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family, which includes other well-known genera like Primula and Lobelia.
