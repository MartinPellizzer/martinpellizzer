Polygonatum multiflorum f. bracteatum

Polygonatum multiflorum f. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clustering stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
