Pandanus globulatus

Pandanus globulatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a dense, cylindrical shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in various island communities.
