Papaver oreophilum f. obtusifolium

Papaver oreophilum f. obtusifolium, commonly known as the obtuse-leaved poppy, is a botanical variant of the mountain poppy.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that give it a distinct appearance compared to other poppy species. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red or orange, typical of the Papaver genus. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
