Prunus × sacra

Prunus × sacra, commonly known as the Japanese cherry.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus serrulata. This plant is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly for its showy flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

It is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and is a symbol of renewal and vitality in many cultures.
