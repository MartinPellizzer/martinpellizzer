Paradrymonia ulei

Paradrymonia ulei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. The plant is characterized by its narrow, aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. It typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils.

Paradrymonia ulei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by Indigenous Australians.
