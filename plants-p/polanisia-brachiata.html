Polanisia brachiata

Polanisia brachiata, commonly known as the hairy bignonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red seed pods. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
