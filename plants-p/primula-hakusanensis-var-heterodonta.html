Primula hakusanensis var. heterodonta

Primula hakusanensis var. heterodonta, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
