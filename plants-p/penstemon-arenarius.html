Penstemon arenarius

Penstemon arenarius, commonly known as the sand penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, particularly in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a perennial species that is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, upright leaves that help reduce water loss.
