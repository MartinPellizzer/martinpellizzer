Phyllanthus polyphyllus var. siamensis

Phyllanthus polyphyllus var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam variegated milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
