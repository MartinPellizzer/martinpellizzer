Pilosocereus werdermannianus var. densilanatus

Pilosocereus werdermannianus var. densilanatus, commonly known as the dense-leaved cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically cylindrical in shape. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
