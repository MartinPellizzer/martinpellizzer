Prestonia lindleyana

Prestonia lindleyana, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by various bird species. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Prestonia lindleyana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
