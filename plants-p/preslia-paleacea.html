Preslia paleacea

Preslia paleacea, commonly known as the pale pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the stems are often covered in fine white hairs.

Preslia paleacea is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
