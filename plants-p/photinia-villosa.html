Photinia villosa

Photinia villosa, commonly known as the silky leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Photinia villosa is relatively low maintenance and adaptable to various environmental conditions.
