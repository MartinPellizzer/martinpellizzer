Pilosocereus tuberculatus

Pilosocereus tuberculatus, commonly known as the Hedgehog Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, tuberculate bumps. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
