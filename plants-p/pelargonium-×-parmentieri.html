Pelargonium × parmentieri

Pelargonium × parmentieri, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong, often pleasant scent when touched. The plant typically has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in clusters.

Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental and for its fragrant foliage.
