Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. mesomorpha

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

mesomorpha, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae. It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

This subspecies is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
