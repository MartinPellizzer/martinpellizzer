Picea smithiana var. nepalensis

Picea smithiana var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its bark is generally smooth and grayish-brown, while its foliage is dense and dark green.

This species plays an important ecological role in mountainous forest ecosystems.
