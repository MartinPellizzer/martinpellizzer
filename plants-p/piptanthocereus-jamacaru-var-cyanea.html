Piptanthocereus jamacaru var. cyanea

Piptanthocereus jamacaru var. cyanea, commonly known as the blue cactus or blue flowered cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tint, and it produces vibrant blue flowers during the rainy season. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates. Its unique coloration and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and botanical collections.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
