Picea glauca subsp. albertiana

Picea glauca subsp.

albertiana, commonly known as the Alberta white spruce, is a subspecies of the white spruce native to Canada. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its pyramidal shape and silver-blue foliage.

The cones are slender and woody, containing two-winged seeds. It thrives in cold climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in boreal forests.
