Polygonum nutans

Polygonum nutans, commonly known as the prostrate knotweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and has a prostrate or ascending growth habit. Its leaves are ovate to rhombic in shape with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The species is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural areas.
