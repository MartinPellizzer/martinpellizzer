Paronychia italica

Paronychia italica, commonly known as Italian nail grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
