Prunus × ferganica f. pseudocerasifera

Prunus × ferganica f. pseudocerasifera, commonly known as the Fergana cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to Central Asia, particularly the Fergana Valley. It has elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside.

The flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and the plant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruit.
