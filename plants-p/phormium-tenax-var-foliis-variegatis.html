Phormium tenax var. foliis-variegatis

Phormium tenax var. foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated New Zealand flax, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves that display a variegated pattern of green, white, and yellow. The plant is evergreen, with a robust growth habit that makes it suitable for both garden beds and as a landscape feature. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
