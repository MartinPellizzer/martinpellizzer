Phyllanthus ekmanii

Phyllanthus ekmanii, commonly known as the African miracle berry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are highly nutritious. It is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamins and minerals.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
