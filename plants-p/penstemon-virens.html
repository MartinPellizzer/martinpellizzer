Penstemon virens

Penstemon virens, commonly known as the green penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces tall spikes of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon virens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
