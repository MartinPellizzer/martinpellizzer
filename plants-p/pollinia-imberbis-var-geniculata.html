Pollinia imberbis var. geniculata

Pollinia imberbis var. geniculata, commonly known as the hairy pollinia variant of the orchid genus Pollinia, is a unique botanical specimen characterized by its distinctive, elongated pollinia that are loosely attached to the stigma.

This variant exhibits a specialized structure that facilitates the transfer of pollen in orchid pollination systems. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its morphology reflects adaptations for specific pollinators, such as bees or birds.

The variation in its pollinia structure highlights the evolutionary diversity within the genus.
