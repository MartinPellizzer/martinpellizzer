Pleurothallis tamboensis

Pleurothallis tamboensis, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structures. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a fragrant scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
