Polygonum ruderale

Polygonum ruderale, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has erect stems with reddish hues, especially near the base, and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Polygonum ruderale is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
