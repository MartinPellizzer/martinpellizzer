Peperomia andina var. pseudoperuviana

Peperomia andina var. pseudoperuviana, commonly known as the Andean pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, often adorned with silver or white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to both indoor and outdoor spaces.
