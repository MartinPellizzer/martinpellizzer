Pleopeltis constricta

Pleopeltis constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive midrib. The plant typically grows on rocks or trees in humid, tropical environments. Its fronds are usually clustered at the base, forming a dense rosette.

Pleopeltis constricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist gardens.
