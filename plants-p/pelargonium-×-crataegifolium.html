Pelargonium × crataegifolium

Pelargonium × crataegifolium, commonly known as the crataegifolium geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and flowering habits.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is known for its serrated, maple-like leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
