Potentilla sulphurea var. achtarovii

Potentilla sulphurea var. achtarovii, commonly known as the sulphur potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, and its dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often at high altitudes, and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its leaves are small, hairy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
