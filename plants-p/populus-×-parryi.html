Populus × parryi

Populus × parryi, commonly known as the Parry's poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the genus Populus, which includes aspen, cottonwood, and poplar species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Populus × parryi is valued for its rapid growth and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
