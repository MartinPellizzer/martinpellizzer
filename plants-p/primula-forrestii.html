Primula forrestii

Primula forrestii, commonly known as the Forster's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Primula forrestii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
