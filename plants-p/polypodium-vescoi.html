Polypodium vescoi

Polypodium vescoi, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its stout, evergreen rhizome and its distinctive, broad, leathery fronds that are often divided into segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a rough, textured surface.

Polypodium vescoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
