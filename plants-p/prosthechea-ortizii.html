Prosthechea ortizii

Prosthechea ortizii, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinct lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates.

Prosthechea ortizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
