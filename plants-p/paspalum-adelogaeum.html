Paspalum adelogaeum

Paspalum adelogaeum, commonly known as the Florida paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer. It is adapted to tolerate flooding and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Paspalum adelogaeum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
