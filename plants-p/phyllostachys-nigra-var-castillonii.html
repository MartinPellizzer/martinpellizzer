Phyllostachys nigra var. castillonii

Phyllostachys nigra var. castillonii, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that are often covered with a thin layer of white powder, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and hardiness in temperate climates. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The variety castillonii is distinguished by its darker coloration and more compact growth habit compared to other black bamboo varieties.
