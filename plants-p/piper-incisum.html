Piper incisum

Piper incisum, commonly known as the narrowleaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a serrated edge, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Piper incisum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
