Polycnemum arvense proles majus

Polycnemum arvense proles majus, commonly known as the greater field sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and triangular leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
