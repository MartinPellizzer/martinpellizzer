Paphiopedilum haynaldianum f. album

Paphiopedilum haynaldianum f. album, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pouch-shaped lip, which is a defining feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a contrasting yellow or green throat, adding to its ornamental appeal. This orchid prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
