Pyrus pyrrhula

Pyrus pyrrhula, commonly known as the red-leaf pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, edible pear that is typically harvested in late summer.

Pyrus pyrrhula is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
