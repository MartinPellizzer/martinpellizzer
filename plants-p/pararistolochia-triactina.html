Pararistolochia triactina

Pararistolochia triactina, commonly known as the three-lobed Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-green fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, white pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
