Petalostemon decumbens

Petalostemon decumbens, commonly known as the creeping strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, showy flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or pink, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Petalostemon decumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
