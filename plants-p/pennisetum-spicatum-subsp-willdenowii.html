Pennisetum spicatum subsp. willdenowii

Pennisetum spicatum subsp.

willdenowii, commonly known as blue oat grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink, adding ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes. Pennisetum spicatum subsp.

willdenowii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
