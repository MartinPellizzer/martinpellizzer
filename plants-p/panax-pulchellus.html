Panax pulchellus

Panax pulchellus, commonly known as the beautiful ginseng, is a rare and endangered plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branched root system that resembles the shape of a human figure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have a serrated margin, while its small white flowers appear in clusters.

Due to its rarity and medicinal potential, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
