Pleurothallis pachyrachis

Pleurothallis pachyrachis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is known for its compact size and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
