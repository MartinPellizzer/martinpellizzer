Psilocaulon schlichtianum

Psilocaulon schlichtianum, commonly known as the Schlichtian false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
