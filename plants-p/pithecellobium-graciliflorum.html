Pithecellobium graciliflorum

Pithecellobium graciliflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
