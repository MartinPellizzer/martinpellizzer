Picea pungens f. glauca

Picea pungens f. glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a cultivar of the white spruce native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and striking blue-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The needles are short, stiff, and often have a bluish waxy coating, which enhances its ornamental appeal. It is a hardy evergreen that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ability to provide year-round visual interest in gardens and parks.
