Podochilus longilabris

Podochilus longilabris, commonly known as the long-labied orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive lip that extends backward, aiding in pollination.

Podochilus longilabris is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
