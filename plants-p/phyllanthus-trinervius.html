Phyllanthus trinervius

Phyllanthus trinervius, commonly known as the three-veined phyllanthus, is a small, evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with three prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Phyllanthus trinervius is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
