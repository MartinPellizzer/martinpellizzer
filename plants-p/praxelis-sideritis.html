Praxelis sideritis

Praxelis sideritis, commonly known as the Greek ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Praxelis sideritis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
