Plectranthus saccatus subsp. saccatus

Plectranthus saccatus subsp.

saccatus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It features hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with silver, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a favored plant among gardeners.
