Pulsatilla alpina var. font-queri

Pulsatilla alpina var. font-queri, commonly known as the Alpine Pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, upright stem with palmately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to pink, with numerous stamens and a central pistil.

Pulsatilla alpina var. font-queri is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
