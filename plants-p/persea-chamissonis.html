Persea chamissonis

Persea chamissonis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and camphor trees. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, egg-shaped berry, turns from green to orange when mature and is edible when fully ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
