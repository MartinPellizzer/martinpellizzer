Piptocarpha sellovii var. sellovii

Piptocarpha sellovii var. sellovii, commonly known as the Sello's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
