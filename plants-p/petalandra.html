Petalandra

Petalandra, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic display in spring. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread.
