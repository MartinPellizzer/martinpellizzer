Pilosella georgieffiana

Pilosella georgieffiana, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features dense, hairy leaves that are typically spoon-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Pilosella georgieffiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
