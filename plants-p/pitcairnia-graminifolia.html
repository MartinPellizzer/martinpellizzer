Pitcairnia graminifolia

Pitcairnia graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a dense, water-holding structure at the center. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as cloud forests. Its leaves are often narrow and grass-like, giving it its common name.

Pitcairnia graminifolia produces striking inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
