Pyrus olivaefolia

Pyrus olivaefolia, commonly known as the olive-leaved pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, olive-green leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are edible pears that are typically small and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus olivaefolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruit.
