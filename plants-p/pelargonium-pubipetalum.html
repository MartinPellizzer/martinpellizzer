Pelargonium pubipetalum

Pelargonium pubipetalum, commonly known as the velvet-leaved geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
