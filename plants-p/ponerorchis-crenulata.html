Ponerorchis crenulata

Ponerorchis crenulata, commonly known as the crested orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. It has a short rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a unique, crested appearance.

Ponerorchis crenulata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
