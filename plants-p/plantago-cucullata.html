Plantago cucullata

Plantago cucullata, commonly known as cow's parsley or buckseed, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine. Its seeds are rich in nutrients and have been used for their anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows and woodland areas.
