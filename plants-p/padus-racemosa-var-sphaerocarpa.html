Padus racemosa var. sphaerocarpa

Padus racemosa var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the red chokecherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and showy clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible for birds and mammals but contain toxic seeds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping, though care must be taken with its potentially harmful fruits.
