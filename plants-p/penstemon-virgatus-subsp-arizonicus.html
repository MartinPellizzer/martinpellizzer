Penstemon virgatus subsp. arizonicus

Penstemon virgatus subsp.

arizonicus, commonly known as Arizona beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It features upright, hairy stems and produces spikes of tubular, violet to purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine绒毛. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
