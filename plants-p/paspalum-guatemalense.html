Paspalum guatemalense

Paspalum guatemalense, commonly known as Guatemalan paspalum, is a perennial grass native to Central America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a tendency to form dense clumps.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
