Pavetta macrosepala var. glabra

Pavetta macrosepala var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth pavetta, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
