Phyllanthus brasiliensis

Phyllanthus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features small, green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, round seeds.

Phyllanthus brasiliensis is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
