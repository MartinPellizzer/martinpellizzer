Phalaris variegata

Phalaris variegata, commonly known as the variegated grass or silvergrass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green variegated foliage, which gives it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Phalaris variegata is valued in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
