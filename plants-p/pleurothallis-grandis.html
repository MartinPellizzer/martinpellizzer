Pleurothallis grandis

Pleurothallis grandis, commonly known as the grand lady orchid, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Pleurothallis grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
