Polygonum scabrinervium

Polygonum scabrinervium, commonly known as the rough-veined knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant features prominently veined, rough-textured leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Polygonum scabrinervium is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
