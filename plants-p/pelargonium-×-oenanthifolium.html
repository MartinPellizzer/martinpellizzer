Pelargonium × oenanthifolium

Pelargonium × oenanthifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, slightly serrated leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
