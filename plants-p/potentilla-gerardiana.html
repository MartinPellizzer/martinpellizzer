Potentilla gerardiana

Potentilla gerardiana, commonly known as Gerard's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in dry, rocky soils and grasslands. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets. It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 30 centimeters tall, with a mat-forming growth habit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
