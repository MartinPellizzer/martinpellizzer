Phymosia longisepala

Phymosia longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled phymosia, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for native wildlife and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
