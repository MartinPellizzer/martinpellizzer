Potentilla heptaphylla subsp. australis

Potentilla heptaphylla subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian shrub cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
