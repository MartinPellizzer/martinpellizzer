Panicum appletonii

Panicum appletonii, commonly known as Appleton's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
