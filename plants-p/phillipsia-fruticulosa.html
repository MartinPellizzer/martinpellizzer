Phillipsia fruticulosa

Phillipsia fruticulosa, commonly known as the Florida starfruit, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like species that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a star-shaped berry that turns red when mature, often used in local cuisine.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
