Piper cavalcantei

Piper cavalcantei, commonly known as the Ceará pepper or Ceará pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Brazil, particularly the state of Ceará, and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Piper cavalcantei make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
