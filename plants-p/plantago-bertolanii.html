Plantago bertolanii

Plantago bertolanii, commonly known as the Bertolani's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental or for its medicinal properties.
