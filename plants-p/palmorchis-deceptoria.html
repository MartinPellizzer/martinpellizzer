Palmorchis deceptoria

Palmorchis deceptoria, commonly known as the deceptive palm orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a slender, palm-like stem and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing in the understory of dense tropical forests. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
