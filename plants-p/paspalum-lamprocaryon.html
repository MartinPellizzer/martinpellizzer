Paspalum lamprocaryon

Paspalum lamprocaryon, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and prairies, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Paspalum lamprocaryon plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
