Platanthera stapfii

Platanthera stapfii, commonly known as the Stapf's lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and a striking, fragrant inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure that facilitates specific pollination by insects.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of orchid morphology and ecological adaptation.
