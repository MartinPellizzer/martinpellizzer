Pandanus leram var. macrocarpus

Pandanus leram var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that can reach several meters in length. Its fruit, known as a "pandanus nut," is edible and has a unique, nutty flavor.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal and wetland habitats.
