Phaseolus schottii var. longifolius

Phaseolus schottii var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved common bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant produces oval, flat pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically grown as an annual crop.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
