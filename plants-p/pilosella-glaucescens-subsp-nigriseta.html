Pilosella glaucescens subsp. nigriseta

Pilosella glaucescens subsp.

nigriseta, commonly known as the black-hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a serrated margin and a distinctive silvery or grayish underside.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.
