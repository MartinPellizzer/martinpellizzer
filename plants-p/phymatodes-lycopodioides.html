Phymatodes lycopodioides

Phymatodes lycopodioides, commonly known as the Texas clubmoss, is a member of the family Lycopodiales and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and creeping growth habit.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant has a distinctive, branching structure with sporangia borne on specialized stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
