Potamogeton pusillus var. colpophilus

Potamogeton pusillus var. colpophilus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide, which help it thrive in slow-moving or still waters. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in shallow, nutrient-rich lakes and rivers. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
