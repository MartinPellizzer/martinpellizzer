Passiflora cumbalensis var. caucana

Passiflora cumbalensis var. caucana, commonly known as the Cauca passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
