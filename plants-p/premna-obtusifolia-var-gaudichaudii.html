Premna obtusifolia var. gaudichaudii

Premna obtusifolia var. gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's broadleaf premna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a reddish calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
