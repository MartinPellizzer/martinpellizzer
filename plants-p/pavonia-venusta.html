Pavonia venusta

Pavonia venusta, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Pavonia venusta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in well-drained soil.
