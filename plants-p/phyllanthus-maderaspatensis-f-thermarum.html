Phyllanthus maderaspatensis f. thermarum

Phyllanthus maderaspatensis f. thermarum, commonly known as the thermarum variant of the Indian spurge, is a unique botanical form characterized by its distinct morphological traits.

This variant exhibits a compact growth habit with smaller, more densely packed leaves compared to the typical form. It thrives in warm, humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Its foliage is notable for its glossy texture and vibrant coloration, making it a desirable ornamental plant in horticulture.
