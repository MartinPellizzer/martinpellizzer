Potentilla tristis

Potentilla tristis, commonly known as the sorrowful cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with five oval leaflets and produces small, yellowish-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Potentilla tristis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
