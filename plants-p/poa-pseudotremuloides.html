Poa pseudotremuloides

Poa pseudotremuloides, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and compact growth habit, which allows it to thrive in high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows in open, rocky areas and is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor soils. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, blue-gray florets.

Poa pseudotremuloides plays a vital role in stabilizing alpine ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
