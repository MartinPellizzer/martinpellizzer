Polygala subglobosa

Polygala subglobosa, commonly known as the globular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
