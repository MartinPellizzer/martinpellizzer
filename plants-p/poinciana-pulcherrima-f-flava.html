Poinciana pulcherrima f. flava

Poinciana pulcherrima f. flava, commonly known as the yellow poinciana, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and clusters of bright yellow bracts that resemble flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to provide shade.

The yellow poinciana is also known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the year under optimal conditions.
