Pyrethrum cheilanthifolium

Pyrethrum cheilanthifolium, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, alpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its stems are usually hairy.

Pyrethrum cheilanthifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
