Prunus serrulata var. pendula

Prunus serrulata var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping sakura, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the autumn.

The tree thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
