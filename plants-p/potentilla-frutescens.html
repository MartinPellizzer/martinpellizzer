Potentilla frutescens

Potentilla frutescens, commonly known as shrub cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
