Potentilla multifida f. cana

Potentilla multifida f. cana, commonly known as the hairy potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
