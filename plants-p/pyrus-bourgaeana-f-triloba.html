Pyrus bourgaeana f. triloba

Pyrus bourgaeana f. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed pear, is a unique variant of the pear tree characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The fruit is typically small, yellow, and sweet, though it is not commonly cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also noted for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
