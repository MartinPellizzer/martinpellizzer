Passiflora hirsuta var. parviflora

Passiflora hirsuta var. parviflora, commonly known as the hairy passion vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive texture. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, typical of the Passifloraceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit, though not commonly consumed, is edible and has a mild, tropical flavor.
