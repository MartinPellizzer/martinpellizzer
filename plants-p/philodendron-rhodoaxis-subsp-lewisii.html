Philodendron rhodoaxis subsp. lewisii

Philodendron rhodoaxis subsp.

lewisii, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. Native to the rainforests of Central and South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant belongs to the Araceae family, known for its unique inflorescence structure consisting of a spadix and spathe. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

While it is relatively easy to care for, it requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
