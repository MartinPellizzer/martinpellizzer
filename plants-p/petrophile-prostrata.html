Petrophile prostrata

Petrophile prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate waratah, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal and heathland environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including salt spray and poor soil nutrients.

Petrophile prostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
