Pseudovanilla ternatensis

Pseudovanilla ternatensis, commonly known as the Ternate vanilla orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Indonesian island of Ternate and is characterized by its slender, climbing stem and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, curved pods. These pods are known for their vanilla-like aroma and are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Pseudovanilla ternatensis is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
