Protorhus deflexa

Protorhus deflexa, commonly known as the bent-stemmed bramble, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Protorhus deflexa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
