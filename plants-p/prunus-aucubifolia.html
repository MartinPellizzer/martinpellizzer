Prunus aucubifolia

Prunus aucubifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

Prunus aucubifolia prefers partial shade to full sun and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
