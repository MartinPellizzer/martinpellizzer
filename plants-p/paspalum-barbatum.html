Paspalum barbatum

Paspalum barbatum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, fluffy panicles that are often adorned with hairy bracts. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its common name.

Paspalum barbatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
