Phlox drummondii var. tenuis

Phlox drummondii var. tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved garden phlox, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its varietal name "tenuis," which means "thin" in Latin. It produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in early to mid-summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant.
