Potamogeton natans var. mexicanus

Potamogeton natans var. mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican submerged pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters, often found in ponds, lakes, and marshes.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is an important part of aquatic ecosystems.
