Psidium salutare var. laxum

Psidium salutare var. laxum, commonly known as the laxum guava, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with warm and humid climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits, which are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the bark is smooth and light-colored.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.
