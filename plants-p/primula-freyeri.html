Primula freyeri

Primula freyeri, commonly known as the Freyer's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, or purple. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Primula freyeri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
