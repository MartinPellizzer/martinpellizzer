Phyteuma sallei

Phyteuma sallei, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of blue or purple, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.
