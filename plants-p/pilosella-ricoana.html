Pilosella ricoana

Pilosella ricoana, commonly known as the rich mountain hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers with ray florets. It thrives in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Pilosella ricoana is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
