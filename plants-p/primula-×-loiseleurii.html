Primula × loiseleurii

Primula × loiseleurii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
