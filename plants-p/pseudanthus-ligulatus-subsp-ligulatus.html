Pseudanthus ligulatus subsp. ligulatus

Pseudanthus ligulatus subsp.

ligulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pseudanthus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has linear, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of small yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
