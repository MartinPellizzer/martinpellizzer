Primula matthioli subsp. pekinensis

Primula matthioli subsp.

pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
