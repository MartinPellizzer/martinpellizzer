Peperomia dryadum

Peperomia dryadum, commonly known as the Tree Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia dryadum is noted for its slow growth and ability to adapt to a variety of indoor conditions.
