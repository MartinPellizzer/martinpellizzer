Protea lemairei

Protea lemairei, commonly known as the Lemaire's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted by the nectar.

Protea lemairei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
