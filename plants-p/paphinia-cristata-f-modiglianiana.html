Paphinia cristata f. modiglianiana

Paphinia cristata f. modiglianiana, commonly known as the Modigliani's crested orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, crested foliage and vibrant, orchid-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include lanceolate leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
