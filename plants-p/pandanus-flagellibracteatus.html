Pandanus flagellibracteatus

Pandanus flagellibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots system is fibrous and helps it thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in its native regions.
