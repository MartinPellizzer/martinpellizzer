Polygonum franchetii

Polygonum franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its erect, somewhat woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish tint. Its small, greenish flowers appear in dense clusters, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Polygonum franchetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
