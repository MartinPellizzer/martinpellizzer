Prunus africana

Prunus africana, commonly known as the African cherry, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, red drupes. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Prunus africana also plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
