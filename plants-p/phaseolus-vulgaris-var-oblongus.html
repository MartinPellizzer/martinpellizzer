Phaseolus vulgaris var. oblongus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. oblongus, commonly known as the oblong bean, is a variety of common bean native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong-shaped pods and seeds, which are typically smaller than those of standard beans. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its culinary use, offering a mild flavor and tender texture when cooked.
