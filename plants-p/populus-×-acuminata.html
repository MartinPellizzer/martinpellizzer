Populus × acuminata

Populus × acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cottonwood, is a hybrid species within the poplar family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Populus × acuminata typically grows in riparian zones, thriving near rivers and streams.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species for reforestation and erosion control in wetland environments.
