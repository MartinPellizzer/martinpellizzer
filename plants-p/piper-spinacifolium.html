Piper spinacifolium

Piper spinacifolium, commonly known as spinach-leaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, spinach-like leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, slender fruits that turn red when mature. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
