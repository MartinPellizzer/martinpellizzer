Phaseolus persistentus

Phaseolus persistentus, commonly known as the persistent bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
