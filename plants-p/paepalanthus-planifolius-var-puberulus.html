Paepalanthus planifolius var. puberulus

Paepalanthus planifolius var. puberulus, commonly known as the velvetleaf buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are broad, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and floral structure make it a notable species within the genus Paepalanthus.
