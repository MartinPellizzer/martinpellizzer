Prunus alleghaniensis var. davisii

Prunus alleghaniensis var. davisii, commonly known as the Allegheny Serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a bright red or orange color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting local pollinators.
