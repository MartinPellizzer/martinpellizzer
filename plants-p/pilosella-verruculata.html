Pilosella verruculata

Pilosella verruculata, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features basal rosettes of hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the summer. Its flowers are typically surrounded by a ring of bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella verruculata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
