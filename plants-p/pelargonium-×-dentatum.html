Pelargonium × dentatum

Pelargonium × dentatum, commonly known as the serrated geranium, is a hybrid plant species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its lobed, serrated leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. Native to South Africa, this plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium × dentatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
