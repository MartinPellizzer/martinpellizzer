Pachycornia tenuis

Pachycornia tenuis, commonly known as the slender pachycornia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the dry season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep underground water sources.

Pachycornia tenuis plays a vital role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for various desert flora and fauna.
