Poinsettia insulana

Poinsettia insulana, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species. Poinsettia insulana thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular holiday plant, especially during the Christmas season.
