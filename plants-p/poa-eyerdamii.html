Poa eyerdamii

Poa eyerdamii, commonly known as Eyerda's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Poa eyerdamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
