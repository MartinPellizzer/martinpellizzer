Philodendron × domesticum

Philodendron × domesticum, commonly known as the Boston Philodendron, is a hybrid plant that originated from crosses between Philodendron bipinnatifidum and Philodendron selloum.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid environments. It has a climbing habit and can grow quite large if provided with adequate support.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
