Pithecellobium tenellum

Pithecellobium tenellum, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, paired leaflets and produces yellowish to pale green flowers clustered in racemes. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its fruit is a pod that contains several edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
