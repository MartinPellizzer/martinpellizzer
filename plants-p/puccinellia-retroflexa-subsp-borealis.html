Puccinellia retroflexa subsp. borealis

Puccinellia retroflexa subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern puccinellia, is a perennial grass native to northern regions of North America. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows.

The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
