Pelargonium oreophilum

Pelargonium oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Pelargonium oreophilum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
