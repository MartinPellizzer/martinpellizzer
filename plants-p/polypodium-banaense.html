Polypodium banaense

Polypodium banaense, commonly known as the Banaense fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically divided into smaller, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Polypodium banaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
