Poa maydellii

Poa maydellii, commonly known as Maydell's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Poa maydellii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
