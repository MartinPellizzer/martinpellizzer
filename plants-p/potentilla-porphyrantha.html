Potentilla porphyrantha

Potentilla porphyrantha, commonly known as the purpleflower cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are arranged in an alternate pattern along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
