Psoralea lagopus

Psoralea lagopus, commonly known as the mountain psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
