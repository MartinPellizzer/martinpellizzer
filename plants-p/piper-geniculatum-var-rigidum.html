Piper geniculatum var. rigidum

Piper geniculatum var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
