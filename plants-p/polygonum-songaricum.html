Polygonum songaricum

Polygonum songaricum, commonly known as the Songaric knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

Polygonum songaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
