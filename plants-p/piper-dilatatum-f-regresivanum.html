Piper dilatatum f. regresivanum

Piper dilatatum f. regresivanum, commonly known as the Regresivanum pepper plant, is a unique variant of the pepper vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and pharmacological studies.
