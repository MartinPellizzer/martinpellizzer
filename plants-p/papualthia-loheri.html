Papualthia loheri

Papualthia loheri, commonly known as the Loher's papua orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the highlands of Papua, Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a pleasant fragrance. This plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its growth is slow, and it typically produces only a few blooms per year, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Papualthia loheri is considered vulnerable and is often found in specialized botanical gardens.
