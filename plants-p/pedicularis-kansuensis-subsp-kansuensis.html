Pedicularis kansuensis subsp. kansuensis

Pedicularis kansuensis subsp.

kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China. It is characterized by its erect stems and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense spikes. Pedicularis kansuensis subsp.

kansuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
