Piper imberbe

Piper imberbe, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a peppery taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic species in certain ecosystems.
