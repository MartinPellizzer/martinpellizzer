Peltandra virginica subsp. luteospadix

Peltandra virginica subsp.

luteospadix, commonly known as the yellow spatheed plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, yellowish-green inflorescences that are characteristic of the Araceae family.

The plant has broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
