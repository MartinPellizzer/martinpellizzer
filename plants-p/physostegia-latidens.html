Physostegia latidens

Physostegia latidens, commonly known as the broadleaf false dragonhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, two-lipped flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Physostegia latidens is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
