Primula soongii

Primula soongii, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rock crevices. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

Primula soongii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
