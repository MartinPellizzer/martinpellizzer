Pelargonium longiscapum

Pelargonium longiscapum, commonly known as the long-stemmed geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
