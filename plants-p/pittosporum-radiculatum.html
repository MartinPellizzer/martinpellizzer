Pittosporum radiculatum

Pittosporum radiculatum, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Pittosporum radiculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and horticulture.
