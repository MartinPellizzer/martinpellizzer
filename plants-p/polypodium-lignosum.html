Polypodium lignosum

Polypodium lignosum, commonly known as the cliff fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, woody rhizome and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant features large, leathery, bipinnate fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in forest understories.

Polypodium lignosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
