Piper macbrideanum

Piper macbrideanum, commonly known as the MacBride's pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and elongated, elliptical berries. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforests, where it relies on other plants for support. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

The fruit is edible and has a peppery flavor, making it a potential candidate for culinary or medicinal use.
