Papaver tauricola

Papaver tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The Tauric poppy is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
