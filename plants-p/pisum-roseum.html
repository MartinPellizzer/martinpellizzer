Pisum roseum

Pisum roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, with a distinctive rose-like appearance. Its seeds, which are edible, are enclosed in pods and often used in traditional cuisines.

Pisum roseum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
