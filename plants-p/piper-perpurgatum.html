Piper perpurgatum

Piper perpurgatum, commonly known as the ghost pepper or bhut jolokia, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped berries that turn red when mature. These berries contain capsaicin, a compound responsible for the plant's intense heat.

It is often cultivated for its spicy flavor and used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
