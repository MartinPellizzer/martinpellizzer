Phyteuma orbiculare subsp. cordatum

Phyteuma orbiculare subsp.

cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and stems that are upright and branched.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, arranged in loose panicles, and bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
