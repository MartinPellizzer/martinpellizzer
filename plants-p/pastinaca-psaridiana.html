Pastinaca psaridiana

Pastinaca psaridiana, commonly known as the Cyprus bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas. It produces small, spiky fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
