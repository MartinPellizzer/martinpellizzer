Pusaetha kirkii

Pusaetha kirkii, commonly known as the African ironwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has compound leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody fruits containing seeds.

Pusaetha kirkii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
