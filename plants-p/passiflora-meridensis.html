Passiflora meridensis

Passiflora meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy, sweet flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora meridensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and ecological value as a food source for pollinators.
