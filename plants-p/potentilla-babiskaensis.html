Potentilla babiskaensis

Potentilla babiskaensis, commonly known as the Babiskan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla babiskaensis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
