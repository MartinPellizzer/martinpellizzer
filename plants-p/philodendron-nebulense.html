Philodendron nebulense

Philodendron nebulense, commonly known as the "cloudy philodendron," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often have a velvety texture and a distinctive mottled or speckled appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves can vary in color from dark green to deep purple, depending on light conditions.

Philodendron nebulense is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
