Piper puyoense

Piper puyoense, commonly known as the Puyo pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and Colombia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, spiky fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Piper puyoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
