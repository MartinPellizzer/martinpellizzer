Polycarpon polycarpoides var. catalaunicum

Polycarpon polycarpoides var. catalaunicum, commonly known as the Catalaunic horsebean, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces elongated, woody pods containing several seeds.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
