Pallasia caspica

Pallasia caspica, commonly known as the Caspian pallasia, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and helps it retain moisture in dry climates.

Pallasia caspica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
