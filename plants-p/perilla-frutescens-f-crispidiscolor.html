Perilla frutescens f. crispidiscolor

Perilla frutescens f. crispidiscolor, commonly known as Chinese basil or purple perilla, is a cultivar of the perilla plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple and green variegated leaves, which are often used in culinary and ornamental applications. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, contributing to its aromatic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardens and traditional medicine.
