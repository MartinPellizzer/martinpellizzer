Pilosella leptobrachia

Pilosella leptobrachia, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features narrow, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat clusters. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Pilosella leptobrachia is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
