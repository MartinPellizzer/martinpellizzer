Picridium tingitanum var. gaditanum

Picridium tingitanum var. gaditanum, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny environments, often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
