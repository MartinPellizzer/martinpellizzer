Photinia benthamiana f. latifolia

Photinia benthamiana f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf photinia, is a variant of the photinia species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
