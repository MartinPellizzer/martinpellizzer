Panicum rowlandii

Panicum rowlandii, commonly known as rowland's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, which are a common feature in the grass family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions where it can outcompete native vegetation.
