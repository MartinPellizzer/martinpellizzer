Piptadenia microphylla

Piptadenia microphylla, commonly known as the Brazilian red cedar, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and durable wood, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
