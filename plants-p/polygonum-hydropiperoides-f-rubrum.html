Polygonum hydropiperoides f. rubrum

Polygonum hydropiperoides f. rubrum, commonly known as the red-veined dock, is a variant of the polygonum genus characterized by its striking red veins on its heart-shaped leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, often calcareous soils. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The red-veined appearance of the leaves makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
