Polygonum minus subsp. procerum

Polygonum minus subsp.

procerum, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged on the underside. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish and appear in late summer.

The stems are slender and somewhat fibrous, with a tendency to root at the nodes.
