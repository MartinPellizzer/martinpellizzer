Ptarmica salicifolia subsp. septentrionalis

Ptarmica salicifolia subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern willow-leaf lichen, is a foliose lichen belonging to the family Parmeliaceae. It is characterized by its pale green to yellowish thallus with a distinctive, finely granular texture.

The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, which contribute to its growth and survival in various environments. It typically grows on bark, stones, or soil in cool, moist habitats.

This subspecies is often found in northern regions, including parts of North America and Europe.
