Primula bellidifolia subsp. adenantha

Primula bellidifolia subsp.

adenantha, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in a cymule, and bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
