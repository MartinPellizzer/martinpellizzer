Peccana glauca

Peccana glauca, commonly known as the blue peccana, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

Peccana glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
