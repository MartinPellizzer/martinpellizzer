Prunus demissa f. angusta

Prunus demissa f. angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscape design.
