Populus × tomentosa var. orbiculata

Populus × tomentosa var. orbiculata, commonly known as the woolly poplar or Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its rounded, often multi-trunked form and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly white pubescence. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin, papery layers.

This species is valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental and reforestation purposes.
