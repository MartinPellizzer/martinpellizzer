Patersonia pygmaea

Patersonia pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf patersonia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Patersonia pygmaea is often found in grasslands and open forests, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
