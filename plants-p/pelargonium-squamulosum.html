Pelargonium squamulosum

Pelargonium squamulosum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in fine, white hairs that give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
