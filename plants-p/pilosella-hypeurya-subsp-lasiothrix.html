Pilosella hypeurya subsp. lasiothrix

Pilosella hypeurya subsp.

lasiothrix, commonly known as the woolly haircup, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, white to grayish woolly covering on its stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in composite heads. It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
