Potentilla zapateri var. longidentata

Potentilla zapateri var. longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with serrated edges, and the variety longidentata is distinguished by its longer, more pronounced teeth along the leaf margins.

This species is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
