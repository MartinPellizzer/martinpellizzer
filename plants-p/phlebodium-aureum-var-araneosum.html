Phlebodium aureum var. araneosum

Phlebodium aureum var. araneosum, commonly known as the golden staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, shield-shaped fronds that resemble the antlers of a stag, giving it its common name. The plant's rhizomes form a dense, fibrous network that anchors it to trees or other structures, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is typically a vibrant golden-green, with a velvety texture that enhances its ornamental appeal.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its aesthetic value and ability to grow in a variety of substrates when provided with proper moisture and light conditions.
