Pleurothallis convergens

Pleurothallis convergens, commonly known as the convergent pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant features slender, often curved stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging species to cultivate outside its natural range.
