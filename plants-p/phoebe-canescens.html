Phoebe canescens

Phoebe canescens, commonly known as the silver phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Phoebe canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
