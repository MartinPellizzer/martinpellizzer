Portulaca pilosa f. maxima

Portulaca pilosa f. maxima, commonly known as the hairy portulaca, is a flowering plant native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
