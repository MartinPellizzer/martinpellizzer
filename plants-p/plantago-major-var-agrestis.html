Plantago major var. agrestis

Plantago major var. agrestis, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, along paths, and in disturbed areas.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
