Philodendron squamiferum

Philodendron squamiferum, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, often reddish underside. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. Its growth habit is typically vining, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a climbing plant in a greenhouse.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
