Physaria arenosa subsp. argillosa

Physaria arenosa subsp.

argillosa, commonly known as the clayblite, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves.

This subspecies thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or clay soils. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with the ability to store water in its thick stems and leaves.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
