Primula vilmoriniana

Primula vilmoriniana, commonly known as the Vilmorin's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
