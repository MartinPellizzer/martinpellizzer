Plantago spinulosa var. typica

Plantago spinulosa var. typica, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces erect, flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild astringent properties.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
