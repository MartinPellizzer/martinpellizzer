Potamogeton sarmaticus

Potamogeton sarmaticus, commonly known as the Sarmatian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and ponds, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually arranged spirally along its stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged or floating. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often used in ecological assessments.
