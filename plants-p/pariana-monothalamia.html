Pariana monothalamia

Pariana monothalamia, commonly known as the desert star cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines that provide protection from herbivores and extreme weather. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem to survive in dry environments.

Pariana monothalamia is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates with minimal rainfall.
