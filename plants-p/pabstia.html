Pabstia

Pabstia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often fragrant flowers that resemble small, ghostly shapes. They typically grow in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Pabstia species are known for their delicate, often white or pale-colored blooms.
