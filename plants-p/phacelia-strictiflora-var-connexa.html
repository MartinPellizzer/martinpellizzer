Phacelia strictiflora var. connexa

Phacelia strictiflora var. connexa, commonly known as the narrow-flowered phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and usually have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
