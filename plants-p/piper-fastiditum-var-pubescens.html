Piper fastiditum var. pubescens

Piper fastiditum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits.

The variety pubescens is distinguished by its dense pubescence, or hairiness, which gives it its common name.
