Protea ascendens

Protea ascendens, commonly known as the mountain protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, often in fynbos vegetation. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces follicles that release seeds through a unique mechanism.

Protea ascendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
