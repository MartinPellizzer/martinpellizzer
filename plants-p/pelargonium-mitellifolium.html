Pelargonium mitellifolium

Pelargonium mitellifolium, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium mitellifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
