Perichasma laetificata var. obovata

Perichasma laetificata var. obovata, commonly known as the joy-bringing perichasma, is a rare and striking plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the wet season. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its limited distribution and susceptibility to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in its native range.
