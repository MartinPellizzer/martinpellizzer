Phaeopappus insignis

Phaeopappus insignis, commonly known as the red sandreed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, succulent-like foliage. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots help it survive in dry conditions, and it is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Phaeopappus insignis is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small wildlife.
