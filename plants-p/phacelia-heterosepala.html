Phacelia heterosepala

Phacelia heterosepala, commonly known as the blue phacelia, is an annual flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry grasslands and open woodlands.

Phacelia heterosepala is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
