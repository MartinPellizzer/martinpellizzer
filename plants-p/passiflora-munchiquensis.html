Passiflora munchiquensis

Passiflora munchiquensis, commonly known as the Munchique passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

Passiflora munchiquensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
