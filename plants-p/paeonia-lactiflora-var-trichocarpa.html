Paeonia lactiflora var. trichocarpa

Paeonia lactiflora var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the black tulippe or black peony, is a cultivar of the peony plant native to China.

It is characterized by its deep purple to almost black flowers, which are double or semi-double in form. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and a robust root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its striking color and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine as well.
