Pharbitis ostrina

Pharbitis ostrina, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces edible beans that are rich in nutrients and often used in culinary applications. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to cover trellises or fences.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
