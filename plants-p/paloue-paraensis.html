Paloue paraensis

Paloue paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Paloue, is a rare and unique plant species native to the dense rainforests of the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the family Palouaceae and is characterized by its striking, bioluminescent leaves that glow faintly in the dark. The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption from the nutrient-poor soil. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of deep purple and violet.

Due to its limited habitat and fragile ecosystem, Paloue paraensis is considered an endangered species.
