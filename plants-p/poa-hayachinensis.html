Poa hayachinensis

Poa hayachinensis, commonly known as the Japanese meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa hayachinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
