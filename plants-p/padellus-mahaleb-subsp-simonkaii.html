Padellus mahaleb subsp. simonkaii

Padellus mahaleb subsp.

simonkaii, commonly known as the Mahaleb cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
