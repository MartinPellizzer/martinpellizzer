Piptadenia duparquetiana

Piptadenia duparquetiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, reddish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oblong, with a leathery texture.

The fruit is a woody pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
