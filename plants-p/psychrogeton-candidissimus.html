Psychrogeton candidissimus

Psychrogeton candidissimus, commonly known as the white snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of alpine regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of plant adaptation to extreme climates.
