Pleurothallis lehmanneptis

Pleurothallis lehmanneptis, commonly known as the Lehman's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
