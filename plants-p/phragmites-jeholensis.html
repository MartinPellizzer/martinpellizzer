Phragmites jeholensis

Phragmites jeholensis, commonly known as the Jehol reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to northeastern China.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has hollow, jointed stems and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
