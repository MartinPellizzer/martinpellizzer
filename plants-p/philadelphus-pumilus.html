Philadelphus pumilus

Philadelphus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Philadelphus pumilus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
