Piper josephii

Piper josephii, commonly known as the Joseph's pepper or the orchid pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is not commonly cultivated for its fruit due to its limited yield and flavor profile.
