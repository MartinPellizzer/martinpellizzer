Pyrus khumbuensis

Pyrus khumbuensis, commonly known as the Khumbu pear, is a rare species native to the high-altitude regions of Nepal.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are adapted to cold climates. The plant typically grows in rocky, mountainous terrain at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
