Premna obtusifolia

Premna obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leafed glorybower, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a smooth, often bluish-green underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have a purple or pink hue.

Premna obtusifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
