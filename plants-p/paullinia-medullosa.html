Paullinia medullosa

Paullinia medullosa, commonly known as the Brazilian sarsaparilla, is a member of the Sapindaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
