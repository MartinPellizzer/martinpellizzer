Peperomia gaultheriifolia

Peperomia gaultheriifolia, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, waxy leaves that have a distinctive silver or grayish-white hue. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which are followed by small, round berries.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and minimal water requirements.
