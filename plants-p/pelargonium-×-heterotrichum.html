Pelargonium × heterotrichum

Pelargonium × heterotrichum, commonly known as the waterfall geranium, is a hybrid plant that originates from South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive, waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its cascading foliage and continuous blooming habit.
