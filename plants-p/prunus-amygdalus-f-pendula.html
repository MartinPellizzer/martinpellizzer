Prunus amygdalus f. pendula

Prunus amygdalus f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping almond, is a cultivar of the almond tree characterized by its elegant, cascading branches.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by almond-like fruits. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its graceful appearance.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin, adding to its visual appeal.
