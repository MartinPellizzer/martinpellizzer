Psephellus gracillimus

Psephellus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, allowing it to conserve water efficiently. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its root system is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its hardy nature and minimal care requirements, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
