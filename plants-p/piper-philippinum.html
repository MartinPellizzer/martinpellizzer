Piper philippinum

Piper philippinum, commonly known as the Philippine pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated berries that resemble peppercorns. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic fruits.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, adding a unique flavor to local cuisines.
