Psidium occidentale

Psidium occidentale, commonly known as the West Indian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically pink to red in color. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it a popular fruit in tropical regions.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
