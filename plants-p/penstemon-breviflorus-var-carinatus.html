Penstemon breviflorus var. carinatus

Penstemon breviflorus var. carinatus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems covered in lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a desirable plant for native gardens.
