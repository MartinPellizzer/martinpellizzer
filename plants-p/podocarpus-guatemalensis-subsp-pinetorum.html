Podocarpus guatemalensis subsp. pinetorum

Podocarpus guatemalensis subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the Guatemalan podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Central America. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The plant typically grows in humid, tropical to subtropical climates, often in mountainous regions. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
