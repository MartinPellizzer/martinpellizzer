Piper jenkinsii

Piper jenkinsii, commonly known as the Jenkins' pepper or the Bornean black pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Borneo and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color, resembling black pepper. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
