Pinus pinaster subsp. escarena

Pinus pinaster subsp.

escarena, commonly known as the Spanish stone pine, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, evergreen foliage.

The cones of this species are typically large and woody, containing edible seeds known as pine nuts. The tree produces a distinctive resin that has been historically used in various traditional applications.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in its native regions.
