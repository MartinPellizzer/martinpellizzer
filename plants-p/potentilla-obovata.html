Potentilla obovata

Potentilla obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and features small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and oval, with a rounded base, and are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
