Petrocoptis pyrenaica subsp. glaucifolia

Petrocoptis pyrenaica subsp.

glaucifolia, commonly known as the glaucous petrocoptis, is a member of the Polygonaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that often appear blue-green in color.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a pale pink or white coloration.

This subspecies is native to the Pyrenees region and is adapted to withstand harsh, mountainous conditions.
