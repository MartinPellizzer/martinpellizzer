Paepalanthus linearifolius

Paepalanthus linearifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in high-altitude environments with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
