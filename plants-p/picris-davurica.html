Picris davurica

Picris davurica, commonly known as the Siberian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
