Piper paludosum

Piper paludosum, commonly known as swamp pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and possesses opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Piper paludosum is often found in wetland habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
