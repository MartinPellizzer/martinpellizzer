Polygala lehmanniana var. pteropus

Polygala lehmanniana var. pteropus, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The leaves are narrow and often hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in coastal or inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
