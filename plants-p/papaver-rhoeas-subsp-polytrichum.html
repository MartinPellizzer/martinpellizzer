Papaver rhoeas subsp. polytrichum

Papaver rhoeas subsp.

polytrichum, commonly known as the lesser poppy, is a lesser-known subspecies of the common poppy. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its delicate, bright red flowers with a yellow center.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

This subspecies is less widespread than its parent species and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits.
