Picea rinzi

Picea rinzi, commonly known as the Rinzi fir, is a species of conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along its branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the coniferous family. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous forests at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Picea rinzi is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
