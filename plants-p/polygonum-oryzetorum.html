Polygonum oryzetorum

Polygonum oryzetorum, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found growing along water edges or in wetlands.

Polygonum oryzetorum is widely cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
