Poa pseudocaesia

Poa pseudocaesia, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

Poa pseudocaesia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
