Prunus pullei var. grandiflora

Prunus pullei var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pink to white. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
