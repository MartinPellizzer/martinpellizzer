Plantago pachystachys

Plantago pachystachys, commonly known as the giant plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, with a waxy surface and a prominent midrib. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant or used in traditional medicine.
