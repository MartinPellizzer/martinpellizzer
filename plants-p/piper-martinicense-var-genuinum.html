Piper martinicense var. genuinum

Piper martinicense var. genuinum, commonly known as the Martinique pepper or the West Indian pepper, is a tropical vine native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn from green to yellow or red, depending on the variety.

This plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
