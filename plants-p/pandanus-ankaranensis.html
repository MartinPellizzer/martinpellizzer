Pandanus ankaranensis

Pandanus ankaranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the Pandanus genus. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to their durability.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, showcasing its resilience to humid and saline conditions.
