Potentilla jamesoniana

Potentilla jamesoniana, commonly known as the Jameson's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla jamesoniana is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in alpine environments.
