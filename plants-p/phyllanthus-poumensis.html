Phyllanthus poumensis

Phyllanthus poumensis, commonly known as the African miracle berry, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a medicinal plant.
