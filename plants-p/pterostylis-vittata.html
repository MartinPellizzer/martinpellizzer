Pterostylis vittata

Pterostylis vittata, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a slender, leafless stem with a single or few flowers that are pale yellow to orange in color. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate, spider-like appearance and are pollinated by specific insects.

The orchid has a short flowering period, usually in spring, and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
