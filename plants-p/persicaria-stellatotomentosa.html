Persicaria stellatotomentosa

Persicaria stellatotomentosa, commonly known as star-tomentose knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes.
