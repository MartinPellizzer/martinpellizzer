Pitcairnia albiflos

Pitcairnia albiflos, commonly known as the white-flowered pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common feature among bromeliads. The flowers are white and clustered in a spike, often blooming in late summer or early autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
