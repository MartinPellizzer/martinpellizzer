Protea brownii

Protea brownii, commonly known as the Brown's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in nutrient-poor soils, a common trait among proteas. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
