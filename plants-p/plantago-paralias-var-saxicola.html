Plantago paralias var. saxicola

Plantago paralias var. saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola plantain, is a perennial herb native to rocky and coastal environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices or on slopes.

This variety is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
