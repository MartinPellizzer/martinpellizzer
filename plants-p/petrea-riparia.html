Petrea riparia

Petrea riparia, commonly known as the silverleaf creeper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to climb and cover structures, making it a popular choice for green walls and trellises.
