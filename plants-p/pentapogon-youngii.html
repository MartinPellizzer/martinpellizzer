Pentapogon youngii

Pentapogon youngii, commonly known as the young's five-lobed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of tiny spikelets that produce three stamens and a single pistil.

Due to its limited distribution, Pentapogon youngii is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
