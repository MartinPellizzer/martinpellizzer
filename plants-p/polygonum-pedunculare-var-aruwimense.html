Polygonum pedunculare var. aruwimense

Polygonum pedunculare var. aruwimense, commonly known as the Aruwim wild buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is characterized by its erect stems and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
