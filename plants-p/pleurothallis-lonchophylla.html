Pleurothallis lonchophylla

Pleurothallis lonchophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, often clustered stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green and glossy. Its flowers are small and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters, and come in a range of colors including white, pink, and purple.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
