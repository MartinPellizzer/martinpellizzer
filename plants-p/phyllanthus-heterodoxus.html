Phyllanthus heterodoxus

Phyllanthus heterodoxus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Phyllanthus heterodoxus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
