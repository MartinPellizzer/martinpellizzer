Piper gracilirameum

Piper gracilirameum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features slender, wiry stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same inflorescence, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
