Patagonium punctatum

Patagonium punctatum, commonly known as the Patagonian dot plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it has a deep root system to access underground moisture.

Patagonium punctatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for small insects.
