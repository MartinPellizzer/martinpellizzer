Piper fulvirameum

Piper fulvirameum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, yellowish stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that mature to a bright red color.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
