Primula hoppei

Primula hoppei, commonly known as the Hoppe's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, silky texture.

Primula hoppei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
