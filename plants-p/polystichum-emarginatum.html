Polystichum emarginatum

Polystichum emarginatum, commonly known as the notched wood fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive notched appearance at the base of the pinnae. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes, often growing on soil or rock.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its lush, fern-like appearance.
