Potentilla terglouensis

Potentilla terglouensis, commonly known as the Terglou cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant has finely divided, hairy leaves that are adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. Potentilla terglouensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
