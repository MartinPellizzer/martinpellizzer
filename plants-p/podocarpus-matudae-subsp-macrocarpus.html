Podocarpus matudae subsp. macrocarpus

Podocarpus matudae subsp.

macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and cone-like structures that contain edible seeds.

The plant exhibits a unique combination of gymnosperm and angiosperm traits, making it botanically intriguing. Its leaves are typically leathery and arranged in whorls, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and potential economic uses.
