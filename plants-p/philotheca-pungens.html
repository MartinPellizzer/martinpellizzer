Philotheca pungens

Philotheca pungens, commonly known as the rough-leaved philotheca, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its serrated, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland and heathland environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Philotheca pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
