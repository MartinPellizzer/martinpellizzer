Penstemon luteus

Penstemon luteus, commonly known as the yellow beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.

Penstemon luteus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
