Pituranthos battandieri

Pituranthos battandieri, commonly known as the Battandieri's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, hair-like leaves that form dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in loose spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and drought tolerance.
