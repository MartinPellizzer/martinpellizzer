Placodiscus splendidus

Placodiscus splendidus, commonly known as the splendid disc fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-bearing structures called sori. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying a striking variegation of colors.

The botanical features of Placodiscus splendidus make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
