Polygonum plumosum

Polygonum plumosum, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-purple inflorescences that resemble small knots. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum plumosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
