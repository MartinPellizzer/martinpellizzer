Pourouma tomentosa

Pourouma tomentosa, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in warm, humid climates. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fruit.

The leaves are used in traditional crafts and the plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
