Pinalia exilis

Pinalia exilis, commonly known as the slender pinyon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family, which includes pines and other conifers, and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its cones are small and contain edible seeds that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Pinalia exilis plays a significant role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and preventing soil erosion.
