Phlox obovata

Phlox obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped to obovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called bloom. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
