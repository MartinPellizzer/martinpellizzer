Polypodium taxodioides

Polypodium taxodioides, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a fern species native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can grow up to 3 meters in length, allowing it to thrive in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant's rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring it to the bottom of water bodies while its fronds remain buoyant on the surface. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
