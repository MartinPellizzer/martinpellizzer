Ponerorchis beesiana

Ponerorchis beesiana, commonly known as the bees' orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a prominent labellum that resembles a bee, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by bees.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ponerorchis beesiana is considered an endangered species requiring conservation efforts.
