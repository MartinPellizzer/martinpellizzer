Piper leucofustum

Piper leucofustum, commonly known as the white-veined pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed, elongated berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with prominent white veins, giving it its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
