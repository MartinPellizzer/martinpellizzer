Psoralea pedunculata

Psoralea pedunculata, commonly known as the slender psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
