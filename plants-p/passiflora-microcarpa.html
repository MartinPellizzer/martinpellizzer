Passiflora microcarpa

Passiflora microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora microcarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming effects.
