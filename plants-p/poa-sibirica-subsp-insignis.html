Poa sibirica subsp. insignis

Poa sibirica subsp.

insignis, commonly known as the Siberian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
