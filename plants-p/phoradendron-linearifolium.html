Phoradendron linearifolium

Phoradendron linearifolium, commonly known as the linear-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, linear leaves and produces white, waxy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar for pollinators like bees and birds. Its berries, which are dark blue to black, are an important food source for wildlife, including birds and small mammals.

The species plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its forest ecosystems.
