Potamogeton suboblongus

Potamogeton suboblongus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its dense growth can provide habitat for aquatic organisms and help stabilize sediment in water bodies.
