Passiflora sururuca

Passiflora sururuca, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, pulpy flesh. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora sururuca is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
