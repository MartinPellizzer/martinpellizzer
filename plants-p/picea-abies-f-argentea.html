Picea abies f. argentea

Picea abies f. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white undersides on its needles, which give it a unique appearance. This form is typically found in alpine or subalpine regions where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscape design.
