Potamogeton pinnatum

Potamogeton pinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem, which can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton pinnatum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
