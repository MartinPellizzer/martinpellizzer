Peltogyne

Peltogyne, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plants are characterized by their hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. Peltogyne species typically have bipinnate leaves and produce small, pod-like fruits containing seeds.

They play an important ecological role in their native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
