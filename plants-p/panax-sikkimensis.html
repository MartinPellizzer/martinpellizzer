Panax sikkimensis

Panax sikkimensis, commonly known as Sikkim ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered root system. The plant typically grows in cool, moist forest environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, though it is less commercially exploited compared to other ginseng species.
