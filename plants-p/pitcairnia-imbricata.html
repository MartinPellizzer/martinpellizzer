Pitcairnia imbricata

Pitcairnia imbricata, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping leaves that form a rosette, creating a water-holding structure that supports its unique ecosystem. The plant's foliage is often covered in a thick, velvety coating, giving it its common name. It produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
