Panicum simpliciusculum

Panicum simpliciusculum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a clumping growth habit, making it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.

It is also used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
