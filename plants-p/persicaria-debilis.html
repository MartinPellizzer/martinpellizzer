Persicaria debilis

Persicaria debilis, commonly known as slender smartweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria debilis thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, ditches, and along stream banks.
