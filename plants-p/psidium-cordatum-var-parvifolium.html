Psidium cordatum var. parvifolium

Psidium cordatum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by orange or red berries. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are usually smaller than those of the standard Psidium cordatum. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor and a high vitamin C content.
