Polygonum longisetum f. albiflorum

Polygonum longisetum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
