Pandanus novohibernicus var. pseudoturritus

Pandanus novohibernicus var. pseudoturritus, commonly known as the New Ireland pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various tropical climates.
