Phreatia subsacculata

Phreatia subsacculata, commonly known as the sac-like orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, sac-like structure in its floral bracts, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and pale green or yellow, adapted for specific pollinators.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
