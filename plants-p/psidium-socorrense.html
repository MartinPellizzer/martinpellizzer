Psidium socorrense

Psidium socorrense, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit yield.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
