Plantago sawadae

Plantago sawadae, commonly known as the Japanese plantain, is a flowering plant species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in a specialized structure called an ovary, which aids in seed dispersal.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
