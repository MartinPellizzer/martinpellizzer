Phyllocactus bradei

Phyllocactus bradei, commonly known as the Brade's phyllocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its flattened, fleshy stems that form a rosette shape, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Phyllocactus bradei is well-suited for cultivation in dry, well-draining soil and requires minimal watering.
