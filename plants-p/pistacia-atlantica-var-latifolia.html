Pistacia atlantica var. latifolia

Pistacia atlantica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pistachio, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, often multi-stemmed growth habit and thick, leathery foliage. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive bluish-green color with a waxy coating. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, stony fruits.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
