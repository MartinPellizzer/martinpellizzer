Paratriaina xerophila

Paratriaina xerophila, commonly known as the desert lily, is a succulent plant adapted to arid environments.

It possesses thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Paratriaina xerophila is often found in rocky or sandy soils in desert regions.
