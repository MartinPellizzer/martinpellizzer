Piper longeacuminatum

Piper longeacuminatum, commonly known as the long-leaf pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical berries containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery scent when crushed.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
