Polygala refracta var. steudeliana

Polygala refracta var. steudeliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ecological role and ornamental potential.
