Platanthera × erdingeri

Platanthera × erdingeri, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to central Europe.

It is a hybrid between Platanthera chlorophaea and Platanthera fuchsioides, characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. It has a short, creeping rhizome and produces showy, fragrant flowers during the summer months.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, it is highly threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
