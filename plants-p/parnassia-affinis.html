Parnassia affinis

Parnassia affinis, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, forming a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Parnassia affinis is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
