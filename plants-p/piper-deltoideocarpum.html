Piper deltoideocarpum

Piper deltoideocarpum, commonly known as the delta-fruit pepper or delta pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing a spicy, aromatic compound similar to other peppers. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy texture with a distinct aromatic scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
