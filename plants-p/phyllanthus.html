Phyllanthus

Phyllanthus, commonly known as the cheesewood or stonebreaker, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It comprises over 400 species, many of which are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their simple, opposite leaves and small yellow flowers. They often have a smooth, waxy bark and produce fruits that are capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.

Phyllanthus species are known for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
