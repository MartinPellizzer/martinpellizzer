Pourouma villosa

Pourouma villosa, commonly known as the silky bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to South America, particularly the Amazon basin, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo. The plant has large, elongated leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
