Panicum depauperatum

Panicum depauperatum, commonly known as the poor panicgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing the landscape.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded areas.
