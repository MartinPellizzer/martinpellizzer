Panicum belmonte

Panicum belmonte, commonly known as Belmont grass, is a warm-season grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that often have a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for use in erosion control and ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense stands make it a valuable plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
