Phagnalon saxatile var. telonense

Phagnalon saxatile var. telonense, commonly known as the rock phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, often blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. It typically grows on limestone or sandstone substrates, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its distribution includes parts of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East, where it is adapted to harsh, sun-exposed habitats.
