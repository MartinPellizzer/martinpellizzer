Piper amplectens

Piper amplectens, commonly known as the climbing pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. Its aerial stems allow it to climb and spread through various habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
