Pelargonium bellermannii

Pelargonium bellermannii, commonly known as the Bellermann's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Pelargonium bellermannii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
