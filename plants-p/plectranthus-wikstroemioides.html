Plectranthus wikstroemioides

Plectranthus wikstroemioides, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to ovate in shape. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often arranged in clusters.

Plectranthus wikstroemioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
