Plagiocheilus tanacetoides

Plagiocheilus tanacetoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
