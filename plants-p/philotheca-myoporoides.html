Philotheca myoporoides

Philotheca myoporoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myopor, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small, shrub-like species with slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
