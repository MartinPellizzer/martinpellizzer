Psoralea pariensis

Psoralea pariensis, commonly known as the mountain psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often bipinnate, and the pods are slender and hairy.
