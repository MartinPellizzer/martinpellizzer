Pleioluma lamprophylla

Pleioluma lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental value.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
