Protea obtusata

Protea obtusata, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers that resemble a cluster of snowflakes. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. Its flowering period typically occurs during the winter months, adding a unique floral display to the landscape.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
