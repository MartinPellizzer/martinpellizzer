Paronychia lugdunensis

Paronychia lugdunensis, commonly known as the woolly burr.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow flowers. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in dry, rocky environments. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often covered in a dense layer of bristles.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
