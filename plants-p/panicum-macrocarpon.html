Panicum macrocarpon

Panicum macrocarpon, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, drooping inflorescences that produce numerous small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread rapidly.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
