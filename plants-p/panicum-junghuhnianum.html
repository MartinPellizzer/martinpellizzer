Panicum junghuhnianum

Panicum junghuhnianum, commonly known as Junghuhn's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, often forming dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
