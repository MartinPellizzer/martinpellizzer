Plumeria kunthiana

Plumeria kunthiana, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in white, yellow, or pink hues, typically blooming in summer. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and often peels to reveal a mottled appearance.

The flowers are used in traditional ceremonies and have cultural significance in many regions.
