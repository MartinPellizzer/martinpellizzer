Paphiopedilum glanduliferum var. praestans

Paphiopedilum glanduliferum var. praestans, commonly known as the Singapore orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, erect stem bearing several showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow to cream with a distinctive dark purple or brown lip, and they bloom during the summer months. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.
