Pouteria aurea

Pouteria aurea, commonly known as the golden spondias, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, glossy green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its fruit is highly valued for its nutritional content and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional dishes.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
