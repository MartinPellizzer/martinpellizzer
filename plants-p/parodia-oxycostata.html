Parodia oxycostata

Parodia oxycostata, commonly known as the sharp-ribbed cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
