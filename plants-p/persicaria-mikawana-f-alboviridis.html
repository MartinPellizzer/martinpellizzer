Persicaria mikawana f. alboviridis

Persicaria mikawana f. alboviridis, commonly known as the white-veined knotweed, is a rare and striking variant of the knotweed genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive white veins on light green leaves, which give it a striking appearance. This plant is native to Japan and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as woodland floors. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late summer, and it produces distinctive, three-lobed fruits.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
