Picea alcoquiana subsp. reflexa

Picea alcoquiana subsp.

reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed spruce, is a subspecies of the alcoquiana spruce native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are often reflexed, or curved backward, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
