Pisum sativum var. belgicum

Pisum sativum var. belgicum, commonly known as the Belgian pea, is a cultivated variety of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and typically produces small, sweet pods. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, and it is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.

This variety is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and culinary use.
