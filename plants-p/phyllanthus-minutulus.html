Phyllanthus minutulus

Phyllanthus minutulus, commonly known as the dwarf gooseberry, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Phyllanthus minutulus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
