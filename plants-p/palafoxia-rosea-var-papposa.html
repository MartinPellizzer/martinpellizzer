Palafoxia rosea var. papposa

Palafoxia rosea var. papposa, commonly known as the silky palafoxia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, silky foliage and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, cottony hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
