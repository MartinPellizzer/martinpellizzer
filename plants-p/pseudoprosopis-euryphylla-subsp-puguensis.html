Pseudoprosopis euryphylla subsp. puguensis

Pseudoprosopis euryphylla subsp.

puguensis, commonly known as the Pugu acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and feathery in appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The wood of this plant is often used for fuel and in traditional crafts.
