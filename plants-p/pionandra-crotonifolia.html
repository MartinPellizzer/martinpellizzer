Pionandra crotonifolia

Pionandra crotonifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered pionandra, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.
