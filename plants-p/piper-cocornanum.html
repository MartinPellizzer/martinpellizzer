Piper cocornanum

Piper cocornanum, commonly known as the Cocora pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the spicy compound responsible for its flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its unique taste and limited cultivation, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
