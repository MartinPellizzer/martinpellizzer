Platycodon mariesii var. albus

Platycodon mariesii var. albus, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early summer.
