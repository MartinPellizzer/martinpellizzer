Pinus thunbergii var. pendula

Pinus thunbergii var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Japanese black pine, is a variant of the Japanese black pine characterized by its distinctive drooping branches.

This evergreen conifer is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
