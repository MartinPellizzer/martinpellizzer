Pavonia macrobracteolata

Pavonia macrobracteolata, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas, and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia macrobracteolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
