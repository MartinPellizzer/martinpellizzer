Pyrus scannelliana

Pyrus scannelliana, commonly known as the Scannell's pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible pomes that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
