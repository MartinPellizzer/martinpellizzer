Pterocaulon decurrens

Pterocaulon decurrens, commonly known as the trailing pterocaulon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Pterocaulon decurrens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
