Pimenta guatemalensis

Pimenta guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemalan pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark red berries. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the edible fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
