Poa koksuensis

Poa koksuensis, commonly known as the Koksu grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and steppe environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
