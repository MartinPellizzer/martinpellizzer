Protium glomerulosum

Protium glomerulosum, commonly known as the clustered tamanu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
