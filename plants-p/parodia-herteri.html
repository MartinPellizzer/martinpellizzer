Parodia herteri

Parodia herteri, commonly known as the Herter's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. It is well adapted to dry environments, with a shallow root system that allows it to efficiently absorb rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
