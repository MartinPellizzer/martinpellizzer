Phyllanthus anceps

Phyllanthus anceps, commonly known as the African spurge or false crossandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Phyllanthus anceps is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
