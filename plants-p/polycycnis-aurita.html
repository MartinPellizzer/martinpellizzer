Polycycnis aurita

Polycycnis aurita, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening purposes.
