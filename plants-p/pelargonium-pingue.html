Pelargonium pingue

Pelargonium pingue, commonly known as the sticky geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
