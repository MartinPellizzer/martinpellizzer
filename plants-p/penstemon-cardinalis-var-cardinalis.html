Penstemon cardinalis var. cardinalis

Penstemon cardinalis var. cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract hummingbirds and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
