Pinalia sicaria

Pinalia sicaria, commonly known as the Sicilian pinyon, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family, which includes pines and other coniferous trees. The plant is characterized by its needle-like leaves and produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. Pinalia sicaria thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
