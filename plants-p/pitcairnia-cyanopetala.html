Pitcairnia cyanopetala

Pitcairnia cyanopetala, commonly known as the blue-flowered pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its rosette-shaped leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help collect and retain moisture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
