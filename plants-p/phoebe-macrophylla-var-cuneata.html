Phoebe macrophylla var. cuneata

Phoebe macrophylla var. cuneata, commonly known as the broadleaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often broader than they are long. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
