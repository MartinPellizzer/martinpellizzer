Patagonium ulicinum

Patagonium ulicinum, commonly known as the Patagonian willow, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in catkins, and it is pollinated primarily by wind. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Patagonium ulicinum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
