Paracaryum ancyritanum

Paracaryum ancyritanum, commonly known as the giant waterwheel, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, spiral-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in rivers and lakes. Its unique morphology allows it to efficiently capture sunlight while providing habitat for aquatic life.

Paracaryum ancyritanum is also noted for its ability to stabilize riverbanks and prevent erosion.
