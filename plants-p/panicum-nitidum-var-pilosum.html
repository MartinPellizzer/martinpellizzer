Panicum nitidum var. pilosum

Panicum nitidum var. pilosum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with small, spikelet-based flowers.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
