Podocarpus rospigliosii

Podocarpus rospigliosii, commonly known as the Corsican pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
