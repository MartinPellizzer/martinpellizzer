Pleurothallis capillaris

Pleurothallis capillaris, commonly known as the hair-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Pleurothallis capillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
