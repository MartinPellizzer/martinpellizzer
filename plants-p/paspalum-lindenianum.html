Paspalum lindenianum

Paspalum lindenianum, commonly known as the Lindenia grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and riparian zones, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Paspalum lindenianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
