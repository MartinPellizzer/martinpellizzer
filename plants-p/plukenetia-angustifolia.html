Plukenetia angustifolia

Plukenetia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sacha inchi, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its seeds, which are rich in omega-3 fatty acids and protein. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also valued in traditional medicine and agriculture.
