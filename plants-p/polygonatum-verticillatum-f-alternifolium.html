Polygonatum verticillatum f. alternifolium

Polygonatum verticillatum f. alternifolium, commonly known as the alternate-leaved Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive alternate arrangement of leaves, which differ from the typical whorled leaf structure of the species. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage and flowering habits make it a valuable addition to ornamental gardens and ecological landscapes.
