Potentilla denticulata

Potentilla denticulata, commonly known as the toothed cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Potentilla denticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
