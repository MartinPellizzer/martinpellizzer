Pouteria glabra

Pouteria glabra, commonly known as the smooth pouteria, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic uses in traditional medicine and food.
