Podocarpus angustifolius

Podocarpus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
