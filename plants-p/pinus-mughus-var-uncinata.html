Pinus mughus var. uncinata

Pinus mughus var. uncinata, commonly known as the Kashmir pine, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Kashmir pine tree native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and unique, twisted cones that set it apart from other pine species. The plant thrives in cold, mountainous environments with well-drained soils, often growing at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its cones are typically pendulous and can remain attached to the tree for several years before opening.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its ecological significance and potential adaptations to high-altitude conditions.
