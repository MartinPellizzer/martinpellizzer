Punica granatum var. legrelliae

Punica granatum var. legrelliae, commonly known as the Crimson Beauty pomegranate, is a cultivar prized for its vibrant, showy flowers and ornamental value.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of red, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces striking red-orange fruits with a thick, juicy aril, though the fruit is typically smaller than the standard pomegranate. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated for its visual impact rather than its fruit yield.
