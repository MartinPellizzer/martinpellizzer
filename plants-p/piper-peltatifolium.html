Piper peltatifolium

Piper peltatifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or peltate-leaf pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
