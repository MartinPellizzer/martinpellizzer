Polygala globulifera var. kachinensis

Polygala globulifera var. kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin polygala, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its small, tubular flowers are often white or pale yellow, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
