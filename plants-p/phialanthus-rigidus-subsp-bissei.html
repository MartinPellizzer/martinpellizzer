Phialanthus rigidus subsp. bissei

Phialanthus rigidus subsp.

bissei, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits. Its leaves and stems contain milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience to drought.
