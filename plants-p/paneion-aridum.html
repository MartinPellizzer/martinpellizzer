Paneion aridum

Paneion aridum, commonly known as the desert panicle, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

Paneion aridum produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across open, disturbed soils.
