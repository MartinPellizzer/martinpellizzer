Phylica bolusii

Phylica bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes, typically ranging in color from red to yellow. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Phylica bolusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
