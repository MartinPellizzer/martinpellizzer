Phaseolus adenanthus var. genuinus

Phaseolus adenanthus var. genuinus, commonly known as the slender bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces small, yellow flowers and cylindrical, green pods that contain several round seeds. The plant is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and twining stems that help it climb supports. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content.
