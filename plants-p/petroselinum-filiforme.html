Petroselinum filiforme

Petroselinum filiforme, commonly known as slender parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like dill and caraway. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used as a culinary herb. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Petroselinum filiforme is valued for its fresh, parsley-like flavor and is often used in Mediterranean cuisine.
