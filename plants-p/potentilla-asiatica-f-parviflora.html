Potentilla asiatica f. parviflora

Potentilla asiatica f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered Asiatic cinquefoil, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear small, bright yellow flowers clustered in cymes. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval, serrated leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
