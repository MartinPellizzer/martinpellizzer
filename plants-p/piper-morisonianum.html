Piper morisonianum

Piper morisonianum, commonly known as the Morison's pepper or the Indian pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
