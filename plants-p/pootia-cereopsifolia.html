Pootia cereopsifolia

Pootia cereopsifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf pootia, is a rare and unique plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing efficient water retention in its arid environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, adapted for pollination by wind rather than insects. Its root system is highly specialized, enabling it to access deep underground water sources.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Pootia cereopsifolia is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme conditions.
