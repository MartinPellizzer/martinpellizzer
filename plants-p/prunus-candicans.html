Prunus candicans

Prunus candicans, commonly known as the white cherry or sweet cherry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, edible fruits. Prunus candicans thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
