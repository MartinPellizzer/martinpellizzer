Papualthia heteropetala

Papualthia heteropetala, commonly known as the variable petal orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Papualthia heteropetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
