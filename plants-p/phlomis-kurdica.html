Phlomis kurdica

Phlomis kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish torchflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-blue hairs, and it produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, often with a strong, mint-like aroma.

Phlomis kurdica is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
