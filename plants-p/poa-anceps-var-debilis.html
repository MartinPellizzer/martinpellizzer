Poa anceps var. debilis

Poa anceps var. debilis, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often less than 30 cm tall, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small and inconspicuous.

This variety is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other wetland plant species.
