Peraltea

Peraltea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Peraltea is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
