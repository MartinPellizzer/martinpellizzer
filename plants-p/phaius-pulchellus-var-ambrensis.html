Phaius pulchellus var. ambrensis

Phaius pulchellus var. ambrensis, commonly known as the Ambrensis Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of white, green, and yellow hues. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique floral display.
