Platystemon cernuus

Platystemon cernuus, commonly known as the drooping platystemon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland habitats. The plant features slender, hairy stems that often bend downward, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
