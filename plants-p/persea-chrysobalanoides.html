Persea chrysobalanoides

Persea chrysobalanoides, commonly known as the golden spondias, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically edible and have a sweet, tropical flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
