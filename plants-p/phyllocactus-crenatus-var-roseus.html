Phyllocactus crenatus var. roseus

Phyllocactus crenatus var. roseus, commonly known as the roseate phyllocactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive rose-pink to reddish-pink spines. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers during the summer months, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
