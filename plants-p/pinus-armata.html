Pinus armata

Pinus armata, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the high elevations of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and long lifespan, often living over 1,000 years. The tree has a distinctive appearance with a twisted trunk and dense, needle-like foliage. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and plays a vital role in its native ecosystems.
