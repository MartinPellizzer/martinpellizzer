Peperomia flagellispica

Peperomia flagellispica, commonly known as the whip-like peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, dark green foliage with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, and the plant is known for its slow growth and compact form.

Peperomia flagellispica is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
