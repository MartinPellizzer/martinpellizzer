Panax simplex var. quercifolius

Panax simplex var. quercifolius, commonly known as the red ginseng, is a variant of the ginseng plant native to Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown roots, which are rich in ginsenosides and other bioactive compounds. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soil and a temperate climate. Its leaves are broad and resemble those of an oak tree, giving it its scientific name.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is highly valued in traditional Korean medicine.
