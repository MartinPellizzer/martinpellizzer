Papaver nudicaule var. tenue

Papaver nudicaule var. tenue, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically pink to red in color, with five petals and a prominent central stigma. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
