Prunus × yedoensis var. angustipetala

Prunus × yedoensis var. angustipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Yoshino cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and is noted for its early blooming period, typically in late winter to early spring. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers, which are highly ornamental and attract pollinators. It is often cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes for its aesthetic value.

This variety is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
