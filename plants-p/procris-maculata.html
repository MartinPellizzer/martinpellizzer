Procris maculata

Procris maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Procris maculata is often found in clusters, with leaves that are lance-shaped and have a waxy texture.
