Ptarmica krylovii

Ptarmica krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine.

Ptarmica krylovii thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in rocky or alpine environments.
