Platyopuntia pituitosa

Platyopuntia pituitosa, commonly known as the yellow nightshade cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in clusters of spines and small, yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may turn red. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

The botanical features of Platyopuntia pituitosa make it well-suited for desert ecosystems.
