Polygonum oreophilum

Polygonum oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense inflorescences.

Polygonum oreophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
