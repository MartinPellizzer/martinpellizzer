Phyllanthus linearis var. genuinus

Phyllanthus linearis var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India and Malaysia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by greenish, capsule-like fruits.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
