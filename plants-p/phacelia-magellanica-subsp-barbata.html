Phacelia magellanica subsp. barbata

Phacelia magellanica subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the barbed phacelia, is a flowering plant native to southern South America. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy, barbed bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
