Phyllanthus vitilevuensis

Phyllanthus vitilevuensis, commonly known as the Taveu tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Vanuatu.

It belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, lowland areas and is often found in forested regions. It has a woody stem and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native habitat.
