Phoradendron emarginatum var. obovatifolium

Phoradendron emarginatum var. obovatifolium, commonly known as the obovate-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stem. The plant produces white or yellowish flowers followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

Its botanical structure reflects its parasitic lifestyle, with reduced roots and chlorophyll content compared to free-living plants.
