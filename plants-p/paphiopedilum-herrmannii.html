Paphiopedilum herrmannii

Paphiopedilum herrmannii, commonly known as the Hermann's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a specialized structure for trapping pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a mix of green, yellow, and white, with intricate patterns that enhance its visual appeal.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after in the horticultural trade.
