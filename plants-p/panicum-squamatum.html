Panicum squamatum

Panicum squamatum, commonly known as silky grass or silver grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements further contribute to its appeal in native plant gardens.
