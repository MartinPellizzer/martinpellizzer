Prunus mutabilis f. racemiflora

Prunus mutabilis f. racemiflora, commonly known as the racemiflora cherry, is a unique variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a range of colors from white to pink and even red. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8. Its foliage is typically dark green, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant blooms.

This ornamental shrub or small tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal display.
