Phanera pierrei

Phanera pierrei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native habitat. Its botanical features include slender stems, lance-shaped leaves, and intricate floral structures that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, Phanera pierrei is considered a conservation concern.
