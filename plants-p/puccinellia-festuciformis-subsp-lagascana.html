Puccinellia festuciformis subsp. lagascana

Puccinellia festuciformis subsp.

lagascana, commonly known as the Mediterranean glassworts, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in saline or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soils and high salinity, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for studying plant adaptation and ecological resilience.
