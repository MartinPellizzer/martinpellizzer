Penstemon laevigatus subsp. digitalis

Penstemon laevigatus subsp.

digitalis, commonly known as the digital penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of blue to violet.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. This subspecies is well adapted to rocky and dry habitats, often found in open woodlands and slopes.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
