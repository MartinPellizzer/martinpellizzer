Plantago truncata

Plantago truncata, commonly known as the cutleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often truncate at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months. It is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
