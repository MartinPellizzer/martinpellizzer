Potentilla malyana

Potentilla malyana, commonly known as the Malyan cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Potentilla malyana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
