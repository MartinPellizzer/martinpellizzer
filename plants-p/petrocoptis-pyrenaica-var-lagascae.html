Petrocoptis pyrenaica var. lagascae

Petrocoptis pyrenaica var. lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasce rock-cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone substrates, thriving in well-drained, calcareous soils. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with four petals. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh alpine environments and its ecological significance in mountainous ecosystems.
