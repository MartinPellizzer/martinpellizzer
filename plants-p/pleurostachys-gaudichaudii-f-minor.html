Pleurostachys gaudichaudii f. minor

Pleurostachys gaudichaudii f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
