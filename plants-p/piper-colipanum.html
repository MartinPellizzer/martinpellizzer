Piper colipanum

Piper colipanum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruits are small berries that contain the aromatic compounds responsible for its distinctive flavor.

Piper colipanum is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
