Pinus sosnowskyi

Pinus sosnowskyi, commonly known as the Sosnowsky's pine, is a rare and critically endangered conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces long, slender cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cold, dry climates and well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss and overgrazing, its population has drastically declined, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
