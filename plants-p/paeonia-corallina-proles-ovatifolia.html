Paeonia corallina proles ovatifolia

Paeonia corallina proles ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaf coral peony, is a rare and striking member of the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive coral-like appearance. The plant features ovate-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental value. Native to specific regions in China, it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rarity and unique botanical features, it is highly valued in horticulture and botanical research.
