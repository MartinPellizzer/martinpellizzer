Ptilotus exaltatus var. glaber

Ptilotus exaltatus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth spinifex, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairless leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
