Pachycymbium deflersianum

Pachycymbium deflersianum, commonly known as the deflersian cycad, is a unique species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil. It produces cones rather than flowers, and the female cones develop into seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
