Pearcea bella

Pearcea bella, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Pearcea bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
