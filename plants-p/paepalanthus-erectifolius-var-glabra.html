Paepalanthus erectifolius var. glabra

Paepalanthus erectifolius var. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf puya, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, smooth leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
