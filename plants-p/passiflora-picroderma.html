Passiflora picroderma

Passiflora picroderma, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers, which feature a unique blend of petals, sepals, and modified leaves. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. Its botanical structure includes tendrils for climbing and a complex corona of filaments surrounding the reproductive parts.

Passiflora picroderma is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
