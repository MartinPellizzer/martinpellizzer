Picris rivularis

Picris rivularis, commonly known as water ragwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Water ragwort is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in areas with partial to full sunlight.
