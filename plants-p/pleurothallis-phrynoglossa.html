Pleurothallis phrynoglossa

Pleurothallis phrynoglossa, commonly known as the "lipsnake orchid," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often colorful flowers that resemble a snake's tongue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Pleurothallis phrynoglossa is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
