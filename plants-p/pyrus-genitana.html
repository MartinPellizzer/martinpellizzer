Pyrus genitana

Pyrus genitana, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has a broad, rounded crown and produces small, edible fruits known as whitebeams. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

Pyrus genitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
