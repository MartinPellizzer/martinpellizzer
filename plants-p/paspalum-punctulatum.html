Paspalum punctulatum

Paspalum punctulatum, commonly known as the silvergrass or narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Paspalum punctulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
