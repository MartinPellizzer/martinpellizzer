Philibertia linearis var. hirtella

Philibertia linearis var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy leaf filbert, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in loose racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant collections.
