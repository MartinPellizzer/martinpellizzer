Piper padifolium var. corcovadense

Piper padifolium var. corcovadense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, followed by dark purple to black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its aromatic leaves and berries have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
