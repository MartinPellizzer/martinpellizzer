Pristimera andina

Pristimera andina, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has adapted to the cold, arid conditions of its native habitat. It features a compact growth form with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.
