Pygeum oblongum

Pygeum oblongum, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible and has a tangy, acidic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It also has medicinal uses, particularly in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
