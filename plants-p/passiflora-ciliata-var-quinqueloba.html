Passiflora ciliata var. quinqueloba

Passiflora ciliata var. quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, violet, and white hues, often with a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, pulpy flesh. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral structure and attractive foliage.
