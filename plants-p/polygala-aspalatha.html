Polygala aspalatha

Polygala aspalatha, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
