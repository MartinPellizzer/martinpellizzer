Phaseolus linearis f. intermedius

Phaseolus linearis f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate runner bean, is a botanical variant of the common runner bean.

It is characterized by its elongated, linear pod shape and typically exhibits a mix of traits between the standard runner bean and other Phaseolus species. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible beans. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is often used in both culinary and ornamental gardening.
