Paris tetraphylla f. corollata

Paris tetraphylla f. corollata, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white veining pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
