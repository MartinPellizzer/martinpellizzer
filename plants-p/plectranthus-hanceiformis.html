Plectranthus hanceiformis

Plectranthus hanceiformis, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking violet-pink flowers and glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
