Peltopus thelantyx

Peltopus thelantyx, commonly known as the silverleaf cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, feathery foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are scale-like and covered in a silvery-white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to release seeds.

This plant is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
