Passiflora vitifolia var. bracteosa

Passiflora vitifolia var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa passionflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and role in ecological systems.
