Parthenocissus vitacea

Parthenocissus vitacea, commonly known as the Boston ivy, is a climbing vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its tendrils allow it to climb and spread over walls, trellises, and buildings.

Botanically, it belongs to the Vitaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide seasonal color.
