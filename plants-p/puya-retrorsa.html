Puya retrorsa

Puya retrorsa, commonly known as the Queen of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette structure. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear numerous showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in cool, dry climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
