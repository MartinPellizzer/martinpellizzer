Poa megastachya var. minor

Poa megastachya var. minor, commonly known as the lesser tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
