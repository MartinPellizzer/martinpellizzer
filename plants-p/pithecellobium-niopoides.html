Pithecellobium niopoides

Pithecellobium niopoides, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible, sweet, and sticky seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional food.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it well-suited for humid, tropical climates.
