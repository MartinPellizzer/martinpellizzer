Pelargonium × micans

Pelargonium × micans, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves that release a pleasant scent when touched. The plant typically features broad, lobed leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value and fragrant foliage make it a versatile plant for both decorative and aromatic purposes.
