Phaseolus linearis var. coriaceus

Phaseolus linearis var. coriaceus, commonly known as the coriaceus bean, is a variety of the scarlet runner bean characterized by its distinctive, corrugated, leathery pod.

The plant is native to the Americas and thrives in warm climates, producing vibrant red flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The pods are typically elongated and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length, containing edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its nutritional content.
