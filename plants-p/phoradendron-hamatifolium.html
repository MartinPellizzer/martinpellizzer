Phoradendron hamatifolium

Phoradendron hamatifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe of the Caribbean, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the tropical regions of the Americas.

The plant produces small, white or yellowish berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its often arid habitat.

Phoradendron hamatifolium plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its environment, influencing both the host tree and the local wildlife.
