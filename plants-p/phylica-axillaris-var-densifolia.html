Phylica axillaris var. densifolia

Phylica axillaris var. densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved silver bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
