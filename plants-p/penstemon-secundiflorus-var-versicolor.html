Penstemon secundiflorus var. versicolor

Penstemon secundiflorus var. versicolor, commonly known as the blue mist penstemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of tubular blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
