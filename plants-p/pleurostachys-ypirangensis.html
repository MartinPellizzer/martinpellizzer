Pleurostachys ypirangensis

Pleurostachys ypirangensis, commonly known as the Ypiranga grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
