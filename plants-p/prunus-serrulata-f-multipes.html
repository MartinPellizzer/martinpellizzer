Prunus serrulata f. multipes

Prunus serrulata f. multipes, commonly known as the multi-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its profuse flowering, with numerous small, white to pink blossoms appearing in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
