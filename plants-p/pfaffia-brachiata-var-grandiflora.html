Pfaffia brachiata var. grandiflora

Pfaffia brachiata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are the primary part used for medicinal purposes, containing compounds believed to have adaptogenic properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
