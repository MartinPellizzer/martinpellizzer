Phacelia nemophiloides

Phacelia nemophiloides, commonly known as the woodland phacelia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Phacelia nemophiloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
