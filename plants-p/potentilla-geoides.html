Potentilla geoides

Potentilla geoides, commonly known as the rock potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Potentilla geoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardiness and adaptability.
