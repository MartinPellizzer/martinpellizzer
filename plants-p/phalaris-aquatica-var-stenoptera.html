Phalaris aquatica var. stenoptera

Phalaris aquatica var. stenoptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watergrass, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable and sometimes invasive species in certain environments.
