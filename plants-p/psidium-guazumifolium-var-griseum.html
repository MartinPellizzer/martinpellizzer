Psidium guazumifolium var. griseum

Psidium guazumifolium var. griseum, commonly known as the silverleaf guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its fruit in some regions.
