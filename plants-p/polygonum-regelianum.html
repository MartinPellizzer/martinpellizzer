Polygonum regelianum

Polygonum regelianum, commonly known as the Regelian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features erect stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated edge and are typically covered in a waxy coating.

Polygonum regelianum is known for its ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.
