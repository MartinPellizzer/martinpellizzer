Pyrus ludlowii

Pyrus ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
