Pterocactus australis var. arnoldianus

Pterocactus australis var. arnoldianus, commonly known as the Arnold's Australian cactus, is a rare and spiny cactus native to specific regions of Australia.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
