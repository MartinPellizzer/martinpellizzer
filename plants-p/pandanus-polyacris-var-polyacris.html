Pandanus polyacris var. polyacris

Pandanus polyacris var. polyacris, commonly known as the hairy pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Indo-Pacific region, including parts of Australia, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. It produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental plants.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
