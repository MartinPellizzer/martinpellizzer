Puccinellia pauciflora var. holmii

Puccinellia pauciflora var. holmii, commonly known as Holm's sparseflower grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with few flowers. It is often found in steppe and desert-steppe ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local vegetation in its native habitat.
