Passiflora venusta

Passiflora venusta, commonly known as the beautiful passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces bright orange or yellow berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and dark green color.

Passiflora venusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
