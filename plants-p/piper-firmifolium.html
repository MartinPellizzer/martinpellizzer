Piper firmifolium

Piper firmifolium, commonly known as the thick-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to survive in dry conditions with minimal water.
