Plantago coronopus subvar. major

Plantago coronopus subvar. major, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, leafy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters at the tips of the stems, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
