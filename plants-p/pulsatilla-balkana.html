Pulsatilla balkana

Pulsatilla balkana, commonly known as the Balkan pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces achenes with feathery pappus for wind dispersal.

Pulsatilla balkana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native environment.
