Phaseolus incanus

Phaseolus incanus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and compound leaves. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaseolus incanus is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal.
