Potentilla nitida f. albiflora

Potentilla nitida f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower cinquefoil, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive white flowers.

It is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. The plant features finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in heads, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
