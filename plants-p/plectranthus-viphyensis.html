Plectranthus viphyensis

Plectranthus viphyensis, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Plectranthus viphyensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
