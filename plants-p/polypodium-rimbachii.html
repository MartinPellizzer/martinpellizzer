Polypodium rimbachii

Polypodium rimbachii, commonly known as the Rimbach's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with rhizomes and absorbing moisture from the air. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, elongated shape with a smooth margin.

Polypodium rimbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
