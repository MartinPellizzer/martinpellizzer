Potentilla bolanderi var. clevelandii

Potentilla bolanderi var. clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland's potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is often found in maritime chaparral habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
