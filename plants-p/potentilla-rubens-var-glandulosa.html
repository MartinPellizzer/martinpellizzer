Potentilla rubens var. glandulosa

Potentilla rubens var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and the plant often has a distinctive reddish tinge along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
