Piper trachyphyllum

Piper trachyphyllum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a bright red color, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in local herbal remedies for its potential health benefits.
