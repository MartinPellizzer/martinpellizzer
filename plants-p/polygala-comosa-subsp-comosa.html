Polygala comosa subsp. comosa

Polygala comosa subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a distinctive spurred structure.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.
