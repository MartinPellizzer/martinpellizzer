Papaver dubium var. errabundum

Papaver dubium var. errabundum, commonly known as the wandering poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its variable and often unpredictable flowering patterns.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range from pale pink to deep red, with a distinctive, papery texture. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
