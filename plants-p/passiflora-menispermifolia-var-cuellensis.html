Passiflora menispermifolia var. cuellensis

Passiflora menispermifolia var. cuellensis, commonly known as the Cuellensis passionflower, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and unique floral structure.
