Pyrus aria subsp. rupicola

Pyrus aria subsp.

rupicola, commonly known as the rock pear, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its hardy nature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
