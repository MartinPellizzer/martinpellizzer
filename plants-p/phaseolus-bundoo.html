Phaseolus bundoo

Phaseolus bundoo, commonly known as the Bundoo bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with trifoliate structures. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

The botanical features of Phaseolus bundoo make it an important crop for both nutritional and agricultural purposes in its native habitat.
