Pterocarpus carolinensis

Pterocarpus carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina trumpetwood, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The tree has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, oval seeds. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
