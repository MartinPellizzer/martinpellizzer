Pyrus fauriei var. pyriformis

Pyrus fauriei var. pyriformis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. Its fruit, resembling a pear, is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.

This variety is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its elegant form and seasonal display.
