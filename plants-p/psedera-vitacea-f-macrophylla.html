Psedera vitacea f. macrophylla

Psedera vitacea f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its berries are usually red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of South America and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
