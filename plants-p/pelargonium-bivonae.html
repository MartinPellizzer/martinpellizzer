Pelargonium bivonae

Pelargonium bivonae, commonly known as the Cape pelargonium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring aromatic leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in spring and summer. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and containers.
