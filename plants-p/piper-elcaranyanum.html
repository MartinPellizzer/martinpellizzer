Piper elcaranyanum

Piper elcaranyanum, commonly known as the Khasi pepper, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and long pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often require support. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy surface and a distinct aroma.

The fruit, which is a small, dark berry, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
