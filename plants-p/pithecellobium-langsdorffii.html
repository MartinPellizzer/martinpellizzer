Pithecellobium langsdorffii

Pithecellobium langsdorffii, commonly known as the yellow burr acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.

The species is also used in traditional medicine in some areas.
