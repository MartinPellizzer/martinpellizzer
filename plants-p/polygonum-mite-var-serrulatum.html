Polygonum mite var. serrulatum

Polygonum mite var. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated polygonum mite, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It produces clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
