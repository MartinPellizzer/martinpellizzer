Pomaderris obovata

Pomaderris obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaved pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are broadly ovate in shape and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its fruits are woody, spherical follicles that release seeds when mature.

Pomaderris obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
