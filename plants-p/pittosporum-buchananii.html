Pittosporum buchananii

Pittosporum buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by yellow berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum buchananii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
