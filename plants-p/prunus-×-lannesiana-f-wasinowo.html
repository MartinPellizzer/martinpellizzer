Prunus × lannesiana f. wasinowo

Prunus × lannesiana f. wasinowo, commonly known as the Wasinowo cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is known for its ornamental value, particularly its vibrant pink to red flowers that appear in spring. It typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, with smooth, dark bark and glossy leaves.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
