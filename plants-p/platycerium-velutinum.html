Platycerium velutinum

Platycerium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique epiphytic fern native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that resemble a fern but are actually modified leaves called phyllodes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on the trunks of trees in rainforests. Its dark, leathery foliage gives it a velvety texture, which contributes to its common name.

Platycerium velutinum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ease of care in the right conditions.
