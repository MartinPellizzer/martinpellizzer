Passiflora calypilosa

Passiflora calypilosa, commonly known as the "Cape gooseberry" or "Inca berry," is a species of flowering plant in the Passifloraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces small, orange to yellow berries that are encased in a spiky, green calyx. Its flowers are showy, with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
