Polygonum calostachyum

Polygonum calostachyum, commonly known as the purpleleaf smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces small, three-lobed leaves.

Polygonum calostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
