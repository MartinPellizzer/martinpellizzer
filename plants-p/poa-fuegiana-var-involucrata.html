Poa fuegiana var. involucrata

Poa fuegiana var. involucrata, commonly known as the Fuegian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are often covered with a pale, hairy sheath. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments such as mountainous areas and moist meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
