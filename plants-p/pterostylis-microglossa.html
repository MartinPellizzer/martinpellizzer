Pterostylis microglossa

Pterostylis microglossa, commonly known as the small-lipped greenhood, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces a single, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive hood-like labellum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as under dense forest canopies.

Pterostylis microglossa is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
