Pachyphytum rzedowskii

Pachyphytum rzedowskii, commonly known as the silver moon cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, a typical feature of succulent plants. The plant has a distinctive silver-blue coloration due to the dense covering of fine hairs on its stems. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Pachyphytum rzedowskii is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
