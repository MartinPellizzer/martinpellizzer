Prunus dielsiana

Prunus dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hard fruits called drupes, which are typically red or yellow when mature. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.

Prunus dielsiana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
