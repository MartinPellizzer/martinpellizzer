Puccinellia gorodkovii

Puccinellia gorodkovii, commonly known as the Siberian glassworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to thrive in saline and arid environments, often found in coastal areas and salt marshes.

Puccinellia gorodkovii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
