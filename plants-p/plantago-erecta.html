Plantago erecta

Plantago erecta, commonly known as erect plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, ribbed stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically arranged in two distinct tiers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
