Phyllostachys viridis f. aurata

Phyllostachys viridis f. aurata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species characterized by its striking yellow-green culms.

This ornamental grass is native to East Asia and is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant features slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening and privacy screens.
