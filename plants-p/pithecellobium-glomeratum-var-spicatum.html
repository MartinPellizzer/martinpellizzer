Pithecellobium glomeratum var. spicatum

Pithecellobium glomeratum var. spicatum, commonly known as the spicata cluster acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
