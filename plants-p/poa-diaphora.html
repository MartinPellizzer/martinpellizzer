Poa diaphora

Poa diaphora, commonly known as the translucent grass or ghost grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves that often appear silvery or translucent when viewed from certain angles. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet that is often hidden among the foliage.

Poa diaphora is notable for its unique appearance and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
