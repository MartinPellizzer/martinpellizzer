Parnassia palustris var. multiseta

Parnassia palustris var. multiseta, commonly known as the swamp parnassia, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and bog environments in North America.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers with prominent yellow spots, which are characteristic of the Parnassia genus. It thrives in poorly drained soils and requires consistent moisture.

This variant is distinguished by its multiple stamens, which give it the specific epithet "multiseta.".
