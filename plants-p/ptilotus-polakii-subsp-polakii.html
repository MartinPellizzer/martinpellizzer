Ptilotus polakii subsp. polakii

Ptilotus polakii subsp.

polakii, commonly known as the "picket fence" or "desert star," is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia. It is a low-growing shrub with distinctive, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, featuring succulent leaves and deep root systems. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it plays a role in supporting local insect populations.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
