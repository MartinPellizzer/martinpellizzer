Plantago eriopoda var. tweedyi

Plantago eriopoda var. tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States, particularly found in California. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This species is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
