Pleioblastus viridistriatus f. chrysophyllus

Pleioblastus viridistriatus f. chrysophyllus, commonly known as the golden foliage bamboo, is a variant of the green-striped bamboo characterized by its striking yellowish-green foliage.

This ornamental bamboo is native to East Asia and is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It features slender, upright culms with a smooth, pale green surface that turns yellowish in the summer. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 3 meters, making it suitable for privacy screens or as a focal point in garden design.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
