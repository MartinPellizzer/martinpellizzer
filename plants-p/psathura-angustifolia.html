Psathura angustifolia

Psathura angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf psathura, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with trifoliate leaves composed of three narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. Its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Psathura angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
