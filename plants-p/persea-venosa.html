Persea venosa

Persea venosa, commonly known as the Mexican white sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The fruit is a large, spiny capsule that contains a single, creamy white, edible pulp. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with high rainfall.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and the nutritious fruit it produces.
