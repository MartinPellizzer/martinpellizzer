Piper pervelutinum

Piper pervelutinum, commonly known as the velvet pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species known for their aromatic properties. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper pervelutinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
