Petalodiscus oblongifolius

Petalodiscus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf petalodiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, oblong leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Petalodiscus oblongifolius is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
