Pleiocarpa bagshawei

Pleiocarpa bagshawei, commonly known as the Chinese starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is notable for its unique shape and sweet, tangy flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes other economically important trees.
