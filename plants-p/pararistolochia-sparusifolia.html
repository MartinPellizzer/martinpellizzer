Pararistolochia sparusifolia

Pararistolochia sparusifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pipevine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble small pipes. The plant produces a strong, unpleasant odor, which helps deter herbivores. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, making it a common sight in tropical rainforests.

The species is also known for its toxic properties, particularly in its leaves and stems.
