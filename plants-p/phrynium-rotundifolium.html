Phrynium rotundifolium

Phrynium rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used as a starch source.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
