Poa implexa

Poa implexa, commonly known as the tufted bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Poa implexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixtures.
