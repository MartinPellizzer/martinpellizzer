Piper pilovarium

Piper pilovarium, commonly known as the pilose pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
