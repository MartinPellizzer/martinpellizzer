Psammisia sodiroi

Psammisia sodiroi, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, reducing water loss.

The showy yellow flowers appear in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
