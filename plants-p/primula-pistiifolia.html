Primula pistiifolia

Primula pistiifolia, commonly known as the desert primrose, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It blooms in early spring, often being one of the first plants to emerge after winter.

Primula pistiifolia is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
