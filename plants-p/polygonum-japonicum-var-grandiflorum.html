Polygonum japonicum var. grandiflorum

Polygonum japonicum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread rapidly and aggressively. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a problematic weed in many regions outside its native range.
