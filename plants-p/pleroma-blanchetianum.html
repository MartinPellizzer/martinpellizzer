Pleroma blanchetianum

Pleroma blanchetianum, commonly known as the purple-flowered plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, violet to purple flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

Pleroma blanchetianum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, thriving in warm, temperate climates.
