Picea excelsa f. barryi

Picea excelsa f. barryi, commonly known as the Barry's white spruce, is a botanical variant of the white spruce species.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. This subspecies is native to specific regions of Canada, particularly in the Yukon and Alaska. It typically grows in cold, high-altitude environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in boreal forest ecosystems.
