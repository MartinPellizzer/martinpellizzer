Panicum crus-galli var. angustifolium

Panicum crus-galli var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf barnyardgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it a common weed in many ecosystems.
