Pourthiaea laevis var. parvifolia

Pourthiaea laevis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved pourthiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
