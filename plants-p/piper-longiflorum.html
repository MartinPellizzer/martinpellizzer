Piper longiflorum

Piper longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, green berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
