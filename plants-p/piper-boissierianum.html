Piper boissierianum

Piper boissierianum, commonly known as the Boissier's pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the spicy compound piperine. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated margin and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
