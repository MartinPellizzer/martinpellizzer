Poinciana pulcherrima var. flava

Poinciana pulcherrima var. flava, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like leaves that turn brilliant yellow during the flowering season. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
