Pauridiantha afzelii

Pauridiantha afzelii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The flowers emit a strong, sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
