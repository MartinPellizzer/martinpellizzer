Pandanus latifolius

Pandanus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils.
