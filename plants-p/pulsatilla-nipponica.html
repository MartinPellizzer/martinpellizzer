Pulsatilla nipponica

Pulsatilla nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or mountainous regions. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and upright.

Pulsatilla nipponica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring bloom.
