Passiflora cinnabarina

Passiflora cinnabarina, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces intricate flowers with a unique structure featuring five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive organs. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
