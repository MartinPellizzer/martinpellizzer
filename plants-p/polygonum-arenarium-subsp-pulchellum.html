Polygonum arenarium subsp. pulchellum

Polygonum arenarium subsp.

pulchellum, commonly known as the sand polygon, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, erect spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within the Polygonaceae family.
