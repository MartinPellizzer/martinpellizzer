Prunus × fruticans subsp. ligerina

Prunus × fruticans subsp.

ligerina, commonly known as the wild cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features fragrant white or pink flowers in spring.

The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, edible drupes that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
