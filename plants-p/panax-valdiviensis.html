Panax valdiviensis

Panax valdiviensis, commonly known as the Valdivian ginseng, is a rare and endangered plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the genus Panax, which includes other ginseng species, and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy roots and compound leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, though its conservation status remains critical due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
