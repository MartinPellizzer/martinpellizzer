Pleurothallis hammelii

Pleurothallis hammelii, commonly known as the Hammel's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact inflorescence that produces multiple blooms.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
