Pavetta indica f. subvelutina

Pavetta indica f. subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
