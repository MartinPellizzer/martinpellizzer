Parnassia lutea

Parnassia lutea, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble a cluster of small crow's feet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have five to eight sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

Parnassia lutea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
