Physaria gracilis var. repanda

Physaria gracilis var. repanda, commonly known as the slender bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has slender, branching stems and small, pinnately divided leaves.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, and the plant produces seed pods that split open to release seeds.
