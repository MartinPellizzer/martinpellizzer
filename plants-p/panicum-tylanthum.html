Panicum tylanthum

Panicum tylanthum, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured foliage and slender, arching stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Panicum tylanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
