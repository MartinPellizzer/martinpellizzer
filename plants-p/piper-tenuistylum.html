Piper tenuistylum

Piper tenuistylum, commonly known as the slenderleaf pepper or small-leaved long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its small, elongated berries. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

The botanical features of Piper tenuistylum make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
