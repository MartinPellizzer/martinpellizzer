Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum f. normale

Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum f. normale, commonly known as the large-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
