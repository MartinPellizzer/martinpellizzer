Prunus × shiro-fumotoii

Prunus × shiro-fumotoii, commonly known as the Shiro-fumotoii cherry, is a hybrid species originating from Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse flowering, typically producing white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its hybrid nature, it may exhibit variability in growth and flowering characteristics.
