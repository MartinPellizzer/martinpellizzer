Pelargonium villosum

Pelargonium villosum, commonly known as the hairy geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Pelargonium villosum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
