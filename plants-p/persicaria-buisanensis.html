Persicaria buisanensis

Persicaria buisanensis, commonly known as the Bisanese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often swollen nodes. The plant typically features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib. Persicaria buisanensis produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
