Pseudomertensia rosulata

Pseudomertensia rosulata, commonly known as the clustered mertensia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that form a dense cluster. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
