Philodendron triangulare

Philodendron triangulare, commonly known as the Triangular Philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Philodendron triangulare is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
