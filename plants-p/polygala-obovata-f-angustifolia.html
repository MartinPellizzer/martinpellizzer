Polygala obovata f. angustifolia

Polygala obovata f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, giving the plant its specific epithet "angustifolia.". It is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

This plant is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
