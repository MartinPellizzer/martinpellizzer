Parietaria ruwenzoriensis subsp. keniensis

Parietaria ruwenzoriensis subsp.

keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan wall-rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of East Africa. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while its hairy stems provide a unique texture.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
