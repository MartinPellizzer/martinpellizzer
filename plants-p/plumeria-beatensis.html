Plumeria beatensis

Plumeria beatensis, commonly known as the sacred frangipani of Madagascar, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the island nation.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, often appearing in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few days, and are pollinated by insects and birds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in local traditions.
