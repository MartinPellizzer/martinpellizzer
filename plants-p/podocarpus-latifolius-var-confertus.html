Podocarpus latifolius var. confertus

Podocarpus latifolius var. confertus, commonly known as the thick-leafed Chinese podocarp, is a species of evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The leaves are typically broad and leathery, providing a dense canopy. This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by supporting local biodiversity.
