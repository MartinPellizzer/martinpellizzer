Pteris currorii

Pteris currorii, commonly known as the Curran's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often pale green with a bluish tinge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant across the forest floor.

Pteris currorii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
