Parathesis macrocarpa

Parathesis macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited parathesis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its woody stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Parathesis macrocarpa is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
