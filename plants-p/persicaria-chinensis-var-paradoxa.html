Persicaria chinensis var. paradoxa

Persicaria chinensis var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
