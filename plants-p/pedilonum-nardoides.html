Pedilonum nardoides

Pedilonum nardoides, commonly known as the fragrant rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Pedilonum nardoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aromatic blooms.
