Prunus persica lusus pyramidalis

Prunus persica lusus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its upright, pyramid-shaped growth habit.

It produces sweet, juicy fruits with a smooth, thin skin and a vibrant yellow or red blush. The plant is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It flowers in the spring with white or pink blossoms, followed by fruiting in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible yield, making it a popular choice for both gardens and orchards.
