Podocarpus affinis

Podocarpus affinis, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or Taiwan podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like structures. The tree typically grows in humid, tropical to subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat.

Podocarpus affinis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its durable wood.
