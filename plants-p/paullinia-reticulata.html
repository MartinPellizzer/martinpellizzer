Paullinia reticulata

Paullinia reticulata, commonly known as the Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces a distinctive, spicy aroma.

The fruit is popular in local cuisines and is also used in traditional medicine.
