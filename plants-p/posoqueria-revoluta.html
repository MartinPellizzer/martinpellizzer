Posoqueria revoluta

Posoqueria revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in the leaf axils. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Posoqueria revoluta is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain regions.
