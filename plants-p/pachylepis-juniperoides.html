Pachylepis juniperoides

Pachylepis juniperoides, commonly known as the New Zealand bottlebrush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red flowers that resemble bottle brushes, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

The botanical features of Pachylepis juniperoides make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
