Piper consanguineum

Piper consanguineum, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and are followed by small, red berries that contain the seeds.

Piper consanguineum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
