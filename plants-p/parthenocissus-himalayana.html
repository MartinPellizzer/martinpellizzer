Parthenocissus himalayana

Parthenocissus himalayana, commonly known as the Himalayan ivy, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible for birds. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically dark green and turn vibrant red in the fall, adding visual interest to gardens.
