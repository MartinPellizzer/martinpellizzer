Prunus serrulata f. contorta

Prunus serrulata f. contorta, commonly known as the twisted cherry or katsura tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its twisted, gnarled branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by small red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique form and seasonal color changes make it a distinctive addition to horticultural collections.
