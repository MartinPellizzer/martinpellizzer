Pavonia ovata

Pavonia ovata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically on tall, branching stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Pavonia ovata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
