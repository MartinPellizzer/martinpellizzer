Psidium rotundidiscum

Psidium rotundidiscum, commonly known as the round-leaf guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, similar to the common guava, is typically small and has a mild flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.
