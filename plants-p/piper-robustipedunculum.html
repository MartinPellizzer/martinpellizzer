Piper robustipedunculum

Piper robustipedunculum, commonly known as the robustipedunculate pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, aerial roots that help it adhere to trees and other structures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a distinct aroma, while the small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white in color.

The fruit, which is a small, berry-like capsule, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
