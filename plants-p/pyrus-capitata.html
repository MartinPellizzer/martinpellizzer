Pyrus capitata

Pyrus capitata, commonly known as the clustered pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble pears, often with a yellow or green skin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The botanical characteristics of Pyrus capitata make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable.
