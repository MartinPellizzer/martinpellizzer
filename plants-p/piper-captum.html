Piper captum

Piper captum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species like black pepper. The plant produces small, clustered purple berries that are edible and have a slightly spicy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The vine thrives in humid, warm climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.
