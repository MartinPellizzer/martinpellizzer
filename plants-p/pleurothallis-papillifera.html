Pleurothallis papillifera

Pleurothallis papillifera, commonly known as the papillifera orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its blooming period usually occurs during the rainy season, and it is known for its intricate floral structure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
