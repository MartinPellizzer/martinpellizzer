Primula malacoides

Primula malacoides, commonly known as the Malacca primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Primula malacoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
