Pterocarpus indicus f. echinatus

Pterocarpus indicus f. echinatus, commonly known as the echinate pterocarpus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woody, and often buttressed trunk, with compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
