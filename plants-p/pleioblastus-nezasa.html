Pleioblastus nezasa

Pleioblastus nezasa, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant typically grows in clumps and produces small, inconspicuous green flowers in early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ornamental plant.

Its low maintenance and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
