Psidium guyanense var. amplexicaule

Psidium guyanense var. amplexicaule, commonly known as the Guianese guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, elliptical leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential as a fruit crop in tropical regions.
