Polylepis australis var. subcalva

Polylepis australis var. subcalva, commonly known as the Andean cloud willow, is a shrub or small tree native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, often growing in cloud forests. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing some protection against harsh weather conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by supporting local wildlife and contributing to soil stability.
