Pyrus mulica

Pyrus mulica, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Pyrus mulica is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and contributing to biodiversity in its native habitats.
