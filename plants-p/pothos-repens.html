Pothos repens

Pothos repens, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that can vary in color from solid green to variegated patterns. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its rapid growth rate, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its stems can elongate significantly, often requiring support or pruning to manage its spread.

Pothos repens is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
