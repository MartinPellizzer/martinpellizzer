Prunus × pseudofruticans

Prunus × pseudofruticans, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has ovate to elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
