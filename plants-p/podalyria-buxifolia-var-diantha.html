Podalyria buxifolia var. diantha

Podalyria buxifolia var. diantha, commonly known as the silverleaf rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
