Piper trichophlebium

Piper trichophlebium, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Piper trichophlebium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
