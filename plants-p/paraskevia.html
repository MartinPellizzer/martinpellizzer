Paraskevia

Paraskevia, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Paraskevia is known for its strong, aromatic scent and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
