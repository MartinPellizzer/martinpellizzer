Panicum lherminieri

Panicum lherminieri, commonly known as the golden panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, golden-yellow spikelets that are arranged in panicles, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Panicum lherminieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
