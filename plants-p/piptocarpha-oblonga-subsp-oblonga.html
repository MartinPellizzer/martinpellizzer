Piptocarpha oblonga subsp. oblonga

Piptocarpha oblonga subsp.

oblonga, commonly known as the narrowleaf bushveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open, arid, or semi-arid habitats, often found in bushveld and savanna regions.

The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
