Pluchea tetranthera

Pluchea tetranthera, commonly known as slenderleaf false fleabane, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and it produces achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

Pluchea tetranthera is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.
