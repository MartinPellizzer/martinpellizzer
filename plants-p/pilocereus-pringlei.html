Pilocereus pringlei

Pilocereus pringlei, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to the desert regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in spines and are adapted to store water. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Pilocereus pringlei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
