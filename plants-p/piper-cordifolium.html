Piper cordifolium

Piper cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved pepper or sacred pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
