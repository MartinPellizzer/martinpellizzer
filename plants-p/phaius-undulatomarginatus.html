Phaius undulatomarginatus

Phaius undulatomarginatus, commonly known as the wavy-margin orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces large, showy inflorescences with pendulous flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive wavy margin on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
