Paeonia × chamaeleon

Paeonia × chamaeleon, commonly known as the tree peony, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Paeonia suffruticosa and Paeonia lactiflora.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a range of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, during the spring season. It is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The tree peony is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
