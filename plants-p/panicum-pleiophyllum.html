Panicum pleiophyllum

Panicum pleiophyllum, commonly known as the clustered panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with small, delicate flowers that often appear in summer.

Panicum pleiophyllum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
