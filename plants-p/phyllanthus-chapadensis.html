Phyllanthus chapadensis

Phyllanthus chapadensis, commonly known as Brazilian starfruit or wild starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Phyllanthus chapadensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
