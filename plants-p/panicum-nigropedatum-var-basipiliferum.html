Panicum nigropedatum var. basipiliferum

Panicum nigropedatum var. basipiliferum, commonly known as blackfoot grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black rhizomes and culms, which give it its common name. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It thrives in wetland habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.
