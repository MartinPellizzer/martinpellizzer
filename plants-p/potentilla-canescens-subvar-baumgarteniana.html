Potentilla canescens subvar. baumgarteniana

Potentilla canescens subvar. baumgarteniana, commonly known as the Baumann's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for arid landscapes.
