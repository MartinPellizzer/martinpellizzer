Pleurothallis tripterantha

Pleurothallis tripterantha, commonly known as the three-winged pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that are often pale green or white with subtle markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The genus Pleurothallis is known for its diversity, with many species exhibiting unique floral structures and adaptations to their specific habitats.
