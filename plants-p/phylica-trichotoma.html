Phylica trichotoma

Phylica trichotoma, commonly known as the Natal tree or Natal fever tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Phylica trichotoma is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
