Petalostemon tenuifolius

Petalostemon tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaf petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows from a short, woody stem and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
