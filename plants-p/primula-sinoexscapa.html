Primula sinoexscapa

Primula sinoexscapa, commonly known as the Sinoexscapa primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and showy stamens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
