Pilea pedroi

Pilea pedroi, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Pilea pedroi is a compact, evergreen perennial that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
