Piper cyclophyllum

Piper cyclophyllum, commonly known as the warty pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its warty, rough-textured stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.
