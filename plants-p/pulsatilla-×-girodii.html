Pulsatilla × girodii

Pulsatilla × girodii, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Pulsatilla vulgaris and Pulsatilla patens, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine regions. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are palmately divided, and it is known for its early spring blooming habit.

Pulsatilla × girodii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
