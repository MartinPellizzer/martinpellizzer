Plantago major f. ovata

Plantago major f. ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its broader, more rounded leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are thick and hairy, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on skin irritations and respiratory ailments.
