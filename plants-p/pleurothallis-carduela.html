Pleurothallis carduela

Pleurothallis carduela, commonly known as the Crimson Spatulate Orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive spatulate shape. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Pleurothallis carduela is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
