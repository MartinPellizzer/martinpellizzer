Pinalia matthewsii

Pinalia matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spherical, fuzzy inflorescence that resembles a pincushion. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Pinalia matthewsii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valued ornamental plant in horticulture.
