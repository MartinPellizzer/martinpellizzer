Pterocarpus latifolius

Pterocarpus latifolius, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree's bark is thick and deeply fissured, and it is known for its heavy, dark wood that is highly valued in woodworking. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is also notable for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
