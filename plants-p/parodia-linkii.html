Parodia linkii

Parodia linkii, commonly known as the "Candelabra Cactus," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that often branch into multiple stems, forming a tree-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Parodia linkii is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care.
