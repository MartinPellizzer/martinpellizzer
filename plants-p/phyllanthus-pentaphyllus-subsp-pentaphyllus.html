Phyllanthus pentaphyllus subsp. pentaphyllus

Phyllanthus pentaphyllus subsp.

pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf sandmat, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in clusters of five at the ends of its branches.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
