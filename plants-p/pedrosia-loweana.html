Pedrosia loweana

Pedrosia loweana, commonly known as the New Zealand rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to its natural environment.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
