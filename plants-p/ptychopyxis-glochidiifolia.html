Ptychopyxis glochidiifolia

Ptychopyxis glochidiifolia, commonly known as the spiny leaf acanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed with sharp, glochidiate (spiny) margins, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The flowers are usually violet or purple in color and are clustered in dense inflorescences.
