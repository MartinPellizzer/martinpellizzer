Prunus spinosa subsp. cyclopetala

Prunus spinosa subsp.

cyclopetala, commonly known as the cyclopetal cherry, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are elliptical, with a slightly serrated margin, and the plant produces small, red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
