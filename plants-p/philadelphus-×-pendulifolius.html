Philadelphus × pendulifolius

Philadelphus × pendulifolius, commonly known as the weeping philadelphus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its cascading, weeping branches that bear fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its showy blooms and graceful form.
