Porana megalantha

Porana megalantha, commonly known as the giant porana, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring large, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
