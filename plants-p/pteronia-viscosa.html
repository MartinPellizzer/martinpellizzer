Pteronia viscosa

Pteronia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Pteronia viscosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
