Polypodium angustifolium f. densifolium

Polypodium angustifolium f. densifolium, commonly known as the dense-leaved maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its compact, densely packed fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of North America, particularly the eastern United States. The fronds are typically narrow and elongated, with a dark green color and a leathery texture. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
