Protea coronata

Protea coronata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

The silver protea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
