Phryma nana

Phryma nana, commonly known as dwarf phryma, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
