Piper rivulare var. hygrophilum

Piper rivulare var. hygrophilum, commonly known as the water pepper, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, three-lobed berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
