Puria trilobata

Puria trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain areas.
