Phyllanthus triandrus

Phyllanthus triandrus, commonly known as the three-lobed spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces yellow berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
