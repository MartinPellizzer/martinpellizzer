Peperomia soratana

Peperomia soratana, commonly known as the Soratana peppergrass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

Peperomia soratana is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive foliage.
