Parthenium argentatum var. dissectum

Parthenium argentatum var. dissectum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.

Due to its allergenic properties, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
