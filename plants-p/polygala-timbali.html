Polygala timbali

Polygala timbali, commonly known as the Timbali milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.

Polygala timbali is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
