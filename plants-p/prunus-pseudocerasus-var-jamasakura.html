Prunus pseudocerasus var. jamasakura

Prunus pseudocerasus var. jamasakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown when young.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
