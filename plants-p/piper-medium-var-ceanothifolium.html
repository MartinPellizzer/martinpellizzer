Piper medium var. ceanothifolium

Piper medium var. ceanothifolium, commonly known as the Ceanothifolium pepper plant, is a tropical species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its foliage has a distinct, aromatic scent, often described as peppery or citrusy, which contributes to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

The leaves are typically harvested for their aromatic properties and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
