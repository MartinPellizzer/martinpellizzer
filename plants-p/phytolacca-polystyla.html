Phytolacca polystyla

Phytolacca polystyla, commonly known as the clustered pokeweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Phytolaccaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its toxic properties, particularly in its leaves and roots.

Despite its toxicity, it has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.
