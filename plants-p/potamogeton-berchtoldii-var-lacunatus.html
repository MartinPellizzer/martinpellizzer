Potamogeton berchtoldii var. lacunatus

Potamogeton berchtoldii var. lacunatus, commonly known as the lacunate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often notched or serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
