Plukenetia multiglandulosa

Plukenetia multiglandulosa, commonly known as African butterfruit, is a tropical plant native to western Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, with smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, green fruits resembling small pumpkins. The fruit's pulp is rich in oils and has been traditionally used for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts in its native region.
