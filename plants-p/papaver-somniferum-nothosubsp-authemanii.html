Papaver somniferum nothosubsp. authemanii

Papaver somniferum nothosubsp.

authemanii, commonly known as the Armenian poppy, is a hybrid subspecies that exhibits unique botanical characteristics. It is a herbaceous plant with striking, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain opium-derived alkaloids, similar to its parent species, Papaver somniferum. It is native to the Armenian Highlands and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal and narcotic properties, it is subject to strict regulations in many regions.
