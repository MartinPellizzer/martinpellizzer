Psidium misionum

Psidium misionum, commonly known as the Misionese guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The leaves are ovate, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
