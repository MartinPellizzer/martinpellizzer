Phacelia integrifolia var. arenicola

Phacelia integrifolia var. arenicola, commonly known as the sand phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. This annual plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
