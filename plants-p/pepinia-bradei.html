Pepinia bradei

Pepinia bradei, commonly known as the Brade's peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a distinctive purple or greenish hue.

It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
