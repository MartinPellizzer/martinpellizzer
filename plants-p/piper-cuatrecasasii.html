Piper cuatrecasasii

Piper cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuetzalan pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the cloud forests of central Mexico, specifically the states of Puebla and Oaxaca. The plant is characterized by its small, pendant flowers and elongated, slender fruits that resemble peppers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
