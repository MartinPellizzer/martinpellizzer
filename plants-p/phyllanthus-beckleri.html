Phyllanthus beckleri

Phyllanthus beckleri, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
