Paronychia argentea

Paronychia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Paronychia argentea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
