Primula monbeigii

Primula monbeigii, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Primula monbeigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
