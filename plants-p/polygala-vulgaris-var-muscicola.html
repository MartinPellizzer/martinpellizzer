Polygala vulgaris var. muscicola

Polygala vulgaris var. muscicola, commonly known as the musk polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers with distinctive, spurred petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
