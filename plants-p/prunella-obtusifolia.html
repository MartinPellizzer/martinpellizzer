Prunella obtusifolia

Prunella obtusifolia, commonly known as blunt-leaved heather, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a blunt or rounded tip, and are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a common sight in meadows and woodland edges.

This plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
