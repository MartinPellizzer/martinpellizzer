Pyrus glabra

Pyrus glabra, commonly known as the white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. Pyrus glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in fruit cultivation.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
