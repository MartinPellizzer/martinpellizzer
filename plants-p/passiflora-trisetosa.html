Passiflora trisetosa

Passiflora trisetosa, commonly known as the thorned passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces orange to red berries that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Passiflora trisetosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
