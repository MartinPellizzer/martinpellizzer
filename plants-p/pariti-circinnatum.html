Pariti circinnatum

Pariti circinnatum, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters along its trailing stems. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms.

Pariti circinnatum is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained on trellises.
