Pentagonia spathicalyx

Pentagonia spathicalyx, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.
