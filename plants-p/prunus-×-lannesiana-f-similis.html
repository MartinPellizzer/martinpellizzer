Prunus × lannesiana f. similis

Prunus × lannesiana f. similis, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

The fruit is a small, yellow or orange drupe that ripens in late spring or early summer.
