Pogostemon benthamianus

Pogostemon benthamianus, commonly known as the Benthams' pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rainforest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
