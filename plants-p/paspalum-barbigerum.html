Paspalum barbigerum

Paspalum barbigerum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along roadsides.

Paspalum barbigerum is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in erosion control and wildlife habitats.
