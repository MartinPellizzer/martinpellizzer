Pyracantha crenulata var. emarginata

Pyracantha crenulata var. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate firethorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, orange-red berries that persist through winter. The leaves are elliptical with finely toothed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in hedges or as an ornamental plant.

The plant is relatively drought-tolerant and attracts birds with its berries.
