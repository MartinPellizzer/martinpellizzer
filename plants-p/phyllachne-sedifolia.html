Phyllachne sedifolia

Phyllachne sedifolia, commonly known as the thread-leaf grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like structure. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.

Phyllachne sedifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
