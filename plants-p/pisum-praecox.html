Pisum praecox

Pisum praecox, commonly known as early pea, is an annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, green pods that contain several seeds, which are typically harvested when mature but before the pods dry. Pisum praecox is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.

Its botanical features make it a versatile plant in both traditional and modern farming systems.
