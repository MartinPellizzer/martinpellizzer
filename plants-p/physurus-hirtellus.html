Physurus hirtellus

Physurus hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically lavender to violet in color. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and mild aromatic scent.

Physurus hirtellus prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
