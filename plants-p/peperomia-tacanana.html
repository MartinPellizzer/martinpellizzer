Peperomia tacanana

Peperomia tacanana, commonly known as the Tacana peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental foliage, it is often appreciated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
