Pycreus lancelotii

Pycreus lancelotii, commonly known as the Lancelot's pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Pycreus lancelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
