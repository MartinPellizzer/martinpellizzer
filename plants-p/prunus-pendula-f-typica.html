Prunus pendula f. typica

Prunus pendula f. typica, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the Prunus pendula species.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The plant is native to China and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
