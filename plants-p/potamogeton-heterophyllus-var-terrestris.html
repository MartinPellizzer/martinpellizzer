Potamogeton heterophyllus var. terrestris

Potamogeton heterophyllus var. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in spike-like clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
