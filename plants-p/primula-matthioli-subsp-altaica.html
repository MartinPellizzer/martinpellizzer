Primula matthioli subsp. altaica

Primula matthioli subsp.

altaica, commonly known as the Altai primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a low-growing rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
