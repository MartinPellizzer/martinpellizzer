Piper nigrispicum

Piper nigrispicum, commonly known as black-spined pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
