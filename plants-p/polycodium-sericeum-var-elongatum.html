Polycodium sericeum var. elongatum

Polycodium sericeum var. elongatum, commonly known as the silky polycodium, is a variant of the polycodium genus native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely haired stems and small, clustered leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often arranged in clusters, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
