Phaius pallidus

Phaius pallidus, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The pale orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
