Polygonum fimbriatum

Polygonum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a serrated or fringed edge, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer.

Polygonum fimbriatum is often found in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
