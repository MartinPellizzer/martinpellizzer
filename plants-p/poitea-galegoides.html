Poitea galegoides

Poitea galegoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed poitea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to certain regions of South America, it thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
