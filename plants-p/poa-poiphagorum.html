Poa poiphagorum

Poa poiphagorum, commonly known as the Stewart's bentgrass, is a species of grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Poa poiphagorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
