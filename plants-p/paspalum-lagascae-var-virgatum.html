Paspalum lagascae var. virgatum

Paspalum lagascae var. virgatum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil.

Paspalum lagascae var. virgatum is also known for its tolerance to flooding and its role in supporting local wildlife.
