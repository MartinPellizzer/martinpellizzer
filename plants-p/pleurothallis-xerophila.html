Pleurothallis xerophila

Pleurothallis xerophila, commonly known as the dryland pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elongated leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
