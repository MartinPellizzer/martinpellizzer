Paphiopedilum × holdenii

Paphiopedilum × holdenii, commonly known as the Holden's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species belonging to the genus Paphiopedilum, which is part of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, featuring a specialized lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other substrates in its natural habitat. Its flowers exhibit a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, with a velvety texture that enhances its ornamental appeal.

This hybrid is valued in horticulture for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
