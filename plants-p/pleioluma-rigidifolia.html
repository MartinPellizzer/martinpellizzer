Pleioluma rigidifolia

Pleioluma rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed pleioluma, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand strong winds and intense sunlight.

Pleioluma rigidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
