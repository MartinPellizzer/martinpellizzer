Paeonia foliosa

Paeonia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant features a robust root system and has compound leaves with numerous leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern. Its botanical name reflects the foliage's prominent role in its appearance.

Paeonia foliosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
