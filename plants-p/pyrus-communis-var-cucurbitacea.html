Pyrus communis var. cucurbitacea

Pyrus communis var. cucurbitacea, commonly known as the cucurbitaceous pear, is a rare variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, which resembles that of members of the Cucurbitaceae family. The plant exhibits a distinct growth habit, often with a more sprawling structure compared to typical pear trees. Its flowers are similar to those of other pears, featuring five petals and numerous stamens.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphological traits and potential hybrid origins.
