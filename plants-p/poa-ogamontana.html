Poa ogamontana

Poa ogamontana, commonly known as the Ogamontan grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. This perennial grass is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry culms. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Poa ogamontana is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
