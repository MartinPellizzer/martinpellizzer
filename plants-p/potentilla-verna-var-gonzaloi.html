Potentilla verna var. gonzaloi

Potentilla verna var. gonzaloi, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, often hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with a serrated margin and a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh, high-altitude conditions.
