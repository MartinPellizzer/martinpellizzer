Protasparagus retrofractus

Protasparagus retrofractus, commonly known as the backward spear plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that curve backward, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest regions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
