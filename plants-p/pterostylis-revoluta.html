Pterostylis revoluta

Pterostylis revoluta, commonly known as the rolled-leaf greenhood, is an orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure that is typically green with a yellowish or reddish lip. The plant produces a single, erect flower spike with a single flower, which is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests and understoreys.

The species is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, involving specific insect pollinators.
