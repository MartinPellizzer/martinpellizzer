Potentilla virgata var. pinnatifida

Potentilla virgata var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage structure.
