Peperomia subconcava

Peperomia subconcava, commonly known as the concave peperomia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically borne on short, upright spikes. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and minimal watering requirements.

Its unique leaf shape and ease of care make it a popular choice for houseplants.
