Piper blandum

Piper blandum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries, which can be either yellow or red depending on the maturity. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Piper blandum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
