Pithecellobium ramiflorum

Pithecellobium ramiflorum, commonly known as the branching legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
