Piper collinum

Piper collinum, commonly known as the colline pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and potentially medicinal properties.

Its botanical structure includes compound leaves with three to five leaflets and a unique, branched root system adapted for climbing.
