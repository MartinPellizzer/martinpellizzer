Platyclinis longifolia

Platyclinis longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf platyclinis, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
