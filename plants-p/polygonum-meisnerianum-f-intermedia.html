Polygonum meisnerianum f. intermedia

Polygonum meisnerianum f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate polygonum, is a variant of the genus Polygonum, which belongs to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a broad, ovate shape with a prominent midrib. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, with a pinkish to reddish hue.

This form is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
