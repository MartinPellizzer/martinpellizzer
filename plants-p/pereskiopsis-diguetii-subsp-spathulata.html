Pereskiopsis diguetii subsp. spathulata

Pereskiopsis diguetii subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf pereskiopsis, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
