Panicum andringitrense

Panicum andringitrense, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a species of grass native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
