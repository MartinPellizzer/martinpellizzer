Pinus tokunagae

Pinus tokunagae, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often remain attached to the branches for a long time. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Pinus tokunagae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
