Petalostemon alopecuroides

Petalostemon alopecuroides, commonly known as the silverleaf petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
