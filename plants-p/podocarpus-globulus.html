Podocarpus globulus

Podocarpus globulus, commonly known as the Sydney fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves arranged in flat sprays. The tree produces cones that contain edible, fleshy seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Podocarpus globulus is also valued for its durable wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture-making.
