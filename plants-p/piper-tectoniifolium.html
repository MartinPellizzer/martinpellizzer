Piper tectoniifolium

Piper tectoniifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
