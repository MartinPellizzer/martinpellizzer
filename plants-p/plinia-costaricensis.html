Plinia costaricensis

Plinia costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican plinia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica, and is often found in tropical moist forests. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it has small, elliptical leaves with a glossy texture. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
