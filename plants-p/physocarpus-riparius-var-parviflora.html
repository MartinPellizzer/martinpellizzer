Physocarpus riparius var. parviflora

Physocarpus riparius var. parviflora, commonly known as the smooth hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves that are opposite and often have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in panicle clusters during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
