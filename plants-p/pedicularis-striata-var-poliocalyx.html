Pedicularis striata var. poliocalyx

Pedicularis striata var. poliocalyx, commonly known as the spotted lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting red or purple base. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
