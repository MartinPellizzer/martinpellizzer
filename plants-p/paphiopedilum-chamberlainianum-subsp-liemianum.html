Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum subsp. liemianum

Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum subsp.

liemianum, commonly known as the Liemianum Paphiopedilum, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and its unique, intricate flower structure, which resembles a pouch or lip.

The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
