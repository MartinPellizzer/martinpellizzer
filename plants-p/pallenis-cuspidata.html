Pallenis cuspidata

Pallenis cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pallenis cuspidata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
