Primula partschiana

Primula partschiana, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers are clustered in a raceme.

Primula partschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
