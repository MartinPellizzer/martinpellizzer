Polygala abyssinica var. parviflora

Polygala abyssinica var. parviflora, commonly known as the Abyssinian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
