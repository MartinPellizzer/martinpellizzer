Pelargonium laxum subsp. karooicum

Pelargonium laxum subsp.

karooicum, commonly known as the Karoo geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
