Pyrus crataegaster

Pyrus crataegaster, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to pears but are typically less commonly cultivated.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
