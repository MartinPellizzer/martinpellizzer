Pogostemon glaber var. glaber

Pogostemon glaber var. glaber, commonly known as the broad-leaved pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom during the warm seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
