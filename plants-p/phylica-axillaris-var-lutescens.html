Phylica axillaris var. lutescens

Phylica axillaris var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-leafed silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that turn a striking yellow during the flowering season. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
