Potentilla thyrsiflora var. argenteiformis

Potentilla thyrsiflora var. argenteiformis, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in compact, conical clusters called thyrses. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by both its foliage and its delicate floral display.
