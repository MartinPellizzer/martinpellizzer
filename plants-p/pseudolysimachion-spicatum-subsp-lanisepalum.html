Pseudolysimachion spicatum subsp. lanisepalum

Pseudolysimachion spicatum subsp.

lanisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal piper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants.

The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its subsp. lanisepalum distinguishes itself with broader sepals compared to other subspecies.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
