Parsonsia esculenta

Parsonsia esculenta, commonly known as the edible nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruits are edible when ripe, though they contain toxic compounds that require careful preparation.
