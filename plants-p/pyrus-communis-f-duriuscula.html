Pyrus communis f. duriuscula

Pyrus communis f. duriuscula, commonly known as the rough-leaved pear, is a horticultural form of the common pear.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are more textured than those of the typical pear. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are generally not suitable for eating but are valued for ornamental purposes. It is typically grown as a decorative plant in gardens and is well-suited to temperate climates.

The variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
