Pyrus californica

Pyrus californica, commonly known as the California pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It features alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy green above and grayish-white beneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet to subacid pears in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and Mediterranean climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in California's agricultural history.
