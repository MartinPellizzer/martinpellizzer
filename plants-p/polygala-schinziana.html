Polygala schinziana

Polygala schinziana, commonly known as the Schinz's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are often pink or purple and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
