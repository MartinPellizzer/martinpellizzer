Phaseolus deliciosus

Phaseolus deliciosus, commonly known as the sweet bean or the evergreen bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Phaseolus deliciosus is valued for its nutritional content and ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
