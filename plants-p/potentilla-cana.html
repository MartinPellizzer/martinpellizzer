Potentilla cana

Potentilla cana, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features dark green, ovate leaves with a rough texture and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla cana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
