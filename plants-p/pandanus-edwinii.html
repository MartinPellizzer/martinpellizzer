Pandanus edwinii

Pandanus edwinii, commonly known as the Edwin's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Fiji and Tonga. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves.

The plant is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
