Penstemon linarioides subsp. sileri

Penstemon linarioides subsp.

sileri, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its floral structure is typical of the genus Penstemon, with showy blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
