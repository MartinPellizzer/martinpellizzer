Phaseolus pitcheri

Phaseolus pitcheri, commonly known as the Michigan bean, is a rare and endangered legume native to the Upper Peninsula of Michigan.

It is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, dry pods containing one or two elliptical seeds. It thrives in sandy, acidic soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to habitat loss, it is listed as a threatened species in the United States.
