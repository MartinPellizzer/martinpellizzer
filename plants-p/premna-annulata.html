Premna annulata

Premna annulata, commonly known as the ringed or spotted vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated leaves that feature white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb or trail.

Premna annulata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
