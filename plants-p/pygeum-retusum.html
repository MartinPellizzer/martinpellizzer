Pygeum retusum

Pygeum retusum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ecological importance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
