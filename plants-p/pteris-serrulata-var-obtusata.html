Pteris serrulata var. obtusata

Pteris serrulata var. obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a variant of the common water fern characterized by its broader, more rounded fronds compared to the typical variety.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its fronds are pinnately divided with serrated margins, and the spores are produced on the underside of the leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens or as a houseplant.
