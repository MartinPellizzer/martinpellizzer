Potamogeton alpinus var. linearifolius

Potamogeton alpinus var. linearifolius, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and a healthy aquatic ecosystem.
