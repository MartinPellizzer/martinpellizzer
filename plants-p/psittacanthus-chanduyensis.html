Psittacanthus chanduyensis

Psittacanthus chanduyensis, commonly known as the Chanduyensis cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the rainforests of Myanmar. This plant is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnately compound leaves that are typically dark green in color. It reproduces through cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
