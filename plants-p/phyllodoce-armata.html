Phyllodoce armata

Phyllodoce armata, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas, often found in the understory of coniferous or mixed forests. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are reddish in color, especially when young, and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Phyllodoce armata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small mammals.
