Phyllanthus tenuipes

Phyllanthus tenuipes, commonly known as the slender-leaf phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
