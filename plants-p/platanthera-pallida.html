Platanthera pallida

Platanthera pallida, commonly known as the pale lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to white flowers that are arranged in a loose, loose raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and bogs. It has a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its fragrant, sweet-smelling flowers that attract various pollinators.
