Pancheria pirifolia

Pancheria pirifolia, commonly known as the pear-leaved pancheria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes caraway and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive pear-shaped appearance, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pink flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Pancheria pirifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
