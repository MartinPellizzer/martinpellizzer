Phylica strigosa var. australia

Phylica strigosa var. australia, commonly known as the Australian bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically red or pink in color. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
