Piriqueta constellata

Piriqueta constellata, commonly known as the starry piriqueta, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow, often with a subtle blue or purple hue. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas where the climate is dry and salty.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
