Polygonum guttuliferum

Polygonum guttuliferum, commonly known as the spotted knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish tint, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It often grows in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and horticultural practices.
