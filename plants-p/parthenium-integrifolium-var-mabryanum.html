Parthenium integrifolium var. mabryanum

Parthenium integrifolium var. mabryanum, commonly known as Mabry's roughhopper, is a flowering plant native to the south-western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, rough-hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with entire margins and are covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The small yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
