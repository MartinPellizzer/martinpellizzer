Pyrus iowensis

Pyrus iowensis, commonly known as the Iowa serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

Pyrus iowensis is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
