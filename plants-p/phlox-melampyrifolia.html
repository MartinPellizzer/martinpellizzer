Phlox melampyrifolia

Phlox melampyrifolia, commonly known as the flame phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a dark red throat. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Phlox melampyrifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and hummingbirds.
