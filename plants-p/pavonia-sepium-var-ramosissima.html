Pavonia sepium var. ramosissima

Pavonia sepium var. ramosissima, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branching habit and clusters of showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable to local wildlife.
