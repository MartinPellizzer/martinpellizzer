Piper adamatum

Piper adamatum, commonly known as the Adam's pepper or the West Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
