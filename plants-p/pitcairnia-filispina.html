Pitcairnia filispina

Pitcairnia filispina, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety coating of trichomes, giving it a unique texture and appearance. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences that can last for several months, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Its leaves are adapted to capture and store water, which is essential for survival in its humid, shaded habitat.

Pitcairnia filispina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments with proper care.
