Portulaca constricta

Portulaca constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. It thrives in arid and sandy soils, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Its botanical characteristics include a branching stem, opposite leaves, and a short flowering period during the warmer months.
