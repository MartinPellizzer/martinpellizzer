Phlox richardsonii subsp. borealis

Phlox richardsonii subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
