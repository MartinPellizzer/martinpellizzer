Phelipanche floribunda

Phelipanche floribunda, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces attractive, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.
