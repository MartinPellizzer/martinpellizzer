Passiflora callistemma

Passiflora callistemma, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora callistemma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
