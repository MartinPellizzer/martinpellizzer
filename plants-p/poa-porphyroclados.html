Poa porphyroclados

Poa porphyroclados, commonly known as the purple cliff grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark purple to reddish hues on its leaf blades. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Poa porphyroclados plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is often found in association with other hardy mountain flora.
