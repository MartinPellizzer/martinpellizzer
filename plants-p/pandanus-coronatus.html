Pandanus coronatus

Pandanus coronatus, commonly known as the crown pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanus genus, characterized by its large, feather-like leaves that form a rosette around the stem. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, making it useful for erosion control in coastal areas.

The leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.
