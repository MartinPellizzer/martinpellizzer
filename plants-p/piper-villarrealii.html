Piper villarrealii

Piper villarrealii, commonly known as the Villarreal's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in the understory of humid forests. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny green flowers that develop into red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing along riverbanks and in dense vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
