Pandanus conglomeratus

Pandanus conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into woody, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in the region.
