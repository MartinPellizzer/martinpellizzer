Plantago bicallosa var. hirsutior

Plantago bicallosa var. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with dense, soft hairs, especially on the underside. The plant produces erect, branching stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The species is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and woodland edges.
