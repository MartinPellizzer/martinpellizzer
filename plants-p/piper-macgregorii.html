Piper macgregorii

Piper macgregorii, commonly known as the "macGregor's pepper," is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruits are tiny, three-lobed berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.

However, it is not typically used for culinary purposes, unlike its more famous relatives such as black pepper.
