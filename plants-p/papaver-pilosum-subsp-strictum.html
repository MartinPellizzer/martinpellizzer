Papaver pilosum subsp. strictum

Papaver pilosum subsp.

strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, typically found in rocky or calcareous soils.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems, lanceolate leaves, and showy, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact growth habit compared to other varieties of Papaver pilosum.
