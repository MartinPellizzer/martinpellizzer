Pancratium skinnerianum

Pancratium skinnerianum, commonly known as the golden bulb, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and a bulbous base that stores nutrients. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

Pancratium skinnerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
