Panicum repens var. ischaemoides

Panicum repens var. ischaemoides, commonly known as the silky panic grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, feathery appearance, and it produces small, delicate spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes. Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it a common weed in disturbed areas.

The foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
