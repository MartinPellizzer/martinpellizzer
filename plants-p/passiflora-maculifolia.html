Passiflora maculifolia

Passiflora maculifolia, commonly known as the spotted passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with distinctive white spots and a vibrant, complex flower that features a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Passiflora maculifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
