Piper martinicense var. montis-pilati

Piper martinicense var. montis-pilati, commonly known as the Montes Pilos pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into peppery fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
