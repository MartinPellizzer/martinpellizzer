Plantago philippiana

Plantago philippiana, commonly known as Philippine plantain, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
