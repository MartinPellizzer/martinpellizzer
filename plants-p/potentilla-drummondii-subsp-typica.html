Potentilla drummondii subsp. typica

Potentilla drummondii subsp.

typica, commonly known as Drummond's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces fruit that is a dry, capsule-like structure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
