Pulmonaria × intermedia, commonly known as the lungwort hybrid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Boraginaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to purplish tones. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in early spring. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats.

Its common name reflects its historical use in treating respiratory ailments, though it is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
