Potentilla opaca f. pusilla

Potentilla opaca f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf potentilla, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the summer, adding a delicate touch to rocky or alpine habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
