Panicum caucasicum

Panicum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian millet, is a grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and hardy, making them suitable for cultivation in arid environments.

It is valued for its resilience and historical use in traditional agriculture.
