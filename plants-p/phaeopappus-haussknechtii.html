Phaeopappus haussknechtii

Phaeopappus haussknechtii, commonly known as the yellow pappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and unique inflorescence structure. The plant produces showy yellow ray florets surrounded by a pappus of bristly hairs, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
