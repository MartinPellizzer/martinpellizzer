Posoqueria panamensis subsp. grandiflora

Posoqueria panamensis subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the Panamanian giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can span over a meter in diameter, providing a unique habitat for aquatic life.

The plant produces showy, white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and rivers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
