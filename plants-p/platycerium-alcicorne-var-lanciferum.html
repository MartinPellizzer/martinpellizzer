Platycerium alcicorne var. lanciferum

Platycerium alcicorne var. lanciferum, commonly known as the lance-leaf staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, antler-like fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant's structure consists of a central rosette of sterile fronds surrounded by fertile fronds that produce spores. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
