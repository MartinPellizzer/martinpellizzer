Polycnemum arvense var. multicaule

Polycnemum arvense var. multicaule, commonly known as the common field sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides, fields, and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and establish quickly.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
