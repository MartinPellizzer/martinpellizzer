Platystele adelphe

Platystele adelphe, commonly known as the Adelphe's ghost plant, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and unique, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Due to habitat loss, Platystele adelphe is considered endangered and is often found in conservation programs aimed at preserving its delicate ecosystem.
