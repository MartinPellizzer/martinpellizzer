Petrophile seminuda var. indivisa

Petrophile seminuda var. indivisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves and thrives in well-drained sandy soils. It is often found in open heathlands and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
