Primula firmipes

Primula firmipes, commonly known as the firm-footed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and cool climates. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula firmipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
