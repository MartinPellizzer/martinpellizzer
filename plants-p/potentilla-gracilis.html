Potentilla gracilis

Potentilla gracilis, commonly known as slender cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five narrow lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla gracilis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
