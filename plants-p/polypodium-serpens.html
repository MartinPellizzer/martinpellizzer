Polypodium serpens

Polypodium serpens, commonly known as the snake fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes and fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in forested areas. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to several feet.

Polypodium serpens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
