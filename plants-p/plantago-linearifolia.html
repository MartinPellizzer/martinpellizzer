Plantago linearifolia

Plantago linearifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, slender stems that bear alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on spikes at the top of the stems. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used traditionally for their soothing and healing effects.
