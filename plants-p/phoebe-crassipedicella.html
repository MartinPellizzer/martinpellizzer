Phoebe crassipedicella

Phoebe crassipedicella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during late spring, followed by small, oval berries.

It is valued in horticulture for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
