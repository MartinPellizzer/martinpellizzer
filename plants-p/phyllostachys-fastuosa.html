Phyllostachys fastuosa

Phyllostachys fastuosa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a tall, fast-growing grass native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems that are often bright yellow to golden green in color, making it visually striking. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, drooping panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Phyllostachys fastuosa is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
