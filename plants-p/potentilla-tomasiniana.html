Potentilla tomasiniana

Potentilla tomasiniana, commonly known as the Tomasinian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla tomasiniana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
