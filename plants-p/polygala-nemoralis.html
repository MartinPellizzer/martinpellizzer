Polygala nemoralis

Polygala nemoralis, commonly known as the common milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pale green or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
