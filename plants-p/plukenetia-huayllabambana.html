Plukenetia huayllabambana

Plukenetia huayllabambana, commonly known as the Andean peach, is a shrub native to the Peruvian Andes.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, nutty flavor. The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
