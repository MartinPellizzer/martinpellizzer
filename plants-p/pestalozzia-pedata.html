Pestalozzia pedata

Pestalozzia pedata, commonly known as the spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its long, arching leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate watering. It is known for its ability to produce offsets, which can be easily propagated.

Pestalozzia pedata is also noted for its air-purifying qualities and is often used in indoor gardening.
