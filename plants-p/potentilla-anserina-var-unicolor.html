Potentilla anserina var. unicolor

Potentilla anserina var. unicolor, commonly known as the white-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, with five oval leaflets, and it has a low-growing, spreading habit.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
