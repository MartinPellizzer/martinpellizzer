Pouteria porphyrocarpa

Pouteria porphyrocarpa, commonly known as the purple-fruited spondias, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, purple to black berries. Its fruit is edible, though often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential economic value.
