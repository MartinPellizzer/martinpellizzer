Plectranthus venustus

Plectranthus venustus, commonly known as the velvet-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus venustus is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
