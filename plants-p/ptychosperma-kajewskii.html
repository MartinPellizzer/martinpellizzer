Ptychosperma kajewskii

Ptychosperma kajewskii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a species of palm native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to dates. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The leaves are also used in traditional crafts and as thatching material in some regions.
