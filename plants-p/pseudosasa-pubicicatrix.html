Pseudosasa pubicicatrix

Pseudosasa pubicicatrix, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, which can help prevent soil erosion.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
