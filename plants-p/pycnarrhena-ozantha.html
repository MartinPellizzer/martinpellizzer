Pycnarrhena ozantha

Pycnarrhena ozantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
