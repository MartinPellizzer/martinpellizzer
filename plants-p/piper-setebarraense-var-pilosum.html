Piper setebarraense var. pilosum

Piper setebarraense var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy sedge pepper, is a tropical vine native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its aromatic properties.
