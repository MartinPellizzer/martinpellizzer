Potentilla subacaulis var. latiloba

Potentilla subacaulis var. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rose-like flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in shades of white or pink. The plant has slender, woody stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
