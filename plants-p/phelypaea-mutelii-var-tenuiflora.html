Phelypaea mutelii var. tenuiflora

Phelypaea mutelii var. tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered water clover, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern on the water surface. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with ample sunlight.

Its roots help stabilize sediment, making it beneficial for aquatic ecosystems.
