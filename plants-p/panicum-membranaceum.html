Panicum membranaceum

Panicum membranaceum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to the Americas.

It is a grass-like perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with delicate, thread-like flowers. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other grasses in native plant communities.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
