Polygonum striatulum

Polygonum striatulum, commonly known as the striped smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. Its stems are often striped with alternating light and dark bands, giving it its common name.

Polygonum striatulum thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas.
