Pavetta indica var. typica

Pavetta indica var. typica, commonly known as the Indian pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
