Pedicularis inaequilobata

Pedicularis inaequilobata, commonly known as the unequal-lipped monkey orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Orobanchaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that resemble a monkey's face. The plant is hemiparasitic, meaning it partially relies on host plants for nutrients through its root system. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive lower lip that is longer than the upper lip.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
