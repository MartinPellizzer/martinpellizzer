Passiflora gardneri

Passiflora gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a unique arrangement of petals and sepals. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Passiflora gardneri is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
