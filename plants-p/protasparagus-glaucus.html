Protasparagus glaucus

Protasparagus glaucus, commonly known as the blue-green asparagus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, leafless stems that often appear blue-green due to a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are scale-like and tightly pressed against the stems, contributing to its unique appearance.

It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.
