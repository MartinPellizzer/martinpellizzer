Phleum pratense subsp. hubbardii

Phleum pratense subsp.

hubbardii, commonly known as Hubbard's timothy, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically green to purplish in color, and it blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
