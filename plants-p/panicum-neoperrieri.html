Panicum neoperrieri

Panicum neoperrieri, commonly known as the Perrier grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Madagascar and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Panicum neoperrieri is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
