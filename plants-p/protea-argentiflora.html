Protea argentiflora

Protea argentiflora, commonly known as the silver-flowered protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads, which are surrounded by a dense, woolly involucre. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in fynbos vegetation. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

Protea argentiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
