Paphiopedilum nigritum

Paphiopedilum nigritum, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and its striking, dark purple to black flowers with a distinctive, intricate lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom during the rainy season.

Paphiopedilum nigritum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
