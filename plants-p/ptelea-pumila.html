Ptelea pumila

Ptelea pumila, commonly known as the smoothleaf ptelea, is a small shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-green leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Ptelea pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
