Phaca plattensis

Phaca plattensis, commonly known as the Plattens Island fern, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or partially emergent. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, producing spores for propagation.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Phaca plattensis is considered endangered in many areas.
