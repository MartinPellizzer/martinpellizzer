Paspalum ovatum

Paspalum ovatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces pale to dark green leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Paspalum ovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
