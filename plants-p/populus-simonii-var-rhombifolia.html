Populus simonii var. rhombifolia

Populus simonii var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped to rhomboid leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow catkins in early spring, which are typical of the Populus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and lowlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
