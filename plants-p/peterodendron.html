Peterodendron

Peterodendron, commonly known as the "honey tree," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in dry, arid environments. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, compound leaves, and clusters of small, yellow or white flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The Peterodendron is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
