Panicum insigne

Panicum insigne, commonly known as the showy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from golden yellow to reddish-brown. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along creek banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced during the growing season.

Panicum insigne is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
