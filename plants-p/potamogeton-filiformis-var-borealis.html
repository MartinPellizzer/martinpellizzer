Potamogeton filiformis var. borealis

Potamogeton filiformis var. borealis, commonly known as the northern filament grass, is a submerged aquatic plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender, thread-like leaves that are often ribbon-shaped, aiding in its identification. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for underwater pollination.

This variety plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
