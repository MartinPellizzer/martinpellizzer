Pleurothallis pariaensis

Pleurothallis pariaensis, commonly known as the Paria orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in the rainforests of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact inflorescence that produces numerous tiny blooms, making it a striking yet subtle addition to botanical collections.
