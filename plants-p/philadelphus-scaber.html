Philadelphus scaber

Philadelphus scaber, commonly known as the smooth mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. These flowers are often followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds.

Philadelphus scaber is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
