Prunus incisa var. kinkiensis

Prunus incisa var. kinkiensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Kinki cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, branching form and is prized for its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin, and turn a vibrant red in the autumn. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its role in traditional Japanese culture and as a symbol of renewal and beauty.
