Placus polycephalus

Placus polycephalus, commonly known as the multi-headed moss, is a unique species of moss characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant exhibits a complex structure with multiple small, clustered shoots that give it its distinctive "multi-headed" appearance. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on specialized stems called setae, which release spores into the air.

This moss plays a vital role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its ecosystem.
