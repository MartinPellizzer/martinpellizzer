Passiflora gritensis

Passiflora gritensis, commonly known as the Gritensis passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
