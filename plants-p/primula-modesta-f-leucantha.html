Primula modesta f. leucantha

Primula modesta f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered primrose, is a unique variant of the Primula modesta species.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which stand out against its dark green foliage. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The foliage is typically broad and heart-shaped, with a textured surface that adds to its ornamental appeal.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty and ability to bloom in early spring.
