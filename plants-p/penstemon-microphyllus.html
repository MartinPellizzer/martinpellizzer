Penstemon microphyllus

Penstemon microphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

Penstemon microphyllus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
