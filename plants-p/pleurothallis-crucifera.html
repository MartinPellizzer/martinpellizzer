Pleurothallis crucifera

Pleurothallis crucifera, commonly known as the crucifix orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has thin, wiry stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are characterized by their cross-shaped appearance, giving it its common name.

Pleurothallis crucifera is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
