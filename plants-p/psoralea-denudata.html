Psoralea denudata

Psoralea denudata, commonly known as the naked psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually pinnately compound, with small, linear leaflets.

Psoralea denudata is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.
