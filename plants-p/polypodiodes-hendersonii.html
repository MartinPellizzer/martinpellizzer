Polypodiodes hendersonii

Polypodiodes hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's polypodiodes, is a rare fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive in high humidity. Its spores are dispersed via wind, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction, producing sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered threatened and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
