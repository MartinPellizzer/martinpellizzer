Polypodium amoenum var. xerophyticum

Polypodium amoenum var. xerophyticum, commonly known as the desert huperzia, is a terrestrial fern that thrives in arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are adapted to conserve moisture. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and establish colonies in harsh conditions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in regions with low rainfall and high temperatures.
