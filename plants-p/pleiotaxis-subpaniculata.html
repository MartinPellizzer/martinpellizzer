Pleiotaxis subpaniculata

Pleiotaxis subpaniculata, commonly known as the panicle lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence composed of numerous small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
