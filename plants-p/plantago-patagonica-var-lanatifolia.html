Plantago patagonica var. lanatifolia

Plantago patagonica var. lanatifolia, commonly known as the Patagonian plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces erect flowering stems with dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and broad leaves make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
