Pinalia globifera

Pinalia globifera, commonly known as the globe-fig, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody structure and produces small, spherical fruits that are often green when mature. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
