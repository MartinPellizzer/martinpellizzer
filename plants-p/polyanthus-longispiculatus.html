Polyanthus longispiculatus

Polyanthus longispiculatus, commonly known as the long-spined bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Polyanthus longispiculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
