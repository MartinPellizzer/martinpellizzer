Paronychia mexicana subsp. monandra

Paronychia mexicana subsp.

monandra, commonly known as the Mexican sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
