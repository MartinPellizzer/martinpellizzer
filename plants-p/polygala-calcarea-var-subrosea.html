Polygala calcarea var. subrosea

Polygala calcarea var. subrosea, commonly known as the pink-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often pinkish stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale pink to rose-colored, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
