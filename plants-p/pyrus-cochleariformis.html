Pyrus cochleariformis

Pyrus cochleariformis, commonly known as the cochlea-shaped pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling, cochlea-like fruit, which is unique among pear species. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated margins.

It typically grows in moist, temperate forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
