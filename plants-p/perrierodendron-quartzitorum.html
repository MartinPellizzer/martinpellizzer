Perrierodendron quartzitorum

Perrierodendron quartzitorum, commonly known as the quartzwood tree, is a unique plant species characterized by its crystalline bark that resembles quartz.

It thrives in arid environments, where it has adapted to store water in its thick, fibrous roots. The plant's leaves are small and waxy, reducing water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are pale blue and bloom only during brief periods of rainfall.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and potential medicinal properties.
