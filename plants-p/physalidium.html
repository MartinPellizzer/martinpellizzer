Physalidium

Physalidium, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive, inflated, balloon-like fruits that contain seeds. The leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals and a superior ovary.

Physalidium species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing as vines or shrubs.
