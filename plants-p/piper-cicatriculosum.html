Piper cicatriculosum

Piper cicatriculosum, commonly known as the scarred pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elliptical berries that turn dark when mature.

The plant has limited cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements and is primarily found in its native habitat.
