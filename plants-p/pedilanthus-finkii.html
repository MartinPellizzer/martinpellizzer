Pedilanthus finkii

Pedilanthus finkii, commonly known as the yellow butterfly plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces a fragrant, nectar-rich flower that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
