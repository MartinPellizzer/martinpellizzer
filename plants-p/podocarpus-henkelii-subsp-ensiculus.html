Podocarpus henkelii subsp. ensiculus

Podocarpus henkelii subsp.

ensiculus, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and distinctive yellowish-green leaves.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain edible, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and used in reforestation projects.
