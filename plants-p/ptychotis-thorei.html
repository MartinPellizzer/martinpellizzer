Ptychotis thorei

Ptychotis thorei, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Ptychotis thorei is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
