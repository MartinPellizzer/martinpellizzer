Phialanthus spicatus

Phialanthus spicatus, commonly known as the spiked phialanthus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in regions with suitable climates.
