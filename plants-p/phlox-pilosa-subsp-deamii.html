Phlox pilosa subsp. deamii

Phlox pilosa subsp.

deamii, commonly known as the Deam's fuzzy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shady areas. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
