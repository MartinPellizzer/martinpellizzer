Prunus × donarium subsp. elegans

Prunus × donarium subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia. It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its slender, graceful branches and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
