Pedicularis × glantschnigiana

Pedicularis × glantschnigiana, commonly known as the Alpine crowfoot, is a hybrid species within the genus Pedicularis, native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal and lance-shaped, often with a reddish hue, while its stems are slender and erect.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
