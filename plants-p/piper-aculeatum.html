Piper aculeatum

Piper aculeatum, commonly known as the curly hair vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and vertical gardens.
