Plantago atrata var. spadicea

Plantago atrata var. spadicea, commonly known as the pale plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically borne on tall, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wet meadows.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
