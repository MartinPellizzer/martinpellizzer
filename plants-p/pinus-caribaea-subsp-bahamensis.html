Pinus caribaea subsp. bahamensis

Pinus caribaea subsp.

bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian pine, is a species of conifer native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas and parts of Cuba. It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of two, and it produces cones that contain seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
