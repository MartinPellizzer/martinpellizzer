Paepalanthus supinus

Paepalanthus supinus, commonly known as the creeping sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the sunflower family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Paepalanthus supinus thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.
