Padus wilsonii

Padus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive samaras that aid in seed dispersal. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and clusters of fruit that mature in late summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
