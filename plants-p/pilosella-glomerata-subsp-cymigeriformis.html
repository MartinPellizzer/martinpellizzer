Pilosella glomerata subsp. cymigeriformis

Pilosella glomerata subsp.

cymigeriformis, commonly known as the clustered goatherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine pubescence. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family, with a unique floral structure and growth pattern.
