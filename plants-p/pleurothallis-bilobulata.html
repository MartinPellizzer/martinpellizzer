Pleurothallis bilobulata

Pleurothallis bilobulata, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters and bloom during the wet season.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
