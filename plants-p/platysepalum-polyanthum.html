Platysepalum polyanthum

Platysepalum polyanthum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has palm-like leaves that are palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
