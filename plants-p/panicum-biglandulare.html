Panicum biglandulare

Panicum biglandulare, commonly known as the broad-leaved panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its coarse, serrated leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the grass family, Poaceae.
