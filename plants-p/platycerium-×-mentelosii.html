Platycerium × mentelosii

Platycerium × mentelosii, commonly known as the hybrid fern-fern or hybrid staghorn fern, is a unique hybrid species resulting from the cross between Platycerium bifidum and Platycerium superbum.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, featuring a tree-like appearance with a central trunk and fronds that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically on trees in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are leathery and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, providing a striking visual display.

This hybrid is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled environments.
