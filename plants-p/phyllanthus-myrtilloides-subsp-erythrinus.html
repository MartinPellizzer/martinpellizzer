Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp. erythrinus

Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp.

erythrinus, commonly known as the red-fruited spurge, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are a notable feature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry or disturbed soils.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
