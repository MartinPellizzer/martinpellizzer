Phaca intonsa

Phaca intonsa, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique and rare plant species native to the humid forests of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom primarily at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its often-dry habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Phaca intonsa is considered endangered and is the subject of ongoing conservation efforts.
