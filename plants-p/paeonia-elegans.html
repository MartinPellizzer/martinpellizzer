Paeonia elegans

Paeonia elegans, commonly known as the Chinese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink and red, often with a distinctive double bloom. The plant has palmately compound leaves with 5-7 leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in a thick, reddish-brown bark. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that are highly valued in horticulture.

Paeonia elegans is also notable for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and partial shade.
