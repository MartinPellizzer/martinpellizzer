Pyrethrum kasakhstanicum

Pyrethrum kasakhstanicum, commonly known as the Kazakh pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, spherical flower heads that contain numerous fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. These trichomes contain natural insecticides called pyrethrins, which are used in organic pest control.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.
