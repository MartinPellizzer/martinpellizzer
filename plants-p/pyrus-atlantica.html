Pyrus atlantica

Pyrus atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic pear, is a species of pear native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of white flowers in spring. Its fruit is small, usually less than 2 cm in diameter, and has a tough, bitter skin.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
