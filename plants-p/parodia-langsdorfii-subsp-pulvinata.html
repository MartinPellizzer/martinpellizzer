Parodia langsdorfii subsp. pulvinata

Parodia langsdorfii subsp.

pulvinata, commonly known as the "Pulvinata Cactus," is a compact, globular cactus native to southern Africa. It features a ribbed, spiny stem with a distinctive swollen base, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

It is prized for its unique shape and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
