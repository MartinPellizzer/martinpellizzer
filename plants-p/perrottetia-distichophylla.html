Perrottetia distichophylla

Perrottetia distichophylla, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in mountainous areas. The plant features showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
