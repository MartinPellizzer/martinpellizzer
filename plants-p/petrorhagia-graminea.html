Petrorhagia graminea

Petrorhagia graminea, commonly known as the grass-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Petrorhagia graminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
