Phyteuma lycium

Phyteuma lycium, commonly known as the red poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.

Phyteuma lycium is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
