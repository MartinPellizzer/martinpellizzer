Pimpinella major

Pimpinella major, commonly known as greater cow-weed, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance, and it produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

Pimpinella major is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
