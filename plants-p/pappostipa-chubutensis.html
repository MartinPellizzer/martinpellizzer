Pappostipa chubutensis

Pappostipa chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut grass, is a flowering plant native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and high solar radiation.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
