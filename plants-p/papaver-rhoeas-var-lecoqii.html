Papaver rhoeas var. lecoqii

Papaver rhoeas var. lecoqii, commonly known as the Lecoq's poppy, is a variant of the common poppy, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features showy, bright red petals that are typically arranged in a radiate pattern, with a central stigma that is prominent and often yellow or orange in color. The plant is an annual herb with a short, stout stem and leaves that are usually lobed and somewhat hairy. It produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also found in various regions with suitable climates.
