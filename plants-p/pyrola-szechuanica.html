Pyrola szechuanica

Pyrola szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its low-growing habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments, typically found in coniferous or mixed forests.

Pyrola szechuanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in woodland gardens for its delicate appearance.
