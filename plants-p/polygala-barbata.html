Polygala barbata

Polygala barbata, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, showy flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
