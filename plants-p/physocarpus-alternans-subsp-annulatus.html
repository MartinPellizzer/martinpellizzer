Physocarpus alternans subsp. annulatus

Physocarpus alternans subsp.

annulatus, commonly known as the coralberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its clusters of bright red berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy fruit display.
