Phragmipedium × dominianum

Phragmipedium × dominianum, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phragmipedium laevis and Phragmipedium sanderianum.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its fleshy, dark green leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant produces large, slipper-shaped blooms in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
