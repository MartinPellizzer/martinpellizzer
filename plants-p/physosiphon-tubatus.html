Physosiphon tubatus

Physosiphon tubatus, commonly known as the tubular spongeplant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive tubular structures.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant's morphology includes a central axis with branching, spongy tubules that aid in gas exchange and nutrient absorption. Its reproductive structures are often hidden within the tubular formations, making it less visible to the naked eye.

Physosiphon tubatus plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering suspended particles and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
