Poa venosa

Poa venosa, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.

Poa venosa is a hardy plant that is relatively easy to grow in gardens with adequate sunlight and water.
