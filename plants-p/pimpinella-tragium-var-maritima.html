Pimpinella tragium var. maritima

Pimpinella tragium var. maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of coastal flora.
