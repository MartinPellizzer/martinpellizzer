Philotheca sericea

Philotheca sericea, commonly known as silky philotheca, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often found in dry sclerophyllous forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
