Polygala comosa subsp. provincialis

Polygala comosa subsp.

provincialis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are visited by various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the nominate form of Polygala comosa.
