Piper xylopioides

Piper xylopioides, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or guaco, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic leaves and its role in local ecosystems and herbal practices.
