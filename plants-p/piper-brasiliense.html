Piper brasiliense

Piper brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

Piper brasiliense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
