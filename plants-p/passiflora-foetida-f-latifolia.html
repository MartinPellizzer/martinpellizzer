Passiflora foetida f. latifolia

Passiflora foetida f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved passionflower, is a unique variant of the passionflower genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and striking, intricate flowers with a mix of purple, blue, and white petals. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
