Parapiptadenia pterosperma

Parapiptadenia pterosperma, commonly known as the Brazilian pequi tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of fleshy, orange-colored fruits.

The seeds inside the fruits are encased in a hard shell and are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
