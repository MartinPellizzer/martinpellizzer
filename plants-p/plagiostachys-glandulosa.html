Plagiostachys glandulosa

Plagiostachys glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are often surrounded by glandular structures, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly rough texture.

Plagiostachys glandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
