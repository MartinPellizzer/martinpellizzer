Polypodium irregulare

Polypodium irregulare, commonly known as the irregular huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of rhizomes. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The fronds are usually leathery and have a dark green color with a rough texture.

Polypodium irregulare is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
