Pentadenia spathulata

Pentadenia spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pittosporum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which appear in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Pentadenia spathulata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
