Polygala chacoensis

Polygala chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands, thriving in arid or semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are often pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala chacoensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its harsh environment.
