Papaver dubium proles collinum

Papaver dubium proles collinum, commonly known as the creeping poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, with a distinctive dark center. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats.
