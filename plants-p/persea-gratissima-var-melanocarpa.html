Persea gratissima var. melanocarpa

Persea gratissima var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It produces a dark, smooth-skinned fruit that turns black when ripe, containing a creamy, sweet pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season. Its leaves are large, alternate, and ovate with a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
