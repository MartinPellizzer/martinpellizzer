Paphiopedilum vittatum

Paphiopedilum vittatum, commonly known as the streaked lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, hairy stem and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive pouch-shaped lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, helping to retain moisture.

The orchid's unique morphology and elegant appearance make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
