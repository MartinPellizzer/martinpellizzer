Pontederia gigantea

Pontederia gigantea, commonly known as the giant water hyacinth, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, often with a waxy coating that aids in buoyancy. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats that can impact water flow and aquatic life.

Due to its rapid growth rate, it is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
