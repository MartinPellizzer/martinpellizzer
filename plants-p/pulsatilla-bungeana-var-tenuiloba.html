Pulsatilla bungeana var. tenuiloba

Pulsatilla bungeana var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, woolly sepals. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates, often blooming in late spring. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
