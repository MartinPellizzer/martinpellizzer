Prunus serrulata f. affinis

Prunus serrulata f. affinis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a stunning visual display. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, giving it the species name "serrulata.". This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and public spaces.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
