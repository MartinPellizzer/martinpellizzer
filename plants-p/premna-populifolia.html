Premna populifolia

Premna populifolia, commonly known as the Indian nettle or white-flowered premna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Premna populifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.
