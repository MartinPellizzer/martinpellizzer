Picea excelsa f. leucogyna

Picea excelsa f. leucogyna, commonly known as the white-leaf white spruce, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white spruce species.

It is characterized by its pale, often silvery-white foliage, which sets it apart from the typical green needles of the species. This form is native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant exhibits a conical shape with dense, compact growth, making it well-suited to its alpine environment.

Its unique coloration and habitat make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
