Potentilla matsuokana subsp. crebridens

Potentilla matsuokana subsp.

crebridens, commonly known as the broadleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage of oval to elliptic leaves.

The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually clustered in corymbs, and the species is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.

This subspecies is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
