Prunus domestica f. plantieri

Prunus domestica f. plantieri, commonly known as the Turkish cherry or the large-fruited cherry, is a botanical variety of the domesticated cherry.

It is characterized by its large, sweet, and juicy berries that are typically dark red to black in color. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to certain diseases compared to other Prunus species.
