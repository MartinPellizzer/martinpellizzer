Pectinopitys ferruginea

Pectinopitys ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty pine, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its botanical features include a tall, straight trunk and a conical crown, typical of many pine species.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays a significant role in its ecosystem.
