Pleurothallis brenneri

Pleurothallis brenneri, commonly known as the Brenner's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a compact rosette, helping it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis brenneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
