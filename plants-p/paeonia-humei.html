Paeonia humei

Paeonia humei, commonly known as the Hume's peony, is a species of herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with large, palmately divided leaves. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Paeonia humei is valued for both its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
