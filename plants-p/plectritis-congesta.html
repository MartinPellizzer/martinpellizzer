Plectritis congesta

Plectritis congesta, commonly known as the clustered pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Plectritis congesta is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
