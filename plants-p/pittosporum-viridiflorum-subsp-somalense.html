Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp. somalense

Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp.

somalense, commonly known as the Somali greenflower pittosporum, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Somalia. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
