Paepalanthus canescens f. angustifolia

Paepalanthus canescens f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved puya, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
