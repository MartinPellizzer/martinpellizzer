Placus chinensis

Placus chinensis, commonly known as Chinese placus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Placaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, while its flowers are often white or pale pink.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
