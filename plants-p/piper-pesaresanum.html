Piper pesaresanum

Piper pesaresanum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are harvested when mature for their aromatic and spicy properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.
