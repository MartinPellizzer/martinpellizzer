Phacelia humilis var. lobata

Phacelia humilis var. lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
