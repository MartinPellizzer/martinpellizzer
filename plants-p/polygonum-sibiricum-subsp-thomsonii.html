Polygonum sibiricum subsp. thomsonii

Polygonum sibiricum subsp.

thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
