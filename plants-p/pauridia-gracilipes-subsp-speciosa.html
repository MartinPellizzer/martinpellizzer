Pauridia gracilipes subsp. speciosa

Pauridia gracilipes subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the slender beauty bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
