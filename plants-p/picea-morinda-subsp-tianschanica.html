Picea morinda subsp. tianschanica

Picea morinda subsp.

tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan fir, is a coniferous tree native to the Tianshan mountain range in Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches.

The cones are typically small and cylindrical, with persistent bracts that give them a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to poor, well-drained soils.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
