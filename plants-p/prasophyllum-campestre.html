Prasophyllum campestre

Prasophyllum campestre, commonly known as the field leek, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, white or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize new areas.

Prasophyllum campestre is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.
