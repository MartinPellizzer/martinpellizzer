Pennellia tricornuta

Pennellia tricornuta, commonly known as the three-lobed pennywort, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Pennellia tricornuta is often found growing along the edges of water bodies, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
