Primula × legueana

Primula × legueana, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with clustered leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
