Pleurandra cinerea

Pleurandra cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf pleurandra, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Pleurandra cinerea prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
