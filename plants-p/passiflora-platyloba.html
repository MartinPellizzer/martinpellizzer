Passiflora platyloba

Passiflora platyloba, commonly known as the flat-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central reproductive structure. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora platyloba is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
