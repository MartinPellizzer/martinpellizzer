Penstemon deustus var. suffrutescens

Penstemon deustus var. suffrutescens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale lavender to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowering structure is adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, with a central cone-like cluster of stamens.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.
