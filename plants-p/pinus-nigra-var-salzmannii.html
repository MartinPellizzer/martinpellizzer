Pinus nigra var. salzmannii

Pinus nigra var. salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's pine, is a variant of the black pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 3 centimeters long. The plant produces cones that are usually 5 to 8 centimeters in length, containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
