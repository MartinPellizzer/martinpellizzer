Pourouma velutina

Pourouma velutina, commonly known as the velvet areca, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a tall, slender palm that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small coconuts.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
