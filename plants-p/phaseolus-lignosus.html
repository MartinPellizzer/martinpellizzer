Phaseolus lignosus

Phaseolus lignosus, commonly known as the wood bean, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
