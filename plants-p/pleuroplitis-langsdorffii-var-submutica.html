Pleuroplitis langsdorffii var. submutica

Pleuroplitis langsdorffii var. submutica, commonly known as the rough-leaved pleuroplitis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy pubescence, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
