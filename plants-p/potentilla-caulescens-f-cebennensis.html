Potentilla caulescens f. cebennensis

Potentilla caulescens f. cebennensis, commonly known as the Cebenna potentilla, is a variant of the shrub Potentilla caulescens.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
