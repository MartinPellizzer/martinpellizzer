Pueraria bicalcarata

Pueraria bicalcarata, commonly known as Kudzu, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a vigorous vine that can grow rapidly, often reaching lengths of several meters. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale purple flowers. Its roots are thick and tuberous, which store nutrients and contribute to its aggressive growth.

Pueraria bicalcarata is also known for its role in traditional medicine and as a potential invasive species in certain regions.
