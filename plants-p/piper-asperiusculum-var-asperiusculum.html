Piper asperiusculum var. asperiusculum

Piper asperiusculum var. asperiusculum, commonly known as the rough piper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a rough, pubescent texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, followed by three-lobed berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
