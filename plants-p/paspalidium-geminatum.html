Paspalidium geminatum

Paspalidium geminatum, commonly known as the twinleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalidium geminatum is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
