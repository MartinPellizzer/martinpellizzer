Prunella cordata

Prunella cordata, commonly known as heartleaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It typically grows in moist, woodland areas and thrives in partial shade.

Prunella cordata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
