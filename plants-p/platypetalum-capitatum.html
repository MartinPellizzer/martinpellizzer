Platypetalum capitatum

Platypetalum capitatum, commonly known as the golden cup tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small cups, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has palm-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Platypetalum capitatum is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
