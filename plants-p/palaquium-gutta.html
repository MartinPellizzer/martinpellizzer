Palaquium gutta

Palaquium gutta, commonly known as the gutta tree, is a species of tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its latex, known as gutta-percha, is a natural rubber-like substance used historically in various industrial applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical forests.
