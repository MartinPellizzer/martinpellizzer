Paua

Paua, commonly known as the New Zealand sea snail, is a marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Hippospondidae.

It is characterized by its large, spiral-shaped shell, which can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The shell is often adorned with intricate patterns and a smooth, glossy surface. Paua are found in rocky intertidal zones and shallow coastal waters around New Zealand.

They play an important ecological role in their habitat and are also valued for their culinary and cultural significance.
