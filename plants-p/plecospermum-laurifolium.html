Plecospermum laurifolium

Plecospermum laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf plecospermum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
