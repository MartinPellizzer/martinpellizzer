Physurus vesicifer

Physurus vesicifer, commonly known as the bladderleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant's name refers to the inflated, bladder-like structures on its spore cases, which aid in spore dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The botanical features of Physurus vesicifer make it a unique and interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
