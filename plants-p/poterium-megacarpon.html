Poterium megacarpon

Poterium megacarpon, commonly known as the giant bladder nut.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its unique, inflated fruit that resembles a bladder. The plant produces large, woody capsules that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Poterium megacarpon is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
