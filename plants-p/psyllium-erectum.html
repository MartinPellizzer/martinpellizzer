Psyllium erectum

Psyllium erectum, commonly known as the common psyllium, is a flowering plant in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a mucilaginous substance when its seeds are soaked in water, which is used in various culinary and medicinal applications. Psyllium erectum is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.

Its fibrous seed husks are valued for their high content of soluble fiber.
