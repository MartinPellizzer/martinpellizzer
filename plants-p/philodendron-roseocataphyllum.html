Philodendron roseocataphyllum

Philodendron roseocataphyllum, commonly known as the rose-veined philodendron, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often feature a unique rose-colored vein pattern, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is glossy and can reach several feet in length, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to flourish.
