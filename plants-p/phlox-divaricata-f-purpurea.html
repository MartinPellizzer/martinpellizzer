Phlox divaricata f. purpurea

Phlox divaricata f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple wild phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
