Pilocereus nigricans

Pilocereus nigricans, commonly known as the black night beauty, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black spines and nocturnal blooming habit, producing large, fragrant white flowers. The plant has a ribbed, cylindrical stem that stores water, typical of desert plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
