Phlox carolina subsp. altissima

Phlox carolina subsp.

altissima, commonly known as tall meadow phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
