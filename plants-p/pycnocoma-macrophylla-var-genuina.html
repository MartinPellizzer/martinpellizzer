Pycnocoma macrophylla var. genuina

Pycnocoma macrophylla var. genuina, commonly known as the giant pycnocoma, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
