Plantago arborescens subsp. arborescens

Plantago arborescens subsp.

arborescens, commonly known as the tree plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the ends of its branches. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

This species is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
