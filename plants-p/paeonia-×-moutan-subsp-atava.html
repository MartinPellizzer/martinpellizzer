Paeonia × moutan subsp. atava

Paeonia × moutan subsp.

atava, commonly known as the Chinese tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow.

The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces woody stems that can grow up to several meters in height. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.

The subspecies is closely related to other peony species and is part of the Paeoniaceae family.
