Poa chaixii var. rubens

Poa chaixii var. rubens, commonly known as the red-stemmed bentgrass, is a variant of the bentgrass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish coloration in the stems and leaves, which becomes more pronounced under certain environmental conditions. This grass thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks. Its compact growth habit and fine texture make it a desirable plant for ornamental purposes in gardens.

The variety is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
