Phaseolus umbellatus

Phaseolus umbellatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers that range from white to pink and red. The plant produces elongated, green pods that contain edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and a fibrous root system.

Phaseolus umbellatus is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible yield.
