Piper augustum

Piper augustum, commonly known as the wild pepper plant, is a flowering shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and can be used to make a spicy seasoning similar to black pepper.
