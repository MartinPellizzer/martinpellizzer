Phyllanthus omahakensis var. cuanavalensis

Phyllanthus omahakensis var. cuanavalensis, commonly known as the Cuanavalan phyllanthus, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
