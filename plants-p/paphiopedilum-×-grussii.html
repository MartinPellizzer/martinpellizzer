Paphiopedilum × grussii

Paphiopedilum × grussii, commonly known as the Grussii orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive lip that often features a deep red or orange hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Paphiopedilum × grussii is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively ease of cultivation.
