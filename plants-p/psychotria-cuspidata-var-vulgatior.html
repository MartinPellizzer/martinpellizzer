Psychotria cuspidata var. vulgatior

Psychotria cuspidata var. vulgatior, commonly known as the red-eyed wonder, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers with contrasting white markings, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant is an evergreen shrub that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, while its stems are woody and somewhat succulent.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in shaded gardens.
