Primulina minutihamata

Primulina minutihamata, commonly known as the miniature primula, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor gardens.
