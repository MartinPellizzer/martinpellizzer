Ptarmica cristata

Ptarmica cristata, commonly known as the crowned bedstraw, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces tiny, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Ptarmica cristata is known for its ornamental value and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
