Paspalum unispicatum

Paspalum unispicatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass or narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Paspalum unispicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
