Pothos drupaceus

Pothos drupaceus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a trailing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slight yellowish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It is a member of the Araceae family and is well-known for its adaptability to low-light conditions.

Its trailing growth habit makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor decoration.
