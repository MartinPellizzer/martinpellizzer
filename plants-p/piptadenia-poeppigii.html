Piptadenia poeppigii

Piptadenia poeppigii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink araçá, is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The tree also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil fertility.
