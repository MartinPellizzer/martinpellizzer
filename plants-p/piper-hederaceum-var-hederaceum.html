Piper hederaceum var. hederaceum

Piper hederaceum var. hederaceum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody stem covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
