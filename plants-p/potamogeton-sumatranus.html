Potamogeton sumatranus

Potamogeton sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Its presence is important for providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
