Parnassia palustris var. palustris

Parnassia palustris var. palustris, commonly known as the marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and marshes.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
