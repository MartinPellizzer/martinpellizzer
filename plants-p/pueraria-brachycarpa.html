Pueraria brachycarpa

Pueraria brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green pods that mature into brown, spherical fruits. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used for forage and soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique fruit structure and its role in nitrogen fixation.
