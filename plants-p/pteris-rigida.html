Pteris rigida

Pteris rigida, commonly known as the hard fern, is a species of fern characterized by its rigid, leathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to various regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and the Indian subcontinent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive, upright growth habit that distinguishes it from other fern species.

Pteris rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
