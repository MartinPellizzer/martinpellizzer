Plectronia rhamnoides

Plectronia rhamnoides, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.
