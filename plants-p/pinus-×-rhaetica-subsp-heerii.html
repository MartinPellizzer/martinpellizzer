Pinus × rhaetica subsp. heerii

Pinus × rhaetica subsp.

heerii, commonly known as the Rhaetian pine, is a subspecies of the hybrid pine that exhibits distinct botanical features. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically longer and more slender compared to other pine species.

The cones of this subspecies are generally smaller and more compact, with a reddish-brown coloration. It thrives in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and cooler climates.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to high-altitude environments, making it a notable species in alpine ecosystems.
