Palmijuncus tigrinus

Palmijuncus tigrinus, commonly known as the tiger palm, is a unique and rare plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, striped leaves that resemble the pattern of a tiger's coat, giving it its common name. The plant is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes other well-known palms, but it possesses distinct morphological features that set it apart. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments and has a slow growth rate, making it a prized specimen in botanical gardens.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, conservation efforts are essential to ensure its survival.
