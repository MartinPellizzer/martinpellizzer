Prunus sachalinensis f. floribunda

Prunus sachalinensis f. floribunda, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry or Sakhalin weeping cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its weeping habit, with long, cascading branches that create a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or black berries in late summer. Its foliage is typically dark green, turning red or orange in autumn, adding seasonal interest.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
