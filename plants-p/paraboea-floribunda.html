Paraboea floribunda

Paraboea floribunda, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of yellow-orange, bell-shaped flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Paraboea floribunda is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
