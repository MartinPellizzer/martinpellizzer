Potentilla canadensis f. ochroleuca

Potentilla canadensis f. ochroleuca, commonly known as the whiteflower cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a variant of the common cinquefoil, distinguished by its pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. It has a low, spreading growth habit with compound leaves and produces small, showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
