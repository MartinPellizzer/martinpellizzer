Prunus cerasus flore-albopleno

Prunus cerasus flore-albopleno, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a species of cherry tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The tree produces small, sweet fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features include simple leaves and a compact growth habit, suitable for both ornamental and edible purposes.
