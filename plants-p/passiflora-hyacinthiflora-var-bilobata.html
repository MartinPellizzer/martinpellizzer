Passiflora hyacinthiflora var. bilobata

Passiflora hyacinthiflora var. bilobata, commonly known as the bilobed passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a deep green color. This variety is noted for its unique floral structure, which includes a distinctive, bi-lobed appearance at the base of the flower.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
