Pterolepis alpestris var. imbricata

Pterolepis alpestris var. imbricata, commonly known as the Alpine pterolepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, overlapping leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The leaves are often arranged in a distinctive imbricate pattern, giving the plant its varietal name. It produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
