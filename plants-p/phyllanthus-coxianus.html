Phyllanthus coxianus

Phyllanthus coxianus, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by distinctive star-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
