Picea morindoides

Picea morindoides, commonly known as the Morindoid spruce, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, dark green foliage that forms a conical shape. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of conifers. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Picea morindoides is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
