Primula knorringiana

Primula knorringiana, commonly known as the Knorring's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and Nepal.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula knorringiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
