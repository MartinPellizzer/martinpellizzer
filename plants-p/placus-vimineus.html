Placus vimineus

Placus vimineus, commonly known as the creeping ivy, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Placus vimineus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover ground quickly.
