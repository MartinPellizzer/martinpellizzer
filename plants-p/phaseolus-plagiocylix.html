Phaseolus plagiocylix

Phaseolus plagiocylix, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a climbing legume that produces elongated, edible pods with a mild, sweet flavor. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional cuisine and can be dried and stored for long periods.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
