Proustia pyrifolia var. canescens

Proustia pyrifolia var. canescens, commonly known as the ash-leaf pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescences composed of small, fluffy white flowers that resemble pincushions. The leaves are typically ash-gray in color, giving the plant its common name, and are often covered in a fine, pubescent layer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in arid regions for its drought tolerance.
