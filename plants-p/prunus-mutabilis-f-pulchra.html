Prunus mutabilis f. pulchra

Prunus mutabilis f. pulchra, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces clusters of white or pink double flowers in spring. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and pruning for optimal growth.

The leaves turn red in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal.
