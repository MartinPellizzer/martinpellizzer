Polygala chapadensis

Polygala chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature and can be toxic to some animals. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
