Pelargonium rhodanthum

Pelargonium rhodanthum, commonly known as the red-stemmed geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated edge and a velvety texture.

Pelargonium rhodanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
