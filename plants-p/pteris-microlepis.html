Pteris microlepis

Pteris microlepis, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The fronds are typically divided into pinnae that are narrow and closely spaced, giving the plant a dense appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
