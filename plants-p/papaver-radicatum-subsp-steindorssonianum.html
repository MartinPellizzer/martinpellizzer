Papaver radicatum subsp. steindorssonianum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

steindorssonianum, commonly known as the Icelandic poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Iceland. It features striking blue to violet flowers with prominent yellow centers, typical of the Papaver genus.

The plant has a short, stout stem with basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy. It thrives in rocky and coastal environments, adapting to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the study of alpine and arctic flora.
