Pseuderanthemum viriduliflorum

Pseuderanthemum viriduliflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, tubular, and typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
