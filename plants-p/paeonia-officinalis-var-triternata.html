Paeonia officinalis var. triternata

Paeonia officinalis var. triternata, commonly known as the Turkish peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a distinctive red or purple eye. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically serrated and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.

The flowers are also used in perfumery and as a source of medicinal compounds.
