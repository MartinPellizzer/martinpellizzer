Panicum rariflorum

Panicum rariflorum, commonly known as the rareflower panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate inflorescences that produce numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced during the growing season.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
