Panax diffusus

Panax diffusus, commonly known as ginseng, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish root system, which is the primary part used in traditional medicine. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical name reflects its historical significance in herbal medicine.

Panax diffusus thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.
