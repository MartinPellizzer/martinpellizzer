Prunus triloba f. normalis

Prunus triloba f. normalis, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage, flowers, and seasonal fruit display.
