Pentaphylloides dahurica

Pentaphylloides dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features a creeping rhizome and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments.

The botanical characteristics of Pentaphylloides dahurica make it an important species in ecological studies of cold-climate flora.
