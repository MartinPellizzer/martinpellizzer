Pelargonium scariosum

Pelargonium scariosum, commonly known as the scarious geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of pink to red, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
