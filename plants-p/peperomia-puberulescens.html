Peperomia puberulescens

Peperomia puberulescens, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia puberulescens is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
