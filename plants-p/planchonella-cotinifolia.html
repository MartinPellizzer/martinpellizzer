Planchonella cotinifolia

Planchonella cotinifolia, commonly known as the wild lime or kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and pinnate leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, green fruits that turn yellow when ripe. The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
