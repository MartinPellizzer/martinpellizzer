Pedicularis erecta

Pedicularis erecta, commonly known as the erect monkey orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble a monkey's face. The plant is an hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially depends on other plants for water and nutrients. It typically grows in alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular subject in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.
