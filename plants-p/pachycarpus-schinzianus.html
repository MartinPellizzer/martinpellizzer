Pachycarpus schinzianus

Pachycarpus schinzianus, commonly known as the Schinz's thick-fruit, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has woody stems and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The fruit is edible and has been used traditionally by local communities for its flavor and nutritional value.
