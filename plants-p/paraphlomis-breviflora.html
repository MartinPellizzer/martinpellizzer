Paraphlomis breviflora

Paraphlomis breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered paraphlomis, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
