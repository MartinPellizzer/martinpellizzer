Pleurothallis tetrapetala

Pleurothallis tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
