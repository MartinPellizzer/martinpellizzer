Phyllanthus assamicus

Phyllanthus assamicus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright orange to red berries. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
