Psephellus zuvandicus subsp.

zuvandicus, commonly known as the Zuvandic rockrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
