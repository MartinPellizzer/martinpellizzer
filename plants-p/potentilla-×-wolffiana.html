Potentilla × wolffiana

Potentilla × wolffiana, commonly known as the silver weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are covered in a distinctive silvery-gray bloom. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

It is also known for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates.
