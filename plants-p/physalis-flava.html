Physalis flava

Physalis flava, commonly known as the yellow husk tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow berries enclosed in a papery, inflated calyx. The fruit is edible when ripe, though it has a tart flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
