Plantago dilatata

Plantago dilatata, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in meadows, and in gardens.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized historically for skin ailments and digestive issues.
