Panicum monodactylum

Panicum monodactylum, commonly known as bloodgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive crimson-colored inflorescences that resemble grass-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the leaves often displaying a reddish hue.

Panicum monodactylum is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking visual appeal and is also valued for its ecological benefits.
