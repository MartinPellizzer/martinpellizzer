Pavetta crassipes var. major

Pavetta crassipes var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent stamen. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
