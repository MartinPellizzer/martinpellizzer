Pollalesta ferruginea

Pollalesta ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-hued orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown markings on its leaves and petals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a complex structure typical of orchids.

Pollalesta ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
