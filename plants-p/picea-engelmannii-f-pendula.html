Picea engelmannii f. pendula

Picea engelmannii f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a unique variant of the Engelmann spruce.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. This form is a natural mutation that occurs in certain populations of the species. The tree has needle-like leaves and produces cones that are typically found on the upper parts of the branches.

It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in mountainous regions of western North America.
