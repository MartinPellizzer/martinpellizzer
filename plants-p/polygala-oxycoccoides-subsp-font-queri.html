Polygala oxycoccoides subsp. font-queri

Polygala oxycoccoides subsp.

font-queri, commonly known as the Font-Queri's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color.

The plant produces small, bead-like fruits called follicles, which contain the seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and limestone soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Polygala genus, valued for its ecological and horticultural significance.
