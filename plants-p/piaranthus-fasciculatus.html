Piaranthus fasciculatus

Piaranthus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered grassbladder, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Piaranthus fasciculatus is characterized by its unique, bladder-like seed pods that contain the plant's distinctive seeds.
