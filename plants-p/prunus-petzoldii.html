Prunus petzoldii

Prunus petzoldii, commonly known as the Petzold cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Prunus petzoldii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to a range of environmental conditions.
