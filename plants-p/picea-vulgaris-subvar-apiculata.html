Picea vulgaris subvar. apiculata

Picea vulgaris subvar. apiculata, commonly known as the white spruce, is a coniferous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The cones are typically elongated and woody, containing two to three seeds. This subspecies is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
