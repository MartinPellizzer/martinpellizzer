Persica subcursonia

Persica subcursonia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peach.

It is a species of shrub or small tree native to parts of Central Asia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring. Its fruit is similar to the peach, with a fuzzy skin and sweet, juicy flesh.

Persica subcursonia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
