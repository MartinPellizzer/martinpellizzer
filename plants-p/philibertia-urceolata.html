Philibertia urceolata

Philibertia urceolata, commonly known as the cup-shaped filbert, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that form a cup-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers in summer, which are clustered in a terminal umbel. Its unique leaf morphology and flowering habit make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
