Philotheca buxifolia subsp. falcata

Philotheca buxifolia subsp.

falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ghostpipe, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Australia. It features small, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
