Polyactium multiradiatum

Polyactium multiradiatum, commonly known as the star-tipped polychaete, is a unique marine invertebrate belonging to the phylum Annelida.

It is characterized by its segmented body and the presence of numerous radiating bristles along its body segments. This species is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it plays a role in the benthic ecosystem. Its morphology includes a distinct head region with sensory structures and a body covered in setae for movement.

Due to its specialized habitat and ecological role, it is of interest to marine biologists studying biodiversity in coastal environments.
