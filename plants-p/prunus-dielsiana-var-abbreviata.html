Prunus dielsiana var. abbreviata

Prunus dielsiana var. abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact shrub native to China.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and hairy on the underside. The plant produces white to pink flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its fruits are small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its compact size and seasonal blooms.
