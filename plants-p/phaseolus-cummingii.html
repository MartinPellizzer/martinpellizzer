Phaseolus cummingii

Phaseolus cummingii, commonly known as the Mexican coffee bean or Cumming's bean, is a species of legume native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods. Its seeds are typically dark brown and have a mild, nutty flavor.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional culinary practices.
