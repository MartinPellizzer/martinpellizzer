Pouzolzia parasitica

Pouzolzia parasitica, commonly known as the parasitic pouzolzia, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, typically growing on the stems of other plants. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It is found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments.

Its parasitic nature makes it an interesting subject for studies on plant interactions and ecological dependencies.
