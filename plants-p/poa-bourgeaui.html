Poa bourgeaui

Poa bourgeaui, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane and subalpine regions. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are small and closely arranged.

Poa bourgeaui plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
