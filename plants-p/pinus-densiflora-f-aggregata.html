Pinus densiflora f. aggregata

Pinus densiflora f. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered Japanese red pine, is a variant of the Japanese red pine characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit.

This form exhibits compact foliage and shorter, more clustered branches compared to the typical species. It is native to southern Japan and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and landscapes.
