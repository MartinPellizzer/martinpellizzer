Passiflora suberosa var. minima

Passiflora suberosa var. minima, commonly known as the miniature passionflower, is a compact variant of the passionflower genus.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that resemble a crown. The plant produces a unique, fragrant bloom with a central cluster of filaments that resemble a crown of threads. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is particularly prized for its miniature size and vibrant floral display.
