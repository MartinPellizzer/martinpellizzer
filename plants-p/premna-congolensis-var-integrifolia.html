Premna congolensis var. integrifolia

Premna congolensis var. integrifolia, commonly known as the African nettle or African horse chestnut, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually entire or slightly notched. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
