Prunus jamasakura f. microflora

Prunus jamasakura f. microflora, commonly known as the dwarf cherry blossom, is a compact variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle yet beautiful display to gardens. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for smaller spaces. Its leaves are typically a vibrant green, providing a striking contrast to the pale pink blossoms.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and seasonal interest.
