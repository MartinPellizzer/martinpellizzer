Polygonum persicaria subsp. laxiflorum

Polygonum persicaria subsp.

laxiflorum, commonly known as looseflower smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its adaptability to wet soils and its role in supporting local insect populations.
