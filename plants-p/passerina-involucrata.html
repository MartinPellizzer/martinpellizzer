Passerina involucrata

Passerina involucrata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces bracts that form a distinctive, cone-shaped structure at the base of the flower head. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Passerina involucrata is also known for its medicinal properties and is a popular plant in native gardens.
