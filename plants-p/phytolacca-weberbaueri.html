Phytolacca weberbaueri

Phytolacca weberbaueri, commonly known as Weberbauer's pokeweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Phytolaccaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible when ripe but toxic when unripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.
