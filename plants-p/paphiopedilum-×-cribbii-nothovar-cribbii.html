Paphiopedilum × cribbii nothovar. cribbii

Paphiopedilum × cribbii nothovar. cribbii, commonly known as the Cribb's phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid that combines traits from Paphiopedilum and Phalaenopsis species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble moth wings, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces a single, upright stem with several blooms, and its leaves are broad and leathery. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting mix.

This nothovar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
