Parahebe lavaudiana

Parahebe lavaudiana, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
