Panicum taurinum

Panicum taurinum, commonly known as Taurine Panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and sandy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, panicle-shaped inflorescences that bloom in the summer. It is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
