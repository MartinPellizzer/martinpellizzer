Primula praticola

Primula praticola, commonly known as the meadow primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems with heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Primula praticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
