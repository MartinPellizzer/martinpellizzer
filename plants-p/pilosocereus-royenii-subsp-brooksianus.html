Pilosocereus royenii subsp. brooksianus

Pilosocereus royenii subsp.

brooksianus, commonly known as the Brooksianus cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
