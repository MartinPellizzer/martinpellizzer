Phyllospadix × choshiensis

Phyllospadix × choshiensis, commonly known as the Japanese sea holly, is a hybrid plant native to the coastal regions of Japan.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spathaceous inflorescence that resembles a crown of flowers. The plant typically grows in saline or brackish environments, such as tidal flats and estuaries, and is adapted to withstand fluctuating salinity levels. Its leaves are often fleshy and succulent, which helps it retain moisture in its often harsh coastal habitat.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
