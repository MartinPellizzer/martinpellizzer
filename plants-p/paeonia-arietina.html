Paeonia arietina

Paeonia arietina, commonly known as the Armenian peony, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features compound, trifoliate leaves and has a long, hairy stem that supports its blooms. Paeonia arietina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
