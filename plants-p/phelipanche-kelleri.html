Phelipanche kelleri

Phelipanche kelleri, commonly known as the Mediterranean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Phelipanche kelleri is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
