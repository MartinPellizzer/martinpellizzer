Phyllanthus moonii

Phyllanthus moonii, commonly known as moonleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often silver or gray in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Phyllanthus moonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
