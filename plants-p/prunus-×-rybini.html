Prunus × rybini

Prunus × rybini, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red or black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for hedges and landscape design.
