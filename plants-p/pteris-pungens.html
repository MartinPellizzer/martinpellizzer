Pteris pungens

Pteris pungens, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Pteris pungens is also known for its ability to purify water, making it a useful plant in aquatic environments.
