Persica vulgaris f. magnifica

Persica vulgaris f. magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent peach.

This cultivar is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often exceed the size of the fruit. The plant exhibits vigorous growth and is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its potential for fruit production.
