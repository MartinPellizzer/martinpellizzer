Pyrus variolosa

Pyrus variolosa, commonly known as the variable pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China, Korea, and Japan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are often serrated. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and pear-shaped.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
