Pelargonium obtusum

Pelargonium obtusum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a low, compact shrub, often used in rock gardens and containers due to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean climates.

Pelargonium obtusum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
