Pleurothallis muriculata

Pleurothallis muriculata, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid tropical forests. The plant features intricate, hairy structures on its leaves and stems, which give it its common name. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of white, yellow, or green.

Pleurothallis muriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
