Phaca artemisiarum

Phaca artemisiarum, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Phaca artemisiarum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
