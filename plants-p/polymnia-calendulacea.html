Polymnia calendulacea

Polymnia calendulacea, commonly known as the goldenrods, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Polymnia calendulacea plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies during its blooming season.
