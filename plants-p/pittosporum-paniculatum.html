Pittosporum paniculatum

Pittosporum paniculatum, commonly known as the white-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy floral display.
