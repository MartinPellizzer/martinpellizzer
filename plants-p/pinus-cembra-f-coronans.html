Pinus cembra f. coronans

Pinus cembra f. coronans, commonly known as the Alpine fir, is a rare and distinctive variant of the European fir.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude regions of the Alps. The plant exhibits a unique crown formation, giving it a bushy appearance that sets it apart from other fir species. Its foliage consists of short, stiff needles that are typically a dark green color.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh mountain environments, displaying resilience to cold and strong winds.
