Pseudognaphalium obtusifolium subsp. praecox

Pseudognaphalium obtusifolium subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early flatweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, sprawling growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
