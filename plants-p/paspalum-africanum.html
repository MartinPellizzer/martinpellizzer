Paspalum africanum

Paspalum africanum, commonly known as African grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum africanum is often used in ornamental landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.
