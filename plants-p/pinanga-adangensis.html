Pinanga adangensis

Pinanga adangensis, commonly known as the Adangensian bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the island of Mindanao. The plant is characterized by its clustered growth habit and possesses long, slender, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga adangensis is often used in local traditional practices and is of interest for its potential ecological and horticultural value.
