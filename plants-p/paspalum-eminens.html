Paspalum eminens

Paspalum eminens, commonly known as the tall grass paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wetland environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum eminens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
