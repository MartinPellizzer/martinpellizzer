Potamogeton macrophylloides

Potamogeton macrophylloides, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and typically have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes.
