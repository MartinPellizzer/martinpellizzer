Paphiopedilum primulinum

Paphiopedilum primulinum, commonly known as the golden mottled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, broad, leathery leaf and produces striking flowers with a unique pouch-shaped lip. The flowers are typically yellow with orange and green markings, and they bloom in late summer or early autumn. This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

It prefers shady, moist environments and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
