Penstemon cinicola

Penstemon cinicola, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Penstemon cinicola is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
