Polygonum pensylvanicum var. genuinum

Polygonum pensylvanicum var. genuinum, commonly known as Pennsylvania smartweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and forest edges. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the tips. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins and are alternate along the stem.

The small, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish, appearing in late summer to early autumn.
