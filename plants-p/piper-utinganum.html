Piper utinganum

Piper utinganum, commonly known as the Philippine pepper or Utinganum pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora.
