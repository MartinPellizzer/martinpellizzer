Piper lentaginoides

Piper lentaginoides, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Piper lentaginoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
