Pilosella ziziana subsp. pachyphyes

Pilosella ziziana subsp.

pachyphyes, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal regions.

Its subspecies name, *pachyphyes*, refers to its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.
