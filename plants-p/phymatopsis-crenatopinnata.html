Phymatopsis crenatopinnata

Phymatopsis crenatopinnata, commonly known as the crested bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, with showy red or pink flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Phymatopsis crenatopinnata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
