Pachira rurrenabaqueana

Pachira rurrenabaqueana, commonly known as the Rurrenabaque pachira, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its roots are often submerged, helping to stabilize the sediment and improve water clarity.

Pachira rurrenabaqueana is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic ecosystems.
