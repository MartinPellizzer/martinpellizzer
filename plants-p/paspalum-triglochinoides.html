Paspalum triglochinoides

Paspalum triglochinoides, commonly known as narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common species in marshy or riparian habitats.

Paspalum triglochinoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
