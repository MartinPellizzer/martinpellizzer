Piper amplexifolium

Piper amplexifolium, commonly known as the clasping-leaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often encircle the stems, giving it a unique appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
