Pentace burmanica

Pentace burmanica, commonly known as Burmese pentace, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
