Peramium pubescens

Peramium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy peramium, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, and it can spread through both vegetative growth and seed dispersal.

Peramium pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
