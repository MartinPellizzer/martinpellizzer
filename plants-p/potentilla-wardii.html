Potentilla wardii

Potentilla wardii, commonly known as the Ward's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla wardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
