Phyllostachys hispida var. glabrivagina

Phyllostachys hispida var. glabrivagina, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-blue sheen that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It is a clumping bamboo that spreads slowly, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
