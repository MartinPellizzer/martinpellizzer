Polemonium caeruleum subsp. laxiflorum

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

laxiflorum, commonly known as bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
