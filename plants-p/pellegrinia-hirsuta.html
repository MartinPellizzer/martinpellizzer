Pellegrinia hirsuta

Pellegrinia hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly pellgrinia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Pellegrinia hirsuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique texture and low maintenance requirements.
