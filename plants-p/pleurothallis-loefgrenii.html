Pleurothallis loefgrenii

Pleurothallis loefgrenii, commonly known as the Loefgren's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees in humid, tropical forests, relying on its host for physical support rather than nutrients. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often clustered in a raceme. The plant has linear, strap-like leaves that are adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
