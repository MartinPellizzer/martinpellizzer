Phyllostachys puberula var. nigropunctata

Phyllostachys puberula var. nigropunctata, commonly known as the black dot bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms that exhibit a distinctive black dot pattern on the nodes. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and prefers well-drained soil in a sunny to partially shaded location. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This bamboo is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscape design for its elegant, flowing appearance.
