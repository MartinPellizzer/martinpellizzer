Polygonum bussei

Polygonum bussei, commonly known as the Bessie knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems that often branch near the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum bussei is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in meadows.
