Psoralea frutescens

Psoralea frutescens, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, followed by distinctive, three-lobed pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and the plant is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Psoralea frutescens is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
