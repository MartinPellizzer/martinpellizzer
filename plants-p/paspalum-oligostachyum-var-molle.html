Paspalum oligostachyum var. molle

Paspalum oligostachyum var. molle, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in panicles, which are typically visible in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it suitable for use in landscapes and erosion control.
