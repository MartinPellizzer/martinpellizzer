Priotropis cytisoides

Priotropis cytisoides, commonly known as the yellow flame acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of small, feather-like leaflets, and it has a robust root system adapted to drought conditions.
