Premna corymbosa var. sambucina

Premna corymbosa var. sambucina, commonly known as the Indian elderberry or sambucina, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers arranged in cymes, which bloom in summer. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
