Pygmaeothamnus zeyheri var. zeyheri

Pygmaeothamnus zeyheri var. zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's pygmy thornbill, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It has a compact growth habit and is used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
