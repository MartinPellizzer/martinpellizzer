Phylica excelsa var. laxa

Phylica excelsa var. laxa, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped leaves and bright red, brush-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
