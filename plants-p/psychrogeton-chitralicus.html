Psychrogeton chitralicus

Psychrogeton chitralicus, commonly known as the Chitral gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are usually short-lived, blooming for only a few days during the summer months.

Psychrogeton chitralicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in mountainous regions of Pakistan and northern India.
