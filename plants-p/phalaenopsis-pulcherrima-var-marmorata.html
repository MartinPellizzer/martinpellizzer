Phalaenopsis pulcherrima var. marmorata

Phalaenopsis pulcherrima var. marmorata, commonly known as the marbled phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking, marbled patterns on its velvety leaves and long-lasting, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it typically grows as an epiphyte on trees. The plant features broad, dark green leaves with distinctive white and green mottling, and its flowers are usually white or pale pink with a contrasting lip.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining potting media to flourish.
