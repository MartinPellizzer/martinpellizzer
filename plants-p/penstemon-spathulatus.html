Penstemon spathulatus

Penstemon spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
