Pelargonium laxatum

Pelargonium laxatum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. It produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often blooming throughout the summer months.

This plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
