Pluchea longifolia

Pluchea longifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with slender, branching stems and hairy leaves. Its small, yellow to pale orange flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pluchea longifolia is often found in disturbed soils, wetlands, and along stream banks, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environments.
