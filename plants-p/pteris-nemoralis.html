Pteris nemoralis

Pteris nemoralis, commonly known as the common water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodland areas or near water sources. Its rhizomes are wiry and creeping, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Pteris nemoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
