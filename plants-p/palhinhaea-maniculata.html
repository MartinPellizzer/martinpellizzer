Palhinhaea maniculata

Palhinhaea maniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Palhinhaea maniculata is also valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which has been historically used in the production of musical instruments.
