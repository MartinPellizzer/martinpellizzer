Pithecoctenium echinatum var. intermedium

Pithecoctenium echinatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate echinate pith.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its erect, herbaceous growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
