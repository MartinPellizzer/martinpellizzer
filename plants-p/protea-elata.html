Protea elata

Protea elata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of multiple bracts and tiny white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and often covered in a silvery-white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Protea elata is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience in diverse climates.
