Ptychotis carvifolia

Ptychotis carvifolia, commonly known as the caraway-leaved ptychotis, is a species of flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features finely divided, aromatic leaves that resemble those of caraway, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

Ptychotis carvifolia is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
