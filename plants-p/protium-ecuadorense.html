Protium ecuadorense

Protium ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian tamanu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, characterized by its aromatic resins and smooth, fissured bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with prominent veins.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for skin conditions and inflammation.
