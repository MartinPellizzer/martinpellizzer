Paeonia veitchii var. purpurea

Paeonia veitchii var. purpurea, commonly known as the Veitch's peony with purple flowers, is a cultivar of the peony plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and typically pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are green and somewhat fern-like in appearance. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical significance in horticultural cultivation.
