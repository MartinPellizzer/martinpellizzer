Pentatropis kempeana

Pentatropis kempeana, commonly known as the Kempe's pentatropis, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and South Carolina. This shrub typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compound leaves are pinnate, and it produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
