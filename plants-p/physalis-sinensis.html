Physalis sinensis

Physalis sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or winter cherry, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive papery calyx that encloses the bright orange or red berries. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. The berries, which resemble small lanterns, are typically harvested when fully mature.

Physalis sinensis thrives in temperate climates and is often grown in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
