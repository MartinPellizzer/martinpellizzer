Phyllostachys macroculmis var. edulis

Phyllostachys macroculmis var. edulis, commonly known as the edulis bamboo, is a species native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This bamboo is also valued for its edible shoots, which are commonly consumed in Asian cuisine.
