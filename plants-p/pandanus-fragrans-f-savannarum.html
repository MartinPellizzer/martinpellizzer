Pandanus fragrans f. savannarum

Pandanus fragrans f. savannarum, commonly known as the savanna pandanus, is a variant of the fragrant pandanus species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its adaptability to sandy and well-drained soils makes it suitable for coastal and arid environments.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions to its native habitats.
