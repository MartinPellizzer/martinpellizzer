Passiflora gustaviana

Passiflora gustaviana, commonly known as the Gustaviana passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit, which develops after the flowers, is typically a small, yellowish berry.

Passiflora gustaviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral structure.
