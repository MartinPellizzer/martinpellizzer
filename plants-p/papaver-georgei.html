Papaver georgei

Papaver georgei, commonly known as George's poppy, is a rare and striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers have a distinctive, saucer-like shape with numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil.

Papaver georgei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its rarity and unique appearance.
