Prunus sontagiae

Prunus sontagiae, commonly known as the Sonntag plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
