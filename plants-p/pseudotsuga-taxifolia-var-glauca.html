Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. glauca

Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. glauca, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a variant of the Japanese fir native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a blue-gray waxy coating. The foliage exhibits a distinctive bluish-white hue, which gives the plant its common name. It produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic qualities.
