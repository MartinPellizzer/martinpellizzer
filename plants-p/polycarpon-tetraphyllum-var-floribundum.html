Polycarpon tetraphyllum var. floribundum

Polycarpon tetraphyllum var. floribundum, commonly known as the four-leafed sow-thistle, is a variant of the species Polycarpon tetraphyllum.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

The varietal name 'floribundum' refers to its profuse flowering habit.
