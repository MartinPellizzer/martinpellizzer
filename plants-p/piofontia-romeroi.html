Piofontia romeroi

Piofontia romeroi, commonly known as the Romero's piofont, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the spring season. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Piofontia romeroi is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
