Pavonia fruticosa

Pavonia fruticosa, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, often thorny stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough, hairy texture.

Pavonia fruticosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.
