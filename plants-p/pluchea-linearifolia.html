Pluchea linearifolia

Pluchea linearifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf mistletoe, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its linear, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. It typically grows as a hemi-parasitic shrub, attaching itself to host trees for partial nourishment.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of plant-parasite interactions.
