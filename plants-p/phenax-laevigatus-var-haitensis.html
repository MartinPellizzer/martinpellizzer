Phenax laevigatus var. haitensis

Phenax laevigatus var. haitensis, commonly known as the Haitian smoothleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It features elongated, smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
