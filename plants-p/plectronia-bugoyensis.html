Plectronia bugoyensis

Plectronia bugoyensis, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane forests. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss, Plectronia bugoyensis is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
