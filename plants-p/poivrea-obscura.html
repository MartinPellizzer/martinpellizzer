Poivrea obscura

Poivrea obscura, commonly known as the dark privet, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are often glossy and oval in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its berries are black when mature and are an important food source for birds.

Poivrea obscura is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
