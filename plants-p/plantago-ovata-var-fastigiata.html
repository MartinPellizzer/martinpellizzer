Plantago ovata var. fastigiata

Plantago ovata var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its upright growth habit.

It is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of psyllium husk for its dietary fiber content.
