Pyrus rupicoloides

Pyrus rupicoloides, commonly known as the alpine pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Tian Shan and Pamir Mountains. This plant is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear-like berry that matures in late summer.

It is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
