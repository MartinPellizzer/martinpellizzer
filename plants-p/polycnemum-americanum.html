Polycnemum americanum

Polycnemum americanum, commonly known as the American water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces oval, smooth-seeded fruits.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
