Polygonum nepalense var. adenothrix

Polygonum nepalense var. adenothrix, commonly known as the Nepalese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its fibrous roots and rhizomes make it a hardy species capable of spreading through vegetative propagation.
