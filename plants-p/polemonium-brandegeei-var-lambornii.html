Polemonium brandegeei var. lambornii

Polemonium brandegeei var. lambornii, commonly known as the Lamborn's Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains of the United States.

It is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cascading clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
