Parillax laurifolia

Parillax laurifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf laurel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Parillax laurifolia is typically evergreen, with a dense, compact growth habit that makes it suitable for hedges or ornamental planting.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
