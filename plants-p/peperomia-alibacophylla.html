Peperomia alibacophylla

Peperomia alibacophylla, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically silver or grayish in color, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for succulent and tropical plant collections.
