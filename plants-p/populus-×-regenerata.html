Populus × regenerata

Populus × regenerata, commonly known as the hybrid aspen, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Populus tremuloides (quaking aspen) and Populus balsamifera (balsam poplar). The plant is characterized by its smooth, white bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest edges.

Populus × regenerata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
