Polystichopsis lurida var. sericea

Polystichopsis lurida var. sericea, commonly known as the silky cliff brake, is a variant of the cliff brake fern native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-green fronds that give it a distinctive, almost feathery appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils, often found on cliff faces or in open, sunny habitats. Its fronds are typically leathery and have a glossy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant.
