Penstemon floridus var. austini

Penstemon floridus var. austini, commonly known as the Florida beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and butterflies, supporting local pollinator populations.
