Pratia irrigua

Pratia irrigua, commonly known as the water thistle, is a perennial herb native to regions of southern Africa.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish hue. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a somewhat bitter taste.

Pratia irrigua is often found in moist, well-drained soils near water sources such as rivers and streams.
