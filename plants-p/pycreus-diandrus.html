Pycreus diandrus

Pycreus diandrus, commonly known as the double-flowered pycreus, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, double-layered flowers that resemble small, intricate blossoms. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a vibrant green.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
