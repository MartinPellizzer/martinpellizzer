Potamogeton marinus var. alpinus

Potamogeton marinus var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments.

It typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its preference for cooler temperatures, it is often found in high-altitude lakes and mountain streams.
