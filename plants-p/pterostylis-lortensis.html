Pterostylis lortensis

Pterostylis lortensis, commonly known as the Lortons' hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like labellum and a single, erect stem bearing a single spike of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, hooded shape that resembles a protective covering.

Pterostylis lortensis is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
