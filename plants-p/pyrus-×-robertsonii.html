Pyrus × robertsonii

Pyrus × robertsonii, commonly known as the Robertson pear, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyrifolia (Asian pear).

It is characterized by its medium to large size, with fruits that are typically round or slightly elongated. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, which is often used in culinary applications.
