Polygala vergrandis

Polygala vergrandis, commonly known as the large-leaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spicate inflorescences, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
