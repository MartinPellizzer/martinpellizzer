Polygala calcarea var. angustifolia

Polygala calcarea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in compact racemes, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, often branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Polygalaceae family.
