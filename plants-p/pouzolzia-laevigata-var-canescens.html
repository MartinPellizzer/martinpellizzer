Pouzolzia laevigata var. canescens

Pouzolzia laevigata var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and forest edges. The plant features slender, woody stems covered in fine, whitish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic to ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain wildlife species.
