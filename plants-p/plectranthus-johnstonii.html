Plectranthus johnstonii

Plectranthus johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's plectranthus, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

Plectranthus johnstonii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
