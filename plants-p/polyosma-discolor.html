Polyosma discolor

Polyosma discolor, commonly known as the variable holly or variable boxwood, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that varies in color from deep green to reddish hues. The plant has small, white or greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by blue-black berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture.

Polyosma discolor is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
