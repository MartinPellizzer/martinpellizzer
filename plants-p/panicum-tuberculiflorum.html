Panicum tuberculiflorum

Panicum tuberculiflorum, commonly known as the tubercled panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, tubercle-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage is usually narrow and linear, with a rough texture.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
