Poa sodiroana

Poa sodiroana, commonly known as the New Zealand tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.

Due to its ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in restoration projects.
