Piper falcatum

Piper falcatum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot or wild arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other species like black pepper. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its underground stems, known as rhizomes, are edible and have a starchy texture similar to yams.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
