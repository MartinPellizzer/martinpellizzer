Penstemon azureus subsp. angustissimus

Penstemon azureus subsp.

angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluecurls, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, blue-green leaves and striking, tubular blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water once established.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
