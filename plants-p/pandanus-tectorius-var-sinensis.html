Pandanus tectorius var. sinensis

Pandanus tectorius var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are fibrous and are used to make thatch, mats, and other woven items.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found near coastal areas or in wetlands.
