Polystichum imbricans subsp. curtum

Polystichum imbricans subsp.

curtum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood fern, is a perennial fern native to the temperate regions of Asia. It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and wiry, supporting the growth of its delicate, compound leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
