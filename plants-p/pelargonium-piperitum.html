Pelargonium piperitum

Pelargonium piperitum, commonly known as the pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery scent, which is derived from its leaves. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often used in culinary applications. Pelargonium piperitum is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine and cooking.
