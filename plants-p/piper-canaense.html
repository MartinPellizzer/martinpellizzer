Piper canaense

Piper canaense, commonly known as the red pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
