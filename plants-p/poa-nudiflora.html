Poa nudiflora

Poa nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that lack bracts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa nudiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
