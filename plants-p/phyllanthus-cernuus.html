Phyllanthus cernuus

Phyllanthus cernuus, commonly known as the hanging plant or weeping plant, is a flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fleshy leaves that droop from the stems, giving it a unique appearance. Native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, it thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-colored fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
