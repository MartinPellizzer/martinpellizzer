Phaius blumei

Phaius blumei, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often display a striking blue or violet hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, with long, strap-like leaves and a robust root system. Its flowers are borne on tall, erect spikes and are known for their intricate structure and fragrance.

Phaius blumei is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique coloration.
