Polygala cernua

Polygala cernua, commonly known as the nodding milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spicate inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
