Pancratium ringens

Pancratium ringens, commonly known as the sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong coastal winds.

Pancratium ringens is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
