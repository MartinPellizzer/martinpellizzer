Phanera semibifida var. perkinsiae

Phanera semibifida var. perkinsiae, commonly known as the Perkins' flame tree, is a tropical plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the warmer months. The leaves are compound, typically consisting of two to three pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. This variant is noted for its ornamental value, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub.
