Paspalum strigosum

Paspalum strigosum, commonly known as hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Paspalum strigosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
