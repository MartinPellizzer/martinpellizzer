Pollinia ciliata var. laxa

Pollinia ciliata var. laxa, commonly known as the slender cat's-paw, is a variant of the cat's-paw plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its distinctive, claw-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its pollination mechanism involves the formation of pollinia, which are masses of pollen that adhere to the bodies of pollinators.
