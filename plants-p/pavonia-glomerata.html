Pavonia glomerata

Pavonia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and leaves, as well as capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Pavonia glomerata is often found in moist, open habitats like woodlands and wetlands.
