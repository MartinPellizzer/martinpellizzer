Papaver racemosum

Papaver racemosum, commonly known as the clustered poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a slender, branching stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its capsule-like fruit contains numerous small, elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Papaver racemosum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and wildflower settings.
