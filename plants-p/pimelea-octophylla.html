Pimelea octophylla

Pimelea octophylla, commonly known as the eight-leafed riceflower, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Western Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal heathland environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in winter, adding a striking contrast to its grey-green foliage. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pimelea octophylla is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
