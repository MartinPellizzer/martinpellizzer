Protium acrense

Protium acrense, commonly known as the Brazilian sand-leaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are often dotted with small glands. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
