Pentapogon sclerophyllus

Pentapogon sclerophyllus, commonly known as the hard-leaved pentapogon, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are thick and leathery, a feature typical of sclerophyllous plants. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
