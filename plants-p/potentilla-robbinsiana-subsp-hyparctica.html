Potentilla robbinsiana subsp. hyparctica

Potentilla robbinsiana subsp.

hyparctica, commonly known as the Prairie smoke, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its fluffy, pink to reddish-purple seed heads that resemble smoke, which appear in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are palmately divided and small, upright flowering stems.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in native plant gardens.
