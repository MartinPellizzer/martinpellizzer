Pappophorum nigricans var. barbinode

Pappophorum nigricans var. barbinode, commonly known as the black bristle grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dark-colored, bristly inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, textured surface.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
