Palala argentea

Palala argentea, commonly known as the silver palala, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-white leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss, Palala argentea is classified as endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
