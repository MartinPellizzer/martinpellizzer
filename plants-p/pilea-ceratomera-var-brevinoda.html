Pilea ceratomera var. brevinoda

Pilea ceratomera var. brevinoda, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically round or oval in shape, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its attractive foliage and ease of care, making it a popular houseplant.
