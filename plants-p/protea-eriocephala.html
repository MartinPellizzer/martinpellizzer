Protea eriocephala

Protea eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Protea eriocephala is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
