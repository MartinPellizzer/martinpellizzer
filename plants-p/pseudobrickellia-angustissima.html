Pseudobrickellia angustissima

Pseudobrickellia angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf false brickellbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It is often found in the understory of forests and is adapted to partial shade.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
