Paspalum plenum

Paspalum plenum, commonly known as the broadleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open areas, including lawns, pastures, and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.

Its seed heads are often used in floral arrangements due to their ornamental appearance.
