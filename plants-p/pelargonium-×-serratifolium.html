Pelargonium × serratifolium

Pelargonium × serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated, lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a favored plant in horticulture.
