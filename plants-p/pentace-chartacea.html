Pentace chartacea

Pentace chartacea, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Pentaceae and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological roles, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical regions.
