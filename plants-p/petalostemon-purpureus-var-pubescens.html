Petalostemon purpureus var. pubescens

Petalostemon purpureus var. pubescens, commonly known as the purple petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers and hairy, pubescent foliage, which give it its specific epithet "pubescens.". The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge, adding to its ornamental value.

The species is valued in horticulture for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in partial shade.
