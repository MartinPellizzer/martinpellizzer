Piper coniunctonis

Piper coniunctonis, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or the Jamaican pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by three-lobed, orange-red berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
