Potentilla amurensis

Potentilla amurensis, commonly known as the Amur cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Potentilla amurensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
