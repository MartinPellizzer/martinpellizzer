Passiflora solomonii

Passiflora solomonii, commonly known as Solomon's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
