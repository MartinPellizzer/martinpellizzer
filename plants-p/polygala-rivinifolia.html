Polygala rivinifolia

Polygala rivinifolia, commonly known as the river polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found growing near rivers or in swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
