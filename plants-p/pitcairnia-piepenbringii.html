Pitcairnia piepenbringii

Pitcairnia piepenbringii, commonly known as the velvet pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety rosette of dark green, fibrous leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences with vibrant orange-red flowers, which bloom in the early morning. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is also valued in horticultural and conservation efforts.
