Piptochaetium ovatum var. purpurascens

Piptochaetium ovatum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in open habitats such as savannas and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
