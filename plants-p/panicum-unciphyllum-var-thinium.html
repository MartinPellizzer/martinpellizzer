Panicum unciphyllum var. thinium

Panicum unciphyllum var. thinium, commonly known as slender finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
