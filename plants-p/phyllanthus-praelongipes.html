Phyllanthus praelongipes

Phyllanthus praelongipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
