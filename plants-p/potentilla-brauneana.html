Potentilla brauneana

Potentilla brauneana, commonly known as the Braune's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla brauneana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species found in alpine and subalpine regions.
