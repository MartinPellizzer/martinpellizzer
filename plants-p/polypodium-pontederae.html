Polypodium pontederae

Polypodium pontederae, commonly known as the Pontedera fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, round spore cases called sporangia, which are clustered in groups called sori on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium pontederae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
