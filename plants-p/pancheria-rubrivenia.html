Pancheria rubrivenia

Pancheria rubrivenia, commonly known as the red-veined pancheria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting various pollinators. Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
