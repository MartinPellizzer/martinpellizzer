Polygonum libani

Polygonum libani, commonly known as the Lebanon knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with toothed margins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

Polygonum libani is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
