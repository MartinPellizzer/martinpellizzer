Piper chrysolepidum

Piper chrysolepidum, commonly known as golden leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

Piper chrysolepidum is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
