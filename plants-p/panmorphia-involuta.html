Panmorphia involuta

Panmorphia involuta, commonly known as the twisted panmorph, is a rare and enigmatic plant species characterized by its highly variable morphology.

It exhibits a unique combination of features, including spiral-arranged leaves and a stem that twists dramatically as it grows. The plant is native to a specific region in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often hidden among its dense foliage.

Due to its rarity and unusual structure, it is of great interest to botanists studying plant evolution and adaptation.
