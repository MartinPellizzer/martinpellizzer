Paeonia tartarica

Paeonia tartarica, commonly known as the Turkish peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features showy, double or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are dark green and heart-shaped. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and help the plant survive harsh winters.

Paeonia tartarica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
