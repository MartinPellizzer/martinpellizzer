Petroselinum crispum var. petroselinum

Petroselinum crispum var. petroselinum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that are typically bright green and aromatic. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters called umbels, which appear in the second year of growth. Its root system is fibrous, and it prefers well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Parsley is widely cultivated for its leaves and seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
