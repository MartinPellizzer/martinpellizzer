Pyrus trilobata var. rumelica

Pyrus trilobata var. rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelica pear, is a variant of the three-lobed pear native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits in late summer. Its fruit is often used in jams and preserves due to its unique flavor.

The Rumelica pear is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in American horticulture.
