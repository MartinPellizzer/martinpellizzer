Pachypodium brevicaule subsp. brevicaule

Pachypodium brevicaule subsp.

brevicaule, commonly known as the dwarf bottle tree, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its short, thick stem that stores water, and its dense clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
