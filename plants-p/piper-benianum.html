Piper benianum

Piper benianum, commonly known as the pepper plant or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, especially in Southeast Asian cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
