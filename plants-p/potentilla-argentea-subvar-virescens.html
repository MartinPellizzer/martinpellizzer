Potentilla argentea subvar. virescens

Potentilla argentea subvar. virescens, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

The subspecies virescens is distinguished by its more robust and darker green leaves compared to the typical silver-green of the main species.
