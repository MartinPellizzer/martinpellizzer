Plumbagidium auriculatum

Plumbagidium auriculatum, commonly known as the silverleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Plumbagaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
