Potentilla × coryletii

Potentilla × coryletii, commonly known as the evergreen potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant for gardens in temperate climates.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping and rock gardens.
