Penstemon ramaleyi

Penstemon ramaleyi, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun.

Penstemon ramaleyi is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
