Pelea peduncularis

Pelea peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered pelea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Pelea peduncularis is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
