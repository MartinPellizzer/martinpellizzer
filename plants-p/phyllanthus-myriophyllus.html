Phyllanthus myriophyllus

Phyllanthus myriophyllus, commonly known as the many-leafed phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
