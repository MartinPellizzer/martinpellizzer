Podocarpus utilior

Podocarpus utilior, commonly known as the common podocarp, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments. It is often used in landscaping and has economic value for its timber and ornamental qualities.

The leaves are typically leathery and can be used in traditional medicine in some regions.
