Psoralea melilotus

Psoralea melilotus, commonly known as the yellow sweet pea or melilot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Psoralea melilotus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
