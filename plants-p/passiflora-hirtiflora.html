Passiflora hirtiflora

Passiflora hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking flowers with colorful, intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. Its fruit is a edible, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Passiflora hirtiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
