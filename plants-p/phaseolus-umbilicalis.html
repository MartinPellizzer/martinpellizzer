Phaseolus umbilicalis

Phaseolus umbilicalis, commonly known as the creeping pole bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing vines and small, oval-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale purple, and the pods are thin-walled with edible seeds inside. This plant is adapted to grow in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional agriculture and ecological studies.
