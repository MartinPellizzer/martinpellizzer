Phragmipedium hartwegii var. baderi

Phragmipedium hartwegii var. baderi, commonly known as the Bader's phragmipedium, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces pendulous, fragrant flowers with showy, white to pale yellow petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.

The variety is distinguished by its unique floral structure and coloration compared to other Phragmipedium species.
