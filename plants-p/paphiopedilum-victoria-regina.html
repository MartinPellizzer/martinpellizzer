Paphiopedilum victoria-regina

Paphiopedilum victoria-regina, commonly known as the Victoria Regina orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, dark green to brownish flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
