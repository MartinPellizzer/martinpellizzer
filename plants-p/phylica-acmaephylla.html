Phylica acmaephylla

Phylica acmaephylla, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a woody structure with smooth, greyish bark and produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardiness.

Phylica acmaephylla is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
