Piper disparispicum

Piper disparispicum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the humid forests of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery scent when crushed.

Piper disparispicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
