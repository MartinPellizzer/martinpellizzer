Polystachya confusa

Polystachya confusa, commonly known as the confused polystachya, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found growing in forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
