Potentilla intermedia var. elongata

Potentilla intermedia var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in garden settings.
