Phylogyne × rugulosa

Phylogyne × rugulosa, commonly known as the rugose phylogym, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between two distinct species within the genus Phylogyne.

It is characterized by its rugged, textured leaves that are typically a deep green with a matte finish. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is appreciated by botanists and gardeners alike.
