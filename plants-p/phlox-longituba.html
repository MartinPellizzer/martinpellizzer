Phlox longituba

Phlox longituba, commonly known as the long-tube phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and are adapted to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged opposite along the stems.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
