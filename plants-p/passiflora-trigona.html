Passiflora trigona

Passiflora trigona, commonly known as the tripoint passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and dark green color.

Passiflora trigona is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and ecological value to pollinators.
