Pachylophus cespitosa

Pachylophus cespitosa, commonly known as the tufted pachylophus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are often pale green or yellowish.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
