Platanthera neottioides

Platanthera neottioides, commonly known as the Neottioides lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a striking, fragrant inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, erect flowering stem.

Platanthera neottioides is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
