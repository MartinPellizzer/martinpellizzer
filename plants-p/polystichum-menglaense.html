Polystichum menglaense

Polystichum menglaense, commonly known as Mengla fern, is a species of fern native to China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are divided into pinnae, which are arranged in a feather-like pattern, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.
