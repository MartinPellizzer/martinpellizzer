Paphiopedilum superbiens var. curtisii

Paphiopedilum superbiens var. curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's velvet slipper orchid, is a rare and elegant orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendulous petals and a distinctive, slipper-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid forest understories. Its flowers are usually a vibrant pink or reddish hue, with a velvety texture that gives it its common name.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and limited distribution.
