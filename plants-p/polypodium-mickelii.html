Polypodium mickelii

Polypodium mickelii, commonly known as the Mickel's rock fern, is a terrestrial fern native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fronds that are leathery and deeply divided. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of many ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
