Pelargonium anemonifolium

Pelargonium anemonifolium, commonly known as the anemone-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are deeply lobed, resembling the petals of an anemone. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental in gardens and is well-suited for containers due to its compact growth habit. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil to thrive.

Pelargonium anemonifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
