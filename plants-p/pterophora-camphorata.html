Pterophora camphorata

Pterophora camphorata, commonly known as the camphor tree leaf miner, is a species of moth belonging to the family Pterophoridae.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow wings that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a pale, powdery appearance. The larvae of this species feed on the leaves of the camphor tree, causing distinctive mining damage. The plant is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Indian subcontinent.

Its life cycle is closely tied to the seasonal growth of its host plant, making it an important species in ecological studies of plant-insect interactions.
