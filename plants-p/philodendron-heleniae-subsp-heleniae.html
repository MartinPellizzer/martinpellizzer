Philodendron heleniae subsp. heleniae

Philodendron heleniae subsp.

heleniae, commonly known as the Helen's Philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
