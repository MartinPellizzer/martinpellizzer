Parodia subterranea var. slabana

Parodia subterranea var. slabana, commonly known as the Slabana cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that often remains close to the ground. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne on short, lateral branches. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is prized for its compact growth and striking floral display, making it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
