Primula aretioides

Primula aretioides, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, basal leaves that form a rosette, and it produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Primula aretioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
