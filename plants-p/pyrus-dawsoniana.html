Pyrus dawsoniana

Pyrus dawsoniana, commonly known as the Dawson's crabapple, is a small deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
