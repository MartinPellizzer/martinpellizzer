Pilea puracensis

Pilea puracensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, as well as parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins, which give it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color.

Pilea puracensis is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
