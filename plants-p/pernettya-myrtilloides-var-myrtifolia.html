Pernettya myrtilloides var. myrtifolia

Pernettya myrtilloides var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the dwarf blueberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark blue berries in the summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use of its berries.
