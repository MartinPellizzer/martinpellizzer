Photinia notoniana f. micrantha

Photinia notoniana f. micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The foliage provides habitat for birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
