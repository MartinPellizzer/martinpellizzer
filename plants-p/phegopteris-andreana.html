Phegopteris andreana

Phegopteris andreana, commonly known as the giant fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which release spores for propagation. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and enabling it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

This fern is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
