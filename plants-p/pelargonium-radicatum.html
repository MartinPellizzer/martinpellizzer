Pelargonium radicatum

Pelargonium radicatum, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of bright pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system that allows it to store water efficiently. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Pelargonium radicatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
