Primula rigida

Primula rigida, commonly known as the rigid primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
