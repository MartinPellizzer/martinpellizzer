Paepalanthus fasciculatus f. rigida

Paepalanthus fasciculatus f. rigida, commonly known as the rigid clustered pincushion cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that form compact, cushion-like clusters. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
