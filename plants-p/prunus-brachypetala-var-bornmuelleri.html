Prunus brachypetala var. bornmuelleri

Prunus brachypetala var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Chinese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white and pink. The leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth texture, turning yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, hard fruits that are typically not edible by humans but are important for wildlife.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
