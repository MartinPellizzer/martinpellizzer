Pandanus sermollianus

Pandanus sermollianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, preferring warm, humid climates.

Its fibrous leaves are valued for their strength and durability in various cultural applications.
