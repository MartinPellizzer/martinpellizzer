Primula strumosa var. strumosa

Primula strumosa var. strumosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred lower petal. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
