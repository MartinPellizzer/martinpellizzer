Penducella pendens

Penducella pendens, commonly known as the hanging bell plant, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from long, slender stems. Its botanical features include elongated, lanceolate leaves and a complex inflorescence structure.

Penducella pendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
