Potentilla approximata

Potentilla approximata, commonly known as the common potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features small, rounded leaves that are densely arranged along its stems. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticulture.
