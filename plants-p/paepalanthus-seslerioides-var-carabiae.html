Paepalanthus seslerioides var. carabiae

Paepalanthus seslerioides var. carabiae, commonly known as the Carabian stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
