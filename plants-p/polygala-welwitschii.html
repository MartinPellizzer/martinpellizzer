Polygala welwitschii

Polygala welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's milkweed, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical name honors the German botanist Adolf Engler, who described it.

Polygala welwitschii is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
