Polystichum mucronifolium

Polystichum mucronifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaved wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features upright, branched fronds that are dark green and have a leathery texture. The fronds are characterized by their blunt-tipped, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, narrow, and gradually narrowing shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The plant produces spore-bearing fiddleheads in early spring, contributing to its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
