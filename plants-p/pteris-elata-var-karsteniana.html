Pteris elata var. karsteniana

Pteris elata var. karsteniana, commonly known as the Karsten's water fern, is a variant of the water fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their aesthetic appeal. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, a characteristic common to many fern species.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
