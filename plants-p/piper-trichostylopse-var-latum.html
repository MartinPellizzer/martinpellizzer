Piper trichostylopse var. latum

Piper trichostylopse var. latum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elliptical berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The varietal form *latum* distinguishes it from other varieties through its broader foliage and slightly different growth habits.
