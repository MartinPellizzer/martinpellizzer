Plinia longa

Plinia longa, commonly known as the long-fruited guava, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence. The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its aromatic, sweet flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
