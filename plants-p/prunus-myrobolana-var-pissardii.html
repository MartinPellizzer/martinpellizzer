Prunus myrobolana var. pissardii

Prunus myrobolana var. pissardii, commonly known as the Pissard's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, sweet-tart cherries. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
