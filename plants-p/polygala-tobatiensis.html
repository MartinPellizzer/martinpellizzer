Polygala tobatiensis

Polygala tobatiensis, commonly known as the Tobati's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
