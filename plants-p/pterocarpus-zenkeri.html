Pterocarpus zenkeri

Pterocarpus zenkeri, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued in the furniture and musical instrument industries. The tree has bipinnate leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruit is a flat, woody pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
