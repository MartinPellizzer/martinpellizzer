Plectranthus fangii

Plectranthus fangii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus fangii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
