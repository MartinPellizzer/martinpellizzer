Prunus sericea var. batalinii

Prunus sericea var. batalinii, commonly known as the silverleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
