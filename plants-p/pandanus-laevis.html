Pandanus laevis

Pandanus laevis, commonly known as the smooth pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically smooth and waxy in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in spikes, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its roots are used in traditional crafts, and the leaves are utilized in thatching and basket weaving.

Pandanus laevis thrives in coastal and wetland environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
