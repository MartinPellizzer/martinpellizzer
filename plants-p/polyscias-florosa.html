Polyscias florosa

Polyscias florosa, commonly known as the white soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often followed by yellow or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
