Persicaria japonica

Persicaria japonica, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread aggressively and form dense stands. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a problematic species in many regions outside its native range.
