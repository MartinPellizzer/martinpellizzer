Panax japonicus f. xanthocarpus

Panax japonicus f. xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow-fruited ginseng, is a rare variant of the ginseng plant characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange berries.

This subspecies is native to specific regions of East Asia, where it thrives in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. The plant features a perennial herb with a thick, fleshy root system that is often sought after for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The yellow fruiting bodies are not only visually striking but also contain compounds that may have health benefits.
