Pyrus allochroa

Pyrus allochroa, commonly known as the New Zealand whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible, round berries that are typically yellow or red.

It is often found in forest margins and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates with moderate rainfall.
