Piper purpusianum

Piper purpusianum, commonly known as the Purpus's pepper or the mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
