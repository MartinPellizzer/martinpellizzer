Pulsatilla transsilvanica

Pulsatilla transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces follicles that contain the seeds.

Pulsatilla transsilvanica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
