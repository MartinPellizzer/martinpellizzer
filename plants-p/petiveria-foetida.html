Petiveria foetida

Petiveria foetida, commonly known as the stinkweed or stinkplant, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, white or greenish flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, unpleasant odor. Its leaves contain various volatile compounds, which contribute to its distinctive smell and have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Petiveria foetida is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
