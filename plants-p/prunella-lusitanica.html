Prunella lusitanica

Prunella lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
