Pleurothallis spathilabia

Pleurothallis spathilabia, commonly known as the pink-tinged orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, pendulous flowers with a unique pinkish-white coloration. Its inflorescence is often adorned with a distinctive spathe, giving it its species name "spathilabia.".

Pleurothallis spathilabia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
