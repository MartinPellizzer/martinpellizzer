Pachygenium robustum

Pachygenium robustum, commonly known as the giant tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a tangy, sour flavor. Its thick, woody stems are adapted to withstand heavy rainfall and flooding.

Pachygenium robustum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
