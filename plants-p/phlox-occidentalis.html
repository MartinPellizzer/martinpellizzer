Phlox occidentalis

Phlox occidentalis, commonly known as western phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open woodlands and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
