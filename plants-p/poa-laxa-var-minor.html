Poa laxa var. minor

Poa laxa var. minor, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and wetlands. The plant has slender, often tufted stems that are usually less than 30 cm in height. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
