Pilosella glaucescens subsp. branae

Pilosella glaucescens subsp.

branae, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers are small and blue or white in color.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems, supporting various insect pollinators.
