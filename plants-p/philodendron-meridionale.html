Philodendron meridionale

Philodendron meridionale, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences that are surrounded by a colorful spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush foliage.

Philodendron meridionale is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
