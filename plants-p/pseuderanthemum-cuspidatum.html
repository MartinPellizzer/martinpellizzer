Pseuderanthemum cuspidatum

Pseuderanthemum cuspidatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Pseuderanthemum cuspidatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
