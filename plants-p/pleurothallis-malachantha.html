Pleurothallis malachantha

Pleurothallis malachantha, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive silver-tinged lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it blend into its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
