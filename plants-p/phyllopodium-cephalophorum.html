Phyllopodium cephalophorum

Phyllopodium cephalophorum, commonly known as the crownleaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are arranged in a compact, upward-curving pattern. The plant exhibits a basal growth habit with a central stem that bears clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This species is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.
