Paphiopedilum × aspersum var. trantuananhii

Paphiopedilum × aspersum var. trantuananhii, commonly known as the Trantuananhii Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits a blend of characteristics from its parent species.

It is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique lip structure typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, velvety texture.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper care.
