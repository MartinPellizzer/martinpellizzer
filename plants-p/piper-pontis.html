Piper pontis

Piper pontis, commonly known as the Pongam tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The Pongam tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
