Phaseolus gonospermos var. castaneus

Phaseolus gonospermos var. castaneus, commonly known as the chestnut bean, is a variety of the genus Phaseolus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive chestnut-colored seeds, which are typically oval in shape and have a smooth, firm texture. The plant is an annual legume that produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with a matte green surface.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
