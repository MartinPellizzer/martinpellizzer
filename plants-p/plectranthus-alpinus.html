Plectranthus alpinus

Plectranthus alpinus, commonly known as alpine plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in hue, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Plectranthus alpinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
