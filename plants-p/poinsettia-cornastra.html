Poinsettia cornastra

Poinsettia cornastra, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its short blooming season, typically during the winter months. Its foliage is often variegated with white and green patterns, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Poinsettia cornastra thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
