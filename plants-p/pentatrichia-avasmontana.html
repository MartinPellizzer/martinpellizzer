Pentatrichia avasmontana

Pentatrichia avasmontana, commonly known as the desert feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. The plant produces feathery, silver-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in harsh, dry conditions by efficiently absorbing and storing water.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
