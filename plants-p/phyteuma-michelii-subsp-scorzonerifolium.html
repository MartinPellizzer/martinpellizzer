Phyteuma michelii subsp. scorzonerifolium

Phyteuma michelii subsp.

scorzonerifolium, commonly known as the scorzonerifolium subtype, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
