Pharbitis congesta

Pharbitis congesta, commonly known as morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures. Its flowers are typically blue, violet, or white, and they emit a mild fragrance.
