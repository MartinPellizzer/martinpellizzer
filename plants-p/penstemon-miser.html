Penstemon miser

Penstemon miser, commonly known as the hoary penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and tall spikes of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

Penstemon miser is known for its hardiness and low maintenance, suitable for xeriscaping in arid climates.
