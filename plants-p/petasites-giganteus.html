Petasites giganteus

Petasites giganteus, commonly known as the giant cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its distinctive appearance and growth habits, it is a notable species in botanical studies and ecological research.
