Peperomia subsericata

Peperomia subsericata, commonly known as the silver velvet peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery or pale underside, giving it a unique, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and red, though they are not the main ornamental feature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia subsericata is popular as a houseplant due to its ability to tolerate low light and minimal watering.
