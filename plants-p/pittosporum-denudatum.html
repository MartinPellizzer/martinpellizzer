Pittosporum denudatum

Pittosporum denudatum, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to a striking bronze color. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, followed by small, orange-red berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

Pittosporum denudatum is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
