Polygonum schurii

Polygonum schurii, commonly known as Schur's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats and knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its stout, creeping stems that often form dense mats, and its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple hue, especially when mature.

Polygonum schurii is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
