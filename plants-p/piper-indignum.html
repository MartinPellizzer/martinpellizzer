Piper indignum

Piper indignum, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a visually striking vine.
