Phyllanthus humbertianus

Phyllanthus humbertianus, commonly known as the Humbert's phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

The flowers are small and yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences, and the plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.
