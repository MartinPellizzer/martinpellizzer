Premna coriacea var. villosa

Premna coriacea var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy roughleaf mallow, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
