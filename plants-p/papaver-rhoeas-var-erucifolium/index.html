Papaver Rhoeas Var. Erucifolium

Papaver rhoeas var. erucifolium is a rare and fascinating variant of the common poppy. It is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape, which resembles the leaves of the erucoid plant. This plant thrives in specific environmental conditions, often found in disturbed soils and sunny areas. Its unique appearance makes it a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike. Despite its beauty, it remains underappreciated and little studied compared to its more common relatives.
