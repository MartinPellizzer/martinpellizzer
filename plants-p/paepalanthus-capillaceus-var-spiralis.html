Paepalanthus capillaceus var. spiralis

Paepalanthus capillaceus var. spiralis, commonly known as the spiral-haired grass-vellvet plant, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive spiral arrangement of fine, hair-like structures along the leaf margins. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
