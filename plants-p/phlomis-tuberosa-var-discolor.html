Phlomis tuberosa var. discolor

Phlomis tuberosa var. discolor, commonly known as the spotted mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, hairy stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, often adorned with a dense cluster of yellow flowers at the top. The leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green in color, and covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's unique spotted leaves and showy inflorescences make it a distinctive addition to gardens.
