Penstemon roezlii

Penstemon roezlii, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Penstemon roezlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine environments.
