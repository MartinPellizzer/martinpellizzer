Prunus × hybrida

Prunus × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid cherry, is a cross between different species of the Prunus genus, often resulting in ornamental varieties prized for their showy flowers.

These plants typically exhibit a range of flower colors, including white, pink, and red, and are cultivated for their aesthetic appeal in gardens. They are deciduous trees or shrubs with simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape.

The fruit, when present, is a small, hard drupe that may be edible or ornamental depending on the cultivar. Prunus × hybrida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
