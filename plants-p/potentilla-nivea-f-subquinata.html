Potentilla nivea f. subquinata

Potentilla nivea f. subquinata, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers and dense, compact growth habit.

It is a subspecies of Potentilla nivea, which is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Tibetan Plateau. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually rounded and arranged in a quinquefoliate pattern, giving it the common name.

The subspecies subquinata is distinguished by its slightly modified leaf structure compared to the typical form.
