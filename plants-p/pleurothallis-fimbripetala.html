Pleurothallis fimbripetala

Pleurothallis fimbripetala, commonly known as the fringed petal pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers with elongated petals. The plant typically features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers are often borne in clusters and can exhibit a range of colors, from white to pale pink, depending on the environmental conditions.
