Phaseolus dysophyllus

Phaseolus dysophyllus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain seeds used in various cuisines. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional content.
