Petastoma ophthalmicum

Petastoma ophthalmicum, commonly known as the eye leaf, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, green leaves that are shaped like eyes, giving it its common name. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, white flowers during the spring season. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Petastoma ophthalmicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unusual appearance.
