Plumeria velutina

Plumeria velutina, commonly known as the velvet tallow tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces a heavy, milky sap that is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
