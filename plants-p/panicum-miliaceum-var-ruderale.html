Panicum miliaceum var. ruderale

Panicum miliaceum var. ruderale, commonly known as wild millet, is a drought-tolerant grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant thrives in poor soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for marginal landscapes. Its seeds are often used as a traditional food source and for forage.

The variety ruderale is particularly noted for its adaptability and resilience in arid environments.
