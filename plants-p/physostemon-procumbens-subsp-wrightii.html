Physostemon procumbens subsp. wrightii

Physostemon procumbens subsp.

wrightii, commonly known as Wright's blue mistflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats or clumps. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.
