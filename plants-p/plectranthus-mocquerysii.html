Plectranthus mocquerysii

Plectranthus mocquerysii, commonly known as the Cape lavender, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its gray-green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus mocquerysii is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate appearance and pleasant scent.
