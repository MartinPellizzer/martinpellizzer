Psoralea tenuiflora var. bigelovii

Psoralea tenuiflora var. bigelovii, commonly known as the narrow-flowered psoralea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It features small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are often trifoliate.

This variety is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its ecological role in desert ecosystems.
