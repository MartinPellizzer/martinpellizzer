Paeonia peregrina var. banatica

Paeonia peregrina var. banatica, commonly known as the Banat peony, is a rare and ancient variant of the peony species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to deep pink, with a distinctive double bloom structure. The plant thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation in temperate climates. Its botanical features include broad, heart-shaped leaves and a robust root system, which contribute to its resilience in natural habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
