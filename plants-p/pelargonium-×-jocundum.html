Pelargonium × jocundum

Pelargonium × jocundum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.
