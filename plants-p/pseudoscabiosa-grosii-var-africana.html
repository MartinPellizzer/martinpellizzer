Pseudoscabiosa grosii var. africana

Pseudoscabiosa grosii var. africana, commonly known as the African scabious, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
