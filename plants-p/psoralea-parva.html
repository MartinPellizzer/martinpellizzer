Psoralea parva

Psoralea parva, commonly known as the small psoralea, is a small annual herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. The plant has slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and borne in clusters, and it produces small, lens-shaped seeds.

Psoralea parva is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
