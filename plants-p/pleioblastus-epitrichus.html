Pleioblastus epitrichus

Pleioblastus epitrichus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching culms that are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in late summer, followed by small, triangular seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
