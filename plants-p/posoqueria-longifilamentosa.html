Posoqueria longifilamentosa

Posoqueria longifilamentosa, commonly known as the long filament fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, delicate, thread-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it is often challenging to cultivate outside its natural habitat.

The fronds are typically bright green and can develop a slight variegation in certain light conditions.
