Platycodon grandiflorus var. striatus

Platycodon grandiflorus var. striatus, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower or striated bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant features upright, hairy stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
