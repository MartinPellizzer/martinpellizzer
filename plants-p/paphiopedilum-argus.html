Paphiopedilum argus

Paphiopedilum argus, commonly known as the golden-lantern orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns resembling lanterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette shape.

Paphiopedilum argus is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.
