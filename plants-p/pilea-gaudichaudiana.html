Pilea gaudichaudiana

Pilea gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are round and fleshy, resembling small coins. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters.

Pilea gaudichaudiana is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
