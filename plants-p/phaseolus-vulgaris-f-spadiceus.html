Phaseolus vulgaris f. spadiceus

Phaseolus vulgaris f. spadiceus, commonly known as the golden bean, is a variant of the common bean plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow pod color and is primarily cultivated for its edible seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value, combined with its culinary uses, makes it a popular choice in both gardens and agricultural settings.
