Piper parcirameum

Piper parcirameum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant has slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by tiny, round berries that turn red when mature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
