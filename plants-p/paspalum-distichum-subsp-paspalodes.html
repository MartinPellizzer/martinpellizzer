Paspalum distichum subsp. paspalodes

Paspalum distichum subsp.

paspalodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
