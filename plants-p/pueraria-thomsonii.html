Pueraria thomsonii

Pueraria thomsonii, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a vigorous climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system, which can form a dense network underground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry systems for soil enrichment.

Pueraria thomsonii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
