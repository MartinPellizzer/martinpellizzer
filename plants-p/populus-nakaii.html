Populus nakaii

Populus nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai's poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Korea, and Russia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous areas.

Populus nakaii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
