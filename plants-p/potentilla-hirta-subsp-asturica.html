Potentilla hirta subsp. asturica

Potentilla hirta subsp.

asturica, commonly known as the Asturian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or coastal regions.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
