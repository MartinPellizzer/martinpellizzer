Persicaria hastatosagittata

Persicaria hastatosagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf knotweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are often hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a persistent weed and a habitat for various invertebrates.
