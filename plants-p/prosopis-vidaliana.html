Prosopis vidaliana

Prosopis vidaliana, commonly known as the vidalia mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand prolonged drought.

Prosopis vidaliana is also valued for its hardy nature and role in stabilizing desert soils.
