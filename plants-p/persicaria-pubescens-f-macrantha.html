Persicaria pubescens f. macrantha

Persicaria pubescens f. macrantha, commonly known as the woolly smartweed or showy smartweed, is a flowering plant characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

It produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as wetlands and stream banks. It is often found in wet or damp environments and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
