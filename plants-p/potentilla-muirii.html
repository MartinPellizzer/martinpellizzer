Potentilla muirii

Potentilla muirii, commonly known as Muir's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, which give it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla muirii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
