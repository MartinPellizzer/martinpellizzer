Pilea supersedens

Pilea supersedens, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its round, rosette-like leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea supersedens thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
