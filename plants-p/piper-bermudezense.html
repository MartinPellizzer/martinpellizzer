Piper bermudezense

Piper bermudezense, commonly known as the Bermudez pepper or Borneo pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a glossy green surface.

The small, clustered fruits are usually green when unripe and turn red or black when mature, containing tiny seeds.
