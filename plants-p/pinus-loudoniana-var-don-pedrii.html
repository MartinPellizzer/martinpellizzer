Pinus loudoniana var. don-pedrii

Pinus loudoniana var. don-pedrii, commonly known as the Spanish red pine, is a variant of the loudoniana pine native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish in color and contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscaping.
