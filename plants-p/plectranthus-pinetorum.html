Plectranthus pinetorum

Plectranthus pinetorum, commonly known as the pineapple mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, serrated leaves with a minty scent, and its clusters of small, tubular flowers bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers. It is often used in aromatherapy and as a culinary herb due to its distinctive flavor.

Plectranthus pinetorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
