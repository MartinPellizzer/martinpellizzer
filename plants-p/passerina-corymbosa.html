Passerina corymbosa

Passerina corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Passerina corymbosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
