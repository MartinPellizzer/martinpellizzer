Polygala persicariifolia f. fazogliana

Polygala persicariifolia f. fazogliana, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are often pale pink or white.
