Pachycarpus rostratus

Pachycarpus rostratus, commonly known as the rostrated pachycarpus, is a shrub native to the Cape Region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.
