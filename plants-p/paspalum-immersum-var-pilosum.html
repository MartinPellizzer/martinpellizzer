Paspalum immersum var. pilosum

Paspalum immersum var. pilosum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often hidden beneath the foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
