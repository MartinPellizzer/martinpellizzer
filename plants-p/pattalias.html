Pattalias

Pattalias, commonly known as Indian bellflowers, are flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

They are characterized by their bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. These plants typically grow in moist, well-drained soils and prefer partial to full sunlight. Pattalias are native to regions of Asia, particularly India and the Himalayas.

Their ornamental value makes them popular in gardens and horticultural collections.
