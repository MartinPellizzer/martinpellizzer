Planchonella rheophytopsis

Planchonella rheophytopsis, commonly known as the streamside hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows near streams and in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom from late winter to early spring. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by birds and water.

Planchonella rheophytopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
