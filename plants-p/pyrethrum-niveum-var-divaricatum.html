Pyrethrum niveum var. divaricatum

Pyrethrum niveum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the white pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal. Its foliage consists of finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a graceful appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
