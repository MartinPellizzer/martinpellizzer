Potentilla recta f. hirsutior

Potentilla recta f. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in well-drained soils.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a notable botanical feature, distinguishing it from other members of the Potentilla genus.
