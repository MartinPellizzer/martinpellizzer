Passiflora adenophylla

Passiflora adenophylla, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature intricate structures such as the corona and five sepals. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a unique, tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Passiflora adenophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
