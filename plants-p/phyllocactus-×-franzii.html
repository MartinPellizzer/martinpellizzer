Phyllocactus × franzii

Phyllocactus × franzii, commonly known as the hybrid cactus, is a unique combination of Phyllocactus and other cactus species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and segmented stems that resemble leaves, a feature typical of its parent species. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
