Penstemon crandallii var. taosensis

Penstemon crandallii var. taosensis, commonly known as Taos penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

Taos penstemon is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
