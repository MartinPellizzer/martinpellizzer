Phymatopsis hastata

Phymatopsis hastata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in arid environments with minimal water. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

Phymatopsis hastata plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
