Polygonum cirrhosum

Polygonum cirrhosum, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Polygonum cirrhosum is often found in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
