Protea polygaloides

Protea polygaloides, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small flowers, which are actually modified leaves called bracts. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees and butterflies, and it produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Protea polygaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
