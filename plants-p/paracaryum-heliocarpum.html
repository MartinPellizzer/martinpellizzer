Paracaryum heliocarpum

Paracaryum heliocarpum, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces fruit that resembles a breadfruit in appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fruit is edible when mature and has a starchy, sweet flesh.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in traditional medicine.
