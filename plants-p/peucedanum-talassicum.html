Peucedanum talassicum

Peucedanum talassicum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often growing in maritime environments. Its tall, slender stems can reach up to 1 meter in height, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Peucedanum talassicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
