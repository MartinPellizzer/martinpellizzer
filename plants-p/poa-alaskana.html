Poa alaskana

Poa alaskana, commonly known as Alaska bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the northwestern United States and Alaska.

It thrives in moist, cool climates and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically blue-green in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish hue that gives it its common name.

Poa alaskana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
