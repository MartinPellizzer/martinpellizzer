Pimpinella atropurpurea

Pimpinella atropurpurea, commonly known as purple bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has fern-like, bipinnate leaves that are finely divided and covered in a soft, downy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pimpinella atropurpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
