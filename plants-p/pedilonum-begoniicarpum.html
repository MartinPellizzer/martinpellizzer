Pedilonum begoniicarpum

Pedilonum begoniicarpum, commonly known as the begonia with a unique fruiting structure, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by distinctive, fleshy berries that are an adaptation for seed dispersal. Its growth habit is mostly herbaceous, with some species exhibiting semi-woody stems.

The botanical features of Pedilonum begoniicarpum make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
