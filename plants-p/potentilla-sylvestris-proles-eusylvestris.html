Potentilla sylvestris proles eusylvestris

Potentilla sylvestris proles eusylvestris, commonly known as the European cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
