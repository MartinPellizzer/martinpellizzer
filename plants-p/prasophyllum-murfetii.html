Prasophyllum murfetii

Prasophyllum murfetii, commonly known as the Murfet's leek orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is a terrestrial herb with a rosette of strap-shaped leaves and produces a single, slender flower spike. The plant is characterized by its pale green to brownish-green foliage and is typically found in heathlands and open forests. Prasophyllum murfetii is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is often found in the south-eastern regions of Australia.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native range.
