Phymatodes lingulata

Phymatodes lingulata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. Its leaves and flowers are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Phymatodes lingulata plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
