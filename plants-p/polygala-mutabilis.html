Polygala mutabilis

Polygala mutabilis, commonly known as the variable milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

The flowers are followed by capsule fruits that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
