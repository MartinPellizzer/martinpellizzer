Pygeum microphyllum

Pygeum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved African wild almond, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain hard, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
