Pachypterygium stocksii

Pachypterygium stocksii, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, star-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
