Phoebe salicifolia

Phoebe salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf phoebe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, willow-like leaves that are alternate and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
