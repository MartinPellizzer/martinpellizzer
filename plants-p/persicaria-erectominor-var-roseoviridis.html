Persicaria erectominor var. roseoviridis

Persicaria erectominor var. roseoviridis, commonly known as the pink-stemmed knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are distinctly pink to reddish in color, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.
