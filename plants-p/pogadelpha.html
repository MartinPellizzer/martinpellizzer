Pogadelpha

Pogadelpha, commonly known as the red-flowered pogadelpha, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in regions with sandy soils and arid climates. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Pogadelpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
