Panicum divaricatum var. puberulum

Panicum divaricatum var. puberulum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive soft texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
