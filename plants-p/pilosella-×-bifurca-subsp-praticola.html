Pilosella × bifurca subsp. praticola

Pilosella × bifurca subsp.

praticola, commonly known as the common hawk's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It is often used in wildflower gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
