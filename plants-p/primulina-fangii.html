Primulina fangii

Primulina fangii, commonly known as the Fang's primulina, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually glossy and oval-shaped, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Primulina fangii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
