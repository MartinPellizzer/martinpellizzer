Pinus glauca

Pinus glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray needles and conical shape, which make it a popular ornamental plant. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its foliage is dense and retains its color year-round, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in landscaping.

Pinus glauca is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
