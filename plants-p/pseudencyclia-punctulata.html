Pseudencyclia punctulata

Pseudencyclia punctulata, commonly known as the spotted cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, palm-like appearance. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. Its leaves are large, pinnate, and composed of numerous narrow, waxy leaflets.

Pseudencyclia punctulata is adapted to arid environments and exhibits a slow growth rate, making it a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
