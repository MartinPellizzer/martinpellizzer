Passiflora quadrangularis var. acubifolia

Passiflora quadrangularis var. acubifolia, commonly known as the Jamaican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and showy, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
