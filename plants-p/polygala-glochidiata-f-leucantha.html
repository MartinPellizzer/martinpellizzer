Polygala glochidiata f. leucantha

Polygala glochidiata f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, while the flowers are usually white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators. This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats.

It is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
