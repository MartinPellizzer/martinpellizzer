Plantago hispida

Plantago hispida, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems with small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and in gardens.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
