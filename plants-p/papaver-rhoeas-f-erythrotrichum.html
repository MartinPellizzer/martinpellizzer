Papaver rhoeas f. erythrotrichum

Papaver rhoeas f. erythrotrichum, commonly known as the scarlet poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its vivid red petals.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and fields. The flowers are typically large, showy, and bloom in the summer months. The plant has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
