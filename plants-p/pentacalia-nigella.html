Pentacalia nigella

Pentacalia nigella, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with five petals, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its toxicity, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
