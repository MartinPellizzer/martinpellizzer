Pandanus spurius

Pandanus spurius, commonly known as the spurious pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.
