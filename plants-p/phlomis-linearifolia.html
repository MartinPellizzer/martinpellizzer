Phlomis linearifolia

Phlomis linearifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf torchflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with soft, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces striking, tall spikes of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, which gives it its specific epithet "linearifolia.".

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
