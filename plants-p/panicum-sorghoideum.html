Panicum sorghoideum

Panicum sorghoideum, commonly known as eastern gamagrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to heights of 2 to 6 meters and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often feathered or bristly in appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in wetlands, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its robust growth makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable forage plant.
