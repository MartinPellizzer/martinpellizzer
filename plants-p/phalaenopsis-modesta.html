Phalaenopsis modesta

Phalaenopsis modesta, commonly known as the modest moth orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces long, arching inflorescences with numerous fragrant, moth-like flowers. The plant typically blooms once a year, with flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are broad, flat, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Phalaenopsis modesta is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
