Prunus jacquemontii

Prunus jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China.

It produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and is often found in rocky or forested habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

Prunus jacquemontii is also noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types and climates.
