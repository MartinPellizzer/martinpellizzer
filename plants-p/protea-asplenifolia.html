Protea asplenifolia

Protea asplenifolia, commonly known as the asplenifoliate protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous individual flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and is also of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations in Mediterranean climates.
