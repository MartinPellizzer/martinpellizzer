Pterocarya spachiana

Pterocarya spachiana, commonly known as the Chinese wingnut, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive winged seeds. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its fruits are large, woody capsules that split open to release the seeds.

Pterocarya spachiana is valued for its ornamental features and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
