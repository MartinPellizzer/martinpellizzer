Peperomia thomsonii

Peperomia thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, often with a glossy surface and varying shades of green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spadices, which are leaf-like bracts. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for collectors and indoor gardeners alike.
