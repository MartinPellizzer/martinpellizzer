Penstemon breviflorus subsp. glabrisepalus

Penstemon breviflorus subsp.

glabrisepalus, commonly known as the smooth-sepaled penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smooth, hairless sepals, which give it its specific epithet. It typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
