Pogostemon parviflorus

Pogostemon parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Pogostemon parviflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
