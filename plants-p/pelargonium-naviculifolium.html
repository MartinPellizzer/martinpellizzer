Pelargonium naviculifolium

Pelargonium naviculifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, boat-shaped leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and its ability to attract pollinators.
