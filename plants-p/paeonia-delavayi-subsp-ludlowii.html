Paeonia delavayi subsp. ludlowii

Paeonia delavayi subsp.

ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan in China. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red, with a distinctive, slightly fragrant scent.

The plant features deeply lobed leaves and produces underground rhizomes, which are typical of the Paeonia genus. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
