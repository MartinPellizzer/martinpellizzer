Padus mahaleb f. monstrosa

Padus mahaleb f. monstrosa, commonly known as the twisted cherry or mock cherry, is a unique variant of the common cherry.

It is characterized by its twisted, gnarled branches and irregular growth form, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are similar to those of the common cherry. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
