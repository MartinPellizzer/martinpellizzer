Pacourina cirsiifolia

Pacourina cirsiifolia, commonly known as the creeping ironweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
