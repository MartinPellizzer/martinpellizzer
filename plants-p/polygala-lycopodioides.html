Polygala lycopodioides

Polygala lycopodioides, commonly known as the small-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, often clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, and it is adapted to grow in arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is a member of the genus Polygala, which includes many species with distinctive floral structures.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant adaptations in dry climates.
