Periballia involucrata

Periballia involucrata, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh environments make it a distinctive species in its ecosystem.

Periballia involucrata is also known for its ability to thrive with minimal water, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
