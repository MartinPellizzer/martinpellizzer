Pachyphyllum gracillimum

Pachyphyllum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise muted appearance. Its slow growth rate and compact size make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Pachyphyllum gracillimum is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it an ideal plant for low-maintenance gardening.
