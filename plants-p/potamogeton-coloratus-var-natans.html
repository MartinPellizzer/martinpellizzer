Potamogeton coloratus var. natans

Potamogeton coloratus var. natans, commonly known as the floating pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

However, it can become invasive in certain water bodies if not managed properly.
