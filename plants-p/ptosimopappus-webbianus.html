Ptosimopappus webbianus

Ptosimopappus webbianus, commonly known as the webbianus thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a head.

The plant produces small, bract-like structures that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds.
