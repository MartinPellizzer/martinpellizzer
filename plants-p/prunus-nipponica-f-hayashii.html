Prunus nipponica f. hayashii

Prunus nipponica f. hayashii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a decorative flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a stunning visual display. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and public spaces. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, hard drupe.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in Japanese traditions.
