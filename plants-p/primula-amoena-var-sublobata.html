Primula amoena var. sublobata

Primula amoena var. sublobata, commonly known as the sublobate primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are oval to lance-shaped, with a distinctive sublobate base that gives the variety its name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
