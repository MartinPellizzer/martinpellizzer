Primula bullata var. forrestii

Primula bullata var. forrestii, commonly known as the Forster's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically oval and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

It prefers cool, moist conditions and is typically found in shaded woodland environments.
