Peucedanum sibiricum

Peucedanum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
