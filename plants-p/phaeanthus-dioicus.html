Phaeanthus dioicus

Phaeanthus dioicus, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has a distinct dichogamous reproductive system, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The botanical features of Phaeanthus dioicus make it a notable species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
