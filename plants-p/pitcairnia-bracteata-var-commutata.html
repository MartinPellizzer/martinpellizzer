Pitcairnia bracteata var. commutata

Pitcairnia bracteata var. commutata, commonly known as the Commutate Pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, recurved leaf rosette and vibrant, long-lasting inflorescence that can range from red to orange in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in many botanical gardens and private collections.
