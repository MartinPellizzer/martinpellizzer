Piper bakeri

Piper bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits.

Piper bakeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
