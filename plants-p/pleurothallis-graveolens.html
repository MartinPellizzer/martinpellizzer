Pleurothallis graveolens

Pleurothallis graveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, white to pale yellow blooms that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, especially in the evening. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique aroma and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
