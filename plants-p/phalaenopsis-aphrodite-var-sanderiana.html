Phalaenopsis aphrodite var. sanderiana

Phalaenopsis aphrodite var. sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a popular epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, arching inflorescences with numerous white to pale pink flowers that resemble moths, hence its common name. The plant has broad, dark green leaves and thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light. It is prized for its elegant appearance and ability to bloom multiple times a year.

This variety is relatively easy to care for, making it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
