Pterocarpus adansonii

Pterocarpus adansonii, commonly known as African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
