Pterodon polygaliflorus

Pterodon polygaliflorus, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens in arid regions.

The yellow bell is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
