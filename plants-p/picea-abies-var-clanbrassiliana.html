Picea abies var. clanbrassiliana

Picea abies var. clanbrassiliana, commonly known as the Clanbrassilian spruce, is a variant of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The cones are typically slender and elongated, with scales that are often reddish-brown when mature. This variety is native to specific regions in Europe and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
