Pyrus sharmae

Pyrus sharmae, commonly known as the Sharma's pear, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears. Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential for hybridization with other pear species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
