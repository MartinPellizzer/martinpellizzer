Pyrus communis var. variegata

Pyrus communis var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

Its foliage features a mix of green, white, and yellow shades, creating an ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, usually non-edible fruits, though some varieties may yield edible pears. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
