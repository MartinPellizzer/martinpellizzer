Prunus spinosa f. fruticans

Prunus spinosa f. fruticans, commonly known as the prickly cherry or blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in making jams and wines, and the plant has historical uses in herbal medicine.
