Pittosporum halophiloides

Pittosporum halophiloides, commonly known as the coastal pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
