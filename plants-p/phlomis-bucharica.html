Phlomis bucharica

Phlomis bucharica, commonly known as the Caucasian blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features upright, branched stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis bucharica is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
