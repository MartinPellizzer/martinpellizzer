Phyllanthus calycinus var. obovatus

Phyllanthus calycinus var. obovatus, commonly known as the obovate leaf phyllanthus, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, obovate-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
