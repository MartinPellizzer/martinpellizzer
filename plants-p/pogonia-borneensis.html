Pogonia borneensis

Pogonia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure and striking, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers typically bloom in the summer months, producing a delicate, fragrant aroma that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat destruction and its elusive nature, Pogonia borneensis is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
