Paphiopedilum fairrieanum f. bohlmannianum

Paphiopedilum fairrieanum f. bohlmannianum, commonly known as the Bohlmann's Paphiopedilum, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique, pouch-like lip that is a hallmark of the genus Paphiopedilum. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, moist environments with rich, organic soil. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple hues, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
