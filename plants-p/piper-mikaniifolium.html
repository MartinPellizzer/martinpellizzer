Piper mikaniifolium

Piper mikaniifolium, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny greenish-yellow flowers that develop into small, edible berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is also valued as a ornamental plant due to its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
