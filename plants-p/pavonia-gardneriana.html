Pavonia gardneriana

Pavonia gardneriana, commonly known as the Mexican flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red to orange-pink bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with compound leaves and showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
