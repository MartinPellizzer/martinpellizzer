Pandanus odoratissimus var. spurius

Pandanus odoratissimus var. spurius, commonly known as the fragrant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its roots and leaves are utilized in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The varietal form, spurius, is distinguished by its unique fragrance and slightly different leaf structure compared to the standard variety.
