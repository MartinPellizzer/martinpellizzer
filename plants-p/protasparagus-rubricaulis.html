Protasparagus rubricaulis

Protasparagus rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed asparagus, is a unique plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems, which are often more pronounced than those of other asparagus species. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typical of many asparagus relatives.

Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
