Phyllanthus analamerae

Phyllanthus analamerae, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Madagascar, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky habitats and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
