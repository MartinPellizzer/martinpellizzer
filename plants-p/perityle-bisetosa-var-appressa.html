Perityle bisetosa var. appressa

Perityle bisetosa var. appressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved perityle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue and a fuzzy texture. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
