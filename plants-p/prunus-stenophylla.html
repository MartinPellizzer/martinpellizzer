Prunus stenophylla

Prunus stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark red to black fruits.

Prunus stenophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its hardiness in colder climates.
