Poa caesia subsp. glauca

Poa caesia subsp.

glauca, commonly known as blue mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that give it its distinctive appearance.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets, which are typically pale blue to silver in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
