Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. linicola

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

linicola, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.

The subspecies linicola is distinguished by its slender stems and reduced leaf size compared to the typical form of Persicaria lapathifolia.
