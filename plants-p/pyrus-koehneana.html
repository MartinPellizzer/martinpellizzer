Pyrus koehneana

Pyrus koehneana, commonly known as the Koehne's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a small, edible pear-like berry.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and plant conservation.
