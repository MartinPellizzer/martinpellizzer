Primula veris var. columnae

Primula veris var. columnae, commonly known as the columnar primrose, is a variant of the common primrose characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit.

This perennial plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
