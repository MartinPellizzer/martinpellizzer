Piper propinquum var. grande

Piper propinquum var. grande, commonly known as the large pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its long, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though it is less commonly cultivated than other pepper species.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.
