Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. latiaxialis

Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. latiaxialis, commonly known as the broad-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically broad and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
