Pyrus koreana

Pyrus koreana, commonly known as the Korean pear or Korean white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet, white fruits in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
