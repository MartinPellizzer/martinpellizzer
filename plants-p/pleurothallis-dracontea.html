Pleurothallis dracontea

Pleurothallis dracontea, commonly known as the dragon's tail orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate and often colorful floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a variety of hues that range from white to deep purples.

Pleurothallis dracontea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
