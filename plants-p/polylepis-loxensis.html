Polylepis loxensis

Polylepis loxensis, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often flaky bark. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is adapted to the harsh, cold, and dry conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are small, alternate, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Polylepis loxensis plays an important ecological role in its environment, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
