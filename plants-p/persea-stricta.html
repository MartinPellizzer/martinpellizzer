Persea stricta

Persea stricta, commonly known as the avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It produces a large, pear-shaped fruit that is rich in healthy fats and nutrients. The tree has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The fruit's pericarp, or outer layer, is typically green and turns dark when ripe.

The plant is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and has significant economic and nutritional value.
