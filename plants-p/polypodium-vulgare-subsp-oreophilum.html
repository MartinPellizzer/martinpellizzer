Polypodium vulgare subsp. oreophilum

Polypodium vulgare subsp.

oreophilum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors.

The plant has a rhizomatous structure that allows it to spread and colonize new areas. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it easily identifiable.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often found in temperate regions.
