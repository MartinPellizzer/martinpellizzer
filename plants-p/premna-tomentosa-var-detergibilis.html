Premna tomentosa var. detergibilis

Premna tomentosa var. detergibilis, commonly known as the woolly mulberry or velvetleaf, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round berries.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
