Paleista procumbens

Paleista procumbens, commonly known as the creeping paleista, is a low-growing perennial plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features wiry, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Paleista procumbens is often found in rocky or clay soils and is valued for its resilience in challenging conditions.
