Pavonia oxyphylla

Pavonia oxyphylla, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling tiny hearts. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Pavonia oxyphylla is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through self-seeding.
