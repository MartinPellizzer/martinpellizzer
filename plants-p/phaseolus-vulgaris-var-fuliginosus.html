Phaseolus vulgaris var. fuliginosus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. fuliginosus, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is typically grown for its high-protein seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
