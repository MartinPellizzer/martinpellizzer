Prunus cornuta f. villosa

Prunus cornuta f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus cornuta species.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, tart cherries. It is typically found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

This cultivar is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
