Pelargonium obnatum

Pelargonium obnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it blooms throughout the warmer months.

Pelargonium obnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
