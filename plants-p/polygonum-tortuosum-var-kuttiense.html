Polygonum tortuosum var. kuttiense

Polygonum tortuosum var. kuttiense, commonly known as the Kuttian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and distinctive, often reddish or purple-hued stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped or ovate, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
