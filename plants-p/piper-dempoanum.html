Piper dempoanum

Piper dempoanum, commonly known as the Dempo pepper or Dempo vine, is a climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth surface and a slightly aromatic scent.

Piper dempoanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
