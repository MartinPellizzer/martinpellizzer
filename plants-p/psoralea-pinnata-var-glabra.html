Psoralea pinnata var. glabra

Psoralea pinnata var. glabra, commonly known as smooth psoralea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense spikes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
