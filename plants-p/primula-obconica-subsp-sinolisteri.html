Primula obconica subsp. sinolisteri

Primula obconica subsp.

sinolisteri, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. It features rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly elongated petals and compact growth habit compared to other Primula species.
