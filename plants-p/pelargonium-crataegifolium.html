Pelargonium crataegifolium

Pelargonium crataegifolium, commonly known as the hawthorn-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, hawthorn-like leaves that are deeply lobed and serrated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
