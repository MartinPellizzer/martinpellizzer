Phylica capitata

Phylica capitata, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and is well adapted to arid environments. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife.
