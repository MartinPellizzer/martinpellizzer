Papaver quintuplinervium

Papaver quintuplinervium, commonly known as the five-veined poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with a prominent central stigma. The plant features finely divided, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
