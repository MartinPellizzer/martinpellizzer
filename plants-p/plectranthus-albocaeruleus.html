Plectranthus albocaeruleus

Plectranthus albocaeruleus, commonly known as the Blue African Mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in clusters. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in ornamental gardening.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
