Paepalanthus macropodus var. glaber

Paepalanthus macropodus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic life. The plant's flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
