Polygonum hastatoauriculatum

Polygonum hastatoauriculatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with auriculate (ear-shaped) bases and serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age.

Polygonum hastatoauriculatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
