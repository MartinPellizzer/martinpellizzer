Piper seemannianum

Piper seemannianum, commonly known as the Siam pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
