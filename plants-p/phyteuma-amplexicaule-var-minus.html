Phyteuma amplexicaule var. minus

Phyteuma amplexicaule var. minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often clasping at the base. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that are clustered in racemes, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting flowers make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
