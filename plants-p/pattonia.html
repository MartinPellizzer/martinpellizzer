Pattonia

Pattonia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, often colorful flowers that bloom in spring and summer. They are popular as houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements. Pattonia species typically thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The genus includes several species, with Pattonia kirkii being one of the most commonly cultivated varieties.
