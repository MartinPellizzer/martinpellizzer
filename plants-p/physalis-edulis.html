Physalis edulis

Physalis edulis, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or husk tomato, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive papery calyx that encases the fruit, which resembles a small, inflated balloon. The fruit is typically orange to red in color and contains a cluster of small, edible berries. Native to the Andes region of South America, it has been cultivated in various parts of the world for its ornamental and culinary uses.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often grown as an annual in cooler regions.
