Plumeria retusa

Plumeria retusa, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its flowers are used in traditional ceremonies and are also popular in horticulture.
