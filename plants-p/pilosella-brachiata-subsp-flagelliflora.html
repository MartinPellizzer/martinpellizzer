Pilosella brachiata subsp. flagelliflora

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

flagelliflora, commonly known as the long-stemmed pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated flower stems, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
