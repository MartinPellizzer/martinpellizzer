Picris arvalis

Picris arvalis, commonly known as common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces yellow flowers from June to August.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
