Psydrax fasciculatus

Psydrax fasciculatus, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect stems and opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
