Petalostemon sonorae

Petalostemon sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Petalostemon sonorae plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its desert habitat.
