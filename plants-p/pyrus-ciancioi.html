Pyrus ciancioi

Pyrus ciancioi, commonly known as the Italian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside.

Pyrus ciancioi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in rocky or dry soils.
