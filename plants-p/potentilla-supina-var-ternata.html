Potentilla supina var. ternata

Potentilla supina var. ternata, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It features woody stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread horizontally along the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla supina var. ternata is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
