Pisum sativum var. hibernicum

Pisum sativum var. hibernicum, commonly known as the Irish pea, is a variety of the common pea plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, edible pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically produces flowers in shades of white or pale purple, which are followed by the characteristic pea pods. It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated for both its edible seeds and ornamental value.

This variety is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is relatively resistant to certain pests and diseases.
