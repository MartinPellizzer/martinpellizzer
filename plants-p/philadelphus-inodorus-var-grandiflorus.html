Philadelphus inodorus var. grandiflorus

Philadelphus inodorus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the sweet shrub or mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in hedging or as a specimen plant.
