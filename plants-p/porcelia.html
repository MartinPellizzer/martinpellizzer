Porcelia

Porcelia, commonly known as the porcelain flower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, glossy leaves that resemble porcelain, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate beauty to its appearance. Porcelia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage and elegant blossoms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
