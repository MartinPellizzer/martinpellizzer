Pycreus plicatus

Pycreus plicatus, commonly known as the pleated fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pleated fronds that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are bioluminescent, a rare trait among ferns, which helps in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant's reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.
