Paphiopedilum erythroanthum

Paphiopedilum erythroanthum, commonly known as the red-flowered slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique slipper-shaped pouch, which is a distinctive feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a contrasting white or yellow lip.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
