Pycreus longistolon

Pycreus longistolon, commonly known as the long-stemmed pycreus, is a rare and unique member of the Pycreus genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, creeping stems that can form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface that helps in moisture retention.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Pycreus longistolon is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
