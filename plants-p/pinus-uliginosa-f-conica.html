Pinus uliginosa f. conica

Pinus uliginosa f. conica, commonly known as the bog pine or the dwarf pine, is a unique form of the jack pine species.

It is characterized by its compact, conical growth habit, which distinguishes it from the more sprawling form of the typical jack pine. This variety thrives in wet, acidic soils, often found in bogs and swampy areas across the northern regions of North America. Its needles are typically shorter and more densely packed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is well adapted to harsh, moist environments and plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
