Phyllanthus ruscoides

Phyllanthus ruscoides, commonly known as the creeping spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Phyllanthus ruscoides is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
