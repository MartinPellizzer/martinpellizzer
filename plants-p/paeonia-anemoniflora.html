Paeonia anemoniflora

Paeonia anemoniflora, commonly known as the Anemone-flowered Peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound, trifoliate leaves with a serrated margin, and its stems are often hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Paeonia anemoniflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
