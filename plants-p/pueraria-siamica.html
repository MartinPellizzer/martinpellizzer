Pueraria siamica

Pueraria siamica, commonly known as the Siam velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Pueraria siamica is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
