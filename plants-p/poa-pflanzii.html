Poa pflanzii

Poa pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that gives it its common name.

Poa pflanzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
