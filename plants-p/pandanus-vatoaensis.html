Pandanus vatoaensis

Pandanus vatoaensis, commonly known as the Vatoaensis pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that often hang in clusters from the plant's stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color, which develop into fibrous fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is an important species in its native habitats.
