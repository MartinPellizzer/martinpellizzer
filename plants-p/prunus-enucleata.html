Prunus enucleata

Prunus enucleata, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, red fruits that are edible but not widely consumed. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Prunus enucleata prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and parks.
