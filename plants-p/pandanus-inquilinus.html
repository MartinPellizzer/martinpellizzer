Pandanus inquilinus

Pandanus inquilinus, commonly known as the hibiscus pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, fibrous leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, coastal environments.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
