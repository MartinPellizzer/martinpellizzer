Paspalum virgatum var. schreberianum

Paspalum virgatum var. schreberianum, commonly known as the Schreber's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
