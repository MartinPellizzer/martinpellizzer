Plantago hirtella f. minor

Plantago hirtella f. minor, commonly known as the hairy plantain or lesser plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along streambanks.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal uses, including its application in treating wounds and respiratory ailments.
