Pouteria macropoda

Pouteria macropoda, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a hard, woody fruit that contains a single large, oily seed, which is the source of the commercially valuable Brazil nut. Its growth is slow, and it can reach heights of up to 40 meters, making it a significant component of the rainforest canopy.

The plant is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
