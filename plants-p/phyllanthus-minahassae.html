Phyllanthus minahassae

Phyllanthus minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa spurge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, which are modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
