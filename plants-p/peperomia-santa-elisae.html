Peperomia santa-elisae

Peperomia santa-elisae, commonly known as the Santa Elisae Peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, velvety foliage with deep green, waxy leaves that are often speckled with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or red, which are followed by round, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia santa-elisae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental leaves and ease of care.
