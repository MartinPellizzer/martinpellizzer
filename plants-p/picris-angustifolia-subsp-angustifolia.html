Picris angustifolia subsp. angustifolia

Picris angustifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features slender, branching stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
