Pleurothallis coccinea

Pleurothallis coccinea, commonly known as the red pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
