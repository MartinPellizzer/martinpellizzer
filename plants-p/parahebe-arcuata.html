Parahebe arcuata

Parahebe arcuata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive zigzag pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its compact form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
