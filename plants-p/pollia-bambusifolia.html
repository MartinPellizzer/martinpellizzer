Pollia bambusifolia

Pollia bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo leaf cycad, is a unique plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves that resemble bamboo in appearance. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its leaves are evergreen and can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its striking visual presence.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
