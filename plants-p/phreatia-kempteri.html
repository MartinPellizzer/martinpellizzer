Phreatia kempteri

Phreatia kempteri, commonly known as the Kempter's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically features a pseudobulb and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Phreatia kempteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
