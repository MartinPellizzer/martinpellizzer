Primula microphylla

Primula microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a compact growth habit with broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
