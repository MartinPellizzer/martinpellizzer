Parkia panurensis

Parkia panurensis, commonly known as the Panurensis Flame Tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red and yellow floral display. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces pods that contain flattened, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
