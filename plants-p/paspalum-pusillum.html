Paspalum pusillum

Paspalum pusillum, commonly known as the small paspalum, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum pusillum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
