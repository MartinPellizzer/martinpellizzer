Pulsatilla patens var. multifida

Pulsatilla patens var. multifida, commonly known as the crown imperial, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces a crown of leaves that are deeply lobed and often hairy. Native to central and eastern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
