Phlox amabilis

Phlox amabilis, commonly known as the garden phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, purple, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces fragrant blooms in the late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of down.

Phlox amabilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
