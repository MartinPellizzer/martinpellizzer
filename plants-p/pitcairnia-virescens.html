Pitcairnia virescens

Pitcairnia virescens, commonly known as the green pitcairnia, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding structure. The plant produces vibrant orange or red inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically long, narrow, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection from intense sunlight.

Pitcairnia virescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and unique growth habit.
