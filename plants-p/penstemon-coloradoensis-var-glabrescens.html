Penstemon coloradoensis var. glabrescens

Penstemon coloradoensis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Colorado penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich blossoms attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
