Piper brevicaule

Piper brevicaule, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces tiny, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its aromatic properties and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists alike.
