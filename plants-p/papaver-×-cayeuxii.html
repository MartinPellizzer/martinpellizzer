Papaver × cayeuxii

Papaver × cayeuxii, commonly known as the French poppy, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver somniferum (opium poppy).

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces showy, papery petals that are often adorned with a dark central eye. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The botanical features of Papaver × cayeuxii make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in various habitats.
