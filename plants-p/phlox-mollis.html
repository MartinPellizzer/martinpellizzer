Phlox mollis

Phlox mollis, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy clusters of flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Phlox mollis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from early summer to late summer.
