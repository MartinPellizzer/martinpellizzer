Pandanus sanderi

Pandanus sanderi, commonly known as the Sander's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanus genus, characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its flowering stems produce small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

The fruit, known as a "pandanus nut," is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor.
