Polygala nicaeensis subsp. corsica

Polygala nicaeensis subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with five petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is a rare and endemic species, making it an important subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
