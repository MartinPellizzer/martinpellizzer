Physalis waterfallii

Physalis waterfallii, commonly known as the waterfall gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America and is characterized by its vibrant orange, inflated calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant typically grows in rocky, mountainous regions and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its fruit, which resembles a small, lantern-like structure, is edible and has a tart, tangy flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and culinary uses.
