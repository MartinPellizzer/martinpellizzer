Paepalanthus pulcher

Paepalanthus pulcher, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant that features a dense covering of white, silky hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle floral display to its otherwise spiny structure. Paepalanthus pulcher thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions and high sunlight exposure.
