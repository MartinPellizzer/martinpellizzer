Polygonum venantianum

Polygonum venantianum, commonly known as the Venetian mallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near waterways.

Polygonum venantianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
