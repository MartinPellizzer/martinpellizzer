Piper berembunense

Piper berembunense, commonly known as the Bornean pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by berries that mature to a dark red color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
