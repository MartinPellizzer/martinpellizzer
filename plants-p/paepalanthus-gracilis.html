Paepalanthus gracilis

Paepalanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus characterized by its dense, spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions. Its unique structure provides habitat for various small animals and insects, while its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
