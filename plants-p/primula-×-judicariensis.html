Primula × judicariensis

Primula × judicariensis, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris, resulting in a plant with distinctive floral characteristics. The species is known for its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, white, or pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
