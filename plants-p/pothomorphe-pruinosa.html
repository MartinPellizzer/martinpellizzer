Pothomorphe pruinosa

Pothomorphe pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf pothomorphe, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Pothomorphe pruinosa is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
