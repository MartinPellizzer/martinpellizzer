Pholiurus persicus

Pholiurus persicus, commonly known as Persian goat willow, is a small tree or shrub native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showing adaptability to dry climates. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces small, round fruits.

The bark is smooth and grayish, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.
