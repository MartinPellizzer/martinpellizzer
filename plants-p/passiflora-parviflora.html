Passiflora parviflora

Passiflora parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and woody stems, which help it climb and spread. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
