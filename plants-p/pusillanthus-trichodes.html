Pusillanthus trichodes

Pusillanthus trichodes, commonly known as the slender hair-leaf plant, is a small, herbaceous species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in late spring.

Pusillanthus trichodes is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
