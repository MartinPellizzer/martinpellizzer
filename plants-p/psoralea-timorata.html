Psoralea timorata

Psoralea timorata, commonly known as Timor psoralea, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
