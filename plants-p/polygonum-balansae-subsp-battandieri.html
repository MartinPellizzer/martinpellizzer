Polygonum balansae subsp. battandieri

Polygonum balansae subsp.

battandieri, commonly known as the Battandieri knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of southern Europe. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible seeds.

The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and the plant often forms dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
