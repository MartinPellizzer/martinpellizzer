Pentas micrantha subsp. wyliei

Pentas micrantha subsp.

wyliei, commonly known as the Wylie's pentas, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.
