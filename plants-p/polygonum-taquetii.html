Polygonum taquetii

Polygonum taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Polygonum taquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
