Pleuridium vulcanicum

Pleuridium vulcanicum, commonly known as the volcanic pleuridium, is a rare and specialized fern that thrives in extreme volcanic environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are adapted to withstand high temperatures and nutrient-poor soils. This plant is typically found in areas with recent volcanic activity, such as lava fields and ash deposits. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination for propagation.

Due to its unique habitat requirements, Pleuridium vulcanicum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in volcanic ecosystems.
