Pereskia argentina

Pereskia argentina, commonly known as the Argentine cactus or the tree cactus, is a unique species native to South America.

It is a shrub-like plant with thick, fleshy stems that are covered in short, soft spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are broad and oval, and they are often mistaken for leaves in other plants.

Pereskia argentina is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
