Physinga acreensis

Physinga acreensis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically opposite and have a smooth, glossy surface.
