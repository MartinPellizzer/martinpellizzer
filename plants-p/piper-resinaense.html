Piper resinaense

Piper resinaense, commonly known as the resinous pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of tiny, aromatic berries. Its most notable feature is the secretion of a yellowish resin from its stems and leaves, which has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and potential medicinal properties.
