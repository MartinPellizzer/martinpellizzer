Plectranthus strigosus var. lucidus

Plectranthus strigosus var. lucidus, commonly known as the glossy plectranthus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and square stems, typical of the Lamiaceae family. The leaves are usually opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a distinctive glossy appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or violet, which are clustered in whorled inflorescences.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
