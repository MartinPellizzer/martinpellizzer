Passiflora gigantifolia

Passiflora gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature intricate patterns of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can become invasive in certain environments.

Passiflora gigantifolia is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly pollinators like bats and birds.
