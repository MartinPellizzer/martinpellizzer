Pisum sativum var. zeylanicum

Pisum sativum var. zeylanicum, commonly known as the Madras bean, is a variant of the common pea that is native to South Asia.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its tendrils and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several round, edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is used in various traditional cuisines.
