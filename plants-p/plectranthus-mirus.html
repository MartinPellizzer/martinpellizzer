Plectranthus mirus

Plectranthus mirus, commonly known as the silverleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus mirus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
