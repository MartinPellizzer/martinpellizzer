Pachyrhizus angulatus

Pachyrhizus angulatus, commonly known as the angular peanut, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular pods and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and oil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both food and forage.

Its root system also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the soil.
