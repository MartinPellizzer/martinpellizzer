Pyrus moschatella

Pyrus moschatella, commonly known as the muscat pear, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape with serrated edges. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are white with a slight fragrance, appearing before the leaves in early spring.
