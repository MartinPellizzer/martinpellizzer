Plectranthus blakei

Plectranthus blakei, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its velvety leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Plectranthus blakei is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
