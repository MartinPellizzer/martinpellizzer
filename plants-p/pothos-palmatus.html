Pothos palmatus

Pothos palmatus, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pothos palmatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying properties.
