Pelargonium gibbosum

Pelargonium gibbosum, commonly known as the African ivy or rock ivy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Pelargonium gibbosum is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
