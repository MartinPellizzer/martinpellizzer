Pardoglossum cheirifolium var. arundanum

Pardoglossum cheirifolium var. arundanum, commonly known as the Arundanum orchid, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, slender stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
