Protea angolensis var. roseola

Protea angolensis var. roseola, commonly known as the roseola protea, is a striking South African flowering plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous papery, brightly colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in coastal and mountainous regions. Its flowering period extends from late summer to early spring, and it is adapted to a Mediterranean climate with dry summers and wet winters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local biodiversity.
