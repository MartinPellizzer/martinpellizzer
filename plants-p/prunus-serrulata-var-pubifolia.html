Prunus serrulata var. pubifolia

Prunus serrulata var. pubifolia, commonly known as the Japanese maple with hairy leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated, pubescent (hairy) leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to botanical collections.
