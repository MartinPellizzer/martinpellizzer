Paphiopedilum barbigerum var. lannaense

Paphiopedilum barbigerum var. lannaense, commonly known as the Lanna slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a thick, fleshy rhizome and a single, hairy pseudobulb. The plant produces showy, lip-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive red or orange callus. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under appropriate conditions.
