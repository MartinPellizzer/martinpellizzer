Plectranthus klossii var. major

Plectranthus klossii var. major, commonly known as the Kloss's plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woolly leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
