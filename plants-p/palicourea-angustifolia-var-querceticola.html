Palicourea angustifolia var. querceticola

Palicourea angustifolia var. querceticola, commonly known as the narrow-leaf palicourea, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, spherical berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.

The leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
