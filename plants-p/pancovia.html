Pancovia

Pancovia, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with partial shade. Pancovia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
