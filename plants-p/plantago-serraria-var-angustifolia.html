Plantago serraria var. angustifolia

Plantago serraria var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
