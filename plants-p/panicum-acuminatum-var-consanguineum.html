Panicum acuminatum var. consanguineum

Panicum acuminatum var. consanguineum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and its ability to form dense mats or clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for use in ornamental landscapes or as a ground cover.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
