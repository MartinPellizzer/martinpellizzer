Pimelea punicea

Pimelea punicea, commonly known as the pink swamp tea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers bloom from late winter to early spring.

Pimelea punicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
