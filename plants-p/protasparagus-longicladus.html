Protasparagus longicladus

Protasparagus longicladus, commonly known as the long-clad asparagus, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its rarity, Protasparagus longicladus is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
