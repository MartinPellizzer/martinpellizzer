Phaius carronii

Phaius carronii, commonly known as the Carron's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces inflorescences with numerous pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Phaius carronii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
