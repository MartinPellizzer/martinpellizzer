Polypodium pohlianum

Polypodium pohlianum, commonly known as the Pohlian's shield fern, is a terrestrial fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and pinnately compound fronds. The fronds are typically dark green, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, shield-like appearance on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
