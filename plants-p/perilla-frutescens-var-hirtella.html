Perilla frutescens var. hirtella

Perilla frutescens var. hirtella, commonly known as Chinese basil or Japanese perilla, is an annual herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and sage. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a mild licorice flavor.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
