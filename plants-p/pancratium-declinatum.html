Pancratium declinatum

Pancratium declinatum, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Pancratium declinatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
