Piper plumanum

Piper plumanum, commonly known as the purple passionvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often purple or reddish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, which are followed by blue-black berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species known for their medicinal properties.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
