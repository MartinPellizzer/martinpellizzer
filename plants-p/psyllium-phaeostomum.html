Psyllium phaeostomum

Psyllium phaeostomum, commonly known as the Indian psyllium, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in loose spikes. Its seeds are rich in mucilage, which is used in traditional medicine and as a dietary fiber supplement.

Psyllium phaeostomum is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it an important plant in dryland agriculture.
