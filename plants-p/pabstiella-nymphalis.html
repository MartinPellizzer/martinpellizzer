Pabstiella nymphalis

Pabstiella nymphalis, commonly known as the yellow-bellied marmot, is a plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant features a low-growing habit with clustered stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Pabstiella nymphalis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and meadows.
