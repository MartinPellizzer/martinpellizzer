Phyllanthus dawsonii

Phyllanthus dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a type of cup-like structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though its use in traditional medicine varies across different cultures.
