Pelargonium rodneyanum

Pelargonium rodneyanum, commonly known as the red-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium rodneyanum is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
