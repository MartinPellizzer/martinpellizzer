Pyrus reimeri

Pyrus reimeri, commonly known as the Reimer's pear, is a small deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. It is typically found in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
