Ptelea trifoliata var. persicifolia

Ptelea trifoliata var. persicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by blue-black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
