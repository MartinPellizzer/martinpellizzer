Pyrus pyraster var. hungarica

Pyrus pyraster var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a variant of the common whitebeam.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and Eastern Europe. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.
