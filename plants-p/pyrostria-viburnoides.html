Pyrostria viburnoides

Pyrostria viburnoides, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually opposite and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, globose drupe that turns red when mature.

Pyrostria viburnoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
