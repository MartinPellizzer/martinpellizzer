Peperomia crispipila

Peperomia crispipila, commonly known as the wavy-leaved peperomia, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, waxy leaves that often have a wavy or serrated edge. The leaves are typically green with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements.
