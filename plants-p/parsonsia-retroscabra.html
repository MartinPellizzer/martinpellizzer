Parsonsia retroscabra

Parsonsia retroscabra, commonly known as the backward-flowered parsonsia, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous, orange-red flowers that face downward, creating a unique visual appearance. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweed and related species. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Parsonsia retroscabra is typically cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique floral orientation.
