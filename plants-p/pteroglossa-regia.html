Pteroglossa regia

Pteroglossa regia, commonly known as the royal pteroglossa, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, vibrant green leaves that form a rosette shape, capable of holding water. Its flowers are showy, with bright orange petals that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
