Phalaenopsis grandiflora var. ruckeri

Phalaenopsis grandiflora var. ruckeri, commonly known as the Rucker's phalaenopsis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with intricate patterns. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a compact rosette, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
