Parthenocissus laetevirens

Parthenocissus laetevirens, commonly known as the Boston ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant is characterized by its ability to climb using adhesive pads on its tendrils, which allow it to cling to surfaces. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces edible, though often bitter, red berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping for its dense, cascading growth habit.
