Psiadia inuloides

Psiadia inuloides, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant features silvery-gray foliage that reflects light, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Psiadia inuloides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal and drought tolerance.
