Piper fortunaense

Piper fortunaense, commonly known as the Fortuna pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
