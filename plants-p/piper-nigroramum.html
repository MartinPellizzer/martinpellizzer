Piper nigroramum

Piper nigroramum, commonly known as black pepper, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark berries that contain the aromatic compound piperine. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit, when dried, is widely used as a spice and in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper nigroramum make it an important crop in both culinary and pharmaceutical industries.
