Pericaulon cordatum

Pericaulon cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf pericula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
