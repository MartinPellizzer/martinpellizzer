Persicaria pubescens

Persicaria pubescens, commonly known as fuzzy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a pubescent surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
