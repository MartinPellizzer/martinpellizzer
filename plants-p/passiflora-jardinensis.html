Passiflora jardinensis

Passiflora jardinensis, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a unique blend of colors, including red, blue, and yellow. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Passiflora jardinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
