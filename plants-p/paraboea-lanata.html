Paraboea lanata

Paraboea lanata, commonly known as the hairy paraboea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

Paraboea lanata is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
