Polystachya maculata subsp. muloloensis

Polystachya maculata subsp.

muloloensis, commonly known as the Mulolo mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern regions of Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
