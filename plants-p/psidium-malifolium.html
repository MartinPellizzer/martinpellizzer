Psidium malifolium

Psidium malifolium, commonly known as the sour guava or false guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
