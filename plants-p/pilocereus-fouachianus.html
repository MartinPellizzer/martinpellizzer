Pilocereus fouachianus

Pilocereus fouachianus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its ability to produce fragrant, white flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are typically short-lived, opening only for a few hours during the night.

Pilocereus fouachianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
