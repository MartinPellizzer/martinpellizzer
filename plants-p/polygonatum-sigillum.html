Polygonatum sigillum

Polygonatum sigillum, commonly known as the Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands.

Its rhizomatous root system makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
