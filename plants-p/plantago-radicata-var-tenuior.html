Plantago radicata var. tenuior

Plantago radicata var. tenuior, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features erect, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that have a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in cultivated fields.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
