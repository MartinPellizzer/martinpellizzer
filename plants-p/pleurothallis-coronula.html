Pleurothallis coronula

Pleurothallis coronula, commonly known as the crown orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structures. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often arranged in a dense cluster. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in moisture retention.

Pleurothallis coronula is well adapted to shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.
