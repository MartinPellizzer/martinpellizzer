Poa pratensis subsp. vulgaris

Poa pratensis subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as meadow grass, is a cool-season grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This subspecies is widely used in turfgrass management due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
