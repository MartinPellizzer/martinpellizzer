Plumeria portoricensis

Plumeria portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican plumeria, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with smooth, pale green bark and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom during the dry season, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in local traditions.
