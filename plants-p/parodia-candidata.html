Parodia candidata

Parodia candidata, commonly known as the White Ghost Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and white or pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
