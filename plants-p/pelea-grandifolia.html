Pelea grandifolia

Pelea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved pelea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
