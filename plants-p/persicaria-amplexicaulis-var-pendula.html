Persicaria amplexicaulis var. pendula

Persicaria amplexicaulis var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging heartleaf knotweed, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its unique foliage and growth habit.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often clasping around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for water gardeners and those interested in native plant species.
