Phyllanthus caroliniensis subsp. guianensis

Phyllanthus caroliniensis subsp.

guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan false mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels, and it produces small, fleshy, berry-like fruits.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
