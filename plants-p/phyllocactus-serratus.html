Phyllocactus serratus

Phyllocactus serratus, commonly known as the serrated phyllocactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for succulent collections.
