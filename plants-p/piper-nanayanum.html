Piper nanayanum

Piper nanayanum, commonly known as the Malabar pepper or small pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
