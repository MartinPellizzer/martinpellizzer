Phyllanthus denticulatus

Phyllanthus denticulatus, commonly known as the African date tree or wild date tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
