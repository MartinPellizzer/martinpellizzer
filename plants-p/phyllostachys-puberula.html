Phyllostachys puberula

Phyllostachys puberula, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the bamboo family, Gramineae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, silver-green culms covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green color that adds to its aesthetic appeal.

Phyllostachys puberula is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
