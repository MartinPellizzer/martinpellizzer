Penstemon procerus var. procerus

Penstemon procerus var. procerus, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray hue.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
