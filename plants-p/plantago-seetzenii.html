Plantago seetzenii

Plantago seetzenii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically arranged in a terminal cluster. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

This species is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.
