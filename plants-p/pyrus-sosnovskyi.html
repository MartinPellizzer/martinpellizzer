Pyrus sosnovskyi

Pyrus sosnovskyi, commonly known as the Sosnovsky's gooseberry, is a species of shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, tart berries that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained soils. It has alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruits.
