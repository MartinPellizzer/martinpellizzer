Parathesis columnaris

Parathesis columnaris, commonly known as the columnar thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces spiny, purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Parathesis columnaris is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
