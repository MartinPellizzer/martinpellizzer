Plectritis davyana

Plectritis davyana, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woods, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Plectritis davyana is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
