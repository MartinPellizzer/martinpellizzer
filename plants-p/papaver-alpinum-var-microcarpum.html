Papaver alpinum var. microcarpum

Papaver alpinum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its small, capsule-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across mountainous landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine setting.
