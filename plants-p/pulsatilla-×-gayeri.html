Pulsatilla × gayeri

Pulsatilla × gayeri, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It produces a fibrous root system and has compound leaves with serrated edges.

Pulsatilla × gayeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
