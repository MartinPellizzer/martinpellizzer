Polygonum convolvulus var. heterophyllum

Polygonum convolvulus var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the heterophyllous knotweed, is a variant of the common knotweed species.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, which varies depending on environmental conditions. The plant typically grows as a prostrate or creeping perennial, with stems that can become woody over time. Its small, pink or white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of habitats, including gardens and agricultural fields.
