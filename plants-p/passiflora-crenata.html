Passiflora crenata

Passiflora crenata, commonly known as the serrated passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with serrated edges and its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The flowers are typically white or pale blue with a central cluster of red filaments, and they bloom during the summer months. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates.
