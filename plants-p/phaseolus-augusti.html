Phaseolus augusti

Phaseolus augusti, commonly known as the August bean, is a species of legume native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, bean-like fruits that mature to a yellowish or brownish color. The plant typically grows in cool, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Phaseolus augusti is valued for its nutritional content and has been traditionally used in local cuisines.
