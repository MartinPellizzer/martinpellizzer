Persea negracotensis

Persea negracotensis, commonly known as the black-fruit persea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly found in the understory of humid forests. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a drupe, typically dark purple to black when mature, and is edible.

Persea negracotensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
