Porophyllum greggii

Porophyllum greggii, commonly known as Gregg's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped, providing a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification.
