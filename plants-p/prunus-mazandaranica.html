Prunus mazandaranica

Prunus mazandaranica, commonly known as the Khorassan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, sweet-tart berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
