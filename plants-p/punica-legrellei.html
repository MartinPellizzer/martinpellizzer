Punica legrellei

Punica legrellei, commonly known as the Indian pomegranate, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Punicaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruit, similar to the common pomegranate, is edible and contains numerous seeds surrounded by a juicy, tart aril.

Punica legrellei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
