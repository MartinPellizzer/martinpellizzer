Platycodon mariesii f. albonanus

Platycodon mariesii f. albonanus, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a distinctive cultivar of the bellflower genus.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical blue or purple hues of the species. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique coloration.
