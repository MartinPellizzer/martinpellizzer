Panicum earlei

Panicum earlei, commonly known as the Earlei panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Panicum earlei plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
