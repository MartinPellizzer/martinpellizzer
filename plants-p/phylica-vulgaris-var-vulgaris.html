Phylica vulgaris var. vulgaris

Phylica vulgaris var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common hakea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, cone-shaped flower clusters that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fibrous, woody coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in various parts of the world.
