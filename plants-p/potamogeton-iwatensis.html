Potamogeton iwatensis

Potamogeton iwatensis, commonly known as the Iwat Islands watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Its presence is important for maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
