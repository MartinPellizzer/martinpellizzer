Phrynium laterale

Phrynium laterale, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne on tall, leafless stems. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araceae, which includes other plants such as taro and calla lilies.
