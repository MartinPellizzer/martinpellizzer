Plectranthus longitubus

Plectranthus longitubus, commonly known as the long-tubed plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and containers.

Plectranthus longitubus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
