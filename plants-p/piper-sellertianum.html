Piper sellertianum

Piper sellertianum, commonly known as the sacred pepper or wild betel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are sometimes used in rituals and are also a source of essential oils.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has cultural significance in various regions.
