Prunus pachyclada

Prunus pachyclada, commonly known as the Chinese date tree or the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that resemble dates in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Prunus pachyclada is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its various health benefits.
