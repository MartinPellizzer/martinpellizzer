Polygonum infestum

Polygonum infestum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into three-winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many regions.
