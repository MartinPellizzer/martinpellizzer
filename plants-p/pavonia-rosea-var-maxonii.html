Pavonia rosea var. maxonii

Pavonia rosea var. maxonii, commonly known as the pink wood rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
