Pyrus shansiensis

Pyrus shansiensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
