Phyllorkis caespitosa

Phyllorkis caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted rock-heath, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland areas. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
