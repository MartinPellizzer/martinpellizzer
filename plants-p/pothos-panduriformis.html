Pothos panduriformis

Pothos panduriformis, commonly known as the heartleaf pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with shades of green and cream. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by orange-red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Pothos panduriformis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
