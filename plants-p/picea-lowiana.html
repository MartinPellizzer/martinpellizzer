Picea lowiana

Picea lowiana, commonly known as the Sitka spruce, is a large evergreen conifer native to the coastal regions of southeast Alaska and western Canada.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage that turns a striking silver-blue in the winter. The tree produces small, cone-like fruits that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its roots are extensive and help stabilize coastal soils, making it an important species in forest ecosystems.

Picea lowiana is also valued for its strong, lightweight wood used in construction and musical instruments.
