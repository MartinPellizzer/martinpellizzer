Pterolophus lanceolatus

Pterolophus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate acanthus, is a striking plant characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a dense foliage that makes it suitable for both ornamental and garden use.

Pterolophus lanceolatus thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.
