Pseudopodospermum suberosum subsp. suberosum

Pseudopodospermum suberosum subsp.

suberosum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed cycad, is a unique cycad species characterized by its robust, woody stem and dense foliage. It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and has a distinctive appearance with its large, pinnate leaves. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
