Polystachya poikilantha var. leucorhoda

Polystachya poikilantha var. leucorhoda, commonly known as the white-flowered staghorn fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear numerous white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a thick, leathery rhizome that serves as a storage organ, and its leaves are typically broad and strap-like. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
