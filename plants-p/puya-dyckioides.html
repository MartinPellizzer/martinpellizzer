Puya dyckioides

Puya dyckioides, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are long, narrow, and form a rosette that collects rainwater, aiding in its survival in high-altitude environments.

Puya dyckioides is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
