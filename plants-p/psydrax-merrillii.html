Psydrax merrillii

Psydrax merrillii, commonly known as the Malabar cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

Psydrax merrillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
