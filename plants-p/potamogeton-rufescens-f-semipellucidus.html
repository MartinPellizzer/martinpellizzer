Potamogeton rufescens f. semipellucidus

Potamogeton rufescens f. semipellucidus, commonly known as the translucent pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves.

The leaves are typically thin and semi-transparent, allowing light to pass through, which is a distinguishing feature of this form. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the base of the stems.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and a healthy aquatic ecosystem.
