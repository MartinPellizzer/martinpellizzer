Piper carniovarium

Piper carniovarium, commonly known as the red-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive red veins on the leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, narrow berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
