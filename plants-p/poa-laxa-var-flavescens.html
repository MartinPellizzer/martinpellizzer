Poa laxa var. flavescens

Poa laxa var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a yellowish hue.

This variation is notable for its coloration and ecological adaptability.
