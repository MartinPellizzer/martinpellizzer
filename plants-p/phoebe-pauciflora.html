Phoebe pauciflora

Phoebe pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, which contains a single seed.

Phoebe pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
