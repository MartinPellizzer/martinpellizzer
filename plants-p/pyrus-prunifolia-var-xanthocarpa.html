Pyrus prunifolia var. xanthocarpa

Pyrus prunifolia var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited pear, is a variant of the common pear species characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange fruit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the flowers are white with a pinkish tinge, appearing in early spring.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is small, sweet, and has a crisp texture, making it suitable for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
