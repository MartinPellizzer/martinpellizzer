Pterostylis tylosa

Pterostylis tylosa, commonly known as the pink-stained terrestrial orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, slender, pinkish-brown flower spike. The flower has a distinctive, hood-like labellum that is often adorned with a prominent, pinkish stain. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
