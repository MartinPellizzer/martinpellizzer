Polygonum bidwelliae

Polygonum bidwelliae, commonly known as Bidwell's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streambanks. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to red in color, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Polygonum bidwelliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
