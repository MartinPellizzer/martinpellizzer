Pterocarpus marsupium var. acuminata

Pterocarpus marsupium var. acuminata, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and distinctive, palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its timber and has traditional medicinal uses in Ayurvedic medicine.
