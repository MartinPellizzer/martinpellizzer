Plantago psyllium var. parviflora

Plantago psyllium var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered plantain, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

This species is known for its medicinal uses, particularly for its mucilage-rich seeds, which are used in herbal remedies.
