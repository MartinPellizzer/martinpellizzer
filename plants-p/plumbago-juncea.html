Plumbago juncea

Plumbago juncea, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a woody stem, which contribute to its adaptability in various climates.
