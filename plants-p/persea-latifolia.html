Persea latifolia

Persea latifolia, commonly known as the Cuban avocado or broadleaf avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruit is a large, pear-shaped berry with a thick, smooth skin and a creamy, oily flesh.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients.
