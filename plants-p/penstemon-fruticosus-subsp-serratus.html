Penstemon fruticosus subsp. serratus

Penstemon fruticosus subsp.

serratus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
