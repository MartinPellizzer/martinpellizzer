Primula elliptica

Primula elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features upright stems with ovate to elliptic-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
