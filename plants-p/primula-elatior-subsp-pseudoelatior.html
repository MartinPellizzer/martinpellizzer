Primula elatior subsp. pseudoelatior

Primula elatior subsp.

pseudoelatior, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is known for its ability to bloom in early spring.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
