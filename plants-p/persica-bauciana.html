Persica bauciana

Persica bauciana, commonly known as the Baucian peach, is a species native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a small tree or shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically small and yellow when ripe, with a sweet, juicy flesh. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other peach species.
