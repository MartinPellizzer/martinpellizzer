Pomaderris sieberiana

Pomaderris sieberiana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often silver-haired stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces yellow flowers in globular clusters, which appear in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and heathland habitats.

Pomaderris sieberiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators.
