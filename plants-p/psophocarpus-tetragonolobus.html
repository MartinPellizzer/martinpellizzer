Psophocarpus tetragonolobus

Psophocarpus tetragonolobus, commonly known as the winged bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with four oval leaflets and distinctive wing-like structures along its stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds and fleshy, nutritious arils. It is valued for its high protein content and is cultivated for both food and forage.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
