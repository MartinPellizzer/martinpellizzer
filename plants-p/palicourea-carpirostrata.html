Palicourea carpirostrata

Palicourea carpirostrata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often glandular leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
