Phyllanthus loandensis

Phyllanthus loandensis, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Phyllanthus loandensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
