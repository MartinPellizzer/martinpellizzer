Palmijuncus micranthus

Palmijuncus micranthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved palm, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, tree-like plant characterized by its slender trunk and clustered, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color, followed by the development of small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
