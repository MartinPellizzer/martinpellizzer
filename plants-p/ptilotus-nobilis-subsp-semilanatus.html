Ptilotus nobilis subsp. semilanatus

Ptilotus nobilis subsp.

semilanatus, commonly known as the yellow bush morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.
