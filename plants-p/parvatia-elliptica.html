Parvatia elliptica

Parvatia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf parvatia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Parvatia elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
