Pentacalia sabinoi

Pentacalia sabinoi, commonly known as the Sabino's pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, sprawling shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters, and bloom primarily in the spring.

It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
