Pelargonium pallidoflavum

Pelargonium pallidoflavum, commonly known as the pale yellow geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are usually covered in fine hairs.

Pelargonium pallidoflavum is cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
