Pandanus racemosus

Pandanus racemosus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense canopy. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are followed by fibrous, spiky fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in many island communities.
