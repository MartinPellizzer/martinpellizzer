Podionapus flexuosus

Podionapus flexuosus, commonly known as the creeping spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive, waxy texture, often with a bluish or purplish hue.

Podionapus flexuosus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
