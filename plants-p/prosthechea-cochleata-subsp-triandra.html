Prosthechea cochleata subsp. triandra

Prosthechea cochleata subsp.

triandra, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive, curling appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.
