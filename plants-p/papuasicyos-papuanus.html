Papuasicyos papuanus

Papuasicyos papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan bottle tree, is a unique plant native to the Pacific islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, an adaptation to its arid and seasonal environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, helping to reduce water loss through transpiration.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
