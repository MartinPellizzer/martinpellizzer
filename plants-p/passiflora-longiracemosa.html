Passiflora longiracemosa

Passiflora longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a unique arrangement of sepals and petals. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora longiracemosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
