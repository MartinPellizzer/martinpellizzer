Pseudodictamnus mediterraneus subsp. mediterraneus

Pseudodictamnus mediterraneus subsp.

mediterraneus, commonly known as Mediterranean pseudodictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features upright, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a powdery, white bloom.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly aromatic, with a distinct scent when crushed.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.
