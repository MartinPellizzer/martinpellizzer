Pennantia cunninghamii

Pennantia cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain hakea, is an evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Pennantia cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
