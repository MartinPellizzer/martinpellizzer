Polypodium nigrescens

Polypodium nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called soruses on the underside of its leaves. Polypodium nigrescens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
