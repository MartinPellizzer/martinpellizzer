Polygonum giranense

Polygonum giranense, commonly known as the Giran knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by distinctive nodes along its stems and small, often clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces tiny greenish-white flowers in late summer.

Polygonum giranense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
