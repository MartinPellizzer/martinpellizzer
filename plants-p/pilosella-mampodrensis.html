Pilosella mampodrensis

Pilosella mampodrensis, commonly known as the Mampodre bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to the region's unique climate. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
