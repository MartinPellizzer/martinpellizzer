Platanthera blephariglottis var. holopetala

Platanthera blephariglottis var. holopetala, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale green flowers, which are often adorned with dark red or purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, and it produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is often considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
