Pleroma capitatum

Pleroma capitatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Pleroma capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
