Pygeum apiculatum

Pygeum apiculatum, commonly known as the African wild almond or red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional medicine.
