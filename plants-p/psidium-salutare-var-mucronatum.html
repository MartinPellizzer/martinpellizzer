Psidium salutare var. mucronatum

Psidium salutare var. mucronatum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or mucronate guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various regions for both its fruit and medicinal properties.
