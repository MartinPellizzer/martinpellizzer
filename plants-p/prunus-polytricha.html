Prunus polytricha

Prunus polytricha, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is renowned for its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink or white blossoms that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, oval fruits called drupes, which are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Prunus polytricha is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its beauty and cultural significance.
