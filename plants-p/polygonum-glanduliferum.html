Polygonum glanduliferum

Polygonum glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Polygonum glanduliferum is often found in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows.
