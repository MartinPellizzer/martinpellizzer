Pogonia maderoi

Pogonia maderoi, commonly known as the Madera bog orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, showy violet or purple flower. The plant thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and requires a consistently moist environment. It is often found in sphagnum moss-dominated bogs and is highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pogonia maderoi is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
