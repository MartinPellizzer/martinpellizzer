Pachyptera ventricosa

Pachyptera ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

Pachyptera ventricosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
