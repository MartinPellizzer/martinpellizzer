Pithecellobium teysmannii

Pithecellobium teysmannii, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
