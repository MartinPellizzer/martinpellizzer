Poulsenia aculeata

Poulsenia aculeata, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a tall, single-stemmed tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the top of the trunk. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into dark purple fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
