Pteronia hirsuta

Pteronia hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pteronia hirsuta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
