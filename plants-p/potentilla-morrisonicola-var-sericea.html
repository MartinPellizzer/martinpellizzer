Potentilla morrisonicola var. sericea

Potentilla morrisonicola var. sericea, commonly known as the silver potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in colder climates.
