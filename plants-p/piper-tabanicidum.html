Piper tabanicidum

Piper tabanicidum, commonly known as the poison pepper or tabanid pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn red, and the plant is known for its potent chemical defenses against herbivores.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
