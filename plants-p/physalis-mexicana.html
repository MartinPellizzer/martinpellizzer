Physalis mexicana

Physalis mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries enclosed in a papery calyx that turns red as the fruit matures. These berries are edible when fully ripe and have a tart, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a minor food crop in some regions.
