Podocarpus neriifolius var. ridleyi

Podocarpus neriifolius var. ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's satinwood, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a prominent midrib. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones that contain edible seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for landscaping purposes.
