Pyrola uliginosa

Pyrola uliginosa, commonly known as the bog pink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in acidic, moist environments such as bogs and heathlands. The plant features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that help retain moisture. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered at the top of the stem.

Pyrola uliginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
