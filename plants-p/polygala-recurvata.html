Polygala recurvata

Polygala recurvata, commonly known as the recurved milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, known for its distinctive, often zigzag-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
