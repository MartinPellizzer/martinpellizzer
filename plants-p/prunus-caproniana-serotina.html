Prunus caproniana serotina

Prunus caproniana serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single white to pink flowers that bloom late in the spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits known as cherries, which ripen in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

Prunus caproniana serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
