Persea macrocarpa

Persea macrocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces large, orange to reddish-brown fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a high sugar content and a unique, sweet flavor.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic wood.
