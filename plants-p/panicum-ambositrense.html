Panicum ambositrense

Panicum ambositrense, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
