Prunus salzeri

Prunus salzeri, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually gland-dotted and have a slightly serrated edge. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

Prunus salzeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
