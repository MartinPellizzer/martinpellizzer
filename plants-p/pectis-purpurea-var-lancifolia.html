Pectis purpurea var. lancifolia

Pectis purpurea var. lancifolia, commonly known as the purple pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers that bloom in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
