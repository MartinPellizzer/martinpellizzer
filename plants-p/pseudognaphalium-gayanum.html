Pseudognaphalium gayanum

Pseudognaphalium gayanum, commonly known as the Gamanthi or Gayanum daisy, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It has small, hairy leaves and produces numerous small flowers that bloom in summer.

Pseudognaphalium gayanum is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
