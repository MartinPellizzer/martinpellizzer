Primula latifolia subsp. iungens

Primula latifolia subsp.

iungens, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features semi-evergreen foliage with broad, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and rockeries.
