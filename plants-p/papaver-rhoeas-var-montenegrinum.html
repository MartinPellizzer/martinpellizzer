Papaver rhoeas var. montenegrinum

Papaver rhoeas var. montenegrinum, commonly known as the Montenegro poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its striking red petals and a distinctive yellow center.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to the mountainous regions of Montenegro, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to meadows and disturbed soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
