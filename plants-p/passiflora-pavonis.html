Passiflora pavonis

Passiflora pavonis, commonly known as the velvet passion flower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy, sweet flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Passiflora pavonis is cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
