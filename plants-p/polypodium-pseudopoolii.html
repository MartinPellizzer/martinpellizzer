Polypodium pseudopoolii

Polypodium pseudopoolii, commonly known as the false huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium pseudopoolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
