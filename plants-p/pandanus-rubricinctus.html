Pandanus rubricinctus

Pandanus rubricinctus, commonly known as the red-veined pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are often bright green with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in clusters at the tips of the leaves. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while the fruit is edible and has a sweet, fibrous pulp.

Pandanus rubricinctus thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.
