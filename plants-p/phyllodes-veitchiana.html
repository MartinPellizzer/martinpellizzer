Phyllodes veitchiana

Phyllodes veitchiana, commonly known as the Veitch's glory tree, is a species of acacia native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are often mistaken for leaves but are actually modified stems. These phyllodes are typically narrow and linear, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they play a crucial role in photosynthesis. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months.

Phyllodes veitchiana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
