Pittosporum pentandrum var. hainanense

Pittosporum pentandrum var. hainanense, commonly known as the Hainan pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes hairy underside. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring and are followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
