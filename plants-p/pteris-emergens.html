Pteris emergens

Pteris emergens, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bright green and often have a waxy coating. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources such as streams and ponds. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in spreading the plant across the ground.

Pteris emergens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
