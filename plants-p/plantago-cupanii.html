Plantago cupanii

Plantago cupanii, commonly known as the cupani's plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in rocky and coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
