Paramignya

Paramignya, commonly known as the "Indian mulberry," is a deciduous tree native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fuzzy fruits. The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines. Its bark and leaves have also been used in herbal medicine for their medicinal properties.

Paramignya thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
