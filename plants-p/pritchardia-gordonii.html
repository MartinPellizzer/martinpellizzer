Pritchardia gordonii

Pritchardia gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's palm, is a species of cycad native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, Pritchardia gordonii is considered a vulnerable species.
