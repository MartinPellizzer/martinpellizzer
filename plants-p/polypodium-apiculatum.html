Polypodium apiculatum

Polypodium apiculatum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
