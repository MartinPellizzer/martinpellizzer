Prostanthera saxicola

Prostanthera saxicola, commonly known as the mountain mint bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, oval-shaped berries.

Prostanthera saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
