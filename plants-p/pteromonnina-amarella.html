Pteromonnina amarella

Pteromonnina amarella, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow ray florets and a central disk of tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Pteromonnina amarella is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
