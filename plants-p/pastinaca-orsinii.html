Pastinaca orsinii

Pastinaca orsinii, commonly known as the Italian burdock.

It is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spiky, bur-like fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.

Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
