Populus nana

Populus nana, commonly known as dwarf balm of Gilead, is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are catkin-like, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Populus nana is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
