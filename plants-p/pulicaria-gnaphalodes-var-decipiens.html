Pulicaria gnaphalodes var. decipiens

Pulicaria gnaphalodes var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive pulicaria, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and grayish-green foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic properties and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
