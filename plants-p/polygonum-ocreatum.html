Polygonum ocreatum

Polygonum ocreatum, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and streambanks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
