Panicum boscii

Panicum boscii, commonly known as the Bosci grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers that are often tinged with purple or red.

Panicum boscii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
