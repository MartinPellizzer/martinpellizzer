Polygonum emodi

Polygonum emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Polygonum emodi is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine systems.
