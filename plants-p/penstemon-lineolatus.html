Penstemon lineolatus

Penstemon lineolatus, commonly known as the spotted beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Penstemon lineolatus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to support local wildlife.
