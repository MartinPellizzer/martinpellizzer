Palaquium krantzianum

Palaquium krantzianum, commonly known as the Krantz's wax apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits. The plant produces a sticky, resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structure.

The tree is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its potential economic uses.
