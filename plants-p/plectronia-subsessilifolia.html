Plectronia subsessilifolia

Plectronia subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectronia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
