Parietaria obtusa

Parietaria obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved wall-rocket, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and small white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found growing on walls or in shaded areas.

Parietaria obtusa is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
