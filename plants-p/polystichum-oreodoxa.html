Polystichum oreodoxa

Polystichum oreodoxa, commonly known as the broad-leafed wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern characterized by its large, leathery, bipinnate fronds.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with the rhizomes often covered in a dense layer of brown, hairy scales. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Polystichum oreodoxa is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
