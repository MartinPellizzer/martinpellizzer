Poa grimburgii

Poa grimburgii, commonly known as the grimburg's meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, Poa grimburgii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
