Phaseoloides mannii

Phaseoloides mannii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, often branched stems. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

Its deep root system helps in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
