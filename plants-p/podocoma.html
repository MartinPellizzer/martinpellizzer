Podocoma

Podocoma, commonly known as the desert holly, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

These plants are characterized by their succulent leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, white, and pink. Podocoma species are typically low-growing shrubs with a compact, bushy form, adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Their fleshy stems and leaves store water, allowing them to thrive in conditions with minimal rainfall.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
