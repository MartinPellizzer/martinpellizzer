Polybotrya cervina f. transitoria

Polybotrya cervina f. transitoria, commonly known as the transient coral tree, is a rare and intriguing botanical specimen native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This fern-like plant exhibits unique morphological features, including its delicate, branching fronds that resemble coral structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. The plant's foliage is characterized by its vibrant green color and intricate, feather-like leaves.
