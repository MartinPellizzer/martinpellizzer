Prunus domestica subsp. pomariorum

Prunus domestica subsp.

pomariorum, commonly known as the Italian prune, is a subspecies of the common cherry plum. It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically used in jams, jellies, and dried fruit products.

The plant produces white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive purple or black berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

The subspecies is closely related to other Prunus species and shares similar growth habits and ecological preferences.
