Piper speculatoris

Piper speculatoris, commonly known as the ghost pepper or Bhut jolokia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of India.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and these berries contain a high concentration of capsaicin, making them extremely spicy. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that grow in clusters.
