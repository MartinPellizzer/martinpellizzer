Phyllostegia heterodoxa

Phyllostegia heterodoxa, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllostegia, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Phyllostegia heterodoxa thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
