Piper marginatum

Piper marginatum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant produces tiny, red berries that are harvested when mature for their pungent, peppery flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
