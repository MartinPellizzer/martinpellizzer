Posoqueria grandiflora

Posoqueria grandiflora, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant features showy white or pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its roots anchor it in the muddy bottom of slow-moving or still waters.

Posoqueria grandiflora plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
