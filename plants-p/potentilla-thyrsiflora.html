Potentilla thyrsiflora

Potentilla thyrsiflora, commonly known as the thrysothorn, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Potentilla thyrsiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
