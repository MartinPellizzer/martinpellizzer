Polystichum proliferum

Polystichum proliferum, commonly known as the spreading wood fern, is a perennial fern native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its dense, bushy fronds that often form a compact, mat-like growth. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a dark green, glossy appearance and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystichum proliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
