Pulsatilla polyscapa

Pulsatilla polyscapa, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem with several branches, and its leaves are basal, palmately lobed, and hairy. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant is part of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
