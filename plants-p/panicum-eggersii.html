Panicum eggersii

Panicum eggersii, commonly known as Eggers' panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
