Polygala lonchophylla

Polygala lonchophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish and have a distinctive, spurred structure.

It is often found in open vegetation types such as grasslands and shrublands.
