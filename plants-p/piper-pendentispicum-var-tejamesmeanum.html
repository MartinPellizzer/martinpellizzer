Piper pendentispicum var. tejamesmeanum

Piper pendentispicum var. tejamesmeanum, commonly known as the hanging pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by black, peppery fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises in gardens.
