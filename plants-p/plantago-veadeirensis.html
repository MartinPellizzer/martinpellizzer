Plantago veadeirensis

Plantago veadeirensis, commonly known as the Veadeirensis Plantain, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its robust root system and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
