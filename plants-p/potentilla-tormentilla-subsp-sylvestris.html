Potentilla tormentilla subsp. sylvestris

Potentilla tormentilla subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in woodland edges, open woodlands, and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
