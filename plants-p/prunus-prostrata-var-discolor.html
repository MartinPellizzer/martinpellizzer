Prunus prostrata var. discolor

Prunus prostrata var. discolor, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf cherry or weeping cherry, is a compact, shrub-like species native to Japan.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often with a slight reddish tinge. The leaves are ovate, glossy, and turn a striking red or orange in the fall. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a bonsai specimen.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
