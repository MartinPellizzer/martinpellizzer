Polypodium peruvianum var. subgibbosum

Polypodium peruvianum var. subgibbosum, commonly known as the Peruvian holly fern, is a tropical fern species native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves with a serrated margin, often arranged in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread to form clumps, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening in moist, partially shaded areas.
