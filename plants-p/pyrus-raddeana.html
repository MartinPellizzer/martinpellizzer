Pyrus raddeana

Pyrus raddeana, commonly known as the Russian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
