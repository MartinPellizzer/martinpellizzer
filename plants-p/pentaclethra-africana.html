Pentaclethra africana

Pentaclethra africana, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to West and Central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in panicles. The tree yields a valuable hardwood that is highly sought after for its durability and fine grain. Its seeds are encased in a thick, woody pod and are dispersed by animals.

Due to overharvesting, it is also considered an endangered species in some regions.
