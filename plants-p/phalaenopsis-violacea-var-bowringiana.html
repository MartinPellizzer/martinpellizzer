Phalaenopsis violacea var. bowringiana

Phalaenopsis violacea var. bowringiana, commonly known as the Bowring's Phalaenopsis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper conditions.
