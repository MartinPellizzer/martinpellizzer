Panicum hookerianum

Panicum hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
