Psacalium amplifolium

Psacalium amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf psacalium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
