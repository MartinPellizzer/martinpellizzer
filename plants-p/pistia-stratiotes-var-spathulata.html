Pistia stratiotes var. spathulata

Pistia stratiotes var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf water lettuce, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically light green to reddish in color, with a waxy surface that helps repel water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in non-native habitats, impacting local ecosystems.
