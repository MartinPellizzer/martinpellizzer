Pavonia guerkeana var. minor

Pavonia guerkeana var. minor, commonly known as the small pavonia or dwarf pavonia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in the summer months. Its foliage is typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, hairy texture.

It is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
