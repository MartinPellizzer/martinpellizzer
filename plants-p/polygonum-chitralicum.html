Polygonum chitralicum

Polygonum chitralicum, commonly known as the Chitral knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracteate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Polygonum chitralicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
