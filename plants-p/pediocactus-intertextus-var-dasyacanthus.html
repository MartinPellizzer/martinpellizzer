Pediocactus intertextus var. dasyacanthus

Pediocactus intertextus var. dasyacanthus, commonly known as the dense-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and desert gardeners.
