Panicum mystasipum

Panicum mystasipum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy panicles that turn a striking purple hue as they mature. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum mystasipum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
