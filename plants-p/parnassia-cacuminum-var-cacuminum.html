Parnassia cacuminum var. cacuminum

Parnassia cacuminum var. cacuminum, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad, oval, and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
