Pittosporum callicarpeum

Pittosporum callicarpeum, commonly known as the coastal pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange when ripe. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils.

Pittosporum callicarpeum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
