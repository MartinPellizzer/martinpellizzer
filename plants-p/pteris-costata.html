Pteris costata

Pteris costata, commonly known as the crested water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that have a wavy, undulating edge and a unique, crested appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are biennial, with the fertile fronds appearing in the second year.

Pteris costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
