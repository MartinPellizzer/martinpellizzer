Pilosella iserana subsp. subnigriceps

Pilosella iserana subsp.

subnigriceps, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cichorium, is a flowering plant species native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its subspecies name, subnigriceps, refers to the dark-colored heads of its flower heads.

It is an important component of alpine vegetation and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
