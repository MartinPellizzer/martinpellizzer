Phialodiscus zambesiacus

Phialodiscus zambesiacus, commonly known as the Zambesi waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves that resemble the spokes of a wheel. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as rivers and lakes. Its leaves are typically narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a waxy coating.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
