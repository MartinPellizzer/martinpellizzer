Protea convexa

Protea convexa, commonly known as the broad-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of multiple bracts and numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Protea convexa is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region, with a dormant period during the winter months.
