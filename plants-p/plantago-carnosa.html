Plantago carnosa

Plantago carnosa, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
