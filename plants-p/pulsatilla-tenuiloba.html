Pulsatilla tenuiloba

Pulsatilla tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in soft, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pulsatilla tenuiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
