Patagonium dessaueri

Patagonium dessaueri, commonly known as the Patagonian dwarf lichen, is a unique foliose lichen that thrives in the cold, arid environments of southern South America.

It is characterized by its small, leaf-like structures that are tightly packed and often covered in a thin, crust-like layer. The plant forms symbiotic relationships with algae and fungi, which contribute to its ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions. It is typically found on rock surfaces or soil in high-altitude regions.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
