Pithecellobium grisebachianum

Pithecellobium grisebachianum, commonly known as the silver acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, making it valuable in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
