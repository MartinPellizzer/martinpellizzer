Prunus capollin

Prunus capollin, commonly known as the Capollin cherry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2-5 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by dark red or black berries in the autumn. Its leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
