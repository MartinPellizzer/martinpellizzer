Passiflora toxicaria

Passiflora toxicaria, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-color flowers that feature a blend of purple, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora toxicaria is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
