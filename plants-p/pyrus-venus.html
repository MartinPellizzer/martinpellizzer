Pyrus venus

Pyrus venus, commonly known as the Venus pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its ornamental value. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruit that is typically yellow or green. Its leaves are broad and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

The Venus pear is often cultivated for both its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.
