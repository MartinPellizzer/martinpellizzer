Pavonia uniflora

Pavonia uniflora, commonly known as the scarlet bower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features showy, single pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Pavonia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
