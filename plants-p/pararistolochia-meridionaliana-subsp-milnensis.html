Pararistolochia meridionaliana subsp. milnensis

Pararistolochia meridionaliana subsp.

milnensis, commonly known as the southern false blistering vine, is a member of the Aristolochiaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular, and often fragrant flowers that resemble a snake or other insects, a trait known as mimicry.

The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture, and the stems are climbing or trailing.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
