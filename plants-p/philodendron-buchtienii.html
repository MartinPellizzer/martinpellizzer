Philodendron buchtienii

Philodendron buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtienii Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among indoor gardeners due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
