Polygonum kumaonum

Polygonum kumaonum, commonly known as the Kumaon knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Polygonum kumaonum is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
