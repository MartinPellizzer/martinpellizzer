Pogostemon latifolius

Pogostemon latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
