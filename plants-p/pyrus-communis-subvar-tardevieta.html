Pyrus communis subvar. tardevieta

Pyrus communis subvar. tardevieta, commonly known as the Tardevieta pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and fruit characteristics. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. It produces small, aromatic fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value as well as its agricultural significance.
