Penstemon moffatii subsp. marcusii

Penstemon moffatii subsp.

marcusii, commonly known as the Marcus's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers.

The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and are arranged in whorls along the stem, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
