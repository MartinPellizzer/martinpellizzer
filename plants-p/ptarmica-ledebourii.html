Ptarmica ledebourii

Ptarmica ledebourii, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to the fynbos biome of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Ptarmica ledebourii plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
