Polygonum pilosum

Polygonum pilosum, commonly known as hairy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy glands. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a hairy surface and a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.
