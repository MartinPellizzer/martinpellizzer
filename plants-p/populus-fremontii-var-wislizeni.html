Populus fremontii var. wislizeni

Populus fremontii var. wislizeni, commonly known as the Fremont's cottonwood, is a variant of the cottonwood tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and catkin flowers. The plant thrives in riparian zones, often growing along rivers and streams. Its bark is typically gray and smooth, with a tendency to peel in thin layers.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
