Papaver pinnatifidum var. simonii

Papaver pinnatifidum var. simonii, commonly known as the Simonii poppy, is a variant of the pinnatifidum poppy native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, large, papery petals that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
