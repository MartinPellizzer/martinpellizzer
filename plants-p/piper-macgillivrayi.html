Piper macgillivrayi

Piper macgillivrayi, commonly known as the MacGillivray's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor, making it a unique addition to local cuisines.
