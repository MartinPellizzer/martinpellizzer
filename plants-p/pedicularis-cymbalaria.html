Pedicularis cymbalaria

Pedicularis cymbalaria, commonly known as the lesser flame violet, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with downward-facing petals that resemble a flame, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Pedicularis cymbalaria is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
