Psychotria medica var. rigida

Psychotria medica var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid psychotria, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, red berries. It contains alkaloids, including tetrahydrocannabinol (THC), which contribute to its psychoactive properties.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used in traditional medicine and spiritual practices.
