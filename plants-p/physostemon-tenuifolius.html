Physostemon tenuifolius

Physostemon tenuifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved physostemon, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
