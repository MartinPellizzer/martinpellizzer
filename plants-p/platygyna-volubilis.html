Platygyna volubilis

Platygyna volubilis, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a trailing perennial that produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and violet. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Platygyna volubilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread rapidly through its trailing growth habit.
