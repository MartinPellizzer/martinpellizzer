Polycarpaea rubioides

Polycarpaea rubioides, commonly known as the red-stemmed broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

Polycarpaea rubioides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
