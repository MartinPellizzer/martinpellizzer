Psoralea stipularis

Psoralea stipularis, commonly known as the stipular psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant has compound leaves with stipules, which are small, leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalks. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.

Psoralea stipularis is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
