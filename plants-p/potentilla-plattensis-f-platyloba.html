Potentilla plattensis f. platyloba

Potentilla plattensis f. platyloba, commonly known as the flat-lobed potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
