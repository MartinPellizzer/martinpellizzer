Photinia flavidiflora

Photinia flavidiflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered photinia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that develops after flowering and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
