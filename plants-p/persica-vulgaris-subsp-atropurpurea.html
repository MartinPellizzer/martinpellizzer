Persica vulgaris subsp. atropurpurea

Persica vulgaris subsp.

atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf peach, is a subspecies of the peach tree characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged foliage. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant produces small, ornamental flowers that transition into sweet, juicy fruits similar to common peaches. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and a vibrant purple hue on the underside.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens and orchards.
