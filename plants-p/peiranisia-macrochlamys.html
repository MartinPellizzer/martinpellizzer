Peiranisia macrochlamys

Peiranisia macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique botanical features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and landscaping in tropical regions.
