Panicum occidentale

Panicum occidentale, commonly known as western grass, is a perennial grass species native to the central and southern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing panicles during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
