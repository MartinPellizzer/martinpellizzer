Prenanthella exigua

Prenanthella exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, with four petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Prenanthella exigua is often found in nutrient-rich waters and is an important part of aquatic ecosystems.
