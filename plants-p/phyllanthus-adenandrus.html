Phyllanthus adenandrus

Phyllanthus adenandrus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
