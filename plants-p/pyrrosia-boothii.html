Pyrrosia boothii

Pyrrosia boothii, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically bright green with a waxy coating. The plant grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sporangia on the underside of the fronds.

Pyrrosia boothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
