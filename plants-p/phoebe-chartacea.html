Phoebe chartacea

Phoebe chartacea, commonly known as the broadleaf phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and bay laurel. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with large, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries.

Phoebe chartacea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along riverbanks.
