Piper regnellii var. parvifolium

Piper regnellii var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppercorn-like berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and stimulant properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
