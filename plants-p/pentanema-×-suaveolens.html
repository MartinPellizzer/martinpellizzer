Pentanema × suaveolens

Pentanema × suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented pentanema, is a hybrid plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a pleasant scent, particularly when crushed. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
