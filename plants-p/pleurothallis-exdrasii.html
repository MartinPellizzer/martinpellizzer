Pleurothallis exdrasii

Pleurothallis exdrasii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is part of the large and diverse Orchidaceae family, which includes thousands of species with varied growth habits.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in conservation-focused botanical gardens.
