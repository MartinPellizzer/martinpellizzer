Penstemon neotericus

Penstemon neotericus, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
