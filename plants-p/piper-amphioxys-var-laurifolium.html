Piper amphioxys var. laurifolium

Piper amphioxys var. laurifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper amphioxys species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing or shrub-like plant characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for humid, shaded environments.

It is often found in rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
