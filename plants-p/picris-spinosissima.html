Picris spinosissima

Picris spinosissima, commonly known as the spiny goadweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in spiny hairs, giving it a rugged appearance. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and the leaves are linear with serrated edges.

Picris spinosissima is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is considered a weed in some regions.
