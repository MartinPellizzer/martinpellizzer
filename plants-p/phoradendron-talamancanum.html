Phoradendron talamancanum

Phoradendron talamancanum, commonly known as the Talamancanum mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host's leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to Central America and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various bird species and contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
