Pulsatilla grandis f. polypetala

Pulsatilla grandis f. polypetala, commonly known as the showy pasqueflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple, petal-like bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge, making it a notable early indicator of seasonal change.

The f. polypetala form is distinguished by its more elaborate and showy bracts compared to the typical form of Pulsatilla grandis.
