Perezia eryngioides

Perezia eryngioides, commonly known as the silverleaf perezia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
