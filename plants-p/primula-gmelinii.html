Primula gmelinii

Primula gmelinii, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
