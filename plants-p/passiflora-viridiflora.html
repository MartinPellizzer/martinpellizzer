Passiflora viridiflora

Passiflora viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of colors, including green, blue, and white. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

Passiflora viridiflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
