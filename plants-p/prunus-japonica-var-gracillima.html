Prunus japonica var. gracillima

Prunus japonica var. gracillima, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is celebrated for its ornamental value, particularly for the showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are not only beautiful but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
