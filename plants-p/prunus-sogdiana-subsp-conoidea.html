Prunus sogdiana subsp. conoidea

Prunus sogdiana subsp.

conoidea, commonly known as the Sogdian cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive conical shape and small, dark red to black fruits.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
