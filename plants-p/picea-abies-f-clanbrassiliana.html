Picea abies f. clanbrassiliana

Picea abies f. clanbrassiliana, commonly known as the Clanbrassiliana Norway spruce, is a rare and naturally occurring form of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which is typically smaller and more densely packed compared to the typical species. The cones are usually shorter and more slender, and the bark is smoother and lighter in color. This form is often found in specific microhabitats, such as on acidic, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of study in plant taxonomy and ecology.
