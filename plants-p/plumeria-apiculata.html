Plumeria apiculata

Plumeria apiculata, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that come in shades of yellow, pink, and white. The plant produces waxy, papery petals that are often used in floral arrangements and traditional ceremonies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth, glossy texture.

The tree is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
