Pelargonium × recurvifolium

Pelargonium × recurvifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy, ivy-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its ornamental foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Pelargonium × recurvifolium prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
