Paspalum acuminatum

Paspalum acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated paspalum, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Paspalum acuminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in certain ecosystems.
