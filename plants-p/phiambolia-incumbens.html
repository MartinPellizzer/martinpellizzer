Phiambolia incumbens

Phiambolia incumbens, commonly known as the narrowleaf phiambolia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
