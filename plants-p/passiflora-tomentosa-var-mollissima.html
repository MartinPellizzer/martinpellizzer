Passiflora tomentosa var. mollissima

Passiflora tomentosa var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. Its fruits are edible, containing a sweet, pulpy interior with numerous small seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
