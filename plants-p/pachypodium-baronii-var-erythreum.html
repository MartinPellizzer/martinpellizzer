Pachypodium baronii var. erythreum

Pachypodium baronii var. erythreum, commonly known as the Red Sea Pachypodium, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically small and spiny, providing some protection from herbivores.

This species is well adapted to extreme environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
