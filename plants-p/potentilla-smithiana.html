Potentilla smithiana

Potentilla smithiana, commonly known as Smith's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, round fruits called achenes.

Smith's cinquefoil is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
