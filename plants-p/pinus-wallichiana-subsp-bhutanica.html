Pinus wallichiana subsp. bhutanica

Pinus wallichiana subsp.

bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutan blue pine, is a coniferous tree native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green needles and cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

The tree typically grows at high altitudes, preferring cool and moist environments. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also important in local ecosystems.
