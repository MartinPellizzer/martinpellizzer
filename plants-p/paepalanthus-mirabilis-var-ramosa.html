Paepalanthus mirabilis var. ramosa

Paepalanthus mirabilis var. ramosa, commonly known as the branched peacock plant, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers, which are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic or terrestrial species, with rosette-shaped leaves that collect water and nutrients. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, white or pale flowers, adding to its visual appeal.

The varietal form 'ramosa' distinguishes it by its more branched inflorescence compared to the typical variety.
