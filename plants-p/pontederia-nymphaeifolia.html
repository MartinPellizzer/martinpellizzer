Pontederia nymphaeifolia

Pontederia nymphaeifolia, commonly known as the water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom atop buoyant, waxy leaves. The plant has a complex root system that anchors it in water and helps absorb nutrients. Its rapid growth rate makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and an invasive species in many regions.

It plays a role in water purification but can disrupt ecosystems if left unchecked.
