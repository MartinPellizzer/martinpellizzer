Prunus venusta

Prunus venusta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or weeping cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its graceful, weeping form and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval, glossy, and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blossoms and seasonal foliage changes.
