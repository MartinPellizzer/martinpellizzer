Petalochilus ornatus

Petalochilus ornatus, commonly known as the ornate orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its flowers are characterized by their intricate petal structure and vibrant coloration, often displaying shades of white, pink, and purple.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
