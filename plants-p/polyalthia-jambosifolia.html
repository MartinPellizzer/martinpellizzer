Polyalthia jambosifolia

Polyalthia jambosifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or Malabar flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often covering the entire tree during the flowering season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
