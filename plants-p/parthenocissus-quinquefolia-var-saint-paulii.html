Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. saint-paulii

Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. saint-paulii, commonly known as the Saint-Paulii ivy, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of five leaflets, which are often lobed and have a distinctive, palmate appearance. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial to full sunlight and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls or trellises.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
