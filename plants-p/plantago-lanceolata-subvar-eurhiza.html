Plantago lanceolata subvar. eurhiza

Plantago lanceolata subvar. eurhiza, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces erect flowering stalks that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

This species is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas due to its adaptability and resilience.
