Pilea microphylla var. peregrina

Pilea microphylla var. peregrina, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of glossy, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have contributed to its widespread cultivation.
