Prunus × fruticans subsp. ovofruticans

Prunus × fruticans subsp.

ovofruticans, commonly known as the desert peach, is a hybrid species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is a small, multi-stemmed shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, oval drupe that turns yellow or orange when ripe, and it is edible but often tart.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
