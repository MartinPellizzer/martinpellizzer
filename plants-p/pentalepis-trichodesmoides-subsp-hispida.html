Pentalepis trichodesmoides subsp. hispida

Pentalepis trichodesmoides subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hispid sandmat, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in stiff, bristly hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are fleshy and alternate, often with a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
