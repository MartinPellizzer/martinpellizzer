Pelexia geralia

Pelexia geralia, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Pelexia geralia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant plants.
