Polygala erioptera var. foliosa

Polygala erioptera var. foliosa, commonly known as the leafy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, often clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its ability to retain moisture.

The flowers, which are usually pale yellow or greenish, are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
