Piper calcaratum

Piper calcaratum, commonly known as the horned pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough texture with a strong, peppery aroma.

Piper calcaratum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
