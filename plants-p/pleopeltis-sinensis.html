Pleopeltis sinensis

Pleopeltis sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on rocks or trees in tropical forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptation make it a notable species in botanical studies.
