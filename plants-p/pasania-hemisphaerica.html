Pasania hemisphaerica

Pasania hemisphaerica, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing shrub that produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The flowers are typically borne in clusters and have a unique hemispherical shape, giving the plant its scientific name. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are woody and often branched.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
