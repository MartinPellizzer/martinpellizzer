Philadelphus cordatus

Philadelphus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a spreading, rounded form. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and pleasant scent.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
