Philadelphus microphyllus var. argyrocalyx

Philadelphus microphyllus var. argyrocalyx, commonly known as silverleaf mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are covered in a fine silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
