Phyllostachys guizhouensis

Phyllostachys guizhouensis, commonly known as Guizhou bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Guizhou province, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and smooth, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Guizhou bamboo is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and its potential use in sustainable forestry practices.
