Pterozonium scopulinum

Pterozonium scopulinum, commonly known as the silverleaf tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and prefers well-drained soils in sunny locations.

Its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine make it a notable species in botanical gardens.
