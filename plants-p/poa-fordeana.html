Poa fordeana

Poa fordeana, commonly known as the Forde's bentgrass, is a species of grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering stems produce small, spikelet-like inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Poa fordeana is often used in lawns and landscaping due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
