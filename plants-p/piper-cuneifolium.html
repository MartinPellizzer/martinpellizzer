Piper cuneifolium

Piper cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into small, round berries containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and medicinal research.
