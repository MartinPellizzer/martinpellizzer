Polygonum lapathifolium var. angustifolium

Polygonum lapathifolium var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist soils along streambanks, wetlands, and disturbed areas, and is often found in partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
