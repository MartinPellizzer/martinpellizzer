Pyrus gonggashanensis

Pyrus gonggashanensis, commonly known as the Gonggashan pear, is a rare species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool and humid climates. Its flowers are white and clustered, blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is a small pear-like fruit, which is edible but not widely cultivated.
