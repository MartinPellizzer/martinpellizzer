Ptilotus astrolasius var. luteolus

Ptilotus astrolasius var. luteolus, commonly known as the yellow bush morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in drought-tolerant gardens.
