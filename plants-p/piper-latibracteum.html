Piper latibracteum

Piper latibracteum, commonly known as the broad-bract pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery fruits. Its botanical structure supports its role as a climbing plant, with adventitious roots along its stems.

The plant's morphology is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments within its native range.
