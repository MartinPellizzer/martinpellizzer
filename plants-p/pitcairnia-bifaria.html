Pitcairnia bifaria

Pitcairnia bifaria, commonly known as the Two-lobed Pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pitcairnia bifaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
