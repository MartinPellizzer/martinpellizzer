Polypodium morbillosum

Polypodium morbillosum, commonly known as the sickle fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are often sickle-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of many ferns.

Polypodium morbillosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in various regions with suitable climates.
