Platyclinis stachyodes

Platyclinis stachyodes, commonly known as the silverleaf ironwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
