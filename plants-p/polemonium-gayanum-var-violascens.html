Polemonium gayanum var. violascens

Polemonium gayanum var. violascens, commonly known as the Gaya bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its aesthetic value in horticultural settings.
