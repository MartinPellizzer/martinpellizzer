Phrynium sinicum

Phrynium sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese arrowroot or arrowleaf, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellow or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rhizomes can be harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisine.
