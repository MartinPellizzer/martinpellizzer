Phlomis taurica subsp. majkopensis

Phlomis taurica subsp.

majkopensis, commonly known as the Caucasian phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage.

The plant produces showy, pinkish-white to lavender flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in its native environment.
