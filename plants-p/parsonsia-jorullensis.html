Parsonsia jorullensis

Parsonsia jorullensis, commonly known as the Jorullan flame vine, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
