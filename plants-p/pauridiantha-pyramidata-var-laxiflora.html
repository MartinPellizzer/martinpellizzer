Pauridiantha pyramidata var. laxiflora

Pauridiantha pyramidata var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, cascading floral displays.
