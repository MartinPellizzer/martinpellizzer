Phylica tysonii var. tysonii

Phylica tysonii var. tysonii, commonly known as the Natal bottlebrush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottlebrush-like inflorescences, which are composed of dense clusters of red or pink flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation.

The Natal bottlebrush is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
