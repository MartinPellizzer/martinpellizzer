Potamogeton apicalis

Potamogeton apicalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Potamogeton apicalis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and improve water quality.
