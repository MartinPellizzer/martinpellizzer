Peristylus odontoglossus

Peristylus odontoglossus, commonly known as the toothed tongue orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spikes that bear small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of forests. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces a distinctive, toothed lip on its flowers.

Peristylus odontoglossus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
