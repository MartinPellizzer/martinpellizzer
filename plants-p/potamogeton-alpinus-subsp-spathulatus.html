Potamogeton alpinus subsp. spathulatus

Potamogeton alpinus subsp.

spathulatus, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, temperate regions. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater lakes and streams, often in nutrient-rich sediments.

The plant has long, narrow leaves that are ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, green flowers that bloom in the summer. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
