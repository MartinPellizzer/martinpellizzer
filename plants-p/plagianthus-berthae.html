Plagianthus berthae

Plagianthus berthae, commonly known as the Natal plumeveld, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Plagianthus berthae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
