Penstemon franklinii

Penstemon franklinii, commonly known as Franklin's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.
