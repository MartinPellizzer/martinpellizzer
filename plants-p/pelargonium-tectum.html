Pelargonium tectum

Pelargonium tectum, commonly known as the shield-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive shield-shaped leaves, which are thick and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium tectum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
