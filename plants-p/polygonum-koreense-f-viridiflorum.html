Polygonum koreense f. viridiflorum

Polygonum koreense f. viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered knotweed, is a variant of the Korean knotweed species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia, particularly Korea. The plant features striking greenish-white flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.
