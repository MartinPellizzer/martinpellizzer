Pithecellobium leucospermum

Pithecellobium leucospermum, commonly known as the white-flowered pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

The fruit is a legume that splits open to release small, edible seeds.
