Phyllanthus ziziphoides

Phyllanthus ziziphoides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by yellow, fleshy berries. Its fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
