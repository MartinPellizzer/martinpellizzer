Platystele halbingeriana

Platystele halbingeriana, commonly known as the Halbinger's ghost orchid, is a rare and critically endangered epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate, and often translucent leaves. The plant produces minute, white flowers that are typically clustered in small inflorescences, making it difficult to spot in its natural habitat. Due to its restricted distribution and habitat loss, it is highly vulnerable to extinction.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect and propagate this unique botanical species.
