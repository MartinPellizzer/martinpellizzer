Plectronia grandifolia

Plectronia grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora plectronia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from pink to red, and are attractively arranged in inflorescences.

Plectronia grandifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
