Poa badensis var. multiflora

Poa badensis var. multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora bluegrass, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters, with small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
