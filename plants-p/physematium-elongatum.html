Physematium elongatum

Physematium elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms for nutrient absorption. The plant typically grows in shallow water, where it can float or anchor itself to substrates. Its leaves are usually thin and ribbon-like, adapted for efficient water movement.

Physematium elongatum is known for its unique ability to capture and digest prey, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
