Polianthes rosei

Polianthes rosei, commonly known as the Mexican rain lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the southern United States and Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its foliage consists of strap-like, evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year.

Polianthes rosei is relatively drought-tolerant and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
