Pyrethrum corymbosum subsp. achilleae

Pyrethrum corymbosum subsp.

achilleae, commonly known as African pyrethrum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant is notable for its high concentration of pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal gardening.
