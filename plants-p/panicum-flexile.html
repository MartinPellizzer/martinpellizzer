Panicum flexile

Panicum flexile, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-blue foliage and slender, arching stems that often form a low, mat-like growth. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive, airy appearance, especially in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
