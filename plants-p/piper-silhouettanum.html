Piper silhouettanum

Piper silhouettanum, commonly known as the Black Beauty pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, spicy fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit, which is edible, has a unique flavor profile that combines spiciness with a subtle sweetness.
