Potentilla hirta stricta

Potentilla hirta stricta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

Potentilla hirta stricta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
