Phragmanthera rufescens

Phragmanthera rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed phragmanthera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its stems are characterized by their reddish-brown color, which gives it its common name.

Phragmanthera rufescens is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.
