Physaria acutifolia var. canaani

Physaria acutifolia var. canaani, commonly known as the Canary Island bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to a Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
