Pedilonum kuhlii

Pedilonum kuhlii, commonly known as the Kuhlii pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular leaves that form deep, pitcher-like structures. These pitchers secrete a sticky fluid that traps insects, which are then digested by enzymes to provide essential nutrients. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and specialized feeding mechanism make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
