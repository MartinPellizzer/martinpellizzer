Pyrus malus striatofasciata

Pyrus malus striatofasciata, commonly known as the striped-barked apple, is a unique species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark that features alternating light and dark stripes, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically tart and slightly acidic. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is native to certain regions of Asia and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
