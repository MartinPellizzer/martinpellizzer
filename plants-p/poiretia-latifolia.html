Poiretia latifolia

Poiretia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf poiretia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its broad, flat leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
