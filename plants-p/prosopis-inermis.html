Prosopis inermis

Prosopis inermis, commonly known as the screwbean mesquite, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has small, compound leaves that are typically grayish-green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions.

Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper underground sources.
