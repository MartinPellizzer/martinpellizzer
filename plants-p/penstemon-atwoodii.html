Penstemon atwoodii

Penstemon atwoodii, commonly known as Atwood's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
