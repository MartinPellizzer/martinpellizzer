Potamogeton palustris

Potamogeton palustris, commonly known as marsh pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton palustris is often found in shallow, still or slow-moving waters.
