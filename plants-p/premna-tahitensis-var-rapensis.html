Premna tahitensis var. rapensis

Premna tahitensis var. rapensis, commonly known as the Tahitian glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often fragrant. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, hairy seeds.

It thrives in coastal and moist woodland habitats, often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
