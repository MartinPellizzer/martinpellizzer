Polygala makaschwilii

Polygala makaschwilii, commonly known as the Natal milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland areas with a Mediterranean climate. It produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The Natal milkvetch is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
