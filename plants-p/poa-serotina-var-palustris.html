Poa serotina var. palustris

Poa serotina var. palustris, commonly known as marsh bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, creeping stems that form dense mats, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 2 mm wide. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered at the base of the stems, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is adapted to persist in waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
