Paphiopedilum appletonianum f. immaculatum

Paphiopedilum appletonianum f. immaculatum, commonly known as the immaculate velvet orchid, is a rare and elegant orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves and striking, showy flowers with a unique lip that resembles a pouch. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with a deep red or orange lip, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized gardens.
