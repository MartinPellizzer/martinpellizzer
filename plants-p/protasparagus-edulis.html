Protasparagus edulis

Protasparagus edulis, commonly known as the edible asparagus fern, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible potential, it is cultivated in some regions for both decorative and culinary purposes.
