Pycnorhachis maingayi

Pycnorhachis maingayi, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

The seeds of Pycnorhachis maingayi are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
