Phyllodes lutea

Phyllodes lutea, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Phyllodes lutea plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
