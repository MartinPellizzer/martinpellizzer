Pittosporum ochrosiifolium

Pittosporum ochrosiifolium, commonly known as the golden-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a yellow or golden tint on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum ochrosiifolium is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
