Polygala ambigens

Polygala ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the genus Polygala, which is part of the family Polygalaceae, known for its diverse and often ornate flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its native habitat.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its potential use in traditional medicine.
