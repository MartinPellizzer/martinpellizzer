Phoradendron platycaulon

Phoradendron platycaulon, commonly known as the flat-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along its slender stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its roots form specialized structures called haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb water and nutrients.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for birds and other animals.
