Pimelea lanceolata

Pimelea lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved riceflower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
