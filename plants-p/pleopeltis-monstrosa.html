Pleopeltis monstrosa

Pleopeltis monstrosa, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a large, umbrella-like frond that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity. Its fronds are composed of numerous pinnae, which are arranged in a distinctive, fan-like pattern.

Pleopeltis monstrosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
