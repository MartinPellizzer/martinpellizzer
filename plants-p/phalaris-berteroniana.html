Phalaris berteroniana

Phalaris berteroniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and dense clusters of spike-like inflorescences that produce small, reddish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native habitat.
