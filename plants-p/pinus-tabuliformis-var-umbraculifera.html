Pinus tabuliformis var. umbraculifera

Pinus tabuliformis var. umbraculifera, commonly known as the Chinese white pine, is a variant of the Chinese pine native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and conical shape, which provide a distinctive appearance in forest settings. The tree has a slow growth rate and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
