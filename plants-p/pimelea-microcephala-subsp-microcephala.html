Pimelea microcephala subsp. microcephala

Pimelea microcephala subsp.

microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits known as follicles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
