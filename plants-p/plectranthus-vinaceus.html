Plectranthus vinaceus

Plectranthus vinaceus, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins contrasting against the dark green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus vinaceus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
