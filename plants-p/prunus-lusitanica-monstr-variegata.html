Prunus lusitanica monstr. variegata

Prunus lusitanica monstr.

variegata, commonly known as the variegated Portuguese laurel, is a decorative shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its broad, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, cherry-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its attractive appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
