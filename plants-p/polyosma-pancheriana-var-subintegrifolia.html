Polyosma pancheriana var. subintegrifolia

Polyosma pancheriana var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of the Western Ghats in India.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
