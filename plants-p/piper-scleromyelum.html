Piper scleromyelum

Piper scleromyelum, commonly known as the rough-seeded pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
