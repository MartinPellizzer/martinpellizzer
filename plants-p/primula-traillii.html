Primula traillii

Primula traillii, commonly known as the Traill's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a notable early-season wildflower. Its leaves are dark green, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Primula traillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
