Passiflora cornuta

Passiflora cornuta, commonly known as the horned passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in small, stiff hairs.

Passiflora cornuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
