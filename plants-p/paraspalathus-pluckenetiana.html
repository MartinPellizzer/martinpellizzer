Paraspalathus pluckenetiana

Paraspalathus pluckenetiana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.

Paraspalathus pluckenetiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
