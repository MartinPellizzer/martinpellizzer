Pilea melastomoides

Pilea melastomoides, commonly known as the silver shield plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, silver-green leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded garden beds.
