Primula munroi subsp. schizocalyx

Primula munroi subsp.

schizocalyx, commonly known as the Schizocalyx primrose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink.

The leaves are typically hairy and have a rounded to ovate shape. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
