Polypodium leptochiloides

Polypodium leptochiloides, commonly known as the slender-lobed huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hypnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with closely spaced, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
