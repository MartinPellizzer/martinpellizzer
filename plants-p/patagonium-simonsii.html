Patagonium simonsii

Patagonium simonsii, commonly known as the Patagonian lily, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Patagonian steppe. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Patagonium simonsii plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
