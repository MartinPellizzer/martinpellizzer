Psathyrotes pilifera

Psathyrotes pilifera, commonly known as the featherleaf dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces a capsule-like fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a subject of ecological study.
