Pentagonocarpus pentacarpus

Pentagonocarpus pentacarpus, commonly known as the five-fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into woody, five-lobed fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its unique fruit structure and its role in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
