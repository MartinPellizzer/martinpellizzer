Pyrus salviifolia var. amygdaliformis

Pyrus salviifolia var. amygdaliformis, commonly known as the broadleaf pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its broad, salvia-like leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits in the summer. Its foliage provides ornamental value, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The tree is relatively hardy and adaptable to various soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
