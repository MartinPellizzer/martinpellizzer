Phyllanthus oblongiglans

Phyllanthus oblongiglans, commonly known as the broad-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or arid environments. The plant has alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round, fleshy fruits.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
