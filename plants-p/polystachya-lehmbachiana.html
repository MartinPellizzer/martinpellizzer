Polystachya lehmbachiana

Polystachya lehmbachiana, commonly known as the Lehmbach's spider orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescence that consists of numerous small, showy flowers clustered along a central stem. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and the flowers are usually greenish-yellow with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
