Psorophytum balearicum

Psorophytum balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Psorophytum balearicum is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
