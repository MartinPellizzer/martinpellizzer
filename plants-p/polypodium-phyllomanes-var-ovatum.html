Polypodium phyllomanes var. ovatum

Polypodium phyllomanes var. ovatum, commonly known as the ovate fern, is a variant of the polypodium genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its ovate-shaped fronds, which are typically leathery and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori, a common feature in ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to humid climates, with a rhizomatous growth habit that aids in nutrient absorption and propagation.
