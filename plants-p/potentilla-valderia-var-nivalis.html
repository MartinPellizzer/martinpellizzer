Potentilla valderia var. nivalis

Potentilla valderia var. nivalis, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine regions of the European Alps.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suited to harsh mountain climates.
