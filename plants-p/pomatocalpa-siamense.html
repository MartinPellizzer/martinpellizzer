Pomatocalpa siamense

Pomatocalpa siamense, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces dark violet to purple flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
