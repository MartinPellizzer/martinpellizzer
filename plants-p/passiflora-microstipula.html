Passiflora microstipula

Passiflora microstipula, commonly known as the small-stippled passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and modified leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture and small, raised stipules along the edges.

Passiflora microstipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its unique floral display.
