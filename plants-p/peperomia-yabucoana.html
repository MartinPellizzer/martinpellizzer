Peperomia yabucoana

Peperomia yabucoana, commonly known as the Yabucoa peperomia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and in horticultural research.
