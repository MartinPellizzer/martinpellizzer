Polyachyrus carduoides

Polyachyrus carduoides, commonly known as the thistle-like aster, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is well adapted to dry environments with its deep root system. It often grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
