Pilosella pseudeffusa

Pilosella pseudeffusa, commonly known as the false hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered heads, which are surrounded by involucral bracts. It is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Pilosella pseudeffusa is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.
