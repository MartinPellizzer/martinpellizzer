Phragmipedium lindleyanum

Phragmipedium lindleyanum, commonly known as the Lindley’s lady’s tresses, is a species of orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its striking, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often forming clumps of erect, strap-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a loose, horizontal spike and are known for their intricate, three-lobed petals.

Phragmipedium lindleyanum is valued in horticulture for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements in the right growing conditions.
