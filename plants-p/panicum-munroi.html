Panicum munroi

Panicum munroi, commonly known as the Mexican dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for native plant gardens.

Panicum munroi is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
