Prunus insititia

Prunus insititia, commonly known as the wild cherry or sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, tart red cherries in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish-brown. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and can be processed into jams, jellies, or wines.
