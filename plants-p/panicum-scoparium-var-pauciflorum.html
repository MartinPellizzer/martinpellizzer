Panicum scoparium var. pauciflorum

Panicum scoparium var. pauciflorum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems.

This variety is noted for its low-growing habit and drought tolerance, making it a valuable species for ecological restoration.
