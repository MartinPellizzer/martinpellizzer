Pyrostria alaotrensis

Pyrostria alaotrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pine, is a unique coniferous tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and a distinctive conical shape. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat. It plays an important ecological role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat for various species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
