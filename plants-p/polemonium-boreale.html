Polemonium boreale

Polemonium boreale, commonly known as the northern bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polemonium boreale is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local pollinators.
