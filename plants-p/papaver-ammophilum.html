Papaver ammophilum

Papaver ammophilum, commonly known as the sand poppy, is a flowering plant native to the sandy soils of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Papaver ammophilum is often found in habitats such as dunes, steppes, and rocky areas where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
