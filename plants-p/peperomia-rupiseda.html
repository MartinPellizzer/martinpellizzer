Peperomia rupiseda

Peperomia rupiseda, commonly known as the rock peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features succulent, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially along the edges, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to shady, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
