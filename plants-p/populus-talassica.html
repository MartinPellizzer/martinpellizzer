Populus talassica

Populus talassica, commonly known as the Tashkent poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often weeping form and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Populus genus. Its bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found growing in river valleys and along watercourses.

Populus talassica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
