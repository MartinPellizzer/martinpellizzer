Pelargonium × aceroides

Pelargonium × aceroides, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various pelargonium species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.
