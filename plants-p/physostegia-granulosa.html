Physostegia granulosa

Physostegia granulosa, commonly known as the roughleaf spikeweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its showy, two-lipped flowers are typically pink to purple and bloom in summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
