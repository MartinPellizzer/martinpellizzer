Pilosella caespitosa subsp. leptocaulon

Pilosella caespitosa subsp.

leptocaulon, commonly known as the slender-stemmed hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and small, yellow composite flowers arranged in a single head. Its leaves are linear to oblanceolate, often with hairy surfaces, and the stems are slender and somewhat erect.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
