Plectranthus welwitschii

Plectranthus welwitschii, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
