Polyachyrus sphaerocephalus

Polyachyrus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe-headed polyachyrus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
