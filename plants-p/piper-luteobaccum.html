Piper luteobaccum

Piper luteobaccum, commonly known as the yellow-barked pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and green, typically growing in clusters, while the fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens to orange.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has traditional uses in local cuisines and herbal medicine.
