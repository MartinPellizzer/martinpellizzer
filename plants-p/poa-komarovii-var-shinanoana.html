Poa komarovii var. shinanoana

Poa komarovii var. shinanoana, commonly known as the Shinano bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green color, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
