Polygonum stypticum

Polygonum stypticum, commonly known as the bleeding heart plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, reddish-pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous, and it prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Polygonum stypticum is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
