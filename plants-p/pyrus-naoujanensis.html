Pyrus naoujanensis

Pyrus naoujanensis, commonly known as the Naouja Valley pear, is a species of tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, clustered flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its fruit is a small, edible pear-like berry.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential for genetic diversity in its native habitat.
