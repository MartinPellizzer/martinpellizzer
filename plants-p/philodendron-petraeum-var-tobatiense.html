Philodendron petraeum var. tobatiense

Philodendron petraeum var. tobatiense, commonly known as the Tobatiense Philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, deep green leaves that often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among indoor gardeners due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
