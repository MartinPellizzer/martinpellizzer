Pelargonium × malachrifolium

Pelargonium × malachrifolium, commonly known as the Malachite geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that resemble the pattern of a malachite stone.

It is native to South Africa and belongs to the Geraniaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its foliage and flowers make it a valuable addition to both decorative and ecological landscapes.
