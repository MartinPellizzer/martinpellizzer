Paphiopedilum javanicum

Paphiopedilum javanicum, commonly known as the Java slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, fleshy rhizome and a single, erect stem that produces several leaves. The plant produces showy, slipper-shaped flowers with a distinctive lip that is often yellow or orange in color. Its flowers are typically borne on a scape that rises above the foliage, and it blooms during the rainy season.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
