Polyalthia bracteosa

Polyalthia bracteosa, commonly known as the flame tree or golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Polyalthia bracteosa make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
