Passiflora pyriformis

Passiflora pyriformis, commonly known as the eggplant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a heart or an egg, hence its common name. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora pyriformis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
