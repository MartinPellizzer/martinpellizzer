Pleurothallis gigiportillae

Pleurothallis gigiportillae, commonly known as the Gigiportilla orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
