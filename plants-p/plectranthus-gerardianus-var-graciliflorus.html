Plectranthus gerardianus var. graciliflorus

Plectranthus gerardianus var. graciliflorus, commonly known as the slenderflower plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
