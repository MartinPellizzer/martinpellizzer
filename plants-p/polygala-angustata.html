Polygala angustata

Polygala angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, yellow or greenish flowers arranged in clusters.

Polygala angustata is often used in traditional medicine and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
