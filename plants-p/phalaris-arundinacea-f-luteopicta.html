Phalaris arundinacea f. luteopicta

Phalaris arundinacea f. luteopicta, commonly known as the golden painted reed, is a variant of the common reed.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage with intricate patterns of pale green and gold. This ornamental grass thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. Its slender, arching stems produce fluffy, seed-filled spikes that add visual interest in the fall.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife and stabilizing wetland soils.
