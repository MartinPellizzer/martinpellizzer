Psathura lancifolia

Psathura lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved psathura, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features slender, upright stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Psathura lancifolia is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.
