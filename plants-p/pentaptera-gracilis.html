Pentaptera gracilis

Pentaptera gracilis, commonly known as the slender pentaptera, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves that often exhibit a feathery or finely divided appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in inflorescences that add to its ornamental appeal.

Pentaptera gracilis is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
