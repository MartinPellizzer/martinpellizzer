Paepalanthus capanemae

Paepalanthus capanemae, commonly known as the Capanema burrobrush, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces seeds in small, capsule-like structures.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
