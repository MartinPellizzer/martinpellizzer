Peucedanum capense var. latifolium

Peucedanum capense var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved cow-plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in open woodland or grassland habitats. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with broad, lance-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
