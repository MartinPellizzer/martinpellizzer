Piper submultinerve var. nandanicum

Piper submultinerve var. nandanicum, commonly known as the Nandanicum pepper, is a variant of the Piper submultinerve species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, narrow berries. The berries are typically harvested when mature, yielding a mild to spicy flavor used in local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
