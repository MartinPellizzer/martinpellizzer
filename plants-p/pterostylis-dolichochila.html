Pterostylis dolichochila

Pterostylis dolichochila, commonly known as the long-styled duck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically less than 5 cm in height. The plant produces a single, erect spike bearing several small, elongated flowers with a distinctive labellum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

The orchid's pollination mechanism involves specific insect pollinators, showcasing its ecological significance in its native habitat.
