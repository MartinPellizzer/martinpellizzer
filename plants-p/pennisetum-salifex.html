Pennisetum salifex

Pennisetum salifex, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty and drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Pennisetum salifex has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.

Its ability to thrive in saline soils makes it an important plant for ecological restoration and land rehabilitation projects.
