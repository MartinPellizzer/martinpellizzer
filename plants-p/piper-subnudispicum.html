Piper subnudispicum

Piper subnudispicum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
