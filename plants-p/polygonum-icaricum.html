Polygonum icaricum

Polygonum icaricum, commonly known as the redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has reddish stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Polygonum icaricum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
