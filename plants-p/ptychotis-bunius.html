Ptychotis bunius

Ptychotis bunius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spurge, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often on rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.
