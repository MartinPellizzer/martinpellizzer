Pandanus flabellistigma

Pandanus flabellistigma, commonly known as the fan palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically found at the base of the leaf sheaths. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, coastal environments.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in various island communities.
