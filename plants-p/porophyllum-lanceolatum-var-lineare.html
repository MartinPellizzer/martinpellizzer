Porophyllum lanceolatum var. lineare

Porophyllum lanceolatum var. lineare, commonly known as the linearleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are small and blue to purple in color, appearing in clustered inflorescences.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal practices.
