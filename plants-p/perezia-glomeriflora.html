Perezia glomeriflora

Perezia glomeriflora, commonly known as the clustered-flowered perezia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
