Palaquium selendit

Palaquium selendit, commonly known as the Selendit wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that lead to the development of round, fleshy fruits with a waxy coating.

The fruit contains a single large seed and is valued for its edible, sweet pulp, which is often used in local cuisines.
