Plectranthus exilis

Plectranthus exilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus exilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
