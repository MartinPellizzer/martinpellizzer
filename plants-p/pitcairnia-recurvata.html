Pitcairnia recurvata

Pitcairnia recurvata, commonly known as the recurved pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of stiff, sword-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
