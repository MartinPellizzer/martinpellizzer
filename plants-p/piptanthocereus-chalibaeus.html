Piptanthocereus chalibaeus

Piptanthocereus chalibaeus, commonly known as the Chihuahuan prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of North America, particularly the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as bats and moths. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines.

The plant's adaptations, including spines and shallow roots, make it well-suited for survival in desert ecosystems.
