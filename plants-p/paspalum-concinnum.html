Paspalum concinnum

Paspalum concinnum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Paspalum concinnum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
