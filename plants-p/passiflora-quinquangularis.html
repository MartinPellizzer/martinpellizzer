Passiflora quinquangularis

Passiflora quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five prominent sepals and five petals, giving it its common name. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tangy flavor. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive five-lobed structure.

Passiflora quinquangularis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and ecological value for pollinators.
