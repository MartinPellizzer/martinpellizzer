Poa parviceps var. jujuyensis

Poa parviceps var. jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy hairgrass, is a variant of the Poa parviceps species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its morphology includes slender culms and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptation to cold, arid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
