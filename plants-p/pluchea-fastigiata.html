Pluchea fastigiata

Pluchea fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small blue or purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and streambanks.
