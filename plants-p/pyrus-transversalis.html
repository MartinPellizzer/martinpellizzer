Pyrus transversalis

Pyrus transversalis, commonly known as the Transverse Crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small apple-like pome, which can be yellow or red in color, and is often used in local horticulture.
