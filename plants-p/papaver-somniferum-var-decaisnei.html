Papaver somniferum var. decaisnei

Papaver somniferum var. decaisnei, commonly known as the Decaisne opium poppy, is a botanical variant of the opium poppy native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces opium-containing capsules, which are used in the production of various narcotics. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its medicinal and illicit uses, it is subject to strict cultivation regulations in many regions.
