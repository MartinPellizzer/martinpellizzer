Penstemon cyaneus

Penstemon cyaneus, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces striking blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon cyaneus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to enhance the aesthetic appeal of gardens.
