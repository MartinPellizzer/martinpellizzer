Piptadenia quadrifolia

Piptadenia quadrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pequi tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces edible seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule, which is used in traditional cuisine and local medicine. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential pharmacological properties, making it valuable in ethnobotanical studies.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
