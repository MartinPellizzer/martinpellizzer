Pectinaria namaquensis

Pectinaria namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It is often found in rocky soils and is a common sight in the Namaqua region.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
