Packera neomexicana var. mutabilis

Packera neomexicana var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

The flowers, which appear in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
