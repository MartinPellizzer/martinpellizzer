Phrynium textile

Phrynium textile, commonly known as the velvetleaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces striking yellow and red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
