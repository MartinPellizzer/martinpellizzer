Persica davidiana var. alba

Persica davidiana var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered peach tree, is a cultivar of the peach species native to China.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The tree produces small, sweet fruits that are similar to regular peaches but often have a more delicate flavor. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its edible yield.
