Polygala oblongata

Polygala oblongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often arranged in pairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala oblongata is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
