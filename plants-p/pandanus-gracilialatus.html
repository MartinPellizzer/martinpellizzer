Pandanus gracilialatus

Pandanus gracilialatus, commonly known as the slender pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that grow in a fan-like arrangement, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and construction.

The species is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
