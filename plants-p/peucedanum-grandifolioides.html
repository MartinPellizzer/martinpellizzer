Peucedanum grandifolioides

Peucedanum grandifolioides, commonly known as the large-leaved cowparsley, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, pinnately divided leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Peucedanum grandifolioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
