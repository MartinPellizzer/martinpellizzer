Photinia wrightiana

Photinia wrightiana, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange-red foliage, which stands out prominently in the autumn season. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin, with a glossy appearance.

Photinia wrightiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
