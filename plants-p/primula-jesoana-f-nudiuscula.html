Primula jesoana f. nudiuscula

Primula jesoana f. nudiuscula, commonly known as the naked-stemmed primrose, is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often leafless stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from white to pale pink and bloom in early spring. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
