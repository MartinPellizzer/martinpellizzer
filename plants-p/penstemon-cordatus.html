Penstemon cordatus

Penstemon cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.

Penstemon cordatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
