Phyteuma tenerum var. microphyllum

Phyteuma tenerum var. microphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of daisy-like yellow flowers with purple centers, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
