Phlox sickmannii

Phlox sickmannii, commonly known as the Sierra phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
