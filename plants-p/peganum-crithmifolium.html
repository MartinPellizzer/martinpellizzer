Peganum crithmifolium

Peganum crithmifolium, commonly known as desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, linear leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh desert environments.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in extreme conditions.
