Plerandra pancheri

Plerandra pancheri, commonly known as the Pancheri arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are not showy but play a crucial role in attracting pollinators such as beetles and flies.

Plerandra pancheri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
