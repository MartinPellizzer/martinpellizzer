Physalis pensylvanica

Physalis pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red capsules containing numerous bright orange berries. These berries are often eaten by wildlife and have a tangy, slightly tart flavor.

It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
