Pyrus nivalis f. polycarpa

Pyrus nivalis f. polycarpa, commonly known as the snow pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is dense and dark green, providing ornamental value in gardens.

This form is notable for its hardiness and ability to thrive in colder climates.
