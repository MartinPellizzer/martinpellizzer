Polemonium pauciflorum subsp. typicum

Polemonium pauciflorum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
