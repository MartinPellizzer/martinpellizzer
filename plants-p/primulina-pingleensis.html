Primulina pingleensis

Primulina pingleensis, commonly known as the Pingle's primula, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces small, brightly colored blooms in shades of pink and white, which appear during the spring and early summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
