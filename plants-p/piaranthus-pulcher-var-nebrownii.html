Piaranthus pulcher var. nebrownii

Piaranthus pulcher var. nebrownii, commonly known as the Nebrown's beauty bell, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

This perennial herb is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
