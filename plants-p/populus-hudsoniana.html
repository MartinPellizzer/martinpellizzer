Populus hudsoniana

Populus hudsoniana, commonly known as Hudson's poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands.

Hudson's poplar is valued for its rapid growth and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
