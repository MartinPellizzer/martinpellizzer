Poinsettia frangulifolia

Poinsettia frangulifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf poinsettia, is a species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver leaves and bright red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes other ornamental and medicinal plants. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

Poinsettia frangulifolia is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
