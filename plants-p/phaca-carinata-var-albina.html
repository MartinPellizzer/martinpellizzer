Phaca carinata var. albina

Phaca carinata var. albina, commonly known as the white-flowered phaca, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
