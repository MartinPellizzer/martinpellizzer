Podocarpus colliculatus

Podocarpus colliculatus, commonly known as the mountain pine or African yellowwood, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, rounded cones. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

Podocarpus colliculatus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
