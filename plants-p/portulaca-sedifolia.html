Portulaca sedifolia

Portulaca sedifolia, commonly known as the stonecrop or moss rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Portulaca sedifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
