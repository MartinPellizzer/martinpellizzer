Potamogeton ferrugineus

Potamogeton ferrugineus, commonly known as rusty pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically green with a reddish or rusty hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Rusty pondweed is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds, lakes, and marshes.
