Paeonia feminea var. banatica

Paeonia feminea var. banatica, commonly known as the Banat peony, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves, and it produces distinctive, fragrant blooms in late spring.

Due to its limited distribution and aesthetic value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
