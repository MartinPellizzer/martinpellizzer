Polanisia beattiana

Polanisia beattiana, commonly known as the Beattie's polyanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, yellow-orange ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, making it visually striking. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Polanisia beattiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
