Phoradendron ensifolium

Phoradendron ensifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a source of food for birds and other animals. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries and then excrete the seeds onto other host trees.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by contributing to the biodiversity and food web.
