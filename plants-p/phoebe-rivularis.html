Phoebe rivularis

Phoebe rivularis, commonly known as the river phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white in color and appear in the spring.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a dark purple or black color, providing food for various wildlife species.
