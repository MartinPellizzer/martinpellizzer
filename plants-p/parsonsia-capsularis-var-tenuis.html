Parsonsia capsularis var. tenuis

Parsonsia capsularis var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has slender, climbing stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types in suitable climates.
