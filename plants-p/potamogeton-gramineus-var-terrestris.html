Potamogeton gramineus var. terrestris

Potamogeton gramineus var. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green and can grow up to several centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikes, which are often submerged. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in marshes and pond edges.

This variety is important for aquatic ecosystems as it provides habitat and food for various water organisms.
