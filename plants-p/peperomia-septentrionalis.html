Peperomia septentrionalis

Peperomia septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense, foliage-rich appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, which can be either green or red depending on the variety. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia septentrionalis is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and its ability to add a touch of greenery to any interior space.
