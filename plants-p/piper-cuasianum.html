Piper cuasianum

Piper cuasianum, commonly known as the Cusco pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery aroma.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Andean medicine.
