Pithecellobium triflorum

Pithecellobium triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters of three, which give it its common name. Its root system is fibrous, and it often grows in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
