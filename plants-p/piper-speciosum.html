Piper speciosum

Piper speciosum, commonly known as the ornamental pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that range in color from yellow to red, depending on their maturity. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardeners and botanists interested in its unique morphology.
