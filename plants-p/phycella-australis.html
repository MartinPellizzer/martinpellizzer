Phycella australis

Phycella australis, commonly known as the Australian sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes or rocky shorelines. Its flowers bloom in spring and are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Phycella australis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
