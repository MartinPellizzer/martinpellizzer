Persoonia lanceolata var. angustifolia

Persoonia lanceolata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geebung, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
