Prosopis domingensis

Prosopis domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican mesquite, is a small to medium-sized leguminous tree native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate and bear small, delicate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a deep root system that enables it to access groundwater.

This species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in reforestation and soil stabilization efforts.
