Prasophyllum barnettii

Prasophyllum barnettii, commonly known as the Barnett leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern region of South Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils within open woodlands or grasslands. It is a small to medium-sized orchid with a slender rhizome and is often found in areas with good drainage.

Prasophyllum barnettii is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is protected under Australian conservation laws.
