Prunus arguta

Prunus arguta, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or dwarf apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are tart and often used in culinary applications. Prunus arguta is adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
