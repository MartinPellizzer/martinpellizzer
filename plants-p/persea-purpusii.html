Persea purpusii

Persea purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
