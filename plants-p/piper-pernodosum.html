Piper pernodosum

Piper pernodosum, commonly known as the ghost pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which is a small, elongated berry, contains the highly pungent and spicy seeds.

The plant is notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of capsaicin, the compound responsible for its heat.
