Plectranthus aurifer

Plectranthus aurifer, commonly known as the golden plectranthus, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers range in color from yellow to orange, attracting various pollinators. This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively easy to care for in a warm, humid environment.
