Piper antioquiense

Piper antioquiense, commonly known as the Antioquian pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of Colombia and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, peppery fruits. It is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
