Piper rumphii

Piper rumphii, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated berries that resemble peppercorns. The plant produces a pungent, aromatic compound known as piperine, which contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
