Plectranthus menthoides

Plectranthus menthoides, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and container growing.

Due to its fragrant properties, it is often used in herbal teas and aromatherapy.
