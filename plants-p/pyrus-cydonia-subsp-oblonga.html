Pyrus cydonia subsp. oblonga

Pyrus cydonia subsp.

oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a subspecies of the common pear. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit is typically oblong, with a smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic flesh. This subspecies is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and orchards.
