Poa airoides var. purpurascens

Poa airoides var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple false bentgrass, is a grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces compact, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, often with a purple tinge on the underside. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
