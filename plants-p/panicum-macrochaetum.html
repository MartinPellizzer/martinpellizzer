Panicum macrochaetum

Panicum macrochaetum, commonly known as the broad-hair panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae. Its fibrous roots make it suitable for erosion control and soil stabilization in degraded lands.

Panicum macrochaetum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in sustainable landscaping.
