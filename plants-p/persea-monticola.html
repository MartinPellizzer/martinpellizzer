Persea monticola

Persea monticola, commonly known as the mountain avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a smooth, thin skin and a creamy, nutty flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical regions.
