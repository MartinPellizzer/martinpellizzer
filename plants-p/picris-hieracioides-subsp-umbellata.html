Picris hieracioides subsp. umbellata

Picris hieracioides subsp.

umbellata, commonly known as the umbellate sow-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small umbrellas.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It features basal rosettes of lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
