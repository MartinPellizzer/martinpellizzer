Primula pauliana var. pauliana

Primula pauliana var. pauliana, commonly known as the Paulian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring and early summer, adding a delicate touch to gardens and natural habitats. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a deep green color that provides a striking contrast to its colorful flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils in temperate climates.
