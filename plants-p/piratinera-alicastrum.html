Piratinera alicastrum

Piratinera alicastrum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant has elliptical leaves with a rough texture and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
