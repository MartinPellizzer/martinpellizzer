Pelargonium alpinum

Pelargonium alpinum, commonly known as the alpine geranium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
