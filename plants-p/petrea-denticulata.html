Petrea denticulata

Petrea denticulata, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine or white jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its bark is smooth and can be light to dark brown, and it has a woody stem with a characteristic scent.

Petrea denticulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
