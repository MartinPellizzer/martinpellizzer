Polygonum owenii

Polygonum owenii, commonly known as Owen's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive swollen nodes and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in meadows. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in dense clusters during the summer months.

Polygonum owenii is characterized by its erect stem and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.
