Phlomis lunariifolia var. russeliana

Phlomis lunariifolia var. russeliana, commonly known as the moonflower phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
