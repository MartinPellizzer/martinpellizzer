Polygonum achoreum

Polygonum achoreum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences and often succulent stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a waxy texture, while its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters.

Polygonum achoreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
