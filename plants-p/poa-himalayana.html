Poa himalayana

Poa himalayana, commonly known as Himalayan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Asia.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

Poa himalayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
