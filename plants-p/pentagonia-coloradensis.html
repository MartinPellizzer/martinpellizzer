Pentagonia coloradensis

Pentagonia coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado pentagonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, pentagonal-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, adapting to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Pentagonia coloradensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
