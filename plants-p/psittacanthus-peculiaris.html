Psittacanthus peculiaris

Psittacanthus peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and leathery. The plant produces cones, with male cones clustered at the base and female cones emerging from the crown, facilitating its dioecious reproductive system. Native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soils.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
