Polygonum lapathifolium var. laxum

Polygonum lapathifolium var. laxum, commonly known as the looseleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a slightly serrated margin, often appearing in alternate arrangement along the stem. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late summer and early fall.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.
