Pulsatilla cottianaea

Pulsatilla cottianaea, commonly known as the Alpine Pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and appear after the flowers, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to cold climates.

Pulsatilla cottianaea is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
