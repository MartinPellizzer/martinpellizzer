Polygala vulgaris proles caliptera

Polygala vulgaris proles caliptera, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, pink or purple flowers arranged in spikes, and its leaves are typically hairy or woolly in texture. The plant belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
