Piper nigricans

Piper nigricans, commonly known as black pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Malabar Coast of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark berries that are harvested for their aromatic and pungent seeds. The plant is a vine that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its fruit develops in clusters and is typically dried to produce the spice widely used in global cuisine.

The botanical structure of Piper nigricans supports its role as a key economic crop in tropical regions.
