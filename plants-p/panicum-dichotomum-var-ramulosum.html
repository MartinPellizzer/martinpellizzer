Panicum dichotomum var. ramulosum

Panicum dichotomum var. ramulosum, commonly known as the tufted panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often branch at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
