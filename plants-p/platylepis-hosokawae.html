Platylepis hosokawae

Platylepis hosokawae, commonly known as the Japanese flat-leafed yew, is a rare and endangered plant species native to Japan.

It is a member of the Taxaceae family, characterized by its coniferous appearance and toxic compounds. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
