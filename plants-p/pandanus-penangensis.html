Pandanus penangensis

Pandanus penangensis, commonly known as the Penang pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Penang in Malaysia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters at the tips of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in coastal areas.

Pandanus penangensis is an important species in the local ecosystem and has cultural significance in the region.
