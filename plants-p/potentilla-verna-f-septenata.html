Potentilla verna f. septenata

Potentilla verna f. septenata, commonly known as the seven-leaf potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its compact form and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically features seven leaflets on each compound leaf, giving it its distinguishing name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
