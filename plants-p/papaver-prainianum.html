Papaver prainianum

Papaver prainianum, commonly known as the Prain's poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are borne on tall, slender stems.

Papaver prainianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
