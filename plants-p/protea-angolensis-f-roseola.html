Protea angolensis f. roseola

Protea angolensis f. roseola, commonly known as the roseola protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pink to rose-colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in the coastal regions of South Africa. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with leathery, lance-shaped leaves.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
