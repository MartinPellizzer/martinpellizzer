Peperomia amplexicaulis

Peperomia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging pepper plant, is a compact, low-growing species native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that often wrap around its stems, giving it a unique, almost cascading appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and terrariums.
