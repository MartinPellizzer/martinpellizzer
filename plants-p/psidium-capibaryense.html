Psidium capibaryense

Psidium capibaryense, commonly known as the capibara guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and agroforestry systems.
