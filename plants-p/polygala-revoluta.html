Polygala revoluta

Polygala revoluta, commonly known as the spiral spurge or mountain spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, and it often grows in rocky or woodland habitats.

Polygala revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
