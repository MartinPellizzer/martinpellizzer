Pritzelia sanguinea

Pritzelia sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a short stem with a single, palmate leaf. Its botanical name reflects the reddish color of its roots, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Pritzelia sanguinea is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
