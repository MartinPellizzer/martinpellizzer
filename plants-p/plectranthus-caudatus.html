Plectranthus caudatus

Plectranthus caudatus, commonly known as the Lion's Tail, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trailing stems and dense, velvety foliage that gives it a soft, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its popularity among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
