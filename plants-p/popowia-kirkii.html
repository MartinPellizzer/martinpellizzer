Popowia kirkii

Popowia kirkii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with low rainfall.

Popowia kirkii is also known for its latex, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.
