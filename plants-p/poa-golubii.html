Poa golubii

Poa golubii, commonly known as blue grass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa golubii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
