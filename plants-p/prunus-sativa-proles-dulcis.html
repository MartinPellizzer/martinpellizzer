Prunus sativa proles dulcis

Prunus sativa proles dulcis, commonly known as the peanut, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces pods that contain one or more edible seeds, known as peanuts. These seeds are rich in protein, healthy fats, and various nutrients.

The botanical features of Prunus sativa proles dulcis make it a valuable crop in agriculture and nutrition.
