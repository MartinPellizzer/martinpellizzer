Pentasacme caudatum

Pentasacme caudatum, commonly known as the hairy plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
