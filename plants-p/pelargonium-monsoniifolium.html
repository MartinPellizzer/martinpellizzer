Pelargonium monsoniifolium

Pelargonium monsoniifolium, commonly known as the Monson's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
