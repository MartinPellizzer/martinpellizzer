Prunus communis depressa

Prunus communis depressa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, rounded foliage with dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, sweet-tart berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are white or pale pink and arranged in clusters.

Prunus communis depressa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
