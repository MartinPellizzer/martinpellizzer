Pavonia rubriphylla

Pavonia rubriphylla, commonly known as the red-leaf pavonia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its striking red-tinged leaves and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. It typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 feet.

Pavonia rubriphylla is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
