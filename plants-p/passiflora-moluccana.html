Passiflora moluccana

Passiflora moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora moluccana thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.
