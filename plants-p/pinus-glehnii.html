Pinus glehnii

Pinus glehnii, commonly known as the Glehn's pine, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The cones of Pinus glehnii are woody and often remain attached to the branches for many years. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
