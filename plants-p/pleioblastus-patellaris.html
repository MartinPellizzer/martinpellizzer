Pleioblastus patellaris

Pleioblastus patellaris, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a member of the bamboo family (Gramineae).

It is a clump-forming plant with slender, arching culms that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The leaves are linear, narrow, and have a bluish-green hue with a slight waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to eastern Asia.

This ornamental plant is valued for its delicate appearance and ability to form dense, attractive foliage in gardens.
