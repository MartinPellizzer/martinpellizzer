Primula violodora

Primula violodora, commonly known as the violet viola, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Primula violodora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
