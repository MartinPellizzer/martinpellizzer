Pyrus commixta f. microphylla

Pyrus commixta f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese white pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and compact growth habit, making it suitable for smaller gardens or container planting. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It is a cold-hardy species, capable of thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and mild, flavorful fruit.
