Paspalum heterotrichon var. paucispicatum

Paspalum heterotrichon var. paucispicatum, commonly known as the narrow-panicle paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and soil stabilization.
