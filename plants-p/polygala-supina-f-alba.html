Polygala supina f. alba

Polygala supina f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered milkweed or white polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
