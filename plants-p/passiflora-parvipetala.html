Passiflora parvipetala

Passiflora parvipetala, commonly known as the small-flowered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora parvipetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
