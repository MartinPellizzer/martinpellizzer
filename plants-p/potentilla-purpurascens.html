Potentilla purpurascens

Potentilla purpurascens, commonly known as the purple cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla purpurascens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
