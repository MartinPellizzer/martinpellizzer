Platyclinis latifolia

Platyclinis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed platyclinis, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
