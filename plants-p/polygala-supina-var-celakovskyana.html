Polygala supina var. celakovskyana

Polygala supina var. celakovskyana, commonly known as the prostrate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often prostrate stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in spicate inflorescences, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
