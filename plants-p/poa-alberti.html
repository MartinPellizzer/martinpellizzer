Poa alberti

Poa alberti, commonly known as Albert's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, sod-forming turf. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Poa alberti is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
