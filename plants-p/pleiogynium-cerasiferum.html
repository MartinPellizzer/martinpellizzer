Pleiogynium cerasiferum

Pleiogynium cerasiferum, commonly known as the cherry-leaf pleiogynium, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged beneath. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
