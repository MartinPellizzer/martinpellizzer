Pygeum pilinospermum

Pygeum pilinospermum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its woody shrub form with compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods containing angular seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.
