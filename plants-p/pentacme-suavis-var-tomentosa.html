Pentacme suavis var. tomentosa

Pentacme suavis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the sweet-scented pentacme, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
