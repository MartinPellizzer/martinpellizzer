Parnassia speciosa

Parnassia speciosa, commonly known as the showy parnassia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its striking white flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

Parnassia speciosa is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is generally not cultivated for commercial use.
