Pittosporum crassifolium

Pittosporum crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Pittosporum crassifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
