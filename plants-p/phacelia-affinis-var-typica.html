Phacelia affinis var. typica

Phacelia affinis var. typica, commonly known as the common phacelia, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting native wildlife.
