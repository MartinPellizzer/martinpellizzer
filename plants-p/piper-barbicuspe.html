Piper barbicuspe

Piper barbicuspe, commonly known as the spiny pipevine, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
