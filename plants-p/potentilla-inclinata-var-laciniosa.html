Potentilla inclinata var. laciniosa

Potentilla inclinata var. laciniosa, commonly known as the fringed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often sprawling growth habit and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The leaves are typically lobed or deeply divided, giving the plant a distinctive, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in harsh environmental conditions.
