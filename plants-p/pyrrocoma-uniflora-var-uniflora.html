Pyrrocoma uniflora var. uniflora

Pyrrocoma uniflora var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower pyrrocoma, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, showy flower that is usually pink or purple in color, blooming during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
