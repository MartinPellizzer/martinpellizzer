Passerina hirsuta var. oscilans

Passerina hirsuta var. oscilans, commonly known as the hairy pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers appear in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
