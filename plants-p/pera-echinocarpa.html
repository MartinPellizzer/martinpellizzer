Pera echinocarpa

Pera echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny-fruited pera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spiny fruit, which is typically yellow or orange when mature. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
