Phelypaea ramosissima

Phelypaea ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phelypaea ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
