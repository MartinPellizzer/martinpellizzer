Parasenecio palmatisectus

Parasenecio palmatisectus, commonly known as the palmate-leaved senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves that give it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
