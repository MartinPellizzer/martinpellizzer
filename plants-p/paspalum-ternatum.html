Paspalum ternatum

Paspalum ternatum, commonly known as the three-awn grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and the presence of three awns on its seed heads. The plant typically thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Paspalum ternatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
