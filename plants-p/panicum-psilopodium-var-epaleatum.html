Panicum psilopodium var. epaleatum

Panicum psilopodium var. epaleatum, commonly known as the pale panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass genus native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and distinctive, pale-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include narrow leaves and spikelets that are often pale green or whitish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
