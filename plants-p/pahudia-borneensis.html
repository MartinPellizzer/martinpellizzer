Pahudia borneensis

Pahudia borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers with vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy of tropical forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
