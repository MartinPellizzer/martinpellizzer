Pandanus boninensis

Pandanus boninensis, commonly known as the Bonin pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing near the shoreline.

The leaves are used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance in the region.
