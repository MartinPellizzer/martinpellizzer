Pelargonium × phaedimon

Pelargonium × phaedimon, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant indoors.
