Proclesia cordata

Proclesia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit, it is often used in ornamental gardening.
