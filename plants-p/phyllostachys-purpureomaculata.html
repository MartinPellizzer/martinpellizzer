Phyllostachys purpureomaculata

Phyllostachys purpureomaculata, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that exhibit a distinctive purplish-red coloration, especially under full sun. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by feathery seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it both a decorative and functional plant in horticulture.
