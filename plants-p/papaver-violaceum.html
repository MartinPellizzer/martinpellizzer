Papaver violaceum

Papaver violaceum, commonly known as the violet poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple petals and a distinctive, often deeply lobed leaf structure. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms in the spring. Its flowers are bisexual, with both stamens and pistils present, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Papaver violaceum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
