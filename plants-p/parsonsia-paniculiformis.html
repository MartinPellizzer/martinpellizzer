Parsonsia paniculiformis

Parsonsia paniculiformis, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant is a shrub or small tree with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy beneath. Its flowers are typically arranged in panicles, giving it the species name *paniculiformis*.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
