Plutarchia speciosa

Plutarchia speciosa, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of showy, orange-red flowers with a distinctive, flame-like appearance. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
