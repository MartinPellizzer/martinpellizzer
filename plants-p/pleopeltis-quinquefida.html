Pleopeltis quinquefida

Pleopeltis quinquefida, commonly known as the five-lobed huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and distinctive, lanceolate leaves with five distinct lobes. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or shaded forest floors, thriving in humid, tropical environments. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
