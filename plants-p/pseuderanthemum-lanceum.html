Pseuderanthemum lanceum

Pseuderanthemum lanceum, commonly known as the lanceleaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or vine, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Pseuderanthemum lanceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
