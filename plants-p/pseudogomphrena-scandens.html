Pseudogomphrena scandens

Pseudogomphrena scandens, commonly known as the climbing gomphrena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect or prostrate growth habit and is native to regions of South America. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pseudogomphrena scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
