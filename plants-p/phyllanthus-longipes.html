Phyllanthus longipes

Phyllanthus longipes, commonly known as the longipes gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive glandular texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
