Potentilla canescens proles typica

Potentilla canescens proles typica, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Potentilla canescens proles typica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
