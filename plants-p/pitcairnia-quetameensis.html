Pitcairnia quetameensis

Pitcairnia quetameensis, commonly known as the Quetameensis air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water absorption. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a striking display of colors ranging from white to red.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
