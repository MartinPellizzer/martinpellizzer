Piper brassii

Piper brassii, commonly known as the African black pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper brassii make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
