Pyrus aucuparia subsp. fenenkiana

Pyrus aucuparia subsp.

fenenkiana, commonly known as the Fenenkina crabapple, is a subspecies native to parts of Eastern Europe and Central Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree with lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath.

The flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and are followed by small, edible apples that are often used in local cuisines. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.

Its fruit is also a food source for various wildlife species.
