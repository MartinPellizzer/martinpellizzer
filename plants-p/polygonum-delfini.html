Polygonum delfini

Polygonum delfini, commonly known as the Delphinium knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright, branching stems that are often reddish in color and bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters and come in shades of blue, purple, and white.

Polygonum delfini is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
