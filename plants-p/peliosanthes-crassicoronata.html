Peliosanthes crassicoronata

Peliosanthes crassicoronata, commonly known as the broad-crowned cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, fan-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
