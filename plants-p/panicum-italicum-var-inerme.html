Panicum italicum var. inerme

Panicum italicum var. inerme, commonly known as Italian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to suppress weeds make it a valuable plant in sustainable landscaping.
