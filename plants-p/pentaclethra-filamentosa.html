Pentaclethra filamentosa

Pentaclethra filamentosa, commonly known as the slender-leafed legume, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, linear leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.

The species is also valued for its timber, though it is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss.
