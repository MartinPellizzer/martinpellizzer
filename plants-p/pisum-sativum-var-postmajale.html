Pisum sativum var. postmajale

Pisum sativum var. postmajale, commonly known as the postmajale pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its distinct pod and seed morphology, which differ from the typical garden pea. This variety is native to specific regions in Eastern Europe and has adapted to local climatic conditions. Its growth habit includes climbing or trailing stems, typical of many legumes.

The plant's flowers are usually purple, and it produces edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines.
