Potentilla petiolulosa

Potentilla petiolulosa, commonly known as the slenderleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Potentilla petiolulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging habitats.
