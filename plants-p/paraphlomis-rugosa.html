Paraphlomis rugosa

Paraphlomis rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf paroissia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
