Polygala hildebrandtii var. angustisepala

Polygala hildebrandtii var. angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in clusters, making it a distinctive and ornamental species.
