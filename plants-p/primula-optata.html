Primula optata

Primula optata, commonly known as the Persian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of basal leaves that are typically hairy and slightly fleshy, adapted to its arid environment. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine arrangements.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
