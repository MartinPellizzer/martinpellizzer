Passiflora kermesina lemicheziana

Passiflora kermesina lemicheziana, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed fresh or in jams. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
