Plantago schneideri

Plantago schneideri, commonly known as Schneider's plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind.

This species is valued for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
