Polygonum effusum

Polygonum effusum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its erect, reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Polygonum effusum is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine.
