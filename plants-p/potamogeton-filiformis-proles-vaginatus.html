Potamogeton filiformis proles vaginatus

Potamogeton filiformis proles vaginatus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its long, thin, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters near the water surface. Its leaves are often covered in a thin layer of mucus, which helps in reducing water loss and protecting against herbivores.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
