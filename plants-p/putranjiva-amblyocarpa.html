Putranjiva amblyocarpa

Putranjiva amblyocarpa, commonly known as the giant dayflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, fleshy calyx. The plant has a creeping growth habit and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
