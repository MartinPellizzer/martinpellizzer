Piper roxburghianum

Piper roxburghianum, commonly known as Indian pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, clustered berries. The plant produces small, green, oval fruits that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a pungent aroma.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
