Pentaptera alata

Pentaptera alata, commonly known as the winged pentaptery, is a distinctive flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which features five lobes with wing-like extensions, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with woody stems and alternate leaves.

Pentaptera alata is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its role in local ecosystems.
