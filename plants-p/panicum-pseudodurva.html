Panicum pseudodurva

Panicum pseudodurva, commonly known as purple false panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, slender growth habit and produces dense, fluffy seed heads that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and along roadsides. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds ornamental value to landscapes.

Panicum pseudodurva is often used in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
