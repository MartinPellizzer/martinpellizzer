Pelargonium hirsutum

Pelargonium hirsutum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy, often lobed leaves and showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, depending on the climate, and produces clusters of five-petaled flowers. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to different growing conditions.

The seeds are small and contain a high oil content, which can be used in various industrial applications.
