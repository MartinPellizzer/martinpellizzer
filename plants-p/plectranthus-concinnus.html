Plectranthus concinnus

Plectranthus concinnus, commonly known as the velvetleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators.
