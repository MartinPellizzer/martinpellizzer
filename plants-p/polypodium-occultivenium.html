Polypodium occultivenium

Polypodium occultivenium, commonly known as the hidden fern, is a rare and enigmatic species belonging to the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble those of other ferns, though it exhibits unique morphological traits. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a feature common to many ferns.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it remains a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
