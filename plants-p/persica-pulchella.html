Persica pulchella

Persica pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful peach.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. Its flowers are white with a pinkish tinge and appear in clusters, followed by small, sweet fruits.

Persica pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
