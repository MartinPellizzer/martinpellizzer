Piper labeculatum

Piper labeculatum, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic foliage.
