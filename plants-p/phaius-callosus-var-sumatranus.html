Phaius callosus var. sumatranus

Phaius callosus var. sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces a single, elongated inflorescence bearing numerous flowers, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
