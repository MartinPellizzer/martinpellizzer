Plantago bellardii var. pygmaea

Plantago bellardii var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf plantain, is a small perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer. It is characterized by its compact size and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
