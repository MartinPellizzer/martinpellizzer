Poa bulbosa f. vivipara

Poa bulbosa f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its ability to produce viviparous shoots, which are capable of rooting and growing into new plants while still attached to the parent. This feature allows for rapid vegetative propagation, making it a resilient species in various habitats. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and forms dense, low-growing mats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique reproductive strategy and adaptability to different soil conditions.
