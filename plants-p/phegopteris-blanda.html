Phegopteris blanda

Phegopteris blanda, commonly known as the common huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phegopteris blanda is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in maintaining forest floor ecosystems.
