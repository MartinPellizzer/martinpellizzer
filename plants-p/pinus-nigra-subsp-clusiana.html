Pinus nigra subsp. clusiana

Pinus nigra subsp.

clusiana, commonly known as the Spanish black pine, is a subspecies native to the Iberian Peninsula and southern France. It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer with dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters of five.

The cones are woody and can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters, containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in forestry and landscaping.
