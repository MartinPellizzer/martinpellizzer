Phebalium alpinum

Phebalium alpinum, commonly known as the alpine hakea, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is adapted to harsh, alpine conditions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
