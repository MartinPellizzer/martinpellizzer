Polygonum stenophyllum

Polygonum stenophyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaved knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which attract various pollinators.
