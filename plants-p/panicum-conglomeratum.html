Panicum conglomeratum

Panicum conglomeratum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink, adding visual interest. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, Panicum conglomeratum is widely used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
