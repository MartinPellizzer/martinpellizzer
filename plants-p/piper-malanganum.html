Piper malanganum

Piper malanganum, commonly known as the Malanggan pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are also used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic fruits.
