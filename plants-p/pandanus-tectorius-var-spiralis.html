Pandanus tectorius var. spiralis

Pandanus tectorius var. spiralis, commonly known as the spiral pandanus, is a variant of the pandanus plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its resilience in humid environments.

This variety is often used in traditional crafts and for its aesthetic value in landscaping.
