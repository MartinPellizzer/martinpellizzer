Primulina silaniae

Primulina silaniae, commonly known as the Silanian violet, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant violet and purple foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain water in its humid, shaded habitat.

Primulina silaniae is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific care requirements.
