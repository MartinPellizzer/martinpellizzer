Pileus pentaphyllus

Pileus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-lobed cap mushroom, is a species of fungus characterized by its distinctive cap shape.

It belongs to the family Tricholomataceae and is typically found in temperate forest regions. The cap is broad and convex, often with a central depression, and is covered in a fine, silky texture. Its gills are closely spaced and usually pale in color, while the stem is slender and sometimes slightly curved.

This mushroom is often associated with decaying wood and can be found in both coniferous and broadleaf forests.
