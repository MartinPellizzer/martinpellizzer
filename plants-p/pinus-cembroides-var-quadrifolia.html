Pinus cembroides var. quadrifolia

Pinus cembroides var. quadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf mountain pine, is a variant of the Mexican mountain pine.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its short, stout needles and dense, compact growth habit. Its cones are typically small and woody, containing two to three seeds.

This species is well adapted to rocky soils and can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
