Plantago barbata subvar. taraxacoides

Plantago barbata subvar. taraxacoides, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy leaves.

It produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and wound-healing benefits.

The subspecies taraxacoides is distinguished by its more pronounced hairiness and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other varieties of Plantago barbata.
