Pimentus laurinus

Pimentus laurinus, commonly known as the sacred laurel, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers. It produces berries that are typically orange or red in color, which contain the seeds.

Pimentus laurinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
