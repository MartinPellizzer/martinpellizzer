Pittosporum resinosum

Pittosporum resinosum, commonly known as the resinous pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

Pittosporum resinosum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
