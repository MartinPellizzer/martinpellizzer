Psydrax dicoccos var. dicoccos

Psydrax dicoccos var. dicoccos, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in nutrients, including carbohydrates, proteins, and various micronutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The tree is also valued for its potential in agroforestry and as a source of traditional medicine.
