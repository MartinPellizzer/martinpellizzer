Philodendron hookeri

Philodendron hookeri, commonly known as the velvetleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron hookeri is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
