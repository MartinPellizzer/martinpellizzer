Poa fuegiana

Poa fuegiana, commonly known as the Falkland grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of South America, particularly the Falkland Islands and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a soft layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous blue-green flowers that are arranged in dense spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in coastal and maritime climates, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Poa fuegiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
