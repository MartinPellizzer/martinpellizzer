Pyrola rotundifolia var. asarifolia

Pyrola rotundifolia var. asarifolia, commonly known as the asarifolia pyrola, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the northern hemisphere.

It features rosettes of oval, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This variety is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape compared to the typical form of Pyrola rotundifolia.
