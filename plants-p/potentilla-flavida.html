Potentilla flavida

Potentilla flavida, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible.

Potentilla flavida is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
