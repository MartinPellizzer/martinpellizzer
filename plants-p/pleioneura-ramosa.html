Pleioneura ramosa

Pleioneura ramosa, commonly known as the branched pleione, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
