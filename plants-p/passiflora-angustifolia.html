Passiflora angustifolia

Passiflora angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, round, blue or black berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface and serrated edges.

Passiflora angustifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
