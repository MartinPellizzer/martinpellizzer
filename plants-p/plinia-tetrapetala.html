Plinia tetrapetala

Plinia tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled huckleberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Plinia tetrapetala thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
