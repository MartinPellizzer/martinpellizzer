Potentilla opaca var. vindobonensis

Potentilla opaca var. vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vindobonensis cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Central Europe.

It typically features small, dark green leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
