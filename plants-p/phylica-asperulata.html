Phylica asperulata

Phylica asperulata, commonly known as the Natal hakea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Phylica asperulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
