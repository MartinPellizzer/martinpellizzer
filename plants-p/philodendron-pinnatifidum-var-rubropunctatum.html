Philodendron pinnatifidum var. rubropunctatum

Philodendron pinnatifidum var. rubropunctatum, commonly known as the red-spotted philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive red or orange dot pattern along the veins, which gives it its common name. The plant has a vining growth habit and can reach significant lengths, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained on supports. Its foliage is usually a deep green with contrasting red or orange markings, adding visual interest to indoor spaces.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
