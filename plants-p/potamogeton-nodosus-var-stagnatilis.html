Potamogeton nodosus var. stagnatilis

Potamogeton nodosus var. stagnatilis, commonly known as the nodding pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
