Poterium sanguisorba var. microcarpum

Poterium sanguisorba var. microcarpum, commonly known as narrowleaf bloodwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its distinctive, bead-like fruits are covered in fine hairs and mature in the fall.

It is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
