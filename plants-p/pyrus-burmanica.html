Pyrus burmanica

Pyrus burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Myanmar, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit, a small, yellowish pear, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.

Pyrus burmanica plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
