Prunus serrulata f. splendens

Prunus serrulata f. splendens, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping sakura, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. Its foliage is dark green, and it produces small, edible fruits that are used in traditional cuisine.

The tree is relatively hardy and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
