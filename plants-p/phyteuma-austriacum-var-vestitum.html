Phyteuma austriacum var. vestitum

Phyteuma austriacum var. vestitum, commonly known as the Austrian beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a fine, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
