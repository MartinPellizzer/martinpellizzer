Pachystela laurentii

Pachystela laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, star-shaped white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period is brief, typically lasting only a few weeks in late spring.

Pachystela laurentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
