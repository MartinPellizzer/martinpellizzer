Pollinia ciliata var. glabrata

Pollinia ciliata var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-fruited pollinia, is a variant of the pollinia species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous surfaces and distinctive floral structures that facilitate its unique pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical forests. Its flowers produce small, fragrant blooms that attract specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological niche.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
