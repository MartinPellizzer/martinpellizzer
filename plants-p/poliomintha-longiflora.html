Poliomintha longiflora

Poliomintha longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered poliomintha, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
