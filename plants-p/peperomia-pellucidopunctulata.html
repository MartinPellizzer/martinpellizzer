Peperomia pellucidopunctulata

Peperomia pellucidopunctulata, commonly known as the translucent-punctuated peperomia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, waxy leaves that display a unique combination of translucent and dotted markings. The leaves are typically dark green with a pale or silver underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and red, which are followed by tiny, round berries.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its slow growth and tolerance of low light conditions.
