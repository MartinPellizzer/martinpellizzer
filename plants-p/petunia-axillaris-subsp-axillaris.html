Petunia axillaris subsp. axillaris

Petunia axillaris subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the axillary petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors.

The plant produces small, oval leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ability to bloom continuously throughout the growing season adds ornamental value to gardens.
