Psidium sericiflorum

Psidium sericiflorum, commonly known as the silverflower guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, which are followed by edible, yellow or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
