Potamogeton porrigens

Potamogeton porrigens, commonly known as the curly pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, flexible stems that often curl or twist, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, and it has whorled leaves that are narrow and pointed. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton porrigens is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as lakes, ponds, and marshes.
