Polygala hospita subsp. bosniaca

Polygala hospita subsp.

bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are pale pink or white with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
