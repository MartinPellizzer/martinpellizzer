Pleurothallis acutidentata

Pleurothallis acutidentata, commonly known as the sharp-toothed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous tiny blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
