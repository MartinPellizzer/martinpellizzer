Petradoria graminea

Petradoria graminea, commonly known as the grassy petradoria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green flowers. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its low profile and adaptive features, it is well-suited for survival in harsh, dry climates.
