Pleioblastus maculatus var. longitubus

Pleioblastus maculatus var. longitubus, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a variety of bamboo characterized by its distinctive mottled green and yellow foliage.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with slender, upright culms that have a smooth, often yellowish surface. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a pointed tip and a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in landscaping and as a potted plant.
