Pinus densata subsp. wilsonii

Pinus densata subsp.

wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's dense pine, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five.

The tree produces cones that are often reddish-brown in color and contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
