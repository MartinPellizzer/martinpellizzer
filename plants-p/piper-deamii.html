Piper deamii

Piper deamii, commonly known as the Deam's pepper or Deam's piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly southern Mexico and northern Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, fleshy berries.

Piper deamii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
