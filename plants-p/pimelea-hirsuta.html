Pimelea hirsuta

Pimelea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has glandular-hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Pimelea hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
