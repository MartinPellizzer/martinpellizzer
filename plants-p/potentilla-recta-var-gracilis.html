Potentilla recta var. gracilis

Potentilla recta var. gracilis, commonly known as slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically features wiry, upright stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, oval to lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This species is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
