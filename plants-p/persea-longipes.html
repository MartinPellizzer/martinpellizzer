Persea longipes

Persea longipes, commonly known as the longipes persimmon, is a species of evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, yellowish berry that matures in late summer and is edible when fully ripe.
