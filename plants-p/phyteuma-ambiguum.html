Phyteuma ambiguum

Phyteuma ambiguum, commonly known as the uncertain phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing violet or blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among gardeners.
