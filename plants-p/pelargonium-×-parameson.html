Pelargonium × parameson

Pelargonium × parameson, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically features lobed, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Pelargonium × parameson is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
