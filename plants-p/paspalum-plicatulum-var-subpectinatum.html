Paspalum plicatulum var. subpectinatum

Paspalum plicatulum var. subpectinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is adapted to both full sun and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
