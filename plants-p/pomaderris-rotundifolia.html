Pomaderris rotundifolia

Pomaderris rotundifolia, commonly known as the Round-leaf Pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, round, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is commonly found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
