Panicum sanguinale var. bicorne

Panicum sanguinale var. bicorne, commonly known as the scarlet bristlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive, reddish-purple inflorescences that resemble bristles. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically arranged in a panicle structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

This species is often found in open, sunny habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils.
