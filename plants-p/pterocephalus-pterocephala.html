Pterocephalus pterocephala

Pterocephalus pterocephala, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy leaves and produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
