Patagonula glabra

Patagonula glabra, commonly known as the smooth Patagonian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Patagonula glabra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
