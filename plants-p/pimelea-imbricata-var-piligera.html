Pimelea imbricata var. piligera

Pimelea imbricata var. piligera, commonly known as the Grey Leafed Heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with small, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
