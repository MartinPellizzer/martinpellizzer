Pseuderanthemum diachylum

Pseuderanthemum diachylum, commonly known as the mountain pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
