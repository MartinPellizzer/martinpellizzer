Piper esquivelanum

Piper esquivelanum, commonly known as the Esquivel's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, three-lobed berries.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
