Platanthera × lueri

Platanthera × lueri, commonly known as the hybrid marsh orchid, is a rare and fascinating plant that results from the cross between Platanthera chlorophaea and Platanthera falcata.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, acidic habitats such as bogs and fens. The plant produces tall, slender stems with showy, greenish-white flowers that are often fragrant. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Platanthera × lueri is considered endangered in many regions.
