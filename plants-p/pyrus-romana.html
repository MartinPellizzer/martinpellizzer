Pyrus romana

Pyrus romana, commonly known as the Roman pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and have a distinctive grayish-green color.

The Roman pear is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
