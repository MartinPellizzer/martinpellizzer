Panicum imbricatum

Panicum imbricatum, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and produces fluffy, pinkish-white inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Silky bluegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
