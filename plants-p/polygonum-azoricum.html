Polygonum azoricum

Polygonum azoricum, commonly known as Azorean knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Azores.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded areas. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Polygonum azoricum is notable for its adaptability to rocky and coastal environments.
