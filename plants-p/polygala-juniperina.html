Polygala juniperina

Polygala juniperina, commonly known as the Juniper Bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, bladder-like structures that aid in water retention.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to arid environments.
