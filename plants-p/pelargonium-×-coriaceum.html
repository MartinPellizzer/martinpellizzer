Pelargonium × coriaceum

Pelargonium × coriaceum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
