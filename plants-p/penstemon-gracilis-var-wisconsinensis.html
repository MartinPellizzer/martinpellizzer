Penstemon gracilis var. wisconsinensis

Penstemon gracilis var. wisconsinensis, commonly known as the Wisconsin beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in a basal rosette. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
