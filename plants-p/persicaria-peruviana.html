Persicaria peruviana

Persicaria peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria peruviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
