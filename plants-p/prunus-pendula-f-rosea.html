Prunus pendula f. rosea

Prunus pendula f. rosea, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the Prunus pendula species.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The flowers are often tinged with a soft rose color, giving the plant its cultivar name "rosea.". This ornamental tree thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and parks.

Its delicate appearance and seasonal blooms make it a beloved addition to landscapes in temperate climates.
