Pelargonium × malacophyllum

Pelargonium × malacophyllum, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and velvety to the touch. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium × malacophyllum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
