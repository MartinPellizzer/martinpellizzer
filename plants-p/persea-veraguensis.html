Persea veraguensis

Persea veraguensis, commonly known as the avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a large, pear-shaped berry with a smooth, thin skin and a creamy, oily pulp. The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritious and flavorful fruit.
