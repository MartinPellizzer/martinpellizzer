Polygala vittata

Polygala vittata, commonly known as the spotted milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

Polygala vittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
