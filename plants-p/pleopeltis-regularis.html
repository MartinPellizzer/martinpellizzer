Pleopeltis regularis

Pleopeltis regularis, commonly known as the regular pleopeltis, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. Its fronds are usually divided into segments, with the lower leaves being more elongated than the upper ones.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in indoor conditions with proper care.
