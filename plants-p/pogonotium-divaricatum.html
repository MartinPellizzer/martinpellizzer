Pogonotium divaricatum

Pogonotium divaricatum, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems that often exhibit a zigzag or twisted appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-brown flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Pogonotium divaricatum plays an important role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
