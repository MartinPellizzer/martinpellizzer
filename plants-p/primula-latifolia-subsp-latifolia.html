Primula latifolia subsp. latifolia

Primula latifolia subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It features basal rosettes of broad, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands or rocky areas.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
