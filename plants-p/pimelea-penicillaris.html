Pimelea penicillaris

Pimelea penicillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf devil's-bit, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Pimelea penicillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
