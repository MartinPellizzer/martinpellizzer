Pentopetia elastica

Pentopetia elastica, commonly known as the rubber vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fast-growing, sprawling habit and rubbery, fleshy stems that can be harvested for latex. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or white. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Pentopetia elastica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
