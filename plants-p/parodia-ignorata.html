Parodia ignorata

Parodia ignorata, commonly known as the "Papalote" or "Cactus of the Holy Father," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized botanical specimen.
