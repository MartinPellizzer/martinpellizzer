Pedilonum tubiflorum

Pedilonum tubiflorum, commonly known as the tuberous bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive tuberous roots and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Pedilonum tubiflorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
