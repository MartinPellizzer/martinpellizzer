Pyrus acidula

Pyrus acidula, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, yellowish-green fruits that are tart and acidic. Pyrus acidula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in traditional Chinese cuisine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
