Potamogeton pectinatus var. latifolius

Potamogeton pectinatus var. latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in spikelets, which are borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
