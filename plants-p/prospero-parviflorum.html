Prospero parviflorum

Prospero parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower prospero, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, delicate white or pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in garden settings.
