Pyrus spectabilis var. albescens

Pyrus spectabilis var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered service tree, is a variety of the service tree species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its white, showy flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
