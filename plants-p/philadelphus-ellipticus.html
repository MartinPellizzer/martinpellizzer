Philadelphus ellipticus

Philadelphus ellipticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, elliptic-shaped leaves that are usually hairy beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and pleasant, citrus-like scent.
