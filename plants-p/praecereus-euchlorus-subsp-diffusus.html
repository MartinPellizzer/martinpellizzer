Praecereus euchlorus subsp. diffusus

Praecereus euchlorus subsp.

diffusus, commonly known as the Ghost Cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its unique, branching structure and pale green to bluish-gray stems that often appear translucent.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and xeriscaping.
