Primula tayloriana subsp. scopulorum

Primula tayloriana subsp.

scopulorum, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine downy coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its resilience and beauty.
