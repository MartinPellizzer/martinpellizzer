Picrasma excelsa

Picrasma excelsa, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tall, evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The tree has a thick, rugged bark and produces small, woody fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
