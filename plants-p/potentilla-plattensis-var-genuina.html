Potentilla plattensis var. genuina

Potentilla plattensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Platt's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
