Philadelphus microphyllus var. linearis

Philadelphus microphyllus var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its varietal name. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
