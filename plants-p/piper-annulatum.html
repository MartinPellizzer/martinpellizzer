Piper annulatum

Piper annulatum, commonly known as the ringed pepper or annular pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into the aromatic, peppery fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when mature and dried to produce a spice with a unique, slightly bitter flavor.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice in various Southeast Asian cuisines.
