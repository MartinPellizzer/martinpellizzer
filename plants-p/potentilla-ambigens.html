Potentilla ambigens

Potentilla ambigens, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the northwestern United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla ambigens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
