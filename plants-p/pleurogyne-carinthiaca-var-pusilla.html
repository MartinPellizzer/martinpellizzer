Pleurogyne carinthiaca var. pusilla

Pleurogyne carinthiaca var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf pleurogyne, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a sought-after species among horticultural enthusiasts.
