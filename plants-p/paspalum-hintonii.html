Paspalum hintonii

Paspalum hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's paspalum, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Paspalum hintonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
