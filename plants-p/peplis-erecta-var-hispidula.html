Peplis erecta var. hispidula

Peplis erecta var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy peplis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "hispidula.". The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid and semi-arid environments.
