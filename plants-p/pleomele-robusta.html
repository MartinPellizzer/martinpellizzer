Pleomele robusta

Pleomele robusta, commonly known as the mountain cycad, is a unique plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to several meters tall, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, while its trunk is often covered in a rough, fibrous bark.

Pleomele robusta is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, elevated areas.
