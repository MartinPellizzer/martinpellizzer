Pervinca major

Pervinca major, commonly known as the greater periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pervinca major is also valued for its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
