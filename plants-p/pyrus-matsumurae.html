Pyrus matsumurae

Pyrus matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its fruit in certain regions.

Pyrus matsumurae is often found in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
