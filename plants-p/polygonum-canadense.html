Polygonum canadense

Polygonum canadense, commonly known as Canada knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Polygonum canadense is a member of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
