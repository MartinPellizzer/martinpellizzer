Pelargonium × trifurcatum

Pelargonium × trifurcatum, commonly known as the triple-lobed geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
