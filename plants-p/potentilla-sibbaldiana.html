Potentilla sibbaldiana

Potentilla sibbaldiana, commonly known as the Sibbald's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Potentilla sibbaldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
