Pholidota globosa

Pholidota globosa, commonly known as the globular tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its woody stems are often covered in a rough, bark-like texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
