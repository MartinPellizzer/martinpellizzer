Paepalanthus batocephalus

Paepalanthus batocephalus, commonly known as the golden cup-moss, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial bromeliad that forms dense, cushion-like growths. The plant is characterized by its tightly packed, grass-like leaves that create a rosette structure. It produces striking yellow flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

This species is native to the Andean regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
