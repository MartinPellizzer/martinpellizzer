Philodendron disparile

Philodendron disparile, commonly known as the Variegated Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with white and green patterns, adding visual interest to its foliage. The plant has a vining growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a climbing plant in shaded areas. Its leaves are glossy and can grow quite large, often reaching up to 12 inches in length.

Philodendron disparile thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its vibrant appearance.
