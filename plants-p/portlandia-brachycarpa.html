Portlandia brachycarpa

Portlandia brachycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited pincushion plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open, sunny habitats. Its small, berry-like fruits are borne on short stalks, giving it its specific epithet "brachycarpa," meaning "short fruit.".

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
