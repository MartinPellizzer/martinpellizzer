Plinia sebastianopolitana

Plinia sebastianopolitana, commonly known as the Sebastianopolis plum, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble plums. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green upper surface.

The flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
