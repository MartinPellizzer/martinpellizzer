Protea scolopendrina

Protea scolopendrina, commonly known as the red protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows in open, sunny environments, often found in fynbos vegetation. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, especially sugarbirds, due to their bright red color and nectar-rich structure.

The protea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
