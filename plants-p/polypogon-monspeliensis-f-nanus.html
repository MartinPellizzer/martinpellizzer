Polypogon monspeliensis f. nanus

Polypogon monspeliensis f. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf panicgrass, is a compact, mat-forming grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length, with a dense, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are borne on short, ascending stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
