Penstemon acuminatus var. fendleri

Penstemon acuminatus var. fendleri, commonly known as the Desert Penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its foliage is typically narrow and gray-green, and it produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
