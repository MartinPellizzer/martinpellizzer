Physalis hastata

Physalis hastata, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which turns red as the fruit ripens. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its edible fruits and is often cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
