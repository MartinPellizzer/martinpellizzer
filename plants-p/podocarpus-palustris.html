Podocarpus palustris

Podocarpus palustris, commonly known as the swamp cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its scale-like leaves and distinctive, pendulous cones. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its botanical features include a tall, slender trunk and a conical crown, which contribute to its ornamental value.

Podocarpus palustris plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
