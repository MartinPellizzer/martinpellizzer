Pleione hookeriana var. brachyglossa

Pleione hookeriana var. brachyglossa, commonly known as the Hooker's pleione, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its flowering period occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
