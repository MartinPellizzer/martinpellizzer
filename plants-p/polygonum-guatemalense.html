Polygonum guatemalense

Polygonum guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible or medicinal properties. The plant typically features slender, branching stems covered in small, sticky hairs. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and may have a reddish or purple tinge.

Polygonum guatemalense is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
