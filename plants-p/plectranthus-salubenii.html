Plectranthus salubenii

Plectranthus salubenii, commonly known as the Salubenii mint, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and compact growth habit.
