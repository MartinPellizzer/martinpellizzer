Plantago sericea subsp. argyrophylla

Plantago sericea subsp.

argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, roadside areas, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
