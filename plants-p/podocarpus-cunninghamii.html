Podocarpus cunninghamii

Podocarpus cunninghamii, commonly known as the hoop pine, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia and parts of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and long, cylindrical cones. The tree can grow up to 40 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, conical crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually dark green, while its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its durable timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
