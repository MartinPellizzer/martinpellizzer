Peponidium comorense

Peponidium comorense, commonly known as the Comoros pumpkin, is a climbing vine native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It produces large, elongated fruits that are typically orange to yellow in color, with a rough, hairy surface. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. Its leaves are lobed and alternate, often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine, valued for its nutritional content and medicinal properties.
