Pandanus grantii

Pandanus grantii, commonly known as the common pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in local crafts and cuisine. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that give way to yellowish or orange fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for thatching and weaving.

Pandanus grantii thrives in coastal and wetland environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
