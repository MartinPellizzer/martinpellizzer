Phaseolus canescens

Phaseolus canescens, commonly known as the hairy bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible beans. It is a climbing plant that often grows along fences or other structures.

Phaseolus canescens is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional cuisines in its native regions.
