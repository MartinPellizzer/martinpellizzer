Pilea chiriquina

Pilea chiriquina, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick, fleshy, and often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Pilea chiriquina is also noted for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
