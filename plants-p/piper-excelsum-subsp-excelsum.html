Piper excelsum subsp. excelsum

Piper excelsum subsp.

excelsum, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to the rainforests of New Guinea. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to 30 meters in height, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits containing aromatic, peppery seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its size and unique appearance, it is a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
