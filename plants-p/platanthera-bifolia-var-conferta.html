Platanthera bifolia var. conferta

Platanthera bifolia var. conferta, commonly known as the dense-flowered lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
