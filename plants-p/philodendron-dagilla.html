Philodendron dagilla

Philodendron dagilla, commonly known as the Emerald Queen, is a popular houseplant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, deep green leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in its natural habitat, and can reach heights of up to 10 feet when mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Philodendron dagilla is known for its rapid growth and is often used for indoor decoration due to its aesthetic appeal.
