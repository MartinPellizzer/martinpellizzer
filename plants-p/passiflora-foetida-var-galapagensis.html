Passiflora foetida var. galapagensis

Passiflora foetida var. galapagensis, commonly known as the Galápagos passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a significant role in the ecological balance of the Galápagos ecosystem.
