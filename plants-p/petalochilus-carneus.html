Petalochilus carneus

Petalochilus carneus, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and sepals, which are often marked with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
