Piper jamaicense f. microphyllum

Piper jamaicense f. microphyllum, commonly known as the Jamaican pepper plant or small-leaved pipevine, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, three-lobed berries. Native to the Caribbean, it thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and traditional uses.
