Parathesis violacea

Parathesis violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

The orchid's reproductive system involves a specialized relationship with specific pollinators, enhancing its ecological significance.
