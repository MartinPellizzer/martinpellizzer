Pennisetum glaucifolium

Pennisetum glaucifolium, commonly known as blue foxtail, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage and fluffy, feathery seed heads that resemble a fox's tail. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
