Potentilla caulescens f. multidentata

Potentilla caulescens f. multidentata, commonly known as the many-toothed cinquefoil, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. Its leaves are typically arranged in a basal rosette, with serrated margins that give it the "many-toothed" designation. The flowers are usually yellow or white, clustered in umbels, and bloom in early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
