Pedicularis × incarnatoides

Pedicularis × incarnatoides, commonly known as the hybrid Indian pipe, is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on its host for water and nutrients while still performing photosynthesis. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
