Podostemum griffithii

Podostemum griffithii, commonly known as the aquatic basketweed, is a unique aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shallow, fast-flowing streams and rivers, where it attaches itself to rocks or submerged vegetation. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often submerged, with some floating leaves that help in photosynthesis. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a characteristic known as dioecy.

Podostemum griffithii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic organisms and contributing to the overall biodiversity of freshwater ecosystems.
