Piper ambiguum

Piper ambiguum, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
