Piper dilatatum f. diamantense

Piper dilatatum f. diamantense, commonly known as the diamond leaf pepper or diamond leaf.

This plant is a variant of the broadleaf pepper, characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and glossy leaves with a diamond-like appearance. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
