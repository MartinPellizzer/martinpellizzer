Prunus serrulata f. purpurea

Prunus serrulata f. purpurea, commonly known as the pink flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking pink to magenta flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant display to gardens and landscapes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 10 to 15 feet, with a rounded, compact form.

This variety is highly valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often planted in parks, streets, and private gardens.
