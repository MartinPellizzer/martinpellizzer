Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. perglandulosum

Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. perglandulosum, commonly known as the silverleaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
