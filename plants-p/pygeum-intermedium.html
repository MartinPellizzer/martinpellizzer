Pygeum intermedium

Pygeum intermedium, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.
