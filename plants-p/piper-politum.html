Piper politum

Piper politum, commonly known as the smooth pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
