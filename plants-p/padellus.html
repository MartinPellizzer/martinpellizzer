Padellus

Padellus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, ghost-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a unique structure, with a single, slender stem and no true leaves, relying instead on its roots for photosynthesis. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found growing on the trunks of cypress trees.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Padellus is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
