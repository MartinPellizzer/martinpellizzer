Pholidota missionariorum

Pholidota missionariorum, commonly known as the missionary boxwood, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in opposite pairs. It produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
