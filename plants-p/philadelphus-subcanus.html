Philadelphus subcanus

Philadelphus subcanus, commonly known as the silver-leaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant belongs to the family Rosaceae and is closely related to other species in the Philadelphus genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage and flowers provide seasonal interest, with the leaves turning yellow in autumn before shedding.
