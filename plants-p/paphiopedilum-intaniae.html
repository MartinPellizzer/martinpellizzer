Paphiopedilum intaniae

Paphiopedilum intaniae, commonly known as the Malayan ghost orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble a ghostly face, hence its common name. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, typically arranged in a fan shape.

Paphiopedilum intaniae thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
