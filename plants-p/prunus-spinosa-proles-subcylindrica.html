Prunus spinosa proles subcylindrica

Prunus spinosa proles subcylindrica, commonly known as the European blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple berries and thorny branches, which provide habitat for various wildlife species. The plant typically grows in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small white flowers in early spring.

The botanical name reflects its cylindrical fruit structure and its classification within the Prunus genus.
