Phyllanthus indicus

Phyllanthus indicus, commonly known as the cheesewood or Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, green leaves. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and can grow up to several meters in height. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The leaves and roots of Phyllanthus indicus have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
