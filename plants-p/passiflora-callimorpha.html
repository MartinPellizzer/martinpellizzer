Passiflora callimorpha

Passiflora callimorpha, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.

Passiflora callimorpha thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.
