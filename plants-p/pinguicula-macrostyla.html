Pinguicula macrostyla

Pinguicula macrostyla, commonly known as the giant butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in bogs or wetlands. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and appear in late spring to early summer.

Pinguicula macrostyla is a perennial plant that relies on insect prey for essential nutrients.
