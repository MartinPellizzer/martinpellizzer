Panzeria multifida

Panzeria multifida, commonly known as the forked panzeria, is a unique plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and well adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Panzeria multifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant foliage and resilience in dry conditions.
