Prunus jamasakura f. pura

Prunus jamasakura f. pura, commonly known as the pure white cherry blossom, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate aesthetic to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy green surface.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in temperate climates for its seasonal beauty.
