Passiflora altebilobata

Passiflora altebilobata, commonly known as the high-lobed passionflower, is a species of flowering plant in the family Passifloraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and deeply lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral displays.
