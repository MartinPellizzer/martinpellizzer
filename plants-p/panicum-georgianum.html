Panicum georgianum

Panicum georgianum, commonly known as the Georgian grass, is a perennial grass native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle made up of numerous small spikelets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in natural environments.
