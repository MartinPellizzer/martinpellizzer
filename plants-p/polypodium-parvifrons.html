Polypodium parvifrons

Polypodium parvifrons, commonly known as the small-leaf wall fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically grow in a rosette or clustered formation. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Polypodium parvifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
