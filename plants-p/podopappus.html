Podopappus

Podopappus, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Podopappus has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
