Pimpinella aurea

Pimpinella aurea, commonly known as golden cowpea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces lens-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
