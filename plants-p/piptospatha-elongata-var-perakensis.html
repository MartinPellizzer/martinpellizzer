Piptospatha elongata var. perakensis

Piptospatha elongata var. perakensis, commonly known as the Khasi flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Caesalpiniaceae and is characterized by its striking red and orange floral display during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and produces elongated, pendulous inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
