Platyopuntia brunneogemmia

Platyopuntia brunneogemmia, commonly known as the brown-pearled prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Platyopuntia brunneogemmia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
