Platythyra

Platythyra, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Platythyridaceae.

These plants are typically found in coastal areas and are adapted to saline environments. They have distinctive blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, often surrounded by spiny bracts. The leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping the plant retain moisture in arid conditions.

Platythyra species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing coastal soils.
