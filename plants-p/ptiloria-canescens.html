Ptiloria canescens

Ptiloria canescens, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

Ptiloria canescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry regions.
