Paspalum niquelandiae

Paspalum niquelandiae, commonly known as the Niquelandia grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, which are usually spike-like in structure.

Paspalum niquelandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
