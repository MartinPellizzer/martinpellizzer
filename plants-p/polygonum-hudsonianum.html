Polygonum hudsonianum

Polygonum hudsonianum, commonly known as Hudson's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems that often branch near the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum hudsonianum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
