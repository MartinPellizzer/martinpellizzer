Piper capense var. capense

Piper capense var. capense, commonly known as the African pepper or African long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and the leaves are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
