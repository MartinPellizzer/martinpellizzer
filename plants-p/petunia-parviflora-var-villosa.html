Petunia parviflora var. villosa

Petunia parviflora var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy petunia, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for containers and garden borders.
