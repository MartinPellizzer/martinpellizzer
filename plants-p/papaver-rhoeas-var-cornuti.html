Papaver rhoeas var. cornuti

Papaver rhoeas var. cornuti, commonly known as the Cornut's poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It features striking red or orange petals with a dark central blotch, typical of the Papaver genus. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is also noted for its medicinal properties, similar to other poppy species.
