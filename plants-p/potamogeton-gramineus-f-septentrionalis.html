Potamogeton gramineus f. septentrionalis

Potamogeton gramineus f. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

This subspecies is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
