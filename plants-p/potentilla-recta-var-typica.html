Potentilla recta var. typica

Potentilla recta var. typica, commonly known as the upright potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a soft, grayish-white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
