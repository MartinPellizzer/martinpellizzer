Puccinellia embergeri

Puccinellia embergeri, commonly known as the Emberger's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Puccinellia embergeri plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
