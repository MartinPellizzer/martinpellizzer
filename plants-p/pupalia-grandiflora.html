Pupalia grandiflora

Pupalia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered piper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine.
