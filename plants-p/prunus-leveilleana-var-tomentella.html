Prunus leveilleana var. tomentella

Prunus leveilleana var. tomentella, commonly known as the tomentose cherry, is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic with a smooth margin and a pale underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are dark red to black in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
