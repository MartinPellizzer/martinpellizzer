Pogostemon helferi

Pogostemon helferi, commonly known as the Helfer's pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Pogostemon helferi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
