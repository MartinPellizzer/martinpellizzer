Philodendron heteropleurum

Philodendron heteropleurum, commonly known as the Variegated Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often feature a striking variegation of green, white, and sometimes yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by berries, though it is more valued for its ornamental foliage than for its flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its attractive appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
