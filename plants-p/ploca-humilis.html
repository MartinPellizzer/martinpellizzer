Ploca humilis

Ploca humilis, commonly known as the low ploca, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Ploca humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
