Piper fasciculare

Piper fasciculare, commonly known as the clustered pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on different plants, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are usually green when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shady environments.
