Pilea floridana

Pilea floridana, commonly known as the Florida pilea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which are followed by spathes that enclose the true flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Pilea floridana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
