Pultenaea neurocalyx var. major

Pultenaea neurocalyx var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically grey-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or red, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
