Polygonatum urceolatum

Polygonatum urceolatum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous stems that produce clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark blue or black and are typically eaten by birds.

Polygonatum urceolatum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
