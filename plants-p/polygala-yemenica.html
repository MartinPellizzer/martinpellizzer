Polygala yemenica

Polygala yemenica, commonly known as Yemen's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Polygala yemenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
