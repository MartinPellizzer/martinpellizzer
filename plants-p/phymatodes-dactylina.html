Phymatodes dactylina

Phymatodes dactylina, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that produces basal rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant features a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions by storing water. It produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems.
