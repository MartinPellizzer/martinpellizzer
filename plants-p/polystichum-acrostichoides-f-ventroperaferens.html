Polystichum acrostichoides f. ventroperaferens

Polystichum acrostichoides f. ventroperaferens, commonly known as the ventral frond fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf wood fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that curve downward, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically leathery and have a dark green color with a slightly glossy surface.

This fern is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
