Potentilla verna var. velutina

Potentilla verna var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
