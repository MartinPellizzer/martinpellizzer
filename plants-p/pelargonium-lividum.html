Pelargonium lividum

Pelargonium lividum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
