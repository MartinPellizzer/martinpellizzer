Potamogeton indicus

Potamogeton indicus, commonly known as Indian water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

Potamogeton indicus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
