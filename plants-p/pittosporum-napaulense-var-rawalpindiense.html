Pittosporum napaulense var. rawalpindiense

Pittosporum napaulense var. rawalpindiense, commonly known as the Rawalpindi pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
