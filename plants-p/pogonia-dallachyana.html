Pogonia dallachyana

Pogonia dallachyana, commonly known as the pink fairy-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its habitat.
