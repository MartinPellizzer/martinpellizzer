Piper heydei

Piper heydei, commonly known as the Heyde's pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant's structure and growth habits are typical of many tropical vines, with long stems and compound leaves.
