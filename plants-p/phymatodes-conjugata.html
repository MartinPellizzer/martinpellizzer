Phymatodes conjugata

Phymatodes conjugata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring, producing small, capsule-like fruits that disperse seeds by wind.
