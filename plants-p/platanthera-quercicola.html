Platanthera quercicola

Platanthera quercicola, commonly known as the oak-leaf lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear dense clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, and it has a short, fibrous root system.

Platanthera quercicola is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
