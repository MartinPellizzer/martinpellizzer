Plantago paralias

Plantago paralias, commonly known as sea plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes. The plant has broad, fleshy leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional herbal remedies.
