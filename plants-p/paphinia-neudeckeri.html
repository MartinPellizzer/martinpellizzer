Paphinia neudeckeri

Paphinia neudeckeri, commonly known as the Neudecker's paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil.

Paphinia neudeckeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
