Paepalanthus acuminatus

Paepalanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Paepalanthus acuminatus is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
