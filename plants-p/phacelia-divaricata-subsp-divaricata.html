Phacelia divaricata subsp. divaricata

Phacelia divaricata subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
