Pyrethrum foeniculaceum

Pyrethrum foeniculaceum, commonly known as sweet wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and small yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains compounds such as artemisinin, which are used in traditional medicine and modern pharmaceuticals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been cultivated for centuries for its therapeutic uses.
