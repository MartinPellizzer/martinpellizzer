Pappagrostis

Pappagrostis, commonly known as silvergrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, silky leaves and fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that give it a distinctive appearance. These plants are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils. Pappagrostis species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their graceful, feathery foliage.

They play a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
