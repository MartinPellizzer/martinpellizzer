Phrygilanthus eucalyptifolius

Phrygilanthus eucalyptifolius, commonly known as the eucalyptus-leaved phrygilanthus, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of eucalyptus species, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
