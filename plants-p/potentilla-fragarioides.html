Potentilla fragarioides

Potentilla fragarioides, commonly known as sandbank cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes or disturbed areas.

Potentilla fragarioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
