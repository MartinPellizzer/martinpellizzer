Psilocarya pauloensis

Psilocarya pauloensis, commonly known as the Pauloense Psilocybe, is a species of psychedelic mushroom native to Brazil.

It belongs to the genus Psilocybe, which includes several psychoactive fungi known for their hallucinogenic properties. The fruiting bodies of this species typically have a pale to medium brown cap with a distinct annulus and a slender, fibrous stipe. It contains the psychoactive compounds psilocybin and psilocin, which are responsible for its hallucinogenic effects.

Due to its potency and legal status, it is often sought after by mycologists and psychonauts alike.
