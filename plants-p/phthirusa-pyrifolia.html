Phthirusa pyrifolia

Phthirusa pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its fruit, known as the nashi pear, is valued for its crisp texture and sweet, aromatic flavor.

The tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its culinary uses.
