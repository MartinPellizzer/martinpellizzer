Paspalum difforme

Paspalum difforme, commonly known as flatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its flat, spreading leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, lawns, and along roadsides.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
