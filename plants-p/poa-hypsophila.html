Poa hypsophila

Poa hypsophila, commonly known as alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences with blue-green flowers, which are adapted to cold and nutrient-poor conditions. Its rhizomatous root system helps it anchor in unstable mountain soils and withstand harsh weather.

Poa hypsophila is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for specialized wildlife.
