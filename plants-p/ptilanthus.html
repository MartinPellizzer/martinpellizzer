Ptilanthus

Ptilanthus, commonly known as the African broom or wild broom, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and consists of several species, including Ptilanthus africanus and Ptilanthus tamarindoides. These shrubs are characterized by their slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Ptilanthus species are adapted to arid environments and are often found in rocky or sandy soils.
