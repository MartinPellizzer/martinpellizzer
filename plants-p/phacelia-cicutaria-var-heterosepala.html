Phacelia cicutaria var. heterosepala

Phacelia cicutaria var. heterosepala, commonly known as the heterosepala phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and disturbed sites.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
