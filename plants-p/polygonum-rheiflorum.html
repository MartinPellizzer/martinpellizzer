Polygonum rheiflorum

Polygonum rheiflorum, commonly known as the redflower knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum rheiflorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
