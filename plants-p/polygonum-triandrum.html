Polygonum triandrum

Polygonum triandrum, commonly known as the red root or flame flower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum triandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
