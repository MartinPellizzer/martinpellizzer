Piptadenia peruviana

Piptadenia peruviana, commonly known as the pink bean or Peruvian pink bean, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for reforestation and erosion control in degraded lands.
