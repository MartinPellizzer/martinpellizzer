Potentilla thuringiaca f. macropetala

Potentilla thuringiaca f. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered thuringian cinquefoil, is a botanical variant characterized by its notably larger petals compared to the typical species.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Central Europe, particularly Germany and the Czech Republic. The plant features a low-growing shrub with compound leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
