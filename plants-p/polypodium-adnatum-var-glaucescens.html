Polypodium adnatum var. glaucescens

Polypodium adnatum var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and lanceolate, serrated leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

The fronds are evergreen and remain throughout the year, making it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant in humid climates.
