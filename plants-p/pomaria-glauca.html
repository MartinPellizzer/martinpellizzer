Pomaria glauca

Pomaria glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Pomaria glauca is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
