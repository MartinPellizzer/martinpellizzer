Pandanus akeassii var. limitaneus

Pandanus akeassii var. limitaneus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, white flowers. Its fibrous leaves are often used in traditional crafts and thatching.

This variety is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
