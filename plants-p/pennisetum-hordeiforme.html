Pennisetum hordeiforme

Pennisetum hordeiforme, commonly known as finger grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces distinctive, finger-like seed heads that give it its common name.

Pennisetum hordeiforme is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
