Piptocarpha carioca

Piptocarpha carioca, commonly known as Brazilian ironwood, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant is known for its hard, heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and density. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local wildlife species.
