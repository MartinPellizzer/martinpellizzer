Polycycnis lepida

Polycycnis lepida, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of its stems, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Polycycnis lepida is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
