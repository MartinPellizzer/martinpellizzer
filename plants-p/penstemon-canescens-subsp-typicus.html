Penstemon canescens subsp. typicus

Penstemon canescens subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the gray beard plant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, pale blue to lavender flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky soils and sunny environments, often found in alpine and arid regions.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable species in its ecological habitat.
