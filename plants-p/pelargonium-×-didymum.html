Pelargonium × didymum

Pelargonium × didymum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of both geranium and pelargonium species.

It is native to South Africa and is known for its trailing growth habit and ability to form dense mats. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, which gives it a unique appearance. Its flowers are typically small, tubular, and come in shades of red, pink, and white, often appearing in clusters.

Pelargonium × didymum is widely cultivated as a decorative plant, particularly in hanging baskets and as ground cover.
