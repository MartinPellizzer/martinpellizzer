Phyteuma spicatum var. jurassicum

Phyteuma spicatum var. jurassicum, commonly known as the Jurassic phyteuma, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette, while the plant produces elongated, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
