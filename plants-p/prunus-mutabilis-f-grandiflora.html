Prunus mutabilis f. grandiflora

Prunus mutabilis f. grandiflora, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to Japan.

This ornamental plant is characterized by its showy, double flowers that typically appear in shades of white, pink, and red. The flowers are borne in clusters and are highly valued for their aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, although they are not as flavorful as those of other cherry varieties.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
