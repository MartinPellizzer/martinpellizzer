Phycella ignea var. glauca

Phycella ignea var. glauca, commonly known as the blue flame phycella, is a unique variant of the phycella genus characterized by its striking blue-green foliage.

This plant is native to arid regions and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically small, waxy, and arranged in a compact rosette formation. The plant produces delicate, pale purple flowers during the summer months.

It is valued in xeriscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
