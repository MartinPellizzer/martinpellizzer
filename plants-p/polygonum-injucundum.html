Polygonum injucundum

Polygonum injucundum, commonly known as the bitter knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are often reddish or bronze in color, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clustered inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
