Palala diversifolia

Palala diversifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf palala, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly varied leaf shapes, which range from elongated to broad and lobed, adapting to different light conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in its natural habitat.

Palala diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
