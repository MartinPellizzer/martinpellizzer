Pisum sativum var. wickeri

Pisum sativum var. wickeri, commonly known as the Wicker pea, is a variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique, twisted foliage that resembles woven reeds. The plant produces small, pea-shaped pods that contain several edible seeds. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe.
