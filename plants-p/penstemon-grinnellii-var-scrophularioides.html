Penstemon grinnellii var. scrophularioides

Penstemon grinnellii var. scrophularioides, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, open habitats such as meadows and slopes. The plant features erect, branched stems and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
