Panax japonicus f. dolichocarpus

Panax japonicus f. dolichocarpus, commonly known as the long-seed ginseng, is a botanical variant of Panax japonicus, a species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its elongated, spindle-shaped roots and distinctive, lanceolate leaves. The plant belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties, similar to other ginseng species. Its fruits are typically small and yellowish, containing seeds that are used in traditional herbal medicine.

This form is less commonly cultivated than other ginseng varieties but is appreciated for its unique morphology and potential therapeutic benefits.
