Phylica pumila

Phylica pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes, typically ranging in color from red to pink. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Phylica pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a hedge.
