Pimelea weippugiana

Pimelea weippugiana, commonly known as the silverleaf mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, mist-like inflorescences. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, silver-gray leaves that provide a striking contrast to the delicate flowers.

Pimelea weippugiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
