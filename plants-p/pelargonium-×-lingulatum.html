Pelargonium × lingulatum

Pelargonium × lingulatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
