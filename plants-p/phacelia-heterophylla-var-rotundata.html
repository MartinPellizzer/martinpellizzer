Phacelia heterophylla var. rotundata

Phacelia heterophylla var. rotundata, commonly known as the roundleaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves that vary in shape, with the variety rotundata having notably rounded leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
