Prunus acutissima

Prunus acutissima, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or myrobalan.

It is a small deciduous shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features small, pointed leaves and produces clusters of white flowers in spring. Its fruit, a small, hard drupe, is typically yellow or red when mature.

Prunus acutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
