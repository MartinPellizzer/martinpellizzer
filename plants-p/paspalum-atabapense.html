Paspalum atabapense

Paspalum atabapense, commonly known as the Atabapense grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum atabapense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
