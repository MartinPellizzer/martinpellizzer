Psyllium mauritanicum

Psyllium mauritanicum, commonly known as the desert psyllium, is a flowering plant native to North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed capsules that contain fine, fibrous seeds. These seeds are rich in mucilage, which is used in traditional medicine and as a dietary fiber supplement.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important plant in regions with limited water resources.
