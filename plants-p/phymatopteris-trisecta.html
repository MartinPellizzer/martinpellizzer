Phymatopteris trisecta

Phymatopteris trisecta, commonly known as the three-part maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas with rich, organic soil. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
