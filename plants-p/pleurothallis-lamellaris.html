Pleurothallis lamellaris

Pleurothallis lamellaris, commonly known as the lamellar pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Pleurothallis lamellaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
