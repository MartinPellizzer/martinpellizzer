Passiflora capparidifolia

Passiflora capparidifolia, commonly known as the Capparidifolia passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
