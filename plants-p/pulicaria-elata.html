Pulicaria elata

Pulicaria elata, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and limestone areas. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its yellow flowers are clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Pulicaria elata is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
