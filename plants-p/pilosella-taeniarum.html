Pilosella taeniarum

Pilosella taeniarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella taeniarum is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
