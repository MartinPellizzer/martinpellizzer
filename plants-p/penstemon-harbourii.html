Penstemon harbourii

Penstemon harbourii, commonly known as the Vancouver penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Penstemon harbourii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
