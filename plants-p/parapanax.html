Parapanax

Parapanax, commonly known as New Zealand arrowroot, is a genus of flowering plants native to New Zealand.

It consists of several species, most notably Parapanax dieffenbachii, which is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by dark blue berries. Parapanax is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
