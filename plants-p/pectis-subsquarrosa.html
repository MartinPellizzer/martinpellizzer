Pectis subsquarrosa

Pectis subsquarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a woolly texture, and its flowers are typically yellow with a pale center. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native range.
