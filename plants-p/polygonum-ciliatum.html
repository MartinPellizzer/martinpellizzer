Polygonum ciliatum

Polygonum ciliatum, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a soft, downy texture along the edges.

Polygonum ciliatum is often found in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
