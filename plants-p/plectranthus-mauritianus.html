Plectranthus mauritianus

Plectranthus mauritianus, commonly known as the Madagascar verbena, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
