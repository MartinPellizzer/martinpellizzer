Pandanus ovalauensis

Pandanus ovalauensis, commonly known as the Ovalau pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant produces long, narrow inflorescences bearing small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing near the shoreline.

Its fibrous leaves have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for thatching and weaving.
