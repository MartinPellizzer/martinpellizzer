Pinus jeffreyi var. baja-californica

Pinus jeffreyi var. baja-californica, commonly known as the Jeffrey pine, is a subspecies of the Jeffrey pine native to the southern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a tall, evergreen conifer with long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are woody and contain seeds surrounded by a papery covering. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers and wet winters.

This subspecies is notable for its role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
