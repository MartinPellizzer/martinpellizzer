Potentilla ikonnikovii

Potentilla ikonnikovii, commonly known as the Ikonnikov's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
