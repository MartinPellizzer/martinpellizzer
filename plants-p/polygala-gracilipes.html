Polygala gracilipes

Polygala gracilipes, commonly known as the slender milkwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are small, lance-shaped, and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers are usually clustered in small inflorescences and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
