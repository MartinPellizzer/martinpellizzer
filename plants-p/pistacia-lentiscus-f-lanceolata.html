Pistacia lentiscus f. lanceolata

Pistacia lentiscus f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf mastic, is a variant of the mastic tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and smooth, aromatic bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing resinous seeds. Its leaves are typically evergreen, with a leathery texture and a distinctive aromatic scent.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine and resin production.
