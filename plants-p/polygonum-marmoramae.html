Polygonum marmoramae

Polygonum marmoramae, commonly known as the marble-leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a mix of green, white, and purple hues. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

Polygonum marmoramae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
