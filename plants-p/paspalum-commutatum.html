Paspalum commutatum

Paspalum commutatum, commonly known as rough paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum commutatum is often found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along roadsides.
