Polygala geniculata

Polygala geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are alternate and narrow, often with a slight zigzag pattern along the edges.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
