Pitcairnia yaupi-bajaensis

Pitcairnia yaupi-bajaensis, commonly known as the Yaupi-Bajaensis air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the high Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, woolly covering, which helps retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and horticultural enthusiasts.
