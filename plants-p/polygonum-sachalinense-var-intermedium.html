Polygonum sachalinense var. intermedium

Polygonum sachalinense var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, covered with reddish hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and flowering display.
