Pandanus decorus

Pandanus decorus, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense canopy. The plant produces striking inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, hence its common name. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Pandanus decorus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
