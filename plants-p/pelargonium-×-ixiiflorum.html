Pelargonium × ixiiflorum

Pelargonium × ixiiflorum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that come in a wide range of hues, including red, pink, purple, and white. The plant typically has rounded, serrated leaves with distinct color zones, which give it its common name. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as potted plants.

Pelargonium × ixiiflorum thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
