Pittosporum pullifolium

Pittosporum pullifolium, commonly known as the coastal pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.

Pittosporum pullifolium is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
