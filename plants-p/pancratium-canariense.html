Pancratium canariense

Pancratium canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island torch flower, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and features showy, red-orange flowers that resemble torches, which bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its resilience to coastal conditions and minimal water requirements.
