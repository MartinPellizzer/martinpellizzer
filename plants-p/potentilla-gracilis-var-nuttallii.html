Potentilla gracilis var. nuttallii

Potentilla gracilis var. nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's slender cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven oval leaflets, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
