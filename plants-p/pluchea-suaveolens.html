Pluchea suaveolens

Pluchea suaveolens, commonly known as sweetweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and hairy leaves. Its small yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green to white ray florets, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela.

Pluchea suaveolens is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
