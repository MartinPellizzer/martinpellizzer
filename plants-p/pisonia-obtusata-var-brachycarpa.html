Pisonia obtusata var. brachycarpa

Pisonia obtusata var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited pisonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that produce small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its adaptability in tropical and subtropical environments.
