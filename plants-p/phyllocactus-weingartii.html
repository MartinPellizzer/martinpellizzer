Phyllocactus weingartii

Phyllocactus weingartii, commonly known as the Weingartii cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, ribbed stems that resemble leaves, a feature that helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. Its growth habit is slow, making it suitable for indoor cultivation with minimal care.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, requiring infrequent watering to prevent root rot.
