Pleurothallis braidiana

Pleurothallis braidiana, commonly known as the Braid's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
