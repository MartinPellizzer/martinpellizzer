Passiflora tetraden

Passiflora tetraden, commonly known as the four-lobed passionflower, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture.

Passiflora tetraden thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
