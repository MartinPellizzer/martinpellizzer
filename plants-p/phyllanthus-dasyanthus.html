Phyllanthus dasyanthus

Phyllanthus dasyanthus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, elliptical leaves that are often gland-dotted and have a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

The plant is well-known for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
