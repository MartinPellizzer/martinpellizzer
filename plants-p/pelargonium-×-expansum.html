Pelargonium × expansum

Pelargonium × expansum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, double flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Pelargonium × expansum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
