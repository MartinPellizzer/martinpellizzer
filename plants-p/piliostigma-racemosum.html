Piliostigma racemosum

Piliostigma racemosum, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a deciduous shrub native to the savannas of sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
