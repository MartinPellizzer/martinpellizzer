Psyllium arenarium

Psyllium arenarium, commonly known as sand psyllium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and fibrous seed capsules. The plant produces a type of seed that is rich in soluble fiber, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement. Its ability to thrive in sandy soils makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions.
