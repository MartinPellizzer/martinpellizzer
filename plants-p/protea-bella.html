Protea bella

Protea bella, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant features a woody stem with a fibrous, leafless base, and its leaves are narrow, leathery, and often covered in a waxy coating. Native to the fynbos biome, it thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
