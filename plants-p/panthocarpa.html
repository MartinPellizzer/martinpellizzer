Panthocarpa

Panthocarpa, commonly known as the "pomegranate tree," is a fruit-bearing plant native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and sprawling, evergreen branches. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that give way to the iconic pomegranate fruit, which contains numerous seeds encased in a juicy, edible aril. Panthocarpa thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nutritious fruit.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
