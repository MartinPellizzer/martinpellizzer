Phreatia albofarinosa

Phreatia albofarinosa, commonly known as the white-flowered phreatia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers with a distinctive pale yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in forest understories.

Its botanical features include pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves that help it store water and nutrients.
