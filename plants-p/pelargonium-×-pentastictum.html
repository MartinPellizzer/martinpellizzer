Pelargonium × pentastictum

Pelargonium × pentastictum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring a mix of green, white, and red or pink markings. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in summer. It is a popular ornamental plant, often used in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Pelargonium × pentastictum thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
