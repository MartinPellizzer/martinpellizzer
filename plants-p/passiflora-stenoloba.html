Passiflora stenoloba

Passiflora stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive flowers, which feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that surround the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its potential medicinal properties.
