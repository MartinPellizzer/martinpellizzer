Pseudoblepharis obtusisepala

Pseudoblepharis obtusisepala, commonly known as the broad-leafed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are broad and flat, adapted for both photosynthesis and trapping prey.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and specialized adaptations for nutrient acquisition in nutrient-poor environments.
