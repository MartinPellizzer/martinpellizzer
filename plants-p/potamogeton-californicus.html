Potamogeton californicus

Potamogeton californicus, commonly known as California pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with moderate to high light penetration.

Potamogeton californicus is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands, where it contributes to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.
