Philadelphus chinensis

Philadelphus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese Mock Orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden landscapes for its pleasant fragrance and showy blooms.
