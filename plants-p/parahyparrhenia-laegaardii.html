Parahyparrhenia laegaardii

Parahyparrhenia laegaardii, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
