Panicum nephelophilum var. xerophilum

Panicum nephelophilum var. xerophilum, commonly known as the cloud-loving grass, is a variant of the Panicum genus that thrives in semi-arid to arid environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight, helping to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, enhancing its ability to colonize new areas. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, where it demonstrates remarkable drought tolerance.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in xerophytic ecosystems.
