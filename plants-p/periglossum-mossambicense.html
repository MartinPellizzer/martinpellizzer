Periglossum mossambicense

Periglossum mossambicense, commonly known as the African velvet moss, is a member of the Pottiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

This small, mat-forming moss typically grows on soil surfaces in shaded, moist environments. It has a dense, velvety appearance due to its closely packed, overlapping leaves. The leaves are usually narrowly lance-shaped and have a distinct midrib.

Periglossum mossambicense is notable for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
