Pogostemon petiolaris

Pogostemon petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pogostemon petiolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
