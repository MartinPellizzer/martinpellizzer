Polystachya danieliana

Polystachya danieliana, commonly known as Daniel's bamboo orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Polystachya danieliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
