Pentagonia osapinnata

Pentagonia osapinnata, commonly known as the five-angled tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Pentagonia osapinnata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
