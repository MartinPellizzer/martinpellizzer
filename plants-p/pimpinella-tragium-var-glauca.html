Pimpinella tragium var. glauca

Pimpinella tragium var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous cow-weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately divided and have a distinctive bluish-gray hue due to a waxy coating. This plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and along roadsides.

It is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
