Pollinia aurea

Pollinia aurea, commonly known as the golden pollinia, is a unique orchid species characterized by its distinctive, compact pollinia that are typically golden in color.

The plant produces small, showy flowers with intricate, fragrant petals that attract specific pollinators. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with the pollinia adhering to the bodies of visiting insects for transport. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Pollinia aurea is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
