Phaseolus vulgaris var. galactopardus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. galactopardus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variant of the common bean characterized by its distinctive purplish-pink pods and seeds.

This variety is native to the Andean regions of South America and is valued for its vibrant color and culinary versatility. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates, producing climbing vines with trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and the pods mature to a striking purple hue, often retaining some color even when dried.

The seeds are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and edible gardening.
