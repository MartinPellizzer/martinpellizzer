Prunus cerasifera purpurea

Prunus cerasifera purpurea, commonly known as the purple leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-bronze foliage, which provides vibrant seasonal color changes throughout the year. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Prunus cerasifera purpurea is relatively low maintenance but requires well-drained soil and regular pruning to maintain its shape.
