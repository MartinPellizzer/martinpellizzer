Parodia roseoalba

Parodia roseoalba, commonly known as the White Rose Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white spines and rounded, ribbed stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
