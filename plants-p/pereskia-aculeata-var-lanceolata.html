Pereskia aculeata var. lanceolata

Pereskia aculeata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the sharpleaf cactus or prickly pear, is a succulent plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often armed with spines and have a somewhat shrub-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible fruits resembling small, green figs. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and may be reduced or modified into spines, which help in reducing water loss.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.
