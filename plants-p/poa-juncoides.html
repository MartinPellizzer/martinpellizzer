Poa juncoides

Poa juncoides, commonly known as the tufted grass or tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and forms compact clumps or tussocks. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Poa juncoides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
