Piper andakiensis

Piper andakiensis, commonly known as the Andean pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Piper andakiensis is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
