Pandanus sussea

Pandanus sussea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts, and the leaves are utilized for thatching and weaving.

Pandanus sussea thrives in coastal and wetland environments, showing adaptability to saline and humid conditions.
