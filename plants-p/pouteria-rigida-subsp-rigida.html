Pouteria rigida subsp. rigida

Pouteria rigida subsp.

rigida, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest. It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The tree is renowned for its edible seeds, which are encased in a hard, woody shell and are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. Its fruit is a large, woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a wide range of wildlife.
