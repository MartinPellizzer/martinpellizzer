Polygala hypoglauca

Polygala hypoglauca, commonly known as the blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and often ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its small, blue to purple flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
