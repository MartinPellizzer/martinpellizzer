Plantago brasiliensis var. cordobensis

Plantago brasiliensis var. cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces erect flowering stems that bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
