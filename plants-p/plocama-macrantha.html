Plocama macrantha

Plocama macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered plocama, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that matures in the fall.

Plocama macrantha prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
