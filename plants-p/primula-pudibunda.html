Primula pudibunda

Primula pudibunda, commonly known as the shy primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
