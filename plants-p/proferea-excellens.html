Proferea excellens

Proferea excellens, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a glossy, dark green hue. The plant produces clusters of vibrant orange-red tubular flowers, which bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in both residential and public landscapes.
