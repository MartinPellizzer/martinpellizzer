Pyrus pollveria

Pyrus pollveria, commonly known as the common pear.

It is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its broad leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible fruit, which is typically harvested in late summer or early autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils.

Pyrus pollveria is widely cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value in gardens and orchards.
