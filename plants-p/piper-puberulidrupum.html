Piper puberulidrupum

Piper puberulidrupum, commonly known as the velvet-leafed pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
