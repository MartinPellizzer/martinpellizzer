Pellaea dealbata

Pellaea dealbata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery underside on its fronds, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its fronds are pinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Pellaea dealbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
