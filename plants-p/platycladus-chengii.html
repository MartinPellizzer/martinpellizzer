Platycladus chengii

Platycladus chengii, commonly known as the Cheng's Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
