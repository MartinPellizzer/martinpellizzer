Plantago pilosa

Plantago pilosa, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its robust, succulent foliage.
