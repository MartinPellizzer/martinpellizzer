Piptochaetium setifolium

Piptochaetium setifolium, commonly known as the silky hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, silky leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, which helps it thrive in harsh environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a hairy texture, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in panicles. It is adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in dry landscapes.

Piptochaetium setifolium is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
