Ptilotus drummondii

Ptilotus drummondii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its unique, lantern-shaped bracts that resemble small, ghostly faces. The plant is known for its striking appearance, with vibrant red or orange hues that stand out against its green foliage. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
