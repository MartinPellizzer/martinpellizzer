Prunus orazii

Prunus orazii, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, single-layered flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to ovate in shape with a smooth margin. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Prunus orazii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
