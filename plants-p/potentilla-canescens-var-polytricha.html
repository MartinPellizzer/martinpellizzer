Potentilla canescens var. polytricha

Potentilla canescens var. polytricha, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, heaths, and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
