Phoebe sellowiana var. lanata

Phoebe sellowiana var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly phoebe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens in suitable climates.
