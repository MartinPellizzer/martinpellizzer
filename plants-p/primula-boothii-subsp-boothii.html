Primula boothii subsp. boothii

Primula boothii subsp.

boothii, commonly known as the Booth's primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant is evergreen, with hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful attention to its specific growing conditions.
