Picris spinulosa var. pinnatifida

Picris spinulosa var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the slender sow-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has a taproot system and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
