Piper resalutatum

Piper resalutatum, commonly known as the Resalutatum pepper or Resalutatum vine, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its unique flavor and potential medicinal properties.
