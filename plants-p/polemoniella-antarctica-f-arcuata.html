Polemoniella antarctica f. arcuata

Polemoniella antarctica f. arcuata, commonly known as the Antarctic polemonium, is a rare and cold-adapted plant native to the Antarctic region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, clustered leaves that are adapted to withstand extreme cold and strong winds. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom during the short Antarctic summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas where the climate is slightly milder.
