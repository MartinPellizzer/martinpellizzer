Penstemon compactus

Penstemon compactus, commonly known as the compact penstemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to well-drained soils.

Penstemon compactus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
