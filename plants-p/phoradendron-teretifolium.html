Phoradendron teretifolium

Phoradendron teretifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually evergreen and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by berries that are often red or yellow in color. It relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while also deriving energy through photosynthesis.

This species is found in various regions of the Americas and is known for its ecological role in supporting bird populations.
