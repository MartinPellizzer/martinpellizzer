Piper mekongense

Piper mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong pepper or wild long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit, a small, dark purple drupe, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The botanical features of Piper mekongense make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
