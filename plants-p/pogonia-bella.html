Pogonia bella

Pogonia bella, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiraled arrangement of small, white or pale pink flowers along a hairy stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, erect flower spike.

Pogonia bella is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance as a pollinator attractant.
