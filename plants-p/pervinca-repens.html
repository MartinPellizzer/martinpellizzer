Pervinca repens

Pervinca repens, commonly known as the common yellow arum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking yellowish-white flowers with a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish underside. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Pervinca repens is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
