Paspalum morichalense

Paspalum morichalense, commonly known as the Morichal grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, including marshes, swamps, and riparian zones. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
