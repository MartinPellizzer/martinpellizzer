Papaver libanoticum subsp. libanoticum

Papaver libanoticum subsp.

libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon poppy, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent central stigma, typical of the Papaveraceae family.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in rocky or arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
