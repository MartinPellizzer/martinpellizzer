Pisum pannonicum

Pisum pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian pea, is a perennial legume native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features compound leaves with oval leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are typically small and dark in color. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its historical and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
