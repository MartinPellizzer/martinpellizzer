Panicum gardneri

Panicum gardneri, commonly known as silverleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or brown.

Panicum gardneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
