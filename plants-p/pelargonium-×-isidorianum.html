Pelargonium × isidorianum

Pelargonium × isidorianum, commonly known as the isidorianum geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geranium genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and containers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Pelargonium × isidorianum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
