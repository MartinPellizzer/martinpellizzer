Polygala monticola

Polygala monticola, commonly known as the mountain polygala, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowers are usually small and occur in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its habitat specificity, Polygala monticola is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
