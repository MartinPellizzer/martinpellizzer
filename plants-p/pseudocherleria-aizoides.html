Pseudocherleria aizoides

Pseudocherleria aizoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kangaroo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Pseudocherleria aizoides is often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and along coastal areas.
