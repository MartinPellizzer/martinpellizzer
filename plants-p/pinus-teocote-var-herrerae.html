Pinus teocote var. herrerae

Pinus teocote var. herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's Mexican pine, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and dark, fissured bark. The needles are typically arranged in bundles of five, with a bluish-green color and a slight waxy coating. Its cones are woody, cylindrical, and can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters.

This variety plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
