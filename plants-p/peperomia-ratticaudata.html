Peperomia ratticaudata

Peperomia ratticaudata, commonly known as the cat's tail peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tint on the underside, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, which are not particularly showy but are important for pollination. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia ratticaudata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
