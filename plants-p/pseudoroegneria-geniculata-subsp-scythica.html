Pseudoroegneria geniculata subsp. scythica

Pseudoroegneria geniculata subsp.

scythica, commonly known as the Scythian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic features.
