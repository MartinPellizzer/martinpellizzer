Pseudocytisus integrifolius

Pseudocytisus integrifolius, commonly known as the smooth broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are entire, meaning they have no serrations or lobes, and are typically narrow and lance-shaped.

Pseudocytisus integrifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
