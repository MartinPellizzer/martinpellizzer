Peucedanum augymnodes

Peucedanum augymnodes, commonly known as the giant cow parsley, is a tall biennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features large, umbrella-like inflorescences composed of small white to pale purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

The seeds are small, dry, and contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
