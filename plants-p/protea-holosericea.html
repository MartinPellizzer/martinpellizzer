Protea holosericea

Protea holosericea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, white to pale pink inflorescence, which consists of a central cone surrounded by numerous petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
