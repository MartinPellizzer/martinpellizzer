Parsonsia flexilis

Parsonsia flexilis, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often described as resembling spiders due to their shape. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and typically grows as a herbaceous perennial or shrub. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Parsonsia flexilis is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
