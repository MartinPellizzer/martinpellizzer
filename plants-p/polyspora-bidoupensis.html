Polyspora bidoupensis

Polyspora bidoupensis, commonly known as the Bidoupensis star moss, is a rare and endemic species native to the rainforests of southern Vietnam.

It is a member of the Polysporaceae family, characterized by its unique morphology and ecological adaptations. The plant exhibits a dense, cushion-like growth form, with small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of moss-like structures. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found on rocky substrates or in the undergrowth of dense vegetation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native region.
