Podocarpus gibbsiae

Podocarpus gibbsiae, commonly known as the dwarf podocarp, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It has a slow growth rate and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its compact form and attractive appearance.

Podocarpus gibbsiae is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its use in landscaping and reforestation projects.
