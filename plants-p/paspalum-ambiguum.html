Paspalum ambiguum

Paspalum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Paspalum ambiguum is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its ability to spread rapidly in suitable habitats.
