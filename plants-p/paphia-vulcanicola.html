Paphia vulcanicola

Paphia vulcanicola, commonly known as the volcanic orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the volcanic regions of Indonesia.

It thrives in nutrient-rich, volcanic soil and is adapted to the high temperatures and mineral content of its environment. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and produces striking, fragrant flowers with intricate patterns. Its growth is slow, and it requires specific conditions to bloom, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited habitat, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native region.
