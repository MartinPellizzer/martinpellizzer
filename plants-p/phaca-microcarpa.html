Phaca microcarpa

Phaca microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its small, capsule-like fruits contain numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Phaca microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
