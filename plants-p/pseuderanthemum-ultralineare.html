Pseuderanthemum ultralineare

Pseuderanthemum ultralineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false heather, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color and are clustered in whorls along the stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and compact size.
