Polygala polymorpha var. vulgaris

Polygala polymorpha var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, often yellow or greenish flowers arranged in spikes. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This species is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and role in soil enrichment.
