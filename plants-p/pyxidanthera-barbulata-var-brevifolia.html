Pyxidanthera barbulata var. brevifolia

Pyxidanthera barbulata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved featherflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear small, white, nodding flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
