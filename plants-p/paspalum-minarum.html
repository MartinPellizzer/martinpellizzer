Paspalum minarum

Paspalum minarum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in areas with high moisture levels. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often coated with a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, two-lobed flowers.

Paspalum minarum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
