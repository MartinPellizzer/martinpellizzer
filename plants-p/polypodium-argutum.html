Polypodium argutum

Polypodium argutum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are also valued in horticulture for their ornamental appearance.
