Pleurothallis epiglottis

Pleurothallis epiglottis, commonly known as the epiglottis orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in a spike. Its flowers are notable for their unique shape, resembling the structure of an epiglottis, hence its common name.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its intricate floral display.
