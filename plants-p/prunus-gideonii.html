Prunus gideonii

Prunus gideonii, commonly known as Gideon's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible cherries that are typically small and tart. It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub form and has dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

Prunus gideonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
