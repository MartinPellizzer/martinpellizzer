Polypogon demissus

Polypogon demissus, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological research.
