Phormium hirtum

Phormium hirtum, commonly known as the Hairy Fireplant, is a striking member of the Asphodelaceae family native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking spikes of bright orange or red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
