Pappophorum lindleyanum

Pappophorum lindleyanum, commonly known as the Lindley's pappophore, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces lightweight, feathery seeds that are dispersed by the wind, a feature typical of the grass family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
