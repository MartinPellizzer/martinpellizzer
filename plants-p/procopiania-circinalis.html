Procopiania circinalis

Procopiania circinalis, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of clustered blooms in shades of pink and purple, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
