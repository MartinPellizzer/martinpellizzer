Pinus exserta

Pinus exserta, commonly known as the exserta pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are initially yellowish-green but mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The exserta pine is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
