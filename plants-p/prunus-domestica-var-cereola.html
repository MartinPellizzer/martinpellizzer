Prunus domestica var. cereola

Prunus domestica var. cereola, commonly known as the Cereola plum, is a variety of European plum characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

It produces small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves and develops showy pink to red flowers in early spring. It is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a versatile addition to gardens and orchards.
