Panicum mohavense

Panicum mohavense, commonly known as Mohave panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
