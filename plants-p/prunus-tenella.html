Prunus tenella

Prunus tenella, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces delicate pink or white flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for garden borders or as a specimen plant.

Prunus tenella prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
