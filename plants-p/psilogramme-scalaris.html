Psilogramme scalaris

Psilogramme scalaris, commonly known as the scallop orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, scalloped-shaped petals. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Psilogramme scalaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
