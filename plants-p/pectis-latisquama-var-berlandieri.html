Pectis latisquama var. berlandieri

Pectis latisquama var. berlandieri, commonly known as the Desert Paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its ability to bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its native habitat.
