Pimelea brevistyla subsp. minor

Pimelea brevistyla subsp.

minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved riceflower, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, often in well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
