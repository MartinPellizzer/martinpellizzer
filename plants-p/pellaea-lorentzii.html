Pellaea lorentzii

Pellaea lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests or rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its leaves, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
