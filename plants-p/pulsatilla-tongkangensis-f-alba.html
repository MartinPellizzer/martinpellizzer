Pulsatilla tongkangensis f. alba

Pulsatilla tongkangensis f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered meadow buttercup, is a botanical variant of the genus Pulsatilla, characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or grassy areas. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are deeply lobed, and it produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
