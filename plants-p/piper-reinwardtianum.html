Piper reinwardtianum

Piper reinwardtianum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that contain the aromatic seeds used as a spice. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of Piper reinwardtianum make it a valuable species in both horticulture and the spice industry.
