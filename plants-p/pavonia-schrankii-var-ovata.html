Pavonia schrankii var. ovata

Pavonia schrankii var. ovata, commonly known as the ovalleaf false mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features elongated, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its showy, pink to red flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant collections.
