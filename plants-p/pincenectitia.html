Pincenectitia

Pincenectitia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant produces delicate, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. Pincenectitia thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found growing on the trunks of trees.

Due to its habitat specificity and declining population, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
