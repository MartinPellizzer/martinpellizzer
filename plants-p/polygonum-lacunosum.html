Polygonum lacunosum

Polygonum lacunosum, commonly known as the lacunar knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose inflorescences, and are usually pink or red in color.

Polygonum lacunosum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
