Polygonum virgatum

Polygonum virgatum, commonly known as soft-stemmed knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

Polygonum virgatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
