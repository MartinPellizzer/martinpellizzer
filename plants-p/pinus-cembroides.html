Pinus cembroides

Pinus cembroides, commonly known as the silver fir or Mexican fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are often reddish-brown and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against fire and drought.

Pinus cembroides is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
