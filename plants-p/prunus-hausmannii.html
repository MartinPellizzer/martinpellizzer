Prunus hausmannii

Prunus hausmannii, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or Huarachuela, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible drupes that are typically red or yellow when mature. Prunus hausmannii is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
