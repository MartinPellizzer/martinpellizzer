Poa carpatica

Poa carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in moist meadows and forest edges.

Poa carpatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
