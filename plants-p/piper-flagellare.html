Piper flagellare

Piper flagellare, commonly known as the whip pepper or creeping pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread along the ground or climb supports.
