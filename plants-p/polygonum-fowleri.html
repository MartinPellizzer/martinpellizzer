Polygonum fowleri

Polygonum fowleri, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect stems that often have reddish hues, especially near the base. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum fowleri is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun conditions.
