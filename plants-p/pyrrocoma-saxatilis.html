Pyrrocoma saxatilis

Pyrrocoma saxatilis, commonly known as the rock pygmyweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to drought conditions.

Pyrrocoma saxatilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
