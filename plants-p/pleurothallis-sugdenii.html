Pleurothallis sugdenii

Pleurothallis sugdenii, commonly known as the Sugden's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Ecuador.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Pleurothallis sugdenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
