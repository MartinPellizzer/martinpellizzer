Ptychopyxis arborea var. cacuminum

Ptychopyxis arborea var. cacuminum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
