Persicaria sungareensis

Persicaria sungareensis, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and riverbanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. Its root system is robust and can spread aggressively, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
