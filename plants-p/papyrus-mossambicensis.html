Papyrus mossambicensis

Papyrus mossambicensis, commonly known as the African papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It has long, spongy stems that can grow up to several meters in length, adapted for floating or submerged environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which are followed by spore-bearing capsules. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Papyrus mossambicensis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
