Plantago lanceolata var. communis

Plantago lanceolata var. communis, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are ribbed and covered in fine hairs, typically growing in dense rosettes. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing capabilities.
