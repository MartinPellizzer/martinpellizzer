Ptilotus sericostachyus subsp. roseus

Ptilotus sericostachyus subsp.

roseus, commonly known as the pink ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its striking pink to violet bracts that resemble ghostly shapes, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh environments make it a notable species in its native habitat.
