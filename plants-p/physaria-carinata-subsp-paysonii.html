Physaria carinata subsp. paysonii

Physaria carinata subsp.

paysonii, commonly known as the Payson bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy, branching stems and produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.
