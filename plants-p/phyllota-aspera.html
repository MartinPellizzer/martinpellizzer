Phyllota aspera

Phyllota aspera, commonly known as the rough phyllota, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Phyllota aspera is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment in degraded landscapes.
