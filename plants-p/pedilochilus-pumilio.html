Pedilochilus pumilio

Pedilochilus pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a few leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in small inflorescences, and the species is noted for its compact size and ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
