Piper peculiare

Piper peculiare, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Despite its beauty, it is not widely used for culinary purposes compared to other pepper species.
