Penstemon stenophyllus var. dasyphyllus

Penstemon stenophyllus var. dasyphyllus, commonly known as the stiffleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in both ecological and horticultural settings.
