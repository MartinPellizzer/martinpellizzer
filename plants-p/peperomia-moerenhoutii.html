Peperomia moerenhoutii

Peperomia moerenhoutii, commonly known as the Moerenhout's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually greenish-white or pale yellow, which are followed by small, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of ornamental plants.
