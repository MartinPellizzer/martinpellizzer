Persea hexanthera

Persea hexanthera, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
