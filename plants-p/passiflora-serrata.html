Passiflora serrata

Passiflora serrata, commonly known as the serrated passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and white petals, along with crown-like structures and filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are typically lobed and have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

Passiflora serrata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
