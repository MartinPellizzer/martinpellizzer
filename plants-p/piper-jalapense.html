Piper jalapense

Piper jalapense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub native to Central America and northern South America. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
