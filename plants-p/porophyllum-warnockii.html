Porophyllum warnockii

Porophyllum warnockii, commonly known as the Mexican fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small yellow to brown flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it has a taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions.
