Paspalum leoninum

Paspalum leoninum, commonly known as lemon grass or citron grass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum leoninum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
