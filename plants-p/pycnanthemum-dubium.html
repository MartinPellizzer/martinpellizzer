Pycnanthemum dubium

Pycnanthemum dubium, commonly known as rough blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 2 feet.

Pycnanthemum dubium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
