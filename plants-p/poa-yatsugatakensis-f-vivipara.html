Poa yatsugatakensis f. vivipara

Poa yatsugatakensis f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous alpine grass, is a rare variant of the species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the production of viviparous shoots, which allow it to propagate vegetatively. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a botanically significant and ecologically unique species.
