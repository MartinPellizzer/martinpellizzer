Potentilla articulata var. articulata

Potentilla articulata var. articulata, commonly known as the articulated cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and distinctive, five-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
