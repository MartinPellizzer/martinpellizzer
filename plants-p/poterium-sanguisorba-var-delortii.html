Poterium sanguisorba var. delortii

Poterium sanguisorba var. delortii, commonly known as the Delort's bloodroot, is a variant of the bloodroot plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features distinctive reddish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding its foliage. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, textured surface that helps in identifying the species.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
