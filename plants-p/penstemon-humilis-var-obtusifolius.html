Penstemon humilis var. obtusifolius

Penstemon humilis var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the bluntleaf beardtongue, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are rounded at the tips, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
