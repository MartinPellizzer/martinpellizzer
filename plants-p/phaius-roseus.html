Phaius roseus

Phaius roseus, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, erect stems with large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem. Its flowers bloom in late summer or early autumn, producing a fragrant, orchid-like scent.

Phaius roseus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
