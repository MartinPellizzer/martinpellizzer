Paspalum rhizomatosum

Paspalum rhizomatosum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.
