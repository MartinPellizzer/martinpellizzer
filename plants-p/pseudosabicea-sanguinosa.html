Pseudosabicea sanguinosa

Pseudosabicea sanguinosa, commonly known as the bloodroot saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Pseudosabicea sanguinosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
