Pothos anceps

Pothos anceps, commonly known as the Variegated Pothos, is a vigorous trailing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking variegation of light green and cream. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is primarily cultivated for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in low-light conditions and is highly tolerant of neglect, making it a popular houseplant.

The leaves are glossy and can grow quite large, often reaching several inches in diameter.
