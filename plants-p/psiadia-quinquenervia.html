Psiadia quinquenervia

Psiadia quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined psiadia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Psiadia quinquenervia is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
