Protea homblei

Protea homblei, commonly known as the Hobbleskook, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal regions, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive central cone-like structure.

Protea homblei is a hardy evergreen shrub that plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
