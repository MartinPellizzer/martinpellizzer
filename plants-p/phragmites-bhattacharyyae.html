Phragmites bhattacharyyae

Phragmites bhattacharyyae, commonly known as the Indian common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous growth and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces fluffy, white plumes that are often used in traditional crafts and as a natural indicator of wetland health. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.

Phragmites bhattacharyyae plays a crucial role in maintaining ecological balance in marshes and coastal areas.
