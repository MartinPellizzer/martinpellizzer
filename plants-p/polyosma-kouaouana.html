Polyosma kouaouana

Polyosma kouaouana, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds.
