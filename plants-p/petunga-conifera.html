Petunga conifera

Petunga conifera, commonly known as the conifer petunga, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its conifer-like appearance, with needle-like leaves and a woody stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in moist, rocky soils.

Petunga conifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
