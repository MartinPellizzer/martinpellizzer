Peucedanum alsaticum

Peucedanum alsaticum, commonly known as love turnip or cow parsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust taproot and a tall, branched stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small, dry, and often used in traditional medicine for their carminative and digestive properties.
