Piper zacatense var. percaudatum

Piper zacatense var. percaudatum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, attracting various wildlife species.
