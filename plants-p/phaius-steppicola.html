Phaius steppicola

Phaius steppicola, commonly known as the steppe orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base of the stem. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinct lip that is often darker in color.

Phaius steppicola thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.
