Polygonum flagelliforme

Polygonum flagelliforme, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a reddish tinge.

Polygonum flagelliforme is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it both a useful plant in horticulture and a potential weed in natural areas.
