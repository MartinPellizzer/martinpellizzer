Persea grijsii

Persea grijsii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
