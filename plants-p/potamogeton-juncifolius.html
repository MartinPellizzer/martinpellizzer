Potamogeton juncifolius

Potamogeton juncifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, which helps it maximize light absorption in water. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it often grows in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
