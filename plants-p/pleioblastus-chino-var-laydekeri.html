Pleioblastus chino var. laydekeri

Pleioblastus chino var. laydekeri, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or Laydeker's bamboo, is a variant of the pleioblastus genus native to East Asia.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, featuring slender, green to bluish-green culms. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, providing a lush appearance in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
