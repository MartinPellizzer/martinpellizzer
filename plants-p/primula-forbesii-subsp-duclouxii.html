Primula forbesii subsp. duclouxii

Primula forbesii subsp.

duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central China. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and lilac, often with a distinctive dark spot at the base. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
