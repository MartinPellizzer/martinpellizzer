Pucara

Pucara, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Pucara is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional uses in Andean medicine.
