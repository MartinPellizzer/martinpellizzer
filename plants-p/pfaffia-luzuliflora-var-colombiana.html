Pfaffia luzuliflora var. colombiana

Pfaffia luzuliflora var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian purple false shamrock, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage, which is often adorned with intricate patterns resembling those of true shamrocks. The plant typically grows as a low-growing perennial, forming dense mats of foliage that thrive in cool, moist environments. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by clusters of red berries.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is increasingly popular in horticultural collections and shaded garden settings.
