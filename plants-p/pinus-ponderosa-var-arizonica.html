Pinus ponderosa var. arizonica

Pinus ponderosa var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona ponderosa pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically longer than those of the typical ponderosa pine. The cones of this variety are often larger and more robust, containing edible seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
