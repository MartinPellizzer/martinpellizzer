Pandanus leptocarpus

Pandanus leptocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-fruit pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that grow in a fan-like arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by elongated, narrow fruits. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to salty air and sandy soils.
