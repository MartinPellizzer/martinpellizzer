Pseudognaphalium canescens

Pseudognaphalium canescens, commonly known as silverleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The flowers are arranged in compact clusters and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
