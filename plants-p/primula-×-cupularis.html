Primula × cupularis

Primula × cupularis, commonly known as the cupular primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically features dark green, glossy leaves that are heart-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Its blooming period usually occurs in early spring, making it a popular choice for adding color to shaded areas.
