Picea excelsa f. inverta

Picea excelsa f. inverta, commonly known as the inverted white spruce, is a botanical variant of the white spruce species.

It is characterized by its unique inverted cone shape, which distinguishes it from the typical upright cones of the standard white spruce. The foliage is dense and needle-like, with a silvery-blue hue that adds to its ornamental appeal. It is native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist climates.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and hardiness in temperate conditions.
