Polygala amara var. uliginosa

Polygala amara var. uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while its fruit is a legume that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insect species.
