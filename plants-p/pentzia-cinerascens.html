Pentzia cinerascens

Pentzia cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
