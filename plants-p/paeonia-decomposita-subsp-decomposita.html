Paeonia decomposita subsp. decomposita

Paeonia decomposita subsp.

decomposita, commonly known as the western peony, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
