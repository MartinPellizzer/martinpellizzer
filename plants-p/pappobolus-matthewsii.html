Pappobolus matthewsii

Pappobolus matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthew's burr, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed heads that resemble small, rounded balls. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its unique, persistent fruiting bodies. Its seeds are enclosed in a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal.

Pappobolus matthewsii is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
