Pseuderanthemum bracteatum

Pseuderanthemum bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
