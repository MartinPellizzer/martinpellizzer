Plantago lagopus subsp. lagopus

Plantago lagopus subsp.

lagopus, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, especially on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and urban areas.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
