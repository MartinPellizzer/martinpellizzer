Pseudosasa projecta

Pseudosasa projecta, commonly known as the flat bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and long, slender culms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Pseudosasa projecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic and structural qualities.
