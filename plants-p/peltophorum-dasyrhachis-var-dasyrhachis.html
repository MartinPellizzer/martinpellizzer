Peltophorum dasyrhachis var. dasyrhachis

Peltophorum dasyrhachis var. dasyrhachis, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The tree has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and light grey, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and as a street tree due to its rapid growth and showy blooms.
