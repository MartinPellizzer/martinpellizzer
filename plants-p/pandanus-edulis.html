Pandanus edulis

Pandanus edulis, commonly known as the edible pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as roofing material. The plant produces fragrant, orange-colored fruits that are edible and used in various culinary applications. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Overall, Pandanus edulis plays a significant role in both cultural and ecological contexts within its native regions.
