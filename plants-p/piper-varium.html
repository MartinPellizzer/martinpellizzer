Piper varium

Piper varium, commonly known as the variable pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, aromatic berries. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with adventitious roots and a preference for warm, humid climates.

Piper varium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
