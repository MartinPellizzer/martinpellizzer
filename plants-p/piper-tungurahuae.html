Piper tungurahuae

Piper tungurahuae, commonly known as the Tungurahua pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of Ecuador and is characterized by its small, pendant-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and has dark green, ovate leaves. It is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its limited distribution.

The species is also valued for its potential economic and medicinal applications.
