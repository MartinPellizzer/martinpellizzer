Polypodium hirsutissimum

Polypodium hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are compound, with a broad, shield-shaped structure that helps it retain moisture.

Polypodium hirsutissimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
