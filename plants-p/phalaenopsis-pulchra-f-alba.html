Phalaenopsis pulchra f. alba

Phalaenopsis pulchra f. alba, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid variety that showcases striking white petals and a pale green lip.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical climates. This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several months. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat.

Its botanical features include a robust pseudobulb and lance-shaped leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.
