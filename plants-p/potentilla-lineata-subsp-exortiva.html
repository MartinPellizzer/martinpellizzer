Potentilla lineata subsp. exortiva

Potentilla lineata subsp.

exortiva, commonly known as the exortiva cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
