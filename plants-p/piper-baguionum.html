Piper baguionum

Piper baguionum, commonly known as the Guiana pepper or Guiana soursop, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns yellow when ripe and is used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
