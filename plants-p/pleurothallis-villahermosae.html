Pleurothallis villahermosae

Pleurothallis villahermosae, commonly known as the Villahermosa orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with three sepals and three petals. It is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in the wild.
