Pavonia fonsecana

Pavonia fonsecana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

Pavonia fonsecana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
