Parosela capitulata

Parosela capitulata, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces a single, showy yellow flower at the top of a short stem. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. Its flowers bloom in spring and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Parosela capitulata is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
