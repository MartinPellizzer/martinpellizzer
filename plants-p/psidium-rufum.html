Psidium rufum

Psidium rufum, commonly known as the red guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a strong aromatic scent.

Psidium rufum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage and unique fruit.
