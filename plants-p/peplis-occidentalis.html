Peplis occidentalis

Peplis occidentalis, commonly known as the western peplis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to dry climates and can thrive in full sun.

Peplis occidentalis is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance.
