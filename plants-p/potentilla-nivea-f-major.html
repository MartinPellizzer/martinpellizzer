Potentilla nivea f. major

Potentilla nivea f. major, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, rounded leaves with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
