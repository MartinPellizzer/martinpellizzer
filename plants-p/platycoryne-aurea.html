Platycoryne aurea

Platycoryne aurea, commonly known as the golden flat-leaf orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, flat, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal.
