Phyteuma corniculatum var. petraeum

Phyteuma corniculatum var. petraeum, commonly known as the rock phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as part of alpine displays.

The species is also notable for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
