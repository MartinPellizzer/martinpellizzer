Pectis brevicaulis

Pectis brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Pectis brevicaulis is adapted to dry conditions and is considered a hardy, drought-resistant species.
