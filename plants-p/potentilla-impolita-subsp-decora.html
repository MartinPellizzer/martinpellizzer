Potentilla impolita subsp. decora

Potentilla impolita subsp.

decora, commonly known as the Decorate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and bright floral display.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
