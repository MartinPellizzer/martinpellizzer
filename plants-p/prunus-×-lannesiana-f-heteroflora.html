Prunus × lannesiana f. heteroflora

Prunus × lannesiana f. heteroflora, commonly known as the variable-flowered cherry, is a rare and ornamental plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus incisa, characterized by its distinctive floral morphology. The plant typically exhibits a mix of white and pink flowers, with variations in petal shape and coloration. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is valued in botanical gardens and for its ecological significance.
