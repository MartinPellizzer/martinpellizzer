Podocarpus montanus var. meridensis

Podocarpus montanus var. meridensis, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a medium to large evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and cylindrical, clustered cones. The leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to its pale bark. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
