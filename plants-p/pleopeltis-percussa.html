Pleopeltis percussa

Pleopeltis percussa, commonly known as the hammer fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves that are often bright green and have a leathery texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are typically narrow and elongated, with a unique arrangement of pinnae that give it a striking appearance.

Pleopeltis percussa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
