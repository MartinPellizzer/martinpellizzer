Phlox ovata

Phlox ovata, commonly known as the common phlox, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Phlox ovata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
