Potentilla valderia

Potentilla valderia, commonly known as Valderia cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Potentilla valderia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
