Pilocereus divaricatus

Pilocereus divaricatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its blooming period is typically during the warmer months, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

Pilocereus divaricatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal flowering habit.
