Phlox longifolia subsp. compacta

Phlox longifolia subsp.

compacta, commonly known as the compact phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 15 centimeters.

The plant features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, gray-green leaves. Its flowers are showy, bell-shaped, and come in shades of pink to lavender, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
