Penstemon eriantherus var. grandis

Penstemon eriantherus var. grandis, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to six feet, making it a prominent feature in gardens and natural habitats. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with prominent white or yellow markings on the upper lip. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Penstemon eriantherus var. grandis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a valuable addition to xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
