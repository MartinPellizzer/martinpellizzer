Plagiopteron suaveolens

Plagiopteron suaveolens, commonly known as sweet thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often borne on the stems and branches. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its pleasant, sweet scent, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom.
