Psephellus khalkhalensis

Psephellus khalkhalensis, commonly known as the Khalkhal flax, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces fibrous capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
