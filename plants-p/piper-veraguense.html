Piper veraguense

Piper veraguense, commonly known as the Veraguas pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing a spicy, aromatic compound similar to capsaicin. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
