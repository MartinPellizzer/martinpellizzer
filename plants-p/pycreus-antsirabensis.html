Pycreus antsirabensis

Pycreus antsirabensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a rare and endemic species found exclusively in the eastern rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant, often colorful blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are highly specialized, with intricate petal arrangements that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Pycreus antsirabensis is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
