Passiflora phellos

Passiflora phellos, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a distinctive, fleshy, red calyx. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora phellos is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
