Paratropia longifolia

Paratropia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved paratropia, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Paratropia longifolia is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
