Plectranthus fulvescens

Plectranthus fulvescens, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically purple or violet in color, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a visually appealing addition to both indoor and outdoor spaces.
