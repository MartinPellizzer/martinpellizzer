Peixotoa hirta

Peixotoa hirta, commonly known as the hairy peixotoa, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive hairy appearance.

Peixotoa hirta is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
