Platanthera freynii

Platanthera freynii, commonly known as the Frey's lady's tresses, is a flowering plant in the orchid family.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and long, spiral-like inflorescence of small, white to pale yellow flowers. This terrestrial orchid typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It is native to regions of North America, particularly the eastern United States.

The plant is notable for its fragrant blooms and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and moths.
