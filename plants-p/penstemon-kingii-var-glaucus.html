Penstemon kingii var. glaucus

Penstemon kingii var. glaucus, commonly known as the blue curl penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a powdery blue-gray coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
