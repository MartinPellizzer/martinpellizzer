Phoradendron dussii

Phoradendron dussii, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has small, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its roots penetrate the host tree's bark to absorb water and nutrients, while it performs photosynthesis using its own leaves.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for birds and other wildlife.
