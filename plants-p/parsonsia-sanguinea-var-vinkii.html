Parsonsia sanguinea var. vinkii

Parsonsia sanguinea var. vinkii, commonly known as the Vink's flame vine, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to tropical gardens and botanical collections.
