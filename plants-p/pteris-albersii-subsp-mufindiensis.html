Pteris albersii subsp. mufindiensis

Pteris albersii subsp.

mufindiensis, commonly known as the Mufindi water fern, is a fern species native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
