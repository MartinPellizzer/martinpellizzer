Phreatia lorentzii

Phreatia lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Phreatia lorentzii is part of the Epidendroideae subfamily and belongs to the Orchidaceae family.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
