Prunus insititia subsp. claudiana

Prunus insititia subsp.

claudiana, commonly known as the black-cherry subsp. claudiana, is a subspecies native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with serrated edges.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
