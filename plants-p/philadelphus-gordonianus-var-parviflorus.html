Philadelphus gordonianus var. parviflorus

Philadelphus gordonianus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf goldsmith honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in early summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
