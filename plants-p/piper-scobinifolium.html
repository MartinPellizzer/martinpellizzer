Piper scobinifolium

Piper scobinifolium, commonly known as the scobinifoliate pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
