Pleopeltis accedens

Pleopeltis accedens, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that often grow in dense clusters on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. The plant produces spores in small, clustered sporangia located on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
