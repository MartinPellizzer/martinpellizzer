Porroglossum raoorum

Porroglossum raoorum, commonly known as the Red-veined Orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety petals that often display intricate red veins against a backdrop of green or white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its flowers are usually small to medium in size, with a distinctive lip that is often more colorful than the rest of the flower.

Porroglossum raoorum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
