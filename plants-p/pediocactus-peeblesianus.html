Pediocactus peeblesianus

Pediocactus peeblesianus, commonly known as the Peebles' prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and are capable of storing water. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation programs.
