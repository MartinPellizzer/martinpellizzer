Protium pullei

Protium pullei, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The tree is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
