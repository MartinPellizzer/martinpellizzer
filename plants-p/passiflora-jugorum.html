Passiflora jugorum

Passiflora jugorum, commonly known as the Jamaican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tropical flavor. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora jugorum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value to pollinators.
