Polygonum macrophyllum

Polygonum macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along streambanks and in wet meadows.

Its rapid growth and extensive root system make it an invasive species in some regions.
