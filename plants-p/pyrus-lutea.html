Pyrus lutea

Pyrus lutea, commonly known as the yellow pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial purposes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems where it provides food for various wildlife species.
