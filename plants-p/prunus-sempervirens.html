Prunus sempervirens

Prunus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits known as cherries, which are similar to those of other Prunus species. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

The leaves remain evergreen throughout the year, contributing to its name and making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
