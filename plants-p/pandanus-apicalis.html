Pandanus apicalis

Pandanus apicalis, commonly known as the coastal pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy environments, tolerating salt spray and wind. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

Pandanus apicalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various species.
