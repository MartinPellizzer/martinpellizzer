Panax pseudoginseng var. elegantior

Panax pseudoginseng var. elegantior, commonly known as the elegant ginseng, is a rare variant of the ginseng genus native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated root system and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red berries, which are typically consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its rarity and medicinal potential, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
