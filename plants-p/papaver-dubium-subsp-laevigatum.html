Papaver dubium subsp. laevigatum

Papaver dubium subsp.

laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and striking, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are surrounded by a series of sepals that form a cup-like structure, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
