Packera glabella

Packera glabella, commonly known as the smoothleaf fleabane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are smooth and alternate, with a distinctive bluish-green color.

It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
