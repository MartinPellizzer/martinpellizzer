Potamogeton pectinatus var. vaillantii

Potamogeton pectinatus var. vaillantii, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Europe and Asia.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. Its rhizomes help stabilize sediment, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of P. pectinatus.
