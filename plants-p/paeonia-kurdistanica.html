Paeonia kurdistanica

Paeonia kurdistanica, commonly known as the Kurdish peony, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, and red during the spring. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environment.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its habitat.
