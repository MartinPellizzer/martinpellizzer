Paralasianthus dichotomus

Paralasianthus dichotomus, commonly known as the dichotomous flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that appear in clusters, often blooming in summer. The plant has pinnate leaves with several pairs of leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in a smooth, greyish bark. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to withstand moderate drought conditions.
