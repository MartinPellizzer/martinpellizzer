Pavetta indica var. obovata

Pavetta indica var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, obovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by ellipsoid berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
