Piliostigma tortuosum

Piliostigma tortuosum, commonly known as the African sickle tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its zigzagging, woody stems and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing flat, oval seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

The plant has traditional uses in local medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
