Palinetes falcata

Palinetes falcata, commonly known as the sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny environments.

Palinetes falcata is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
