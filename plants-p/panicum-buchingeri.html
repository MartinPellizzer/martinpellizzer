Panicum buchingeri

Panicum buchingeri, commonly known as the Buchinger's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
