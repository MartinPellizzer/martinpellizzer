Patagonium radicans var. filicaule

Patagonium radicans var. filicaule, commonly known as the Filicaule Patagonian Creeper, is a unique variant of the Patagonium radicans species.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant exhibits a robust root system that allows it to thrive in rocky and arid environments. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

This variant is native to the southern regions of South America, particularly in Patagonia, where it adapts to the harsh climate.
