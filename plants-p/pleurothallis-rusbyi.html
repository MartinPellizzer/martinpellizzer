Pleurothallis rusbyi

Pleurothallis rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without being parasitic.

Pleurothallis rusbyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
