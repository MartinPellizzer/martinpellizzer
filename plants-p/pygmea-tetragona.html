Pygmea tetragona

Pygmea tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled pygmy gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, four-angled stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
