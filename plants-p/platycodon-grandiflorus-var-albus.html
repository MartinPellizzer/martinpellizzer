Platycodon grandiflorus var. albus

Platycodon grandiflorus var. albus, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and grows up to 1 meter tall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
