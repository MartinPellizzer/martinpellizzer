Philadelphus incanus f. kulingensis

Philadelphus incanus f. kulingensis, commonly known as the Kuling mock orange, is a variant of the mock orange plant native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both gardens and ecological landscapes.
