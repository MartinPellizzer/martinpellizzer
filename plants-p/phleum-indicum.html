Phleum indicum

Phleum indicum, commonly known as Indian barley, is a perennial grass native to Asia.

It has slender, tufted stems that grow up to 60 cm in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous roots and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
