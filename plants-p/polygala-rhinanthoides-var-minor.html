Polygala rhinanthoides var. minor

Polygala rhinanthoides var. minor, commonly known as the lesser yellowhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils, often in open woodlands or grasslands. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers have a distinctive, spurred structure.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
