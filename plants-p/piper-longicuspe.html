Piper longicuspe

Piper longicuspe, commonly known as the long-stemmed pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic compound responsible for its characteristic flavor. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, with a mild peppery scent when crushed.

Piper longicuspe is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruits in local cuisines.
