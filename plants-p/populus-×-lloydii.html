Populus × lloydii

Populus × lloydii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada white fir, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is a tall, coniferous tree with a straight trunk and a conical crown. Its needles are short, bluish-green, and arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers and cones, typical of the Pinaceae family.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
