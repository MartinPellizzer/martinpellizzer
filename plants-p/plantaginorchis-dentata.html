Plantaginorchis dentata

Plantaginorchis dentata, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, underground rhizome and a single, erect stem. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
