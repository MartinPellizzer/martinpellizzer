Potentilla davidii

Potentilla davidii, commonly known as David's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Potentilla davidii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
