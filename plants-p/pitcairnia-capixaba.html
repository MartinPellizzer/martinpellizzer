Pitcairnia capixaba

Pitcairnia capixaba, commonly known as the Capixaba air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among many bromeliads. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
