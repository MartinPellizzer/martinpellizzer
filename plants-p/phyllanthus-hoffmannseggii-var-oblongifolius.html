Phyllanthus hoffmannseggii var. oblongifolius

Phyllanthus hoffmannseggii var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptic or oblong leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with unisexual male and female flowers on separate inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
