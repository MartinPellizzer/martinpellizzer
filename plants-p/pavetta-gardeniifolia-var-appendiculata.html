Pavetta gardeniifolia var. appendiculata

Pavetta gardeniifolia var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
