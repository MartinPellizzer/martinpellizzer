Primula × juliana

Primula × juliana, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early-season color. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

Primula × juliana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in shaded garden beds or as a container plant.
