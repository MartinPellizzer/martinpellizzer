Pulmonaria apennina

Pulmonaria apennina, commonly known as the Italian lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves with distinctive purple and white markings. The plant produces upright spikes of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream sides.

Pulmonaria apennina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
