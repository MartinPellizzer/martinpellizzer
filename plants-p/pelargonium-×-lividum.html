Pelargonium × lividum

Pelargonium × lividum, commonly known as the purple-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage that contrasts beautifully with its vibrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Pelargonium × lividum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
