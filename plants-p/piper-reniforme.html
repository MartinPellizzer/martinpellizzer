Piper reniforme

Piper reniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or the kidneyleaf pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that develop into peppery, green berries. The plant belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
