Paphiopedilum godefroyae f. album

Paphiopedilum godefroyae f. album, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its unique floral morphology and striking white petals.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure, known as a resupinate lip, which is a key feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
