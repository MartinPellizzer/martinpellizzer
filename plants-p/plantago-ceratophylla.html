Plantago ceratophylla

Plantago ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and prominent central veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
