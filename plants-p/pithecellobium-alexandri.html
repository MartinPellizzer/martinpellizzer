Pithecellobium alexandri

Pithecellobium alexandri, commonly known as the Alexander's pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered together, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its root system is well-developed, allowing it to thrive in various soil types, including those with poor drainage.
