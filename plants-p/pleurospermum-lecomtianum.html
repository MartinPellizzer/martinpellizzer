Pleurospermum lecomtianum

Pleurospermum lecomtianum, commonly known as the Lecomte's pleurospermum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the end of each stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
