Phaseolus sphaericus var. bicolor

Phaseolus sphaericus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bean, is a unique variant of the globe bean species.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicoloration, typically featuring a mix of white and green on its pod and seeds. This plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, tropical climates. The pods are round and compact, often used in traditional cuisines for their mild flavor.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural innovation.
