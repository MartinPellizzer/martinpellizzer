Paphiopedilum × mittrachianum

Paphiopedilum × mittrachianum, commonly known as the Mittrachian hybrid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from crosses between Paphiopedilum niveum and Paphiopedilum suavissimum.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy petals and sepals that often display a mix of white, green, and pink hues. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate.

This hybrid is prized for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
