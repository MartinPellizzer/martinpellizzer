Poa bulbosa subsp. perligulata

Poa bulbosa subsp.

perligulata, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, grassy habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, two-ranked spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
