Psidium mouririoides

Psidium mouririoides, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or mouriri, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

These berries are rich in nutrients and are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.
