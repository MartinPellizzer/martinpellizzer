Parthenocissus radicantissima

Parthenocissus radicantissima, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a vigorous vine native to eastern North America.

It features palmate leaves that typically turn bright red in the fall, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It has a climbing habit, using adhesive pads on its stems to cling to surfaces.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.
