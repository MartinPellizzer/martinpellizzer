Phylica strigosa

Phylica strigosa, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phylica strigosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
