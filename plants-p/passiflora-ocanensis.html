Passiflora ocanensis

Passiflora ocanensis, commonly known as the Ocanense passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

Passiflora ocanensis also plays a role in supporting local pollinators and biodiversity in its native habitats.
