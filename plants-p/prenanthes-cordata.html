Prenanthes cordata

Prenanthes cordata, commonly known as roundleaf waterplantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and have a waxy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in a dense, spike-like inflorescence.

Prenanthes cordata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
