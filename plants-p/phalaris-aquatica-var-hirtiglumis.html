Phalaris aquatica var. hirtiglumis

Phalaris aquatica var. hirtiglumis, commonly known as the hairy bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush native to wetland environments.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the lower leaves often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to aquatic conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
