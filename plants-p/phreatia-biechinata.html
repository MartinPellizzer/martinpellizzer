Phreatia biechinata

Phreatia biechinata, commonly known as the Biechinata orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with red or brown markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic-rich soil.

Phreatia biechinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
