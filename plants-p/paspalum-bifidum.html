Paspalum bifidum

Paspalum bifidum, commonly known as the bifid paspalum, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist or wetland habitats, thriving in areas with high humidity.

Paspalum bifidum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
