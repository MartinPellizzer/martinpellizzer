Phaseolus minimus var. borealis

Phaseolus minimus var. borealis, commonly known as the northern dwarf bean, is a wild legume native to North America.

It is a small, annual plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold climates, often flowering and setting seed in early summer.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
