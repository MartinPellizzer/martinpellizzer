Physurus lindleyanus

Physurus lindleyanus, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive if not properly managed.
