Psephellus galactochroa

Psephellus galactochroa, commonly known as the milkweed aster, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Psephellus galactochroa is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
