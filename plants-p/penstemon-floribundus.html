Penstemon floribundus

Penstemon floribundus, commonly known as the showy penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Penstemon floribundus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
