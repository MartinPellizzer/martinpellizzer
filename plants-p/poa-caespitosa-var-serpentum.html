Poa caespitosa var. serpentum

Poa caespitosa var. serpentum, commonly known as the serpent's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-rowed florets. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
