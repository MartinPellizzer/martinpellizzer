Plantago australis subsp. pretoana

Plantago australis subsp.

pretoana, commonly known as black plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, dark green, waxy leaves that are often tinged with purple or black at the base.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
