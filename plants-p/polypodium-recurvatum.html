Polypodium recurvatum

Polypodium recurvatum, commonly known as the rock fern or staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically arched or recurved, giving it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and often grows on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

Polypodium recurvatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
