Paeonia Brownii Subsp. Californica

Paeonia brownii subsp. californica is a rare and striking member of the peony family native to the western United States. This subspecies is known for its large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a unique addition to native plant gardens. Despite its beauty, it is often overlooked due to its limited distribution and subtle appearance. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this delicate and ecologically important plant.
