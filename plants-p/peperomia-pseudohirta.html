Peperomia pseudohirta

Peperomia pseudohirta, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia pseudohirta is known for its low maintenance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
