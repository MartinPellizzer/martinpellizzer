Potamogeton pectinatus var. filiformis

Potamogeton pectinatus var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically smooth and dark green in color. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and oxygen levels in the environment.
