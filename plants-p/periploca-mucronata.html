Periploca mucronata

Periploca mucronata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and has been used traditionally for various purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
