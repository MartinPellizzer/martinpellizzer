Piper pseudoviridicaule

Piper pseudoviridicaule, commonly known as the green-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, green stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
