Polygonum jaxarticum

Polygonum jaxarticum, commonly known as the creeping smartweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and distinctive pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, ditches, and along stream banks.

Polygonum jaxarticum is known for its rapid spread and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
