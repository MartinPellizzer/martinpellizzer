Phlox clarkioides

Phlox clarkioides, commonly known as Clark's phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Phlox clarkioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
