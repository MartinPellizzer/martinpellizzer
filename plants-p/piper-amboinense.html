Piper amboinense

Piper amboinense, commonly known as Java pepper or long pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, reddish berries that resemble peppercorns. The plant produces a pungent, aromatic compound similar to black pepper, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their distinctive flavor.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary properties.
