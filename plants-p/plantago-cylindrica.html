Plantago cylindrica

Plantago cylindrica, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features erect, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils, and is known for its medicinal properties.

The leaves and seeds have been used traditionally for their healing and detoxifying effects.
