Phacelia magellanica var. calycosa

Phacelia magellanica var. calycosa, commonly known as the Magellan phacelia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support pollinators in native gardens.
