Panicum nudiculme

Panicum nudiculme, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum nudiculme plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
