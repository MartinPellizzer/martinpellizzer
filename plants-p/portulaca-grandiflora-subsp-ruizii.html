Portulaca grandiflora subsp. ruizii

Portulaca grandiflora subsp.

ruizii, commonly known as the Mexican portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the Americas. It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow.

This species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot and dry climates. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and attractive blooms.
