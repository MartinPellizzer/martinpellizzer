Platanthera halconensis

Platanthera halconensis, commonly known as the Halconense Lady's Tresses, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, and densely packed inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered in many areas, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
