Primula fedtschenkoi

Primula fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Siberian primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is well adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions.

Primula fedtschenkoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
