Phyllanthus leucanthus

Phyllanthus leucanthus, commonly known as white-flowered spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish-green stems. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-winged fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Phyllanthus leucanthus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
