Pinasgelon

Pinasgelon, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to three meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly waxy, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Pinasgelon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
