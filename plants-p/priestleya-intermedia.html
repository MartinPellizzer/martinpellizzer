Priestleya intermedia

Priestleya intermedia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking epiphytic orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale green, leafless stems and showy, white or pale pink flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in pine flatwoods. Its unique morphology and limited natural range make it a subject of both botanical interest and conservation concern.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is listed as endangered in several states.
