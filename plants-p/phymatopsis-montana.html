Phymatopsis montana

Phymatopsis montana, commonly known as the mountain phymatopsis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture.

Phymatopsis montana is often found in rocky soils and is considered a characteristic species of desert and mountainous regions.
