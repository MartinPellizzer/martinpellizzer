Panicum curviflorum

Panicum curviflorum, commonly known as curlyflower grass, is a native North American grass species characterized by its distinctive, coiled inflorescence.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers that mature into fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Panicum curviflorum is drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
