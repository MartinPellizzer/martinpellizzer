Pyrus tytthocarpa

Pyrus tytthocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in temperate climates.
