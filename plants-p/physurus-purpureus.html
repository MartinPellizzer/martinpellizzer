Physurus purpureus

Physurus purpureus, commonly known as the purple-stemmed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Physurus purpureus is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length.
