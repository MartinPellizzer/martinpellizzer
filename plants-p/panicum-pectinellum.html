Panicum pectinellum

Panicum pectinellum, commonly known as silky grass or silky panicum, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle, which produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Panicum pectinellum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
