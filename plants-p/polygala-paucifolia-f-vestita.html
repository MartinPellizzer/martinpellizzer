Polygala paucifolia f. vestita

Polygala paucifolia f. vestita, commonly known as the velvet leaf milkvetch, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of China.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are densely covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are often overlooked due to their modest size. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
