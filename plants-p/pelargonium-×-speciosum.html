Pelargonium × speciosum

Pelargonium × speciosum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, serrated leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Pelargonium × speciosum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for hanging baskets, window boxes, and garden borders.
