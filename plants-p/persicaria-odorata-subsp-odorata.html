Persicaria odorata subsp. odorata

Persicaria odorata subsp.

odorata, commonly known as African blue basil, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with purple or blue on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. It is cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.

The subspecies odorata is distinguished by its stronger scent and more robust growth compared to other varieties.
