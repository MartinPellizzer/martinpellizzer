Podochilus malabaricus

Podochilus malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
