Packera paupercula

Packera paupercula, commonly known as the meadow saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats such as meadows and alpine slopes. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Packera paupercula is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in disturbed or sunny areas.
