Prostanthera sejuncta subsp. sejuncta

Prostanthera sejuncta subsp.

sejuncta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. Prostanthera sejuncta subsp.

sejuncta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
