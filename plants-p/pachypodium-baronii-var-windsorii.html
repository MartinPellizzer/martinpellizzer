Pachypodium baronii var. windsorii

Pachypodium baronii var. windsorii, commonly known as the Windsor's thick-leafed pachypodium, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves, which help it store water and deter herbivores. The plant produces small, white flowers during the dry season, typically in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
