Pentanema × semiamplexicaule

Pentanema × semiamplexicaule, commonly known as the hybrid snowbell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it is less commonly cultivated than its parent species.
