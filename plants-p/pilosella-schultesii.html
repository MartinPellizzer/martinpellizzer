Pilosella schultesii

Pilosella schultesii, commonly known as the Schultesi's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in a spherical shape, giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are typically grayish-green and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its common name.

Pilosella schultesii is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
