Pelargonium × dilutum

Pelargonium × dilutum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with distinct color zones, giving the plant its common name. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and containers.

It prefers full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
