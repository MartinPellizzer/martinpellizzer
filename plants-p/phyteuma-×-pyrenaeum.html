Phyteuma × pyrenaeum

Phyteuma × pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its hardy nature.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
