Piper enckea

Piper enckea, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a woody climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits.

Piper enckea is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid, shaded environments.
