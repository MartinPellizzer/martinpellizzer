Pasania bonnetii

Pasania bonnetii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are arranged in terminal clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Pasania bonnetii is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
