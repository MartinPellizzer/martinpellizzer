Phlomoides tuberosa

Phlomoides tuberosa, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
