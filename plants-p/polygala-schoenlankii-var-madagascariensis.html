Polygala schoenlankii var. madagascariensis

Polygala schoenlankii var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and exhibits a unique morphology with stipitate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its restricted distribution and distinctive botanical features.
