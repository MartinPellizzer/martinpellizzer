Phelipanche lavandulacea subsp. trichocalyx

Phelipanche lavandulacea subsp.

trichocalyx, commonly known as the purple witchhazel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitats.
