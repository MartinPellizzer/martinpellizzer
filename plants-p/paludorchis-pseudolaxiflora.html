Paludorchis pseudolaxiflora

Paludorchis pseudolaxiflora, commonly known as the bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, nutrient-poor soils and thrives in environments with high moisture levels. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic, waterlogged soil in which it grows.

Paludorchis pseudolaxiflora is often found in association with other wetland flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
