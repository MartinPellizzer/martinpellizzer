Phleum phalaris

Phleum phalaris, commonly known as needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in a variety of environmental conditions. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

Phleum phalaris is also valued in livestock feed due to its high nutritional content.
