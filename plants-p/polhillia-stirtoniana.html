Polhillia stirtoniana

Polhillia stirtoniana, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to orange-red flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
