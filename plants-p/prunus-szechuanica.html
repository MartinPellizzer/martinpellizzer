Prunus szechuanica

Prunus szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Prunus szechuanica is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its various health benefits.
