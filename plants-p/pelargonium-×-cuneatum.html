Pelargonium × cuneatum

Pelargonium × cuneatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Pelargonium × cuneatum is known for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in moderate climates.
