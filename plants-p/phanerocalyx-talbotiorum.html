Phanerocalyx talbotiorum

Phanerocalyx talbotiorum, commonly known as the talbot's loropetalum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late spring. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, with showy, recurved petals that attract pollinators. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside.

It is well-suited for landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
