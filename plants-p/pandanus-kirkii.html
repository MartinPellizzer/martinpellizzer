Pandanus kirkii

Pandanus kirkii, commonly known as the kirk's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Polynesia and Micronesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically appearing in the upper part of the plant.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
