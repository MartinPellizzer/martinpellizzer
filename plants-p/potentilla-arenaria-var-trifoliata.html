Potentilla arenaria var. trifoliata

Potentilla arenaria var. trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

This species is often found in tundra and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to cold and wind.
