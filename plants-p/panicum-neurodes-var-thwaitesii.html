Panicum neurodes var. thwaitesii

Panicum neurodes var. thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' panicgrass, is a variant of the panicum genus native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
