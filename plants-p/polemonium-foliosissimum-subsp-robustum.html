Polemonium foliosissimum subsp. robustum

Polemonium foliosissimum subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the robust Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States. It features lanceolate, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its showy inflorescence is surrounded by broad, reflexed bracts that add to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ecological and aesthetic contributions to native gardens.
