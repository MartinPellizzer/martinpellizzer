Parthenocissus inserta f. macrophylla

Parthenocissus inserta f. macrophylla, commonly known as the giant ivy or large-leaf woodbine, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 10 inches in diameter, providing dense foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its strong, flexible stems allow it to climb walls, trees, and trellises with ease.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover structures.
