Polygonum bistorta subsp. ochotense

Polygonum bistorta subsp.

ochotense, commonly known as the Siberian bistort, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
