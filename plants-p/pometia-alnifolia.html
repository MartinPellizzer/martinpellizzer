Pometia alnifolia

Pometia alnifolia, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a small evergreen tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests. The plant has smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. Its small, white flowers are followed by edible, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Pometia alnifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
