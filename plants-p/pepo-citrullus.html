Pepo citrullus

Pepo citrullus, commonly known as watermelon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, green rind and juicy, red or yellow flesh. The fruit develops from a flowering stem and is typically harvested when the rind turns a deep shade.

Watermelon contains high water content and is rich in nutrients such as vitamins A and C. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous outer layer, a spongy mesocarp, and a central cavity filled with seeds.
