Pinus strobus var. monticola

Pinus strobus var. monticola, commonly known as the Appalachian white pine, is a variant of the eastern white pine native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically lighter in color compared to the standard white pine. The tree produces cones that are smaller and more compact than those of the typical variety, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in timber production.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
