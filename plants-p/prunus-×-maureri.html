Prunus × maureri

Prunus × maureri, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, sweet fruits similar to apricots, though they are typically not edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Botanically, it is a hybrid between Prunus mandshurica and Prunus serrulata, showcasing genetic diversity in the Prunus genus.
