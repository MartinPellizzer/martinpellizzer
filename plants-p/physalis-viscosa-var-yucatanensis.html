Physalis viscosa var. yucatanensis

Physalis viscosa var. yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan strawberry or Cape gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that persists on the fruit, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to the Yucatan Peninsula and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its fruit is small, yellow-orange, and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

It is often used in local cuisines and has ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and foliage.
