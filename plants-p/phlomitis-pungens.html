Phlomitis pungens

Phlomitis pungens, commonly known as the alpine phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Phlomitis pungens is often found in alpine meadows and is a hardy plant that can withstand harsh mountain conditions.
