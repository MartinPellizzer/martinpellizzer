Poa nemoralis var. coarctata

Poa nemoralis var. coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tightly packed, forming dense mats. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually less than 2 cm in length.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
