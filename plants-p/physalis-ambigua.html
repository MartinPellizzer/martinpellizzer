Physalis ambigua

Physalis ambigua, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx that turns red as the fruit matures. These fruits are edible when fully ripe and have a tangy, sweet flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
