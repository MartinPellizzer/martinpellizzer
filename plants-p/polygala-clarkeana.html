Polygala clarkeana

Polygala clarkeana, commonly known as the Clarke's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China and India.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
