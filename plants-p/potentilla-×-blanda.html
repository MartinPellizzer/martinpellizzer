Potentilla × blanda

Potentilla × blanda, commonly known as sand spurrey, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy as well. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
