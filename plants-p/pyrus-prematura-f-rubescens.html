Pyrus prematura f. rubescens

Pyrus prematura f. rubescens, commonly known as the early pear with red foliage, is a botanical variant of the early pear species.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish leaves that emerge in early spring, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, with a rounded habit. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
