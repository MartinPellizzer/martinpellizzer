Potentilla togasii

Potentilla togasii, commonly known as the Tibetan cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments, and is adapted to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually palmately divided, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

Potentilla togasii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
