Passiflora elongata

Passiflora elongata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces edible, orange-red berries. Its botanical features include a woody stem with tendrils for climbing and a unique reproductive structure called a gynostemon.

Passiflora elongata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
