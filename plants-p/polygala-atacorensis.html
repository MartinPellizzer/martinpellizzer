Polygala atacorensis

Polygala atacorensis, commonly known as the Atacorensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, showy flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
