Potentilla canescens var. uechtritzii

Potentilla canescens var. uechtritzii, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has hairy, grayish leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species well-suited to harsh, high-altitude environments.
