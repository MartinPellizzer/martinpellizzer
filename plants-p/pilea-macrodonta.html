Pilea macrodonta

Pilea macrodonta, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear on short stalks. It is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other plants like philodendrons and peace lilies.

Pilea macrodonta is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
