Piper cernuum

Piper cernuum, commonly known as the hanging pepper or weeping pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its drooping, pendulous flowers that give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries.

The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly glossy texture.
