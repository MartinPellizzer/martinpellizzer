Pseudobaeckea teres

Pseudobaeckea teres, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pseudobaeckea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
