Pseudarthria densiflora

Pseudarthria densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered legume, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats. It is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Pseudarthria densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
