Penstemon deustus

Penstemon deustus, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, supporting local ecosystems.
