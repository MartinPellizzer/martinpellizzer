Pterostylis agrestis

Pterostylis agrestis, commonly known as the grassland duck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in open, grassy areas and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, slender flower spike. The orchid's flowers are usually green or yellowish-green, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Pterostylis agrestis is pollinated by insects, particularly beetles, which are attracted to its unique floral structure.
