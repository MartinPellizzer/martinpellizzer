Phlox speciosa subsp. euspeciosa

Phlox speciosa subsp.

euspeciosa, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
