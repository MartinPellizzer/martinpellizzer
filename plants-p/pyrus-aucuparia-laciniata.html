Pyrus aucuparia laciniata

Pyrus aucuparia laciniata, commonly known as the fragrant pear, is a hybrid plant that combines characteristics of the European pear (Pyrus communis) and the Japanese pear (Pyrus pyrifolia).

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring large, lobed, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically not as flavorful as traditional pears but are appreciated for their aroma. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Pyrus aucuparia laciniata is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
