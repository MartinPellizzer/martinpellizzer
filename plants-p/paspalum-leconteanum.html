Paspalum leconteanum

Paspalum leconteanum, commonly known as the Leconte's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed or moist soils and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

Paspalum leconteanum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
