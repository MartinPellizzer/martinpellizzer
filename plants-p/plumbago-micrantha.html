Plumbago micrantha

Plumbago micrantha, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Plumbago micrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
