Panicum densiglume

Panicum densiglume, commonly known as the dense-leaved panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured appearance.

Panicum densiglume is adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a common ornamental and forage plant in certain climates.
