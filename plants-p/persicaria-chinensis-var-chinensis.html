Persicaria chinensis var. chinensis

Persicaria chinensis var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish or purplish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a serrated edge. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a useful ornamental and a potential weed.
