Pleurothallis divexa

Pleurothallis divexa, commonly known as the "Pleurothallis divexa," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning it attaches itself to other plants without parasitizing them.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
