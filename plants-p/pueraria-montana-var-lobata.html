Pueraria montana var. lobata

Pueraria montana var. lobata, commonly known as Kudzu, is a vigorous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Kudzu is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it both ecologically significant and a potential invasive species.

Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for their medicinal properties.
