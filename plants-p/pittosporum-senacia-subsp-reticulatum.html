Pittosporum senacia subsp. reticulatum

Pittosporum senacia subsp.

reticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has dark green, glossy leaves with a distinctive reticulate (net-like) venation pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
