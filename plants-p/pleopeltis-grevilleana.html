Pleopeltis grevilleana

Pleopeltis grevilleana, commonly known as the Grevill's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that often grow on trees or rocks. The plant has a compact rosette structure with hairy, glandular leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat. It prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
