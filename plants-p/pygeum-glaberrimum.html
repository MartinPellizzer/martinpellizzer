Pygeum glaberrimum

Pygeum glaberrimum, commonly known as the smooth African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and showy, yellowish-white inflorescences. The tree produces woody, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
