Paullinia interrupta

Paullinia interrupta, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that turns orange when ripe.

Paullinia interrupta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to both drought and flooding.
