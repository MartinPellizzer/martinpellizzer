Piper descourtilsianum

Piper descourtilsianum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or the Guiana pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon basin, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. It is often found in humid, shaded environments within dense rainforests.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
