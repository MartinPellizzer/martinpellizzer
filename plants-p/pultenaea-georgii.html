Pultenaea georgii

Pultenaea georgii, commonly known as George's wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and flowering structures. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow to orange cylindrical flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry conditions.

Pultenaea georgii is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
