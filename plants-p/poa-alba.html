Poa alba

Poa alba, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, white to pale green flowers.

Poa alba is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for wildlife.
