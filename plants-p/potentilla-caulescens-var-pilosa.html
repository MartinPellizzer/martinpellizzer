Potentilla caulescens var. pilosa

Potentilla caulescens var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
