Phrynium coloratum

Phrynium coloratum, commonly known as the red-tipped plantain, is a tropical ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white, yellow, and red markings. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Phrynium coloratum is often cultivated for its striking foliage and is used in both ornamental landscaping and traditional medicine.
