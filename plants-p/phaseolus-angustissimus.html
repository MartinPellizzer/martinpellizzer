Phaseolus angustissimus

Phaseolus angustissimus, commonly known as the narrowleaf bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, ovate leaves with a narrow shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it produces small, elongated pods containing one to three seeds.

This species is valued for its edible beans, which are used in traditional cuisines and have potential for cultivation in agricultural systems.
