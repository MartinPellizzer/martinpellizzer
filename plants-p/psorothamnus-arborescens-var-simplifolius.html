Psorothamnus arborescens var. simplifolius

Psorothamnus arborescens var. simplifolius, commonly known as the simpleleaf burrobrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert regions. It has a low-growing habit with multiple stems and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is distinguished by its simplified leaf structure compared to other varieties of Psorothamnus.
