Pandanus sparganioides

Pandanus sparganioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are typically narrow and linear in shape. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish flowers, which are followed by fibrous, spadix-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in many island communities.
