Pulsatilla chinensis f. plurisepala

Pulsatilla chinensis f. plurisepala, commonly known as the Chinese pasqueflower, is a variant of the pasqueflower species native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers with multiple sepals, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high elevations. Its foliage consists of basal, hairy leaves that are lobed and often covered in a white, waxy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
