Pilocereus marschalleckianus

Pilocereus marschalleckianus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit, where large, fragrant white flowers open at night. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments. Its flowers typically last only one night, adding to its allure and rarity.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
