Passiflora prolata

Passiflora prolata, commonly known as the creeping passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

Passiflora prolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid environments.
