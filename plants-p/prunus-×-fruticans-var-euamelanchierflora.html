Prunus × fruticans var. euamelanchierflora

Prunus × fruticans var. euamelanchierflora, commonly known as the European flowering almond, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits characteristics of both Prunus avium and Prunus serrulata.

It is native to the temperate regions of Europe and is known for its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–4 meters and has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe that ripens in late summer, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
