Philadelphus hirsutus

Philadelphus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on both sides, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile garden plant.

The hairy mock orange is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
