Potamogeton × sudermanicus

Potamogeton × sudermanicus, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton foliosus. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes and ponds.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it can impact aquatic ecosystems by altering water clarity and habitat structure.
