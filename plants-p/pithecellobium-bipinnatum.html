Pithecellobium bipinnatum

Pithecellobium bipinnatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
