Pouteria saltuensis

Pouteria saltuensis, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces an aggregate fruit known as a "cupuacu," which contains a sweet, yellow pulp and seeds. The fruit is highly valued for its flavor and nutritional content, particularly its high vitamin C levels.

Pouteria saltuensis plays a vital role in the ecological balance of the Amazon, supporting a wide range of wildlife.
