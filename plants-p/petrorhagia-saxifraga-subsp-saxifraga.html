Petrorhagia saxifraga subsp. saxifraga

Petrorhagia saxifraga subsp.

saxifraga, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
