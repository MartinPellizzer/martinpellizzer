Primula jesoana

Primula jesoana, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and mountainous regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Primula jesoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
