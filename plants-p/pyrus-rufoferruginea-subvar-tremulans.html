Pyrus rufoferruginea subvar. tremulans

Pyrus rufoferruginea subvar. tremulans, commonly known as the red-brown crabapple, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. The fruit is a small, yellowish apple that ripens in late summer.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
