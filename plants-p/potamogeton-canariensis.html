Potamogeton canariensis

Potamogeton canariensis, commonly known as the Canary pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually dark green in color and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters.

Potamogeton canariensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
