Peperomia pseudocasaretti

Peperomia pseudocasaretti, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a compact, low-maintenance houseplant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate watering.

Its dense foliage makes it an excellent choice for tabletop displays or hanging baskets.
