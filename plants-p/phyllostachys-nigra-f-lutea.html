Phyllostachys nigra f. lutea

Phyllostachys nigra f. lutea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms, which contrast sharply with its dark green leaves. This ornamental bamboo is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.

The plant is relatively hardy and can be propagated through rhizome division or seeds.
