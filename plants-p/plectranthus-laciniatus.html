Plectranthus laciniatus

Plectranthus laciniatus, commonly known as the purpleleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens for its compact growth habit.

Plectranthus laciniatus is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
