Penstemon monoensis

Penstemon monoensis, commonly known as the Mono lake penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in meadows and along the shores of Mono Lake in California.

Penstemon monoensis plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
