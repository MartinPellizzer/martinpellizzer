Physaria saximontana

Physaria saximontana, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.
