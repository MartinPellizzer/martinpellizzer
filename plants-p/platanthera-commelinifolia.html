Platanthera commelinifolia

Platanthera commelinifolia, commonly known as the Commelinifoliate Lady's Slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has a slender, erect stem with few leaves and produces a single, large orchid-like flower at the top. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species in the orchid family.
