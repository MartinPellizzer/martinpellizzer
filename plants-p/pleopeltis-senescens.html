Pleopeltis senescens

Pleopeltis senescens, commonly known as the old man's beard fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading fronds that resemble flowing hair, giving it its common name. The plant has a fibrous root system that allows it to attach to trees and other surfaces in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture in humid environments.

Pleopeltis senescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a houseplant.
