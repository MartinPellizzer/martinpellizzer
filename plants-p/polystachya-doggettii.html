Polystachya doggettii

Polystachya doggettii, commonly known as the Doggett's pinflower, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Polystachya doggettii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
