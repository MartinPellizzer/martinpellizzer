Pentaple

Pentaple, commonly known as the five-leaf plant, is a small herbaceous species native to temperate regions.

It typically features a basal rosette of five leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique leaf arrangement, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
