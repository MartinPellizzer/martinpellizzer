Penstemon caespitosus var. thompsoniae

Penstemon caespitosus var. thompsoniae, commonly known as Thompson's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of blue to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
