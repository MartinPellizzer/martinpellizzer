Punctillaria magnipunctata

Punctillaria magnipunctata, commonly known as the giant punctillaria, is a unique freshwater alga characterized by its intricate and branched structure.

It belongs to the order Punctillariales and is known for its distinctive, needle-like projections that give it a feathery appearance. This plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often found in ponds and lakes with soft, nutrient-rich sediments. Its cellular structure is complex, featuring multiple nuclei and specialized cells for photosynthesis.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an indicator species for certain aquatic ecosystems.
