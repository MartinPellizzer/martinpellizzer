Phaius crinita

Phaius crinita, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive, hairy appearance on its stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
