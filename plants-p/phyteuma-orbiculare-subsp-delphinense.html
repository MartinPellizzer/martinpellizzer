Phyteuma orbiculare subsp. delphinense

Phyteuma orbiculare subsp.

delphinense, commonly known as the Delphinian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
