Polypogon rioplatensis f. robustus

Polypogon rioplatensis f. robustus, commonly known as the robust form of the Rio Plata grass, is a variant of a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and broader leaves compared to the typical form. The plant typically grows in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a clump-forming structure with dense foliage, making it suitable for ornamental use in landscaping.

Its flowering period occurs in the spring, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.
