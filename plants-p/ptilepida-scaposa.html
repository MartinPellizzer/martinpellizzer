Ptilepida scaposa

Ptilepida scaposa, commonly known as the slender scapose orchid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a single, elongated flowering stem.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
