Prunus pseudocerasus ukon

Prunus pseudocerasus ukon, commonly known as the Chinese date or sugar plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are sweet and juicy. The fruits are typically harvested in autumn and are used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
