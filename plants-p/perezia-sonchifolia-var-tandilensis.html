Perezia sonchifolia var. tandilensis

Perezia sonchifolia var. tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features yellow ray flowers and a central disk of tubular florets, typical of the genus Perezia.

Its leaves are generally lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
