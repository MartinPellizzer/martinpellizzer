Phaca exaltata

Phaca exaltata, commonly known as the tall phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in warm, sunny climates. Its flowers are often pink or purple, and they bloom during the summer months.

Phaca exaltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
