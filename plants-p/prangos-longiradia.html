Prangos longiradia

Prangos longiradia, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Prangos longiradia is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display and hardy nature.
