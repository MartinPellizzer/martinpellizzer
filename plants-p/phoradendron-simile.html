Phoradendron simile

Phoradendron simile, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis using sunlight. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds. Its seeds are dispersed through bird droppings, allowing it to spread to new hosts.

Despite its parasitic nature, Phoradendron simile plays a role in ecological systems by supporting bird populations and contributing to forest biodiversity.
