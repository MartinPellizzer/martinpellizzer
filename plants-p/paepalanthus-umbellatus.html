Paepalanthus umbellatus

Paepalanthus umbellatus, commonly known as the white-veined grass.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique appearance and resilience.
