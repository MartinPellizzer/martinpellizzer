Papaver mcconnellii

Papaver mcconnellii, commonly known as the McConnell's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. Its capsule-like seed pods release seeds through a mechanism called explosive dehiscence, aiding in its dispersal.

This species is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
