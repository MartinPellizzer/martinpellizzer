Plantaginella crassifolia

Plantaginella crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and thick, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or white, often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
