Potentilla aucheriana

Potentilla aucheriana, commonly known as the Austrian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in a fine pubescence.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in horticultural landscapes for its ornamental value.
