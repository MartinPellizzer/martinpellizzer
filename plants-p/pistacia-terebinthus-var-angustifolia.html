Pistacia terebinthus var. angustifolia

Pistacia terebinthus var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved turpentine tree, is a variant of the terebinth species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive, fragrant resinous sap. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves and bark contain compounds used in traditional medicine, and it is often found in rocky or arid soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient cultures.
