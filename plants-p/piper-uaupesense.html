Piper uaupesense

Piper uaupesense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Brazilian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in spikes. Its fruits are berries that turn red when ripe, and they contain seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in the biodiversity of the Amazon region.
