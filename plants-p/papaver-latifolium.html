Papaver latifolium

Papaver latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

The broadleaf poppy is a member of the Papaveraceae family, known for its opium-producing latex in some species.
