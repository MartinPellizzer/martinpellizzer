Pleurothallis bupleurifolia

Pleurothallis bupleurifolia, commonly known as the bupleurifoliate pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers and elongated, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is a rare and less commonly cultivated orchid in horticulture.
