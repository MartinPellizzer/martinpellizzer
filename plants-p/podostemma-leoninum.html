Podostemma leoninum

Podostemma leoninum, commonly known as the goldenpod stemless plant, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows near streams and rivers, where it thrives in moist, rocky habitats. The plant has slender, often stemless stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually green and glossy. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract various pollinators.

Podostemma leoninum is notable for its unique adaptation to aquatic environments and its role in local ecosystems.
