Panicum khasianum

Panicum khasianum, commonly known as Khasi panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northeastern regions of India.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
