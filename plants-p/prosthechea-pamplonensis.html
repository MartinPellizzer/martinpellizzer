Prosthechea pamplonensis

Prosthechea pamplonensis, commonly known as the Pamplona orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip, which is a common feature among orchids. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and good light penetration for optimal growth.

Prosthechea pamplonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
