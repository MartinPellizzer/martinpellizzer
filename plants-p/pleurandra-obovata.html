Pleurandra obovata

Pleurandra obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.
