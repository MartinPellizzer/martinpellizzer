Polycarpaea corymbosa var. brasiliensis

Polycarpaea corymbosa var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
