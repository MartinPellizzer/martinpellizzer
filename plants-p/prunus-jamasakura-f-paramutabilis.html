Prunus jamasakura f. paramutabilis

Prunus jamasakura f. paramutabilis, commonly known as the early-flowering cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its early blooming period, typically in late winter or early spring, before the leaves emerge. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a delicate and fragrant aroma. This form is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.

Its growth habit is generally compact, making it suitable for smaller landscapes.
