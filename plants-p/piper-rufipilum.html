Piper rufipilum

Piper rufipilum, commonly known as red-hair pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a tiny berry, turns from green to red as it matures.

Piper rufipilum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
