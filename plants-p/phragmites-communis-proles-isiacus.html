Phragmites communis proles isiacus

Phragmites communis proles isiacus, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and flat, grass-like leaves. This plant is highly adaptable and can grow in a variety of waterlogged soils, making it a common sight in marshes, riverbanks, and coastal areas. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its extensive rhizome system allows it to spread rapidly and form dense stands.
