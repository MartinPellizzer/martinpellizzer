Pleurothallis scalpricaulis

Pleurothallis scalpricaulis, commonly known as the scalpel-leaf orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically small and white or pale green, with intricate, delicate structures.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
