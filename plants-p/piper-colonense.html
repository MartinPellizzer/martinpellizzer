Piper colonense

Piper colonense, commonly known as the Colombian pepper plant, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
