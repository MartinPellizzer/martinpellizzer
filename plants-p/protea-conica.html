Protea conica

Protea conica, commonly known as the conical protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a flower, though it is actually a cluster of individual blooms. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea conica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant, long-lasting floral display.
