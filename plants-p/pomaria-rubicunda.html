Pomaria rubicunda

Pomaria rubicunda, commonly known as the crimson flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and deep orange sepals, which give it its common name. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread across the forest floor. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical gardens.
