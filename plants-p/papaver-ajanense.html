Papaver ajanense

Papaver ajanense, commonly known as the Ajanense poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
