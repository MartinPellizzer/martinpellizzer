Pomaderris phylliraeoides var. nitidula

Pomaderris phylliraeoides var. nitidula, commonly known as the glossy leaf pomaderris, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is glossy and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers in spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical in shape, and have a leathery texture.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
