Peruvocereus albispinus var. roseospinus

Peruvocereus albispinus var. roseospinus, commonly known as the pink-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and a distinctive pink or reddish coloration on its spines. The plant produces white flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is valued both for ornamental purposes and for its ecological role in its native habitat.
