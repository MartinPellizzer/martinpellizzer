Placus barbatus

Placus barbatus, commonly known as the feathered bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland environments.

It possesses specialized bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. The plant's leaves are modified into tendrils that aid in capturing prey. It thrives in nutrient-poor water, using its prey to supplement its nutrient intake.

Its unique morphology and predatory behavior make it an intriguing subject in botanical studies.
