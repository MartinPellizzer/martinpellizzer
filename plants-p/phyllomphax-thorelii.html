Phyllomphax thorelii

Phyllomphax thorelii, commonly known as the Thorell's leafy orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its growth habit is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without being parasitic.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
