Pterophyllus

Pterophyllus, commonly known as the fern-like plant, is a genus of flowering plants characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage.

It belongs to the family Pterophyllaceae and is native to various regions in the Americas. The plants typically exhibit small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale in color. They thrive in moist, shaded environments and are often found in woodland areas.

Due to their ornamental value, Pterophyllus species are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
