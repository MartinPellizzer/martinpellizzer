Primulina depressa

Primulina depressa, commonly known as the dwarf gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features broad, waxy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
