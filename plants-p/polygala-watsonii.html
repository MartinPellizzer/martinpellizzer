Polygala watsonii

Polygala watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom during the warmer months.

Polygala watsonii is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
