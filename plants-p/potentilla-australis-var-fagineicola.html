Potentilla australis var. fagineicola

Potentilla australis var. fagineicola, commonly known as the bog potentilla, is a shrub native to wetland and bog environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in acidic, waterlogged soils and is often found in peat bogs and marshes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
