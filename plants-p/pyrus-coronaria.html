Pyrus coronaria

Pyrus coronaria, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by fruit that ripens in the autumn. The fruit, known as a medlar, has a unique texture and is often used in cooking or fermented into wine.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
