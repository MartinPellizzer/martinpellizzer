Persicaria odorata subsp. macrantha

Persicaria odorata subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as African blueberry or *Ruellia macrantha*, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Polygonaceae family, sharing similar botanical traits.
