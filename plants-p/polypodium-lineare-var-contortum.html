Polypodium lineare var. contortum

Polypodium lineare var. contortum, commonly known as the twisted shield fern, is a variant of the common shield fern, characterized by its distinctive, twisted fronds.

It is a perennial evergreen fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile fronds producing spores on the underside. Its fronds are usually leathery and dark green, with a unique curling or twisting pattern that gives it its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
