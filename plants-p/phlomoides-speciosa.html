Phlomoides speciosa

Phlomoides speciosa, commonly known as the showy phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces striking clusters of purple to pink flowers. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
