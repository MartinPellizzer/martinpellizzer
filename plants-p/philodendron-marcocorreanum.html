Philodendron marcocorreanum

Philodendron marcocorreanum, commonly known as the Marcocorreanum Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, and often variegated leaves that can grow quite large. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
