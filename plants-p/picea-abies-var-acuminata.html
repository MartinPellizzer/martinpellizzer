Picea abies var. acuminata

Picea abies var. acuminata, commonly known as the Norwegian spruce, is a variant of the Norway spruce native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cone structure. The tree thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its needles are typically longer and more pointed compared to the standard Norway spruce.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in horticulture and forestry.
