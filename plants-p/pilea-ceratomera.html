Pilea ceratomera

Pilea ceratomera, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, shield-shaped fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular indoor plant.

Pilea ceratomera is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care, though it can be somewhat slow-growing.
