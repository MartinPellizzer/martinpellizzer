Pimelea baxteri

Pimelea baxteri, commonly known as the Baxter's everlasting, is a low-growing shrub native to Western Australia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in winter and spring, adding a subtle yet striking presence to its habitat. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Pimelea baxteri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and prolonged flowering period.
