Phyllitis scolopendrium

Phyllitis scolopendrium, commonly known as the lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy rhizomes and its distinctive inflorescence of spirally arranged, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, while the flowers are usually pink or purple.

Phyllitis scolopendrium is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
