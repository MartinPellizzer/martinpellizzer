Plantago psyllium var. durandoi

Plantago psyllium var. durandoi, commonly known as the African psyllium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds, which are rich in soluble fiber, are often used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
