Peplis australis var. biflora

Peplis australis var. biflora, commonly known as the Australian peplis, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in coastal or sandy habitats. It is a perennial herb that grows low to the ground, often forming dense mats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
