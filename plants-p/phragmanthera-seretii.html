Phragmanthera seretii

Phragmanthera seretii, commonly known as the Seret's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with translucent, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the dry season, often in shaded, humid environments. Due to its restricted habitat and threatened status, it is considered critically endangered.
