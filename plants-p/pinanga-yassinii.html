Pinanga yassinii

Pinanga yassinii, commonly known as the Yassinii palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
