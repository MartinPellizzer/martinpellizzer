Phebalium brachyphyllum

Phebalium brachyphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peacock wattles, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal scrub.

Phebalium brachyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
