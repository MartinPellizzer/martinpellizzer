Persea fastigiata var. sericea

Persea fastigiata var. sericea, commonly known as the silky evergreen or silky persea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a soft, silvery sheen on the underside of the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Native to tropical regions of Central America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
