Perlebia macrantha subsp. serpae

Perlebia macrantha subsp.

serpae, commonly known as the giant perlebia, is a striking member of the Araceae family native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a broad, waxy spathe.

The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, often with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens or as a houseplant.
