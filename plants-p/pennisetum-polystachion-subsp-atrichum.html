Pennisetum polystachion subsp. atrichum

Pennisetum polystachion subsp.

atrichum, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to the Americas. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy panicles that turn a striking purple color in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract wildlife further enhance its ecological value.
