Prunus jamasakura f. linearisepala

Prunus jamasakura f. linearisepala, commonly known as the linear-sepal cherry, is a unique variant of the Japanese cherry species.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow sepals that give it a distinct appearance compared to typical cherry blossoms. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Japan, where it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, producing delicate pink or white petals.

The linear sepals are a notable botanical feature, contributing to its classification as a floral form within the Prunus genus.
