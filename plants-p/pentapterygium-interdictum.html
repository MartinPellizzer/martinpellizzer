Pentapterygium interdictum

Pentapterygium interdictum, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and intriguing species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant has a unique reproductive structure, with sporangia arranged in a distinctive pentagonal pattern on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pentapterygium interdictum is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
