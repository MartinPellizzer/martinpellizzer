Plantago sericea subsp. perreymondii

Plantago sericea subsp.

perreymondii, commonly known as the silky plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
