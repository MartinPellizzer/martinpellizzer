Polianthes cernua

Polianthes cernua, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Polianthes cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
