Persea microcarpa

Persea microcarpa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and dense foliage.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, honey-like flavor.
