Phymatopteris connexa

Phymatopteris connexa, commonly known as the Arizona maidenhair fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its rhizomes are short and thick, allowing it to anchor itself in crevices and withstand periods of drought.

The spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in challenging habitats.
