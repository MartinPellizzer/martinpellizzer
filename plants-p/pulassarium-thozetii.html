Pulassarium thozetii

Pulassarium thozetii, commonly known as the Zambesi lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in shallow freshwater environments, often found in lakes and rivers. Its leaves are long and ribbon-like, floating on the water's surface.

Pulassarium thozetii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
