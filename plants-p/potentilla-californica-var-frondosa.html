Potentilla californica var. frondosa

Potentilla californica var. frondosa, commonly known as the frondose potentilla, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
