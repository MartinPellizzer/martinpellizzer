Prasopepon

Prasopepon, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically alternate along the stem. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a elongated, spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Prasopepon is often found in moist, tropical regions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
