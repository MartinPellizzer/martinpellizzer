Pandanus rivularis

Pandanus rivularis, commonly known as the river pandanus, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found near rivers and in coastal areas.

The fruit, which is a spiky, fibrous structure, is edible and used in local cuisines.
