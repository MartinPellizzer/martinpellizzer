Plumeria multiflora

Plumeria multiflora, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are borne in clusters and bloom during the dry season, often lasting for several weeks.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
