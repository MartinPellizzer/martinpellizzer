Piper tenuipedunculum

Piper tenuipedunculum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, upright stature. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a glossy surface, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Piper tenuipedunculum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flavor profile.
