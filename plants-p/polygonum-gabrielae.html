Polygonum gabrielae

Polygonum gabrielae, commonly known as Gabriel's knotweed, is a species of flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features upright, somewhat woody stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
