Phaseolus striatus

Phaseolus striatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant orange to red flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary use. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaseolus striatus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.
