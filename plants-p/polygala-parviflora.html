Polygala parviflora

Polygala parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in slender racemes, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, narrow leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
