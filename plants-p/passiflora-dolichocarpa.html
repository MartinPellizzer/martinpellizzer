Passiflora dolichocarpa

Passiflora dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are typically consumed raw or used in jams and beverages. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Passiflora dolichocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
