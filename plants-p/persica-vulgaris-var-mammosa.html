Persica vulgaris var. mammosa

Persica vulgaris var. mammosa, commonly known as the mammoth peach.

This variant is characterized by its exceptionally large fruit, often weighing several pounds, with thick, fuzzy skin and a sweet, juicy flesh. It is a cultivar of the domesticated peach, known for its vigorous growth and heavy cropping. The plant typically features broad, ovate leaves and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring.

It is well-suited for warmer climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
