Pharus angustifolius

Pharus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pharus, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
