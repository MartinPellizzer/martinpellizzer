Prunus virginiana monstr. monstrosifolia

Prunus virginiana monstr.

monstrosifolia, commonly known as the scarlet flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which turns vibrant shades of orange and crimson in the fall.

The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are popular among birds and small mammals. Its unique, often twisted growth habit gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
