Piptospatha burbidgei

Piptospatha burbidgei, commonly known as the Burbridge's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
