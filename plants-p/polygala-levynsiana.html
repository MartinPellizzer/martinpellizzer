Polygala levynsiana

Polygala levynsiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bluish-white flowers that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
