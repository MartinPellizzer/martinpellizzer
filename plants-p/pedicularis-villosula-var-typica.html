Pedicularis villosula var. typica

Pedicularis villosula var. typica, commonly known as the woolly monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments, typically found in forests or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species within the genus Pedicularis.
