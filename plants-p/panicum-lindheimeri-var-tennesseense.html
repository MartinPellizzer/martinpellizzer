Panicum lindheimeri var. tennesseense

Panicum lindheimeri var. tennesseense, commonly known as the Tennessee dropseed, is a native grass species found in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces delicate, feathery seed heads that add ornamental value to landscapes. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a low-maintenance plant that is drought-tolerant once established.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
