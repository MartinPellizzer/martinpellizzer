Prunus apetala var. pilosa

Prunus apetala var. pilosa, commonly known as the downy peach.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, characterized by its hairy, downy leaves and pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not eaten by humans but are used in traditional medicine. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other peach and apricot species.

Its unique fuzzy texture and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
