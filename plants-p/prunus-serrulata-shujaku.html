Prunus serrulata shujaku

Prunus serrulata shujaku, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a breathtaking display. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and it produces small, oval fruits called drupes.

The tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in Japanese traditions.
