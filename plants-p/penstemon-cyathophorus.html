Penstemon cyathophorus

Penstemon cyathophorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon cyathophorus is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
