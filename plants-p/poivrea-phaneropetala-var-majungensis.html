Poivrea phaneropetala var. majungensis

Poivrea phaneropetala var. majungensis, commonly known as the Majungensis honeysuckle, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens.
