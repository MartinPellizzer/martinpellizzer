Premna foetida

Premna foetida, commonly known as stinking nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and emit a strong, unpleasant odor. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Premna foetida is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
