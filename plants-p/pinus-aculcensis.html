Pinus aculcensis

Pinus aculcensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The cones of Pinus aculcensis are woody and produce edible seeds that are dispersed by wind. This plant thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
