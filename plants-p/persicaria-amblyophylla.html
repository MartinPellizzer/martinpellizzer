Persicaria amblyophylla

Persicaria amblyophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, making it both a persistent weed and a valuable plant in ecological restoration.
