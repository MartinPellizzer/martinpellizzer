Paraphlomis javanica f. albinervia

Paraphlomis javanica f. albinervia, commonly known as the white-veined Paraphlomis, is a variant of the Paraphlomis javanica species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with distinctive white or pale veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in loose spikes, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture due to its unique leaf patterns and adaptability to humid conditions.
