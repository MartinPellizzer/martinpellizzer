Poa longiligula var. wyomingensis

Poa longiligula var. wyomingensis, commonly known as the Wyoming slender grass, is a variant of the slender grass species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hair-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
