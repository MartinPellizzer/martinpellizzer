Plinia espinhacensis

Plinia espinhacensis, commonly known as the Espinhacense myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest biome in eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically small, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

Due to habitat loss, Plinia espinhacensis is considered endangered and is of interest for conservation and botanical research.
