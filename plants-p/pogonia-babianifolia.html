Pogonia babianifolia

Pogonia babianifolia, commonly known as the Babianifolia Pinkroot, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and striking, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a complex root system that plays a crucial role in its ability to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soils in which it grows. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Pogonia babianifolia is considered endangered, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
