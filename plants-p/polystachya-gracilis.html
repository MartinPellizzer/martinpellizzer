Polystachya gracilis

Polystachya gracilis, commonly known as the slender spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces racemes of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystachya gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
