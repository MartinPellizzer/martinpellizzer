Piper arborescens var. angustilimbum

Piper arborescens var. angustilimbum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
