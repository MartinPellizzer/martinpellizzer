Pappobolus matthewsii var. viridifolius

Pappobolus matthewsii var. viridifolius, commonly known as the greenleaf pappobolus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green in color, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dryland landscapes.
