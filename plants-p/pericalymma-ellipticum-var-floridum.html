Pericalymma ellipticum var. floridum

Pericalymma ellipticum var. floridum, commonly known as the Florida pericalymma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with showy, pinkish-white petals. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
