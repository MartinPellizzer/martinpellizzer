Pavonia spinigera

Pavonia spinigera, commonly known as the spiny pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy pink to purple flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia spinigera is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
