Passiflora leptopoda

Passiflora leptopoda, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves and striking, fragrant flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
