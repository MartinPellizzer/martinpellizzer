Psephellus kemulariae

Psephellus kemulariae, commonly known as the Kemularia sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
