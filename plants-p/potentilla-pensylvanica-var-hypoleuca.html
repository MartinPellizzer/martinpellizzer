Potentilla pensylvanica var. hypoleuca

Potentilla pensylvanica var. hypoleuca, commonly known as white potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.
