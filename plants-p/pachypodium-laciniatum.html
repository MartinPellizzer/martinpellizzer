Pachypodium laciniatum

Pachypodium laciniatum, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a swollen, bottle-shaped stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant has spiny, segmented stems and produces clusters of small, white flowers during the dry season. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in full sun with minimal water.

Pachypodium laciniatum is well adapted to its native environment, showcasing unique botanical characteristics typical of desert flora.
