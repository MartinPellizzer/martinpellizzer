Platychaete aucheri

Platychaete aucheri, commonly known as the narrowleaf arnica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

Platychaete aucheri is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
