Pelma bolaninum

Pelma bolaninum, commonly known as the Bolan mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in high-altitude environments.

Pelma bolaninum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
