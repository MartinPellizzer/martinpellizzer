Polygonum convolvulus var. ciliinode

Polygonum convolvulus var. ciliinode, commonly known as the hairy bladder campion, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and typically have ciliate margins, giving it its varietal name. The flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and feature five petals that are pink to purple in color.

This plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
