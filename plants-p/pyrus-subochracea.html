Pyrus subochracea

Pyrus subochracea, commonly known as the white-leafed pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to white leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential in horticultural breeding programs.
