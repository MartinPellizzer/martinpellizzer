Pennisetum vahlii

Pennisetum vahlii, commonly known as the African fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces dense, fluffy plumes of light brown to white flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pennisetum vahlii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
