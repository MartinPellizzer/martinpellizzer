Panicum fasciculatum

Panicum fasciculatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and restoration projects.
