Penstemon rubescens

Penstemon rubescens, commonly known as the red beard plant, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant red to pinkish-purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Penstemon rubescens is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
