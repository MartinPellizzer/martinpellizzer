Pseudotsuga douglasii pumila

Pseudotsuga douglasii pumila, commonly known as the dwarf mountain hemlock, is a shrub-like variant of the hemlock tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and short, dark green needles. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in moist, cool climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its slow growth and unique form make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
