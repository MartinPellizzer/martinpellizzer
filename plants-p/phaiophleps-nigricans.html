Phaiophleps nigricans

Phaiophleps nigricans, commonly known as the black-stemmed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green to blackish stems, which are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the understory of forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
