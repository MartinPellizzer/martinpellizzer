Piper tiliifolium var. paulownifolium

Piper tiliifolium var. paulownifolium, commonly known as the Pacific Island pepper or sweet pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by oval, green berries that turn red when mature. The berries are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

This plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and ornamental value.
