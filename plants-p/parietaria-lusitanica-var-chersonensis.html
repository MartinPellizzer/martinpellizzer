Parietaria lusitanica var. chersonensis

Parietaria lusitanica var. chersonensis, commonly known as the Chersonese wallcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides, in waste areas, and near human settlements. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many plants with medicinal and agricultural significance.
