Phlox douglasii subsp. andicola

Phlox douglasii subsp.

andicola, commonly known as the Andicola phlox, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
