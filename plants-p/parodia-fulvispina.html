Parodia fulvispina

Parodia fulvispina, commonly known as the yellow-spined cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Parodia fulvispina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
