Piper papilliferum

Piper papilliferum, commonly known as the papillate pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its finely serrated, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its unique leaf structure, with tiny papillae on the surface, gives it its scientific name.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
