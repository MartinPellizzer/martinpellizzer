Potentilla × burjatica var. laeta

Potentilla × burjatica var. laeta, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.

The leaves are typically dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
