Phaseolus surinamensis

Phaseolus surinamensis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and vigorous growth habit. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
