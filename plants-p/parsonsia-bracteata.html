Parsonsia bracteata

Parsonsia bracteata, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in clusters during the holiday season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Parsonsia bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
