Poa pratensis var. eragrostiformis

Poa pratensis var. eragrostiformis, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. This grass species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
