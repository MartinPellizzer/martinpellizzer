Polymnia crenata

Polymnia crenata, commonly known as the wavyleaf polymnia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, wavy-edged leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polymnia crenata is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
