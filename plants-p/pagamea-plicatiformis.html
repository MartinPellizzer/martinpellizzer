Pagamea plicatiformis

Pagamea plicatiformis, commonly known as the pleated leaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pleated leaves that give it its common name, and it produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
