Pyrus hubeiana

Pyrus hubeiana, commonly known as the Hubei pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
