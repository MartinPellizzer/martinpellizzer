Phoebe hypoleuca

Phoebe hypoleuca, commonly known as the white phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in shaded forest understories. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in late spring.

The fruit is a small, dark blue drupe that contains a single seed.
