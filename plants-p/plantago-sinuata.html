Plantago sinuata

Plantago sinuata, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with wavy edges, which are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes that rise from the center of the rosette. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
