Panicum papposum

Panicum papposum, commonly known as the silky wild millet, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and panicles that are covered with silky, white hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are wind-pollinated, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Panicum papposum thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
