Plectorrhiza erecta

Plectorrhiza erecta, commonly known as the upright root plant, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often branching root system that can extend deep into the soil. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique root morphology and ecological adaptations.
