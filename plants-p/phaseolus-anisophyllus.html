Phaseolus anisophyllus

Phaseolus anisophyllus, commonly known as the winged bean, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged pods that enclose edible seeds and beans. The plant produces compound leaves with a trifoliate structure, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds and foliage.

The species is valued for its high protein content and adaptability to various soil conditions.
