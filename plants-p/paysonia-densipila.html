Paysonia densipila

Paysonia densipila, commonly known as the dense-hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, dense hairs that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Paysonia densipila is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
