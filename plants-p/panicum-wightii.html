Panicum wightii

Panicum wightii, commonly known as Wight's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including India and Nepal.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a slight sheen. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Panicum wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
