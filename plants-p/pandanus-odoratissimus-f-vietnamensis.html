Pandanus odoratissimus f. vietnamensis

Pandanus odoratissimus f. vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, spirally arranged leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as roofing material. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, adapting well to saline and waterlogged soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
