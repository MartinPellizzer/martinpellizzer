Pera frutescens

Pera frutescens, commonly known as the red-flowered pera, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
