Pimpinella manzanedoi

Pimpinella manzanedoi, commonly known as the Manzaneda pepper plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and small, yellowish flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with a fern-like appearance. Its fruit, which is a small, dry schizocarp, contains two mericarps that split open upon maturity.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional Mexican cuisine.
