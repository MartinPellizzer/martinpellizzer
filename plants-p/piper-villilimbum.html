Piper villilimbum

Piper villilimbum, commonly known as the "wild pepper" or "sour pepper," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small, round berry, turns from green to red when mature and is often used in traditional medicine.

Piper villilimbum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various herbal remedies.
