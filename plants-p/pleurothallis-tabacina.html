Pleurothallis tabacina

Pleurothallis tabacina, commonly known as the tobacco orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Pleurothallis tabacina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
