Pandanus piricus

Pandanus piricus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into spiny, triangular fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing in sandy or limestone soils.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it an important species in the region's flora.
