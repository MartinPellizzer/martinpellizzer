Phaseoloides leiogynum

Phaseoloides leiogynum, commonly known as the smoothleaf phaseoloid, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features trifoliate leaves with smooth, rounded leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink. Its fruit is a legume, containing several seeds that are typically dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
