Piper gracilipes

Piper gracilipes, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, while its fruits are three-lobed berries that mature to a dark purple color.

Piper gracilipes is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
