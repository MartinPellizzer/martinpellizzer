Pyrola incarnata

Pyrola incarnata, commonly known as the pink wintergreen, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen foliage. The plant produces striking pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and remain green throughout the year, providing winter interest.

Pyrola incarnata thrives in acidic, moist soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
