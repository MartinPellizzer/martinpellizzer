Pancheria alaternoides var. lanceolata

Pancheria alaternoides var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate pancheria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environmental conditions.
