Peronocactus neohorstii

Peronocactus neohorstii, commonly known as the Mexican golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick, cylindrical stem that is covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing succulent that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
