Pauridiantha pyramidata var. velutina

Pauridiantha pyramidata var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet pyramid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its unique morphology makes it a sought-after species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

The variety velutina is distinguished by its fine, soft hairs covering the leaves and stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
