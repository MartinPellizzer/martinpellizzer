Potentilla saximontana

Potentilla saximontana, commonly known as saximon tangleweed, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
