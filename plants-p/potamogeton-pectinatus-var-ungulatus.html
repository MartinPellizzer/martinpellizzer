Potamogeton pectinatus var. ungulatus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. ungulatus, commonly known as the horned pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomes are robust and help it establish dense underwater colonies.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
