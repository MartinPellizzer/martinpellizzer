Pyrethrum neglectum

Pyrethrum neglectum, commonly known as the wild pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals. Its foliage is typically hairy and aromatic, contributing to its natural pest-repelling properties.

Pyrethrum neglectum is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and potential insecticidal uses.
