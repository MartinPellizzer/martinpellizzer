Pleurothallis trachychlamys

Pleurothallis trachychlamys, commonly known as the woolly pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique morphology includes a hairy stem and bracts that give it its common name.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
