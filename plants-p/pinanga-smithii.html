Pinanga smithii

Pinanga smithii, commonly known as the Smith's bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, broad leaves that can reach up to several meters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga smithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
