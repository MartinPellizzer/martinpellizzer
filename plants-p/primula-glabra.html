Primula glabra

Primula glabra, commonly known as the smooth primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Primula glabra is also known for its ability to thrive in cool climates and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
