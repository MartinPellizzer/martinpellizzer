Pomaderris paniculosa subsp. novae-zelandiae

Pomaderris paniculosa subsp.

novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand pomaderris, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native insect populations.
