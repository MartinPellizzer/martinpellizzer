Phlebochilus exstans

Phlebochilus exstans, commonly known as the slender tansy, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are covered in tiny, glandular hairs.

Phlebochilus exstans is native to the Mediterranean region and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
