Primula senanensis

Primula senanensis, commonly known as the Senan's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
