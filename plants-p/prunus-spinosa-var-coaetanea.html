Prunus spinosa var. coaetanea

Prunus spinosa var. coaetanea, commonly known as the common blackthorn, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which ripen in late summer. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are densely covered with sharp spines. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sunny conditions.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and can be fermented to make alcoholic beverages.
