Paspalum micranthum

Paspalum micranthum, commonly known as dwarf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in areas with abundant moisture. The plant has slender, spreading stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Paspalum micranthum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
