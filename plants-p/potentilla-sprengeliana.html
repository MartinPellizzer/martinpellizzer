Potentilla sprengeliana

Potentilla sprengeliana, commonly known as the Sprengel's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
