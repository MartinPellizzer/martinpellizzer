Phalaris canariensis var. villosula

Phalaris canariensis var. villosula, commonly known as the Canary grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-green foliage due to the dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in Mediterranean climates.
