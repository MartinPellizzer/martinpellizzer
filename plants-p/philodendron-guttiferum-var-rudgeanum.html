Philodendron guttiferum var. rudgeanum

Philodendron guttiferum var. rudgeanum, commonly known as the Rudge's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal.
