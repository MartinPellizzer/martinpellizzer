Phacelia distans var. scabrella

Phacelia distans var. scabrella, commonly known as the rough phacelia, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The species produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and attract pollinators.
