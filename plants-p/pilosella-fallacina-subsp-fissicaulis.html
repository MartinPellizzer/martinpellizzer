Pilosella fallacina subsp. fissicaulis

Pilosella fallacina subsp.

fissicaulis, commonly known as the fissured leaf pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, fissured leaves that are typically hairy and grayish-green in color. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.
