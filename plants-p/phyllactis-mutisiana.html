Phyllactis mutisiana

Phyllactis mutisiana, commonly known as the Mexican cactus apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. It produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
