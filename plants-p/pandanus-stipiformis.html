Pandanus stipiformis

Pandanus stipiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal environments.
