Pulsatilla chinensis

Pulsatilla chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-centered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, upright stem with few leaves, and its flowers are typically surrounded by a crown of bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Pulsatilla chinensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
