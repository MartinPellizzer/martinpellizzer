Paramicropholis acutangula

Paramicropholis acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-pointed fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a thin layer of fine hairs. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Paramicropholis acutangula make it a unique and interesting subject for study in tropical botany.
