Panicum phragmitoides var. lasioneuron

Panicum phragmitoides var. lasioneuron, commonly known as the narrow-leaved panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically pale green to brown.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
