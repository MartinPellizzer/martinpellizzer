Pellaea jonesii

Pellaea jonesii, commonly known as Jones' maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive, glossy appearance and a dark green color.

Pellaea jonesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
