Polychilos violacea

Polychilos violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

The flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.
