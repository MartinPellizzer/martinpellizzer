Prunus myrtifolia var. glaziovii

Prunus myrtifolia var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's cherry, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, cherry-like fruits. The plant typically grows in shrub form, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface, and it produces clusters of white to pink flowers in spring.

The fruit is edible and has a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
