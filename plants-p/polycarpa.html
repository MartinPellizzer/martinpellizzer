Polycarpa

Polycarpa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Polycarpa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
