Pelargonium phymatanthoides

Pelargonium phymatanthoides, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
