Polypodium hirsutissimum var. sericeum

Polypodium hirsutissimum var. sericeum, commonly known as the silky cliff fern, is a variant of the hairy polypody, characterized by its finely haired fronds.

It is a terrestrial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or along stream banks. The fronds are typically lance-shaped with a serrated margin and a distinctive silvery sheen due to the dense covering of fine hairs. This variety is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, where it grows in humid, temperate climates.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
