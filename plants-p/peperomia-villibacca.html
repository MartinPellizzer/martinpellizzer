Peperomia villibacca

Peperomia villibacca, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture, often adorned with intricate patterns or variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually green or reddish, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
