Pyrus amphigenea dominii

Pyrus amphigenea dominii, commonly known as the Spanish pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellow-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, followed by small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and ecological significance.
