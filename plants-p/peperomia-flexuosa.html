Peperomia flexuosa

Peperomia flexuosa, commonly known as the curl-leaf peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and waxy, glossy leaves that often curl at the edges. The leaves are typically dark green with a subtle sheen, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia flexuosa is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through stem cuttings or division.
