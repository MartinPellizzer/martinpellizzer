Pseuderanthemum lilacinum

Pseuderanthemum lilacinum, commonly known as the purple false heather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Pseuderanthemum lilacinum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
