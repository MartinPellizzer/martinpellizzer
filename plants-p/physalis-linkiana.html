Physalis linkiana

Physalis linkiana, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruits that enclose bright yellow berries. These fruits are typically harvested when they turn a deep orange-red color.

Physalis linkiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which have a tart, tangy flavor.
