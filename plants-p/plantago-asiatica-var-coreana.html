Plantago asiatica var. coreana

Plantago asiatica var. coreana, commonly known as Korean plantain, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
