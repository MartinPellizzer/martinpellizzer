Persea gratissima var. schiedeana

Persea gratissima var. schiedeana, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe, containing astringent tannins that soften as the fruit matures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its aromatic wood and traditional medicinal uses.
