Phyteuma angustifolium

Phyteuma angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
