Pinus taeda var. variabilis

Pinus taeda var. variabilis, commonly known as the loblolly pine, is a variant of the loblolly pine species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits variations in growth form and foliage, which contribute to its adaptability to different soil and climate conditions. It plays a significant role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability in various industrial applications.
