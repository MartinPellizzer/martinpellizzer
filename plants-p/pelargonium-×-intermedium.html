Pelargonium × intermedium

Pelargonium × intermedium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers due to its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.

Pelargonium × intermedium is relatively low maintenance, thriving in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
