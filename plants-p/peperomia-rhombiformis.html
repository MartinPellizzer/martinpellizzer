Peperomia rhombiformis

Peperomia rhombiformis, commonly known as the rhomboid peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features upright, branching stems with thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, often white or pale pink, which are followed by small, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia rhombiformis is valued for its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities.
