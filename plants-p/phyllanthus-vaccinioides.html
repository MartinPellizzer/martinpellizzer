Phyllanthus vaccinioides

Phyllanthus vaccinioides, commonly known as the African star apple or wild coffee, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
