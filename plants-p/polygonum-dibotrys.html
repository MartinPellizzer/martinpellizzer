Polygonum dibotrys

Polygonum dibotrys, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
