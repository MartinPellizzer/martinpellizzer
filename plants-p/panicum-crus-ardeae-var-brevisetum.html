Panicum crus-ardeae var. brevisetum

Panicum crus-ardeae var. brevisetum, commonly known as the short-bracted panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in open, grassy habitats and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to dry and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
