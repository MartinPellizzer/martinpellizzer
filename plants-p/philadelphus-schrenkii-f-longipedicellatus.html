Philadelphus schrenkii f. longipedicellatus

Philadelphus schrenkii f. longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-peduncled mock orange, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its pleasant fragrance.
