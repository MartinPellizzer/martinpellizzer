Plectranthus densus

Plectranthus densus, commonly known as the dense plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, which are typically arranged in whorls along the stems.

Plectranthus densus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its compact form and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
