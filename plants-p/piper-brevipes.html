Piper brevipes

Piper brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered leaves. It produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
