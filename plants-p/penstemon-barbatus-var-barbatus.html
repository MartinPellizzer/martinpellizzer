Penstemon barbatus var. barbatus

Penstemon barbatus var. barbatus, commonly known as the beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, dark-colored seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Penstemon barbatus var. barbatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
