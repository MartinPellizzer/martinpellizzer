Pityrodia uncinata

Pityrodia uncinata, commonly known as the hooked pityrodia, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands.

Pityrodia uncinata is adapted to dry conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
