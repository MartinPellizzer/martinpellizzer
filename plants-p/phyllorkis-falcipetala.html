Phyllorkis falcipetala

Phyllorkis falcipetala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, humid environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its blooming period occurs during the spring and early summer, producing clusters of small, colorful blossoms.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
