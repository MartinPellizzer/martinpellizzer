Pulicaria dumulosa

Pulicaria dumulosa, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Pulicaria dumulosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
