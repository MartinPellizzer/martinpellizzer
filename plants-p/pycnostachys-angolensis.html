Pycnostachys angolensis

Pycnostachys angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan flame tree, is a tropical tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. Its bark is smooth and often fissured, and the wood is valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal.

Pycnostachys angolensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
