Petunia integrifolia subsp. depauperata

Petunia integrifolia subsp.

depauperata, commonly known as the slender petunia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The leaves are typically ovate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact size and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
