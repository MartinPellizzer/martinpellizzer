Peltogyne catingae subsp. catingae

Peltogyne catingae subsp.

catingae, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is native to northeastern Brazil and is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, reddish hue.

The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in racemes.

The species is highly valued for its timber, which is prized in the production of musical instruments and fine furniture.
