Pelargonium × polytrichum

Pelargonium × polytrichum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, slightly serrated leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in gardens and landscaping.
