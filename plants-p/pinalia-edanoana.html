Pinalia edanoana

Pinalia edanoana, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that radiate from a central stem, giving it a star-shaped appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically a vibrant green, with a slightly waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Pinalia edanoana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
