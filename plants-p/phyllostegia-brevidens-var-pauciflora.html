Phyllostegia brevidens var. pauciflora

Phyllostegia brevidens var. pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its opposite, narrow leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The species is notable for its seasonal flowering, typically occurring during the rainy season. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
