Prunus tardeflorens

Prunus tardeflorens, commonly known as the late-flowering cherry, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom later in the spring compared to other cherry species. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically edible but not widely consumed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The botanical features of Prunus tardeflorens make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
