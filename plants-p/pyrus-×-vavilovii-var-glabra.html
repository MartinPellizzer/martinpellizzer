Pyrus × vavilovii var. glabra

Pyrus × vavilovii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth pear, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to common pears. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

This variety is also valued for its cold hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
