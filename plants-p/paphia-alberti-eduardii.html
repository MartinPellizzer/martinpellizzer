Paphia alberti-eduardii

Paphia alberti-eduardii, commonly known as the Alberti-Eduardii paphiopedilum, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers with intricate patterns that resemble lace. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
