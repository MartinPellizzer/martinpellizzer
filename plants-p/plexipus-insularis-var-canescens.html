Plexipus insularis var. canescens

Plexipus insularis var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf plexipus, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific island habitats.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, hair-like trichomes. The plant typically grows in well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in coastal or limestone-rich environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of pale yellow or green.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
