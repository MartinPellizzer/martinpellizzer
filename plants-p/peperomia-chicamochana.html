Peperomia chicamochana

Peperomia chicamochana, commonly known as the Andean peperomia, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often display a variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate light conditions.

Peperomia chicamochana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to indoor environments.
