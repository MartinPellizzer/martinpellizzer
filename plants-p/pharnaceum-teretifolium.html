Pharnaceum teretifolium

Pharnaceum teretifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaf pharnaceum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
