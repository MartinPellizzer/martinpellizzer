Pleioblastus variegatus var. akebono

Pleioblastus variegatus var. akebono, commonly known as the Akebono bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It features slender, gracefully arching stems with a distinctive variegated foliage, often displaying shades of green, white, and yellow. The leaves are narrow and have a soft, leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial to full shade and is prized for its compact size and year-round visual interest.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic value and ease of care in garden settings.
