Prestonia vallis

Prestonia vallis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
