Phinaea caripensis

Phinaea caripensis, commonly known as the Caribbean bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in coastal areas. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Phinaea caripensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
