Pleurothallis genychila

Pleurothallis genychila, commonly known as the white-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree bark or in the understory of forests. Its flowers are usually short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
