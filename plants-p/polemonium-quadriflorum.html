Polemonium quadriflorum

Polemonium quadriflorum, commonly known as the four-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, showy blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
