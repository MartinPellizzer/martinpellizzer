Polygonum bioritauense

Polygonum bioritauense, commonly known as the Bioritauense knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in non-native environments.
