Panicum leiophyllum

Panicum leiophyllum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Silky panicgrass plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
