Pyrus thimphuensis

Pyrus thimphuensis, commonly known as the Bhutanese pear, is a species native to the Himalayan region, particularly Bhutan.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in forested areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

The botanical characteristics of Pyrus thimphuensis make it an important species for both ecological and potential horticultural use in its native habitat.
