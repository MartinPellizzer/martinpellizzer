Piptocoma acuminata

Piptocoma acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators such as bees and birds.
