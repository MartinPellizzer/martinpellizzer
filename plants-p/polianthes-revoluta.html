Polianthes revoluta

Polianthes revoluta, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Polianthes revoluta is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early summer.
