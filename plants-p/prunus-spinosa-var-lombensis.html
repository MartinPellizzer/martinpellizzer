Prunus spinosa var. lombensis

Prunus spinosa var. lombensis, commonly known as the Lombardy cherry, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark red berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland edges and rocky soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces pinkish-white flowers in early spring.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
