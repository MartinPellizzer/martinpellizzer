Photinia arguta var. villosa

Photinia arguta var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly photinia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southeastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and thick, woolly grayish-white pubescence on the stems and leaves. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a leathery texture and a glossy dark green surface. It produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter.

This plant is well-suited for use as a hedge or specimen plant in gardens due to its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
