Pentace macrophylla

Pentace macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved pentace, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Pentaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on long peduncles. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
