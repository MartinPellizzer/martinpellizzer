Porana racemosa var. violacea

Porana racemosa var. violacea, commonly known as the violet raceme.

This plant is a flowering species native to certain regions of Asia, characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens with adequate drainage.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
