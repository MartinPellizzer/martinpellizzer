Potentilla dascia

Potentilla dascia, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, mat-forming habit, typically reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla dascia thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
