Piper cujabanum

Piper cujabanum, commonly known as the Cujabanum pepper or wild long pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, elongated berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface and a strong, peppery scent.

Piper cujabanum is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
