Philodendron herthae

Philodendron herthae, commonly known as the Herth's philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
