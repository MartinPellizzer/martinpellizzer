Primula nepalensis

Primula nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in mountain forests and meadows. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a fine, silvery down, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Primula nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
