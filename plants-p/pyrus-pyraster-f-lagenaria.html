Pyrus pyraster f. lagenaria

Pyrus pyraster f. lagenaria, commonly known as the large-fruited pear, is a cultivated form of the European pear.

It is characterized by its notably larger and more elongated fruits compared to the typical species. The plant is a deciduous tree with a spreading habit and can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is typically yellowish-green when mature and is valued for its culinary and ornamental qualities.
