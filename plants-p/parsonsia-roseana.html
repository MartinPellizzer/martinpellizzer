Parsonsia roseana

Parsonsia roseana, commonly known as the golden torch or paradise flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that resemble torches. The plant features showy, tubular flowers with prominent stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Apocynaceae family. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Parsonsia roseana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
