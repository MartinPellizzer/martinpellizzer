Potamogeton × lintonii

Potamogeton × lintonii, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is a natural hybrid between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton praelongus, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Potamogeton × lintonii plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
