Pyrethrum hispidum

Pyrethrum hispidum, commonly known as the woolly pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces fine, hairy leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and they bloom in the summer months.

Pyrethrum hispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential insecticidal properties.
