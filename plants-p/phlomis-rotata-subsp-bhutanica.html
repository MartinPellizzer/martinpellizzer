Phlomis rotata subsp. bhutanica

Phlomis rotata subsp.

bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and adjacent areas. It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower clusters in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and aromatic, with a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.
