Potentilla pensylvanica var. typica

Potentilla pensylvanica var. typica, commonly known as Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
