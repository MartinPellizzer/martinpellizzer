Piper guapense

Piper guapense, commonly known as the Guapense pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, slender fruits that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
