Prunus vitrea

Prunus vitrea, commonly known as the white sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
