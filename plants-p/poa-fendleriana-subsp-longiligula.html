Poa fendleriana subsp. longiligula

Poa fendleriana subsp.

longiligula, commonly known as the long ligule grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves have a distinctive long ligule, which is a feature that helps in identifying the species.

This grass is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
