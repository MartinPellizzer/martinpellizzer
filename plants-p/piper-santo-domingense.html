Piper santo-domingense

Piper santo-domingense, commonly known as the Santo Domingo pepper, is a tropical vine native to the Caribbean, particularly the island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the warm, moist climate of its native region.
