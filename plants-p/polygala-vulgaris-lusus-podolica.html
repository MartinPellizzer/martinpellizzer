Polygala vulgaris lusus podolica

Polygala vulgaris lusus podolica, commonly known as the Podolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
