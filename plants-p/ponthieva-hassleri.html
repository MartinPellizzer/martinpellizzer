Ponthieva hassleri

Ponthieva hassleri, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of dark green, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically in the spring and summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ponthieva hassleri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
