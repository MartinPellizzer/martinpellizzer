Podolepis gracilis f. superba

Podolepis gracilis f. superba, commonly known as the slender podolepis.

It is a rare and diminutive fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are usually less than 30 centimeters in length. Its rhizomes are wiry and fibrous, contributing to its ability to anchor itself in rocky or loamy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often sought after by enthusiasts of rare flora.
