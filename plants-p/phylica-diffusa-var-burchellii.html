Phylica diffusa var. burchellii

Phylica diffusa var. burchellii, commonly known as the silver bush or Burchell's silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
