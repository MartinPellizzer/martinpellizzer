Pitcairnia aurea

Pitcairnia aurea, commonly known as the golden pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spathaceous inflorescence that displays a range of colors from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant features a rosette of grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in the soil, while its aerial roots help in nutrient absorption.

Pitcairnia aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
