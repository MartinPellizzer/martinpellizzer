Potentilla peduncularis var. elongata

Potentilla peduncularis var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
