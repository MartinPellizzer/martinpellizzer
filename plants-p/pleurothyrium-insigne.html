Pleurothyrium insigne

Pleurothyrium insigne, commonly known as the golden wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
