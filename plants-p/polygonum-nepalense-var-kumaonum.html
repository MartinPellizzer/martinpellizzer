Polygonum nepalense var. kumaonum

Polygonum nepalense var. kumaonum, commonly known as the Kumaon knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and are often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually greenish-white in color.
