Pilosella pseudogalliciana

Pilosella pseudogalliciana, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella pseudogalliciana is often found in meadows and open slopes at high altitudes.
