Polypodium leuconeuron var. latifolium

Polypodium leuconeuron var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, delicate appearance.

This variant is noted for its broader leaves compared to the typical variety, making it a desirable ornamental plant.
