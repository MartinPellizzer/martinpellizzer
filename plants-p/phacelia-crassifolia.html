Phacelia crassifolia

Phacelia crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved phacelia, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Boraginaceae family, which is known for its hairy stems and showy flowers.
