Polanisia uniglandulosa

Polanisia uniglandulosa, commonly known as the single-gland polanisia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
