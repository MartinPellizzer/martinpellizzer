Peperomia hispiduliformis

Peperomia hispiduliformis, commonly known as the woolly peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, velvety foliage with a distinctive grayish-green hue, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia hispiduliformis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
