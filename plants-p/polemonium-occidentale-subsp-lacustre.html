Polemonium occidentale subsp. lacustre

Polemonium occidentale subsp.

lacustre, commonly known as the western blueflag, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic qualities.
