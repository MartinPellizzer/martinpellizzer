Polypodium anisomeron var. elongatum

Polypodium anisomeron var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated wall fern, is a variant of the polypodium genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a fern characterized by its elongated, strap-like fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found on rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to substrates, while its sporangia are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
