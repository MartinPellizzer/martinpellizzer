Prunus serrulata f. longipedunculata

Prunus serrulata f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping sakura, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, giving it a distinctive and ornamental look. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink flowers in early spring, which are highly prized for their beauty. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal.

This cultivar is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic value and cultural significance.
