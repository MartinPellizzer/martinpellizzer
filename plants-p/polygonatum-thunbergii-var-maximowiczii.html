Polygonatum thunbergii var. maximowiczii

Polygonatum thunbergii var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous root system makes it a hardy and long-lived species.
