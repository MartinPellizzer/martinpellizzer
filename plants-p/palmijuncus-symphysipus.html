Palmijuncus symphysipus

Palmijuncus symphysipus, commonly known as the Socotra cactus, is a unique succulent native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in the arid conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in clusters at the tips of the stems.

The plant's unusual morphology and adaptations make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
