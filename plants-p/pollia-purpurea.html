Pollia purpurea

Pollia purpurea, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple petals and intricate floral structure, which are adapted for specific pollination by certain insects. The plant produces small, elongated seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pollia purpurea is valued not only for its beauty but also for its cultural significance in some African communities.
