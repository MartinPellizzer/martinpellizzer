Piper tepezontesense

Piper tepezontesense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper-like plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by elliptical, blue-black berries.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
