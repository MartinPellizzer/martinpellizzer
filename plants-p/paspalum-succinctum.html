Paspalum succinctum

Paspalum succinctum, commonly known as flat grass or slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open fields.

Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to spread and establish in new locations.
