Potentilla verna var. haplotricha

Potentilla verna var. haplotricha, commonly known as the spring cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in groups of five, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a resilient addition to native plant gardens.
