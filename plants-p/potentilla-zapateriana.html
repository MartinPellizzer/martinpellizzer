Potentilla zapateriana

Potentilla zapateriana, commonly known as the Zapater's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a system of rhizomes that help it survive in harsh climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
