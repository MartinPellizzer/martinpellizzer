Paspalum ramosissimum

Paspalum ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum ramosissimum is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
