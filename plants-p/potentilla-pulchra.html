Potentilla pulchra

Potentilla pulchra, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are pinnately divided into five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla pulchra is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
