Phyllostachys nigra var. hanchiku

Phyllostachys nigra var. hanchiku, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a variety of bamboo characterized by its dark, almost black culms that are often adorned with a silvery sheen.

It is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are a deep green.

This bamboo is often used in traditional Chinese gardens and is also cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and structural strength.
