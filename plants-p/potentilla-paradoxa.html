Potentilla paradoxa

Potentilla paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla paradoxa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and showy blooms.
