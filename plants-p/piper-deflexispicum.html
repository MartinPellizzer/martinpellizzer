Piper deflexispicum

Piper deflexispicum, commonly known as the flexed pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments.
