Pedicularis mucida

Pedicularis mucida, commonly known as the sticky-pod lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which is part of the Orobanchaceae family, commonly known as the broomrape family. This species is characterized by its glandular hairs that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.
