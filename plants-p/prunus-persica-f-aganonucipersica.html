Prunus persica f. aganonucipersica

Prunus persica f. aganonucipersica, commonly known as the Aganum peach, is a unique cultivar of peach characterized by its distinctive fruit morphology.

The fruit exhibits a reduced number of seeds, often referred to as "nucipersica," which is a notable botanical feature. This variety is typically smaller than standard peaches and has a more compact, rounded shape. The skin is usually thinner and may display a range of colors from yellow to red.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
