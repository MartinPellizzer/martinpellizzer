Psidium arayan

Psidium arayan, commonly known as the golden guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor with a thin, yellowish skin. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
