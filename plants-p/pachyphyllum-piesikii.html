Pachyphyllum piesikii

Pachyphyllum piesikii, commonly known as the desert rose or the stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its compact growth habit makes it an attractive addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Pachyphyllum piesikii is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for low-maintenance gardening.
