Pediomelum aromaticum

Pediomelum aromaticum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and emit a subtle, pleasant fragrance.

Pediomelum aromaticum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
