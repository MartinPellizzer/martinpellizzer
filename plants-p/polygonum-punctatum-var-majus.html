Polygonum punctatum var. majus

Polygonum punctatum var. majus, commonly known as the spotted polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features striking, palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive purplish sheen and red-veined patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, shade-tolerant plants.
