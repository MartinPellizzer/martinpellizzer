Pouteria stipulifera

Pouteria stipulifera, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive potential in some regions, it is both cultivated and regulated in various parts of the world.
