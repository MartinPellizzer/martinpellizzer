Pimelea nitens

Pimelea nitens, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its narrow, upright stems and dense clusters of small, red or orange-red flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Pimelea nitens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
