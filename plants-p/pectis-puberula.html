Pectis puberula

Pectis puberula, commonly known as the silky pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features soft, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Pectis puberula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
