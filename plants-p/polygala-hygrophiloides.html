Polygala hygrophiloides

Polygala hygrophiloides, commonly known as the water-loving milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or purple.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used as a ornamental plant in gardens.
