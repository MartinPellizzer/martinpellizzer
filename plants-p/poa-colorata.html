Poa colorata

Poa colorata, commonly known as the blue grass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its striking blue-green coloration, which is due to the presence of a bluish waxy coating on its leaves. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage is dense and forms compact clumps, making it an attractive ornamental plant.

Poa colorata is also valued for its ability to withstand harsh weather conditions and its low maintenance requirements.
