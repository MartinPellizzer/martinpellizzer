Primula parryi

Primula parryi, commonly known as Parry's primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically hairy and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, often with a yellow center. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Primula parryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
