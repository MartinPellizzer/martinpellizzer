Petasites × rechingeri

Petasites × rechingeri, commonly known as the giant edelweiss, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fuzzy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Europe and thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and protected areas.
