Piper puncticulatum

Piper puncticulatum, commonly known as the punctuated pepper or the zigzag pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered yellow flowers. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark blue or black color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
