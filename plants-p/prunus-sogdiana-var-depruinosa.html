Prunus sogdiana var. depruinosa

Prunus sogdiana var. depruinosa, commonly known as the Sogdian cherry, is a rare and ancient species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark red berries and deciduous leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and small, often clustered flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical and ecological studies.
