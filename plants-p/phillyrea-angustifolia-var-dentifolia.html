Phillyrea angustifolia var. dentifolia

Phillyrea angustifolia var. dentifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
