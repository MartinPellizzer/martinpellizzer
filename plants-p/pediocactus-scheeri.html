Pediocactus scheeri

Pediocactus scheeri, commonly known as the Scheeri's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible fruits. Its thick, fleshy pads are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Pediocactus scheeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-water landscapes.
