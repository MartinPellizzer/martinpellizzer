Piper latum

Piper latum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries.

The berries are typically used in traditional medicine and have a mild peppery flavor.
