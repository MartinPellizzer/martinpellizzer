Premna grossa

Premna grossa, commonly known as roughleaf glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Premna grossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
