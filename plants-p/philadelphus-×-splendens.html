Philadelphus × splendens

Philadelphus × splendens, commonly known as the golden spire honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and dense, thornless stems that form a compact, rounded shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers in early summer, which are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Philadelphus × splendens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
