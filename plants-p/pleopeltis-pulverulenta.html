Pleopeltis pulverulenta

Pleopeltis pulverulenta, commonly known as the powdered shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that give it a soft, powdery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments, and it produces spores in clusters on the underside of its fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
