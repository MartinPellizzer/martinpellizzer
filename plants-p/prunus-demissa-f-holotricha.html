Prunus demissa f. holotricha

Prunus demissa f. holotricha, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a hairy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
