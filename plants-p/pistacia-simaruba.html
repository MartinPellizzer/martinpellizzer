Pistacia simaruba

Pistacia simaruba, commonly known as the Cuban pistache, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the cashew and mango. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
