Paeonia mascula subsp. icarica

Paeonia mascula subsp.

icarica, commonly known as the Icaric or Icaric peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmate, deeply lobed leaves that are usually green and velvety to the touch. Its roots are fleshy and tuberous, which help it survive in its native habitat.

The species is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
