Pyrus communis var. mariana

Pyrus communis var. mariana, commonly known as the Marianna pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often russet-colored fruit with a sweet and juicy flesh. The plant is deciduous, producing clusters of white flowers in the spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its use in both culinary and ornamental horticulture.
