Phyllanthus fuscoluridus var. parviflorus

Phyllanthus fuscoluridus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
