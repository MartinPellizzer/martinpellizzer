Piper tristachyum

Piper tristachyum, commonly known as the wild pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries containing a single seed. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.
