Podospermum coronipifolium

Podospermum coronipifolium, commonly known as the crown-leaved podospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its unique leaf arrangement and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
