Pinus tenangaensis var. ortgiesiana

Pinus tenangaensis var. ortgiesiana, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth, long lifespan, and unique needle-like leaves that are often covered in a distinctive white or grayish bloom. The tree produces cones that are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. This variety is adapted to high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying plant adaptation and ecological resilience in mountain ecosystems.
