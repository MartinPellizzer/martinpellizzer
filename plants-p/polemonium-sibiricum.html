Polemonium sibiricum

Polemonium sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus, which is part of the family Polemoniaceae. The plant features slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
