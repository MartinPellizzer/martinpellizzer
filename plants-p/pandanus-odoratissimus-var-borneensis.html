Pandanus odoratissimus var. borneensis

Pandanus odoratissimus var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and construction.

This variant is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a resilient species in tropical regions.
