Psoralea ensifolia

Psoralea ensifolia, commonly known as the sicklepod or narrowleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by slender, pod-like fruits. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Psoralea ensifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
