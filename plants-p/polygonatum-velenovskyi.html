Polygonatum velenovskyi

Polygonatum velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and arching stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early summer, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a dense rosette, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Polygonatum velenovskyi is valued for its adaptability to woodland and shaded environments.
