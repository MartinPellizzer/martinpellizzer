Pseudolmedia bucidifolia

Pseudolmedia bucidifolia, commonly known as the false balsa tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are known for their ability to absorb water efficiently, making it adaptable to moist, humid environments.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
