Polemonium villosum f. glabrum

Polemonium villosum f. glabrum, commonly known as the smoothleaf Jacob's ladder, is a variant of the Jacob's ladder plant characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is native to eastern North America. The plant features delicate, cascading clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and grows in dense, low mats, making it a suitable ground cover in woodland gardens.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, thriving in cool, humid environments.
