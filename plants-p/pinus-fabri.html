Pinus fabri

Pinus fabri, commonly known as the Formosa fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color and contain winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates with high rainfall and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
