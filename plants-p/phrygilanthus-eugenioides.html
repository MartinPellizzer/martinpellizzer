Phrygilanthus eugenioides

Phrygilanthus eugenioides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and seasonal display make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscaped areas.
