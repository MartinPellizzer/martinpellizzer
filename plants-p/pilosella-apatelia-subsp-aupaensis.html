Pilosella apatelia subsp. aupaensis

Pilosella apatelia subsp.

aupaensis, commonly known as the Andean hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by a dense ring of pale green to white ray florets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to high-altitude environments and its role in local ecosystems.
