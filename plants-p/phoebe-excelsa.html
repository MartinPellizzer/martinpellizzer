Phoebe excelsa

Phoebe excelsa, commonly known as the giant phoebe, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by spherical, fleshy berries containing glossy black seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in tropical forests.
