Polypodium fallacissimum

Polypodium fallacissimum, commonly known as the false bird's nest fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped, evergreen fronds that are often covered in a dense, woolly layer of brownish spores. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to form dense colonies over time.

The spores are released from the underside of the fronds, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
