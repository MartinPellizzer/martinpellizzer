Polycarpaea glauca

Polycarpaea glauca, commonly known as blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear small, blue-violet flowers with prominent yellow centers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially sunny areas.

The plant has a fibrous root system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.
