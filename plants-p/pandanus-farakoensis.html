Pandanus farakoensis

Pandanus farakoensis, commonly known as the Farako pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or swampy areas.
