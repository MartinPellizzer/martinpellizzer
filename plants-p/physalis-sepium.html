Physalis sepium

Physalis sepium, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or Indian gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange, inflated calyces that enclose the fruit, which resembles small lanterns. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.
