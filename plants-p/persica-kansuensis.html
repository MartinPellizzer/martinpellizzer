Persica kansuensis

Persica kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by oval, fuzzy fruits that are typically edible. Persica kansuensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

Its fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
