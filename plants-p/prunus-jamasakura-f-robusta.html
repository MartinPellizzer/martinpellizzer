Prunus jamasakura f. robusta

Prunus jamasakura f. robusta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a botanical variant of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its resilience and adaptability in garden settings.
