Plumbago humboldtiana

Plumbago humboldtiana, commonly known as the blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Plumbago humboldtiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
