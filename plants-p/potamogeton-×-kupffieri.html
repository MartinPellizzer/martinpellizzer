Potamogeton × kupffieri

Potamogeton × kupffieri, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton praelongus. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered near the water surface.

Potamogeton × kupffieri plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
