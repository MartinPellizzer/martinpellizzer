Passiflora pedicellaris

Passiflora pedicellaris, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of white filaments and a central cluster of red and blue sepals. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora pedicellaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
