Pleurothallis persimilis

Pleurothallis persimilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth. Its roots are often epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants rather than in soil.

Pleurothallis persimilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
