Plinia plicatocostata

Plinia plicatocostata, commonly known as the pleated-costate myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southern Brazil and is characterized by its distinctive, pleated leaf surfaces and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small, white to pink, and clustered in inflorescences, while its fruit is a small, berry-like drupe.

Plinia plicatocostata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
