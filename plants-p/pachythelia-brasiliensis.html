Pachythelia brasiliensis

Pachythelia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, resembling a starfruit, is edible and has a unique, tangy flavor.

The plant plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
