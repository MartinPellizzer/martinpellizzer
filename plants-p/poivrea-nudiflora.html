Poivrea nudiflora

Poivrea nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
