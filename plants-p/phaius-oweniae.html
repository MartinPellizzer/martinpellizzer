Phaius oweniae

Phaius oweniae, commonly known as the Owen's ginger, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust rhizomes and erect, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Phaius oweniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
