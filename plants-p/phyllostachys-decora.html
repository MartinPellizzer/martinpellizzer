Phyllostachys decora

Phyllostachys decora, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-green culms and feathery, light-green foliage. This bamboo is a clumping type, forming dense stands that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a windbreak in various climates.
