Primula villosa subsp. irmingardis

Primula villosa subsp.

irmingardis, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist environments. It blooms in spring, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
