Pachycymbium rogersii

Pachycymbium rogersii, commonly known as the Rogers' thick-leafed cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, fleshy stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. Native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil. Its reproductive structures include a large, cone-like strobilus that produces both male and female spores.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
