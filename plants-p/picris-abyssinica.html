Picris abyssinica

Picris abyssinica, commonly known as Ethiopian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
