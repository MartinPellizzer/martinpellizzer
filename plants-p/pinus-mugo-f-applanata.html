Pinus mugo f. applanata

Pinus mugo f. applanata, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a rare and unique form of the European dwarf pine.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and short, thick needles. The plant is adapted to harsh alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in rocky soils and cold climates. Its foliage is dense and dark green, often with a bluish tint, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds.

This form is particularly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
