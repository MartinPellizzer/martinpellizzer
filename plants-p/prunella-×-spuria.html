Prunella × spuria

Prunella × spuria, commonly known as selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Prunella × spuria is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

It has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
