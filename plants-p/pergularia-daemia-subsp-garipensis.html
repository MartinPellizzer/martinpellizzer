Pergularia daemia subsp. garipensis

Pergularia daemia subsp.

garipensis, commonly known as the Garip Peninsula plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines, where it thrives in salty and well-drained conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its coastal habitat.
