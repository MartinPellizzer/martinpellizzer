Pilea fuertesii

Pilea fuertesii, commonly known as the Mexican mint plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a distinctive mint-like aroma. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds or indoor cultivation.

Its aromatic foliage and easy care requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in both residential and commercial landscapes.
