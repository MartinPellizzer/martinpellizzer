Paphiopedilum exstaminodium

Paphiopedilum exstaminodium, commonly known as the Singapore orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, erect leaf and produces a single spike with several flowers. The flowers are characterized by their intricate, spurred petals and a distinctive, often yellowish lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral structure.
