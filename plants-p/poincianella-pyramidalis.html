Poincianella pyramidalis

Poincianella pyramidalis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bract-like flowers that resemble flames, which appear in late winter to early spring. The tree has a broad, umbrella-shaped crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, turning yellow in the fall before shedding.

Poincianella pyramidalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.
