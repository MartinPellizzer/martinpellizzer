Pithecellobium scalare

Pithecellobium scalare, commonly known as the scalary pisonia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
