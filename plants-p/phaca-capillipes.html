Phaca capillipes

Phaca capillipes, commonly known as the slender feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features fine, hair-like leaves that are often described as resembling delicate feathers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, characteristic of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Phaca capillipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
