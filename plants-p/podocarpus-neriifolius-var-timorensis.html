Podocarpus neriifolius var. timorensis

Podocarpus neriifolius var. timorensis, commonly known as the Timor satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the island of Timor.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. The tree produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple to black berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional craftsmanship and ornamental horticulture.
