Phaseolus lunatus f. salicis

Phaseolus lunatus f. salicis, commonly known as the sickle bean or salicifolius bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves and smaller, elongated pods. The plant is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content.
