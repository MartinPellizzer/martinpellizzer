Primula brotheri

Primula brotheri, commonly known as the Brother's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 15-30 centimeters and features basal leaves that are oval and hairy.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
