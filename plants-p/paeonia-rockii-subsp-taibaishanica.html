Paeonia rockii subsp. taibaishanica

Paeonia rockii subsp.

taibaishanica, commonly known as the Taibaishan peony, is a rare and endemic species native to China. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to white, with a distinctive double bloom structure.

The plant is a deciduous herbaceous perennial, often found in rocky, mountainous regions with well-drained soil. It has lance-shaped leaves that are compound and arranged alternately along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
