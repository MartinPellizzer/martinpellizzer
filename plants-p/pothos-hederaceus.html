Pothos hederaceus

Pothos hederaceus, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant is known for its adaptability, thriving in low-light conditions and requiring minimal care. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though these are rarely seen in cultivation.

Pothos hederaceus is popular as a houseplant due to its ability to purify air and its trailing growth habit.
