Paspalum familiare

Paspalum familiare, commonly known as the common paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in various ecological and agricultural settings.
