Pandanus kedahensis

Pandanus kedahensis, commonly known as the Kedah pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for their aromatic properties.

Pandanus kedahensis thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to saline conditions.
