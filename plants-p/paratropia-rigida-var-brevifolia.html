Paratropia rigida var. brevifolia

Paratropia rigida var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved hardy fig, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and a dense canopy of foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rocky garden settings.
