Paspalum setiglume

Paspalum setiglume, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes. Its flowering structures consist of spikelets that are often arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

Paspalum setiglume is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic plants in its native habitat.
