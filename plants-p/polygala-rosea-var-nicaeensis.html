Polygala rosea var. nicaeensis

Polygala rosea var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
