Potentilla bornmuelleri var. calvafrondea

Potentilla bornmuelleri var. calvafrondea, commonly known as the Sierra foothill cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is distinguished by its pale greenish-yellow foliage, which contrasts with its bright yellow flowers.
