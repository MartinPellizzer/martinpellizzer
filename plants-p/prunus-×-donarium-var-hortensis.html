Prunus × donarium var. hortensis

Prunus × donarium var. hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The tree typically grows to a height of 8–15 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a serrated margin, and turn yellow in autumn.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
