Prunus capuli

Prunus capuli, commonly known as the Kashmir cherry or Himalayan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries but are typically smaller and have a more acidic flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and bloom in early spring.

Prunus capuli is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
