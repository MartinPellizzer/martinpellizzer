Parahebe derwentiana subsp. maideniana

Parahebe derwentiana subsp.

maideniana, commonly known as the Derwent parahebe, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to New Zealand. It features dense, dark green foliage with a mat-forming habit, often used in rock gardens or as ground cover.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in spring, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth and seasonal flowering.
