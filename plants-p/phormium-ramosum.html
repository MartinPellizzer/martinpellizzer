Phormium ramosum

Phormium ramosum, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a robust perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces striking, spikelike inflorescences with vibrant purple or red flowers. Its leaves are fibrous and have been historically used for making textiles and baskets.

Phormium ramosum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.
