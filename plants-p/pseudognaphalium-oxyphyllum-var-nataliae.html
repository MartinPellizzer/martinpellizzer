Pseudognaphalium oxyphyllum var. nataliae

Pseudognaphalium oxyphyllum var. nataliae, commonly known as Natal's everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellow disk florets surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets, which give it a delicate appearance. It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
