Poa arundinacea

Poa arundinacea, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands.

Poa arundinacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
