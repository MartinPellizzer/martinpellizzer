Perilla frutescens

Perilla frutescens, commonly known as shiso, is an annual herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly peppery leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers and oval-shaped seeds, which are rich in oils and nutrients. Perilla thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its leaves and seeds contain compounds such as rosmarinic acid and perillaldehyde, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and potential health benefits.
