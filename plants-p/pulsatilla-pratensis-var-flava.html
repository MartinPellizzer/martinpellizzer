Pulsatilla pratensis var. flava

Pulsatilla pratensis var. flava, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces achenes with silky, white hairs.

It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
