Podochilus confusus

Podochilus confusus, commonly known as the confused podochilus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small and fragrant, often displaying a mix of white and green hues.

Podochilus confusus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
