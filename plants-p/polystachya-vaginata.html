Polystachya vaginata

Polystachya vaginata, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces striking, showy inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, and the flowers often display a range of colors, including pink, purple, and white.

Polystachya vaginata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, semi-shaded conditions.
