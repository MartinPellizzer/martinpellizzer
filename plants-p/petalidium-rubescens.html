Petalidium rubescens

Petalidium rubescens, commonly known as the red petal plant, is a flowering species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a compact, shrub-like structure with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Petalidium rubescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny soils.
