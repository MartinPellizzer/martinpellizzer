Papaver orientale var. normale

Papaver orientale var. normale, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, with a distinctive black center. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are typically lobed and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

The Persian poppy is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
