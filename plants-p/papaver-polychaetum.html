Papaver polychaetum

Papaver polychaetum, commonly known as the many-hairs poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to central and southern Europe.

It features striking, bright red or orange petals that are typically arranged in a single, showy bloom. The plant has a hairy stem and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Papaver polychaetum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
