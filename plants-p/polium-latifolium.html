Polium latifolium

Polium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf polium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It features slender, grass-like stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spike-like clusters and bloom in the summer months.
