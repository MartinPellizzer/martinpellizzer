Polygala stenosepala

Polygala stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a unique structure that resembles a bird's beak, aiding in pollination by specific insect species.
