Prunus ovalis

Prunus ovalis, commonly known as the ovalleaf cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that matures in summer.

Prunus ovalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
