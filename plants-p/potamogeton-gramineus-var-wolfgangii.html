Potamogeton gramineus var. wolfgangii

Potamogeton gramineus var. wolfgangii, commonly known as the Wolfgang's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, usually found near the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with soft sediment substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
