Poa nemoralis var. miboroides

Poa nemoralis var. miboroides, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming habit and produces small, pale blue-green flowers in the spring.

The variety miboroides is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
