Pandanus compressus

Pandanus compressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of palm native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some island cultures.
