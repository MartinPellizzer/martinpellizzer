Piper pseudoacreanum

Piper pseudoacreanum, commonly known as the Java pepper or Malabar pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries contain the aromatic compounds responsible for its spicy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil and requires support for its vine growth.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is sometimes used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
