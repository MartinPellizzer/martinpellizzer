Potentilla recta diminuta

Potentilla recta diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf upright cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has wiry, branched stems and narrow, serrated leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
