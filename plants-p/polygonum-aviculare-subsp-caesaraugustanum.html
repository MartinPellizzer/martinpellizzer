Polygonum aviculare subsp. caesaraugustanum

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

caesaraugustanum, commonly known as Caesar's Augustan knotgrass, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically has lanceolate leaves with a prominent midrib and produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in terminal clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in various temperate regions.
