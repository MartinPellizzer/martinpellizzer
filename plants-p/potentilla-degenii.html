Potentilla degenii

Potentilla degenii, commonly known as Degen's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Potentilla degenii is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
