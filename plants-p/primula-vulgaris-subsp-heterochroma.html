Primula vulgaris subsp. heterochroma

Primula vulgaris subsp.

heterochroma, commonly known as the heterochrome primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles. It features basal rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

The flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive dark purple or brown blotch at the base, contributing to its striking appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
