Phyteumopsis lanceolata

Phyteumopsis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf phyteum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
