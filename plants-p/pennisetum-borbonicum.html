Pennisetum borbonicum

Pennisetum borbonicum, commonly known as the Bourbon grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant, arching foliage and showy inflorescences.

Pennisetum borbonicum is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
