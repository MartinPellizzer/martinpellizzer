Potamogeton foliosus var. californicus

Potamogeton foliosus var. californicus, commonly known as the California pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in freshwater lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has long, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its small, green flowers are usually clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This variety is adapted to tolerate a range of water conditions, making it a common sight in many freshwater habitats.
