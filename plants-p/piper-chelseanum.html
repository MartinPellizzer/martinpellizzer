Piper chelseanum

Piper chelseanum, commonly known as the Chelsea pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics make it distinct from other Piper species, with unique leaf morphology and fruit structure.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and mild peppery scent.
