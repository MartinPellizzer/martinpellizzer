Potentilla canescens var. podolica

Potentilla canescens var. podolica, commonly known as the Podolian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Carpathian region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely toothed, silver-white leaves that are covered in a fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
