Pavetta indica

Pavetta indica, commonly known as Indian pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Pavetta indica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
