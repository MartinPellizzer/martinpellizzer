Pilosella florentina

Pilosella florentina, commonly known as the Italian hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy and narrow, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella florentina is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate, white flower heads.
