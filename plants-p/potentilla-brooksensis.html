Potentilla brooksensis

Potentilla brooksensis, commonly known as Brooks' cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features wiry stems and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early to mid-summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, which give it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
