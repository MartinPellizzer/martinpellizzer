Panicum flaccidum

Panicum flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Panicum flaccidum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
