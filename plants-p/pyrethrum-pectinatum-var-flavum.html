Pyrethrum pectinatum var. flavum

Pyrethrum pectinatum var. flavum, commonly known as the golden pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the cultivation of natural pesticides due to its pyrethrin content.
