Piper cojimaranum

Piper cojimaranum, commonly known as the Cojimaranum pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, vine-like stems that can reach several meters in length. Its small, green berries are typically harvested when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper cojimaranum make it an important plant in both ecological and cultural contexts.
