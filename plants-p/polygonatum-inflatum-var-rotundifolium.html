Polygonatum inflatum var. rotundifolium

Polygonatum inflatum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, rhizomatous stems that produce clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring. The leaves are broadly ovate to round, with a smooth texture and a waxy appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in umbels and are followed by dark blue to black berries.

This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
