Phaseolus principissae

Phaseolus principissae, commonly known as the Royal Bean, is a rare and endangered legume native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain one to three dark brown seeds. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
