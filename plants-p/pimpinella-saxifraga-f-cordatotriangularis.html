Pimpinella saxifraga f. cordatotriangularis

Pimpinella saxifraga f. cordatotriangularis, commonly known as the heart-shaped cow parsley, is a variant of the cow parsley species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

The plant features trifoliate leaves that are cordate-triangular in shape, with a heart-like base and a pointed apex. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. The flowers are small, white, and arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
