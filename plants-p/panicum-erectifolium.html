Panicum erectifolium

Panicum erectifolium, commonly known as stiffleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, stiff leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for ornamental landscaping. Its foliage remains green throughout the growing season, providing visual interest year-round.

Panicum erectifolium is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
