Pollia dielsii

Pollia dielsii, commonly known as the African velvet tassel, is a striking member of the Polypodiaceae family, native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, pendulous inflorescences that resemble tassels, composed of densely packed, showy spore-bearing structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are leathery and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Pollia dielsii is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its ecological role in supporting various invertebrates.
