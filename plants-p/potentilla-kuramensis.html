Potentilla kuramensis

Potentilla kuramensis, commonly known as Kuram bentgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla kuramensis is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments.
