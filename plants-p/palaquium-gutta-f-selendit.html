Palaquium gutta f. selendit

Palaquium gutta f. selendit, commonly known as the Selendit Gutta, is a variant of the gutta tree native to Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its small, waxy leaves and distinctive red or orange fruits that contain a sticky, latex-like substance. The leaves are typically alternate and elliptical, with a glossy surface that reflects light. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant's unique properties have made it of interest in both traditional medicine and commercial applications.
