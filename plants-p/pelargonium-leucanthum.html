Pelargonium leucanthum

Pelargonium leucanthum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
