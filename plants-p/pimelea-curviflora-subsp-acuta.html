Pimelea curviflora subsp. acuta

Pimelea curviflora subsp.

acuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower clusters that are typically red or orange in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
