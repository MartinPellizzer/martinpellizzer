Psidium triphyllum

Psidium triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaf guava or swamp guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly the southeastern United States, the Caribbean, and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a tart flavor with a high acidity level.

The plant is often found in wet, swampy areas and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
