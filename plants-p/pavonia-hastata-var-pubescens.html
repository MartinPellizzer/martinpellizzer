Pavonia hastata var. pubescens

Pavonia hastata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often covered in fine pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
