Penstemon albertinus

Penstemon albertinus, commonly known as the Albertine beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces spikes of pink to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Penstemon albertinus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
