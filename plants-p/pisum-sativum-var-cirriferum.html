Pisum sativum var. cirriferum

Pisum sativum var. cirriferum, commonly known as the curly pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its distinctive curled and spiral-shaped pods, which set it apart from the typical straight-podded peas. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and produces small, edible seeds. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and white or purple flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique pod morphology.
