Pandanus vacqua

Pandanus vacqua, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, arranged in clusters at the ends of the leaves. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiny structure that contains edible seeds.

Pandanus vacqua is often used in traditional crafts and for its ornamental value in landscaping.
