Pavonia dictyocarpa

Pavonia dictyocarpa, commonly known as the clustered false pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Pavonia dictyocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
