Poterium muricatum var. stenolophum

Poterium muricatum var. stenolophum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmate leaves with serrated edges and produces capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and birdlife.
