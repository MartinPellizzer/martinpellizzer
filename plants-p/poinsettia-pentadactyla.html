Poinsettia pentadactyla

Poinsettia pentadactyla, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the small, inconspicuous true flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions. Its foliage is typically green, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Poinsettia pentadactyla is widely cultivated as a holiday ornamental due to its festive appearance and seasonal blooming.
