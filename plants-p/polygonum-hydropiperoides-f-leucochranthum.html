Polygonum hydropiperoides f. leucochranthum

Polygonum hydropiperoides f. leucochranthum, commonly known as the white-flowered smartweed, is a variant of the smartweed species characterized by its pale white flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and often reddish in color.

The white flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
