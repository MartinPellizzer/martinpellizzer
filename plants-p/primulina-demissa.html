Primulina demissa

Primulina demissa, commonly known as the velvet leaf.

It is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The plant typically grows as a compact, evergreen shrub with broad, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or white in color.

Primulina demissa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
