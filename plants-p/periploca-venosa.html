Periploca venosa

Periploca venosa, commonly known as the veiny periploca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with distinct venation patterns. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a tubular corolla, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Periploca venosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
