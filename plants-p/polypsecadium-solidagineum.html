Polypsecadium solidagineum

Polypsecadium solidagineum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many areas.
