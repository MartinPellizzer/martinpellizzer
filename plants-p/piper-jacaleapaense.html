Piper jacaleapaense

Piper jacaleapaense, commonly known as the Jacalepaense pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when mature and used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological research.
