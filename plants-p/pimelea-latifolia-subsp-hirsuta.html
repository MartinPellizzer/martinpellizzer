Pimelea latifolia subsp. hirsuta

Pimelea latifolia subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its subspecific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open forests or heathlands.

The hairy pimelea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
