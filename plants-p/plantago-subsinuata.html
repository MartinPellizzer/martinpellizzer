Plantago subsinuata

Plantago subsinuata, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike. The plant is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their healing properties.
