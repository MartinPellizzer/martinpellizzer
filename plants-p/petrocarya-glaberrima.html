Petrocarya glaberrima

Petrocarya glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth petrocarya, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Hippocastanaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
