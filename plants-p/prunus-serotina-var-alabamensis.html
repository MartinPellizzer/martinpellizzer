Prunus serotina var. alabamensis

Prunus serotina var. alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but often tart.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along streambanks.
