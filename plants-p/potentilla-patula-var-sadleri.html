Potentilla patula var. sadleri

Potentilla patula var. sadleri, commonly known as Sadler's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged in a compact, mat-like formation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
