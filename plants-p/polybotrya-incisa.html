Polybotrya incisa

Polybotrya incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polybotryaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
