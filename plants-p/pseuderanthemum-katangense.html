Pseuderanthemum katangense

Pseuderanthemum katangense, commonly known as Katanga's false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pseuderanthemum katangense is adapted to grow in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
