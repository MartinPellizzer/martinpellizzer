Pyrus devoniensis

Pyrus devoniensis, commonly known as the Devonian pear, is a rare and ancient species of flowering plant.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pears. Native to the UK, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors.

Due to its historical significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study.
