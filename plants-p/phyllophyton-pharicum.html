Phyllophyton pharicum

Phyllophyton pharicum, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in small, umbrella-like clusters called sorus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant collections.
