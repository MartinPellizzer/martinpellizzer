Potentilla tephroleuca

Potentilla tephroleuca, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
