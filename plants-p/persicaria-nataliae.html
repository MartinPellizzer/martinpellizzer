Persicaria nataliae

Persicaria nataliae, commonly known as Natal's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often vibrant flowers. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Natal's knotweed is often found in rocky or woodland habitats, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
