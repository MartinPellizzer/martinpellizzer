Parasenecio hastatus var. ramosus

Parasenecio hastatus var. ramosus, commonly known as the branched roughdock, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and its flowering stems are often branched, giving it its varietal name. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The flowers are yellow and are arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
