Plantago major f. nervata

Plantago major f. nervata, commonly known as the narrow-veined plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its slender, elongated leaves with distinct, parallel veins.

This plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are broad and ribbed, with a rough, sandpapery texture, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally for its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.
