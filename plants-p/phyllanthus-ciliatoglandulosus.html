Phyllanthus ciliatoglandulosus

Phyllanthus ciliatoglandulosus, commonly known as the hairy glandular phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves with glandular hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
