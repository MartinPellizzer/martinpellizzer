Piper elongatum f. subglabrum

Piper elongatum f. subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved long pepper, is a botanical variant of the long pepper plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and elongated, clustered fruiting spikes. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is cultivated for its peppery berries used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal preparations due to their aromatic properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to the typical form of Piper elongatum.
