Potamogeton oblongus var. lancifolius

Potamogeton oblongus var. lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it in muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
