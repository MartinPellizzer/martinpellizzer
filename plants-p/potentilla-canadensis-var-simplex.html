Potentilla canadensis var. simplex

Potentilla canadensis var. simplex, commonly known as Canadian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, unbranched inflorescences.
