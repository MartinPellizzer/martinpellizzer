Piper torbecanum

Piper torbecanum, commonly known as the Torbecanum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed.
