Piper borbonense

Piper borbonense, commonly known as the Bourbon pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
