Pleopeltis pedunculata

Pleopeltis pedunculata, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with fleshy, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are often pink or red, with a distinctive spurred petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
