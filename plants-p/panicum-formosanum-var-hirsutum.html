Panicum formosanum var. hirsutum

Panicum formosanum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy foxtail, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, hair-like bristles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive growth habit.
