Penstemon pseudohumilis

Penstemon pseudohumilis, commonly known as the lowland penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

Penstemon pseudohumilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
