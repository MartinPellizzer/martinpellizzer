Pararistolochia soyauxiana

Pararistolochia soyauxiana, commonly known as the Soyaux's horsewhin, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, complex, and often aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, hooded flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its roots contain various bioactive compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

However, like many members of its family, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
