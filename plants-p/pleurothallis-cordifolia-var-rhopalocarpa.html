Pleurothallis cordifolia var. rhopalocarpa

Pleurothallis cordifolia var. rhopalocarpa, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
