Pastinaca stenophylla

Pastinaca stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
