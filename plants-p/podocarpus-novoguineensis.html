Podocarpus novoguineensis

Podocarpus novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland and montane forests. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, while its wood is dense and valued for its durability.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
