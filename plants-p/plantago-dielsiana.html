Plantago dielsiana

Plantago dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are broad and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
