Palmijuncus micracanthus

Palmijuncus micracanthus, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, tree-like structure with a single trunk and dense, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
