Poa clelandii

Poa clelandii, commonly known as Cleland's bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, mat-like growths. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial species that is drought-tolerant and has a low-growing habit.

Poa clelandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with limited water availability.
