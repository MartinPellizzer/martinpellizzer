Passiflora lutea

Passiflora lutea, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces orange to red berries that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Passiflora lutea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
