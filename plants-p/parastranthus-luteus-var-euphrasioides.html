Parastranthus luteus var. euphrasioides

Parastranthus luteus var. euphrasioides, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
