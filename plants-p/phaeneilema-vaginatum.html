Phaeneilema vaginatum

Phaeneilema vaginatum, commonly known as the golden bower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often covered in a thin, leafy sheath, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.
