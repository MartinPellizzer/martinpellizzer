Peritassa smaragdina

Peritassa smaragdina, commonly known as the emerald peritassa, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant green, waxy leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its botanical structure includes a short, sturdy stem and a compact rosette of leaves.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, it is considered a conservation concern.
