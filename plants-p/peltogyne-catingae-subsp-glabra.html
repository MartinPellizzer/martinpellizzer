Peltogyne catingae subsp. glabra

Peltogyne catingae subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth balsa tree, is a species of legume native to Brazil. It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.

Due to its lightweight wood, it has historical significance in construction and craftsmanship.
