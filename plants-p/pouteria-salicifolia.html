Pouteria salicifolia

Pouteria salicifolia, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a sprawling or upright form depending on environmental conditions.

Pouteria salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for its fruit and leaves.
