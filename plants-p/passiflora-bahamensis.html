Passiflora bahamensis

Passiflora bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian passionflower, is a species native to the Caribbean islands, including the Bahamas.

It is a climbing vine with striking, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers with a unique structure featuring five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its visual appeal.
