Piper sulcinervosum

Piper sulcinervosum, commonly known as the smooth-veined pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves with smooth veins. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
