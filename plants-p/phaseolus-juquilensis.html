Phaseolus juquilensis

Phaseolus juquilensis, commonly known as the Mexican bean or Huauzontle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is often grown for its nutritional value.

Phaseolus juquilensis is a hardy annual that is valued both for its culinary uses and its role in traditional agriculture.
