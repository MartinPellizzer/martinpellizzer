Phaseolus pascuorum

Phaseolus pascuorum, commonly known as the creeping bean or wild bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. The plant features slender, trailing stems that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its small, yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by pods containing small, edible seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compound leaves and is part of the legume family, Fabaceae.
