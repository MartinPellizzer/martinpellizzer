Petastoma multiglandulosum

Petastoma multiglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular petasite, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellowish to brownish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with prominent veins and are covered in numerous small glands.

The flowers are usually clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.
