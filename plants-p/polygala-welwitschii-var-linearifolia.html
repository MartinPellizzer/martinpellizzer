Polygala welwitschii var. linearifolia

Polygala welwitschii var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and delicate, spicate inflorescences. The plant produces small, often yellow or greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
