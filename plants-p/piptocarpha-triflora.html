Piptocarpha triflora

Piptocarpha triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered bushveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with three to five flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing drought tolerance.

Piptocarpha triflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
