Plumbago rosea

Plumbago rosea, commonly known as the blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
