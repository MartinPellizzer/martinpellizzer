Plectranthus longiflorus

Plectranthus longiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely appreciated in horticultural settings.
