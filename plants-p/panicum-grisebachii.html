Panicum grisebachii

Panicum grisebachii, commonly known as gray panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a grayish hue. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Panicum grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
