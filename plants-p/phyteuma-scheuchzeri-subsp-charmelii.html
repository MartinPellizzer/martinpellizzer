Phyteuma scheuchzeri subsp. charmelii

Phyteuma scheuchzeri subsp.

charmelii, commonly known as the Alpine phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers.

The flowers are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive tubular shape, and bloom in late summer. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.

It plays a role in alpine ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in mountainous regions.
