Paepalanthus ciliolatus

Paepalanthus ciliolatus, commonly known as the silky leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in soft, woolly hair. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
