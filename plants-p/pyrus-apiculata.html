Pyrus apiculata

Pyrus apiculata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet, and juicy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in temperate climates.
