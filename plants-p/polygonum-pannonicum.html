Polygonum pannonicum

Polygonum pannonicum, commonly known as the Hungarian knotgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum pannonicum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many regions.
