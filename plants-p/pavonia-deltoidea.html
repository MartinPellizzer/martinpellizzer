Pavonia deltoidea

Pavonia deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid false pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 feet tall, featuring slender stems and palmate leaves with a distinctive deltoid shape. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods.

Pavonia deltoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
