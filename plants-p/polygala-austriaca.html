Polygala austriaca

Polygala austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, calcareous soils, often found in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in spikelike clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
