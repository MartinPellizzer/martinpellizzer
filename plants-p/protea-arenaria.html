Protea arenaria

Protea arenaria, commonly known as the coastal protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of a central cone-shaped structure surrounded by colorful bracts. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and yellow, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects.

Protea arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
