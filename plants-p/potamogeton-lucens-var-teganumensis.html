Potamogeton lucens var. teganumensis

Potamogeton lucens var. teganumensis, commonly known as the Teganumensis pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.
