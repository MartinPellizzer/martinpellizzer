Pimelea aeruginosa

Pimelea aeruginosa, commonly known as the silverleaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Pimelea aeruginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
