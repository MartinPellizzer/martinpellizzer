Pilosella praealta subsp. albidobractea

Pilosella praealta subsp.

albidobractea, commonly known as the white bract pilosella, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale bracts that resemble the petals, giving it a unique appearance. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in temperate climates.

This plant is relatively short-lived and has a limited distribution, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
