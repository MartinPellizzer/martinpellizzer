Pisonia tomentosa

Pisonia tomentosa, commonly known as the silky pisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.
