Pelargonium × paternum

Pelargonium × paternum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pelargonium × hortorum and Pelargonium zonale.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Pelargonium × paternum is widely cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
