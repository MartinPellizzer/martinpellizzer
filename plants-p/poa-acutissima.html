Poa acutissima

Poa acutissima, commonly known as sharpawn, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Poa acutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
