Panicum zelayense

Panicum zelayense, commonly known as the Zelaya panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces panicles that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant typically thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and floodplains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum zelayense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
