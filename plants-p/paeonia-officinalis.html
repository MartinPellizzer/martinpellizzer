Paeonia officinalis

Paeonia officinalis, commonly known as the tree peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the芍药属 (Paeonia) genus within the family Ranunculaceae. The plant is characterized by its showy, often double flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, white, and yellow. Its leaves are palmately compound, and it produces erect, woody stems that can grow up to several feet tall.

Paeonia officinalis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
