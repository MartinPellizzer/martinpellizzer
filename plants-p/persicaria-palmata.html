Persicaria palmata

Persicaria palmata, commonly known as palmate knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.

Persicaria palmata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
