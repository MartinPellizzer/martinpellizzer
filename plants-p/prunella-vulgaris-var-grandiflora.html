Prunella vulgaris var. grandiflora

Prunella vulgaris var. grandiflora, commonly known as large-flowered selfheal, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces dense clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
