Phleum montanum var. glabrispiculatum

Phleum montanum var. glabrispiculatum, commonly known as mountain fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is distinguished by its glabrous (smooth) spikelets, which give it its specific epithet.
