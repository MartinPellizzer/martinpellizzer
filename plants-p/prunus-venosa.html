Prunus venosa

Prunus venosa, commonly known as the variegated cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features white and green patterns on the leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in spring, followed by dark red or black berries in autumn. Prunus venosa thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique leaf patterns and seasonal blooms make it a visually appealing addition to any landscape.
