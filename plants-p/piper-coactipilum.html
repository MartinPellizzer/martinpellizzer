Piper coactipilum

Piper coactipilum, commonly known as the yellow pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, yellowish-green berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
