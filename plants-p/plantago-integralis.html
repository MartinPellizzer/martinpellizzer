Plantago integralis

Plantago integralis, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
