Potentilla polemochora

Potentilla polemochora, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Potentilla polemochora is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
