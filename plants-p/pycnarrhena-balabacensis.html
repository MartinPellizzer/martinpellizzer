Pycnarrhena balabacensis

Pycnarrhena balabacensis, commonly known as the Balabac bamboo, is a unique grass species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
