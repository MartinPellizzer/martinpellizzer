Phegopteris refulgens

Phegopteris refulgens, commonly known as the shining water fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
