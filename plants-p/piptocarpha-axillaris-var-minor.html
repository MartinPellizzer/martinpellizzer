Piptocarpha axillaris var. minor

Piptocarpha axillaris var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluebunch wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
