Plectranthus inconspicuus

Plectranthus inconspicuus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically arranged in clusters.

Plectranthus inconspicuus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
