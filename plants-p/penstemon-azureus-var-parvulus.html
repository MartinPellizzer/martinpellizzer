Penstemon azureus var. parvulus

Penstemon azureus var. parvulus, commonly known as the Bluecurls Penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, curling flower spikes that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements enhance its appeal in arid landscapes.
