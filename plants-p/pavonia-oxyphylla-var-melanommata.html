Pavonia oxyphylla var. melanommata

Pavonia oxyphylla var. melanommata, commonly known as the black-stemmed pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural habitats.
