Peperomia apoda

Peperomia apoda, commonly known as the "naked foot peperomia," is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically rounded or ovate in shape, with a deep green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in hue, which are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia apoda is valued for its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
