Puya venezuelana

Puya venezuelana, commonly known as the "Queen of the Andes," is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves that form a rosette structure, capable of storing water in arid environments. The plant produces showy, spikelike inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its adaptability to extreme climates makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
