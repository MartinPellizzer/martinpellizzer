Pozoa ranunculus

Pozoa ranunculus, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

The species is often found in temperate regions and is known for its preference for waterlogged soils.
