Phaca virgata

Phaca virgata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterplantain, is a flowering plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating in water. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often found near the water's surface. Phaca virgata thrives in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and ditches, and is often found in moist, muddy environments.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and serving as a food source for herbivorous wildlife.
