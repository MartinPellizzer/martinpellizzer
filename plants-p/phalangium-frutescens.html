Phalangium frutescens

Phalangium frutescens, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a low-growing annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are often reddish in color, which help it conserve water in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, it can become a weed in agricultural areas.
