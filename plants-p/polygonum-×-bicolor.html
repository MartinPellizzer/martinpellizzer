Polygonum × bicolor

Polygonum × bicolor, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically display a red or violet upper petal and a white or yellow lower petal. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces cylindrical seed pods that split open when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Polygonum × bicolor is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
