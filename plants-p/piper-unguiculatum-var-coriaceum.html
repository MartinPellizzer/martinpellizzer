Piper unguiculatum var. coriaceum

Piper unguiculatum var. coriaceum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper plant, is a variant of the genus Piper native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are often used in medicinal preparations due to their aromatic properties.

The varietal name "coriaceum" refers to the leathery texture of its foliage, distinguishing it from other Piper species.
