Penstemon glaberrimus

Penstemon glaberrimus, commonly known as the smooth beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit. The plant produces striking blue to purple tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair.

Penstemon glaberrimus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
