Psidium kennedyanum

Psidium kennedyanum, commonly known as the Kennedy's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit, similar to the common guava, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
