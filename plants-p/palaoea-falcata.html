Palaoea falcata

Palaoea falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf palaoea, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
