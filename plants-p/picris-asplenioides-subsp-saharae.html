Picris asplenioides subsp. saharae

Picris asplenioides subsp.

saharae, commonly known as the Saharan hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow composite flowers.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.
