Pyrus subadpressa

Pyrus subadpressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically small and yellowish in color, similar to other pear species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The botanical features of Pyrus subadpressa make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
