Passiflora papilio

Passiflora papilio, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and a crown of feathery filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Passiflora papilio is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
