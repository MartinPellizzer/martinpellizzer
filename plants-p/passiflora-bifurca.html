Passiflora bifurca

Passiflora bifurca, commonly known as the bifurcated passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora bifurca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
