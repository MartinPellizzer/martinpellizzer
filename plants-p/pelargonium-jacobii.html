Pelargonium jacobii

Pelargonium jacobii, commonly known as the Jacob's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features serrated, hairy leaves and produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for rock gardens or containers.

Pelargonium jacobii is also valued for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.
