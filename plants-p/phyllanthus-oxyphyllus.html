Phyllanthus oxyphyllus

Phyllanthus oxyphyllus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, yellow fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
