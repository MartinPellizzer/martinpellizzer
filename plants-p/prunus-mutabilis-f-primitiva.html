Prunus mutabilis f. primitiva

Prunus mutabilis f. primitiva, commonly known as the wild cherry or Prunus fruticosa, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with thornless stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and small landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blossoms and attractive fruit.
