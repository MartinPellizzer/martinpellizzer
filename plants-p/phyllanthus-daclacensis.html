Phyllanthus daclacensis

Phyllanthus daclacensis, commonly known as the Philippine spurge, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, followed by orange or red berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
