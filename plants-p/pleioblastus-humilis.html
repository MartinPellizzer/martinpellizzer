Pleioblastus humilis

Pleioblastus humilis, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its growth habit makes it an excellent choice for landscaping in gardens and as a ground cover.

Pleioblastus humilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive clumps over time.
