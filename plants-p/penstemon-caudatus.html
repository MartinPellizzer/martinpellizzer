Penstemon caudatus

Penstemon caudatus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Penstemon caudatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
