Paspalum setaceum var. muhlenbergii

Paspalum setaceum var. muhlenbergii, commonly known as Muhlenberg's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for use in native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Its ecological value includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
