Persea orizabae

Persea orizabae, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to other persimmons but have a unique flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
