Pleurothallis speciosa

Pleurothallis speciosa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit.

Pleurothallis speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
