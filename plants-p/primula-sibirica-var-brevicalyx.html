Primula sibirica var. brevicalyx

Primula sibirica var. brevicalyx, commonly known as the Siberian primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring bloom.
