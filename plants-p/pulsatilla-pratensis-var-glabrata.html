Pulsatilla pratensis var. glabrata

Pulsatilla pratensis var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth meadow pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
