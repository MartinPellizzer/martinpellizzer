Panicum chinense

Panicum chinense, commonly known as Chinese foxtail, is a warm-season grass native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are often mistaken for foxtail grasses. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
