Protea viscosa

Protea viscosa, commonly known as the sticky protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a sticky texture, hence its common name. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
