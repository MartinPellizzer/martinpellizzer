Polemonium himalaicum

Polemonium himalaicum, commonly known as the Himalayan Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus, which is part of the Violaceae family, known for its showy flowers. The plant features slender, branching stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.
