Pithecellobium incurvatum

Pithecellobium incurvatum, commonly known as the bent-stem pita, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
