Paradaniellia

Paradaniellia, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate, and often translucent leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. Paradaniellia is known for its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
