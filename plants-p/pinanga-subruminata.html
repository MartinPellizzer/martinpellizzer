Pinanga subruminata

Pinanga subruminata, commonly known as the slender bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with a cluster of long, narrow, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in a spadix, followed by the development of small, edible fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
