Primula eximia

Primula eximia, commonly known as the exclamation primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, often with a waxy coating.

Primula eximia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in early spring.
