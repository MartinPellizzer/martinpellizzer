Paullinia cupana var. sorbilis

Paullinia cupana var. sorbilis, commonly known as Brazilian guarana, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant is notable for its high caffeine content, which is concentrated in its seeds. The berries are typically harvested and processed into various products, including beverages and dietary supplements.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
