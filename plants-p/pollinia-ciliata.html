Pollinia ciliata

Pollinia ciliata, commonly known as the silky pollinia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique pollination mechanism involving sticky pollinia. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with narrow, lance-shaped petals. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette.

Pollinia ciliata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy habitats.
