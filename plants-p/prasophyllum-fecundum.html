Prasophyllum fecundum

Prasophyllum fecundum, commonly known as the fertile leek orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as heathlands and grasslands. The plant produces a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and bears a spike of small, greenish-white flowers. It is characterized by its ability to form dense colonies and is often found in disturbed or coastal areas.

Prasophyllum fecundum is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ecological significance in supporting local pollinators.
