Potentilla rectiformis

Potentilla rectiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a distinctive five-leaflet pattern.

Potentilla rectiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
