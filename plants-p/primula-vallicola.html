Primula vallicola

Primula vallicola, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs.

Primula vallicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
