Pleurothallis johannis

Pleurothallis johannis, commonly known as the John's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and decaying plant matter.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
