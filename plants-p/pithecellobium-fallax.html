Pithecellobium fallax

Pithecellobium fallax, commonly known as the yellow pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces fruit that resembles a legume pod, containing edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is also cultivated for ecological and agricultural purposes.
