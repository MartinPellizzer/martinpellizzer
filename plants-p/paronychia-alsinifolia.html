Paronychia alsinifolia

Paronychia alsinifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or coastal landscapes. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its decorative appearance.

Paronychia alsinifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils, making it suitable for maritime environments.
