Piper reductipes var. amplum

Piper reductipes var. amplum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by elongated, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative vine in warm climates.
