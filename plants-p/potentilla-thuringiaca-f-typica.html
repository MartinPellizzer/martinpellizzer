Potentilla thuringiaca f. typica

Potentilla thuringiaca f. typica, commonly known as the typical form of the Thuringian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Central Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological gardens and native plant restoration projects.
