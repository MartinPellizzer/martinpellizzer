Polygonum hydropiper

Polygonum hydropiper, commonly known as water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It features erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, sticky hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly serrated edge. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to moist or waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.
