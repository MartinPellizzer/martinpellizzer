Phyllochlamys taxoides

Phyllochlamys taxoides, commonly known as the Australian water lily, is a perennial aquatic plant native to southeastern Australia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender petioles. The plant produces small, white flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water pollination, with stamens and pistils positioned to facilitate pollination by water movement.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
