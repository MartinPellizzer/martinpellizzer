Persea meridensis

Persea meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a tendency to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.

Persea meridensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often used in local cuisine.
