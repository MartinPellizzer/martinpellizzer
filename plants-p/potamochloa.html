Potamochloa

Potamochloa, commonly known as water reed, is a genus of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of rivers and lakes. The plants are characterized by their tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. They play an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.

Potamochloa species are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
