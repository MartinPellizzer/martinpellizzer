Polygonum dombeyanum

Polygonum dombeyanum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Dombey's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly wavy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming from late summer to early autumn.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
