Peperomia sarcostachya var. repens

Peperomia sarcostachya var. repens, commonly known as creeping peperomia, is a low-growing, trailing plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a waxy texture and a variety of colors, including green, silver, and variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually green or reddish in color. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its compact size and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Its succulent-like leaves make it drought-tolerant, requiring minimal watering once established.
