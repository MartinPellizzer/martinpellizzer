Phyllostachys nigella

Phyllostachys nigella, commonly known as black bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that are often covered in a fine, silky sheen. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking feature in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Black bamboo is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in traditional Chinese and Japanese gardens.
