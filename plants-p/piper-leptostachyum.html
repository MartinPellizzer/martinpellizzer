Piper leptostachyum

Piper leptostachyum, commonly known as the slender-stalked pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a glossy green surface.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in spikes and are followed by small, round berries that are typically green when mature.
