Passiflora monnieri

Passiflora monnieri, commonly known as the lesser passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive organs. The plant produces small, edible berries that are yellow or orange in color when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Passiflora monnieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
