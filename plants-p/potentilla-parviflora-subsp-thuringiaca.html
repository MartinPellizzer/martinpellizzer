Potentilla parviflora subsp. thuringiaca

Potentilla parviflora subsp.

thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to Central Europe. It features small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
