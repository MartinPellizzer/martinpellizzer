Prunus serrulata f. campanulata

Prunus serrulata f. campanulata, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal.
