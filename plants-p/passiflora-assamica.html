Passiflora assamica

Passiflora assamica, commonly known as the Assam passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora assamica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
