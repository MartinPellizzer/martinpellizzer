Persea semecarpifolia

Persea semecarpifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, orange-yellow berries in the fall. Its fruit is highly valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in traditional cuisine.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic wood, which is used in making incense and other aromatic products.
