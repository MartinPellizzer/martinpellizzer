Polygala vulgaris proles gariodiana

Polygala vulgaris proles gariodiana, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has small, pinnately divided leaves and produces small, legume-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
