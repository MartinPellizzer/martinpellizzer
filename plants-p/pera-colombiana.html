Pera colombiana

Pera colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian persimmon, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a unique fruit with a thick, waxy skin and a sweet, mealy flesh when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The plant's growth habit includes a woody stem and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat.
