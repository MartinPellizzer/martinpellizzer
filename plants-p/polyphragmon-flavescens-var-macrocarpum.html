Polyphragmon flavescens var. macrocarpum

Polyphragmon flavescens var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered polyphragmon, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow ray florets and a dense, bushy growth habit. The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, composite flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is native to certain regions of North America and is often found in dry, open habitats.
