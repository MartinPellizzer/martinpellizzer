Passiflora sexocellata

Passiflora sexocellata, commonly known as the six-celled passionflower, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Passiflora.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its intricate and showy flowers, which feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and modified leaves. Its fruit is a berry that ripens to a bright orange or red color, attracting various wildlife.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
