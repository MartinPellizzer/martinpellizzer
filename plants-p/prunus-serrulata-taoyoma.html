Prunus serrulata taoyoma

Prunus serrulata taoyoma, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, oval berries that ripen to a dark red or black color in late summer. Its leaves are elliptical with finely serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in Japanese tradition.
