Poteridium

Poteridium, commonly known as field thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Poteridium has a rosette growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

It serves as a food source for certain insects and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
