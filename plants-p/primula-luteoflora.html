Primula luteoflora

Primula luteoflora, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of tubular flowers that are rich in nectar. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Primula luteoflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
