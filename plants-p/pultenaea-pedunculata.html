Pultenaea pedunculata

Pultenaea pedunculata, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Pultenaea pedunculata is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
