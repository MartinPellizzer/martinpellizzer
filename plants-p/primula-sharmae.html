Primula sharmae

Primula sharmae, commonly known as the Sharma's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a splash of color to mountainous landscapes. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture, and it prefers cool, moist environments.

Primula sharmae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and alpine settings.
