Psephellus straminicephalus

Psephellus straminicephalus, commonly known as the green-headed sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a robust stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive green center, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
