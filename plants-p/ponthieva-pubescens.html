Ponthieva pubescens

Ponthieva pubescens, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, and hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its leaves are often mistaken for moss due to their texture and appearance.

Ponthieva pubescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
