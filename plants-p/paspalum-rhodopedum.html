Paspalum rhodopedum

Paspalum rhodopedum, commonly known as the redroot grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often display a reddish tinge at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Paspalum rhodopedum is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate various environmental conditions.
