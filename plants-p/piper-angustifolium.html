Piper angustifolium

Piper angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature.

Piper angustifolium is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
