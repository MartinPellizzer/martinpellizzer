Populus suaveolens f. angustifolia

Populus suaveolens f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poplar, is a botanical variant of the poplar genus characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found near rivers or in wetlands. Its leaves are usually a dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks.
