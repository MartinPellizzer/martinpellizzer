Plagiostachys breviramosa

Plagiostachys breviramosa, commonly known as the short-branched bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

The inflorescence consists of clustered, spike-like structures that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
