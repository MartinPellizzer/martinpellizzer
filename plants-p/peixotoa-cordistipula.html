Peixotoa cordistipula

Peixotoa cordistipula, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Peixotoa cordistipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
