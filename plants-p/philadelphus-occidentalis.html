Philadelphus occidentalis

Philadelphus occidentalis, commonly known as the mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus occidentalis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
