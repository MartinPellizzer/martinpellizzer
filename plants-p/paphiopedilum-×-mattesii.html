Paphiopedilum × mattesii

Paphiopedilum × mattesii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cultivated hybrid resulting from the cross between Paphiopedilum niveum and Paphiopedilum hirsutum.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring pseudobulbs and fleshy roots adapted for storing moisture. The plant produces showy, slipper-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for horticultural purposes.
