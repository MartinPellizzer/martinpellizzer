Prunus sachalinensis f. pulchra

Prunus sachalinensis f. pulchra, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry, is a botanical variant native to the Far East of Russia.

It is characterized by its attractive, double pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
