Phlomis cashmirica

Phlomis cashmirica, commonly known as Kashmir spurge, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
