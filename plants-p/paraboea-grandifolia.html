Paraboea grandifolia

Paraboea grandifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf paraboea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Paraboea grandifolia is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
