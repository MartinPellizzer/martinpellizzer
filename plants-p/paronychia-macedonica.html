Paronychia macedonica

Paronychia macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its geographic origin and its historical significance in local horticulture.
