Pyxidaria molluginodes

Pyxidaria molluginodes, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it an invasive species in some areas.
