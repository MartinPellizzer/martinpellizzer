Plectranthus flaccidus

Plectranthus flaccidus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus flaccidus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
