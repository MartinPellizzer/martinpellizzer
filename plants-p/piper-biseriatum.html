Piper biseriatum

Piper biseriatum, commonly known as the two-rowed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. These berries contain numerous small, dark seeds and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both horticultural and medicinal contexts.
