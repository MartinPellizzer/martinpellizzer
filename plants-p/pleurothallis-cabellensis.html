Pleurothallis cabellensis

Pleurothallis cabellensis, commonly known as the Cabell's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
