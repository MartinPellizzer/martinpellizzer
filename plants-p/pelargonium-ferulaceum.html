Pelargonium ferulaceum

Pelargonium ferulaceum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features serrated leaves and produces clusters of small, bright red or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium ferulaceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
