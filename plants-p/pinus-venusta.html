Pinus venusta

Pinus venusta, commonly known as the Mexican weeping pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and slender, needle-like leaves that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, Pinus venusta is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
