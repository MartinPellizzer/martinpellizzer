Pandanus somersetensis

Pandanus somersetensis, commonly known as Somerset's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of Somerset in the Indian Ocean, specifically the Chagos Archipelago. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by three-lobed fruit capsules.

This species is adapted to coastal environments and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils.
