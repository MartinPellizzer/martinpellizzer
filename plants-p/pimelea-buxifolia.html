Pimelea buxifolia

Pimelea buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf banksia, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are often a dark green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, cone-like seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Pimelea buxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting flowers.
