Prenanthes petiolata

Prenanthes petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its small, white flowers bloom in late summer and are clustered in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in shallow, freshwater habitats.
