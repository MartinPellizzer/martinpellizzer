Pachyrhizanthe nipponica

Pachyrhizanthe nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese spider flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, spider-like inflorescences with small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.
