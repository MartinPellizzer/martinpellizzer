Primula barbeyana

Primula barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbey's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are oval and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
