Piptadenia patens var. macrophylla

Piptadenia patens var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or sweet acacia, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in panicles, which are followed by pod fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
