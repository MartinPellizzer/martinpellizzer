Panicum punctulatum

Panicum punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive, hairy leaves with prominent, white or pale green markings. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that mature into light brown to reddish seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
