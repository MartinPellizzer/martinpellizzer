Pygeum rubiginosum

Pygeum rubiginosum, commonly known as the rusty African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rubiaceae and is characterized by its rough, reddish bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a serrated margin.

Pygeum rubiginosum is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
