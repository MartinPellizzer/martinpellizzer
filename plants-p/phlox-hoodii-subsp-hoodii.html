Phlox hoodii subsp. hoodii

Phlox hoodii subsp.

hoodii, commonly known as the Hood's phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has linear to lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive flowers make it a valuable addition to pollinator-friendly landscapes.
