Polygonum bonaerense

Polygonum bonaerense, commonly known as the Buenos Aires knotweed, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often edible rhubarb-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish or purple tint, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

While not widely cultivated, it is sometimes found in gardens and naturalized areas due to its adaptability.
