Phillyrea longifolia subsp. briquetii

Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

briquetii, commonly known as the Corsican Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

briquetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
