Polygala crassitesta

Polygala crassitesta, commonly known as the thick-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its erect, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are often pink or purple in color.

Polygala crassitesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
