Phoenicanthemum polystachyum

Phoenicanthemum polystachyum, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom during the monsoon season. The plant is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

The plant has cultural and traditional significance in various regions of India.
