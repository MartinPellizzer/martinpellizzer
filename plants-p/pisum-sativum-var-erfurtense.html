Pisum sativum var. erfurtense

Pisum sativum var. erfurtense, commonly known as the Erfurt pea, is a variant of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded pods compared to standard garden peas. The plant typically produces yellow flowers and edible seeds that are used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its historical significance and adaptability to specific agricultural conditions.
