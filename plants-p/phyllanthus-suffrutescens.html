Phyllanthus suffrutescens

Phyllanthus suffrutescens, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small shrub native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Phyllanthus suffrutescens is also valued for its ornamental use due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
