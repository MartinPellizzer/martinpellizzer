Pisum sativum var. burbridgii

Pisum sativum var. burbridgii, commonly known as the Burbridge pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded pods compared to typical garden peas. This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for genetic and horticultural studies.
