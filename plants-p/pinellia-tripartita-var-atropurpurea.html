Pinellia tripartita var. atropurpurea

Pinellia tripartita var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple-veined ginger, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces upright, hairy stems with lance-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its striking purple-veined, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
