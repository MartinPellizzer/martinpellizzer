Parathesis oblanceolata

Parathesis oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-ovate parathesis, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often pale green to white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
