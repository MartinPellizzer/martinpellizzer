Plantago coronopus var. maroccana

Plantago coronopus var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
