Polygonum amphibium var. coenosum

Polygonum amphibium var. coenosum, commonly known as the marsh knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to thrive in wetland environments.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is notable for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing muddy banks. It is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often found in marshes, fens, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and potential for use in wetland restoration projects.
