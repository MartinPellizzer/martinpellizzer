Potentilla pensylvanica var. nivea

Potentilla pensylvanica var. nivea, commonly known as the white potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, rounded form and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture, and they are arranged oppositely along the stems. This variety is known for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens where its compact size and attractive foliage make it a desirable addition.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
