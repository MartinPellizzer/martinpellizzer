Passiflora glandulosa var. stoupyana

Passiflora glandulosa var. stoupyana, commonly known as the Stoupy's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of colorful filaments and a central cluster of blue or purple anthers. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by local communities. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
