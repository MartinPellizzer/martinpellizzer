Peucedanum trilobatum

Peucedanum trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

It is native to Central Asia and grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in a ribbed, three-lobed fruit, which is typical of the genus.

Peucedanum trilobatum is valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional medicine for digestive and anti-inflammatory purposes.
