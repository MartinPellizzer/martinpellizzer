Persea levinei

Persea levinei, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include smooth, bark and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Persea levinei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit.
