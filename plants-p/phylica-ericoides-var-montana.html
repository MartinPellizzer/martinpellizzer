Phylica ericoides var. montana

Phylica ericoides var. montana, commonly known as the mountain phylica, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright red, bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer. These flowers are rich in nectar, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
