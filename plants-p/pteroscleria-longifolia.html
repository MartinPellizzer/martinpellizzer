Pteroscleria longifolia

Pteroscleria longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved pteroscleria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Pteroscleria longifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
