Pilosella erythropoides

Pilosella erythropoides, commonly known as the red-leaf pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and fuzzy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Pilosella erythropoides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
