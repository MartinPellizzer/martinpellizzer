Phalaenopsis doweryensis

Phalaenopsis doweryensis, commonly known as the Dowery's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has strap-like leaves that are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat. It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or rocks rather than in soil.

Phalaenopsis doweryensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty in home and garden settings.
