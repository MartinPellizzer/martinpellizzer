Prunus serrulata f. udzuzakyra

Prunus serrulata f. udzuzakyra, commonly known as the Udzuzakyra cherry, is a rare and ornamental variation of the Japanese cherry.

This cultivar is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves and clusters of delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring moderate watering and pruning to maintain its shape.

Due to its rarity, it is a prized specimen among botanists and horticulturists.
