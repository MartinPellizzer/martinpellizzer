Pleopeltis tuanensis

Pleopeltis tuanensis, commonly known as the Tuan's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lobed fronds that often grow on tree trunks or rocks. The plant typically has a short, creeping rhizome with small, clustered roots. Its leaves are usually dark green and can form dense, mat-like growths on its host.

Pleopeltis tuanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
