Pultenaea weindorferi

Pultenaea weindorferi, commonly known as the Weindorfer's bush pea, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruit and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features small, bipinnate leaves and produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
