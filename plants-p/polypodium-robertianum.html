Polypodium robertianum

Polypodium robertianum, commonly known as the Robert's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. This plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments, exhibiting a climbing or epiphytic growth habit. Its fronds are pinnate, with a leathery texture and dark green color, and it produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori.

Polypodium robertianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
