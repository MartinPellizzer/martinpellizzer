Papaver nudicaule var. labradoricum

Papaver nudicaule var. labradoricum, commonly known as Labrador poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the coastal regions of Labrador and parts of Newfoundland.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple central blotch. The plant has smooth, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh coastal environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
