Plantago multinervis

Plantago multinervis, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
