Piper spathulatum

Piper spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper or Indian pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. The berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical research.
