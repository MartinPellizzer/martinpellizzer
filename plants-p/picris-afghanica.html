Picris afghanica

Picris afghanica, commonly known as Afghan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
