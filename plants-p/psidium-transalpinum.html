Psidium transalpinum

Psidium transalpinum, commonly known as the Transalpine Guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit, similar to a guava, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Psidium transalpinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
