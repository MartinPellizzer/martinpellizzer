Pinalia vittata

Pinalia vittata, commonly known as the spotted pinyon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters and its oval, glossy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Pinalia vittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and floral display.
