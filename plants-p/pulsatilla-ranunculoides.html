Pulsatilla ranunculoides

Pulsatilla ranunculoides, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
