Passiflora rupestris

Passiflora rupestris, commonly known as the rock passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Passiflora rupestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
