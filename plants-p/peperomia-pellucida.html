Peperomia pellucida

Peperomia pellucida, commonly known as the shining pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a glossy appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia pellucida is also valued for its air-purifying properties and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
