Potentilla australis elatior

Potentilla australis elatior, commonly known as the tall silverweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-white, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
