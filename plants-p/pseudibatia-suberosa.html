Pseudibatia suberosa

Pseudibatia suberosa, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Pseudibatia suberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
