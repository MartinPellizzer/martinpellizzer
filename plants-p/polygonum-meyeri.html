Polygonum meyeri

Polygonum meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and range in color from white to pink.

Polygonum meyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
