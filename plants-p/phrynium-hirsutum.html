Phrynium hirsutum

Phrynium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy phrynium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are usually yellow or greenish in color.

Phrynium hirsutum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
