Philodendron talamancae

Philodendron talamancae, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen philodendron, is a climbing plant native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with light green or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
