Palmijuncus wightii

Palmijuncus wightii, commonly known as the Wight's palm, is a tropical plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
