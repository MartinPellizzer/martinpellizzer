Patagonium arboreum var. pauciflora

Patagonium arboreum var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Patagonian tree heath, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking visual contrast to its pale or yellowish flowers.

This species is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
