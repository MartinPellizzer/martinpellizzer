Pisum sativum var. melileucum

Pisum sativum var. melileucum, commonly known as the white-seed pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its pale yellow flowers and pods that contain white or cream-colored seeds. This variety is typically shorter and more compact than standard pea plants, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.
