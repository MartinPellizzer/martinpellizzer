Pironneava

Pironneava, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Pironneava is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
