Paspalum dilatatum var. pauciciliatum

Paspalum dilatatum var. pauciciliatum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, erect leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
