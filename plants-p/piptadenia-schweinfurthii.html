Piptadenia schweinfurthii

Piptadenia schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African locust bean tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces legume pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and as a source of protein. It is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and supports various wildlife species.
