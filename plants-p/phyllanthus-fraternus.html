Phyllanthus fraternus

Phyllanthus fraternus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, often hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Phyllanthus fraternus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
