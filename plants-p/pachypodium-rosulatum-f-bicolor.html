Pachypodium rosulatum f. bicolor

Pachypodium rosulatum f. bicolor, commonly known as the Variegated Madagascar Booba, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its swollen, bottle-shaped stems and sparse, upright leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a red or orange throat, typically in late winter to early spring. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The variegation in its leaves adds visual interest, making it a standout in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
