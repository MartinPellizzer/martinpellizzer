Potentilla glandulosa f. hansenii

Potentilla glandulosa f. hansenii, commonly known as the Hansen's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
