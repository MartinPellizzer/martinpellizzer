Podalyria liparioides

Podalyria liparioides, commonly known as the Littoral Podalyria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense tufts.

Podalyria liparioides is well adapted to maritime climates and is often found in areas with high salt exposure.
