Passiflora moluccana var. glaberrima

Passiflora moluccana var. glaberrima, commonly known as the Moluccan passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
