Polymnia quichensis

Polymnia quichensis, commonly known as the Quechua grass, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its adaptability and ecological significance, it plays an important role in the local ecosystems of the Andean region.
