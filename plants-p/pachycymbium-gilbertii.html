Pachycymbium gilbertii

Pachycymbium gilbertii, commonly known as the Gilbert's thick-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its often arid environment. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies from white to pale yellow.

This orchid thrives in shaded, humid conditions and is often found in forest understories.
