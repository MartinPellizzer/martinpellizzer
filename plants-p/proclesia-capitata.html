Proclesia capitata

Proclesia capitata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. These flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
