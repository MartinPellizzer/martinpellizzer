Primula abchasia

Primula abchasia, commonly known as the Abkhazian primrose, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and mountainous areas. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. Its leaves are broadly ovate and covered in a fine, soft pubescence.

Primula abchasia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
