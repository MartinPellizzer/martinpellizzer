Prunus myrobalana var. ligustrifolia

Prunus myrobalana var. ligustrifolia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The Persian lilac is valued for its beauty and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
