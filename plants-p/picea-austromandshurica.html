Picea austromandshurica

Picea austromandshurica, commonly known as the Far Eastern spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in cold, humid climates and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and cone-shaped cones. The tree has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches, and its bark is thick and scaly.

It is often found in forested areas at high altitudes, where it plays a vital role in the local ecosystem.
