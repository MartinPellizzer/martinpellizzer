Prunus mutabilis f. hiemalis

Prunus mutabilis f. hiemalis, commonly known as the winter cherry, is a unique variant of the Japanese flowering cherry.

This plant is characterized by its early blooming habit, producing clusters of small, white or pink flowers in late winter or early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its early floral display, adding a touch of color to the landscape during the colder months.
