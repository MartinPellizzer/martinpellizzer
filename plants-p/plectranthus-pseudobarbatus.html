Plectranthus pseudobarbatus

Plectranthus pseudobarbatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely appreciated by gardeners and botanists alike.
