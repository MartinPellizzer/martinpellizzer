Portlandia tetrandra

Portlandia tetrandra, commonly known as the four-threaded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its four stamens, which give it its common name, are prominent features of its floral structure.
