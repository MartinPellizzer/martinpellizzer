Potamogeton × subdentatus

Potamogeton × subdentatus, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that exhibits a combination of characteristics from its parent species, Potamogeton obtusifolius and Potamogeton subdentatus.

It typically has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged spirally along the stem, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin depending on the genetic influence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

However, its invasive potential can lead to dense growth that may alter water quality and compete with native vegetation.
