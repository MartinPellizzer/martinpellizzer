Pleurothallis pulvinata

Pleurothallis pulvinata, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is a small epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest canopy. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Pleurothallis pulvinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
