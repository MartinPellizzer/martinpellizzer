Pitcairnia arida

Pitcairnia arida, commonly known as the desert pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among bromeliads. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear showy, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its adaptations to drought include thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss.

Pitcairnia arida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
