Penicillaria nigricans

Penicillaria nigricans, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, and its berries are typically black when mature. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, often fragrant aroma.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
