Piscidia erythrina

Piscidia erythrina, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish hue. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Piscidia erythrina is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
