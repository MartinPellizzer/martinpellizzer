Prunus demissa f. trichodisca

Prunus demissa f. trichodisca, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a variant of the Prunus demissa species.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries.

The hairy cherry is native to regions of Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
