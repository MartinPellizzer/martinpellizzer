Plantago cafra

Plantago cafra, commonly known as African plantain or broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its resilience and medicinal properties, it is used in traditional medicine for treating various ailments.
