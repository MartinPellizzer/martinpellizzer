Potentilla repens

Potentilla repens, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, spreading stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, with five to seven leaflets that are oval to ovate in shape.

Potentilla repens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
