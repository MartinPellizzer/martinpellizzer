Psoralea maleolens

Psoralea maleolens, commonly known as the narrowleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces slender, wiry pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments.

Psoralea maleolens is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
