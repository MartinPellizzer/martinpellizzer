Poa pratensis subsp. attica

Poa pratensis subsp.

attica, commonly known as the Greek meadow grass, is a subspecies native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly rolled, with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
