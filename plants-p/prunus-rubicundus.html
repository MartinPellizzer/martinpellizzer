Prunus rubicundus

Prunus rubicundus, commonly known as the red cherry tree, is a deciduous species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that ripen in the fall, which are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. The tree has slender, upright branches and produces fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its leaves are ovate and turn a striking yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

Prunus rubicundus is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty and fruit production in suitable climates.
