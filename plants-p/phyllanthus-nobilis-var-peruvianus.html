Phyllanthus nobilis var. peruvianus

Phyllanthus nobilis var. peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian gooseberry, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a smooth, spherical berry that turns red when mature, often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
