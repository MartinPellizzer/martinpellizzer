Prunus communis f. pendula

Prunus communis f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the common almond tree.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it a popular ornamental plant. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, typically pink or white, which add to its aesthetic appeal. Its foliage is dark green and provides a striking contrast to the delicate blossoms.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and parks.
