Pseudoroegneria ninae

Pseudoroegneria ninae, commonly known as the Nina's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, demonstrating drought tolerance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, with small, two-lobed glumes.

Pseudoroegneria ninae is valued for its low maintenance requirements and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
