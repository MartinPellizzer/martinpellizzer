Parathesis sinuata

Parathesis sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous parathesis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
