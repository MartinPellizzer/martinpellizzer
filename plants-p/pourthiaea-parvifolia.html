Pourthiaea parvifolia

Pourthiaea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved pourthiaea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters.

It is often found in dry habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
