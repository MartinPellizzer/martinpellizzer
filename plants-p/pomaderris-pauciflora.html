Pomaderris pauciflora

Pomaderris pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pomaderris, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to low nutrient soils.

Pomaderris pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscapes.
