Piper inaequale

Piper inaequale, commonly known as the unequal pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red upon maturity. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a distinct, peppery aroma.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
