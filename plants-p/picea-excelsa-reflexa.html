Picea excelsa reflexa

Picea excelsa reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed white spruce, is a rare and distinctive conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its needle-like leaves that are often reflexed, or curved backward, giving it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds dispersed by wind, a common reproductive strategy among conifers. Its bark is typically thin and reddish-brown, offering some protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Picea excelsa reflexa is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
