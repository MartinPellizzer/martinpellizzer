Protea macrocephala

Protea macrocephala, commonly known as the giant sugar bush, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pale pink to white floral bracts. The plant is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds and insects, and it produces small, hard-coated seeds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
