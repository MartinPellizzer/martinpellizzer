Phellodendron nikkomontanum

Phellodendron nikkomontanum, commonly known as the Japanese yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark that often exudes a yellowish sap. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange to reddish berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
