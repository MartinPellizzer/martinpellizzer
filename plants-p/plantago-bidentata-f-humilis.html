Plantago bidentata f. humilis

Plantago bidentata f. humilis, commonly known as the low-growing plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, giving them a soft texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies.
