Photinia benthamiana var. glabrescens

Photinia benthamiana var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaf photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red or pink berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
