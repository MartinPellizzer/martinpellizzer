Phaseolus truxillensis f. sericea

Phaseolus truxillensis f. sericea, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a unique horticultural form of the passionflower genus.

It is characterized by its silky, hairy foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that display the typical passionflower structure with corona rays. This variety is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. The plant is a climbing vine that requires support for growth and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
