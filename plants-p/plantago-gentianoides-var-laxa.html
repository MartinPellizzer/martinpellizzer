Plantago gentianoides var. laxa

Plantago gentianoides var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, blue or purple flowers in late spring. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to its native environment.
