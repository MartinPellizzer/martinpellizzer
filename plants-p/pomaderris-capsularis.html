Pomaderris capsularis

Pomaderris capsularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honey bush, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Rhamnaceae family. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.

Pomaderris capsularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
