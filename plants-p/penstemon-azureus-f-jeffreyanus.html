Penstemon azureus f. jeffreyanus

Penstemon azureus f. jeffreyanus, commonly known as the Jeffrey's blue penstemon, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces spikes of bright blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies enhance its ecological value.
