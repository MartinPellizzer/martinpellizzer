Piper soledadense var. angustum

Piper soledadense var. angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sumpweed, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
