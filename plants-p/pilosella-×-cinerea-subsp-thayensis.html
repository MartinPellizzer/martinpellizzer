Pilosella × cinerea subsp. thayensis

Pilosella × cinerea subsp.

thayensis, commonly known as the silverleaf mouseear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it its common name, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flowers.

This subspecies is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
