Pseudomertensia echioides var. lahulensis

Pseudomertensia echioides var. lahulensis, commonly known as Lahul Himalayan vetch, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in raceme inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
