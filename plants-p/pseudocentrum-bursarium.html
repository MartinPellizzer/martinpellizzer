Pseudocentrum bursarium

Pseudocentrum bursarium, commonly known as the Bursera tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Burseraceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and smooth, often aromatic bark. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish aril.

Pseudocentrum bursarium is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
