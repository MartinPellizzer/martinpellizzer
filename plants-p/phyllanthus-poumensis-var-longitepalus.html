Phyllanthus poumensis var. longitepalus

Phyllanthus poumensis var. longitepalus, commonly known as the long-stalked phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
