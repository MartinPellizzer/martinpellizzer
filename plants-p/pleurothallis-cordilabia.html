Pleurothallis cordilabia

Pleurothallis cordilabia, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocky surfaces. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose cluster.

Pleurothallis cordilabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
