Phyteuma orbiculare subsp. ellipticifolium

Phyteuma orbiculare subsp.

ellipticifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf globe flower, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe. It features upright stems with lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and slightly rough to the touch.

The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of pale yellow to orange-red flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
