Piper machucanum

Piper machucanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Machuca pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and they are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the dense rainforests of its native habitat.
