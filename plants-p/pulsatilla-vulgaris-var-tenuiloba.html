Pulsatilla vulgaris var. tenuiloba

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms with purple petals and a central yellow stamen. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and prefers well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its delicate structure and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
