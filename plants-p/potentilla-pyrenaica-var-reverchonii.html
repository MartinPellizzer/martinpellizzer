Potentilla pyrenaica var. reverchonii

Potentilla pyrenaica var. reverchonii, commonly known as the Pyrenean cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically thrives in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually elliptical with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
