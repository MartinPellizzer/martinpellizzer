Pedilonum parvifolium

Pedilonum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pedilonum, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Pedilonum parvifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservation areas.
