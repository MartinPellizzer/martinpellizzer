Pollinia spectabilis var. ternatana

Pollinia spectabilis var. ternatana, commonly known as the Ternatan orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that display a variety of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces pollinia, which are compact masses of pollen that are transferred by specialized pollinators such as bees and wasps. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This orchid is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
