Phylica grisea

Phylica grisea, commonly known as the grey bristled phylica, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its greyish, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It is adapted to tolerate drought and salt spray, making it suitable for coastal environments.

Phylica grisea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine.
