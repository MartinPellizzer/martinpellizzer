Pinus mughus var. depressa

Pinus mughus var. depressa, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a variant of the mountain pine native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, stout needles that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, oval cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in cold, dry climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its resilience and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
