Persicaria lapathifolia var. linicola

Persicaria lapathifolia var. linicola, commonly known as the slender hogweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with toothed margins and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
