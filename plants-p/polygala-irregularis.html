Polygala irregularis

Polygala irregularis, commonly known as the irregular spikeflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged inflorescence. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, delicate flowers are often pink or white, and it produces a distinctive, spiral-shaped cluster of blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
