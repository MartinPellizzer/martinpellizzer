Prunus communis f. dulcis

Prunus communis f. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet cherry.

This variety is a cultivar of the common cherry, characterized by its large, sweet, and juicy fruits. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. It produces fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the edible berries.

The fruit's high sugar content and mild flavor make it a popular choice for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
