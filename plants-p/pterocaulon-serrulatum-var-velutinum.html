Pterocaulon serrulatum var. velutinum

Pterocaulon serrulatum var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvet leaf pterocaulon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often serrated, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

This variant is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
