Potentilla verna subsp. opaca

Potentilla verna subsp.

opaca, commonly known as the spring cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully develop.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and alternate, palmately compound leaves with five leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for garden cultivation.
