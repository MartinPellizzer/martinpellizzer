Plantago tomentosa subsp. selloana

Plantago tomentosa subsp.

selloana, commonly known as the woolly plantain, is a flowering plant species native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
