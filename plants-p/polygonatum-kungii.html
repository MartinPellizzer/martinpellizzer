Polygonatum kungii

Polygonatum kungii, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose panicles, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Polygonatum kungii is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
