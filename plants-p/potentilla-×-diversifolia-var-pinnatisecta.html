Potentilla × diversifolia var. pinnatisecta

Potentilla × diversifolia var. pinnatisecta, commonly known as the pinnatisecta cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems that are often covered in soft, grayish hairs. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, forming clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
