Pedalium caillaudii

Pedalium caillaudii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, legume-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its nocturnal blooming habit makes it a unique addition to gardens and greenhouses.
