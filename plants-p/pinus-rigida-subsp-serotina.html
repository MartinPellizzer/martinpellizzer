Pinus rigida subsp. serotina

Pinus rigida subsp.

serotina, commonly known as the Appalachian pine, is a subspecies native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically 4 to 6 inches long.

The cones of this subspecies are usually retained on the tree for several years before opening, a trait known as serotiny. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This resilient tree plays an important role in providing habitat and stabilizing soil in its native range.
