Pseuderanthemum sneidernii

Pseuderanthemum sneidernii, commonly known as the sneidern's false flower, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture, while its inflorescences consist of clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful bracts.

Pseuderanthemum sneidernii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
