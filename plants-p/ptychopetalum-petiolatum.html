Ptychopetalum petiolatum

Ptychopetalum petiolatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ptychopetalum* tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are often pink or white in color. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree has a shallow root system and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various soil types.
