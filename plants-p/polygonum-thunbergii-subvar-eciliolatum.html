Polygonum thunbergii subvar. eciliolatum

Polygonum thunbergii subvar. eciliolatum, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that often form dense clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is known for its vigorous growth and ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and rhizomatous root system.
