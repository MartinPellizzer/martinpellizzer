Pollinia setifolia

Pollinia setifolia, commonly known as the silky pollinia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique pollinia, which are clustered and covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing in dense clumps.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
