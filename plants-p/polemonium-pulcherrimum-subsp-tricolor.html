Polemonium pulcherrimum subsp. tricolor

Polemonium pulcherrimum subsp.

tricolor, commonly known as the three-color bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that display three distinct colors—typically white, pink, and purple—on the same plant.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
