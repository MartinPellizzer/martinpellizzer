Plectranthus argentatus

Plectranthus argentatus, commonly known as silver mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to lavender in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus argentatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic foliage.
