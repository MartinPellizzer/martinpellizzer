Persoonia angustiflora var. burracoppinensis

Persoonia angustiflora var. burracoppinensis, commonly known as the Burra coppin pear, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

This variety is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
