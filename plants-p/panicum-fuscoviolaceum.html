Panicum fuscoviolaceum

Panicum fuscoviolaceum, commonly known as the purple false lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, tufted inflorescences that bear small, violet-colored spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and grasslands. Its foliage is often bluish-green, and the leaves are narrow with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
