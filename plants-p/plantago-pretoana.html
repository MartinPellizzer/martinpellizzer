Plantago pretoana

Plantago pretoana, commonly known as black plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
