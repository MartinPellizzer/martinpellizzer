Pithecellobium coccineum

Pithecellobium coccineum, commonly known as the red pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
