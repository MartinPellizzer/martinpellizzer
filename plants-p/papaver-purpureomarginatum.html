Papaver purpureomarginatum

Papaver purpureomarginatum, commonly known as the purple-margin poppy, is a striking annual herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, with distinctive purple margins. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its vibrant blooms.
