Piper controversum

Piper controversum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but often have a spicy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
