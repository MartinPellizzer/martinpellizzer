Ptilotus siphonandrus

Ptilotus siphonandrus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pale pink or white flowers that resemble ghostly shapes. The plant has a low-growing, prostrate habit with succulent, hairy stems and narrow, fleshy leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects and are known for their unique, ephemeral appearance.
