Prunus cerasifera var. stenodrunacea

Prunus cerasifera var. stenodrunacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, cherry-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various climates and suitable for landscaping purposes.
