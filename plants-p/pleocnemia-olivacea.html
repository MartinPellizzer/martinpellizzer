Pleocnemia olivacea

Pleocnemia olivacea, commonly known as the olive fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in small, rounded structures called sorus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Pleocnemia olivacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
