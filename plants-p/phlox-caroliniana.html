Phlox caroliniana

Phlox caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native gardens and wetland habitats.
