Pellaea bridgesii

Pellaea bridgesii, commonly known as the Bridgess' maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

Pellaea bridgesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
