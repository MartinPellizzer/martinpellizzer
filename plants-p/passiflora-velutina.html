Passiflora velutina

Passiflora velutina, commonly known as the velvet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a edible, orange-red berry that contains multiple seeds.

Passiflora velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
