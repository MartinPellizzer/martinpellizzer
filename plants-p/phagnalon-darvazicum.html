Phagnalon darvazicum

Phagnalon darvazicum, commonly known as the Darvazian phagnalon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Afghanistan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in composite flower heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes and in dry grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
