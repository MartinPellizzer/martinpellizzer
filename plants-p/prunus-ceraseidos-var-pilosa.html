Prunus ceraseidos var. pilosa

Prunus ceraseidos var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries that ripen in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique foliage and fruit make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
