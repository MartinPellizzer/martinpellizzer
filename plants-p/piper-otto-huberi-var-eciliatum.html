Piper otto-huberi var. eciliatum

Piper otto-huberi var. eciliatum, commonly known as the Otto-Huber's pepper or the Madagascar pepper, is a tropical vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the Piper genus.
