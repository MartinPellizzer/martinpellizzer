Pimelea prostrata var. urvilleana

Pimelea prostrata var. urvilleana, commonly known as the prostrate bottlebrush, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It features dense, wiry stems that often root at the nodes, forming a mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bright red flowers in cylindrical clusters, which are characteristic of the bottlebrush genus. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

This variety is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
