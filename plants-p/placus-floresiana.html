Placus floresiana

Placus floresiana, commonly known as the Flores Island orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Indonesian island of Flores.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its delicate, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink.

Due to habitat destruction, Placus floresiana is considered endangered and is protected in its native region.
