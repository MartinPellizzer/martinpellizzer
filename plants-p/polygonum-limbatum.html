Polygonum limbatum

Polygonum limbatum, commonly known as heartleaf polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, often forming dense mats.

Polygonum limbatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
