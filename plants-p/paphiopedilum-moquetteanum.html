Paphiopedilum moquetteanum

Paphiopedilum moquetteanum, commonly known as the Moquettean Paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green to brownish leaves and its unique, intricate flower structure, which resembles a pouch or a slipper. The plant produces fragrant, showy blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade, making it a challenging but rewarding species for orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited natural habitat and threats from over-collection, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
