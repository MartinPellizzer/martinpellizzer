Psoralea bigelovii

Psoralea bigelovii, commonly known as the desert false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and a woody stem, adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Psoralea bigelovii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
