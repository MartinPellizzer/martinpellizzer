Pellionia calcifera

Pellionia calcifera, commonly known as the limestone pellionia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is typically found in rocky or calcareous soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually white or pale green and appear in clusters.

Pellionia calcifera is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
