Panicum strigosum var. puberulum

Panicum strigosum var. puberulum, commonly known as silky grassland panicum, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open prairies and disturbed sites.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
