Protea petiolaris subsp. petiolaris

Protea petiolaris subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a species native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to reddish-pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. It is part of the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy inflorescences and ecological importance in fire-prone ecosystems.

The subspecies is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the region's rich biodiversity.
