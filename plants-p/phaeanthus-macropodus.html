Phaeanthus macropodus

Phaeanthus macropodus, commonly known as the large-lobed phaeanthus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Phaeanthus macropodus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with adequate sunlight.
