Pladera virgata

Pladera virgata, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 30 inches tall, often with a few leaves along their length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are characteristic of the orchid family. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

Pladera virgata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and microorganisms.
