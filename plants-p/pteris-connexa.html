Pteris connexa

Pteris connexa, commonly known as the crested water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed fronds that often have a crested or curled appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the propagation of the plant.

Pteris connexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
