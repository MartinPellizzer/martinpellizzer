Pouteria ambelaniifolia

Pouteria ambelaniifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and distinctive, elongated fruits that contain edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in panicles, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in traditional medicine.
