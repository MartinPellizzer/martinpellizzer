Paederia foetida

Paederia foetida, commonly known as stinking beggar's titi, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable in its flowers and fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Paederia foetida is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
