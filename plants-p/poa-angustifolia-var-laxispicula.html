Poa angustifolia var. laxispicula

Poa angustifolia var. laxispicula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass with long awns, is a variant of the narrow-leaved grass species.

It is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in open, grassy areas and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters with long, slender awns.

This variety is often found in meadows and along the edges of woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
