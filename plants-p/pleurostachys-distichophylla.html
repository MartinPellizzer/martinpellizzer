Pleurostachys distichophylla

Pleurostachys distichophylla, commonly known as the two-ranked fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, two-ranked arrangement of leaflets, which gives it a unique and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are usually elongated and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
