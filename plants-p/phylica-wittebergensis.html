Phylica wittebergensis

Phylica wittebergensis, commonly known as the Witteberg bottlebrush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds enclosed in a fibrous capsule, which is adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phylica wittebergensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
