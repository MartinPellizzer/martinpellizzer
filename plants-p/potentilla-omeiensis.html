Potentilla omeiensis

Potentilla omeiensis, commonly known as the Sichuan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, and it produces small, aggregate fruits.

Potentilla omeiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
