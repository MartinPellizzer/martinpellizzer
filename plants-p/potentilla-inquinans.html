Potentilla inquinans

Potentilla inquinans, commonly known as the black potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are generally dark green and have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla inquinans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
