Prunus moritziana var. robusta

Prunus moritziana var. robusta, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features oval to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellow-orange drupes. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
