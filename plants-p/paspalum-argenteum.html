Paspalum argenteum

Paspalum argenteum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months.

Paspalum argenteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
