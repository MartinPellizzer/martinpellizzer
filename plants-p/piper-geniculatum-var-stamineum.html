Piper geniculatum var. stamineum

Piper geniculatum var. stamineum, commonly known as the staminal pepper or staminal betel, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly pubescent surface.

The plant is notable for its unique floral structure, with stamens that are prominently displayed and often used in traditional herbal practices.
