Primula chamissonis

Primula chamissonis, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often at high altitudes. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat, blooming in early spring.

Primula chamissonis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes.
