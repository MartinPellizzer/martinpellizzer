Pandanus kamptos

Pandanus kamptos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the top of the plant. Its roots and leaves are sometimes used in local cuisines and medicinal practices.

Overall, Pandanus kamptos plays an important ecological and cultural role in its native habitats.
