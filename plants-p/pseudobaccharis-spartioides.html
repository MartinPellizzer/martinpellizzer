Pseudobaccharis spartioides

Pseudobaccharis spartioides, commonly known as silverleaf baccharis, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs that reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Its structure and adaptations make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
