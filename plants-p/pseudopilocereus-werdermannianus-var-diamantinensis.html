Pseudopilocereus werdermannianus var. diamantinensis

Pseudopilocereus werdermannianus var. diamantinensis, commonly known as the Diamond Nightshade Cactus, is a succulent native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
