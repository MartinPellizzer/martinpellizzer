Pyrus vsevolodovii

Pyrus vsevolodovii, commonly known as the Vsevolodov's pear, is a species of pear native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats at high altitudes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are generally not cultivated for commercial use. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptation to harsh environmental conditions.
