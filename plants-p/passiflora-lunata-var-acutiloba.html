Passiflora lunata var. acutiloba

Passiflora lunata var. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough, textured surface, while the stems are hairy and woody. This species produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
