Phyllostachys edulis f. holochrysa

Phyllostachys edulis f. holochrysa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms that contrast with its green leaves. This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant is cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
