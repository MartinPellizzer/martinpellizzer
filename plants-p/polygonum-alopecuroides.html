Polygonum alopecuroides

Polygonum alopecuroides, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both an ornamental and potentially invasive species.
