Phyllodes pavonina

Phyllodes pavonina, commonly known as the pink phyllode acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are typically pink to red in color. These phyllodes are modified stems that perform the function of leaves, aiding in photosynthesis. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
