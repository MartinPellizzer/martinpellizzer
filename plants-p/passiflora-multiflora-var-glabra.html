Passiflora multiflora var. glabra

Passiflora multiflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties in the Passiflora genus. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that ripens to a yellow or orange color.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and ecological value for pollinators.
