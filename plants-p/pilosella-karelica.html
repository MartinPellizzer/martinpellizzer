Pilosella karelica

Pilosella karelica, commonly known as Karelian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, linear, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella karelica is often considered a weed but has ecological value in supporting pollinators.
