Phleum pratense f. bracteatum

Phleum pratense f. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracted timothy grass, is a variant of the timothy grass species.

It is characterized by its distinctive bracts that are often more prominent than in the typical form. This plant is typically found in meadows and grasslands, thriving in temperate climates. The leaves are long and narrow, with a bluish-green hue, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle.

It plays a role in both ecological and agricultural contexts, serving as forage for livestock and supporting local biodiversity.
