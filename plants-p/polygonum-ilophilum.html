Polygonum ilophilum

Polygonum ilophilum, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a preference for well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Polygonum ilophilum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
