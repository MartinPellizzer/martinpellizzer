Puccinellia battandieri

Puccinellia battandieri, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to saline and drought-prone environments, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Its ecological significance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
