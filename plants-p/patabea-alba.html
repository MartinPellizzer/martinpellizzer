Patabea alba

Patabea alba, commonly known as the white patabea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate white petals and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Patabea alba has limited commercial use but is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in its native habitat.
