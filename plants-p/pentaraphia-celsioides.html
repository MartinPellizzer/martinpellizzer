Pentaraphia celsioides

Pentaraphia celsioides, commonly known as the celsia orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
