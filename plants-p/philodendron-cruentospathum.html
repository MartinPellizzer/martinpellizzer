Philodendron cruentospathum

Philodendron cruentospathum, commonly known as the Crimson-veined Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins against a deep green background. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant appearance.

It prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
