Pappophorum pumilio

Pappophorum pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf pappophore, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is characterized by its delicate, wind-dispersed seeds, which are enclosed in a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in seed distribution. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its small size and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
