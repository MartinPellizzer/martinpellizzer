Prunus mume var. rosea

Prunus mume var. rosea, commonly known as the pink Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which bloom in early spring with a delicate pink hue. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture, and it has a compact growth habit.

Prunus mume var. rosea is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
