Panicum bicolor

Panicum bicolor, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a warm-season grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden to yellowish inflorescences that resemble a foxtail, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in disturbed areas and agricultural fields. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

Panicum bicolor is often used as a forage crop and can also serve as a ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance.
