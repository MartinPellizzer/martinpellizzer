Plantago lanceolata subvar. eriophora

Plantago lanceolata subvar. eriophora, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is commonly used in herbal remedies.
