Philodendron immixtum

Philodendron immixtum, commonly known as the mixed-leaf philodendron, is a climbing tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns, adding visual interest to its foliage. The plant has a vining growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trained on supports. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a glossy texture, which helps it thrive in humid environments.

Philodendron immixtum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
