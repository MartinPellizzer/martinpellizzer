Pistorinia hispanica var. minor

Pistorinia hispanica var. minor, commonly known as the Spanish thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its medicinal and culinary uses, it has been historically valued in traditional herbal practices.
