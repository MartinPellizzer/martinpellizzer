Prunus jamasakura f. regalis

Prunus jamasakura f. regalis, commonly known as the Royal Cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree characterized by its distinctive floral display.

This ornamental plant is noted for its large, showy white flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring. The tree is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and public spaces. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Prunus species such as the Japanese cherry (Prunus serrulata).

The Royal Cherry is valued for its graceful form and its ability to thrive in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
