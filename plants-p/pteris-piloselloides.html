Pteris piloselloides

Pteris piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris piloselloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
