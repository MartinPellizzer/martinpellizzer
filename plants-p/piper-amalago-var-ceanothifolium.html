Piper amalago var. ceanothifolium

Piper amalago var. ceanothifolium, commonly known as the Ceanothifolium pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper amalago species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in an alternate pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that eventually develop into oval, berry-like fruits. Its foliage has a distinct aromatic quality, often described as peppery or spicy.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional herbal practices.
