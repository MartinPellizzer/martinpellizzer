Pinguicula lateciliata

Pinguicula lateciliata, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are covered in glandular hairs that secrete digestive enzymes.

Pinguicula lateciliata is a popular specimen for carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological role.
