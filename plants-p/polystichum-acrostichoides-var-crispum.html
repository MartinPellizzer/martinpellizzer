Polystichum acrostichoides var. crispum

Polystichum acrostichoides var. crispum, commonly known as the curly fern, is a variant of the acrostichoides fern species.

It is a perennial evergreen plant native to the eastern United States, particularly in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features fronds that are characterized by their wavy, curled edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
