Pancratium ovatum

Pancratium ovatum, commonly known as the sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

Pancratium ovatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
