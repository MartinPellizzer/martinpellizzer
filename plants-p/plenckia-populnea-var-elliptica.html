Plenckia populnea var. elliptica

Plenckia populnea var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
