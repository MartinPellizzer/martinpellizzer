Porpa repens

Porpa repens, commonly known as the creeping porpa, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions.

It features slender, trailing stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Porpa repens is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
