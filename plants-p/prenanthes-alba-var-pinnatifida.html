Prenanthes alba var. pinnatifida

Prenanthes alba var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the white rayed beggar's-ticks, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are finely toothed along the edges. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in late summer. Its stems are hairy and have sticky glands, and the plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
