Plectaneia elastica var. firingalavensis

Plectaneia elastica var. firingalavensis, commonly known as the Fireleaf Elastica, is a unique variant of the Elastica genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like foliage that ranges from deep red to orange hues, which intensifies under direct sunlight. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with thick, waxy leaves that provide resistance to drought and high temperatures. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability and ornamental value, often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
