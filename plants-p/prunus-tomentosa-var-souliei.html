Prunus tomentosa var. souliei

Prunus tomentosa var. souliei, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage that turns reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-sour red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for garden use in temperate climates.
