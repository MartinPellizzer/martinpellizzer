Pilosella flagellaris

Pilosella flagellaris, commonly known as the slender pappus weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its small, yellowish flowers are surrounded by a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Pilosella flagellaris is often considered a weed but has ecological value in supporting pollinators.
