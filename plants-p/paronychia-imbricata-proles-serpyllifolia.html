Paronychia imbricata proles serpyllifolia

Paronychia imbricata proles serpyllifolia, commonly known as the creeping bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense mat, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense cover make it both a desirable ground cover and a potential weed in cultivated areas.
