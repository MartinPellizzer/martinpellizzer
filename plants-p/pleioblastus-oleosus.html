Pleioblastus oleosus

Pleioblastus oleosus, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, feathery plumes that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Pleioblastus oleosus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its use in traditional landscaping.
