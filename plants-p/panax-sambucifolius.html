Panax sambucifolius

Panax sambucifolius, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by red berries. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its medicinal value, it is often cultivated for use in herbal remedies and supplements.
