Paeonia albiflora f. nuda

Paeonia albiflora f. nuda, commonly known as the naked peony, is a botanical variant of the white peony.

It is characterized by its lack of stipules on the leaf stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, white, single flowers with a subtle fragrance. It is native to regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This form is prized in horticulture for its elegant blooms and hardy nature.
