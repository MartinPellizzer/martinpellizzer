Papaver alpinum subvar. angustius

Papaver alpinum subvar. angustius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved alpine poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly the Alps and Carpathian Mountains. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. Its narrow, elongated leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

This species thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats at high elevations.
