Prunus communis subsp. divaricata

Prunus communis subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the spreading cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, often arching branches that give it a graceful appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible red berries in the summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
