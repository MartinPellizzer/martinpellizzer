Pentas lanceolata subsp. lanceolata

Pentas lanceolata subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf pentas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.

The plant is typically shrub-like, with stems that can be either erect or prostrate, depending on the growing conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
