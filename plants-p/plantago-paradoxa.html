Plantago paradoxa

Plantago paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spikelike inflorescence. Its leaves are characterized by their distinctive wavy margins and a thick, waxy coating.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
