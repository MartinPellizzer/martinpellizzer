Pandanus unwinii

Pandanus unwinii, commonly known as the Cook Islands pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Cook Islands and nearby Pacific islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often used in traditional crafts. Its leaves are fibrous and have been historically used for thatching and weaving.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and windy conditions.
