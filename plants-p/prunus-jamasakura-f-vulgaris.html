Prunus jamasakura f. vulgaris

Prunus jamasakura f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common cherry blossom, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, single-layered flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal beauty. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The fruit, a small cherry, is edible but less commonly consumed compared to other Prunus species.
