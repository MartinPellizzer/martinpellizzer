Polymnia grandis

Polymnia grandis, commonly known as the giant polymnia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and marshy areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain insect species and its potential medicinal properties.
