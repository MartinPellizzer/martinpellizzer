Panargyrus spinosus

Panargyrus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny pteridium, is a fern-like plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its feathery fronds and spiny structures along the rachis. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Panargyrus spinosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
