Phyllochilium

Phyllochilium, commonly known as the liverworts, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the division Marchantiophyta.

These plants typically grow in moist, shaded environments and are often found on rocks, soil, or decaying wood. Phyllochilium species are characterized by their leaf-like structures, which are usually arranged in a rosette or layered pattern. They lack true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having structures called thallus that absorb water and nutrients directly.

Due to their small size and preference for humid conditions, they play an important role in ecological systems as pioneer species in moist habitats.
