Pilea javanica

Pilea javanica, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that resemble coins. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with a short, thick stem. Its leaves are glossy and have a slightly waxy texture, often with a pale green underside.

Pilea javanica is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
