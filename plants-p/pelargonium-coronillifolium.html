Pelargonium coronillifolium

Pelargonium coronillifolium, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
