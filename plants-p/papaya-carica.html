Papaya carica

Papaya carica, commonly known as the papaya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a single, large, non-branching fruit that ripens quickly and is rich in vitamins and enzymes. Its latex, known as papain, is used in food processing and pharmaceuticals.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
