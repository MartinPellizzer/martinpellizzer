Pithecellobium bertolonii

Pithecellobium bertolonii, commonly known as the Brazilian burr acacia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a woody pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.
