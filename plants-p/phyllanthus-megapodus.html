Phyllanthus megapodus

Phyllanthus megapodus, commonly known as the giant phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
