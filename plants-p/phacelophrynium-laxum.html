Phacelophrynium laxum

Phacelophrynium laxum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention in its humid habitat. Its root system is notable for producing starchy tubers, which are used as a food source in some regions.

Phacelophrynium laxum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rainforests.
