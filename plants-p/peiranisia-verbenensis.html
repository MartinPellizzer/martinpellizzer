Peiranisia verbenensis

Peiranisia verbenensis, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Peiranisia verbenensis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
