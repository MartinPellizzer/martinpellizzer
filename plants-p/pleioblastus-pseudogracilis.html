Pleioblastus pseudogracilis

Pleioblastus pseudogracilis, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, arching culms that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture and a bluish-green hue.

Pleioblastus pseudogracilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
