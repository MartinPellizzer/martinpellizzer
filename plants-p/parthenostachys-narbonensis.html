Parthenostachys narbonensis

Parthenostachys narbonensis, commonly known as the Narbonne grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as fields and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
