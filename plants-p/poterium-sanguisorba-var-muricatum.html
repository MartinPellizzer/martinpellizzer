Poterium sanguisorba var. muricatum

Poterium sanguisorba var. muricatum, commonly known as the red-rooted sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are palmately lobed with a rough, serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and flies. Its roots are rich in tannins and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
