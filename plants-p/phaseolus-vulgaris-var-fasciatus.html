Phaseolus vulgaris var. fasciatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. fasciatus, commonly known as the streaked bean, is a variant of the common bean characterized by its distinctive mottled or striped foliage.

This plant is native to the Americas and belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a deep green background and lighter green or white streaks running through them. It is primarily cultivated for its edible pods, which are harvested before the beans inside fully mature.

The streaked bean is valued for its ornamental appeal and can be grown as both a vegetable and a decorative plant.
