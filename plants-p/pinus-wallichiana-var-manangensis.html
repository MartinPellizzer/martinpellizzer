Pinus wallichiana var. manangensis

Pinus wallichiana var. manangensis, commonly known as the Manang pine, is a variant of the blue pine native to the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a distinct morphology with a conical shape and thick, bark-like trunk. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

The botanical features of this species are adapted to withstand cold temperatures and high-altitude conditions.
