Prunus mahaleb subsp. typica

Prunus mahaleb subsp.

typica, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a species of shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often considered bitter. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The bark and leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
