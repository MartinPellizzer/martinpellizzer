Pleroma salviifolium

Pleroma salviifolium, commonly known as the purple-flowered pleroma, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pleroma salviifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
