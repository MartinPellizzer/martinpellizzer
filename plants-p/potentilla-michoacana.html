Potentilla michoacana

Potentilla michoacana, commonly known as Mexican cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged oppositely on the stems.

Potentilla michoacana is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
