Panicum brachytrichum

Panicum brachytrichum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or blue during the flowering season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a common component of prairie ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
