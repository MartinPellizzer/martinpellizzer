Peperomia parvisagittata

Peperomia parvisagittata, commonly known as the small arrowleaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, waxy, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or bronze tinge on the underside. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, arranged in clusters near the base of the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia parvisagittata is a low-maintenance plant well-suited for indoor cultivation.
