Pyracantha lucida

Pyracantha lucida, commonly known as the evergreen hawthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are a food source for various birds and small mammals. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.

Pyracantha lucida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
