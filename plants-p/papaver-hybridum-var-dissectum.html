Papaver hybridum var. dissectum

Papaver hybridum var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected poppy, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features showy, vibrant red or orange flowers with a distinctive, deeply lobed corona. The plant has finely dissected, fern-like leaves that add to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
