Peloria genistifolia

Peloria genistifolia, commonly known as the white broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Peloria genistifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
