Pleconax coniflora

Pleconax coniflora, commonly known as the pinecone flower, is a unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-like inflorescences that resemble pinecones, which are actually clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pleconax coniflora is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
