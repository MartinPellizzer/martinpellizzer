Polystichum magnificum

Polystichum magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped, dark green fronds that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile spore-bearing spikes developing on the upper portion of the fronds. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

The leaves are typically leathery and have a distinctive, elegant appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
