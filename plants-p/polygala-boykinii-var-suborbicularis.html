Polygala boykinii var. suborbicularis

Polygala boykinii var. suborbicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a slightly curled or rolled appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
