Paphiopedilum barbigerum f. aureum

Paphiopedilum barbigerum f. aureum, commonly known as the golden-liped vulture orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous sepals and petals that often display a golden hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, lip-like structure that is highly specialized for pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

This orchid is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
