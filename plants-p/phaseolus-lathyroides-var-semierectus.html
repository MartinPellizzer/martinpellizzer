Phaseolus lathyroides var. semierectus

Phaseolus lathyroides var. semierectus, commonly known as the creeping pole bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical pods. The plant is characterized by its semi-erect growth habit, which distinguishes it from other Phaseolus species. Its seeds are typically small and oval, with a smooth surface.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in both ornamental and agricultural settings.
