Pycnandra sessiliflora

Pycnandra sessiliflora, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and small, clustered flowers. The plant has smooth, dark bark and produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
