Paspalum sanguinale

Paspalum sanguinale, commonly known as red paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish tones in its foliage and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in areas with abundant moisture. Its flowering spikes are often reddish-brown, and the leaves are narrow with a bluish-green hue.

Paspalum sanguinale is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
