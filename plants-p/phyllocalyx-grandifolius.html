Phyllocalyx grandifolius

Phyllocalyx grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved phyllocalyx, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
