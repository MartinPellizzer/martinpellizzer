Pterocarpus brachypterus

Pterocarpus brachypterus, commonly known as the Malacca ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

The tree is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
