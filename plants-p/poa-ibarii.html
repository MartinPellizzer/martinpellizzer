Poa ibarii

Poa ibarii, commonly known as the Ibarian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Poa ibarii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
