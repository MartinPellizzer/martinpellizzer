Prunus fimbriisepala

Prunus fimbriisepala, commonly known as the fringed-petaled cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive, fringed petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible fruits.

The botanical features of Prunus fimbriisepala make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
