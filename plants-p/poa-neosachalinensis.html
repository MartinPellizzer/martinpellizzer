Poa neosachalinensis

Poa neosachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Russian Far East, particularly Sakhalin Island.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Poa neosachalinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and resilience.
