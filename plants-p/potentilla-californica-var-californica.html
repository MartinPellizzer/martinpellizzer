Potentilla californica var. californica

Potentilla californica var. californica, commonly known as California cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
