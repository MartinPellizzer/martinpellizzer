Passiflora mansoi

Passiflora mansoi, commonly known as the Thai passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild fragrance.

Passiflora mansoi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
