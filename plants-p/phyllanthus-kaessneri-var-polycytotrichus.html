Phyllanthus kaessneri var. polycytotrichus

Phyllanthus kaessneri var. polycytotrichus, commonly known as the African soursop or African starfruit, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, green berries. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

The berries are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.
