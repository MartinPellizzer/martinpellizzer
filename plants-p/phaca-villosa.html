Phaca villosa

Phaca villosa, commonly known as the hairy phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along riverbanks.

Phaca villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
