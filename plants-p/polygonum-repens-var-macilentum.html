Polygonum repens var. macilentum

Polygonum repens var. macilentum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features prostrate growth habit with rhizomatous stems that spread along the ground. The leaves are elongated, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. Small, greenish-white flowers are produced in clusters, typically in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.
