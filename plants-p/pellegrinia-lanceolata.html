Pellegrinia lanceolata

Pellegrinia lanceolata, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a silvery-green color, covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
