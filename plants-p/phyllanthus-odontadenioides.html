Phyllanthus odontadenioides

Phyllanthus odontadenioides, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, featuring palm-like leaves that are pinnately compound. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by yellowish fruits resembling dates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient choice for landscaping in arid regions.
