Potentilla gracilis var. elmeri

Potentilla gracilis var. elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or sagebrush habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
