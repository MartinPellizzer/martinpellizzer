Phellodendron piriforme

Phellodendron piriforme, commonly known as the Indian birch or wild walnut, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin layers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a pointed tip.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the bark containing compounds used in traditional medicine.
