Paepalanthus brachypus

Paepalanthus brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape with dense, spiny stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged exterior. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Paepalanthus brachypus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
