Piper laevilimbum

Piper laevilimbum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species known for their peppery flavor. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible but have a sharp, peppery taste.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
