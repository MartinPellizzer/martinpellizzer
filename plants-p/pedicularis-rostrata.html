Pedicularis rostrata

Pedicularis rostrata, commonly known as the beakflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes over 300 species of hemiparasitic plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers, which resemble a beak, and its ability to partially parasitize other plants for nutrients. It typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.
