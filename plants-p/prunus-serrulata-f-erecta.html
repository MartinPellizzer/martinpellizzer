Prunus serrulata f. erecta

Prunus serrulata f. erecta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry tree, is a cultivar of the Japanese flowering cherry.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, compact form, making it suitable for small spaces. The plant produces showy pink or white flowers in early spring, which bloom before the leaves emerge. It is a deciduous tree with simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
