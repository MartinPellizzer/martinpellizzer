Prunus × lannesiana f. gosho-odora

Prunus × lannesiana f. gosho-odora, commonly known as the fragrant cherry, is a unique hybrid plant that combines traits from Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and its most notable feature, the highly fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, emit a sweet, floral aroma that attracts pollinators.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and aromatic qualities.
