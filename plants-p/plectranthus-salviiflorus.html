Plectranthus salviiflorus

Plectranthus salviiflorus, commonly known as the blue plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically violet or blue in hue, arranged in clusters.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
