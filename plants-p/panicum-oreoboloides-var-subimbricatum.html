Panicum oreoboloides var. subimbricatum

Panicum oreoboloides var. subimbricatum, commonly known as the mountain panicgrass, is a grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
