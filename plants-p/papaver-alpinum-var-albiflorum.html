Papaver alpinum var. albiflorum

Papaver alpinum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the alpine white poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
