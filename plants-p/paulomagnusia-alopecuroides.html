Paulomagnusia alopecuroides

Paulomagnusia alopecuroides, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
