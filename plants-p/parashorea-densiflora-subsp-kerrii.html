Parashorea densiflora subsp. kerrii

Parashorea densiflora subsp.

kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage.

The plant typically grows in lowland and hill forests, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and plays a significant role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
