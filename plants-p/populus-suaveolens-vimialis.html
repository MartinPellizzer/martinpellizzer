Populus suaveolens vimialis

Populus suaveolens vimialis, commonly known as the sweetgum or American sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves with five to seven lobes and its showy, spherical clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The tree produces winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its ability to spread across various habitats. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming furrowed with age, and it has a rapid growth rate, making it a common sight in forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is also notable for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions, enhancing its ecological adaptability.
