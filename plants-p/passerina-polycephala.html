Passerina polycephala

Passerina polycephala, commonly known as the hairy pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant produces compound leaves with a hairy texture and features bright yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in alpine environments and is adapted to cold climates.
