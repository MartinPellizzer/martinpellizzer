Phyllodes peruviana

Phyllodes peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian needle-scale, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
