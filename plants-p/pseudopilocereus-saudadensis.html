Pseudopilocereus saudadensis

Pseudopilocereus saudadensis, commonly known as the Sahuarita cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are often reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This cactus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
