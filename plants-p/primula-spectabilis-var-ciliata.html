Primula spectabilis var. ciliata

Primula spectabilis var. ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features basal rosettes of ovate, hairy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and horticulture.
