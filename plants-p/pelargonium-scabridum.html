Pelargonium scabridum

Pelargonium scabridum, commonly known as the roughleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces seed capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.

Pelargonium scabridum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
