Potentilla glandulifera var. longifolia

Potentilla glandulifera var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with glandular dots, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
