Phragmites communis var. ruscinonensis

Phragmites communis var. ruscinonensis, commonly known as the European common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous growth and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves and produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that disperse easily by wind. It plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread rapidly, often forming dense stands in marshes and along lake shores.
