Potamogeton × raunkiaeri

Potamogeton × raunkiaeri, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often rounded at the base. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters, and it thrives in shallow, freshwater environments.

Potamogeton × raunkiaeri plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
