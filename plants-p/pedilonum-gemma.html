Pedilonum gemma

Pedilonum gemma, commonly known as the gemmed foot plant, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive basal rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that form a compact structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, emerging from the center of the rosette. Its leaves are often adorned with tiny, bead-like structures called gemmae, which are used for vegetative reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, Pedilonum gemma is considered a rare and endangered species.
