Pithecellobium hondurense

Pithecellobium hondurense, commonly known as the Honduras pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces edible pods that contain small, sweet seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in traditional medicine.
