Piper lorentzii

Piper lorentzii, commonly known as Lorentz's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the Piper genus.
