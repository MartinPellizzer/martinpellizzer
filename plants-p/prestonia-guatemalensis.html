Prestonia guatemalensis

Prestonia guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan prestonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Guatemala, and is characterized by its woody vines and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually pink or red and attract various pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
