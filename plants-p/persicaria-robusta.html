Persicaria robusta

Persicaria robusta, commonly known as hardy flossflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and knotweed. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, with large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other aquatic environments.

Persicaria robusta is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
