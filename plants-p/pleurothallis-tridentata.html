Pleurothallis tridentata

Pleurothallis tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with three prominent sepals and petals. Its blooming period usually occurs during the rainy season, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
