Pilosella × cinerea

Pilosella × cinerea, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silver-gray foliage due to dense, fine hairs covering its leaves and stems. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Pilosella × cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
