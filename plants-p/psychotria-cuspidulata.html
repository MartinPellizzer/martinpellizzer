Psychotria cuspidulata

Psychotria cuspidulata, commonly known as the "Cape jasmine" or "Cape rose," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Psychotria cuspidulata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
