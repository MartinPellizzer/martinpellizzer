Pilosella pseudovahlii

Pilosella pseudovahlii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, which helps reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Pilosella pseudovahlii is often found in areas with limited soil, showcasing its adaptability to extreme climates.
