Pongamia elongata

Pongamia elongata, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Pongamia elongata is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
