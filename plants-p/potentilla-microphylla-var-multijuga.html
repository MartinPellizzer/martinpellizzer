Potentilla microphylla var. multijuga

Potentilla microphylla var. multijuga, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens as a decorative plant.
