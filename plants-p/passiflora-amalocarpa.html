Passiflora amalocarpa

Passiflora amalocarpa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora amalocarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and ecological importance for pollinators.
