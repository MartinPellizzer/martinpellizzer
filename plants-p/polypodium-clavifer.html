Polypodium clavifer

Polypodium clavifer, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to various parts of the world, including Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped fronds that are typically bipinnate, with small, clustered pinnae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or in forest undergrowth. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Polypodium clavifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
