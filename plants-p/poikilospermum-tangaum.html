Poikilospermum tangaum

Poikilospermum tangaum, commonly known as the Tangaum starfruit, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its botanical classification places it within the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to its unique flavor and potential health benefits.
