Plectocephalus formosus

Plectocephalus formosus, commonly known as the golden star ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters at the top of its upright stems. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
