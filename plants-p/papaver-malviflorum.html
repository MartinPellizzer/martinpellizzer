Papaver malviflorum

Papaver malviflorum, commonly known as the velvetleaf poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with a distinctive white or yellowish center. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to both cultivated gardens and natural landscapes.
