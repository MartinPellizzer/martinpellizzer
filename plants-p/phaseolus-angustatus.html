Phaseolus angustatus

Phaseolus angustatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and the seeds within the pods are edible when mature.

This plant is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
