Paeonia × moutan var. ocellata

Paeonia × moutan var. ocellata, commonly known as the white peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are dark green and velvety in texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many cultivated peonies.
