Phebalium elaeagnoides

Phebalium elaeagnoides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically in spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions.

Phebalium elaeagnoides is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
