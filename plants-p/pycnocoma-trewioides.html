Pycnocoma trewioides

Pycnocoma trewioides, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
