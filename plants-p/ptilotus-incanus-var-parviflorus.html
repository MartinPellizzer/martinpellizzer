Ptilotus incanus var. parviflorus

Ptilotus incanus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with spiky, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, often in dense clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance.

This variety is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
