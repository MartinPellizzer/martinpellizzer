Parsonsia crebriflora

Parsonsia crebriflora, commonly known as the red lily or red bellflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting yellow center. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, and it produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Parsonsia crebriflora thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
