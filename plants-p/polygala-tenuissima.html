Polygala tenuissima

Polygala tenuissima, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or violet, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
