Procris rectangularis

Procris rectangularis, commonly known as the rectangular fern, is a species of fern characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

Its fronds are typically elongated and have a distinct rectangular shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. It produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which release spores for reproduction.

Procris rectangularis is native to tropical regions and is valued for its aesthetic and ecological roles in its natural habitat.
