Plantago serraria var. microdon

Plantago serraria var. microdon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
