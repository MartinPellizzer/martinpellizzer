Platystylis alata

Platystylis alata, commonly known as the broad-leaved pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous rhizomes and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional practices and for their aromatic properties.

Platystylis alata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or wetland habitats.
