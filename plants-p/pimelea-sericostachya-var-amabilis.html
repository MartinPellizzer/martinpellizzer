Pimelea sericostachya var. amabilis

Pimelea sericostachya var. amabilis, commonly known as the silky-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-green leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens and landscaping.

The species is also notable for its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
