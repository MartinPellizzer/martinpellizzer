Pyrus chinensis

Pyrus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sweet, yellow or white fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fruit production.
