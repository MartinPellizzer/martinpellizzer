Pulicaria glutinosa subsp. somalensis

Pulicaria glutinosa subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali stickyweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its succulent stems and leaves help it retain moisture in harsh environments.

It is often found in coastal and inland desert areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
