Platydesma spathulatum

Platydesma spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaves and hollow, stinging hairs.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The botanical features of Platydesma spathulatum make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
