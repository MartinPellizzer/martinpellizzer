Polygala chamaebuxus var. purpurea

Polygala chamaebuxus var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple flame or purple milkvetch, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has wiry, branching stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
