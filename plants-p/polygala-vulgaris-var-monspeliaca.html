Polygala vulgaris var. monspeliaca

Polygala vulgaris var. monspeliaca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or greenish-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are often surrounded by leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
