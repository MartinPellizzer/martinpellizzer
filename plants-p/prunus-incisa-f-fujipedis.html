Prunus incisa f. fujipedis

Prunus incisa f. fujipedis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it suitable for hedging or container gardening.
