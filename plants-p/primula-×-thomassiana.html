Primula × thomassiana

Primula × thomassiana, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring, with flowers that are slightly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
