Prunus × donarium f. shojo

Prunus × donarium f. shojo, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The tree typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters and has smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, turning green in summer and yellow in autumn.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and cultural significance in Japan.
