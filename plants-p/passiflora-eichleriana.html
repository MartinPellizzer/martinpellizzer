Passiflora eichleriana

Passiflora eichleriana, commonly known as the Eichler's passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

Passiflora eichleriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
