Pedicularis tanacetifolia

Pedicularis tanacetifolia, commonly known as the meadow monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which is part of the family Orobanchaceae, commonly known as the broomrape family. This species is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that resemble a monkey's face, hence its common name. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
