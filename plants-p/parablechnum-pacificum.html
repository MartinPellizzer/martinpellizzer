Parablechnum pacificum

Parablechnum pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters, with slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments or along coastal bluffs.

Parablechnum pacificum is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and moderate maritime climates.
