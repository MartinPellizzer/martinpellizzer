Palmifolium pumilum

Palmifolium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, compact shrub native to tropical regions.

It features narrow, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds, which can be dispersed by animals.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
