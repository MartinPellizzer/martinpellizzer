Primulina bogneriana

Primulina bogneriana, commonly known as the Bogner's primula, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
