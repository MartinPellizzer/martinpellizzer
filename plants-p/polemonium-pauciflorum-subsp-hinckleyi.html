Polemonium pauciflorum subsp. hinckleyi

Polemonium pauciflorum subsp.

hinckleyi, commonly known as Hinckley's Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces a basal rosette of leaves and has a short, upright stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
