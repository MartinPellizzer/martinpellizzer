Pyrus arakiana

Pyrus arakiana, commonly known as the Arakian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, specifically Honshu and Kyushu. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its fruit in certain regions.
