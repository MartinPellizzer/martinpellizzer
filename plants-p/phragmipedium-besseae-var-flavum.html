Phragmipedium besseae var. flavum

Phragmipedium besseae var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, and its leaves are usually elliptical with a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
