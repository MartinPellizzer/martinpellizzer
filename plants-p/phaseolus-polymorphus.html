Phaseolus polymorphus

Phaseolus polymorphus, commonly known as the variable bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant produces a variety of bean types, including kidney, pinto, and red beans, which are widely cultivated for their nutritional value. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

Phaseolus polymorphus is an important species in both traditional and modern agriculture due to its adaptability and high yield.
