Pilosocereus goianus

Pilosocereus goianus, commonly known as the Goiania cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
