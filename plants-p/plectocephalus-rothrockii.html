Plectocephalus rothrockii

Plectocephalus rothrockii, commonly known as the Rothrock's milkweed, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert habitats.

Plectocephalus rothrockii is a valuable plant for pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees, due to its nectar-rich flowers.
