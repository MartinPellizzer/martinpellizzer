Pseudaechmea ambigua

Pseudaechmea ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous pseudaechmea, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom in clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in semi-shaded conditions.
