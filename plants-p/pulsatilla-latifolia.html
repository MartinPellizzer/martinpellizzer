Pulsatilla latifolia

Pulsatilla latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers and toxic sap. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are usually pale yellow to pink, with numerous stamens and a distinctive, rounded ovary.

The leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
