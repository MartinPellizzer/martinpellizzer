Plantago coronopus subvar. erecta

Plantago coronopus subvar. erecta, commonly known as upright plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and wound-healing properties.
