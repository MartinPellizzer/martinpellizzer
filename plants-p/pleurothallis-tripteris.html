Pleurothallis tripteris

Pleurothallis tripteris, commonly known as the three-winged orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the warmer months.

Pleurothallis tripteris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
