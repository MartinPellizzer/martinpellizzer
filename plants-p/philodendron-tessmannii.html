Philodendron tessmannii

Philodendron tessmannii, commonly known as the Tessmann's philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish-brown stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by berries containing seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
