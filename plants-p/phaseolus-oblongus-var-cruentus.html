Phaseolus oblongus var. cruentus

Phaseolus oblongus var. cruentus, commonly known as the crimson runner bean, is a variety of leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated, green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often harvested when young and tender. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
