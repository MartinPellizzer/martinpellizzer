Polium perenne

Polium perenne, commonly known as field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters and features a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types. The plant is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.
