Pleienta dodecandra

Pleienta dodecandra, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is often found in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to saline environments.
