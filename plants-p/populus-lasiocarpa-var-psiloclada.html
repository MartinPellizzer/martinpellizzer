Populus lasiocarpa var. psiloclada

Populus lasiocarpa var. psiloclada, commonly known as the Indian paintbrush, is a variant of the poplar species characterized by its striking, colorful bracts.

This plant is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, rocky soils. Its botanical features include slender stems and clusters of showy, petal-less bracts that range in color from red to orange. The leaves are typically small, lance-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
