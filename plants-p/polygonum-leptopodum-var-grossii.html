Polygonum leptopodum var. grossii

Polygonum leptopodum var. grossii, commonly known as the broadleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often reddish stems. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a prominent midrib and a hairy underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters called spikes.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
