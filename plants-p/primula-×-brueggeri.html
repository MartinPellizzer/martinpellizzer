Primula × brueggeri

Primula × brueggeri, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Primula japonica and Primula × sedgewickii.

It is characterized by its showy, bowl-shaped flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including white, pink, red, and purple. The plant is typically evergreen, with thick, fleshy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Primula × brueggeri make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
