Pedicularis minima

Pedicularis minima, commonly known as the dwarf monkeyflower, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it has a short, upright stem.

Pedicularis minima is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
