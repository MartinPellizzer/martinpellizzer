Polhillides velutina subsp. longibracteata

Polhillides velutina subsp.

longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract polhillides, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that enclose the inflorescence, giving it a unique appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
