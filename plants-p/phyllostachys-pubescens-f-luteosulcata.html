Phyllostachys pubescens f. luteosulcata

Phyllostachys pubescens f. luteosulcata, commonly known as the golden stripe bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green stripe pattern on the culms, which contrasts with the pale green base. This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
