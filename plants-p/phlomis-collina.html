Phlomis collina

Phlomis collina, commonly known as narrowleaf blazingstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Phlomis collina is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
