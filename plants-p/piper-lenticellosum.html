Piper lenticellosum

Piper lenticellosum, commonly known as the warty pepper or warty sarsaparilla, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous pepper species. The plant is characterized by its woody vines and heart-shaped leaves with distinctive warty bumps. Its flowers are small and green, typically clustered in catkin-like inflorescences.

The fruit, a small, three-lobed berry, is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regional cuisines.
