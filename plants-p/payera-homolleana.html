Payera homolleana

Payera homolleana, commonly known as the yellow-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, yellow blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a fine, waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Payera homolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
