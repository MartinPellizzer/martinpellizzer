Protea levisanus

Protea levisanus, commonly known as the White Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white, papery bracts that enclose small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
