Parathesis parvissima

Parathesis parvissima, commonly known as the small-leaf parathesis, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
