Pharnacium malabaricum

Pharnacium malabaricum, commonly known as Malabar thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
