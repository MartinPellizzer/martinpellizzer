Paeonia festiva

Paeonia festiva, commonly known as the Festive Peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and help the plant survive harsh winters.

Paeonia festiva is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
