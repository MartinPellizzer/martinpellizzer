Phoenix paludosa

Phoenix paludosa, commonly known as the marsh date palm, is a rare and unique species native to the wetlands of Florida.

It is characterized by its slender, clustering stems and long, feathery fronds that give it a grass-like appearance. Unlike typical palms, Phoenix paludosa thrives in moist, swampy environments rather than dry, sunny areas. Its small, clustered inflorescences produce fragrant, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
