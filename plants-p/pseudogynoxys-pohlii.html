Pseudogynoxys pohlii

Pseudogynoxys pohlii, commonly known as the Mexican goldenweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the spring and are followed by small, dry fruits.

Pseudogynoxys pohlii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
