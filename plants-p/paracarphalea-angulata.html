Paracarphalea angulata

Paracarphalea angulata, commonly known as the angular leaf goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features narrow, angular leaves with a distinctive veining pattern, and its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
