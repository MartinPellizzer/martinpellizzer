Piper externum

Piper externum, commonly known as the wild pepper or Indian pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 200 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive glossy appearance.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
