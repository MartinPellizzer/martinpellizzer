Poa lunata

Poa lunata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-green foliage and distinctive, tufted inflorescence that resembles a small, rounded cloud. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Poa lunata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
