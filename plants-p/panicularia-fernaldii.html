Panicularia fernaldii

Panicularia fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's panicled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It produces small, panicled inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
