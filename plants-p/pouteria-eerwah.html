Pouteria eerwah

Pouteria eerwah, commonly known as the black sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces large, egg-shaped fruits with a thick, leathery skin. The fruit's interior contains a smooth, creamy, pale yellow pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, nutty flavor. The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both agricultural and horticultural purposes.
