Populus sosnowskyi

Populus sosnowskyi, commonly known as the Siberian poplar, is a fast-growing tree native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. This species is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and exhibiting tolerance to extreme temperatures. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that aid in its dispersal.

The tree is often used in reforestation projects due to its resilience and rapid growth rate.
