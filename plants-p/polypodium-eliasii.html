Polypodium eliasii

Polypodium eliasii, commonly known as the Elias's shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to substrates like soil or rock.

Polypodium eliasii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
