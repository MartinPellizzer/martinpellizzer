Polygala praetermissa

Polygala praetermissa, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred petal.

It is often found in the western United States and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique floral structure.
