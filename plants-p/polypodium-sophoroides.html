Polypodium sophoroides

Polypodium sophoroides, commonly known as the Indian water fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous structure with dense clusters of fronds that are pinnately divided and often covered in fine hairs. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
