Polycarena silenoides

Polycarena silenoides, commonly known as the silvery sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Polycarena silenoides plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
