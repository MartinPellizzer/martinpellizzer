Pinus halepensis var. ceciliae

Pinus halepensis var. ceciliae, commonly known as the Cecilia Aleppo pine, is a variant of the Aleppo pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically shorter and more clustered than those of the typical Aleppo pine. The cones of this variety are usually smaller and more compact, with a distinctive shape that sets it apart from other Pinus species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

This variant is often found in rocky or coastal areas where it contributes to local biodiversity and ecosystem stability.
