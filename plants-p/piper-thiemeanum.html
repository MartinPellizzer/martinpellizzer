Piper thiemeanum

Piper thiemeanum, commonly known as the Thai pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered berries that mature to a dark red color. The plant produces a pungent, peppery flavor due to the presence of alkaloids such as piperine. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
