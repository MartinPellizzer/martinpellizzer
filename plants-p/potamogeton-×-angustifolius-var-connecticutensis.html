Potamogeton × angustifolius var. connecticutensis

Potamogeton × angustifolius var. connecticutensis, commonly known as the Connecticut pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinct appearance in freshwater habitats. The plant thrives in shallow to moderately deep water, often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with staminate and pistillate flowers occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of dioecious species.

This variety plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
