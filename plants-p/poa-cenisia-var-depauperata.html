Poa cenisia var. depauperata

Poa cenisia var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial grass with fine, elongated leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is characterized by its delicate appearance and ability to form dense mats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
