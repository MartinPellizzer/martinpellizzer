Paspalum mayanum

Paspalum mayanum, commonly known as Mayan grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
