Pelexia laminata

Pelexia laminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pelexia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Pelexia laminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
