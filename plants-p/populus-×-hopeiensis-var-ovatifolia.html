Populus × hopeiensis var. ovatifolia

Populus × hopeiensis var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poplar, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrower than those of its parent species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Populus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetland areas.

Its fast growth rate makes it a valuable species for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
