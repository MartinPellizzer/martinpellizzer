Pilea rotundata var. subintegrifolia

Pilea rotundata var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the African nettle or velvetleaf, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are slightly hairy and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
