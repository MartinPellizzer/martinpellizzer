Potentilla taurica var. bornmuelleri

Potentilla taurica var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Caucasian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
