Palaquium amboinense

Palaquium amboinense, commonly known as the Amboina apple or red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling apples in appearance. The fruit's pericarp is rich in antioxidants and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
