Phlox kelseyi subsp. kelseyi

Phlox kelseyi subsp.

kelseyi, commonly known as Kelse's phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
