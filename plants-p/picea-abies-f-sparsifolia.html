Picea abies f. sparsifolia

Picea abies f. sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparse-foliage Norway spruce, is a horticultural form of the Norway spruce (Picea abies).

It is characterized by its sparse and irregular arrangement of needle-like leaves, which gives it a distinctive, open appearance. The plant retains its foliage year-round, making it an evergreen with a unique aesthetic. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This form is particularly valued for its visual interest and suitability in landscape design.
