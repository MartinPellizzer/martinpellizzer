Podophyllum peltatum f. callicarpum

Podophyllum peltatum f. callicarpum, commonly known as the red-berried American bellflower, is a variant of the American bellflower plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive red berries that contrast with its large, shield-shaped leaves. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it produces showy white to pale yellow flowers in the spring.

The red berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
