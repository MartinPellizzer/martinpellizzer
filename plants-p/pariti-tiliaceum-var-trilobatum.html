Pariti tiliaceum var. trilobatum

Pariti tiliaceum var. trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed silk tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or trilobate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
