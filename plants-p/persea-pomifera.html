Persea pomifera

Persea pomifera, commonly known as the cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a unique, fragrant fruit with a creamy, custard-like texture. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind that contains a soft, edible pulp surrounded by large, inedible seeds. Botanically, it belongs to the family Lauraceae and is closely related to avocados and laurel trees.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
