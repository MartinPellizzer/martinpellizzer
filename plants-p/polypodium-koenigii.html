Polypodium koenigii

Polypodium koenigii, commonly known as the Indian kidney fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial evergreen fern characterized by its bi-pinnate fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued as an ornamental plant.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sporangia, which are clustered in sori.
