Pterocarpus tinctorius var. odoratus

Pterocarpus tinctorius var. odoratus, commonly known as the fragrant ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark wood and distinctive aromatic leaves that emit a sweet scent when crushed. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its hard, durable timber used in traditional crafts and furniture. It also produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's leaves and bark have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
