Peronocactus

Peronocactus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a genus of cacti native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant typically has a single stem with a crown of spines, and its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to red. Peronocactus species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

They thrive in full sun and well-draining soil, making them popular among cactus enthusiasts.
