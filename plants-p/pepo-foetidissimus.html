Pepo foetidissimus

Pepo foetidissimus, commonly known as the stinkapple, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, foul-smelling fruit which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural pesticide. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture.

The fruit ripens to a yellowish-orange color and is often used in local remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
