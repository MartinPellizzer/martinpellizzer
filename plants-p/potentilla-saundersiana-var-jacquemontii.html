Potentilla saundersiana var. jacquemontii

Potentilla saundersiana var. jacquemontii, commonly known as the Jacquemont's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, silvery-white or grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in a similar silvery pubescence.

It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and alpine gardens.
