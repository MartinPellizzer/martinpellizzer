Pleioblastus mayumianus

Pleioblastus mayumianus, commonly known as the Mayumian bamboo, is a species native to China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
