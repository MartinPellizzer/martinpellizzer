Primula amoena var. intermedia

Primula amoena var. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
