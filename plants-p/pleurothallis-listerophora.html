Pleurothallis listerophora

Pleurothallis listerophora, commonly known as the Lister's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing epiphytically on trees in the understory of rainforests. Its inflorescence is usually a single, elongated spike bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are often clustered closely together.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
