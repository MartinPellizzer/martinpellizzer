Pelargonium pilosum

Pelargonium pilosum, commonly known as the hairy geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium pilosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
