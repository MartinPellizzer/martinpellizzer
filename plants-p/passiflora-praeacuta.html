Passiflora praeacuta

Passiflora praeacuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Passiflora praeacuta is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
