Pilosella paragoga subsp. phalacrodes

Pilosella paragoga subsp.

phalacrodes, commonly known as the rock pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
