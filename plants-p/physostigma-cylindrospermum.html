Physostigma cylindrospermum

Physostigma cylindrospermum, commonly known as the African water plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit, with long, cylindrical leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Physostigma cylindrospermum is also noted for its role in traditional medicine, where various parts of the plant have been used for their purported health benefits.

However, it can also be invasive in some regions, posing challenges to local ecosystems.
