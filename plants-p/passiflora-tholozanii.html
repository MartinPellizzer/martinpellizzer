Passiflora tholozanii

Passiflora tholozanii, commonly known as the Tholozan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, humid environments.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
