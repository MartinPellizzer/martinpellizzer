Piper chamissonis

Piper chamissonis, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
