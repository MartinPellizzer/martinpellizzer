Philodendron haematinum

Philodendron haematinum, commonly known as the Crimson-veined Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep crimson veins against a dark green background. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value.

It can be propagated through stem cuttings, making it relatively easy to cultivate.
