Palmijuncus draco

Palmijuncus draco, commonly known as the dragon's tail palm, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky leaves that resemble a dragon's tail, giving it its common name. The plant has a short trunk and grows in a rosette formation, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Despite its rugged appearance, Palmijuncus draco is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
