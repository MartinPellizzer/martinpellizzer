Papaver radicatum f. parvifolium

Papaver radicatum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved poppy, is a botanical variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its diminutive, narrow leaves and striking, bright red to orange flowers. This plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open, sunny habitats. It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
