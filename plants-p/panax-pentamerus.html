Panax pentamerus

Panax pentamerus, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fleshy roots, which are the primary part used medicinally. The plant typically grows in forested areas with partial shade and well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences.

The botanical features of Panax pentamerus make it a valuable species in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
