Prunus cerasifera subsp. myrobalana

Prunus cerasifera subsp.

myrobalana, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries but are often used in jams and preserves.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
