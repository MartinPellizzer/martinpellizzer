Potamogeton × zizii f. pulcherrimus

Potamogeton × zizii f. pulcherrimus, commonly known as the golden pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green leaves, which are often submerged in freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense mats that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowers are small and greenish, often clustered in spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in ponds and water gardens.
