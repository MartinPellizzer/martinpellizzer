Pieris bodinieri

Pieris bodinieri, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue, waxy berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Pieris bodinieri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add structure to woodland landscapes.
