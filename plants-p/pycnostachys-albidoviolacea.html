Pycnostachys albidoviolacea

Pycnostachys albidoviolacea, commonly known as the white violet orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy surface, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
