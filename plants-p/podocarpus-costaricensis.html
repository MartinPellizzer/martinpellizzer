Podocarpus costaricensis

Podocarpus costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in humid, tropical lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its wood is durable and has been historically used for construction and furniture.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
