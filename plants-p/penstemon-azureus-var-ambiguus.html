Penstemon azureus var. ambiguus

Penstemon azureus var. ambiguus, commonly known as the blue beard penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
