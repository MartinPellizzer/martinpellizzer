Pilea sphenophylla

Pilea sphenophylla, commonly known as the "Chinese money plant," is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are heart-shaped and often variegated. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea sphenophylla is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
