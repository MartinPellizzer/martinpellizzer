Piper subnudibracteum

Piper subnudibracteum, commonly known as the narrow-bract pepper vine, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy berries containing a single seed. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.

The species is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
