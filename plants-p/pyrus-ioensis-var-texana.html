Pyrus ioensis var. texana

Pyrus ioensis var. texana, commonly known as the Texas crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible apples that are typically tart and used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact size and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
