Potentilla reducta

Potentilla reducta, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America and Eurasia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Potentilla reducta plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine and tundra ecosystems.
