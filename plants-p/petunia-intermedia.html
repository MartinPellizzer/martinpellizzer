Petunia intermedia

Petunia intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid petunia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in a wide range of colors. The plant is native to South America and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs.

Petunia intermedia is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as bedding plants.
