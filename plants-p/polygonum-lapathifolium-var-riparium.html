Polygonum lapathifolium var. riparium

Polygonum lapathifolium var. riparium, commonly known as water hemp or river smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along streams, rivers, and lake shores. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often reddish or purplish at the base. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed aquatic environments.
