Pepo indicus

Pepo indicus, commonly known as the Indian bitter gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, warty fruit that turns from green to orange when mature. The plant is native to South Asia and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms.

The fruit is often used in traditional Indian cuisine and has medicinal properties due to its high nutrient content.
