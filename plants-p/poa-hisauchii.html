Poa hisauchii

Poa hisauchii, commonly known as the Japanese bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Poa hisauchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
