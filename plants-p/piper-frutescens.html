Piper frutescens

Piper frutescens, commonly known as long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered berries that resemble peppercorns. The plant produces a pungent spice used in traditional cuisines and herbal medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
