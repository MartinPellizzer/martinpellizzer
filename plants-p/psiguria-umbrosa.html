Psiguria umbrosa

Psiguria umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy psiguria, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the undergrowth of forests. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green coloration. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive corolla.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.
