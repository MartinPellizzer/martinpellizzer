Pabstiella miragliae

Pabstiella miragliae, commonly known as the Miraglia's pabstiella, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth form, which allows it to store water efficiently in its thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying adaptation to extreme environments.
