Phyllanthus phillyreifolius

Phyllanthus phillyreifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or lanceolate in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical berries. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
