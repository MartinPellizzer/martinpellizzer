Pithecellobium calycinum

Pithecellobium calycinum, commonly known as the pink velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its roots are also valued for their nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it useful in soil enrichment.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
