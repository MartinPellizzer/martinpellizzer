Pourouma palmata

Pourouma palmata, commonly known as the giant river palm, is a large, herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its massive, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are pollinated by wind and insects. Its trunk is fibrous and can grow up to 10 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in the understory of tropical forests.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
