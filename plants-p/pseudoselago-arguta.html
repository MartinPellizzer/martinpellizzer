Pseudoselago arguta

Pseudoselago arguta, commonly known as the hairy false heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acidophilous plants adapted to nutrient-poor soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on both sides. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Pseudoselago arguta is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
