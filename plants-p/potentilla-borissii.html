Potentilla borissii

Potentilla borissii, commonly known as the Borissii cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla borissii is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
