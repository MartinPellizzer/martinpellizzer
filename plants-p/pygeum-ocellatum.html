Pygeum ocellatum

Pygeum ocellatum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pygeum ocellatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
