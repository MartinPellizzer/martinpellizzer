Pulmonaria kochii

Pulmonaria kochii, commonly known as the Koch's lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of velvety, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive purple and green markings. The plant produces upright spikes of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Pulmonaria kochii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in its native range.
