Polygonum macrochaetum

Polygonum macrochaetum, commonly known as the large-haired knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Polygonum macrochaetum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
