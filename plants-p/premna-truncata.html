Premna truncata

Premna truncata, commonly known as the Indian nettle or small-leaved glorybower, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a common subject in horticultural studies and ecological research.
