Pulsatilla montana var. nigella

Pulsatilla montana var. nigella, commonly known as the white pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces a distinctive, helmet-like seed capsule that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Pulsatilla montana var. nigella is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its early spring blooming habit.
