Polychilos cornu-cervi

Polychilos cornu-cervi, commonly known as the horned deer orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, deer-like appearance. The plant features a slender, hairy stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive horn-shaped spur. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The orchid's specialized structure supports pollination by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological significance.
