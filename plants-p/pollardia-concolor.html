Pollardia concolor

Pollardia concolor, commonly known as the white-flowered lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, evergreen leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Pollardia concolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
