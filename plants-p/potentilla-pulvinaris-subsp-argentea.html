Potentilla pulvinaris subsp. argentea

Potentilla pulvinaris subsp.

argentea, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
