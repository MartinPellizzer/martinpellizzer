Pandanus leuconotus

Pandanus leuconotus, commonly known as the white-veined pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy, whitish coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in brackish or saline soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in various island communities.
