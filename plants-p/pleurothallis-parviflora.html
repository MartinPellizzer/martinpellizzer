Pleurothallis parviflora

Pleurothallis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in small inflorescences. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it to conserve moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
