Prunus wilsonii

Prunus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. It is often found in rocky or mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Prunus wilsonii is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
