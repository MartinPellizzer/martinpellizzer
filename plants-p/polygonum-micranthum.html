Polygonum micranthum

Polygonum micranthum, commonly known as dwarf knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture. Small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and the plant produces small, three-lobed seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
