Primula saundersiana

Primula saundersiana, commonly known as the Saunders' primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white. It blooms in early spring, often before the surrounding vegetation emerges.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
