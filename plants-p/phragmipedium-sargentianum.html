Phragmipedium sargentianum

Phragmipedium sargentianum, commonly known as the Sargent's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, slipper-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
