Portulaca eruca

Portulaca eruca, commonly known as the common purslane, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in hot, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils, gardens, and along roadsides. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly tart flavor, while the seeds are dispersed by wind.

Portulaca eruca is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is considered a weed in many areas.
