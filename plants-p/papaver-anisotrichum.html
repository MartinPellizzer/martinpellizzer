Papaver anisotrichum

Papaver anisotrichum, commonly known as the variable poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stem is erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Papaver anisotrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
