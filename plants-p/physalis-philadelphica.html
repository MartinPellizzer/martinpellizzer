Physalis philadelphica

Physalis philadelphica, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow-orange berries. The plant produces small, round fruits enclosed in a papery calyx that turns red as the fruit matures. The berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
