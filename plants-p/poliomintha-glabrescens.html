Poliomintha glabrescens

Poliomintha glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth poliomintha, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
