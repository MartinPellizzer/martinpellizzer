Pulmonaria saccharata subsp. affinis

Pulmonaria saccharata subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the Siberian lungwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features distinctive basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a velvety texture and a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and early-season blooms.
