Polygonum amphibium

Polygonum amphibium, commonly known as the greater bladderwort, is a semi-aquatic plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are often submerged or floating. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive leaf shapes and often edible seeds.

Polygonum amphibium plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
