Pholidota lugardii

Pholidota lugardii, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Pholidota lugardii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
