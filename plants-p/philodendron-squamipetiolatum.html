Philodendron squamipetiolatum

Philodendron squamipetiolatum, commonly known as the velvet-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually a deep green with a slight sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
