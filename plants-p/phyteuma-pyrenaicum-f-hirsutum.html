Phyteuma pyrenaicum f. hirsutum

Phyteuma pyrenaicum f. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy foliage, which is a distinguishing characteristic of this form. It grows in rocky or grassy habitats and is adapted to mountainous environments.

Its botanical structure includes a robust root system and erect, branching stems.
