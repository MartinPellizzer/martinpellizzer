Papaver albiflorum subsp. austromoravicum

Papaver albiflorum subsp.

austromoravicum, commonly known as the Austro-Moravian white poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
