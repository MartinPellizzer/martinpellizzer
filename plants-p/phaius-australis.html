Phaius australis

Phaius australis, commonly known as the Australian marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are long and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with a distinct lip.

Phaius australis plays an important ecological role in its native environment by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
