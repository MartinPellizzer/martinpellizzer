Plectranthus pachyphyllus

Plectranthus pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus pachyphyllus is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its ornamental foliage and drought tolerance.
