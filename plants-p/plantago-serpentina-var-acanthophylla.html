Plantago serpentina var. acanthophylla

Plantago serpentina var. acanthophylla, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. It is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional remedies for skin irritations and respiratory issues.

The variant acanthophylla is distinguished by its more pronounced hairy foliage compared to other forms of Plantago serpentina.
