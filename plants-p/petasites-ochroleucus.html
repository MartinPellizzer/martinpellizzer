Petasites ochroleucus

Petasites ochroleucus, commonly known as the white-veined buttercup, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Ranunculaceae.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with numerous stamens, typical of the buttercup genus. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
