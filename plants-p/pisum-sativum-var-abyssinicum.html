Pisum sativum var. abyssinicum

Pisum sativum var. abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea species.

It is native to the highlands of Ethiopia and is characterized by its unique morphological features. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and the pods contain several small, round seeds.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential for agricultural use.
