Pandanus barterianus

Pandanus barterianus, commonly known as the bottle palm, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are a staple in some island cuisines. Its robust growth and adaptability make it a valuable species in tropical ecosystems.

The leaves are also used in traditional crafts and ceremonies across its native regions.
