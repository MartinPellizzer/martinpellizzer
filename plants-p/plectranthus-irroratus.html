Plectranthus irroratus

Plectranthus irroratus, commonly known as the silver bush or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its gray-green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Plectranthus irroratus is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in herbal teas or as a natural air freshener.
