Panicum capillare var. pampinosum

Panicum capillare var. pampinosum, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, fluffy panicles that are often wind-dispersed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and as a component of native plant restoration projects.
