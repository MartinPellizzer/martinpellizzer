Piper calvescens var. potrerillosense

Piper calvescens var. potrerillosense, commonly known as the Potrerillos pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of tiny, aromatic fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, while its leaves are sometimes used in local cuisines.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
