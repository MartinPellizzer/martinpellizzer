Primula cortusoides

Primula cortusoides, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale yellow blooms in early spring, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its foliage consists of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating.

Primula cortusoides is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude meadows and tundra habitats.
