Pyrus stenophylla

Pyrus stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in the Alps and Carpathians. Its fruit, a small, yellowish pear, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.

Pyrus stenophylla is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
