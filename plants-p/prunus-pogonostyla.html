Prunus pogonostyla

Prunus pogonostyla, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, hard, dark red fruits known as cherries, which are edible but typically not consumed fresh. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Prunus pogonostyla is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
