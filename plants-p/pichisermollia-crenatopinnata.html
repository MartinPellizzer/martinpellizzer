Pichisermollia crenatopinnata

Pichisermollia crenatopinnata, commonly known as the crested pinnate fern, is a unique species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, feathery fronds that exhibit a distinctive crenate or scalloped edge. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky substrates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been the subject of botanical studies due to its unique morphology.
