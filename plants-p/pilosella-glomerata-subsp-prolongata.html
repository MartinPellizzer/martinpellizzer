Pilosella glomerata subsp. prolongata

Pilosella glomerata subsp.

prolongata, commonly known as the long-headed globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, globular inflorescence composed of small, yellowish to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with fine, silky hairs.

The subspecies is notable for its prolonged flowering period, which extends into late summer.
