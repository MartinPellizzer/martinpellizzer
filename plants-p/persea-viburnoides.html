Persea viburnoides

Persea viburnoides, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe, though they can be astringent when unripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
