Pyrus hakkiarica

Pyrus hakkiarica, commonly known as the Turkish pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with compound leaves and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in local cuisines.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its smooth, gray bark and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
