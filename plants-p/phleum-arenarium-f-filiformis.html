Phleum arenarium f. filiformis

Phleum arenarium f. filiformis, commonly known as the silky bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a soft, silky appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes or as a ground cover.
