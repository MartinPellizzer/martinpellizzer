Pterocarpus gracilis

Pterocarpus gracilis, commonly known as the slender legume tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, flat, winged pods. Its wood is highly valued for its dark color and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in furniture-making.

The plant also has cultural significance in some regions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
