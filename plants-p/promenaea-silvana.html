Promenaea silvana

Promenaea silvana, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and undergrowth. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, adapting to low-light conditions.

Promenaea silvana plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to forest biodiversity.
