Poa dahurica

Poa dahurica, commonly known as Siberian grass or Meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa dahurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a groundcover.
