Pancovia africana

Pancovia africana, commonly known as the African pancake plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Pancovia africana is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
