Platyopuntia cordobensis

Platyopuntia cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant in dry climates.
