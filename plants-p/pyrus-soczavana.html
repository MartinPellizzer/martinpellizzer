Pyrus soczavana

Pyrus soczavana, commonly known as the Soczawa pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Caucasus region, specifically Georgia, and is characterized by its deciduous nature and ornamental value. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring, followed by yellowish-green fruits.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
