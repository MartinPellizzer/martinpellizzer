Phacelia geraniifolia

Phacelia geraniifolia, commonly known as blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, fern-like leaves that resemble those of geraniums. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Phacelia geraniifolia is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
