Papaver feddei

Papaver feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its capsule fruits contain numerous small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
