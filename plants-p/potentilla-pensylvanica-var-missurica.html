Potentilla pensylvanica var. missurica

Potentilla pensylvanica var. missurica, commonly known as the Missourian potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
