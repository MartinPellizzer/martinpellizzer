Phlomis agraria

Phlomis agraria, commonly known as narrowleaf plumeweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Phlomis agraria is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.
