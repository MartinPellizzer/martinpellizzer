Paronychia sintenisii

Paronychia sintenisii, commonly known as the red sandrat plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
