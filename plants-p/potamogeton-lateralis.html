Potamogeton lateralis

Potamogeton lateralis, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, often found near the water surface. Its roots help stabilize sediments, contributing to water clarity and habitat for aquatic life.

Potamogeton lateralis is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
