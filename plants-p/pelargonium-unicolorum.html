Pelargonium unicolorum

Pelargonium unicolorum, commonly known as the single-colored geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its single-colored flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium unicolorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
