Pachypleuria brackenridgei

Pachypleuria brackenridgei, commonly known as the Brackenridge's cycad, is a unique species of cycad native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its slow growth rate and long lifespan make it a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
