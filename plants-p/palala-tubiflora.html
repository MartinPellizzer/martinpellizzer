Palala tubiflora

Palala tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous flax, is a perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its underground tubers, which are the primary storage organs for nutrients and water. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose panicles, typically displaying shades of white or pale blue.

Palala tubiflora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xerophytic gardens.
