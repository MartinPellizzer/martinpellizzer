Pyxidaria cymulosa

Pyxidaria cymulosa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils. It is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Pyxidaria cymulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
