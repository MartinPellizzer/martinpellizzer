Paphiopedilum callosum var. sublaeve

Paphiopedilum callosum var. sublaeve, commonly known as the pale-leafed lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color with intricate patterns.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
