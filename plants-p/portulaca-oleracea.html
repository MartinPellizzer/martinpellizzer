Portulaca oleracea

Portulaca oleracea, commonly known as purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in hot, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and gardens. Its leaves are edible and rich in omega-3 fatty acids, making it a nutritious addition to salads.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, which helps it survive in arid conditions.
