Piper hispidum

Piper hispidum, commonly known as the rough pepper or hispid pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that develop from the flowers and are often used in traditional medicine.

Piper hispidum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
