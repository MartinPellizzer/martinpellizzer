Potentilla andicola

Potentilla andicola, commonly known as the Andean potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Potentilla andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar ecological requirements.
