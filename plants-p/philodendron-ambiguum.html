Philodendron ambiguum

Philodendron ambiguum, commonly known as the variegated philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with white or cream variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spathes containing berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Philodendron ambiguum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
