Pavonia sessiliflora var. mattogrossensis

Pavonia sessiliflora var. mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosemary or Brazilian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that resemble those of hibiscus. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
