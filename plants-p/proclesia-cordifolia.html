Proclesia cordifolia

Proclesia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Proclesia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
