Polygonatum obtusifolium

Polygonatum obtusifolium, commonly known as the blunt-leaf Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers followed by red berries. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a blunt tip, giving it its common name.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.
