Padus virginiana subsp. melanocarpa

Padus virginiana subsp.

melanocarpa, commonly known as black chokecherry, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, black berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant is part of the Rosaceae family and is often found in moist woodlands and along streambanks. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The berries are toxic to humans but are a food source for birds and wildlife.
