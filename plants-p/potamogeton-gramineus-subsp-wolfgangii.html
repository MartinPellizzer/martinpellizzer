Potamogeton gramineus subsp. wolfgangii

Potamogeton gramineus subsp.

wolfgangii, commonly known as the Wolfgang's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Europe. It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its long, cylindrical leaves that are often ribbed and have a smooth surface.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in spike-like clusters. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
