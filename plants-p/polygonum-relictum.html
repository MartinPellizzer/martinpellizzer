Polygonum relictum

Polygonum relictum, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread aggressively and form dense colonies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged, especially in cooler weather. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are typically pollinated by wind.

Polygonum relictum is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
