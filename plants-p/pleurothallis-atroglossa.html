Pleurothallis atroglossa

Pleurothallis atroglossa, commonly known as the dark-tongue orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, often with a distinctive lip that resembles a tongue. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.
