Pinguicula vulgaris subsp. bohemica

Pinguicula vulgaris subsp.

bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that trap small insects, aiding in its nutrient acquisition.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small and purple or pink, adding to its ornamental value.

It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, humid conditions.
