Pyrus aria subvar. parvula

Pyrus aria subvar. parvula, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by edible, yellowish fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in British horticulture.
