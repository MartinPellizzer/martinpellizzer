Pilea pennellii

Pilea pennellii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by spathes, which are the modified leaves that enclose the true flowers. Pilea pennellii is a low-maintenance houseplant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is popular for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
