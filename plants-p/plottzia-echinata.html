Plottzia echinata

Plottzia echinata, commonly known as the spiny sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal areas, often tolerating saline and wind-swept environments. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.
