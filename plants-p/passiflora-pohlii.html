Passiflora pohlii

Passiflora pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora pohlii is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and sedative properties.
