Polypodium veitchii var. glaucopsis

Polypodium veitchii var. glaucopsis, commonly known as the Veitch's polypody, is a fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped fronds that are typically light green to bluish-green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ease of cultivation.
