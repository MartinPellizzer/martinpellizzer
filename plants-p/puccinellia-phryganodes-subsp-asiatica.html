Puccinellia phryganodes subsp. asiatica

Puccinellia phryganodes subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the Asian bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
