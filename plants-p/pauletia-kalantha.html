Pauletia kalantha

Pauletia kalantha, commonly known as the red-flowered kalanchoe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant red, pink, or orange flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
