Pteronia baccharoides

Pteronia baccharoides, commonly known as the silverleaf pteronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Pteronia baccharoides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
