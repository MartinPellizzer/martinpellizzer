Parietaria hespera var. californica

Parietaria hespera var. californica, commonly known as California wall-rocket, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its preference for well-drained soil.

The species is often found in coastal regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
