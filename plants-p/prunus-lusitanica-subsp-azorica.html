Prunus lusitanica subsp. azorica

Prunus lusitanica subsp.

azorica, commonly known as the Azores cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Azores. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
