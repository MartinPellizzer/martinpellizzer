Pterospermum littorale var. littorale

Pterospermum littorale var. littorale, commonly known as the coastal pterospermum, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically alternate and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody pods containing oval seeds. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in coastal ecology and plant adaptation studies.
