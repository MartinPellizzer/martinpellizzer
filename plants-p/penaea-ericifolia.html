Penaea ericifolia

Penaea ericifolia, commonly known as the heath buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, dark blue berries. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive waxy coating.

Penaea ericifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
