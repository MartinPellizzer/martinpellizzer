Pentanura

Pentanura, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in juices and desserts. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Pentanura is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in tropical and subtropical regions.
