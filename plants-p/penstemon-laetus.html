Penstemon laetus

Penstemon laetus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features tall, erect stems with whorled leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. Penstemon laetus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and pollinator attraction.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
