Panicum divaricatum var. latifolium

Panicum divaricatum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and panicle-like inflorescences that produce small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

This variety is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and wildlife habitat.
