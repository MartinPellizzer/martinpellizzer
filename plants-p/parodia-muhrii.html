Parodia muhrii

Parodia muhrii, commonly known as the Muhrii Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in arid gardens.
