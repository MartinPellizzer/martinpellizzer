Passiflora cumbalensis var. sparrei

Passiflora cumbalensis var. sparrei, commonly known as the Cumbal passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
