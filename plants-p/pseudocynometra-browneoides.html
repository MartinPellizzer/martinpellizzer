Pseudocynometra browneoides

Pseudocynometra browneoides, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, elongated seeds. It is often found in moist forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

However, it is considered threatened due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
