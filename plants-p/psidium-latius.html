Psidium latius

Psidium latius, commonly known as the broadleaf guava, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, sweet fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The broadleaf guava is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
