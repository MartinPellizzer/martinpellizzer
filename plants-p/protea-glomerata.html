Protea glomerata

Protea glomerata, commonly known as the sugar bush, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with rough, fissured bark. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, often with a distinctive white or yellowish underside.

Protea glomerata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the Western Cape region.
