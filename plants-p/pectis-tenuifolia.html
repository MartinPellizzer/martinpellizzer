Pectis tenuifolia

Pectis tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pectis tenuifolia is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
