Passiflora hybrida

Passiflora hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid passionflower, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature intricate patterns of red, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in warm climates.

Passiflora hybrida is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
