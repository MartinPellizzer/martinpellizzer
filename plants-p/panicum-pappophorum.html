Panicum pappophorum

Panicum pappophorum, commonly known as the burr panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, seed-producing structures called pappi, which aid in wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
