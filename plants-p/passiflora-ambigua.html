Passiflora ambigua

Passiflora ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and modified leaves. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora ambigua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
