Phryma leptostachya var. nana

Phryma leptostachya var. nana, commonly known as dwarf phryma, is a compact variant of the phryma plant native to eastern North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it well-suited for small garden spaces. The plant features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale green, appearing in late summer.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland settings.
