Polycarpon englerianum

Polycarpon englerianum, commonly known as the Engler's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has a robust root system and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Polycarpon englerianum is known for its ability to thrive in challenging conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
