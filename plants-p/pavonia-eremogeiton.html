Pavonia eremogeiton

Pavonia eremogeiton, commonly known as the desert pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Pavonia eremogeiton is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
