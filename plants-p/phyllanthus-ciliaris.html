Phyllanthus ciliaris

Phyllanthus ciliaris, commonly known as mouse ear or parviflora, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, elliptical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
