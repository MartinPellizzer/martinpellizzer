Penstemon caespitosus subsp. suffruticosus

Penstemon caespitosus subsp.

suffruticosus, commonly known as the rough hairless penstemon, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
