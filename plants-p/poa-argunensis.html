Poa argunensis

Poa argunensis, commonly known as the Argun grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa argunensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme climates.
