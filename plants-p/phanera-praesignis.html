Phanera praesignis

Phanera praesignis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phanera praesignis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
