Penstemon lewisii

Penstemon lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of tubular, violet to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon lewisii is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
