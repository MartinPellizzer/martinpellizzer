Pandanus cumingianus

Pandanus cumingianus, commonly known as the Cuming's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its fruit, known as a pandanus or screw pine fruit, is often used in traditional crafts and cuisine.

This species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it a resilient and ecologically significant plant.
