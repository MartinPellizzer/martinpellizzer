Plinia costata

Plinia costata, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive serrated margin.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, which eventually develop into the fruit.
