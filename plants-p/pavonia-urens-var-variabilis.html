Pavonia urens var. variabilis

Pavonia urens var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, red-orange bracts resembling flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established. It has a sprawling growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its vibrant color.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its distinctive appearance.
