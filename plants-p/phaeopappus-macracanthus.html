Phaeopappus macracanthus

Phaeopappus macracanthus, commonly known as the purple pappus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant features showy purple to violet flower heads that are often surrounded by pappus structures, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Phaeopappus macracanthus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops.
