Prunus × donarium var. pubescens

Prunus × donarium var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent cherry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) leaves and flowers, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces small, sweet-tart fruits similar to other cherry species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blossom display.

The varietal name 'pubescens' refers to the fine, soft hairs that cover its leaves and stems.
