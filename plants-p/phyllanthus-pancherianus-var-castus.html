Phyllanthus pancherianus var. castus

Phyllanthus pancherianus var. castus, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a species native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its flowers are small and clustered in panicles. The fruit is a drupe, usually yellow or orange when ripe, and contains a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
