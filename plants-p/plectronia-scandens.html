Plectronia scandens

Plectronia scandens, commonly known as the climbing plectronia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.

Plectronia scandens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
