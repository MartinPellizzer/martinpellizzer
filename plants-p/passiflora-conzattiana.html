Passiflora conzattiana

Passiflora conzattiana, commonly known as the Conzattian passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of violet, yellow, and white hues. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits similar to those of other passionfruit species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
