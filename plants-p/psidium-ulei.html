Psidium ulei

Psidium ulei, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Psidium ulei thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
