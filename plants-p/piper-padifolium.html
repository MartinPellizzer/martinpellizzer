Piper padifolium

Piper padifolium, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
