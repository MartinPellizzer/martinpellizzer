Plinia baileyi

Plinia baileyi, commonly known as the Bailey's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry with a sweet and aromatic flavor.

Plinia baileyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
