Pogostemon glaber var. tsingpingensis

Pogostemon glaber var. tsingpingensis, commonly known as the Tsingping broadleaf betony, is a species native to China.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
