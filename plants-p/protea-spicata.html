Protea spicata

Protea spicata, commonly known as the spike protea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Protea spicata is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
