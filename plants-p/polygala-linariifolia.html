Polygala linariifolia

Polygala linariifolia, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and it produces small, bean-like seed pods that contain several seeds.

Polygala linariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
