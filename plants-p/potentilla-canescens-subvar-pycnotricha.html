Potentilla canescens subvar. pycnotricha

Potentilla canescens subvar. pycnotricha, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subvariety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
