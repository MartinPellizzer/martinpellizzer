Polygala vulgaris var. oxyptera

Polygala vulgaris var. oxyptera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in spikelike inflorescences, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
