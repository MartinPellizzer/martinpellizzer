Pyrus confusa

Pyrus confusa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish-green pears.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing plant in temperate climates.
