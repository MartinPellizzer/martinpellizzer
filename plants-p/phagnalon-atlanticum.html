Phagnalon atlanticum

Phagnalon atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic phagnalon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. Native to coastal areas of the Atlantic coast, it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. The plant plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion due to its extensive root system.

It is also adapted to withstand salt spray and moderate levels of drought.
