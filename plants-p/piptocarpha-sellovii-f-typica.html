Piptocarpha sellovii f. typica

Piptocarpha sellovii f. typica, commonly known as the Sello's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, woolly inflorescence and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
