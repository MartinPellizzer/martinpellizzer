Planchonia sundaica

Planchonia sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The tree has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds.

Planchonia sundaica is valued for its durable timber and is often found in lowland rainforests.
