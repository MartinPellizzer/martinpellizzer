Pyrus raunii

Pyrus raunii, commonly known as the Raunii pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often used in jams and desserts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively hardy, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
