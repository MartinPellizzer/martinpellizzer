Pinus arizonica var. cooperi

Pinus arizonica var. cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's pinyon, is a variant of the Arizona pine native to the southwestern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen conifer with a pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces edible seeds enclosed in woody cones, which are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is adapted to well-drained soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for local fauna.
