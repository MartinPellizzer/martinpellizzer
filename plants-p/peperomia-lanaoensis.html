Peperomia lanaoensis

Peperomia lanaoensis, commonly known as the Lanao peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Mindanao, where it grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in clusters and are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits.

Peperomia lanaoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and unique foliage.
