Papaver przewalskii

Papaver przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's poppy, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a distinctive, elongated seed capsule that resembles a lantern. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its natural habitat.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
