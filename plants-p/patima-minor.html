Patima minor

Patima minor, commonly known as the lesser patima, is a small herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters, with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the late spring to early summer. Its flowers are usually arranged in umbels, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or stream banks.

Patima minor is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
