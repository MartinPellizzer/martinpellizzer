Portulaca oleracea subsp. nicaraguensis

Portulaca oleracea subsp.

nicaraguensis, commonly known as Nicaraguan purslane, is a succulent plant native to Central America. It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its leaves and stems are edible and often used in local cuisines for their mild, tangy flavor.
