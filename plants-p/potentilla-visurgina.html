Potentilla visurgina

Potentilla visurgina, commonly known as the Visurgian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla visurgina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
