Pseudorchis albida subsp. albida

Pseudorchis albida subsp.

albida, commonly known as the white lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, white or pale green inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair, hence its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, showy flowers with three sepals and three petals.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
