Pelargonium ternatum

Pelargonium ternatum, commonly known as the three-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

Pelargonium ternatum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
