Protea adscendens

Protea adscendens, commonly known as the mountain protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant is typically found in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It is adapted to the cool, misty conditions of mountainous regions, often growing at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters.

The protea adscendens plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for various pollinators such as birds and insects.
