Plantago holosteum subsp. macrantha

Plantago holosteum subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the large-fruit plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and wetlands.

The plant features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and the fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
