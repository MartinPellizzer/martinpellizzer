Pyrus setschwanensis

Pyrus setschwanensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by white, sweet-smelling fruits in late summer. The fruits are typically pear-shaped and have a crisp, juicy texture with a mild, sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
