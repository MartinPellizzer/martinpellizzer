Potentilla recta f. parviflora

Potentilla recta f. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
