Psidium amygdalinum

Psidium amygdalinum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cherimoya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a unique, creamy fruit with a texture similar to a mix between a banana and a melon. Its fruit contains a high concentration of nutrients, including vitamins C and B-complex, as well as minerals like potassium and magnesium.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
