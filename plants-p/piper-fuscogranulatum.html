Piper fuscogranulatum

Piper fuscogranulatum, commonly known as the black granular pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark, granular fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
