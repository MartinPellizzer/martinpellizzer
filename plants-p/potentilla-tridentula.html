Potentilla tridentula

Potentilla tridentula, commonly known as the three-toothed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with woody stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Potentilla tridentula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
