Phacelia campanularia subsp. vasiformis

Phacelia campanularia subsp.

vasiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and a branched stem that supports its flowering display.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
