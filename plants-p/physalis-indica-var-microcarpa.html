Physalis indica var. microcarpa

Physalis indica var. microcarpa, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange berries that are encased in a papery calyx. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
