Prionosciadium mexicanum var. acuminatum

Prionosciadium mexicanum var. acuminatum, commonly known as Mexican parsley or epazote, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils in full sun. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional Mexican cooking.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties, though it contains compounds that can be toxic in large quantities.
