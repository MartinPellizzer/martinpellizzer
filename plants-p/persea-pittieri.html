Persea pittieri

Persea pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its growth habit includes both deciduous and evergreen forms depending on the region.
