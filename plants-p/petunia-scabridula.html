Petunia scabridula

Petunia scabridula, commonly known as the rough-leaved petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Petunia scabridula prefers well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant once established.
