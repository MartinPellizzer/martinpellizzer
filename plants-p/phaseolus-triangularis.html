Phaseolus triangularis

Phaseolus triangularis, commonly known as the three-lobed bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are three-lobed and often heart-shaped. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various soil types.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
