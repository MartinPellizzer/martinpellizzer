Poa hideaki-ohbae

Poa hideaki-ohbae, commonly known as the Ohbae bentgrass, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically forms dense, mat-like turf. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in forest understories.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered at the base of the plant, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
