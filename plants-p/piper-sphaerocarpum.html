Piper sphaerocarpum

Piper sphaerocarpum, commonly known as the globe fruit pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and they are encased in a unique, spherical fruit structure. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
