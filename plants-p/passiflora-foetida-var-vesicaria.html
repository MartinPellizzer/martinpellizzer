Passiflora foetida var. vesicaria

Passiflora foetida var. vesicaria, commonly known as the blister passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of red, yellow, and blue hues, with prominent filaments and a unique central structure. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
