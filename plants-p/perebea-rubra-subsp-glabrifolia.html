Perebea rubra subsp. glabrifolia

Perebea rubra subsp.

glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved perebea, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and leathery in texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial use in traditional medicine.
