Prunus demissa var. melanocarpa

Prunus demissa var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant produces black, spherical berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full to partial sunlight.

The bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
