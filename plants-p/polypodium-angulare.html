Polypodium angulare

Polypodium angulare, commonly known as the angled holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in shaded, moist environments. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

Polypodium angulare is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
