Phyllanthus alaternoides

Phyllanthus alaternoides, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 5 meters. The plant has simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round, woody fruits.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
