Phlox × leopoldiana

Phlox × leopoldiana, commonly known as the New England phlox, is a hybrid perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
