Protea oblongifolia

Protea oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small flowers arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape. The plant produces distinctive bracts that can range in color from white to pink, red, or even yellow, depending on the variety. Native to the coastal regions of South Africa, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
