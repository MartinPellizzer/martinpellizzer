Porpax gigantea

Porpax gigantea, commonly known as the giant porpax, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family, native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and brightly colored leaves that form a rosette shape, often displaying vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that can reach several feet in height, featuring numerous small, tubular flowers. It thrives in warm, moist environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
