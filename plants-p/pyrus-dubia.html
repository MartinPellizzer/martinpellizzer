Pyrus dubia

Pyrus dubia, commonly known as the rare or obscure pear species, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers and edible, though less commonly cultivated, fruit. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Pyrus dubia is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in botanical studies.
