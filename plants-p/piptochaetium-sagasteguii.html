Piptochaetium sagasteguii

Piptochaetium sagasteguii, commonly known as the Andean grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
