Phyllanthus ovalis

Phyllanthus ovalis, commonly known as the oval leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its simple, opposite, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, berry-like fruits.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and arid environments.
