Piper sugandhi var. brevipilis

Piper sugandhi var. brevipilis, commonly known as the Indian black pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a climbing vine with dark green, ovate leaves that have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by black, peppercorn-like berries. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
