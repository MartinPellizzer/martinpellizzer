Paspalum fuscum

Paspalum fuscum, commonly known as blackgrass or common paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.

Paspalum fuscum is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, contributing to its ecological and agricultural significance.
