Paepalanthus prolifer

Paepalanthus prolifer, commonly known as the clustered pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions with its deep root system.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a distinctive and resilient species within its native habitat.
