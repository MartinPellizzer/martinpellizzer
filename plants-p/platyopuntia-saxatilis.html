Platyopuntia saxatilis

Platyopuntia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Platyopuntia saxatilis is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy and resilient species.
