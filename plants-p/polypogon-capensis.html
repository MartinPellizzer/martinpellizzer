Polypogon capensis

Polypogon capensis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soil.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
