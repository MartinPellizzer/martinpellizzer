Pyrus bacillaris

Pyrus bacillaris, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Pyrus bacillaris is often found in woodlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
