Polygonum bistorta subsp. plumosum

Polygonum bistorta subsp.

plumosum, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia. It features upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.
