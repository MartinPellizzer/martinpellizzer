Polygonum typhoniifolium

Polygonum typhoniifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed soils.

Polygonum typhoniifolium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
