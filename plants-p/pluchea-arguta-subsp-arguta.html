Pluchea arguta subsp. arguta

Pluchea arguta subsp.

arguta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense heads.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
