Pilosella nigrogudarica

Pilosella nigrogudarica, commonly known as the black-haired pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features soft, hairy stems and leaves with a silvery-gray appearance due to dense pubescence. Its flowers are typically small, yellowish, and arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

Pilosella nigrogudarica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
