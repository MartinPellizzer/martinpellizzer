Polypodium vulgare subsp. californicum

Polypodium vulgare subsp.

californicum, commonly known as the western wall-r Fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family. It is a perennial fern characterized by its wiry, creeping rhizomes and densely packed, dark green, leathery fronds.

The fronds are typically pinnately compound, with small, ovate pinnae that are closely spaced. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rock crevices or on shaded forest floors.

This subspecies is native to western North America, including regions of California and Oregon.
