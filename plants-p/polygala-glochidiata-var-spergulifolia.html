Polygala glochidiata var. spergulifolia

Polygala glochidiata var. spergulifolia, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, showcasing a deep root system for water retention. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, providing some protection against water loss.

This species is part of the Polygalaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy flowers.
