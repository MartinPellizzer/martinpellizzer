Pentagonocarpus virginicus

Pentagonocarpus virginicus, commonly known as the American hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and serrated edges. Its flowers are showy, with five petals, often in shades of pink or red, and are accompanied by five sepals.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in wetland ecosystems.
