Primula bella subsp. bonatiana

Primula bella subsp.

bonatiana, commonly known as the Bonatiana primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, with a deep green color. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded rock crevices.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
