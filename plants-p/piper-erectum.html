Piper erectum

Piper erectum, commonly known as the upright pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-chambered berries. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
