Piper multinervium

Piper multinervium, commonly known as the multineuric pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its finely veined leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
