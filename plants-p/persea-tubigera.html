Persea tubigera

Persea tubigera, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. The fruit has a sweet, slightly astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
