Plectocomia macrostachya

Plectocomia macrostachya, commonly known as the giant plectocomia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy inflorescences that resemble plume-like structures. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its striking floral display.
