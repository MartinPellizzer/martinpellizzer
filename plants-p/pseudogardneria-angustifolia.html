Pseudogardneria angustifolia

Pseudogardneria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pseudogardneria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its narrow, linear leaves are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
