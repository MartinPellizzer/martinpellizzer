Polygala curtissii var. pycnostachya

Polygala curtissii var. pycnostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the flowers are often pink or purple in color.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions.
