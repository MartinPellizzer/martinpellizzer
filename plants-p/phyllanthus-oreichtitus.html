Phyllanthus oreichtitus

Phyllanthus oreichtitus, commonly known as the African pear or false pear, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
