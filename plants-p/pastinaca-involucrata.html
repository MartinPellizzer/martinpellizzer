Pastinaca involucrata

Pastinaca involucrata, commonly known as the woolly bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
