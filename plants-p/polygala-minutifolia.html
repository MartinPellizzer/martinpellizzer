Polygala minutifolia

Polygala minutifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is a perennial herb that produces small, pod-like fruits containing seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
