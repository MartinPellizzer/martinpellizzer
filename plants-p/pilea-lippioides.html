Pilea lippioides

Pilea lippioides, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear on short stalks above the foliage. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and minimal watering needs.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for tabletop displays and hanging baskets.
