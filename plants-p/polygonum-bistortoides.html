Polygonum bistortoides

Polygonum bistortoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bistort, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often swollen stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland conditions.
