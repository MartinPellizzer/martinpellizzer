Prunus oxyphylla

Prunus oxyphylla, commonly known as the Chinese plum or Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish or orange fruits that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Prunus oxyphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
