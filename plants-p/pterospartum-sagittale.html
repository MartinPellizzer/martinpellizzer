Pterospartum sagittale

Pterospartum sagittale, commonly known as the arrowleaf pterospartum, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with arrow-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, followed by legume-like pods containing several seeds.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
