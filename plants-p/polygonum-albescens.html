Polygonum albescens

Polygonum albescens, commonly known as white knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Native to temperate regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas.

This plant is often noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.
