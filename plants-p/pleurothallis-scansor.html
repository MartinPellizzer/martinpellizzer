Pleurothallis scansor

Pleurothallis scansor, commonly known as the climbing pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has thin, wiry stems that allow it to climb and spread across its substrate. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and pink.

Pleurothallis scansor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
