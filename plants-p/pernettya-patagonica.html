Pernettya patagonica

Pernettya patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red berries that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Pernettya patagonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystem.
