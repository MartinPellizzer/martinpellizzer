Pithecellobium jupunba

Pithecellobium jupunba, commonly known as the yellow glory pea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
