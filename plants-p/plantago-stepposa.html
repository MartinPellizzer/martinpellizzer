Plantago stepposa

Plantago stepposa, commonly known as the steppe plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
