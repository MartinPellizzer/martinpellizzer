Peplis erecta subsp. timeroyi

Peplis erecta subsp.

timeroyi, commonly known as the Timeroy's peplis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lanceolate leaves that are typically pubescent.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, temperate climate.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
