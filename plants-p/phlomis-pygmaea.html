Phlomis pygmaea

Phlomis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, adding vibrant color to its habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a similar fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Phlomis pygmaea is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
