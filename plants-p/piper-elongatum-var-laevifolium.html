Piper elongatum var. laevifolium

Piper elongatum var. laevifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper elongatum species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.

The leaves and fruits are used in various culinary and medicinal applications in its native habitats.
