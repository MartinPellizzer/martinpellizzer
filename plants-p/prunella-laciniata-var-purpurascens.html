Prunella laciniata var. purpurascens

Prunella laciniata var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and a distinctive purple-tinged underside. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in whorls along the stems, typically in shades of purple and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in gardens and cultivated areas.
