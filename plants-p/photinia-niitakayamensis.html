Photinia niitakayamensis

Photinia niitakayamensis, commonly known as the Japanese photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features oval, glossy dark green leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
