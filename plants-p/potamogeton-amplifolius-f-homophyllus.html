Potamogeton amplifolius f. homophyllus

Potamogeton amplifolius f. homophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically broader than those of the typical form, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often emerging from the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable aquatic habitats.
