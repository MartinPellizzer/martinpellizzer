Penstemon confertus var. violaceus

Penstemon confertus var. violaceus, commonly known as the violet-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage is typically gray-green, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
