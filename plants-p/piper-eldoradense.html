Piper eldoradense

Piper eldoradense, commonly known as the Eldorado pepper or Brazilian pepper vine, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, reddish-purple berry that contains several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
