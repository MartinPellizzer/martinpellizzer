Potentilla reptans f. adscendens

Potentilla reptans f. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending cinquefoil, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial shrub native to Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, creeping stems that can ascend slightly, giving it a distinctive growth habit. Its leaves are pinnately divided into five oval-shaped leaflets, which are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
