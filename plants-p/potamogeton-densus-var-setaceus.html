Potamogeton densus var. setaceus

Potamogeton densus var. setaceus, commonly known as the dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its dense growth habit, which can form thick mats on the bottom of lakes and ponds. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, its rapid growth can sometimes lead to water quality issues if it becomes overly abundant.
