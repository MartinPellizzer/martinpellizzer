Protea crinita

Protea crinita, commonly known as the "Cape rose" or "Cape orchid," is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its resilience. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, and are often used in floral arrangements.

Protea crinita is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
