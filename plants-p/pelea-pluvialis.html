Pelea pluvialis

Pelea pluvialis, commonly known as the rain pelea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually white or pale blue in color.

Pelea pluvialis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
