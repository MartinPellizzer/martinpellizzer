Potentilla gossypina

Potentilla gossypina, commonly known as Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like roses and strawberries.
