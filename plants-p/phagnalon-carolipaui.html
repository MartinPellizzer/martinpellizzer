Phagnalon carolipaui

Phagnalon carolipaui, commonly known as the Carolipau's phagnalon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
