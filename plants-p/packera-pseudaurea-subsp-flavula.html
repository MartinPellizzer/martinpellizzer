Packera pseudaurea subsp. flavula

Packera pseudaurea subsp.

flavula, commonly known as the yellow mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
