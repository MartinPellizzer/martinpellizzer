Phoberos

Phoberos, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer in the dim forest light, giving it an ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, pale flowers that emit a faint, bioluminescent glow at night, which is believed to attract specific nocturnal pollinators. Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, Phoberos is considered critically endangered and is rarely seen in the wild.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists alike.
