Pleurothallis chamaelepanthes

Pleurothallis chamaelepanthes, commonly known as the yellow-tipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with small, yellowish-green blooms that often have a subtle orange or reddish hue. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
