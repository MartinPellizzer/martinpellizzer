Pseuderanthemum hirtipistillum

Pseuderanthemum hirtipistillum, commonly known as the hairy-pistil pseuderanthemum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy on both surfaces, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically displaying shades of white and purple.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
