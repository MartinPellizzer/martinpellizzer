Psidium rufum var. rotundifolium

Psidium rufum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically small and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
