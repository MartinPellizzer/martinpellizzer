Paeonia lusitanica

Paeonia lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Paeoniaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. Its roots are fleshy and tuberous, which help it survive in dry conditions.

Paeonia lusitanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
