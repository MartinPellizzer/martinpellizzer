Polygonum persicaria var. incanum

Polygonum persicaria var. incanum, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer.

This plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along stream banks and in wet meadows.
