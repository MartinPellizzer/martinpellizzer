Pentaschistis basutorum

Pentaschistis basutorum, commonly known as the Basuto thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, sometimes spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small and tightly packed.

Pentaschistis basutorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native environment.
