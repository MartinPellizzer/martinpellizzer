Piper leonense

Piper leonense, commonly known as the Leonense pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
