Phyllactis polybotrya

Phyllactis polybotrya, commonly known as the African nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Phyllactis polybotrya is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
