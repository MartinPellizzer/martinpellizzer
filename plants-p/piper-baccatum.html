Piper baccatum

Piper baccatum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, dark red berries that resemble peppercorns. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent flavor.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
