Photinia amphidoxa var. kwangsiensis

Photinia amphidoxa var. kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small white flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in subtropical climates.
