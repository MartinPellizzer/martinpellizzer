Photinia bodinieri var. longifolia

Photinia bodinieri var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It features glossy, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
