Primula × goebelii

Primula × goebelii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from white to pink and yellow, blooming in early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
