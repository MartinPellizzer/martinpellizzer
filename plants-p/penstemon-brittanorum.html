Penstemon brittanorum

Penstemon brittanorum, commonly known as Brittan's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon brittanorum is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
