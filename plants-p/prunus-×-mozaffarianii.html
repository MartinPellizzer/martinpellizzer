Prunus × mozaffarianii

Prunus × mozaffarianii, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.

The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe, though it is not typically eaten by humans.
