Petastoma physaloides

Petastoma physaloides, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, green fruit that resembles a balloon, which splits open to release seeds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
