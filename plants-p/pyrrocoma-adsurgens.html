Pyrrocoma adsurgens

Pyrrocoma adsurgens, commonly known as the ascending pygmy grass, is a small herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in the spring, producing small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

Pyrrocoma adsurgens is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil nutrients.
