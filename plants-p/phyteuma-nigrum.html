Phyteuma nigrum

Phyteuma nigrum, commonly known as black phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
