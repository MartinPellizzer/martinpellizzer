Phyteuma pauciflorum var. tirolense

Phyteuma pauciflorum var. tirolense, commonly known as the Alpine phyteuma, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
