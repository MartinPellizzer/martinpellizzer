Pinalia merrittii

Pinalia merrittii, commonly known as the Merritt's pinyon, is a species of conifer native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, edible seeds that are enclosed in woody cones. The plant has needle-like leaves and produces both male and female cones on the same tree. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The botanical features of Pinalia merrittii make it an important part of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical research.
