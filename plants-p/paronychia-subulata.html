Paronychia subulata

Paronychia subulata, commonly known as the slenderleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, four-petaled white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Paronychia subulata is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.
