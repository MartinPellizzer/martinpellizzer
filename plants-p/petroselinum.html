Petroselinum

Petroselinum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in umbrella-shaped clusters. The plant thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, often used both as a culinary herb and for its medicinal properties. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive aroma and flavor.

Petroselinum is also a source of vitamins and minerals, making it a valuable addition to many diets.
