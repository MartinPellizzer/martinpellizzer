Pterospermum grewiifolium

Pterospermum grewiifolium, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are often hairy and oval in shape. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release its seeds, which are encased in a fluffy, wind-dispersed covering.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
