Piper huallaganum

Piper huallaganum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or Huallaganum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits.

The fruit contains aromatic seeds that are used in traditional Andean medicine and cuisine.
