Prunus concinna

Prunus concinna, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or California dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits that are edible but often tart. Prunus concinna thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a generally hardy nature that allows it to withstand drought conditions.
