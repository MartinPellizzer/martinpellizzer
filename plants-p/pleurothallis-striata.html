Pleurothallis striata

Pleurothallis striata, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive striped pattern on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
