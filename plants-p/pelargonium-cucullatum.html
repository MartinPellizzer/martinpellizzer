Pelargonium cucullatum

Pelargonium cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like calyx that encloses the flower, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, bright pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically rounded and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium cucullatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
