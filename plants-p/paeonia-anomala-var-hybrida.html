Paeonia anomala var. hybrida

Paeonia anomala var. hybrida, commonly known as the Turkish peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive red or purple eye. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are green and hairy, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental appeal and historical significance, it is widely cultivated for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
