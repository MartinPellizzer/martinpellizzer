Pedicularis oederi var. angustiflora

Pedicularis oederi var. angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lousewort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features striking red or orange flowers that are often adorned with distinctive purple spots, which help attract pollinators. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a notable species in botanical gardens and wildflower collections.
