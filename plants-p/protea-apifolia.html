Protea apifolia

Protea apifolia, commonly known as the honeycomb protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that resemble a honeycomb. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are pollinated primarily by birds and insects. Protea apifolia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
