Plantago platypetala

Plantago platypetala, commonly known as the flat-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
