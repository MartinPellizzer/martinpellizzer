Phlox longifolia var. humilis

Phlox longifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the low phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
