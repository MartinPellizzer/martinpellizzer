Polygala vulgaris proles morisiana

Polygala vulgaris proles morisiana, commonly known as the Morisian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, adapting well to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain the seeds.
