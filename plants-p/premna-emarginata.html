Premna emarginata

Premna emarginata, commonly known as the Indian nettle or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that have serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
