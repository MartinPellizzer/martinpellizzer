Panicum lanuginosum var. fasciculatum

Panicum lanuginosum var. fasciculatum, commonly known as silky panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden brown florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
