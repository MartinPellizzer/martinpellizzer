Pilosella ziziana subsp. petasodes

Pilosella ziziana subsp.

petasodes, commonly known as the woolly fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, white to pale yellow ray florets and a central disk of yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
