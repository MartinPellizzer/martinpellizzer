Potentilla argentea angustata

Potentilla argentea angustata, commonly known as silverleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Potentilla argentea angustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
