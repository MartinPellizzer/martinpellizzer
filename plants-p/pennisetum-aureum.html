Pennisetum aureum

Pennisetum aureum, commonly known as golden foxtail, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of golden-orange flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Pennisetum aureum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
