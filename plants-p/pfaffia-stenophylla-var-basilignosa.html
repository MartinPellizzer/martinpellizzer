Pfaffia stenophylla var. basilignosa

Pfaffia stenophylla var. basilignosa, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species.
