Plumeria longifolia

Plumeria longifolia, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, waxy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches, and they emit a sweet, fragrant aroma.

Plumeria longifolia is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine and as a symbol in various cultures.
