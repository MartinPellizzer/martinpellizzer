Potentilla argentea var. villosa

Potentilla argentea var. villosa, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-white, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and erosion control.
