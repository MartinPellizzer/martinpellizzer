Prunus serrulata f. rubida

Prunus serrulata f. rubida, commonly known as the crimson cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in early spring, contrasting beautifully with its green foliage. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for gardens and public landscapes.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.
