Pyrus macropoda

Pyrus macropoda, commonly known as the large-fruited pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces large, yellow or yellow-green pears that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Pyrus macropoda is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
