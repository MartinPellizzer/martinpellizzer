Prunus spinosa var. subcinerea

Prunus spinosa var. subcinerea, commonly known as the white thorned cherry or pale bramble, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, lance-shaped leaves, and small white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, hard, dark-colored berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedging and landscape design.
