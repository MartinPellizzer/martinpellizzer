Pleioblastus niitakayamensis

Pleioblastus niitakayamensis, commonly known as the Niitakayama bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often used in traditional Japanese crafts. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
