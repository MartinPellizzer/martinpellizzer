Phylica disticha var. cuneata

Phylica disticha var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
