Paeonia daurica subsp. wittmanniana

Paeonia daurica subsp.

wittmanniana, commonly known as the Daurian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountains of eastern Asia. It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are stout and covered in a layer of silky hairs. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller size and more delicate flowers compared to other peony species.
