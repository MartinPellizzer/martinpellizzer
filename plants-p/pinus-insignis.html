Pinus insignis

Pinus insignis, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and long lifespan, often living for several centuries. The tree has a distinctive appearance with its twisted, gnarled trunk and dense, needle-like foliage. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, dry climates with well-drained soils.
