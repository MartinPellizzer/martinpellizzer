Polemonium muricatum

Polemonium muricatum, commonly known as the roughleaf Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus within the family Polemoniaceae, which includes several species of flowering plants. The plant features upright, branched stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its showy, clustered flowers are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
