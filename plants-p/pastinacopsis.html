Pastinacopsis

Pastinacopsis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in coastal areas and are adapted to saline and sandy soils. They produce striking blue or purple flower heads that are often surrounded by spiny bracts. The leaves are usually narrow and sometimes covered with fine hairs.

Pastinacopsis species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing coastal dunes.
