Piper rukshagandhum

Piper rukshagandhum, commonly known as Indian black pepper, is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of India and is characterized by its small, black, peppercorn-like berries. The plant produces a pungent and aromatic compound called piperine, which contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses. Its leaves are ovate and have a glossy surface, while its stems are woody and twining.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its valuable spice.
