Perezia sonchifolia

Perezia sonchifolia, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and develop into fluffy seed heads, similar to those of the common dandelion.

Perezia sonchifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
