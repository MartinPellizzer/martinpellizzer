Potentilla miyabei

Potentilla miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and trifoliate leaves.

Potentilla miyabei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
