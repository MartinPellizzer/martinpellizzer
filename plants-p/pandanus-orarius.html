Pandanus orarius

Pandanus orarius, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes at the top of the plant. Its robust and fibrous leaves are often used in traditional crafts and thatching.

Pandanus orarius thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
