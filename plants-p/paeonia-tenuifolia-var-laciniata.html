Paeonia tenuifolia var. laciniata

Paeonia tenuifolia var. laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, white, and red during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both a visually striking and ecologically valuable addition to gardens.
