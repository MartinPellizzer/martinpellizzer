Potentilla hebiichigo f. leucocephala

Potentilla hebiichigo f. leucocephala, commonly known as the White-Headed Cinquefoil, is a unique horticultural form of the species.

It is characterized by its white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red hues of the parent plant. The plant is a low-growing shrub with fine, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for smaller garden spaces.
