Pithecellobium sessile

Pithecellobium sessile, commonly known as the sessile pisonia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Pithecellobium sessile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
