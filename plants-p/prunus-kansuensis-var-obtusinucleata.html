Prunus kansuensis var. obtusinucleata

Prunus kansuensis var. obtusinucleata, commonly known as the Kansu cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet cherries. Its fruit is notable for its thick skin and firm, juicy flesh.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms and edible fruit.
