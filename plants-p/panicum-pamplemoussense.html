Panicum pamplemoussense

Panicum pamplemoussense, commonly known as the grapefruit panicum, is a tropical grass species native to the Caribbean.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and produces small, fluffy panicles of pale green to white flowers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
