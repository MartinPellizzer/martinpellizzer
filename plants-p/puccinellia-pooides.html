Puccinellia pooides

Puccinellia pooides, commonly known as slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks, preferring moist to somewhat saline soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.

Puccinellia pooides is also valued for its ornamental appeal in certain landscaping applications.
