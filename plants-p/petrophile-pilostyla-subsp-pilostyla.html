Petrophile pilostyla subsp. pilostyla

Petrophile pilostyla subsp.

pilostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock heath, is a shrub native to the south-western regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, pink to reddish flowers during the cooler months.

This plant is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and sandplain areas. It is a member of the Proteaceae family, which includes many species adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
