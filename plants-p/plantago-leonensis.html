Plantago leonensis

Plantago leonensis, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its robust growth and adaptability make it a common sight in temperate regions.
