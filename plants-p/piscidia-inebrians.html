Piscidia inebrians

Piscidia inebrians, commonly known as the drunkard's tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, and it is often found in humid, lowland areas. Its name originates from the hallucinogenic effects of its bark, which contains psychoactive compounds.
