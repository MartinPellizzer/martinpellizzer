Parahyparrhenia jaegeriana

Parahyparrhenia jaegeriana, commonly known as the dwarf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing clumps. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
