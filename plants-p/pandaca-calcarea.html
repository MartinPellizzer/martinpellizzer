Pandaca calcarea

Pandaca calcarea, commonly known as the calcareous pandaca, is a unique plant species characterized by its calcified stem structure.

It is native to specific regions with limestone-rich soils, which contribute to its distinct mineral composition. The plant exhibits a low-growing habit with fleshy, succulent leaves adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying a pale or white coloration.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
