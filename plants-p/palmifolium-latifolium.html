Palmifolium latifolium

Palmifolium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to regions with warm, humid climates. The plant's leaves are typically broad and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits.

The trunk is slender and often develops a fibrous outer layer as the plant matures.
