Plantago sericea var. decaisnei

Plantago sericea var. decaisnei, commonly known as the silky plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Its fibrous roots and dense foliage make it a useful plant for erosion control and as a ground cover.
