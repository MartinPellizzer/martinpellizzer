Panicum immersum

Panicum immersum, commonly known as water panicgrass, is a grass species native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce delicate, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant thrives in moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Panicum immersum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and water filtration.
