Polycarpaea corymbosa var. parviflora

Polycarpaea corymbosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered corymbosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
