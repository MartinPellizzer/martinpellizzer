Penstemon bleaklyi

Penstemon bleaklyi, commonly known as the Bitterroot Penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces bright red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

Penstemon bleaklyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
