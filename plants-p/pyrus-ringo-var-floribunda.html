Pyrus ringo var. floribunda

Pyrus ringo var. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded, spreading habit and smooth, gray bark. Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes.
