Paris polyphylla var. latifolia

Paris polyphylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved Indian arrowroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features a stout rhizome that produces multiple fleshy, broad leaves with a waxy surface. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
