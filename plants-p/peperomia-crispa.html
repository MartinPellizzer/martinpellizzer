Peperomia crispa

Peperomia crispa, commonly known as the wrinkled peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves with a distinctive crinkled or wavy texture, often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or red, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia crispa is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal watering and moderate humidity to flourish.
