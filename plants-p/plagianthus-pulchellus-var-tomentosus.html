Plagianthus pulchellus var. tomentosus

Plagianthus pulchellus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the velvet plume, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, dark green foliage that is covered in a fine, fuzzy white coating, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, plume-like inflorescences in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens for its unique texture and seasonal floral display.
