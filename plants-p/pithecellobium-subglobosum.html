Pithecellobium subglobosum

Pithecellobium subglobosum, commonly known as the globular bottle tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, adapting to arid environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
