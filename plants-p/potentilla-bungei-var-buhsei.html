Potentilla bungei var. buhsei

Potentilla bungei var. buhsei, commonly known as the Buhse's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in full sun and cool climates.
