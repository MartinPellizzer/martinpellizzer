Papyrus odoratus

Papyrus odoratus, commonly known as sweet sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer. Papyrus odoratus is often found in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its aromatic leaves and showy inflorescences make it an attractive addition to water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
