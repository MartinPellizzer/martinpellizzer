Populus tschoudiana

Populus tschoudiana, commonly known as the Crimean poplar, is a species of poplar native to the Black Sea region.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows along riverbanks and in moist soils. The tree has smooth, light gray bark and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize riverbanks.
