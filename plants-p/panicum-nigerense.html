Panicum nigerense

Panicum nigerense, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
