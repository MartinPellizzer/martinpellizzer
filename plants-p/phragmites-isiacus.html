Phragmites isiacus

Phragmites isiacus, commonly known as common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It has long, slender leaves and produces dense clusters of feathery flowers. This plant is highly invasive and can dominate large areas of water bodies. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomes spread rapidly, making it a challenging species to control in aquatic ecosystems.
