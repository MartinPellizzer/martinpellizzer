Prunus sibirica

Prunus sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that ripen in late summer. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and its ability to thrive in cold climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit.

The wood of Prunus sibirica is also valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts.
