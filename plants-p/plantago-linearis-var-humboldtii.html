Plantago linearis var. humboldtii

Plantago linearis var. humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt plantain, is a variant of the common plantain native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
