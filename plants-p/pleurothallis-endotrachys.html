Pleurothallis endotrachys

Pleurothallis endotrachys, commonly known as the "blue-lipped pleurothallis," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive blueish lip. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with a prominent blue or violet hue on the lip.

Pleurothallis endotrachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral characteristics.
