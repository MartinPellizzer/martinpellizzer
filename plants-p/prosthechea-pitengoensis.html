Prosthechea pitengoensis

Prosthechea pitengoensis, commonly known as the Pitengo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clustered, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
