Polygala serpyllifolia var. vincoides

Polygala serpyllifolia var. vincoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish in color.

It is often found in open habitats such as gravelly slopes and coastal areas.
