Passiflora organensis var. marmorata

Passiflora organensis var. marmorata, commonly known as the marble passionflower, is a striking climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals, with a prominent crown of filaments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the fruits are edible berries that contain small, black seeds. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
