Phrynium heliconioides

Phrynium heliconioides, commonly known as the spiral ginger or banana leaf plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers arranged in a spiral pattern, which is a distinctive feature. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common ornamental and sometimes invasive species.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in landscaping for its dramatic foliage.
