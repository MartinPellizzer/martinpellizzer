Phlox spinosilla

Phlox spinosilla, commonly known as the spiny phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in stiff, spiny hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Phlox spinosilla is often found in open woods and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sun.
