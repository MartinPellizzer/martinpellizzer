Poulsenia

Poulsenia, commonly known as the fan palm, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Arecaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Poulsenia armata and Poulsenia zebrina, which are native to Central and South America. These plants are characterized by their large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in diameter. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their striking appearance and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Poulsenia species are also valued for their hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
