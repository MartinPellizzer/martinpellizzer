Pelargonium stenanthum

Pelargonium stenanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Pelargonium stenanthum is a hardy perennial that is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
