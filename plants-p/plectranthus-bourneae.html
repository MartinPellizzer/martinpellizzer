Plectranthus bourneae

Plectranthus bourneae, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
