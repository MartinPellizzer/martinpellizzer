Piper lepidotum

Piper lepidotum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like fruits that range in color from green to purple. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
