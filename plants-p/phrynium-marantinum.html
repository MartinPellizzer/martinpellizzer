Phrynium marantinum

Phrynium marantinum, commonly known as the red-veined taro, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking red veins. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is typically grown for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Phrynium marantinum is also occasionally cultivated for its edible tuberous roots in some regions.
