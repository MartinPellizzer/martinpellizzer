Pavonia rhodantha var. genuina

Pavonia rhodantha var. genuina, commonly known as the redstem false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pavonia rhodantha var. genuina is often found in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.
