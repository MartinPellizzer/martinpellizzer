Psilanthus leroyi

Psilanthus leroyi, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its root system is deep and helps stabilize soil, making it valuable in reforestation efforts.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
