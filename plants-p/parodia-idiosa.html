Parodia idiosa

Parodia idiosa, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its stems are ribbed and capable of storing water, making it highly adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Parodia idiosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
