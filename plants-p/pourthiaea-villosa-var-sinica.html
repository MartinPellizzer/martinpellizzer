Pourthiaea villosa var. sinica

Pourthiaea villosa var. sinica, commonly known as the Chinese silky dandelion, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, white composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
