Phleum alpinum var. scribnerianum

Phleum alpinum var. scribnerianum, commonly known as the Scribner's alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soils.
