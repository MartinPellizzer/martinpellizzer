Polygala myrtillopsis

Polygala myrtillopsis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear clusters of small, showy flowers.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
