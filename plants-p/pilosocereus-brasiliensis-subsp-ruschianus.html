Pilosocereus brasiliensis subsp. ruschianus

Pilosocereus brasiliensis subsp.

ruschianus, commonly known as the Brazilian night bloomer, is a cactus native to Brazil. It is a spineless species with a cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique flowering behavior and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens.
