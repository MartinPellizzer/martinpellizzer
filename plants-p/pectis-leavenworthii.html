Pectis leavenworthii

Pectis leavenworthii, commonly known as the Leavenworth's pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Pectis leavenworthii is adapted to arid environments and is considered a drought-tolerant species.
