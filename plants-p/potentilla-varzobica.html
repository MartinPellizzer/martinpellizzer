Potentilla varzobica

Potentilla varzobica, commonly known as the Altai cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla varzobica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
