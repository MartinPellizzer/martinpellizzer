Piper emarginatum

Piper emarginatum, commonly known as the marginated pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Piper emarginatum is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
