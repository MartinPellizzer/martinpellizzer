Primula calderiana subsp. calderiana

Primula calderiana subsp.

calderiana, commonly known as the Calder's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
