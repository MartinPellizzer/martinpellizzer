Podochilus callosus

Podochilus callosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often hairy or rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies by species.

Podochilus callosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
