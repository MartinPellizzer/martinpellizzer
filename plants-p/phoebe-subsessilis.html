Phoebe subsessilis

Phoebe subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are usually white or pale green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Phoebe subsessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
