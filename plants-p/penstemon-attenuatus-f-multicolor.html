Penstemon attenuatus f. multicolor

Penstemon attenuatus f. multicolor, commonly known as the multicolor beardtongue, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple and blue, depending on the cultivar. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.
