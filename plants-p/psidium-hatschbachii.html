Psidium hatschbachii

Psidium hatschbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or the Natal cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
