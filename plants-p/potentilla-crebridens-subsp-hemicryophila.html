Potentilla crebridens subsp. hemicryophila

Potentilla crebridens subsp.

hemicryophila, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, often hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a woody stem, trifoliate leaves, and flowers with five petals.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes.
