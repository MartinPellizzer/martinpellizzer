Pavonia acuminata

Pavonia acuminata, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more branched as it matures. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
