Protea elongata

Protea elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shrub form, featuring narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, elongated shape that gives the species its scientific name.

Protea elongata is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is characteristic of the Cape Floristic Region.
