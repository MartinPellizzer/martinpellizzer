Platystele filamentosa

Platystele filamentosa, commonly known as the filamentous platystele, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its small, tubular pseudobulbs and delicate, linear leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered together, and it relies on specific humidity and light conditions for optimal growth. Due to its restricted habitat and threatened status, it is considered a conservation concern.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
