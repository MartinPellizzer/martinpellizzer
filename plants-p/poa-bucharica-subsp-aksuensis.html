Poa bucharica subsp. aksuensis

Poa bucharica subsp.

aksuensis, commonly known as the Aksu bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
