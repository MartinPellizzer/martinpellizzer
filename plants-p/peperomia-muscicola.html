Peperomia muscicola

Peperomia muscicola, commonly known as the moss peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, velvety foliage with leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy, giving it a unique, moss-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, arranged in clusters near the base of the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia muscicola is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to tolerate low light conditions and minimal water requirements.
