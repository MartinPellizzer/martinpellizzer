Potentilla krasanii

Potentilla krasanii, commonly known as the Krasan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Potentilla krasanii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
