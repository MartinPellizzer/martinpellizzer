Periloba sessiliflora

Periloba sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower periloba, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with showy, white to pale pink petals that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in humid environments.
