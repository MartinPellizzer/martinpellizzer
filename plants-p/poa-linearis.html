Poa linearis

Poa linearis, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It is often found in association with other herbaceous plants in grassland ecosystems.

Poa linearis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
