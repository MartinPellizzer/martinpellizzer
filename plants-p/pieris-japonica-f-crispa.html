Pieris japonica f. crispa

Pieris japonica f. crispa, commonly known as the Japanese pieris with crinkled leaves, is a cultivar of the evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and crinkled foliage that adds a unique texture to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during spring. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in both its foliage and the delicate floral display.
