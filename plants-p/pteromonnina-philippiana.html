Pteromonnina philippiana

Pteromonnina philippiana, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its root system is specialized for nutrient absorption, and it plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a threatened species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
