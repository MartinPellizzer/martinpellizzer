Polygonum comptonianum

Polygonum comptonianum, commonly known as the Indian madder or Compton's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its bright red roots, which have been historically used as a natural dye. Polygonum comptonianum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
