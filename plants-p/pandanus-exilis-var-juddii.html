Pandanus exilis var. juddii

Pandanus exilis var. juddii, commonly known as the Judd's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
