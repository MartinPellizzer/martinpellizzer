Phoebe mollis

Phoebe mollis, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Phoebe mollis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
