Parasilaus

Parasilaus, commonly known as the parasitic orchid, is a genus of flowering plants that exhibits a unique parasitic lifestyle.

These plants lack chlorophyll and rely entirely on host plants for nutrients, often attaching themselves to the roots of other species. They are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where they thrive in shaded, humid environments. The flowers of Parasilaus are usually small and delicate, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to their specialized growth habits, they play a niche but important role in the ecological balance of their habitats.
