Panicum rubigerum

Panicum rubigerum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to tolerate drought, it is often used for erosion control and as a forage crop.
