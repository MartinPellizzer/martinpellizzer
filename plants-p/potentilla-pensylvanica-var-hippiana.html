Potentilla pensylvanica var. hippiana

Potentilla pensylvanica var. hippiana, commonly known as the Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are oval-shaped and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woods and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
