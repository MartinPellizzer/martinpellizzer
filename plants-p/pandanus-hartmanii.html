Pandanus hartmanii

Pandanus hartmanii, commonly known as the Hartman's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves are fibrous and have been traditionally used for making thatch and rope.

Pandanus hartmanii thrives in coastal and wetland areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
