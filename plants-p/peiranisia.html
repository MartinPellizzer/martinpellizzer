Peiranisia

Peiranisia, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. Peiranisia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
