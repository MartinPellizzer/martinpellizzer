Polygonum sinomontanum

Polygonum sinomontanum, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often zigzagging stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Polygonum sinomontanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
