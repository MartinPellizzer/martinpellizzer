Pouteria martinicensis

Pouteria martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique sapote, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons, with a smooth, orange-colored skin and a sweet, creamy pulp. Its growth habit includes a thick, woody trunk and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas across its native range.
