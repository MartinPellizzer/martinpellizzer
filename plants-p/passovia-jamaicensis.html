Passovia jamaicensis

Passovia jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that resemble the shape of a heart, with five petals and a distinctive corona of filaments. The plant belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is known for its complex floral structure, which includes both stamens and carpels. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passovia jamaicensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
