Philodendron mexicanum

Philodendron mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
