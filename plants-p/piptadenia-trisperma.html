Piptadenia trisperma

Piptadenia trisperma, commonly known as the yellow fever tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the dry season, followed by fruit that splits open to release seeds.

It is often found in riverine forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
