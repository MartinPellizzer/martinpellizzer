Potentilla polyphylla var. miranda

Potentilla polyphylla var. miranda, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and compact growth form.
