Pedicularis resupinata subsp. teucriifolia

Pedicularis resupinata subsp.

teucriifolia, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features striking blue to violet flowers that are tubular in shape and often adorned with intricate patterns.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and covered with glandular dots. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to cultivate rare and ornamental flora.
