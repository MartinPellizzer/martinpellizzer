Pernettya melanocarpa

Pernettya melanocarpa, commonly known as the black bilberry, is a species of shrub native to the boreal and temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other ericaceous plants like heathers and blueberries. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a glossy appearance.

Pernettya melanocarpa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
