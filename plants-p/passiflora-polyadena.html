Passiflora polyadena

Passiflora polyadena, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown of white filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Passiflora polyadena is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported calming and sedative properties.
