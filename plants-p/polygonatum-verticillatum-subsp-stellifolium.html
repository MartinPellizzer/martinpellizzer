Polygonatum verticillatum subsp. stellifolium

Polygonatum verticillatum subsp.

stellifolium, commonly known as the star-leaved Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It features a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, dark green leaves arranged in whorls.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in loose clusters, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
