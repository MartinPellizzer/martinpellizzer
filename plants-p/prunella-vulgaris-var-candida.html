Prunella vulgaris var. candida

Prunella vulgaris var. candida, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

White dead-nettle is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows.
