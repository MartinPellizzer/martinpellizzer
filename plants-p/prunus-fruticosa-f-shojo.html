Prunus fruticosa f. shojo

Prunus fruticosa f. shojo, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, rounded form and profuse blooms of pink or white flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for bonsai and small garden spaces.
