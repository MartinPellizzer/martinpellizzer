Passiflora alata var. acuminata

Passiflora alata var. acuminata, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are palmately lobed and can grow quite large, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local wildlife.
