Plectrocarpa tetracantha

Plectrocarpa tetracantha, commonly known as the four-branch screw vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its helical growth pattern and distinctive, four-angled stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Plectrocarpa tetracantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
