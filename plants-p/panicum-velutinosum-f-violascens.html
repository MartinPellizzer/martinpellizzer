Panicum velutinosum f. violascens

Panicum velutinosum f. violascens, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive purple to violet-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
