Pearcea pileifolia

Pearcea pileifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf pearcea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
