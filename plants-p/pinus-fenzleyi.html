Pinus fenzleyi

Pinus fenzleyi, commonly known as the Fenzley pine, is a rare and endangered species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The tree has short, twisted branches and produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
