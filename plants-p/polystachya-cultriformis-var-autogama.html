Polystachya cultriformis var. autogama

Polystachya cultriformis var. autogama, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, arching fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping underground stems that allow it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
