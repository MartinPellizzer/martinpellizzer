Physostegia virginiana f. alba

Physostegia virginiana f. alba, commonly known as white whorled pogostemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features showy, tubular white flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
