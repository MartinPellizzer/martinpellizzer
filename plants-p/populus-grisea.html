Populus grisea

Populus grisea, commonly known as the gray poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its silver-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Populus grisea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
