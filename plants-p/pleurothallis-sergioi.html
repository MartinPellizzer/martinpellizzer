Pleurothallis sergioi

Pleurothallis sergioi, commonly known as the Sergio's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
