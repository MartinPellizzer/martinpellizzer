Piper longifolium

Piper longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic foliage.
