Physurus vaginatus

Physurus vaginatus, commonly known as the hooded violets, is a species of flowering plant in the family Violaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive violet-colored flowers with a hood-like structure over the petals. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas.

The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
