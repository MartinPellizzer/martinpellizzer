Panicum bulawayense

Panicum bulawayense, commonly known as the Bulawayo grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used for erosion control.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
