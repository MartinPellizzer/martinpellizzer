Potentilla crassinervia var. genuina

Potentilla crassinervia var. genuina, commonly known as the broad-veined cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically compound, with five to seven leaflets that are serrated and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and floral display.
