Phlox alyssifolia subsp. collina

Phlox alyssifolia subsp.

collina, commonly known as the alpine phlox, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging alpine environments.
