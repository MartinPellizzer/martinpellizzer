Potentilla micropetala subsp. byssitecta

Potentilla micropetala subsp.

byssitecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, serrated leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, arranged in a compact, mat-like growth habit.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky environments.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
