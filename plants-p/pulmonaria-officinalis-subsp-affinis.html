Pulmonaria officinalis subsp. affinis

Pulmonaria officinalis subsp.

affinis, commonly known as lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It features distinctive, spotted leaves with a broad, lance-shaped form and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its glandular hairs on the leaves and stems. It thrives in moist, shady woodland areas and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its common name reflects its historical use for respiratory ailments.
