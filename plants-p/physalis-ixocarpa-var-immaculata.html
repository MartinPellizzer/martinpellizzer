Physalis ixocarpa var. immaculata

Physalis ixocarpa var. immaculata, commonly known as the goldenberry or Inca berry, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub that produces small, orange-yellow fruits encased in a papery calyx. The plant is native to South America, particularly the Andean region, and thrives in temperate climates. Its fruit is edible, sweet, and often used in jams, candies, and as a decorative element.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit characteristics.
