Pteronia oppositifolia

Pteronia oppositifolia, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in a shrub form, with opposite, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Pteronia oppositifolia is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
