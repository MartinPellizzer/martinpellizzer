Phymatopteris taiwanensis

Phymatopteris taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan wall-r Fern, is a species of fern native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
