Potentilla holosericea

Potentilla holosericea, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, which give it its common name.

Potentilla holosericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
