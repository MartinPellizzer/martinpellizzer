Piper ceibense

Piper ceibense, commonly known as the ceiba pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often trail along the ground or climb trees. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.
