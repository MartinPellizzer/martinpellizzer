Pandanus multidrupaceus

Pandanus multidrupaceus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clustered, fragrant inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its fruit, known as a "breadfruit," is edible and rich in nutrients.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
