Plerandra grayi

Plerandra grayi, commonly known as the Grey's plerandra, is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant features a stout, leafy stem with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, enclosed by a spathe, a typical feature of the Araceae family.

Plerandra grayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and exotic appearance.
