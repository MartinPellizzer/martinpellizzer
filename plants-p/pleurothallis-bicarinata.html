Pleurothallis bicarinata

Pleurothallis bicarinata, commonly known as the two-ribbed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters along the inflorescence. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in two rows, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pleurothallis bicarinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
