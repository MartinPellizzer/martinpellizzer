Pyrus asterope

Pyrus asterope, commonly known as the starleaf pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Pyrus asterope prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
