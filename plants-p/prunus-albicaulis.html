Prunus albicaulis

Prunus albicaulis, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its fruit is a small, hard drupe that turns red when mature.

Prunus albicaulis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
