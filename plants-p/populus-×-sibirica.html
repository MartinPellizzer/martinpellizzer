Populus × sibirica

Populus × sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in river valleys and forest edges.

Its fast growth rate makes it a valuable species for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
