Pabstiella teschiana

Pabstiella teschiana, commonly known as the Teschian sunburst, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Pabstiella teschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
