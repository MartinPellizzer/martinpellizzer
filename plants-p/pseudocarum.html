Pseudocarum

Pseudocarum, commonly known as the false caraway, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia. The plants typically have aromatic leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. They are often found in dry, rocky habitats and are valued for their medicinal properties.

Pseudocarum species are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
