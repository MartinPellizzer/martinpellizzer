Pavonia riedelii

Pavonia riedelii, commonly known as the redstem false mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are typically hairy and reddish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia riedelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
