Phaseolus xolocotzii

Phaseolus xolocotzii, commonly known as the Mexican bean or Xolocotzti bean, is a species native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing legume with trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers, typical of the Phaseolus genus. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This species is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for cultivation and genetic diversity.
