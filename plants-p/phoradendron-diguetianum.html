Phoradendron diguetianum

Phoradendron diguetianum, commonly known as the Brazilian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a succulent, leafy appearance with small, elliptical leaves and produces white or yellow flowers. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it photosynthesizes to obtain other nutrients. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain sticky seeds that adhere to branches, facilitating propagation.

This species plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for birds and other animals.
