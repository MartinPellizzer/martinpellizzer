Pyrus jintaroana

Pyrus jintaroana, commonly known as the Jintaro apple, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. Its fruit has a tart flavor and is sometimes used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential horticultural uses.
