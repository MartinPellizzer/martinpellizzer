Polypogon monspeliensis var. monolepis

Polypogon monspeliensis var. monolepis, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
