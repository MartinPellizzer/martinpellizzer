Pyrus polyanthema

Pyrus polyanthema, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet, yellowish pears in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Pyrus polyanthema is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a food source in its native regions.
