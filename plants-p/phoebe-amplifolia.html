Phoebe amplifolia

Phoebe amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly found in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, dark berries.

Phoebe amplifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and pleasant scent.
