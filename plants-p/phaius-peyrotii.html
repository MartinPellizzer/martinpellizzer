Phaius peyrotii

Phaius peyrotii, commonly known as the Peyrot's orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces large, fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in a loose spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Phaius peyrotii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
