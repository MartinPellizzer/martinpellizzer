Philodendron meridense

Philodendron meridense, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with deep green foliage and prominent red veins, giving it a vibrant, eye-catching appearance. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and aquatic plants. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron meridense is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
