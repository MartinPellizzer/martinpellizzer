Polygala equisetoides

Polygala equisetoides, commonly known as the horse-hair plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often resemble those of the horsetail plant, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually greenish-white or pale yellow, and they bloom in late summer or early autumn.
