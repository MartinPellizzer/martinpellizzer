Pyrethrum komarovii

Pyrethrum komarovii, commonly known as the Tibetan pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Tibet and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant produces pyrethrin, a natural insecticide, which is extracted from its flower heads. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Pyrethrum komarovii is also valued in organic pest control due to its biodegradable and non-toxic nature.
