Pabstiella sparsiflora

Pabstiella sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparsely flowering orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its root system is specialized for absorbing nutrients from the soil, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
