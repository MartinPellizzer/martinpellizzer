Polygala monticola var. obovata

Polygala monticola var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to obovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers that are often clustered in racemes.

It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates.
