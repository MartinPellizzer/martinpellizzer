Pugiopappus

Pugiopappus, commonly known as the desert pappus, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are often yellow or white, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Pugiopappus is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
