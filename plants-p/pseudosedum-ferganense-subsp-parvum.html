Pseudosedum ferganense subsp. parvum

Pseudosedum ferganense subsp.

parvum, commonly known as the small-flowered stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, typical of xerophytic plants.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in dry climates.
