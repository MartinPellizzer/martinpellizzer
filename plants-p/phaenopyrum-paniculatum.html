Phaenopyrum paniculatum

Phaenopyrum paniculatum, commonly known as the golden chain tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

Phaenopyrum paniculatum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
