Phebalium glandulosum subsp. riparium

Phebalium glandulosum subsp.

riparium, commonly known as the river phebalium, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows along riverbanks and damp soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit with lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its subspecies is distinguished by its glandular leaves and preference for moist habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing riverbanks and provides habitat for native wildlife.
