Pandanus medialinermis

Pandanus medialinermis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. Its root system is fibrous and well adapted to coastal and sandy soils.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its cultural and practical uses in local communities.
