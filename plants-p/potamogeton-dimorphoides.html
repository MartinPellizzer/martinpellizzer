Potamogeton dimorphoides

Potamogeton dimorphoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Potamogeton dimorphoides plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
