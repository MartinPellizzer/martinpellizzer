Plantago major subsp. lutulenta

Plantago major subsp.

lutulenta, commonly known as greater plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks.

The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in a variety of soil types, including disturbed or compacted soils. It is commonly found in lawns, fields, and along roadsides, where it can spread readily through its rhizomatous growth.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves historically used to treat wounds and digestive issues.
