Piptochaetium stipoides

Piptochaetium stipoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate moderate grazing.

Piptochaetium stipoides is valued for its potential use in forage and soil stabilization in arid environments.
