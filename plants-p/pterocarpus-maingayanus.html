Pterocarpus maingayanus

Pterocarpus maingayanus, commonly known as the Philippine padauk, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
