Pelargonium cucullatum subsp. tabulare

Pelargonium cucullatum subsp.

tabulare, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features upright, branched stems with hairy surfaces and dark green, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
