Phoenicaulis eurycarpa

Phoenicaulis eurycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited phoenicea, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, followed by distinctive, broad, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

However, it is relatively rare and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
