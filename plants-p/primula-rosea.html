Primula rosea

Primula rosea, commonly known as the pink primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and prefers cool, moist environments. Its flowers are tubular and have five petals, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from pale pink to almost white.

Primula rosea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
