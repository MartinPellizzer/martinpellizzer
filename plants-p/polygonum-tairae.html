Polygonum tairae

Polygonum tairae, commonly known as Tairai knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish hue, especially along the veins.

Polygonum tairae is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
