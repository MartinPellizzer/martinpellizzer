Piper arborioccupans

Piper arborioccupans, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vining growth habit. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Piper arborioccupans is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
