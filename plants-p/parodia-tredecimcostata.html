Parodia tredecimcostata

Parodia tredecimcostata, commonly known as the thirteen-costate cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and xerophytic gardens.
