Polygala amblyptera var. pubescens

Polygala amblyptera var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with distinctive spurred petals that attract specific pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
