Plinia microcycla

Plinia microcycla, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with elliptical, leathery leaves. Its flowers are small and white, and it produces fruit in clusters.

The berries are valued for their tart flavor and are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
