Prunus argentinensis

Prunus argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible cherries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
