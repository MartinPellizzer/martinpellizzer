Pyrus elaeagnifolia

Pyrus elaeagnifolia, commonly known as the Russian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, greenish-yellow fruits. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, often turning red in the fall. This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in urban environments.
