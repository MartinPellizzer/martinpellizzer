Piper boehmeriifolium var. glabricaule

Piper boehmeriifolium var. glabricaule, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed pepper plant, is a variant of the pepper plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing the aromatic seeds used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often used in traditional medicine and cooking.
