Pulsatilla patens subsp. flavescens

Pulsatilla patens subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from yellow to pale pink.

The plant produces a single, upright stem with palmate leaves and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The flower's structure and coloration make it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a symbol of resilience in harsh environments.
