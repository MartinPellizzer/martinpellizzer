Piper venamoense var. venezuelense

Piper venamoense var. venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries, which are typically used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
