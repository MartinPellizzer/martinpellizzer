Psidium pyriferum var. glabrum

Psidium pyriferum var. glabrum, commonly known as the glabrous guava, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, citrus-scented fruits. The fruit is typically round or oval, with a thin, smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
