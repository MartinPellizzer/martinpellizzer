Psorospermum androsaemifolium

Psorospermum androsaemifolium, commonly known as the Andros Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Andros.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and moist lowland forests. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
