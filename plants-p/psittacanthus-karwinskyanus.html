Psittacanthus karwinskyanus

Psittacanthus karwinskyanus, commonly known as the golden button fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly curled appearance, and it produces small, golden spore cases known as sporangia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
