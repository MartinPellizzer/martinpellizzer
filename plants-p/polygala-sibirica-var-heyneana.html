Polygala sibirica var. heyneana

Polygala sibirica var. heyneana, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate to subarctic environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, and the flowers are often pale yellow or greenish in color.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
