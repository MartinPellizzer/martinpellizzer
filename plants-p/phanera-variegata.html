Phanera variegata

Phanera variegata, commonly known as the variegated phanera, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Phanera variegata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal.
