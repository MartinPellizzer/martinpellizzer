Pachystachys badiospica

Pachystachys badiospica, commonly known as the dark-spined pachystachys, is a striking member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves with a dark green hue and prominent, spiny structures along the leaf edges. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Pachystachys badiospica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
