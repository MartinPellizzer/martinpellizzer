Pyrus talgarica

Pyrus talgarica, commonly known as the Talgaric pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible pears that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
