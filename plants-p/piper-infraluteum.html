Piper infraluteum

Piper infraluteum, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are often yellow or orange when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
