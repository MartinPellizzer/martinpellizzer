Paphiopedilum × lushuiense

Paphiopedilum × lushuiense, commonly known as the Lushui Orchid, is a hybrid species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a specialized lip that aids in pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in shades of white, pink, and purple, with a velvety texture.

Paphiopedilum × lushuiense is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and resilience in various growing conditions.
