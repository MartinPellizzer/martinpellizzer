Perralderia purpurascens

Perralderia purpurascens, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and thrives in marshy or swampy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its distinctive red stems and foliage make it visually striking in its natural habitat.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
