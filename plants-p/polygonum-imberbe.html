Polygonum imberbe

Polygonum imberbe, commonly known as the smooth knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive swollen nodes along the stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, producing tiny, three-lobed berries.

Polygonum imberbe is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in moist to wet conditions.
