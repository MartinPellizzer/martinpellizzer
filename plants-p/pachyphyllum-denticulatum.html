Pachyphyllum denticulatum

Pachyphyllum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
