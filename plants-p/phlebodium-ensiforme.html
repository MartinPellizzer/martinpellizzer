Phlebodium ensiforme

Phlebodium ensiforme, commonly known as the bird's nest fern, is a tropical fern native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that resemble a bird's nest, hence its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome that forms dense mats, often growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Phlebodium ensiforme is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
