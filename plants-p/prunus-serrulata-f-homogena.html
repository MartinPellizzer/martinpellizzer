Prunus serrulata f. homogena

Prunus serrulata f. homogena, commonly known as the Japanese cherry tree, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its showy spring blossoms. The tree produces small, oval leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The flowers, which appear in early spring, are typically pale pink to white and bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual effect to the environment.
