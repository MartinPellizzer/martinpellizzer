Pronephrium aquatiloides

Pronephrium aquatiloides, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized structures for gas exchange.

It possesses bladder-like organs called pneumatophores that help it absorb oxygen from the water. The plant is typically found in shallow, still freshwater environments where it can thrive. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, while the main body is composed of a central stem.

Pronephrium aquatiloides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
