Pyrus daliensis

Pyrus daliensis, commonly known as the Dali pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive pears.

The Dali pear is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
