Protea anethifolia

Protea anethifolia, commonly known as the anise-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of a central cone surrounded by colorful bracts. The leaves are narrow and resemble anise leaves, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

This plant is not only ornamental but also plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
