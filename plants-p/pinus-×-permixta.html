Pinus × permixta

Pinus × permixta, commonly known as the cross-pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus ponderosa and Pinus strobiformis.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically longer and more slender than those of its parent species. The plant produces cones that are often reddish-brown in color and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to various environmental conditions.
