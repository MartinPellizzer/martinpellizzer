Platanthera bracteata

Platanthera bracteata, commonly known as the bracted twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. The plant has a distinctive appearance with pale green to yellowish leaves and a single, slender flowering stem. Its flowers are small, white or greenish, and are arranged in a spike, often with a single flower at the tip.

The species is known for its mycorrhizal association, relying on fungal partners for nutrient uptake, which is a common feature among orchids.
