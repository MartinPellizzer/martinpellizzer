Pithecellobium collinum

Pithecellobium collinum, commonly known as the mountain acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, woody pods containing edible seeds.
