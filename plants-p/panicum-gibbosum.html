Panicum gibbosum

Panicum gibbosum, commonly known as the rough bluestem, is a native grass species found in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of native grasslands and prairies.

Panicum gibbosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
