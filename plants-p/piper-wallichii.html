Piper wallichii

Piper wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan black pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a peppery aroma, contributing to its medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dense forest understories.
