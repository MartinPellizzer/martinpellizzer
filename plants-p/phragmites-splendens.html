Phragmites splendens

Phragmites splendens, commonly known as the splendid reed, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in brackish or freshwater marshes and is often found in coastal areas.

Phragmites splendens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
