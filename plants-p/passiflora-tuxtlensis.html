Passiflora tuxtlensis

Passiflora tuxtlensis, commonly known as the Tuxtlans passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America, particularly found in Mexico.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex floral structure. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often blue or purple in color, with a distinctive crown of filaments. Its fruit, a small, round berry, turns from green to orange when mature.

Passiflora tuxtlensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
