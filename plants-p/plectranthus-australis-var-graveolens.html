Plectranthus australis var. graveolens

Plectranthus australis var. graveolens, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, slender leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in herbal teas and as a natural insect repellent.
