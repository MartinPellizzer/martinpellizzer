Pternopetalum vulgare var. foliosum

Pternopetalum vulgare var. foliosum, commonly known as the common pternopetalum, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2-5 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
