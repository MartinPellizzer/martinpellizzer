Pleroma vimineum

Pleroma vimineum, commonly known as the climbing plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in clusters. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Pleroma vimineum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along supports.
