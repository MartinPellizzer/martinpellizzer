Panax quinquefolius var. coreensis

Panax quinquefolius var. coreensis, commonly known as the Korean ginseng, is a variant of the American ginseng species native to Korea.

It is characterized by its distinctive fleshy, yellowish root and dark green, palmately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, forested areas with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a short, rhizomatous stem and compound leaves with five leaflets.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Korean medicine.
