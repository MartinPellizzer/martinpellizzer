Polygonum romanum

Polygonum romanum, commonly known as buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its triangular, edible seeds and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Polygonum romanum is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal medicine.
