Pithecellobium pubescens

Pithecellobium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
