Phoebe blepharopus

Phoebe blepharopus, commonly known as the narrowleaf phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
