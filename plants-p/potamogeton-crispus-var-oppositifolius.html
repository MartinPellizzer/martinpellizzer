Potamogeton crispus var. oppositifolius

Potamogeton crispus var. oppositifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a distinct serrated edge. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in clusters, and it often grows in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often used as a bioindicator in ecological studies.
