Phylica comptonii

Phylica comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red flowers that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Phylica comptonii thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
