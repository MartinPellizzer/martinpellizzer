Pultenaea divaricata

Pultenaea divaricata, commonly known as the variable pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are alternate and often have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Pultenaea divaricata is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathlands and woodland areas.
