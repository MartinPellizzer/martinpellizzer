Pandanus matukuensis

Pandanus matukuensis, commonly known as the Matukuensi pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in regions such as French Polynesia and Tonga. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by fibrous, spiky fruits.

This species is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in the communities where it is found.
