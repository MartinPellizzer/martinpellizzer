Potamogeton zosterifolius f. magnus

Potamogeton zosterifolius f. magnus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving rivers.
