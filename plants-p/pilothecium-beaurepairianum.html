Pilothecium beaurepairianum

Pilothecium beaurepairianum, commonly known as the golden cup fungus, is a species of ascomycete fungus characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped fruiting body.

It is typically found in forested areas, particularly in regions with moist, shaded environments. The fungus produces spores in a unique, sac-like structure called an ascus, which is a defining feature of the Ascomycota division. Its habitat and morphology make it an important indicator species for certain ecosystems.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is relatively rare and often overlooked in ecological studies.
