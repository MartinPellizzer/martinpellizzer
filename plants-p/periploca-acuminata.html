Periploca acuminata

Periploca acuminata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals, containing cardiac glycosides. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Periploca acuminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
