Papaver somniferum var. hortense

Papaver somniferum var. hortense, commonly known as the garden opium poppy, is a variant of the opium poppy characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It typically produces vibrant red or pink petals that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It has a taproot system and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with serrated, lance-shaped leaves.

While it is cultivated for its beauty, it is also the source of alkaloids like morphine and codeine.
