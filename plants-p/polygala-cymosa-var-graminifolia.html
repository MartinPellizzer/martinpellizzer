Polygala cymosa var. graminifolia

Polygala cymosa var. graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically pale pink or white. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
