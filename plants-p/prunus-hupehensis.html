Prunus hupehensis

Prunus hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

Prunus hupehensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
