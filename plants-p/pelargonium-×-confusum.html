Pelargonium × confusum

Pelargonium × confusum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its compact growth and ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium × confusum is native to South Africa and is valued for its adaptability to various growing conditions.
