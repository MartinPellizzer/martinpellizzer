Prunus serrulata f. longa

Prunus serrulata f. longa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties may be referred to as the weeping cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its pendulous, weeping branches that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
