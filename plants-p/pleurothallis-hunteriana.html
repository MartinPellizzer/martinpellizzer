Pleurothallis hunteriana

Pleurothallis hunteriana, commonly known as the Hunter's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Pleurothallis hunteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
