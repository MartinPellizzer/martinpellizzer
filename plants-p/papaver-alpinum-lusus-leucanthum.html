Papaver alpinum lusus leucanthum

Papaver alpinum lusus leucanthum, commonly known as the white alpine poppy, is a rare and striking member of the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its unique appearance and limited natural distribution.
