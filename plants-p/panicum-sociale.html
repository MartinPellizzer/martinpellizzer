Panicum sociale

Panicum sociale, commonly known as lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common ornamental and forage plant.

Its ability to form dense mats makes it useful for erosion control and landscaping.
