Polygala andringitrensis

Polygala andringitrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces showy, often pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to the dry and rocky environments of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
