Papaver × ambiguum

Papaver × ambiguum, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a naturally occurring cross between *Papaver rhoeas* (common poppy) and *Papaver orientale* (oriental poppy).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
