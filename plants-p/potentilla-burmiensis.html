Potentilla burmiensis

Potentilla burmiensis, commonly known as Burmese cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and has a compact, shrub-like structure. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually reddish in color.

Potentilla burmiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
