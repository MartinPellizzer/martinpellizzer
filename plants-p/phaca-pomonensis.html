Phaca pomonensis

Phaca pomonensis, commonly known as the apple tree of the tropics, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, sweet, and edible fruits that are similar in taste to apples. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaca pomonensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
