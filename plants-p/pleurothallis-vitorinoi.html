Pleurothallis vitorinoi

Pleurothallis vitorinoi, commonly known as the Vitorino's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and a single, slender flower spike. The flowers are typically white or pale green with a yellowish lip, and they bloom during the rainy season. This species is part of the large and diverse Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species of orchids.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
