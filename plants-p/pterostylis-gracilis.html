Pterostylis gracilis

Pterostylis gracilis, commonly known as the slender greenhood, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a single, slender stem with a single leaf and a single, greenish-brown flower. Its orchidaceous structure includes a specialized labellum that aids in pollination by specific insects.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
