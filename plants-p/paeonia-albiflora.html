Paeonia albiflora

Paeonia albiflora, commonly known as the white peony, is a deciduous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant blooms.

Paeonia albiflora is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
