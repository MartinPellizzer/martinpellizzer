Pterocephalus szovitsii

Pterocephalus szovitsii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are small and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Pterocephalus szovitsii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
