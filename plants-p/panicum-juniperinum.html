Panicum juniperinum

Panicum juniperinum, commonly known as Junipergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, contributing to its ecological importance in native ecosystems.
