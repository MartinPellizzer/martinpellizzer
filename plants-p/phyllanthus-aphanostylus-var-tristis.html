Phyllanthus aphanostylus var. tristis

Phyllanthus aphanostylus var. tristis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
