Prunus chiapensis

Prunus chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico, specifically the state of Chiapas, and is known for its small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white or pink flowers in the spring.

The fruits are small, yellow or red when ripe, and are used in traditional local cuisine.
