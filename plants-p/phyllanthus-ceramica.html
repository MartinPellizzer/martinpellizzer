Phyllanthus ceramica

Phyllanthus ceramica, commonly known as the Ceramica spurge, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bead-like fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
