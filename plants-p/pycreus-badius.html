Pycreus badius

Pycreus badius, commonly known as the broad-leaved pycreus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and can spread moderately, making it adaptable to various soil types.

Pycreus badius is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
