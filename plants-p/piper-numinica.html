Piper numinica

Piper numinica, commonly known as the sacred pepper or numinica pepper, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are often used in traditional medicine and rituals. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Piper numinica is valued not only for its botanical features but also for its cultural significance in indigenous communities.
