Peschiera salzmannii

Peschiera salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
