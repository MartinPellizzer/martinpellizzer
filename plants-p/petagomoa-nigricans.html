Petagomoa nigricans

Petagomoa nigricans, commonly known as the black-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis using its scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or yellow flowers, followed by berries that are dispersed by birds. Its root system forms haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract resources.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
