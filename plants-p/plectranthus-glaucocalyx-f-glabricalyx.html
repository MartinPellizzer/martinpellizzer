Plectranthus glaucocalyx f. glabricalyx

Plectranthus glaucocalyx f. glabricalyx, commonly known as the smooth-leaved blue mint, is a variant of the blue mint plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, smooth leaves that are typically a striking blue-green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is often grown for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
