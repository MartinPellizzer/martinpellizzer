Persea kobu

Persea kobu, commonly known as the African persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits with a sweet, nutty flavor. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
