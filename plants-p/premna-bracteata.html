Premna bracteata

Premna bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The leaves and fruits are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
