Polypogon brachyphyllus

Polypogon brachyphyllus, commonly known as the short-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, cone-like structures containing the flowers.

Polypogon brachyphyllus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
