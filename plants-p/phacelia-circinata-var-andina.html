Phacelia circinata var. andina

Phacelia circinata var. andina, commonly known as the Andean phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
