Phacelia hispida var. heliophila

Phacelia hispida var. heliophila, commonly known as the hairy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
