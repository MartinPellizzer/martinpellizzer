Phoradendron uncinatum

Phoradendron uncinatum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

The plant produces small, white or green berries that contain toxic seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Its sticky seeds adhere to the bark of trees, allowing the plant to establish new growth.

Phoradendron uncinatum plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing tree growth and providing food for certain wildlife species.
