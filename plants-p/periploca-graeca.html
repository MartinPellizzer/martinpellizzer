Periploca graeca

Periploca graeca, commonly known as Greek spurge, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers, which are followed by orange-red berries. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

Periploca graeca is also known for its latex sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
