Patagonium glaucescens

Patagonium glaucescens, commonly known as the Patagonian bluebush, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of southern South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and blue-gray foliage, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often studied for its role in alpine ecosystems.
