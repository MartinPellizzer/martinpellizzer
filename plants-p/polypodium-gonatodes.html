Polypodium gonatodes

Polypodium gonatodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Polypodium gonatodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
