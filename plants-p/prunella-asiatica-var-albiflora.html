Prunella asiatica var. albiflora

Prunella asiatica var. albiflora, commonly known as white flowered selfheal, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
