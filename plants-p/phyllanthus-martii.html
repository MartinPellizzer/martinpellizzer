Phyllanthus martii

Phyllanthus martii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also cultivated as an ornamental due to its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
