Pyrus teodori

Pyrus teodori, commonly known as the Teodor's pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits.

Pyrus teodori is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential agricultural use, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
