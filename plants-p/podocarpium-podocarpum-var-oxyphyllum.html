Podocarpium podocarpum var. oxyphyllum

Podocarpium podocarpum var. oxyphyllum, commonly known as the sweet-scented fern, is a perennial plant characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features rhizomes that produce clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive sweet aroma. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.
