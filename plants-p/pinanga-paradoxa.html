Pinanga paradoxa

Pinanga paradoxa, commonly known as the giant bamboo of Borneo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 15 meters in height, forming dense clumps. The plant has long, narrow leaves with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Pinanga paradoxa plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
