Planera carpinifolia

Planera carpinifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and parks around the world.
