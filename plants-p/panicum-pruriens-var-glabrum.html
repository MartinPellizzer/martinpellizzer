Panicum pruriens var. glabrum

Panicum pruriens var. glabrum, commonly known as velvet grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, and hairy foliage, which gives it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
