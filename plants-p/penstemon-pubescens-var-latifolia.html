Penstemon pubescens var. latifolia

Penstemon pubescens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
