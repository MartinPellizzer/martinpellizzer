Primula macrophylla subsp. moorcroftiana

Primula macrophylla subsp.

moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Highland primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine white or silver down.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
