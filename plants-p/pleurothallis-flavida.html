Pleurothallis flavida

Pleurothallis flavida, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. It has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant blooms.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
