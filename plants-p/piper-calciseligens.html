Piper calciseligens

Piper calciseligens, commonly known as the limestone pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and less-studied species within its family.
