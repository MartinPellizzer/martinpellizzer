Primula strumosa var. peralata

Primula strumosa var. peralata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
