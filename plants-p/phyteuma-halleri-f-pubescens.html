Phyteuma halleri f. pubescens

Phyteuma halleri f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, giving it the "pubescent" designation.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in partial shade.
