Plagiosiphon emarginatus

Plagiosiphon emarginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds and streams, where it forms dense, floating mats. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with a distinctive wavy margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, producing yellowish-green blooms.

Plagiosiphon emarginatus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
