Poterium guestphalicum

Poterium guestphalicum, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and its tall, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. The flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
