Picea × moseri

Picea × moseri, commonly known as the Sitka white spruce, is a hybrid conifer native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically 10–20 mm long. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its timber and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
