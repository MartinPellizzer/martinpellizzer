Primula stuartii

Primula stuartii, commonly known as Stuart's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring, and its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves. Primula stuartii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
