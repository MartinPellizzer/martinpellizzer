Primula blinii

Primula blinii, commonly known as the Blinii primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves that form a dense, compact structure. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
