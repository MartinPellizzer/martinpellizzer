Perakanthus longipetalus

Perakanthus longipetalus, commonly known as the long-petaled perakanthus, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in dense forests. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a deep green hue.

Perakanthus longipetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
