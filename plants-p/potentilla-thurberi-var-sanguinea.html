Potentilla thurberi var. sanguinea

Potentilla thurberi var. sanguinea, commonly known as the red potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
