Polygonum bicorne

Polygonum bicorne, commonly known as the two-horned knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhomboidal or arrowhead-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish and covered with fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous.

Polygonum bicorne is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
