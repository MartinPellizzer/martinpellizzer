Pollinia ciliata var. breviaristata

Pollinia ciliata var. breviaristata, commonly known as the short-armed cat's paw, is a variant of the cat's paw plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finger-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include a succulent stem and fleshy, lobed leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in local communities.
