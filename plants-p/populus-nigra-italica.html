Populus nigra italica

Populus nigra italica, commonly known as the Italian black poplar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, aggregate fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

This species is valued for its ecological role and has historical significance in forestry and landscaping.
