Polemonium caeruleum var. chinense

Polemonium caeruleum var. chinense, commonly known as Chinese bluebell, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
