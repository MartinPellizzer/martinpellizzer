Proustia cuneifolia var. integrifolia

Proustia cuneifolia var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf proustia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are smooth-edged, giving it its varietal name "integrifolia.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its foliage is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
