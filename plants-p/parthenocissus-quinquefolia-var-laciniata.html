Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. laciniata

Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf ivy, is a climbing vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, lobed leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red to orange berries in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to cover walls or trellises.

Its foliage changes color in the autumn, adding seasonal visual interest to gardens.
