Polygonum persicaria var. amblyophyllum

Polygonum persicaria var. amblyophyllum, commonly known as broadleaf hogweed, is a variant of the common hogweed species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, riverbanks, and along roadsides. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
