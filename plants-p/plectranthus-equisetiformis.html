Plectranthus equisetiformis

Plectranthus equisetiformis, commonly known as the horse-tail plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Plectranthus equisetiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
