Pleurothallis canidentis

Pleurothallis canidentis, commonly known as the toothed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees, relying on its host for physical support rather than nutrients. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and are notable for their intricate, spurred petals.

Pleurothallis canidentis is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
