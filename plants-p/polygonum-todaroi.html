Polygonum todaroi

Polygonum todaroi, commonly known as the Malabar knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white or reddish, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Polygonum todaroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
