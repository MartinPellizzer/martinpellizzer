Piper denudatum

Piper denudatum, commonly known as the naked pipevine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruits are three-winged berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
