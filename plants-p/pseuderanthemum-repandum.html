Pseuderanthemum repandum

Pseuderanthemum repandum, commonly known as the red-stemmed pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting red or purple calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
