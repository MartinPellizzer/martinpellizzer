Persicaria vacciniifolia

Persicaria vacciniifolia, commonly known as the Vaccinium-leaved knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that resemble those of the blueberry plant, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

Persicaria vacciniifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
