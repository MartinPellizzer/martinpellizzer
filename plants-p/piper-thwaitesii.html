Piper thwaitesii

Piper thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants.

Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this vine is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red upon maturation. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The botanical features of Piper thwaitesii make it a unique and interesting species within the Piper genus.
