Porophyllum confertum

Porophyllum confertum, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Porophyllum confertum thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species adapted to harsh conditions.
