Petasites japonicus f. pubescens

Petasites japonicus f. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese buttercup fern, is a variant of the Japanese petasites.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, fleshy rhizome. The plant is native to parts of East Asia, including Japan and Korea. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
