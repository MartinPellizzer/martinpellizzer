Panicum walense

Panicum walense, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop.
