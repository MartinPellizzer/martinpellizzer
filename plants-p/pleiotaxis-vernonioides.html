Pleiotaxis vernonioides

Pleiotaxis vernonioides, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, woody structure and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its thick, waxy leaves.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.
