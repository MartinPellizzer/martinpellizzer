Persepolium spinosum

Persepolium spinosum, commonly known as the spiny persian plant, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Persepolium spinosum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
