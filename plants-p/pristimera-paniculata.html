Pristimera paniculata

Pristimera paniculata, commonly known as the panicle grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, branching inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture, and its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Pristimera paniculata is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive, feathery appearance.
