Polygonum brachypodum

Polygonum brachypodum, commonly known as the shortfoot smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum brachypodum is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed heads and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
