Poa matthewsii var. tenuis

Poa matthewsii var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender matthews sedge, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it well adapted to its wetland habitat, with rhizomes that help in stabilizing the soil.
