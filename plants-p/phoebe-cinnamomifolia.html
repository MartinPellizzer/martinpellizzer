Phoebe cinnamomifolia

Phoebe cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf phoebe, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often fragrant and resemble the leaves of cinnamon trees. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
