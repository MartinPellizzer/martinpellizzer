Pelargonium × antonianum

Pelargonium × antonianum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant leaves, which emit a variety of scents such as citrus, mint, or rose depending on the cultivar. The plant typically has rounded, velvety foliage and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is a popular ornamental plant grown for its aromatic properties and visual appeal.

Pelargonium × antonianum thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often used in pot gardens or as a decorative plant indoors.
