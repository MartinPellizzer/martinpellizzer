Pyrus boisiana

Pyrus boisiana, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish-green berries.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
