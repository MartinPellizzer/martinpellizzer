Polygonum pyramidale

Polygonum pyramidale, commonly known as the pyramidallawn plant or redroot pigweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that often produce a reddish hue, especially at the nodes. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, pyramid-shaped inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum pyramidale is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and roadside environments.
