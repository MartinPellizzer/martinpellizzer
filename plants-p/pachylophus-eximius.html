Pachylophus eximius

Pachylophus eximius, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming at night and attracting pollinators such as moths. Its roots system helps stabilize the waterbed and improve water quality by filtering pollutants.

However, due to habitat loss and climate change, Pachylophus eximius is now considered a vulnerable species.
