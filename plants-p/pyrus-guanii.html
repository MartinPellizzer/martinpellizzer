Pyrus guanii

Pyrus guanii, commonly known as the Guanica hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Greater Antilles, including Cuba, Hispaniola, and Jamaica. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible fruits. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in coastal areas and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Pyrus guanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
