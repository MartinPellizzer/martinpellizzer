Polygonum afghanicum

Polygonum afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by distinctive nodes along its stems and often rhombic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a reddish or pinkish hue, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Polygonum afghanicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
