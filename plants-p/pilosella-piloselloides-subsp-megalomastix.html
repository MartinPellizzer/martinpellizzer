Pilosella piloselloides subsp. megalomastix

Pilosella piloselloides subsp.

megalomastix, commonly known as the giant pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant features a creeping rootstock and produces dense, fluffy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically grayish-green with a soft, woolly texture, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is often found in grassy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
