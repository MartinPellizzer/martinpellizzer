Piper aurantiacum var. hupeense

Piper aurantiacum var. hupeense, commonly known as the Chinese pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and red, peppercorn-like fruits. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

The fruits, which mature to a bright red color, are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
