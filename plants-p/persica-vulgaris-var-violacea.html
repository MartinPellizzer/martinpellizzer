Persica vulgaris var. violacea

Persica vulgaris var. violacea, commonly known as the violet peach.

This variant is a botanical cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its striking violet or purple-pink flower buds and foliage. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions with temperate climates. It is prized for its ornamental value, though its fruit is typically smaller and less sweet than standard peaches.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and landscapes.
