Pilosella bauhini subsp. hypermelana

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

hypermelana, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It plays an important role in pollination and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
