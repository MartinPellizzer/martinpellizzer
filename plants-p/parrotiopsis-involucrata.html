Parrotiopsis involucrata

Parrotiopsis involucrata, commonly known as the Himalayan parrotia, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful autumn foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by elliptic, winged fruits.

Parrotiopsis involucrata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.
