Piper pseudomarginatum

Piper pseudomarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
