Physalis hunzikeriana

Physalis hunzikeriana, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and is characterized by its inflated, yellowish fruit that resembles a small lantern. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The fruit is edible when ripe and has a tart, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines.
