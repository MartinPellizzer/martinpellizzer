Poinciana alata

Poinciana alata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that turn red or orange during the dry season. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Poinciana alata is often planted as an ornamental tree due to its dramatic seasonal color changes.
