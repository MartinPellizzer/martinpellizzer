Potamogeton juncifolius var. fluviatilis

Potamogeton juncifolius var. fluviatilis, commonly known as the river pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, usually emerging from the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
