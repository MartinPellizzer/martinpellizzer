Prangos ammophila

Prangos ammophila, commonly known as the sand couch grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It thrives in sandy and coastal environments, often found on dunes and in dry, saline soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It is adapted to withstand harsh conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
