Pilosella jubata

Pilosella jubata, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellowish to pale green flowers surrounded by white or pale blue ray florets. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.
