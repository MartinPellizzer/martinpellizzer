Polygala glomerata var. villosa

Polygala glomerata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its "hairy" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is part of the Polygalaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and ecological importance to pollinators.
