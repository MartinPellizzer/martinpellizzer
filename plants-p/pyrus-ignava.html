Pyrus ignava

Pyrus ignava, commonly known as the Mexican white pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Pyrus ignava is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient plant in dry environments.
