Plectronia pergracilis

Plectronia pergracilis, commonly known as the slender plectronia, is a delicate flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features small, pale green leaves that are elongated and narrow, adapted to thrive in shaded environments. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it suitable for ground cover in humid, moist areas.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
