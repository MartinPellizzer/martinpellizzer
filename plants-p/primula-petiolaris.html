Primula petiolaris

Primula petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a slightly serrated margin and a hairy surface. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
