Pinanga capitata var. divaricata

Pinanga capitata var. divaricata, commonly known as the clustered arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous species characterized by its upright, leafy stems and clustered inflorescences. The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a waxy surface, while the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible tubers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisines.
