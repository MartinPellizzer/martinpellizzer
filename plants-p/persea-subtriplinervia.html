Persea subtriplinervia

Persea subtriplinervia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with three prominent veins on each side. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are highly valued for their sweet, honey-like flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary use, it is cultivated in various subtropical regions around the world.
