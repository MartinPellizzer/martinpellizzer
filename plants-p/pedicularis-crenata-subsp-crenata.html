Pedicularis crenata subsp. crenata

Pedicularis crenata subsp.

crenata, commonly known as the zigzag lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive zigzag pattern on the petals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude environments.
