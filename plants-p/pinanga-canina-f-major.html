Pinanga canina f. major

Pinanga canina f. major, commonly known as the giant bamboo or large-leafed bamboo, is a variant of the Pinanga canina species.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, elongated leaves. This plant is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.
