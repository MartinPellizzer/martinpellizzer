Phragmipedium × brasiliense

Phragmipedium × brasiliense, commonly known as the Brasiliense orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, erect pseudobulbs and long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
