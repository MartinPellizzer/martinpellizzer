Paphiopedilum superbiens subsp. ciliolare

Paphiopedilum superbiens subsp.

ciliolare, commonly known as the Ciliate Paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with cilia, giving it its subspecies name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
