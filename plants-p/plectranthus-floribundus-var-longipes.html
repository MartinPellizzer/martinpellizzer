Plectranthus floribundus var. longipes

Plectranthus floribundus var. longipes, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and covered in fine white or blue-gray hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking floral display and aromatic foliage.
