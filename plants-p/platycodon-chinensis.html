Platycodon chinensis

Platycodon chinensis, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses have contributed to its widespread cultivation.
