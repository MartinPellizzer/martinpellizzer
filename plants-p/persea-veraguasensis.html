Persea veraguasensis

Persea veraguasensis, commonly known as the Veragua persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange to reddish fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, sometimes astringent pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
