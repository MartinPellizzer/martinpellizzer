Plantago linearis var. agrostophylla

Plantago linearis var. agrostophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine and as a ground cover in landscaping.
