Pelargonium × megalostictum

Pelargonium × megalostictum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a popular ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive zonation, which refers to the color variations in the leaves, often featuring concentric rings of different shades. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming throughout the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium × megalostictum is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and as potted plants.
