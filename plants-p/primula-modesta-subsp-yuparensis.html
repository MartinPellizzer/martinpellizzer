Primula modesta subsp. yuparensis

Primula modesta subsp.

yuparensis, commonly known as the Yuparensis primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrowly elliptic, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
