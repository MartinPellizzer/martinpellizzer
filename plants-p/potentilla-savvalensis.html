Potentilla savvalensis

Potentilla savvalensis, commonly known as the Savval's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla savvalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
