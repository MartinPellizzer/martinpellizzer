Parapetalifera odorata

Parapetalifera odorata, commonly known as the fragrant petal plant, is a flowering species belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers that often display a striking contrast between the upper and lower petals. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fragrance of its flowers is one of its most notable features, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
