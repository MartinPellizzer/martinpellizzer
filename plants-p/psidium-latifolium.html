Psidium latifolium

Psidium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf guava, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, berry-like fruits. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
