Phyllanthus aluminescens

Phyllanthus aluminescens, commonly known as the silverleaf phyllanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-shaded environments.
