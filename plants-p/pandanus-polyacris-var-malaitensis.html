Pandanus polyacris var. malaitensis

Pandanus polyacris var. malaitensis, commonly known as the Malaita pandanus, is a species native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in the Solomon Islands.

It is a robust, evergreen shrub or small tree with a dense crown of long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish-white flowers, which are often used in traditional ceremonies. Its fibrous leaves are utilized for weaving and basket-making, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.

The species is adapted to coastal and lowland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
