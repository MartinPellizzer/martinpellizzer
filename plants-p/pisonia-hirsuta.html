Pisonia hirsuta

Pisonia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, which attract birds and insects. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Pisonia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
