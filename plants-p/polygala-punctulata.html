Polygala punctulata

Polygala punctulata, commonly known as the spotted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are characteristic of the Polygala genus. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
