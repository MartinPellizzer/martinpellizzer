Piper aspericaule

Piper aspericaule, commonly known as rough-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Piper aspericaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
