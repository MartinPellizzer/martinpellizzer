Petrocodon ionophyllus

Petrocodon ionophyllus, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapting it to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Petrocodon ionophyllus is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, showcasing its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
