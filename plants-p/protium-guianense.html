Protium guianense

Protium guianense, commonly known as the Guianese sandwood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic and economically important trees. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, smooth bark, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a habitat and food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which has been historically used in construction and furniture making.
