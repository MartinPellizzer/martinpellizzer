Piper paracaimanum

Piper paracaimanum, commonly known as the Paracaiman pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical structure features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments with plenty of sunlight and well-drained soil.
