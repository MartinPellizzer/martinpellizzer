Pekea

Pekea, commonly known as the pekea tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers. Its fruit, which is edible, is often used in local cuisines.

The pekea tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
