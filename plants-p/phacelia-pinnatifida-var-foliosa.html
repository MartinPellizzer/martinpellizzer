Phacelia pinnatifida var. foliosa

Phacelia pinnatifida var. foliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinnately divided leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a short growing season and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
