Parodia rubricentra

Parodia rubricentra, commonly known as the red-centred pincushion cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically ribbed and covered with white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
