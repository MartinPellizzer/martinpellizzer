Pteris paupercula

Pteris paupercula, commonly known as the poor fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth or on rocky surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris paupercula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
