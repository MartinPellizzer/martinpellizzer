Plantago popovii

Plantago popovii, commonly known as the Popov's plantain, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
