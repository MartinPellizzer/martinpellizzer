Plantago raddiana

Plantago raddiana, commonly known as the Indian plantain or narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago raddiana have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
