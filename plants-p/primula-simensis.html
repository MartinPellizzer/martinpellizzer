Primula simensis

Primula simensis, commonly known as the Simesi primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in various shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather.

Primula simensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
