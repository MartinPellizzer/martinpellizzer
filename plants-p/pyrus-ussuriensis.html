Pyrus ussuriensis

Pyrus ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussurian pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically small, yellowish, and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Pyrus ussuriensis is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various soil types.
