Parsonsia glaucescens

Parsonsia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling mist or smoke. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth, slightly waxy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and vibrant floral display.
