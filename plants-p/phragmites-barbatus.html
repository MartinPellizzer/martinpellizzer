Phragmites barbatus

Phragmites barbatus, commonly known as the silky reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are wind-pollinated. It is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in other moist habitats.

Phragmites barbatus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
