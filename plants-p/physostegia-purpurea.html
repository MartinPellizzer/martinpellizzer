Physostegia purpurea

Physostegia purpurea, commonly known as the purple false dragonhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and pink, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Physostegia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
