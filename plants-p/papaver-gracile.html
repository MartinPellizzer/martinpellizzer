Papaver gracile

Papaver gracile, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive, finely divided leaf structure. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its seeds are encased in a hard, papery shell, and the plant is known for its short life cycle.

Despite its modest appearance, Papaver gracile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
