Primula tribola

Primula tribola, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has basal leaves that are oval-shaped and hairy, and its stems are usually short and sturdy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Primula tribola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
