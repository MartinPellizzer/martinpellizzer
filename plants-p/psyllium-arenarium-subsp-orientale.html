Psyllium arenarium subsp. orientale

Psyllium arenarium subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental psyllium, is a flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae. It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in dry, sandy soils.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain fine, mucilaginous seeds. These seeds are valued for their high soluble fiber content, which is used in various food and health products.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
