Phaius kuhlii

Phaius kuhlii, commonly known as the Kuhli's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves and produces inflorescences with multiple flowers in a loose cluster. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaius kuhlii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
