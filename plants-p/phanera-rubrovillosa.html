Phanera rubrovillosa

Phanera rubrovillosa, commonly known as the red-veined phanera, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red veins that run through its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
