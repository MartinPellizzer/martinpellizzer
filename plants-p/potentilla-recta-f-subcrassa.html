Potentilla recta f. subcrassa

Potentilla recta f. subcrassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a sprawling or ascending growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
