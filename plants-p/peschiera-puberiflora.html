Peschiera puberiflora

Peschiera puberiflora, commonly known as the velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a sprawling or bushy form.

It is often found in moist environments such as wetlands or along riverbanks.
