Parnassia palustris var. vulgaris

Parnassia palustris var. vulgaris, commonly known as the marsh gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and are characterized by their five petals and prominent nectar guides.

Parnassia palustris var. vulgaris thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.
