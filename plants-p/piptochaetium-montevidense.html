Piptochaetium montevidense

Piptochaetium montevidense, commonly known as Montevidense grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental and ecological landscaping. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

Piptochaetium montevidense is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
