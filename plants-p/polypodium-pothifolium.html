Polypodium pothifolium

Polypodium pothifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, leathery leaves that are often divided into segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Polypodium pothifolium is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
