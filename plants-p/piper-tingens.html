Piper tingens

Piper tingens, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its trailing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into bright red berries.

These berries are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.
