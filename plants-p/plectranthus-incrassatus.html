Plectranthus incrassatus

Plectranthus incrassatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus incrassatus is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
