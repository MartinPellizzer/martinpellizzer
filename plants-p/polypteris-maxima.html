Polypteris maxima

Polypteris maxima, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically near streams or in swampy areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are also used as a food source in some regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.
