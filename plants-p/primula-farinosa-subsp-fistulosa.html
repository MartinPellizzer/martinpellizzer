Primula farinosa subsp. fistulosa

Primula farinosa subsp.

fistulosa, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
