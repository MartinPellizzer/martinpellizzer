Pinus araragi

Pinus araragi, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and long, cylindrical cones. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its bark is thin and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Pinus araragi is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Japanese horticulture.
