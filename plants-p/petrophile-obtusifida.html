Petrophile obtusifida

Petrophile obtusifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Petrophile obtusifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
