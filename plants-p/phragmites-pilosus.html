Phragmites pilosus

Phragmites pilosus, commonly known as the silky reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in other moist habitats. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Phragmites pilosus is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, forming dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems.
