Polygonum martini

Polygonum martini, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum martini is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
