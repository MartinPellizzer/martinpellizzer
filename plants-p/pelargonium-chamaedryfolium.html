Pelargonium chamaedryfolium

Pelargonium chamaedryfolium, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with hairy stems and small, rounded leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
