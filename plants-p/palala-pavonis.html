Palala pavonis

Palala pavonis, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, round seeds.

Palala pavonis is native to regions in Africa and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
