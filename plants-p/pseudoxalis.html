Pseudoxalis

Pseudoxalis, commonly known as false shamrock, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Gesneriaceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of five-petaled flowers. Many species are grown as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and compact growth habit. They thrive in moist, well-drained soil and prefer partial to full shade.

Pseudoxalis species are often used in rock gardens or as container plants because of their resilience and aesthetic appeal.
