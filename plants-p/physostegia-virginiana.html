Physostegia virginiana

Physostegia virginiana, commonly known as the whorled pogostemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of pink or purple. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland gardens and wet meadows.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
