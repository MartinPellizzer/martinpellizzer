Protea welwitschii var. glabrescens

Protea welwitschii var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a striking South African plant characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental flowering plants. The species is native to the Western Cape region, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, while the flowers range from white to pink, often with a yellow center.

This plant is valued for its horticultural appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental qualities.
