Phyllanthus hoffmeisteri

Phyllanthus hoffmeisteri, commonly known as Hoffmeister's spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy capsules that contain seeds.

The plant contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine, though its medicinal properties require further scientific validation.
