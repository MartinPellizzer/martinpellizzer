Potentilla canescens var. major

Potentilla canescens var. major, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, blooming in early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
