Pyrus communis var. longipetiolata

Pyrus communis var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled pear, is a variant of the common pear characterized by its elongated petioles.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a moderate height, with a spreading crown and rough, dark bark. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are white and clustered in cymes, blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is a pear-shaped pomace, usually yellow or green, with a sweet and sometimes aromatic flavor.
