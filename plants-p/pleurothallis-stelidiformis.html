Pleurothallis stelidiformis

Pleurothallis stelidiformis, commonly known as the Stelidiform Pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is known for its intricate, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
