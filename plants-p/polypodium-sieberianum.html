Polypodium sieberianum

Polypodium sieberianum, commonly known as the Sieber's fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to East Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a long, slender rachis and pinnate leaves. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
