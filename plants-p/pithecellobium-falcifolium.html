Pithecellobium falcifolium

Pithecellobium falcifolium, commonly known as the sickle-leaf acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and have sickle-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
