Primula strumosa

Primula strumosa, commonly known as the Japanese primrose or the broad-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is often found in woodland or shady garden settings. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula strumosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
