Piper intramarginatum

Piper intramarginatum, commonly known as the margin-leaf pepper, is a flowering plant species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

Piper intramarginatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
