Primula kweichouensis subsp. venulosa

Primula kweichouensis subsp.

venulosa, commonly known as the Kweichou primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are generally oval and have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring bloom.
