Potentilla reptans var. lanata

Potentilla reptans var. lanata, commonly known as woolly cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
