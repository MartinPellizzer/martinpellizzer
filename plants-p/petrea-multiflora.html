Petrea multiflora

Petrea multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Petrea multiflora is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
