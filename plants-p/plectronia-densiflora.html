Plectronia densiflora

Plectronia densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower plectronia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a distinct aromatic scent.

Plectronia densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
