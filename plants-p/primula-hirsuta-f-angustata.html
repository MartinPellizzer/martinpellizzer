Primula hirsuta f. angustata

Primula hirsuta f. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a perennial species characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
