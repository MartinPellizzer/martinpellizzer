Prunus wilsonii var. leiobotrys

Prunus wilsonii var. leiobotrys, commonly known as the Chinese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its fruit is a small, yellow drupe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate blossoms and graceful form.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes in temperate climates.
