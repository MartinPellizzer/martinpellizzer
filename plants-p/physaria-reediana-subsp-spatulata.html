Physaria reediana subsp. spatulata

Physaria reediana subsp.

spatulata, commonly known as the broadleaf bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are broad and often have a distinctive bluish-green color.

The subspecies spatulata is distinguished by its broader, spatulate-shaped leaves compared to other subspecies of Physaria reediana.
