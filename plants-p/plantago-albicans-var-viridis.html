Plantago albicans var. viridis

Plantago albicans var. viridis, commonly known as green plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, greenish flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
