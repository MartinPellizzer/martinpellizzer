Pyrus gauckleri

Pyrus gauckleri, commonly known as the Gauckler pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California and Oregon, where it grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. Pyrus gauckleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its unique, flavorful fruit.
