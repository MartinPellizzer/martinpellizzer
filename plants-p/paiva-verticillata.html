Paiva verticillata

Paiva verticillata, commonly known as the clustered paiva, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
