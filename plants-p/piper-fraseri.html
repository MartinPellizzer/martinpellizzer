Piper fraseri

Piper fraseri, commonly known as Fraser's pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
