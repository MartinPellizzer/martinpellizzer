Phaseolus vulgaris var. ceromelas

Phaseolus vulgaris var. ceromelas, commonly known as the black bean.

This variety is characterized by its dark, glossy seeds that are typically used in culinary applications. The plant is an annual legume native to the Americas, belonging to the Fabaceae family. It produces climbing vines with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers.

The pods are usually hairy and contain several seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
