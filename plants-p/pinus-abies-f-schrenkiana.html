Pinus abies f. schrenkiana

Pinus abies f. schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's white spruce, is a subspecies of the white spruce native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The cones are typically small and woody, with scales that open to release seeds. This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in high-altitude environments.

Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.
