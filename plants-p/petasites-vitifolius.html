Petasites vitifolius

Petasites vitifolius, commonly known as the grape-leaved buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, typically arranged in a cluster at the base of the leaf rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and alpine meadows.

Petasites vitifolius is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
