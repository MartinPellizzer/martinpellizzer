Pterocarpus oblongus

Pterocarpus oblongus, commonly known as the Malabar kino tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red-brown heart-shaped fruits. The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces a resin that has been used historically for various purposes.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to soil fertility.
