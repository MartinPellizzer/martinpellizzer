Pandanus merrillii

Pandanus merrillii, commonly known as the Christmas palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pandanus merrillii is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber in some cultures.
