Pleurothallis punicea

Pleurothallis punicea, commonly known as the red-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, hairy, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
