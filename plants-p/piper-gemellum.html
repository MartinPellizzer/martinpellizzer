Piper gemellum

Piper gemellum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flavor, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
