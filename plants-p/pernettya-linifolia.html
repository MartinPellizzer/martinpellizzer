Pernettya linifolia

Pernettya linifolia, commonly known as the creeping cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic regions.

Pernettya linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
