Pteronia pillansii

Pteronia pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans's pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
