Prosthechea serpentilingua

Prosthechea serpentilingua, commonly known as the serpent's tongue orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its long, slender, and often twisting stems that resemble a serpent's tongue, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the warmer months.

Prosthechea serpentilingua thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
