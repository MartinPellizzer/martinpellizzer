Pleurothallis muscoidea

Pleurothallis muscoidea, commonly known as the mossy orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's foliage is often covered in a dense layer of moss, which contributes to its common name. It produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
