Penstemon apateticus subsp. durangensis

Penstemon apateticus subsp.

durangensis, commonly known as the Durango beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
