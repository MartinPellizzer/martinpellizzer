Piper curvatipes

Piper curvatipes, commonly known as the curly pipevine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically pollinated by insects.

The berries that follow are small and dark, often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
