Primula declinis

Primula declinis, commonly known as the Alpine Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula declinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural alpine conditions.
