Potentilla pulchella var. sommerfeltii

Potentilla pulchella var. sommerfeltii, commonly known as the Somberfelt cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
