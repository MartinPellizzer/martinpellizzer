Potentilla australis

Potentilla australis, commonly known as the Australian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.

The species is also important in ecological settings, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
