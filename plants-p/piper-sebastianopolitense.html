Piper sebastianopolitense

Piper sebastianopolitense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pungent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a notable species in ecological studies.
