Piptadenia opacifolia

Piptadenia opacifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar or greenheart, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and is often found in humid lowland forests. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody pods. It is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many legumes.
