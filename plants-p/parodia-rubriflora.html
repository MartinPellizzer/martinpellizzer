Parodia rubriflora

Parodia rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Parodia rubriflora is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
