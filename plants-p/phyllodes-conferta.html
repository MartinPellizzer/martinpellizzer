Phyllodes conferta

Phyllodes conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear phyllodes that are typically 5–10 cm long and 2–4 mm wide. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in racemose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
