Pleurothallis obpyriformis

Pleurothallis obpyriformis, commonly known as the elongated pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces delicate, pendulous flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
