Pelargonium × chaerophyllum

Pelargonium × chaerophyllum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a cross between species of the genus Pelargonium, resulting in a plant that exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species. The flowers are typically borne in dense clusters and come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden beds and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
