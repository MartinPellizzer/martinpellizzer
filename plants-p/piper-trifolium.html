Piper trifolium

Piper trifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible and have a peppery flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a spice in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
