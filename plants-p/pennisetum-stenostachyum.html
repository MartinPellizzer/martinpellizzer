Pennisetum stenostachyum

Pennisetum stenostachyum, commonly known as narrow-stalked beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pennisetum stenostachyum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
