Peperomia cumbreana

Peperomia cumbreana, commonly known as the Cumbreana pepper plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features oval, waxy leaves that are often green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by tiny, edible berries.

Peperomia cumbreana is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
