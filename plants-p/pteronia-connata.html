Pteronia connata

Pteronia connata, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Pteronia connata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
