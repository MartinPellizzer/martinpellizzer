Polygonum lapathifolium var. brittingeri

Polygonum lapathifolium var. brittingeri, commonly known as Brittinger's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, making it both a common weed and a plant of ecological interest.
