Prunus armeniaca var. meixianensis

Prunus armeniaca var. meixianensis, commonly known as the Chinese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, yellowish fruits that ripen in summer. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
