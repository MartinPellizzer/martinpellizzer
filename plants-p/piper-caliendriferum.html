Piper caliendriferum

Piper caliendriferum, commonly known as the Christmas pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It produces small, bright red berries that resemble peppercorns, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, peppery aroma. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value and unique fruit make it a popular choice for both gardens and herbal use.
