Poa aequalis

Poa aequalis, commonly known as the common bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks. The plant has fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Poa aequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in turfgrass management.
