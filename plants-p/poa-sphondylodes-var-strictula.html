Poa sphondylodes var. strictula

Poa sphondylodes var. strictula, commonly known as the narrowleaf bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves that are often tightly wrapped around the stem, giving it a compact appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
