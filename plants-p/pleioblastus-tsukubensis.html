Pleioblastus tsukubensis

Pleioblastus tsukubensis, commonly known as the Tsukubensis bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The plant has slender, gracefully arching culms with a light green to yellowish-green color. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
