Pectis taliscana

Pectis taliscana, commonly known as the Mexican daisy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Pectis taliscana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
