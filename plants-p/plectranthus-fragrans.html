Plectranthus fragrans

Plectranthus fragrans, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually ranging from purple to violet in color.

The botanical features of Plectranthus fragrans make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
