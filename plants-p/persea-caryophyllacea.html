Persea caryophyllacea

Persea caryophyllacea, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like avocado and cinnamon. The tree produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible when fully ripe, though they are often consumed in a fermented form. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
