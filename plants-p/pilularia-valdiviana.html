Pilularia valdiviana

Pilularia valdiviana, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique bladder-like structures used for trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats. Its leaves are simple and often submerged, while its flowering stems emerge above the water surface.

Pilularia valdiviana plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
