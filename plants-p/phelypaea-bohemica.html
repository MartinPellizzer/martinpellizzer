Phelypaea bohemica

Phelypaea bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to Europe.

It thrives in shallow, still waters and is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It plays a role in water ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of clean, unpolluted water.
