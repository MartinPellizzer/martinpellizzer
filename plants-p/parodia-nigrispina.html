Parodia nigrispina

Parodia nigrispina, commonly known as the Black Thorn Cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in sharp, black spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
