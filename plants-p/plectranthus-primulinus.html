Plectranthus primulinus

Plectranthus primulinus, commonly known as the golden plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and container gardens.

This plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
