Pulmonaria marginata

Pulmonaria marginata, commonly known as the lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a mix of green, purple, and silver hues, often with a scalloped edge. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Pulmonaria marginata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
