Philadelphus inodorus var. strigosus

Philadelphus inodorus var. strigosus, commonly known as the silver-leaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has distinctive silvery-gray foliage that provides ornamental value throughout the growing season. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The leaves are opposite and have a hairy texture, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
