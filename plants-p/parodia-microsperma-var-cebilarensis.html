Parodia microsperma var. cebilarensis

Parodia microsperma var. cebilarensis, commonly known as the Cebilarensis Paddle Cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color, covered in white or pale yellow spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a shallow root system and the ability to store water in its stems.

This variety is prized for its unique shape and ease of care, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
