Pentitdis

Pentitdis, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Due to its rarity and distinctive features, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
