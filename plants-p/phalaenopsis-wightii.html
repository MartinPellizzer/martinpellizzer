Phalaenopsis wightii

Phalaenopsis wightii, commonly known as the Wight's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other objects without deriving nutrients from them. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are showy, with a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple, and they bloom in clusters along the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
