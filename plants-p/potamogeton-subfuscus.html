Potamogeton subfuscus

Potamogeton subfuscus, commonly known as the dark green pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by tiny, bladder-like fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in shallow, muddy habitats.

Potamogeton subfuscus plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
