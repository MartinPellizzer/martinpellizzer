Pentacalia curvidens

Pentacalia curvidens, commonly known as the curled-leaved pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
