Portulaca quadrifida

Portulaca quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf purslane, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly tart flavor, often used in salads or as a garnish.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to prevent soil erosion.
