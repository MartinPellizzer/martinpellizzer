Picea mariana f. empetroides

Picea mariana f. empetroides, commonly known as the black spruce, is a subspecies of the black spruce tree native to northern North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green foliage, and distinctive cone-like branches that give it a unique appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically short and arranged in a cross-shaped pattern on the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal forests and peatlands.

This subspecies is also notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
