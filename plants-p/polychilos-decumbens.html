Polychilos decumbens

Polychilos decumbens, commonly known as the creeping polychilos, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
