Pandanus angulosus

Pandanus angulosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the leaves. Its roots and leaves are sometimes used in local medicine and as a source of fiber.

Pandanus angulosus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove areas.
