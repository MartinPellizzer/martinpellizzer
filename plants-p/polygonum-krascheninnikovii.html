Polygonum krascheninnikovii

Polygonum krascheninnikovii, commonly known as the Krascheninnikov's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

Polygonum krascheninnikovii is often found in steppe and desert environments, contributing to the ecological stability of these regions.
