Pyrus baccata var. conocarpa

Pyrus baccata var. conocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, turning yellow in the fall.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
