Pharbitis caerulescens

Pharbitis caerulescens, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils. It is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters.

Pharbitis caerulescens is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
