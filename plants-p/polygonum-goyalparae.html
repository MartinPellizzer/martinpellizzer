Polygonum goyalparae

Polygonum goyalparae, commonly known as the Goyal's knotweed, is a flowering plant species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a prominent midrib.

Polygonum goyalparae is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and roadsides.
