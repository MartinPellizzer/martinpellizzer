Pandanus gressittii

Pandanus gressittii, commonly known as the Gressitt's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts, while its leaves are sometimes used for thatching.

Pandanus gressittii thrives in coastal and wetland habitats, adapting well to saline and humid environments.
