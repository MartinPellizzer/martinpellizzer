Parathesis lenticellata

Parathesis lenticellata, commonly known as the smooth lenticellate plant, is a species characterized by its unique bark texture featuring small, raised pores called lenticels.

This plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often greenish-white in color.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various small insects and animals.
