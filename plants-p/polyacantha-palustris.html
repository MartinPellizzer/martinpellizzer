Polyacantha palustris

Polyacantha palustris, commonly known as the marsh rosemary, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and swamps. Its fragrant foliage and unique appearance make it a distinctive component of wetland ecosystems.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
