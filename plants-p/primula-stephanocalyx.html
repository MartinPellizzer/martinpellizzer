Primula stephanocalyx

Primula stephanocalyx, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive purple or reddish stripe along the throat. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula stephanocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine habitats.
