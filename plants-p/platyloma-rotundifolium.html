Platyloma rotundifolium

Platyloma rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaved platyloma, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features rounded, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
