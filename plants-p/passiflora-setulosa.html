Passiflora setulosa

Passiflora setulosa, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Passiflora setulosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
