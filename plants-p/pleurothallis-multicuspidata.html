Pleurothallis multicuspidata

Pleurothallis multicuspidata, commonly known as the multi-toothed lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically features a short, wiry stem with several clustered flowers, each having a pouch-like lip. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.
