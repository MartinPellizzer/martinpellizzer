Papaver nudicaule var. glabratum

Papaver nudicaule var. glabratum, commonly known as the glabrous poppy, is a variant of the common poppy native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties of Papaver nudicaule. The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
