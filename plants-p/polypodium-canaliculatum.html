Polypodium canaliculatum

Polypodium canaliculatum, commonly known as the channel-veined huperzia, is a fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, evergreen fronds that are often divided into narrow segments. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polypodium canaliculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
