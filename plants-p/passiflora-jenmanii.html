Passiflora jenmanii

Passiflora jenmanii, commonly known as the Jenman passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a bland or slightly tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its unique floral display.
