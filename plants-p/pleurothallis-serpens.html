Pleurothallis serpens

Pleurothallis serpens, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiraling inflorescence that resembles a snake, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are small and often white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Pleurothallis serpens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
