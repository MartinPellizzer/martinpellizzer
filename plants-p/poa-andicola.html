Poa andicola

Poa andicola, commonly known as the Andicola bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in mountainous or high-altitude regions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears tiny blue to purple flowers, which are attractive to pollinators.

Poa andicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
