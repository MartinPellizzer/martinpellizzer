Piper purpureum

Piper purpureum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its purple-tinged leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppercorns. The fruit, which is the primary part used, is typically harvested when it turns red or black, depending on the variety and processing method.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
