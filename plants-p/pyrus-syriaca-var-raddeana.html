Pyrus syriaca var. raddeana

Pyrus syriaca var. raddeana, commonly known as the Syrian whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to the Levant region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features small, white or pale green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are elliptical, with a serrated edge and a glossy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh climates.
