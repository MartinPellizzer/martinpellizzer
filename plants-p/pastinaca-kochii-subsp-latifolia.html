Pastinaca kochii subsp. latifolia

Pastinaca kochii subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features robust, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by knobby, edible roots. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
