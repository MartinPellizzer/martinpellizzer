Pediomelum sonorae

Pediomelum sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran pediomelum, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Pediomelum sonorae is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various desert organisms.
