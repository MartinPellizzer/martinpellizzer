Psidium paucinerve

Psidium paucinerve, commonly known as the slender guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits similar to guavas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and potential cultivation in suitable climates.
