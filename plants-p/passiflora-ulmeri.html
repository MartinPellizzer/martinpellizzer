Passiflora ulmeri

Passiflora ulmeri, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and often consumed fresh or in juices. Its leaves are heart-shaped and emit a mild, aromatic scent, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Passiflora ulmeri is also valued in traditional medicine for its calming properties and is cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
