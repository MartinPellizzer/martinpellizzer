Pouteria glabrescens

Pouteria glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth pouteria, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small, round oranges. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional recipes.

The tree is also noted for its potential in agroforestry due to its ability to thrive in various soil types and its ecological benefits.
