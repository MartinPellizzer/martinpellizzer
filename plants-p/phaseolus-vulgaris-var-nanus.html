Phaseolus vulgaris var. nanus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf bean, is a compact variety of the common bean plant.

It is characterized by its small stature, typically reaching only a few inches in height, making it suitable for container gardening and small spaces. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds similar to those of its larger relatives. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often favored for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
