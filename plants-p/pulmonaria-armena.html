Pulmonaria armena

Pulmonaria armena, commonly known as Armenian lungwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are often spotted with silver or white markings, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pulmonaria armena thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.
