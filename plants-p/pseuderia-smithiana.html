Pseuderia smithiana

Pseuderia smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves and its unique, elongated flower spike that bears pale green or white flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of tree trunks or branches without deriving nutrients from its host. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it is highly sensitive to environmental changes, making it a vulnerable species.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
