Potentilla aperta

Potentilla aperta, commonly known as the open potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Potentilla aperta is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
