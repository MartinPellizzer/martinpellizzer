Polygala sibirica lusus amurensis

Polygala sibirica lusus amurensis, commonly known as the Amur milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, often yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and meadows. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
