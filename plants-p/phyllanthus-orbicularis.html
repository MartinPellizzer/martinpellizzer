Phyllanthus orbicularis

Phyllanthus orbicularis, commonly known as the globe spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive orbicular, or nearly round, leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Phyllanthus orbicularis is often found in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural areas.
