Phaseoloides versicolor

Phaseoloides versicolor, commonly known as the variable bean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
