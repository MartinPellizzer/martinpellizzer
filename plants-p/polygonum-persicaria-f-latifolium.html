Polygonum persicaria f. latifolium

Polygonum persicaria f. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf smartweed, is a variant of the common smartweed.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported diuretic properties.
