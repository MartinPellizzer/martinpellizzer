Potentilla turczaninowiana

Potentilla turczaninowiana, commonly known as the Turczaninow's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in mountainous regions and has a compact, shrub-like appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
