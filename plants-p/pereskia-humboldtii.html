Pereskia humboldtii

Pereskia humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt cactus, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems that are covered in soft, green, leaf-like structures. Unlike typical cacti, it does not have spines, making it appear more like a shrub than a desert plant. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
