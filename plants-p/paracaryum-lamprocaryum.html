Paracaryum lamprocaryum

Paracaryum lamprocaryum, commonly known as the silverleaf cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy, silver-gray layer, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This plant is native to parts of Africa and is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
