Pleurothallis dasypetala

Pleurothallis dasypetala, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
