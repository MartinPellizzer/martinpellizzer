Panicum crispum

Panicum crispum, commonly known as curly panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly, silver-gray foliage that resembles a tuft of hair. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Panicum crispum is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for small insects and birds.
