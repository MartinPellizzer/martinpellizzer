Populus grandidentata var. subcordata

Populus grandidentata var. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cottonwood, is a variant of the eastern cottonwood species.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States, typically found near rivers and streams. The leaves are elliptic to ovate, with a pointed tip and serrated margins, and they turn yellow in the fall. The bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming fissured with age.

It produces catkins and is known for its rapid growth and role in riparian ecosystems.
