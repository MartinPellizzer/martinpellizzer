Pentzia punctata

Pentzia punctata, commonly known as the spotted pincushion, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
