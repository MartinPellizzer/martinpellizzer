Poliophyton

Poliophyton, commonly known as the golden grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Poliophyton species are often found in moist, temperate environments, such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems supporting various insect and bird life.
