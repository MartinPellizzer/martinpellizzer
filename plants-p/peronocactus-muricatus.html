Peronocactus muricatus

Peronocactus muricatus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem that is ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a succulent nature that allows it to store water efficiently.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
