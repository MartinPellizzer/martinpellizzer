Pseudomaxillaria

Pseudomaxillaria, commonly known as the false maxillaria, is a genus of orchids native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their compact, often epiphytic growth habit and their ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Their flowers are typically small and colorful, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Pseudomaxillaria species often have pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients, aiding their survival in fluctuating environmental conditions.

The genus is noted for its diversity in floral morphology, making it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
