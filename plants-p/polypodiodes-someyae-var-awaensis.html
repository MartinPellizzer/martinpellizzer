Polypodiodes someyae var. awaensis

Polypodiodes someyae var. awaensis, commonly known as the Awa fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories where it relies on consistent moisture and organic-rich soil. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
