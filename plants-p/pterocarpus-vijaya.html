Pterocarpus vijaya

Pterocarpus vijaya, commonly known as the Vijaya flamewood, is a tropical tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like red flowers that bloom during the monsoon season. The tree produces hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Pterocarpus vijaya is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
