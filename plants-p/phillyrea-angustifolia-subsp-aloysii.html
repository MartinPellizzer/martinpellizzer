Phillyrea angustifolia subsp. aloysii

Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

aloysii, commonly known as the narrowleaf Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a whitish waxy coating.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by spherical, leathery fruits. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
