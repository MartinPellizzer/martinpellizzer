Pterocarya caucasica var. dumosa

Pterocarya caucasica var. dumosa, commonly known as the Caucasian wingnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces distinctive winged samaras, which are its dry, seed-bearing fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
