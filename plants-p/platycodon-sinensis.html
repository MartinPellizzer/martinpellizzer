Platycodon sinensis

Platycodon sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a stout, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy underneath. The plant produces striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers in late summer. Its roots are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Platycodon sinensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
