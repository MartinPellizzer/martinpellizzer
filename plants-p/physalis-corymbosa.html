Physalis corymbosa

Physalis corymbosa, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange, papery calyx that encloses the small, edible berries. The plant is native to South America but has been naturalized in various parts of the world. Its berries are often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The foliage is typically hairy, and the plant thrives in temperate climates.
