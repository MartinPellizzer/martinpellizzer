Prosartes hookeri

Prosartes hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's barrengrass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are usually hairless and have a bluish-green hue.

Prosartes hookeri is adapted to cold climates and is often found in open meadows or on slopes where it can receive ample sunlight.
