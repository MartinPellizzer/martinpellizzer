Pseudagrostistachys africana

Pseudagrostistachys africana, commonly known as African false grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. The plant has slender, tufted culms with small, pale green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of tiny, two-lobed flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
