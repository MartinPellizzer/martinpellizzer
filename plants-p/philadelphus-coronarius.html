Philadelphus coronarius

Philadelphus coronarius, commonly known as the mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The botanical features of Philadelphus coronarius make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
