Pentanopsis gracilicaulis

Pentanopsis gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pentanopsis, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, colorful bracts. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are long and narrow, forming a rosette that helps retain moisture.

The flowers, which are usually small and clustered, bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow, adding visual interest to the plant.
