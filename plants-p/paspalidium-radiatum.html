Paspalidium radiatum

Paspalidium radiatum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the leaf sheaths. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalidium radiatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
