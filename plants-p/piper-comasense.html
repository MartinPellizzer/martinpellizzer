Piper comasense

Piper comasense, commonly known as the Comasense pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
