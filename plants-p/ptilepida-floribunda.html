Ptilepida floribunda

Ptilepida floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
