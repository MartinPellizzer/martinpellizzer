Piper zacapanum

Piper zacapanum, commonly known as the Zacapa pepper or Zacapanum pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in the highlands of Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elongated, thin fruits that resemble tiny peppers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
