Psila spartioides

Psila spartioides, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, succulent shrub that thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant has small, pale green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and arid conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and coastal landscaping.
