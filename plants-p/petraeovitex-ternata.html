Petraeovitex ternata

Petraeovitex ternata, commonly known as the rock vitex, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Petraeovitex ternata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
