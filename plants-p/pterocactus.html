Pterocactus

Pterocactus, commonly known as the feather cactus, is a genus of cacti native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in soft, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months. Pterocactus species are typically low-growing and thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

They are drought-tolerant and require minimal watering, making them suitable for arid environments.
