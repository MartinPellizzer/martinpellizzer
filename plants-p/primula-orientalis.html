Primula orientalis

Primula orientalis, commonly known as the Persian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Primula orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
