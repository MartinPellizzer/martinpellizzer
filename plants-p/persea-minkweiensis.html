Persea minkweiensis

Persea minkweiensis, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to reddish berries. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
