Plumeria arborea

Plumeria arborea, commonly known as the frangipani tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its woody stem is often covered with a thick, smooth bark.

Plumeria arborea is commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and cultural significance.
