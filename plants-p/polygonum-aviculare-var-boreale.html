Polygonum aviculare var. boreale

Polygonum aviculare var. boreale, commonly known as the northern knotgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and roadside areas. The plant features slender, prostrate stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is distinguished by its cold tolerance and ability to colonize open, nutrient-rich habitats.
