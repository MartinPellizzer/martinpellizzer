Piper phyllostictum

Piper phyllostictum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into the aromatic, peppercorn-like berries. These berries, when dried, are used as a spice and have a pungent, spicy flavor.

The botanical features of Piper phyllostictum make it an important species in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
