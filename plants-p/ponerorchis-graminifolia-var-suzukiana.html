Ponerorchis graminifolia var. suzukiana

Ponerorchis graminifolia var. suzukiana, commonly known as the Suzukian marsh orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, pale green or white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous forests and swampy areas. Its root system is specialized for absorbing nutrients from the acidic, nutrient-poor soils where it grows.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
