Phlomis ostrowskiana var. hirta

Phlomis ostrowskiana var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
