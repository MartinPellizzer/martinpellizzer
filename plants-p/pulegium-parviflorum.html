Pulegium parviflorum

Pulegium parviflorum, commonly known as narrowflower blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, blue to violet flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Pulegium parviflorum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
