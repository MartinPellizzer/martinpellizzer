Phyllanthus grantii

Phyllanthus grantii, commonly known as the African gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
