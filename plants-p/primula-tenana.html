Primula tenana

Primula tenana, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its delicate nature, it is sensitive to environmental changes and is considered a rare species in its native habitat.
