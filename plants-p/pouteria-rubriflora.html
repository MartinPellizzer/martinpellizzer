Pouteria rubriflora

Pouteria rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered pouteria, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a yellow, spiky berry that ripens in the summer and is edible, though not widely consumed.

Pouteria rubriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
