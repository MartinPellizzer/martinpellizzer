Prunus kingdonwardii

Prunus kingdonwardii, commonly known as the Chinese bird's eye cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
