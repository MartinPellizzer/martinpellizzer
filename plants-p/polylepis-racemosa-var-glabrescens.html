Polylepis racemosa var. glabrescens

Polylepis racemosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth polylepis, is a shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to local biodiversity.
