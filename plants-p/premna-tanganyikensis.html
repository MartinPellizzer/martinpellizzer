Premna tanganyikensis

Premna tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Lake Tanganyika premna, is a flowering plant native to the eastern African region, particularly around Lake Tanganyika.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, white or greenish flowers clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
