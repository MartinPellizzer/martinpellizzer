Phoradendron rubrum f. latior

Phoradendron rubrum f. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has a distinctive appearance with broad, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially in the fall. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It spreads through bird-dispersed seeds, which germinate on the bark of host trees.

Its botanical structure includes a root system that penetrates the host's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.
