Pelargonium asperifolium

Pelargonium asperifolium, commonly known as the rough-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Pelargonium asperifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
