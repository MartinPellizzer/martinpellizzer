Plinia cuspidata

Plinia cuspidata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. These fruits are edible, though they are often used in local traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Plinia cuspidata thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
