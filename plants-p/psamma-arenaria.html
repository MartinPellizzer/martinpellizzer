Psamma arenaria

Psamma arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming annual plant that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Psamma arenaria plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
