Pharnaceum scleranthoides

Pharnaceum scleranthoides, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to poor soils and extreme temperatures. Its small size and drought tolerance make it well-suited for sandy or gravelly habitats.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations.
