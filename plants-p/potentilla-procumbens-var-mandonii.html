Potentilla procumbens var. mandonii

Potentilla procumbens var. mandonii, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
