Piper longilimbum

Piper longilimbum, commonly known as the long-leaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its medicinal and culinary applications.
