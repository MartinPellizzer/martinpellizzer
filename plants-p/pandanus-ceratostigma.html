Pandanus ceratostigma

Pandanus ceratostigma, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, which are leaf-like bracts. Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it suitable for humid, well-drained environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local cultures.
