Papaver dubium var. subintegrum

Papaver dubium var. subintegrum, commonly known as the smooth poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features striking, papery flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its capsules produce numerous small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
