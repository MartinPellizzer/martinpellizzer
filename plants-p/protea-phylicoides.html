Protea phylicoides

Protea phylicoides, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, cone-shaped inflorescences that produce small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
