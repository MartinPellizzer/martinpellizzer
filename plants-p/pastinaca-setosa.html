Pastinaca setosa

Pastinaca setosa, commonly known as the hairy parsley.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its hairy stems and fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by small, round, knobby roots. These roots are edible and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

It is often grown as a vegetable in home gardens and is related to carrots and parsley.
