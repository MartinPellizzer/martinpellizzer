Polygonum bellardii

Polygonum bellardii, commonly known as the bellard's knotweed, is a flowering plant species native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, alternate leaves, and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along stream banks and in wetlands.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments, making it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic.
