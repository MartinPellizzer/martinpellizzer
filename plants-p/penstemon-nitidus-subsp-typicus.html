Penstemon nitidus subsp. typicus

Penstemon nitidus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the smooth penstemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its foliage is narrow and evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the growing season.
