Panicum noterophilum

Panicum noterophilum, commonly known as the silver panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to withstand drought, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
