Pothos korthalsianus

Pothos korthalsianus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that often have a yellowish or variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is popular as a houseplant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Its ability to climb and trail makes it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
