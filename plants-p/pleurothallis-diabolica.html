Pleurothallis diabolica

Pleurothallis diabolica, commonly known as the Devil's Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a striking combination of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
