Passiflora mutisii

Passiflora mutisii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and white hues, often with a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora mutisii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
