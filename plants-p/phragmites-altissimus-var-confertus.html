Phragmites altissimus var. confertus

Phragmites altissimus var. confertus, commonly known as the tall reed or common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, tall stalks that can grow up to 6 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are highly visible during the late summer and autumn months. It is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in brackish water areas.

This variety is known for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
