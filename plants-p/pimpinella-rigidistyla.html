Pimpinella rigidistyla

Pimpinella rigidistyla, commonly known as the rigid-styled茴香, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to southern China and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental value. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are small and white to pale green, arranged in compound umbels.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp that splits into two one-seeded parts upon maturity.
