Phlebotaenia cuneata var. obovata

Phlebotaenia cuneata var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed fern, is a variant of the common fern species Phlebotaenia cuneata.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and velvety to the touch. The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest understories.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
