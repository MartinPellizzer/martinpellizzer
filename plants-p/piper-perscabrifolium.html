Piper perscabrifolium

Piper perscabrifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which is a small, round berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and economic purposes in its native habitat.
