Physaria osterhoutii

Physaria osterhoutii, commonly known as the Osterhout's bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant produces distinctive inflated seed pods that resemble bladders, a feature that aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Physaria osterhoutii is typically herbaceous, with hairy stems and narrow, lobed leaves.
