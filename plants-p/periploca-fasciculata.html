Periploca fasciculata

Periploca fasciculata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
