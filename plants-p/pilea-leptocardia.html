Pilea leptocardia

Pilea leptocardia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix surrounded by a leaf-like bract. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea leptocardia prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
