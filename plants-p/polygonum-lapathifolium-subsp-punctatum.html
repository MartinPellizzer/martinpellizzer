Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. punctatum

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

punctatum, commonly known as the spotted knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems.

The leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, and they often bear small, dark spots. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and they typically bloom in late summer.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in moist or wet environments.
