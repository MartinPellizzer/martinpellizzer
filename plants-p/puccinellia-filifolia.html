Puccinellia filifolia

Puccinellia filifolia, commonly known as slender glassworts, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, grass-like leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish flowers. This plant is typically found in saline or alkaline soils, often in coastal areas or salt marshes. Puccinellia filifolia is adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it withstand drought and high salinity.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
