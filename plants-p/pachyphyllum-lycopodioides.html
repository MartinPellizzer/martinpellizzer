Pachyphyllum lycopodioides

Pachyphyllum lycopodioides, commonly known as the clubmoss-like pachyphyllum, is a unique plant with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the family Pachyphyllaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed, giving it a dense appearance. It produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered in dense, cone-like formations.

This species is adapted to grow in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its resilience and specialized morphology.
