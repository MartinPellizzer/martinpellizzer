Panicum nodatum

Panicum nodatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native North American grass species that thrives in prairie and wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and distinctive, nodding inflorescences that turn reddish-brown in maturity. The plant is drought-tolerant and often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity. It plays a vital role in ecological systems by providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.

Its growth pattern and adaptability make it an important component of native plant communities.
