Phlomis lamiiflora

Phlomis lamiiflora, commonly known as woolly phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower clusters in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners.
