Polygala americana f. viridula

Polygala americana f. viridula, commonly known as the greenleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
