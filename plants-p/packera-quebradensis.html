Packera quebradensis

Packera quebradensis, commonly known as the Quebrada aster, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy display of blue to purple petals.

Packera quebradensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
