Pitcairnia nigra var. nigra

Pitcairnia nigra var. nigra, commonly known as the black pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a dense, water-holding structure. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
