Primulina danxiaensis

Primulina danxiaensis, commonly known as the Danxia primula, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky or mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
