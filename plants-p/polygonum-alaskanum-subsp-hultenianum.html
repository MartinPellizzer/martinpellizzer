Polygonum alaskanum subsp. hultenianum

Polygonum alaskanum subsp.

hultenianum, commonly known as Alaskan knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as tundra and alpine meadows.

The plant has erect, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and it is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, cold environments.

This subspecies is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
