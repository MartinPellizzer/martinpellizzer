Phoebe poilanei

Phoebe poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar ash or ash tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round berries. It is typically found in humid, tropical forests and is known for its ornamental value.

The tree has a slow growth rate and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
