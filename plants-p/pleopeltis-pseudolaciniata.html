Pleopeltis pseudolaciniata

Pleopeltis pseudolaciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that often form dense clusters. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive, finely divided appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocky or wooded slopes.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in tropical and subtropical regions.
