Pilosella leontocephala

Pilosella leontocephala, commonly known as the lion's head weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, mat-forming habit, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact, spherical inflorescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Pilosella leontocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
