Parashorea macrophylla

Parashorea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its durable timber.
