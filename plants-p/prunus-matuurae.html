Prunus matuurae

Prunus matuurae, commonly known as the Japanese cherry tree, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, known for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Prunus matuurae is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
