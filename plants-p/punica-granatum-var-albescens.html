Punica granatum var. albescens

Punica granatum var. albescens, commonly known as the white pomegranate, is a cultivar of the pomegranate plant characterized by its pale pink to white flowers and smaller, less vibrant fruits compared to the typical red variety.

This variety is native to the same regions as the common pomegranate and thrives in warm, arid climates. Its flowers are often more showy than its fruit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces edible seeds, though the fruit is not as large or as intensely flavored as the standard pomegranate.

It is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
