Plantago maritima var. pauciflora

Plantago maritima var. pauciflora, commonly known as sea plantain, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shores and dunes. The plant features rosettes of narrow, blue-green leaves with a waxy coating. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is well adapted to saline environments and is often used in coastal restoration projects.
