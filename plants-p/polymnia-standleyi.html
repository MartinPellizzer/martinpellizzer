Polymnia standleyi

Polymnia standleyi, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Polymnia standleyi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
