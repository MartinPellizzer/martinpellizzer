Potamogeton natans f. rotundifolius

Potamogeton natans f. rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It has long, slender stems that grow from the bottom of water bodies and produce small, green, rounded leaves. The plant flowers with tiny, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and ponds.
