Petrophile shirleyae

Petrophile shirleyae, commonly known as Shirley's bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Shirley's bottlebrush is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
