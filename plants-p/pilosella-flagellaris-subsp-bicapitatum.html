Pilosella flagellaris subsp. bicapitatum

Pilosella flagellaris subsp.

bicapitatum, commonly known as the two-headed devil's-bit manroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a soft, velvety appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure, which features two distinct flower heads on a single stem.
