Pinus parviflora var. pentaphylla

Pinus parviflora var. pentaphylla, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a variant of the Japanese white pine native to Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and soft, silvery-blue needles. The tree produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its foliage is dense and provides a striking visual contrast against the bark, which is typically gray and fissured.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
