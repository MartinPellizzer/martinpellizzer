Pachira nervosa

Pachira nervosa, commonly known as the Guiana chestnut, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Pachira nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
