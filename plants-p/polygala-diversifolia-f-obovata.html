Polygala diversifolia f. obovata

Polygala diversifolia f. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, often clustered inflorescences.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is native to regions of southern Africa. The leaves are typically broad and obovate, giving the plant its specific epithet. Its flowers are usually small and pale, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

This variant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
