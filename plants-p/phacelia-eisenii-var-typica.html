Phacelia eisenii var. typica

Phacelia eisenii var. typica, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The Sierra phacelia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
