Panicum digitatum

Panicum digitatum, commonly known as the finger grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, fluffy inflorescences that resemble fingers, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum digitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
