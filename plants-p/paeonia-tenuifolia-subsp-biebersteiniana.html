Paeonia tenuifolia subsp. biebersteiniana

Paeonia tenuifolia subsp.

biebersteiniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, single or semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained, loamy soils in full sun to partial shade. It is a member of the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves and slightly smaller flowers compared to the typical P. tenuifolia.
