Potamogeton densus var. major

Potamogeton densus var. major, commonly known as the dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
