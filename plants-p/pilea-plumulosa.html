Pilea plumulosa

Pilea plumulosa, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of thick, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically light green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care have contributed to its popularity as a decorative and low-maintenance plant.
