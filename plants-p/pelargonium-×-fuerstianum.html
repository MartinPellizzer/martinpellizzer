Pelargonium × fuerstianum

Pelargonium × fuerstianum, commonly known as the Fuerst's geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, double flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
