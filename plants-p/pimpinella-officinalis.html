Pimpinella officinalis

Pimpinella officinalis, commonly known as cow parsley, is a flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that appear in umbels. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia and thrives in moist, disturbed soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.

Pimpinella officinalis has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
