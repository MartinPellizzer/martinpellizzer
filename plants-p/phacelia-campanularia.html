Phacelia campanularia

Phacelia campanularia, commonly known as blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and it typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is often used in wildflower gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Phacelia campanularia is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
