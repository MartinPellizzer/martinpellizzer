Pieris bracteata

Pieris bracteata, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is an evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage, flowers, and seasonal fruiting.
