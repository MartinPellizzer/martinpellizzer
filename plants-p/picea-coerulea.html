Picea coerulea

Picea coerulea, commonly known as the sky pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles that are blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking visual appeal.

Picea coerulea is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in well-drained soils at higher elevations.
