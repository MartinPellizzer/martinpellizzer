Phaseoloides cinereum

Phaseoloides cinereum, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The flowers are typically large, trumpet-shaped, and come in shades of blue, purple, or white. It produces showy, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain several oblong seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
