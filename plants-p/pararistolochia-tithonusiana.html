Pararistolochia tithonusiana

Pararistolochia tithonusiana, commonly known as the giant Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and intricate, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
