Petroselinum crispum var. giganteum

Petroselinum crispum var. giganteum, commonly known as giant parsley, is a variety of parsley characterized by its large, fern-like leaves and robust growth habit.

It is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region, often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and culinary uses. The plant features a taproot system and produces small, yellow flowers in its second year, which eventually yield seeds. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, contributing to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often used in gardens for both ornamental and practical purposes.
