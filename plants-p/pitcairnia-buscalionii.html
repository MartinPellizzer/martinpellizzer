Pitcairnia buscalionii

Pitcairnia buscalionii, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's use. The leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture and often have a reddish or purple hue at the base. It produces tall, branching inflorescences with showy, colorful flowers that attract pollinators.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance for low light and ability to thrive in humid environments.
