Phyteuma betonicifolium var. sessilifolium

Phyteuma betonicifolium var. sessilifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a sessile base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance.
