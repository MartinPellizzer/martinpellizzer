Pimpinella tenuissima

Pimpinella tenuissima, commonly known as wild carrot or bishop's weed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pimpinella tenuissima is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
