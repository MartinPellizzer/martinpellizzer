Piper monostachion

Piper monostachion, commonly known as the single-stem pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a mild peppery scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of spices.
