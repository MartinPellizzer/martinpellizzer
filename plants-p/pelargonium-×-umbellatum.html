Pelargonium × umbellatum

Pelargonium × umbellatum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium × umbellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
