Poa pyramidata

Poa pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramidal grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike that bears small, two-lobed flowers.

Poa pyramidata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
