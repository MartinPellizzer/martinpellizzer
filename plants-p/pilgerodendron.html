Pilgerodendron

Pilgerodendron, commonly known as the Chilean pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Andes Mountains of Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. Pilgerodendron thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the unique ecosystems found in southern Chile.
