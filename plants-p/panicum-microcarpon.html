Panicum microcarpon

Panicum microcarpon, commonly known as littleseed panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum microcarpon plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
