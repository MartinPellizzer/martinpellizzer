Plantago asiatica var. yakusimensis

Plantago asiatica var. yakusimensis, commonly known as Yakushima plantain, is a variant of the common plantain native to Japan.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors, and is often found in the mountainous regions of Yakushima Island.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
