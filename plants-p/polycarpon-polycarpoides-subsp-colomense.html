Polycarpon polycarpoides subsp. colomense

Polycarpon polycarpoides subsp.

colomense, commonly known as the Colombian polycarp, is a perennial herb native to South America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust root system and adaptability to humid climates.
