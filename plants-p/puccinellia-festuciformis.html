Puccinellia festuciformis

Puccinellia festuciformis, commonly known as slender puccinellia, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
