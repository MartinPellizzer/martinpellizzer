Podolepis gracilis f. rosea

Podolepis gracilis f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-flowered slender podolepis, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant is distinguished by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
