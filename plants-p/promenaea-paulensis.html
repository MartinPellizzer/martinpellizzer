Promenaea paulensis

Promenaea paulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and can exhibit a wide range of hues, including red, yellow, and orange.

Promenaea paulensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
