Peuce

Peuce, commonly known as the peuce tree or *Peuce* species, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, often found in woodland and scrub habitats. Peuce species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The fruit is a legume, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.
