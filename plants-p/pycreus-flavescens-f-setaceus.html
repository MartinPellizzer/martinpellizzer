Pycreus flavescens f. setaceus

Pycreus flavescens f. setaceus, commonly known as the golden tansy.

This variant is a cultivated form of the species, characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and compact growth habit. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other members such as daisies and sunflowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.
