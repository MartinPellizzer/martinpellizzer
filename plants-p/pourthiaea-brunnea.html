Pourthiaea brunnea

Pourthiaea brunnea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and has hairy, glandular leaves. Its stems are often purplish in color, and it produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
