Pritzelago alpina subsp. auerswaldii

Pritzelago alpina subsp.

auerswaldii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are showy, bell-shaped, and typically blue or purple in color. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
