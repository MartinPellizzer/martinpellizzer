Plumeria trinitensis

Plumeria trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes throughout the region.
