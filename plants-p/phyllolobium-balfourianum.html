Phyllolobium balfourianum

Phyllolobium balfourianum, commonly known as the Balfour's phyllolobium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
