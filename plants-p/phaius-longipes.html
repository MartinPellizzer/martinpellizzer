Phaius longipes

Phaius longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Phaius longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in humid gardens.
