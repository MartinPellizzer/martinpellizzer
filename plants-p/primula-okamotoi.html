Primula okamotoi

Primula okamotoi, commonly known as the Okamoto's primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Primula okamotoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
