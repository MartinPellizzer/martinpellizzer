Pueraria calycina

Pueraria calycina, commonly known as the yellow passion vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Pueraria calycina thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
