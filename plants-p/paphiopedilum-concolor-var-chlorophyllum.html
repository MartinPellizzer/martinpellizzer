Paphiopedilum concolor var. chlorophyllum

Paphiopedilum concolor var. chlorophyllum, commonly known as the green-flowered slipper orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a defining feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unusual coloration and rarity in cultivation.
