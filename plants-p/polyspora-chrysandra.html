Polyspora chrysandra

Polyspora chrysandra, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, narrow leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Polyspora chrysandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
