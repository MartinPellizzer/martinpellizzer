Psacalium arboreum

Psacalium arboreum, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Psacalium arboreum thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in warm climates.
