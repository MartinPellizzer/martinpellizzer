Polygonum appressum

Polygonum appressum, commonly known as creeping knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread aggressively through underground stems. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Polygonum appressum is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides.
