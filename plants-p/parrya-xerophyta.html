Parrya xerophyta

Parrya xerophyta, commonly known as the red-flowered parrya, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves and vibrant red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Parrya xerophyta is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
