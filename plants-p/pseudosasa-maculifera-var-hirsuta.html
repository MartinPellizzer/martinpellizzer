Pseudosasa maculifera var. hirsuta

Pseudosasa maculifera var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a variant of the spotted bamboo species.

It is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia, particularly China. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are covered in fine, hairy textures. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a slight sheen.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
