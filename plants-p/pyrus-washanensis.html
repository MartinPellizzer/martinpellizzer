Pyrus washanensis

Pyrus washanensis, commonly known as the Wasan pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible pears that ripen in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and fruit-bearing qualities.

However, it is relatively rare and requires specific care to flourish.
