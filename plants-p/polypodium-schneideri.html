Polypodium schneideri

Polypodium schneideri, commonly known as the Schneider's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that are typically leathery and dark green in color. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a feature common among ferns. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America.

Polypodium schneideri is often found growing on rocks or in shaded, moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to diverse habitats.
