Polygonum crassicaule

Polygonum crassicaule, commonly known as broad-stemmed knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its stout, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.
