Pleurothallis illudens

Pleurothallis illudens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with vivid yellow or orange hues, and it produces a unique, elongated fruit.

Pleurothallis illudens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
