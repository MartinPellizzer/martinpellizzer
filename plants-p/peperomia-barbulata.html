Peperomia barbulata

Peperomia barbulata, commonly known as the hairy peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, which appear in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its compact size and ornamental foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
