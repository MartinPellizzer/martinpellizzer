Protea ciliata

Protea ciliata, commonly known as the serrated protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its showy, inflorescence-like structure composed of numerous small flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Protea ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
