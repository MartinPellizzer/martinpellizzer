Phacelia californica

Phacelia californica, commonly known as California phacelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
