Psyllium strictum

Psyllium strictum, commonly known as the common psyllium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a mucilage-rich seed, which is widely used in herbal medicine and as a dietary fiber supplement. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it typically grows in dry, disturbed soils.

Psyllium strictum is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in traditional herbal remedies.
