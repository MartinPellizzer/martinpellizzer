Pleurothallis isthmica

Pleurothallis isthmica, commonly known as the Isthmian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often clustered in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
