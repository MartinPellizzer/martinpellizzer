Pastinaca sativa var. arvensis

Pastinaca sativa var. arvensis, commonly known as wild parsnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the carrot family (Apiaceae).

It is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often pale yellow and have a strong, aromatic scent.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America.
