Phyllanthus oligospermus subsp. oligospermus

Phyllanthus oligospermus subsp.

oligospermus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by orange to red fruits that are edible. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
