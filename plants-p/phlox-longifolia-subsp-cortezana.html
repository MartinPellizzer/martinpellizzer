Phlox longifolia subsp. cortezana

Phlox longifolia subsp.

cortezana, commonly known as the Cortezan phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits after flowering.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to drought conditions.
