Parodia alacriportana subsp. brevihamata

Parodia alacriportana subsp.

brevihamata, commonly known as the short-stemmed parodia, is a compact cactus species native to southern Africa. It features a short, cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently.

The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.
