Potamogeton × scoliophyllus f. barensis

Potamogeton × scoliophyllus f. barensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and canals. The plant has a submerged growth habit, with stems that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in dense clusters. Its leaves are often covered in a thin layer of mucilage, which helps in reducing water loss and protecting against herbivory.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
