Polygonum dissitiflorum var. tortuosum

Polygonum dissitiflorum var. tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted-flowered smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming from late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
