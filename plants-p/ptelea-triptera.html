Ptelea triptera

Ptelea triptera, commonly known as the fragrant sumac, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the poison ivy and mango. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
