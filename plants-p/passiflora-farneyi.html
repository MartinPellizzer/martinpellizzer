Passiflora farneyi

Passiflora farneyi, commonly known as the Farney's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, pulpy flesh. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Passiflora farneyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
