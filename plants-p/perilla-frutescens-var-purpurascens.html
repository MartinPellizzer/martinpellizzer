Perilla frutescens var. purpurascens

Perilla frutescens var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple perilla, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its purple-tinged leaves and small, pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinctive flavor and high content of essential oils.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
