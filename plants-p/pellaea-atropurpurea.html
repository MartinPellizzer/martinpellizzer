Pellaea atropurpurea

Pellaea atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark purple to red stems and fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, intricate structure.

Pellaea atropurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
