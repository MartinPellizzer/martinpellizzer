Pandanus zea

Pandanus zea, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a spiky, fibrous structure that contains edible seeds.

Pandanus zea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its versatility in various cultural and practical applications.
