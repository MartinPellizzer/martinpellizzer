Planchonella myrsinifolia

Planchonella myrsinifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
