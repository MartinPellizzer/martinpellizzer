Polygala birmanica

Polygala birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
