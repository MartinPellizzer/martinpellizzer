Philadelphus lewisii var. platyphyllus

Philadelphus lewisii var. platyphyllus, commonly known as the flatleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, slender peduncles. Its botanical features include a woody stem with gray bark and simple, alternate leaves.

It is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments.
