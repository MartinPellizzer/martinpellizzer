Pentas elata

Pentas elata, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of brightly colored, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a trailing or shrub-like growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pentas elata thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring regular watering for optimal growth.
