Persicaria abscissa

Persicaria abscissa, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels.

Persicaria abscissa is often found in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
