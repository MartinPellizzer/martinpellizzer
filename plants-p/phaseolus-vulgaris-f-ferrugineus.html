Phaseolus vulgaris f. ferrugineus

Phaseolus vulgaris f. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusted common bean, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive rust-colored markings on the leaves and pods.

This variety belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is native to the Americas. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with compound leaves and small, bean-like pods. Its unique appearance makes it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.

The rusted common bean is also valued for its potential in crop diversity and genetic studies.
