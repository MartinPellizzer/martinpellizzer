Pilosella auriculoides subsp. polytila

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

polytila, commonly known as the Mexican hairless hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Mexico. It features basal rosettes of narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called capitula. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
