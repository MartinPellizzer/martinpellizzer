Piptochaetium ruprechtianum

Piptochaetium ruprechtianum, commonly known as Ruprecht's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
