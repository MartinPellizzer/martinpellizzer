Phacelia lutea var. calva

Phacelia lutea var. calva, commonly known as the golden phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow to golden flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
