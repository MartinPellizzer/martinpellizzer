Pleurothallis silvae

Pleurothallis silvae, commonly known as the forest pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Pleurothallis silvae is a member of the Orchidaceae family, known for its diverse and often intricate floral structures.
