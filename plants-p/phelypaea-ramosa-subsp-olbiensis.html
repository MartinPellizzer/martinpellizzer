Phelypaea ramosa subsp. olbiensis

Phelypaea ramosa subsp.

olbiensis, commonly known as the Olbiense fern, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The plant features fronds that are pinnately compound, with a delicate, feathery appearance. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
