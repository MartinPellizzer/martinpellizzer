Passiflora tuberosa

Passiflora tuberosa, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tropical flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora tuberosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
