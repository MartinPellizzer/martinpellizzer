Peltobryon pubescens

Peltobryon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Peltobryaceae.

It is characterized by its small, leaf-like structures that are covered in fine, hair-like projections, giving it a fuzzy appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or soil in temperate regions. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are typically borne on short stalks and release spores when mature.

Peltobryon pubescens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
