Poa rodnensis

Poa rodnensis, commonly known as the Rodnian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, glabrous leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool climates.

Poa rodnensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
