Pachypodium lealii subsp. saundersii

Pachypodium lealii subsp.

saundersii, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The plant has a distinctive appearance with a swollen, bottle-shaped trunk and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are typically absent, with the stem serving as the primary photosynthetic structure.

This species is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
