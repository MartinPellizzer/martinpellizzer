Paederia scandens var. mairei

Paederia scandens var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's climbing false indigo, is a vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces indehiscent fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
