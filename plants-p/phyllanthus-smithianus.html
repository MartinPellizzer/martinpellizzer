Phyllanthus smithianus

Phyllanthus smithianus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
