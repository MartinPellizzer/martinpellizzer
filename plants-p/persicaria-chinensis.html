Persicaria chinensis

Persicaria chinensis, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a valuable ornamental and a potential invasive species.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
