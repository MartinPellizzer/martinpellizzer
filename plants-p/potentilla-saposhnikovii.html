Potentilla saposhnikovii

Potentilla saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla saposhnikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
