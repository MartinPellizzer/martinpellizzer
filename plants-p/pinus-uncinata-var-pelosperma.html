Pinus uncinata var. pelosperma

Pinus uncinata var. pelosperma, commonly known as the silky pine or the silky mountain pine, is a variant of the creeping pine, native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate growth habit and its distinctive silvery-gray bark. The leaves are needle-like, typically shorter and more curved than those of the typical Pinus uncinata. It produces small, pale yellow cones that release winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

This plant is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing resilience to harsh alpine conditions.
