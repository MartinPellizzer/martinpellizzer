Pseudopodospermum phaeopappum

Pseudopodospermum phaeopappum, commonly known as the brown pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence. Its distinctive brown pappus, a feathery structure, aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.
