Panicum arizonicum var. laeviglume

Panicum arizonicum var. laeviglume, commonly known as Arizona grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in desert ecosystems.
