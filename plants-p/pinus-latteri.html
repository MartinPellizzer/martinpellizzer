Pinus latteri

Pinus latteri, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pine, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife.
