Psoralea semota

Psoralea semota, commonly known as the narrow-leaved psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Psoralea semota is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.
