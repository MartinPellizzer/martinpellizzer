Populus davidiana var. pilosa

Populus davidiana var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
