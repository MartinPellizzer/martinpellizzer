Phyllanthus collinsiae

Phyllanthus collinsiae, commonly known as Collins' spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of swamps and in wetlands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

It is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often found in areas with high groundwater.
