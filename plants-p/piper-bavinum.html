Piper bavinum

Piper bavinum, commonly known as the Bavin's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-lobed, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
