Poa glabrata

Poa glabrata, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikelets, which are a common feature in the Poaceae family.

Poa glabrata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
