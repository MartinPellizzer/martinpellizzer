Praxelis diffusa

Praxelis diffusa, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Praxelis diffusa is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
