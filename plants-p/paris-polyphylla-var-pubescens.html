Paris polyphylla var. pubescens

Paris polyphylla var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy Paris, is a member of the Melanthiaceae family and is native to the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its clustered, rhizomatous growth habit and striking, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
