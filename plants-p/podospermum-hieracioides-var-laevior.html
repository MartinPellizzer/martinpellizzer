Podospermum hieracioides var. laevior

Podospermum hieracioides var. laevior, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, giving it its common name. Its showy purple or violet flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
