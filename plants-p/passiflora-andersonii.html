Passiflora andersonii

Passiflora andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or violet berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Passiflora andersonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
