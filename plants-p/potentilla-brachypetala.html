Potentilla brachypetala

Potentilla brachypetala, commonly known as the short-stemmed cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Potentilla brachypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
