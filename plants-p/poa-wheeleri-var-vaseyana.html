Poa wheeleri var. vaseyana

Poa wheeleri var. vaseyana, commonly known as Wheeler's meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
