Paullinia cirrhipes

Paullinia cirrhipes, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas. It has a woody, sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Its flowers are usually white or pink and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
