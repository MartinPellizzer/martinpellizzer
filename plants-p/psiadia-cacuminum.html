Psiadia cacuminum

Psiadia cacuminum, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often feather-like in appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display during the growing season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Psiadia cacuminum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
