Piper albomaculatum

Piper albomaculatum, commonly known as the white-spotted pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white spots on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
