Ptilostemon dyricola

Ptilostemon dyricola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ptilostemon, is a small, perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It features linear, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or white, appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as plains and sand dunes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
