Phacelia ramosissima var. ramosissima

Phacelia ramosissima var. ramosissima, commonly known as the slender phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, ovate leaves that are hairy and often arranged opposite along the stem.

This species is valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in ecological restoration projects.
