Pectis monocephala

Pectis monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

Pectis monocephala is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions.
