Pleurothallis gongylodes

Pleurothallis gongylodes, commonly known as the golden rosette orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
