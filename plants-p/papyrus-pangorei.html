Papyrus pangorei

Papyrus pangorei, commonly known as the African papyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and papermaking. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

Papyrus pangorei thrives in marshy environments and plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
