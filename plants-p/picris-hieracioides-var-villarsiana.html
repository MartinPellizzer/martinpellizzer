Picris hieracioides var. villarsiana

Picris hieracioides var. villarsiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow flowers in elongated clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a smooth stem, alternate leaves, and small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
