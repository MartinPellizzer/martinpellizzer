Phyteuma nigrum f. vulgare

Phyteuma nigrum f. vulgare, commonly known as the common evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces capsules containing numerous small, oval seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
