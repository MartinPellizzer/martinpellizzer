Pseudolysimachion longifolium subsp.

pseudolongifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved pseudolysimachion, is a member of the Ericaceae family. It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
