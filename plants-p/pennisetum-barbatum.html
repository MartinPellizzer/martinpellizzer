Pennisetum barbatum

Pennisetum barbatum, commonly known as beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted, hairy inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

Pennisetum barbatum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
