Pteris hillii

Pteris hillii, commonly known as the Hill's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often arching or drooping. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to moist soil or rock surfaces.

Pteris hillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
