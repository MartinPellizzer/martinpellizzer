Protea conocarpa

Protea conocarpa, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of bracts. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils. Its flowers are adapted for insect pollination, with long, narrow tubes that accommodate pollinators like bees and wasps.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
