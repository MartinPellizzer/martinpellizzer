Polygonum persicaria f. humile

Polygonum persicaria f. humile, commonly known as the dwarf smartweed, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pale pink to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along streambanks.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and modest flowering display.
