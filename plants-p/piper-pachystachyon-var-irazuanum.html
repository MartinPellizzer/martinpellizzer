Piper pachystachyon var. irazuanum

Piper pachystachyon var. irazuanum, commonly known as the Irazu pepper, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, spiny fruits. The fruits contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it distinct within the Piper genus, with specific adaptations to its humid, forested habitat.
