Pachystroma longifolium

Pachystroma longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf pachystroma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
