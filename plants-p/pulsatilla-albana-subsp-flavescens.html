Pulsatilla albana subsp. flavescens

Pulsatilla albana subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from yellow to pale orange in color.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

The subspecies is native to parts of Central Asia and is known for its early spring blooming habit.
