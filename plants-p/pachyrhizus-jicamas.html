Pachyrhizus jicamas

Pachyrhizus jicamas, commonly known as the Mexican bean or jicama, is a leguminous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and deep, tuberous roots that store water and nutrients. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its roots are the primary edible part, valued for their crisp texture and mild flavor.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
