Peltogyne glaziovii

Peltogyne glaziovii, commonly known as the red cedar or Brazilian cedar, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods and are dispersed by wind.

Due to its valuable timber and ecological importance, it is also considered a threatened species in some regions.
