Pecluma ptilotos var. pilosa

Pecluma ptilotos var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy pecluma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive hairy covering. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The hairy pecluma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
