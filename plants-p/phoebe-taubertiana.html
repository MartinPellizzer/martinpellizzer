Phoebe taubertiana

Phoebe taubertiana, commonly known as Taubert's phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as avocado and cinnamon. The plant typically grows as a small shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with simple, opposite leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

Phoebe taubertiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
