Plantago juncoides var. californica

Plantago juncoides var. californica, commonly known as the California plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and broad leaves make it a hardy and adaptable species within its native habitat.
