Poterium polygamum

Poterium polygamum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Poterium polygamum is valued for its ornamental appeal and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
