Polygala amblyptera var. nicaeensis

Polygala amblyptera var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish or greenish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation to arid environments.
