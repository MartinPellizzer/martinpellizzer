Potentilla erythrocaulon

Potentilla erythrocaulon, commonly known as the red-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or reddish-brown stems, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla erythrocaulon is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
