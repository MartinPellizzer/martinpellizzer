Phlomoides calophyta

Phlomoides calophyta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phlomoides, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, usually displaying shades of purple or pink.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
