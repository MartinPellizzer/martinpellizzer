Polycnemum recurvum

Polycnemum recurvum, commonly known as the curly dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, ribbed stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by dark, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wetlands.

Polycnemum recurvum is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
