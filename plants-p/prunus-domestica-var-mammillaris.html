Prunus domestica var. mammillaris

Prunus domestica var. mammillaris, commonly known as the European plum or the common European plum, is a variety of the domesticated plum tree.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and often edible fruits that are typically used in jams, jellies, and baked goods. The plant has a deciduous habit, shedding its leaves in autumn, and produces small, white flowers in the spring. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and greyish-brown.

This variety is well adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
