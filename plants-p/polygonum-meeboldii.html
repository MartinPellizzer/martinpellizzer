Polygonum meeboldii

Polygonum meeboldii, commonly known as the Meebold's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spherical inflorescences, often appearing in late summer.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a rough, textured surface, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
