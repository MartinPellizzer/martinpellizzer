Phlox nana f. oculata

Phlox nana f. oculata, commonly known as the dwarf garden phlox, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it an excellent addition to smaller spaces.
