Paeonia corallina var. orientalis

Paeonia corallina var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental peony, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive coral-like hue. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are green and finely divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
