Pogochloa

Pogochloa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, elongated leaves that can reach several meters in length. Native to Southeast Asia, Pogochloa species are often found in wetland or swampy habitats. The plant's rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps make it an important component of its ecosystem.
