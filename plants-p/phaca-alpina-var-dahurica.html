Phaca alpina var. dahurica

Phaca alpina var. dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subarctic environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with a flowering stem that bears blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. Its flowers are tubular with five lobes, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
