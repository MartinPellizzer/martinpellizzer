Polygala nicaeensis subsp. gerundensis

Polygala nicaeensis subsp.

gerundensis, commonly known as the Gerundensian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure that aids in pollination by insects.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and gardeners due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
