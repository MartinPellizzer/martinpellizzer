Potentilla pensylvanica subsp. hispida

Potentilla pensylvanica subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hispid potentilla, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its stiff, bristly hairs, which give it a rough texture. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
