Pleurothallis armeniaca

Pleurothallis armeniaca, commonly known as the Armenian pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, linear leaves and a slender stem that bears a single, fragrant flower. The flower typically has a white or pale pink lip with intricate patterns, and the rest of the flower is usually a contrasting color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
