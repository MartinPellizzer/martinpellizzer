Psyllocarpus laricoides

Psyllocarpus laricoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
