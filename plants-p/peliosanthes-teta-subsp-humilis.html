Peliosanthes teta subsp. humilis

Peliosanthes teta subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the dwarf yew, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It features small, needle-like leaves that are dark green and densely packed on its stems.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and valuable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
