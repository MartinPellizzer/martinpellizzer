Paepalanthus leucocephalus

Paepalanthus leucocephalus, commonly known as the white-headed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and distinctive white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
