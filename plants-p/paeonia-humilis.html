Paeonia humilis

Paeonia humilis, commonly known as the Turkish peony or dwarf peony, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. Paeonia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
