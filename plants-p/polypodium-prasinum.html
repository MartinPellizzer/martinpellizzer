Polypodium prasinum

Polypodium prasinum, commonly known as the emerald moss, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, carpet-like growth habit and bright green fronds that are often covered in a waxy coating. This plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. Its rhizomes form a network that helps anchor it to surfaces and absorb nutrients from the surrounding substrate.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
