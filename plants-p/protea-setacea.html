Protea setacea

Protea setacea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The protea setacea plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for various pollinators.
