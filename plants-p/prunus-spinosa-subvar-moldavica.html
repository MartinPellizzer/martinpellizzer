Prunus spinosa subvar. moldavica

Prunus spinosa subvar. moldavica, commonly known as the Moldavian blackthorn, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Basin and parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark blue to black berries. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, rocky soils, and disturbed areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are white and appear in early spring, followed by the development of its edible, though tart, fruits.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
