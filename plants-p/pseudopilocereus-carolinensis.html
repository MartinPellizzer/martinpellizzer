Pseudopilocereus carolinensis

Pseudopilocereus carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina night bloomer, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habits. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
