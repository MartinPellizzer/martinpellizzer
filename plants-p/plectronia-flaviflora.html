Plectronia flaviflora

Plectronia flaviflora, commonly known as the golden-flowered plectronia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and have a slightly glossy surface, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Plectronia flaviflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
