Prunus laurocerasus f. caucasica

Prunus laurocerasus f. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian laurel cherry, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, red-orange berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This form is often cultivated for its dense foliage and winter hardiness.
