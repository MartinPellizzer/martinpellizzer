Pouzolzia pentandra var. hypericifolia

Pouzolzia pentandra var. hypericifolia, commonly known as the African tea or wild tea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically pubescent and have a serrated margin. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a herbal tea due to their aromatic properties.
