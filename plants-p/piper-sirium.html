Piper sirium

Piper sirium, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruits in traditional medicine.
