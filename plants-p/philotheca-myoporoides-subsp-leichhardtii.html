Philotheca myoporoides subsp. leichhardtii

Philotheca myoporoides subsp.

leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's myopor, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features small, oval to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
