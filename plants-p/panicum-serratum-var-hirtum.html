Panicum serratum var. hirtum

Panicum serratum var. hirtum, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, hairy texture, especially along the margins. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are often tinged with red or purple hues.

This plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
