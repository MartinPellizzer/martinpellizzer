Piper punctulatum

Piper punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by black, peppery fruits.

Piper punctulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild culinary uses in some regions.
