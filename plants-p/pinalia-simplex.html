Pinalia simplex

Pinalia simplex, commonly known as the simple pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged exterior. Pinalia simplex is adapted to thrive in low-water environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a popular specimen in succulent collections.
