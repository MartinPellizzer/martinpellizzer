Primula farinosa subsp. hornemanniana

Primula farinosa subsp.

hornemanniana, commonly known as the hornemannian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features erect stems with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
