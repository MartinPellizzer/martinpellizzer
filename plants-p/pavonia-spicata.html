Pavonia spicata

Pavonia spicata, commonly known as the scarlet flame or red broom, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
