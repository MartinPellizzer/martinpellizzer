Paraprasophyllum odoratum

Paraprasophyllum odoratum, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that emit a sweet, floral aroma. The plant typically grows in dense, shaded undergrowth, thriving in humid and moist environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
