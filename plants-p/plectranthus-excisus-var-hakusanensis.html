Plectranthus excisus var. hakusanensis

Plectranthus excisus var. hakusanensis, commonly known as the Hakusan mint, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.

Its leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
