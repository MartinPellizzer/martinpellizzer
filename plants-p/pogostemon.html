Pogostemon

Pogostemon, commonly known as the betel plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in traditional practices and as a stimulant. Pogostemon species typically have square stems and opposite leaves with a rough texture.

The plant's essential oils are also valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
