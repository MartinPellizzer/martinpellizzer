Polygala wolfgangiana

Polygala wolfgangiana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its showy, spire-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers.

Polygala wolfgangiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
