Paullinia cupana

Paullinia cupana, commonly known as guarana, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon basin of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into bright red berries containing elliptical seeds. The primary botanical interest lies in its seeds, which are rich in caffeine and other alkaloids.

It is often cultivated for its stimulant properties and is used in traditional medicine and modern energy supplements.
