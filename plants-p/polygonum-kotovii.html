Polygonum kotovii

Polygonum kotovii, commonly known as the Kotov's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants characterized by their distinctive nodes and often edible stems. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as riverbanks and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum kotovii is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its potential use in traditional medicine.
