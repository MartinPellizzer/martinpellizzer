Pinus sylvestris var. hybrida

Pinus sylvestris var. hybrida, commonly known as the Scots pine hybrid, is a variant of the Scots pine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
