Populus peronaeana

Populus peronaeana, commonly known as the silky poplar or cottonwood, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy, cotton-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones along rivers and streams.

Populus peronaeana is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes planted for ornamental purposes.
