Praxelis pauciflora

Praxelis pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered praxelis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Praxelis pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
