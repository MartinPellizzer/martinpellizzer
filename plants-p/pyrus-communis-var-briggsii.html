Pyrus communis var. briggsii

Pyrus communis var. briggsii, commonly known as the Bartlett pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its large, juicy, and sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has a moderate growth rate and is often grown in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
