Polypodium irioides f. cristatum

Polypodium irioides f. cristatum, commonly known as the cristate polypody, is a fern species characterized by its unique, crisped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds are typically green and may have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
