Plinia hatschbachii

Plinia hatschbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia hatschbachii*, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.

Plinia hatschbachii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.
