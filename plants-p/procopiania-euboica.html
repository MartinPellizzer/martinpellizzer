Procopiania euboica

Procopiania euboica, commonly known as the Euboic thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant features spiny leaves and produces purple to pink flower heads that attract pollinators. It thrives in rocky and dry soils, often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems and traditional herbal practices.
