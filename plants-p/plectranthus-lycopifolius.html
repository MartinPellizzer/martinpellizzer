Plectranthus lycopifolius

Plectranthus lycopifolius, commonly known as the lemon verbena plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Its leaves are often used in teas and aromatherapy due to their calming properties.
