Passiflora lunata

Passiflora lunata, commonly known as the moonflower passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that open at night, emitting a faint fragrance to attract pollinators. The plant produces bright orange to red berries that contain inedible, toxic seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and deep green color.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique floral display.
