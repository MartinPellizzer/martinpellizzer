Potamogeton preussii

Potamogeton preussii, commonly known as the Preuss's water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while also contributing to the ecosystem by providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton preussii plays a role in water filtration and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
