Phyllagathis stellata

Phyllagathis stellata, commonly known as the star-leaved piper, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes other plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, fleshy berries as fruit.

Phyllagathis stellata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
