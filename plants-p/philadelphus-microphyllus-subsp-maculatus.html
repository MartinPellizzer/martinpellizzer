Philadelphus microphyllus subsp. maculatus

Philadelphus microphyllus subsp.

maculatus, commonly known as the spotted mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a distinctive white or yellowish spot near the base.

The plant produces fragrant, white, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
