Panicum coarctatum

Panicum coarctatum, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
