Panicum stapfianum

Panicum stapfianum, commonly known as the Stapf's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often appear in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Panicum stapfianum is often found in rocky outcrops and along dry streambeds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.
