Pothos barberianus

Pothos barberianus, commonly known as the velvetleaf pothos, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a popular choice for indoor gardening and a potential weed in certain ecosystems.
