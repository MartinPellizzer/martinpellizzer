Puccinellia glaucescens var. osteniana

Puccinellia glaucescens var. osteniana, commonly known as the pale blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine blue or grayish bloom. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
