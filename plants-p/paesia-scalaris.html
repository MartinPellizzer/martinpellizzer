Paesia scalaris

Paesia scalaris, commonly known as the scalloped bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a scalloped edge, giving it its common name.

Paesia scalaris produces blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
