Pilocarpus pennatifolius f. latifoliolatus

Pilocarpus pennatifolius f. latifoliolatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed wild coffee, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark brown fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its chemical compounds, including pilocarpine, which has medicinal applications.
