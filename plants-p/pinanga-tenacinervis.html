Pinanga tenacinervis

Pinanga tenacinervis, commonly known as the slender-veined bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves. The leaves are typically long and narrow, with a prominent midrib and delicate, feathery veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
