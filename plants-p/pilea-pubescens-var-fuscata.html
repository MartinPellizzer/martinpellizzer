Pilea pubescens var. fuscata

Pilea pubescens var. fuscata, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The leaves are typically a deep green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in spring, followed by small, round berries.

This plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
