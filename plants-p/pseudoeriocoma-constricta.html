Pseudoeriocoma constricta

Pseudoeriocoma constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ericaceous plant, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, leathery fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
