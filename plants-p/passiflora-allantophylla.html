Passiflora allantophylla

Passiflora allantophylla, commonly known as the golden passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Passiflora allantophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
