Pentacalia millei

Pentacalia millei, commonly known as the glassy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pentacalia millei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils.
