Phlomis oppositiflora

Phlomis oppositiflora, commonly known as the opposite-flowered mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and it produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native environment.
