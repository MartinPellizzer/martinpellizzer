Philotheca nodiflora subsp. latericola

Philotheca nodiflora subsp.

latericola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved philotheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
