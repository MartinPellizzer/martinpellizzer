Phyllopappus

Phyllopappus, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Phyllopappus is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
