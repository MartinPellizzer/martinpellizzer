Paspalum vescum

Paspalum vescum, commonly known as little barley, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in fields, meadows, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum vescum is an important forage plant for livestock and also serves as a host for certain agricultural pests.
