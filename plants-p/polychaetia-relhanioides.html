Polychaetia relhanioides

Polychaetia relhanioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

Polychaetia relhanioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
