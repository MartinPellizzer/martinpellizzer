Pilosella permutata

Pilosella permutata, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella permutata is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often found in open, sunny environments.
