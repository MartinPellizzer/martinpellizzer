Paepalanthus longifolius

Paepalanthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved puya, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that display vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
