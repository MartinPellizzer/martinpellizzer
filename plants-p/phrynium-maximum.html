Phrynium maximum

Phrynium maximum, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often borne on tall stalks. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to that of taro.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its culinary uses.
