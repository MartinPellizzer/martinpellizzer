Peperomia hygrophiloides

Peperomia hygrophiloides, commonly known as the water-loving peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid environments and prefers well-draining soil with consistent moisture.

Peperomia hygrophiloides is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
