Peltogyne paraensis

Peltogyne paraensis, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, dark seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
