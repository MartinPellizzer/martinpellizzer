Pyrus theodorovii var. latifolia

Pyrus theodorovii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is typically small, sweet, and edible, though it is not as widely cultivated as other pear varieties. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit production.
