Phaca brachytropis

Phaca brachytropis, commonly known as the short-stemmed phaca, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists.
