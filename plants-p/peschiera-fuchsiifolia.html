Peschiera fuchsiifolia

Peschiera fuchsiifolia, commonly known as the Fuchsia-leaved Peschiera, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fuchsia-colored leaves that contrast beautifully with its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses. Its foliage remains lush throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for shady or partially shaded areas.

However, it requires well-drained soil and moderate watering to thrive.
