Polyscias rufosepala

Polyscias rufosepala, commonly known as the red-sepaled sycamore, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
