Panax japonicus var. parvifolius

Panax japonicus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng or small-leaved ginseng, is a variant of the Panax genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-brown root system. The plant is typically found in shaded, forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

The leaves and roots are used to make herbal remedies believed to enhance vitality and immunity.
