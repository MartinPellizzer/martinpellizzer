Plantago graminea

Plantago graminea, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Plantago graminea is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
