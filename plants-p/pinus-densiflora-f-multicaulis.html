Pinus densiflora f. multicaulis

Pinus densiflora f. multicaulis, commonly known as the multi-stemmed Japanese black pine, is a unique form of the Japanese black pine characterized by its multiple trunks emerging from a single root system.

This form exhibits dense, dark green foliage with a needle-like structure typical of the species. It is native to southern Japan and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and parks.

Its compact growth habit and distinctive appearance make it a popular choice for bonsai and landscape design.
