Potamogeton confervoides

Potamogeton confervoides, commonly known as the filamentous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender, branching stems and small, spirally arranged leaves that are often less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. Potamogeton confervoides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
