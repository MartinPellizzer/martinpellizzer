Persea tenuipilis

Persea tenuipilis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-yellow fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruit is valued for its nutritional content and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, subtropical climates.
