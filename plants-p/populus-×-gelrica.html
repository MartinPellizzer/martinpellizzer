Populus × gelrica

Populus × gelrica, commonly known as the European aspen.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between white poplar (Populus alba) and black poplar (Populus nigra). This tree is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found along riverbanks and in moist soils.

Populus × gelrica is valued for its fast growth and historical use in paper production.
