Potamogeton pusillus var. lacunatus

Potamogeton pusillus var. lacunatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, with a smooth, often slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spikes, and it relies on water currents for pollination. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in providing habitat and food for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
