Pocockia lineata

Pocockia lineata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in dense clusters at the base of the plant. Pocockia lineata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
