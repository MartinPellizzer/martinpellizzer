Pandanus vidalii

Pandanus vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color.

Pandanus vidalii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
