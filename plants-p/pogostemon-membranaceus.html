Pogostemon membranaceus

Pogostemon membranaceus, commonly known as the slender pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
