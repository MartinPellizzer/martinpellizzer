Polygonatum kalapanum

Polygonatum kalapanum, commonly known as the Keralan Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and traditional medicine.
