Potentilla gordonii var. chaetophora

Potentilla gordonii var. chaetophora, commonly known as the hairy potentilla, is a flowering plant native to regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
