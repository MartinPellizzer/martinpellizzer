Pseudotsuga jezoensis

Pseudotsuga jezoensis, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones. The tree thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress.

The Japanese white fir plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
