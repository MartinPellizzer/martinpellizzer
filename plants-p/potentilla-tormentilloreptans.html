Potentilla tormentilloreptans

Potentilla tormentilloreptans, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla tormentilloreptans is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
