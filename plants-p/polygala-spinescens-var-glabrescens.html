Polygala spinescens var. glabrescens

Polygala spinescens var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth spiny milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are usually narrow and sometimes hairy, though the variety glabrescens is distinguished by its smoother, hairless foliage. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

This plant is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
