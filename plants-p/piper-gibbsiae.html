Piper gibbsiae

Piper gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains aromatic, peppery seeds used in traditional indigenous practices.

Due to its unique ecological niche and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
