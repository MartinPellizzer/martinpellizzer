Pyrus eugenii-kelleri

Pyrus eugenii-kelleri, commonly known as the Kessler pear, is a rare and unique species of pear native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, often irregularly shaped fruits with a thick, waxy skin. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates with distinct seasonal variations. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
