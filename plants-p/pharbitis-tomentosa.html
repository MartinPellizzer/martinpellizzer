Pharbitis tomentosa

Pharbitis tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that are often followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is toxic to livestock and humans due to the presence of certain alkaloids.
