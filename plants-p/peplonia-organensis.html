Peplonia organensis

Peplonia organensis, commonly known as the Organ Islands peplonia, is a rare and endemic plant species found exclusively in the subtropical regions of the Organ Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a humid, warm climate to flourish. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Peplonia organensis is classified as a vulnerable species.
