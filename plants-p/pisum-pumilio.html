Pisum pumilio

Pisum pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf pea, is a small annual legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 10–30 centimeters, making it one of the smallest species in the pea family. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain 2–4 seeds, which are often used as a food source. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it has tendrils that help it climb.

The flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
