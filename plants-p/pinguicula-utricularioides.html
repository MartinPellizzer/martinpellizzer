Pinguicula utricularioides

Pinguicula utricularioides, commonly known as the bladderwort orchid, is a carnivorous plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is characterized by its small, pitcher-shaped leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant produces delicate, orchid-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its specialized morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
