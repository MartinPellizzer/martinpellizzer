Potentilla douglasii var. parviflora

Potentilla douglasii var. parviflora, commonly known as the Douglas's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, arching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
