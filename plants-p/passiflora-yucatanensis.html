Passiflora yucatanensis

Passiflora yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan passionflower, is a striking vine native to the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, complex flowers that display a mix of purple, yellow, and red hues, often with a crown-like structure at the base. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange or yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Passiflora yucatanensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
