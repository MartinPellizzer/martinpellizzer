Persicaria tenella var. kawagoeana

Persicaria tenella var. kawagoeana, commonly known as Kawagoe's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Its small, pinkish-white flowers bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.
