Pterotheca dioscoridis

Pterotheca dioscoridis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disc florets. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pale blue or white, with a distinctive, delicate appearance.

Pterotheca dioscoridis is adapted to grow in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
