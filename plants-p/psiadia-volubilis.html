Psiadia volubilis

Psiadia volubilis, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Psiadia volubilis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
