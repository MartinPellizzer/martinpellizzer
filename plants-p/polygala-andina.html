Polygala andina

Polygala andina, commonly known as Andean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often yellow or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Polygala andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
