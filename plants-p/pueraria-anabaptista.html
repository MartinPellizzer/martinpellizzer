Pueraria anabaptista

Pueraria anabaptista, commonly known as the white cowpea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, edible seeds that are used in various cuisines. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches soil fertility.

Pueraria anabaptista is often grown as a forage crop and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
