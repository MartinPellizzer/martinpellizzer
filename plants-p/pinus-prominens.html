Pinus prominens

Pinus prominens, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, needle-like foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against fires and harsh environmental conditions.

Pinus prominens is well adapted to high-altitude environments with poor soil and limited water availability.
