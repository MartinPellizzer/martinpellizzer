Potamogeton heterophyllus var. major

Potamogeton heterophyllus var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves that vary in shape depending on water depth, a characteristic known as heterophylly. Its flowers are small and green, often clustered in spike-like inflorescences.
