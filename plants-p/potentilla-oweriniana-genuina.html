Potentilla oweriniana genuina

Potentilla oweriniana genuina, commonly known as the Kashmiri cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
