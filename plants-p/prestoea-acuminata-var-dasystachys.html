Prestoea acuminata var. dasystachys

Prestoea acuminata var. dasystachys, commonly known as the red mahoe, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
