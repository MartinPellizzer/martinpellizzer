Piper avene

Piper avene, commonly known as the sacred pepper or the ghost pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that mature to a bright red color, containing numerous tiny seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, which includes capsaicinoids responsible for its intense heat.

Despite its fiery reputation, Piper avene is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
