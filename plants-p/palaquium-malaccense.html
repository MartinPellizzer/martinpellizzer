Palaquium malaccense

Palaquium malaccense, commonly known as the Malacca palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common understory plant in tropical forests.
