Ptarmica erba-rotta

Ptarmica erba-rotta, commonly known as tormentil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Tormentil is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its astringent qualities.
