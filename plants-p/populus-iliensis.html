Populus iliensis

Populus iliensis, commonly known as the Ili poplar, is a species of poplar native to Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in river valleys and mountainous regions. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a significant amount of pollen, which is characteristic of the Populus genus.

The wood of Populus iliensis is lightweight and has been used historically for various purposes, including construction and papermaking.
