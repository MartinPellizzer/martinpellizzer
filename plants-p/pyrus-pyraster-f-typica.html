Pyrus pyraster f. typica

Pyrus pyraster f. typica, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant has dark, glossy leaves that are oval in shape and provide dense foliage. Its fruits are small, round pomes that mature to a yellowish color.

This species is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
