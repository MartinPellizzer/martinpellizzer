Phyllocephalum anthelminticum

Phyllocephalum anthelminticum, commonly known as the spiral liverwort, is a non-vascular plant belonging to the order Marchantiales.

It is characterized by its thalloid structure, which is typically lobed and flat, with a distinctive spiral arrangement of the lobes. This liverwort thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on damp soil or rocks. Its reproductive structures include both sexual and asexual spores, contributing to its ability to spread in suitable habitats.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various small organisms.
