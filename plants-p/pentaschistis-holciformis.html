Pentaschistis holciformis

Pentaschistis holciformis, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has small, alternate leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are typically arranged in flat-topped clusters, making it a distinctive and ornamental species.
