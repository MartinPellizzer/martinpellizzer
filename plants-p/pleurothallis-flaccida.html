Pleurothallis flaccida

Pleurothallis flaccida, commonly known as the limp orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a pale yellow or greenish hue, and are often fragrant.

Pleurothallis flaccida is appreciated for its unique appearance and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
