Prasophyllum gracillimum

Prasophyllum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, grass-like stem with a single spike of small, pale green to white flowers. It blooms between September and November, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance.

Prasophyllum gracillimum is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
