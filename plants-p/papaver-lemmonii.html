Papaver lemmonii

Papaver lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's poppy, is a striking annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, with a distinctive blackish center. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces slender, hairy stems that grow up to 3 feet tall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Its showy blooms make it a popular choice for dry gardens and wildflower displays.
