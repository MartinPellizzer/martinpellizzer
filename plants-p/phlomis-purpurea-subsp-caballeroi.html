Phlomis purpurea subsp. caballeroi

Phlomis purpurea subsp.

caballeroi, commonly known as the purple mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, purple to pink flower clusters that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and wildflower meadows.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
