Pellaea reynoldsii

Pellaea reynoldsii, commonly known as Reynolds' maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically dark green with a reddish hue on the underside. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
