Phaseolus falcatus

Phaseolus falcatus, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pods that mature into curved, sickle-shaped beans. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces edible seeds that are used in various cuisines.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often cultivated for both its seeds and foliage.
