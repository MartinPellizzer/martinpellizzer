Phacelia magellanica var. griseophylla

Phacelia magellanica var. griseophylla, commonly known as the gray-leafed phacelia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are characterized by a distinctive grayish-blue hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
