Potamogeton pectinatus var. scoparius

Potamogeton pectinatus var. scoparius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and Asia.

It has long, slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, often with a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in dense spikes, and it is characterized by its ability to form dense underwater vegetation. It plays a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This species is often found in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes.
