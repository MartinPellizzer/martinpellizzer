Penstemon flowersii

Penstemon flowersii, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon flowersii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
