Puya macropoda

Puya macropoda, commonly known as the giant puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it one of the tallest flowering plants in the world. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, capable of storing water in arid environments. Its showy, violet to purple flowers bloom at the top of the plant, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, Puya macropoda is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
