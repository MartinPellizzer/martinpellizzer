Potentilla matsumurae f. paucidentata

Potentilla matsumurae f. paucidentata, commonly known as the sparse-toothed potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, often hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The species is native to parts of East Asia, particularly China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage is typically dense and compact, with leaves that have fewer teeth compared to the typical form of the species.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
