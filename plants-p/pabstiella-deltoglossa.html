Pabstiella deltoglossa

Pabstiella deltoglossa, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. It requires specific environmental conditions, including high humidity and filtered sunlight, to thrive.

Due to habitat destruction and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.
