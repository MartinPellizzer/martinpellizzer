Potentilla nivea var. himalaica

Potentilla nivea var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan snow willow, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems that are covered in dense, white or silver-gray hairs. The leaves are small, ovate, and also covered in a similar pubescent layer, giving the plant a frosty appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers clustered in cymes, which bloom in early summer.

This plant thrives in cold, alpine environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
