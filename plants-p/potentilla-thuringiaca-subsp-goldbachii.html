Potentilla thuringiaca subsp. goldbachii

Potentilla thuringiaca subsp.

goldbachii, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to central Europe. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
