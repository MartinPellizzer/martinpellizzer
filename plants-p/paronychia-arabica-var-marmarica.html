Paronychia arabica var. marmarica

Paronychia arabica var. marmarica, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky areas and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
