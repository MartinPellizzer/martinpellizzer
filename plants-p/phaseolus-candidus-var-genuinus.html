Phaseolus candidus var. genuinus

Phaseolus candidus var. genuinus, commonly known as the white bean.

This plant is a climbing legume native to the Americas, characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, white or cream-colored seeds. The plant thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
