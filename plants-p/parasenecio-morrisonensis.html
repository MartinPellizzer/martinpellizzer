Parasenecio morrisonensis

Parasenecio morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's senecio, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, sandy, or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
