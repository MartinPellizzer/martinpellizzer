Pennisetum cenchroides var. echioides

Pennisetum cenchroides var. echioides, commonly known as purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce clusters of soft, purple plumes, which are visually striking and attract pollinators. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a slight sheen. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to reseed itself contribute to its adaptability in various garden settings.
