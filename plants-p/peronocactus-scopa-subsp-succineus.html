Peronocactus scopa subsp. succineus

Peronocactus scopa subsp.

succineus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with white, woolly spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid climates.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
