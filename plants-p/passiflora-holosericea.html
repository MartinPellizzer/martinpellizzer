Passiflora holosericea

Passiflora holosericea, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a vibrant mix of red, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora holosericea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
