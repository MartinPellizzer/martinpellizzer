Phymatosorus katuii

Phymatosorus katuii, commonly known as the Katu Island huperjia, is a fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of its fertile leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
