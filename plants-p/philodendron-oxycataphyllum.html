Philodendron oxycataphyllum

Philodendron oxycataphyllum, commonly known as the cataphyll philodendron, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces unique cataphylls, which are modified leaves that resemble bracts and enclose the flowers. These cataphylls are often brightly colored, adding to the plant's ornamental appeal.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
