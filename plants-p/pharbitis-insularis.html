Pharbitis insularis

Pharbitis insularis, commonly known as the island morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring heart-shaped leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant produces spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Pharbitis insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world.
