Panicum raddianum

Panicum raddianum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted clusters of slender, reddish-brown inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Panicum raddianum is also valued for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
