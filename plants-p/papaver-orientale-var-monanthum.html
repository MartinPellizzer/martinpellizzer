Papaver orientale var. monanthum

Papaver orientale var. monanthum, commonly known as the single-flowered oriental poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, with a distinctive black center. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
