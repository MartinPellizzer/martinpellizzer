Pulicaria guestii

Pulicaria guestii, commonly known as the Mediterranean groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense heads. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pulicaria guestii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
