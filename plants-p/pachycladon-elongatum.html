Pachycladon elongatum

Pachycladon elongatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pachycladon, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its woody stems are highly branched and can form dense thickets, providing habitat for various small animals.

Pachycladon elongatum is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in semi-desert ecosystems.
