Panicum brachiatum

Panicum brachiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
