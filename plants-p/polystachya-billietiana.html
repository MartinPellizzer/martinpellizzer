Polystachya billietiana

Polystachya billietiana, commonly known as the Madagascar spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, fragrant flowers arranged in a dense cluster. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders.

Polystachya billietiana is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
