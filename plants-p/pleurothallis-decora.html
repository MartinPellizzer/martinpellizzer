Pleurothallis decora

Pleurothallis decora, commonly known as the Decorated Pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its blooming period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
