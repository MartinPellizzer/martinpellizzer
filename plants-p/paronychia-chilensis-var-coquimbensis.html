Paronychia chilensis var. coquimbensis

Paronychia chilensis var. coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and basal rosette of leaves. It has small, glandular hairs that help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
