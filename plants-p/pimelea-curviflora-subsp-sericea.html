Pimelea curviflora subsp. sericea

Pimelea curviflora subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically bright yellow or orange in color.

The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
