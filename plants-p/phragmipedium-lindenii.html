Phragmipedium lindenii

Phragmipedium lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's slipper orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb and produces showy, spurred orchid flowers in shades of yellow and green. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Phragmipedium lindenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
