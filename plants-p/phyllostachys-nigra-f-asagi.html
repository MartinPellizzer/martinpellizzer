Phyllostachys nigra f. asagi

Phyllostachys nigra f. asagi, commonly known as the black bamboo or asagi bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that turn a deep purple when exposed to sunlight. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green on top and lighter green underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and traditional Chinese gardens.
