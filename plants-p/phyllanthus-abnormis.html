Phyllanthus abnormis

Phyllanthus abnormis, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a tropical shrub native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
