Penstemon × gloxinioides

Penstemon × gloxinioides, commonly known as the hybrid penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a cross between Penstemon gandavensis and Penstemon gloxinianus, resulting in a species with unique hybrid characteristics. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage is often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.
