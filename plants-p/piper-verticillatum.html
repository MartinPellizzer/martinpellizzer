Piper verticillatum

Piper verticillatum, commonly known as the whorled pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.
