Panicum echinolaena var. ciliatum

Panicum echinolaena var. ciliatum, commonly known as the silky panic grass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-tipped inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
