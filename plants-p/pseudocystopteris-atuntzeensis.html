Pseudocystopteris atuntzeensis

Pseudocystopteris atuntzeensis, commonly known as the Atuntze rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, compact fronds with a distinctive, often serrated margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many ferns.

The species is native to parts of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates.
