Pavonia prionophylla

Pavonia prionophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false mallow, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pavonia prionophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
