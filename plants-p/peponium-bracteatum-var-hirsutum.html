Peponium bracteatum var. hirsutum

Peponium bracteatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy pumpkin, is a variant of the pepo genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, hairy, and often elongated fruits that can reach up to several feet in length. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically followed by the distinctive, rough-skinned fruits. The leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and the stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
