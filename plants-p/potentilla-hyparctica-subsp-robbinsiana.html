Potentilla hyparctica subsp. robbinsiana

Potentilla hyparctica subsp.

robbinsiana, commonly known as the Robbins' cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woods and along streambanks.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native plant gardens.
