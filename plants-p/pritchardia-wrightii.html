Pritchardia wrightii

Pritchardia wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's cycad, is a rare and endemic cycad species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinecone-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal areas or lowland forests.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural reproduction, it is considered critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
