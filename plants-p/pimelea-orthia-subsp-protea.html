Pimelea orthia subsp. protea

Pimelea orthia subsp.

protea, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that bloom in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in coastal regions.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
