Psidium sartorianum

Psidium sartorianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit that is typically green when immature and turns yellow or orange when ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
