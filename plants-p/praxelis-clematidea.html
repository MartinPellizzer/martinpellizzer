Praxelis clematidea

Praxelis clematidea, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Praxelis clematidea is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
