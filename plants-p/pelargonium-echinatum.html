Pelargonium echinatum

Pelargonium echinatum, commonly known as the spiked geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features hairy, upright stems and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. Its leaves are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pelargonium echinatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
