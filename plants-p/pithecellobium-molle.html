Pithecellobium molle

Pithecellobium molle, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain sweet, fleshy seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
