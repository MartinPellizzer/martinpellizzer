Pleopeltis hirsutissima

Pleopeltis hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its fronds are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Pleopeltis hirsutissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, moist gardens.
