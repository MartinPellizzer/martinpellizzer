Phlox speciosa var. occidentalis

Phlox speciosa var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western showy phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
