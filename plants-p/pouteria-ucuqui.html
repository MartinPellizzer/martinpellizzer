Pouteria ucuqui

Pouteria ucuqui, commonly known as the Ucuqui or Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a fruit known as a capsule, which contains the edible seeds prized for their nutritional value. The bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
