Phyllanthus campanulatus

Phyllanthus campanulatus, commonly known as the bell-shaped phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Phyllanthus campanulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact size and attractive floral display.
