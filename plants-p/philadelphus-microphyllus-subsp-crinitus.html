Philadelphus microphyllus subsp. crinitus

Philadelphus microphyllus subsp.

crinitus, commonly known as the hairyleaf mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
