Phyllopentas concinna

Phyllopentas concinna, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Phyllopentas concinna is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
