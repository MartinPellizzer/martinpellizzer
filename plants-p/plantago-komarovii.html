Plantago komarovii

Plantago komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in steppes and mountainous regions.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used traditionally for their soothing and healing effects.
