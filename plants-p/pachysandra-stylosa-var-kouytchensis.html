Pachysandra stylosa var. kouytchensis

Pachysandra stylosa var. kouytchensis, commonly known as the Kouytchensis pachysandra, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a mat-forming growth habit, making it ideal for ground cover. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

Its foliage remains lush throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.
