Phillyrea cordifolia subsp. sallentii

Phillyrea cordifolia subsp.

sallentii, commonly known as the Sallent Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
