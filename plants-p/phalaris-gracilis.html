Phalaris gracilis

Phalaris gracilis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Phalaris gracilis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
