Passiflora palmata

Passiflora palmata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-colored flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Passiflora palmata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
