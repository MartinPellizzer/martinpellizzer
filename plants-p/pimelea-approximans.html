Pimelea approximans

Pimelea approximans, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pimelea approximans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
