Parrya stenocarpa var. pinnatisecta

Parrya stenocarpa var. pinnatisecta, commonly known as the narrow-fruit parrya, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by distinctive, narrow, woody fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
