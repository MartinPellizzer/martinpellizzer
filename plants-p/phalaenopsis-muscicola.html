Phalaenopsis muscicola

Phalaenopsis muscicola, commonly known as the mossy orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and long-lasting, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media, such as bark or sphagnum moss. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the air.

Phalaenopsis muscicola is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
