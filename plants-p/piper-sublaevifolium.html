Piper sublaevifolium

Piper sublaevifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pepper vine, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by three-lobed berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates.
