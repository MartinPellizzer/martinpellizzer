Polygonum aviculare

Polygonum aviculare, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

It features small, rounded leaves that resemble mouse ears, and it produces clusters of tiny, pale pink or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and is often found in lawns, gardens, and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and vegetative propagation.

Polygonum aviculare is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants with similar leaf shapes and flowering structures.
