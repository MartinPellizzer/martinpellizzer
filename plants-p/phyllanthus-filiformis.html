Phyllanthus filiformis

Phyllanthus filiformis, commonly known as the slender phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, simple leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in dry, sandy soils. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its adaptability to arid environments and its potential medicinal properties.
