Pentas lanceolata f. velutina

Pentas lanceolata f. velutina, commonly known as the velvety pentas, is a cultivar of the pentas plant characterized by its soft, velvety texture on the foliage.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically bright red or orange, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

The velvety leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
