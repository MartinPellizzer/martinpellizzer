Phalaris arundinacea var. rotgesii

Phalaris arundinacea var. rotgesii, commonly known as the silver reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.

Its adaptability to moist environments makes it a useful plant for ecological landscaping.
