Priestleya boucheri

Priestleya boucheri, commonly known as the Boucher's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale green to white flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows on the bark of trees in moist, shaded forest environments. It has a unique morphology with a sympodial growth habit and a single, slender stem.

Due to its habitat specificity and rarity, it is considered a conservation concern.
