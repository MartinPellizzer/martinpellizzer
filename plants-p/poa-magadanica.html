Poa magadanica

Poa magadanica, commonly known as Magadan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including frost and low temperatures.

Poa magadanica is often found in alpine and tundra regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
