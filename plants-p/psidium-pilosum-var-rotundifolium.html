Psidium pilosum var. rotundifolium

Psidium pilosum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf guava, is a variant of the guava species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically small, with a smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
