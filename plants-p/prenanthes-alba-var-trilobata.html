Prenanthes alba var. trilobata

Prenanthes alba var. trilobata, commonly known as white burrweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, three-lobed bracts that give it its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This species is part of the Araceae family and is known for its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
