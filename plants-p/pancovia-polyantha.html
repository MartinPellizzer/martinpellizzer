Pancovia polyantha

Pancovia polyantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture.

Pancovia polyantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
