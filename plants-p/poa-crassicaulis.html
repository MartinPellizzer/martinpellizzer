Poa crassicaulis

Poa crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-leaved grass, is a species of grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and coarse, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple.

Poa crassicaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
