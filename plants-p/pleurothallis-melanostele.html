Pleurothallis melanostele

Pleurothallis melanostele, commonly known as the black-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive dark red or black stigma, which is a notable feature in its morphology.

Pleurothallis melanostele is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
