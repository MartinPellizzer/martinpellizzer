Physalis parviflora

Physalis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red capsules. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands or along roadsides. Its fruit, which resembles a tiny tomato, is edible when fully ripe and has a tangy, sweet flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
