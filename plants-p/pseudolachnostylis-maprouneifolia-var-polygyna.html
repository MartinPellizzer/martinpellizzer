Pseudolachnostylis maprouneifolia var. polygyna

Pseudolachnostylis maprouneifolia var. polygyna, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
