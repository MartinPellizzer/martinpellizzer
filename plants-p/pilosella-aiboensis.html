Pilosella aiboensis

Pilosella aiboensis, commonly known as the Aiboena hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pilosella aiboensis is a perennial species that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
