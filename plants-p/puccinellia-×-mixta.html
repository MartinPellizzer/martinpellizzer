Puccinellia × mixta

Puccinellia × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid glassworts, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline and coastal environments.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Puccinellia tenuiflora and Puccinellia simplon, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is typically found in salt marshes, estuaries, and other brackish water habitats. Its succulent leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences are adapted to withstand high salinity and fluctuating water levels.

Puccinellia × mixta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
