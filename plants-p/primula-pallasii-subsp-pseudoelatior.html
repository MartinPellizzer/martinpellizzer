Primula pallasii subsp. pseudoelatior

Primula pallasii subsp.

pseudoelatior, commonly known as the Pallas's primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features rosettes of oval, dark green leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive central gland.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
