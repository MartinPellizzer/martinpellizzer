Pseudolobivia longispina

Pseudolobivia longispina, commonly known as the long-spined lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the dry soil, allowing it to survive in harsh environments.

Pseudolobivia longispina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
