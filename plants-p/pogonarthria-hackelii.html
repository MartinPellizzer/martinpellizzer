Pogonarthria hackelii

Pogonarthria hackelii, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Pogonarthria hackelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
