Pteris decursiva

Pteris decursiva, commonly known as the walking fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizomes that produce new fronds along their length, giving the appearance of the plant "walking.". The fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance and a dark green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
