Piper leucophyllum

Piper leucophyllum, commonly known as white-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico, Guatemala, and parts of the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its glossy, light green to white-variegated leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
