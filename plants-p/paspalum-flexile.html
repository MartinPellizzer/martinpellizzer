Paspalum flexile

Paspalum flexile, commonly known as flexible grass or slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum flexile is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
