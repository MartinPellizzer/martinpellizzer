Phyllanthus pinnatus

Phyllanthus pinnatus, commonly known as the candle bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by small, round, red fruits.

Phyllanthus pinnatus is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
