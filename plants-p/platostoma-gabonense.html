Platostoma gabonense

Platostoma gabonense, commonly known as the Gabon shield fern, is a species of fern native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of root hairs, which aid in water absorption.

The fronds are usually bioluminescent, a unique trait that helps the plant attract nocturnal pollinators.
