Potentilla bogenhardiana

Potentilla bogenhardiana, commonly known as the Bogenhard's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, which contribute to its common name.

Potentilla bogenhardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its native alpine climate.
