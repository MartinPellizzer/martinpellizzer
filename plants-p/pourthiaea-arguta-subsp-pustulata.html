Pourthiaea arguta subsp. pustulata

Pourthiaea arguta subsp.

pustulata, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
