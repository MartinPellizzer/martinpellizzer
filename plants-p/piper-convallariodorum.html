Piper convallariodorum

Piper convallariodorum, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
