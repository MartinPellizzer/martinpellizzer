Piper taquetii

Piper taquetii, commonly known as the Taquetii pepper or the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure with dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
