Potentilla sischanensis var. peterae

Potentilla sischanensis var. peterae, commonly known as the Sichuan potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with numerous small, upright stems. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
