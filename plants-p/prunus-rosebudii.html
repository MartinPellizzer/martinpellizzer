Prunus rosebudii

Prunus rosebudii, commonly known as the rosebud cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender branches and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or black when mature. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
