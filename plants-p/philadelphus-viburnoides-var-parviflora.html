Philadelphus viburnoides var. parviflora

Philadelphus viburnoides var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets that are usually medium green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It blooms before the leaves fully emerge, creating a striking display.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and compact growth habit.
