Panicum spathellosum

Panicum spathellosum, commonly known as the silver panicum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a landscape plant in arid climates.
