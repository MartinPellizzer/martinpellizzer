Painteria nitida

Painteria nitida, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant's leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

The flowers of Painteria nitida are typically white or pale yellow, with a subtle, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.
