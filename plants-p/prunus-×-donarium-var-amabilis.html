Prunus × donarium var. amabilis

Prunus × donarium var. amabilis, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is renowned for its showy, early spring blossoms that display shades of pink and white, creating a striking visual display. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and fragrant flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit it produces is a small, hard drupe, though it is not typically edible for humans.
