Passiflora philippinensis

Passiflora philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and white hues. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces edible, orange-colored berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
