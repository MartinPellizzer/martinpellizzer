Panicum indicum var. brachiatum

Panicum indicum var. brachiatum, commonly known as little barley, is a grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue.

It is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or used in traditional medicine.
