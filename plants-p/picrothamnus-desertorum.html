Picrothamnus desertorum

Picrothamnus desertorum, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, which are adapted for pollination by birds and insects. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

Picrothamnus desertorum is well adapted to survive in low-nutrient, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.
