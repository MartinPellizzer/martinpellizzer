Paeonia japonica f. hisuta

Paeonia japonica f. hisuta, commonly known as the Japanese peony with white petals, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features showy, double flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has dark green, palmately lobed leaves and produces erect, branched stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Japanese horticulture.
