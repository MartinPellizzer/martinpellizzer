Piper macerispicum

Piper macerispicum, commonly known as the rough-skinned pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its berries are typically harvested when ripe for use in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
