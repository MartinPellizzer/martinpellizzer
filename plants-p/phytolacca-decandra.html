Phytolacca decandra

Phytolacca decandra, commonly known as the Indian physic or roselle, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It contains toxic compounds, including phytolaccatoxin, which require careful handling.

Its botanical features make it both a valuable and potentially hazardous plant in herbal practices.
