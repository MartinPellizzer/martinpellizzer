Pinguicula vulgaris var. pusilla

Pinguicula vulgaris var. pusilla, commonly known as the small bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to moist, shaded environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that form specialized structures called bladders, which trap and digest small insects. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, bogs, and along the edges of ponds, where it can thrive in nutrient-poor conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and they appear in early summer.

Due to its unique trapping mechanism and habitat requirements, it is a fascinating example of adaptation in the plant kingdom.
