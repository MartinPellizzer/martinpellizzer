Piper strigillosicaule

Piper strigillosicaule, commonly known as the rough-stemmed pepper plant, is a species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries containing aromatic seeds. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other economically important spice plants.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
