Poa deylii f. pietrosuana

Poa deylii f. pietrosuana, commonly known as the Alpine bluegrass, is a variant of the Poa deylii species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or alpine soils. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions. Its flowers are small and spike-like, with pale blue to greenish hues, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized plant in its native range.
