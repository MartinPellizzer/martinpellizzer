Phacelia campestris

Phacelia campestris, commonly known as blue flax, is an annual flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces slender, hairy stems that often branch near the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.

Phacelia campestris is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
