Paepalanthus polyanthus f. villosus

Paepalanthus polyanthus f. villosus, commonly known as the hairy polyanthus cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of pale green or white hairs, which helps protect it from intense sunlight and water loss. The plant typically forms a low, compact rosette with short, thick stems and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections of succulent plants.
