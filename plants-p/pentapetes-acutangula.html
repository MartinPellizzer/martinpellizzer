Pentapetes acutangula

Pentapetes acutangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pentapetes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, elliptic leaves with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in racemes.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
