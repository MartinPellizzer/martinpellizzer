Prismatocarpus rhodesicus

Prismatocarpus rhodesicus, commonly known as the Rhodes kaffir lime, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, citrus-like fruits. The fruit has a unique bumpy, prismatic appearance, which gives the plant its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruit, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
