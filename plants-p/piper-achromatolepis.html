Piper achromatolepis

Piper achromatolepis, commonly known as the white-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into the peppercorn-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
