Pandanus odoratissimus var. hueensis

Pandanus odoratissimus var. hueensis, commonly known as the Hainan pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often fragrant, especially when crushed. The plant produces long, branched inflorescences bearing small, clustered flowers. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
