Pedilonum theionanthum

Pedilonum theionanthum, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble flames, typically in shades of red and orange. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant and eye-catching display.
