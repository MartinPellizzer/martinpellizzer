Picris hieracioides subsp. barcinonensis

Picris hieracioides subsp.

barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcinoan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
