Perebea humilis

Perebea humilis, commonly known as the dwarf perebea, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant typically grows to a height of less than one meter, featuring simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Perebea humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact size and aesthetic foliage.
