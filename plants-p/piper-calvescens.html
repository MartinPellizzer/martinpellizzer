Piper calvescens

Piper calvescens, commonly known as the pepperleaf or wild pepper, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
