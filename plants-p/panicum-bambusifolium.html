Panicum bambusifolium

Panicum bambusifolium, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that resemble bamboo in appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering spikes are often pale green or brown, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
