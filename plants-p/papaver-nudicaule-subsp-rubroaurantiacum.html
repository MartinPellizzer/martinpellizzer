Papaver nudicaule subsp. rubroaurantiacum

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

rubroaurantiacum, commonly known as the red-orange poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia. It features delicate, papery petals that range in color from pale yellow to vibrant red-orange, depending on the variety.

The plant produces a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It blooms in early summer, producing showy flowers that are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
