Pseuderucaria teretifolia var. grandiflora

Pseuderucaria teretifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora variant of the slender-leafed pseuderucaria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as tropical rainforests. Its botanical features include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
