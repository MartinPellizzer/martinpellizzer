Piper zentanum

Piper zentanum, commonly known as Zentanum pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. The fruits contain numerous tiny seeds and are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
