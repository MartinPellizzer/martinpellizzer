Paepalanthus pseudoelongatus

Paepalanthus pseudoelongatus, commonly known as the elongated puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base, creating a unique visual structure. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sunny environments.
