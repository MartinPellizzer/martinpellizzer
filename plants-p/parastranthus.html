Parastranthus

Parastranthus, commonly known as the "wild lettuce" or "false lettuce," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their alternate, lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow to orange composite flowers. They are native to southern Africa and are often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Parastranthus species are annual or biennial herbs that produce small, fleshy fruits called cypsela.

The genus is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
