Picridium perenne

Picridium perenne, commonly known as the everlasting pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.

The everlasting pea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
