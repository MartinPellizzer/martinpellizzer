Prostanthera incisa var. pubescens

Prostanthera incisa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Prostanthera incisa var. pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
