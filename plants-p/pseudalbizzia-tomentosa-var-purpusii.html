Pseudalbizzia tomentosa var. purpusii

Pseudalbizzia tomentosa var. purpusii, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, silky-hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with each leaflet being small and ovate.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
