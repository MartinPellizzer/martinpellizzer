Primula cavanillesii

Primula cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable addition to alpine gardens. Its foliage consists of broad, hairy leaves that help it withstand cold and high-altitude conditions.

Primula cavanillesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil with partial shade.
