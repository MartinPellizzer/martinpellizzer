Passiflora × rosea

Passiflora × rosea, commonly known as the Passion Flower or Passion Vine, is a hybrid species within the Passifloraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a unique blend of colors, often including red, yellow, and blue. The plant is native to the tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits.

Passiflora × rosea is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its fruit, which is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
