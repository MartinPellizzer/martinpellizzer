Panicum malacophyllum var. cordobense

Panicum malacophyllum var. cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoba panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable regions.
