Pimelea spiculigera var. thesioides

Pimelea spiculigera var. thesioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ghostpipe shrub, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
