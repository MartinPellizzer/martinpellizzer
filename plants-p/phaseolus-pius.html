Phaseolus pius

Phaseolus pius, commonly known as the common bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is widely cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and climbing or trailing growth habits. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and the pods contain several bean seeds.

The plant is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil.
