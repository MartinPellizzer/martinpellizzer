Piper luzonense

Piper luzonense, commonly known as Luzon pepper or Philippine pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.
