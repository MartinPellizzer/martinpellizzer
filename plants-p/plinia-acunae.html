Plinia acunae

Plinia acunae, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark berries. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Plinia acunae is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
