Potentilla bithynica

Potentilla bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, compact form with hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla bithynica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
