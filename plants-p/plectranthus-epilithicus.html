Plectranthus epilithicus

Plectranthus epilithicus, commonly known as the stonecrop plectranthus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
