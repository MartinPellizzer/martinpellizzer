Pachystoma thomsonianum

Pachystoma thomsonianum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in the early morning. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater during droughts.

Pachystoma thomsonianum plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
