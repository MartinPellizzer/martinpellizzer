Panicum fasciculatum var. strictum

Panicum fasciculatum var. strictum, commonly known as stiff panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their visual appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for dry, disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
