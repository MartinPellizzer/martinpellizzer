Pentapogon avenoides

Pentapogon avenoides, commonly known as the creeping wheatgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features slender, rhizomatous stems that spread along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
