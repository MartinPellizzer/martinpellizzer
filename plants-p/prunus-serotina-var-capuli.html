Prunus serotina var. capuli

Prunus serotina var. capuli, commonly known as the black cherry or American cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its doubly serrated leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red drupes that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained habitats.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in woodworking.
