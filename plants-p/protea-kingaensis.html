Protea kingaensis

Protea kingaensis, commonly known as the Kingaensis protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the Western Cape region and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its floral structure consists of a central cone-like receptacle surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Protea kingaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
