Plantago candollei

Plantago candollei, commonly known as the Andean plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the plantain family, Plantaginaceae, and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
