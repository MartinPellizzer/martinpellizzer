Pleurothallis crateriformis

Pleurothallis crateriformis, commonly known as the crater-shaped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, urn-shaped flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is highly valued in horticultural circles.
