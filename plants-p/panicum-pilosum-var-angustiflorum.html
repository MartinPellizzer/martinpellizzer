Panicum pilosum var. angustiflorum

Panicum pilosum var. angustiflorum, commonly known as narrow-flowered beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with narrow, lance-shaped flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
