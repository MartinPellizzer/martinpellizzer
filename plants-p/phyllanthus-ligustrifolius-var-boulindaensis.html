Phyllanthus ligustrifolius var. boulindaensis

Phyllanthus ligustrifolius var. boulindaensis, commonly known as the Boulinda phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.
