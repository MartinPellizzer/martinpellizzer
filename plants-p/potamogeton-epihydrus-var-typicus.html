Potamogeton epihydrus var. typicus

Potamogeton epihydrus var. typicus, commonly known as the water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and streams.

Potamogeton epihydrus var. typicus plays a vital role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.
