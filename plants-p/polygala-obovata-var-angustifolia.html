Polygala obovata var. angustifolia

Polygala obovata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, angular stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lanceolate to ovate, with a narrow, tapering shape, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
