Phyllanthus hyssopifolioides

Phyllanthus hyssopifolioides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, round fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
