Phrynium truncatum

Phrynium truncatum, commonly known as the African shield plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a colorful spathe, often in shades of red or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
