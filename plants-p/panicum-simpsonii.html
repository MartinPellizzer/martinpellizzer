Panicum simpsonii

Panicum simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces fluffy, pinkish to purple-tinted seed heads during the summer months. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control. Its delicate appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
