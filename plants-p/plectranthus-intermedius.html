Plectranthus intermedius

Plectranthus intermedius, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that range from green to purple in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants.

Plectranthus intermedius is often grown as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
