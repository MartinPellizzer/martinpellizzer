Parodia larapuntensis

Parodia larapuntensis, commonly known as the "Larapunta cactus," is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented appearance. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
