Pilosella triplex

Pilosella triplex, commonly known as the three-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Pilosella triplex has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed.

It is often mistaken for other hawkweed species due to its similar appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soil.
