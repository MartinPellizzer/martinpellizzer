Panicum philadelphicum var. campestre

Panicum philadelphicum var. campestre, commonly known as eastern dropseed, is a native grass species of the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and produces slender, spike-like flower clusters. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and woodland edges. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The plant is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
