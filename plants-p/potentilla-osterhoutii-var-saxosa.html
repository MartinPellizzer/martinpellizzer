Potentilla osterhoutii var. saxosa

Potentilla osterhoutii var. saxosa, commonly known as the saxosa potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
