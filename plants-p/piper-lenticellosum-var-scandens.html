Piper lenticellosum var. scandens

Piper lenticellosum var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural and ecological studies.
