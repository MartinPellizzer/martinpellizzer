Pelargonium rubromaculatum

Pelargonium rubromaculatum, commonly known as the red-spotted geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features attractive, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red markings and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Pelargonium rubromaculatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and easy care requirements.
