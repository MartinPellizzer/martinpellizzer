Poa pratensis var. glauca

Poa pratensis var. glauca, commonly known as blue meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in lawns and natural landscapes. This grass thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It has a fine texture and forms a dense turf, making it suitable for ornamental and turfgrass applications.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves and a fibrous root system that aids in soil stabilization.
