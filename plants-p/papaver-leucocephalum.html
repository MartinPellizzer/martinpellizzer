Papaver leucocephalum

Papaver leucocephalum, commonly known as white poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its white, papery petals that resemble a poppy in shape, though it is not related to the true poppies. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its delicate, herbaceous appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique and striking floral display.
