Photinopteris acuminata

Photinopteris acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Hymenophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern on the underside of the fronds.

Photinopteris acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
