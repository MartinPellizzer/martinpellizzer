Prangos quasiperforata

Prangos quasiperforata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centipede plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with finely serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers with prominent, thread-like filaments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
