Pavonia racemifera

Pavonia racemifera, commonly known as the scarlet mimosa, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, drought-tolerant plants.
