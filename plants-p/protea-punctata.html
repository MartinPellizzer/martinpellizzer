Protea punctata

Protea punctata, commonly known as the speckled protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant features a woody stem with dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish or grayish bloom. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the Western Cape region.

Protea punctata is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms.
