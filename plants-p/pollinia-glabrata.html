Pollinia glabrata

Pollinia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth pollinia, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized reproductive structures.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant's most distinctive feature is its pollinia, which are dense masses of pollen that adhere to the bodies of pollinators. These pollinia are smooth and compact, facilitating efficient transfer during pollination.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, Pollinia glabrata is relatively rare and often found in humid, shaded environments.
