Piper sempervirens

Piper sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly the Amazon basin. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
