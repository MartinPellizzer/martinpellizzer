Pleurothallis peniculus

Pleurothallis peniculus, commonly known as the penicillus orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

Pleurothallis peniculus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
