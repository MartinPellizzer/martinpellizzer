Physalis neomexicana

Physalis neomexicana, commonly known as the Mexican husk tomato, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, bright orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which gives it a unique appearance. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its vibrant fruit and foliage.
