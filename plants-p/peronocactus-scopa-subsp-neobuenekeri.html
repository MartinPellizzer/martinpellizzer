Peronocactus scopa subsp. neobuenekeri

Peronocactus scopa subsp.

neobuenekeri, commonly known as the Neobuenekeri Pencil Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is a slow-growing cactus that is well adapted to hot, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
