Porophyllum spathulatum

Porophyllum spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and often aromatic foliage. The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces that contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its flowering stems are usually unbranched and bear clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Porophyllum spathulatum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
