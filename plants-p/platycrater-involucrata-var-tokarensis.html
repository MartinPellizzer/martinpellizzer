Platycrater involucrata var. tokarensis

Platycrater involucrata var. tokarensis, commonly known as the Tokarensis Spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
