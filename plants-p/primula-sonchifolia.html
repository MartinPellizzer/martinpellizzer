Primula sonchifolia

Primula sonchifolia, commonly known as the meadow primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow or orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
