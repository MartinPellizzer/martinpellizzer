Potentilla chrysantha var. concinna

Potentilla chrysantha var. concinna, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, compact form. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
