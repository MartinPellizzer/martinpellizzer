Prunus pseudoarmeniaca var. puberula

Prunus pseudoarmeniaca var. puberula, commonly known as the Armenian cherry or Persian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The variety puberula is distinguished by its pubescent (hairy) foliage, which gives it a unique appearance compared to other varieties.
