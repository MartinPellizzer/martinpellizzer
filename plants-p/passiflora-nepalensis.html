Passiflora nepalensis

Passiflora nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that resemble the structure of a passion fruit. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Passiflora nepalensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional medicinal uses in the regions where it is native.
