Pterisanthes sinuosa

Pterisanthes sinuosa, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often ribbed and have a zigzag or sinuous appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters near the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountain meadows and forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
