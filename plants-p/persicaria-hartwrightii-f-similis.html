Persicaria hartwrightii f. similis

Persicaria hartwrightii f. similis, commonly known as the Hartwright's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and usually appear in shades of pink to white.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
