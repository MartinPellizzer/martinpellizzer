Picris sancta

Picris sancta, commonly known as holy thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the Holy Land and its use in traditional medicine.
