Piper regelii

Piper regelii, commonly known as the Java pepper or pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces tiny berries that turn red when mature, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Piper regelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
