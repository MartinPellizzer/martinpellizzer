Paspalum crucense

Paspalum crucense, commonly known as the common paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to light blue flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along roadsides.

Paspalum crucense is valued for its ornamental use and has been introduced to other parts of the world for erosion control and landscaping.
