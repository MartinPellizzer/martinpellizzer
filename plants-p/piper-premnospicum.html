Piper premnospicum

Piper premnospicum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
