Polycarpon tetraphyllum var. densum

Polycarpon tetraphyllum var. densum, commonly known as the dense-stemmed four-leaf groundsel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are arranged in a compact, cushion-like structure. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, disc-like inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its dense foliage and is often found in moist, calcareous soils.
