Pinus otteana

Pinus otteana, commonly known as the Otte's pine, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of five, and its distinctive cone structure. The tree is adapted to cold and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Pinus otteana plays a vital role in its ecosystem, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
