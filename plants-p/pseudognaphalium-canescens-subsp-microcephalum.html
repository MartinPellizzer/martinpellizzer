Pseudognaphalium canescens subsp. microcephalum

Pseudognaphalium canescens subsp.

microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in compact, spherical clusters, and the leaves are usually gray-green with a woolly texture.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in dry landscapes.
