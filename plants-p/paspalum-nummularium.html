Paspalum nummularium

Paspalum nummularium, commonly known as silvergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Paspalum nummularium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
