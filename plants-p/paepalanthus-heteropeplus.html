Paepalanthus heteropeplus

Paepalanthus heteropeplus, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool and temperate climates. It features yellowish to orange flowers with a distinctive central disc, and its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
