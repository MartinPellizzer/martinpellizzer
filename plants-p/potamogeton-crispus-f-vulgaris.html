Potamogeton crispus f. vulgaris

Potamogeton crispus f. vulgaris, commonly known as curly pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across the Northern Hemisphere.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically curled or wavy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, rivers, and ponds.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in some regions, impacting water quality and aquatic ecosystems.
