Pterocarpus macrophyllus

Pterocarpus macrophyllus, commonly known as the greater sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, dark purple flowers. The tree is valued for its heartwood, which is highly prized in traditional medicine and as a source of sandalwood oil. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

The plant has cultural and economic significance, particularly in countries like India and Indonesia.
