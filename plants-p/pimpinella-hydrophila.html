Pimpinella hydrophila

Pimpinella hydrophila, commonly known as water cowpea, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces oval, ribbed fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Pimpinella hydrophila is often found in marshy environments and is adapted to periodic flooding.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
