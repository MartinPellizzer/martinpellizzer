Perebea integrifolia

Perebea integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed perebea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
