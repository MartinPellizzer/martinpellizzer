Polygonum gramineum

Polygonum gramineum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they alternate along the stem. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.

This plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments, such as wetlands and stream banks.
