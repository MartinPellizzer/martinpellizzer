Paspalum chrysostachyum

Paspalum chrysostachyum, commonly known as golden false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically golden in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum chrysostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
