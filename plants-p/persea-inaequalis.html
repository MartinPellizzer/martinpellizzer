Persea inaequalis

Persea inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. Its leaves are alternate, elliptic to ovate in shape, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, yellowish-orange berry that ripens in the fall and is edible when fully mature.
