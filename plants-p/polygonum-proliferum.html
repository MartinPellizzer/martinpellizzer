Polygonum proliferum

Polygonum proliferum, commonly known as the creeping knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread rapidly through underground stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Polygonum proliferum is often found in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.
