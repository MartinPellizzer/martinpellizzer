Plectranthus scopulicola

Plectranthus scopulicola, commonly known as the rock plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Plectranthus scopulicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
