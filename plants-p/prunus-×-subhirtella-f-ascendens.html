Prunus × subhirtella f. ascendens

Prunus × subhirtella f. ascendens, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus subhirtella and Prunus × yedoensis, resulting in its distinctive weeping form. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental tree is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
