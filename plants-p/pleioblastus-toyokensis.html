Pleioblastus toyokensis

Pleioblastus toyokensis, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a species of bamboo native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in clumps and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant, feathery texture.

Pleioblastus toyokensis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
