Pavonia schweinfurthii

Pavonia schweinfurthii, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and desert scrub habitats. It has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Pavonia schweinfurthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
