Prunus insititia var. syriaca

Prunus insititia var. syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian cherry or wild cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible when ripe, though they are often sour and tart. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it flowers with white or pale pink petals in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
