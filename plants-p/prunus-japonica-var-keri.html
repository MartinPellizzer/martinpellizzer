Prunus japonica var. keri

Prunus japonica var. keri, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Keri cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, oval berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic appeal.
