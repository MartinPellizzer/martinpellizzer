Phrygilanthus longibracteatus

Phrygilanthus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract phrygilanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored inflorescences that consist of clustered, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
