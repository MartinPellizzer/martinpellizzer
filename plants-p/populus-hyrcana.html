Populus hyrcana

Populus hyrcana, commonly known as the Central Asian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
