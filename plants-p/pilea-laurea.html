Pilea laurea

Pilea laurea, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spathaceous inflorescences.

Pilea laurea is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
