Phaseolus grandidieri

Phaseolus grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar bean, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that give way to elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Due to its unique genetic makeup, it is of interest in botanical research and crop diversity studies.
