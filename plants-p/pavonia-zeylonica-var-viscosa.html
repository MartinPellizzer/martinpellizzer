Pavonia zeylonica var. viscosa

Pavonia zeylonica var. viscosa, commonly known as the sticky pavonia, is a perennial shrub native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its sticky foliage is a notable botanical feature, contributing to its unique appearance and ecological interactions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
