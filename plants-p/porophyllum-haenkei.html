Porophyllum haenkei

Porophyllum haenkei, commonly known as Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, which are dispersed by the wind.

The leaves are hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent when crushed.
