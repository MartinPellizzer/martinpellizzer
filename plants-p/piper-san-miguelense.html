Piper san-miguelense

Piper san-miguelense, commonly known as the San Miguel pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the cloud forests of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit and its small, pendant-like flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture and a mildly aromatic scent.

The fruit, a small, green berry, ripens to a bright red and is used in local cuisines for its spicy flavor.
