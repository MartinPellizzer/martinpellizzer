Passiflora mansoi var. mansoi

Passiflora mansoi var. mansoi, commonly known as the Thai passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five prominent sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in traditional medicine. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
