Platycoryne isoetifolia

Platycoryne isoetifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterwheel plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that resemble the blades of a waterwheel, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
