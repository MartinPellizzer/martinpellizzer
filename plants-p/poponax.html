Poponax

Poponax, commonly known as the New Zealand flax.

It is a flowering plant native to New Zealand, belonging to the family Asparagaceae. The plant is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional Maori basket weaving.

Poponax thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and forested areas.
