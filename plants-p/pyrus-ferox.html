Pyrus ferox

Pyrus ferox, commonly known as the bristly pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or mountainous areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible but tart berries. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a bristly appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in horticulture for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
