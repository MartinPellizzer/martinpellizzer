Psoralea bituminosa var. atropurpurea

Psoralea bituminosa var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are bipinnate, and it produces pods that contain small, round seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
