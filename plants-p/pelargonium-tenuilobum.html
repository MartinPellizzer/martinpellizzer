Pelargonium tenuilobum

Pelargonium tenuilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves with finely serrated margins. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium tenuilobum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
