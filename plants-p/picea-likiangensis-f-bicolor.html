Picea likiangensis f. bicolor

Picea likiangensis f. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor fir, is a rare and unique variant of the Likiang fir.

It is characterized by its striking bicolor foliage, with upper surfaces of the needles appearing white or silvery and lower surfaces green. This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its distinctive appearance makes it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems at elevations above 3,000 meters.
