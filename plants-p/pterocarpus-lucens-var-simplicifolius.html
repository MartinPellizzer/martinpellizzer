Pterocarpus lucens var. simplicifolius

Pterocarpus lucens var. simplicifolius, commonly known as the simple-leaved flamewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It produces woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
