Parkia microcephala

Parkia microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.

The tree also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
