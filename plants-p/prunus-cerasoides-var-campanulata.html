Prunus cerasoides var. campanulata

Prunus cerasoides var. campanulata, commonly known as the Kashmir cherry or Himalayan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in spring, which are followed by red or dark purple berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
