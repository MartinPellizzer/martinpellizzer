Psophiza

Psophiza, commonly known as the mountain lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. Psophiza species typically grow in high-altitude environments, including Andean regions, where they adapt to cooler temperatures and lower oxygen levels. Their flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, especially hummingbirds, which are attracted to their nectar-rich blooms.
