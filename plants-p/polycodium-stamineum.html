Polycodium stamineum

Polycodium stamineum, commonly known as the stamen plant, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with prominent stamens, which are its most distinguishing feature. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its wetland habitat, supporting its role in local ecosystems.
