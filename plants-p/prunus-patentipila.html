Prunus patentipila

Prunus patentipila, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white blossoms that appear in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn green in summer and yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a small, sweet drupe that ripens in late summer.

Prunus patentipila is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and cultural significance.
