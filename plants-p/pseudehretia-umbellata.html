Pseudehretia umbellata

Pseudehretia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that resemble an umbrella, hence its common name. The plant has a short, stout rhizome from which the fronds emerge. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, with a smooth texture.

Pseudehretia umbellata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
