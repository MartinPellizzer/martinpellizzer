Pelargonium christophoranum

Pelargonium christophoranum, commonly known as the Christoffel's geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, and is known for its attractive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is well-suited to temperate climates.
