Poa montevansii

Poa montevansii, commonly known as the Montevansii bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Poa montevansii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
