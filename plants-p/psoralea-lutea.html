Psoralea lutea

Psoralea lutea, commonly known as the yellow psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Psoralea lutea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
