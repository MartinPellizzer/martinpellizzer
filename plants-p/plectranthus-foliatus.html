Plectranthus foliatus

Plectranthus foliatus, commonly known as the silver bush or silverleaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to blue in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and medicinal plant collections.
