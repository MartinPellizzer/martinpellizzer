Palmijuncus periacanthus

Palmijuncus periacanthus, commonly known as the red-fruited palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, red berries that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
