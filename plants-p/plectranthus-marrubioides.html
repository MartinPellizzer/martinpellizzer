Plectranthus marrubioides

Plectranthus marrubioides, commonly known as African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often resembling those of true violets. Its leaves are typically velvety and heart-shaped, with a distinctive grayish-green color.

Plectranthus marrubioides is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
