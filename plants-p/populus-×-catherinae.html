Populus × catherinae

Populus × catherinae, commonly known as the Catherina poplar, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Populus balsamifera and Populus deltoides.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Populus genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions for landscaping and ecological restoration.
