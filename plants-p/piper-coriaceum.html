Piper coriaceum

Piper coriaceum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
