Piper mayanum

Piper mayanum, commonly known as Mayan pepper or wild chili, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and pungent, spicy fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a distinctive aroma.

The fruit, which is a small, yellow or red berry, contains seeds and is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
