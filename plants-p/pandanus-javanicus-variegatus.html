Pandanus javanicus variegatus

Pandanus javanicus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated pandanus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, strap-like leaves that exhibit a distinctive variegation pattern of green and white or yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its foliage is often used in basket weaving and other traditional crafts due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in humid, coastal environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
