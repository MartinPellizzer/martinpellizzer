Poa acroleuca f. submoniliformis

Poa acroleuca f. submoniliformis, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, often pale green blades.

It typically forms dense mats or clumps, with narrow, slightly compressed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is usually short and compact, bearing small, two-ranked spikelets. This variety is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
