Pieris esquirolii var. leucocalyx

Pieris esquirolii var. leucocalyx, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
