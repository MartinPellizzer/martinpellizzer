Potentilla argentea var. macrotoma

Potentilla argentea var. macrotoma, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along streambanks. It has a low-growing habit with multiple stems and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
