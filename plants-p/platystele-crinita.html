Platystele crinita

Platystele crinita, commonly known as the hairy leafless orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant native to the cloud forests of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its lack of leaves and its unique, hairy stems. Its flowers are typically small and white or pale green, with a distinctive, spurred shape.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, Platystele crinita is considered a rare and endangered species.
