Phacelia tanacetifolia subvar. tenuisecta

Phacelia tanacetifolia subvar. tenuisecta, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
