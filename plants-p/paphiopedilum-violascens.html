Paphiopedilum violascens

Paphiopedilum violascens, commonly known as the violet-lilac lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique, pouch-like lip that is typically violet or lilac in color, which is a defining characteristic of the genus Paphiopedilum. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
