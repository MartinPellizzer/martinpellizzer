Puccinellia parishii

Puccinellia parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system.

Puccinellia parishii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
