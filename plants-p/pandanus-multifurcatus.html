Pandanus multifurcatus

Pandanus multifurcatus, commonly known as the multi-branched pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, coconut-like fruits. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to grow in wet, swampy environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
