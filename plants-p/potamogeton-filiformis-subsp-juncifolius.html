Potamogeton filiformis subsp. juncifolius

Potamogeton filiformis subsp.

juncifolius, commonly known as slender water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

The plant has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense spikes and appear during the summer months.

This subspecies is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
