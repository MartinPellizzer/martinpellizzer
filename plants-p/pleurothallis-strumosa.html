Pleurothallis strumosa

Pleurothallis strumosa, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiar inflorescence, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spike that bears small, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Pleurothallis strumosa is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
