Phyllanthus lamprophyllus

Phyllanthus lamprophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leaf phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, lance-shaped leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
