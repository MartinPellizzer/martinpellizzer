Pogonophora cunuri

Pogonophora cunuri, commonly known as the Brazilian beard lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a distinctive appearance with a leathery, dark green to blackish upper surface and a lighter, often whitish underside. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provide photosynthetic capabilities, while the fungal component offers structural support and protection. It is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in humid environments.

Its slow growth and unique morphology make it an important indicator species for ecological health.
