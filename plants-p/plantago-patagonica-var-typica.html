Plantago patagonica var. typica

Plantago patagonica var. typica, commonly known as Patagonian plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
