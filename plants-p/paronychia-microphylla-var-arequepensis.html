Paronychia microphylla var. arequepensis

Paronychia microphylla var. arequepensis, commonly known as the Arequipa cushion plant, is a low-growing succulent native to the coastal areas of southern Peru.

It features dense, compact growth with small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its compact form and drought tolerance make it an attractive choice for xeriscaping.
