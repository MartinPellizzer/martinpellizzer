Pyrrothrix ruficapillis

Pyrrothrix ruficapillis, commonly known as the red-headed hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or brown at the base. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain spikelets with two to three florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Pyrrothrix ruficapillis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
