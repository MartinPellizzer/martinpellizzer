Parkia parvifoliola

Parkia parvifoliola, commonly known as the small-leaf parkia, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually pinnately arranged with small, elliptical leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by flat, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
