Potentilla fragiformis var. parviflora

Potentilla fragiformis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
