Panicum junceum var. subnutans

Panicum junceum var. subnutans, commonly known as the drooping panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets that hang downward.

This variety is often found in the southeastern United States and is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.
