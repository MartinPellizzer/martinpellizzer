Paepalanthus paludosus

Paepalanthus paludosus, commonly known as the swamp milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like butterflies and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Paepalanthus paludosus plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
