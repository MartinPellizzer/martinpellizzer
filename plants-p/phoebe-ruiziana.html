Phoebe ruiziana

Phoebe ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's phoebe, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Caribbean.

It typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in coastal areas or near water bodies. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
