Pepinia pruinosa

Pepinia pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, fuzzy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pepinia pruinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
