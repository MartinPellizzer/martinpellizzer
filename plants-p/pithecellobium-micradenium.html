Pithecellobium micradenium

Pithecellobium micradenium, commonly known as the small-fruited acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in tough, leathery pods, which can remain on the plant for extended periods.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
