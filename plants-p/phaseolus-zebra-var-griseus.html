Phaseolus zebra var. griseus

Phaseolus zebra var. griseus, commonly known as the gray zebra bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its distinctive mottled or striped seed coat, which displays a unique combination of gray and green patterns. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and pubescent.

The flowers are usually white or pale purple, and the pods contain several kidney-shaped seeds that are harvested when mature.
