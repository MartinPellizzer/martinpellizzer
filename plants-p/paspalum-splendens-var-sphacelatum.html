Paspalum splendens var. sphacelatum

Paspalum splendens var. sphacelatum, commonly known as the velvet paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-green foliage with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

This variant is particularly valued for its ability to tolerate both drought and moderate shade.
