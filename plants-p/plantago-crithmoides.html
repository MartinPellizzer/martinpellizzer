Plantago crithmoides

Plantago crithmoides, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and ribbed, with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along trails.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on skin irritations.
