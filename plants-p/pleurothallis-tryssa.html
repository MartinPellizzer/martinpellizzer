Pleurothallis tryssa

Pleurothallis tryssa, commonly known as the Tryssa orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is part of the Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
