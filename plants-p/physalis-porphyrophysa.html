Physalis porphyrophysa

Physalis porphyrophysa, commonly known as the purple husk tomato, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple calyces that encase the small, orange-red berries. The plant produces clusters of these berries, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are hairy.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.
