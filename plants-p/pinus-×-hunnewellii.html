Pinus × hunnewellii

Pinus × hunnewellii, commonly known as the Hunnewell pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus contorta and Pinus banksiana.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of the pine family. The tree exhibits a pyramidal shape with a dense crown, and its bark becomes fissured with age. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and temperate forest regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
