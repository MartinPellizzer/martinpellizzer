Phyllanthus banksii

Phyllanthus banksii, commonly known as the Australian sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit and features small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Phyllanthus banksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
