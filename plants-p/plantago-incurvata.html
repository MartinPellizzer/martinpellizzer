Plantago incurvata

Plantago incurvata, commonly known as the curled plantain, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has a rosette of broad, wavy-edged leaves that curl inward, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in a spike and bloom in the late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
