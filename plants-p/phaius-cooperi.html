Phaius cooperi

Phaius cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's orchid, is a tropical terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Phaius cooperi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
