Pelargonium whytei

Pelargonium whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge. This species thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
