Phaseolus vulgaris var. atrofasciatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. atrofasciatus, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a variety of common bean native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, compound leaves and pods that mature to a dark brown color. The plant produces small, kidney-shaped seeds that are typically black or dark brown in color. It is a warm-season crop that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
