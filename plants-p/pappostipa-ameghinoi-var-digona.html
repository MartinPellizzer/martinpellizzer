Pappostipa ameghinoi var. digona

Pappostipa ameghinoi var. digona, commonly known as the Patagonian grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three bract-like structures at the base. Its leaves are narrow and usually have a bluish-green color, and it thrives in dry, open habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils in arid and semi-arid environments.
