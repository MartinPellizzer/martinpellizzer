Pulsatilla rubra subsp. hispanica

Pulsatilla rubra subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with a distinctive, fuzzy center. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.
