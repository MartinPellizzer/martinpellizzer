Phyllanthus retroversus

Phyllanthus retroversus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various animals.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
