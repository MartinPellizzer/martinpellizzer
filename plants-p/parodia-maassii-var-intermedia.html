Parodia maassii var. intermedia

Parodia maassii var. intermedia, commonly known as the "Cristata" or "Cristate" barrel cactus, is a rare and striking variant of the Parodia maassii species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often ribbed stems that form a rounded, barrel-like shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a vibrant contrast to its green foliage. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
