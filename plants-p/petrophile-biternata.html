Petrophile biternata

Petrophile biternata, commonly known as the Three-lobed Burro's Tail, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland heathlands.
