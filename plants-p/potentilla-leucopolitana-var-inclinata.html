Potentilla leucopolitana var. inclinata

Potentilla leucopolitana var. inclinata, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
