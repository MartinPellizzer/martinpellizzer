Pachiloma

Pachiloma, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in spikes, and can range in color from white to pink.

Pachiloma thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
