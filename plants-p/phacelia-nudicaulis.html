Phacelia nudicaulis

Phacelia nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stem phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It blooms in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Phacelia nudicaulis is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
