Pygeum ceylanicum

Pygeum ceylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon almond tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, almond-like fruits. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and a tendency to form dense, compact growth.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
