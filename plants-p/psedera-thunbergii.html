Psedera thunbergii

Psedera thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
