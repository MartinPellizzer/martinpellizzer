Psittacanthus acuminatus

Psittacanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its tree-like appearance with a central stem and multiple fronds. The plant has a distinctive rosette of fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into long, narrow leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
