Pingraea scandens

Pingraea scandens, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing stems that can climb or trail along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Pingraea scandens thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in woodland gardens and wetlands.
