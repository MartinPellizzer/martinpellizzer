Parodia mammulosa subsp. brasiliensis

Parodia mammulosa subsp.

brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil. It features a spherical or slightly elongated stem covered in dense, white to pale yellow spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
