Phthirusa loranthoidea

Phthirusa loranthoidea, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Phthirusa, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using limited chlorophyll. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in attachment to new hosts. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in dense forests.

Its life cycle involves both asexual reproduction through vegetative growth and sexual reproduction through bird-dispersed seeds.
