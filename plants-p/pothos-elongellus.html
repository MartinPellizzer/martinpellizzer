Pothos elongellus

Pothos elongellus, commonly known as the satin pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is more valued for its ornamental foliage than its blooms. It thrives in indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its lush appearance.

Pothos elongellus is often used in hanging baskets or as a trailing plant due to its ability to grow long, flexible stems.
