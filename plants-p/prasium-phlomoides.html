Prasium phlomoides

Prasium phlomoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Prasium phlomoides is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is known for its delicate, lance-shaped leaves.
