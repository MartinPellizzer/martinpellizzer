Plantago canescens

Plantago canescens, commonly known as hoary plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces upright flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pubescence and adaptive growth patterns.
