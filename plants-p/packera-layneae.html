Packera layneae

Packera layneae, commonly known as Layne's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. This perennial herb typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
