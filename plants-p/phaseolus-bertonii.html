Phaseolus bertonii

Phaseolus bertonii, commonly known as the Berton's bean, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often grown for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
