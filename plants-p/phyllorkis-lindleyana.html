Phyllorkis lindleyana

Phyllorkis lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's phyllorkis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.
