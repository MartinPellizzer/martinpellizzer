Polypodium mannianum

Polypodium mannianum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a terrestrial fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds are usually leathery and have a distinctive, segmented appearance.

Polypodium mannianum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
