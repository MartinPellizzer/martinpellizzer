Prunus serrulata f. singularis

Prunus serrulata f. singularis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a unique flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is a naturally occurring form of Prunus serrulata, characterized by its single, prominent bloom on each branch rather than the typical clustered flowers. The plant is renowned for its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a symbol of transience and beauty in Japanese culture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and cultural celebrations.
