Polyosma fulva

Polyosma fulva, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves contain volatile oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

Polyosma fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in its native habitat.
