Pithecellobium affine

Pithecellobium affine, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a velvety appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and the fruit is a flattened pod containing edible seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds and foliage.
