Populus × krauseana

Populus × krauseana, commonly known as the hybrid poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Populus, which includes species such as aspen, balm of Gilead, and cottonwood. This plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and catkin flowers, which are typical of the Populus family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Populus × krauseana is valued for its fast growth and adaptability, making it useful for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
