Pimelea orthia subsp. orthia

Pimelea orthia subsp.

orthia, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, exhibiting a compact, bushy form.

The plant produces small, yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive grey-green coloration.

It is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
