Prunus jamasakura f. superflua

Prunus jamasakura f. superflua, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to Japan, and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture, and it produces small, edible fruits that are not typically consumed by humans.

This cultivar is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in Japanese traditions.
