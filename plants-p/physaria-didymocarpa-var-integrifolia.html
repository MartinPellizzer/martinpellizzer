Physaria didymocarpa var. integrifolia

Physaria didymocarpa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Brassicaceae family.
