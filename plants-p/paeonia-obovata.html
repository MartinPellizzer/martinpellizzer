Paeonia obovata

Paeonia obovata, commonly known as the Tibetan peony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and northern India.

It features large, showy flowers with five to eight petals that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant has thick, fleshy rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

The botanical characteristics of Paeonia obovata make it a unique and valuable species in both horticultural and medicinal contexts.
