Parietaria debilis subvar. hyalina

Parietaria debilis subvar. hyalina, commonly known as the silver groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically features small, pale green to silver-colored leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies, though its medicinal properties remain understudied.
