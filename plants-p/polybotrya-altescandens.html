Polybotrya altescandens

Polybotrya altescandens, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are typically broad and serrated, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Due to its unique habitat and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
