Phacelia amabilis

Phacelia amabilis, commonly known as the sweet phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Phacelia amabilis is a short-lived perennial that plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
