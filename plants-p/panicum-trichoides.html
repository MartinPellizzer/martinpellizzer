Panicum trichoides

Panicum trichoides, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that give it a silky appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it valuable for erosion control in wetland environments.

Silky panicgrass is also an important food source for various wildlife species, including waterfowl and small mammals.
