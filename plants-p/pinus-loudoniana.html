Pinus loudoniana

Pinus loudoniana, commonly known as the Loudon pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that grow in clusters of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds enclosed in woody scales. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in upland forests.

The Loudon pine is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
