Pentas glabrescens

Pentas glabrescens, commonly known as the velvetflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 3 meters tall, with opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy when young. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological value in the region.
