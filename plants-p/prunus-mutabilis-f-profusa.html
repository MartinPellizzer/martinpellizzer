Prunus mutabilis f. profusa

Prunus mutabilis f. profusa, commonly known as the profuse-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its abundant flowering.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for horticultural use.

The flowers gradually change color from white to pink as they age, adding visual interest throughout the blooming season.
