Pyrethrum

Pyrethrum, commonly known as chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant contains natural insecticides called pyrethrins, which are effective against a wide range of pests. Pyrethrum is widely cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of organic pesticides.

Its botanical properties make it a valuable plant in horticulture and agriculture.
