Penstemon whitedii subsp. dayanus

Penstemon whitedii subsp.

dayanus, commonly known as the White Mountains penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial with showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds via wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
