Physalis arborescens

Physalis arborescens, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden berry, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features hairy, branching stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red calyces enclosing the fruit. The fruit is a small, egg-shaped berry with a sweet, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has culinary uses in various cuisines.
