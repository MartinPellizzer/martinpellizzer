Pisum sativum var. waterlooensis

Pisum sativum var. waterlooensis, commonly known as the Waterloo pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded pods compared to typical garden peas. The plant produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional culinary practices. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is notable for its historical significance in botanical studies and its adaptation to specific regional growing conditions.
