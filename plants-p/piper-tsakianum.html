Piper tsakianum

Piper tsakianum, commonly known as the Tsakian pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, green berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper tsakianum make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
