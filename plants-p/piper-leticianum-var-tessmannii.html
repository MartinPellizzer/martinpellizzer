Piper leticianum var. tessmannii

Piper leticianum var. tessmannii, commonly known as the Tessmann's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
