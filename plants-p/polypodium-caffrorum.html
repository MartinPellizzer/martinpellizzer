Polypodium caffrorum

Polypodium caffrorum, commonly known as the African wall fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows on rocky outcrops or in shaded forest areas. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fronds that are pinnately divided and covered in small, rounded pinnae. Its leaves are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Polypodium caffrorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, fern-like appearance.
