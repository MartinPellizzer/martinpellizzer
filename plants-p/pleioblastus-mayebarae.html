Pleioblastus mayebarae

Pleioblastus mayebarae, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage that adds a delicate texture to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile ornamental plant.

Its clumping growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it popular in both traditional and modern landscaping.
