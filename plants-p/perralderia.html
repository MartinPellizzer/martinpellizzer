Perralderia

Perralderia, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its enormous, round, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant's flowers are showy, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of shallow waters, while its leaves provide shelter for various aquatic life.

Perralderia plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water filtration.
