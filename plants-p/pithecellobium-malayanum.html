Pithecellobium malayanum

Pithecellobium malayanum, commonly known as the Malayan legume or Malayan acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
