Piper clavibracteum

Piper clavibracteum, commonly known as the narrow-bracted pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark purple color, often containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and growth habit.
