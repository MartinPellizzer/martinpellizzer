Phaseolus glabellus

Phaseolus glabellus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and long, edible pods. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the seeds inside the pods are often used in culinary applications.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and nutritional content.
