Paeonia obovata subsp. willmottiae

Paeonia obovata subsp.

willmottiae, commonly known as the Willmott's peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a distinctive red eye.

The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are obovate in shape, giving it a lush, foliage-filled appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
