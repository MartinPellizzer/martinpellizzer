Pyrus obtusa

Pyrus obtusa, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves that are often slightly hairy beneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries that are used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.
