Pleurothallis caligularis

Pleurothallis caligularis, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale green, often with a distinctive silver or metallic sheen on the petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Pleurothallis caligularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
