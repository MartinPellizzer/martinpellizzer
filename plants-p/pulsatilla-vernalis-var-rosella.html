Pulsatilla vernalis var. rosella

Pulsatilla vernalis var. rosella, commonly known as the spring pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem that supports its single, large, cup-shaped flower. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, and the plant produces follicles that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.
