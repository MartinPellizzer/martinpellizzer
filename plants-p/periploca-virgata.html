Periploca virgata

Periploca virgata, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Periploca virgata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
