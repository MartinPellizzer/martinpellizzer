Polygala gariodiana

Polygala gariodiana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Polygala.
