Piqueria quinqueflora

Piqueria quinqueflora, commonly known as the fiveflower piqueria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a low shrub with gray-green foliage and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Piqueria quinqueflora plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
