Pleurothallis pristeoglossa

Pleurothallis pristeoglossa, commonly known as the golden-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in loose inflorescences, and the species is noted for its striking, golden-orange lip.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
