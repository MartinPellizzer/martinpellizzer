Primula cortusoides var. grandiflora

Primula cortusoides var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its delicate appearance and historical significance.
