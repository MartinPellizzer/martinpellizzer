Pyrus aquosa

Pyrus aquosa, commonly known as the water pear, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that resemble pears. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and horticultural settings.
