Pleurothallis tanyrhina

Pleurothallis tanyrhina, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green blooms that are clustered in racemes, often with a subtle golden hue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
