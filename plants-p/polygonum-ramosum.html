Polygonum ramosum

Polygonum ramosum, commonly known as the prostrate knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats. The plant has slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, giving it a prostrate appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are typically borne on short pedicels.

Polygonum ramosum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in moist, shady environments.
