Pistacia chinensis subsp. chinensis

Pistacia chinensis subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese pistache, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The tree produces small, orange-red drupes that are edible but often have a mild, tangy flavor. It is valued for its ornamental qualities, including its vibrant fall foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

This species is also used in traditional medicine and has ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife.
