Panicum corymbosum

Panicum corymbosum, commonly known as switchgrass, is a native North American grass species valued for its ecological and agricultural significance.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in open woodlands and prairie habitats. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces dense, branching clusters of small, fluffy seeds. Switchgrass is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also an important species for biofuel production and wildlife habitat.
