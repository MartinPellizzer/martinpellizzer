Podolepis rugata subsp. trullata

Podolepis rugata subsp.

trullata, commonly known as the roughleaf podolepis, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand dry and saline conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
