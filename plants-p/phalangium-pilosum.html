Phalangium pilosum

Phalangium pilosum, commonly known as the hairy fingerling, is a species of millipede characterized by its elongated, segmented body covered in fine, soft hairs.

It belongs to the order Polydesmida and is typically found in moist, forested environments where it thrives in leaf litter and soil. The plant-like appearance of its body, with numerous legs and a segmented structure, can sometimes lead to confusion with true plants.

While not a plant itself, it plays a vital role in soil aeration and nutrient cycling within its ecosystem.
