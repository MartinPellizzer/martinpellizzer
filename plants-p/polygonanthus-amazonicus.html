Polygonanthus amazonicus

Polygonanthus amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian polygonanthus, is a flowering plant native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible seeds. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of the Amazon basin.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in tropical ecosystems.
