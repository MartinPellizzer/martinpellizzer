Pandanus porrittianus

Pandanus porrittianus, commonly known as the Porritt's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, windy conditions. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds.

The fruit, which is a fibrous structure, contains seeds that are dispersed by water or animals.
