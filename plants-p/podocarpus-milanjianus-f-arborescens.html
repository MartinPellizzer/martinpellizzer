Podocarpus milanjianus f. arborescens

Podocarpus milanjianus f. arborescens, commonly known as the silver pine, is a variant of the podocarpus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
