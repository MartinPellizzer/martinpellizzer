Psephellus pergamaceus

Psephellus pergamaceus, commonly known as the parchment leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, waxy leaves that resemble parchment, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
