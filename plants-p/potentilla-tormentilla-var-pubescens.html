Potentilla tormentilla var. pubescens

Potentilla tormentilla var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has hairy, pubescent stems and leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
