Piper hispidum var. gamboanum

Piper hispidum var. gamboanum, commonly known as the Gamboanum pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its leaves are also used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
