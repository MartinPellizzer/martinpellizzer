Pulsatilla × Wilczekii

Pulsatilla × wilczekii is a unique hybrid plant known for its striking, fluffy flower heads. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is often referred to as the "pasque flower" in some regions. This plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its name honors the botanist Wilczek, who contributed significantly to its study. The plant's beauty and resilience make it a popular choice among gardeners and conservationists alike.
