Plantago coronopus var. filiformis

Plantago coronopus var. filiformis, commonly known as slender plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. This plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued in ecological contexts for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
