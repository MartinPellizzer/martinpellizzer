Plinia haitiensis

Plinia haitiensis, commonly known as the Haitian guava, is a species native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It is a small evergreen tree belonging to the Myrtaceae family, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, aromatic skin with a sweet, juicy pulp.

It is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
