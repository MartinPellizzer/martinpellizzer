Pseudochirita trifoliata

Pseudochirita trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf piper, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a Y-shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
