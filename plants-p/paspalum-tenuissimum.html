Paspalum tenuissimum

Paspalum tenuissimum, commonly known as slender false brome, is a low-growing grass species native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to survive in arid and semi-arid regions.
