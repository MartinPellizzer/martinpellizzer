Poa lipskyi var. kungeica

Poa lipskyi var. kungeica, commonly known as the Kungei meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets. This variety thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
