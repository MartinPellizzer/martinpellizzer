Plumeria articulata

Plumeria articulata, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and pink. The plant has palm-like leaves and a branched, woody stem that often appears segmented. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Plumeria articulata is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
