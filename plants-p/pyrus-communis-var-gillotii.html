Pyrus communis var. gillotii

Pyrus communis var. gillotii, commonly known as the Gillotii pear, is a variety of the common pear that exhibits distinct botanical features.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped fruits with a smooth, yellowish-brown skin that develops a slight russeting as it matures. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, typical of the Pyrus genus. The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are clustered in inflorescences.

It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
