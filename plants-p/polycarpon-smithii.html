Polycarpon smithii

Polycarpon smithii, commonly known as Smith's bladderwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures called bladders, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms. These bladders are attached to thin, branching stems and are often submerged in water.

Polycarpon smithii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microscopic life.
