Phlox speciosa var. whitedii

Phlox speciosa var. whitedii, commonly known as the White's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
