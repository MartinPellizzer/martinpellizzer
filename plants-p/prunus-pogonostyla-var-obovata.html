Prunus pogonostyla var. obovata

Prunus pogonostyla var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its obovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slight pubescent underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart cherries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
