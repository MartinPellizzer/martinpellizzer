Pleurothallis cogniauxiana

Pleurothallis cogniauxiana, commonly known as the Cogniaux's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
