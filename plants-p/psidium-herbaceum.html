Psidium herbaceum

Psidium herbaceum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, bright red berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
