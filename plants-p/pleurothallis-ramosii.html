Pleurothallis ramosii

Pleurothallis ramosii, commonly known as the branched pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
