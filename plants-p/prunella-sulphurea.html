Prunella sulphurea

Prunella sulphurea, commonly known as yellow dead-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves with a serrated edge. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, and it typically blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Prunella sulphurea is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
