Pterocelastrus rehmannii

Pterocelastrus rehmannii, commonly known as the Chinese winged vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, winged fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, winged berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.

Pterocelastrus rehmannii is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
