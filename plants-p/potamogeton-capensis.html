Potamogeton capensis

Potamogeton capensis, commonly known as the Cape pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands.

Potamogeton capensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
