Polydora angustifolia

Polydora angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf polydora, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in width. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
