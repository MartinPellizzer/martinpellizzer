Primulina atroglandulosa

Primulina atroglandulosa, commonly known as the dark glandular primula, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by specific insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
