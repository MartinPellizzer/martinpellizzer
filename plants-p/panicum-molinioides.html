Panicum molinioides

Panicum molinioides, commonly known as millet grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Panicum molinioides is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
