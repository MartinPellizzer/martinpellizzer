Pouzolzia indica

Pouzolzia indica, commonly known as Indian pouzolzia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

Pouzolzia indica is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
