Phacelia fimbriata var. boykinii

Phacelia fimbriata var. boykinii, commonly known as the Boykin's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
