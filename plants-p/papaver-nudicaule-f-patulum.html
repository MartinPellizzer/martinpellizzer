Papaver nudicaule f. patulum

Papaver nudicaule f. patulum, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, pale pink to white flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elongated seeds. Its foliage consists of alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
