Pastinaca viscidula

Pastinaca viscidula, commonly known as the sticky burdock.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, spiky flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
