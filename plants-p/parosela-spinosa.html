Parosela spinosa

Parosela spinosa, commonly known as the spiny parosela, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves, which provide a natural defense against herbivores. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
