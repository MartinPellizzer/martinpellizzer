Penstemon breviculus

Penstemon breviculus, commonly known as the shortstem penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features upright, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in native gardens.
