Plantago coronopus subvar. minor

Plantago coronopus subvar. minor, commonly known as lesser plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense, rosette-like foliage composed of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
