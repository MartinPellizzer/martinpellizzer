Platanus occidentalis var. hispanica

Platanus occidentalis var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish plane tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces spherical, spiky fruit clusters that contain small, winged seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often planted in urban areas for its shade and ornamental value.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance throughout the year.
