Parodia camblayoides

Parodia camblayoides, commonly known as the Camblayoides cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters and has a rounded, almost spherical shape. Its flowers are yellow with red or orange markings, appearing in late summer.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
