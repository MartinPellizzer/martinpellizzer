Petunia violacea var. hirta

Petunia violacea var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of purple and violet. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators.

The hairy texture of its leaves is a distinguishing feature that helps in identifying the species.
