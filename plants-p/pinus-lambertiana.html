Pinus lambertiana

Pinus lambertiana, commonly known as the sugar pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive, often drooping cones. The tree can grow up to 200 feet tall and has a trunk that can reach diameters of over 5 feet. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against forest fires.

The sugar pine is also notable for its large, edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife species.
