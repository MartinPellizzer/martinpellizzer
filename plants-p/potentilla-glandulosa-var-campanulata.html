Potentilla glandulosa var. campanulata

Potentilla glandulosa var. campanulata, commonly known as the campanulata cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
