Polycodium stamineum var. virginianum

Polycodium stamineum var. virginianum, commonly known as the Virginia polycodium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and distinctive, triangular leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and the presence of silica-based structures called phytoliths in its tissues.

This variant is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
