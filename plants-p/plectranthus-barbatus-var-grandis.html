Plectranthus barbatus var. grandis

Plectranthus barbatus var. grandis, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, mint-like fragrance. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and as a natural insect repellent.
