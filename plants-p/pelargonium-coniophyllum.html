Pelargonium coniophyllum

Pelargonium coniophyllum, commonly known as the conical geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive conical inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a rough texture, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium coniophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
