Porpax compacta

Porpax compacta, commonly known as the compact porpax, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, dense growth habit and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in wet soils.

Porpax compacta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
