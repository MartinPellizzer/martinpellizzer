Pterygota aubrevillei

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that exhibit a pinnate structure with visible wing-like projections along the leaf margins. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that are clustered in sori, typical of ferns.

The botanical features of Pterygota aubrevillei make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and evolutionary botany.
