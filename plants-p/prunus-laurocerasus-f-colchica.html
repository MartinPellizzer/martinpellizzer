Prunus laurocerasus f. colchica

Prunus laurocerasus f. colchica, commonly known as the Colchic cherry laurel, is a variant of the cherry laurel species native to the Caucasus region.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its slow growth rate and dense foliage make it suitable for hedges and topiary.
