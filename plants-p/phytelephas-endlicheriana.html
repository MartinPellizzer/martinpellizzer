Phytelephas endlicheriana

Phytelephas endlicheriana, commonly known as the Amazonian peanut palm, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its botanical significance lies in its role within the ecological systems of the Amazon and its potential for agricultural use.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
