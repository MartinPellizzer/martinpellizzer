Phaseolus leptostachyus f. purpureus

Phaseolus leptostachyus f. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-leaf scarlet runner bean, is a botanical variant of the scarlet runner bean.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage, which contrasts with the typical green leaves of the species. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible, elongated pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible beans.

This variety is valued in both horticulture and agriculture for its aesthetic appeal and nutritional content.
