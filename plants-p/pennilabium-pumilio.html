Pennilabium pumilio

Pennilabium pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under the canopy of rainforests. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Pennilabium pumilio is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
