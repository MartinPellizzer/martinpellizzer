Phyllanthus swartzii

Phyllanthus swartzii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and savannah regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
