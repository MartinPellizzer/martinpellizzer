Phyllanthus nigericus

Phyllanthus nigericus, commonly known as the black nightshade or African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that turn red when mature. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
