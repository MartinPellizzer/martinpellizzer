Pelargonium × colleyanum

Pelargonium × colleyanum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Pelargonium × colleyanum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for hanging baskets and containers.
