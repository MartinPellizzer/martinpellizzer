Panicum striatum

Panicum striatum, commonly known as striped foxtail, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-tan stripe running along the central vein of its leaves. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are often mistaken for wheat or other grasses. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread distribution and ecological significance.
