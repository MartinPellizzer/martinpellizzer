Patrinia graveolens

Patrinia graveolens, commonly known as Chinese dropwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Patrinia graveolens is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
