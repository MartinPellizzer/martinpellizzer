Potentilla pulchella var. elatior

Potentilla pulchella var. elatior, commonly known as the tall potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
