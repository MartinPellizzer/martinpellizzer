Polygala longeracemosa var. retamoides

Polygala longeracemosa var. retamoides, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green to silver-colored leaves. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
