Pterochaete mucronifolia

Pterochaete mucronifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pterochaete, is a flowering plant species native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy habitats, often growing in disturbed or open areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
