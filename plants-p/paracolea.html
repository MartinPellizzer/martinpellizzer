Paracolea

Paracolea, commonly known as the spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread easily. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by berries that turn red when mature.

Paracolea is well adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
