Pelargonium × dendriticum

Pelargonium × dendriticum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
