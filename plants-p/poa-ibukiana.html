Poa ibukiana

Poa ibukiana, commonly known as the Bukian grass, is a species of grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa ibukiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
