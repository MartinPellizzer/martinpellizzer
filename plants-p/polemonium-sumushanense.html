Polemonium sumushanense

Polemonium sumushanense, commonly known as the Korean garden bell, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus within the family Polemoniaceae, which includes several species of flowering plants. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green hue.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
