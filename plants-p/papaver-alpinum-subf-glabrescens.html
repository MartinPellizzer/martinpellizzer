Papaver alpinum subf. glabrescens

Papaver alpinum subf.

glabrescens, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange petals that resemble those of the common poppy.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
