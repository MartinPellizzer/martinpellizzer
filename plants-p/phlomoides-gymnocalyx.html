Phlomoides gymnocalyx

Phlomoides gymnocalyx, commonly known as the narrowleaf phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in dense spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
