Phyllostachys nigra var. flavescens

Phyllostachys nigra var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden bamboo or black bamboo, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking combination of dark green culms that develop a golden hue as they mature. The plant has long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense stands, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
