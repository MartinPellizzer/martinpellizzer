Parosela astragalina

Parosela astragalina, commonly known as the Greek milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its roots are known to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Parosela astragalina is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along coastal areas.
