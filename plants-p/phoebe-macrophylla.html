Phoebe macrophylla

Phoebe macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf phoebe, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by round, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal forests and lowland areas.

Its foliage and fruit make it an important component of local ecosystems.
