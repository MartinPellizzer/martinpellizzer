Phlox stansburyi

Phlox stansburyi, commonly known as the desert phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 inches. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
