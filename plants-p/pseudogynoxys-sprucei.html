Pseudogynoxys sprucei

Pseudogynoxys sprucei, commonly known as the Mexican false holly, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky environments. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellowish-green and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Pseudogynoxys sprucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
