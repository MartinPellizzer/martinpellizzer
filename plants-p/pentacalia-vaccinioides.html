Pentacalia vaccinioides

Pentacalia vaccinioides, commonly known as the Florida holly or scrub holly, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Its adaptability to harsh, sunny conditions makes it a notable species in xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
