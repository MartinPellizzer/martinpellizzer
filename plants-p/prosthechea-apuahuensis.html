Prosthechea apuahuensis

Prosthechea apuahuensis, commonly known as the Apuahua orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, branching growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Prosthechea apuahuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
