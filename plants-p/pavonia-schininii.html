Pavonia schininii

Pavonia schininii, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and clusters of vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its flowers are arranged in dense, showy inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pavonia schininii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
