Pilea beguinotii

Pilea beguinotii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features round, waxy leaves that resemble coins, which are typically a vibrant green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
