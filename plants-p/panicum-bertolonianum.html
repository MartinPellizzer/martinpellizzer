Panicum bertolonianum

Panicum bertolonianum, commonly known as the Bertoloni Panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it thrive in arid environments.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or ornamental purposes.
