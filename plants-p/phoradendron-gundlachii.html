Phoradendron gundlachii

Phoradendron gundlachii, commonly known as the Florida mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the southeastern United States.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while producing its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using its green stems. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain sticky seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the ecological dynamics of its host trees.
