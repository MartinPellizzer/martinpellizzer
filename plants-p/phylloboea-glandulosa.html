Phylloboea glandulosa

Phylloboea glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky glands. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
