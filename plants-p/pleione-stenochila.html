Pleione stenochila

Pleione stenochila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pleione, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with white petals and a yellow lip. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers typically bloom in late winter or early spring, adding a touch of elegance to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
