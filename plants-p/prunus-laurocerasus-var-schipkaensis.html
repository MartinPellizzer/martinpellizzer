Prunus laurocerasus var. schipkaensis

Prunus laurocerasus var. schipkaensis, commonly known as the Schipka cherry or Schipka laurel, is a variant of the cherry laurel shrub native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is a hardy, evergreen shrub that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The variety is distinguished by its slightly smaller size and more compact growth habit compared to the standard Prunus laurocerasus.
