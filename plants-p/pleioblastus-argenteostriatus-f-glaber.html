Pleioblastus argenteostriatus f. glaber

Pleioblastus argenteostriatus f. glaber, commonly known as the silver-striped bamboo, is a variant of the pleioblastus genus native to eastern Asia.

This plant is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery-white sheen. The leaves are typically lighter in color compared to the standard variety, with a distinct silver stripe running along the midrib. It is a hardy, evergreen bamboo that thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
