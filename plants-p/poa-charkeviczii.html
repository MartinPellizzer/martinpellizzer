Poa charkeviczii

Poa charkeviczii, commonly known as Charkevich's bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny blue to purple flowers.

Poa charkeviczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
