Poa versicolor subsp. buriatica

Poa versicolor subsp.

buriatica, commonly known as the Buriatic meadowgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its foliage is usually a mix of green and silver hues, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is noted for its adaptability to varying environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
