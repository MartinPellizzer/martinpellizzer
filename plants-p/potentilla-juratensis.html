Potentilla juratensis

Potentilla juratensis, commonly known as the Jurassian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

Potentilla juratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
