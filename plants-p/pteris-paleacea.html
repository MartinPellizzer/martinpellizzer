Pteris paleacea

Pteris paleacea, commonly known as the pale fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green with a pale underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris paleacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
