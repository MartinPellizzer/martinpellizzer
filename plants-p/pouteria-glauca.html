Pouteria glauca

Pouteria glauca, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, elliptical leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange fruits resembling small mangosteens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacology.
