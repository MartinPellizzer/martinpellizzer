Psidium acutangulum var. acidum

Psidium acutangulum var. acidum, commonly known as the sour guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to fruit with a tangy, acidic flavor. Its fruit is typically harvested when it turns yellow or orange, and it is often used in culinary applications due to its distinctive taste.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
