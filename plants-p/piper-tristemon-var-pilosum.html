Piper tristemon var. pilosum

Piper tristemon var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
