Pandanus blancoi

Pandanus blancoi, commonly known as the White Pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences at the top of the plant. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatch material due to their durability and flexibility.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
