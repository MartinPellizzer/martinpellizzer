Phyteuma linearifolium

Phyteuma linearifolium, commonly known as the slender phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and elongated, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
