Potentilla meifolia

Potentilla meifolia, commonly known as the meadow potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is native to regions in Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Potentilla meifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
