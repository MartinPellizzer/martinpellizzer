Potentilla bicrenata

Potentilla bicrenata, commonly known as the two-crested cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla bicrenata is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
