Piper pogonioneuron

Piper pogonioneuron, commonly known as the long-bearded pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
