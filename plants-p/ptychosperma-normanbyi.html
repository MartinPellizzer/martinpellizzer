Ptychosperma normanbyi

Ptychosperma normanbyi, commonly known as the Normanby's palm, is a tall, slender palm native to northern Australia.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant is dioecious, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red berries. It thrives in wet tropical climates and is often found in rainforests or near water sources.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
