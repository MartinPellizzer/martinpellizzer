Piper discolor var. ovatifolium

Piper discolor var. ovatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper discolor species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that develop into peppery fruits. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
