Plantago australis subsp. supina

Plantago australis subsp.

supina, commonly known as the prostrate plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit, with dense rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like inflorescences that rise above the foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
