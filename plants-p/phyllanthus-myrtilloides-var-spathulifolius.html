Phyllanthus myrtilloides var. spathulifolius

Phyllanthus myrtilloides var. spathulifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed phyllanthus, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to broadly ovate leaves that are often glabrous or sparingly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round, black berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
