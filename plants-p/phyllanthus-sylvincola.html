Phyllanthus sylvincola

Phyllanthus sylvincola, commonly known as the forest phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits.

The botanical features of Phyllanthus sylvincola make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
