Paeonia corallina var. coriifolia

Paeonia corallina var. coriifolia, commonly known as the coral peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from pink to coral in color, often with a white or pale pink center. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a distinctive, coriaceous texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
