Pongamiopsis viguieri

Pongamiopsis viguieri, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its latex sap contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested.
