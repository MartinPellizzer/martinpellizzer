Polygonum lacerum

Polygonum lacerum, commonly known as the fringed polygonum, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive fringed edge. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late summer.

Polygonum lacerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
