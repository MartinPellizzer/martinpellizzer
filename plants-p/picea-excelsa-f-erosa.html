Picea excelsa f. erosa

Picea excelsa f. erosa, commonly known as the erose white spruce, is a distinctive variant of the white spruce species.

It is characterized by its finely toothed or erose leaf margins, which give it a unique appearance compared to the typical white spruce. The plant is a coniferous evergreen, belonging to the family Pinaceae, and is native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. Its foliage is dense and silvery-white, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to specific environmental conditions.
