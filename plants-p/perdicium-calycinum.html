Perdicium calycinum

Perdicium calycinum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Perdicium calycinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
