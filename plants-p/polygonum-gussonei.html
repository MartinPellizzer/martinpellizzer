Polygonum gussonei

Polygonum gussonei, commonly known as the Gussone's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. The plant features upright stems covered with soft, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and have a bluish-green hue. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

Polygonum gussonei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
