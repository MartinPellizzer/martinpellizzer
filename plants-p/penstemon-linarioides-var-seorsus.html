Penstemon linarioides var. seorsus

Penstemon linarioides var. seorsus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
