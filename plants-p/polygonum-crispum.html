Polygonum crispum

Polygonum crispum, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It is known for its aggressive growth habits and can become invasive in non-native environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
