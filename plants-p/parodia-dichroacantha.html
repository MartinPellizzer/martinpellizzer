Parodia dichroacantha

Parodia dichroacantha, commonly known as the two-colored cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a compact, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne in clusters at the top of the plant. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for succulent collectors.
