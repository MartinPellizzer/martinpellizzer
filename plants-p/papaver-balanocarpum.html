Papaver balanocarpum

Papaver balanocarpum, commonly known as the Balkan poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, hairy stems and lobed leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its capsule fruit contains numerous small, black seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Papaver balanocarpum is often found in meadows, forests, and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates.
