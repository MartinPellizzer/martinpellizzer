Piper porrectum

Piper porrectum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by tiny, elliptical berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
