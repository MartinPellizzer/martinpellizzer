Polygala tellezii

Polygala tellezii, commonly known as Tellez's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, characterized by its small, often showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, while its flowers range from white to pinkish in color.

Tellez's milkvetch plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
