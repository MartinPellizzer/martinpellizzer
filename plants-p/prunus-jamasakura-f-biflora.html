Prunus jamasakura f. biflora

Prunus jamasakura f. biflora, commonly known as the double-flowered Japanese cherry, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its distinctive double flowers, which consist of numerous small petals instead of the typical single bloom. This cultivar is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to those of other cherry species.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
