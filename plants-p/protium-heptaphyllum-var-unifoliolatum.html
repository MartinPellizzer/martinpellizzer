Protium heptaphyllum var. unifoliolatum

Protium heptaphyllum var. unifoliolatum, commonly known as the single-leaf persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellow-orange fruits resembling small persimmons. Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf morphology and its role in local ecosystems.

This variant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
