Pomatocalpa armigerum

Pomatocalpa armigerum, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with a distinct lip that attracts pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
