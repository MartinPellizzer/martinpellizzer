Punica granatum var. sanguinea

Punica granatum var. sanguinea, commonly known as the crimson pomegranate, is a cultivar of the pomegranate tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and elliptical, thick-skinned fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also edible, prized for its tart flavor and nutritional value.
