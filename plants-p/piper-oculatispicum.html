Piper oculatispicum

Piper oculatispicum, commonly known as the eye-leaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves that feature a distinct eye-like pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not widely cultivated. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
