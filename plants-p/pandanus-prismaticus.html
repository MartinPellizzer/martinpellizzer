Pandanus prismaticus

Pandanus prismaticus, commonly known as the rainbow pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that range from yellow to red, orange, and purple, creating a vibrant display. The plant has long, narrow, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
