Pedicularis × pseudoasplenifolia

Pedicularis × pseudoasplenifolia, commonly known as the "False Bird's Beak," is a hybrid species within the genus Pedicularis, native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling the beak of a bird. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually basal, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
