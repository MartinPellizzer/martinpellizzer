Pimelea axiflora var. alpina

Pimelea axiflora var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine Heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its rocky or sandy habitats. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds. It is often found in open heathlands and is well-suited to poor, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
