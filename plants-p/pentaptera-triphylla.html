Pentaptera triphylla

Pentaptera triphylla, commonly known as the three-leafed fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

The plant belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to certain regions of South America. Its fronds are typically divided into three parts, giving it the common name. The leaves are delicate and often have a feathery appearance, typical of many fern species.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
