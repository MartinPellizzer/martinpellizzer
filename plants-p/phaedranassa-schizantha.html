Phaedranassa schizantha

Phaedranassa schizantha, commonly known as the white-flowered spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a perennial herb with a short, upright stem and is characterized by its delicate, spider-like appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
