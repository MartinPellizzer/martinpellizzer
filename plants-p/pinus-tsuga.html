Pinus tsuga

Pinus tsuga, commonly known as the hemlock, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its flat, needle-like leaves and its distinctive cone structure. The tree typically grows in moist, cool climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is thin and scaly, and it can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

The plant plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
