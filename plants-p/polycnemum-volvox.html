Polycnemum volvox

Polycnemum volvox, commonly known as the water clover, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its intricate, branching structure that resembles a network of leaves.

It is typically found in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native plants and altering local biodiversity.
