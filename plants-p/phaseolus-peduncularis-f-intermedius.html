Phaseolus peduncularis f. intermedius

Phaseolus peduncularis f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate scarlet runner bean, is a botanical variant of the scarlet runner bean.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and nutritional value.
