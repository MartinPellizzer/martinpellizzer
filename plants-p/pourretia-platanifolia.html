Pourretia platanifolia

Pourretia platanifolia, commonly known as the plane-leaf pourretia, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that resemble those of the plane tree, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora and horticulture.
