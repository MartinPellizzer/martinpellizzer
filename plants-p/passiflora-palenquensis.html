Passiflora palenquensis

Passiflora palenquensis, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a unique arrangement of petals and sepals. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora palenquensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
