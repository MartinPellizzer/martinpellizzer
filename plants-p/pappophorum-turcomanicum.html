Pappophorum turcomanicum

Pappophorum turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential forage plant.
