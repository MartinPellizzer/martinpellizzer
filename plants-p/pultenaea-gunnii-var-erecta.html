Pultenaea gunnii var. erecta

Pultenaea gunnii var. erecta, commonly known as the erect pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellowish to orange flowers in spring, which are followed by small, woody pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
