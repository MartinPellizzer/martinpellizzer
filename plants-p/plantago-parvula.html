Plantago parvula

Plantago parvula, commonly known as small plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, meadows, and wetlands. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, producing a modest amount of seeds.

Botanically, it is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
