Potamogeton subobtusus

Potamogeton subobtusus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

The plant thrives in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands, where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes. Its leaves are usually dark green and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
