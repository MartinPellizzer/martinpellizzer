Phaseolus candidus subf. intermedius

Phaseolus candidus subf.

intermedius, commonly known as the white bean or white phaseolus, is a species within the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with white to pale green foliage.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its pods are typically slender and contain small, white or cream-colored seeds.

This species is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds in some regions.
