Pandanus longifolius

Pandanus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces panicles of small, fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or green in color. Its fibrous leaves are used by local communities for thatching and weaving.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal environments.
