Pyrethrum tanacetum

Pyrethrum tanacetum, commonly known as chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties. Chamomile is widely used in herbal medicine for its calming effects and is also a popular ingredient in teas.

Its essential oils, extracted from the flowers, are known for their insecticidal and antiseptic properties.
