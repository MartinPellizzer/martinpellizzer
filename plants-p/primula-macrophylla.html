Primula macrophylla

Primula macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
