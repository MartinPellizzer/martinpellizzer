Polygonum aridum

Polygonum aridum, commonly known as desert knotweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Polygonum aridum is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low precipitation.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder for livestock.
