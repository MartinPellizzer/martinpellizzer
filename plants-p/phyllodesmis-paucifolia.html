Phyllodesmis paucifolia

Phyllodesmis paucifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved phyllodesmis, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. The plant is characterized by its reduced leaves, which are often modified into phyllodes, functioning as photosynthetic structures. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing pale yellow to pinkish blooms.

Phyllodesmis paucifolia is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
