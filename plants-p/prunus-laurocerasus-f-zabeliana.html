Prunus laurocerasus f. zabeliana

Prunus laurocerasus f. zabeliana, commonly known as the Zabel's laurel cherry, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a leathery texture and emits a mild, sweet fragrance. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its year-round visual interest.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade.
