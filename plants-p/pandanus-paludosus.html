Pandanus paludosus

Pandanus paludosus, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found near coastal areas or in freshwater marshes. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while its fruit is edible and contains a starchy, fibrous pulp.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
