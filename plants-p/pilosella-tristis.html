Pilosella tristis

Pilosella tristis, commonly known as the weeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features wiry, upright stems covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and bloom in clusters, forming a bell-like shape.

Pilosella tristis typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
