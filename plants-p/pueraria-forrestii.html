Pueraria forrestii

Pueraria forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese kudzu, is a vigorous climbing legume native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and often have a heart-shaped base. The plant produces showy, purple or violet pea-like flowers that bloom in racemes. Its roots are tuberous and can grow quite large, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

Pueraria forrestii is closely related to other kudzu species and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
