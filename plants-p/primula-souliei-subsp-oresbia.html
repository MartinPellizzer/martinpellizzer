Primula souliei subsp. oresbia

Primula souliei subsp.

oresbia, commonly known as the Kashmir primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

This subspecies is closely related to other Primula species and shares similar horticultural traits.
