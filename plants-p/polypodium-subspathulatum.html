Polypodium subspathulatum

Polypodium subspathulatum, commonly known as the narrow-sheath huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fronds that are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is short and thick, supporting the growth of its delicate foliage.

Polypodium subspathulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
