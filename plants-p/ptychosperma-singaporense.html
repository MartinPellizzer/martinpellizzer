Ptychosperma singaporense

Ptychosperma singaporense, commonly known as the Singapore cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to Singapore.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in Singapore's conservation efforts.
