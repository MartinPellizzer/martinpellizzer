Plectranthus javanicus

Plectranthus javanicus, commonly known as Java mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to blue in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
