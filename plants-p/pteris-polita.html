Pteris polita

Pteris polita, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant prefers moist, shady environments and is often found near water sources. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies.

Pteris polita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
