Pulsatilla vernalis var. albescens

Pulsatilla vernalis var. albescens, commonly known as the white pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces a distinctive, hairy, and often fragrant flower that blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
