Pedilonum goldschmidtianum

Pedilonum goldschmidtianum, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble stars, with intricate patterns of darker hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
