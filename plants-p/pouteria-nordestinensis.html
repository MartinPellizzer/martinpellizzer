Pouteria nordestinensis

Pouteria nordestinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peach or araçá-do-brejo, is a tropical tree native to northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-colored fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp.

Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies, and the tree is valued for its ornamental and economic significance in local ecosystems.
