Pavonia paucidentata

Pavonia paucidentata, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, showy flowers in shades of red and yellow. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty.

Pavonia paucidentata is often found in moist, open habitats such as roadsides and woodland edges.
