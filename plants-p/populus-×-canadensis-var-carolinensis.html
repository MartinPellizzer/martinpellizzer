Populus × canadensis var. carolinensis

Populus × canadensis var. carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
