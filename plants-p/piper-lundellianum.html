Piper lundellianum

Piper lundellianum, commonly known as the Lundell's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens.
