Plantinia cylindracea

Plantinia cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest aquatic insects, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor environments. The plant has thin, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss. It reproduces both sexually through seeds and asexually through vegetative propagation.

Its unique adaptations make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
