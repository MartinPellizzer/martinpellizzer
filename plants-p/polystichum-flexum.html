Polystichum flexum

Polystichum flexum, commonly known as the flexible wood fern, is a species of fern native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The fronds are pinnately compound, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often curled when young. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium.
