Prunus × donarium f. mikurumakaisi

Prunus × donarium f. mikurumakaisi, commonly known as the Mikurumakaisi cherry, is a unique botanical cultivar within the Prunus genus.

It is a hybrid species, resulting from the cross between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus africana, and is native to Japan. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, dark red leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. It produces small, sweet, and tart fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
