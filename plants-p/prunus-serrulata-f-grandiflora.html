Prunus serrulata f. grandiflora

Prunus serrulata f. grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or yozakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species, native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant is a deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, reddish bark. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are highly valued for their aesthetic appeal in ornamental gardening and traditional festivals.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
