Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. genuinus

Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandmat, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is adapted to thrive in sandy soils with low moisture. Its leaves and stems contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.
