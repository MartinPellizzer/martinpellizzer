Plumbago

Plumbago, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rubiaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Plumbago is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
