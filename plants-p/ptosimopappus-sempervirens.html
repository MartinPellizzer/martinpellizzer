Ptosimopappus sempervirens

Ptosimopappus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen shrub, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
