Potentilla flabellifolia var. lasiocarpa

Potentilla flabellifolia var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the silky leaf potentilla, is a variant of the flabellifolia species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
