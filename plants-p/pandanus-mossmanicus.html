Pandanus mossmanicus

Pandanus mossmanicus, commonly known as the Mossman pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by the development of spiky, fibrous fruits. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize the soil in its natural habitat.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance among Indigenous communities.
