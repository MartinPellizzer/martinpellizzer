Pyrethrum ambiguum

Pyrethrum ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for gardens and landscapes.

Pyrethrum ambiguum is also noted for its potential use in natural pest control due to the presence of pyrethrin compounds in its flowers.
