Potentilla canescens f. sadleri

Potentilla canescens f. sadleri, commonly known as the Sadler's cinquefoil, is a horticultural form of the species Potentilla canescens.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
