Piper apodum

Piper apodum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and shrublands.
