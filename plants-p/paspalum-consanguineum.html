Paspalum consanguineum

Paspalum consanguineum, commonly known as the silver paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, pale blue to silver-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Paspalum consanguineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
