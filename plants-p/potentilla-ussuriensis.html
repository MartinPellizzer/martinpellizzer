Potentilla ussuriensis

Potentilla ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
