Potamogeton japonicus

Potamogeton japonicus, commonly known as Japanese pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

Its rapid growth can lead to dense vegetation, impacting water flow and aquatic ecosystems.
