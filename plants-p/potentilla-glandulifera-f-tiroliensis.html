Potentilla glandulifera f. tiroliensis

Potentilla glandulifera f. tiroliensis, commonly known as the Tirolian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, with glandular dots that give it its scientific name.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
