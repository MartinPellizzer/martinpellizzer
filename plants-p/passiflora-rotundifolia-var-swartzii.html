Passiflora rotundifolia var. swartzii

Passiflora rotundifolia var. swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically rounded and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture. This species produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
