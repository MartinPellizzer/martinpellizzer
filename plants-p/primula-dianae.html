Primula dianae

Primula dianae, commonly known as the Dianae primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms in late spring, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
