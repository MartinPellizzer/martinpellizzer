Parnassia palustris var. californica

Parnassia palustris var. californica, commonly known as the California fairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, white, cup-shaped flowers with yellow centers, and its leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as marshes and wet meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, greenish fruits that contain seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
