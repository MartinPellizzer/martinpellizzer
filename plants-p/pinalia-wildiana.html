Pinalia wildiana

Pinalia wildiana, commonly known as the wild pineleaf, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a pine-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pinalia wildiana is considered a vulnerable species.
