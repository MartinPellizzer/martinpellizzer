Pleurothallis octophrys

Pleurothallis octophrys, commonly known as the eight-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators such as bees and moths.

Pleurothallis octophrys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
