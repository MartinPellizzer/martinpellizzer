Poa trivialis var. prorepens

Poa trivialis var. prorepens, commonly known as the common bentgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense, mat-like turf and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is well adapted to a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common component of temperate grassland ecosystems.
