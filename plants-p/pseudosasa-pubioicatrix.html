Pseudosasa pubioicatrix

Pseudosasa pubioicatrix, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a perennial grass native to eastern China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense foliage. The plant features a distinctive hairy texture on its leaves, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pseudosasa pubioicatrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in humid climates.
