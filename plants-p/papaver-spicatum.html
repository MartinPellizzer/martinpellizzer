Papaver spicatum

Papaver spicatum, commonly known as the spiked poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its seeds are small and contained within a capsule that splits open when mature.

Papaver spicatum is also known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
