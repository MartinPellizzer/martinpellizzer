Pleurothallis montana

Pleurothallis montana, commonly known as the mountain pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its delicate floral display.
