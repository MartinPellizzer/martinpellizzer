Potentilla transsylvanica

Potentilla transsylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas. It has slender, upright stems with alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped.

Potentilla transsylvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
