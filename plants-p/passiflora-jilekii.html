Passiflora jilekii

Passiflora jilekii, commonly known as the Jilek's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible but often have a mild, somewhat tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Passiflora jilekii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
