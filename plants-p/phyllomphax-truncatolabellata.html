Phyllomphax truncatolabellata

Phyllomphax truncatolabellata, commonly known as the narrow-lipped phyllomphax, is a unique orchid species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structure, featuring a short, narrow labellum. The plant typically has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
