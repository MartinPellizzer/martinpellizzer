Phymatosorus cuspidatus

Phymatosorus cuspidatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, which helps it store water and nutrients.

The spores are produced in small, clustered clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
