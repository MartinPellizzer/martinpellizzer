Pandanus tupaiensis

Pandanus tupaiensis, commonly known as the Malabar pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fibrous leaves are harvested for making thatched roofing and baskets.

Pandanus tupaiensis thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps and along riverbanks.
