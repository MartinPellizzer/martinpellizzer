Phyllolepidum rupestre subsp. scardicum

Phyllolepidum rupestre subsp.

scardicum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, arid conditions, and it is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
