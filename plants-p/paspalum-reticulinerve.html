Paspalum reticulinerve

Paspalum reticulinerve, commonly known as the net-veined grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent network of veins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its root system is fibrous and often forms dense mats, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Paspalum reticulinerve is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, including saline and waterlogged soils.
