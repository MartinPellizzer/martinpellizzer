Puya lepidota

Puya lepidota, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous bright red flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette, storing water to adapt to its arid environment. Puya lepidota is known for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.
