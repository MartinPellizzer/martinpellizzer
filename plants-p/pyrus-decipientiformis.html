Pyrus decipientiformis

Pyrus decipientiformis, commonly known as the false whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, particularly in regions with temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, edible apple-like berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
