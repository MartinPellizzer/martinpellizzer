Psidium guyanense

Psidium guyanense, commonly known as the Guyana persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is less commonly cultivated than other psidium species.
