Padus obtusata

Padus obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the fall and are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

The bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed.
