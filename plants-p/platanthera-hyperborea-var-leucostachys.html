Platanthera hyperborea var. leucostachys

Platanthera hyperborea var. leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed bog orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species found in northern temperate regions.

It thrives in wet, acidic, and nutrient-poor habitats such as bogs and fens. The plant has a slender, erect stem that is often covered in a dense, white to pale green pubescence. Its flowers are small and arranged in a raceme, with white petals and sepals that are typically tinged with green.
