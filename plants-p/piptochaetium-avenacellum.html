Piptochaetium avenacellum

Piptochaetium avenacellum, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor and drought-prone soils.

Due to its hardiness and low water requirements, it is often used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
