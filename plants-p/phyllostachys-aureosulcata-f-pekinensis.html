Phyllostachys aureosulcata f. pekinensis

Phyllostachys aureosulcata f. pekinensis, commonly known as the Peking bamboo, is a variant of the golden bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its golden-yellow streaks on the culms, which become more pronounced with age. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, and is known for its elegant, slender culms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both ornamental and garden use.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
