Phaeopappus kotschyi var. floccosus

Phaeopappus kotschyi var. floccosus, commonly known as the woolly phaeopappus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy appearance, with dense, white to grayish hairs covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often resembling a spherical or woolly mass. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
