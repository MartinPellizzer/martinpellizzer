Periploca alboflavescens

Periploca alboflavescens, commonly known as the white-flowered periploca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring or early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
