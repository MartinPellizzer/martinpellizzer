Primula russeola subsp. lancifolia

Primula russeola subsp.

lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
