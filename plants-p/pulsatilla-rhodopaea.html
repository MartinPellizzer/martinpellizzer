Pulsatilla rhodopaea

Pulsatilla rhodopaea, commonly known as the Greek pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem that supports its single, cup-shaped flower, which is surrounded by several leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of several lobed leaflets that are hairy and gray-green in color.

Pulsatilla rhodopaea thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
