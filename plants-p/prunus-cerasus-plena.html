Prunus cerasus plena

Prunus cerasus plena, commonly known as the double cherry blossom or double sweet cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus cerasus species.

It is characterized by its dense, double-layered petals that give it a more elaborate and showy appearance compared to single-flowered varieties. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces small, sweet, edible fruits. It is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant display of pink to landscapes.
