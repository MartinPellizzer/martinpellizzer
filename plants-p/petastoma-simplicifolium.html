Petastoma simplicifolium

Petastoma simplicifolium, commonly known as the simple-leaf aster, is a flowering plant characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar plants such as daisies and sunflowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Petastoma simplicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
