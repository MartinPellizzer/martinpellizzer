Polygala steudeliana

Polygala steudeliana, commonly known as the hooded milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Polygala steudeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
