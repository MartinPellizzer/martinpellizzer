Piper graveolens

Piper graveolens, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into the familiar black, white, and green peppercorns. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit, the peppercorn, is the primary part used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Piper graveolens is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
