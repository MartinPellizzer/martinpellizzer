Phyllodolon fistulosum

Phyllodolon fistulosum, commonly known as the hollow-stemmed phyllodolon, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive hollow stems, which are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are reduced to small scales, a feature that helps it conserve water in arid environments.

Phyllodolon fistulosum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
