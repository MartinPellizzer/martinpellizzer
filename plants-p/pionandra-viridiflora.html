Pionandra viridiflora

Pionandra viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered pionandra, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale green to yellow, which is where its common name originates. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
