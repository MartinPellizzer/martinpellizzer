Paullinia colombiana

Paullinia colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian soursop or *Guanabana*, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, spiky rind and a soft, sweet, aromatic pulp that contains numerous black seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, while the fruit is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
