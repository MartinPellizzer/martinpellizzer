Paspalum rigidifolium

Paspalum rigidifolium, commonly known as rigid foliaged grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, rigid leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum rigidifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
