Picea parryana glauca-pendens

Picea parryana glauca-pendens, commonly known as the blue pendulous spruce, is a rare and striking conifer native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its unique blue-gray foliage, which becomes more pronounced with age, and its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, almost weeping appearance. The tree typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its cones are small and cylindrical, containing two winged seeds, and it produces both male and female cones on the same plant.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
