Phymatopteris simplicifolia

Phymatopteris simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a feathery appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Phymatopteris simplicifolia plays an important role in maintaining ecological balance by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
