Pteris atropurpurea

Pteris atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically dark purple to bronze in color, especially when young. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous growth habit. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction.

Pteris atropurpurea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
