Polygonum divaricatum var. limosum

Polygonum divaricatum var. limosum, commonly known as the sticky smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, streambanks, and ditches. The plant features upright, slender stems that are often covered in a sticky substance, which is a characteristic feature of the species. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive reddish hue, especially along the veins.

The small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense clusters and bloom in late summer, producing tiny, three-lobed seeds.
