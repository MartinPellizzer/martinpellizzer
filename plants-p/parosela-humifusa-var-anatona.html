Parosela humifusa var. anatona

Parosela humifusa var. anatona, commonly known as the Anatolian parosela, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valued addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
