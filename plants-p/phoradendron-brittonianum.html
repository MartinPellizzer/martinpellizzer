Phoradendron brittonianum

Phoradendron brittonianum, commonly known as the Britton's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has a distinctive appearance with smooth, glossy leaves and small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange, which contain seeds that are dispersed by birds. Its life cycle involves both parasitic and photosynthetic phases, with the plant relying on its host for water and nutrients while also producing its own food through photosynthesis.

This unique relationship makes it an important component of the ecological dynamics in its native habitat.
