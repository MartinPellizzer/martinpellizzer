Patagonium leiocarpum var. elatum

Patagonium leiocarpum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall whitebark.

This variant is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the Andean regions of Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, leathery fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cold, dry conditions of its native habitat.
