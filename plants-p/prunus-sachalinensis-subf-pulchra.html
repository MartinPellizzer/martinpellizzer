Prunus sachalinensis subf. pulchra

Prunus sachalinensis subf.

pulchra, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, oval fruits that are typically red or dark purple when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
