Potentilla tanacetifolia var. shantungensis

Potentilla tanacetifolia var. shantungensis, commonly known as Shantung cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
