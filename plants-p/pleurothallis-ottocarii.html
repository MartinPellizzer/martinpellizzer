Pleurothallis ottocarii

Pleurothallis ottocarii, commonly known as the Otto's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees and other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrate for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
