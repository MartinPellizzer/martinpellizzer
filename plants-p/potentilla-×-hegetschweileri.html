Potentilla × hegetschweileri

Potentilla × hegetschweileri, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a deciduous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
