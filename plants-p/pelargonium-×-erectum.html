Pelargonium × erectum

Pelargonium × erectum, commonly known as the upright geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture. This plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and containers.
