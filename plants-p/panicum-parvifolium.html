Panicum parvifolium

Panicum parvifolium, commonly known as littleleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and slender, branching stems.

Panicum parvifolium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native wetland ecosystems.
