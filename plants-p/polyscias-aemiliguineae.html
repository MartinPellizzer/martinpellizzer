Polyscias aemiliguineae

Polyscias aemiliguineae, commonly known as the broadleaf sycamore, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by black, spherical berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
