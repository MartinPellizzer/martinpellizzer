Phaseolus bilobatus

Phaseolus bilobatus, commonly known as the twin-lobed bean, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed seed pods and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are typically white or pale yellow and are used in traditional cuisines.

This legume is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.
