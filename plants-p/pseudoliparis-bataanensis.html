Pseudoliparis bataanensis

Pseudoliparis bataanensis, commonly known as the Bataan sea whip, is a marine flowering plant found in the coastal waters of the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Bataanaceae and is characterized by its unique, branching structure that resembles a whip. The plant has small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which help in attracting pollinators. It thrives in shallow, warm waters and plays a role in the local marine ecosystem by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
