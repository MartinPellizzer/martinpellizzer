Polygonatum kiotense

Polygonatum kiotense, commonly known as the Korean Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Polygonatum kiotense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
