Philadelphus dasycalyx

Philadelphus dasycalyx, commonly known as the dense calyx phellodendron, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
