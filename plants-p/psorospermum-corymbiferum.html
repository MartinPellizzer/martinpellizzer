Psorospermum corymbiferum

Psorospermum corymbiferum, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange inflorescences that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits.
