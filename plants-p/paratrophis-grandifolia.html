Paratrophis grandifolia

Paratrophis grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved paratrophis, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with a faintly fragrant scent, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
