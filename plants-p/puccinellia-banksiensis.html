Puccinellia banksiensis

Puccinellia banksiensis, commonly known as Banks' needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers.

Puccinellia banksiensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
