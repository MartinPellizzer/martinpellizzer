Piper diospyrifolium

Piper diospyrifolium, commonly known as the Jamaican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. The fruits are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for their pungent flavor and potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.
