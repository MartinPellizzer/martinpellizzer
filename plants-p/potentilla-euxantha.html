Potentilla euxantha

Potentilla euxantha, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid climates.

Potentilla euxantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging garden settings.
