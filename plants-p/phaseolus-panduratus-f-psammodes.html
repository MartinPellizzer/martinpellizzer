Phaseolus panduratus f. psammodes

Phaseolus panduratus f. psammodes, commonly known as the sandbean or desert bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped seeds and hairy, pubescent stems. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and pointed.

The plant is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional agriculture in certain regions of the Americas.
