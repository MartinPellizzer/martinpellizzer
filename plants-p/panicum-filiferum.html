Panicum filiferum

Panicum filiferum, commonly known as the silky grass or silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-gray panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which contribute to its role in ecological systems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
