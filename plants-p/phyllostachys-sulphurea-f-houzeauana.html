Phyllostachys sulphurea f. houzeauana

Phyllostachys sulphurea f. houzeauana, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms that turn golden in the autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its unique coloration and growth habit make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscaping projects.
