Panicum pilosum var. polychaetum

Panicum pilosum var. polychaetum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small and pale purple, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
