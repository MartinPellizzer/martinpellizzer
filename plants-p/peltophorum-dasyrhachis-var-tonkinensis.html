Peltophorum dasyrhachis var. tonkinensis

Peltophorum dasyrhachis var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink floral displays that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces distinctive, pendulous inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
