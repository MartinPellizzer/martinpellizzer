Pleurothallis paspaliformis

Pleurothallis paspaliformis, commonly known as the paspaliform orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found clinging to tree branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, which contributes to its common name.

The orchid's structure and growth habits make it well adapted to its natural habitat in the understory of tropical forests.
