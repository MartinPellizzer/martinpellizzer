Pleurothallis marioi

Pleurothallis marioi, commonly known as the Mario's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is known for its intricate floral structure and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
