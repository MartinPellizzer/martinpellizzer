Primula delavayi subsp. coxii

Primula delavayi subsp.

coxii, commonly known as the Coxi's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant features rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
