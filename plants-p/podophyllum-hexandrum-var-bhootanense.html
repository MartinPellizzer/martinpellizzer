Podophyllum hexandrum var. bhootanense

Podophyllum hexandrum var. bhootanense, commonly known as the Bhutan yellowpod, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and India.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow berries that contrast with its dark green, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high altitudes. Its roots contain toxic compounds, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountainous ecosystems.
