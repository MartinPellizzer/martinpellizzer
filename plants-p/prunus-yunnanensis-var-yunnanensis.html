Prunus yunnanensis var. yunnanensis

Prunus yunnanensis var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in summer, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in traditional Chinese horticulture.
