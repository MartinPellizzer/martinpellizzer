Poa duriuscula

Poa duriuscula, commonly known as the hardstem bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a key role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
