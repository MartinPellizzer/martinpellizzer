Premna sessilifolia

Premna sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf morning glory, is a tropical shrub belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in clusters. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Premna sessilifolia is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
