Panicum oplismenoides

Panicum oplismenoides, commonly known as blue panic grass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which resembles a bottlebrush. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Panicum oplismenoides also serves as a valuable host plant for certain insect species and can contribute to soil stabilization in erosion-prone areas.
