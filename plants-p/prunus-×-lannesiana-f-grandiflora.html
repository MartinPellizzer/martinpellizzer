Prunus × lannesiana f. grandiflora

Prunus × lannesiana f. grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus known for its showy, early spring blooms.

It is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus incisa, characterized by its large, single or semi-double white to pink flowers. The plant is deciduous, reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters, with a spreading canopy and attractive, ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and parks.

Its blooming period attracts pollinators and adds aesthetic value to landscapes.
