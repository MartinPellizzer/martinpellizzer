Potentilla pilosa var. pinnatifida

Potentilla pilosa var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately divided, giving the plant its scientific name, and are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
