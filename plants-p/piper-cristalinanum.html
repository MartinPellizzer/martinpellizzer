Piper cristalinanum

Piper cristalinanum, commonly known as the crystal vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
