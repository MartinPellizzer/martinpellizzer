Pachypodium lamerei var. ramosum

Pachypodium lamerei var. ramosum, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its swollen, tree-like trunk and clusters of small, spiny leaves. It produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers during the warmer months.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
