Piper sociorum

Piper sociorum, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are tiny, spherical berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

Piper sociorum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
