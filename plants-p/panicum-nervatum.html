Panicum nervatum

Panicum nervatum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces slender, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its delicate appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
