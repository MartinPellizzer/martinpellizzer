Panicum hirticalycinum

Panicum hirticalycinum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Panicum hirticalycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
