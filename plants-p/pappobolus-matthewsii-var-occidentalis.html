Pappobolus matthewsii var. occidentalis

Pappobolus matthewsii var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.
