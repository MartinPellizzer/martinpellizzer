Padus mahaleb var. typica

Padus mahaleb var. typica, commonly known as the Mahaleb cherry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red, edible fruits. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of cherry fruits.
