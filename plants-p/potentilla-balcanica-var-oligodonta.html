Potentilla balcanica var. oligodonta

Potentilla balcanica var. oligodonta, commonly known as the Balkan potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
