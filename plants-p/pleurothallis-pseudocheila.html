Pleurothallis pseudocheila

Pleurothallis pseudocheila, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features intricate, delicate flowers with a golden-yellow coloration that resembles the sun, hence its common name. It has a short, wiry stem and produces numerous small, clustered blooms.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
