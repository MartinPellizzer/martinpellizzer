Prunus vachuschtii f. meczibuche

Prunus vachuschtii f. meczibuche, commonly known as the Meczbuchewicz cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

It is a hybrid species, resulting from crosses between Prunus padus and Prunus sibirica, which contributes to its unique botanical traits. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface, providing aesthetic appeal throughout the growing season.

This variety is well-suited for ornamental gardening and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
