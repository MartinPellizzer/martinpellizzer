Pyrus amygdaliformis var. cuneifolia

Pyrus amygdaliformis var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its distinctive cuneifoliate leaves.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are elongated and taper to a point, giving it a unique appearance compared to other pear species. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and the fruit is small, edible, and has a sweet, delicate flavor.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
