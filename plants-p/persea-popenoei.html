Persea popenoei

Persea popenoei, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like avocado and cinnamon. The tree produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color when ripe. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Persea popenoei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
