Pedilonum chlorinum

Pedilonum chlorinum, commonly known as the green-footed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or green in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
