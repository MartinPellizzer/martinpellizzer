Pimelea crinita

Pimelea crinita, commonly known as the silky pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pimelea crinita is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
