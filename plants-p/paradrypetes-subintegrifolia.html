Paradrypetes subintegrifolia

Paradrypetes subintegrifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
