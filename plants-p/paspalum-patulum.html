Paspalum patulum

Paspalum patulum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in areas with high moisture levels and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
