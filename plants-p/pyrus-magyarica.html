Pyrus magyarica

Pyrus magyarica, commonly known as the Hungarian pear, is a deciduous tree native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in late summer, though they are not typically cultivated for commercial use. It is often found in moist, calcareous soils and can thrive in a variety of temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
