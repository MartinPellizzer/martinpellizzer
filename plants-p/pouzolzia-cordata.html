Pouzolzia cordata

Pouzolzia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically thick and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
