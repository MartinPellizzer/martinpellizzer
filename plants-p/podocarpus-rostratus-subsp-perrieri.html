Podocarpus rostratus subsp. perrieri

Podocarpus rostratus subsp.

perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to Madagascar. It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, tropical forest environments.

The plant has slender, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the plant's seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
