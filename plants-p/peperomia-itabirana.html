Peperomia itabirana

Peperomia itabirana, commonly known as the Itabira peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a waxy texture and a variety of colorations, including green, silver, and variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually white or green, which are followed by distinctive, elongated seed capsules. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia itabirana is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal water and occasional fertilization during the growing season.
