Piper auritum var. amplifolium

Piper auritum var. amplifolium, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant or giant pepper vine, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy, and often large leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red, peppercorn-like berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
