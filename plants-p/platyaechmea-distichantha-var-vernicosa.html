Platyaechmea distichantha var. vernicosa

Platyaechmea distichantha var. vernicosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf cycad, is a unique cycad species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery, and waxy foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost velvety appearance. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
