Piper laurifolium

Piper laurifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
