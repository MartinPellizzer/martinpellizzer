Primula aretia

Primula aretia, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features rosettes of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in its alpine habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and cool climates, often growing at elevations above 1,500 meters.

Primula aretia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
