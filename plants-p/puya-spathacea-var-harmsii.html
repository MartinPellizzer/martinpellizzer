Puya spathacea var. harmsii

Puya spathacea var. harmsii, commonly known as the Harms' torchflower, is a striking member of the bromeliad family, native to the Andean regions of South America.

This perennial plant is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy inflorescences that resemble torches, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a water-holding cup at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its visual impact in botanical gardens and private collections.
