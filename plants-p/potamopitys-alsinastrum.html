Potamopitys alsinastrum

Potamopitys alsinastrum, commonly known as the mountain pine, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its roots are adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils, making it a hardy species in mountainous regions.

The plant plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
