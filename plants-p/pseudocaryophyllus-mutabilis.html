Pseudocaryophyllus mutabilis

Pseudocaryophyllus mutabilis, commonly known as the variable caryophyllaceae, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to white. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Due to its variable flower colors and graceful appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
