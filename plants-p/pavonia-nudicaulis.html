Pavonia nudicaulis

Pavonia nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender pavonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and bear small, pink to purple flowers. The plant has alternate, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glandular and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Pavonia nudicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various insects.
