Papaver alpinum subvar. rubellum

Papaver alpinum subvar. rubellum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange-red petals and a tall, slender stem. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
