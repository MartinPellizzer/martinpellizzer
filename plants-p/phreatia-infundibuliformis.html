Phreatia infundibuliformis

Phreatia infundibuliformis, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-like structure formed by the fusion of the sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Phreatia infundibuliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
