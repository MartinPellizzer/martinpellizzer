Polyspora ovalis

Polyspora ovalis, commonly known as the oval spore moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped spores and a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Polyspora ovalis plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
