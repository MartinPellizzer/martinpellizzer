Prunus communis var. insititia

Prunus communis var. insititia, commonly known as the wild cherry or European bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, bright red berries in the autumn. Its fruit is edible but has a tart flavor, and it is often used in jams and preserves.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
