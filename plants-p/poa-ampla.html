Poa ampla

Poa ampla, commonly known as the mountain grass or tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine environments. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Poa ampla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
