Polygala pseudocelosioides

Polygala pseudocelosioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures and often small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters.

The species is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
