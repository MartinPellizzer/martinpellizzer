Panicum venezuelae

Panicum venezuelae, commonly known as the Venezuelan panicgrass, is a grass species native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Panicum venezuelae is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
