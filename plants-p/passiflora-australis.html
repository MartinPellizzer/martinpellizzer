Passiflora australis

Passiflora australis, commonly known as the Australian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Australia.

It features striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of hair. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits similar to passionfruit, though smaller in size. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora australis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests and along coastal areas.
