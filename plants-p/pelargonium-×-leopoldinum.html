Pelargonium × leopoldinum

Pelargonium × leopoldinum, commonly known as the leopoldii geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, red, and white. The plant typically has serrated, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Pelargonium × leopoldinum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for horticultural use.
