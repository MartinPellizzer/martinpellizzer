Penstemon heterodoxus var. heterodoxus

Penstemon heterodoxus var. heterodoxus, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is known for its showy, long-blooming flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
