Pyrus conspicua

Pyrus conspicua, commonly known as the showy pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus conspicua is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
