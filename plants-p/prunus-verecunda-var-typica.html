Prunus verecunda var. typica

Prunus verecunda var. typica, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and mammals. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and turn vibrant orange or red in the fall.

Prunus verecunda var. typica is well adapted to coastal and mountainous regions with well-drained soils.
