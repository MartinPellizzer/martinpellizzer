Pedicularis resupinata var. lactea

Pedicularis resupinata var. lactea, commonly known as the milkweed lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

This variant is distinguished by its pale, milky sap, which gives it its common name.
