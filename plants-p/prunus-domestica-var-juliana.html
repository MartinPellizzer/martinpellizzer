Prunus domestica var. juliana

Prunus domestica var. juliana, commonly known as the Julian plum, is a cultivar of the European plum tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and high ornamental value, making it suitable for small gardens. The tree produces small, dark purple fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for both its aesthetic appeal and its edible, sweet-tart fruits.
