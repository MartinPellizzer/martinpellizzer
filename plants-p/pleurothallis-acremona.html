Pleurothallis acremona

Pleurothallis acremona, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleurothallis acremona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
