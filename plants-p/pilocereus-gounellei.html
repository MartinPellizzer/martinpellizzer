Pilocereus gounellei

Pilocereus gounellei, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a swollen, segmented stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a single night.

Despite its small size, it is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
