Phyllanthus tenuipedicellatus var. tontoutaensis

Phyllanthus tenuipedicellatus var. tontoutaensis, commonly known as the Tontouta phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a capsule containing several small, smooth seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
