Poterium muricatum var. platylophum

Poterium muricatum var. platylophum, commonly known as the broad-lobed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent central boss. The plant has palmate leaves that are typically broad and lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
