Palicourea adusta

Palicourea adusta, commonly known as the purple beauty leaf, is a striking tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety purple leaves that contrast beautifully with its green veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage is widely appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Palicourea adusta is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
