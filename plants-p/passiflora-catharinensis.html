Passiflora catharinensis

Passiflora catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature intricate structures such as the corona and sepals. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Passiflora catharinensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
