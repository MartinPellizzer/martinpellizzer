Primula cuneifolia subsp. saxifragifolia

Primula cuneifolia subsp.

saxifragifolia, commonly known as the saxifragleaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are characterized by their narrow, elongated shape and a distinctive cuneate base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
