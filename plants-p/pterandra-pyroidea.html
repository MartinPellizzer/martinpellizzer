Pterandra pyroidea

Pterandra pyroidea, commonly known as the flameleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which are borne on long, pendulous stalks. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pterandra pyroidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
