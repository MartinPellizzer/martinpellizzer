Pulsatilla vulgaris var. rubra

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. rubra, commonly known as the red pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem with large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
