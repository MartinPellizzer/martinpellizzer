Potamogeton natans var. fluitans

Potamogeton natans var. fluitans, commonly known as floating pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that float on the water surface, with small, oval-shaped leaves arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, canoe-shaped fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain water bodies if not managed properly.
