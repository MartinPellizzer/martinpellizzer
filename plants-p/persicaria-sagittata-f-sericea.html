Persicaria sagittata f. sericea

Persicaria sagittata f. sericea, commonly known as the silky arrowleaf plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
