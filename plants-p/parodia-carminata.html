Parodia carminata

Parodia carminata, commonly known as the Red Zebra Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that are typically green with red or purple markings. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are borne on short, lateral branches. It is a compact, slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens and collections.
