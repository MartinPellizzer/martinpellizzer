Pedicularis palustris var. wlassowiana

Pedicularis palustris var. wlassowiana, commonly known as the marsh monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in bogs or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is part of the Snapdragon family (Plantaginaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
