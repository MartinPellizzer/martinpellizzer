Pinus mandschurica

Pinus mandschurica, commonly known as the Manchurian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically two to five centimeters long and grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are often reddish-brown and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
