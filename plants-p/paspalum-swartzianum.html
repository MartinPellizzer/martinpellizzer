Paspalum swartzianum

Paspalum swartzianum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum swartzianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
