Passiflora seguinii

Passiflora seguinii, commonly known as Seguin's passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Passiflora seguinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
