Petastoma trachyphyllum

Petastoma trachyphyllum, commonly known as the roughleaf petasite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive waxy coating, while its flowers are arranged in composite inflorescences.

Petastoma trachyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
