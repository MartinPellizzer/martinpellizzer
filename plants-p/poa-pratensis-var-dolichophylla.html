Poa pratensis var. dolichophylla

Poa pratensis var. dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that can reach up to 15 centimeters in length. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of grassland ecosystems.
