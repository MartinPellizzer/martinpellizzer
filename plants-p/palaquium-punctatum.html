Palaquium punctatum

Palaquium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for local use.
