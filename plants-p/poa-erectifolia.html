Poa erectifolia

Poa erectifolia, commonly known as the upright bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and along streambanks.
