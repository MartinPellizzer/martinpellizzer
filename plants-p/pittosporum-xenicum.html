Pittosporum xenicum

Pittosporum xenicum, commonly known as the New Zealand white pine, is a small evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks around the world.
