Pomaderris ledifolia

Pomaderris ledifolia, commonly known as silver wattle, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically in spring, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and dry sclerophyll forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
