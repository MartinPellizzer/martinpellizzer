Pteropyrum ericoides

Pteropyrum ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features compact, dense foliage with leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
