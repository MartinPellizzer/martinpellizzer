Pyrus rehderi

Pyrus rehderi, commonly known as Rehder's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with compound leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears but are less widely cultivated.

Pyrus rehderi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
