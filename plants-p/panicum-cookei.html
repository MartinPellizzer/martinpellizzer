Panicum cookei

Panicum cookei, commonly known as Cooke's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental potential, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
