Prenanthes sonchifolia

Prenanthes sonchifolia, commonly known as the common burrweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands, exhibiting a herbaceous growth habit. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into prickly, burr-like fruits.

The species is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
