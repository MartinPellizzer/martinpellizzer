Parsonsia virgata

Parsonsia virgata, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The plant has a climbing habit, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pollinated by nectar-feeding birds such as honeyeaters.

Parsonsia virgata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
