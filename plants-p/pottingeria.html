Pottingeria

Pottingeria, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, silvery-gray foliage that provides a striking contrast to its green stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Pottingeria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
