Passiflora punicea

Passiflora punicea, commonly known as the crimson passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

Passiflora punicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
