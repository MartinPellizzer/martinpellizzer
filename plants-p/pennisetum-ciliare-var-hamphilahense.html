Pennisetum ciliare var. hamphilahense

Pennisetum ciliare var. hamphilahense, commonly known as the Hamphilahense fountain grass, is a variant of the fountain grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-green plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with fine, hair-like structures along the edges of the leaves.

This ornamental grass is often used in landscaping for its decorative plumes and ability to add texture to garden designs.
