Priva crenata

Priva crenata, commonly known as the serrated privet, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
