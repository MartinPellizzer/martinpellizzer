Primula × austriaca

Primula × austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula obconica.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, featuring basal rosettes of oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
