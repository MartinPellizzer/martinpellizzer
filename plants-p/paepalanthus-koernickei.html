Paepalanthus koernickei

Paepalanthus koernickei, commonly known as the desert pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme temperatures and limited rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in arid gardens as an ornamental species.
