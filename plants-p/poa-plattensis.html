Poa plattensis

Poa plattensis, commonly known as Platt's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowers are small and spike-like, with slender, blue-green inflorescences.

Poa plattensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
