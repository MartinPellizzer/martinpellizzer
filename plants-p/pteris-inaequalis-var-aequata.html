Pteris inaequalis var. aequata

Pteris inaequalis var. aequata, commonly known as the crested water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled fronds that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in damp forests. Its fronds are bipinnate, with small, closely spaced pinnae that contribute to its intricate foliage.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in water gardens and as a decorative houseplant.
