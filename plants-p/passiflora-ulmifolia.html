Passiflora ulmifolia

Passiflora ulmifolia, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a support structure for its vine growth.

Passiflora ulmifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
