Penstemon goodrichii

Penstemon goodrichii, commonly known as Goodrich's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Penstemon goodrichii is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
