Potamogeton hubeiensis

Potamogeton hubeiensis, commonly known as Hubei pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its linear, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute flowers, which are typically unisexual. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
