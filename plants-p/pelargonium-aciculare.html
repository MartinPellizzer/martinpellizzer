Pelargonium aciculare

Pelargonium aciculare, commonly known as the needle-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is known for its ability to produce copious amounts of nectar, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pelargonium aciculare is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
