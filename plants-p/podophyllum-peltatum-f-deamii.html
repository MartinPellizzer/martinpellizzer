Podophyllum peltatum f. deamii

Podophyllum peltatum f. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's American bellflower, is a cultivar of the American bellflower plant.

It is characterized by its broad, shield-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red berries that contrast with its white or pinkish flowers. This variety is native to North America and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal berries.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
