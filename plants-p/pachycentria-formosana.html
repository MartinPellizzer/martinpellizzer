Pachycentria formosana

Pachycentria formosana, commonly known as the Formosan mountain bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
