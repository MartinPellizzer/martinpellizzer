Persoonia arida

Persoonia arida, commonly known as the staghorn lichen or staghorn persoonia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, woody, branching stems that resemble antlers, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in late summer, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. Persoonia arida is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
