Potentilla lanata

Potentilla lanata, commonly known as silvery cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Potentilla lanata is also valued for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
