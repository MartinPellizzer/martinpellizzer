Pharbitis tricolor

Pharbitis tricolor, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement throughout the day. The plant produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. Its seeds are enclosed in a spiral-shaped capsule that opens when mature.

Pharbitis tricolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
