Poa pufontii

Poa pufontii, commonly known as the Pufont's bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Poa pufontii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
