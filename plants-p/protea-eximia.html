Protea eximia

Protea eximia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of individual flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by numerous petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white to pale pink, with a distinctive silver or grayish hue on the bracts.

Protea eximia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
