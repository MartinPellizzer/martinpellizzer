Pulsatilla montana var. sibirica

Pulsatilla montana var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, showy, cup-shaped flower with bright yellow petals and a hairy center. The plant has palmate, hairy leaves that are typically divided into three to five lobes. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Pulsatilla montana var. sibirica is known for its early spring blooming habit and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
