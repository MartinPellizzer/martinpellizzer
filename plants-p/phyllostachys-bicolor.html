Phyllostachys bicolor

Phyllostachys bicolor, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. This plant is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring a mix of green and white or silver hues along the leaf blades. Phyllostachys bicolor is a clumping bamboo that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
