Passiflora smilacifolia

Passiflora smilacifolia, commonly known as the creeping passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Passiflora smilacifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential sedative properties.
