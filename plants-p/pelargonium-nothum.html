Pelargonium nothum

Pelargonium nothum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers range in color from pink to red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
