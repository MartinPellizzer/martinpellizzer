Phyllis

Phyllis, commonly known as the Phyllis (Phyllis glaberrima), is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Phyllis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
