Plumea

Plumea, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. The plants are characterized by their showy, palmate leaves and large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. They are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
