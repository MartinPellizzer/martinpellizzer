Pseudomammillaria camptotricha

Pseudomammillaria camptotricha, commonly known as the twisted-pear cactus, is a low-growing, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem that is typically green to blue-green in color, covered with small, white tubercles. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
