Polygala rosea

Polygala rosea, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

Polygala rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
