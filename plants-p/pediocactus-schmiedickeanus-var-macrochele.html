Pediocactus schmiedickeanus var. macrochele

Pediocactus schmiedickeanus var. macrochele, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a unique cactus native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines, which help to reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted color palette. It is a slow-growing species that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its striking appearance and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
