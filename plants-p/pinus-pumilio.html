Pinus pumilio

Pinus pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a small coniferous tree native to the alpine regions of North America.

It is characterized by its short, twisted trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in harsh, high-altitude environments with limited soil and cold temperatures.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
