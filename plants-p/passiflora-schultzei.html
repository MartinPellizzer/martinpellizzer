Passiflora schultzei

Passiflora schultzei, commonly known as the Schultze's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated edge, and it has a woody stem covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora schultzei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
