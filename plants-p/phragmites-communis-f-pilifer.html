Phragmites communis f. pilifer

Phragmites communis f. pilifer, commonly known as the silky reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and shores. The plant is distinguished by its silky, hairy leaves that give it a soft, textured appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

This form is adapted to moist soils and plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife.
