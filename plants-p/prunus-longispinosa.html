Prunus longispinosa

Prunus longispinosa, commonly known as the long-spined cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Prunus longispinosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
