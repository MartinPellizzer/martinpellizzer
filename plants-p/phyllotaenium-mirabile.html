Phyllotaenium mirabile

Phyllotaenium mirabile, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, ghost-like appearance, with translucent, waxy leaves that resemble spectral forms. The plant's foliage is often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which contrast with its unusual foliage.

Despite its eerie appearance, Phyllotaenium mirabile is a relatively low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
