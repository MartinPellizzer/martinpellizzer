Phyllanthus montrouzieri var. poyaensis

Phyllanthus montrouzieri var. poyaensis, commonly known as the Poya's gooseberry, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that turn red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
