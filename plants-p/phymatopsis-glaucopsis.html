Phymatopsis glaucopsis

Phymatopsis glaucopsis, commonly known as the blueleaf phymatopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
