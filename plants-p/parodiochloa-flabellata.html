Parodiochloa flabellata

Parodiochloa flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
