Polyscias cissiflora

Polyscias cissiflora, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Polyscias cissiflora is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
