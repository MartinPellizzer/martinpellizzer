Payena nigropunctata

Payena nigropunctata, commonly known as the black-punctured payena, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and rugged, often featuring distinctive black spots or punctures, which give it its common name.

Payena nigropunctata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
