Protium brasiliense var. subacuminatum

Protium brasiliense var. subacuminatum, commonly known as the Brazilian wild pear, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptic leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by pear-shaped fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color. Its bark is aromatic and contains essential oils, making it valuable for both traditional medicine and commercial use.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
