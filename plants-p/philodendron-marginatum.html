Philodendron marginatum

Philodendron marginatum, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with distinctive white or silver margins. The plant has a vining growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when given support. Its stems are thick and often have a reddish or purple tint.

Philodendron marginatum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
