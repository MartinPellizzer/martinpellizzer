Populus × vernirubens

Populus × vernirubens, commonly known as the red-leaf poplar, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Populus tremuloides and Populus balsamifera.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, making it a visually striking ornamental tree. The plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in riparian zones. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its natural spread.

The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters and is valued for its aesthetic appeal in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
