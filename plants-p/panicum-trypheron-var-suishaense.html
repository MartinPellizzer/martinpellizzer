Panicum trypheron var. suishaense

Panicum trypheron var. suishaense, commonly known as the Suisha panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
