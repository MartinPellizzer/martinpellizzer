Piper pedicellatum

Piper pedicellatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical species belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and small, opposite leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, three-lobed berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
