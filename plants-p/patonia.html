Patonia

Patonia, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers, which bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to the harsh climate of the Andes. Its deep root system allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Patonia plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain insects and birds.
