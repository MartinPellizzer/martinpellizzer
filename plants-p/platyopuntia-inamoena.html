Platyopuntia inamoena

Platyopuntia inamoena, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has spiny areoles and produces small, globular fruits that contain seeds. Its succulent leaves are modified into spines, allowing it to store water efficiently in arid environments.

Platyopuntia inamoena is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in xerophytic ecosystems.
