Pedilochilus sulphureus f. coloratus

Pedilochilus sulphureus f. coloratus, commonly known as the golden-tinged orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange to yellow flowers with intricate patterns, often resembling a crown. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
