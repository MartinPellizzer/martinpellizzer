Placolobium watsonii

Placolobium watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It is characterized by its flat, ribbon-like leaves and is often found in riparian zones or along riverbanks.

Placolobium watsonii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
