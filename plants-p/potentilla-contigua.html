Potentilla contigua

Potentilla contigua, commonly known as the mountain cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Potentilla contigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
