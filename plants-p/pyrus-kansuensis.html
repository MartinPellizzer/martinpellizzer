Pyrus kansuensis

Pyrus kansuensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Pyrus kansuensis prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
