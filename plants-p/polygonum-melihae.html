Polygonum melihae

Polygonum melihae, commonly known as the Meliha knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often saline soils and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in late summer.

Polygonum melihae is often found in wetland environments and is adapted to thrive in challenging, waterlogged conditions.
