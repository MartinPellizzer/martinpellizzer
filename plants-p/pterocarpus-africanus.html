Pterocarpus africanus

Pterocarpus africanus, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces distinctive yellow flowers and flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is also known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
