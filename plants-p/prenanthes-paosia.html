Prenanthes paosia

Prenanthes paosia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowering heads consist of both ray and disk flowers, typical of the daisy family.

Prenanthes paosia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
