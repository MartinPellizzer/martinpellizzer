Phyllanthus pycnophyllus

Phyllanthus pycnophyllus, commonly known as the dense-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with diverse morphological features. The plant is characterized by its dense, overlapping leaves that form a compact, almost spherical shape. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in structures called cyathia, a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family.

Phyllanthus pycnophyllus is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appearance.
