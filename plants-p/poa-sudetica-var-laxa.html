Poa sudetica var. laxa

Poa sudetica var. laxa, commonly known as the loose meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed flowers.

This grass is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
