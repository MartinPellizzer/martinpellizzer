Palmijuncus adspersus

Palmijuncus adspersus, commonly known as the spiny palm, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its clustered growth habit and distinctive, spiny leaves that provide protection against herbivores. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures include small, yellow flowers that develop into edible fruits.

The spiny morphology and adaptability to various environmental conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
