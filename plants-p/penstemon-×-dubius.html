Penstemon × dubius

Penstemon × dubius, commonly known as the hybrid penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Penstemon digitalis and Penstemon barbatus. The plant features upright stems and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
