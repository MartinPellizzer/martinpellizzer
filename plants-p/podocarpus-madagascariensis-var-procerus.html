Podocarpus madagascariensis var. procerus

Podocarpus madagascariensis var. procerus, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has small, needle-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by cone-like fruits. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and durable wood, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
