Pilea paniculigera

Pilea paniculigera, commonly known as the silver thread plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Pilea paniculigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
