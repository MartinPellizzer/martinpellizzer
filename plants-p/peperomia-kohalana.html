Peperomia kohalana

Peperomia kohalana, commonly known as the Hawaiian peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy texture and shades of green, red, or purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant foliage.

Peperomia kohalana is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
