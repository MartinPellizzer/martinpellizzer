Pyrus aria monstr. lutescens

Pyrus aria monstr.

lutescens, commonly known as the golden spire apple, is a rare and ornamental species belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage and unique, spire-like growth habit.

The plant typically features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, tart apples in late summer. Native to specific regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
