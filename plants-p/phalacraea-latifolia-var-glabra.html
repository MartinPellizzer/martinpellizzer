Phalacraea latifolia var. glabra

Phalacraea latifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved phalacraea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are smooth and lack the hairy texture found in other varieties of the species. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
