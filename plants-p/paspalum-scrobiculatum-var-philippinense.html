Paspalum scrobiculatum var. philippinense

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine paspalum, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in wetland areas and can thrive in both freshwater and brackish environments.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
