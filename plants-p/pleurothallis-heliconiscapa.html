Pleurothallis heliconiscapa

Pleurothallis heliconiscapa, commonly known as the "Heliconiopsis orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often clustered in racemes. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
