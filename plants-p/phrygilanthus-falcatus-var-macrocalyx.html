Phrygilanthus falcatus var. macrocalyx

Phrygilanthus falcatus var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered phrygilanthus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange, with prominent, falcate (sickle-shaped) petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
