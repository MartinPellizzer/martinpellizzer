Peperomia pitiguayana

Peperomia pitiguayana, commonly known as the Brazilian peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver, adding visual interest to any collection. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia pitiguayana is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
