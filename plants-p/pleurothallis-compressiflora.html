Pleurothallis compressiflora

Pleurothallis compressiflora, commonly known as the compressed-flower orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on tree branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
