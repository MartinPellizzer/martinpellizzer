Potamogeton octandrus var. asiaticus

Potamogeton octandrus var. asiaticus, commonly known as the Asian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Asia.

It typically grows in shallow lakes, rivers, and slow-moving streams, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually less than 1 cm wide and arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and greenish, often emerging from the water surface in late summer.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
