Prunus × stacei

Prunus × stacei, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yamazakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are usually pink to white, and it produces small, reddish fruits known as cherries.

Prunus × stacei is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in Japanese gardens.
