Prunus cerasifera var. crisica

Prunus cerasifera var. crisica, commonly known as the Turkish cherry or velvet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in late summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
