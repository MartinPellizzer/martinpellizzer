Pindarea dubia

Pindarea dubia, commonly known as the rare mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred orchid flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soil and requires cool, humid conditions to thrive. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a short, underground rhizome.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Pindarea dubia is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
