Phaseolus sphaericus var. atrovariegatus

Phaseolus sphaericus var. atrovariegatus, commonly known as the black-eyed bean, is a unique variety of the common bean genus.

It is characterized by its spherical seed shape and distinctive dark, almost black, eye-like markings on the seed coat. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with compound leaves and produces small, green flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a stem that is often hairy.

This variety is primarily cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions.
