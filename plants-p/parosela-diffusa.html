Parosela diffusa

Parosela diffusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Parosela diffusa is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
