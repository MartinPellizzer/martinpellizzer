Placus sericans

Placus sericans, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that display a striking golden hue during the flowering season. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
