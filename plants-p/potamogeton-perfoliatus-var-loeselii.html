Potamogeton perfoliatus var. loeselii

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. loeselii, commonly known as the Loesel's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, with the leaves sometimes appearing to be perforated at the base. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and canals.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems.
