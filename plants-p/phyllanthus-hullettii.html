Phyllanthus hullettii

Phyllanthus hullettii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers and capsule fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as succulent leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
