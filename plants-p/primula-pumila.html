Primula pumila

Primula pumila, commonly known as the cushion primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It forms dense, compact cushions of foliage that help it survive in harsh alpine conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are rosette-shaped, hairy, and adapted to retain moisture and withstand cold temperatures.

Primula pumila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
