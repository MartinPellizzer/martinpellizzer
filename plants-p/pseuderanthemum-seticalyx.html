Pseuderanthemum seticalyx

Pseuderanthemum seticalyx, commonly known as the woolly pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
