Potamogeton longifolius

Potamogeton longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
