Pelargonium × herminae

Pelargonium × herminae, commonly known as the Herminia geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The leaves are usually rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.

It is often cultivated as a garden ornamental due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various climates.
