Pimelea parvifolia

Pimelea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered geebung, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense heads. The plant is typically found in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as part of native plant displays.

Pimelea parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
