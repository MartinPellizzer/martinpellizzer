Psidium anglohondurense

Psidium anglohondurense, commonly known as the Honduras huckleberry, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or purple when mature, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for agricultural purposes.
