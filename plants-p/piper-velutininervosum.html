Piper velutininervosum

Piper velutininervosum, commonly known as the velvet-veined pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by bright red berries that are edible.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
