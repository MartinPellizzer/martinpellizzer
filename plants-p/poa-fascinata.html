Poa fascinata

Poa fascinata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing tufts. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Poa fascinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
