Premna protrusa

Premna protrusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a tropical shrub belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Premna protrusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
