Phyllostachys glauca var. variabilis

Phyllostachys glauca var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable bamboo, is a variety of the silver bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, green to bluish-green culms that often exhibit a range of colors, including purple and pink, depending on the light exposure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers and has feathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
