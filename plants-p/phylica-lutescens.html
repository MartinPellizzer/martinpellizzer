Phylica lutescens

Phylica lutescens, commonly known as the yellow phylica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs that help reduce water loss.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic value.
