Pyrus fusca var. diversifolia

Pyrus fusca var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen in the summer, adding ornamental and culinary value. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a subtle texture that becomes more pronounced with age.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
