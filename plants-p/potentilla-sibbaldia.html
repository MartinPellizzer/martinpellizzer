Potentilla sibbaldia

Potentilla sibbaldia, commonly known as the sibbald's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and tundra environments. Its flowers are typically clustered in groups of five, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla sibbaldia is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
