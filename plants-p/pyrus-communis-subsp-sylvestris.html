Pyrus communis subsp. sylvestris

Pyrus communis subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the wild pear, is a subspecies of the common pear native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, often bitter fruits and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture and traditional medicine.
