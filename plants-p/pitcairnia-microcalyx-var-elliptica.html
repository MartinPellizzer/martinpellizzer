Pitcairnia microcalyx var. elliptica

Pitcairnia microcalyx var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of long, narrow, waxy leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red bracts that contrast beautifully with its green foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
