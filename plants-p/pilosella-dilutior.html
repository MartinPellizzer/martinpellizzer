Pilosella dilutior

Pilosella dilutior, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite flower heads. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. Native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Pilosella dilutior is often found in moist soils and is considered an invasive species in some areas outside its native range.
