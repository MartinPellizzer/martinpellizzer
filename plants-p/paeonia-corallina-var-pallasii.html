Paeonia corallina var. pallasii

Paeonia corallina var. pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas' coral peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
