Poa nemoralis var. montana

Poa nemoralis var. montana, commonly known as mountain bluegrass, is a variant of the common bluegrass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green blades. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually blue-green in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
