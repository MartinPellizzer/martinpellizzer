Piper antonense

Piper antonense, commonly known as the Anton's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Piper antonense is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
