Potamogeton natans var. canariensis

Potamogeton natans var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Potamogetonaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and slow-moving streams.

Its presence often indicates good water quality and is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
