Ptilotus polystachyus f. ruber

Ptilotus polystachyus f. ruber, commonly known as the red spinifex or red spinifex grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and bright red bracts that resemble small flames. The plant is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showcasing a high tolerance for salt and drought. Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

The inflorescences are typically arranged in compact, spike-like clusters, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.
