Perrottetia caudata

Perrottetia caudata, commonly known as the silver bush or mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center, blooming in clusters.

Perrottetia caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
