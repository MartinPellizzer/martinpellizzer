Populus serrata

Populus serrata, commonly known as the Japanese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and lowlands.

Populus serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
