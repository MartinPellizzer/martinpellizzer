Phaseolus vulgaris var. sarconaus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. sarconaus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible pods that contain kidney-shaped beans. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. It is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

This variety is particularly noted for its striking color and vigorous growth habit.
