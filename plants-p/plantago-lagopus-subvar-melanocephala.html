Plantago lagopus subvar. melanocephala

Plantago lagopus subvar. melanocephala, commonly known as the black-headed plantain, is a variant of the common plantain.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive dark green to blackish coloration on the underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
