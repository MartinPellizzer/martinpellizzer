Platycoryne crocea subsp. ochrantha

Platycoryne crocea subsp.

ochrantha, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive dark green leaves with prominent red veins and its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color.

The plant prefers shady, humid environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season and are pollinated by insects.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
