Polyphlebium borbonicum

Polyphlebium borbonicum, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale-green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
