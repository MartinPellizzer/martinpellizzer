Protea loranthifolia

Protea loranthifolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a flower head, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-green, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Protea loranthifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
