Phlox condensata

Phlox condensata, commonly known as summer phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Phlox condensata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
