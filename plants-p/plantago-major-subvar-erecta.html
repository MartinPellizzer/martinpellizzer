Plantago major subvar. erecta

Plantago major subvar. erecta, commonly known as the upright plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces erect, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
