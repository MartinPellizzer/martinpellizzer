Pittosporum amplectens

Pittosporum amplectens, commonly known as the broadleaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Pittosporum amplectens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for landscaping and erosion control.
