Perovskia × hybrida

Perovskia × hybrida, commonly known as Russian sage, is a flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and spikes of blue-lavender flowers. Native to the Caucasus region, it is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance. The plant produces small, four-lobed flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for borders, rock gardens, and as a perennial in USDA hardiness zones 5–9.
