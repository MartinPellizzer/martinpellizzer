Parathesis amplifolia

Parathesis amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf parenthesis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
