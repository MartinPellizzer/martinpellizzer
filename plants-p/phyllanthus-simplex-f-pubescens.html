Phyllanthus simplex f. pubescens

Phyllanthus simplex f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a variant of the spurge family (Euphorbiaceae) characterized by its distinctive pubescent foliage.

This plant typically features small, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in small umbels, typical of the genus Phyllanthus. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
