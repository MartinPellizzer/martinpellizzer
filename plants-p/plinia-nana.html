Plinia nana

Plinia nana, commonly known as the dwarf persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Plinia nana thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
