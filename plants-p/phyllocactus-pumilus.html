Phyllocactus pumilus

Phyllocactus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf phyllocactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and features short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, woolly white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
