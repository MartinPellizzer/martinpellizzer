Philodendron dyscarpium var. dyscarpium

Philodendron dyscarpium var. dyscarpium, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with striking red veins against a deep green background. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
