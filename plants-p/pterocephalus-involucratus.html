Pterocephalus involucratus

Pterocephalus involucratus, commonly known as the white poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a branching stem with alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small seeds.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
