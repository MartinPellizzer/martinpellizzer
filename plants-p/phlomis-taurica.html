Phlomis taurica

Phlomis taurica, commonly known as Taurian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and gray-green, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Phlomis taurica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
