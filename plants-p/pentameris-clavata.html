Pentameris clavata

Pentameris clavata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
