Phoenix iberica

Phoenix iberica, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a species of palm native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible date-like fruits. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
