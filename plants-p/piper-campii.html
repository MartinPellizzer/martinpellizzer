Piper campii

Piper campii, commonly known as the Campi pepper or Campi piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature and are used in traditional medicine.

Piper campii is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
