Pelargonium hermansdorpense

Pelargonium hermansdorpense, commonly known as the Herman's Dorp geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually rounded and slightly hairy, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium hermansdorpense is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
