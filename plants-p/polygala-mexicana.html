Polygala mexicana

Polygala mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, bean-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala mexicana is often found in limestone soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
