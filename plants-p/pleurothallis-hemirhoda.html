Pleurothallis hemirhoda

Pleurothallis hemirhoda, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip. It is known for its intricate floral structure, which is adapted for specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is often found in conservation efforts aimed at preserving its natural ecosystem.
