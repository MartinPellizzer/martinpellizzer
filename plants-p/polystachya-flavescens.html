Polystachya flavescens

Polystachya flavescens, commonly known as the golden-stem orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces numerous tiny, greenish or yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

Polystachya flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
