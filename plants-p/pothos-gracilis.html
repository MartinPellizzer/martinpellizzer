Pothos gracilis

Pothos gracilis, commonly known as the silver pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery sheen on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is more valued for its ornamental foliage than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular houseplant.

Pothos gracilis is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, enhancing its appeal as a decorative and functional plant.
