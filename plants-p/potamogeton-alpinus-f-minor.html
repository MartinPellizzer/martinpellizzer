Potamogeton alpinus f. minor

Potamogeton alpinus f. minor, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. This plant thrives in nutrient-rich, shallow waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and stable aquatic ecosystems.

The plant plays a vital role in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
