Piper leucophaeocaule

Piper leucophaeocaule, commonly known as the white-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, pale-green stems and small, opposite leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppercorn-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
