Poa acroleuca f. robusta

Poa acroleuca f. robusta, commonly known as the robust bentgrass, is a variant of the bentgrass species native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial grass characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its fine texture and ability to form lush, green lawns.
