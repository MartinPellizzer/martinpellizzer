Pappostipa speciosa

Pappostipa speciosa, commonly known as the showy pappus grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicle-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-bearing branches. The plant produces distinctive, feathery structures called pappi, which aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
