Peperomia amplexifolia

Peperomia amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a unique, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia amplexifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
