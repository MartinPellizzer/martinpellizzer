Peperomia arcuatispica

Peperomia arcuatispica, commonly known as the curling peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia arcuatispica is relatively easy to care for and thrives in humid environments with moderate temperatures.
