Panicum sarmentosum var. parvispiculatum

Panicum sarmentosum var. parvispiculatum, commonly known as dwarf panicgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats or clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
