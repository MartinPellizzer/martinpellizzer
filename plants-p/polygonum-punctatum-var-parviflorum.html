Polygonum punctatum var. parviflorum

Polygonum punctatum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red. The leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture and are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

This plant is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.
