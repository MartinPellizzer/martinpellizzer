Physalis campechiana

Physalis campechiana, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in warm, tropical climates. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which turns red when ripe. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and branched.

This plant is valued for its edible fruits and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
