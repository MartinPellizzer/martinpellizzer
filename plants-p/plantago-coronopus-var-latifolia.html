Plantago coronopus var. latifolia

Plantago coronopus var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved plantain, is a flowering plant in the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically clustered near the base of the plant. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Plantago genus and is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
