Peristylus scrotiformis

Peristylus scrotiformis, commonly known as the bladder bladder or bladderleaf, is a unique aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its inflated, bladder-like leaves that float on the water's surface, helping it to remain buoyant. The plant's structure allows it to trap small aquatic organisms, which it then absorbs through its leaf surfaces. Peristylus scrotiformis typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its distinctive morphology makes it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and botanical classification.
