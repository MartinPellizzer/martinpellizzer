Phaca candidissima

Phaca candidissima, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in temperate regions.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It plays an important ecological role by supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
