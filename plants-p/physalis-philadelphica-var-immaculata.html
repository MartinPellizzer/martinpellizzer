Physalis philadelphica var. immaculata

Physalis philadelphica var. immaculata, commonly known as the white-berried groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features sprawling stems with hairy foliage and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into distinctive, white, lantern-shaped fruits. The fruits are edible, though often tart, and are often used in culinary applications or as ornamental elements in gardens. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or disturbed habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
