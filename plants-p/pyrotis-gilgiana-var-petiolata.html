Pyrotis gilgiana var. petiolata

Pyrotis gilgiana var. petiolata, commonly known as the petioled flame flower, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a glossy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture but is considered rare in the wild.
