Pistolochia pumila f. papillosa

Pistolochia pumila f. papillosa, commonly known as the hairy pistol plant, is a unique variant of the pistol plant species.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, hairy textures, which distinguish it from other varieties.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
