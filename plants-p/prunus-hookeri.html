Prunus hookeri

Prunus hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin, and its fruits are small, dark red cherries that are edible.

Prunus hookeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
