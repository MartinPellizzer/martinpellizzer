Ptilotus exaltatus var. semilanatus

Ptilotus exaltatus var. semilanatus, commonly known as the "blue ghost" or "ghost flower," is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, conical clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and sandy garden environments.
