Pleurothallis spannageliana

Pleurothallis spannageliana, commonly known as the Spangenberg's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette.

This orchid thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
