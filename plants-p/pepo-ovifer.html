Pepo ovifer

Pepo ovifer, commonly known as the eggplant, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its fleshy, edible fruit that is typically purple in color, though varieties can also be white, yellow, or green. The fruit develops from a modified flower called a pepo, which is a type of berry with a thick, fleshy rind. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are usually yellow with five petals.

Pepo ovifer is cultivated worldwide for its versatile and nutritious fruit, which is used in various cuisines.
