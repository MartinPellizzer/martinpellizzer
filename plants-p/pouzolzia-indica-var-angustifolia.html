Pouzolzia indica var. angustifolia

Pouzolzia indica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, angular stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly serrated margin, often with a pale underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
