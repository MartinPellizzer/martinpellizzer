Pleurothallis agathophylla

Pleurothallis agathophylla, commonly known as the golden rosette orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, rosette-like arrangement of dark green, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually small, yellowish-green, and clustered in a spike, producing a subtle, sweet fragrance.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
