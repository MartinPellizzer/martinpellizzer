Polygonum romanum subsp. balearicum

Polygonum romanum subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balearic Islands. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinct nodes and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in wetland or streamside habitats. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
