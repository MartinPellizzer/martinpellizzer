Pyrus adamii

Pyrus adamii, commonly known as the Adam's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Kazakhstan, and the Russian Federation. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit, which is a small, round berry, is typically harvested in late summer and is known for its tart flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
