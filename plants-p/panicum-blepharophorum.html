Panicum blepharophorum

Panicum blepharophorum, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its inflorescence is a loose panicle with small, spikelet-based flowers.

Panicum blepharophorum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its delicate, grass-like foliage.
