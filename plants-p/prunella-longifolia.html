Prunella longifolia

Prunella longifolia, commonly known as self-heal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces spikes of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Prunella longifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
