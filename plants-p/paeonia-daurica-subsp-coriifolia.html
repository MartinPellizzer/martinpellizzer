Paeonia daurica subsp. coriifolia

Paeonia daurica subsp.

coriifolia, commonly known as the Siberian peony or narrow-leaf peony, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with a serrated edge and produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink, red, or white.

The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is known for its hardy nature, thriving in cooler climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history in traditional Chinese medicine.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
