Placolobium travancoricum

Placolobium travancoricum, commonly known as the Travancore grass, is a perennial herb native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
