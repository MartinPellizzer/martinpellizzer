Pandanus absonus

Pandanus absonus, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its stiff, sword-like leaves arranged in a fan shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by spiny, fibrous fruits. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional crafts and for making thatch.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
