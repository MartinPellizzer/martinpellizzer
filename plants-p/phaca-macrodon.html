Phaca macrodon

Phaca macrodon, commonly known as the large-leaf water plantain, is a semi-aquatic herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in early summer.

Phaca macrodon plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for aquatic invertebrates and amphibians.
