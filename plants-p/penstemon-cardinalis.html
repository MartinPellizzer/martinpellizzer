Penstemon cardinalis

Penstemon cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions. Its tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
