Panicum catangense

Panicum catangense, commonly known as the catangense grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and forest edges.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
