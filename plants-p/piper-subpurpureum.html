Piper subpurpureum

Piper subpurpureum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper subpurpureum make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
