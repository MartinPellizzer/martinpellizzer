Pelargonium × allenii

Pelargonium × allenii, commonly known as the Zonal Geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves with distinct color zones, typically in shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Pelargonium × allenii is also valued for its aromatic foliage and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
