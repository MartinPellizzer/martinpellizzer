Podocarpus aristulatus subsp. buchii

Podocarpus aristulatus subsp.

buchii, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits that mature to a reddish-brown color.

The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in compact clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden settings.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and durability.
