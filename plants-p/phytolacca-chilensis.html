Phytolacca chilensis

Phytolacca chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean pokeweed, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Phytolaccaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its roots are fleshy and contain latex-like substances.
