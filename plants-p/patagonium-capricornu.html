Patagonium capricornu

Patagonium capricornu, commonly known as the horned spire, is a unique flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling inflorescence that resembles a twisted horn, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Patagonium capricornu is considered a vulnerable species.
