Prenanthes alba var. ovata

Prenanthes alba var. ovata, commonly known as white burrcup, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flower heads. Its fruit is a spiny, bur-like structure that aids in seed dispersal by attaching to animals.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
