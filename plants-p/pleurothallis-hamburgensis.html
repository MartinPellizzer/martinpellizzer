Pleurothallis hamburgensis

Pleurothallis hamburgensis, commonly known as the Hamburg orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other plants without being parasitic. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it a notable species within the Pleurothallis genus.
