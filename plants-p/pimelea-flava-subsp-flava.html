Pimelea flava subsp. flava

Pimelea flava subsp.

flava, commonly known as the yellow geebung, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters along the stems.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
