Paepalanthus freyreissii

Paepalanthus freyreissii, commonly known as the Freyreiss's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are dispersed by animals.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
