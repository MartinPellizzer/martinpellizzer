Plantago monticola

Plantago monticola, commonly known as mountain plantain, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowering stems bear dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
