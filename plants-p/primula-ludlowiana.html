Primula ludlowiana

Primula ludlowiana, commonly known as the Ludlow's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces numerous small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Primula ludlowiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
