Pterostylis trullifolia

Pterostylis trullifolia, commonly known as the clubbed helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often curved floral structure that resembles a helmet or club. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually green or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Pterostylis trullifolia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
