Pycnocomon boeticum

Pycnocomon boeticum, commonly known as the Mediterranean thyme, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, pink or purple flowers and its woody, creeping stems. Its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their essential oils.

Pycnocomon boeticum is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
