Picea nobilis var. magnifica

Picea nobilis var. magnifica, commonly known as the noble fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The cones are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, containing winged seeds. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage and structure make it a popular choice for ornamental planting in gardens and landscapes.
