Pterostylis saxosa

Pterostylis saxosa, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a spike. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, adapted to the dry, rocky environments it inhabits.

Pterostylis saxosa is often found growing on rock faces or in sandy soils, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
