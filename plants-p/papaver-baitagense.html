Papaver baitagense

Papaver baitagense, commonly known as the Baitagin poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that typically range from red to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
