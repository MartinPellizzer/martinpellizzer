Pieris japonica f. rubra

Pieris japonica f. rubra, commonly known as the Japanese pieris with red leaves, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that turns a striking red or bronze color in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to the garden. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique leaf coloration and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
