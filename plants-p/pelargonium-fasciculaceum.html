Pelargonium fasciculaceum

Pelargonium fasciculaceum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for borders and garden beds.
