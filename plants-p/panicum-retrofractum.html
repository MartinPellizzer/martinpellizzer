Panicum retrofractum

Panicum retrofractum, commonly known as the broken-back grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum retrofractum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
