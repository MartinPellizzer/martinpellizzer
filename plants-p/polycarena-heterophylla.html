Polycarena heterophylla

Polycarena heterophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaf polycarena, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or swampy environments. The plant exhibits morphological variation in its leaves, which can be either broad or narrow depending on environmental conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity within its habitat.
