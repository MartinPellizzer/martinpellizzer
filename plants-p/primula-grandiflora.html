Primula grandiflora

Primula grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Primula grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
