Pelargonium nephrophyllum

Pelargonium nephrophyllum, commonly known as the kidney-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves, which are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
