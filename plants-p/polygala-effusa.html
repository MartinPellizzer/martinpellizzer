Polygala effusa

Polygala effusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure that aids in pollination by insects.
