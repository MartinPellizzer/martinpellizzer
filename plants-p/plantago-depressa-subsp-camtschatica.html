Plantago depressa subsp. camtschatica

Plantago depressa subsp.

camtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates.

The plant features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense spikes and are pollinated by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
