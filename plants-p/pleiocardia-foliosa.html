Pleiocardia foliosa

Pleiocardia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy pleiocardia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying tropical flora.
