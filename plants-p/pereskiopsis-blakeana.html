Pereskiopsis blakeana

Pereskiopsis blakeana, commonly known as the Blake's pereskiopsis, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage composed of narrow, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
