Pandanus pistos

Pandanus pistos, commonly known as the common pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruit, known as a "pandanus nut," is edible and has a nutty flavor.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
