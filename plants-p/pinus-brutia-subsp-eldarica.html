Pinus brutia subsp. eldarica

Pinus brutia subsp.

eldarica, commonly known as the Eldarica pine, is a subspecies native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and its ability to thrive in rocky, arid environments.

The tree produces long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and landscapes for its unique appearance.
