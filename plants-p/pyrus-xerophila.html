Pyrus xerophila

Pyrus xerophila, commonly known as the desert pear, is a rare and specialized plant adapted to arid environments.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rugged leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically not edible for humans but may be consumed by certain wildlife. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it blooms in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Pyrus xerophila is considered a unique example of xerophytic adaptation.
