Potentilla foersteriana var. keysseri

Potentilla foersteriana var. keysseri, commonly known as the Keyser's potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has holly-like leaves that are dark green and slightly serrated, providing a dense, decorative appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for specialized horticultural collections.
