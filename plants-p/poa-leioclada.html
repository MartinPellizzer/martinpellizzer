Poa leioclada

Poa leioclada, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in coastal and alpine environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Poa leioclada plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
