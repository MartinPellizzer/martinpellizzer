Penstemon confusus subsp. typicus

Penstemon confusus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the western penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its foliage is often gray-green, with lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.
