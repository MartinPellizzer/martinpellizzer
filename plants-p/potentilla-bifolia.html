Potentilla bifolia

Potentilla bifolia, commonly known as white potentilla, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
