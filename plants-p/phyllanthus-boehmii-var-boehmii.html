Phyllanthus boehmii var. boehmii

Phyllanthus boehmii var. boehmii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some parts of the world.
