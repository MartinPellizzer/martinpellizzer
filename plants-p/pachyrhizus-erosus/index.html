Pachyrhizus Erosus

Pachyrhizus erosus is a unique leguminous plant native to the Andean regions of South America. Known for its impressive tuberous roots, it is also called the "mammoth bean" due to its large size. This plant is valued for its nutritional content and has been used by indigenous communities for centuries. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important crop for sustainable agriculture. Despite its potential, it remains relatively unknown outside of its native habitat.
