Peperomia baumannii

Peperomia baumannii, commonly known as the Baumann's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Peperomia baumannii is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
