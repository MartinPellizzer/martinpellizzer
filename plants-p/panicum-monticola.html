Panicum monticola

Panicum monticola, commonly known as mountain panicum, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, awn-tipped lemmas. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum monticola is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.
