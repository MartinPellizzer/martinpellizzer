Paramanglietia aromatica

Paramanglietia aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic manglietia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Magnoliaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Paramanglietia aromatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
