Pseudobromus africanus

Pseudobromus africanus, commonly known as the African false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds.

Pseudobromus africanus is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
