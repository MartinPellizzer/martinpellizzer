Pinus ayacahuite subsp. oaxacana

Pinus ayacahuite subsp.

oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxaca pine, is a subspecies native to southern Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The tree typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. It produces small, woody cones that release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and is also significant in local biodiversity.
