Pilosella flaccida

Pilosella flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, somewhat fuzzy appearance. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pilosella flaccida typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
