Pachycarpus corniculatus

Pachycarpus corniculatus, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like bracts that surround the flower cluster. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in its native habitat.
