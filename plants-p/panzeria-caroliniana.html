Panzeria caroliniana

Panzeria caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina panther vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange to yellow fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
