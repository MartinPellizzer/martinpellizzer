Phlox albomarginata var. minor

Phlox albomarginata var. minor, commonly known as the white-margin phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to lavender in color with distinctive white margins. The plant has lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
