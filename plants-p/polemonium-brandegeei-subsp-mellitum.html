Polemonium brandegeei subsp. mellitum

Polemonium brandegeei subsp.

mellitum, commonly known as the white pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.
