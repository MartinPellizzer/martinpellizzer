Pleiocarpidia cephalotes

Pleiocarpidia cephalotes, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive helmet-shaped flower, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest environments. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The orchid's flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a prominent, hood-like structure that houses the reproductive organs.
