Polygala ballii

Polygala ballii, commonly known as the Ball's milk-vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, often yellow or greenish, pea-like flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually hairy, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

Polygala ballii is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
