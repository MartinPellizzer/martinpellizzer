Prunus mume var. bungo

Prunus mume var. bungo, commonly known as the Bungo Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The Bungo variety is particularly noted for its ornamental value and cold hardiness.
