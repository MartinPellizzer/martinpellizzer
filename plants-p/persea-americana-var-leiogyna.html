Persea americana var. leiogyna

Persea americana var. leiogyna, commonly known as the Mexican avocado or the Guatemalan avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The fruit is a large, pear-shaped drupe with a thick, smooth skin that turns from green to black as it ripens. The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued for its nutrient-rich fruit, which is widely used in culinary and commercial applications.
