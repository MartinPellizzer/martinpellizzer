Pinguicula diversifolia

Pinguicula diversifolia, commonly known as the diverse-leaved butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It features small, sticky leaves that trap and digest insects, providing it with essential nutrients. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity to flourish.

Its unique morphology and carnivorous habits make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
