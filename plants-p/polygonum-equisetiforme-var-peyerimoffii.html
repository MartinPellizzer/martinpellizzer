Polygonum equisetiforme var. peyerimoffii

Polygonum equisetiforme var. peyerimoffii, commonly known as the Peyer's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

It thrives in moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
