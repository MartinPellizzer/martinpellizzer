Piper kawakamii

Piper kawakamii, commonly known as Kawakami's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Piper kawakamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in its native habitat.
