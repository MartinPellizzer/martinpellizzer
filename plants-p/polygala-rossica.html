Polygala rossica

Polygala rossica, commonly known as the Russian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, while its flowers are usually arranged in spikes.

Polygala rossica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
