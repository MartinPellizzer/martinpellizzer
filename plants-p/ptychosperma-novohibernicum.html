Ptychosperma novohibernicum

Ptychosperma novohibernicum, commonly known as the New Ireland palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the island of New Ireland in the Bismarck Archipelago, part of Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
