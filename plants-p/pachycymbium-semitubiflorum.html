Pachycymbium semitubiflorum

Pachycymbium semitubiflorum, commonly known as the tuberous orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique tuberous roots and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive tubular shape.

Pachycymbium semitubiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
