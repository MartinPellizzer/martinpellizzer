Phyteuma spicatum f. crenatum

Phyteuma spicatum f. crenatum, commonly known as the crested phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a wavy or serrated margin. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
