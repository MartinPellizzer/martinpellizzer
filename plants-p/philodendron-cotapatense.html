Philodendron cotapatense

Philodendron cotapatense, commonly known as the Cotapata philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
