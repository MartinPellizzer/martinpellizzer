Piliocalyx bullatus

Piliocalyx bullatus, commonly known as the hairy calyx bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated calyx that resembles a small bell, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Piliocalyx bullatus is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
