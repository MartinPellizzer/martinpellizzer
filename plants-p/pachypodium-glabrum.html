Pachypodium glabrum

Pachypodium glabrum, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a short, rounded trunk and clusters of small, waxy leaves. It produces yellow or orange flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators like birds and insects.

Due to its unique structure and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
