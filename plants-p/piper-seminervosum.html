Piper seminervosum

Piper seminervosum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its botanical structure is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and aromatic leaves.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
