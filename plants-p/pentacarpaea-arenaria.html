Pentacarpaea arenaria

Pentacarpaea arenaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in sandy or well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats, where it tolerates salty and windy conditions.

Pentacarpaea arenaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
