Pyrus transitoria

Pyrus transitoria, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit, a small, edible berry, is usually red or yellow when ripe and is consumed by various bird species.

Pyrus transitoria is often found in woodlands and hedgerows, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
