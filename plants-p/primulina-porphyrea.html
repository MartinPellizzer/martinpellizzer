Primulina porphyrea

Primulina porphyrea, commonly known as the purple-leafed gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Primulina porphyrea is popular among collectors for its ornamental value and unique leaf coloration.
