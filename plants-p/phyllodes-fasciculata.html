Phyllodes fasciculata

Phyllodes fasciculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like phyllodes, which are modified leaves adapted for reducing water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
