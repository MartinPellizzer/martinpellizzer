Persicaria tinctoria

Persicaria tinctoria, commonly known as red root or Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when young. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Persicaria tinctoria is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to various parts of the world, sometimes becoming invasive.
