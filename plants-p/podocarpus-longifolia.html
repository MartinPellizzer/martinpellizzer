Podocarpus longifolia

Podocarpus longifolia, commonly known as the Indian white cedar, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones containing edible seeds. Its leaves are leathery, dark green, and arranged in a spiral pattern on the branches.

Podocarpus longifolia is valued for its durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
