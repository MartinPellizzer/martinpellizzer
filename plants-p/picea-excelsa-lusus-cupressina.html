Picea excelsa lusus cupressina

Picea excelsa lusus cupressina, commonly known as the dwarf white spruce, is a rare and unique variant of the white spruce tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, conical shape, which distinguishes it from its larger relatives. The foliage consists of short, sharp needles that are typically a silvery-blue color, providing a striking visual contrast. This plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to grow in cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its rarity and its role in specialized alpine ecosystems.
