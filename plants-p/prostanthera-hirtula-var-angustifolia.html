Prostanthera hirtula var. angustifolia

Prostanthera hirtula var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens and for its medicinal properties.
