Pleurothallis ochreata subsp. cylindrifolia

Pleurothallis ochreata subsp.

cylindrifolia, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are elliptical and often covered with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
