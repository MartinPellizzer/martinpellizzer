Pilosella densiflora subsp. umbellifera

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

umbellifera, commonly known as the denseflower pearly everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in short, stiff hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
