Primula cunninghamii

Primula cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's primrose, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves with a velvety texture.

Primula cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
