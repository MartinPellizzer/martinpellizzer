Portulaca fischeri

Portulaca fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's moss rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Portulacaceae.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, spiny leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow. Native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, it thrives in hot, dry climates. The plant's ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to desert environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
