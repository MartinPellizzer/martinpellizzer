Potentilla potaninii var. subdigitata

Potentilla potaninii var. subdigitata, commonly known as the subdigitate potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The leaves are typically divided into fine, thread-like segments, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
