Persicaria conspicua

Persicaria conspicua, commonly known as the showy polygon, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, bright red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in dense, spikelike inflorescences.

Its foliage is often bright green with a bluish tinge, adding visual interest to wetland environments.
