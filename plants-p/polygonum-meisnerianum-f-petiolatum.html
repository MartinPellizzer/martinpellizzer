Polygonum meisnerianum f. petiolatum

Polygonum meisnerianum f. petiolatum, commonly known as the petioled knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum meisnerianum, which belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive petioled leaves, which are longer and more prominent than those of the typical form. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough, textured surface and a reddish or purple hue.

The inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to the plant.
