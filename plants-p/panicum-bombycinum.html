Panicum bombycinum

Panicum bombycinum, commonly known as the silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
