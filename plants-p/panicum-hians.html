Panicum hians

Panicum hians, commonly known as the purpletop millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed sites, preferring full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum hians is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
