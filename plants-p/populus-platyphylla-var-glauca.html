Populus platyphylla var. glauca

Populus platyphylla var. glauca, commonly known as the blue poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth margin and a slightly waxy texture. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
