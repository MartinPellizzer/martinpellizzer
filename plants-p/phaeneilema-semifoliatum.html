Phaeneilema semifoliatum

Phaeneilema semifoliatum, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, light-green fronds that give it a delicate and airy appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly across the ground. Its leaves are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments, and it produces spores rather than seeds for reproduction.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.
