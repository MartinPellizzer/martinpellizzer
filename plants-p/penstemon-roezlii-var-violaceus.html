Penstemon roezlii var. violaceus

Penstemon roezlii var. violaceus, commonly known as the violet-lavender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and a tall, upright stem that supports its showy inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
