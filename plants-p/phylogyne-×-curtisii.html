Phylogyne × curtisii

Phylogyne × curtisii, commonly known as the hybrid camellia, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Camellia sasanqua and Camellia japonica.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
