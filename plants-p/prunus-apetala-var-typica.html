Prunus apetala var. typica

Prunus apetala var. typica, commonly known as the California wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible drupes that turn from yellow to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
