Plantago juncoides var. decipiens

Plantago juncoides var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive plantain, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its common name refers to the way its flowers blend in with the leaves, making them difficult to spot.
