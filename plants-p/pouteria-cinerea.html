Pouteria cinerea

Pouteria cinerea, commonly known as the white spondias, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
