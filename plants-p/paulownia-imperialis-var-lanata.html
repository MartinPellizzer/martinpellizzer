Paulownia imperialis var. lanata

Paulownia imperialis var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly paulownia, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly white pubescence. The tree produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in late spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its rapid growth and lightweight, strong wood, making it useful in woodworking and as an ornamental plant.
