Penar-valli

Penar-valli, commonly known as the "lady's tears" or "white lady," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white, spadix-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its foliage and inflorescence are both ornamental and have been used in traditional medicine.

Penar-valli is also valued for its ability to purify water in its natural habitat.
