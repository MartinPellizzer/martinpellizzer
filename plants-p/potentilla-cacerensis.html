Potentilla cacerensis

Potentilla cacerensis, commonly known as the Caceres cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. It has compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla cacerensis is relatively hardy and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
