Prunus × arnoldiana

Prunus × arnoldiana, commonly known as the Arnold cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or black in color, though they are not as commonly consumed as other cherry varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal throughout the growing season.
