Podocarpus nankoensis

Podocarpus nankoensis, commonly known as the Japanese podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and narrow, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
