Polypodium bangii

Polypodium bangii, commonly known as the mountain holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their purported medicinal properties.

The fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it easily identifiable.
