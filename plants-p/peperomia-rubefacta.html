Peperomia rubefacta

Peperomia rubefacta, commonly known as the red-veined peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves with striking red veins that contrast beautifully against its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, appearing in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental leaves, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and container displays.
