Pinanga malaiana var. landakensis

Pinanga malaiana var. landakensis, commonly known as the Taveuni fern, is a species of fern native to the island of Taveuni in Fiji.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender tree-like structure with a single trunk. The plant produces large, pinnately compound leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
