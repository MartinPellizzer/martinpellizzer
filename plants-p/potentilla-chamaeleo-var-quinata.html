Potentilla chamaeleo var. quinata

Potentilla chamaeleo var. quinata, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its scientific name "quinata," which means "five-leaved.". The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a favored plant in horticulture.
