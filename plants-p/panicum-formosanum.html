Panicum formosanum

Panicum formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
