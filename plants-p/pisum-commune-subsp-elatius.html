Pisum commune subsp. elatius

Pisum commune subsp.

elatius, commonly known as the tall pea, is a subspecies of the pea plant characterized by its erect growth habit and slender stems. It produces small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in clusters, followed by green, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a flowering period that usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a cover crop.
