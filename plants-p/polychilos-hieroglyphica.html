Polychilos hieroglyphica

Polychilos hieroglyphica, commonly known as the Egyptian hieroglyph cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of Egypt.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in small, spine-like structures that help reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. Its name is derived from the resemblance of its spines to ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
