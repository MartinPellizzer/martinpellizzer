Pastinaca aurea

Pastinaca aurea, commonly known as the golden bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its edible roots, which have been used traditionally in some cultures.
