Pimelea pendens

Pimelea pendens, commonly known as the weeping bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bright red floral spikes that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Pimelea pendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
