Panicum glaucidulum

Panicum glaucidulum, commonly known as silver panicum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, grass-like leaves. Its foliage is characterized by a silvery sheen, which is particularly noticeable in dry conditions.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas.
