Phlomis melanantha f. pallidior

Phlomis melanantha f. pallidior, commonly known as the pale-flowered heliotrope, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family, and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, lavender to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its aromatic and potentially medicinal properties.

The pale-flowered heliotrope is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils in full sun.
