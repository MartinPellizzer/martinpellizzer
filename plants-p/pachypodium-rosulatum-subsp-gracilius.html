Pachypodium rosulatum subsp. gracilius

Pachypodium rosulatum subsp.

gracilius, commonly known as the slender pachypodium, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its tall, branching stem and sparse, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the dry season, which are pollinated by insects. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
