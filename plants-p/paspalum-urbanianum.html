Paspalum urbanianum

Paspalum urbanianum, commonly known as the urban paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and urban environments due to its adaptability.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions make it a common weed in many regions.
