Passiflora paraguayensis

Passiflora paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by both humans and animals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Passiflora paraguayensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
