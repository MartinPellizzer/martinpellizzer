Polygonum deflexipilosum

Polygonum deflexipilosum, commonly known as the flexed-hair knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bead-like seeds and often swollen nodes. The plant features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, glandular hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer.
