Paspalum scalare

Paspalum scalare, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and prairies. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Paspalum scalare is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
