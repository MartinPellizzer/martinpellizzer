Potentilla hyparctica

Potentilla hyparctica, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to alpine landscapes. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Potentilla hyparctica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
