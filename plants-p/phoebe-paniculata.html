Phoebe paniculata

Phoebe paniculata, commonly known as the fragrant panicled phoebe, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely cut, ovate leaves that turn bright yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Phoebe paniculata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
