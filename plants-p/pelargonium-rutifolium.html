Pelargonium rutifolium

Pelargonium rutifolium, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a compact growth habit.
