Persicaria pubescens var. acuminata

Persicaria pubescens var. acuminata, commonly known as the hairy smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
