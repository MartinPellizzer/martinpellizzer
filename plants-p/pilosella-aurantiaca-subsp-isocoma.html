Pilosella aurantiaca subsp. isocoma

Pilosella aurantiaca subsp.

isocoma, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive basal rosette formation.

It produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various regions.
