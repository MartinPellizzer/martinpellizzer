Phlomis paohsingensis

Phlomis paohsingensis, commonly known as the Chinese torchflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, spiky flower clusters that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Phlomis paohsingensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun.
