Passiflora cuellensis

Passiflora cuellensis, commonly known as the Cuell's passionflower, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of violet, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Passiflora cuellensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
