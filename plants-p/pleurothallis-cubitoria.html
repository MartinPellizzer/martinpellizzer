Pleurothallis cubitoria

Pleurothallis cubitoria, commonly known as the Cuban orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
