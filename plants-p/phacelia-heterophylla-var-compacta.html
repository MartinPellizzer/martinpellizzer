Phacelia heterophylla var. compacta

Phacelia heterophylla var. compacta, commonly known as the compact phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, varying in shape depending on the growth conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
