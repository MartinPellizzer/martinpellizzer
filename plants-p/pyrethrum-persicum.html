Pyrethrum persicum

Pyrethrum persicum, commonly known as Persian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant produces small, spherical flower heads composed of numerous ray and disk florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pyrethrum persicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and commercial purposes.
