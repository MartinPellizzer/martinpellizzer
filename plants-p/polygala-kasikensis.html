Polygala kasikensis

Polygala kasikensis, commonly known as the Thai milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a unique structure that resembles a shepherd's crook.

Polygala kasikensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
