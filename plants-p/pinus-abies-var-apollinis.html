Pinus abies var. apollinis

Pinus abies var. apollinis, commonly known as the Apollo fir, is a variant of the European silver fir.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated cones and needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted. This plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Alps, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Its botanical features include a conical shape and a slow growth rate, making it a unique ornamental species.

The Apollo fir is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
