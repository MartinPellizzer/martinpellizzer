Procris splendens

Procris splendens, commonly known as the Splendid Parnassia, is a striking member of the Parnassaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent red-orange spots and a distinctive hooded structure. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
