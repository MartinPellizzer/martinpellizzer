Panicum clutei

Panicum clutei, commonly known as the Clute's grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and wind-pollinated flowers.
