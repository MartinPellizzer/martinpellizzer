Platyaechmea smithiorum

Platyaechmea smithiorum, commonly known as the Smith's star cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, star-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is popular among succulent and bromeliad enthusiasts.
