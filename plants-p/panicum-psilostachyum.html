Panicum psilostachyum

Panicum psilostachyum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its adaptability, it is used in landscaping and as a forage crop in some areas.
