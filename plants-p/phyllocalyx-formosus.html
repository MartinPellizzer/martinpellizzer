Phyllocalyx formosus

Phyllocalyx formosus, commonly known as the Chinese hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a smooth texture and a pleasant fragrance.

Phyllocalyx formosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
