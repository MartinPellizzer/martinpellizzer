Piper tepuiense

Piper tepuiense, commonly known as the tepui pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tepuis of northern South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

Due to its restricted habitat and unique ecological setting, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
