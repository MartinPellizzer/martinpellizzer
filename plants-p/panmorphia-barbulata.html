Panmorphia barbulata

Panmorphia barbulata, commonly known as the feathered phantom orchid, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the misty highlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and delicate, feathery leaves that resemble the plumage of birds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on the trunks of ancient trees.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
