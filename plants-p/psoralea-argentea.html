Psoralea argentea

Psoralea argentea, commonly known as silver psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with numerous small leaflets that contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Psoralea argentea is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
