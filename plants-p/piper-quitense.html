Piper quitense

Piper quitense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Quitense pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green to red berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their aromatic properties.

The botanical characteristics of Piper quitense make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
