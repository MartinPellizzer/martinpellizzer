Passiflora leptoclada

Passiflora leptoclada, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
