Philodendron dyscarpium

Philodendron dyscarpium, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Philodendron dyscarpium is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
