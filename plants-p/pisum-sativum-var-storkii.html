Pisum sativum var. storkii

Pisum sativum var. storkii, commonly known as the stork's bill, is a variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pods that resemble a stork's bill, hence its common name. The plant is an annual legume with compound leaves and small, white or purple flowers. It produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental features and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
