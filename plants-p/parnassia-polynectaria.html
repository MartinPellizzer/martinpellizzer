Parnassia polynectaria

Parnassia polynectaria, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking white, petal-like bracts that surround its small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Parnassia polynectaria is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
