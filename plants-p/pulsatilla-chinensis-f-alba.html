Pulsatilla chinensis f. alba

Pulsatilla chinensis f. alba, commonly known as the white pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, trifoliate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pulsatilla chinensis f. alba is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
