Pollinia longiseta

Pollinia longiseta, commonly known as the long-hair pollinia orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique structure of pollinia, which are elongated, hairy masses that adhere to the insect vectors for pollination. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with brightly colored petals that attract pollinators.

The pollinia are specialized for efficient pollination, making this species an interesting subject for botanical study.
