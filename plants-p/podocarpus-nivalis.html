Podocarpus nivalis

Podocarpus nivalis, commonly known as the snow pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant in temperate climates.
