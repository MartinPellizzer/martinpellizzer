Polygala weberbaueri

Polygala weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing a compact growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Polygala weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
