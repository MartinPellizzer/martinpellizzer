Pentzia trifurcata

Pentzia trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branch pentzia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of vegetation adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region.

Pentzia trifurcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
