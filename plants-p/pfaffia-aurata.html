Pfaffia aurata

Pfaffia aurata, commonly known as golden root, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments. Its roots are the primary part used for medicinal purposes, containing bioactive compounds that have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Pfaffia aurata is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
