Polypodium argyrophanes

Polypodium argyrophanes, commonly known as the silver-veined polypody, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive silvery vein pattern. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The foliage is evergreen, and the plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.
