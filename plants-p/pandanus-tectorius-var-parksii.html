Pandanus tectorius var. parksii

Pandanus tectorius var. parksii, commonly known as the park's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds.

The fruit, which is a fibrous structure containing seeds, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
