Populus alba var. microphylla

Populus alba var. microphylla, commonly known as the white poplar with small leaves, is a variant of the white poplar native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, narrow leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows as a tall, fast-growing tree with a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is smooth and pale, often with a whitish or grayish hue.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
