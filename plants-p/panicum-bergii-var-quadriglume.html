Panicum bergii var. quadriglume

Panicum bergii var. quadriglume, commonly known as the four-awned panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets with four awns, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in open, grassy areas and is adapted to dry or semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms with small, inconspicuous flowers.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
