Pachyphytum cuicatecanum

Pachyphytum cuicatecanum, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its compact growth habit makes it an attractive choice for rock gardens or container planting.

It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring or early summer.
