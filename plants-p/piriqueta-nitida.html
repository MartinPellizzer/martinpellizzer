Piriqueta nitida

Piriqueta nitida, commonly known as the brightleaf cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with its succulent leaves and shallow root system. It produces small, edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

Its unique morphology makes it an important species for ecological studies in arid ecosystems.
