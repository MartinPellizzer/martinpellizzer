Passiflora hircina

Passiflora hircina, commonly known as the goat passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora hircina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
