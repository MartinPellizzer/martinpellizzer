Phillyrea angustifolia var. apiculata

Phillyrea angustifolia var. apiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in horticultural settings.
