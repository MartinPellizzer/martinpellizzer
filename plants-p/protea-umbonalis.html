Protea umbonalis

Protea umbonalis, commonly known as the mountain protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous pink to white bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.

The protea umbonalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
