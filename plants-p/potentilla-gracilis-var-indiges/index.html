Potentilla Gracilis Var. Indiges

Potentilla gracilis var. indiges is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. Known for its slender stems and clusters of small, yellow blooms, it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. This variant is distinguished by its unique growth habits and subtle variations in leaf structure compared to the standard Potentilla gracilis. It is often found in alpine meadows and open slopes where it plays a vital role in local ecosystems. Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
