Pilosocereus aurisetus subsp. werdermannianus

Pilosocereus aurisetus subsp.

werdermannianus, commonly known as the Werdermann's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical shape, often covered in white or pale green spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in dry gardens.
