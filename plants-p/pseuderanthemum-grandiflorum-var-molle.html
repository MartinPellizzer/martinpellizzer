Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. molle

Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaved pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.

The varietal name "molle" refers to its delicate, soft foliage, which distinguishes it from other varieties within the species.
