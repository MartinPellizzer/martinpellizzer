Paspalum mandiocanum var. ellipticum

Paspalum mandiocanum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its robust growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often found in disturbed areas and can be invasive in certain regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration.
