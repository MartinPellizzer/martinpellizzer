Plectranthus bullatus

Plectranthus bullatus, commonly known as the woolly mint or silver mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features hairy, silver-gray leaves that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically lavender to pink in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it both a decorative and potentially useful plant in horticulture.
