Podanthes

Podanthes, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants native to the island of Madagascar.

These plants are characterized by their showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and white. Podanthes species typically grow as shrubs or small trees, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. They thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The genus is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
