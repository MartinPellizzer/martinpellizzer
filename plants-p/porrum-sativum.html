Porrum sativum

Porrum sativum, commonly known as the common potato, is a tuberous plant belonging to the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is characterized by its underground stems that produce edible tubers, which are rich in starch and nutrients. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of hair.

The flowers are usually white or purple and are followed by small, green berries that contain the seeds.
