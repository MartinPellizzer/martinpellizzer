Polycycnis gratiosa

Polycycnis gratiosa, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant features a central stem with a distinctive golden ring at the base, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Polycycnis gratiosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
