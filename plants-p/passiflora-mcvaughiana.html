Passiflora mcvaughiana

Passiflora mcvaughiana, commonly known as the McVaugh passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Passiflora mcvaughiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
