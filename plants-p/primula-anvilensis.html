Primula anvilensis

Primula anvilensis, commonly known as the Anvil Mountain primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in the spring and early summer, adapting to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat. Its leaves are broad and hairy, which helps to retain moisture in the harsh alpine environment.

Primula anvilensis is a subject of interest for botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited geographic distribution.
