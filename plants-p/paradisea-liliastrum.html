Paradisea liliastrum

Paradisea liliastrum, commonly known as the blue-flowered lily, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its beauty and unique color.
