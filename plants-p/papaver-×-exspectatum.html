Papaver × exspectatum

Papaver × exspectatum, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver orientale (oriental poppy).

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its striking, large, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The seeds are produced in a fluffy, seed-bearing capsule that disperses easily by wind.
