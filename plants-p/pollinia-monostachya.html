Pollinia monostachya

Pollinia monostachya, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structure, with showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces pollinia, which are masses of pollen that are transferred by specific pollinators, typically bees and flies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, moist environments it inhabits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
