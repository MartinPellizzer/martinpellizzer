Piper ottoniifolium var. frutescens

Piper ottoniifolium var. frutescens, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, three-lobed berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
