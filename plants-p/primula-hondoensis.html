Primula hondoensis

Primula hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo primrose, is a flowering plant native to the island of Honshu in Japan.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Primula hondoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
