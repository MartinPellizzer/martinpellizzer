Polygonum hookeri

Polygonum hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often reddish stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
