Plantago samojedorum

Plantago samojedorum, commonly known as the Siberian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
