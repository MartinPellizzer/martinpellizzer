Prunus guanaiensis var. micradenia

Prunus guanaiensis var. micradenia, commonly known as the Andean cherry or *Araza*, is a species native to the subtropical and tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, greyish-bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling cherries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and the nutritional content of its fruit, which is rich in vitamin C. The species is also notable for its ecological importance in native forests and its use in traditional medicine.
