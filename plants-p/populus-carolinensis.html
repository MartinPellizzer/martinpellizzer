Populus carolinensis

Populus carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically 5-9 inches long and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins during the spring, followed by samaras that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streambanks.

The bark is smooth and gray when young, becoming fissured with age.
