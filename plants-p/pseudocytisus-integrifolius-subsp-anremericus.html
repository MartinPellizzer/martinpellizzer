Pseudocytisus integrifolius subsp. anremericus

Pseudocytisus integrifolius subsp.

anremericus, commonly known as the American broom, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically entire and glossy green.

The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It has a woody stem with thorn-like branches, typical of the Rhamnaceae family.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in woodland and open habitats.
