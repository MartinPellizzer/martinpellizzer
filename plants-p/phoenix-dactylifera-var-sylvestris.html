Phoenix dactylifera var. sylvestris

Phoenix dactylifera var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild date palm, is a subspecies of the date palm native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves, which are typically more robust and less ornate than those of cultivated date palms. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible dates, though the fruit is generally smaller and less sweet than commercial varieties. It is highly drought-tolerant and adapted to poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

This variety is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential for improving cultivated date palms through breeding programs.
