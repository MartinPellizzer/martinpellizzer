Polyalthia cauliflora

Polyalthia cauliflora, commonly known as the calico tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems and branches, a phenomenon known as cauliflory. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, oval fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in warm climates.
