Pycreus macrostachyos subsp. tremulus

Pycreus macrostachyos subsp.

tremulus, commonly known as the trembling pycreus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and delicate, fern-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
