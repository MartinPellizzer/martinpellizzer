Polygonum aviculare var. ramosissimum

Polygonum aviculare var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the prostrate knotgrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its sprawling growth habit.

It features small, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are often borne on short, erect stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
