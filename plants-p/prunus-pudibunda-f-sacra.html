Prunus pudibunda f. sacra

Prunus pudibunda f. sacra, commonly known as the sacred cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by bright red berries in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking crimson in the fall, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or containers.

Due to its cultural significance in Japanese traditions, it is often cultivated for its beauty and symbolic meaning.
