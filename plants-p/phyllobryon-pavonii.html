Phyllobryon pavonii

Phyllobryon pavonii, commonly known as the rainbow moss, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its vibrant, colorful fronds.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in shaded areas with nutrient-rich substrates. The plant's leaves are broad and strap-like, displaying a range of hues from green to red and purple, depending on light exposure and nutrient levels. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.

Due to its striking appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in freshwater gardens and aquariums.
