Pyrus communis proles cultrensis

Pyrus communis proles cultrensis, commonly known as the Cultrensis pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its medium to large size, with a spreading habit and a dense canopy. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, while the fruit is generally round and has a smooth, thin skin. The fruit ripens in late summer to early autumn and is prized for its sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often grown in temperate climates for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
