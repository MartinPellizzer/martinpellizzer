Potentilla argentea subsp. grandiceps

Potentilla argentea subsp.

grandiceps, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Silver cinquefoil is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
