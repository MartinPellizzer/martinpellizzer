Paspalum scrobiculatum var. polystachyum

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. polystachyum, commonly known as the giant paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Its adaptability makes it a useful plant for erosion control and habitat restoration.
