Phreatia mentosa

Phreatia mentosa, commonly known as the Mentosa orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are usually broad and dark green, adapted to capture limited light.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
