Parodia scoparia

Parodia scoparia, commonly known as the desert rose or moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the growing season. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it stores water in its stem to survive in dry environments.

Parodia scoparia is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
