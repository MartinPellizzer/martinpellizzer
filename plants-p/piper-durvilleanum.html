Piper durvilleanum

Piper durvilleanum, commonly known as the Durville's pepper vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
