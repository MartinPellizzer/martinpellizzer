Psoralea linearifolia

Psoralea linearifolia, commonly known as the slender psoralea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly in countries like Namibia and South Africa. The plant features slender, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

The species is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
