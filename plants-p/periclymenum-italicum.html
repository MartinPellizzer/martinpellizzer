Periclymenum italicum

Periclymenum italicum, commonly known as the Italian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its fruits are red berries that mature in autumn, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

Periclymenum italicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
