Pisophaca flexuosa

Pisophaca flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible rock cress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often at high altitudes. Its leaves are pinnately divided and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and the plant is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
