Piper parryanum

Piper parryanum, commonly known as the purple pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered purple berries that turn red when ripe. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting structure.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
