Peraltea lupinoides

Peraltea lupinoides, commonly known as the slender lupin, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several elliptical seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Peraltea lupinoides is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
