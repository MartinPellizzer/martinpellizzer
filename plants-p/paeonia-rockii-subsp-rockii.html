Paeonia rockii subsp. rockii

Paeonia rockii subsp.

rockii, commonly known as the Chinese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns.

The plant has palmately divided, dark green leaves and produces distinctive, woody, branched stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
