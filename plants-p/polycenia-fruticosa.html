Polycenia fruticosa

Polycenia fruticosa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical name reflects its shrub-like form and the presence of fruiting structures.

Polycenia fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
