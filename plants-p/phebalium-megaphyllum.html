Phebalium megaphyllum

Phebalium megaphyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and salty environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
