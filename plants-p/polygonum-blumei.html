Polygonum blumei

Polygonum blumei, commonly known as the Blume's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its stems are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs.

Polygonum blumei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
