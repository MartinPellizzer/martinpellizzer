Papaver radicatum subsp. porsildii

Papaver radicatum subsp.

porsildii, commonly known as the Porsild's poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It is characterized by its erect stems and distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Papaver radicatum subsp.

porsildii is often found in tundra and alpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
