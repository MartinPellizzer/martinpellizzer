Phlomis reticulata

Phlomis reticulata, commonly known as the carpet bugloss, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
