Patagonium muricatum

Patagonium muricatum, commonly known as the "wild strawberry" or "pepita de monte," is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and shrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is appreciated both for its ornamental value and culinary use.
