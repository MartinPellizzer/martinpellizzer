Paraphlomis javanica var. henryi

Paraphlomis javanica var. henryi, commonly known as Henry's narrowleaf pimpernel, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink or reddish, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
