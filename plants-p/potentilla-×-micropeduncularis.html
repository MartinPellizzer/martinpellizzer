Potentilla × micropeduncularis

Potentilla × micropeduncularis, commonly known as the dwarf potentilla, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow or orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
