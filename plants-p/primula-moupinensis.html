Primula moupinensis

Primula moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in forested areas or on rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense, velvety hair that helps retain moisture.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
