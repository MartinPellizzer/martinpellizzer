Pleurothallis divaricans

Pleurothallis divaricans, commonly known as the variable pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, intricate flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in two rows, giving it a unique, delicate appearance.

Due to its ornamental value and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
