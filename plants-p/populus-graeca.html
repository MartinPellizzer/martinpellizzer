Populus graeca

Populus graeca, commonly known as the Greek poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Populus graeca is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and papermaking.
