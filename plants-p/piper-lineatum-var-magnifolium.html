Piper lineatum var. magnifolium

Piper lineatum var. magnifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
