Pogonia hirsuta

Pogonia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pink tongs, is a striking orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, pinkish-red flowers with a distinctive, hairy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded habitats, often in areas with acidic soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated spike bearing several flowers.

Pogonia hirsuta is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
