Petesia distachya

Petesia distachya, commonly known as the wild sunflower, is an annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Petesia distachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
