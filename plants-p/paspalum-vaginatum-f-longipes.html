Paspalum vaginatum f. longipes

Paspalum vaginatum f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed napier grass, is a variant of the napier grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often used as forage for livestock. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like and composed of small, dense clusters of florets. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
