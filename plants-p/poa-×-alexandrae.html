Poa × alexandrae

Poa × alexandrae, commonly known as the Alexandra grass, is a hybrid species native to the Southern Alps of New Zealand.

It is a low-growing perennial grass with fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a clumping growth habit.

Poa × alexandrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
