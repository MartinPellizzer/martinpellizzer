Pseudosasa japonica f. purpurascens

Pseudosasa japonica f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf bamboo, is a cultivar of the Japanese bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking purple foliage, which provides a vibrant contrast to its green stems. This ornamental plant is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes. The leaves are lance-shaped and can grow up to 30 cm in length, with a smooth texture.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
