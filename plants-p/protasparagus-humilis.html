Protasparagus humilis

Protasparagus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf asparagus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces tiny, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a hardy ground cover.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover in dry landscapes.
