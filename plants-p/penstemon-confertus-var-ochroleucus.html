Penstemon confertus var. ochroleucus

Penstemon confertus var. ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued in gardens for its attractive blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
