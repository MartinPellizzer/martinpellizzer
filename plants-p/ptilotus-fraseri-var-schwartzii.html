Ptilotus fraseri var. schwartzii

Ptilotus fraseri var. schwartzii, commonly known as the pink ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique coloration and seasonal bloom make it a notable species in arid and semi-arid regions.
