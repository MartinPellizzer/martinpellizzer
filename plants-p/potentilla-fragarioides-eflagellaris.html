Potentilla fragarioides eflagellaris

Potentilla fragarioides eflagellaris, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and versatile choice for gardens and natural habitats.
