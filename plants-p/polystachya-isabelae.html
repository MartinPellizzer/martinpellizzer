Polystachya isabelae

Polystachya isabelae, commonly known as Isabel's bamboo, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, contributing to its bamboo-like appearance.

Polystachya isabelae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
