Phyllopentas austro-orientalis

Phyllopentas austro-orientalis, commonly known as the eastern forest fern, is a species of fern native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
