Potentilla verna var. ternata

Potentilla verna var. ternata, commonly known as the spring cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May, depending on the climate. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are usually oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
