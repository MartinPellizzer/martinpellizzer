Primula flava

Primula flava, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland or meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of down, giving them a soft texture.

Primula flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
