Pleurothallis giraldoi

Pleurothallis giraldoi, commonly known as the Giraldo's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are typically elongated and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
