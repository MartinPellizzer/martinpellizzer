Padus racemosa var. borealis

Padus racemosa var. borealis, commonly known as the northern white crabsapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape with serrated margins. Its showy white or pink flowers appear in racemes during late spring to early summer.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to red or yellow, providing food for various wildlife species.
