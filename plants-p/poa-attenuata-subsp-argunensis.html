Poa attenuata subsp. argunensis

Poa attenuata subsp.

argunensis, commonly known as the Argun grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
