Pouteria freitasii

Pouteria freitasii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pouteria* species, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red fruits resembling small cherries. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

It also has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
