Piper brachistopodum

Piper brachistopodum, commonly known as the short-footed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
