Polygala unguiculata

Polygala unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

Polygala unguiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
