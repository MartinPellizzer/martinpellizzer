Penstemon × moronensis

Penstemon × moronensis, commonly known as the hybrid penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Penstemon digitalis and Penstemon strictus. This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds adds to its ecological value.
