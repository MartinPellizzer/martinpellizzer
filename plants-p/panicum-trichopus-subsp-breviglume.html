Panicum trichopus subsp. breviglume

Panicum trichopus subsp.

breviglume, commonly known as the narrow-leaved panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its small, spikelet-based flowers are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait in grasses.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter glumes compared to other subspecies of Panicum trichopus.
