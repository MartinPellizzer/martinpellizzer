Pleurothallis setosa

Pleurothallis setosa, commonly known as the slender pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has thin, wiry stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a faint fragrance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted for capturing moisture from the air.

Pleurothallis setosa is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
