Pleioblastus amarus

Pleioblastus amarus, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, which are followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind.

Pleioblastus amarus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
