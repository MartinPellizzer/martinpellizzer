Pedatyphonium

Pedatyphonium, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in rocky or sandy soils.

Pedatyphonium is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
