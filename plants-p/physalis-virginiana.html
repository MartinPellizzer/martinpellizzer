Physalis virginiana

Physalis virginiana, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The fruit, often called a "pearl apple," is typically yellow or orange when mature and contains a single, edible seed. The plant produces small, white to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
