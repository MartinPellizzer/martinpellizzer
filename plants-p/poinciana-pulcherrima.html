Poinciana pulcherrima

Poinciana pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that turn red or orange during the dry season. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Poinciana pulcherrima thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
