Pilea impressa var. impressa

Pilea impressa var. impressa, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, waxy leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spathes, which are leaf-like structures enclosing the true flowers. It is a popular indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea impressa var. impressa prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
