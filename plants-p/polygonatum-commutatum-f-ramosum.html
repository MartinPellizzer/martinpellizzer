Polygonatum commutatum f. ramosum

Polygonatum commutatum f. ramosum, commonly known as the branched Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that ripen in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
