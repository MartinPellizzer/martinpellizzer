Potamogeton perfoliatus var. densifolius

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy or sandy bottoms.

Due to its rapid growth, it can form dense mats that provide habitat for aquatic life but may also impact water flow and clarity.
