Perezia simulata

Perezia simulata, commonly known as the false spider orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, spider-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its unique appearance.

Perezia simulata is an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity.
