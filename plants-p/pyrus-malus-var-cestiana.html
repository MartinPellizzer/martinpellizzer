Pyrus malus var. cestiana

Pyrus malus var. cestiana, commonly known as the Cestian pear, is a variety of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value, making it suitable for small gardens or as a decorative plant. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, while the flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in early spring. The fruit is small and often not suitable for eating but is prized for its ornamental appeal.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in a range of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
