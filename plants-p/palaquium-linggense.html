Palaquium linggense

Palaquium linggense, commonly known as the Bornean wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, waxy skin that turns red when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
