Platychaete carnosa

Platychaete carnosa, commonly known as the roughleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or magenta in color. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Platychaete carnosa is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
