Portulaca socotrana

Portulaca socotrana, commonly known as Socotra caltrop, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra in the Indian Ocean.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and leaves, which help it conserve water in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Portulaca socotrana is also notable for its unique, spiky fruit that contains several seeds.
