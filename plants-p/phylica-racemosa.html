Phylica racemosa

Phylica racemosa, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
