Polygala ruiziana

Polygala ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are accompanied by distinctive, leaf-like bracts.

Polygala ruiziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
