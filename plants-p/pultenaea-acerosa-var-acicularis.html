Pultenaea acerosa var. acicularis

Pultenaea acerosa var. acicularis, commonly known as the slender wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in regions such as Western Australia and South Australia. The plant features narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
