Psorospermum niloticum

Psorospermum niloticum, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or reddish fruits containing three seeds.

It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
