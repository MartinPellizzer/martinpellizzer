Polycarpaea teneriffae var. aristata

Polycarpaea teneriffae var. aristata, commonly known as the Teneriffe aristate, is a unique plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought and nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces numerous small, erect flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in dry gardens.
