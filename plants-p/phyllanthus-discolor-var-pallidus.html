Phyllanthus discolor var. pallidus

Phyllanthus discolor var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale variegated spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that often display a mix of green and pale yellow or white variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique leaf patterns and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
