Pleurothallis restrepioides

Pleurothallis restrepioides, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis restrepioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
