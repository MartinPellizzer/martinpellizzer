Protea stokoei

Protea stokoei, commonly known as the Stokoe's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the Eastern Cape province and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a threatened species in its native range.
