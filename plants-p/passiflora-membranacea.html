Passiflora membranacea

Passiflora membranacea, commonly known as the membranous passionflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, greenish-yellow berries that are often consumed by birds and other animals. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Passiflora membranacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
