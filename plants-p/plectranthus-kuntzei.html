Plectranthus kuntzei

Plectranthus kuntzei, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Plectranthus kuntzei is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
