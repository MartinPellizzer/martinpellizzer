Plectranthus striatus var. kerrii

Plectranthus striatus var. kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-green coloration and a minty fragrance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators.
