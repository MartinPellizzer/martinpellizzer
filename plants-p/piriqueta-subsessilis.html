Piriqueta subsessilis

Piriqueta subsessilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf piriqueta, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are arranged oppositely along the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Piriqueta subsessilis is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
