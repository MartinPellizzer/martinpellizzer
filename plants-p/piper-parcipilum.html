Piper parcipilum

Piper parcipilum, commonly known as the curly pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into elongated, curved berries. It thrives in warm, moist environments and is often found in shaded undergrowth.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines.
