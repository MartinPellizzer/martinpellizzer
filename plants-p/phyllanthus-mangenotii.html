Phyllanthus mangenotii

Phyllanthus mangenotii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a milky sap, which is the source of its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
