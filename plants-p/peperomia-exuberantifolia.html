Peperomia exuberantifolia

Peperomia exuberantifolia, commonly known as the Wooly Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, velvety foliage with a distinctive waxy texture, giving it a unique, almost fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. Its leaves are typically oval to ovate in shape and often have a slight curl at the edges.

Peperomia exuberantifolia is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.
