Procris pedunculata var. humblotii

Procris pedunculata var. humblotii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink petals with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
