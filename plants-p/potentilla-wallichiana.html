Potentilla wallichiana

Potentilla wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are compound, with multiple small leaflets, and it produces small, rounded fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla wallichiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
