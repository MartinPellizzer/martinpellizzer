Philadelphus bifidus

Philadelphus bifidus, commonly known as the bifid mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Philadelphus bifidus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
