Potamogeton vaginatus f. validior

Potamogeton vaginatus f. validior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, often with a smooth or slightly ribbed surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, emerging from the water surface. It thrives in slow to moderately flowing water and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

Its dense growth can provide important habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.
