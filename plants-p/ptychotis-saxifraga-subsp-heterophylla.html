Ptychotis saxifraga subsp. heterophylla

Ptychotis saxifraga subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the variable saxifrage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features basal leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic, with a hairy surface, and stem leaves that are more linear. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

This subspecies is notable for its morphological variability, which is reflected in its common name.
