Panicum aquaticum var. cartagoense

Panicum aquaticum var. cartagoense, commonly known as the Cartagena grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to moist conditions.

The species is also valued for its potential ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and habitat provision for aquatic life.
