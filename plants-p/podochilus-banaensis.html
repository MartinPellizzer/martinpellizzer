Podochilus banaensis

Podochilus banaensis, commonly known as the Banaense Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air, a feature common in epiphytic orchids.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
