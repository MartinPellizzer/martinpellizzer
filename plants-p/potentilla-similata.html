Potentilla similata

Potentilla similata, commonly known as the similar cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters of five. Its leaves are palmately divided into five leaflets, which are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla similata is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
