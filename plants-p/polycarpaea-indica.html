Polycarpaea indica

Polycarpaea indica, commonly known as the Indian polycarpaea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface, and its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
