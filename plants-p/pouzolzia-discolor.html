Pouzolzia discolor

Pouzolzia discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf pouzolzia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glabrous, often silver-gray leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its unique foliage and hardy nature make it a notable plant in botanical studies and horticulture.
