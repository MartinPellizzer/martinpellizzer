Ptyssiglottis fastidiosa

Ptyssiglottis fastidiosa, commonly known as the redroot pigweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dry, three-lobed seed capsules. It is commonly found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
