Phyllodes pulchella

Phyllodes pulchella, commonly known as the pink phyllodinea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are often pink or red in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Phyllodes pulchella is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
