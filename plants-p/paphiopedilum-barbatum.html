Paphiopedilum barbatum

Paphiopedilum barbatum, commonly known as the beardless orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
