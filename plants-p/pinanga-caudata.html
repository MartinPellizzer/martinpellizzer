Pinanga caudata

Pinanga caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed bamboo or slender bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that grow in a dense, clumping habit. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga caudata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its graceful appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
