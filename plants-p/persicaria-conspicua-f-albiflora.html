Persicaria conspicua f. albiflora

Persicaria conspicua f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and distinctive bracteate inflorescences. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The variety albiflora is distinguished by its pale to white flowers, contrasting with the more common red or pink blooms of the species.
