Passiflora hispidula

Passiflora hispidula, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of pink, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Passiflora hispidula is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display.
