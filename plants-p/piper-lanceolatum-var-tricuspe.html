Piper lanceolatum var. tricuspe

Piper lanceolatum var. tricuspe, commonly known as the three-toothed pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with three distinct tooth-like lobes, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal potential.
