Physalis filipendula

Physalis filipendula, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange or red berries enclosed in a papery calyx. These berries are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.
