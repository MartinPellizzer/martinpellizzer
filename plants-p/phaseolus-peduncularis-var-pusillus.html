Phaseolus peduncularis var. pusillus

Phaseolus peduncularis var. pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces small, edible pods that are typically harvested when immature and tender. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
