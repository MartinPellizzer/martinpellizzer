Petrorhagia cretica

Petrorhagia cretica, commonly known as the Cretan rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and adapted to conserve water, reflecting its drought-tolerant nature.

Petrorhagia cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping.
