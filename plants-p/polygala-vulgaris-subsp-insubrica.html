Polygala vulgaris subsp. insubrica

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

insubrica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant has slender, branching stems and small, usually greenish or yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
