Pygeum griffithii

Pygeum griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
