Pisum sativum var. cimitari

Pisum sativum var. cimitari, commonly known as the Cimitar pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its distinctive pod shape, which resembles a cimeter, a type of curved dagger. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and produces clusters of small, edible pods. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it blooms in the spring.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible seeds.
