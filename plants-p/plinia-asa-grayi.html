Plinia asa-grayi

Plinia asa-grayi, commonly known as the gray's persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically orange to red in color when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
