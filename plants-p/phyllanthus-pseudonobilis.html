Phyllanthus pseudonobilis

Phyllanthus pseudonobilis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
