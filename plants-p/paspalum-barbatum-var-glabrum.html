Paspalum barbatum var. glabrum

Paspalum barbatum var. glabrum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silky foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
