Pelargonium × opulinum

Pelargonium × opulinum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is a cross between different species of geranium, resulting in a variety with diverse foliage and flower colors. The plant typically has broad, velvety leaves and produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a range of growing conditions.

Pelargonium × opulinum is well-suited for gardens, containers, and as a bedding plant due to its adaptability and visual appeal.
