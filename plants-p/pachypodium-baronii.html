Pachypodium baronii

Pachypodium baronii, commonly known as the Baroni's bottle tree, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the dry season, typically in the summer months. Its leaves are spiny and shed during periods of drought, reducing water loss.

Pachypodium baronii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry environments.
