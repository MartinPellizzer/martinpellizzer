Paepalanthus myocephalon var. myocephalon

Paepalanthus myocephalon var. myocephalon, commonly known as the yellow rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a tight cluster near the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is often found in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
