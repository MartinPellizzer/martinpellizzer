Potentilla × beckii

Potentilla × beckii, commonly known as the evergreen potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.

Potentilla × beckii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for alpine and coastal landscapes.
