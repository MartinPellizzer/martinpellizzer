Plagiopetalum esquirolii var. serratum

Plagiopetalum esquirolii var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated plagiopetalum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have serrated edges, contributing to its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
