Picea pungens argentea-pendula

Picea pungens argentea-pendula, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce, is a cultivar of the blue spruce tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking silvery-blue needles that are tightly packed and emit a distinct pine scent. The plant has a conical shape with a slow growth rate, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes. Its foliage turns a deep blue in winter, adding visual interest during the colder months.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
