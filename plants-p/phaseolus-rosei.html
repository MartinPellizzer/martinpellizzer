Phaseolus rosei

Phaseolus rosei, commonly known as the rose bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, pink or white flowers. The plant has compound leaves with a pinnate structure, and its pods contain edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its legume characteristics and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its growth pattern and morphology make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
