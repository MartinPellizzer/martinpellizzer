Pterochloris

Pterochloris, commonly known as the green alga, is a genus of freshwater green algae belonging to the order Caulerpa.

It is characterized by its simple, branching thallus and the presence of a single nucleus in each cell. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, and instead consists of a network of filaments. It is often found in aquatic environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can form dense mats.
