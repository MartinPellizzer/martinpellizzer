Parthenium tetraneuris

Parthenium tetraneuris, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive grayish-green color, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

The plant is known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
