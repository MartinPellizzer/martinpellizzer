Psammophora saxicola

Psammophora saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola sandplant, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Its flowers are usually greenish-white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds.

Due to its adaptations to harsh environments, Psammophora saxicola is well-suited for survival in nutrient-poor, drought-prone habitats.
