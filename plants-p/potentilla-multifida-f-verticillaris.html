Potentilla multifida f. verticillaris

Potentilla multifida f. verticillaris, commonly known as the whorled cinquefoil, is a variant of the multifida potentilla species.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and hardy nature.
