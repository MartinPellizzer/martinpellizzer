Piper cardiophyllum

Piper cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heartleaf pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is not widely cultivated for culinary use.
