Puya pendula

Puya pendula, commonly known as the hanging puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that bear clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that form a rosette, helping to collect moisture from the air.

Puya pendula is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and dramatic appearance.
