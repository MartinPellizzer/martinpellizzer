Pseuderanthemum lindavianum

Pseuderanthemum lindavianum, commonly known as the Lindau's false jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, often with contrasting yellow markings. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Pseuderanthemum lindavianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
