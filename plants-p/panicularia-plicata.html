Panicularia plicata

Panicularia plicata, commonly known as the pleated panicled grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often pleated or folded, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as marshes and wet meadows, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Panicularia plicata plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
