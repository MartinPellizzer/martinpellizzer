Passiflora melahelos

Passiflora melahelos, commonly known as the Melahelos passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
