Polygonum arianum

Polygonum arianum, commonly known as Armenian bistort, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Polygonum arianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
