Poa jeremiadis

Poa jeremiadis, commonly known as the Jeremiad grass, is a species of grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Poa jeremiadis is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
