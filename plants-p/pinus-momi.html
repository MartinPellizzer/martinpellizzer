Pinus momi

Pinus momi, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often have a reddish-brown hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Pinus momi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic beauty.
