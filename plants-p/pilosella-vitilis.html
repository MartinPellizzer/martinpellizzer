Pilosella vitilis

Pilosella vitilis, commonly known as the whitehead mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella vitilis is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
