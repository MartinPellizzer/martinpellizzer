Pandanus exaltatus

Pandanus exaltatus, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional weaving practices.

The species is well adapted to coastal environments and can thrive in saline soils.
