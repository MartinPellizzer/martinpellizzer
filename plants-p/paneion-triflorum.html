Paneion triflorum

Paneion triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered paneion, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters of three, giving it its scientific name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunlit areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
