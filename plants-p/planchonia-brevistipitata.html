Planchonia brevistipitata

Planchonia brevistipitata, commonly known as the short-stalked pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear white or pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in wet, coastal environments.

Planchonia brevistipitata is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in various island communities.
