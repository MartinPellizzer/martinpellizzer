Pseudodracontium anomalum

Pseudodracontium anomalum, commonly known as the strange dragon's tail, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, ribbon-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by spathaceous berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
