Phacelia setigera var. setigera

Phacelia setigera var. setigera, commonly known as blue phacelia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
