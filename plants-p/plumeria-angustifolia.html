Plumeria angustifolia

Plumeria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
