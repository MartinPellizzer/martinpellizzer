Philibertia parviflora var. brevituba

Philibertia parviflora var. brevituba, commonly known as the narrow-tubed filbert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
