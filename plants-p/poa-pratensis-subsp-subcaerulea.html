Poa pratensis subsp. subcaerulea

Poa pratensis subsp.

subcaerulea, commonly known as blue meadowgrass, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and forms dense, tufted clumps.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to whitish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in turfgrass mixtures.
