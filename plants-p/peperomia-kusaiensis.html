Peperomia kusaiensis

Peperomia kusaiensis, commonly known as the Kusaiensis Peperomia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red in color. It is often grown as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia kusaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
