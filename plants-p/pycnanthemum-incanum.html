Pycnanthemum incanum

Pycnanthemum incanum, commonly known as grayhead mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodlands and meadows. It has a strong mint-like aroma and is often used in herbal teas.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems.
