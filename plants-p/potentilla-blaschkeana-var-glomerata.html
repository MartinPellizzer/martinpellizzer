Potentilla blaschkeana var. glomerata

Potentilla blaschkeana var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of five oval-shaped leaflets, which are hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
