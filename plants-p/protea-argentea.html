Protea argentea

Protea argentea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to cream-colored flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a silvery or greyish hue, contributing to its common name.

Protea argentea is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
