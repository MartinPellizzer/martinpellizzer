Phytelephas ruizii

Phytelephas ruizii, commonly known as the Amazonian palm, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating its reproduction. Its fruit is a hard, spiny capsule that contains edible seeds, which are used in traditional practices.

Phytelephas ruizii plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat and is valued for its ornamental and cultural importance.
