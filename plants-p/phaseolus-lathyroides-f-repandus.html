Phaseolus lathyroides f. repandus

Phaseolus lathyroides f. repandus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that are typically green when immature. It is a climbing vine that thrives in warm climates and requires support for growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities and leguminous properties.
