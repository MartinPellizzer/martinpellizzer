Pfaffia sericea var. paraguariensis

Pfaffia sericea var. paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers and is often found in humid, tropical regions. Its most notable feature is the use of its leaves in the preparation of a traditional beverage.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
