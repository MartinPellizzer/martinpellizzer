Pilocereus haworthii

Pilocereus haworthii, commonly known as the Moon cactus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, rounded stems that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
