Palmijuncus tetrastichus

Palmijuncus tetrastichus, commonly known as the slender palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fruits that are typically round and fibrous. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
