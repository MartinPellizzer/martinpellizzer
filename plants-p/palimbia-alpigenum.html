Palimbia alpigenum

Palimbia alpigenum, commonly known as the alpine palimbia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions. Its leaves are thick and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps to retain moisture and reduce water loss.

Palimbia alpigenum is often found in rocky soils and is a valuable species for ecological studies due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude life.
