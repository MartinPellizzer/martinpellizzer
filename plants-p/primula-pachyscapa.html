Primula pachyscapa

Primula pachyscapa, commonly known as the thick-stemmed primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features a distinctive, thickened stem that supports its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring, range in color from white to pale yellow, adding ornamental value to gardens.
