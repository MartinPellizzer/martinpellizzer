Pothos longivaginatus

Pothos longivaginatus, commonly known as the velvet pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its adaptability and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
