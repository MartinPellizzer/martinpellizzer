Polyxena ensifolia

Polyxena ensifolia, commonly known as the swordleaf polyxena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
