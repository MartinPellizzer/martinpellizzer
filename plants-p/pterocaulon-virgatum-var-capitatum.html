Pterocaulon virgatum var. capitatum

Pterocaulon virgatum var. capitatum, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with fleshy, ribbed stems that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels near the base of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a bluish-green hue with a waxy texture.

This species is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
