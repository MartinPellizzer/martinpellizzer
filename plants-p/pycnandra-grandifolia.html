Pycnandra grandifolia

Pycnandra grandifolia, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has a dense, rounded canopy and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy appearance.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
