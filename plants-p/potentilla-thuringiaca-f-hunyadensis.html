Potentilla thuringiaca f. hunyadensis

Potentilla thuringiaca f. hunyadensis, commonly known as the Hungarian potentilla, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Central Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
