Polypodium megalolepis

Polypodium megalolepis, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polypodium megalolepis is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
