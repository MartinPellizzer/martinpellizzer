Parnassia mairei

Parnassia mairei, commonly known as the Maire's parnassia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Parnassiaceae and is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers with a greenish throat. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Parnassia mairei is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
