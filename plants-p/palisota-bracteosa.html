Palisota bracteosa

Palisota bracteosa, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces berries that are edible for some bird species.

Palisota bracteosa is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
