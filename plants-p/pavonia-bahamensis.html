Pavonia bahamensis

Pavonia bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Pavonia bahamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
