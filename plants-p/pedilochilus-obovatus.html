Pedilochilus obovatus

Pedilochilus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate pedilochilus, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that is obovate in shape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
