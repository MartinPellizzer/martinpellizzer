Pycnanthemum tullia

Pycnanthemum tullia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-to-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it often grows in moist to dry woodland habitats. It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties.

Pycnanthemum tullia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
