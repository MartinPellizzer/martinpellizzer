Polyalthia lateriflora var. macrophylla

Polyalthia lateriflora var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically large and glossy, and its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
