Potamogeton pectinatus f. spissus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. spissus, commonly known as the dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it is known for its rapid growth in still or slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-flowing rivers.
