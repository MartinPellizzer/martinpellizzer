Potentilla taurica var. pirotensis

Potentilla taurica var. pirotensis, commonly known as the Pirotinskaya potentilla, is a flowering shrub native to southern Russia and parts of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, with a greyish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
