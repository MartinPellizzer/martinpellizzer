Panicum corvipes

Panicum corvipes, commonly known as the black panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are characteristic of the Panicum genus. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it both a weed and a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
