Prunus × dasycarpa f. persicifolia

Prunus × dasycarpa f. persicifolia, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a hybrid ornamental plant characterized by its dense, double blooms.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is prized for its showy, clustered flowers that appear in spring. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing an attractive backdrop to the blossoms. This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth and flowering.
