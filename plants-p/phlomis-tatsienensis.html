Phlomis tatsienensis

Phlomis tatsienensis, commonly known as the Tibetan mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
