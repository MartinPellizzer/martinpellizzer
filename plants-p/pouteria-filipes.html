Pouteria filipes

Pouteria filipes, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's fruit is a capsule that contains the edible seeds, which are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. The plant has a complex root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
