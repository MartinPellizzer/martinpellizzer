Pyrus pyraster var. vertesensis

Pyrus pyraster var. vertesensis, commonly known as the Hungarian pear, is a variant of the common pear that is native to Hungary.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The fruit is typically small, sweet, and edible, with a texture similar to that of the common pear. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

This variety is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and traditional agriculture.
