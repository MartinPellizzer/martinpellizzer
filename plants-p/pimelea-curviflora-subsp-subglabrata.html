Pimelea curviflora subsp. subglabrata

Pimelea curviflora subsp.

subglabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved gold flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often in heathlands and open forests.

The plant produces small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its grey-green foliage. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, which gives it the subspecies name "subglabrata.".

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
