Pyrus narynensis

Pyrus narynensis, commonly known as the Naryn pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a pear-shaped apple, typically green when mature, and is known for its crisp, sweet flavor. This plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and horticultural studies.
