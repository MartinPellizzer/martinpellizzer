Phlebocarya pilosissima

Phlebocarya pilosissima, commonly known as the silky phlebocarya, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptations.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
