Poa orientalis

Poa orientalis, commonly known as the oriental feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and fluffy, silver-green inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft, feathery texture that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Poa orientalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
