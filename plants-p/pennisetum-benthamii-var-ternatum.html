Pennisetum benthamii var. ternatum

Pennisetum benthamii var. ternatum, commonly known as the tufted hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
