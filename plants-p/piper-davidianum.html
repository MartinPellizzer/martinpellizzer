Piper davidianum

Piper davidianum, commonly known as the Chinese pepper or David's pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are initially green and turn red when mature, resembling tiny peppers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
