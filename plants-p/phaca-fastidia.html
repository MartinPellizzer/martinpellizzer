Phaca fastidia

Phaca fastidia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features a sturdy, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its unique conical flower head is composed of both ray and disk flowers, making it an attractive addition to gardens.

Phaca fastidia is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
