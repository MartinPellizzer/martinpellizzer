Penstemon × crideri

Penstemon × crideri, commonly known as the Crider's beardtongue, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in warmer climates.
