Polystichum tenggerense

Polystichum tenggerense, commonly known as the Tengger fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, and deeply divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to any woodland setting.
