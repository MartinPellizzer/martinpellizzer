Prasophyllum fosteri

Prasophyllum fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in open, sunny environments.

Prasophyllum fosteri is a short-lived perennial, with its flowering period occurring during the cooler months of spring and autumn.
