Pigea filiformis

Pigea filiformis, commonly known as the slender pigeonwruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, orange-colored berries. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in dune environments.

Pigea filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal flowers.
