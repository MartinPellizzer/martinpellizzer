Phyllanthus gracilis

Phyllanthus gracilis, commonly known as the slender phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive, fragrant yellowish-green flower cluster. Its fruit is a small, spherical capsule that contains several seeds.

Phyllanthus gracilis is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates.
