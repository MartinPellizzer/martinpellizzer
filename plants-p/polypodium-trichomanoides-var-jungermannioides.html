Polypodium trichomanoides var. jungermannioides

Polypodium trichomanoides var. jungermannioides, commonly known as the Jungermannioid fern, is a small, evergreen fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and its ability to form dense mats on rocky or shaded surfaces. The fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, finely divided structure with a serrated margin. This variety is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streams.

Its botanical features make it a popular species for rock gardens and moist, shaded landscapes.
