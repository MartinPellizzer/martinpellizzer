Polygala tehuelchum

Polygala tehuelchum, commonly known as the Tehuelchum milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
