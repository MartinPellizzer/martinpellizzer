Piper heptandrum var. crassispicum

Piper heptandrum var. crassispicum, commonly known as the thick-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, thick leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Piper genus.
