Polygala cestrifolia

Polygala cestrifolia, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and linear, while its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

It is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands.
