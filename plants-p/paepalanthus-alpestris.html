Paepalanthus alpestris

Paepalanthus alpestris, commonly known as the alpine pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its ornamental value.
