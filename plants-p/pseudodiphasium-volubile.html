Pseudodiphasium volubile

Pseudodiphasium volubile, commonly known as the climbing grass or false diphasium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Pseudodiphasium volubile is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.
