Pithecellobium pedicellare

Pithecellobium pedicellare, commonly known as the dwarf poinciana, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
