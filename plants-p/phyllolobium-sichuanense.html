Phyllolobium sichuanense

Phyllolobium sichuanense, commonly known as the Sichuan phyllolobium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to its arid environment. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
