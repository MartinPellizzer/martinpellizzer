Pedicularis bipinnatifida

Pedicularis bipinnatifida, commonly known as the twin-lobed lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a unique structure with two pairs of leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
