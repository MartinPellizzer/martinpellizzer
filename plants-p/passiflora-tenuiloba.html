Passiflora tenuiloba

Passiflora tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like leaves and striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora tenuiloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
