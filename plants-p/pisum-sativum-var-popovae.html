Pisum sativum var. popovae

Pisum sativum var. popovae, commonly known as the Popov's pea, is a botanical variety of the pea plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically flowers in early summer and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
