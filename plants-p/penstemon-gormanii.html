Penstemon gormanii

Penstemon gormanii, commonly known as Gorman's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of downy hair.

Penstemon gormanii is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
