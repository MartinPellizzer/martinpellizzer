Phyllocladus billardierei

Phyllocladus billardierei, commonly known as the silver pine, is a unique evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage, which results from the dense covering of small, scale-like leaves. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters, forming a tall, slender trunk. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils, making it an important component of certain Australian ecosystems.
