Primulina rubribracteata

Primulina rubribracteata, commonly known as the red-bract primula, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant and in botanical gardens.
