Patagonium crassicaule

Patagonium crassicaule, commonly known as the thick-stemmed Patagonian grass, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is well adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Patagonium crassicaule plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native range.
