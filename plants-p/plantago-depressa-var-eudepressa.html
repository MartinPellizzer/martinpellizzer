Plantago depressa var. eudepressa

Plantago depressa var. eudepressa, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features broad, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

This variety is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
