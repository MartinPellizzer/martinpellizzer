Potentilla dissecta var. rubricaulis

Potentilla dissecta var. rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feathery leaves and showy clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its stems are distinctive for their reddish coloration, which gives it its common name.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
