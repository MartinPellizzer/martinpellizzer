Pseuderanthemum subviscosum

Pseuderanthemum subviscosum, commonly known as the sticky pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and often have a slightly sticky surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers, which are often borne in terminal or axillary inflorescences.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
