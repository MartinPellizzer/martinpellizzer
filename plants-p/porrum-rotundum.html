Porrum rotundum

Porrum rotundum, commonly known as the round bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, round, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a climbing or trailing structure.

Porrum rotundum is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
