Protea pulchra

Protea pulchra, commonly known as the sweet protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant features leathery, evergreen leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
