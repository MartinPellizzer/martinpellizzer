Psidium sellowianum

Psidium sellowianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
