Prunus buergeri

Prunus buergeri, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively easy to care for.

However, it is susceptible to pests and diseases, requiring regular maintenance.
