Polygonum bornmuelleri

Polygonum bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, clustered inflorescences that bear small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant typically has smooth, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Polygonum bornmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
