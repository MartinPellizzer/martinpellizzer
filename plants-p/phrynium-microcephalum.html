Phrynium microcephalum

Phrynium microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking orange or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it adaptable to various soil types.

Phrynium microcephalum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
