Pandanus temehaniensis

Pandanus temehaniensis, commonly known as the Temehani pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of Temehani in the South Pacific, specifically in the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the genus.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
