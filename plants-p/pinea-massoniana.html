Pinea massoniana

Pinea massoniana, commonly known as the Chinese pine or Masson's pine, is a species of coniferous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of two. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. Pinea massoniana is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
