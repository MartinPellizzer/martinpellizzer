Pavonia intermedia

Pavonia intermedia, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its vibrant floral display.

Pavonia intermedia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
