Pinus devoniana subsp. wincesteriana

Pinus devoniana subsp.

wincesteriana, commonly known as the Wincester pine, is a subspecies native to the southeastern United States. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves.

The cones are typically small and woody, with persistent scales that provide habitat for various wildlife. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem.
