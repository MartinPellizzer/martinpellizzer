Pitcairnia ventidirecta

Pitcairnia ventidirecta, commonly known as the ventidirecta pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. It produces inflorescences with striking, long-lasting bracts that are often brightly colored.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
