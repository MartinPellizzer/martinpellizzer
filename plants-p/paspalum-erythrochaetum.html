Paspalum erythrochaetum

Paspalum erythrochaetum, commonly known as red-hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and distinctive reddish hairs on its leaf blades. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Paspalum erythrochaetum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
