Planchonella tenuipes

Planchonella tenuipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub in humid, lowland forests. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, winged seeds.

Planchonella tenuipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
