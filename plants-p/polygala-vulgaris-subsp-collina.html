Polygala vulgaris subsp. collina

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

collina, commonly known as the common milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in soil stabilization.
