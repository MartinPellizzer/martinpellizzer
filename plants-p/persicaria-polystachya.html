Persicaria polystachya

Persicaria polystachya, commonly known as the spiked smartweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pink to red flowers at the ends of its stems, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria polystachya is known for its ability to thrive in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along riverbanks.
