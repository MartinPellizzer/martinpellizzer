Palmifolium kickxii

Palmifolium kickxii, commonly known as the Kikii palm, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its growth habit is typically slow, making it a long-lived plant with potential ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
