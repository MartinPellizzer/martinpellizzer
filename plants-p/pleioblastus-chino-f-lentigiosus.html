Pleioblastus chino f. lentigiosus

Pleioblastus chino f. lentigiosus, commonly known as the Chinese forest bamboo, is a clump-forming bamboo species native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, dark green culms with a distinctive mottled or speckled appearance on the lower part of the stems. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value. This plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscape design for its elegant, upright growth habit.

It is valued for its ability to form dense stands and its adaptability to various soil types.
