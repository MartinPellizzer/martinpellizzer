Poa conglomerata

Poa conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and compact inflorescence, which consists of tightly packed spikelets. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Poa conglomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its fine texture and resilience.
