Prismatocarpus roelloides var. interruptus

Prismatocarpus roelloides var. interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted prismatocarpus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its unique, segmented stem structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, which are typically clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its adaptation to humid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its distinctive growth form.
