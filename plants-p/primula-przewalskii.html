Primula przewalskii

Primula przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
