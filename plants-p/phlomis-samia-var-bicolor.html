Phlomis samia var. bicolor

Phlomis samia var. bicolor, commonly known as the Two-color Phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, two-toned flowers that range from purple to white, often with a contrasting central stripe. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its bloom period typically occurs in late summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the garden.
