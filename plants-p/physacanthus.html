Physacanthus

Physacanthus, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescences. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. They typically thrive in moist, shaded environments and are often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
