Panicum epacridifolium

Panicum epacridifolium, commonly known as the silver panicum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
