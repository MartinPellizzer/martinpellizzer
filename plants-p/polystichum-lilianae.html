Polystichum lilianae

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate. The plant thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense clusters of foliage, contributing to its ornamental value.

Polystichum lilianae is valued in horticulture for its attractive, architectural appearance and its ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
