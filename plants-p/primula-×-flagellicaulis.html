Primula × flagellicaulis

Primula × flagellicaulis, commonly known as the flagellate primrose, is a hybrid species within the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often pendulous flower stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and delicate floral display.
