Phaseolus lunatus f. limenanus

Phaseolus lunatus f. limenanus, commonly known as the limenan bean, is a botanical variant of the common lima bean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved pods, which are typically green when immature. The plant is native to the Andean region of South America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

The seeds, once mature, are usually white or pale green and are valued for their nutritional content and culinary uses.
