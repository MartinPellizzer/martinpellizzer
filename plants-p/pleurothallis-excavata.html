Pleurothallis excavata

Pleurothallis excavata, commonly known as the "Cave Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the cooler months.

Pleurothallis excavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
