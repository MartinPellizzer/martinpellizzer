Pulsatilloides glaucifolia

Pulsatilloides glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue glaucous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy perennial in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
