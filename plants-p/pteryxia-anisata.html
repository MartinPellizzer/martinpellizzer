Pteryxia anisata

Pteryxia anisata, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
