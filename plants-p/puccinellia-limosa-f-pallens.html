Puccinellia limosa f. pallens

Puccinellia limosa f. pallens, commonly known as the pale form of saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of stabilized dune ecosystems.

Its ecological significance includes soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
