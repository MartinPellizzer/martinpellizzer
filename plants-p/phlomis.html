Phlomis

Phlomis, commonly known as torchroot or gay feverfew, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

These plants are typically shrubs or perennials, characterized by their square stems and opposite leaves. They produce showy, often yellow or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Phlomis species are native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value and some have been used in traditional medicine.
