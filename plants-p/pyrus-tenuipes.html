Pyrus tenuipes

Pyrus tenuipes, commonly known as the slender-leaved pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer.

Pyrus tenuipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to cooler, moist climates.
