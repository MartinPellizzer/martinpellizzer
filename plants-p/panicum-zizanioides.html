Panicum zizanioides

Panicum zizanioides, commonly known as Johnsongrass, is a perennial grass native to the warm temperate and tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing two to three florets, which are typically pale green to brown in color. Johnsongrass is known for its aggressive growth and is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive nature.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.
