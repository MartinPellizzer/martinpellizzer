Poa olajensis

Poa olajensis, commonly known as the Olajen grass, is a species of grass native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is a distinguishing feature of the Poa genus.

Poa olajensis plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and geographic distribution.
