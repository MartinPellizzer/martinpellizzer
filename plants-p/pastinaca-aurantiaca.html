Pastinaca aurantiaca

Pastinaca aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange root or golden burdock, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by fluffy, seed-bearing burrs. Its roots are thick, fleshy, and often orange in color, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
