Pogogyne serpylloides var. typica

Pogogyne serpylloides var. typica, commonly known as the typical bog gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland and bog environments in North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and requires consistent moisture.

This species plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
