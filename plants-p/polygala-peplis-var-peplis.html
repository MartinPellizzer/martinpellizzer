Polygala peplis var. peplis

Polygala peplis var. peplis, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
