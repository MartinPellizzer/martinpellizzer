Potentilla interrupta

Potentilla interrupta, commonly known as the American cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are often hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Potentilla interrupta is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
