Podocarpus ferrugineus

Podocarpus ferrugineus, commonly known as the red-caleda or red-cedar, is a medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Podocarpus ferrugineus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
