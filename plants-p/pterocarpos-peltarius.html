Pterocarpos peltarius

Pterocarpos peltarius, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing flattened seeds. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist forested areas.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
