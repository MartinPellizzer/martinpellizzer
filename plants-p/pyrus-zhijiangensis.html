Pyrus zhijiangensis

Pyrus zhijiangensis, commonly known as the Zhijiang pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with broad leaves and clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant produces pome fruits, which are typically round and have a crisp, juicy texture. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and botany.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
