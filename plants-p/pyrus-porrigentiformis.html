Pyrus porrigentiformis

Pyrus porrigentiformis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
