Polylepis pepei

Polylepis pepei, commonly known as the Andean cloud forest tree, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments. This tree plays a crucial role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species. Polylepis pepei is also valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice for horticultural gardens.

However, it faces threats from habitat loss and climate change, which have led to its classification as a vulnerable species.
