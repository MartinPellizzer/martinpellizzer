Platystele londonoana

Platystele londonoana, commonly known as the London's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, white, tubular flowers and a short, creeping rhizome. Native to the cloud forests of Colombia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high rainfall. The plant has very specific habitat requirements, making it highly susceptible to habitat loss and environmental changes.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is listed as endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
