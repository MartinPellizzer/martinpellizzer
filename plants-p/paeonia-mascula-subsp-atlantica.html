Paeonia mascula subsp. atlantica

Paeonia mascula subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers with five to eight petals.

The plant produces compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are typically green and hairy. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers in shades of pink to red.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and historical significance.
