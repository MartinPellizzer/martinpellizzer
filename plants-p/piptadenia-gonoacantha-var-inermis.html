Piptadenia gonoacantha var. inermis

Piptadenia gonoacantha var. inermis, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has compound leaves with leaflets arranged in pairs, and it produces long, slender pods that contain flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking floral display.

The species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and ecological restoration projects.
