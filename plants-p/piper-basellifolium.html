Piper basellifolium

Piper basellifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a mild peppery scent.

Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to yellow or red when mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
