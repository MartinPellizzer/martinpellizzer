Pisonia areolata

Pisonia areolata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pisonia areolata is also known for its ability to attract birds and insects due to its nectar-rich flowers.
