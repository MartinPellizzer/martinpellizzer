Passiflora panamensis

Passiflora panamensis, commonly known as the Panama passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Passiflora panamensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
