Paphiopedilum ecuadorense

Paphiopedilum ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-shaped lip that is typically yellow with purple markings. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Paphiopedilum ecuadorense is a rare and protected species, making it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
