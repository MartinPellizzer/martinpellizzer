Phlox hoodii subsp. muscoides

Phlox hoodii subsp.

muscoides, commonly known as the mountain phlox, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. Its showy, clustered flowers are typically pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging alpine environments.
