Pisum tuberosum

Pisum tuberosum, commonly known as the Andean pea or batata, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the production of tuberous roots that are edible. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, and it produces small, green pods that contain seeds.

The tubers, which are the primary edible part, are rich in carbohydrates and have a sweet, nutty flavor.
