Pyrus pyraster var. budensis

Pyrus pyraster var. budensis, commonly known as the Budensis pear, is a variant of the European pear species.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruit with a smooth, yellowish skin that often develops a slight russeting. The plant produces sweet, aromatic fruit that is typically harvested in late summer. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice for gardens and orchards.
