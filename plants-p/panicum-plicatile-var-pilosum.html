Panicum plicatile var. pilosum

Panicum plicatile var. pilosum, commonly known as silky grass or silky panicum, is a perennial grass species characterized by its finely textured, silver-green foliage.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are often wind-dispersed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
