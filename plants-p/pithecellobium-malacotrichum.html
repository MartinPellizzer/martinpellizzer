Pithecellobium malacotrichum

Pithecellobium malacotrichum, commonly known as the golden pod tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and distinctive yellow, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root system.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical environments.
