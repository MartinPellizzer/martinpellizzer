Primulina fordii

Primulina fordii, commonly known as the Ford's primula, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have contributed to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
