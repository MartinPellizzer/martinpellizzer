Platyclinis cucumerina

Platyclinis cucumerina, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often feathery in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling small peppers. The fruits are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its culinary uses.
