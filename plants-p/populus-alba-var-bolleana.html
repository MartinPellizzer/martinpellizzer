Populus alba var. bolleana

Populus alba var. bolleana, commonly known as the white poplar or white willow, is a variant of the white poplar species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its white, silky undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping.
