Prasophyllum ovale var. triglochin

Prasophyllum ovale var. triglochin, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. It is a low-growing species that thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
