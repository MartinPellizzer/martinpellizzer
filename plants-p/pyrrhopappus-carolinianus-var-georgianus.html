Pyrrhopappus carolinianus var. georgianus

Pyrrhopappus carolinianus var. georgianus, commonly known as the Georgia goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, known as bloom. It typically grows in dry, open habitats like roadsides and sandy soils.

Its botanical structure includes a robust root system and a branching stem that supports its upright growth habit.
