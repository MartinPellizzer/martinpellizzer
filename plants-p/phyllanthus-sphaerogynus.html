Phyllanthus sphaerogynus

Phyllanthus sphaerogynus, commonly known as the globe-leaf phyllanthus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
