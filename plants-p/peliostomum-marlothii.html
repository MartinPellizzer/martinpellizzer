Peliostomum marlothii

Peliostomum marlothii, commonly known as the marlothii water lily, is a tropical aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its floating, broad, heart-shaped leaves that provide a platform for small aquatic organisms. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that open in the early morning and close by midday, a behavior known as nyctinasty. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow waters and absorbing nutrients from the sediment.

Peliostomum marlothii plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by offering shelter and food for various aquatic life forms.
