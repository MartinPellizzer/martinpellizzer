Pogonia fragrans

Pogonia fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant pogonia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent that attracts insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a slender, upright stem with a single, showy bloom. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base of the stem.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is considered a rare and endangered orchid in many parts of its range.
