Potentilla recta var. kochii

Potentilla recta var. kochii, commonly known as Koch's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
