Phlomis spica-venti

Phlomis spica-venti, commonly known as the common torchroot, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, spires of purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Phlomis spica-venti is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
