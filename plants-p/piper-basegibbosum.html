Piper basegibbosum

Piper basegibbosum, commonly known as the twisted pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twisting stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
