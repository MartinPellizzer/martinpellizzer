Pulmonaria officinalis subsp. maculosa

Pulmonaria officinalis subsp.

maculosa, commonly known as spotted lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, velvety leaves with distinctive white or pale green spots.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
