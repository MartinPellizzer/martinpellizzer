Pepinia carnososepala

Pepinia carnososepala, commonly known as the fleshy-sepaled pepinia, is a unique succulent plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
