Phlox subulata f. hentzii

Phlox subulata f. hentzii, commonly known as the Hentz's stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with silver-blue foliage that is drought-tolerant and hardy in USDA zones 5 through 8. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
