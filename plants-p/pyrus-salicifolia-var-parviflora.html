Pyrus salicifolia var. parviflora

Pyrus salicifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear, is a variety of the common pear tree characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in panicles. Its fruits are small, sweet apples that ripen in late summer, though they are not typically cultivated for commercial use.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
