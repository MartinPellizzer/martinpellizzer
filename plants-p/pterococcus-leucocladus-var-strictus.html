Pterococcus leucocladus var. strictus

Pterococcus leucocladus var. strictus, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody, aromatic fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate.

Due to its valuable timber and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
