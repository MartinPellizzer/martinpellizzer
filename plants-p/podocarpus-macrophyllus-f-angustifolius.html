Podocarpus macrophyllus f. angustifolius

Podocarpus macrophyllus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved umbrella pine, is a variant of the Chinese sandalwood tree.

It is a evergreen coniferous shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a pale underside. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic wood.

This species is well adapted to humid, subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
