Potamogeton acutifolius var. minor

Potamogeton acutifolius var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in spike-like clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
