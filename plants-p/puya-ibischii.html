Puya ibischii

Puya ibischii, commonly known as the Chilean torchflower, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andean regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous bright red to orange flowers. The plant features broad, strap-shaped leaves that form a rosette, often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry conditions and requires well-drained soil.

Puya ibischii is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its dramatic, fiery appearance.
