Portulaca rediviva

Portulaca rediviva, commonly known as the moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments due to its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves and stems. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the summer. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in a fine, powdery coating that helps reduce water loss.

Portulaca rediviva is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its durability and aesthetic appeal in gardens.
