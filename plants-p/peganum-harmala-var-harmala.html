Peganum harmala var. harmala

Peganum harmala var. harmala, commonly known as the Syrian rue, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features small, greenish-yellow flowers and produces distinctive, black, globular fruits. The plant contains various alkaloids, including harmine and harmaline, which have been studied for their psychoactive and medicinal properties. Its leaves and seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic effects.

However, it is toxic in high doses and requires careful handling.
