Pastinaca angulosa

Pastinaca angulosa, commonly known as the prickly sow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lobed, sometimes prickly leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
