Pinus brutia var. densifolia

Pinus brutia var. densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved Turkish pine, is a variant of the Turkish pine native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and stout, needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more clustered than those of the typical variety. The cones are relatively small and often hang in a pendulous manner, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters and hot, dry summers.

Its unique foliage and resilience make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
