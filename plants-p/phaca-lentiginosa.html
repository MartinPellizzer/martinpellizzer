Phaca lentiginosa

Phaca lentiginosa, commonly known as the spotted phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or violet, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
