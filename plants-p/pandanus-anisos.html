Pandanus anisos

Pandanus anisos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Indo-Pacific, including parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its stiff, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or green in color.

Pandanus anisos thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.
