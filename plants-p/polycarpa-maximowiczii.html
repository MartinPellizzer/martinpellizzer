Polycarpa maximowiczii

Polycarpa maximowiczii, commonly known as the Himalayan polycarpa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polycarpa maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
