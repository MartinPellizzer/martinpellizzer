Protorhus

Protorhus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive silvery foliage and small, pod-like fruits. The leaves are typically compound, with a grayish or whitish underside that gives the plant its common name. Protorhus species are often found in dry, rocky habitats and are adapted to arid conditions.

They play an important ecological role in their native environments, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
