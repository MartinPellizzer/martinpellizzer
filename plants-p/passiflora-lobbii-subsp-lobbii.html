Passiflora lobbii subsp. lobbii

Passiflora lobbii subsp.

lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's passionflower, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking violet flowers with intricate patterns of red, yellow, and white.

The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with palmately lobed leaves. Its fruit is a rough, yellowish berry that ripens to a dark orange color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
