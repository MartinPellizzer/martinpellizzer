Prunus hintonii

Prunus hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and red or black berries. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

Prunus hintonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
