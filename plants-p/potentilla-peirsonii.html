Potentilla peirsonii

Potentilla peirsonii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas or open woodlands.

Potentilla peirsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
