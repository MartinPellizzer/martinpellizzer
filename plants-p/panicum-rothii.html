Panicum rothii

Panicum rothii, commonly known as Roth's panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
