Petrorhagia macra

Petrorhagia macra, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes. It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 30 centimeters tall.

Petrorhagia macra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
