Panicum incomptum

Panicum incomptum, commonly known as rough panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is usually erect and bristly.

Panicum incomptum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
