Podochilus smithianus

Podochilus smithianus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight.

Podochilus smithianus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
