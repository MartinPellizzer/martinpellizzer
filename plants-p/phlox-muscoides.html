Phlox muscoides

Phlox muscoides, commonly known as sweet william, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink, red, or white, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden beds.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
