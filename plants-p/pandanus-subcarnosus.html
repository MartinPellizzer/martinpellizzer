Pandanus subcarnosus

Pandanus subcarnosus, commonly known as the thick-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, sword-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit, known as a pandan fruit, is edible and has a fragrant, aromatic flavor.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional cuisine and crafts.
