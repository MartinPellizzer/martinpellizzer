Piper borucanum

Piper borucanum, commonly known as the Borucanum pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
