Pandanus tectorius var. australianus

Pandanus tectorius var. australianus, commonly known as the Australian screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often spirally arranged. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the ends of its branches. Its roots and leaves have been used traditionally for various purposes, including weaving and medicinal applications.

The species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
