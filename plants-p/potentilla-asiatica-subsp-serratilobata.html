Potentilla asiatica subsp. serratilobata

Potentilla asiatica subsp.

serratilobata, commonly known as the serrated-lobed cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The leaves are compound, with serrated margins and a lobed structure, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens.
