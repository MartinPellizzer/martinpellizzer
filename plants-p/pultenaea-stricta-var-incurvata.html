Pultenaea stricta var. incurvata

Pultenaea stricta var. incurvata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pultenaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and narrow, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

The varietal form, incurvata, is distinguished by its curved leaf tips and more compact growth habit compared to the typical variety.
