Phyllanthus phlebocarpus

Phyllanthus phlebocarpus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is commonly found in moist, shaded areas.

The botanical features of P. phlebocarpus make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
