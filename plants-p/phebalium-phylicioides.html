Phebalium phylicioides

Phebalium phylicioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. It produces woody, cone-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Phebalium phylicioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
