Persea borbonia var. pubescens

Persea borbonia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spondias, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-colored fruits resembling small mangoes. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
