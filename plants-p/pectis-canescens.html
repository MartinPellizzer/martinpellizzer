Pectis canescens

Pectis canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pectis canescens is an important component of local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
