Phyllostachys edulis f. huamozhu

Phyllostachys edulis f. huamozhu, commonly known as the Huamozhu bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species Phyllostachys edulis.

It is characterized by its slender, culm-like stems that are typically shorter and more compact than those of the standard species. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a pale green color. This bamboo thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance.

It is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of edible shoots.
