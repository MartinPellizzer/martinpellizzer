Prunus serrulata f. juzukakezakura

Prunus serrulata f. juzukakezakura, commonly known as the Juzukakezakura, is a rare and ornamental form of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its exceptionally early blooming habit, often producing flowers in late winter or early spring before the leaves emerge. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with a delicate and fragrant appearance. This form is prized for its unique flowering pattern and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic variant within the Prunus genus, contributing to the diversity of cherry blossom species.
