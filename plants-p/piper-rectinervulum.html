Piper rectinervulum

Piper rectinervulum, commonly known as the slender nerve pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with distinct venation patterns. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, thin fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
