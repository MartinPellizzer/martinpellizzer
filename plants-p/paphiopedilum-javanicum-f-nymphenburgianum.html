Paphiopedilum javanicum f. nymphenburgianum

Paphiopedilum javanicum f. nymphenburgianum, commonly known as the Nymphenburg orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a deep pink or red lip with intricate patterns. The plant is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other objects without being parasitic. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral structure.
