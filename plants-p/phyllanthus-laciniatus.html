Phyllanthus laciniatus

Phyllanthus laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often have a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
