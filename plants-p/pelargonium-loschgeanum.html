Pelargonium loschgeanum

Pelargonium loschgeanum, commonly known as the Cape pelargonium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a trailing shrub characterized by its fleshy, lobed leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, five-petaled blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
