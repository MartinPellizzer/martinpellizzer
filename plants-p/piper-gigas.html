Piper gigas

Piper gigas, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are typically small, round, and turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper gigas is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
