Pandanus microdontus

Pandanus microdontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanus genus, characterized by its large, feather-like leaves and distinctive, spiky fruit structures. The plant typically grows in coastal and wetland areas, thriving in humid, saline environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials.

The fruit, which is edible, contains a starchy, fibrous pulp that is consumed by both humans and animals.
