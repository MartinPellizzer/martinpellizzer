Paepalanthus incanus

Paepalanthus incanus, commonly known as the silver hair cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the cactus family, Cactaceae, and is characterized by its dense, woolly coating that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
