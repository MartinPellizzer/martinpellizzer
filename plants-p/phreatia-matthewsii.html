Phreatia matthewsii

Phreatia matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces a single, elongated stem with several fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Phreatia matthewsii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
