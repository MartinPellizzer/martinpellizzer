Pterocarpus kaessneri

Pterocarpus kaessneri, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain dark, oily seeds. Its heartwood is highly valued for its rich color and durability, making it sought after in the timber industry.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
