Primula tetrandra

Primula tetrandra, commonly known as the four-lobed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture. Primula tetrandra thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in garden settings.
