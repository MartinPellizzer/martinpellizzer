Pinguicula corsica var. pallidula

Pinguicula corsica var. pallidula, commonly known as the Corsican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and unique method of nutrient acquisition through insectivory.
