Polygonum miserum

Polygonum miserum, commonly known as the weeping knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes species such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum miserum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
