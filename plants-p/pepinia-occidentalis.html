Pepinia occidentalis

Pepinia occidentalis, commonly known as the western pepinia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Pepinia occidentalis thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
