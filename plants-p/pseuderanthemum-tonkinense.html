Pseuderanthemum tonkinense

Pseuderanthemum tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Pseuderanthemum tonkinense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
