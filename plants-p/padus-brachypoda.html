Padus brachypoda

Padus brachypoda, commonly known as the dwarf blackthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local wildlife.
