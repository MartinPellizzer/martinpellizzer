Pholidia woollsiana var. dentata

Pholidia woollsiana var. dentata, commonly known as the Woolly Cabbage, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
