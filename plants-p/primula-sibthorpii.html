Primula sibthorpii

Primula sibthorpii, commonly known as the Kashmir primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula sibthorpii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
