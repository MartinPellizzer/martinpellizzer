Picraena excelsa

Picraena excelsa, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Picraena excelsa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
