Phlox multiflora subsp. typica

Phlox multiflora subsp.

typica, commonly known as the common phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers in the summer.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for borders or rock gardens.
