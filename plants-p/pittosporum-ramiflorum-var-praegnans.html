Pittosporum ramiflorum var. praegnans

Pittosporum ramiflorum var. praegnans, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of fragrant, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
