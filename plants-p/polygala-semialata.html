Polygala semialata

Polygala semialata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

Polygala semialata is often found in coastal and inland regions where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
