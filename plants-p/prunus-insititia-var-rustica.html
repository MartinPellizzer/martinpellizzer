Prunus insititia var. rustica

Prunus insititia var. rustica, commonly known as the wild cherry or sour cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark red to black berries in the fall. The berries are edible but have a tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.
