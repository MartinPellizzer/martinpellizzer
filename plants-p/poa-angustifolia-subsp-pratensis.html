Poa angustifolia subsp. pratensis

Poa angustifolia subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as narrowleaf meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, pastures, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, leafy stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each containing several small, two-lobed florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
