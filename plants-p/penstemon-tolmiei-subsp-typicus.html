Penstemon tolmiei subsp. typicus

Penstemon tolmiei subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Tolle's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from violet to pink.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in winter.
