Pelargonium × brassicatum

Pelargonium × brassicatum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with a distinctive dark center. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy, aromatic leaves that are often glandular and sticky. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
