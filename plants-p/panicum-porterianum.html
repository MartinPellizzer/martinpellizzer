Panicum porterianum

Panicum porterianum, commonly known as the Porter's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as those found in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest to its appearance. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in wetland ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.

Panicum porterianum is generally short-lived and may be difficult to establish in non-native environments.
