Panicum crus-pavonis var. rostratum

Panicum crus-pavonis var. rostratum, commonly known as the rostrated panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
