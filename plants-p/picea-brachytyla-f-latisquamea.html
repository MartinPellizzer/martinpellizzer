Picea brachytyla f. latisquamea

Picea brachytyla f. latisquamea, commonly known as the broad-scaled spruce, is a botanical variant of the brachytyla spruce.

It is characterized by its broader, more widely spaced scales on the cones compared to the typical form. The plant is a coniferous evergreen tree native to specific regions of western North America. Its foliage consists of short, stiff needles that are typically dark green.

The broad-scaled spruce exhibits a unique morphology that distinguishes it within the Picea genus.
