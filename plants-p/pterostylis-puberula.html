Pterostylis puberula

Pterostylis puberula, commonly known as the hairy sun-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a slender, often reddish stem with a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flower has a distinctive, spurred lip.

Pterostylis puberula is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
