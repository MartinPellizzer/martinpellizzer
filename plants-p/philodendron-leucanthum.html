Philodendron leucanthum

Philodendron leucanthum, commonly known as the white-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with striking white veins against a deep green background. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and can reach lengths of up to 10 meters when provided with adequate support. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

Philodendron leucanthum prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
