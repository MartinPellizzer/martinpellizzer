Phyllanthus longeramosus

Phyllanthus longeramosus, commonly known as the African date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, three-lobed capsules that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and as a food source.
