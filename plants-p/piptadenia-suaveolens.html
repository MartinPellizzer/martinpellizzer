Piptadenia suaveolens

Piptadenia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its fragrant, sweet-smelling flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry savannas and forest edges.

The tree has a shallow root system and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
