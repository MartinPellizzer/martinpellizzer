Phrynium brunnescens

Phrynium brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-leafed arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or brownish tinge on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix enclosed by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries containing the seeds.
