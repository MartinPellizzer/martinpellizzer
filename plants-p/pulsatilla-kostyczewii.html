Pulsatilla kostyczewii

Pulsatilla kostyczewii, commonly known as the Kostyczew's pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are usually unbranched with a hairy texture.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
