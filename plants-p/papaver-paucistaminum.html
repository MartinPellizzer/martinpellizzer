Papaver paucistaminum

Papaver paucistaminum, commonly known as the few-stamen poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a contrasting black center. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

The few stamens it possesses are a distinguishing feature in its floral structure.
