Potentilla × neumanniana

Potentilla × neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
