Pfaffia brachiata f. angustifolia

Pfaffia brachiata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf devil's backbone, is a variant of the Pfaffia brachiata species.

It is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the late spring to early summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
