Prunus argentea var. elaeagrifolia

Prunus argentea var. elaeagrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in gardens.
