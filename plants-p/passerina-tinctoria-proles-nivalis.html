Passerina tinctoria proles nivalis, commonly known as the snowberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it has a compact growth habit.
