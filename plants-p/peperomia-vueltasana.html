Peperomia vueltasana

Peperomia vueltasana, commonly known as the Veinscale Peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to Central and South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves with a distinctive veined pattern that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in a rosette form, with short, sturdy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for tabletop displays and decorative plant arrangements.
