Panicum guluense

Panicum guluense, commonly known as Gulu panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or swampy habitats, often in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum guluense is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
