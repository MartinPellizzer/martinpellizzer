Pterocarpus amerimnon

Pterocarpus amerimnon, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain flattened seeds, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
