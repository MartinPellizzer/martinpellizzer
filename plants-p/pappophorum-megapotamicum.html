Pappophorum megapotamicum

Pappophorum megapotamicum, commonly known as the Andean feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
