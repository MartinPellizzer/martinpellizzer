Persicaria fusiformis

Persicaria fusiformis, commonly known as the spindleleaf smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late summer. Persicaria fusiformis thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It is often found in wetlands and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
