Polygonatum odoratum f. attenuatum

Polygonatum odoratum f. attenuatum, commonly known as the fragrant yellow bamboo or Chinese yellow bamboo, is a variant of the yellow bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often curved stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and subtle fragrance.
