Polygala baetica var. balleana

Polygala baetica var. balleana, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
