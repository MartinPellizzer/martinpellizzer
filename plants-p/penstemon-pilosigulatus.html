Penstemon pilosigulatus

Penstemon pilosigulatus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and mountainous regions.

This species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
