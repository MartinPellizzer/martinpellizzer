Plantago atrata subsp. ucrainica

Plantago atrata subsp.

ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
