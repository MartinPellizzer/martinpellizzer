Puccinellia simplex

Puccinellia simplex, commonly known as sea oats, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of the United States and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Puccinellia simplex is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery appearance.
