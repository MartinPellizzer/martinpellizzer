Pilea pubescens

Pilea pubescens, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and slightly hairy, giving it a unique textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries containing seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea pubescens is known for its easy care and ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, enhancing its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
