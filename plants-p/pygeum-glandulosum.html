Pygeum glandulosum

Pygeum glandulosum, commonly known as the rough-leafed African rosewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces woody, aromatic capsules that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
