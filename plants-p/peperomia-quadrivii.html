Peperomia quadrivii

Peperomia quadrivii, commonly known as the four-veined peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, glossy, dark green foliage with distinctive veins that form a quadrilateral pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive appearance make it a favored ornamental plant.
