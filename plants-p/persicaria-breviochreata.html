Persicaria breviochreata

Persicaria breviochreata, commonly known as the short-haired knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Persicaria breviochreata is often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and streambanks.
