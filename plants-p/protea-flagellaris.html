Protea flagellaris

Protea flagellaris, commonly known as the flagellate protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its striking, large, and showy inflorescences. The plant features a woody stem with dense, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a unique, elongated shape that resembles a flagellum, giving it its common name.

Protea flagellaris is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
