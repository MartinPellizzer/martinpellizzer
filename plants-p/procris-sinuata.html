Procris sinuata

Procris sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly sinuous or wavy edge. Procris sinuata typically grows in moist, sandy soils near coastal areas or in wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
