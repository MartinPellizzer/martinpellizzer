Potamogeton pusillus var. capitatus

Potamogeton pusillus var. capitatus, commonly known as the small pondweed with a clustered head, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered at the tips of the stems, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and marshes.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
