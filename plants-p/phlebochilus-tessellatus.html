Phlebochilus tessellatus

Phlebochilus tessellatus, commonly known as the tessellated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
