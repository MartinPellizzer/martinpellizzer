Pilea sanctae-crucis

Pilea sanctae-crucis, commonly known as the Mexican sunlet, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a glossy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are surrounded by a modified leaf called a spadix.

Pilea sanctae-crucis is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
