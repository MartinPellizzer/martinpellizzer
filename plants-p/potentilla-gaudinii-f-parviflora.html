Potentilla gaudinii f. parviflora

Potentilla gaudinii f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf flowered potentilla, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
