Pelargonium × striatum

Pelargonium × striatum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with distinct zonation patterns, which give the plant its common name. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a range of growing conditions.

Pelargonium × striatum is often cultivated in gardens and as potted plants, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
