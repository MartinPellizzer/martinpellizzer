Pelargonium australe var. minor

Pelargonium australe var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a compact, ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems and small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its attractive appearance and ease of care. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

This variety is prized for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
