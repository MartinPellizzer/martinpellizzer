Piptomeris furcellata

Piptomeris furcellata, commonly known as the small-flowered pteris, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular species among fern enthusiasts and botanists.
