Potentilla recta subsp. iranica

Potentilla recta subsp.

iranica, commonly known as Persian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The leaves are typically compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
