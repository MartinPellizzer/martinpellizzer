Phlomis russeliana

Phlomis russeliana, commonly known as Russell's blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in grayish-white hairs, and it produces showy, bright yellow to orange flowers in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its botanical name honors the English botanist John Russell, who contributed to the study of flowering plants.
