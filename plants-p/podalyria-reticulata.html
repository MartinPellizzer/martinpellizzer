Podalyria reticulata

Podalyria reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Podalyria reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
