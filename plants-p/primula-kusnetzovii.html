Primula kusnetzovii

Primula kusnetzovii, commonly known as the Kusnetzov's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in horticultural settings.
