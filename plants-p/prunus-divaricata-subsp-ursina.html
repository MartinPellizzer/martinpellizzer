Prunus divaricata subsp. ursina

Prunus divaricata subsp.

ursina, commonly known as the Himalayan cherry or wild cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia. It typically grows at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, red drupes that are often consumed by birds and mammals.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
