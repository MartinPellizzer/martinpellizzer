Pentastelma auritum

Pentastelma auritum, commonly known as the golden ear orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, auricle-shaped petals that resemble small ears, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or golden in color, with a fragrant aroma that attracts pollinators.

The botanical features of Pentastelma auritum make it a prized species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
