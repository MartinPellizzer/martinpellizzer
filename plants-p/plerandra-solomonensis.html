Plerandra solomonensis

Plerandra solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands arum, is a tropical plant native to the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, surrounded by a showy, often colorful spathe.

Plerandra solomonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
