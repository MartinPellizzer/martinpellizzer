Panicum trichonode

Panicum trichonode, commonly known as bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, composed of tightly packed spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum trichonode plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
