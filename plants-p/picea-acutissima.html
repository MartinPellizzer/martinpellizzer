Picea acutissima

Picea acutissima, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive blue-green foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its needles are short, stiff, and often have two white stomatal bands on their undersides.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and hardiness in various climates.
