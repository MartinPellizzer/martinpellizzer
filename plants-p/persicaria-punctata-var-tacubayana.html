Persicaria punctata var. tacubayana

Persicaria punctata var. tacubayana, commonly known as the Tacubayana knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it often grows in moist, disturbed soils.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
