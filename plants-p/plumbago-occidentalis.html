Plumbago occidentalis

Plumbago occidentalis, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes, though it can be invasive in some regions.

The plant prefers full sun and well-drained soil, thriving in warm, humid climates.
