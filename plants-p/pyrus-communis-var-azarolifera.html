Pyrus communis var. azarolifera

Pyrus communis var. azarolifera, commonly known as the Azarolifera pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit that typically ripens in late summer. The plant produces small, deciduous leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit characteristics.
