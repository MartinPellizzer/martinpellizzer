Polypodium laevigatum var. crispatum

Polypodium laevigatum var. crispatum, commonly known as the curled wall fern, is a variant of the common rock fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curled fronds that give it a unique appearance. This fern is typically found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture.
