Pastinaca sativa var. brevis

Pastinaca sativa var. brevis, commonly known as the short-rooted parsley.

This variety is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter root system compared to other parsley varieties. It produces finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in umbels. The plant is cultivated for its edible roots, which have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.

It is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
