Polygonum baldschuanicum

Polygonum baldschuanicum, commonly known as the Caucasian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and can reach a height of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a prominent midrib, while its flowers form dense, spherical clusters at the top of the stems.

Polygonum baldschuanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
