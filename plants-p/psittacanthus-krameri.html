Psittacanthus krameri

Psittacanthus krameri, commonly known as the golden wax fern, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking golden-yellow to orange bracts that resemble small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Psittacanthus krameri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
