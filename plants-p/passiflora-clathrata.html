Passiflora clathrata

Passiflora clathrata, commonly known as the clustered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora clathrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
