Peperomia saipana

Peperomia saipana, commonly known as the Saipan Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the island of Saipan in the Northern Mariana Islands.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia saipana is relatively low-maintenance and drought-tolerant, requiring minimal watering and pruning.
