Pilosella wolfgangiana

Pilosella wolfgangiana, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are hairy and grass-like, while its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

Pilosella wolfgangiana is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures.
