Pseudomelissitus radiatus

Pseudomelissitus radiatus, commonly known as the radiate pseudomelissitus, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
