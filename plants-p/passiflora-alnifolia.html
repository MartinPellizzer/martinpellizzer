Passiflora alnifolia

Passiflora alnifolia, commonly known as the alnifolia passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Passiflora alnifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
