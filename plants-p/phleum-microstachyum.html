Phleum microstachyum

Phleum microstachyum, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional herbal remedies and as a source of forage for livestock.

Phleum microstachyum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in natural habitats.
