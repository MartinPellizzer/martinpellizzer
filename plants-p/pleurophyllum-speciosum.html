Pleurophyllum speciosum

Pleurophyllum speciosum, commonly known as the New Zealand daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pleurophyllum speciosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in various environmental conditions.
