Psidium subrostrifolium

Psidium subrostrifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, yellowish fruits that are often used in local cuisine. The plant has simple, elliptic leaves with a glossy surface and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
