Phyteuma halleri var. cordifolium

Phyteuma halleri var. cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are broadly ovate to heart-shaped, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
