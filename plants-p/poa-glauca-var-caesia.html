Poa glauca var. caesia

Poa glauca var. caesia, commonly known as the bluegrass meadowgrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in grassland ecosystems.
