Phyllanthus penangensis

Phyllanthus penangensis, commonly known as Penang gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, red berries that are edible.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
