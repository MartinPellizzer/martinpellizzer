Phaseolus puberulus

Phaseolus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods. Its seeds are typically oval and can be found in various colors, including green, yellow, and brown.

Phaseolus puberulus is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
