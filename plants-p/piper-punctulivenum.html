Piper punctulivenum

Piper punctulivenum, commonly known as the spotted pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often bear distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
