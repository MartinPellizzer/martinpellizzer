Polygonum distachyum

Polygonum distachyum, commonly known as the two-stem plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to various regions of Asia.

It belongs to the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive two-stemmed structure. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often lanceolate or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Polygonum distachyum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, fertile soils.
