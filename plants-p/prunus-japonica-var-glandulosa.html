Prunus japonica var. glandulosa

Prunus japonica var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry with glands, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on the leaves, which give it its botanical variant name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant form and seasonal blooms.
