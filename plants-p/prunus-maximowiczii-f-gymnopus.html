Prunus maximowiczii f. gymnopus

Prunus maximowiczii f. gymnopus, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The plant is native to Japan and is typically found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its pale blossoms.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
