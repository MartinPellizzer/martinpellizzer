Parakmeria nitida

Parakmeria nitida, commonly known as the silky parakmeria, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

Parakmeria nitida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
