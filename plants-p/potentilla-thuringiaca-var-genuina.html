Potentilla thuringiaca var. genuina

Potentilla thuringiaca var. genuina, commonly known as the Thuringian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with wiry, reddish stems and alternate leaves that are palmately lobed. It thrives in well-drained, sunny to partially shaded soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
