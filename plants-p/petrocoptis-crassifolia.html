Petrocoptis crassifolia

Petrocoptis crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a resilient species capable of surviving in harsh climatic conditions.
