Parsonsia acuminata

Parsonsia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed parsonsia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive, elongated shape. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.
