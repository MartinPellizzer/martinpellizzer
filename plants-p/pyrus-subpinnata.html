Pyrus subpinnata

Pyrus subpinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant features compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with a distinctive narrow, subpinnate structure. Its flowers are white and clustered in cymes, typically appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is a small, yellowish pear-shaped berry that ripens in late summer.
