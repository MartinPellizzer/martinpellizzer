Paspalum granulare

Paspalum granulare, commonly known as rough paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its coarse, fibrous leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum granulare is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it a common species in coastal ecosystems.
