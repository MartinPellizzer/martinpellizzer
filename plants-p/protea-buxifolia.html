Protea buxifolia

Protea buxifolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage that resembles the leaves of boxwood, giving it its common name. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Protea buxifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
