Phyllanthus deplanchei

Phyllanthus deplanchei, commonly known as the African star apple, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a round, fleshy drupe with a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody endocarp.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
