Piper obumbratum var. puberulum

Piper obumbratum var. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
