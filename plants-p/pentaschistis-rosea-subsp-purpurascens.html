Pentaschistis rosea subsp. purpurascens

Pentaschistis rosea subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family. It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale lavender to deep purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
