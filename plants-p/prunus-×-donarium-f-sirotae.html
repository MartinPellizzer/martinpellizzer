Prunus × donarium f. sirotae

Prunus × donarium f. sirotae, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and a smooth, dark green upper surface.

The flowers, which appear in early spring, are pink to white and are followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer.
