Passiflora lyrifolia

Passiflora lyrifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The leaves are deeply lobed, giving the plant its common name, and they are often covered in fine hairs. This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral displays.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
