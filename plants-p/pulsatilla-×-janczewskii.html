Pulsatilla × janczewskii

Pulsatilla × janczewskii, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Pulsatilla vulgaris and Pulsatilla patens, characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, dark green leaves and produces large, showy flowers with numerous stamens and a prominent pistil. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
