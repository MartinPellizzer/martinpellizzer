Psyllium arborescens

Psyllium arborescens, commonly known as the tree psyllium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has fibrous, mucilaginous seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots can reach significant depths in the soil.

Psyllium arborescens is valued for its potential health benefits, including aiding digestion and promoting gut health.
