Piper pendulum

Piper pendulum, commonly known as the weeping arrowroot, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that hang from long, pendulous stems. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its elegant appearance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
