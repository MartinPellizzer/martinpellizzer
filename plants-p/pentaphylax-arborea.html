Pentaphylax arborea

Pentaphylax arborea, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, white or greenish-yellow, and are clustered in panicle-like inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
