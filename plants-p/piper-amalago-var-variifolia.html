Piper amalago var. variifolia

Piper amalago var. variifolia, commonly known as the variable pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with varying leaflet shapes, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by aromatic, peppery berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
