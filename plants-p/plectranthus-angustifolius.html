Plectranthus angustifolius

Plectranthus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from deep purples to reds and blues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Plectranthus angustifolius is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
