Phyllanthus myrsinites subsp. francavillanus

Phyllanthus myrsinites subsp.

francavillanus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
