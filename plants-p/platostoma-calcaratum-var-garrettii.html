Platostoma calcaratum var. garrettii

Platostoma calcaratum var. garrettii, commonly known as the Garrett's rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
