Pterocereus gaumeri subsp. foetidus

Pterocereus gaumeri subsp.

foetidus, commonly known as the stinking moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and nocturnal blooming habit, producing fragrant, white flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a succulent structure to store water. Its subspecies name, *foetidus*, refers to the strong, unpleasant odor of its flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value despite its distinctive scent.
