Pimpinella pusilla

Pimpinella pusilla, commonly known as small cow-wheat, is a small annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, reaching a height of only a few centimeters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a narrow, dry legume that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic compounds and umbellate inflorescence.
