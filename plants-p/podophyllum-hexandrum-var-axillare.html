Podophyllum hexandrum var. axillare

Podophyllum hexandrum var. axillare, commonly known as the axillary podophyllum, is a variant of the Himalayan mayapple.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas. The plant features a short, stout rhizome with clustered, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in umbels, and it produces red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
