Passiflora caerulea var. glaucophylla

Passiflora caerulea var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and a crown of filaments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, waxy texture. This variety is notable for its vibrant, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
