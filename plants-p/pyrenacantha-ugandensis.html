Pyrenacantha ugandensis

Pyrenacantha ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda thornapple, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, and it is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
