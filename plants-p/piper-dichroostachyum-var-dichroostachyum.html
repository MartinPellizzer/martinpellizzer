Piper dichroostachyum var. dichroostachyum

Piper dichroostachyum var. dichroostachyum, commonly known as the two-colored pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dark red berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
