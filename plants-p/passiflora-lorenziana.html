Passiflora lorenziana

Passiflora lorenziana, commonly known as the Lorentz passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by local communities. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor.

Passiflora lorenziana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
