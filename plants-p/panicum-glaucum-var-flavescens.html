Panicum glaucum var. flavescens

Panicum glaucum var. flavescens, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage and produces fluffy, pale yellow to brown spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, thriving in full sun. It is a hardy annual or short-lived perennial with a sprawling growth habit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental use in landscaping and as a drought-tolerant ground cover.
