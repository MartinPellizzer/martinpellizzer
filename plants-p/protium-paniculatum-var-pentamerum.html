Protium paniculatum var. pentamerum

Protium paniculatum var. pentamerum, commonly known as the five-merous protium, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
