Piper guigual

Piper guigual, commonly known as the Guigual pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they are dark green to purple in color. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic, peppery fruits.
