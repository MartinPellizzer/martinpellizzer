Phyllostachys bambusoides f. katashibo

Phyllostachys bambusoides f. katashibo, commonly known as the Katashibo bamboo, is a unique variant of the bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted culms that exhibit a distinctive blue-green hue due to a waxy coating. The plant typically grows in clusters, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture that adds ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in horticulture.
