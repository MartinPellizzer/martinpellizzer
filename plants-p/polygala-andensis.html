Polygala andensis

Polygala andensis, commonly known as the Andean milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Polygala andensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystem.
