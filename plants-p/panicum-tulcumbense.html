Panicum tulcumbense

Panicum tulcumbense, commonly known as the silky grass or silky panicum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a warm-season grass characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-gray seeds that are often used as ornamental elements in gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for landscapes in humid climates.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
