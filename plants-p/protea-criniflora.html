Protea criniflora

Protea criniflora, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy, white to pale pink inflorescence that resembles a crown, hence its common name. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is a hardy shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea criniflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
