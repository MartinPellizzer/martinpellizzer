Pisum sativum subsp. pumilio

Pisum sativum subsp.

pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf pea, is a subspecies of the common pea that is characterized by its compact growth habit. It typically produces small, edible pods containing round or oval seeds, similar to other pea varieties.

The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is adapted to thrive in cooler climates. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and flowering stems that bear pea-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued in both ornamental and agricultural settings due to its hardy nature and yield.
