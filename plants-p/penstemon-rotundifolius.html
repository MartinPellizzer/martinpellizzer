Penstemon rotundifolius

Penstemon rotundifolius, commonly known as the roundleaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon rotundifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
