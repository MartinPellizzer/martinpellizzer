Periploca calophylla var. mucronata

Periploca calophylla var. mucronata, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly waxy texture. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
