Polypodium nicotianifolium

Polypodium nicotianifolium, commonly known as the tobacco-leaf polypody, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds. The leaves are typically bright green, with a serrated margin and a leathery texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
