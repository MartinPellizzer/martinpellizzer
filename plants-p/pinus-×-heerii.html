Pinus × heerii

Pinus × heerii, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus strobiformis and Pinus armandii.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Mexico and parts of Central America. The tree typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a conical shape with long, soft, bluish-green needles. Its cones are woody and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters, containing edible seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value.
