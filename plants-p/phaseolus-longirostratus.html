Phaseolus longirostratus

Phaseolus longirostratus, commonly known as the long-beaked bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pods and small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and produces flowers in shades of white or pink. Its botanical features include a well-developed root system and a climbing habit, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

The longirostratus species is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its role in agricultural biodiversity.
