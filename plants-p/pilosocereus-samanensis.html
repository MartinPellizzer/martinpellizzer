Pilosocereus samanensis

Pilosocereus samanensis, commonly known as the San Miguel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a rounded or cylindrical shape. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
