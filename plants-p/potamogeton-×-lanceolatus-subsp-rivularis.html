Potamogeton × lanceolatus subsp. rivularis

Potamogeton × lanceolatus subsp.

rivularis, commonly known as the river pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America. It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often found near the water's surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with moderate to high light penetration.

This subspecies plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
