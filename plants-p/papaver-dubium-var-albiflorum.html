Papaver dubium var. albiflorum

Papaver dubium var. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered poppy, is a variant of the common poppy native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking white petals that contrast with its green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, typical of the Papaveraceae family. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
