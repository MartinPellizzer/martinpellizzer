Picea excelsa var. viminalis

Picea excelsa var. viminalis, commonly known as the Himalayan fir, is a variant of the Himalayan fir native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
