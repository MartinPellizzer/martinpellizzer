Passerina ganpi

Passerina ganpi, commonly known as the Ganpi grass, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color.

Passerina ganpi is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
