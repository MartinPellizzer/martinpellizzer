Prunus mume var. flavescens

Prunus mume var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow plum or golden mume, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The fruit is a small, yellowish plum that ripens in late summer, often with a sweet and tart flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
