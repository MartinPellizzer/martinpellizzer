Paeonia mascula var. triternata

Paeonia mascula var. triternata, commonly known as the Chinese peony or tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, double flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features include a woody stem and a long flowering period, typically from late spring to early summer.
