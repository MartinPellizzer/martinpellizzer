Pennisetum hirsutum

Pennisetum hirsutum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and its striking purple-pink flower plumes that appear in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Pennisetum hirsutum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
