Polygala isotricha

Polygala isotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, often clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually white or pale blue, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and supporting biodiversity in its habitat.
