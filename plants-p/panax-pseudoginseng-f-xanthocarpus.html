Panax pseudoginseng f. xanthocarpus

Panax pseudoginseng f. xanthocarpus, commonly known as the yellow-fruited ginseng, is a variant of the pseudoginseng plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish fruiting bodies, which distinguish it from other ginseng species. The plant typically grows in forested regions with moist, well-drained soil. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a root system similar to true ginseng.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
