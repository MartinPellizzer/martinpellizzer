Piper arcteacuminatum

Piper arcteacuminatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are harvested for their aromatic seeds, which are widely used as a spice. The botanical features of Piper arcteacuminatum include its climbing habit, opposite leaves, and the presence of essential oils in its fruits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
