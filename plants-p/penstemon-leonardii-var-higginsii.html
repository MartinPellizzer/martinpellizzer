Penstemon leonardii var. higginsii

Penstemon leonardii var. higginsii, commonly known as the Higgins' beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
