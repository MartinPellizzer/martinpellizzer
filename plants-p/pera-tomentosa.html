Pera tomentosa

Pera tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pera, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in cooler, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
