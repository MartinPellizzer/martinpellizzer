Potentilla palustris var. remotifoliola

Potentilla palustris var. remotifoliola, commonly known as the swamp cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thin, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to moist, acidic soils and plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
