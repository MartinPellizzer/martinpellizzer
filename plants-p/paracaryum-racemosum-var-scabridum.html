Paracaryum racemosum var. scabridum

Paracaryum racemosum var. scabridum, commonly known as the rough-stemmed paracaryum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems that are covered in a rough, scaly texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.
