Psoralea jaubertiana

Psoralea jaubertiana, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent pod that releases seeds when mature.

Psoralea jaubertiana is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
