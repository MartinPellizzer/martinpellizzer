Polygonum scopulinum

Polygonum scopulinum, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly serrated edge, while the stems are upright and often tinged with red.

Polygonum scopulinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
