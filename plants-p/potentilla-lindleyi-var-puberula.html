Potentilla lindleyi var. puberula

Potentilla lindleyi var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent potentilla, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or white, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
