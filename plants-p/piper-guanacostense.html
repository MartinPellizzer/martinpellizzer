Piper guanacostense

Piper guanacostense, commonly known as the Guanacaste pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
