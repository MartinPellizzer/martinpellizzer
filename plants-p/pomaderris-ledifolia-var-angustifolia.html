Pomaderris ledifolia var. angustifolia

Pomaderris ledifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry sclerophyll forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
