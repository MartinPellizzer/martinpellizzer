Pilea wattersii

Pilea wattersii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, low-maintenance houseplant native to China.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that resemble small coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea wattersii is popular for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care, making it a favorite among indoor gardeners.
