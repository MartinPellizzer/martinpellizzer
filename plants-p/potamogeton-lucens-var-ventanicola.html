Potamogeton lucens var. ventanicola

Potamogeton lucens var. ventanicola, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
