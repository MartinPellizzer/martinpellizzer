Phlox stansburyi f. longituba

Phlox stansburyi f. longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed phlox, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its compact growth habit and showy floral display.
