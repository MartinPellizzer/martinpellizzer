Psoralea thomii

Psoralea thomii, commonly known as the African false indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem covered in fine hairs.

Psoralea thomii is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
