Polanisia erosa

Polanisia erosa, commonly known as the European rose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains multiple seeds.

Polanisia erosa is often found in temperate regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
