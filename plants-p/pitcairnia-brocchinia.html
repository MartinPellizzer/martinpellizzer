Pitcairnia brocchinia

Pitcairnia brocchinia, commonly known as the velvet pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are densely covered with a soft, velvety pubescence. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
