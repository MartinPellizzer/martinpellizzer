Phyllanthus ligustrifolius var. ligustrifolius

Phyllanthus ligustrifolius var. ligustrifolius, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are compound and consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, round, yellow fruits. The leaves and fruits are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
