Piper psilophyllum var. psilophyllum

Piper psilophyllum var. psilophyllum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue to black berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly pubescent surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
