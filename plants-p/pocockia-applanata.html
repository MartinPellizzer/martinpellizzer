Pocockia applanata

Pocockia applanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf pothos, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is a climbing plant characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight variegation. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
