Pterocephalus perennis subsp. parnassi

Pterocephalus perennis subsp.

parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia.

The plant is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters. It thrives in alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
