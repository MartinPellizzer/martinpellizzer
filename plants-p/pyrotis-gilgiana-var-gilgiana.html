Pyrotis gilgiana var. gilgiana

Pyrotis gilgiana var. gilgiana, commonly known as the Gilgian flameflower, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
