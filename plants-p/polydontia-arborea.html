Polydontia arborea

Polydontia arborea, commonly known as the tree with many teeth, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, tooth-like structures on its bark.

It is a woody perennial that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or white.

The plant is known for its slow growth and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
