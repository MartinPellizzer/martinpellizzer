Panicum nigritianum

Panicum nigritianum, commonly known as African millet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically brown or pale in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop in arid regions.

Its seeds are often used as a staple food in various African countries.
