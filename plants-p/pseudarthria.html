Pseudarthria

Pseudarthria, commonly known as the false legume, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and it thrives in dry, arid environments. Pseudarthria species are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.

These plants have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enhances their growth in nutrient-poor soils.
