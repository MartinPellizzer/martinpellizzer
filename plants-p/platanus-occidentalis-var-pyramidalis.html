Platanus occidentalis var. pyramidalis

Platanus occidentalis var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal sycamore, is a variant of the American sycamore native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow growth habit and smooth, mottled bark that peels in thin layers. The tree produces distinctive, spherical fruits that disperse seeds via wind, contributing to its ecological role. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a pointed tip and turn yellow in the fall.

This variety is often planted for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in urban environments.
