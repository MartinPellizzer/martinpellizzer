Poa phoenicoides

Poa phoenicoides, commonly known as the purple tussock grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in subalpine and alpine environments.

Poa phoenicoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
