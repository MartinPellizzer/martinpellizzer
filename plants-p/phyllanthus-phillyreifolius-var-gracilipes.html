Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. gracilipes

Phyllanthus phillyreifolius var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
