Polystachya ottoniana

Polystachya ottoniana, commonly known as the Otto's spikegrass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and erect, leafy stems. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in spikes, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystachya ottoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
