Petrocosmea flaccida

Petrocosmea flaccida, commonly known as the limp rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and often pale in color.

Petrocosmea flaccida is noted for its unique morphology and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
