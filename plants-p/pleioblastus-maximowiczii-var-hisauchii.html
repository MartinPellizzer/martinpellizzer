Pleioblastus maximowiczii var. hisauchii

Pleioblastus maximowiczii var. hisauchii, commonly known as the Hisauchii bamboo, is a variant of the Pleioblastus maximowiczii species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
