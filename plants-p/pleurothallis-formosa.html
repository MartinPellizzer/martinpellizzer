Pleurothallis formosa

Pleurothallis formosa, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including yellow, white, and pink. The plant typically produces multiple inflorescences, each bearing several blooms that are often clustered closely together. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
