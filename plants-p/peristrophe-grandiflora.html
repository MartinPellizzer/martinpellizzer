Peristrophe grandiflora

Peristrophe grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered peristrophe, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or purple. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Peristrophe grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a container plant.
