Potamogeton obtusifolius var. angustifolius

Potamogeton obtusifolius var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly ribbed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, which can help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic life.

It is an important component of wetland ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
