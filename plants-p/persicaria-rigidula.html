Persicaria rigidula

Persicaria rigidula, commonly known as rigid polygonum, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other wetland habitats. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Persicaria rigidula is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive nature.
