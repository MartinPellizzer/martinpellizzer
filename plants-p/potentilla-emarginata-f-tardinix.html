Potentilla emarginata f. tardinix

Potentilla emarginata f. tardinix, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers and narrow, serrated leaves. This variety is typically found in moist, acidic soils in woodland or heathland environments. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The plant is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and poor soil conditions.
