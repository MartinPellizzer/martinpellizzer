Phoradendron hexastichum var. ovatum

Phoradendron hexastichum var. ovatum, commonly known as the Mexican mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Americas.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and influencing forest dynamics.
