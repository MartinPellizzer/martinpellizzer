Pouteria petiolata

Pouteria petiolata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *mamoeiro*, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to oval-shaped fruits with a hard, woody pulp. The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential economic importance in local communities.
