Pavonia subhastata var. laetevirens

Pavonia subhastata var. laetevirens, commonly known as the pink wood rose, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
