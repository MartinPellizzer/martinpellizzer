Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. brevibracteata

Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a variant of the Japanese fir.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan, characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The species exhibits distinct botanical features, including its short, bract-like structures at the base of the cones. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

The plant plays a significant role in both ecological systems and horticultural landscapes.
