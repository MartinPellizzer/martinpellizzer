Pandanus tectorius var. fragrans

Pandanus tectorius var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its aromatic qualities. Its roots and leaves are utilized in various cultural and culinary practices.

The species thrives in humid climates and is often found growing near coastal areas.
