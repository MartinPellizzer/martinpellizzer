Pultenaea subumbellata var. cambagei

Pultenaea subumbellata var. cambagei, commonly known as the Cambage's pultenaea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, often prickly foliage and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly honeyeaters, and it produces small, dark seeds within hairy pods.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
