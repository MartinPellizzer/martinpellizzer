Pterocissus

Pterocissus, commonly known as the climbing ivy or grape ivy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lauraceae.

These plants are characterized by their trailing or climbing growth habit and are native to regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture with a distinctive leaf venation pattern. They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
