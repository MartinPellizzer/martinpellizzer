Platystachys complanata

Platystachys complanata, commonly known as the Chinese cliff brake, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which appear in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or cliff habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
