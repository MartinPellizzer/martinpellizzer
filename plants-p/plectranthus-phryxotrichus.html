Plectranthus phryxotrichus

Plectranthus phryxotrichus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Plectranthus phryxotrichus is relatively low maintenance and drought tolerant once established.
