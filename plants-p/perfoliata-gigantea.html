Perfoliata gigantea

Perfoliata gigantea, commonly known as the giant leaf perfoliate, is a striking plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often measure up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

The plant is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are typically pale blue or violet and are arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. The leaves are perfoliate, meaning they have a hole through the center that allows the stem to pass through, a unique feature that aids in water drainage.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
