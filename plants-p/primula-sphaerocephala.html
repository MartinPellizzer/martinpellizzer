Primula sphaerocephala

Primula sphaerocephala, commonly known as the spherical primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are broad and hairy, providing a distinctive appearance.

Primula sphaerocephala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
