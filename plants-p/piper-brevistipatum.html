Piper brevistipatum

Piper brevistipatum, commonly known as the short-tipped pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, narrow berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
