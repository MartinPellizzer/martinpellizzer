Pulsatilla affinis

Pulsatilla affinis, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture.

Pulsatilla affinis is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
