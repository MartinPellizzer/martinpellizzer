Procris melastomoides

Procris melastomoides, commonly known as the Florida melastome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as hammocks and wetlands. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Procris melastomoides produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.
