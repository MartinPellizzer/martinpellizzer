Peschiera hystrix

Peschiera hystrix, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky calyx that surrounds its bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, range in color from white to pale blue.

Peschiera hystrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
