Pachylarnax nitida

Pachylarnax nitida, commonly known as the slender bamboo or silver bamboo, is a unique member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, which are loose, branched clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen that enhances its visual appeal.

Pachylarnax nitida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant form and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
