Pseudopogonatherum contortum var. sinense

Pseudopogonatherum contortum var. sinense, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly folded. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
