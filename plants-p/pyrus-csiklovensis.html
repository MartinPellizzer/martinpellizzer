Pyrus csiklovensis

Pyrus csiklovensis, commonly known as the Csiklovénský pear, is a rare and endemic species native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–8 meters, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The fruit is a pear-like berry that ripens in late summer, featuring a yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
