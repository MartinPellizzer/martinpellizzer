Pteris × wangii

Pteris × wangii, commonly known as the Chinese water fern, is a hybrid species within the genus Pteris, characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species, Pteris cretica and Pteris vittata, resulting in a distinctive morphology. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a graceful, arching appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.

Pteris × wangii is also valued for its ability to purify water, making it useful in aquatic gardens and wetland restoration projects.
