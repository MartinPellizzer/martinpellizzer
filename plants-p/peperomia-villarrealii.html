Peperomia villarrealii

Peperomia villarrealii, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features waxy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or pale green pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is well-suited for container cultivation and requires minimal maintenance.
