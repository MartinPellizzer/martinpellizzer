Potamogeton diversifolius var. spicatus

Potamogeton diversifolius var. spicatus, commonly known as the spiculate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, branching stems that often grow in dense clusters along the bottom of lakes and ponds. The leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, with a distinct midrib and varying leaf shapes along the stem. Its small, green flowers are arranged in spikes, typically found near the tips of the stems.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
