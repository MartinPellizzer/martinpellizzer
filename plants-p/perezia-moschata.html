Perezia moschata

Perezia moschata, commonly known as the musk perezia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive texture.

Perezia moschata is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
