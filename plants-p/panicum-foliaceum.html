Panicum foliaceum

Panicum foliaceum, commonly known as silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and texture.

Panicum foliaceum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
