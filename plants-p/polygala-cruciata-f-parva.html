Polygala cruciata f. parva

Polygala cruciata f. parva, commonly known as the small crucified milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are opposite and linear, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
