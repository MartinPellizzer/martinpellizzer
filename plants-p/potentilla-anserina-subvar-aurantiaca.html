Potentilla anserina subvar. aurantiaca

Potentilla anserina subvar. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, growing at high altitudes.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
