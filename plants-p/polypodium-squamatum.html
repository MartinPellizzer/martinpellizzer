Polypodium squamatum

Polypodium squamatum, commonly known as the scaly huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scaly appearance on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually long and narrow, with a leathery texture.

Polypodium squamatum is often found in temperate to tropical regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
