Paspalum formosanum

Paspalum formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Paspalum formosanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
