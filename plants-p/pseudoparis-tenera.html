Pseudoparis tenera

Pseudoparis tenera, commonly known as the slender pseudoparis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in greenhouses or terrariums.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also occasionally found in horticultural collections.
