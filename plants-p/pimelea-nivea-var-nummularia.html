Pimelea nivea var. nummularia

Pimelea nivea var. nummularia, commonly known as the white ghost everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and features small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a grayish-white hue that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
