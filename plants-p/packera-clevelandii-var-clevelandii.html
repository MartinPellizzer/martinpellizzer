Packera clevelandii var. clevelandii

Packera clevelandii var. clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
