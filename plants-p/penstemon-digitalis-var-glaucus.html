Penstemon digitalis var. glaucus

Penstemon digitalis var. glaucus, commonly known as the blue mist penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
