Plagiopetalum quadrangulum

Plagiopetalum quadrangulum, commonly known as the four-angled spider flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems with opposite, oval to lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with four petals arranged in a symmetrical pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped landscapes.
