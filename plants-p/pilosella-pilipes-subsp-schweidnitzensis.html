Pilosella pilipes subsp. schweidnitzensis

Pilosella pilipes subsp.

schweidnitzensis, commonly known as the Schweidnitz's hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. Its leaves are hairy and alternate, with a distinctive lobed shape.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
