Pappobolus lehmannii var. lehmannii

Pappobolus lehmannii var. lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's burrweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds with silky, wind-dispersed hairs. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow and are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

This species is known for its adaptability and resilience in arid environments.
