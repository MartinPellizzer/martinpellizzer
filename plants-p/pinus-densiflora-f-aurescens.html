Pinus densiflora f. aurescens

Pinus densiflora f. aurescens, commonly known as the golden-capped Japanese red pine, is a variant of the Japanese red pine characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow cones.

This form exhibits a unique coloration that becomes more pronounced as the cones mature, setting it apart from the typical green cones of the species. It is native to southern Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
