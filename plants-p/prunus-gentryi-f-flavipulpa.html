Prunus gentryi f. flavipulpa

Prunus gentryi f. flavipulpa, commonly known as the yellow-leafed cherry, is a rare and ornamental cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which stands out against the typical green leaves of other cherry species. The plant produces small, showy flowers in the spring, adding to its ornamental value. It is native to the United States, specifically found in the southeastern region.

Due to its unique coloration, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
