Pseudobombax heteromorphum

Pseudobombax heteromorphum, commonly known as the variable ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
