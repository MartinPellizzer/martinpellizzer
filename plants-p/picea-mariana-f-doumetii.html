Picea mariana f. doumetii

Picea mariana f. doumetii, commonly known as the Sitka spruce, is a subspecies of the white spruce native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage. The cones are typically slender and pointed, with sharp, prickly bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is used in reforestation efforts.
