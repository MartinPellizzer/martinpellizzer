Papaver dodonei

Papaver dodonei, commonly known as the Dodecanese poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with a prominent central stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its capsule fruit contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Papaver dodonei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
