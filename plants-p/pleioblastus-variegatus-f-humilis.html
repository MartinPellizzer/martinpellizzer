Pleioblastus variegatus f. humilis

Pleioblastus variegatus f. humilis, commonly known as the variegated bamboo, is a compact, ornamental grass native to East Asia.

It features striking variegated foliage with white and green stripes, making it visually appealing in gardens. The plant grows in a clumping form, with slender, bamboo-like stems that remain evergreen throughout the year. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Due to its aesthetic value and low maintenance requirements, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in landscaping.
