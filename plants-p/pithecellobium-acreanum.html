Pithecellobium acreanum

Pithecellobium acreanum, commonly known as the Brazilian acacia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm climates.
