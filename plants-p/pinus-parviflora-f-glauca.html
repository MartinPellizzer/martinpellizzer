Pinus parviflora f. glauca

Pinus parviflora f. glauca, commonly known as the Blue Spruce, is a horticultural form of the Japanese white pine.

It is characterized by its dense, bluish-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, frosty appearance. The needles are short, stiff, and often arranged in a compact, spherical shape. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.
