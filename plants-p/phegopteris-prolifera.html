Phegopteris prolifera

Phegopteris prolifera, commonly known as the creeping polypody, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. Its rhizomes are often creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Phegopteris prolifera is a member of the family Polypodiaceae and is found in various regions across Asia and the Pacific.
