Psyllium squarrosum

Psyllium squarrosum, commonly known as the rough psyllium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its hairy, bristly stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are used in traditional medicine for their laxative properties.

Psyllium squarrosum is also valued for its potential in soil stabilization and as a source of dietary fiber.
