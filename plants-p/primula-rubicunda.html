Primula rubicunda

Primula rubicunda, commonly known as the scarlet primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather.

Primula rubicunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
