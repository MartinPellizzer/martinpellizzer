Pleurothallis paulensis

Pleurothallis paulensis, commonly known as the Pauline's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that help store water and nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
