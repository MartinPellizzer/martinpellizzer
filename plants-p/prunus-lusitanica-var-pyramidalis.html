Prunus lusitanica var. pyramidalis

Prunus lusitanica var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the Portuguese cherry or the Pyramidal cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible red berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it suitable for hedging and landscape design.
