Polypodium elasticum var. glaziovii

Polypodium elasticum var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's shield fern, is a tropical fern species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often arching fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The leaves are typically bipinnate, with a leathery texture and a bright green color that can vary depending on light exposure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
