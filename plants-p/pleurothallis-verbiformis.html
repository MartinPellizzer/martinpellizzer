Pleurothallis verbiformis

Pleurothallis verbiformis, commonly known as the verb-like orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip.

Pleurothallis verbiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
