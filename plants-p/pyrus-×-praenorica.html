Pyrus × praenorica

Pyrus × praenorica, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a hybrid species of the genus Pyrus.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they ripen.

It is native to Europe and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
