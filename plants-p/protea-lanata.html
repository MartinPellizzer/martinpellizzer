Protea lanata

Protea lanata, commonly known as the woolly protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous petal-like bracts that resemble flowers. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.

The woolly protea plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
