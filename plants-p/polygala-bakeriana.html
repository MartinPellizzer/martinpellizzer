Polygala bakeriana

Polygala bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, and it produces small, bean-like seeds.

It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
