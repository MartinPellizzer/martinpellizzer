Potentilla crassicaulis

Potentilla crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold climates with full sun exposure. The plant features dark green, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in early summer.

Potentilla crassicaulis is valued for its hardiness and is often used in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
