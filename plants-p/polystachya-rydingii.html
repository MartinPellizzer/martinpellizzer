Polystachya rydingii

Polystachya rydingii, commonly known as the Ryding's bamboo orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or near streams. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, contributing to its bamboo-like appearance.

Polystachya rydingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
