Polychrysum tadshikorum

Polychrysum tadshikorum, commonly known as the Tibetan golden gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to golden flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Polychrysum tadshikorum is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
