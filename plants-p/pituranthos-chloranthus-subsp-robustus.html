Pituranthos chloranthus subsp. robustus

Pituranthos chloranthus subsp.

robustus, commonly known as the robust pituranthos, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
