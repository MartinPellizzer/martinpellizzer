Piper tuberosum

Piper tuberosum, commonly known as creeping gooseberry, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist tropical forests.

The berries are edible and have a peppery taste, making it a useful plant in some traditional cuisines.
