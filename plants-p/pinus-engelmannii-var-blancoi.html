Pinus engelmannii var. blancoi

Pinus engelmannii var. blancoi, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a variant of the Engelmann pine native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and temperate climates. Its cones are typically woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
