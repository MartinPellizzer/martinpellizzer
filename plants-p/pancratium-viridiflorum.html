Pancratium viridiflorum

Pancratium viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a greenish tinge, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in sunny environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, and the plant can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Pancratium viridiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unique floral display.
