Pteronia spinulosa

Pteronia spinulosa, commonly known as the spiked pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, spiky stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Pteronia spinulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
