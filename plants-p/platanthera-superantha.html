Platanthera superantha

Platanthera superantha, commonly known as the fragrant twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and pale green to white flowers. Native to moist, shaded habitats in North America, it thrives in forest understories and wetlands. The plant is notable for its lack of true roots and its reliance on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Its delicate appearance and ecological significance make it an important indicator species for healthy, undisturbed ecosystems.
