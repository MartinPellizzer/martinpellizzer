Pinus tschonoskiana

Pinus tschonoskiana, commonly known as the Tschonoskian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in cold, dry climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in reforestation efforts in its native habitat.
