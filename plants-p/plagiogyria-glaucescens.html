Plagiogyria glaucescens

Plagiogyria glaucescens, commonly known as the blue moss or blue rock moss, is a species of liverwort found in moist, shaded environments.

It has a dense, mat-like growth form with small, overlapping leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or alpine regions. Its structure consists of a thallus with a central axis and lateral branches, typical of the Plagiothyriaceae family.

Plagiogyria glaucescens plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
