Persicaria minor var. latifolia

Persicaria minor var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful wetland plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
