Peperomia paraguayensis

Peperomia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay peperomia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, often clustered in spikes. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia paraguayensis is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to enhance indoor humidity.
