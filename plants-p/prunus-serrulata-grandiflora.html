Prunus serrulata grandiflora

Prunus serrulata grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is renowned for its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular display. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are borne in clusters on short pedicels. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value and cultural significance.
