Psidium pratense

Psidium pratense, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or wild guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
