Potentilla ambigua var. argentea

Potentilla ambigua var. argentea, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
