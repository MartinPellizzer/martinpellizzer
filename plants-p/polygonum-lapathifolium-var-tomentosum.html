Polygonum lapathifolium var. tomentosum

Polygonum lapathifolium var. tomentosum, commonly known as fuzzy smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
