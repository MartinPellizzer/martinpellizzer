Pentaphyllum concinnum

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap microscopic organisms. This plant is typically found in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it lacks true roots.
