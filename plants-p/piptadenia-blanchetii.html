Piptadenia blanchetii

Piptadenia blanchetii, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flattened seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in savannas and forest edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
