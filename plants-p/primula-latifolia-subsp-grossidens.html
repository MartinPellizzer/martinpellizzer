Primula latifolia subsp. grossidens

Primula latifolia subsp.

grossidens, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
