Pelargonium leptopodium

Pelargonium leptopodium, commonly known as the slender-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
