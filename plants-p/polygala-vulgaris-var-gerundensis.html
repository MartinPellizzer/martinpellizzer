Polygala vulgaris var. gerundensis

Polygala vulgaris var. gerundensis, commonly known as the Gerundense milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems with small, pale green leaves and produces clusters of delicate, yellowish flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a distinctive, branching structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
