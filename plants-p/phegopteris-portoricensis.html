Phegopteris portoricensis

Phegopteris portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican holly fern, is a unique fern species native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a holly-like appearance, featuring sharp, toothed margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sori, which are clusters of sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

Phegopteris portoricensis is considered a rare and endemic species, highlighting its ecological and botanical significance in the Caribbean region.
