Polygonum exsertum

Polygonum exsertum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its reddish stems and triangular leaf shapes are distinctive features, while its seeds are dispersed by wind.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
