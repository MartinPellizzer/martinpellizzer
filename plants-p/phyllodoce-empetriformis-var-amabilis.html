Phyllodoce empetriformis var. amabilis

Phyllodoce empetriformis var. amabilis, commonly known as the pink mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in stabilizing soil in ecologically sensitive areas.
