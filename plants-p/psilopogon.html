Psilopogon

Psilopogon, commonly known as the slender leaved orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers and slender, grass-like leaves. The plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments. Psilopogon species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of trees or other plants without being parasitic.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
