Polypodium benguetense

Polypodium benguetense, commonly known as the Benguet huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Benguet and other northern Luzon provinces. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes. Its fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, serrated margin.

Polypodium benguetense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
