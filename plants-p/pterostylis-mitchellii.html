Pterostylis mitchellii

Pterostylis mitchellii, commonly known as the Mitchell's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a single, erect stem with a single, hooded flower that is often pale green or yellowish in color. Its root system consists of a tuberous structure that helps store nutrients and water.

The orchid relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its complex ecological relationships.
