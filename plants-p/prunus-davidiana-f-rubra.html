Prunus davidiana f. rubra

Prunus davidiana f. rubra, commonly known as the Redleaf Chinese cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus davidiana species.

It is characterized by its striking reddish foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the fall. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of 6 to 12 feet. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red cherries.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
