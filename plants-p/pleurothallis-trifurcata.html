Pleurothallis trifurcata

Pleurothallis trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink, arranged in a loose raceme. It has a compact growth habit with short, fleshy roots and narrow, strap-like leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare orchids.
