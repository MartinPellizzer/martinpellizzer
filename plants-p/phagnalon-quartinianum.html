Phagnalon quartinianum

Phagnalon quartinianum, commonly known as the Quartinian phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in dry, rocky, and calcareous soils. The plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
