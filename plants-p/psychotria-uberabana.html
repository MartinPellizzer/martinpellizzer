Psychotria uberabana

Psychotria uberabana, commonly known as the Brazilian Psychotria, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often fragrant, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Psychotria uberabana is also noted for its potential ethnobotanical uses in traditional medicine.
