Pleonotoma decomposita

Pleonotoma decomposita, commonly known as the decomposer beetle, is a species of beetle that plays a vital role in the decomposition process of organic matter in forest ecosystems.

It is typically found in moist, decaying wood where it feeds on fungi and other decomposing material. The beetle's life cycle is closely tied to the breakdown of dead plant matter, contributing to nutrient cycling in the soil. Its larvae are often the primary decomposers, breaking down complex organic compounds into simpler forms.

This species is an essential component of healthy forest environments, supporting biodiversity and ecological balance.
