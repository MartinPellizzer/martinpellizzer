Pycnanthemum alatum

Pycnanthemum alatum, commonly known as winged goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have distinctive white or pale green margins, giving them a "winged" appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairies and open woodlands.

This plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
