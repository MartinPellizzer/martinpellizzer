Pelargonium × littleanum

Pelargonium × littleanum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × bidwillii. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its foliage is typically dark green and velvety, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium × littleanum is widely cultivated as a garden plant due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
