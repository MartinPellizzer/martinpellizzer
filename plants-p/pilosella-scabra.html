Pilosella scabra

Pilosella scabra, commonly known as rough pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features rough, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

Pilosella scabra is often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
