Primula potaninii

Primula potaninii, commonly known as the Potanin's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are basal, ovate to elliptic, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
