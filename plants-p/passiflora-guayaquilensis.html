Passiflora guayaquilensis

Passiflora guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Guayaquil passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, yellow or orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral morphology and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
