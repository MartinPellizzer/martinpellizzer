Panicum leonis

Panicum leonis, commonly known as lion's tail, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, drooping panicles that resemble a lion's tail, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a branching pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
