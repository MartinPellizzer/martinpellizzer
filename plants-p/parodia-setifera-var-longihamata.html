Parodia setifera var. longihamata

Parodia setifera var. longihamata, commonly known as the Long-stemmed Paddle Cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is a slow-growing species that requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
