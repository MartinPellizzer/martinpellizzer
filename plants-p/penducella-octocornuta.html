Penducella octocornuta

Penducella octocornuta, commonly known as the eight-horned penduncle, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, branching structure.

It features eight prominent, horn-like projections on its main stem, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are small and ribbon-like, arranged along the stem in a spiral pattern.

Penducella octocornuta plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
