Prunus pilosiuscula

Prunus pilosiuscula, commonly known as the silky cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
