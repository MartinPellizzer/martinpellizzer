Pyrrhocactus andicola

Pyrrhocactus andicola, commonly known as the Andean red cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its spiny, barrel-shaped stems and vibrant red flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. Its foliage is adapted to reduce water loss, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

This plant plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
