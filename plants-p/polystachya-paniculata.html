Polystachya paniculata

Polystachya paniculata, commonly known as the panicle bromeliad, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Polystachya paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a decorative plant in many regions.
