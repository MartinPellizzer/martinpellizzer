Pilosella densiflora subsp. cymosiformis

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

cymosiformis, commonly known as the denseflower pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cymose inflorescence and silver-gray foliage.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounded by numerous disk florets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Pilosella densiflora subsp.

cymosiformis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
