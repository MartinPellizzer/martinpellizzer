Pyrus kolupana

Pyrus kolupana, commonly known as the wild pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow fissures, and the wood is fine-grained and used in traditional crafts.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
