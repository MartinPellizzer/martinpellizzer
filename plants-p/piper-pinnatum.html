Piper pinnatum

Piper pinnatum, commonly known as the feathery pepper or piper pinnatum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, three-lobed berries.

Piper pinnatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
