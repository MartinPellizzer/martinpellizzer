Pyrus zertovae

Pyrus zertovae, commonly known as the Zertovae pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a pear-like berry, is typically yellow or green and ripens in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, Pyrus zertovae is considered an endangered species with significant botanical interest.
