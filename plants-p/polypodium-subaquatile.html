Polypodium subaquatile

Polypodium subaquatile, commonly known as the water fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive system, producing spores in sporangia located on the underside of its fronds. Its rhizomes are typically submerged or partially submerged, allowing it to absorb nutrients from water.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to the biological diversity and structural complexity of its habitat.
