Potamogeton gramineus subsp. variifolius

Potamogeton gramineus subsp.

variifolius, commonly known as variable leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that vary in width along their length.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
