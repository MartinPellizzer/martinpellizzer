Psidium rutidocarpum

Psidium rutidocarpum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
