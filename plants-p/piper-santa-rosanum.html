Piper santa-rosanum

Piper santa-rosanum, commonly known as the Santa Rosa pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, it is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a woody vine structure and a distinct aromatic compound that contributes to its medicinal properties.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and researchers studying plant biodiversity.
