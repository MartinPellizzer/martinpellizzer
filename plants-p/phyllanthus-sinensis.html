Phyllanthus sinensis

Phyllanthus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese water pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are smooth and glossy, with a reddish or purplish hue when mature. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
