Pleurothallis blepharoglossa

Pleurothallis blepharoglossa, commonly known as the "ear-shaped" orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, and it produces fragrant blooms in shades of white and yellow.

This orchid is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
