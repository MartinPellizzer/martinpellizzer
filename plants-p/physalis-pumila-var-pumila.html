Physalis pumila var. pumila

Physalis pumila var. pumila, commonly known as the ground cherry or husk tomato, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Americas.

It features hairy, prostrate stems that produce small, yellow flowers, which develop into the distinctive inflated, papery calyces enclosing small, edible fruits. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the fruits are typically orange or red, resembling tiny tomatoes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in culinary applications.
