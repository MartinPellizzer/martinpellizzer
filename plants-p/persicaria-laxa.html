Persicaria laxa

Persicaria laxa, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, white to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
