Pachyphyllum longipedicellatum

Pachyphyllum longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late spring. Its growth habit is compact, forming a low, spreading rosette.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
