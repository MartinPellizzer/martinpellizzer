Pelargonium grimaldiae

Pelargonium grimaldiae, commonly known as the Grimaldi geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family, characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage is often aromatic, a trait common among many pelargonium species.

Pelargonium grimaldiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
