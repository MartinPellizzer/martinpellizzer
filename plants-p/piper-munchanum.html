Piper munchanum

Piper munchanum, commonly known as the Munchanum pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India and Malaysia. The plant is a climbing vine that produces small, aromatic berries, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often utilized in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.
