Prunus jamasakura f. insignis

Prunus jamasakura f. insignis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a small deciduous tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and turn a vibrant green during the growing season.

The species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic appeal.
