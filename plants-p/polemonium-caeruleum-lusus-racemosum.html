Polemonium caeruleum lusus racemosum

Polemonium caeruleum lusus racemosum, commonly known as the blue false forget-me-not, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
