Protea transvaalensis

Protea transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant is native to the grasslands of the northeastern part of South Africa. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections around the world.
