Polygonum thunbergii var. spicatum

Polygonum thunbergii var. spicatum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
