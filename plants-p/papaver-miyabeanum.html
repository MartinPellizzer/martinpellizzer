Papaver miyabeanum

Papaver miyabeanum, commonly known as the Japanese poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
