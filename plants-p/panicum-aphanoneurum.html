Panicum aphanoneurum

Panicum aphanoneurum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a low-growing, grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like blades that are typically less than 10 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure is characteristic of the grass family (Poaceae), with rhizomes and fibrous roots that help stabilize soil in wet environments.
