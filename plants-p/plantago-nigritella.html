Plantago nigritella

Plantago nigritella, commonly known as black plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by dark, oval-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
