Polypodium sarawakense

Polypodium sarawakense, commonly known as the Sarawak huperzia, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in structure.

Polypodium sarawakense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
