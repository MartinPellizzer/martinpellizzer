Pithecellobium parauaquarae

Pithecellobium parauaquarae, commonly known as the Paraguay velvet bean, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, and its fruit is a pod containing edible seeds. The plant is typically shrub-like, with trifoliate leaves and a woody stem. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
