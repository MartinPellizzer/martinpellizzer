Potamogeton × fluitans subsp. petiolaris

Potamogeton × fluitans subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia. It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged.

The plant is characterized by its petiolate leaves, which are distinctly longer than the blade, giving it a unique appearance. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often indicative of clean, well-oxygenated water.
