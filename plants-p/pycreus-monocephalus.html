Pycreus monocephalus

Pycreus monocephalus, commonly known as the single-headed pycreus, is a unique species within the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its solitary, showy inflorescence that typically bears a single flower, which is a distinguishing feature among its relatives. The plant produces a striking, often brightly colored flower with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
