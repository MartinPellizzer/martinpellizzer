Podolepis angustifolia

Podolepis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed podolepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or white flowers. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive foliage and delicate blooms.
