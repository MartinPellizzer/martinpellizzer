Pellegrinia buxifolia

Pellegrinia buxifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves with a silvery-white underside, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Pellegrinia buxifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage.
