Pavetta macrosepala var. macrosepala

Pavetta macrosepala var. macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with showy petals and prominent stamens. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
