Paepalanthus laxifolius

Paepalanthus laxifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus characterized by its pale green, cylindrical stems and small, woolly leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
