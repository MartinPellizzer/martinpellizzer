Pandanus pluriangulatus

Pandanus pluriangulatus, commonly known as the multi-angled pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, multi-angled stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental appearance but also for its cultural and practical uses in local communities.
