Pleurothallis acuminata

Pleurothallis acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique floral structure and compact growth make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
