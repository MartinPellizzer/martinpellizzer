Passiflora spirei

Passiflora spirei, commonly known as the Spires passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora spirei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
