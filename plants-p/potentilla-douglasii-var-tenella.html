Potentilla douglasii var. tenella

Potentilla douglasii var. tenella, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a soft, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is commonly used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
