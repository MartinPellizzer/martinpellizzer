Peperomia emarginella

Peperomia emarginella, commonly known as the Margined Peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features rounded, waxy leaves with a distinctive wavy or notched edge, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or green, often followed by bright red berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.

Peperomia emarginella is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
