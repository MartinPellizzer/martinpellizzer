Plantago officinarum

Plantago officinarum, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, upright flowering stems with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
