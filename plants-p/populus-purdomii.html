Populus purdomii

Populus purdomii, commonly known as the Purdom's poplar, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Populus purdomii plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
