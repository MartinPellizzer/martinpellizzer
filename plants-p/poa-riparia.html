Poa riparia

Poa riparia, commonly known as riparian grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, thriving in wetland and riparian habitats. The plant has slender, tufted culms and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or pink. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa riparia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
