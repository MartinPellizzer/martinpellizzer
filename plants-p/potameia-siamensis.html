Potameia siamensis

Potameia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest understories. Its flowers, which can be pink or white, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Potameia siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation and display.
