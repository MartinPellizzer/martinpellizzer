Passiflora pertriloba

Passiflora pertriloba, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature intricate patterns of blue, purple, and white. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora pertriloba is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
