Pittosporum huegelianum

Pittosporum huegelianum, commonly known as the Huergel's pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often fragrant, and it produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in spring. The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Pittosporum huegelianum is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators and its resilience in temperate climates.
