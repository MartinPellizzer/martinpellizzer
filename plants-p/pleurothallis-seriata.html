Pleurothallis seriata

Pleurothallis seriata, commonly known as the seriated orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Pleurothallis seriata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy cultivation in suitable conditions.
