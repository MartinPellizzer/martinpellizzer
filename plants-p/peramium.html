Peramium

Peramium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple ray florets and a central disk of smaller florets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Peramium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
