Plantago tomentosa var. achalensis

Plantago tomentosa var. achalensis, commonly known as the Achalensis plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species native to the western Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and erect, branching stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white tomentum, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool climates.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
