Parkia singularis subsp. singularis

Parkia singularis subsp.

singularis, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking yellow inflorescences and compound leaves with pinnate arrangement.

The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
