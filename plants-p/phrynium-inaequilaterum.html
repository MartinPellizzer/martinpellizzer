Phrynium inaequilaterum

Phrynium inaequilaterum, commonly known as the unequal-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces striking greenish-white spadices, which are the flowering structures typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source, similar to other arrowroot species.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.
