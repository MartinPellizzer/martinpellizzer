Piper protractispicum

Piper protractispicum, commonly known as the elongated pepper vine, is a member of the Piperaceae family, known for its aromatic and often spicy fruits.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
