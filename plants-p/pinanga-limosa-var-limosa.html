Pinanga limosa var. limosa

Pinanga limosa var. limosa, commonly known as the broad-leafed bamboo palm, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal in both natural and cultivated settings.

It is often used in landscaping due to its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and its air-purifying properties.
