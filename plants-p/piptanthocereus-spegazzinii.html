Piptanthocereus spegazzinii

Piptanthocereus spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a ribbed stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This cactus is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and interesting subject for botanical study.
