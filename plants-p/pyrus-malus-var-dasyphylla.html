Pyrus malus var. dasyphylla

Pyrus malus var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved apple, is a variant of the apple tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sour apples in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The variety is notable for its distinct foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
