Potamogeton × champlainii

Potamogeton × champlainii, commonly known as the Champlain water plant.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. This aquatic plant is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, with some floating leaves at the surface.

Potamogeton × champlainii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
