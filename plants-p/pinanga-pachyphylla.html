Pinanga pachyphylla

Pinanga pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, clustered growth habit and thick, leathery leaves that provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in warm climates.
