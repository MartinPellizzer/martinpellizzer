Phragmipedium boissierianum

Phragmipedium boissierianum, commonly known as the Boissier's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a trap for pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil, often found in montane regions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Phragmipedium boissierianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
