Phragmipedium longifolium f. gracile

Phragmipedium longifolium f. gracile, commonly known as the slender-leafed phragmipedium, is a rare and elegant orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that give it a distinct appearance. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers with a complex structure, featuring a prominent lip and intricate spurs. It thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures, often growing as an epiphyte on tree trunks.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
