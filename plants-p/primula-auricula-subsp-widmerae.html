Primula auricula subsp. widmerae

Primula auricula subsp.

widmerae, commonly known as the Alpine auricula, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or grayish hairs.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
