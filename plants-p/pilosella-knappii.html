Pilosella knappii

Pilosella knappii, commonly known as Knapp's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant is distinguished by its white to pale yellow flowers and silver-tomentose (fuzzy) leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. Pilosella knappii typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
