Polygonum paludosum

Polygonum paludosum, commonly known as swamp knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, along the edges of ponds, and in other moist soils. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.

Polygonum paludosum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
