Protea mollis

Protea mollis, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small white flowers surrounded by a cluster of bracts that appear silver or pale pink. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Protea mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the fynbos vegetation type in South Africa.
