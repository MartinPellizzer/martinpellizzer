Piper transpontinum var. transpontinum

Piper transpontinum var. transpontinum, commonly known as the Transpontine pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with a strong aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
