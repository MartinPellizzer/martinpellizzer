Platycodon

Platycodon, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and grows up to 1 meter in height. Platycodon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
