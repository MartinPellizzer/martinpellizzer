Passiflora ligulifolia

Passiflora ligulifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive flowers, which feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated edge.

Passiflora ligulifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
