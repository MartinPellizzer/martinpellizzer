Polygala chamaebuxus var. lutea

Polygala chamaebuxus var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
