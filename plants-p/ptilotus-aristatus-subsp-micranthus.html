Ptilotus aristatus subsp. micranthus

Ptilotus aristatus subsp.

micranthus, commonly known as the pink burr or desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant typically grows from a short, woody stem and has small, narrow leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. It produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring.

This species is well-suited to harsh environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
