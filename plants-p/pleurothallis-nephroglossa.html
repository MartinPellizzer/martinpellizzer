Pleurothallis nephroglossa

Pleurothallis nephroglossa, commonly known as the kidney-tongue orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, pendulous flowers with white petals and a yellow lip. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often difficult to cultivate outside its natural range.
