Potentilla tridentina f. dumetorum

Potentilla tridentina f. dumetorum, commonly known as the creeping potentilla, is a low-growing shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically divided into three oval-shaped lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to form dense ground cover.
