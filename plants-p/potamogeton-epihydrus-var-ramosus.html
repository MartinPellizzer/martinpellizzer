Potamogeton epihydrus var. ramosus

Potamogeton epihydrus var. ramosus, commonly known as the branched pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems that often grow in dense clusters near the water's surface. The leaves are typically narrow and ribbon-like, with a smooth surface and a slightly pointed tip. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal by water currents.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
