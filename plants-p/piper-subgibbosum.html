Piper subgibbosum

Piper subgibbosum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with a tuft of hair for wind dispersal.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
