Paepalanthus conicus

Paepalanthus conicus, commonly known as the spiny pincushion cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of spines that protect it from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. Its structure is adapted to store water efficiently, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities in desert gardens.
