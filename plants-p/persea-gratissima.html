Persea gratissima

Persea gratissima, commonly known as the cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a large, spiny, green berry that turns yellow when ripe, containing a creamy, sweet, and aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its unique, edible fruit.
