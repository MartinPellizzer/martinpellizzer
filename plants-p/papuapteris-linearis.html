Papuapteris linearis

Papuapteris linearis, commonly known as the linear fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, linear fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Papuapteris linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
