Pelargonium barringtonii

Pelargonium barringtonii, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Pelargonium barringtonii is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
