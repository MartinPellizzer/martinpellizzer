Polygonon persicarium

Polygonon persicarium, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often reddish in color, especially at the base. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant is commonly found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
