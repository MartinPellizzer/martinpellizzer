Pamphilia pedicellata

Pamphilia pedicellata, commonly known as the clustered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it its common name. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and slightly hairy, while the fruit is a spherical, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
