Persicaria galapagensis

Persicaria galapagensis, commonly known as Galápagos knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams and in forest understories.

Persicaria galapagensis plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
