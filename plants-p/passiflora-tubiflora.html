Passiflora tubiflora

Passiflora tubiflora, commonly known as the giant passion flower, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five prominent sepals and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Passiflora tubiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
