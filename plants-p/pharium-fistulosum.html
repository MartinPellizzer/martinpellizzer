Pharium fistulosum

Pharium fistulosum, commonly known as the hollow pharium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant features a bulbous stem and produces clusters of showy blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability to arid conditions contribute to its ornamental value.
