Pelargonium brownii

Pelargonium brownii, commonly known as the Brown's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium brownii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
