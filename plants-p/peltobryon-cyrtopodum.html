Peltobryon cyrtopodum

Peltobryon cyrtopodum, commonly known as the curly-footed liverwort, is a small, non-vascular plant belonging to the family Peltobryaceae.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a thalloid structure with a leafy shoot system and distinctive, curled lobes on its leaves. Its reproductive structures, including male and female gametophytes, are borne on the leaf surfaces.

Peltobryon cyrtopodum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its ecosystem.
