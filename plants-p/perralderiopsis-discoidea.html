Perralderiopsis discoidea

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like structures that form a dense, cushion-like growth. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as on rocks or in forest floors. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short stalks and release spores for propagation.

The species is notable for its small size and unique morphology, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
