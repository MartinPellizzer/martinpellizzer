Plantago spinulosa

Plantago spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, branching stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
