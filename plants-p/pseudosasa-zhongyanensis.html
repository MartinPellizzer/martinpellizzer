Pseudosasa zhongyanensis

Pseudosasa zhongyanensis, commonly known as the Zhongyan bamboo, is a rare and reclusive grass species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered an endangered species.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
