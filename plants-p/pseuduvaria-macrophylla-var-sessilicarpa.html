Pseuduvaria macrophylla var. sessilicarpa

Pseuduvaria macrophylla var. sessilicarpa, commonly known as the sessile-fruited pseuduvaria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically leathery in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use in warm climates.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.
