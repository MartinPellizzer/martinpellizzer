Piper rubrovenosum

Piper rubrovenosum, commonly known as red-veined pepper or red-veined piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive red veins on the leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's ornamental value is also appreciated due to its striking foliage.
