Platanthera klossii var. klossii

Platanthera klossii var. klossii, commonly known as the Kloss's lady's tresses, is a rare orchid species native to the tropical regions of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and long, spirally arranged inflorescences bearing numerous white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, relying on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
