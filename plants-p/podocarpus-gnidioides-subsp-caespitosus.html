Podocarpus gnidioides subsp. caespitosus

Podocarpus gnidioides subsp.

caespitosus, commonly known as the woolly podocarp, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its foliage is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
