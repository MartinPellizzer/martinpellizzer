Primula khasiana

Primula khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi primrose, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Primula khasiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
