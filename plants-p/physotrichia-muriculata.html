Physotrichia muriculata

Physotrichia muriculata, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with a hairy texture on the underside. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, serrated margin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
