Panicum longispiculum

Panicum longispiculum, commonly known as the long-awned panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant features long, bristly awns that give it a distinctive appearance, and its leaves are narrow with a rough texture. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Panicum longispiculum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
