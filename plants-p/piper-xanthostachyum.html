Piper xanthostachyum

Piper xanthostachyum, commonly known as the yellow-stalked pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, yellowish stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, peppery fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
