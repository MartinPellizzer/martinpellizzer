Poterium diandrum

Poterium diandrum, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble buttercups. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, oval fruits known as cypselae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Poterium diandrum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
