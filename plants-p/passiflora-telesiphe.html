Passiflora telesiphe

Passiflora telesiphe, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features vibrant, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces orange-red berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a waxy texture and a distinctive scent.

Passiflora telesiphe is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
