Paeonia tenuifolia f. parviflora

Paeonia tenuifolia f. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peony with small flowers, is a cultivar of the peony plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and compact growth habit.

It produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a subtle yet elegant touch to gardens. The plant is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a border plant.

This cultivar is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
