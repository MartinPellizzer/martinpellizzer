Parasenecio sylviaensis

Parasenecio sylviaensis, commonly known as Sylvia's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a strong, bitter scent.

The flowers are yellow, with a central disc and surrounding ray florets, making it visually distinct within its ecosystem.
