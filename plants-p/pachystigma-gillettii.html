Pachystigma gillettii

Pachystigma gillettii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, ghost-like appearance with translucent, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ethereal look. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
