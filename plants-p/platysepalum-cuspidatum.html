Platysepalum cuspidatum

Platysepalum cuspidatum, commonly known as the Cape rose or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
