Piper nudicaule

Piper nudicaule, commonly known as the small-leaf pepper or slender pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible but often used as a spice in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The species belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many other pepper plants known for their aromatic properties.
