Pyrus turcica

Pyrus turcica, commonly known as the Turkish pear, is a species of tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers. The tree produces small, sweet fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or hilly areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
