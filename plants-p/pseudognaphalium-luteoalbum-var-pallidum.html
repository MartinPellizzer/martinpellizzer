Pseudognaphalium luteoalbum var. pallidum

Pseudognaphalium luteoalbum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to pale yellow flowers and finely divided, silvery-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across suitable environments.

The species is often found in the central and western United States, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.
