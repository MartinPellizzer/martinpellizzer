Phileozera multiflora

Phileozera multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora phileozera, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
