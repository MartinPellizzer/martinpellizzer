Polygala tovariensis

Polygala tovariensis, commonly known as the Tovar's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
