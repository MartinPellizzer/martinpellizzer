Pimelea microcephala var. psilantha

Pimelea microcephala var. psilantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to pink, which appear in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
