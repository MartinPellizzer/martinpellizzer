Phaseoloides monticola

Phaseoloides monticola, commonly known as the mountain bean, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
