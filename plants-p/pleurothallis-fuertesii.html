Pleurothallis fuertesii

Pleurothallis fuertesii, commonly known as the Fuertes' orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small epiphytic plant, typically growing on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
