Pera cinerea

Pera cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf pera, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, covered in a fine, powdery coating that gives them their silvery appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
