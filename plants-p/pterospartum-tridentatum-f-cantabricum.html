Pterospartum tridentatum f. cantabricum

Pterospartum tridentatum f. cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat make it a notable botanical subject.
