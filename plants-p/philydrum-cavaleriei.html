Philydrum cavaleriei

Philydrum cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that resemble those of the buttercup. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers consist of numerous stamens surrounding a central pistil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
