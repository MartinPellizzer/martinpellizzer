Poa hirta

Poa hirta, commonly known as hairy bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that are often covered in short, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Poa hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes.
