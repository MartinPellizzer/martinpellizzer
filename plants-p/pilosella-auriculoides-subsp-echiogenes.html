Pilosella auriculoides subsp. echiogenes

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

echiogenes, commonly known as the golden-leafed hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a single, showy head, with ray florets that are often tinged with orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
