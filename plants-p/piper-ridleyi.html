Piper ridleyi

Piper ridleyi, commonly known as Ridley's pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, spindle-shaped fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when mature and dried for use as a spice.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
