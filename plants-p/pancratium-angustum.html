Pancratium angustum

Pancratium angustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yellow mangosteen, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are known for their strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pancratium angustum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
