Plukenetia volubilis

Plukenetia volubilis, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, yellowish-green flowers and oval, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a thick, inedible rind and a starchy, edible pulp inside, which is used in traditional cuisines. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas.

It plays an important role in local agriculture and has potential for use in sustainable food systems.
