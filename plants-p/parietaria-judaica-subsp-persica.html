Parietaria judaica subsp. persica

Parietaria judaica subsp.

persica, commonly known as Persian wall rocket, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
