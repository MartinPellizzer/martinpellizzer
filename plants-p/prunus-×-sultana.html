Prunus × sultana

Prunus × sultana, commonly known as the sour cherry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits that are widely used in culinary applications such as jams and pastries. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring.

The fruit ripens in late summer, contributing to its importance in both agriculture and horticulture.
