Penaea myrtifolia

Penaea myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf penaea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Penaea myrtifolia is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
