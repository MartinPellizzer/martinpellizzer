Persea petiolaris

Persea petiolaris, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by yellow-orange berries that ripen in the fall. These berries are edible when fully mature and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.
