Pogostemon nudus

Pogostemon nudus, commonly known as the slender pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

Pogostemon nudus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
