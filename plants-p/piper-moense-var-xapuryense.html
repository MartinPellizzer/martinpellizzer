Piper moense var. xapuryense

Piper moense var. xapuryense, commonly known as the Xapury pepper, is a tropical vine native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, thin fruits that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The species is notable for its adaptability to humid, shaded environments in its natural habitat.
