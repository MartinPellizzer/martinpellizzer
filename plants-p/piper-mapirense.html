Piper mapirense

Piper mapirense, commonly known as the Mapire pepper or Mapire pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, specifically found in parts of Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in terminal spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible.

Piper mapirense is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is also used in traditional cooking in some regions.
