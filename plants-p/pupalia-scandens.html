Pupalia scandens

Pupalia scandens, commonly known as the climbing false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets and produces striking blue to violet flowers in late spring. The plant is known for its vigorous climbing habit, often using tendrils to ascend supports. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
