Paepalanthus cylindraceus

Paepalanthus cylindraceus, commonly known as the cylindrical cholla, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are covered in spines and are often used for climbing. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to harsh desert conditions.

Its structure and growth pattern make it a unique and resilient member of the cactus family.
