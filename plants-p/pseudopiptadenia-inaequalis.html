Pseudopiptadenia inaequalis

Pseudopiptadenia inaequalis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink trumpet tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of timber.

Its growth habit includes a fast-growing, often deciduous tree with a spreading canopy.
