Plantago denudata

Plantago denudata, commonly known as the naked plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are hairless and have a waxy surface. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. Its flowers are typically wind-pollinated, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

It is a member of the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
