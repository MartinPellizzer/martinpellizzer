Pinus montana var. pseudopumilio

Pinus montana var. pseudopumilio, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain bristlecone pine, is a variant of the mountain pine species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and longevity, often living over 1,000 years. The plant exhibits a distinctive cone shape and has needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more rigid than those of other pine species. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cold, dry climates and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in alpine ecosystems.
