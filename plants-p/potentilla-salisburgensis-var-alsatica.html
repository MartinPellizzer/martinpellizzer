Potentilla salisburgensis var. alsatica

Potentilla salisburgensis var. alsatica, commonly known as the Salzburg cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Central Europe.

It typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
