Pardisium

Pardisium, commonly known as the paradise tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a fruit resembling a small, spiky gourd, which contains a single, large seed. Botanically, it belongs to the Moraceae family, which also includes figs and mulberries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in various cultures.
