Potentilla angustata

Potentilla angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive narrow shape with serrated edges.

Potentilla angustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
