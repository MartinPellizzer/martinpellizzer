Pulsatilla ovczinnikovii

Pulsatilla ovczinnikovii, commonly known as the Ovczinnikov's pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a short, hairy stem with basal leaves that are palmately divided. Its flowers are typically yellow or pale yellow with a hairy center, and it produces follicles that contain numerous seeds.

Pulsatilla ovczinnikovii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
