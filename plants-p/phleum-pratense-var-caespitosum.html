Phleum pratense var. caespitosum

Phleum pratense var. caespitosum, commonly known as tufted timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are usually greenish or pale brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
