Panicum kisantuense

Panicum kisantuense, commonly known as Kisantu grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swampy areas and riverbanks, and is adapted to humid tropical climates. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets, which are often pink or reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
