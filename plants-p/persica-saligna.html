Persica saligna

Persica saligna, commonly known as the willowleaf peach, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that resemble peaches in appearance. Persica saligna is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to the Prunus genus.
