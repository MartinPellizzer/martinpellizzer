Phlox longifolia var. longipes

Phlox longifolia var. longipes, commonly known as the long-flowered phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are opposite or alternate in arrangement, and its stems are often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
