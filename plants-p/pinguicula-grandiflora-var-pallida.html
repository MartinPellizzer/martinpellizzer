Pinguicula grandiflora var. pallida

Pinguicula grandiflora var. pallida, commonly known as the pale greater butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest small insects. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems with clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic environments and requires high humidity and indirect light.

Its unique botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and scientific study.
