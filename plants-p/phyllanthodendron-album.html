Phyllanthodendron album

Phyllanthodendron album, commonly known as the white phyllanthodendron, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and are often green or yellowish in color.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
