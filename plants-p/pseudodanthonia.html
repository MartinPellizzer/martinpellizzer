Pseudodanthonia

Pseudodanthonia, commonly known as false danthonia, is a genus of grasses native to southern Africa.

These plants typically grow in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. They are characterized by their slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The inflorescence consists of a spike or panicle, bearing small, two-lobed spikelets.

Pseudodanthonia species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
