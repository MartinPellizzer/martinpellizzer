Printzia pyrifolia

Printzia pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers in early spring, which give way to red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
