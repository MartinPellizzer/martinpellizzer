Peperomia polymorpha

Peperomia polymorpha, commonly known as the Watermelon Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a glossy texture and a range of colors from deep green to variegated white and pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique leaf patterns and low maintenance requirements make it both ornamental and easy to care for.
