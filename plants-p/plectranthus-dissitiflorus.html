Plectranthus dissitiflorus

Plectranthus dissitiflorus, commonly known as the velvetleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Plectranthus dissitiflorus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
