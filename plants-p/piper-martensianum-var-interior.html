Piper martensianum var. interior

Piper martensianum var. interior, commonly known as the interior pepper vine, is a climbing perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy texture and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is characterized by its unique, peppery scent and the presence of tiny, berry-like fruits that develop after flowering. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a food source for various wildlife species.
