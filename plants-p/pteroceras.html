Pteroceras

Pteroceras, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, often branched inflorescence and showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments. Pteroceras species have specialized roots that help them absorb nutrients from the soil, and their leaves are usually narrow and strap-like.

The genus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
