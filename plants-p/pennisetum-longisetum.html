Pennisetum longisetum

Pennisetum longisetum, commonly known as the long-haired lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-green foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Pennisetum longisetum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and butterflies.
