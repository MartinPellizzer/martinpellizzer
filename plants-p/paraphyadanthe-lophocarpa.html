Paraphyadanthe lophocarpa

Paraphyadanthe lophocarpa, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, greenish-white blossoms that are often clustered together. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky or sandy soils, allowing it to thrive in harsh mountain climates.
