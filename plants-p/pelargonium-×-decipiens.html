Pelargonium × decipiens

Pelargonium × decipiens, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and prolonged flowering period.

Pelargonium × decipiens thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
