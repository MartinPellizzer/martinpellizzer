Polygonum chevrolatii

Polygonum chevrolatii, commonly known as the Chevrolat's knotweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Polygonum chevrolatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
