Phlebosporum

Phlebosporum, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its distinctive reddish veins on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Phlebosporum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
