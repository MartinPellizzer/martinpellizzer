Picramnia coccinea

Picramnia coccinea, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish hue, which contributes to its ornamental value.

Picramnia coccinea is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty and is adaptable to various soil types.
