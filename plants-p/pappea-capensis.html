Pappea capensis

Pappea capensis, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The African snowball is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
