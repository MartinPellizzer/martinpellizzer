Prunus alleghaniensis davisii

Prunus alleghaniensis davisii, commonly known as the Allegheny cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in late summer.
