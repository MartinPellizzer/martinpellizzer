Puccinellia nuttalliana

Puccinellia nuttalliana, commonly known as Nuttall's wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Puccinellia nuttalliana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
