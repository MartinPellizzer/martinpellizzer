Paepalanthus xeranthemoides

Paepalanthus xeranthemoides, commonly known as the desert candle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit and is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It produces fibrous roots that help it retain water in harsh environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens.
