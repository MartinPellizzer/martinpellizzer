Ponthieva mandonii var. bilobipetala

Ponthieva mandonii var. bilobipetala, commonly known as the Bilobed Petal Ponthieva, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature distinctive bilobed petals, which set it apart from other species in the genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, thriving in the canopy of humid forests where it receives ample light and moisture. Its foliage consists of broad, glossy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and unique botanical features, it is highly valued in horticultural collections and conservation efforts.
