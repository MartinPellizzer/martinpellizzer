Prunus pleuradenia

Prunus pleuradenia, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, cherry-like fruits that are typically sour. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Prunus pleuradenia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
