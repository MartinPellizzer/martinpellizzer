Pimelea spinescens subsp. pubiflora

Pimelea spinescens subsp.

pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy pimelea, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, usually yellow or cream-colored flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy foliage and floral structures, which set it apart from other members of the species. It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant is adapted to coastal and inland regions, showing resilience to drought and salt spray.
