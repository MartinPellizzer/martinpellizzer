Petroselinum sativum var. crispum

Petroselinum sativum var. crispum, commonly known as curly parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, umbrella-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees. Curly parsley is cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in cooking and herbal remedies.

It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and full sun exposure.
