Platyopuntia sulphurea

Platyopuntia sulphurea, commonly known as the yellow prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are typically covered in yellowish spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to arid environments.

The plant's structure and growth patterns make it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert landscaping.
