Pretreothamnus africartus

Pretreothamnus africartus, commonly known as the African thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Pretreothamnus africartus is adapted to withstand drought and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
