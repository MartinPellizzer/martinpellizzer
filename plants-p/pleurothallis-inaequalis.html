Pleurothallis inaequalis

Pleurothallis inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a compact rosette, adapting to its humid, high-altitude habitat.

Pleurothallis inaequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
