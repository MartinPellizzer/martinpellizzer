Platynema laurifolium

Platynema laurifolium, commonly known as the laurel-leafed plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features alternate, simple, and often aromatic leaves that resemble those of laurel plants. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
