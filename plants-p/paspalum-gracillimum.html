Paspalum gracillimum

Paspalum gracillimum, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features slender, tufted stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Paspalum gracillimum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
