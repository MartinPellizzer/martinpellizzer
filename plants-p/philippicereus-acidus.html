Philippicereus acidus

Philippicereus acidus, commonly known as the acidic cactus or the sour cactus, is a species of cactus native to parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, a typical adaptation for arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Philippicereus acidus is often cultivated for its resilience and unique appearance in desert gardens.
