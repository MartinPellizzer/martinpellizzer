Piper tenuiamentum

Piper tenuiamentum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
