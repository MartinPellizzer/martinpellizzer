Prasophyllum plumiforme

Prasophyllum plumiforme, commonly known as the plum-like leek orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, leek-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and moderate temperatures.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
