Pomaderris argyrophylla

Pomaderris argyrophylla, commonly known as silver-leafed pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Pomaderris argyrophylla is often found in dry, sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
