Populus fremontii var. toumeyi

Populus fremontii var. toumeyi, commonly known as the Fremont's cottonwood, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fluffy white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant thrives in riparian zones, often growing along riverbanks and in moist, well-drained soils. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it an important component of arid ecosystem dynamics.
