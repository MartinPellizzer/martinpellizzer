Prunus × donarium var. spontanea

Prunus × donarium var. spontanea, commonly known as the wild cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, yellowish-orange drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
