Paronychia capitata var. bracteosa

Paronychia capitata var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an attractive addition to xeriscaped gardens.
