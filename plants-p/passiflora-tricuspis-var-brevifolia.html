Passiflora tricuspis var. brevifolia

Passiflora tricuspis var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a short, broad base, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
