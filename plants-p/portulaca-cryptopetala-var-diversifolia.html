Portulaca cryptopetala var. diversifolia

Portulaca cryptopetala var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated moss rose, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, colorful flowers in shades of red, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it both a decorative and drought-tolerant plant.
