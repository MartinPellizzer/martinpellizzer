Protea lagopus

Protea lagopus, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fluffy inflorescence that resembles a crown. The plant features large, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a central cluster of small, petal-like structures.

Protea lagopus thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
