Pseuderanthemum haikangense

Pseuderanthemum haikangense, commonly known as the Haikang false jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces small, clustered inflorescences with bright yellow or orange petals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
