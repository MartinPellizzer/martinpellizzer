Pleurothallis adeleae

Pleurothallis adeleae, commonly known as the Adele's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and bloom during the wet season.

Pleurothallis adeleae is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
