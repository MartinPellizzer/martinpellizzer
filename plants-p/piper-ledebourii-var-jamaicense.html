Piper ledebourii var. jamaicense

Piper ledebourii var. jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican black pepper, is a tropical vine native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery fruits that resemble black pepper. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
