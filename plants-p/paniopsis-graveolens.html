Paniopsis graveolens

Paniopsis graveolens, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in shaded environments. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it is important to note that the plant is toxic if ingested in large quantities.
