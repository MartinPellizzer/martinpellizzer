Plectranthus montanus

Plectranthus montanus, commonly known as the mountain plectranthus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or white, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus montanus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
