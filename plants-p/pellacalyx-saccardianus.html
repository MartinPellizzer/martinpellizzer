Pellacalyx saccardianus

Pellacalyx saccardianus, commonly known as the Saccardian calyx plant, is a rare flowering species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated calyx that resembles a sac, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.
