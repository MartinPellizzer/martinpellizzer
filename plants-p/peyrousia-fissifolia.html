Peyrousia fissifolia

Peyrousia fissifolia, commonly known as the fissured leaf peyrousia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Peyrousia fissifolia thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in desert botany and ecological adaptation.
