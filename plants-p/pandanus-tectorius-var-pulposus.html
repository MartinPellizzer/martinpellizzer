Pandanus tectorius var. pulposus

Pandanus tectorius var. pulposus, commonly known as the bottle palm or swamp pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and parts of Southeast Asia, often growing in coastal or wetland areas. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. Its roots and leaves have been utilized in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small animals.
