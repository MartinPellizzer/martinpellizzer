Panicum sanguinale var. simpsonii

Panicum sanguinale var. simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's bloodgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tipped foliage, which adds visual interest to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and forms dense, clump-like growth patterns. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft texture and a bluish-green hue.

Simpson's bloodgrass thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.
