Psidium brevifolium

Psidium brevifolium, commonly known as the short-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to southern Brazil and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
