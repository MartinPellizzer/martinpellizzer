Piper cabagranum

Piper cabagranum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The botanical features of Piper cabagranum make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
