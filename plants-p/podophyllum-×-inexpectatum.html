Podophyllum × inexpectatum

Podophyllum × inexpectatum, commonly known as the hybrid American bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
