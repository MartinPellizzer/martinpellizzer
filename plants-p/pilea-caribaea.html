Pilea caribaea

Pilea caribaea, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically round or heart-shaped, giving it a unique and decorative appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by round, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings adds to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
