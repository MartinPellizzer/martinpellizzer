Podochilus hystricinus

Podochilus hystricinus, commonly known as the hairy podochilus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hairy appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are elongated and covered in fine, hairy structures, which help retain moisture.

The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink, adding to its ornamental value.
