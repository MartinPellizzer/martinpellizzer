Passiflora tarminiana

Passiflora tarminiana, commonly known as the Tarminian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with prominent stamens and a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage consists of palmately lobed leaves, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Passiflora tarminiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
