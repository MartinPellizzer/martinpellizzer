Papaver yildirimlii

Papaver yildirimlii, commonly known as the Turkish poppy, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals that resemble a halo, giving it its Turkish name meaning "halo poppy.". The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The botanical features of Papaver yildirimlii make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
