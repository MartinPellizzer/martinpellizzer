Piper anonifolium f. parvifolium

Piper anonifolium f. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, narrow fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of spices.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and taxonomic study.
