Paeonia officinalis subsp. italica

Paeonia officinalis subsp.

italica, commonly known as the Italian peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features showy, double flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant has large, palmately divided leaves and produces a tall, erect stem. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is closely related to other peony species but is distinguished by its specific floral characteristics and geographic distribution.
