Piper scopulorum

Piper scopulorum, commonly known as the rock pepper or stone pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and elongated, narrow leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in dense undergrowth.

The fruit of Piper scopulorum is a small, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
