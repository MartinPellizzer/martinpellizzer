Pavia rubra

Pavia rubra, commonly known as the red pavia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rhamnaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, red or orange-red flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
