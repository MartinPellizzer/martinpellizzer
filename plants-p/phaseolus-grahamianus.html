Phaseolus grahamianus

Phaseolus grahamianus, commonly known as the Graham's bean, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are typically used for culinary purposes. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaseolus grahamianus is also valued for its nutritional content and potential agricultural significance.
