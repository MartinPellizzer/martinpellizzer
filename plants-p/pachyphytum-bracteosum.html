Pachyphytum bracteosum

Pachyphytum bracteosum, commonly known as the "Bracteose Pachyphytum," is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, typical of many desert plants. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
