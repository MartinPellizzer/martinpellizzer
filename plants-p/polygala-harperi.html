Polygala harperi

Polygala harperi, commonly known as the mountain polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive structure that resembles a spurred or spurred-like appendage.

Polygala harperi is often found in open woodland or shrubland habitats at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.
