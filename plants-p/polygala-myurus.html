Polygala myurus

Polygala myurus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
