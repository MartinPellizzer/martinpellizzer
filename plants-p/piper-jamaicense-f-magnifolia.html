Piper jamaicense f. magnifolia

Piper jamaicense f. magnifolia, commonly known as the Jamaican pepper plant or giant pepper vine, is a tropical species native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The plant's rapid growth and climbing habit make it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in tropical gardens.
