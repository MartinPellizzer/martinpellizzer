Pentacalia magistri

Pentacalia magistri, commonly known as the sacred nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other notable plants like tomatoes and tobacco. The plant is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its toxic properties, it is handled with caution and is not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.
