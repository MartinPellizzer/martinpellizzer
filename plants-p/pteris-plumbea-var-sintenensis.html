Pteris plumbea var. sintenensis

Pteris plumbea var. sintenensis, commonly known as the Sintenens fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive dark green color and a waxy texture. It produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
