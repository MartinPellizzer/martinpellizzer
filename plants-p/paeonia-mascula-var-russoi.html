Paeonia mascula var. russoi

Paeonia mascula var. russoi, commonly known as the Russoi peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant features robust, woody stems and heart-shaped, compound leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its roots are thick and fibrous, adapted to thrive in well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
