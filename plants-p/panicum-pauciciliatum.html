Panicum pauciciliatum

Panicum pauciciliatum, commonly known as the sparse-branched panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and savannas.

Panicum pauciciliatum is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
