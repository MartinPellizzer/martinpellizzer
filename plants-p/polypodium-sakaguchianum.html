Polypodium sakaguchianum

Polypodium sakaguchianum, commonly known as the Japanese wall fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to East Asia.

It typically grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, serrated edge. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori on the underside of the leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
