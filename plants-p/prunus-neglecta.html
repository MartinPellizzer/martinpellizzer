Prunus neglecta

Prunus neglecta, commonly known as the wild cherry or rock cherry, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to ovate in shape, with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and are followed by small, dark red or black berries.

Prunus neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
