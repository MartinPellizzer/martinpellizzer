Pouteria embeliifolia

Pouteria embeliifolia, commonly known as the white sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply veined. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, green fruits with a thick, sweet, yellowish pulp. The fruit is typically eaten fresh or used in culinary applications due to its mild, sweet flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
