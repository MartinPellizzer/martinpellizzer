Poa serotina var. purpurea

Poa serotina var. purpurea, commonly known as purple meadowgrass, is a variant of the serotina meadowgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple tinge on the leaf blades. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
