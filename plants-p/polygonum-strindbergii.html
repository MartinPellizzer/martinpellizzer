Polygonum strindbergii

Polygonum strindbergii, commonly known as Strindberg's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, particularly China and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters.

Polygonum strindbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
