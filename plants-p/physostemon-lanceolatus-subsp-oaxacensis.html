Physostemon lanceolatus subsp. oaxacensis

Physostemon lanceolatus subsp.

oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxacan Physostemon, is a perennial herb native to Mexico. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
