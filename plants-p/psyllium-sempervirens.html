Psyllium sempervirens

Psyllium sempervirens, commonly known as the common psyllium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Its most notable feature is the production of mucilage-rich seeds, which are widely used in herbal remedies and as a dietary fiber supplement.

Psyllium sempervirens thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a versatile plant for both agricultural and medicinal purposes.
