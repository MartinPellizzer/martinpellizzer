Poa calycina var. mathewsii

Poa calycina var. mathewsii, commonly known as Mathews' bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 5 mm wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
