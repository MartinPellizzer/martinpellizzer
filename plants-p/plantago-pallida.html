Plantago pallida

Plantago pallida, commonly known as slender plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a rosette of broad, pale green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
