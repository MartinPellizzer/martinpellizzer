Pelargonium × tyrianthinum

Pelargonium × tyrianthinum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with a dark center. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with serrated, dark green leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period, which can extend from spring through early autumn.
