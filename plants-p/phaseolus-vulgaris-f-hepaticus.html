Phaseolus vulgaris f. hepaticus

Phaseolus vulgaris f. hepaticus, commonly known as the liver bean plant, is a cultivar of the common bean.

It is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped seeds that are typically green when immature. The plant is an annual legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

This variety is often grown for its edible seeds, which can be cooked and consumed as a vegetable or used in various culinary dishes.
