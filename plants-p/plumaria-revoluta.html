Plumaria revoluta

Plumaria revoluta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Its flowers are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, round pod that splits open to release seeds.
