Pyrus minima

Pyrus minima, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to western Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 3 to 6 meters and has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish fruits known as "whitebeams.". Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Pyrus minima is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
