Phacelia purshii var. boykinii

Phacelia purshii var. boykinii, commonly known as Boykin's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It has narrow, hairy leaves and a sprawling growth habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in native landscapes.
