Pothos monopetalus

Pothos monopetalus, commonly known as the single-petaled pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.

Pothos monopetalus is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to purify indoor air.
