Pittosporum pervillei subsp. pervillei

Pittosporum pervillei subsp.

pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its fragrant, white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, round, red or orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
