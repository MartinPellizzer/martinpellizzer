Phlox speciosa f. alba

Phlox speciosa f. alba, commonly known as the white garden phlox, is a cultivar of the native phlox species native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is dark green and finely textured, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

It is well-suited for garden use in borders, rock gardens, or as part of a wildflower meadow.
