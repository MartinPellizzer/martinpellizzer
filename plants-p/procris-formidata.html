Procris formidata

Procris formidata, commonly known as the giant flame violet, is a striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, featuring vibrant red and orange hues with intricate patterns. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in seasonal rainfall environments. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its dramatic blooms.

Procris formidata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
