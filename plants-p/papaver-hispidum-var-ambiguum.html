Papaver hispidum var. ambiguum

Papaver hispidum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the rough poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bright orange to red flowers with a prominent central disk, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
