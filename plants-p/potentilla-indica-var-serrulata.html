Potentilla indica var. serrulata

Potentilla indica var. serrulata, commonly known as the serrated potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, compact foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its botanical name. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
