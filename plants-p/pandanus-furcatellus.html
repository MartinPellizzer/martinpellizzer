Pandanus furcatellus

Pandanus furcatellus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near mangroves.
