Polemonium filicinum

Polemonium filicinum, commonly known as the maidenhair polemonium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its ornamental value in gardens and natural habitats.
