Phoebe montana var. rigida

Phoebe montana var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid phoebe, is a variant of the phoebe tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a glossy surface and a pointed tip. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are attractive to birds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
