Potentilla egedii

Potentilla egedii, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets that are usually elliptical in shape and covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla egedii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
