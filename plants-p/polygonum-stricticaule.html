Polygonum stricticaule

Polygonum stricticaule, commonly known as the upright knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and appear in late summer.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows.
