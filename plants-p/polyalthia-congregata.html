Polyalthia congregata

Polyalthia congregata, commonly known as the Indian flame of the forest, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and glossy in appearance. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a reddish hue, which adds to its ornamental value.

The tree is widely cultivated for its ornamental beauty and is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
