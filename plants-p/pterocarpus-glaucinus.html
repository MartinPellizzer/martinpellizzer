Pterocarpus glaucinus

Pterocarpus glaucinus, commonly known as the yellow-wood or Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hard, heavy wood with a distinctive yellowish hue. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in furniture-making and carving.
