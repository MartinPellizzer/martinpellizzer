Pentagonia nuciformis

Pentagonia nuciformis, commonly known as the nutmeg tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble nuts, hence its common name. The seeds inside the fruit are encased in a hard, aromatic shell.

It is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
