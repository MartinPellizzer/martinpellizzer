Podospermum laciniatum subsp. decumbens

Podospermum laciniatum subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the creeping podospermum, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of South America. It features delicate, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in shaded areas.
