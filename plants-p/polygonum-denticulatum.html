Polygonum denticulatum

Polygonum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often edible parts. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with finely toothed margins, contributing to its common name.

Polygonum denticulatum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
