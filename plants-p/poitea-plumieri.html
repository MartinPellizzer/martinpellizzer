Poitea plumieri

Poitea plumieri, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by bright blue berries that persist through winter. These berries are a valuable food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
