Platanthera bifolia var. carducciana

Platanthera bifolia var. carducciana, commonly known as the Italian lady's tresses, is a rare and delicate orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale yellow flower spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is crucial for its survival.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this variety is considered endangered in many regions.
