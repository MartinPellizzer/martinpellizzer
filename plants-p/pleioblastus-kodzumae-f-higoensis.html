Pleioblastus kodzumae f. higoensis

Pleioblastus kodzumae f. higoensis, commonly known as the Higo bamboo, is a clump-forming bamboo species native to Japan.

It features slender, gracefully arching culms that are often tinged with a pale yellow or pinkish hue, especially in younger shoots. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a delicate, silvery-green appearance. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil in partial shade to full sun.
