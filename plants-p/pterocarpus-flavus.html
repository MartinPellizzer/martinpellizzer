Pterocarpus flavus

Pterocarpus flavus, commonly known as the yellow pterocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces woody pods containing flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
