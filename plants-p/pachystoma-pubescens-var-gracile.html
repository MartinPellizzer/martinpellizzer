Pachystoma pubescens var. gracile

Pachystoma pubescens var. gracile, commonly known as the slender desert bell, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the spring or early summer. Its pubescent (hairy) surface helps reduce water loss and provides some protection against intense sunlight.

This variant is a hardy, low-maintenance plant well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
