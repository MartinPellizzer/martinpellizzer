Platystele brenneri

Platystele brenneri, commonly known as the Brenner's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a unique morphology characterized by its pale green, leaf-like stems and reduced leaves. Native to the cloud forests of Mexico, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high atmospheric moisture. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance.

Its conservation status is critically endangered, primarily due to habitat destruction and over-collection.
