Pongamia hypoleuca

Pongamia hypoleuca, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Its ecological importance includes nitrogen fixation, which enriches the soil.
