Pentaphylloides friedrichsenii

Pentaphylloides friedrichsenii, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on coniferous trees such as pines and firs.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients, forming a symbiotic relationship that can impact the host's growth and health. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. It is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Its unique life cycle and ecological role make it an important species in forest ecosystems.
