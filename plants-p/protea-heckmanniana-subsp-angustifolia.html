Protea heckmanniana subsp. angustifolia

Protea heckmanniana subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, cone-shaped inflorescence that bears numerous small, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a silvery or pale green underside.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
