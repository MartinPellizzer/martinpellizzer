Protea hirtella

Protea hirtella, commonly known as the woolly protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small flowers surrounded by a dense, woolly bract. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often pollinated by birds and insects.

Protea hirtella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species in the study of plant adaptations to Mediterranean climates.
