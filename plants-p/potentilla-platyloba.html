Potentilla platyloba

Potentilla platyloba, commonly known as the flatleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with stems that often lie prostrate on the ground. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a distinctive flat or slightly folded appearance.

Potentilla platyloba is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
