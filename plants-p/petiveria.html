Petiveria

Petiveria, commonly known as wild lettuce, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It includes several species, such as Petiveria alliacea, which is native to the Americas. The plant is characterized by its hairy, aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical features of Petiveria make it adaptable to various environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
