Perezia linearis var. humilis

Perezia linearis var. humilis, commonly known as the slender perezia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in native and rare plants.
