Pabstiella mentigera

Pabstiella mentigera, commonly known as the Mentigera lichen, is a rare and specialized symbiotic organism that forms a lichen with a fungal partner.

It is characterized by its unique thallus structure, which is typically crustose and grows on calcareous substrates. The plant's morphology includes a dark, leathery surface with distinct lobes and a central areola. It is primarily found in humid, temperate regions with high moisture levels.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental health.
