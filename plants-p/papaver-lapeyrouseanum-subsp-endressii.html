Papaver lapeyrouseanum subsp. endressii

Papaver lapeyrouseanum subsp.

endressii, commonly known as the Endress's poppy, is a rare and striking member of the Papaveraceae family. It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that contrast with its pale yellow center, forming a showy, cup-shaped flower.

The plant typically grows in alpine or rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its unique appearance.
