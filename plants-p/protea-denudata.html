Protea denudata

Protea denudata, commonly known as the naked protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small flowers clustered around a central stalk. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the fynbos vegetation of the Western Cape. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Protea denudata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
