Phalaris

Phalaris, commonly known as beakgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

These plants are typically annual or perennial herbs, characterized by their narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. Phalaris species produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, and some varieties are used in ornamental gardening.

The genus includes over 200 species, many of which have adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
