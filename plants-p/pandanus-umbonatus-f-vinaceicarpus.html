Pandanus umbonatus f. vinaceicarpus

Pandanus umbonatus f. vinaceicarpus, commonly known as the red-fruited pandanus, is a variant of the pandanus plant characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple fruits.

This species belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is native to various regions in the Indo-Pacific. The plant typically features broad, sword-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its inflorescences are long and branched, bearing small, fragrant flowers that develop into the characteristic fruiting structures.

The unique coloration of its fruits makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical landscapes.
