Piper palenquense

Piper palenquense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper or piper, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
