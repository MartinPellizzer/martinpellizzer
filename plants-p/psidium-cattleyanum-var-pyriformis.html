Psidium cattleyanum var. pyriformis

Psidium cattleyanum var. pyriformis, commonly known as the Brazilian gooseberry, is a variant of the cape gooseberry plant.

It is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The botanical features of this plant make it well-suited for cultivation in warm, humid environments.
