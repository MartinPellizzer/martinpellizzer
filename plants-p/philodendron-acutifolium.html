Philodendron acutifolium

Philodendron acutifolium, commonly known as the acute-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a pointed tip, giving it its scientific name. The plant has a vining growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when supported. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
