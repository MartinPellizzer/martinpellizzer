Pseudotsuga gaussenii

Pseudotsuga gaussenii, commonly known as Gaussen's fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its conical shape and needle-like leaves. The plant produces cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is typically found at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
