Pithecellobium pistaciifolium

Pithecellobium pistaciifolium, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnate and often have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. These flowers are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
