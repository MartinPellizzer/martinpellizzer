Podocarpus macrophyllus var. liukiuensis

Podocarpus macrophyllus var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Liukiu pine, is a variant of the Chinese pine, native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and long, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
