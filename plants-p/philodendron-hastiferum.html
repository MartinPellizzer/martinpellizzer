Philodendron hastiferum

Philodendron hastiferum, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect light.

Philodendron hastiferum is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
