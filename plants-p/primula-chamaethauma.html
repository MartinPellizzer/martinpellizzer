Primula chamaethauma

Primula chamaethauma, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs that help protect it from cold and desiccation. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with five petals and prominent yellow stamens.

It is a member of the Primulaceae family, known for its showy, often brightly colored blooms.
