Phaeopappus szowitsii

Phaeopappus szowitsii, commonly known as the purple pappus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery pappus structures. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its striking purple to reddish flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
