Poa glauca var. bryophila

Poa glauca var. bryophila, commonly known as the blue grass moss, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant forms dense carpets on forest floors or in rocky crevices, contributing to soil stabilization and moisture retention. It is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.

Due to its delicate structure, it is typically propagated through spores or by division of existing clumps.
