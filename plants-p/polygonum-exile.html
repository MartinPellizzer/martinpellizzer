Polygonum exile

Polygonum exile, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often succulent stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Polygonum exile is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
