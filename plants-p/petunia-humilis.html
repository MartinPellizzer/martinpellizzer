Petunia humilis

Petunia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf petunia, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for container gardening and hanging baskets.

Petunia humilis is known for its resilience and ability to bloom continuously throughout the growing season.
