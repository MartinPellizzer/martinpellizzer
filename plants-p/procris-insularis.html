Procris insularis

Procris insularis, commonly known as the island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to specific island regions in the Pacific.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue petals and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and has a compact, herbaceous structure. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Procris insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
