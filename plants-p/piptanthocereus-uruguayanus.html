Piptanthocereus uruguayanus

Piptanthocereus uruguayanus, commonly known as the Uruguayan night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Uruguay and southern Brazil.

It is a flowering cactus characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
