Pentacalia gelida

Pentacalia gelida, commonly known as the iceplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it well adapted to rocky and arid habitats.

Pentacalia gelida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
