Philodendron verrucapetiolum

Philodendron verrucapetiolum, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes reddish underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
