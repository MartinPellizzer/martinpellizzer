Pilocarpus grandiflorus var. grandiflorus

Pilocarpus grandiflorus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Amazonian prickly ash, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in stimulating the nervous system and treating eye conditions.
