Pilea dauciodora var. crenata

Pilea dauciodora var. crenata, commonly known as the Creaseleaf Pilea, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive wavy or scalloped edge, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
