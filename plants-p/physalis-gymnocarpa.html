Physalis gymnocarpa

Physalis gymnocarpa, commonly known as the naked fruit gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its smooth, yellowish fruits that are enclosed in a papery calyx. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and produces small, green leaves with hairy undersides. Its flowers are usually pale yellow and bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit, which ripens to a bright orange color, is edible and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.
