Plantago major subsp. pachystachya

Plantago major subsp.

pachystachya, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces a tall, erect flowering stem topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
