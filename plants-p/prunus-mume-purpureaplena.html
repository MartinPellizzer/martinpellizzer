Prunus mume purpureaplena

Prunus mume purpureaplena, commonly known as the purple-flowered Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to deep purple, often appearing before the leaves in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as mume, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary and traditional medicine applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Prunus mume purpureaplena is also valued for its cultural significance in Japanese and Chinese traditions.
