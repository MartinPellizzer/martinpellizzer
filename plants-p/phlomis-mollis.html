Phlomis mollis

Phlomis mollis, commonly known as soft mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Phlomis mollis is often used in dry gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
