Psychrogeton pseudoerigeron

Psychrogeton pseudoerigeron, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clustered flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils.

Psychrogeton pseudoerigeron plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh habitat and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme climates.
