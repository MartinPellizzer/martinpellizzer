Passiflora physocalymma

Passiflora physocalymma, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns of red and green. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora physocalymma is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
