Polygonum littorale subsp. latifolium

Polygonum littorale subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along shorelines and tidal flats.

The plant features alternate, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in late summer to early fall.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various insects and birds.
