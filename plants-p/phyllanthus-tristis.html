Phyllanthus tristis

Phyllanthus tristis, commonly known as the bitter leaf or false bitterleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, with alternate, simple, and usually elliptic or ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It is characterized by its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The leaves are often harvested for use in herbal remedies, and the plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
