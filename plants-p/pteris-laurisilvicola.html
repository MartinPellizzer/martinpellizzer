Pteris laurisilvicola

Pteris laurisilvicola, commonly known as the laurel forest fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a light green color and a soft texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests with high humidity. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the forest floor, while its spores are dispersed by wind.

Pteris laurisilvicola plays a role in maintaining soil stability and contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
