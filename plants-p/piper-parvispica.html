Piper parvispica

Piper parvispica, commonly known as the small-spurred pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and it is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and aromatic foliage. Piper parvispica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting subject for horticultural study.
