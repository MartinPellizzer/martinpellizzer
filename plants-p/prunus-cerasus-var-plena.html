Prunus cerasus var. plena

Prunus cerasus var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree known for its dense, showy flowers.

It features numerous small, white or pink petals that give it a full, ornamental appearance, making it popular in gardens. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a small, sweet cherry.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
