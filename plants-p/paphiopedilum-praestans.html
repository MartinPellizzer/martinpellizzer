Paphiopedilum praestans

Paphiopedilum praestans, commonly known as the showy slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like labellum, which is a key feature of the slipper orchid genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with purple or brown markings, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and elegant appearance.
