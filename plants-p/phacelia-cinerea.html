Phacelia cinerea

Phacelia cinerea, commonly known as hoary phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Phacelia cinerea is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
