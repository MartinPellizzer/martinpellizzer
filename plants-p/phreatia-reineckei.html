Phreatia reineckei

Phreatia reineckei, commonly known as the Reinecke's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin petals and a narrow, erect flower spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth where it can receive indirect light. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, which is crucial for its survival in moist, nutrient-rich substrates.

Phreatia reineckei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
