Pyrus pyrifolia var. montana

Pyrus pyrifolia var. montana, commonly known as the mountain pear, is a variant of the Japanese pear native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or golden in color when ripe. The plant produces white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
