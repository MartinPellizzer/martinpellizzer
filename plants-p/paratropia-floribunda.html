Paratropia floribunda

Paratropia floribunda, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small stars. The plant has slender, climbing stems that often twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Paratropia floribunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
