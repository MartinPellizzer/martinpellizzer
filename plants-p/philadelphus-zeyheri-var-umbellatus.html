Philadelphus zeyheri var. umbellatus

Philadelphus zeyheri var. umbellatus, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
