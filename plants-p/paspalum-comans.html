Paspalum comans

Paspalum comans, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Paspalum comans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
