Peperomia espinosae

Peperomia espinosae, commonly known as the Spiny Peperomia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Peperomia espinosae is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
