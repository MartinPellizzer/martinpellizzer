Phlomis pseudopuogens

Phlomis pseudopuogens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blazingstar, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, sparsely branched flower clusters in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.
