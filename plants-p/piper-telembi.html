Piper telembi

Piper telembi, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around supports. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually ovate with a rough texture.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and the fruit is a small, spherical berry that turns red when mature.
