Physostelma wallichii

Physostelma wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive elongated spur at the base. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Campanulaceae family.
