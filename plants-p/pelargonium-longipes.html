Pelargonium longipes

Pelargonium longipes, commonly known as the longipes geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color.

Pelargonium longipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
