Portulaca filifolia

Portulaca filifolia, commonly known as threadleaf purslane, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, fleshy, thread-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in hot, dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Portulaca filifolia is drought-tolerant and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
