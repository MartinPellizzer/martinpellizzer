Pyrus communis var. boyon

Pyrus communis var. boyon, commonly known as the Boyon pear, is a variety of the common pear tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a yellowish-green skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with dense, dark green foliage. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, often cultivated in gardens and orchards.
