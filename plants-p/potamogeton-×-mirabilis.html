Potamogeton × mirabilis

Potamogeton × mirabilis, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus. This plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Potamogeton × mirabilis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
