Physoptychis caspica

Physoptychis caspica, commonly known as the Caspian bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves and specialized structures called bladders, which are used for capturing small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often in regions with soft, muddy substrates. Its reproductive system involves both sexual and asexual methods, allowing it to spread efficiently in suitable environments.

The Caspian bladderwort plays a minor but important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
