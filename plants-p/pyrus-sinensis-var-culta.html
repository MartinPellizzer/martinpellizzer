Pyrus sinensis var. culta

Pyrus sinensis var. culta, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a cultivar of the pear tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and large, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces sweet, white to yellowish fruit with a crisp texture and a mild, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
