Peucedanum lemmonii

Peucedanum lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves with finely divided lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its fruits are small, dry, and three-lobed, containing a single seed each.
