Pteris quadriaurita var. argyraea

Pteris quadriaurita var. argyraea, commonly known as the silver-veined water fern, is a variant of the common water fern, characterized by its striking silver veins on the fronds.

It is a member of the fern family, showcasing typical fern morphology with a rhizomatous growth habit. The fronds are pinnately compound, with a delicate, feathery appearance that enhances its ornamental value. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique variegation makes it a popular choice for both aquatic and terrestrial horticultural displays.
