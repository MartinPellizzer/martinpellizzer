Parthenocissus renukae

Parthenocissus renukae, commonly known as the Himalayan ivy, is a climbing vine native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically inconspicuous and followed by dark blue to black berries. Its stems are equipped with adhesive pads that allow it to cling to surfaces, aiding in its climbing growth habit.

This species is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.
