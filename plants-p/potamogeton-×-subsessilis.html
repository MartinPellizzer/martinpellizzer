Potamogeton × subsessilis

Potamogeton × subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spikelets, and it is often found in shallow, still or slow-moving waters. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, which can influence water clarity and habitat structure.

Potamogeton × subsessilis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing shelter for aquatic organisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
