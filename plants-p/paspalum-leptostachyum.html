Paspalum leptostachyum

Paspalum leptostachyum, commonly known as the slender-stalked paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, preferring saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
