Phyllostachys × tranquillans

Phyllostachys × tranquillans, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a hybrid grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching culms that often display a silvery-blue hue due to the presence of a waxy coating. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, adding a striking visual contrast to its silver stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

This bamboo is relatively fast-growing and can be propagated through rhizomes or seed, making it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers.
