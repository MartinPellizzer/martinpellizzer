Pandanus affinis

Pandanus affinis, commonly known as the common pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that often have a fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into spiky, woody fruits. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
