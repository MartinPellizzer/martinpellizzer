Pycnanthemum verticillatum var. verticillatum

Pycnanthemum verticillatum var. verticillatum, commonly known as sweet goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are hairy and often reddish in color. The plant produces small, golden-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters at the top of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Sweet goldenrod is known for its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist to mesic woodlands and prairies.
