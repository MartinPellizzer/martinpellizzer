Pandanus labyrinthicus var. labyrinthicus

Pandanus labyrinthicus var. labyrinthicus, commonly known as the labyrinth pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it well adapted to coastal and wetland environments.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
