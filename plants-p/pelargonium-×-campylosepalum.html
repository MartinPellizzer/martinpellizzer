Pelargonium × campylosepalum

Pelargonium × campylosepalum, commonly known as the Campylosepalam pelargonium, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from pink to deep red. The leaves are typically rounded and have a velvety texture, often with a slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom profusely throughout the growing season.
