Polygala pseudocoriacea

Polygala pseudocoriacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in grasslands and along rocky outcrops, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
