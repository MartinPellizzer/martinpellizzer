Pittosporum oligophlebium

Pittosporum oligophlebium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Pittosporum oligophlebium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
