Primula × aranensis

Primula × aranensis, commonly known as the Alpina primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula × aranensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in garden settings with proper care.
