Puccinellia vaginata

Puccinellia vaginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is well adapted to harsh conditions, including high salinity and low water availability. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils in coastal and desert regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of salt-tolerant vegetation in ecological restoration projects.
