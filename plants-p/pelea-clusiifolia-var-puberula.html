Pelea clusiifolia var. puberula

Pelea clusiifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the velvet leaf or silverleaf pelea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a glossy, waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to humid, shady environments.
