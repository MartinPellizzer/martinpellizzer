Passiflora sierrae

Passiflora sierrae, commonly known as the Sierra passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Passiflora sierrae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
