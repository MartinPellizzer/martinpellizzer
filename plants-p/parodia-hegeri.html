Parodia hegeri

Parodia hegeri, commonly known as the Heger's torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often spiny and covered in white, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance and unique morphology, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
