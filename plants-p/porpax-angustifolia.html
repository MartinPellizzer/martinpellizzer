Porpax angustifolia

Porpax angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf porpax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Porpax angustifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to dry, arid conditions.
