Potamogeton × roemeri

Potamogeton × roemeri, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant that thrives in shallow freshwater environments.

It is a natural hybrid between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with some floating at the surface. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Potamogeton × roemeri plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
