Pandorea jasminoides

Pandorea jasminoides, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a climbing shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble those of jasmine, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Pandorea jasminoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
