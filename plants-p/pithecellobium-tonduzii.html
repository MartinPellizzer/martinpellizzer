Pithecellobium tonduzii

Pithecellobium tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii pita, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often used in local traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
