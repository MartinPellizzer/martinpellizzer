Pistacia lentiscus f. chia

Pistacia lentiscus f. chia, commonly known as mastic tree or mastic shrub, is a subspecies of the mastic tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

The subspecies is notable for its production of mastic gum, a resin used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
