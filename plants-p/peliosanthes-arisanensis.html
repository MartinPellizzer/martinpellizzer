Peliosanthes arisanensis

Peliosanthes arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a rare and critically endangered plant species native to Japan.

It is a member of the Taxaceae family and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and coniferous-like appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers and red berries, which are important for its ecological role. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is highly threatened in the wild.
