Piper goeldii

Piper goeldii, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or yellow pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The small, yellowish flowers are clustered in spikes, and the fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns red when mature.
