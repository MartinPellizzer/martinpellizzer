Poa rossbergiana

Poa rossbergiana, commonly known as Rossberg's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa rossbergiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
