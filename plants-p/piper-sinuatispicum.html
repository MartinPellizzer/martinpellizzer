Piper sinuatispicum

Piper sinuatispicum, commonly known as the wavy-spurred pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves with wavy margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by peppery berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal uses.
