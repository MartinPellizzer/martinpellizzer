Parasenecio chenopodiifolius

Parasenecio chenopodiifolius, commonly known as the desert cudweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous structure adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant features linear, succulent leaves that reduce water loss, and its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in composite heads. It often grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid ecosystems.
