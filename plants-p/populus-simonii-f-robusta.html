Populus simonii f. robusta

Populus simonii f. robusta, commonly known as the robust poplar, is a variant of the Siberian poplar.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and grayish bark. This species is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China and Kazakhstan. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest-steppe and steppe ecosystems.

The plant is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
