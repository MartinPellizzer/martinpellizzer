Plantago montana subsp. carpatica

Plantago montana subsp.

carpatica, commonly known as Carpathian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
