Persea fastigiata var. bilocularis

Persea fastigiata var. bilocularis, commonly known as the Bilocular Guanabana, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits with a distinctive two-chambered structure, which is reflected in its scientific name. Its botanical features include glossy, dark green foliage and a preference for warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit, which has culinary and medicinal uses.
