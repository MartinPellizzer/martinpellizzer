Phyllorkis roxburghii

Phyllorkis roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright orange, round fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
