Proteocarpus

Proteocarpus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its deciduous, compound leaves that turn silver in color during the dry season. The flowers are typically small and clustered, producing pods that contain edible seeds. Proteocarpus species are often used in traditional medicine and for their ornamental value.

They play an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
