Pternopetalum vulgare var. strigosum

Pternopetalum vulgare var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy pternopetalum, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
