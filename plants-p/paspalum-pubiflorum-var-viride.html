Paspalum pubiflorum var. viride

Paspalum pubiflorum var. viride, commonly known as green paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it suitable for erosion control and as a ground cover in warm climates.
