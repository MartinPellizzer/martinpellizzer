Polygala dimorphophylla

Polygala dimorphophylla, commonly known as the two-leafed milkwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. It is known for its unique floral structure, which includes two distinct types of flowers on the same inflorescence.

Polygala dimorphophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
