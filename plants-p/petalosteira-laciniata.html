Petalosteira laciniata

Petalosteira laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like fronds that give it a delicate and intricate appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Petalosteira laciniata make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
