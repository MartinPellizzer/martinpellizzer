Pithecellobium stipulare

Pithecellobium stipulare, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small stipules, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color.

The fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
