Piper cuspidibracteatum

Piper cuspidibracteatum, commonly known as the pointed bract pepper, is a tropical vine native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and the fruit is a small, three-lobed berry.

This species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
