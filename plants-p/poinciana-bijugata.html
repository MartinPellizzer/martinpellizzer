Poinciana bijugata

Poinciana bijugata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange bipinnate leaves that resemble a flame. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, winged fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is well-suited for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
