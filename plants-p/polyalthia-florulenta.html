Polyalthia florulenta

Polyalthia florulenta, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Polyalthia florulenta make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
