Piper daedalum

Piper daedalum, commonly known as the ghost pepper or Bhut Jolokia, is a flowering plant native to the Eastern Himalayas.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender fruits that mature to a bright red color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is notable for containing one of the hottest capsaicinoids, making it highly valued in spicy cuisine and traditional medicine.
