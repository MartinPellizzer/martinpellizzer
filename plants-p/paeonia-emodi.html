Paeonia emodi

Paeonia emodi, commonly known as the Himalayan peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom. The plant features rhizomatous roots and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

Paeonia emodi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
