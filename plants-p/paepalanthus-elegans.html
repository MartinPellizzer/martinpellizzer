Paepalanthus elegans

Paepalanthus elegans, commonly known as the elegant puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of sword-shaped, glaucous-blue leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces showy, red and yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
