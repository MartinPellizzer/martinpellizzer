Penstemon laetus var. laetus

Penstemon laetus var. laetus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
