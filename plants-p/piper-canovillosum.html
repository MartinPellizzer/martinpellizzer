Piper canovillosum

Piper canovillosum, commonly known as the Canovillo's pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries.

The berries are typically harvested when ripe for their peppery flavor and are used in traditional culinary and medicinal applications.
