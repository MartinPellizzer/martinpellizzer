Pelargonium odoratum

Pelargonium odoratum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a fragrant scent when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
