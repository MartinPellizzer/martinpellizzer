Phlox griseola

Phlox griseola, commonly known as gray phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its erect stem and glandular foliage.
