Pelargonium amatymbicum

Pelargonium amatymbicum, commonly known as the Madagascar geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. It produces small, red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a garden plant.
