Phleum cochinchinense

Phleum cochinchinense, commonly known as Vietnamese oat, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with parallel veins and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a ground cover.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread in suitable environments.
