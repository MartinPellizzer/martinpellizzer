Paepalanthus phaeocephalus var. foliosus

Paepalanthus phaeocephalus var. foliosus, commonly known as the dark-headed grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically with pale yellow to brownish hues.

It is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
