Poa glauca f. arenaria

Poa glauca f. arenaria, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage. The plant forms dense tufts or cushions, adapting to harsh, cold environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to whitish flowers.

This variety is particularly suited to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy pioneer species in disturbed or barren landscapes.
