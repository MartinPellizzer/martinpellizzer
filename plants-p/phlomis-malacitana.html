Phlomis malacitana

Phlomis malacitana, commonly known as the Malakite Phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically pale purple to blue, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late summer.

Phlomis malacitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
