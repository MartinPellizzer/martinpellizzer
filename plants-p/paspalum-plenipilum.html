Paspalum plenipilum

Paspalum plenipilum, commonly known as the broadleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum plenipilum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
