Pleioblastus variegatus f. glaber

Pleioblastus variegatus f. glaber, commonly known as the smooth bamboo, is a variant of the variegated bamboo species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that lack the typical hairy texture found in many other bamboo species. This plant typically grows in dense clumps, forming a compact and upright structure. Its foliage is often a mix of green and white or yellow hues, creating an attractive visual contrast.

It is well-suited for ornamental use in gardens and is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
