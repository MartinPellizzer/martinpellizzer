Pelargonium corymbosum

Pelargonium corymbosum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and containers.

Pelargonium corymbosum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
