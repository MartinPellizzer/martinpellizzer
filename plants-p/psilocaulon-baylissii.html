Psilocaulon baylissii

Psilocaulon baylissii, commonly known as the Bayliss's ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that appear to shimmer in the light, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked due to their muted coloration. Its growth is slow and requires specific microclimatic conditions to thrive.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is of great interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
