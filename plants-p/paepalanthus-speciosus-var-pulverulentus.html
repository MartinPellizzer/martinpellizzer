Paepalanthus speciosus var. pulverulentus

Paepalanthus speciosus var. pulverulentus, commonly known as the powdered pincushion cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny, globular stems that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The varietal name "pulverulentus" refers to its powdery, dusty appearance, which is a distinguishing feature of this plant.
