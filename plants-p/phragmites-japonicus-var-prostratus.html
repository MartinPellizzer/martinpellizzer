Phragmites japonicus var. prostratus

Phragmites japonicus var. prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate Japanese reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits a prostrate growth habit.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous structure, which allows it to spread along the ground and form dense mats. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces tall, flowering stems in the summer.

This variant is notable for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
