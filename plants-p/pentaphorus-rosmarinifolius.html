Pentaphorus rosmarinifolius

Pentaphorus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as the rosemary shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant is evergreen, with a compact, bushy growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often used in Mediterranean-style landscapes.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.
