Pappophorum

Pappophorum, commonly known as the cockspur grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its spike-like inflorescence and small, wind-dispersed seeds called pappi, which aid in its propagation. The plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Pappophorum species are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.

Their botanical features make them an important component of desert and grassland ecosystems.
