Plexipus

Plexipus, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features a distinctive basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a cup-like inflorescence, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
