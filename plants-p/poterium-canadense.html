Poterium canadense

Poterium canadense, commonly known as Canada germander, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Canada germander is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
