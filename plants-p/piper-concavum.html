Piper concavum

Piper concavum, commonly known as the warty betel vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other members like the betel nut plant. The plant is characterized by its dark green, warty leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces berries that are typically harvested for their aromatic properties.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal uses, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
