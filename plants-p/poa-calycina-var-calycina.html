Poa calycina var. calycina

Poa calycina var. calycina, commonly known as the New Zealand hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in short, soft hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
