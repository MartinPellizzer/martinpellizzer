Phylica strigosa var. dregei

Phylica strigosa var. dregei, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hard, red berries that are a food source for various birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
