Pedicularis palustris subsp. borealis

Pedicularis palustris subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern marsh gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern regions of North America and Eurasia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wet or boggy habitats, such as marshes and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base.

This subspecies is part of the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
