Philibertia stipitata

Philibertia stipitata, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Philibertia stipitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
