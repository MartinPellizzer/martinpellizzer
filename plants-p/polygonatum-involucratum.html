Polygonatum involucratum

Polygonatum involucratum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

Polygonatum involucratum is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and aesthetic appeal in gardens.
