Protea pinaster

Protea pinaster, commonly known as the candle bush, is a species of shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive, candle-like flower heads. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Protea pinaster is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
