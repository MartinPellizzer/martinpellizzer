Potentilla semiternata

Potentilla semiternata, commonly known as the three-toothed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often lobed or toothed.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
