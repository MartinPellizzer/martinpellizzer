Paronychia rosetta

Paronychia rosetta, commonly known as the rock rose or rosette plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, dense rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its root system is adapted to shallow soil, enabling it to survive in rocky or well-drained environments.

Paronychia rosetta is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique form and resilience.
