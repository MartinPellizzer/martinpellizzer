Plantago leiopetala

Plantago leiopetala, commonly known as the smoothleaf plantain, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
