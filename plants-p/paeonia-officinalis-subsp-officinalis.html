Paeonia officinalis subsp. officinalis

Paeonia officinalis subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as the tree peony, is a perennial shrub native to Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and has a deep root system. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The flowers come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, white, and yellow.
