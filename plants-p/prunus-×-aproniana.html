Prunus × aproniana

Prunus × aproniana, commonly known as the apricot-almond hybrid, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that exhibits characteristics of both apricot (Prunus armeniaca) and almond (Prunus dulcis).

It typically features smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, fuzzy fruits resembling apricots. Prunus × aproniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.

However, it is important to note that the fruits are generally not as flavorful or nutritious as those of its parent species.
