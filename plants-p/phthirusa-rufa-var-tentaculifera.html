Phthirusa rufa var. tentaculifera

Phthirusa rufa var. tentaculifera, commonly known as the red-veined tentacle fern, is a rare and striking fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds that bear numerous small, tufted spore-bearing structures called sporangia. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with elongated, often curling fronds that give it a whimsical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
