Potentilla hirta f. grossidentata

Potentilla hirta f. grossidentata, commonly known as the rough-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often used in alpine gardens or as a ground cover in harsh environments.
