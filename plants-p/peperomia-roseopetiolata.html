Peperomia roseopetiolata

Peperomia roseopetiolata, commonly known as the pink-veined peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive pinkish or reddish veining, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which are not particularly showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia roseopetiolata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
