Phaseolus longepedunculatus

Phaseolus longepedunculatus, commonly known as the long pedunculate bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing several seeds. Native to the Americas, it is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is grown in various regions for both culinary and horticultural purposes.
