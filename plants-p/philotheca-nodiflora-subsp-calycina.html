Philotheca nodiflora subsp. calycina

Philotheca nodiflora subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain germander, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features small, opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers arranged in dense inflorescences. These flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.

It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
