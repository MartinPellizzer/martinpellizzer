Pelargonium aciculatum

Pelargonium aciculatum, commonly known as the needle-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

Pelargonium aciculatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
