Paspalum debile

Paspalum debile, commonly known as slender grass or slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

Paspalum debile is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both wet and dry conditions.
