Potentilla sterilis crassa

Potentilla sterilis crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in corymbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
