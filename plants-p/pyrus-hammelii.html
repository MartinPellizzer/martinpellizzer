Pyrus hammelii

Pyrus hammelii, commonly known as the Hamel's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are white and clustered, typically appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is a small, yellowish pear that is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
