Piper purusanum var. purusanum

Piper purusanum var. purusanum, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
