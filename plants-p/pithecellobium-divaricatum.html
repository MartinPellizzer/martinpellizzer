Pithecellobium divaricatum

Pithecellobium divaricatum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading canopy and clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and the bark is smooth with a light greenish hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
