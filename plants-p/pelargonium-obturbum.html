Pelargonium obturbum

Pelargonium obturbum, commonly known as the African ivy or the forest geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Pelargonium obturbum is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets and green walls.
