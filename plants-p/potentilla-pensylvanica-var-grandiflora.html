Potentilla pensylvanica var. grandiflora

Potentilla pensylvanica var. grandiflora, commonly known as Pennsylvania rose or the sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
