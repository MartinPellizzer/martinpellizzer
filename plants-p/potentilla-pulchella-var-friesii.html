Potentilla pulchella var. friesii

Potentilla pulchella var. friesii, commonly known as the Fries' cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
