Piper hydrolapathum

Piper hydrolapathum, commonly known as the water pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's name is derived from its tendency to grow in wet areas, reflecting its hydrophilic nature.
