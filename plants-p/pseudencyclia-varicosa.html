Pseudencyclia varicosa

Pseudencyclia varicosa, commonly known as the variegated cycad, is a unique plant species native to southern China.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The leaves are typically green with yellow or white variegation, giving it a striking appearance. This plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and prefers a warm, dry climate.

It is also notable for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
