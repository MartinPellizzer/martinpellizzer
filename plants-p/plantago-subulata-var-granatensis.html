Plantago subulata var. granatensis

Plantago subulata var. granatensis, commonly known as the granate plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
