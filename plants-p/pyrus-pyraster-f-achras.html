Pyrus pyraster f. achras

Pyrus pyraster f. achras, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, followed by edible, subglobose berries.

The species is characterized by its rugged, often twisted growth habit and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
