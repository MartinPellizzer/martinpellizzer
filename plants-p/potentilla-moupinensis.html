Potentilla moupinensis

Potentilla moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a finely toothed margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Potentilla moupinensis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, thriving in cool and moist conditions.
