Pilea kakurang

Pilea kakurang, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing, compact plant with heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea kakurang is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
