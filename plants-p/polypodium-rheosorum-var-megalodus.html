Polypodium rheosorum var. megalodus

Polypodium rheosorum var. megalodus, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Polypodium.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found near streams or in wetlands. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a water lily.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels, making it a resilient aquatic plant.
