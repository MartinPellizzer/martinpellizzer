Paepalanthus plumosus

Paepalanthus plumosus, commonly known as the silky broomstick, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, branching stems covered in soft, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
