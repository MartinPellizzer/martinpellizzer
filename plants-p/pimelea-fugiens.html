Pimelea fugiens

Pimelea fugiens, commonly known as the scarlet geebung, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry open forests, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces showy, bright orange to red flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it is well adapted to drought conditions.

Pimelea fugiens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
