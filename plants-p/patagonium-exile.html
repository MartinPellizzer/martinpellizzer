Patagonium exile

Patagonium exile, commonly known as the Patagonian dwarf shrub, is a low-growing plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its succulent stems and spiny leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the brief rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in the rocky and sandy soils of its habitat.

Patagonium exile plays a vital role in stabilizing the soil and providing limited forage for local wildlife.
