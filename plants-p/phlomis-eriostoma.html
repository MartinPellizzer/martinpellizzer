Phlomis eriostoma

Phlomis eriostoma, commonly known as the woolly mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grayish-green, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Phlomis eriostoma is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and blooms.
