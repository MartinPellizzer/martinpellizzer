Prunus serrulata f. taihaku

Prunus serrulata f. taihaku, commonly known as the Taihaku cherry, is a compact, dwarf form of the Japanese cherry.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and profuse blooms of pale pink to white flowers in early spring. The foliage is dark green, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blossoms. This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and parks.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for smaller spaces.
