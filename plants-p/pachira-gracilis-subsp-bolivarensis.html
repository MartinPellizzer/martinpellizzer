Pachira gracilis subsp. bolivarensis

Pachira gracilis subsp.

bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian pachira, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin. It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
