Potentilla biflora var. biflora

Potentilla biflora var. biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
