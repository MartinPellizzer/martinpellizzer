Potamopitys californica

Potamopitys californica, commonly known as the California redwood, is a species of conifer native to the coastal regions of Northern California and Southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and distinctive reddish-bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of coniferous trees. Its foliage consists of short, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems.

This species is known for its longevity and plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
