Palala ardisiifolia

Palala ardisiifolia, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling flames. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Palala ardisiifolia is also known for its fragrant flowers, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies.
