Piperi schizonephum

Piperi schizonephum, commonly known as the Himalayan monkshood, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are typically smooth and green. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
