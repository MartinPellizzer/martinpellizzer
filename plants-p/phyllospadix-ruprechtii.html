Phyllospadix ruprechtii

Phyllospadix ruprechtii, commonly known as the coastal sea oat, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms low, mat-like growths along sandy or rocky shores. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, which are followed by cylindrical, spiny fruits. It thrives in saline and coastal environments, demonstrating a high tolerance for wind and salt spray.

Phyllospadix ruprechtii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
