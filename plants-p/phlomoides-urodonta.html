Phlomoides urodonta

Phlomoides urodonta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woods.

Phlomoides urodonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
