Pecteilis chlorosepala

Pecteilis chlorosepala, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
