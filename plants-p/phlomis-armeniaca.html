Phlomis armeniaca

Phlomis armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in late summer, arranged in spikes at the top of the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green, and covered with a thick layer of fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.
