Piper dilatatum f. taboganum

Piper dilatatum f. taboganum, commonly known as the Taboganum pepper plant, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Piper dilatatum species.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into round, peppercorn-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and potential medicinal properties.
