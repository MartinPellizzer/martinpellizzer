Persicaria satsumensis

Persicaria satsumensis, commonly known as the Satsuma smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, appearing in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Persicaria satsumensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
