Pauletia cheilantha

Pauletia cheilantha, commonly known as the red-flowered pauletia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool and moist conditions. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pauletia cheilantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
