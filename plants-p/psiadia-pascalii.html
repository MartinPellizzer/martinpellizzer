Psiadia pascalii

Psiadia pascalii, commonly known as the Pascal's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Psiadia pascalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
