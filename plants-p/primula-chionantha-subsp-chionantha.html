Primula chionantha subsp. chionantha

Primula chionantha subsp.

chionantha, commonly known as the snow-flower primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features a basal rosette of velvety, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
