Pollia glaucescens

Pollia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue flowers, which are actually a combination of white and blue pigments that create an iridescent effect. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Pollia glaucescens is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
