Prunus hierochuntica

Prunus hierochuntica, commonly known as the Crimean cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are less commonly cultivated. Its leaves are oval and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Prunus hierochuntica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in the region.
