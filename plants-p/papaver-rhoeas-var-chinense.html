Papaver rhoeas var. chinense

Papaver rhoeas var. chinense, commonly known as Chinese poppy, is a variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and a distinctive, rounded seed pod. This plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, typically blooming in the summer months.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
