Potamogeton semicoloratus

Potamogeton semicoloratus, commonly known as the purple spikegrass, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are long and narrow, typically with a slight keel. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
