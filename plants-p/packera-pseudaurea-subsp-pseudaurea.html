Packera pseudaurea subsp. pseudaurea

Packera pseudaurea subsp.

pseudaurea, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
