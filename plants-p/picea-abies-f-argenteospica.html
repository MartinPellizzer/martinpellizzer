Picea abies f. argenteospica

Picea abies f. argenteospica, commonly known as the silver-spined Norway spruce, is a cultivar of the Norway spruce characterized by its distinctive silvery-white bark and foliage.

It is a slow-growing evergreen conifer that typically reaches a height of 10 to 15 meters. The leaves are needle-like, with a bluish-green hue and a white or silver-colored underside, giving it a striking appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This ornamental variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
