Phaseolus cornutus

Phaseolus cornutus, commonly known as the hairy bean or horned bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, green pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various regions for both garden use and traditional purposes.
