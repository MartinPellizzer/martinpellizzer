Pittosporum parvifolium

Pittosporum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Pittosporum parvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
