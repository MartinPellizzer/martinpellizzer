Paepalanthus bifidus f. frustus

Paepalanthus bifidus f. frustus, commonly known as the frayed puya, is a unique member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family, native to the arid regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that resemble spikes or brushes, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
