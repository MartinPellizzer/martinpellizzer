Poa elliottii

Poa elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with silver. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
