Pimelea latifolia var. parvifolia

Pimelea latifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf waratah, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, red or orange-red flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
