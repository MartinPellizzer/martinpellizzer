Plantago barbata var. monanthos

Plantago barbata var. monanthos, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
