Ptilotus latifolius

Ptilotus latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and vibrant, showy flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or saline soils, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its unique appearance and ecological resilience make it a notable species in coastal and inland regions of Australia.

The plant also plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
