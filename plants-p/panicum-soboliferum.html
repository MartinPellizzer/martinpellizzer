Panicum soboliferum

Panicum soboliferum, commonly known as the silver panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, branching stalks that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Panicum soboliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
