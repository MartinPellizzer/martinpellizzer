Puccinellia distans var. fallax

Puccinellia distans var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax variety of the distans grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to thrive in saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in coastal and desert environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
