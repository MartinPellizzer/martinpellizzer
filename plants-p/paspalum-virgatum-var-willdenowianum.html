Paspalum virgatum var. willdenowianum

Paspalum virgatum var. willdenowianum, commonly known as willdenow's paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often produce panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly folded, with a blue-green hue that becomes more pronounced during the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
