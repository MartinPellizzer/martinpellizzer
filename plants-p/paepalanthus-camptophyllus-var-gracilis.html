Paepalanthus camptophyllus var. gracilis

Paepalanthus camptophyllus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender cholla, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spine-tipped stems that are often branched and covered in small, white tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, typical of many cacti.

It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.
