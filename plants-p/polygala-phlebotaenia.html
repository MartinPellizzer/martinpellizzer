Polygala phlebotaenia

Polygala phlebotaenia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are usually pale pink or white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
