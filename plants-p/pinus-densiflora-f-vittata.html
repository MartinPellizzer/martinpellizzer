Pinus densiflora f. vittata

Pinus densiflora f. vittata, commonly known as the variegated Japanese black pine, is a unique variant of the Japanese black pine characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

This form exhibits leaves that are a mix of dark green and yellow or white, creating a visually appealing contrast. It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.

Its growth habit is typically slow, making it a long-lived and resilient evergreen tree.
