Pyrus torminalis f. mollis

Pyrus torminalis f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved pear, is a cultivar of the European pear.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, with a rounded crown and a slow growth rate. Its flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in early spring before the leaves fully emerge.

The fruit is usually small, sweet, and suitable for eating fresh or cooking.
