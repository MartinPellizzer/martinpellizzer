Phyllanthus oxycoccifolius

Phyllanthus oxycoccifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and dark green coloration. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are often red or yellow when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
