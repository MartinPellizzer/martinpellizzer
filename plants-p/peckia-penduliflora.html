Peckia penduliflora

Peckia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower peckia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Peckia penduliflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
