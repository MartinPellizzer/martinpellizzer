Pisum jomardii

Pisum jomardii, commonly known as the Jomard's pea, is a species of flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seeds are encased in pods and are typically used for ornamental purposes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and lesser-known member of the pea family.
