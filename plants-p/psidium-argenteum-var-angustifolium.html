Psidium argenteum var. angustifolium

Psidium argenteum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed guava, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its ornate foliage and fruit.
