Pleurothallis martinezii

Pleurothallis martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

This small epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees and rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers with intricate patterns. The plant is noted for its compact size and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
