Phoradendron treleasei

Phoradendron treleasei, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Ficus*.

The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. Phoradendron treleasei plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host tree growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
