Piptadenia winkleri

Piptadenia winkleri, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, large, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is well adapted to disturbed soils.
