Paphiopedilum tigrinum var. smaragdinum

Paphiopedilum tigrinum var. smaragdinum, commonly known as the Emerald Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant green and yellow coloration, with intricate patterns resembling a tiger's stripes on its petals. The plant produces large, showy flowers that bloom once a year, typically during the summer months. It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
