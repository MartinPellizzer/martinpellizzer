Paepalanthus conduplicatus var. pubescens

Paepalanthus conduplicatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, spherical shape, resembling a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to tubercles, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its "hairy" appearance.

This species is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
