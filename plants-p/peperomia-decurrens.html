Peperomia decurrens

Peperomia decurrens, commonly known as the spreading peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in glossy, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a reddish or purplish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale green, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia decurrens is known for its ease of care and ability to adapt well to various indoor environments.
