Pouteria crebrifolia

Pouteria crebrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces woody fruits that contain the highly valued Brazil nuts. Its botanical features include a straight trunk, smooth bark, and a long lifespan, often exceeding 100 years.

Pouteria crebrifolia plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
