Pyrus fragrans

Pyrus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet cherry or European cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, red to dark purple cherries.

Pyrus fragrans is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.
