Primula darialica

Primula darialica, commonly known as the Dariyalica primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula darialica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
