Peperomia venezueliana

Peperomia venezueliana, commonly known as the Venezuelan peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its minimal water requirements.

Peperomia venezueliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care, making it a popular choice for plant enthusiasts.
