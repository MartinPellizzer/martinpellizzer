Poa serotina var. fertilis

Poa serotina var. fertilis, commonly known as the fertilized meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, usually blue-green blades. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that mature in late summer, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil in grassland habitats. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of diverse grassland ecosystems.
