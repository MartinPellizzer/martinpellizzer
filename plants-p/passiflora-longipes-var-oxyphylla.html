Passiflora longipes var. oxyphylla

Passiflora longipes var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the long-stalked passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by local communities. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This variant is noted for its distinct floral morphology and ecological significance in its native habitats.
