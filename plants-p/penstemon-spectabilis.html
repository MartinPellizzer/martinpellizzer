Penstemon spectabilis

Penstemon spectabilis, commonly known as the showy penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.
