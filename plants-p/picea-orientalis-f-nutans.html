Picea orientalis f. nutans

Picea orientalis f. nutans, commonly known as the drooping Siberian pine, is a unique variant of the Siberian pine tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in cold, harsh climates. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically a deep green color.

This ornamental tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its visual interest.
