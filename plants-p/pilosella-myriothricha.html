Pilosella myriothricha

Pilosella myriothricha, commonly known as the many-bristled goatweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

The plant's hairy stems and leaves help reduce water loss, making it well adapted to arid conditions.
