Picraena antillana

Picraena antillana, commonly known as the West Indian firebush, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in tropical regions.
