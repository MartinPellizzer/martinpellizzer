Passiflora mascarensis

Passiflora mascarensis, commonly known as the Mascarene passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It produces striking, colorful flowers with a unique structure featuring five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional medicinal uses in the region.
