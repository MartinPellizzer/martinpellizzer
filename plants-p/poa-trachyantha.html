Poa trachyantha

Poa trachyantha, commonly known as the rough bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and typically have a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, dunes, and along coastal areas.
