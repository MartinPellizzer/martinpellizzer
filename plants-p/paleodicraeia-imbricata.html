Paleodicraeia imbricata

Paleodicraeia imbricata, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Paleodicraeia imbricata is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.
