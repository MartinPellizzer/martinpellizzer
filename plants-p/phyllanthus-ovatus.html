Phyllanthus ovatus

Phyllanthus ovatus, commonly known as the fatshark plant or sacred lotus, is a tropical shrub native to Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically oval or egg-shaped, with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red or orange fruits containing multiple seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
