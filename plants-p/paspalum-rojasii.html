Paspalum rojasii

Paspalum rojasii, commonly known as the Andean paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
