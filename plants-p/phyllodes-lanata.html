Phyllodes lanata

Phyllodes lanata, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, hair-covered phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Phyllodes lanata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
