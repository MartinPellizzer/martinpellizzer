Pleurothallis myrtillus

Pleurothallis myrtillus, commonly known as the myrtle orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its intricate, often fragrant blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
