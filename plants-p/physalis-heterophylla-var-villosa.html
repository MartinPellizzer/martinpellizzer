Physalis heterophylla var. villosa

Physalis heterophylla var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of orange-yellow berries enclosed in a thick, papery calyx. These berries are edible when ripe and have a tart, sweet flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible gardening.
