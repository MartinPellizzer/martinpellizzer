Pionocarpus madrensis

Pionocarpus madrensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its hard, dense wood. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Pionocarpus madrensis is valued for its durability and is often used in traditional crafts and construction.
