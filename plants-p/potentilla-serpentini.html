Potentilla serpentini

Potentilla serpentini, commonly known as serpent's root, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Potentilla serpentini thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
