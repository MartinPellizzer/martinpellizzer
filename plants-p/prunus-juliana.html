Prunus juliana

Prunus juliana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Prunus juliana is often cultivated for its beauty and is a popular choice in ornamental horticulture.
