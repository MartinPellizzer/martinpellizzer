Polygala riograndensis

Polygala riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
