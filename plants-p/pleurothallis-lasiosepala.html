Pleurothallis lasiosepala

Pleurothallis lasiosepala, commonly known as the velvet lip orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive violet or purple hue. Its sepals and petals are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name.

Pleurothallis lasiosepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
