Plectronia fililoba

Plectronia fililoba, commonly known as the slender filaments fern, is a delicate and elegant fern species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft and feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Plectronia fililoba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
