Passiflora riparia

Passiflora riparia, commonly known as the river passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or black berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical or subtropical environments, often near rivers or streams.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
