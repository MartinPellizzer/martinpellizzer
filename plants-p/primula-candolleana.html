Primula candolleana

Primula candolleana, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, adding vibrant color to mountainous landscapes.

Primula candolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
