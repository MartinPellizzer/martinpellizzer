Pandanus sinuosus

Pandanus sinuosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes at the top of the plant. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

The species is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a common sight in tropical regions.
