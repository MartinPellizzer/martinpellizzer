Passiflora subulata

Passiflora subulata, commonly known as the needleleaf passionflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and delicate, showy flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
