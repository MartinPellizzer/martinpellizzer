Psidium chiapasense

Psidium chiapasense, commonly known as the Chiapas guava, is a tropical fruit tree native to Central America.

It produces small, round fruits with a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content.
