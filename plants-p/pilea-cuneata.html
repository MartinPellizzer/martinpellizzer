Pilea cuneata

Pilea cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Pilea cuneata is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
