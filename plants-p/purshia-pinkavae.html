Purshia pinkavae

Purshia pinkavae, commonly known as the pinkavae buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible for some wildlife species. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Botanically, it is part of the Rutaceae family, which includes other plants like citrus and buckthorn.
