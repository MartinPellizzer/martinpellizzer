Plinia ambivalens

Plinia ambivalens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia ambivalens*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, edible fruits resembling cherries. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a distinct aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
