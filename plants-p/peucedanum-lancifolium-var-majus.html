Peucedanum lancifolium var. majus

Peucedanum lancifolium var. majus, commonly known as the giant cow parsley, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, umbrella-like compound umbels of white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are deeply lobed, with a distinctive fern-like appearance, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
