Pavonia typhalaea

Pavonia typhalaea, commonly known as the Mexican flame bush, is a shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Pavonia typhalaea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
