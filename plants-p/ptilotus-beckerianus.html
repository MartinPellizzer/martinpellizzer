Ptilotus beckerianus

Ptilotus beckerianus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fluffy, pinkish-white bracts that resemble clouds. The plant typically grows in sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the showy bracts.

Ptilotus beckerianus is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
