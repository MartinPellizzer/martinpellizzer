Plinia dussii

Plinia dussii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria dussii*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers are white or pale pink, with a strong aromatic scent, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
