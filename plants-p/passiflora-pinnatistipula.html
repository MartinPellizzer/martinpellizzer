Passiflora pinnatistipula

Passiflora pinnatistipula, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and intricate structures. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers with a central crown of filaments, often in shades of purple and white. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
