Pyrus apiosa

Pyrus apiosa, commonly known as the Florida crabapple, is a small deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish-green fruits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental horticulture.
