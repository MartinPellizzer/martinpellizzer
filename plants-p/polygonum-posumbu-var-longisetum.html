Polygonum posumbu var. longisetum

Polygonum posumbu var. longisetum, commonly known as the long-hair knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and long, spreading inflorescences that produce small, clustered flowers. Its stems are often reddish in color and covered with fine, bristly hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
