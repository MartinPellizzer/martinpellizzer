Phyllostachys bambusoides subvar. kashirodake

Phyllostachys bambusoides subvar. kashirodake, commonly known as the Kashiro-dake bamboo, is a cold-hardy grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 4 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color and a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to form dense clumps over time.
