Pandanus dicheres

Pandanus dicheres, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands, including Hawaii, Tahiti, and New Caledonia. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
