Piper pseudamboinense

Piper pseudamboinense, commonly known as the Bornean pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature, containing several seeds.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their aromatic properties.
