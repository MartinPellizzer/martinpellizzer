Prasophyllum obovatum

Prasophyllum obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved leek orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, leek-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cooler climates and is often found in regions of southern Australia.

Prasophyllum obovatum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
