Physoleucas arabica

Physoleucas arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small evergreen shrub with dark green, leathery leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate with ample sunlight.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
