Phyllanthus koumacensis

Phyllanthus koumacensis, commonly known as the Madagascar gooseberry, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
