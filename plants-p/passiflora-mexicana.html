Passiflora mexicana

Passiflora mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central America and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a blend of pink, purple, and white petals, along with a unique crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth habit makes it an effective climbing plant, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Passiflora mexicana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators like bees and butterflies.
