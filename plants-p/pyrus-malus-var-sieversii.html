Pyrus malus var. sieversii

Pyrus malus var. sieversii, commonly known as the Sievers' apple, is a wild apple species native to the Central Asian region.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits and is considered a progenitor of many domesticated apple varieties. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas and has a compact, shrub-like form. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for its genetic diversity and its role in apple domestication studies.
