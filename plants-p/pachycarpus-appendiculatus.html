Pachycarpus appendiculatus

Pachycarpus appendiculatus, commonly known as the sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or rocky soils.

Pachycarpus appendiculatus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
