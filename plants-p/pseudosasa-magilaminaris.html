Pseudosasa magilaminaris

Pseudosasa magilaminaris, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The leaves are narrow, elongated, and have a silvery-white underside, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
