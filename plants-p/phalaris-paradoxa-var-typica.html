Phalaris paradoxa var. typica

Phalaris paradoxa var. typica, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
