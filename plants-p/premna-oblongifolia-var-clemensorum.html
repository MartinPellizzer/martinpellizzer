Premna oblongifolia var. clemensorum

Premna oblongifolia var. clemensorum, commonly known as the Florida premna, is a woody shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
