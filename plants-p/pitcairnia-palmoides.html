Pitcairnia palmoides

Pitcairnia palmoides, commonly known as the giant pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a water-holding structure at the center. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in the rich, moist soil of its natural habitat.

Pitcairnia palmoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native environment.
