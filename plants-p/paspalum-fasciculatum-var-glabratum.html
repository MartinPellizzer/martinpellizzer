Paspalum fasciculatum var. glabratum

Paspalum fasciculatum var. glabratum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
