Polycodium melanocarpum var. candicans

Polycodium melanocarpum var. candicans, commonly known as the golden polycodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its most distinctive characteristic is the production of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
