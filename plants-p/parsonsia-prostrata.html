Parsonsia prostrata

Parsonsia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bright orange-red tubular flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Parsonsia prostrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
