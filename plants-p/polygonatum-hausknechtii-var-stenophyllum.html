Polygonatum hausknechtii var. stenophyllum

Polygonatum hausknechtii var. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
