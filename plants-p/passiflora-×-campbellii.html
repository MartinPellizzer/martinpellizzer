Passiflora × campbellii

Passiflora × campbellii, commonly known as the Campbell's passionflower, is a hybrid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature intricate patterns of red, yellow, and blue. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
