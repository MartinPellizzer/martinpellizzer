Pleurothallis cooperi

Pleurothallis cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a variety of colors, and it produces numerous tiny, greenish-yellow pseudobulbs.

Pleurothallis cooperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
