Poa decipiens

Poa decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to produce both fertile and sterile shoots, which can lead to confusion in identification. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a distinctive sheath that is often tinged with silver.

Poa decipiens is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
