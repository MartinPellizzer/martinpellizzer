Pedicellarum paiei

Pedicellarum paiei, commonly known as the black orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its flowers are small but highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, Pedicellarum paiei is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
