Peperomia pruinosa

Peperomia pruinosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, and slightly hairy leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both homes and offices.
