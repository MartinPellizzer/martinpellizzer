Pyrus coronaria var. sempervirens

Pyrus coronaria var. sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn that retains its leaves year-round.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that remain on the plant throughout the winter. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
