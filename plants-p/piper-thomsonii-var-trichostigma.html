Piper thomsonii var. trichostigma

Piper thomsonii var. trichostigma, commonly known as the Thomson's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests, where it contributes to the understory vegetation.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
