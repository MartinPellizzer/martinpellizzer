Piper schiedeanum

Piper schiedeanum, commonly known as the kava plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small green flowers. The plant is cultivated for its root, which contains compounds used to make traditional beverages and medicinal preparations. Its growth habit includes a woody stem with aerial roots that help in climbing.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
