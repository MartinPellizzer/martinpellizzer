Pleioblastus muroianus

Pleioblastus muroianus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fine, light green foliage that creates a delicate, grass-like appearance. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its rhizomes form dense clumps, allowing it to spread slowly and establish a lush ground cover.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
