Pollia verticillata

Pollia verticillata, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Pollia verticillata is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and has been used in traditional crafts and medicine in some regions.
