Pseudolaelia vellozicola

Pseudolaelia vellozicola, commonly known as the Vellozicola orchid, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, brightly colored blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Pseudolaelia vellozicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
