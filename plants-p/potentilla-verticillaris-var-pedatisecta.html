Potentilla verticillaris var. pedatisecta

Potentilla verticillaris var. pedatisecta, commonly known as the cut-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, almost fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
