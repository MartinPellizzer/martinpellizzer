Philodendron verrucosum

Philodendron verrucosum, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight sheen. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
