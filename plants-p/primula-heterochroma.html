Primula heterochroma

Primula heterochroma, commonly known as the heterochromatic primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a striking color variation between the upper and lower surfaces of the leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ornamental species in horticulture.
