Paphiopedilum mohrianum

Paphiopedilum mohrianum, commonly known as the Mohrian's lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and broad, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and collections.
