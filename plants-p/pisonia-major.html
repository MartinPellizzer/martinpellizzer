Pisonia major

Pisonia major, commonly known as the beach hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its woody stem and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, hairy seeds.

Pisonia major thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salt spray.
