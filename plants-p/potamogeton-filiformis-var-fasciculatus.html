Potamogeton filiformis var. fasciculatus

Potamogeton filiformis var. fasciculatus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in dense, fasciculate (bundled) clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in the water, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with muddy or sandy bottoms.

This variety is important for providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
