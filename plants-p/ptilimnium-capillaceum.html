Ptilimnium capillaceum

Ptilimnium capillaceum, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous species characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Native to wet or moist habitats, it is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has specialized structures called bladder cells, which are used to trap small aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
