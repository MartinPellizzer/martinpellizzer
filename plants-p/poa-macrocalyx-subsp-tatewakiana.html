Poa macrocalyx subsp. tatewakiana

Poa macrocalyx subsp.

tatewakiana, commonly known as the Tatewakian meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

The flowers are wind-pollinated, and the species is noted for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
