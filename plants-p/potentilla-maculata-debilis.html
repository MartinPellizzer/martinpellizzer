Potentilla maculata debilis

Potentilla maculata debilis, commonly known as the spotted cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in native plant gardens.
