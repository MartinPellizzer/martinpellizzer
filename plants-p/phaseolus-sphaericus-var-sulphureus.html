Phaseolus sphaericus var. sulphureus

Phaseolus sphaericus var. sulphureus, commonly known as the golden globe bean, is a unique variety of bean characterized by its spherical, yellowish pods.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to Central and South America. The plant produces small, edible seeds enclosed within its distinctive round pods, which are often harvested when mature. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its culinary use in traditional dishes.

Its growth habit is bushy, with upright stems and trifoliate leaves typical of the Phaseolus genus.
