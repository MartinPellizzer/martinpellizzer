Piper rufibracteum

Piper rufibracteum, commonly known as the red-bracted pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from green to red. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple, containing a spicy, peppery flavor.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its potential medicinal properties.
