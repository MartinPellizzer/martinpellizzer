Primulina baishouensis

Primulina baishouensis, commonly known as the Baishouensis Primula, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
