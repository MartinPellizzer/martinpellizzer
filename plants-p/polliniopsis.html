Polliniopsis

Polliniopsis, commonly known as the "flowering grass" or "false grass," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that often lack showy petals. Polliniopsis species are typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plants are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Their reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, which is a common trait among grasses.
