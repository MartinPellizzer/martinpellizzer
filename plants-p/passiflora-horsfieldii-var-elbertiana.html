Passiflora horsfieldii var. elbertiana

Passiflora horsfieldii var. elbertiana, commonly known as the Elbert's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of red, blue, and white petals, along with prominent filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates.
