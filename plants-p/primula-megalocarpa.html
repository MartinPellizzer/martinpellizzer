Primula megalocarpa

Primula megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant primrose, is a striking plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to mountainous landscapes.
