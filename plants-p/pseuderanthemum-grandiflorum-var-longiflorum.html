Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. longiflorum

Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum var. longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant in the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom during the rainy season. This plant thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
