Physostegia virginiana var. formosior

Physostegia virginiana var. formosior, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that resemble a lady's slipper. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
