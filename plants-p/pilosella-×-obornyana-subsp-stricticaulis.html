Pilosella × obornyana subsp. stricticaulis

Pilosella × obornyana subsp.

stricticaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed goatsbeard, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and basal rosettes of lanceolate, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important member of the Asteraceae family.
