Phyllanthus obovatus

Phyllanthus obovatus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical to obovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Phyllanthus obovatus is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
