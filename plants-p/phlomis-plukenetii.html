Phlomis plukenetii

Phlomis plukenetii, commonly known as the Armenian scabious, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, serrated edge. The plant produces showy clusters of bright blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Phlomis plukenetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
