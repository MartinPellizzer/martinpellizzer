Picea nigra var. nana

Picea nigra var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf black spruce, is a compact evergreen shrub that thrives in cold, boreal climates.

It is characterized by its short, dense branches and small, dark green needles that are typically less than an inch in length. The plant produces cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of coniferous species. It is well adapted to acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wetland or bog environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense, wind-resistant thickets in its native habitat.
