Palissya castaneifolia

Palissya castaneifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hickory, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a resinous scent.

Palissya castaneifolia is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
