Paepalanthus elongatus f. graminifolius

Paepalanthus elongatus f. graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaved puya, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and insects. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or arid habitats.

This unique variety exhibits a more elongated and grass-like leaf structure compared to other forms of Paepalanthus.
