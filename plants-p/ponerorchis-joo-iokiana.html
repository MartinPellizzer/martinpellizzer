Ponerorchis joo-iokiana

Ponerorchis joo-iokiana, commonly known as the Iokian orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Taiwan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, compact growth habit and delicate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns, which are adapted for specific pollinators. Its habitat is restricted to high-altitude forests, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a conservation concern.
