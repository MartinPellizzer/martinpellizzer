Plantago durvillei subsp. subnuda

Plantago durvillei subsp.

subnuda, commonly known as the naked plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

This subspecies is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to other members of the species.
