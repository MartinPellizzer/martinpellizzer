Piper insignifolium

Piper insignifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough, slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.
