Persea fastigiata var. pilosa

Persea fastigiata var. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly evergreen.

This plant is a variant of the evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are dark green and leathery, with a rough texture.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits, though the fruit is typically not cultivated for consumption.
