Piper abraense

Piper abraense, commonly known as the Andean pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, dark blue to black berry.

Piper abraense is often found in humid, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
