Paepalanthus duidae var. parvifolius

Paepalanthus duidae var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf puya, is a striking South American plant belonging to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that bears numerous small, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant is native to the Andean regions of Bolivia and Peru, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. It has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense base, supporting the flowering stem.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
