Pulmonaria davurica

Pulmonaria davurica, commonly known as the Siberian lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal rosettes of rounded, velvety leaves that are often speckled with purple or red markings. The plant produces tall, spires of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Pulmonaria davurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
