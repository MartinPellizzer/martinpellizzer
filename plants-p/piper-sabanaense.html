Piper sabanaense

Piper sabanaense, commonly known as the yellow pepper vine, is a tropical plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with humid climates. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to yellow when mature, often used in local cuisines for its mild peppery flavor.
