Polybotrya aureisquama

Polybotrya aureisquama, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in a dense layer of golden-brown spores. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in small, rounded clusters along the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Polybotrya aureisquama is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
