Posoqueria trinitatis

Posoqueria trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidadian grass or the Caribbean grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Posoqueria trinitatis is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize dunes.
