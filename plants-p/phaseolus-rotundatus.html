Phaseolus rotundatus

Phaseolus rotundatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or yellow flowers that give way to long, cylindrical pods containing edible beans. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a well-developed root system.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.
