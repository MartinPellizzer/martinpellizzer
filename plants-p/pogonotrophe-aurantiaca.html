Pogonotrophe aurantiaca

Pogonotrophe aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-tongued orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, orange-colored petals and a distinctive, tongue-like structure at the base of its flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
