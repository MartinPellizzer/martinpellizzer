Pterocarpus tessmannii

Pterocarpus tessmannii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The tree produces hard, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its density and aesthetic qualities. Its fruit is a legume containing seeds enclosed in a fibrous pod.

Pterocarpus tessmannii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
