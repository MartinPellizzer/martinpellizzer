Poa tef

Poa tef, commonly known as false wheat, is a grass species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often referred to as "tef grains.". These grains are highly nutritious and are used as a staple food in Ethiopia.

Poa tef is well adapted to poor soils and can thrive in cool, temperate climates.
