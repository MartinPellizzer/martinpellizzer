Polygonum arachnoideum

Polygonum arachnoideum, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

Polygonum arachnoideum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
