Polygonum forsteri

Polygonum forsteri, commonly known as Forster's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence.

Polygonum forsteri is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its rhizomatous growth habit.
