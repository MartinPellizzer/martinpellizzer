Polygonum phytolaccifolium

Polygonum phytolaccifolium, commonly known as the purpleleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that often display a striking purplish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread readily, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants such as buckwheat and sorrel.
