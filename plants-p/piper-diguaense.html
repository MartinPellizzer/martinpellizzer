Piper diguaense

Piper diguaense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. Its botanical structure supports its role as a climbing plant, with adventitious roots that help it attach to trees and other vegetation.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential medicinal properties.
