Piper tacarcunense

Piper tacarcunense, commonly known as the Yungas pepper, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of northern Bolivia and southern Peru.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and emit a mild peppery scent.

Piper tacarcunense is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
