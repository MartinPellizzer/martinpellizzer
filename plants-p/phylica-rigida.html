Phylica rigida

Phylica rigida, commonly known as the rigid phylica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and rigid, contributing to its common name, and the plant is known for its ornamental value.

Phylica rigida is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
