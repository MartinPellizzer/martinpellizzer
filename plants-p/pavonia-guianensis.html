Pavonia guianensis

Pavonia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan pavonia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Pavonia guianensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
