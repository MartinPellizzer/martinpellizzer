Pleopeltis wrayi

Pleopeltis wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in moisture retention. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
