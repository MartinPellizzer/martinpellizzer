Primula pectinata

Primula pectinata, commonly known as the sticky primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and hairy, adapted to retain moisture in its native climate.

Primula pectinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
