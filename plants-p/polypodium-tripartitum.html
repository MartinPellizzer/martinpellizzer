Polypodium tripartitum

Polypodium tripartitum, commonly known as the three-part huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tripartite fronds that are divided into three segments, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its leaves are usually leathery and dark green, with a rough texture that helps it retain moisture.

Polypodium tripartitum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
