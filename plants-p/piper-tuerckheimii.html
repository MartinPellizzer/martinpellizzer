Piper tuerckheimii

Piper tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly pubescent surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
