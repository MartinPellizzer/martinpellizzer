Polemonium ehrenbergii

Polemonium ehrenbergii, commonly known as the bluebells of the prairies, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and woodland edges.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in native plant ecosystems.
