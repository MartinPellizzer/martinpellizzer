Picea engelmannii f. argentea

Picea engelmannii f. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a variant of the Engelmann spruce, characterized by its distinctive silvery-white underside on the needles.

This frost-resistant plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically found in mountainous regions of western North America. The foliage is dense and conical, with needles that are short and stout, providing a unique texture. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the plant's seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
