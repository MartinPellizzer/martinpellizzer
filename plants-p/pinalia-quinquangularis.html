Pinalia quinquangularis

Pinalia quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled pinalia, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive five-angled seed pods.

It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or pale yellow in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unusual morphology.
