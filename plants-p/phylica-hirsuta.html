Phylica hirsuta

Phylica hirsuta, commonly known as the silver bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Phylica hirsuta is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
