Phacelia magellanica var. corymbosa

Phacelia magellanica var. corymbosa, commonly known as the Magellan phacelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
