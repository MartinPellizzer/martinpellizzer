Pyrus ovata

Pyrus ovata, commonly known as the Japanese nashi or Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet, and slightly tart fruits. The fruit has a smooth, thin skin and a crisp, juicy flesh.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing plant in temperate climates.
