Paspalum pleostachyum

Paspalum pleostachyum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in coastal regions. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are loosely arranged.

Paspalum pleostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
