Piper aguadulcense

Piper aguadulcense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common pepper (Piper nigrum). The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically grown as a climbing vine in gardens and agricultural settings.
