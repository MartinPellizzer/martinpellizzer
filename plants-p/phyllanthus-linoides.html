Phyllanthus linoides

Phyllanthus linoides, commonly known as the Indian cheesenut or stone apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by fleshy, yellowish berries that contain hard, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
