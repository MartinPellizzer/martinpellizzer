Physedra barteri

Physedra barteri, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, which are typically narrow and strap-like. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its flowering stems emerge from the water surface, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

Physedra barteri plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
