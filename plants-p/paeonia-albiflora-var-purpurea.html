Paeonia albiflora var. purpurea

Paeonia albiflora var. purpurea, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous perennial shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces compound, trifoliate leaves that are dark green and glossy. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant, single or semi-double flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.
