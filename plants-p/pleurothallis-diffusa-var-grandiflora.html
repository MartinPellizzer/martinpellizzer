Pleurothallis diffusa var. grandiflora

Pleurothallis diffusa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens.
