Pouteria spruceana

Pouteria spruceana, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree is notable for its large, woody fruits that contain edible seeds, commonly known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and healthy fats.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
