Potentilla glandulosa subsp. reflexa

Potentilla glandulosa subsp.

reflexa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is usually covered with glandular dots, which give it its scientific name.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh conditions.
