Pittosporum madagascariense

Pittosporum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also notable for its potential ecological importance in its native habitat.
