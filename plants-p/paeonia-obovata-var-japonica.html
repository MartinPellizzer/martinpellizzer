Paeonia obovata var. japonica

Paeonia obovata var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of China.

It features large, showy flowers with velvety petals that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces woody stems that become more prominent with age. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its spring blooming period.
