Pasania cerifera

Pasania cerifera, commonly known as the wax tree, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its waxy, leathery leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in yellow or greenish hues.

Pasania cerifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
