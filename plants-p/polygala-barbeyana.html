Polygala barbeyana

Polygala barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbey's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala barbeyana is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
