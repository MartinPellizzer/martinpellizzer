Passiflora foetida subvar. quinqueloba

Passiflora foetida subvar. quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five prominent lobes on the corona, giving it its common name. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
