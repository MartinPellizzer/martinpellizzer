Phreatia globulosa

Phreatia globulosa, commonly known as the globular orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid conditions.

Phreatia globulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
