Pyranthus alasoa

Pyranthus alasoa, commonly known as the Madagascar grass.

It is a flowering plant native to Madagascar, belonging to the Poaceae family. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

Pyranthus alasoa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
