Potamogeton wolfgangii

Potamogeton wolfgangii, commonly known as Wolfgang's pondweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Potamogeton wolfgangii plays an important role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
