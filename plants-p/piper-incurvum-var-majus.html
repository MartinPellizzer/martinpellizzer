Piper incurvum var. majus

Piper incurvum var. majus, commonly known as the giant betel vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by reddish-purple berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, tropical conditions make it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
