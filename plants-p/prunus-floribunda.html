Prunus floribunda

Prunus floribunda, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry or weeping cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, cascading branches that produce clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring. The tree has oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its beauty and seasonal blooms, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions around the world.
