Papaver burseri var. bicolor

Papaver burseri var. bicolor, commonly known as the bicolor poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bi-colored flowers that typically display a combination of red and white or yellow and white petals. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique coloration and delicate, papery petals.
