Pinus divaricata var. musci

Pinus divaricata var. musci, commonly known as the moss pine, is a variant of the red pine that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is distinguished by its dense, moss-like foliage that covers the branches, giving it a distinct appearance. The needles are typically shorter and more clustered compared to the standard red pine, contributing to its unique morphology. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various forest organisms and contributing to soil stabilization.
