Phanera coccinea

Phanera coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered phanera, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
