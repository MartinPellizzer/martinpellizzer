Pulicaria glutinosa subsp. glutinosa

Pulicaria glutinosa subsp.

glutinosa, commonly known as sticky pulicaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The plant features basal rosettes of gray-green, hairy leaves that are coated with a sticky resin, which gives it its common name. Its flowering stems bear yellow flowers arranged in dense, compact clusters, typically blooming from June to August.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
