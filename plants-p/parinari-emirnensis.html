Parinari emirnensis

Parinari emirnensis, commonly known as the Emir's parinari, is a tropical tree native to parts of West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
