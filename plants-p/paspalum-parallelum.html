Paspalum parallelum

Paspalum parallelum, commonly known as the common paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, spreading stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Paspalum parallelum is known for its ability to thrive in moist to wet conditions, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
