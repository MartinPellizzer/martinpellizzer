Phlox puberula

Phlox puberula, commonly known as the downy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers in the early summer. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine, downy hairs, giving it a soft texture.

Phlox puberula thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.
