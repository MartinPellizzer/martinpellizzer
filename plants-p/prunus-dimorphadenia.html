Prunus dimorphadenia

Prunus dimorphadenia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Prunus dimorphadenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Prunus dimorphadenia is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
