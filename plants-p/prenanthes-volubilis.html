Prenanthes volubilis

Prenanthes volubilis, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features slender, climbing stems that often trail along the ground or climb over other vegetation. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Prenanthes volubilis thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
