Penstemon smallii

Penstemon smallii, commonly known as the San Francisco penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds via the wind.

Penstemon smallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and vibrant blooms.
