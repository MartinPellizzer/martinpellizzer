Primulina minor

Primulina minor, commonly known as the dwarf primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its small, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its delicate appearance, it requires careful handling and specific care to flourish.
