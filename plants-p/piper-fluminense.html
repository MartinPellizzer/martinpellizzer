Piper fluminense

Piper fluminense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-lobed berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.

The plant's botanical features reflect its adaptation to humid, shaded environments in the understory of tropical forests.
