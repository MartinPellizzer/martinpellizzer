Pelargonium × savonitianum

Pelargonium × savonitianum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with a distinctive double petal structure. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with succulent, slightly hairy leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, adding color to gardens year-round.
