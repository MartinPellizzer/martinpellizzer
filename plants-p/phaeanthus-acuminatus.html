Phaeanthus acuminatus

Phaeanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phaeanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, strap-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue and bloom in the spring or early summer.
