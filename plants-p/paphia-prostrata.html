Paphia prostrata

Paphia prostrata, commonly known as the flat-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat on the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Paphia prostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in many tropical gardens.
