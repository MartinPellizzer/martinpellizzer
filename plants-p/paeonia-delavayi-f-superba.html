Paeonia delavayi f. superba

Paeonia delavayi f. superba, commonly known as the Chinese peony or the Himalayan peony, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thick, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is prized for its beauty in both garden and horticultural settings.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners interested in rare and exotic flora.
