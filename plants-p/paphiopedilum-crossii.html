Paphiopedilum crossii

Paphiopedilum crossii, commonly known as the Cross's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its unique, hooded flower structure that resembles a pouch, which is a distinctive feature among orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with contrasting yellow markings, adding to its ornamental value.

Paphiopedilum crossii is prized by horticulturists for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
