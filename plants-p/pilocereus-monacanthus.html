Pilocereus monacanthus

Pilocereus monacanthus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its ability to produce night-blooming flowers. The plant has a unique structure, with a colorful, fleshy upper portion called the "body" and a green, stem-like base known as the "foot.". It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking, often fragrant flowers that open at night.
