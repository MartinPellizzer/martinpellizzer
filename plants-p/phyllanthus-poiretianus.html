Phyllanthus poiretianus

Phyllanthus poiretianus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, green, fleshy leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Phyllanthus poiretianus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
