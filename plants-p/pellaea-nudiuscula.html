Pellaea nudiuscula

Pellaea nudiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved maidenhair fern, is a small, evergreen fern native to southern Africa.

It features delicate, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a distinctive, finely serrated margin.

Pellaea nudiuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
