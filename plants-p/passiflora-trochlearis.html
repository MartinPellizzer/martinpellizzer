Passiflora trochlearis

Passiflora trochlearis, commonly known as the Trinidad passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora trochlearis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
