Podanthus

Podanthus, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its attractive, often silver or gray-green foliage. The leaves are typically opposite and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss. Podanthus species produce showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, often arranged in clusters.

These plants are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
