Polygala aspalatha var. comosa

Polygala aspalatha var. comosa, commonly known as the woolly milk-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine, white hairs.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a distinctive spurred structure that aids in pollination by insects.
