Phaeiris × cacique

Phaeiris × cacique, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Phaeiris and Hibiscus species.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
