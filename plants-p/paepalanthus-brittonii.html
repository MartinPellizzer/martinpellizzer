Paepalanthus brittonii

Paepalanthus brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's pincushion cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a spherical or clustered shape, resembling a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to its harsh, dry environment.

Paepalanthus brittonii is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in full sun.
