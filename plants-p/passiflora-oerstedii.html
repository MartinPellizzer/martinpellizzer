Passiflora oerstedii

Passiflora oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora oerstedii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
