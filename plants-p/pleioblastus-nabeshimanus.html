Pleioblastus nabeshimanus

Pleioblastus nabeshimanus, commonly known as the Nabeshima bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are typically green with a bluish tint. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This bamboo is also valued for its ability to provide a fine, delicate texture in landscape designs.
