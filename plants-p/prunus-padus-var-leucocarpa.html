Prunus padus var. leucocarpa

Prunus padus var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited bird cherry, is a variant of the common bird cherry.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its white or pale yellow fruits, which contrast with its dark green leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by the distinctive white berries in late summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
