Plectranthus ferrugineus

Plectranthus ferrugineus, commonly known as the red-stemmed plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features square, hairy stems that are typically reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
