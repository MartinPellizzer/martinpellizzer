Persea fluviatilis

Persea fluviatilis, commonly known as the river avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The tree bears yellow to orange fruits that are similar in appearance to avocados but are typically smaller. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruit in its native habitat.
