Psilogramme sellowiana

Psilogramme sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
