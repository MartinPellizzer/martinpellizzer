Passiflora macfadyenii

Passiflora macfadyenii, commonly known as the MacFadyen's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of red, yellow, and blue hues, along with prominent sepals and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Passiflora macfadyenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators.
