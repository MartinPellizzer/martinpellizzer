Pittosporum glabratum

Pittosporum glabratum, commonly known as the silver pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and leathery, dark green leaves that are glossy on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in late winter to early spring, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum glabratum is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
