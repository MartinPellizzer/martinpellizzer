Pontederia vaginalis

Pontederia vaginalis, commonly known as the broad-leaved water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can outcompete native species and alter aquatic ecosystems.
