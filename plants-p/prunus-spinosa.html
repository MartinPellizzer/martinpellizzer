Prunus spinosa

Prunus spinosa, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in poor, well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in jams and wines, and the plant has historical uses in herbal medicine.
