Phyllanthus nobilis var. genuinus

Phyllanthus nobilis var. genuinus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
