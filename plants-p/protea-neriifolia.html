Protea neriifolia

Protea neriifolia, commonly known as the black-leafed protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces nectar-rich blooms that attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
