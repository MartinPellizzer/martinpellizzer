Philadelphus inodorus var. hirsutus

Philadelphus inodorus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and pleasant scent.
