Phyllanthus temehaniensis

Phyllanthus temehaniensis, commonly known as the Teme-Hani milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is typically found in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various insect species.
