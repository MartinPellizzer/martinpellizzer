Piper infossum var. nudum

Piper infossum var. nudum, commonly known as the naked pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The variety 'nudum' is distinguished by its reduced or absent stipules, giving it its common name.
