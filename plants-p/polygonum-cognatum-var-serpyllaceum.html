Polygonum cognatum var. serpyllaceum

Polygonum cognatum var. serpyllaceum, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streambanks, wetlands, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are erect and branched.

It is a common species in the eastern and central United States, often found in open habitats with partial to full sun.
