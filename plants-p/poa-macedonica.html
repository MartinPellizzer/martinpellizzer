Poa macedonica

Poa macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan region.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted culms and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in temperate climates.
