Potentilla hispanica f. viridis

Potentilla hispanica f. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
