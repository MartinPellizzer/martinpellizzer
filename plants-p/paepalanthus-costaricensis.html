Paepalanthus costaricensis

Paepalanthus costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it thrive in its natural environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly found in the wild.
