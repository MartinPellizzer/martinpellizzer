Penstemon cardinalis subsp. regalis

Penstemon cardinalis subsp.

regalis, commonly known as the cardinal penstemon, is a species of flowering plant native to the central and southern United States. It is characterized by its striking red to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb with a tall, erect stem and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
