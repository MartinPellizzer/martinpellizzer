Pyrrosia lingua var. heteractis

Pyrrosia lingua var. heteractis, commonly known as the heteractis fern, is a variant of the common pyrrosia fern.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, glossy appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
