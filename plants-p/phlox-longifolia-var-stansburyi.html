Phlox longifolia var. stansburyi

Phlox longifolia var. stansburyi, commonly known as Stansbury's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to arid landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
