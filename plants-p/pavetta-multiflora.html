Pavetta multiflora

Pavetta multiflora, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pavetta multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
