Panicum kiyalaense

Panicum kiyalaense, commonly known as the Kiyala grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
