Panicum imbricatum var. oreoboloides

Panicum imbricatum var. oreoboloides, commonly known as the coastal bluestem, is a grass species native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-angled spikelets. It is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a common sight in dune ecosystems.

Its ability to stabilize coastal soils makes it ecologically significant in preventing erosion.
