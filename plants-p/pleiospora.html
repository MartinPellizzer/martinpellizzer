Pleiospora

Pleiospora, commonly known as the "false powdery mildew," is a genus of fungi that belongs to the order Capnodiales.

These organisms are primarily saprophytic, decomposing organic matter in various environments. They are often found on decaying plant material, such as leaves and wood, where they play a role in nutrient cycling. Pleiospora species are typically identified by their dark, sooty colonies and the production of conidia, which are asexual spores.

While they are not parasitic, their presence can indicate the decomposition stage of plant matter in an ecosystem.
