Panicum orthum

Panicum orthum, commonly known as hairgrass, is a grass species native to various parts of the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often seen in open fields and along roadsides. The plant typically grows in full sun and prefers well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and meadow ecosystems. It is an important component of native plant communities and provides forage for wildlife.

Its ornamental value has also led to its use in landscaping and restoration projects.
