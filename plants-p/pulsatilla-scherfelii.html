Pulsatilla scherfelii

Pulsatilla scherfelii, commonly known as the Scherfel's pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that are typically pink or white. The plant features a short, hairy stem with few leaves and produces a single, large, cup-shaped flower. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
