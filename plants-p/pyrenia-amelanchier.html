Pyrenia amelanchier

Pyrenia amelanchier, commonly known as serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a red or purple color in late summer. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

Pyrenia amelanchier is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
