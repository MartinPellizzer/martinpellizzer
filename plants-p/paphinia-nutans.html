Paphinia nutans

Paphinia nutans, commonly known as the nodding paphiopedilum, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, downward-facing flowers that resemble a pouch, a feature typical of the orchid family. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to thrive. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer, are often a mix of white, pink, and green hues.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Paphinia nutans is a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
