Phaseolus chrysanthos var. hirtus

Phaseolus chrysanthos var. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy golden bean, is a variant of the golden bean plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its specific variety name "hirtus.". The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food crop.
