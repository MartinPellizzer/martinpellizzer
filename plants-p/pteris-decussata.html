Pteris decussata

Pteris decussata, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically bi-lobed and have a compact, upright growth habit. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's fronds are usually dark green and possess a leathery texture, with sporangia clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves.

Pteris decussata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
