Prunus padus f. glauca

Prunus padus f. glauca, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry or glaucous cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically a glossy green, and the bark is smooth with a greyish-blue hue. This plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
