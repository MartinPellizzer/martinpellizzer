Prunus × koshiensis var. leucantha

Prunus × koshiensis var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its striking white blossoms that appear in early spring, providing a notable ornamental display. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
