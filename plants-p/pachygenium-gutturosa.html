Pachygenium gutturosa

Pachygenium gutturosa, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet, with distinctive yellow or white markings. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
