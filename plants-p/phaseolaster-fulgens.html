Phaseolaster fulgens

Phaseolaster fulgens, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces long, trailing stems with palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
