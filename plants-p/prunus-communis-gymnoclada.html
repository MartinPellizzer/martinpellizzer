Prunus communis gymnoclada

Prunus communis gymnoclada, commonly known as the common cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ecological settings.
