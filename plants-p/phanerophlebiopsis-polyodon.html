Phanerophlebiopsis polyodon

Phanerophlebiopsis polyodon, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

The orchid's reproductive strategy involves mycorrhizal associations, which are essential for its nutrient uptake and seed germination.
