Pteryxia thapsoides

Pteryxia thapsoides, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to parts of Africa and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature, turning red when ripe.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
