Polygonum aviculare f. erectum

Polygonum aviculare f. erectum, commonly known as upright knotweed, is a variant of the common knotweed species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its erect, upright growth habit compared to the prostrate form of the species. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in moist to wet environments.
