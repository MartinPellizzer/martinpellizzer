Polemonium viscosum var. pilosum

Polemonium viscosum var. pilosum, commonly known as the downy bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are tubular with five petals and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
