Paeonia officinalis var. anemoniflora

Paeonia officinalis var. anemoniflora, commonly known as the Anemone-flowered Peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that bear clusters of showy, single or double flowers with white to pale pink petals. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stem. This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries for both its aesthetic appeal and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
