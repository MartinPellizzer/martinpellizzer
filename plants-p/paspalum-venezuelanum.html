Paspalum venezuelanum

Paspalum venezuelanum, commonly known as the Venezuelan paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, preferring moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
