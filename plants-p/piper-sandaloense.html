Piper sandaloense

Piper sandaloense, commonly known as the sandalwood pepper, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems that emit a distinct scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Due to its unique fragrance and medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional herbal remedies.
