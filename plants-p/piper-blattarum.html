Piper blattarum

Piper blattarum, commonly known as the sacred pepper of the Yoruba people, is a climbing shrub native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and emits a strong, pungent aroma. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The berries are traditionally used in religious ceremonies and as a spice in local cuisines.
