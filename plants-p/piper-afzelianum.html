Piper afzelianum

Piper afzelianum, commonly known as the African black pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery leaves. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper afzelianum make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
