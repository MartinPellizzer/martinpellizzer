Pyrus secundaria

Pyrus secundaria, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark blue or black color.

Pyrus secundaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
