Pulsatilla alpina subsp. font-queri

Pulsatilla alpina subsp.

font-queri, commonly known as the Alpine pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations to high-altitude environments.
