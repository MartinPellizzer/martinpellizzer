Paxtonia rosea

Paxtonia rosea, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The plant features compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets, typically ranging from 7 to 11. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by legume-like pods containing flattened seeds.

Paxtonia rosea is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.
