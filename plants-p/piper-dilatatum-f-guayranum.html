Piper dilatatum f. guayranum

Piper dilatatum f. guayranum, commonly known as the Guayranum pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper dilatatum species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that transition from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
