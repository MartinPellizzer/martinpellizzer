Paspalum crispatum

Paspalum crispatum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its wiry, hair-covered stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum crispatum is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
