Palma pinao

Palma pinao, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves that form a distinctive crown. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible fruits resembling pineapples, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Palma pinao is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in some regions, its fruit is used in traditional cuisine.
