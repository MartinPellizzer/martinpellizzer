Papaver sylvestre

Papaver sylvestre, commonly known as wild poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Papaver sylvestre is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
