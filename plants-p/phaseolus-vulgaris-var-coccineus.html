Phaseolus vulgaris var. coccineus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a tropical vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and edible pods that contain large, kidney-shaped beans. The plant typically grows up to 3 meters in height, with trifoliate leaves and hairy stems. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens and for culinary use due to its flavorful beans and ornamental value.
