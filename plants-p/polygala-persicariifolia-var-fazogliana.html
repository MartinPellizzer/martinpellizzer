Polygala persicariifolia var. fazogliana

Polygala persicariifolia var. fazogliana, commonly known as the Persian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
