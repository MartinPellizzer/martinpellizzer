Primula listeri subsp. austrolisteri

Primula listeri subsp.

austrolisteri, commonly known as the Australian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to southeastern Australia. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Primula genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
