Phlox wheeleriana

Phlox wheeleriana, commonly known as Wheeler's phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vivid pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Phlox wheeleriana prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes in suitable climates.
