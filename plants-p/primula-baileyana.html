Primula baileyana

Primula baileyana, commonly known as the Bailey's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a dense, low-growing habit with broad, oval leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
