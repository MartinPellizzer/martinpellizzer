Potamogeton anglicus

Potamogeton anglicus, commonly known as the English pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves arranged spirally. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
