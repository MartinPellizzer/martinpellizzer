Phyteuma montanum

Phyteuma montanum, commonly known as the mountain phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phyteuma montanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
