Paraprasophyllum spadiceum

Paraprasophyllum spadiceum, commonly known as the golden spathephyllum, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are clustered in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant is native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
