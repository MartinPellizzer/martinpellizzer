Phyllodes bisubulata

Phyllodes bisubulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, flattened phyllodes that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal and sandy soils.
