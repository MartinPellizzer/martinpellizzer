Pygeum membranaceum

Pygeum membranaceum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain smooth, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
