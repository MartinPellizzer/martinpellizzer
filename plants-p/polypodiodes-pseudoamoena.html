Polypodiodes pseudoamoena

Polypodiodes pseudoamoena, commonly known as the false bird's nest fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth and pinnately compound fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

The plant's morphology and growth habits make it well adapted to humid, low-light conditions.
