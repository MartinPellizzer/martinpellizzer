Pandanus cephalotus

Pandanus cephalotus, commonly known as the crown pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences at the apex of the foliage. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to coastal and sandy soils.

Pandanus cephalotus is often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.
