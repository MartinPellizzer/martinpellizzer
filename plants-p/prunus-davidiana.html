Prunus davidiana

Prunus davidiana, commonly known as the David's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Prunus davidiana thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
