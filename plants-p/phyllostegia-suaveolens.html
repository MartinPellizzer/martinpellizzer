Phyllostegia suaveolens

Phyllostegia suaveolens, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweetleaf vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant blossoms that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their aromatic properties.

Phyllostegia suaveolens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
