Phaseolus oblongus var. purpureovariegatus

Phaseolus oblongus var. purpureovariegatus, commonly known as the purple variegated bean, is a cultivar of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple and white variegated foliage, which sets it apart from typical green varieties. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible qualities.
