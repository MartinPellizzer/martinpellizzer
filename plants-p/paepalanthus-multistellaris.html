Paepalanthus multistellaris

Paepalanthus multistellaris, commonly known as the star-shaped puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family, known for its vibrant, star-like flowers that bloom in a tight cluster. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that form a rosette, which helps collect moisture from the air.

The flowers, which are often brightly colored, attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, playing a vital role in its ecological niche.
