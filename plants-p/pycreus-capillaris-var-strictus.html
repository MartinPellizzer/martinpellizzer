Pycreus capillaris var. strictus

Pycreus capillaris var. strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pycreus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged alternately along the stem, giving it a grass-like appearance. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming during the warmer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
