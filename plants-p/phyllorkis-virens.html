Phyllorkis virens

Phyllorkis virens, commonly known as the green-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include rhizomatous growth and a unique floral structure that attracts specific pollinators.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
