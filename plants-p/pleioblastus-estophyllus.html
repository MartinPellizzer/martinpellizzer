Pleioblastus estophyllus

Pleioblastus estophyllus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. This plant is characterized by its slender, silver-green culms and graceful, arching growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Pleioblastus estophyllus is also valued for its ability to form dense clumps, making it suitable for erosion control and habitat creation.
