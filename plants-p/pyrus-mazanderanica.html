Pyrus mazanderanica

Pyrus mazanderanica, commonly known as the Mazanderan pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish-green pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is appreciated in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
