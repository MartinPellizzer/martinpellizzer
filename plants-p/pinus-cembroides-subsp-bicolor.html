Pinus cembroides subsp. bicolor

Pinus cembroides subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored pinyon pine, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its distinctive bark that transitions from reddish-brown to gray as the tree matures.

The needles are typically blue-green in color, and the cones are small, maturing to a reddish-brown hue. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

This plant plays a vital role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
