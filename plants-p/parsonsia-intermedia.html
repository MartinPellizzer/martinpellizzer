Parsonsia intermedia

Parsonsia intermedia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
