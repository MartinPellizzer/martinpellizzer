Pernettya chubutensis

Pernettya chubutensis, commonly known as the Chubut cranberry, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
