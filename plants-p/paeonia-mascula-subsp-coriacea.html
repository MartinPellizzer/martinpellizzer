Paeonia mascula subsp. coriacea

Paeonia mascula subsp.

coriacea, commonly known as the rough peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its thick, rough-textured stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red.

The plant produces pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, and its flowers are borne on long, sturdy peduncles. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
