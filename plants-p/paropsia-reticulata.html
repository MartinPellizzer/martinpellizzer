Paropsia reticulata

Paropsia reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and intricate, net-like vein patterns on its foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
