Proustia mendocina

Proustia mendocina, commonly known as the Mendoza pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Proustia mendocina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
