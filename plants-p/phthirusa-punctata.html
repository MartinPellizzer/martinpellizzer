Phthirusa punctata

Phthirusa punctata, commonly known as the dotted leaf bug, is a species of plant-dwelling insect found primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Phthiracaridae and is characterized by its small, elongated body covered in fine, waxy hairs. This insect is often found on the leaves of various plants, where it feeds on plant sap, causing minor damage. Its coloration typically ranges from green to brown, allowing it to blend with its surroundings.

Due to its cryptic nature, it is often difficult to detect without close inspection.
