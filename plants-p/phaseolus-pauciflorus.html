Phaseolus pauciflorus

Phaseolus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups of two to four. Its pods are usually slender and contain small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and have potential for agricultural cultivation.
