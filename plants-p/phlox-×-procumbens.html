Phlox × procumbens

Phlox × procumbens, commonly known as the creeping phlox, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features dense clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has spreading stems that root at the nodes, forming a carpet-like ground cover. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its foliage is typically a deep green.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental gardens.
