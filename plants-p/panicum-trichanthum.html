Panicum trichanthum

Panicum trichanthum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy panicles that produce a reddish tinge during the flowering stage. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.

The botanical features of Panicum trichanthum contribute to its ecological resilience and widespread distribution.
