Packera hartiana

Packera hartiana, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, cold climates. Its basal leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Packera hartiana plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and supports local pollinators.
