Pleopeltis acutifolia

Pleopeltis acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually clustered and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Pleopeltis acutifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in terrariums or as a houseplant.
