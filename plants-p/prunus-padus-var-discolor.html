Prunus padus var. discolor

Prunus padus var. discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered bird cherry, is a variant of the common bird cherry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its white flowers that appear in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are typically ovate with a serrated edge and turn yellow in the fall.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.
