Paeonia hartwissiana

Paeonia hartwissiana, commonly known as the Hartwissiana peony, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant features palmately divided, dark green leaves and is often found growing in rocky or well-drained soils. It is a herbaceous perennial that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical gardens.
