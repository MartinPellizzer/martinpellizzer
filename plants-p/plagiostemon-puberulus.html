Plagiostemon puberulus

Plagiostemon puberulus, commonly known as the hairy plagiostemon, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a hairy appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating.

Plagiostemon puberulus produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.
