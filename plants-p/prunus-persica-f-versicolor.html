Prunus persica f. versicolor

Prunus persica f. versicolor, commonly known as the variegated peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its unique leaf patterns and flower colors.

This form exhibits a wide range of foliage color variations, including white, yellow, and green, which create a striking visual contrast. The flowers are typically more vibrant and come in shades of pink and white, enhancing the plant's ornamental value. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and the potential for fruit production.
