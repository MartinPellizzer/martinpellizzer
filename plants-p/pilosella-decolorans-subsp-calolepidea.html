Pilosella decolorans subsp. calolepidea

Pilosella decolorans subsp.

calolepidea, commonly known as the paleleaf mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean. It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are usually pale green, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
