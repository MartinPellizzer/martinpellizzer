Paspalum neesii var. monachyrium

Paspalum neesii var. monachyrium, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
