Potentilla cinerea var. trisecta

Potentilla cinerea var. trisecta, commonly known as the gray cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
