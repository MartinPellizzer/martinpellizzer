Prunus serotina var. virens

Prunus serotina var. virens, commonly known as the greenleaf black cherry, is a variant of the black cherry tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark green, glossy leaves that remain vibrant throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, with a slight reddish tint on younger trees.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
