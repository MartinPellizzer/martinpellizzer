Physaria didymocarpa var. normalis

Physaria didymocarpa var. normalis, commonly known as the double-seeded bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and inflated, seed-filled pods that resemble bladders. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique seed morphology and ecological adaptations.
