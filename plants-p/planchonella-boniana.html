Planchonella boniana

Planchonella boniana, commonly known as the Kauri daisy, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, giving it a daisy-like appearance.

Planchonella boniana is adapted to coastal environments and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
