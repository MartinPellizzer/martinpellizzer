Pellionia pachypoda

Pellionia pachypoda, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Pellionia pachypoda thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.
