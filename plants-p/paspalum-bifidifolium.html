Paspalum bifidifolium

Paspalum bifidifolium, commonly known as the bifid-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that often exhibit a distinctive bifid (split) appearance at the base. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers.

Paspalum bifidifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
