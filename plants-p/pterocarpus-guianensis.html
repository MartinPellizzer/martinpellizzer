Pterocarpus guianensis

Pterocarpus guianensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, dark reddish-brown heartwood. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and flat, compound leaves with a feathery appearance. Its bark is thick and flaky, often exuding a strong, aromatic scent.

The tree is valued for its durable and highly figured wood, which has been traditionally used in musical instruments and fine furniture.
