Parrya glabra

Parrya glabra, commonly known as the smooth parrya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pale blue or white, arranged in dense clusters.

Parrya glabra is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
