Potentilla moorcroftii

Potentilla moorcroftii, commonly known as the moorcroft's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features wiry, branched stems and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, which gives it the common name "cinquefoil.".

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
