Phacelia magellanica var. patula

Phacelia magellanica var. patula, commonly known as the Magellan phacelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
