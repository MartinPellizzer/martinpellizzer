Prunus polymorpha insititia

Prunus polymorpha insititia, commonly known as the common cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and oval to elliptical leaves that are typically hairy when young. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that range in color from white to dark red. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
