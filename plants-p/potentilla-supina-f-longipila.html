Potentilla supina f. longipila

Potentilla supina f. longipila, commonly known as the prostrate potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
