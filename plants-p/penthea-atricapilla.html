Penthea atricapilla

Penthea atricapilla, commonly known as the black-capped penthea, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Its dark purple to black flower heads are striking and attract various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
