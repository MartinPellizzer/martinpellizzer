Polyosma induta

Polyosma induta, commonly known as the red-flowered ghost tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of bright red flowers, which bloom in spring.

The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature.
