Persoonia juniperina var. brevifolia

Persoonia juniperina var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved burr-pear, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow, and leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The plant produces distinctive woody fruits that resemble small, rounded burrs, which are an adaptation for seed dispersal by animals. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
