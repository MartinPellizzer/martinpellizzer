Prostanthera wrixonii

Prostanthera wrixonii, commonly known as the Wrixon's mintbush, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Prostanthera wrixonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
