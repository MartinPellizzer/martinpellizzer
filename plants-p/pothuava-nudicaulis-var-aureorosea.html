Pothuava nudicaulis var. aureorosea

Pothuava nudicaulis var. aureorosea, commonly known as the golden rose pothos, is a variant of the pothos plant characterized by its striking, variegated leaves.

This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its foliage features a mix of green, yellow, and rose-colored patterns, making it a visually appealing houseplant. It typically grows as a trailing vine, producing small, inconspicuous white flowers.

The varietal name 'aureorosea' refers to the golden and rose hues that adorn its leaves.
