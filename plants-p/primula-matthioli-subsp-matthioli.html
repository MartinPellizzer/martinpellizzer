Primula matthioli subsp. matthioli

Primula matthioli subsp.

matthioli, commonly known as the Italian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
