Pleurothallis exarticulata

Pleurothallis exarticulata, commonly known as the "Slender Pleurothallis," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, dark green leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often showy inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
