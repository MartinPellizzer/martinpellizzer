Panicum inversum

Panicum inversum, commonly known as the inverted panicgrass, is a grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which is inversely oriented compared to typical panicgrass species. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

Panicum inversum is of interest to botanists due to its distinctive morphology and potential ecological adaptations.
