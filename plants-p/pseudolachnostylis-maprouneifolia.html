Pseudolachnostylis maprouneifolia

Pseudolachnostylis maprouneifolia, commonly known as the African maple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It features a rounded canopy with palmate leaves that are typically lobed and have a maple-like appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
