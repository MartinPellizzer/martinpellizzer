Petroselinum tuberosum

Petroselinum tuberosum, commonly known as globe artichoke, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of large, fleshy, lobed leaves that form a dense, cabbage-like structure. The plant produces a spiny, globe-shaped flower head that is harvested before flowering. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter, nutty flavor.

It is cultivated for both its leaves and its ornamental value in gardens.
