Paeonia corallina var. flavescens

Paeonia corallina var. flavescens, commonly known as the coral peony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-pink to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub with compound leaves and produces showy, peony-like blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include a woody rootstock and a compact growth habit.
