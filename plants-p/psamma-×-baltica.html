Psamma × baltica

Psamma × baltica, commonly known as the Baltic sandwort, is a hybrid plant that results from the cross between Psamma lapponica and Psamma baltica.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. Native to the coastal regions of the Baltic Sea, it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in maritime environments, where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for stabilizing dune ecosystems.
