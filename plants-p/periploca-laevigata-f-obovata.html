Periploca laevigata f. obovata

Periploca laevigata f. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed periploca, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its obovate, glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often borne on short pedicels. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage.

The species is native to certain regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded conditions.
