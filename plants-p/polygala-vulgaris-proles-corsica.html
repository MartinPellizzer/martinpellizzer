Polygala vulgaris proles corsica

Polygala vulgaris proles corsica, commonly known as the Corsican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, usually pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
