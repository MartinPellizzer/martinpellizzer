Potamogeton tenuissimus

Potamogeton tenuissimus, commonly known as narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton tenuissimus thrives in slow-moving to still waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.
