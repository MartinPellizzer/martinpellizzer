Pelargonium crinitum var. congestum

Pelargonium crinitum var. congestum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a compact shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ability to retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
