Pittosporum vernicosum

Pittosporum vernicosum, commonly known as the New Zealand white mistletoe, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in coastal and forested areas, often growing on trees or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
