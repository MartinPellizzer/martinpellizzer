Phymatodes oxyloba

Phymatodes oxyloba, commonly known as the redroot filaree, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.
