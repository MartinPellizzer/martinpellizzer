Phylica albida

Phylica albida, commonly known as the white bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant is often used in coastal restoration projects due to its hardiness.

Phylica albida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
