Piper repens

Piper repens, commonly known as creeping pepper vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elliptical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and fruits have been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cultures.
