Penianthus

Penianthus, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. Penianthus species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

The genus includes several species, with Penianthus acaulis and Penianthus japonicus being the most well-known.
