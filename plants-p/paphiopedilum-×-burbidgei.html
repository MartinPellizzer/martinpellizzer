Paphiopedilum × burbidgei

Paphiopedilum × burbidgei, commonly known as the Burridge's slipper orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique slipper-shaped pouch, which is a specialized structure used to trap pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with intricate patterns, and it blooms throughout the year in warm climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in horticulture.
