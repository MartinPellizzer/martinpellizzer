Potentilla glandulosa var. wrangelliana

Potentilla glandulosa var. wrangelliana, commonly known as Wrangell's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branched stems covered with glandular hairs, which give it a distinctive texture. Its leaves are compound, typically consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along stream banks.
