Panicum trichopus var. lasiostachys

Panicum trichopus var. lasiostachys, commonly known as the silky panic grass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy inflorescences that give it a soft, cloud-like appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-gray hue, and the flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

This variant is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
