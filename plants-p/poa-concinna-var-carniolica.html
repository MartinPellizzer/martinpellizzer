Poa concinna var. carniolica

Poa concinna var. carniolica, commonly known as the Carniolan meadowgrass, is a grass species native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, dark green blades. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that mature into seeds in the summer months.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
