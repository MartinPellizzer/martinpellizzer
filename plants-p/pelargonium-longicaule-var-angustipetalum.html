Pelargonium longicaule var. angustipetalum

Pelargonium longicaule var. angustipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, hairy stems. The leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five narrow petals and prominent stamens.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and compact size.
