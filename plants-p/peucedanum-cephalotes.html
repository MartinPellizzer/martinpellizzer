Peucedanum cephalotes

Peucedanum cephalotes, commonly known as the Ethiopian cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and emit a mild licorice scent. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Peucedanum cephalotes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
