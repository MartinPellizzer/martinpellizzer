Prunus erubescens

Prunus erubescens, commonly known as the red cherry or red almond tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Prunus erubescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
