Poa glycerioides

Poa glycerioides, commonly known as the New Zealand hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, greenish-brown flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Poa glycerioides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
