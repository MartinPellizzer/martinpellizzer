Poa botryoides var. glaucissima

Poa botryoides var. glaucissima, commonly known as the glaucous tufted grass, is a variant of the tufted grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive bluish-gray foliage due to a waxy coating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear fluffy, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
