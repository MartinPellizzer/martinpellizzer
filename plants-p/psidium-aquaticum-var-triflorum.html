Psidium aquaticum var. triflorum

Psidium aquaticum var. triflorum, commonly known as the water guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pink, and it bears small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, humid environments and is often found near water sources such as rivers and swamps.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
