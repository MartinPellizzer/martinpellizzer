Prismatocarpus perfoliatus

Prismatocarpus perfoliatus, commonly known as the perfoliate pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a multiple fruit, consisting of many small drupes clustered together.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in coastal and humid environments.
