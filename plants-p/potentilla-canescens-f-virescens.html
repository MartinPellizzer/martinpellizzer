Potentilla canescens f. virescens

Potentilla canescens f. virescens, commonly known as the green-hairy cinquefoil, is a variant of the common cinquefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, greenish stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered with fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This form is noted for its hardiness and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
