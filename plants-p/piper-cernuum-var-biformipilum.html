Piper cernuum var. biformipilum

Piper cernuum var. biformipilum, commonly known as the two-branched pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically green when mature. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
