Penstemon digitalis var. pubescens

Penstemon digitalis var. pubescens, commonly known as the fuzzy beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it its common name, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is well-suited for xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
