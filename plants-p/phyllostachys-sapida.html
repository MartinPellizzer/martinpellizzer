Phyllostachys sapida

Phyllostachys sapida, commonly known as the bamboo shoot or bamboo grass, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant is often cultivated for its edible shoots, which are harvested in the spring. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slightly pointed tip.

Phyllostachys sapida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in both traditional and modern horticulture.
