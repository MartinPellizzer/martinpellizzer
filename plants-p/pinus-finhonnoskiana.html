Pinus finhonnoskiana

Pinus finhonnoskiana, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often have a distinctive resinous scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pinus finhonnoskiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and landscapes.
