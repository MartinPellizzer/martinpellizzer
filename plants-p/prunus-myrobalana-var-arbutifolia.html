Prunus myrobalana var. arbutifolia

Prunus myrobalana var. arbutifolia, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments.

The wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in traditional craftsmanship and construction.
