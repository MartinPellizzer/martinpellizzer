Phrynium tonkinense var. pedunculatum

Phrynium tonkinense var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Laos. The plant features a stout, upright stem with broad, waxy leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. It produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, warm climates.
