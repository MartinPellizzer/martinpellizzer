Piper valladolidense

Piper valladolidense, commonly known as the Valladolid pepper or Mexican pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits. It is often found in humid, tropical environments and thrives in partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
