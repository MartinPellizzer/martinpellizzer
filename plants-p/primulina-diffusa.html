Primulina diffusa

Primulina diffusa, commonly known as the velvet leaf.

It is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
