Passiflora reflexiflora

Passiflora reflexiflora, commonly known as the reflexed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Passiflora reflexiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
