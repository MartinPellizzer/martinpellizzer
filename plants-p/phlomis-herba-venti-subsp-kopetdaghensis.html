Phlomis herba-venti subsp. kopetdaghensis

Phlomis herba-venti subsp.

kopetdaghensis, commonly known as the Kopetdagh phlomis, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces showy pink to purple flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its aromatic foliage and drought tolerance make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
