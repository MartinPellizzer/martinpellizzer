Piper restio

Piper restio, commonly known as the narrowleaf pepper plant, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the family Piperaceae, which includes many species known for their peppery flavors. The plant typically has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
