Pourouma subplicata

Pourouma subplicata, commonly known as the pink river bamboo, is a striking member of the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 12 meters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with pink or red, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found near rivers and in swampy areas.

Due to its unique coloration and growth habit, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
