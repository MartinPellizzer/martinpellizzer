Populus platyphylla

Populus platyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a grayish or white color, and it prefers well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Populus platyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
