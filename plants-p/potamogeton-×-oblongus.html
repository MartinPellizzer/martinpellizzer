Potamogeton × oblongus

Potamogeton × oblongus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that often grow in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. The plant produces small, green to yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, floating or submerged depending on the water conditions.

Potamogeton × oblongus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
