Polygonatum officinale f. latifolium

Polygonatum officinale f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and forest understories.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in herbal remedies.
