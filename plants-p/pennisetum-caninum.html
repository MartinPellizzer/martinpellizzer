Pennisetum caninum

Pennisetum caninum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, purple-tinted inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various climates. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Pennisetum caninum is also valued for its use in animal forage and as a windbreak in agricultural settings.
