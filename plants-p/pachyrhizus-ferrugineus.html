Pachyrhizus ferrugineus

Pachyrhizus ferrugineus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod containing several edible seeds, which are often used in cooking.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nutritional content and ornamental appeal.
