Potamogeton thunbergii

Potamogeton thunbergii, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, slender stems that grow horizontally in water, often forming dense mats near the bottom of lakes and ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Potamogeton thunbergii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediment and improve water quality.
