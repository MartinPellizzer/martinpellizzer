Protasparagus capensis

Protasparagus capensis, commonly known as the Cape asparagus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
