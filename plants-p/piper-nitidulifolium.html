Piper nitidulifolium

Piper nitidulifolium, commonly known as the glossy-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are peppercorns, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
