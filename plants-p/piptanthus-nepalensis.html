Piptanthus nepalensis

Piptanthus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal false peacock flower, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and alpine meadows. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces small, hard pods that contain several seeds.

Piptanthus nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
