Potentilla cilicia

Potentilla cilicia, commonly known as the Cilicia cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Cyprus.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in heads and attract pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval to elliptic in shape.

Potentilla cilicia is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in harsh, arid environments.
