Peplis portula var. fradinii

Peplis portula var. fradinii, commonly known as the golden sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to rocky or sandy soils. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variety is often found in desert scrub and coastal dunes, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions.
