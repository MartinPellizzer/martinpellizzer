Pygeum ovale

Pygeum ovale, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Pygeum ovale is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating urinary disorders.
