Polygala sennii

Polygala sennii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are often pink or purple and are arranged in clusters, making it an ornamental plant in some regions.

The leaves are usually opposite and have a lance-shaped appearance, contributing to its distinctive botanical profile.
