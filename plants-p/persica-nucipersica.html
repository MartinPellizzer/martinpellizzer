Persica nucipersica

Persica nucipersica, commonly known as the Persian peach, is a species of deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in spring. The fruit, which resembles a peach, is typically small, sweet, and has a fuzzy skin. This plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
