Panicum controversum

Panicum controversum, commonly known as the controversial grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woods.

Due to its adaptability and sometimes invasive growth, it is both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
