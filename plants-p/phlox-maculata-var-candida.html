Phlox maculata var. candida

Phlox maculata var. candida, commonly known as the white garden phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branched stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms make it a valuable addition to wildflower gardens and pollinator habitats.
