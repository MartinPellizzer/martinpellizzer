Podophyllum difforme

Podophyllum difforme, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of its native habitat.
