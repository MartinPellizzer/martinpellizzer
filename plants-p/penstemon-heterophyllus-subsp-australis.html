Penstemon heterophyllus subsp. australis

Penstemon heterophyllus subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
