Paspalum elatum

Paspalum elatum, commonly known as giant paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and large, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
