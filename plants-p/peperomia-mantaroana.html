Peperomia mantaroana

Peperomia mantaroana, commonly known as the Mantaro peperomia, is a species native to the cloud forests of the Peruvian Andes.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining, rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
