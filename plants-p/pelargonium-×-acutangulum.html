Pelargonium × acutangulum

Pelargonium × acutangulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. Native to South Africa, this species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
