Peniocereus lazaro-cardenasii

Peniocereus lazaro-cardenasii, commonly known as the Lazaro-Cardenas night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique morphology and flowering behavior make it a notable species in botanical studies.
