Prunus mutabilis f. formosa

Prunus mutabilis f. formosa, commonly known as the pink-leafed cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, variable flower color that ranges from white to pink and even red, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves of this variety are typically a deep green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.
