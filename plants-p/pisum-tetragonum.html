Pisum tetragonum

Pisum tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, four-angled stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its pods contain several seeds, which are typically used as a food source.

Pisum tetragonum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
