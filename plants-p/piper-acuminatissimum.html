Piper acuminatissimum

Piper acuminatissimum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild long pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
