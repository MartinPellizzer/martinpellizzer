Passiflora foetida var. vitacea

Passiflora foetida var. vitacea, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with purple and white petals, sepals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellowish-orange berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
