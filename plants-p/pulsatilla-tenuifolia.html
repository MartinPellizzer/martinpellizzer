Pulsatilla tenuifolia

Pulsatilla tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and hairy, lobed leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with numerous stamens and a central stigma.

Pulsatilla tenuifolia is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in alpine or subalpine environments.
