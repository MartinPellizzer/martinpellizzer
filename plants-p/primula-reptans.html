Primula reptans

Primula reptans, commonly known as the creeping primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and pink, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, silky down.

Primula reptans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.
