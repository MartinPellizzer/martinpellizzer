Polygala hildebrandtii

Polygala hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
