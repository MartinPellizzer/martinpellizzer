Pyrus korshinskyi

Pyrus korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically used for making jams and jellies. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The plant is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
