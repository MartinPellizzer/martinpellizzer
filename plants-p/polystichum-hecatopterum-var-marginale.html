Polystichum hecatopterum var. marginale

Polystichum hecatopterum var. marginale, commonly known as the margined wood fern, is a variant of the wood fern genus native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds that are often adorned with a pale, wavy margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, with a leathery texture and a deep green color.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
