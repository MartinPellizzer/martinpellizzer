Poecilostachys capuronii

Poecilostachys capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
