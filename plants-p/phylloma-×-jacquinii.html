Phylloma × jacquinii

Phylloma × jacquinii, commonly known as the hybrid phylloma, is a unique plant resulting from the cross between two species of the Phylloma genus.

It exhibits characteristics of both parent species, often displaying a combination of leaf shapes and flower structures. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, while its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or orange.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
