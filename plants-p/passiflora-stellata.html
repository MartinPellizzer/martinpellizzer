Passiflora stellata

Passiflora stellata, commonly known as the star passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically white with a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, pulpy flesh. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are covered in fine, hairy structures.

Passiflora stellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
