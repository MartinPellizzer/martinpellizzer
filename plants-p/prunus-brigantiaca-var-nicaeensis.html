Prunus brigantiaca var. nicaeensis

Prunus brigantiaca var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean cherry, is a rare and ancient species native to the region of Nicaea in Turkey.

It is a small deciduous shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are yellow or orange in color, similar to cherries but with a distinct flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its historical significance and potential for cultivation in dry climates.
