Phanera dasycarpa var. ridleyi

Phanera dasycarpa var. ridleyi, commonly known as the ridge-fruited phanera, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and ecological interactions.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
