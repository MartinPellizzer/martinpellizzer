Pittosporum podocarpum var. molle

Pittosporum podocarpum var. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft down, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
