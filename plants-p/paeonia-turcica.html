Paeonia turcica

Paeonia turcica, commonly known as the Turkish peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Paeoniaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing nutrients and moisture.

Paeonia turcica is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional gardens.
