Polygala phrygia

Polygala phrygia, commonly known as the Greek milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features slender stems and small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Polygala phrygia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
