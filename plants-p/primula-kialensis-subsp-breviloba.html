Primula kialensis subsp. breviloba

Primula kialensis subsp.

breviloba, commonly known as the short-stalked primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers.

The leaves are typically oval and have a hairy texture, adapting to the cool, moist conditions of its habitat. This subspecies is part of the Primulaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
