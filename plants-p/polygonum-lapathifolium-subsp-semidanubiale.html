Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. semidanubiale

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

semidanubiale, commonly known as the Danube knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest edges.

The plant has slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

This subspecies is part of the Polygonaceae family, known for its characteristic bracts and medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
