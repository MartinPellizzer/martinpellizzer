Pinus caribaea

Pinus caribaea, commonly known as the Cuban pine, is a species of conifer native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a tall evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 40 meters, with a straight trunk and a conical crown. The tree produces long, slender needles in bundles of five, and its cones are woody and often hang downward. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
