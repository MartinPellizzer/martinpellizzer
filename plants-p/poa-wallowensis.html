Poa wallowensis

Poa wallowensis, commonly known as the Walloway grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with fine, soft leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

Poa wallowensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small mammals.
