Proclesia melastomoides

Proclesia melastomoides, commonly known as the Florida melastome, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in pine flatwoods and hammocks.
