Pedilonum purpureum

Pedilonum purpureum, commonly known as the purple foot orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, hairy roots and striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky slopes or in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, with a distinctive purplish hue at the base.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
