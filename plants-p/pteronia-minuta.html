Pteronia minuta

Pteronia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf pterony, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring.

Pteronia minuta is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
