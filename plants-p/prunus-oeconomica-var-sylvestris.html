Prunus oeconomica var. sylvestris

Prunus oeconomica var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry or sylvan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
