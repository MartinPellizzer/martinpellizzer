Pyrus cerasifera

Pyrus cerasifera, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland and scrub habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

The whitebeam is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for various wildlife species.
