Pimpinella apiifolia

Pimpinella apiifolia, commonly known as the European cowparsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often hollow stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences, typical of the carrot family. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Pimpinella apiifolia is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.
