Phytolacca esculenta

Phytolacca esculenta, commonly known as Chinese soapberry, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and alternate leaves that are broadly ovate with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, reddish-purple berries that contain toxic seeds. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

However, due to the toxicity of its berries and roots, it should be handled with care.
