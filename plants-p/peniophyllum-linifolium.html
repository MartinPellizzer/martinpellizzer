Peniophyllum linifolium

Peniophyllum linifolium, commonly known as the linear-leaf peniophyllum, is a rare and specialized lichen species that thrives in specific microhabitats.

It is characterized by its unique thallus structure, which consists of a delicate, crust-like growth that adheres to substrates such as bark or stone. The plant's morphology includes a distinct arrangement of photobionts and fungal hyphae, contributing to its ecological niche. It is typically found in humid, shaded environments, often in temperate forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
