Polygala sulphurea

Polygala sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Polygala genus. The plant grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous minute seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
