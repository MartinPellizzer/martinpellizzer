Phacelia nemoralis var. pseudohispida

Phacelia nemoralis var. pseudohispida, commonly known as the rough-hairy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its hairy foliage and showy flowers make it a distinctive and attractive wildflower.
