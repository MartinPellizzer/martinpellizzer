Potentilla peduncularis var. stenophylla

Potentilla peduncularis var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features narrow, elliptic leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months, which are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
