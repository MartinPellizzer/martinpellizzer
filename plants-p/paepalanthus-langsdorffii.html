Paepalanthus langsdorffii

Paepalanthus langsdorffii, commonly known as the langsdorff's pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, spiny, spherical stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the plant. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
