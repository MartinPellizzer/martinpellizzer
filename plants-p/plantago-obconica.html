Plantago obconica

Plantago obconica, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, oval, ribbed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is commonly used in herbal remedies.
