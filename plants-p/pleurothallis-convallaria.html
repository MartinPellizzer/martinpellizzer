Pleurothallis convallaria

Pleurothallis convallaria, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, delicate flowers that are often violet or white in color, with a subtle fragrance. The plant has thin, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that help it conserve water.

Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in diverse habitats make it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
