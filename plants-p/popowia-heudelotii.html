Popowia heudelotii

Popowia heudelotii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Popowia heudelotii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering habit.
