Pavonia ramboi

Pavonia ramboi, commonly known as the red billygoat weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its showy, often bright red or pink bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and sometimes hairy, while the stems are erect and branching.

Pavonia ramboi is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
