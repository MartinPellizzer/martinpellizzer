Pseudosasa parilis

Pseudosasa parilis, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-green plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Pseudosasa parilis is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
