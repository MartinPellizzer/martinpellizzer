Pelargonium ovifolium

Pelargonium ovifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
