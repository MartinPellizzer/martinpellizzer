Pandanus boryi

Pandanus boryi, commonly known as the Bory's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous leaves and branched, spiny inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The flowers are small and fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
