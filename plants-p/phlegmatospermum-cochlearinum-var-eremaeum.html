Phlegmatospermum cochlearinum var. eremaeum

Phlegmatospermum cochlearinum var. eremaeum, commonly known as the desert horned cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its harsh environment. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

This variety is well-adapted to extreme climates, making it a resilient and unique botanical specimen.
