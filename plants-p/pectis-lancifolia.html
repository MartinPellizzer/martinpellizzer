Pectis lancifolia

Pectis lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hence the species name, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Pectis lancifolia produces bright yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
