Pladera pusilla

Pladera pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf pladera, is a small, herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features tiny, clustered leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. Its roots are fibrous and help it anchor in the soil while absorbing nutrients.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
