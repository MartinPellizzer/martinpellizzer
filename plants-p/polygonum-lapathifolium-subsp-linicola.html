Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. linicola

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

linicola, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, streambanks, and marshy areas.

The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender growth habit and is often found in disturbed or wetland habitats.
