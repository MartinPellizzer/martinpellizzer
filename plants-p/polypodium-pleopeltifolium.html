Polypodium pleopeltifolium

Polypodium pleopeltifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, clustered growth habit and its distinctive, broad, leathery fronds. The plant typically features a short, thick rhizome with numerous ascending or descending stolons. Its fronds are usually deeply divided into lanceolate segments, often with a serrated or lobed margin.

Polypodium pleopeltifolium is commonly found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
