Prangos didyma

Prangos didyma, commonly known as the Persian shield, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, shield-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom throughout the summer months. Prangos didyma thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in temperate climates.
