Panicum araneosum

Panicum araneosum, commonly known as silverhair panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble a spider's web, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Panicum araneosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
