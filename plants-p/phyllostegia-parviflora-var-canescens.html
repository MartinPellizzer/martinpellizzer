Phyllostegia parviflora var. canescens

Phyllostegia parviflora var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy phyllostegia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and moist thickets.
