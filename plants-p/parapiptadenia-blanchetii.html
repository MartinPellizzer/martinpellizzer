Parapiptadenia blanchetii

Parapiptadenia blanchetii, commonly known as the yellow jacaranda, is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
