Pachypodium rutenbergianum

Pachypodium rutenbergianum, commonly known as the Rutenberg's thick-stemmed pachypodium, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant has sparse, spiny foliage and produces yellowish-white flowers during the wet season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
