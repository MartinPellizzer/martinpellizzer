Pavetta pleiantha var. velutina

Pavetta pleiantha var. velutina, commonly known as velvet pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in subtropical gardens.
