Podocarpus chilinus

Podocarpus chilinus, commonly known as the Chilean podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and conical shape. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, a feature typical of many conifers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is sometimes cultivated as a landscape plant.
