Paurotis

Paurotis, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer, followed by fluffy, seed-filled spheres. Paurotis typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
