Panicum hochstetteri var. glaberrimum

Panicum hochstetteri var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the glabrous panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairless or nearly so. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control in arid environments.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
