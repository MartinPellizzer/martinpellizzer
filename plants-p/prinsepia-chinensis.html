Prinsepia chinensis

Prinsepia chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a sour taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Prinsepia chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
