Polypogon hackelii

Polypogon hackelii, commonly known as Hackel's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

Polypogon hackelii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
