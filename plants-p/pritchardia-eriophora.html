Pritchardia eriophora

Pritchardia eriophora, commonly known as the Maui pineapple plant, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Hawaii.

It is a small, slow-growing palm characterized by its clustered, spine-tipped leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate of the Hawaiian Islands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
