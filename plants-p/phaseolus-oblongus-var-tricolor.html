Phaseolus oblongus var. tricolor

Phaseolus oblongus var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking tricolor foliage, which typically displays shades of green, white, and purple. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that are often harvested when young and tender. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
