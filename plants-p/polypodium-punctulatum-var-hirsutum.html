Polypodium punctulatum var. hirsutum

Polypodium punctulatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy rock fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, hairy fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows on rocks or in shaded, moist environments, thriving in humid climates. Its fronds are usually lance-shaped with a serrated margin, and the sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various rocky substrates.
