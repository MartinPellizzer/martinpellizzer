Piper melanostictum

Piper melanostictum, commonly known as the black-spotted pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by dark purple to black berries, which are the source of its common name.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
