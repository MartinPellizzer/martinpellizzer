Parodia yamparaezii

Parodia yamparaezii, commonly known as the Yamparaezii Candelabra Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that branches into multiple arms, resembling a candlestick. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the arms. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Parodia yamparaezii is relatively slow-growing and requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
