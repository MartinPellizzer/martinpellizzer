Passiflora arizonica

Passiflora arizonica, commonly known as Arizona passionflower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are favored by birds and small mammals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and a unique addition to native gardens.
