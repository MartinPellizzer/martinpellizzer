Pentisea deformis

Pentisea deformis, commonly known as the twisted pentisea, is a rare and unusual plant species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiraling growth pattern and unique leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pentisea deformis is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of interest for conservationists.
