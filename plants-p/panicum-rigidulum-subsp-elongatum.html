Panicum rigidulum subsp. elongatum

Panicum rigidulum subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as tall panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
