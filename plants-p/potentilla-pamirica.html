Potentilla pamirica

Potentilla pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Pamir Mountains.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla pamirica is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
