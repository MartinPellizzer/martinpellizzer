Pleurothallis pertusa

Pleurothallis pertusa, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, elongated flower spikes. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers with intricate veining, which are often clustered along the inflorescence. Pleurothallis pertusa typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
