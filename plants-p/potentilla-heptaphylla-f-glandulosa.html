Potentilla heptaphylla f. glandulosa

Potentilla heptaphylla f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces small, rounded fruits known as achenes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
