Phaseolus vulgaris var. xanthozebra

Phaseolus vulgaris var. xanthozebra, commonly known as the yellow zebra bean, is a variant of the common bean species characterized by its distinctive yellow and green striped pods.

The plant is an annual legume that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring trifoliate leaves and small white or purple flowers. Its pods are elongated and contain 4–6 kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in culinary applications.

The yellow zebra bean is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its nutritional content, making it a popular choice in both gardens and agricultural settings.
