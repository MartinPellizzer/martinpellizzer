Pithecellobium ligustrinum

Pithecellobium ligustrinum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
