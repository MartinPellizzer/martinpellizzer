Pilosella cymosiformis subsp. francojurassica

Pilosella cymosiformis subsp.

francojurassica, commonly known as the Franco-Jurassica pearly weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically hairy and arranged in a cymose pattern, giving the plant its specific epithet. It is native to regions of Central and Eastern Europe, including parts of France and Germany.

This subspecies is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
