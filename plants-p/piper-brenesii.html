Piper brenesii

Piper brenesii, commonly known as the Brena pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are typically used in local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
