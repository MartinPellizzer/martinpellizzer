Persea psychotrioides

Persea psychotrioides, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The fruit, which resembles a small persimmon, is edible and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
