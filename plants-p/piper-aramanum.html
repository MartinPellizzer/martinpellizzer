Piper aramanum

Piper aramanum, commonly known as the Aramanum pepper or the Malabar pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit, a small, dark red berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Piper aramanum is valued for its pungent flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
