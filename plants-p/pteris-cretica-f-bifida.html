Pteris cretica f. bifida

Pteris cretica f. bifida, commonly known as the crested water fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, bifid (split) pinnae.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features fronds that are typically arching or upright, with a dark green, leathery texture. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sori, which are clustered in groups called sorus.

This fern is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
