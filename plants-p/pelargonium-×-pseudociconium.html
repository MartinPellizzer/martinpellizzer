Pelargonium × pseudociconium

Pelargonium × pseudociconium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
