Portulaca hereroensis

Portulaca hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero carpetweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Portulaca hereroensis is often found in rocky areas and along paths, where it forms dense mats.
