Potentilla multifida var. agrimonioides

Potentilla multifida var. agrimonioides, commonly known as the agrimony cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually divided into five to nine leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
