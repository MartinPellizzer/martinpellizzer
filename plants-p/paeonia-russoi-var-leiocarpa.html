Paeonia russoi var. leiocarpa

Paeonia russoi var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-sepaled peony, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with a serrated margin and a woody root system typical of the peony genus.

Its flowers can be pink, white, or red, depending on the specific cultivar. This variety is valued for its horticultural beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
