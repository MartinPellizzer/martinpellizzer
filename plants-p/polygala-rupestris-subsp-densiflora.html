Polygala rupestris subsp. densiflora

Polygala rupestris subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features slender, ascending stems with small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
