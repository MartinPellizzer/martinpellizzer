Passerina laniflora

Passerina laniflora, commonly known as the woolly passiflora, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant produces small, orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Passerina laniflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
