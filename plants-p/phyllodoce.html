Phyllodoce

Phyllodoce, commonly known as mountain heather, is a low-growing shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and produces bell-shaped, often pink or white flowers. This plant is typically found in moist, acidic soils in forested areas, particularly in mountainous regions. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Phyllodoce is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and natural landscapes.
