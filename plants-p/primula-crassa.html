Primula crassa

Primula crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
