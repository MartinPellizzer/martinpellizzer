Phacelia hirsuta f. albiflora

Phacelia hirsuta f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower phacelia, is a variant of the hairy phacelia, characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense racemes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
