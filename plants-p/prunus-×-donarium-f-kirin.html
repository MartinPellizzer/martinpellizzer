Prunus × donarium f. kirin

Prunus × donarium f. kirin, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yoshino cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 8–12 meters and has a rounded, spreading crown. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This cultivar is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
