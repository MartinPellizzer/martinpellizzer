Pyrus heracleotica

Pyrus heracleotica, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in spring. The tree has dark green, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its fruit is a small, round pome that ripens in late summer. It is often found in woodlands and on rocky soils, and it is relatively hardy, tolerating a range of environmental conditions.

The common whitebeam is also valued for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
