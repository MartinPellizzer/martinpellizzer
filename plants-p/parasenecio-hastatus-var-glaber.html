Parasenecio hastatus var. glaber

Parasenecio hastatus var. glaber, commonly known as the smoothleaf parasenecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, with a prominent central vein.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the cooler months.
