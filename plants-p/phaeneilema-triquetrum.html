Phaeneilema triquetrum

Phaeneilema triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa, characterized by its distinctive triangular stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

Phaeneilema triquetrum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and soil-stabilizing properties.
