Panicum minarum

Panicum minarum, commonly known as the dwarf panicgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically reaches heights of less than 30 centimeters and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and delicate, fluffy seed heads. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is often found in open woodlands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
