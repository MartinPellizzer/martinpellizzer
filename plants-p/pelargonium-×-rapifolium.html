Pelargonium × rapifolium

Pelargonium × rapifolium, commonly known as the root geranium, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to South Africa and is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Pelargonium × rapifolium is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
