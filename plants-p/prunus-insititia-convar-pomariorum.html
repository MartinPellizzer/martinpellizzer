Prunus insititia convar. pomariorum

Prunus insititia convar. pomariorum, commonly known as the common gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while the flowers are white with five petals. The plant is valued for its edible berries, which are rich in vitamin C and used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.
