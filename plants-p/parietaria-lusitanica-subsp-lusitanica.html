Parietaria lusitanica subsp. lusitanica

Parietaria lusitanica subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian wallweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils, often found along walls, roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a distinctive pubescent underside. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are greenish-white and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is adapted to arid conditions and has a widespread distribution across the Iberian Peninsula.
