Picea abies f. compacta

Picea abies f. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a compact form of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and short, twisted branches. The leaves are typically dark green, with a glossy texture, and they remain on the plant for several years. This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes due to its attractive appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a range of growing conditions.
