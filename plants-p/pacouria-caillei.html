Pacouria caillei

Pacouria caillei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, releasing a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Pacouria caillei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
