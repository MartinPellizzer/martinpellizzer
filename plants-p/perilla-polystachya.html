Perilla polystachya

Perilla polystachya, commonly known as Chinese mint or wild basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and cooking for their pungent flavor.

Perilla polystachya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
