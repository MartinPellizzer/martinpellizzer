Phaseolus vulgaris var. carneus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. carneus, commonly known as the pink bean, is a variety of common bean characterized by its distinctive pink to reddish-purple pod and seed color.

It is an annual legume native to the Americas, typically grown for its edible seeds. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate structures and produces flowers that are usually white or pale pink. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

The variety is notable for its unique coloration, which sets it apart from other common bean types.
