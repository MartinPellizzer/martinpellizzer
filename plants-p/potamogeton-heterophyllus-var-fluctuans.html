Potamogeton heterophyllus var. fluctuans

Potamogeton heterophyllus var. fluctuans, commonly known as the floating pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its flexible, branching stems that often float on the water surface. The plant exhibits variable leaf morphology, with leaves that are filamentous or strap-like depending on light and water conditions. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water quality and navigation.
