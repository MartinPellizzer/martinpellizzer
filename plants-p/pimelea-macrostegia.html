Pimelea macrostegia

Pimelea macrostegia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rice flower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in winter and spring. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to the dry conditions of its habitat.

Pimelea macrostegia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
