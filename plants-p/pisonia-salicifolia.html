Pisonia salicifolia

Pisonia salicifolia, commonly known as the silky pisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which are borne in clusters. The leaves are ovate and have a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual display.
