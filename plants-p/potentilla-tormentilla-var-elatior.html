Potentilla tormentilla var. elatior

Potentilla tormentilla var. elatior, commonly known as the tall cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy texture, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its hardiness and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
