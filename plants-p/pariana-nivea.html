Pariana nivea

Pariana nivea, commonly known as the white pariana, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white blossoms that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks or in dense forests. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
