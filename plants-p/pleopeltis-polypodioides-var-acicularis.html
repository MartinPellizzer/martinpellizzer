Pleopeltis polypodioides var. acicularis

Pleopeltis polypodioides var. acicularis, commonly known as the acicular pleopeltis, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like fronds that are typically arranged in a loose, weeping habit. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces both sterile and fertile shoots. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

This variety is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
