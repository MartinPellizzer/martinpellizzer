Polygala hamarensis

Polygala hamarensis, commonly known as the Hamar polygala, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spindly stems and clusters of small, often pink or white, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations above 2,000 meters. It is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat, exhibiting drought resistance and a short growing season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
