Pisum sativum var. viridipunctulum

Pisum sativum var. viridipunctulum, commonly known as the green-speckled pea, is a variety of the common pea plant characterized by its unique foliage.

Its leaves are typically green with distinct yellow or green speckles, giving it a striking appearance. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive leaf patterns. It shares the same basic botanical structure as other pea species, including tendrils, pods, and edible seeds.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
