Prunus × umbellifera

Prunus × umbellifera, commonly known as the European bird cherry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a distinctive umbellate appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are favored by birds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Prunus × umbellifera is often cultivated for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
