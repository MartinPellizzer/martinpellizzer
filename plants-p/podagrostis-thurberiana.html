Podagrostis thurberiana

Podagrostis thurberiana, commonly known as Thurber's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

Thurber's sedge plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
