Peperomia andrei

Peperomia andrei, commonly known as the Andean peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the cloud forests of South America, particularly in regions of Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia andrei is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
