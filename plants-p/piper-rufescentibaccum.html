Piper rufescentibaccum

Piper rufescentibaccum, commonly known as the red-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into small, round berries.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
