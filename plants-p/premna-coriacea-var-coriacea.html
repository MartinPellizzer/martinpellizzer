Premna coriacea var. coriacea

Premna coriacea var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough premna, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, often hairy stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
