Picea heterolepis

Picea heterolepis, commonly known as the sugar pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to western North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive, often drooping cones. The tree can reach heights of up to 100 meters, making it one of the tallest pine species. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against wildfires.

The sugar pine is an important species in both ecological and economic contexts, valued for its timber and role in forest ecosystems.
