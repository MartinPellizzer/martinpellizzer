Pourthiaea villosa var. brunnea

Pourthiaea villosa var. brunnea, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
