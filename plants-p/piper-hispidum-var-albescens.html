Piper hispidum var. albescens

Piper hispidum var. albescens, commonly known as the white-hairy pepper, is a variant of the Piper hispidum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into berries. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a woody stem and can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The fruit is edible and has a mild, peppery flavor, making it a unique addition to culinary applications.
