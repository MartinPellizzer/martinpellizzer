Prestoea acuminata

Prestoea acuminata, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Prestoea acuminata is valued for its durable wood and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
