Pilosella iserana subsp. florida

Pilosella iserana subsp.

florida, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellowish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open mountainous areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
