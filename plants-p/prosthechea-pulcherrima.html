Prosthechea pulcherrima

Prosthechea pulcherrima, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, fleshy leaf and produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant blooms during the holiday season, typically from November to January, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining soil.

Prosthechea pulcherrima is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
