Panicum unguiculatum

Panicum unguiculatum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
