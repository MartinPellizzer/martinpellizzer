Primula farinosa subsp. magellanica

Primula farinosa subsp.

magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan Primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern South America. It features basal rosettes of hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Primulaceae family.
