Paepalanthus glaucophyllus

Paepalanthus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its adaptations to harsh environments include reduced leaves and a highly efficient water storage system.

Paepalanthus glaucophyllus plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various desert wildlife.
