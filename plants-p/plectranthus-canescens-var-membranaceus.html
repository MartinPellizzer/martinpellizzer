Plectranthus canescens var. membranaceus

Plectranthus canescens var. membranaceus, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
