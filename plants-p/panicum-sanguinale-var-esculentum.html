Panicum sanguinale var. esculentum

Panicum sanguinale var. esculentum, commonly known as red millet, is a variant of the species Panicum sanguinale, which is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a grass species characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, red-tinged inflorescences. The plant is typically cultivated for its edible grains, which are rich in carbohydrates and have a nutty flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often grown in arid and semi-arid regions as a valuable food crop.
