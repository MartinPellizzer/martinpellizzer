Phyteuma persicifolium var. albiflorum

Phyteuma persicifolium var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
