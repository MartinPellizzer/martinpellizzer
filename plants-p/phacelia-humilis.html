Phacelia humilis

Phacelia humilis, commonly known as the common phacelia, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phacelia humilis is valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in ecological restoration projects.
