Pourouma subtriloba

Pourouma subtriloba, commonly known as the giant areca palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its trunk is typically smooth and can grow to heights exceeding 10 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
