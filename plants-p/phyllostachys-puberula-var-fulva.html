Phyllostachys puberula var. fulva

Phyllostachys puberula var. fulva, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms.

It is a fast-growing perennial grass native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, hollow stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant color and elegant growth habit.
