Paronychia communis var. pungentifolia

Paronychia communis var. pungentifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are characterized by their sharp, pointed tips, which give the species its common name.

It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.
