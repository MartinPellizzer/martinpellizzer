Platystele microtatantha

Platystele microtatantha, commonly known as the miniature tapanuli orchid, is a rare and endangered species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has a short, compact stem with few leaves and produces a single spike of flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to survive, making it highly sensitive to habitat disruption.

Due to its limited distribution and threats from deforestation, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
