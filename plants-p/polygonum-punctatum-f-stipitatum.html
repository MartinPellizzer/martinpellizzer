Polygonum punctatum f. stipitatum

Polygonum punctatum f. stipitatum, commonly known as the stipulate knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum punctatum, characterized by its distinctive stipules.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine, woolly layer of hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent central vein and a serrated margin.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer.
