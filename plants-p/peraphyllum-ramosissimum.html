Peraphyllum ramosissimum

Peraphyllum ramosissimum, commonly known as the branched pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, branched stems and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, and is often found in disturbed areas.

Peraphyllum ramosissimum is known for its long-lasting flowers, which make it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.
