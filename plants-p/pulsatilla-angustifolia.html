Pulsatilla angustifolia

Pulsatilla angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers with numerous stamens. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
