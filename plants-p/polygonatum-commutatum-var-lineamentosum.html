Polygonatum commutatum var. lineamentosum

Polygonatum commutatum var. lineamentosum, commonly known as the veined Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with distinct veins. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer, followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
