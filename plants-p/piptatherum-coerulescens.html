Piptatherum coerulescens

Piptatherum coerulescens, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, prairies, and woodland edges. Its seed heads are small and spike-like, contributing to its ornamental value.
