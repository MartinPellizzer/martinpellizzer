Podocarpus horsfieldii

Podocarpus horsfieldii, commonly known as the Horsfield's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, rounded leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant also plays a role in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.
