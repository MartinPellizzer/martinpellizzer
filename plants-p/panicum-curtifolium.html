Panicum curtifolium

Panicum curtifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Panicum curtifolium is generally drought-tolerant and can grow in a variety of soil types.
