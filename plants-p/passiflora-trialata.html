Passiflora trialata

Passiflora trialata, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are velvety and dark green, with a distinctive, intricate venation pattern.

Passiflora trialata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.
