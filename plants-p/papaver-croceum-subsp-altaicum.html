Papaver croceum subsp. altaicum

Papaver croceum subsp.

altaicum, commonly known as the Altai poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central disc, typical of the Papaveraceae family.

The plant has smooth, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it is also valued for its historical and potential medicinal uses.
