Paspalum jimenezii

Paspalum jimenezii, commonly known as Jimenez's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, riverbanks, and seasonally flooded areas. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers, which are usually green to reddish in color.

Paspalum jimenezii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
