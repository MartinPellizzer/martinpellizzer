Pharbitis nil var. paichou

Pharbitis nil var. paichou, commonly known as the Chinese morning glory, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. It produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and trellises.
