Phaseolus vulgaris var. rachelianus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. rachelianus, commonly known as the Rachelian bean, is a variety of common bean native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, elongated pods and yellow to brown seeds, which are typically harvested when mature. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and is often cultivated for its edible beans.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it a useful crop in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
