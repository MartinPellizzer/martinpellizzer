Pleurothallis hygrophila var. longicaulis

Pleurothallis hygrophila var. longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed slipper orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and small, delicate flowers that often resemble miniature slipper-shaped blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the humid air. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and adaptability to shaded environments.
