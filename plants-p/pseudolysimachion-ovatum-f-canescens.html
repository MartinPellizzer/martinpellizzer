Pseudolysimachion ovatum f. canescens

Pseudolysimachion ovatum f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy ovate pseudolysimachion, is a variant of the ovate pseudolysimachion, a flowering plant native to specific regions of Asia.

This particular form is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, foliage, which distinguishes it from the typical form of the species. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value.

The hairy texture of its leaves is an adaptation that may aid in water retention and protection against herbivory.
