Papaver dubium var. luteorubrum

Papaver dubium var. luteorubrum, commonly known as the red-and-yellow poppy, is a variant of the common poppy native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red petals that transition to yellow at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and showy flowers.
