Picea jezoensis var. hondoensis

Picea jezoensis var. hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo spruce, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Japanese island of Hokkaido.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
