Probatea

Probatea, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, where it thrives in grasslands and rocky areas. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. Probatea species are perennial herbs with strap-shaped leaves and produce small, ornamental seed heads.
