Phlox depressa

Phlox depressa, commonly known as creeping phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters and features dense clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Phlox depressa thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky areas, meadows, and woodland edges.
