Pelargonium × gangrenosum

Pelargonium × gangrenosum, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink to red flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
