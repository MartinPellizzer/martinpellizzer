Parodia amambayensis

Parodia amambayensis, commonly known as the Amambay cactus, is a species of cactus native to Paraguay.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or gray spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
