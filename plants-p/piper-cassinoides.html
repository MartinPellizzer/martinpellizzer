Piper cassinoides

Piper cassinoides, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Piper cassinoides is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its aromatic properties.
