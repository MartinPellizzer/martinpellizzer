Primula denticulata subsp. sinodenticulata

Primula denticulata subsp.

sinodenticulata, commonly known as the Chinese primrose or the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves, and are pollinated by insects.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden conditions.
