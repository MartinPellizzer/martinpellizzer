Passiflora caatingae

Passiflora caatingae, commonly known as the caatinga passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

It thrives in dry, tropical environments and is often found in the caatinga biome.
