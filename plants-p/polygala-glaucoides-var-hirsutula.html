Polygala glaucoides var. hirsutula

Polygala glaucoides var. hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
