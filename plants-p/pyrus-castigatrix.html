Pyrus castigatrix

Pyrus castigatrix, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Europe and western Asia, typically growing in woodland and along stream banks. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, followed by edible, subglobose fruits that are typically yellow or reddish in color.

The tree is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
