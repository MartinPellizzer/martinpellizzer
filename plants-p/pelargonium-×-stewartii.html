Pelargonium × stewartii

Pelargonium × stewartii, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, slightly waxy leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
