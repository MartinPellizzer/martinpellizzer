Piper endlicherianum

Piper endlicherianum, commonly known as the Endlicher's pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
