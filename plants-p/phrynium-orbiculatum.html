Phrynium orbiculatum

Phrynium orbiculatum, commonly known as the round-leaf elephant's ear, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 60 cm in diameter, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its striking foliage, it is popular as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
