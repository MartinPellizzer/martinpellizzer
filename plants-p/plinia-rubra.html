Plinia rubra

Plinia rubra, commonly known as the red-fruited guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a fragrant aroma.

Plinia rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
