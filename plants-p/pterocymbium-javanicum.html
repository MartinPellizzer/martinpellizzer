Pterocymbium javanicum

Pterocymbium javanicum, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant yellow or orange color with intricate patterns. The plant has a slender, erect stem with several fleshy, elliptical leaves arranged in a rosette formation. Its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
