Prasophyllum tepperi

Prasophyllum tepperi, commonly known as the pink leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features a basal rosette of strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces a spike of pink or purple flowers. It is a perennial species that blooms during the summer months.

Prasophyllum tepperi is part of the orchid family Orchidaceae and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
