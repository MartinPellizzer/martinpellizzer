Piper ladrillense

Piper ladrillense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland forests.
