Parsonsia javanica

Parsonsia javanica, commonly known as the Javan flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Parsonsia javanica is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
