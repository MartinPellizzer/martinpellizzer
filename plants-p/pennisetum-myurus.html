Pennisetum myurus

Pennisetum myurus, commonly known as purple top grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinted foliage and tall, slender stalks that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Pennisetum myurus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract birds and other wildlife.
