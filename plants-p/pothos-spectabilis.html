Pothos spectabilis

Pothos spectabilis, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing or trailing plant characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a yellow or variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries, though these are rarely seen in cultivation. It thrives in low-light conditions and is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and ornamental value.

Pothos spectabilis is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a favored choice for improving air quality in homes.
