Psidium globosum

Psidium globosum, commonly known as the common guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It produces fragrant, white flowers that develop into fleshy, aromatic fruits with a variable number of seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
