Platyopuntia dumetorum

Platyopuntia dumetorum, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a blue or grayish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The plant is well adapted to desert conditions, with a shallow root system that quickly absorbs rainwater.
