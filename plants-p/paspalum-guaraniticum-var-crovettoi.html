Paspalum guaraniticum var. crovettoi

Paspalum guaraniticum var. crovettoi, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or crovetto grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
