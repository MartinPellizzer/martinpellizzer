Porophyllum confertum var. ochroleucum

Porophyllum confertum var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the white-rayed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers consist of blue to purple ray florets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
