Piper wrightii

Piper wrightii, commonly known as Wright's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Piper wrightii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
