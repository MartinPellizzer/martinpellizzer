Pharmacum calycinum

Pharmacum calycinum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its botanical classification places it in the Campanulaceae family, known for its ornamental and medicinal value.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
