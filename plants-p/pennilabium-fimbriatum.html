Pennilabium fimbriatum

Pennilabium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed lip orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers with distinctive fringed petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Pennilabium fimbriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
