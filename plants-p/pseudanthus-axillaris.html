Pseudanthus axillaris

Pseudanthus axillaris, commonly known as the axillary pseudanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Pseudanthus axillaris is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
