Panicum speciosum var. poeppigii

Panicum speciosum var. poeppigii, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that give it a distinctive, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, adding visual interest to landscapes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is known for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
