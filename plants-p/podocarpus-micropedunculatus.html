Podocarpus micropedunculatus

Podocarpus micropedunculatus, commonly known as the dwarf podocarp, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
