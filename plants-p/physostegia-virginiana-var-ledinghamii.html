Physostegia virginiana var. ledinghamii

Physostegia virginiana var. ledinghamii, commonly known as the purple false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.
