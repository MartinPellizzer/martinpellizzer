Pentzia incana

Pentzia incana, commonly known as the silver bush, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pale, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
