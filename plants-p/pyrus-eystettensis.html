Pyrus eystettensis

Pyrus eystettensis, commonly known as the Eystetten pear, is a small, deciduous tree native to the Pyrenees region of Europe.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet, and juicy pears in late summer. The fruit has a thin, yellowish skin and a rich, aromatic flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
