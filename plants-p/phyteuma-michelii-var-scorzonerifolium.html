Phyteuma michelii var. scorzonerifolium

Phyteuma michelii var. scorzonerifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyteuma, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
