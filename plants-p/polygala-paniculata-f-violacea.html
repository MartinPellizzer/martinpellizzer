Polygala paniculata f. violacea

Polygala paniculata f. violacea, commonly known as the violet panicled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-hued inflorescences that resemble small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are slender and branching.
