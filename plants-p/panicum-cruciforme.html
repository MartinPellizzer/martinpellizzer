Panicum cruciforme

Panicum cruciforme, commonly known as the crossleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves and distinctive cross-shaped nodes on its stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often arranged in panicles, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense clumps make it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
