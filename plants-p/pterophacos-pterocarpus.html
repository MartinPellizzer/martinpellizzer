Pterophacos pterocarpus

Pterophacos pterocarpus, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume containing several seeds, and the plant is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a rare and precious resource in the timber trade.
