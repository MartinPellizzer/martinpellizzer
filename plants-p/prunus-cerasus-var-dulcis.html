Prunus cerasus var. dulcis

Prunus cerasus var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by the familiar bright red or yellow fruits. The fruits are typically sweet and juicy, with a thin skin and a firm, crisp flesh.

This variety is widely cultivated for its culinary and ornamental value.
