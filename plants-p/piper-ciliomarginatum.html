Piper ciliomarginatum

Piper ciliomarginatum, commonly known as the hairy-leaf pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with hairy margins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by oval, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
