Pandanus pulposus

Pandanus pulposus, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while its fruit is edible.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
