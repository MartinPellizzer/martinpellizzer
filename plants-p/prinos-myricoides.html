Prinos myricoides

Prinos myricoides, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved prinos, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and myrtle. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, dark blue berries.

Prinos myricoides is typically found in coastal and sandy habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
