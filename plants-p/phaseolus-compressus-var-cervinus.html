Phaseolus compressus var. cervinus

Phaseolus compressus var. cervinus, commonly known as the hairy kidney bean, is a variety of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped pods covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its role in agricultural systems.
