Polygala amara subsp. alpina

Polygala amara subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
