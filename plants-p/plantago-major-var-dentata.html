Plantago major var. dentata

Plantago major var. dentata, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with wavy or toothed margins, which give it its specific epithet "dentata.". The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in lawns, gardens, and disturbed areas.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
