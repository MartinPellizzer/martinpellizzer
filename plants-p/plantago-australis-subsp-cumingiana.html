Plantago australis subsp. cumingiana

Plantago australis subsp.

cumingiana, commonly known as the New Zealand plantain, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikes, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects.
