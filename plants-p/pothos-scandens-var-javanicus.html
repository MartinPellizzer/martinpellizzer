Pothos scandens var. javanicus

Pothos scandens var. javanicus, commonly known as the climbing pothos, is a vigorous vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by yellowish berries, though these are rarely seen in cultivation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth and air-purifying qualities make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
