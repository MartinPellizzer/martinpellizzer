Pyrus jacquemontiana

Pyrus jacquemontiana, commonly known as the Jacquemont's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia, including parts of China, Korea, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus jacquemontiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in horticultural breeding programs.
