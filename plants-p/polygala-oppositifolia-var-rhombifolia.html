Polygala oppositifolia var. rhombifolia

Polygala oppositifolia var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically rhombic in shape. The plant produces delicate, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
