Phaseolus luteus

Phaseolus luteus, commonly known as the yellow bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its yellow flowers and pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with three leaflets. Its root system fixes nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop for sustainable agriculture.

The seeds are rich in protein and are used in various traditional dishes across its native regions.
