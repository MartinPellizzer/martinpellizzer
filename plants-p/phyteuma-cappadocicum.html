Phyteuma cappadocicum

Phyteuma cappadocicum, commonly known as the Cappadocian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Phyteuma cappadocicum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
