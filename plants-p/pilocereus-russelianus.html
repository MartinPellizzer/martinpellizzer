Pilocereus russelianus

Pilocereus russelianus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The flowers typically emit a sweet fragrance, enhancing its appeal during the evening hours.
