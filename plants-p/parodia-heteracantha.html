Parodia heteracantha

Parodia heteracantha, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
