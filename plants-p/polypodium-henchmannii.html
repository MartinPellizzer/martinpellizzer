Polypodium henchmannii

Polypodium henchmannii, commonly known as the Henschmann's wall-rue, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a long, narrow stalk and bipinnate leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern. Its spores are produced in sporangia located on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of the Polypodiaceae family.

Polypodium henchmannii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
