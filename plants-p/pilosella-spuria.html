Pilosella spuria

Pilosella spuria, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and its flowers are usually yellow or white.

Pilosella spuria is often found in open habitats and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
