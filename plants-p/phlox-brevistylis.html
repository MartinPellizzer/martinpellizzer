Phlox brevistylis

Phlox brevistylis, commonly known as the short-styled phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
