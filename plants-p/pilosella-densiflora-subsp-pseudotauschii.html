Pilosella densiflora subsp. pseudotauschii

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

pseudotauschii, commonly known as the denseflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features a basal rosette of leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
