Potentilla eversmanniana

Potentilla eversmanniana, commonly known as the Eversmann's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a rough, hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid climates.

Potentilla eversmanniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
