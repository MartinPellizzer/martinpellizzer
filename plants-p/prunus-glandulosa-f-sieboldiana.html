Prunus glandulosa f. sieboldiana

Prunus glandulosa f. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Siebold's glandular cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus glandulosa species.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which are a key feature in its taxonomic classification. The plant typically exhibits small, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. Its fruits are small drupes that mature to a dark red or black color, often containing a single seed.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
