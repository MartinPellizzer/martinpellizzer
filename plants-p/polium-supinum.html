Polium supinum

Polium supinum, commonly known as the prostrate dock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are elongated and have a distinctive wavy margin, while the small, greenish flowers appear in clusters.

Polium supinum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.
