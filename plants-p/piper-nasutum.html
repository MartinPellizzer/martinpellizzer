Piper nasutum

Piper nasutum, commonly known as the long-stalked pepper plant, is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, thin fruits that contain aromatic seeds.

Piper nasutum is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
