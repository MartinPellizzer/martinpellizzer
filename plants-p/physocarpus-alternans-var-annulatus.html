Physocarpus alternans var. annulatus

Physocarpus alternans var. annulatus, commonly known as the ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its attractive, peeling bark that reveals layers of different colors, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early summer, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
