Pulmonaria × notha

Pulmonaria × notha, commonly known as the common lungwort, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, hairy leaves that are often spotted with silver or white. The plant produces showy clusters of blue or purple flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Pulmonaria × notha is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early bloom.
