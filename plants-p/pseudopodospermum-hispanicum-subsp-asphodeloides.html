Pseudopodospermum hispanicum subsp. asphodeloides

Pseudopodospermum hispanicum subsp.

asphodeloides, commonly known as the Spanish asphodel, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with fleshy, sword-shaped leaves.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white or yellowish flowers. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and minimal water.
