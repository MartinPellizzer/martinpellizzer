Pelargonium patersonii

Pelargonium patersonii, commonly known as the Paterson's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often grown as an ornamental in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium patersonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
