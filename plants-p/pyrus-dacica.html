Pyrus dacica

Pyrus dacica, commonly known as the Caucasian pear, is a species of tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible pears that are typically small to medium in size, with a sweet and sometimes aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Pyrus dacica is relatively hardy and can adapt to a variety of soil types, making it a versatile plant in horticulture.
