Piper multiforme

Piper multiforme, commonly known as the variable pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Piper multiforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
