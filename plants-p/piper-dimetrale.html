Piper dimetrale

Piper dimetrale, commonly known as the wild pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and small, white flowers clustered in spikes.

Piper dimetrale is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
