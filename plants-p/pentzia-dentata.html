Pentzia dentata

Pentzia dentata, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Pentzia dentata is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
