Prescottia lindeniana

Prescottia lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindenberg's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic soils of its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.
