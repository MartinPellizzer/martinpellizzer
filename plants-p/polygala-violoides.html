Polygala violoides

Polygala violoides, commonly known as the violet morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often violet or blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, with slender stems and small, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
