Paronychia incana

Paronychia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf goosefoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, white hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters.

Paronychia incana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
