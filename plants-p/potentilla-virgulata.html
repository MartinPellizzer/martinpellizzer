Potentilla virgulata

Potentilla virgulata, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its leaves are palmately divided into five oval lobes, and it produces small, aggregate fruits that are dispersed by wind.
