Phyllanthus umbratus

Phyllanthus umbratus, commonly known as the dark phyllanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
