Pleurothallis kennedyi

Pleurothallis kennedyi, commonly known as Kennedy's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
