Pieris japonica var. yakushimensis

Pieris japonica var. yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimensis Japanese pieris, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features narrow, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by ornamental, dark red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, offering year-round visual interest.
