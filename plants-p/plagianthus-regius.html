Plagianthus regius

Plagianthus regius, commonly known as the king protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and often pink or red flower heads, which consist of numerous small, petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Eastern and Western Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.

Plagianthus regius is a valuable ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and is also recognized for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
