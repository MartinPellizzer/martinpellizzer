Polygala caespitosa

Polygala caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, often pink or white, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with the flowers appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.
