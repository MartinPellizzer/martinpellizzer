Pteris talamauana

Pteris talamauana, commonly known as the Talamauana water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bioluminescent in certain conditions. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it produces spores rather than seeds.

Pteris talamauana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
