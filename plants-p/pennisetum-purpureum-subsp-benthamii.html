Pennisetum purpureum subsp. benthamii

Pennisetum purpureum subsp.

benthamii, commonly known as giant pennisetum, is a tall, perennial grass native to Africa. It is characterized by its coarse, dark green foliage and striking purple-tinted inflorescences.

This subspecies is highly valued for its robust growth and high biomass yield, making it suitable for forage and bioenergy production. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in agroforestry systems.

Its adaptability to various soil types and resistance to drought contribute to its ecological and agricultural significance.
