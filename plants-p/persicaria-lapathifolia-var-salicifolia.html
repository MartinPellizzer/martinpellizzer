Persicaria lapathifolia var. salicifolia

Persicaria lapathifolia var. salicifolia, commonly known as silky filaree, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
