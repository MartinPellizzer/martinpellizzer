Piptadenia glandulifera

Piptadenia glandulifera, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces long, slender pods containing flat, seed-rich seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
