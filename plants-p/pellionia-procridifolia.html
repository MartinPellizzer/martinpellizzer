Pellionia procridifolia

Pellionia procridifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf pellionia, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often dark green with a soft texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arums. Pellionia procridifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
