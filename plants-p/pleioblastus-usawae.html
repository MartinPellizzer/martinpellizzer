Pleioblastus usawae

Pleioblastus usawae, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its slender, upright stems form dense clumps, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

Pleioblastus usawae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
