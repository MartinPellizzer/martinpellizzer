Packera anonyma

Packera anonyma, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a ghostly appearance.

The flowers are small and yellow, appearing in late summer, and the plant is known for its delicate, ephemeral beauty.
