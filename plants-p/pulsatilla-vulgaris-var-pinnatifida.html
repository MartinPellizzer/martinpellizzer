Pulsatilla vulgaris var. pinnatifida

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
