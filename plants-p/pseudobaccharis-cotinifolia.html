Pseudobaccharis cotinifolia

Pseudobaccharis cotinifolia, commonly known as Brazilian flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

Pseudobaccharis cotinifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in full sun and its drought tolerance.
