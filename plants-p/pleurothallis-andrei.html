Pleurothallis andrei

Pleurothallis andrei, commonly known as the Andean pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is part of the diverse Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
