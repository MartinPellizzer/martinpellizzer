Pyrus armud

Pyrus armud, commonly known as the Armenian white pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated edge. The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisines.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
