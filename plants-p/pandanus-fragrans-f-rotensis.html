Pandanus fragrans f. rotensis

Pandanus fragrans f. rotensis, commonly known as the Red Pandanus, is a variant of the fragrant pandanus species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-tipped leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal and mangrove areas, thriving in humid and saline conditions. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are often used in traditional ceremonies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and cultural significance in local communities.
