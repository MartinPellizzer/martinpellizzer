Perinkara

Perinkara, commonly known as the "flower of the forest," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
