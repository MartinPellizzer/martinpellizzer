Psilotrichum concinnum

Psilotrichum concinnum, commonly known as the slender whisk fern, is a member of the family Psilotaceae and is characterized by its simple, unbranched sporophyte structure.

It is a primitive vascular plant that lacks true roots, leaves, and flowers, instead possessing rhizomes and sporangia. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in small, erect clusters called sporangiophores.

Psilotrichum concinnum plays a role in ecological succession and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
