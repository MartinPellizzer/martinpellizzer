Primula frondosa

Primula frondosa, commonly known as the fronded primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky crevices. Its flowering period typically occurs in spring, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to shaded conditions and its ability to form dense mats in suitable habitats.
