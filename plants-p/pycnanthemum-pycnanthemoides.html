Pycnanthemum pycnanthemoides

Pycnanthemum pycnanthemoides, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves and yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and prairies.

Wild bergamot is also valued for its medicinal properties and as a source of essential oils.
