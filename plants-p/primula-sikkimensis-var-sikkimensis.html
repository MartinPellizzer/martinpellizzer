Primula sikkimensis var. sikkimensis

Primula sikkimensis var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim primrose, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring, with flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and forested areas at high altitudes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Primulaceae family.
