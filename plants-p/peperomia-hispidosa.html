Peperomia hispidosa

Peperomia hispidosa, commonly known as the hairy peperomia, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom during the warmer months. Peperomia hispidosa thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and ornamental appeal contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
