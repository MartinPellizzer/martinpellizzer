Pleioblastus fortunei f. albostriatus

Pleioblastus fortunei f. albostriatus, commonly known as the silver-striped bamboo, is a varietal form of Pleioblastus fortunei, a species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking white or silver streaks that run along the green culms, creating a visually appealing contrast. This bamboo is a clumping species, forming dense, upright clusters that grow to a moderate height. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both garden and landscape use.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant appearance and ability to provide a fine texture in ornamental gardens.
