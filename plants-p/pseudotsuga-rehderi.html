Pseudotsuga rehderi

Pseudotsuga rehderi, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and moderate climates. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

The wood of Pseudotsuga rehderi is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
