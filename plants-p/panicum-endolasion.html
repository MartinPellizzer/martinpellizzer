Panicum endolasion

Panicum endolasion, commonly known as the coastal panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a grass species that typically grows in sandy or coastal soils, often found in dunes and other maritime environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periods of drought.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
