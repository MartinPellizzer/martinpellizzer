Passiflora nelsonii

Passiflora nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's passionflower, is a species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora nelsonii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
