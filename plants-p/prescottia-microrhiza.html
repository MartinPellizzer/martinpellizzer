Prescottia microrhiza

Prescottia microrhiza, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a unique member of the orchid family with distinctive botanical features.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. The plant has a compact growth habit, with a short, stocky rhizome and a single, erect stem bearing a few flowers. Its flowers are small and usually have a greenish or brownish hue, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Prescottia microrhiza is native to specific regions in South America and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
