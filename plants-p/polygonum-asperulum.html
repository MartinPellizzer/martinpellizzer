Polygonum asperulum

Polygonum asperulum, commonly known as sandmat, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its rough, hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Polygonum asperulum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
