Potamogeton illinoensis f. homophyllus

Potamogeton illinoensis f. homophyllus, commonly known as the Illinois pondweed.

This aquatic plant is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It is a submerged herb that thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. The leaves are usually smooth and have a distinct midrib, aiding in its identification.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
