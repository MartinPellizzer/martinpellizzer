Pedilochilus guttulatus

Pedilochilus guttulatus, commonly known as the spotted helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower with a distinctive greenish-yellow color and red-brown markings. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
