Prunus rhamnifructa

Prunus rhamnifructa, commonly known as the Chinese bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that are typically yellow or red in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Prunus rhamnifructa is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
