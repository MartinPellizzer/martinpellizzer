Persea hypoleuca

Persea hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-leaf avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically light green to white in color. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, yellow fruits with a smooth, thin skin. The fruit contains a single large seed and is valued for its mild, creamy flesh.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
