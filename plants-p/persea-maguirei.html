Persea maguirei

Persea maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's avocado, is a species of evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are not typically consumed by humans but are important for wildlife. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
