Papaver jugoricum

Papaver jugoricum, commonly known as the Balkan poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking red or orange flowers with a prominent black stigma, typical of the Papaveraceae family. The plant has a slender, branching stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its seeds are small and capsule-like, dispersing easily by wind.
