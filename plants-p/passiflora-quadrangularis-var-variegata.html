Passiflora quadrangularis var. variegata

Passiflora quadrangularis var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, angular, greenish-yellow stems and leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with five petals and a crown-like structure, followed by orange to yellow fruits that are typically oval in shape. The fruit's outer skin is covered with a rough, hairy texture, and it contains a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounding the seeds.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and edible fruit.
