Polystachya engongensis

Polystachya engongensis, commonly known as the Engong mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the inflorescence consists of multiple racemes.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
