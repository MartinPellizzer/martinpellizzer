Plantago juncoides var. typica

Plantago juncoides var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its resilience to environmental stress.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used in traditional herbal remedies.
