Pseuderanthemum muelleri-fernandii

Pseuderanthemum muelleri-fernandii, commonly known as the Mueller's pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with vibrant, often purple or pinkish petals that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
