Paspalum eriophorum

Paspalum eriophorum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, erect blades and tufted inflorescences that are often tinged with purple. The plant typically grows in moist or wetland environments, such as marshes and riverbanks. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and summer months, producing small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Paspalum eriophorum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
