Phaius minor

Phaius minor, commonly known as the lesser phaius, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and produces strikingly large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and thrives in humid environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive greenish hue.

Phaius minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
