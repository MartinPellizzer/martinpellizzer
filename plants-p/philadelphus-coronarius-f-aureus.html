Philadelphus coronarius f. aureus

Philadelphus coronarius f. aureus, commonly known as the golden weeping philadelphus, is a cultivar of the shrub known for its showy, fragrant flowers.

This variety produces clusters of lemon-yellow blooms that appear in early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens. The plant is characterized by its long, arching branches and dark green, glossy foliage. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and floral arrangements.
