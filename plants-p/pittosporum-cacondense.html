Pittosporum cacondense

Pittosporum cacondense, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to both native and cultivated landscapes.
