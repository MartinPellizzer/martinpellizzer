Philodendron pedatum

Philodendron pedatum, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant has a unique growth habit, often trailing or climbing along surfaces with aerial roots. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red berries.

Philodendron pedatum thrives in warm, humid environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.
