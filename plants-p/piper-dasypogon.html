Piper dasypogon

Piper dasypogon, commonly known as the hairy-pod pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruits are elongated, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
