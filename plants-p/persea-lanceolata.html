Persea lanceolata

Persea lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its fruit is a pear-shaped berry with a smooth, thick skin and a creamy, oily pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible fruit in certain regions.
