Plectranthus excelsus

Plectranthus excelsus, commonly known as the Cape hybrid verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus excelsus is also valued for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in herbal teas and essential oils.
