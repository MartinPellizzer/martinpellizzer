Phyllanthus castus

Phyllanthus castus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
