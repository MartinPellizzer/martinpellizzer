Piper pseudoamalago

Piper pseudoamalago, commonly known as the Malagasy pepper or false amalago, is a climbing shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are also utilized for their aromatic properties.

The botanical features of Piper pseudoamalago make it a unique and valuable species in the study of tropical flora.
