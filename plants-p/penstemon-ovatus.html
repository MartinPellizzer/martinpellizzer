Penstemon ovatus

Penstemon ovatus, commonly known as the woolly penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features dense, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon ovatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
