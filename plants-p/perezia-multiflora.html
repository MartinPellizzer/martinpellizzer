Perezia multiflora

Perezia multiflora, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Perezia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
