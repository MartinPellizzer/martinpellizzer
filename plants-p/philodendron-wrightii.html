Philodendron wrightii

Philodendron wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that attract birds and other wildlife. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
