Phyllanthus loranthoides var. longifolius

Phyllanthus loranthoides var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color, which are followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
