Paepalanthus scopulifer

Paepalanthus scopulifer, commonly known as the rock cholla, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh, dry conditions. The plant features dense clusters of cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny areoles, which are characteristic of the Cactaceae family. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, emerging from the areoles during the warmer months.

Paepalanthus scopulifer is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
