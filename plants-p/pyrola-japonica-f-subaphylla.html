Pyrola japonica f. subaphylla

Pyrola japonica f. subaphylla, commonly known as the Japanese wintergreen.

This plant is a variant of the Japanese pyrola, characterized by its unique foliage structure. It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, leathery leaves that are typically darker in color compared to the typical form. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas of Japan.
