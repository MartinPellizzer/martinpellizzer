Pavonia stolzii

Pavonia stolzii, commonly known as the Stolz's pride-of-India, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in native landscapes.
