Psyllocarpus imbricatus

Psyllocarpus imbricatus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, woody pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
