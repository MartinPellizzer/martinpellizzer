Paspalum pannuceum

Paspalum pannuceum, commonly known as the panicle paspalum, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Paspalum pannuceum is often found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along roadsides.
