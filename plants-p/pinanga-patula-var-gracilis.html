Pinanga patula var. gracilis

Pinanga patula var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and gracefully arching fronds that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, followed by dark, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces.

Its foliage provides a lush, green aesthetic, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
