Piper florencianum

Piper florencianum, commonly known as the Florentine pepper or Hawaiian pepper, is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, including parts of Central and South America. The plant produces small, green to red berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, particularly in the Caribbean and Pacific islands.

Piper florencianum is characterized by its aromatic foliage and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
