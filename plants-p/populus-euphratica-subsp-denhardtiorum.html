Populus euphratica subsp. denhardtiorum

Populus euphratica subsp.

denhardtiorum, commonly known as the Syrian poplar, is a subspecies of the poplar tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found near rivers and lakes in desert environments.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in the spring. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing some resistance to drought and extreme temperatures.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
