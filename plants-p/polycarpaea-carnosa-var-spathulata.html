Polycarpaea carnosa var. spathulata

Polycarpaea carnosa var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed polycarpaea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
