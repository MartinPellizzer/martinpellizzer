Pilocereus vollii

Pilocereus vollii, commonly known as the Vollii night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Pilocereus vollii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
