Potamogeton trichoides var. tuberculosus

Potamogeton trichoides var. tuberculosus, commonly known as the tuberculate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, tuberculate (bumpy) structures, giving it its specific name. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in spike-like clusters.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
