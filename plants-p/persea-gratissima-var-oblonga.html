Persea gratissima var. oblonga

Persea gratissima var. oblonga, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by fruit that turns from green to orange-red when ripe. The fruit is non-astringent and has a sweet, honey-like flavor, making it a popular edible species.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
