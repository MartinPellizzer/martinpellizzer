Physaria geyeri var. purpurea

Physaria geyeri var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features hairy, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, bright purple or violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments.
