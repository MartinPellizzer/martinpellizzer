Pyrrhocactus crispus

Pyrrhocactus crispus, commonly known as the hairy cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its textured foliage. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Overall, Pyrrhocactus crispus is a resilient and distinctive plant with unique botanical features suited to its desert habitat.
