Polygala tetragona

Polygala tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and often have a four-angled cross-section, giving it its scientific name.

Polygala tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
