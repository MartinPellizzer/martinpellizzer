Phalaenopsis pulcherrima f. purpurea

Phalaenopsis pulcherrima f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid prized for its elegant, long-lasting blooms.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features broad, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, purple or violet flowers with white markings. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a branching pattern with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care in indoor environments.
