Pouteria chiricana

Pouteria chiricana, commonly known as the Amazonian cherimoya, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is similar in appearance to a cherimoya, with a smooth, creamy pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ecological and potential economic value, it is of interest for conservation and sustainable agriculture.
