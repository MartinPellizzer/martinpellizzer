Prunus jamasakura f. avicennae

Prunus jamasakura f. avicennae, commonly known as the Avicenna cherry, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Japanese cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and have a delicate, almost ethereal appearance.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic value in gardens and is often used in horticultural displays.
