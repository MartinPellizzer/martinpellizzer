Pectocarya gracilis var. genuina

Pectocarya gracilis var. genuina, commonly known as the slender pectocarya, is a rare and delicate plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment. Its growth is slow, and it often forms dense clumps that help prevent soil erosion.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
