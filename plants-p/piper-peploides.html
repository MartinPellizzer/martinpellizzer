Piper peploides

Piper peploides, commonly known as the silky pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that develop from the flowers and are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper peploides make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
