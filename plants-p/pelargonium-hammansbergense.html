Pelargonium hammansbergense

Pelargonium hammansbergense, commonly known as the Hammansberg geranium, is a rare and endemic species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red. The plant features serrated, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
