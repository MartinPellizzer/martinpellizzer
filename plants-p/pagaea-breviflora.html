Pagaea breviflora

Pagaea breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered pagaea, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

Pagaea breviflora is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
