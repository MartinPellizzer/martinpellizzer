Pycnanthemum tenuifolium var. lawrencei

Pycnanthemum tenuifolium var. lawrencei, commonly known as Lawrence's narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces a strong, mint-like fragrance.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
