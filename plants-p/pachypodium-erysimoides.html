Pachypodium erysimoides

Pachypodium erysimoides, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick stem with a swollen base and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the dry season. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
