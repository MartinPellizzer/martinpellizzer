Protea madiensis var. chevalieri

Protea madiensis var. chevalieri, commonly known as the Knysna protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence that displays a range of colors from white to pink and red. The plant is native to the coastal regions of the Eastern Cape and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
