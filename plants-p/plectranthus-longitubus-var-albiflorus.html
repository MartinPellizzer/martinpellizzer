Plectranthus longitubus var. albiflorus

Plectranthus longitubus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower plectranthus, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers with prominent stamens, which bloom in summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, dry climates.
