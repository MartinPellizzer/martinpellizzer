Prunus brigantina var. clarissa

Prunus brigantina var. clarissa, commonly known as the Clarissa cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in late summer.

The Clarissa cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blossoms.
