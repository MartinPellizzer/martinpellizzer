Pulsatilla × valemiana

Pulsatilla × valemiana, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that typically range from pink to purple. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and hairy stems that support its palmate, deeply lobed leaves. Its flowers consist of showy, petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, making it a striking ornamental plant.

Pulsatilla × valemiana is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
