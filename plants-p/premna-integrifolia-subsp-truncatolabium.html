Premna integrifolia subsp. truncatolabium

Premna integrifolia subsp.

truncatolabium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rubber vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a truncate base and small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in racemes.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Its latex sap is milky and can be used for various traditional purposes.
