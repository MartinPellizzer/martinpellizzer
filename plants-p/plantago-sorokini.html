Plantago sorokini

Plantago sorokini, commonly known as the Sorokina plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or used in traditional medicine.
