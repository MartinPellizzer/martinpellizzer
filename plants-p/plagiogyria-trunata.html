Plagiogyria trunata

Plagiogyria trunata, commonly known as the slender broomrape, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and therefore cannot perform photosynthesis, relying entirely on its host for nutrients. The plant is characterized by its slender, yellowish stems and small, scale-like leaves. It typically grows on the roots of grasses and other herbaceous plants.
