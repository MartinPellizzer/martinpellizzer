Phyllostegia helleri

Phyllostegia helleri, commonly known as Heller's phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Phyllostegia helleri is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and along stream banks.
