Prunus insititia proles italica

Prunus insititia proles italica, commonly known as the Italian white cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries that ripen in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and small orchards.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate blossoms and attractive fruit.
