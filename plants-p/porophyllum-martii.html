Porophyllum martii

Porophyllum martii, commonly known as Mexican fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Porophyllum martii is known for its resilience to arid conditions and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
