Pandanus pluriaculeatus

Pandanus pluriaculeatus, commonly known as the common pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous trunk and large, palm-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that grow in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making mats and ropes.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to salt spray.
