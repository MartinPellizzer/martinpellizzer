Psoralea palustris

Psoralea palustris, commonly known as swamp psoralea, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.

Psoralea palustris is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
