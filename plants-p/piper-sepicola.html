Piper sepicola

Piper sepicola, commonly known as the New Guinea pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.

Piper sepicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
