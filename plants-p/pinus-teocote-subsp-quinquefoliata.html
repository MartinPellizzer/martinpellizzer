Pinus teocote subsp. quinquefoliata

Pinus teocote subsp.

quinquefoliata, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a subspecies native to Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of five.

The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

This subspecies is known for its resilience and ability to grow in arid environments.
