Phlomis majkopensis

Phlomis majkopensis, commonly known as the Balkan torchflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis majkopensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky or dry garden environments.
