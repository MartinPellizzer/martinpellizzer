Paspalum elegans

Paspalum elegans, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, pale blue or white flowers.

Paspalum elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
