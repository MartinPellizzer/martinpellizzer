Phreatia ramosii

Phreatia ramosii, commonly known as the ramose phreatia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and arching inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Phreatia ramosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
