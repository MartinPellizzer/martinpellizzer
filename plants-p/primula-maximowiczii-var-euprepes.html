Primula maximowiczii var. euprepes

Primula maximowiczii var. euprepes, commonly known as the pink primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, while the flowers bloom in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
