Pteris goeldii

Pteris goeldii, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a subtle golden hue. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce spores on the underside of its fronds.

Pteris goeldii is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.
