Phyllanthus casticum var. roxburghianus

Phyllanthus casticum var. roxburghianus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. The fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary value.
