Passiflora apetala

Passiflora apetala, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a unique structure that includes a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellowish-orange berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora apetala is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
