Pyrus crenulata

Pyrus crenulata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with wavy edges. The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by white, sweet-smelling fruit in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Pyrus crenulata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
