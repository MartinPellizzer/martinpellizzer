Pentachondra ericifolia

Pentachondra ericifolia, commonly known as the heath pentachondra, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
