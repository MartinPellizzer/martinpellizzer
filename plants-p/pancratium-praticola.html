Pancratium praticola

Pancratium praticola, commonly known as the yellow crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, slender stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Pancratium praticola is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
