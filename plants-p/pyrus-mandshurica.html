Pyrus mandshurica

Pyrus mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits that ripen in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in its native habitat.
