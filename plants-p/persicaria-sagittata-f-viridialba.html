Persicaria sagittata f. viridialba

Persicaria sagittata f. viridialba, commonly known as the white arrowleaf plant, is a variant of the arrowleaf plantain.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically a striking white or pale green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
