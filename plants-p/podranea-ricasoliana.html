Podranea ricasoliana

Podranea ricasoliana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in gardens.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a sweet, fragrant scent when mature.
