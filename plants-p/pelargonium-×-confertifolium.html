Pelargonium × confertifolium

Pelargonium × confertifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors such as pink, red, and white. Native to South Africa, this species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for many gardening environments.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a range of climates, though it may require protection in colder regions.
