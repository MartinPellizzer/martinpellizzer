Phlox nana var. glabella

Phlox nana var. glabella, commonly known as the dwarf phlox or glabella phlox, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink or lavender in color. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, ornamental plant.
