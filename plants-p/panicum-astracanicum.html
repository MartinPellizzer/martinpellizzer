Panicum astracanicum

Panicum astracanicum, commonly known as Astracan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers, typically in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Panicum astracanicum is often found in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils.
