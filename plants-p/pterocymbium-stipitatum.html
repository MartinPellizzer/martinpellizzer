Pterocymbium stipitatum

Pterocymbium stipitatum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers, which are often white or pale pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.
