Plantago coronopus var. erecta

Plantago coronopus var. erecta, commonly known as upright plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.

This variety is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
