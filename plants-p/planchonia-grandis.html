Planchonia grandis

Planchonia grandis, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a large tree native to the Pacific islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in coastal and tropical environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The tree also produces small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.
