Parodia glaucina

Parodia glaucina, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical shape and its pale blue or greenish-blue coloration, which helps it blend into its arid environment. The plant features white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Parodia glaucina is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.
