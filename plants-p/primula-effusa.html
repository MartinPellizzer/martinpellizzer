Primula effusa

Primula effusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, bell-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers cool climates. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Primula effusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
