Pennisetum mexicanum

Pennisetum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican lovegrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, pink or white plumes that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control. Its rapid growth and ability to spread via rhizomes allow it to form dense stands in disturbed soils.

Pennisetum mexicanum is often used in landscaping for its visual appeal and low maintenance requirements.
