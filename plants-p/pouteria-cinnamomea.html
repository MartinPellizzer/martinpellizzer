Pouteria cinnamomea

Pouteria cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or camu camu tree, is a tropical species native to the Amazon basin.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine, and the fruit is valued for its nutritional content and antioxidant properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.
