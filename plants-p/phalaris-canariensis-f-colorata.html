Phalaris canariensis f. colorata

Phalaris canariensis f. colorata, commonly known as the Canary grass with coloration, is a variant of the species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which displays vibrant hues of red, pink, and purple, particularly in the leaves. This ornamental grass thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with tall, slender stems that produce small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration and texture.
