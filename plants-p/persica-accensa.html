Persica accensa

Persica accensa, commonly known as the Himalayan peach.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Persica accensa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
