Pellaea dura

Pellaea dura, commonly known as the hard fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that remain green throughout the year. The plant has a rosette-like growth habit, with the fronds emerging from a central crown. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant structure.

Pellaea dura thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
