Paeonia × moutan

Paeonia × moutan, commonly known as the tree peony, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Paeonia suffruticosa and Paeonia ostii.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thick, woody stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow, often with a distinctive double or semi-double petal structure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it is native to China.

Paeonia × moutan is valued for its ornamental beauty and is widely cultivated in gardens and horticultural settings.
