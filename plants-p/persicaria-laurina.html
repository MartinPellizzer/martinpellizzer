Persicaria laurina

Persicaria laurina, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands. It has a short, thick rhizome and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Persicaria laurina is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
