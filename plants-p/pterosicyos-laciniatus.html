Pterosicyos laciniatus

Pterosicyos laciniatus, commonly known as the African water spinach, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lobed margin, and its stems are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used as a vegetable in some African countries.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
