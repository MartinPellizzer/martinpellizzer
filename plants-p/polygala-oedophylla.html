Polygala oedophylla

Polygala oedophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Polygala oedophylla is often found in open woodlands and is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
