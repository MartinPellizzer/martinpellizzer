Pinus nigra var. poiretiana

Pinus nigra var. poiretiana, commonly known as the Corsican pine, is a variant of the black pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically 2–4 cm long. The tree produces cones that are typically 5–8 cm in length, containing edible seeds enclosed in a woody covering. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in forestry and landscape design.
