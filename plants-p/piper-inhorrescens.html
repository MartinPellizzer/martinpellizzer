Piper inhorrescens

Piper inhorrescens, commonly known as the rough pepper or wild long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
