Parodia aglaisma

Parodia aglaisma, commonly known as the Lemon Ball Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
