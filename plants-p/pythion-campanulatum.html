Pythion campanulatum

Pythion campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped pythion, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes many species with tubular or bell-shaped blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually blue or white and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
