Palala leucoxyla

Palala leucoxyla, commonly known as the Tahitian sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the islands of French Polynesia, including Tahiti and Moorea. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy of dark green leaves. Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional crafts and perfumery.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
