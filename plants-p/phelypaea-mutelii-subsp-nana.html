Phelypaea mutelii subsp. nana

Phelypaea mutelii subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf phelypaea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.

The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems are short and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
