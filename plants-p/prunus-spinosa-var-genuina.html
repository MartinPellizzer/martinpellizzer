Prunus spinosa var. genuina

Prunus spinosa var. genuina, commonly known as the common blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple-black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are a food source for birds and are used in traditional herbal remedies.
