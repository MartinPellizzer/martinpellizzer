Poincianella bracteosa

Poincianella bracteosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Poincianella bracteosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various soil types.
