Polygonum obscurum

Polygonum obscurum, commonly known as black knot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bead-like nodes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.
