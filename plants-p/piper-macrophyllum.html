Piper macrophyllum

Piper macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

Piper macrophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
