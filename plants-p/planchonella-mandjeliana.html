Planchonella mandjeliana

Planchonella mandjeliana, commonly known as the Madagascar plum.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble plums, with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
