Plinia darienensis

Plinia darienensis, commonly known as the Darien mountain blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in the Darien region of Panama. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, dark purple to black berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

The fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.
