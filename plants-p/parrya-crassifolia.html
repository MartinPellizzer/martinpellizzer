Parrya crassifolia

Parrya crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved parrya, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Parrya crassifolia is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
