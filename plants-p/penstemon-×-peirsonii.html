Penstemon × peirsonii

Penstemon × peirsonii, commonly known as the Peirson's beardtongue, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to lavender. The plant produces showy, glandular-hairy leaves that are often gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon × peirsonii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
