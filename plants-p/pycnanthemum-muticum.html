Pycnanthemum muticum

Pycnanthemum muticum, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woods or along stream banks. Its medicinal properties have been historically used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.

The plant's distinctive scent and chemical composition make it a valuable species in ecological and botanical studies.
