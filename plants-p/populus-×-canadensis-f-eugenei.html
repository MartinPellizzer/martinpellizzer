Populus × canadensis f. eugenei

Populus × canadensis f. eugenei, commonly known as the Eugene poplar, is a hybrid species within the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its fast growth rate and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for reforestation and windbreaks. The plant exhibits striking foliage, with heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in autumn. It produces small, greenish flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in urban and rural landscapes.
