Peperomia tristanensis

Peperomia tristanensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Trinidad and Tobago, and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by reddish berries.

Peperomia tristanensis is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
