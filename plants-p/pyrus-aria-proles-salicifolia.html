Pyrus aria proles salicifolia

Pyrus aria proles salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved pear, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are elongated and serrated. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellow fruits that are similar to pears. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
