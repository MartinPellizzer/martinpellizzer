Prunus sativa proles porcorum

Prunus sativa proles porcorum, commonly known as the wild cherry tree, is a species of shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible drupes that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it prefers well-drained soils in temperate climates.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local wildlife.
