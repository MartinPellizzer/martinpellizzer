Parrya macrocarpa var. turkestanica

Parrya macrocarpa var. turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is adapted to arid environments and exhibits drought tolerance.
