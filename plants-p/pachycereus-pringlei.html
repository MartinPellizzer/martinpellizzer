Pachycereus pringlei

Pachycereus pringlei, commonly known as the organ pipe cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and can grow to impressive heights, often exceeding 10 meters. The plant produces large, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths. Its stems store water efficiently, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Pachycereus pringlei plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various desert species.
