Polemonium boreale subsp. nudipedum

Polemonium boreale subsp.

nudipedum, commonly known as the northern Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, forming a distinctive ladder-like pattern.

The blossoms are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.
