Pyrus fokienensis

Pyrus fokienensis, commonly known as the Fokien pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but less commonly cultivated for commercial purposes. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Pyrus fokienensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
