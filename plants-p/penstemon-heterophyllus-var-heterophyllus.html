Penstemon heterophyllus var. heterophyllus

Penstemon heterophyllus var. heterophyllus, commonly known as the Great Basin penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky soils. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, and it produces tall, branched stems with showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
