Pelargonium usambarense

Pelargonium usambarense, commonly known as the Usambarense geranium, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and it thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium usambarense prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
