Pithecellobium caulostachyum

Pithecellobium caulostachyum, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow, velvety flowers that are often clustered in panicles, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a woody pod containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
