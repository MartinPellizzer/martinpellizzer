Potamogeton lucens f. terrestris

Potamogeton lucens f. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial pondweed, is a variant of the water plantain.

It is a submerged or floating aquatic plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its small, green flowers appear in spike-like clusters, and it produces small, elongated fruits.

This form is adapted to terrestrial conditions, making it unique among its species.
