Pyrus gansuana

Pyrus gansuana, commonly known as the Gansu pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is often found in mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and orchards.
