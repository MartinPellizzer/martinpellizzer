Potentilla nivea subsp. rubricaulis

Potentilla nivea subsp.

rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish stems and hairy leaves, which are typical of the Potentilla genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
