Phrynium dispermum

Phrynium dispermum, commonly known as the two-seeded arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces two seeds per fruit, which is reflected in its scientific name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
