Pterolobium microphyllum

Pterolobium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved pterolobium, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, pinnately compound leaves with a glossy green surface and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
