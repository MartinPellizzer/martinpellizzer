Potentilla × collina var. schultzii

Potentilla × collina var. schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a dark green upper surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cooler climates.
