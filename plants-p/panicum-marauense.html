Panicum marauense

Panicum marauense, commonly known as Brazilian millet, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Panicum marauense is valued for its potential in forage and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
