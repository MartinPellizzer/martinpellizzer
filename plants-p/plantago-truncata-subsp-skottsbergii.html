Plantago truncata subsp. skottsbergii

Plantago truncata subsp.

skottsbergii, commonly known as the Sierra velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
