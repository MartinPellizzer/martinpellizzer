Polygala polymorpha var. buxifolia

Polygala polymorpha var. buxifolia, commonly known as the Buxus-leaved milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with dense, foliage that resembles that of boxwood, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
