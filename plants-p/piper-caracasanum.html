Piper caracasanum

Piper caracasanum, commonly known as the Caracasa pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
