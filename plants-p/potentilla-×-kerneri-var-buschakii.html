Potentilla × kerneri var. buschakii

Potentilla × kerneri var. buschakii, commonly known as the Buschakii cineraria, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage that reflects light and gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to form compact, mounded growth.
