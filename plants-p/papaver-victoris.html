Papaver victoris

Papaver victoris, commonly known as the Victoria poppy, is a striking annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying vibrant red or orange hues. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The Victoria poppy is also noted for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional remedies.
