Polystichum aleuticum

Polystichum aleuticum, commonly known as the Aleutian woodfern, is a species of fern native to the northwestern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive dark green color and a slightly glossy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for small organisms.
