Plantago sericea var. agrostophylla

Plantago sericea var. agrostophylla, commonly known as silky plantain or velvetleaf, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environments, including roadsides and gardens.
