Pycnarrhena castanopsisifolia

Pycnarrhena castanopsisifolia, commonly known as the Castanopsis-leaved pycnarrhena, is a rare shrub native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that resemble those of the Castanopsis genus. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported antioxidant properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
