Pteretis japonica

Pteretis japonica, commonly known as Japanese holly, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, glossy leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Pteretis japonica is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a hedge plant.
