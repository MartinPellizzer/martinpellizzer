Pleroma riparium

Pleroma riparium, commonly known as the water primrose, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water and along the edges of ponds, rivers, and wetlands. The plant has bright green, waxy leaves that are often floating or partially submerged, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. Pleroma riparium is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.

It plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
