Palmijuncus deerratus

Palmijuncus deerratus, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, palm-like plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It has a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are clustered at the base, giving it a compact appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Its unique structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
