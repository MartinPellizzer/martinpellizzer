Penstemon campanulatus f. pulchellus

Penstemon campanulatus f. pulchellus, commonly known as the Bluecurls Penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, preferring full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are upright and branched.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
