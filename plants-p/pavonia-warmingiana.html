Pavonia warmingiana

Pavonia warmingiana, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Pavonia warmingiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
