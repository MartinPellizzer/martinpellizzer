Penstemon teretiflorus

Penstemon teretiflorus, commonly known as the slenderflower beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds via wind.

Penstemon teretiflorus is valued in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
