Primula leiocalyx

Primula leiocalyx, commonly known as the white primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Primula leiocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
