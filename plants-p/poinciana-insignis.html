Poinciana insignis

Poinciana insignis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, red or orange-pink flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is well-suited for urban landscaping due to its adaptability and visual impact.
