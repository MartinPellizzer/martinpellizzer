Polemonium morenonis

Polemonium morenonis, commonly known as the Eastern Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus within the family Polemoniaceae. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polemonium morenonis thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
