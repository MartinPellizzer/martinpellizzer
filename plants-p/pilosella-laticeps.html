Pilosella laticeps

Pilosella laticeps, commonly known as the hairy leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and often lobed, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Pilosella laticeps is often found in dry, sunny environments and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils.
