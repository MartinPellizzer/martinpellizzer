Parmentiera valerii

Parmentiera valerii, commonly known as the Mexican candlewood, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Parmentiera valerii is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
