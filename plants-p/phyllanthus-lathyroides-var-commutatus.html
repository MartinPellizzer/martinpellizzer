Phyllanthus lathyroides var. commutatus

Phyllanthus lathyroides var. commutatus, commonly known as the hairy bladder nut, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, alternate leaves that are typically glandular and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing tiny, hard seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, particularly for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.
