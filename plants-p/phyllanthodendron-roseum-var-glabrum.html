Phyllanthodendron roseum var. glabrum

Phyllanthodendron roseum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth roseleaf phyllanthodendron, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are smooth to the touch, giving it its varietal name "glabrum.". It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by orange-red berries.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
