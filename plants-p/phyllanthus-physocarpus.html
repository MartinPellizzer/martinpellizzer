Phyllanthus physocarpus

Phyllanthus physocarpus, commonly known as the bladder nut or bladderleaf, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, seed-bearing pods that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
