Pouteria macrocarpa

Pouteria macrocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree is notable for its long, woody pods that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, including selenium and healthy fats.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
