Pyrus fischeri

Pyrus fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in the spring. Its fruit, a small, green pear, ripens in the summer and is edible, though less commonly cultivated than other pear species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
