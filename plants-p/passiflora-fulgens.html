Passiflora fulgens

Passiflora fulgens, commonly known as the brilliant passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, all in vibrant colors. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often consumed fresh or in jams. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in warm climates.

Passiflora fulgens is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
