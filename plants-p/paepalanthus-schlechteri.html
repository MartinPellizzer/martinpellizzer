Paepalanthus schlechteri

Paepalanthus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's cholla, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often segmented. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Paepalanthus schlechteri is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy species in desert conditions.

Its structure and growth pattern make it well-suited for survival in harsh, dry climates.
