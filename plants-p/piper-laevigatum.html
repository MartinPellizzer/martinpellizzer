Piper laevigatum

Piper laevigatum, commonly known as the roundleaf pipevine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
