Potentilla cryptotaeniae var. insularis

Potentilla cryptotaeniae var. insularis, commonly known as the island potentilla, is a shrub native to specific island habitats in the western Pacific.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal or forested areas. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable plant for gardens with suitable conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native environment.
