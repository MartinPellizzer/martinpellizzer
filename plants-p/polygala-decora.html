Polygala decora

Polygala decora, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and showy, spicate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

Polygala decora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinator populations.
