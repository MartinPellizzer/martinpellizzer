Poa abbreviata subsp. pattersonii

Poa abbreviata subsp.

pattersonii, commonly known as Patterson's bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments, including rocky soils and high-altitude regions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
