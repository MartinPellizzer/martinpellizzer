Phaseolus multiflorus var. niger

Phaseolus multiflorus var. niger, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods that contain dark, glossy seeds used in culinary and traditional medicinal practices. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting neighboring plants.
