Poa schiedeana

Poa schiedeana, commonly known as Schiede's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes allow it to form dense mats, making it a common component of grasslands and wet meadows.

Poa schiedeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
