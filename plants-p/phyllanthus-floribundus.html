Phyllanthus floribundus

Phyllanthus floribundus, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, characterized by its dense, branched structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, winged fruits. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.
