Phrynium crista-galli

Phrynium crista-galli, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky, arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.

Phrynium crista-galli is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and partial shade.
