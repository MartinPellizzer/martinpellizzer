Potentilla argentea var. tephrodes

Potentilla argentea var. tephrodes, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silver-white, woolly leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in tundra and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
