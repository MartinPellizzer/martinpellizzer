Pinus gracilis

Pinus gracilis, commonly known as the slender pine, is a species of conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The botanical features of Pinus gracilis make it an important component of forest ecosystems in its native range.
