Protea petiolaris

Protea petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which includes many ornamental and ecologically important species. The plant produces striking, cone-like inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve water in its native arid environment.

Protea petiolaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
