Primula rhodochroa var. rhodochroa

Primula rhodochroa var. rhodochroa, commonly known as the Scottish primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It is a herbaceous perennial with dark green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, bell-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance contribute to its significance in both horticulture and natural habitats.
