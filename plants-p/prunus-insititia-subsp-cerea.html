Prunus insititia subsp. cerea

Prunus insititia subsp.

cerea, commonly known as the European whitebird or common whitebird, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, tart berries that are edible but often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it has a compact growth habit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.
