Pleurothallis porphyrantha

Pleurothallis porphyrantha, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance.

The orchid's flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated by small insects, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
