Pluchea dioscoridis

Pluchea dioscoridis, commonly known as Dioscorides' groundsel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
