Pilosocereus pernambucoensis

Pilosocereus pernambucoensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
