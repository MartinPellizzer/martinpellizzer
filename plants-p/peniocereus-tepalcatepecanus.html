Peniocereus tepalcatepecanus

Peniocereus tepalcatepecanus, commonly known as the Tepalcatepec night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a low-growing cactus with a dense, spiny stem that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its flowers are usually 10 to 15 centimeters in diameter and have a tubular shape.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
