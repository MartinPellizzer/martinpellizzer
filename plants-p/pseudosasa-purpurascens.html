Pseudosasa purpurascens

Pseudosasa purpurascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often display a striking purple hue, especially when young. The plant forms dense clumps and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy coating.

Pseudosasa purpurascens prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.
