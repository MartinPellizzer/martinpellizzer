Pilosella obovalis

Pilosella obovalis, commonly known as the ovalleaf mouseear, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are oval in shape, giving it its common name. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Pilosella obovalis produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
