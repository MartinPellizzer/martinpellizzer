Polygala ramosissima

Polygala ramosissima, commonly known as the fragrant milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often adorned with distinctive, hairy bracts. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Polygala ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
