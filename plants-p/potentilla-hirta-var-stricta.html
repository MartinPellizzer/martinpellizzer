Potentilla hirta var. stricta

Potentilla hirta var. stricta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems arising from the base.

This variety is characterized by its upright, dense form and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in harsh climates.
