Piptadenia elliotii

Piptadenia elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's ceiba, is a large tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are clustered in long, pendulous inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.
