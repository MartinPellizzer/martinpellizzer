Perymenium verbesinoides

Perymenium verbesinoides, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and a hollow, cylindrical stem that resembles a cup, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaf arrangement.
