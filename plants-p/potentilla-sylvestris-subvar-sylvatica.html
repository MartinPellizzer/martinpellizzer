Potentilla sylvestris subvar. sylvatica

Potentilla sylvestris subvar. sylvatica, commonly known as the wood potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodland areas and along forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in partial shade.
