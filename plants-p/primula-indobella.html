Primula indobella

Primula indobella, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

Primula indobella plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of mountain ecosystems.
