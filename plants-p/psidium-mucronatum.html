Psidium mucronatum

Psidium mucronatum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or small-fruited guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, round, yellowish fruits that are typically covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a tart flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
