Plinia edulis

Plinia edulis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and highly valued for their sweet, tart flavor. These fruits are often consumed fresh or used in jams, juices, and desserts.

Plinia edulis is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
