Piptatherum karataviense

Piptatherum karataviense, commonly known as the Karatavi grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, semi-arid environments and is often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Piptatherum karataviense is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
