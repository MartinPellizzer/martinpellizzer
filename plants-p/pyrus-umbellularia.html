Pyrus umbellularia

Pyrus umbellularia, commonly known as the western whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, apple-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
