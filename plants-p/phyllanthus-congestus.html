Phyllanthus congestus

Phyllanthus congestus, commonly known as the clustered phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features small, clustered leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
