Podocarpus eurhyncha

Podocarpus eurhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in mountainous or forested areas. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
