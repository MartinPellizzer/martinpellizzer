Pitcairnia similis

Pitcairnia similis, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its rosette-shaped leaves collect water, creating a microhabitat for small aquatic organisms.

Pitcairnia similis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
