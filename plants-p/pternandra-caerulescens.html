Pternandra caerulescens

Pternandra caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-leaved pternandra, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Pternandra caerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
