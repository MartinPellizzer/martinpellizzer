Pleurothallis peduncularis

Pleurothallis peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees and rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters along the stem. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique structure and delicate blooms make it a fascinating subject for botanical study and horticultural appreciation.
