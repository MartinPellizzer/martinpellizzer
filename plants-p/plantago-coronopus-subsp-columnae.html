Plantago coronopus subsp. columnae

Plantago coronopus subsp.

columnae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for various insects and birds.
