Prunus spinosa var. balearica

Prunus spinosa var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic blackthorn, is a shrub native to the Balearic Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, dark blue berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces fragrant white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
