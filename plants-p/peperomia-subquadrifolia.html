Peperomia subquadrifolia

Peperomia subquadrifolia, commonly known as the four-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a glossy green surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia subquadrifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
