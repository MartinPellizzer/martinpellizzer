Poa smirnowii

Poa smirnowii, commonly known as Smirnov's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Poa smirnowii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and its ornamental appeal in specialized gardens.
