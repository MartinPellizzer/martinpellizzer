Penstemon linarioides subsp. taosensis

Penstemon linarioides subsp.

taosensis, commonly known as Taos penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Taos penstemon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
