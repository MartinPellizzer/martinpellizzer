Pandanus analamerensis

Pandanus analamerensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in spikes, followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, adapting well to saline and waterlogged soils.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife.
