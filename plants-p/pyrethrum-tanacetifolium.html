Pyrethrum tanacetifolium

Pyrethrum tanacetifolium, commonly known as the common chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which consist of a central yellow disk surrounded by white to pink petals. The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of pyrethrin, a natural insecticide. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, typically reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet.

Pyrethrum tanacetifolium is a hardy perennial that is often used in gardens and for commercial purposes due to its versatility and aesthetic appeal.
