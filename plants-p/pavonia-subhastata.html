Pavonia subhastata

Pavonia subhastata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flower heads that resemble small coneflowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Pavonia subhastata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
