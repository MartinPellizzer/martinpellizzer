Pycnostachys kaessneri

Pycnostachys kaessneri, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural displays.

Botanically, it belongs to the Leguminosae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
