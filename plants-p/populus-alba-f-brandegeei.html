Populus alba f. brandegeei

Populus alba f. brandegeei, commonly known as the white poplar, is a cultivar of the white poplar tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its white, silky hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin and a pale green underside. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
