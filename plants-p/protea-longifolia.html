Protea longifolia

Protea longifolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a silvery-blue hue.

Protea longifolia is a popular ornamental plant due to its longevity and aesthetic appeal.
