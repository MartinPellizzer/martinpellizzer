Pulsatilla × wilczekii

Pulsatilla × wilczekii, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, sunny conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a single, erect stem topped with the distinctive floral structure.

Pulsatilla × wilczekii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
