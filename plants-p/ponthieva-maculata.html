Ponthieva maculata

Ponthieva maculata, commonly known as the spotted spider plant, is a unique member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with white and green markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries, which are edible for birds. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate watering, making it a low-maintenance houseplant.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
