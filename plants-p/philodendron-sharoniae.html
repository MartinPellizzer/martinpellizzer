Philodendron sharoniae

Philodendron sharoniae, commonly known as Sharon's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a striking silver or white variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by green, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and conservation efforts.
