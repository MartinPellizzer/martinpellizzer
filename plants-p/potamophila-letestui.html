Potamophila letestui

Potamophila letestui, commonly known as the Letestu's waterplantain, is a rare aquatic plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it attaches itself to submerged surfaces. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, depending on light conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and flies.

Due to habitat loss and pollution, Potamophila letestui is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
