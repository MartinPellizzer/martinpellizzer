Piper subaequilaterum

Piper subaequilaterum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, purple berries that turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Piper subaequilaterum make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
