Petrea mexicana

Petrea mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Petrea mexicana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
