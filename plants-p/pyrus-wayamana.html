Pyrus wayamana

Pyrus wayamana, commonly known as the Wayaman pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, specifically found in parts of Cuba and Hispaniola. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
