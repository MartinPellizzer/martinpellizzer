Prunus × sieboldii

Prunus × sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus mandshurica and Prunus serrula.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, particularly Japan and China. The plant is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy pink to white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are elliptical with a serrated edge, and it produces small, edible drupes that ripen in summer.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also valued for its hardiness in temperate climates.
