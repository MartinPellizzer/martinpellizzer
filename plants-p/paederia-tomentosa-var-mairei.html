Paederia tomentosa var. mairei

Paederia tomentosa var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's hairy false indigo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
