Polemonium caeruleum var. himalayanum

Polemonium caeruleum var. himalayanum, commonly known as the Himalayan Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
