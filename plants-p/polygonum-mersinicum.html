Polygonum mersinicum

Polygonum mersinicum, commonly known as the Mersin knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and stream banks.

Polygonum mersinicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
