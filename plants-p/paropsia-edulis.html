Paropsia edulis

Paropsia edulis, commonly known as the edible orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers and elongated, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.

Paropsia edulis is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its practical uses in local cultures.
