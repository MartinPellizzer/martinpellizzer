Poa dacica

Poa dacica, commonly known as the Dacian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, hairless stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Poa dacica is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to moderately dry conditions.
