Plectranthus petrensis

Plectranthus petrensis, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Plectranthus petrensis is well-suited to Mediterranean climates and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
