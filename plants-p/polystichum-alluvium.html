Polystichum alluvium

Polystichum alluvium, commonly known as the alluvial wood fern, is a species of fern native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors, where it can access the moisture it requires. Its rhizomes are perennial, allowing it to persist through seasonal changes and regenerate each growing season.

Polystichum alluvium plays an important ecological role by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
