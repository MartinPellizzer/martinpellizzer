Potamogeton gramineus var. nigrescens

Potamogeton gramineus var. nigrescens, commonly known as black-stem watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a distinctive darkening of the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often used as a bioindicator in ecological studies.
