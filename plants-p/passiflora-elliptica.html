Passiflora elliptica

Passiflora elliptica, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. Its fruit is a green, fleshy berry that turns yellow or orange when ripe.

Passiflora elliptica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
