Papaver decaisnei var. decaisnei

Papaver decaisnei var. decaisnei, commonly known as the De Caesne poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stem is erect and somewhat branched. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
