Phaeneilema paraguayense

Phaeneilema paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Phaeneilema paraguayense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
