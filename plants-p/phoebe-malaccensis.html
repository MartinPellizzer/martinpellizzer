Phoebe malaccensis

Phoebe malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca phoebe, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries.

The wood of Phoebe malaccensis is valued for its fine grain and is used in furniture and cabinetry.
