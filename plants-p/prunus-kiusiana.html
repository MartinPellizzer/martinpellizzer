Prunus kiusiana

Prunus kiusiana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Kiusian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, which are similar to those of other Prunus species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with serrated edges.

Prunus kiusiana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also appreciated for its ecological benefits to pollinators.
