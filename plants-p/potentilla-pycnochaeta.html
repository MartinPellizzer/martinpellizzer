Potentilla pycnochaeta

Potentilla pycnochaeta, commonly known as the denseflower cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Potentilla pycnochaeta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
