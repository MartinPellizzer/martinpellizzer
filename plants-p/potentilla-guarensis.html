Potentilla guarensis

Potentilla guarensis, commonly known as the Guarense cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. It has palmately divided leaves and produces small, red or black fruits that are important for local wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
