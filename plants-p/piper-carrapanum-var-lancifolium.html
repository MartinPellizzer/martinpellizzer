Piper carrapanum var. lancifolium

Piper carrapanum var. lancifolium, commonly known as the lancifolium pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper carrapanum species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by elliptical, dark purple to black berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both horticultural and ecological studies.
