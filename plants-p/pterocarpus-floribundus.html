Pterocarpus floribundus

Pterocarpus floribundus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement, and its bark is often marked with distinctive fissures. It produces legume-like pods containing flat, winged seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral displays.
