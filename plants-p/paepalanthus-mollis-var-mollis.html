Paepalanthus mollis var. mollis

Paepalanthus mollis var. mollis, commonly known as the silky leaf cactus, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its fleshy, pubescent leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky layer of hair. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
