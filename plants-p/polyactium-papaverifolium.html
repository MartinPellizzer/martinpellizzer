Polyactium papaverifolium

Polyactium papaverifolium, commonly known as the papaver leaf bug.

This plant is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and umbellate flowers. It typically grows in moist, woodland areas and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The leaves are finely divided and resemble those of the poppy, giving it its common name.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
