Primula veronicoides

Primula veronicoides, commonly known as the veronicoid primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, clump-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
