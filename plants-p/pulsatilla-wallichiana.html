Pulsatilla wallichiana

Pulsatilla wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant has palmate leaves with lobes that are often hairy, and it produces follicles containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
