Petalostemon violaceus var. pubescens

Petalostemon violaceus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy violet geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its violet-blue flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
