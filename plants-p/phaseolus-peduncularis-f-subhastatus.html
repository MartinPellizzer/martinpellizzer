Phaseolus peduncularis f. subhastatus

Phaseolus peduncularis f. subhastatus, commonly known as the Mexican runner bean or subhastatus runner bean, is a variant of the common runner bean.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a narrow, elongated shape with a slightly heart-shaped base. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional cuisines.
