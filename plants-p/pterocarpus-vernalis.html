Pterocarpus vernalis

Pterocarpus vernalis, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces flat, compound leaves and has a straight, durable wood that is highly valued in woodworking. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant also has a long flowering period, making it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
