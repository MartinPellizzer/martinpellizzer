Poa horneri

Poa horneri, commonly known as Horner's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Poa horneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
