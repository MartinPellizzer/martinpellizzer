Prunella pennsylvanica var. lanceolata

Prunella pennsylvanica var. lanceolata, commonly known as lance-leaved selfheal, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is perennial, with a creeping growth habit and hairy stems.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
