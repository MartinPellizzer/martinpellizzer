Pyrrhocactus villicumensis

Pyrrhocactus villicumensis, commonly known as the Villicum Desert Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, spiny cactus with a globular shape and is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions. The plant features short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the early morning and attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in the ecological balance of its desert habitat.
