Psidium arboreum

Psidium arboreum, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, purple-black berries. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical climates. Its fruit is rich in antioxidants and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Psidium arboreum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
