Pleurothallis allenii

Pleurothallis allenii, commonly known as the Allen's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to capture moisture from the humid air.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
