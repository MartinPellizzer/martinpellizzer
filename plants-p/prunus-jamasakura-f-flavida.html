Prunus jamasakura f. flavida

Prunus jamasakura f. flavida, commonly known as the yellow-flowered cherry, is a rare and distinctive variant of the Japanese cherry blossom.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring, offering a unique color contrast to the typical pink or white blossoms of other Prunus species. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and shares many characteristics with other Prunus species, including its deciduous nature and preference for well-drained soil.
