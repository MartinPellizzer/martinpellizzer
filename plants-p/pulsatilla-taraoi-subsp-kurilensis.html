Pulsatilla taraoi subsp. kurilensis

Pulsatilla taraoi subsp.

kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is native to the Kuril Islands. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive hairy texture, and it blooms in early spring.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
