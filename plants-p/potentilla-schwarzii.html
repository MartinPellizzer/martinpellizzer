Potentilla schwarzii

Potentilla schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla schwarzii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
