Piper obtusistigmum

Piper obtusistigmum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper or black pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to dark red as they mature. The berries contain a single seed and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical collections.
