Pentaraphia gloxinioides

Pentaraphia gloxinioides, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it highly drought-resistant.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water environments.
