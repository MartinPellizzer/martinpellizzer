Parodia cardenasii

Parodia cardenasii, commonly known as the Candelabra Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its distinctive branching pattern that resembles a candlestick. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its spines are typically white or yellow, and it produces small, yellow flowers during the spring.

Parodia cardenasii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
