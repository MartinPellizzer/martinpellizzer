Protea gaguedi subsp. laetans

Protea gaguedi subsp.

laetans, commonly known as the Cape protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in the fynbos vegetation of the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate with dry summers and wet winters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is an important component of local biodiversity.
