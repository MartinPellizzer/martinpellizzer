Piper bisasperatum

Piper bisasperatum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into clustered berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
