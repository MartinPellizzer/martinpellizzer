Protea concolor

Protea concolor, commonly known as the yellow protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant is evergreen and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils, often found in fynbos vegetation. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is adapted to fire-prone environments.

Protea concolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
