Persoonia juniperina var. mollis

Persoonia juniperina var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottlebrushes, which are typically pink or red in color. The leaves are soft, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract native pollinators.
