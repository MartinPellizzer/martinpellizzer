Pyracantha spinosa lalandei

Pyracantha spinosa lalandei, commonly known as the Laland's firethorn, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and bright orange to red berries that persist through the winter. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its berries are toxic to humans and animals, so care should be taken when handling or planting this species.
