Pavonia intermedia var. floribunda

Pavonia intermedia var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
