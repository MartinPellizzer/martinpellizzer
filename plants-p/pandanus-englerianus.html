Pandanus englerianus

Pandanus englerianus, commonly known as the Engler's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped leaves and branched, fibrous roots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional practices in its native regions.
