Phlox mexicana

Phlox mexicana, commonly known as Mexican phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and xeriscaping.
