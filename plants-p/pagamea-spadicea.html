Pagamea spadicea

Pagamea spadicea, commonly known as the white spadix plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered around the spadix, which is often white or pale in color. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the floral structure.

Pagamea spadicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
