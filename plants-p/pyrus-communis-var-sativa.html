Pyrus communis var. sativa

Pyrus communis var. sativa, commonly known as the common pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall, characterized by their smooth skin and firm, juicy flesh. The plant has alternate, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It flowers in spring with clusters of white or cream-colored blossoms.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit, which is used in cooking and baking.
