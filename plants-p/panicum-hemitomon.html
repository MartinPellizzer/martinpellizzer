Panicum hemitomon

Panicum hemitomon, commonly known as silver millet, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds, which are typically wind-dispersed. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or used in traditional medicine.
