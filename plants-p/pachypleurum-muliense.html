Pachypleurum muliense

Pachypleurum muliense, commonly known as the Muliense Spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often arranged in a cyme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
