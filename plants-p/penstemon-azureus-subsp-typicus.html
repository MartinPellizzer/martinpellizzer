Penstemon azureus subsp. typicus

Penstemon azureus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
