Prunus persica f. camelliiflora

Prunus persica f. camelliiflora, commonly known as the camellia-flowered peach, is a unique horticultural form of the peach tree.

It is characterized by its striking, double flowers that resemble those of the camellia, featuring numerous petals and a prominent center. The plant retains the typical fruiting capability of the peach, producing sweet, edible fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This form is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is a popular choice in floral arrangements.
