Poinsettia colorata

Poinsettia colorata, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The true flowers are small and yellow, located at the center of the plant. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light.

It is a popular ornamental plant, especially during the holiday season.
