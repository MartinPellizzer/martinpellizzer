Paullinia carpopodea f. vestita

Paullinia carpopodea f. vestita, commonly known as the velvet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with five petals and a prominent, fleshy, red calyx that resembles a crown. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous small, edible seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
