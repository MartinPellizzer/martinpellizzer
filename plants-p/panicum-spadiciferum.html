Panicum spadiciferum

Panicum spadiciferum, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinted foliage and panicle-like inflorescence that turns golden as it matures. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it both a useful and aesthetically pleasing plant.
