Phrodus pendulus

Phrodus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging phrodus, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Phrodus pendulus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
