Panicum utowanaeum

Panicum utowanaeum, commonly known as the Wampanoag grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Panicum utowanaeum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
