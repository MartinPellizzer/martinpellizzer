Phlox griseola subsp. tumulosa

Phlox griseola subsp.

tumulosa, commonly known as the gray phlox or mountain phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

The plant features showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
