Pithocarpa pulchella var. pulchella

Pithocarpa pulchella var. pulchella, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive pithy center, which is a key botanical feature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually elliptical in shape.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
