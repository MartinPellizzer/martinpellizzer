Peperomia ficta

Peperomia ficta, commonly known as the Shrimp Plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to Central and South America.

It features upright, branching stems with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically greenish-white or reddish, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
