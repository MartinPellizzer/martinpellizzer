Paspalum meyerianum

Paspalum meyerianum, commonly known as Meyer's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Meyer's grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
