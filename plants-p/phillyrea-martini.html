Phillyrea martini

Phillyrea martini, commonly known as the Canary Island Phillyrea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Phillyrea martini is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
