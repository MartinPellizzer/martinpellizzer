Polypodium meniscioides

Polypodium meniscioides, commonly known as the Meniscus fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous small, spore-bearing leaves.

The fronds are usually light green and have a feathery appearance, making it a distinctive and ornamental plant.
