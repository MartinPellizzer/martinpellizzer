Psidium riedelianum

Psidium riedelianum, commonly known as the Riedel's guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
