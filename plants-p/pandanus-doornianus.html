Pandanus doornianus

Pandanus doornianus, commonly known as the Doornian's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that often droop downward. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and wetlands, where it thrives in humid, saline conditions. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in a spike-like structure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic leaves.
