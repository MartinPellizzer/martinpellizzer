Phyllolobium flavovirens

Phyllolobium flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are broad, fleshy, and often tinged with a yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
