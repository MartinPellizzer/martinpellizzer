Prunus × effusa

Prunus × effusa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green and turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed.

Prunus × effusa is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
