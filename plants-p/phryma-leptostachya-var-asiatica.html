Phryma leptostachya var. asiatica

Phryma leptostachya var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian whorled woodbine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and produces clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is characterized by its unique whorled arrangement of leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
