Pellaea scemanni

Pellaea scemanni, commonly known as the Scemann's rock fern, is a perennial fern native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among fern enthusiasts and botanists.
