Phyllanthus fluminis-zambesi

Phyllanthus fluminis-zambesi, commonly known as the African water pear, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by oval, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, sandy soils near rivers and streams.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
