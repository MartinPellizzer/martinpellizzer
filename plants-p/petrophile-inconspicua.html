Petrophile inconspicua

Petrophile inconspicua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock-heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal areas. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Petrophile inconspicua is well adapted to coastal and inland regions with limited water availability.
