Plutonopuntia chaffeyi

Plutonopuntia chaffeyi, commonly known as the Chaffey's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless, flat, paddle-shaped stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, a spherical, fleshy berry, is edible and contains small, seed-like structures.

Plutonopuntia chaffeyi is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
