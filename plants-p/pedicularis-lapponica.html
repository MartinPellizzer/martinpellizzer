Pedicularis lapponica

Pedicularis lapponica, commonly known as the Arctic beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces striking pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Pedicularis lapponica is adapted to cold climates and has a long flowering period, making it a notable species in its ecological niche.
