Paepalanthus sicifolius

Paepalanthus sicifolius, commonly known as the swordleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a feature common in arid environments, allowing it to conserve water efficiently.

Paepalanthus sicifolius is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often used in xerophytic gardens due to its hardy nature.
