Poa laxa var. vivipara

Poa laxa var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species Poa laxa.

It is characterized by its ability to produce viviparous shoots, which are capable of rooting and growing into new plants while still attached to the parent plant. This trait allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas efficiently. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its leaves are slender and grass-like, with a tufted growth habit that contributes to its ecological adaptability.
