Penstemon kennedyi

Penstemon kennedyi, commonly known as Kennedy's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.

Penstemon kennedyi is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is well-suited for xeriscaping.
