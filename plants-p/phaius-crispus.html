Phaius crispus

Phaius crispus, commonly known as the crested iris, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing clusters of pale blue to violet flowers with a distinctive crested appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Phaius crispus is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
