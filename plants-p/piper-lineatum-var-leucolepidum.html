Piper lineatum var. leucolepidum

Piper lineatum var. leucolepidum, commonly known as the white-veined pepper, is a variant of the black pepper plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white veins on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that mature to a dark red color, similar to other Piper species. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
