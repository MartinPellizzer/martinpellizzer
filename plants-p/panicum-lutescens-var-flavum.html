Panicum lutescens var. flavum

Panicum lutescens var. flavum, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, feather-like leaves and produces fluffy, yellowish to golden panicles during the flowering stage. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It is often used in landscapes for its attractive foliage and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
