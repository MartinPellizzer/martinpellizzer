Phaseolus wrightii

Phaseolus wrightii, commonly known as the Mexican bean or Wright's bean, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds, similar to common beans. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish and are borne in clusters.

Phaseolus wrightii is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is an important crop in traditional agriculture.
