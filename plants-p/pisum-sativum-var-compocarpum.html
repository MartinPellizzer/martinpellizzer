Pisum sativum var. compocarpum

Pisum sativum var. compocarpum, commonly known as the compact pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to standard pea varieties. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, similar to other pea species. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a flowering pattern typical of the Fabaceae family.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a space-efficient alternative in gardens.
