Pilosella capillata subsp. microvasconica

Pilosella capillata subsp.

microvasconica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
