Podocarpus pseudobracteatus var. pseudobracteatus

Podocarpus pseudobracteatus var. pseudobracteatus, commonly known as the Philippine podocarp, is a evergreen coniferous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as ornamental planting due to its aesthetic appeal.
