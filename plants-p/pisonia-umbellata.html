Pisonia umbellata

Pisonia umbellata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble coral, which bloom in dense clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Pisonia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
