Photinia × fraseri

Photinia × fraseri, commonly known as the roseola or Fraser photinia, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage that provides year-round color. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red, fuzzy berries that are attractive to birds. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

Photinia × fraseri thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively disease-resistant.
