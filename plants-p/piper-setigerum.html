Piper setigerum

Piper setigerum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark, peppercorn-like fruits. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture. The fruit, which matures to a dark color, is the source of the spice black pepper.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
