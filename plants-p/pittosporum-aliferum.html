Pittosporum aliferum

Pittosporum aliferum, commonly known as the New Zealand Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a fragrant scent. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in loose inflorescences, often appearing in spring.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
