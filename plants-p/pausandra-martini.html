Pausandra martini

Pausandra martini, commonly known as the narrowleaf pipsissewa, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils found in woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during late spring to early summer.

Pausandra martini is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
