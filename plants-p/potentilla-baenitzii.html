Potentilla baenitzii

Potentilla baenitzii, commonly known as the Baenitz's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla baenitzii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
