Papaver sendtneri var. roseolum

Papaver sendtneri var. roseolum, commonly known as the rose-colored poppy, is a rare and striking member of the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in alpine or rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers consist of a central crown of stamens surrounded by a single, showy pistil.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its unique coloration and rarity.
