Psittacanthus cardiphyllus

Psittacanthus cardiphyllus, commonly known as the golden mossy cycad, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and dense, feather-like foliage. The plant produces large, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
