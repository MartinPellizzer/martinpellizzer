Prenanthes lyrata

Prenanthes lyrata, commonly known as the lyrata water shield, is a herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas of North America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are floating on the water surface, with a distinct stalk connecting them to the submerged roots. The plant produces tiny white flowers in clusters, which bloom from July to September. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy layer that helps repel water, aiding in buoyancy.

Prenanthes lyrata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and invertebrates.
