Polygala quinqueangularis

Polygala quinqueangularis, commonly known as the five-angled milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
