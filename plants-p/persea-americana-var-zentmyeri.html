Persea americana var. zentmyeri

Persea americana var. zentmyeri, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. The fruit is rich in nutrients and contains high levels of antioxidants.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value in gardens.
