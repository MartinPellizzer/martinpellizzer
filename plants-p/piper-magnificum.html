Piper magnificum

Piper magnificum, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular ornamental plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.
