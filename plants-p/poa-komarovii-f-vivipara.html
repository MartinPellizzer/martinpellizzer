Poa komarovii f. vivipara

Poa komarovii f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its ability to produce viviparous shoots, which are capable of rooting and growing into new plants while still attached to the parent plant. This trait allows it to propagate efficiently in harsh environments where traditional seed dispersal may be limited. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing tufts with narrow, grass-like leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
