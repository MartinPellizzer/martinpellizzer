Phyteuma vagneri

Phyteuma vagneri, commonly known as the yellow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Phyteuma vagneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
