Prunus cerasifera f. nigra

Prunus cerasifera f. nigra, commonly known as the Black Cherry plum, is a cultivar of the Prunus cerasifera species, characterized by its dark, almost black fruit.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside, turning red or orange in the fall. The flowers are small, white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and the fruit is a drupe with a sweet, tart flavor.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its edible fruit.
