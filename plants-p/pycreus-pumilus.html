Pycreus pumilus

Pycreus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf pycreus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with compact, rosette-forming foliage. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
