Pasithea caerulea var. grandiflora

Pasithea caerulea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the blue pasithea, is a strikingly colorful plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that enhances its ornamental appeal. This variety thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
