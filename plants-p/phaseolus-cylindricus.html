Phaseolus cylindricus

Phaseolus cylindricus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers, which are followed by cylindrical, green pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with a rough texture.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious beans and ornamental value.
