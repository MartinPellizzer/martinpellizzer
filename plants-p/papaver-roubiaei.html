Papaver roubiaei

Papaver roubiaei, commonly known as the Crimean poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its vivid red or orange petals and a distinctive black center, which resembles a poppy's typical structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. It blooms in the summer months, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected in its natural habitat.
