Piper roxburghii

Piper roxburghii, commonly known as long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark red berries that are harvested when fully mature. The plant produces a pungent aroma due to the presence of volatile oils, particularly piperine, which contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy scent.

The fruit is typically used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
