Pseudolysimachion orchideum var. bulgaricum

Pseudolysimachion orchideum var. bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Balkan region.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features a short rhizome and possesses lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is slightly curled at the edges.

This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas with rich, humus-filled soil.
