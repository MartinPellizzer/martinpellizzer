Pogonia moritzii

Pogonia moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's pink, is a rare orchid species native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which bloom during the late summer months. The plant typically grows in swampy or marshy habitats, relying on specific soil conditions and moisture levels for survival. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Pogonia moritzii is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
