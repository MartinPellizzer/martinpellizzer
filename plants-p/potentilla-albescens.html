Potentilla albescens

Potentilla albescens, commonly known as white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Potentilla albescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
