Pilosocereus vilaboensis

Pilosocereus vilaboensis, commonly known as the Vilabo cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, spiny cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a thick, ribbed stem covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in the spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
