Peltaria capensis

Peltaria capensis, commonly known as the Cape peltaria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Peltaria capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
