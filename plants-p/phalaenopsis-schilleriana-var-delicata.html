Phalaenopsis schilleriana var. delicata

Phalaenopsis schilleriana var. delicata, commonly known as the Schiller's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other objects without deriving nutrients from them. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its blooming period typically occurs during the winter months, adding ornamental value to gardens and homes.
