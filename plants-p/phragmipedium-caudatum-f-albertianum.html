Phragmipedium caudatum f. albertianum

Phragmipedium caudatum f. albertianum, commonly known as the Albert's phragmipedium, is a hybrid orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that typically feature a striking combination of pink, white, and purple hues. The plant has long, slender pseudobulbs and linear leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with high air circulation and requires consistent moisture in its growing medium.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
