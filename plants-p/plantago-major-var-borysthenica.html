Plantago major var. borysthenica

Plantago major var. borysthenica, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its larger, broader leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a slightly rough texture.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
