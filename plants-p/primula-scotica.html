Primula scotica

Primula scotica, commonly known as the Scottish primrose, is a perennial herb native to the British Isles.

It typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and rocky outcrops. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. Its leaves are oval and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Primula scotica is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
