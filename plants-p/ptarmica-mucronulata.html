Ptarmica mucronulata

Ptarmica mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mountain sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or white, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are edible and have a tart, tangy flavor, making it a valued plant in traditional cuisines.

Ptarmica mucronulata is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
