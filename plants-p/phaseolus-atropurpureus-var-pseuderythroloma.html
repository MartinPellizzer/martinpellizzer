Phaseolus atropurpureus var. pseuderythroloma

Phaseolus atropurpureus var. pseuderythroloma, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variety of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet-colored pods and seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces edible beans that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional benefits.
