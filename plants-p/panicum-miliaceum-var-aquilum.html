Panicum miliaceum var. aquilum

Panicum miliaceum var. aquilum, commonly known as water millet, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains, which are harvested for food or fodder. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Water millet is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it an important crop in certain agricultural systems.
