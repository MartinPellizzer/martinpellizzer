Pyrus draconis

Pyrus draconis, commonly known as the dragon pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Pyrus draconis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
