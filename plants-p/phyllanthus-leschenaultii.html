Phyllanthus leschenaultii

Phyllanthus leschenaultii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a tropical shrub native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that turn bright orange when mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
