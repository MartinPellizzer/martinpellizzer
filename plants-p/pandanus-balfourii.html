Pandanus balfourii

Pandanus balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often droop downward. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in sandy or coastal soils.

Pandanus balfourii is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in many island communities.
