Piper subcordatum

Piper subcordatum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or subcordate pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, black peppercorns that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
