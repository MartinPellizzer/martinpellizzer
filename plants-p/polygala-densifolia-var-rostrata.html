Polygala densifolia var. rostrata

Polygala densifolia var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
