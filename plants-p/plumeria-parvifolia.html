Plumeria parvifolia

Plumeria parvifolia, commonly known as the frangipani tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, white, and pink. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Plumeria parvifolia is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
