Pandanus whitei

Pandanus whitei, commonly known as the White's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, palm-like structure with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its roots are used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
