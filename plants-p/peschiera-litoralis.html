Peschiera litoralis

Peschiera litoralis, commonly known as the coastal marsh gentian, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands and marshes of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often near the edges of lakes and coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often reddish in color.

Peschiera litoralis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insect species.
