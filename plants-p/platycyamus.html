Platycyamus

Platycyamus, commonly known as the broad-leaved cactus, is a unique plant species characterized by its wide, flat leaves that are adapted to store water.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. The plant features spines that provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are typically bright and showy, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Despite its cactus-like appearance, Platycyamus has a distinct morphology that sets it apart from other cacti.
