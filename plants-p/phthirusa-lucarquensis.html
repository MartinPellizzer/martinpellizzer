Phthirusa lucarquensis

Phthirusa lucarquensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Phthirusa lucarquensis is often found in rocky soils and is an important part of the local flora in its habitat.
