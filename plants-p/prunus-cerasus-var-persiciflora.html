Prunus cerasus var. persiciflora

Prunus cerasus var. persiciflora, commonly known as the Persian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and oval to elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, bright red fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
