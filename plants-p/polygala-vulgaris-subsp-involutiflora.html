Polygala vulgaris subsp. involutiflora

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

involutiflora, commonly known as the curled-flower milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often form dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in cylindrical spikes, which are characteristic of the genus Polygala. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and it thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
