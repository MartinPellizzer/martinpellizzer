Pomaderris ligustrina subsp. latifolia

Pomaderris ligustrina subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pomaderris, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and landscaping.
