Pimpinella isaurica subsp. isaurica

Pimpinella isaurica subsp.

isaurica, commonly known as the Isaurian cow-wheat, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky and limestone soils, often in open woodlands and alpine meadows.

The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers in umbels. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage, which contains volatile oils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
