Physostemon macrorhizus

Physostemon macrorhizus, commonly known as the large-rooted Physostemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is robust and thick, contributing to its common name and making it a resilient plant in various soil conditions.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in herbal remedies.
