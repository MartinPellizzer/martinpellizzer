Protea laurifolia

Protea laurifolia, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name, and its showy inflorescences that range from white to pink. The plant is part of the Proteaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking floral structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Protea laurifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
