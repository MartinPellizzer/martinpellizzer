Pulsatilla ludoviciana f. glabrata

Pulsatilla ludoviciana f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth pasqueflower, is a variant of the pasqueflower species native to the central and northern regions of the United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a rosette of basal leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually smooth and hairless, distinguishing it from other forms of the species.

The flower structure includes numerous stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.
