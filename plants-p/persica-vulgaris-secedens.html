Persica vulgaris secedens

Persica vulgaris secedens, commonly known as the common peach tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing showy pink or white flowers in the spring. The tree bears fruit known as peaches, which are fleshy, juicy, and typically orange or yellow in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Persica vulgaris secedens is widely cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
