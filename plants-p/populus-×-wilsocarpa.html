Populus × wilsocarpa

Populus × wilsocarpa, commonly known as the Weeping Poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a drooping appearance, giving it a graceful and ornamental look. The tree produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in early spring, which are followed by fluffy, cotton-like seeds dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

Populus × wilsocarpa is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained areas across its native range.
