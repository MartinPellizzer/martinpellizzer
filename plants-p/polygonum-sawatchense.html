Polygonum sawatchense

Polygonum sawatchense, commonly known as the Sawatch knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous regions.

Polygonum sawatchense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
