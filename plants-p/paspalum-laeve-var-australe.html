Paspalum laeve var. australe

Paspalum laeve var. australe, commonly known as Australian wallaby grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. It is often used in landscaping and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
