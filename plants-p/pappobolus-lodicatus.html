Pappobolus lodicatus

Pappobolus lodicatus, commonly known as the slender burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in bracts, which aid in its propagation. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

Pappobolus lodicatus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
