Pandanus exarmatus

Pandanus exarmatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, feathery foliage that resembles a bottlebrush. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, arranged in clusters at the tips of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in many island communities.
