Polygala mediterranea var. jordanovii

Polygala mediterranea var. jordanovii, commonly known as Jordan's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
