Potamogeton rutilus

Potamogeton rutilus, commonly known as slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
