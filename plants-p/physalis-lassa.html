Physalis lassa

Physalis lassa, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals, and the fruit develops in a papery calyx that persists after maturity.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible, nutrient-rich fruits.
