Podanthum lobelioides

Podanthum lobelioides, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its often dry habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
