Pittosporum paniculiferum

Pittosporum paniculiferum, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
