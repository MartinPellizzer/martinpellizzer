Pinalia punctata

Pinalia punctata, commonly known as the spotted pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the deserts of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a spherical or cushion-like shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Pinalia punctata is often found growing in rocky soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
