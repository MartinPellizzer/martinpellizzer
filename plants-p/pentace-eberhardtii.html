Pentace eberhardtii

Pentace eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt's pentace, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Pentaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
