Pachystela cinerea var. cuneata

Pachystela cinerea var. cuneata, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, silvery-white coating, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers with red-brown markings, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements in gardens.
