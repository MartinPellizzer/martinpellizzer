Philadelphus verrucosus

Philadelphus verrucosus, commonly known as the roughleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture, contributing to its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological landscapes.
