Pyrola denticulata

Pyrola denticulata, commonly known as the toothed wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive toothed margin, contributing to its common name.

Pyrola denticulata thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
