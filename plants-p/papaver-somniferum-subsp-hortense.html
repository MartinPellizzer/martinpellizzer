Papaver somniferum subsp. hortense

Papaver somniferum subsp.

hortense, commonly known as the garden poppy, is a cultivated variant of the opium poppy. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and pink.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces capsule fruits that contain the latex used in opium production. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is widely grown for ornamental purposes and has a long history in horticulture.
