Phelline

Phelline, commonly known as the Mediterranean hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, gray bark and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Phelline is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
