Passiflora retrorsa

Passiflora retrorsa, commonly known as the backward passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically ovate with a hairy surface, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Passiflora retrorsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
