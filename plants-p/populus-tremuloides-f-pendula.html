Populus tremuloides f. pendula

Populus tremuloides f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping trembling aspen, is a variant of the trembling aspen characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully.

This form exhibits a unique growth habit with long, cascading shoots that create a delicate, flowing appearance. Botanically, it retains the typical features of the species, including opposite leaves with serrated margins and a fibrous root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or wetlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in ecological succession in forested areas.
