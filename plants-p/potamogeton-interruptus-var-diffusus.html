Potamogeton interruptus var. diffusus

Potamogeton interruptus var. diffusus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, flexible stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often forming dense underwater vegetation. Its leaves are typically broad and arrowhead-shaped, with a smooth surface and a waxy coating. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

It is often found in slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and streams.
