Potentilla grayi

Potentilla grayi, commonly known as Gray's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided into five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla grayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and floral display.
