Polypodium sylvicola

Polypodium sylvicola, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that often grow on tree trunks or rocks. The plant typically has a short, creeping rhizome with numerous roots that anchor it to its substrate. Its leaves are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a light green color and a leathery texture.

Polypodium sylvicola thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
