Potentilla okuboi

Potentilla okuboi, commonly known as the Okuboi cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that mature in the fall.

Potentilla okuboi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
