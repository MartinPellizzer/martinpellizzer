Piper coeloneurum

Piper coeloneurum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive spread and ecological impact.
