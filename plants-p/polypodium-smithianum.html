Polypodium smithianum

Polypodium smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, strap-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces spores in clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

This fern is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
