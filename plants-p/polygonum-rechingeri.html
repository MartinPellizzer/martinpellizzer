Polygonum rechingeri

Polygonum rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple in color, appearing in late summer.

Polygonum rechingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
