Pleioluma vieillardii

Pleioluma vieillardii, commonly known as the Vieillard's pleioluma, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

Native to the high-altitude regions of Madagascar, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of dark violet to purple flowers with intricate, pleated petals. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for protective measures.
