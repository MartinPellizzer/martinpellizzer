Pera arborea

Pera arborea, commonly known as the tree of life, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine. Its bark contains compounds with potential pharmacological properties.

Pera arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
