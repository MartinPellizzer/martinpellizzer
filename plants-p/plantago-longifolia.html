Plantago longifolia

Plantago longifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Plantago longifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
