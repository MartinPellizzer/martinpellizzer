Primulina lungzhouensis

Primulina lungzhouensis, commonly known as the Lungzhou Begonia, is a rare and unique species native to China.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, thriving in the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in shades of red or pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
