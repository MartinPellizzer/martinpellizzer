Plagiogyria distinctissima

Plagiogyria distinctissima, commonly known as the distinct fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, marshy habitats and is found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, producing tiny, spike-like inflorescences.

Plagiogyria distinctissima is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize wetland soils and support diverse insect life.
