Primula farinosa var. modesta

Primula farinosa var. modesta, commonly known as the modest primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a slight pink or red tinge. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
