Pterocaulon polypterum

Pterocaulon polypterum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is well adapted to tropical climates. Its leaves are often rosette-shaped, and it produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of purple, blue, and white.

Pterocaulon polypterum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
