Polygonum confusum

Polygonum confusum, commonly known as the fuzzy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and often have a slightly wavy margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white to reddish in color, blooming from late summer to early fall.

This plant thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
