Prunus × lannesiana f. horinji

Prunus × lannesiana f. horinji, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a stunning visual display. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, resulting in a unique combination of traits. It is widely cultivated in Japan and other parts of East Asia for ornamental purposes.

The botanical features include its small, oval leaves and a moderate growth habit, making it suitable for gardens and parks.
