Protea lemairei subsp. trichophylla

Protea lemairei subsp.

trichophylla, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant characterized by its large, showy inflorescences. It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is native to the coastal regions of the Western Cape.

The plant features a woody stem with broad, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. Its flower heads are composed of numerous small, papery flowers arranged in a spherical shape.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
