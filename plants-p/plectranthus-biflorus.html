Plectranthus biflorus

Plectranthus biflorus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically purple or violet, with two lips and a tubular corolla, characteristic of the mint family.

Plectranthus biflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
