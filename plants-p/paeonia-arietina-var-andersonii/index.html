Paeonia Arietina Var. Andersonii

Anderson's peony, scientifically known as Paeonia arietina var. andersonii, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It is prized for its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, displaying a range of colors from white to deep red. This variety is a hybrid of the common peony, known for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates. Its unique characteristics make it a sought-after addition to botanical gardens and private collections. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts to protect such rare species.
