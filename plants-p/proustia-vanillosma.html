Proustia vanillosma

Proustia vanillosma, commonly known as the vanilla proustia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white blossoms that emit a subtle vanilla-like scent, which is a key feature of its botanical identity. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Proustia vanillosma thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and aromatic properties.
