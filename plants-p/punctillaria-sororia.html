Punctillaria sororia

Punctillaria sororia, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a small aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features delicate, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, floating just beneath the water's surface.

Punctillaria sororia is often found in nutrient-rich environments and plays a role in maintaining aquatic ecosystems.
