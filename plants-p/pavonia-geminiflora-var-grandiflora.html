Pavonia geminiflora var. grandiflora

Pavonia geminiflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with slender, hairy stems and pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
