Primula villosa var. norica

Primula villosa var. norica, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high elevations. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellow flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
