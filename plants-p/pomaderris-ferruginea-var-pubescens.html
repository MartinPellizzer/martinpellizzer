Pomaderris ferruginea var. pubescens

Pomaderris ferruginea var. pubescens, commonly known as the rusty pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular-hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a reddish-brown underside. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

This plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
