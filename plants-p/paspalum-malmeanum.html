Paspalum malmeanum

Paspalum malmeanum, commonly known as the Malmean grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Paspalum malmeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
