Pandanus crassiaculeatus

Pandanus crassiaculeatus, commonly known as the thick-leaved pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its robust, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, showing adaptability to saline and waterlogged soils.
