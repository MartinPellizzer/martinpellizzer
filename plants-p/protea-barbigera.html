Protea barbigera

Protea barbigera, commonly known as the woolly protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers surrounded by a dense, woolly bract. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Protea barbigera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
