Pleurothallis tristis

Pleurothallis tristis, commonly known as the weeping orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that hang from the stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with a subtle fragrance.

Pleurothallis tristis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
