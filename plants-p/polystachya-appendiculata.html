Polystachya appendiculata

Polystachya appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate sword fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polystachyaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and slender, sword-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in fine, hairy structures, and the fronds are usually dark green with a leathery texture.

Polystachya appendiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its elegant appearance.
