Paspalum triglume

Paspalum triglume, commonly known as three-awn grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with three awns on each lemma, giving it its common name. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Paspalum triglume is also used in some regions for forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature.
