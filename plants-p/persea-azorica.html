Persea azorica

Persea azorica, commonly known as the Azores persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Azores archipelago.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans due to their bitter taste. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local biodiversity.
