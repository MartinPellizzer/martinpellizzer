Polygala vulgaris f. ciliata

Polygala vulgaris f. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal dunes or grasslands.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
