Peperomia adenocarpa

Peperomia adenocarpa, commonly known as the glandular peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, thick, waxy leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia adenocarpa is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
