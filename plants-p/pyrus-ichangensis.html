Pyrus ichangensis

Pyrus ichangensis, commonly known as the Ichang pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing showy white flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a pear-like apple, which is edible and has a sweet flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its fruit in certain regions.
