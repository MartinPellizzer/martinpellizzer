Passiflora quadrangularis var. sulcata

Passiflora quadrangularis var. sulcata, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rough, quadrangular stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. Its fruit is a hairy, green, egg-shaped berry that turns yellow when mature.

The fruit contains a soft, creamy pulp with numerous black seeds, and it is valued for its flavor and medicinal properties.
