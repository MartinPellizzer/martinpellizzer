Pandanus sylvicola

Pandanus sylvicola, commonly known as the forest pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the pandan family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
