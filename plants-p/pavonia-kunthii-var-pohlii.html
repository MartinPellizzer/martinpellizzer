Pavonia kunthii var. pohlii

Pavonia kunthii var. pohlii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple bracts that surround the small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
