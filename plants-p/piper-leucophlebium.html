Piper leucophlebium

Piper leucophlebium, commonly known as the white-veined pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its finely veined, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
