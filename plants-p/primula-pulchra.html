Primula pulchra

Primula pulchra, commonly known as the beauty flower, is a species of flowering plant in the family Primulaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

The foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.
