Pelargonium peltatum var. scutatum

Pelargonium peltatum var. scutatum, commonly known as the scutate ivy geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense foliage.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive scutate (shield-like) pattern, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular in hanging baskets and as a decorative plant due to its lush appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
