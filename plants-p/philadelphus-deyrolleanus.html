Philadelphus deyrolleanus

Philadelphus deyrolleanus, commonly known as the Deyrolle's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant scent and showy blooms.
