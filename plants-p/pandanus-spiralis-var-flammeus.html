Pandanus spiralis var. flammeus

Pandanus spiralis var. flammeus, commonly known as the flame pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, spiraling leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while the fruit is edible and used in local cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in its native regions.
