Phaseolus monophyllus var. paraguariensis

Phaseolus monophyllus var. paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It produces small, green pods that contain a single, bean-like seed. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. Yerba mate is cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a traditional caffeinated infusion.

It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often grown in shaded conditions.
