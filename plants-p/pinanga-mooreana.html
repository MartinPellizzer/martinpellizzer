Pinanga mooreana

Pinanga mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's bower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are a source of nectar for pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warm climates.
