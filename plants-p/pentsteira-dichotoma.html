Pentsteira dichotoma

Pentsteira dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous pentsteira, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal parts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are simple and opposite, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Pentsteira dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
