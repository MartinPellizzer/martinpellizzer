Parahebe lyallii

Parahebe lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's parahebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
