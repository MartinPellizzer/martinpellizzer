Panicularia nervata var. stricta

Panicularia nervata var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
