Phaseolus pedatus

Phaseolus pedatus, commonly known as the creeping pole bean, is a climbing legume native to Central America.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are trifoliate, typical of many Phaseolus species. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and compound leaves with stipules.

The flowers are usually white or pale purple, and they are pollinated by insects.
