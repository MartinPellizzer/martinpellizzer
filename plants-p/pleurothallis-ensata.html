Pleurothallis ensata

Pleurothallis ensata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
