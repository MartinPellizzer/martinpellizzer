Pinacantha porandica

Pinacantha porandica, commonly known as the Porandic Starbloom, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Porandia.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a short, woody stem and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its limited habitat, it is considered an endangered species.
