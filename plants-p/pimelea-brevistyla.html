Pimelea brevistyla

Pimelea brevistyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, cylindrical flower spikes that are often bright red or orange in color. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight waxy coating, and the plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

It is often found in heathlands and open forests, and it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
