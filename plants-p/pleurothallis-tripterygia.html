Pleurothallis tripterygia

Pleurothallis tripterygia, commonly known as the three-winged orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Pleurothallis tripterygia is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
