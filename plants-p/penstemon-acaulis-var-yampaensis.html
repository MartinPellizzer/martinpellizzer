Penstemon acaulis var. yampaensis

Penstemon acaulis var. yampaensis, commonly known as Yampa mountain beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine environments. It has hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

This variety is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in open meadows and along mountain slopes.
