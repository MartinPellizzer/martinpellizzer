Pilea parietaria var. hispaniolensis

Pilea parietaria var. hispaniolensis, commonly known as the Spanish wall plant, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It is adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
