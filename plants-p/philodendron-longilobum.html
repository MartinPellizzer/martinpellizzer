Philodendron longilobum

Philodendron longilobum, commonly known as the long-lobed philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with deep lobes, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Philodendron longilobum is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
