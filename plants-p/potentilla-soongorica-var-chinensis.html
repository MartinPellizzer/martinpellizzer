Potentilla soongorica var. chinensis

Potentilla soongorica var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to regions of China and Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
