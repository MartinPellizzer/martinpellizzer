Primula hirsuta subsp. valcuvianensis

Primula hirsuta subsp.

valcuvianensis, commonly known as the Valcuvian alpine primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features hairy, glandular leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
