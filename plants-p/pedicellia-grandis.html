Pedicellia grandis

Pedicellia grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white to pinkish blooms that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. Its flowers are borne on long, slender pedicels, giving it its scientific name.

Pedicellia grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its impressive floral display.
