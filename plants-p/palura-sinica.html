Palura sinica

Palura sinica, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or pink throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped blooms and medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
