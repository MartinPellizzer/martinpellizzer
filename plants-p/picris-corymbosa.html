Picris corymbosa

Picris corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
