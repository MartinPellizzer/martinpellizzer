Poa pulchella var. major

Poa pulchella var. major, commonly known as the mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
