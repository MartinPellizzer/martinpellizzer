Proustia cuneifolia f. tipia

Proustia cuneifolia f. tipia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf proustia, is a rare and unique variant of the proustia genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and is of interest to plant enthusiasts and researchers alike.
