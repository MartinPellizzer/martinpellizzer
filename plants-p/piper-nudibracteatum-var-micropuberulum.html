Piper nudibracteatum var. micropuberulum

Piper nudibracteatum var. micropuberulum, commonly known as the small-hairy pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

This variety is distinguished by its fine, short hairs on the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
