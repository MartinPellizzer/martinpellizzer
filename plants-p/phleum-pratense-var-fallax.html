Phleum pratense var. fallax

Phleum pratense var. fallax, commonly known as fallax meadow fescue, is a variant of meadow fescue native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, temperate climates, often found in meadows and pastures. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for environments with moderate to high moisture levels.
