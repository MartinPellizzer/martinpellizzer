Paepalanthus yapacanensis var. hirsutus

Paepalanthus yapacanensis var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy peacock flower, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, forming a rosette that holds water, a feature common in bromeliads.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and eye-catching appearance.
