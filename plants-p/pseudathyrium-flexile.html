Pseudathyrium flexile

Pseudathyrium flexile, commonly known as the flexible fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, pinnately compound fronds with a flexible, arching habit. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Pseudathyrium flexile is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
