Pterygoloma cristatum

Pterygoloma cristatum, commonly known as the crowned pterygoloma, is a unique member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive crown-like structure at the base of its stems. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
