Pterocarpus steinbachianus

Pterocarpus steinbachianus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it produces woody pods containing flat, dark seeds.

The wood is highly valued for its rich color and durability, making it sought after in the furniture and musical instrument industries.
