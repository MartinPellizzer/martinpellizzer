Pultenaea elliptica var. oblongifolia

Pultenaea elliptica var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, often hairy stems and narrow, elliptic leaves that are typically a bright yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers during spring, which are characteristic of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
