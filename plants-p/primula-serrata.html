Primula serrata

Primula serrata, commonly known as the serrated primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
