Persicaria tripterocarpa

Persicaria tripterocarpa, commonly known as the three-winged knotweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Persicaria tripterocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
