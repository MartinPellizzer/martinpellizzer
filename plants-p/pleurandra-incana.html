Pleurandra incana

Pleurandra incana, commonly known as the silverleaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage.
