Piper velutinobracteosum

Piper velutinobracteosum, commonly known as the velvet bract pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white, and the fruit is a three-lobed berry.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
