Phyllanthus bernierianus var. bernierianus

Phyllanthus bernierianus var. bernierianus, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, followed by greenish, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
