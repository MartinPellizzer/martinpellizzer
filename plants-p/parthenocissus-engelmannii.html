Parthenocissus engelmannii

Parthenocissus engelmannii, commonly known as the Virginia creeper, is a deciduous vine native to North America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which typically have five to seven lobes, and its ability to climb using adhesive pads on its tendrils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the summer, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its foliage turns vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The Virginia creeper is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
