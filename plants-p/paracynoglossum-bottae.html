Paracynoglossum bottae

Paracynoglossum bottae, commonly known as the velvetleaf or bottle plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Paracynoglossum bottae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
