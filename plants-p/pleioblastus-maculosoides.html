Pleioblastus maculosoides

Pleioblastus maculosoides, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, featuring slender, arching stems with a distinctive greyish-green color. The leaves are long, narrow, and have a slightly rough texture, often with a bluish tint. This plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often used in landscaping for its graceful, upright growth habit.
