Pilosella aurantiaca subsp. decolorans

Pilosella aurantiaca subsp.

decolorans, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant features a low-growing habit with hairy stems and bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
