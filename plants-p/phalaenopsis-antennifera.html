Phalaenopsis antennifera

Phalaenopsis antennifera, commonly known as the orchid with the antenna-like leaves, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that resemble antennae, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures rather than in soil.

Phalaenopsis antennifera is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and ease of care.
