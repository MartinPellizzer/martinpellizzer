Pithecellobium microcalyx

Pithecellobium microcalyx, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody, thorny stems and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
