Platyloma cordatum

Platyloma cordatum, commonly known as the heart-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as under forest canopies. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a rhizomatous root system.

Platyloma cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
