Philodendron × corsianum

Philodendron × corsianum, commonly known as the Corsican philodendron, is a hybrid plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its lush foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
