Planera richardii

Planera richardii, commonly known as the Mexican buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has some ornamental value due to its compact size and foliage.
