Phlomoides codonantha

Phlomoides codonantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
