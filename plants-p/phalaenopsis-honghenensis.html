Phalaenopsis honghenensis

Phalaenopsis honghenensis, commonly known as the Hong Kong orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to light pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for floral arrangements and indoor gardening.
