Paepalanthus kunhardtii

Paepalanthus kunhardtii, commonly known as the Kunhardt's pincushion cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that resemble a pincushion, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
