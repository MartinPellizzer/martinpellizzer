Prunus chamaecerasus pendula

Prunus chamaecerasus pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that droop gracefully, creating a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, typically pink or white, which are followed by small, edible drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a favored choice for landscape design due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
