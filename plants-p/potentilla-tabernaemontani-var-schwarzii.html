Potentilla tabernaemontani var. schwarzii

Potentilla tabernaemontani var. schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, which are typically hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
