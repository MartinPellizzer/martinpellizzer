Paeonia integra

Paeonia integra, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is known for its large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves composed of 5-7 lobes, and its stems are often covered with a dense layer of brownish-red bark. Paeonia integra is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, fragrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
