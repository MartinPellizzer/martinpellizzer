Podocarpus neoprumnopitys

Podocarpus neoprumnopitys, commonly known as the New Zealand podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters in its natural habitat. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
