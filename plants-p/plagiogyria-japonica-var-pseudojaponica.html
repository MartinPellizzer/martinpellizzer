Plagiogyria japonica var. pseudojaponica

Plagiogyria japonica var. pseudojaponica, commonly known as the Japanese plagiogyria, is a variant of the plagiogyria genus native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowers.
