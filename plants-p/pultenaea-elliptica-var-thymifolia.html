Pultenaea elliptica var. thymifolia

Pultenaea elliptica var. thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small yellow flowers arranged in spherical or cylindrical clusters, typical of the genus Pultenaea. Its leaves are aromatic, with a scent reminiscent of thyme, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and heathlands.
