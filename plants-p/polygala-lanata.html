Polygala lanata

Polygala lanata, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Polygala lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blossoms.
