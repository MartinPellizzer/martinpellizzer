Phyllocladus glaucus

Phyllocladus glaucus, commonly known as the silver pine, is a gymnosperm native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring scale-like leaves and woody cones. The plant has a distinctive silver-blue appearance due to the waxy coating on its foliage. It grows in a variety of habitats, including forests and rocky areas.

Phyllocladus glaucus is valued for its ecological role and is also used in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
