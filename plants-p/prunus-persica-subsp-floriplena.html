Prunus persica subsp. floriplena

Prunus persica subsp.

floriplena, commonly known as the double-flowered peach, is a cultivar characterized by its unique floral morphology. It features multiple layers of petals, creating a dense, showy bloom that distinguishes it from typical peaches.

The plant retains the general structure of the common peach, with similar fruiting habits and growth patterns. Its flowers are typically pink or white, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This subspecies is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to produce edible fruit under suitable conditions.
