Pentas zanzibarica subsp. milangiana

Pentas zanzibarica subsp.

milangiana, commonly known as the Zanzibar pentas, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its vibrant, star-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white or yellow center.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
