Panicum flavidum

Panicum flavidum, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and produces fluffy, yellowish inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for ornamental and erosion control purposes. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes contribute to its adaptability in various environments.
