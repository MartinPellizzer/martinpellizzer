Potentilla freyniana

Potentilla freyniana, commonly known as Freyn's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Freyn's cinquefoil thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
