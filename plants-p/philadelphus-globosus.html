Philadelphus globosus

Philadelphus globosus, commonly known as the globeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered white or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets and produces small, berry-like fruits that ripen in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
