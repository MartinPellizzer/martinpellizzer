Palicourea subtomentosa subsp. subtomentosa

Palicourea subtomentosa subsp.

subtomentosa, commonly known as the hairy palicourea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that range in color from red to black. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
