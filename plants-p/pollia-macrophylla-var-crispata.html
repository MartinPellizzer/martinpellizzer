Pollia macrophylla var. crispata

Pollia macrophylla var. crispata, commonly known as the curly-leaved orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and often curling leaves, which can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant produces showy, orchid-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional horticulture.
