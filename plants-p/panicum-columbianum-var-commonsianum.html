Panicum columbianum var. commonsianum

Panicum columbianum var. commonsianum, commonly known as the common panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, thread-like leaves, which are typically pale green to bluish-green in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
