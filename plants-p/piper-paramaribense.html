Piper paramaribense

Piper paramaribense, commonly known as the Paramaribo pepper or black pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that are harvested for their aromatic seeds, which are used as a spice. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem, opposite leaves, and a climbing or shrub-like growth habit.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
