Panicum texanum

Panicum texanum, commonly known as Texas dropseed, is a native grass species of the United States, particularly found in Texas and parts of the southern United States.

It is a low-growing, clump-forming perennial that produces slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native plant gardens. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for pollinators.

Its ornamental value comes from its fine texture and graceful appearance, especially during the flowering season.
