Petrocodon laxicymosus

Petrocodon laxicymosus, commonly known as the rock larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.
