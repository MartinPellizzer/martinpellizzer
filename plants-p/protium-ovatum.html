Protium ovatum

Protium ovatum, commonly known as the Indian beech or talaud, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
