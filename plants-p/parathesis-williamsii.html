Parathesis williamsii

Parathesis williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' parathesis, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the early morning hours. Its growth habit is predominantly epiphytic, meaning it thrives by growing on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Parathesis williamsii is considered a conservation concern.
