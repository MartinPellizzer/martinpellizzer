Pitcairnia fractifolia

Pitcairnia fractifolia, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of epiphytic cactus native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems that often form a bushy, branching structure. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Pitcairnia fractifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
