Plectronia ciliata

Plectronia ciliata, commonly known as the silky plectronia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests. Its scientific name reflects its feathery appearance, with "ciliata" referring to the finely toothed edges of its leaves.

Plectronia ciliata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
