Pseuderanthemum bradtkei

Pseuderanthemum bradtkei, commonly known as the Bradtke's false anthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, or yellow. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
