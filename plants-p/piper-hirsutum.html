Piper hirsutum

Piper hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
