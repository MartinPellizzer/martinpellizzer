Papaver subumbilicatum

Papaver subumbilicatum, commonly known as the umbilicated poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep red, often with a distinctive central depression. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
