Plectrotropis striata

Plectrotropis striata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It is often found in grasslands and along dry streambeds.

Plectrotropis striata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
