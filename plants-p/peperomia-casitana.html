Peperomia casitana

Peperomia casitana, commonly known as the Casitana peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with green and silver tones. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, arranged in spikes.

Peperomia casitana is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
