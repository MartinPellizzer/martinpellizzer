Pelexia corymbosa

Pelexia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and tall, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
