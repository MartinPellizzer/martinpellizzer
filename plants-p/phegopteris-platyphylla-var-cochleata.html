Phegopteris platyphylla var. cochleata

Phegopteris platyphylla var. cochleata, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a variant of the broad-leafed water fern.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally coiled fronds that give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually bright green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

This fern is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
