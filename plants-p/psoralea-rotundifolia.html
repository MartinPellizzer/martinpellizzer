Psoralea rotundifolia

Psoralea rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and the leaves are rounded, which gives it its common name.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
