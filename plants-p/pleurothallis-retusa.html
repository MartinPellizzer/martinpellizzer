Pleurothallis retusa

Pleurothallis retusa, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or branches. The plant produces delicate, spider-like flowers in shades of white and green, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleurothallis retusa is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
