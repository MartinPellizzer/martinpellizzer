Polystichum spinulosum subsp. dilatatum

Polystichum spinulosum subsp.

dilatatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf wood fern, is a fern species native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped fronds that are often slightly curled at the edges.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin. It prefers moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with adequate moisture and partial shade.
