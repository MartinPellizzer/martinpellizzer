Petalidium variabile var. variabile

Petalidium variabile var. variabile, commonly known as the variable petalidium, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy coating, which helps to retain moisture.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
