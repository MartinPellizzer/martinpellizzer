Potamogeton exstipulatus

Potamogeton exstipulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, which emerge from the water surface. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

Potamogeton exstipulatus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
