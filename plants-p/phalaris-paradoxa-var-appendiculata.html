Phalaris paradoxa var. appendiculata

Phalaris paradoxa var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with blue or purple at the base. The plant produces spikelets that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, with the lower florets often bearing short, hairy bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
