Paulownia × taiwaniana

Paulownia × taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green in summer. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are usually pale blue or purple. Its wood is prized for its light weight and strength, making it valuable in woodworking.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to urban environments.
