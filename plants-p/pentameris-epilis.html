Pentameris epilis

Pentameris epilis, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are opposite and often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
