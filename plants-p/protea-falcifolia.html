Protea falcifolia

Protea falcifolia, commonly known as the sickle-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small flowers, often in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as bloom. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea falcifolia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
