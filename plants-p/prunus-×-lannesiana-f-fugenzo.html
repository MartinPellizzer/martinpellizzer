Prunus × lannesiana f. fugenzo

Prunus × lannesiana f. fugenzo, commonly known as the Fugenzo cherry, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red or orange berries in late summer. Its foliage turns vibrant red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

The Fugenzo cherry is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
