Pinalia foliosa

Pinalia foliosa, commonly known as the leafy pine, is a unique plant species characterized by its dense, foliage-covered structure.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, where it has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, providing efficient water retention. Its growth habit resembles that of a small shrub, with a compact form and a thick, fibrous root system.

Pinalia foliosa is valued for its resilience and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
