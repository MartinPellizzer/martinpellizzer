Pinus lambertiana var. brevifolia

Pinus lambertiana var. brevifolia, commonly known as the sugar pine, is a variant of the sugar pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and large, cone-shaped seeds. The tree can grow to impressive heights, often reaching over 200 feet, making it one of the tallest pine species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against forest fires.

This variety is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in mixed coniferous forests.
