Pyxidium

Pyxidium, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flower clusters that resemble small raindrops. The plant is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Pyxidium species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant blooms and unique foliage.

They also have traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye in some cultures.
