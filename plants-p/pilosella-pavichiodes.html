Pilosella pavichiodes

Pilosella pavichiodes, commonly known as the Italian goatherb, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "pilosella," meaning "hairy.".

The flowers are usually yellow or white, and the plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
