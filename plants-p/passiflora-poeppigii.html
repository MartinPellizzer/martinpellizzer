Passiflora poeppigii

Passiflora poeppigii, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora poeppigii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
