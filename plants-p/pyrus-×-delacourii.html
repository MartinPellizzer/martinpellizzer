Pyrus × delacourii

Pyrus × delacourii, commonly known as the Delacour pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus betulaefolia (Chinese white pear).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 4–6 meters, featuring broad, lobed leaves and clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear with a sweet and aromatic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
