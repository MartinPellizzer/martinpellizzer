Papaver rupifragum var. atlanticum

Papaver rupifragum var. atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic poppy, is a hardy perennial plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions. The plant features striking, bright red, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging coastal environments.
