Potentilla puberula

Potentilla puberula, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Potentilla puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
