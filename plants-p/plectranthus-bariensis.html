Plectranthus bariensis

Plectranthus bariensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green or purple in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, often appearing in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Plectranthus bariensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
