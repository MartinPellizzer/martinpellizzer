Phyllodoce longifolia

Phyllodoce longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf phyllodoce, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested environments.

Phyllodoce longifolia plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing cover and food for various wildlife species.
