Piper subsilvulanum

Piper subsilvulanum, commonly known as the forest pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by small, three-lobed berries.

Piper subsilvulanum is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
