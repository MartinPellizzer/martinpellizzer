Polygala polygama var. obtusata

Polygala polygama var. obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a blunt or rounded tip, giving it its varietal name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
