Phylica parvula

Phylica parvula, commonly known as the silverleaf phylica, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phylica parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
