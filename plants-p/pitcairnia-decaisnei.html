Pitcairnia decaisnei

Pitcairnia decaisnei, commonly known as the Decaisne's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common adaptation among bromeliads. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant purple or red flowers. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
