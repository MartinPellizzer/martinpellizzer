Paspalum aureum

Paspalum aureum, commonly known as golden paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer. This plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit and is well adapted to moist, acidic soils.

Paspalum aureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
