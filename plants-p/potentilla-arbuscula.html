Potentilla arbuscula

Potentilla arbuscula, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

Potentilla arbuscula is valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
