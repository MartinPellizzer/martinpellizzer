Prunus griffithii var. tianshanica

Prunus griffithii var. tianshanica, commonly known as the Tianshan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Tianshan Mountains in China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in late summer, and the plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid garden environments.
