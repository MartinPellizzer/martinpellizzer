Pyrrhocactus sanjuanensis

Pyrrhocactus sanjuanensis, commonly known as the San Juan cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
