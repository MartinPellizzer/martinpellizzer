Pinus merkusii subsp. latteri

Pinus merkusii subsp.

latteri, commonly known as the Sumatran pine, is a subspecies of the pine tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and cones that are typically reddish-brown in color.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. It plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Additionally, its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
