Passiflora hamosa

Passiflora hamosa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, white, and blue hues. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora hamosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
