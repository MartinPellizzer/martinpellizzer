Peperomia sublaxiflora

Peperomia sublaxiflora, commonly known as the woolly peperomia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, evergreen perennial that features thick, fleshy leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
