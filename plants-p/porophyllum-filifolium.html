Porophyllum filifolium

Porophyllum filifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Porophyllum filifolium is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
