Physalis peruviana

Physalis peruviana, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange, berry-like fruits that are encased in a papery calyx. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into the distinctive, lantern-shaped fruit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible, tangy fruit.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
