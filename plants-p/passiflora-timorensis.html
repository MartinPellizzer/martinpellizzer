Passiflora timorensis

Passiflora timorensis, commonly known as the Timor passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed locally. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
