Phlomis carica

Phlomis carica, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish, fuzzy leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Phlomis carica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
