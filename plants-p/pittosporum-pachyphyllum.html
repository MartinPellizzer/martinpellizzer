Pittosporum pachyphyllum

Pittosporum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to its pale flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
