Pavia × versicolor

Pavia × versicolor, commonly known as the hybrid pavia, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Pavia capitata and Pavia rhombifolia.

It is a shrub native to the southern United States, particularly Texas, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, Pavia × versicolor is often used in landscaping and horticultural gardens.
