Phalaris paradoxa

Phalaris paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces dense, tufted clumps with long, narrow leaves and spikes of small, pale blue or greenish flowers. Its unique growth habit and striking appearance make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.

Phalaris paradoxa is also valued for its ornamental qualities in garden settings.
