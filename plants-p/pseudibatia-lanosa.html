Pseudibatia lanosa

Pseudibatia lanosa, commonly known as the woolly false holly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodlands. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
