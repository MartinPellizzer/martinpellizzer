Polianthes sessiliflora

Polianthes sessiliflora, commonly known as the Mexican lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It features tall, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact size and is often grown for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Polianthes sessiliflora is a perennial species that is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
