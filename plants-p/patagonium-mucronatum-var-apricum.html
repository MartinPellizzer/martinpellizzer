Patagonium mucronatum var. apricum

Patagonium mucronatum var. apricum, commonly known as the Patagonian apricot.

This plant is a shrub native to the high Andes of Argentina and Chile, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, apricot-like fruits that are rich in nutrients. The plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to extreme climates and its role in local ecosystems.
