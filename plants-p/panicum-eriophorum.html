Panicum eriophorum

Panicum eriophorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Panicum eriophorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
