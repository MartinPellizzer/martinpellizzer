Prunus mume var. typica

Prunus mume var. typica, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear, showcasing shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its cultural significance is notable in traditional Chinese and Japanese horticulture.
