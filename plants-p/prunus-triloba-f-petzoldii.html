Prunus triloba f. petzoldii

Prunus triloba f. petzoldii, commonly known as the Petzold's dwarf cherry, is a compact, shrub-like form of the Prunus triloba species.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically a glossy green and its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red or black fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated for consumption. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

This cultivar is often used in landscape design due to its compact size and seasonal visual interest.
