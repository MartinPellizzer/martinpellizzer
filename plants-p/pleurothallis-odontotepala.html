Pleurothallis odontotepala

Pleurothallis odontotepala, commonly known as the toothed lip orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive toothed lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are arranged in a spiral pattern around its stem.

Pleurothallis odontotepala is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and subtle beauty.
