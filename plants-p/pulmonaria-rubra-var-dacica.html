Pulmonaria rubra var. dacica

Pulmonaria rubra var. dacica, commonly known as the Dacian lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with distinctive silver-white markings, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and early spring blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
