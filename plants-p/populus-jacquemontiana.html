Populus jacquemontiana

Populus jacquemontiana, commonly known as the Jacquemont's poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
