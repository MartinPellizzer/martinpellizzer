Prostanthera semiteres

Prostanthera semiteres, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Prostanthera semiteres is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
