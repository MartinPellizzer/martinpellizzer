Potentilla multiceps

Potentilla multiceps, commonly known as the many-fruited cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, red or orange fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Potentilla multiceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
