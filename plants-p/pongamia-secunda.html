Pongamia secunda

Pongamia secunda, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by flat, woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and has traditional uses in various cultural practices.
