Pycreus squarrosus

Pycreus squarrosus, commonly known as the rough pycreus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in a cluster that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
