Pothos scandens var. sumatranus

Pothos scandens var. sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with shades of yellow and green. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using aerial roots. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and vertical landscapes.
