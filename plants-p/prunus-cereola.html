Prunus cereola

Prunus cereola, commonly known as the Chinese date or longan, is a deciduous tree native to southern China, Southeast Asia, and northern India.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by edible, fleshy drupes that range in color from yellow to reddish-purple. The fruit is valued for its sweet, juicy pulp and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
