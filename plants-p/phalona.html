Phalona

Phalona, commonly known as the star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Phalona species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette.
