Pelargonium × rowanium

Pelargonium × rowanium, commonly known as the geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × rowanium is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
