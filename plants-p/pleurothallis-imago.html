Pleurothallis imago

Pleurothallis imago, commonly known as the "Imago Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a striking combination of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually short-lived, opening in the early morning and closing by midday.

Pleurothallis imago is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
