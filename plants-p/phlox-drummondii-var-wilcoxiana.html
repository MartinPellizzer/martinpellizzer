Phlox drummondii var. wilcoxiana

Phlox drummondii var. wilcoxiana, commonly known as the Wilcox morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red and are typically trumpet-shaped. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its blooming period occurs in the spring and early summer, adding a striking visual element to the garden.
