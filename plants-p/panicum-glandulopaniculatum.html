Panicum glandulopaniculatum

Panicum glandulopaniculatum, commonly known as the glandular panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lobed glumes and spikelets that are often covered with glandular hairs, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological studies in its native habitats.
