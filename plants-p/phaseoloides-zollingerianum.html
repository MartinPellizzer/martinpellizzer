Phaseoloides zollingerianum

Phaseoloides zollingerianum, commonly known as the Zollinger's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, round, green pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.
