Psidium spodophyllum

Psidium spodophyllum, commonly known as the red mulberry, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its oval, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red when mature, though they can vary in color depending on the species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
