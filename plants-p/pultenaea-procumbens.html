Pultenaea procumbens

Pultenaea procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate pultenaea, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, spreading stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically features compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Pultenaea procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
