Ptilotus stirlingii var. laxus

Ptilotus stirlingii var. laxus, commonly known as the silky bush morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
