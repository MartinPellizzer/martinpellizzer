Pellaea andromedifolia f. pubescens

Pellaea andromedifolia f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent silver maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its fronds.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features delicate, bipinnate fronds with a soft, fuzzy texture on the underside, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
