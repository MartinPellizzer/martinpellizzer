Parodia concinna subsp. agnetae

Parodia concinna subsp.

agnetae, commonly known as the Agneta's moon cactus, is a compact cactus species native to Mexico. It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in white to pale green spines, with a distinctive yellowish or reddish coloration.

The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters at the stem tips. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

This subspecies is known for its slow growth and requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
