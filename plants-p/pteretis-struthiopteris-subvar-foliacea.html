Pteretis struthiopteris subvar. foliacea

Pteretis struthiopteris subvar. foliacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a variant of the common wood fern.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 meter in length. The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture and its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
