Penstemon montanus subsp. idahoensis

Penstemon montanus subsp.

idahoensis, commonly known as the Idaho beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and mountain slopes.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with high elevation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
