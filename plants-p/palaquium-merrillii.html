Palaquium merrillii

Palaquium merrillii, commonly known as the Malayan wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits with a waxy, red or yellow skin. The fruit contains a single large seed and is often used in local cuisines for its sweet, juicy flesh.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
