Phaseolus ellipticus var. citrinus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. citrinus, commonly known as the yellow runner bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptic pods that turn yellow as they mature. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers and has compound leaves with three leaflets. The seeds are typically yellow or orange, and the plant is often cultivated for its edible beans.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for both its nutritional content and ornamental appeal.
