Potentilla splendens var. montana

Potentilla splendens var. montana, commonly known as the mountain potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces showy, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
