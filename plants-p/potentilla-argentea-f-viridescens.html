Potentilla argentea f. viridescens

Potentilla argentea f. viridescens, commonly known as the silver potentilla, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that emit a powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in open cymes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its compact growth and attractive foliage.
