Potentilla yonoweana

Potentilla yonoweana, commonly known as the Yonowean cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five leaflets, and it produces small, rounded fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla yonoweana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
