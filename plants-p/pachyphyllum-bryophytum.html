Pachyphyllum bryophytum

Pachyphyllum bryophytum, commonly known as the stonecrop saxifrage, is a succulent plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically blooming in spring. Its rosette growth pattern helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

Pachyphyllum bryophytum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
