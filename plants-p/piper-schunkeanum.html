Piper schunkeanum

Piper schunkeanum, commonly known as the Schunk's pepper or the Malayan pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
