Pseudocladia minutiflora

Pseudocladia minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered pseudocladia, is a herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are usually pale green or white.

Pseudocladia minutiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
