Prasophyllum jeaneganiae

Prasophyllum jeaneganiae, commonly known as the Jeaneganiae leek orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the wetlands of Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in swampy or seasonally waterlogged soils, thriving in the unique microhabitats of its native region. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered threatened, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for the health of wetland ecosystems.
