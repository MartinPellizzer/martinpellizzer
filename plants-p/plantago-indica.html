Plantago indica

Plantago indica, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including disturbed or urban environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

The seeds of Plantago indica are also edible and have been used in various culinary and medicinal applications throughout history.
