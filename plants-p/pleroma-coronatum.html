Pleroma coronatum

Pleroma coronatum, commonly known as the crown pleroma, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is known for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
