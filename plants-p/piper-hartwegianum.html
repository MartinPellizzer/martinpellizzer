Piper hartwegianum

Piper hartwegianum, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are edible and have a mild, spicy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not commonly found outside its native range.
