Panicum psilopodium var. coloratum

Panicum psilopodium var. coloratum, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant purple inflorescences, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
