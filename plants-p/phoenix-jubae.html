Phoenix jubae

Phoenix jubae, commonly known as the Jubaea palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the island of Socotra, which is located in the Arabian Sea. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

The Jubaea palm is also notable for its slow growth rate and longevity, with some individuals living for centuries.
