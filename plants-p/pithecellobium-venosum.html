Pithecellobium venosum

Pithecellobium venosum, commonly known as the veiny legume, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
