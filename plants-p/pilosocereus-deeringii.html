Pilosocereus deeringii

Pilosocereus deeringii, commonly known as the Deering's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white spines, which help to reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in the evening, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its adaptations to extreme desert conditions make it a resilient and hardy species.

Pilosocereus deeringii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
