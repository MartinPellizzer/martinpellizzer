Prunella vulgaris subvar. surrecta

Prunella vulgaris subvar. surrecta, commonly known as upright dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its subshrubby appearance and the presence of glandular hairs on its leaves and stems.
