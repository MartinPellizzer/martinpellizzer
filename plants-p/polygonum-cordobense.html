Polygonum cordobense

Polygonum cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoban knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Polygonum cordobense is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
