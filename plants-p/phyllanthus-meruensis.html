Phyllanthus meruensis

Phyllanthus meruensis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
