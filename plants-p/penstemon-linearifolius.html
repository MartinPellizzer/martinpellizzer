Penstemon linearifolius

Penstemon linearifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Penstemon linearifolius is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
