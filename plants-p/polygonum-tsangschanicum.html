Polygonum tsangschanicum

Polygonum tsangschanicum, commonly known as the Tibetan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Asia, including Tibet, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Polygonum tsangschanicum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
