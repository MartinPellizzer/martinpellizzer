Pleione × taliensis

Pleione × taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensian pleione, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, making it a prized addition to temperate gardens. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in the wild, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
