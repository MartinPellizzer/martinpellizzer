Phyllostegia electra

Phyllostegia electra, commonly known as the silverleaf strobilant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple and are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Phyllostegia electra is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.
