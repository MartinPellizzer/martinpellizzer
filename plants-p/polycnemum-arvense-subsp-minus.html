Polycnemum arvense subsp. minus

Polycnemum arvense subsp.

minus, commonly known as lesser field sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
