Paspalum renggeri

Paspalum renggeri, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale flowers.

Paspalum renggeri is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
