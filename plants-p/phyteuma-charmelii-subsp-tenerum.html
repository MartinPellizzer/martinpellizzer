Phyteuma charmelii subsp. tenerum

Phyteuma charmelii subsp.

tenerum, commonly known as the soft-leaved phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers.

The leaves are elongated, soft, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ease of cultivation.
