Phrygilanthus myrtifolius

Phrygilanthus myrtifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
