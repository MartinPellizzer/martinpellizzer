Papaver alpinum var. album

Papaver alpinum var. album, commonly known as the Alpine white poppy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has smooth, lobed leaves and grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It is an annual or biennial species that thrives in cool, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
