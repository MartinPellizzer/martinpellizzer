Potentilla recta var. posoniensis

Potentilla recta var. posoniensis, commonly known as the Posonensis cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated margin, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
