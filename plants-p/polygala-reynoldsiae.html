Polygala reynoldsiae

Polygala reynoldsiae, commonly known as Reynolds' milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
