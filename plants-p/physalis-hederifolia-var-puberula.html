Physalis hederifolia var. puberula

Physalis hederifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped fruits that turn orange when mature, encased in a papery calyx. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and moist meadows, where it contributes to biodiversity and supports pollinators.
