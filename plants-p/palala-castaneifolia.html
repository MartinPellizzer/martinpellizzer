Palala castaneifolia

Palala castaneifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by oval, chestnut-like fruits that mature to a dark brown color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
