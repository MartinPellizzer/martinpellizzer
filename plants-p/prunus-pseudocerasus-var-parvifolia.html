Prunus pseudocerasus var. parvifolia

Prunus pseudocerasus var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese cherry or the small-leaved loquat, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically pale green and have a finely toothed margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
