Paspalum laeve

Paspalum laeve, commonly known as smooth paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to moist environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
