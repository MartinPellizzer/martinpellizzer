Prunus cerasa

Prunus cerasa, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant blooms with white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of sweet, juicy fruits. The fruits are typically round, with a red or dark red skin and a sweet, tart flavor.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
