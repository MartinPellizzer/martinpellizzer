Poidium uniolae

Poidium uniolae, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Poidium, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its floating leaves and showy flowers.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. The plant has a distinctive morphology with broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to prevent water loss. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a single pistil and stamens that are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Poidium uniolae plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
