Polygonum hippopotami

Polygonum hippopotami, commonly known as the hippopotamus knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bead-like nodes along the stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
