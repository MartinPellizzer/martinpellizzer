Parablechnum repens

Parablechnum repens, commonly known as the creeping parablechnum, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
