Persicaria viscosa

Persicaria viscosa, commonly known as sticky smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Persicaria viscosa is often found in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats.
