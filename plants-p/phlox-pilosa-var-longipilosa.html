Phlox pilosa var. longipilosa

Phlox pilosa var. longipilosa, commonly known as the woolly phlox or long-haired phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has distinctive, fuzzy, gray-green foliage that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
