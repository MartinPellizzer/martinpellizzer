Parodia unguiculata

Parodia unguiculata, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and spiny, segmented stems that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Parodia unguiculata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
