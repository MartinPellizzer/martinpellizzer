Pleurothallis conchopetala

Pleurothallis conchopetala, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a distinctive helmet-like shape in the lip. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Pleurothallis conchopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
