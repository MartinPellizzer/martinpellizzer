Persea fusca

Persea fusca, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a tendency to grow in dense, shaded environments.

Persea fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
