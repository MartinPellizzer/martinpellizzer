Psammomoya implexa

Psammomoya implexa, commonly known as the tangled sand plant, is a unique species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spiny leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish, adapted to attract specific pollinators.

Psammomoya implexa plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in coastal or desert ecosystems.
