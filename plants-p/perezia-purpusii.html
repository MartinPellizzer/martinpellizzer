Perezia purpusii

Perezia purpusii, commonly known as the Canary Island orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
