Ptilocalyx

Ptilocalyx, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its feathery, silky foliage and produces small, yellow to green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to southern Africa and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a delicate appearance.

Ptilocalyx species are also valued for their ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them useful in ecological restoration projects.
