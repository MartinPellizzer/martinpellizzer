Portulaca mucronata var. paraguariensis

Portulaca mucronata var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan moss rose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that produces vibrant red or pink flowers. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
