Podocytisus americanus

Podocytisus americanus, commonly known as the American rosemary.

This evergreen shrub is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for USDA hardiness zones 5 through 9. The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.

Its botanical features include glandular leaves that release a fragrant essential oil, contributing to its aromatic properties.
