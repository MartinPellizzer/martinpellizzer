Premna flavescens var. gmelinoides

Premna flavescens var. gmelinoides, commonly known as the Gmelin's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It is typically found in moist forest understories and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in shaded garden settings.
