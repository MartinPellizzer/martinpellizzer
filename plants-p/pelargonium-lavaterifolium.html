Pelargonium lavaterifolium

Pelargonium lavaterifolium, commonly known as the lavender-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, slightly serrated leaves that resemble lavender leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
