Physalis pruinosa var. argentina

Physalis pruinosa var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries enclosed in a papery, inflated calyx. These berries are typically harvested when they turn a bright orange-red, indicating maturity.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.
