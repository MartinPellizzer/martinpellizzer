Physalis angulata var. ramosissima

Physalis angulata var. ramosissima, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers that develop into orange, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and dry fields. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, contains numerous small, acidic seeds.
