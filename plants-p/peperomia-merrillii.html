Peperomia merrillii

Peperomia merrillii, commonly known as the Hawaiian peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Hawaii.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store moisture in its native arid environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, often clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate light, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
