Polygonum senegalense var. usambarense

Polygonum senegalense var. usambarense, commonly known as the Usambare flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers with three stamens. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and floodplains, and is often found in association with water. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are erect and sometimes reddish in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
