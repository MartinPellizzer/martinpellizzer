Panicum anceps

Panicum anceps, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and produces fluffy, pale-green to golden inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
