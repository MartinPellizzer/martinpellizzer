Plectranthus excisoides

Plectranthus excisoides, commonly known as the Cape mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and a distinctive minty aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often used in horticulture and traditional medicine.
