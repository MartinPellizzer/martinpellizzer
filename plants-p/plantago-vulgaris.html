Plantago vulgaris

Plantago vulgaris, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces erect, branched flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, cap-like structure called an ovary.

Plantago vulgaris is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
