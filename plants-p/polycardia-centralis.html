Polycardia centralis

Polycardia centralis, commonly known as the central heart plant, is a rare and enigmatic species native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped leaves that are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which bloom in late spring. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to anchor itself in rocky soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Polycardia centralis is considered a conservation concern.
