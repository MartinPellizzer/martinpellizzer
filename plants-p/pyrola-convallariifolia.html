Pyrola convallariifolia

Pyrola convallariifolia, commonly known as the strawberry sorrel, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the northern hemisphere.

It features dark green, leathery leaves with a glossy appearance and produces small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its clustered, red-brown berries that resemble strawberries, hence its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Pyrola convallariifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
