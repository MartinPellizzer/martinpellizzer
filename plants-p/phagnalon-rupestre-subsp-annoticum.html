Phagnalon rupestre subsp. annoticum

Phagnalon rupestre subsp.

annoticum, commonly known as the rock phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments in Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as limestone slopes and gravelly soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand drought and environmental stress.
