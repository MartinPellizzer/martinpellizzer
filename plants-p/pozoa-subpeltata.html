Pozoa subpeltata

Pozoa subpeltata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pozoa, is a member of the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as grasses.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in wet meadows or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet cluster, which contains small, two-lobed flowers.

Pozoa subpeltata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
