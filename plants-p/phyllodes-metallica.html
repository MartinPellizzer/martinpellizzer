Phyllodes metallica

Phyllodes metallica, commonly known as the serrated phyllodine, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often serrated phyllodes, which are modified leaf stems that function as leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage is adapted to reduce water loss, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Phyllodes metallica is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental appearance and hardy nature.
