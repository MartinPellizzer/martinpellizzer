Prunus × subhirtella f. smithii

Prunus × subhirtella f. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with dense foliage of glossy, dark green leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn. The plant produces small, showy flowers in early spring, typically pink or white, followed by edible red cherries that ripen in late summer. It is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

This variety is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
