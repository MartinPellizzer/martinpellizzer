Potamogeton bicupulatus

Potamogeton bicupulatus, commonly known as the two-cupbed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces distinctive two-celled flowers, which are typically found in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
