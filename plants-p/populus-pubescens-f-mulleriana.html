Populus pubescens f. mulleriana

Populus pubescens f. mulleriana, commonly known as the Muller's aspen, is a botanical variant of the aspen genus characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate and subalpine climates. The plant exhibits a silver-gray pubescent underside on its leaves, which distinguishes it from other Populus species. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded crown and smooth, pale bark.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
