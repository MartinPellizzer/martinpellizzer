Prasophyllum nichollsianum

Prasophyllum nichollsianum, commonly known as the Nicholls leek orchid, is an endemic plant species found in Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and sandplains. It is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is a common trait among orchid species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
