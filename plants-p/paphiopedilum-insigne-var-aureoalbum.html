Paphiopedilum insigne var. aureoalbum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. aureoalbum, commonly known as the golden white lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a unique pouch-shaped lip that is typically yellow or white in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This orchid is highly sought after by horticulturists for its ornamental value and challenging cultivation requirements.
