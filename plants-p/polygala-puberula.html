Polygala puberula

Polygala puberula, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinctive spurred petal that attracts pollinators.

Polygala puberula is often found in coastal and inland regions, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
