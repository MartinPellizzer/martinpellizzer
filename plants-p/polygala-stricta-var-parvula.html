Polygala stricta var. parvula

Polygala stricta var. parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, smooth seeds in follicles.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, known for its distinctive floral structures and ecological importance in arid environments.
