Parasenecio longispicus

Parasenecio longispicus, commonly known as the longispicous cudweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical daisy-like appearance. The plant is herbaceous, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, producing small, yellowish flowers that attract various pollinators.

The species is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats, showcasing its resilience to arid conditions.
