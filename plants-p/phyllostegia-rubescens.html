Phyllostegia rubescens

Phyllostegia rubescens, commonly known as the scarlet honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Phyllostegia rubescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
