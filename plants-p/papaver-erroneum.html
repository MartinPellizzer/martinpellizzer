Papaver erroneum

Papaver erroneum, commonly known as the "erroneous poppy," is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It produces striking, vibrant red or orange flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corolla. The plant has capsule-like fruits that split open to release seeds, a common feature in the Papaveraceae family. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Papaver erroneum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
