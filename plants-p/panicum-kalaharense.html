Panicum kalaharense

Panicum kalaharense, commonly known as the Kalahari grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant traits such as deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice in landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
