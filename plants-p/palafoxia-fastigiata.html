Palafoxia fastigiata

Palafoxia fastigiata, commonly known as the redroot ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, purple to reddish-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woods and prairies.

Palafoxia fastigiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
