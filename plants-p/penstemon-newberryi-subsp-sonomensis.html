Penstemon newberryi subsp. sonomensis

Penstemon newberryi subsp.

sonomensis, commonly known as the Sonoma penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
