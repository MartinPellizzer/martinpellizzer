Piper alexii

Piper alexii, commonly known as the Madagascar pepper or African pepper, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while small white flowers are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a small, berry-like structure that turns from green to red as it matures.
