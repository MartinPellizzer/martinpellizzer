Papaver alpinum subvar. lancifolium

Papaver alpinum subvar. lancifolium, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped, hence the subspecies name, and are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
