Plantago pseudopatagonica

Plantago pseudopatagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian plantain, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and spikes of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture in its native environment.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
