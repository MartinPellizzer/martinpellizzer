Polyalthia decora

Polyalthia decora, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets and produces woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
