Potamogeton × zizii subvar. longpeduculatus

Potamogeton × zizii subvar. longpeduculatus, commonly known as the longpedunculate pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It is a submerged herbaceous plant with long, slender stems that often form dense underwater vegetation. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped leaves arranged spirally along the stem. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and they are typically clustered in elongated inflorescences.

This subspecies is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, contributing to water clarity and providing habitat for aquatic life.
