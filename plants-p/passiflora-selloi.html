Passiflora selloi

Passiflora selloi, commonly known as the Sello's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are similar in appearance to those of other passionfruit species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora selloi is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
