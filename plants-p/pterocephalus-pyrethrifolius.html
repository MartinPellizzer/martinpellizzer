Pterocephalus pyrethrifolius

Pterocephalus pyrethrifolius, commonly known as the pyrethrum leaf pterocephalus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant is notable for its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in dry climates for ornamental and potential medicinal purposes.
