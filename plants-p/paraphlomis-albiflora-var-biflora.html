Paraphlomis albiflora var. biflora

Paraphlomis albiflora var. biflora, commonly known as the white-flowered paralytic grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern China.

It features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Lamiaceae family.
