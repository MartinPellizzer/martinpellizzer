Protea welwitschii subsp. adolphi-friderici

Protea welwitschii subsp.

adolphi-friderici, commonly known as the Adolphus Frederik protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pink to white flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape.

The plant is native to the Eastern Cape and Western Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and is also of interest to botanists studying proteoid root systems and floral evolution.
