Potentilla villosa f. carstiensis

Potentilla villosa f. carstiensis, commonly known as the Carstens' cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are compound, with several leaflets arranged oppositely along the stem.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and is found in various regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
