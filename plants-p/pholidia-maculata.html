Pholidia maculata

Pholidia maculata, commonly known as the spotted hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring woody stems with distinctive spots or markings. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
