Polygala hispida

Polygala hispida, commonly known as the hispid milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are small, hairy, and adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Polygala hispida is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
