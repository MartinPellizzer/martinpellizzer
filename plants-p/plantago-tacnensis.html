Plantago tacnensis

Plantago tacnensis, commonly known as the Andean plantain, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, leafless stalks. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
