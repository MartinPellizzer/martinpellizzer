Pelargonium boranense

Pelargonium boranense, commonly known as the Boran geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and rockeries.

Pelargonium boranense is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
