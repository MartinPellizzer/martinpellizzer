Pallasia dentata

Pallasia dentata, commonly known as the toothed pallasia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Pallasia dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
