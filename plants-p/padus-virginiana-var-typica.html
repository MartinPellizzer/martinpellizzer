Padus virginiana var. typica

Padus virginiana var. typica, commonly known as the American white ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its compound leaves with 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its strong, flexible wood, which has been used historically for various purposes.
