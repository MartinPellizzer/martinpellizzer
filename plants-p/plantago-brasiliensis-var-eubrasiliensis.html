Plantago brasiliensis var. eubrasiliensis

Plantago brasiliensis var. eubrasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
