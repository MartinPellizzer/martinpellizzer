Primula × hugueninii

Primula × hugueninii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula obconica.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

This plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a bedding plant.
