Pitcairnia platypetala

Pitcairnia platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, flattened, violet-blue leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps collect and retain moisture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
