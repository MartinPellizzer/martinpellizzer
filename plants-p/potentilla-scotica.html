Potentilla scotica

Potentilla scotica, commonly known as Scottish cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has compound leaves with five oval leaflets, which are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Potentilla scotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
