Papaver nudicaule subsp. americanum

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

americanum, commonly known as the American poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features showy, bright red or orange flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry prairies and open woodlands.

The seeds are encased in a fluffy, wind-dispersed pappus, aiding in its propagation.
