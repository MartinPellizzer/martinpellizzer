Polyadenia pipericarpa

Polyadenia pipericarpa, commonly known as the pepperpod or pepperseed plant, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in sandy or loamy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, elongated pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria, making it a valuable component of ecological and agricultural systems.
