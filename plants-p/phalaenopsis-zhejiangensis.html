Phalaenopsis zhejiangensis

Phalaenopsis zhejiangensis, commonly known as the Zhejiang orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It is a type of orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its elegant, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a velvety texture and a subtle fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
