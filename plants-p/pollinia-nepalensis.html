Pollinia nepalensis

Pollinia nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its unique pollinia, which are solid masses of pollen enclosed in a sticky sheath. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Pollinia nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
