Pilea glaberrima var. subintegerrima

Pilea glaberrima var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, round, and slightly waxy leaves that resemble silver coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
