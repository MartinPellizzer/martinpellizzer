Phyllanthus regnellianus

Phyllanthus regnellianus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
