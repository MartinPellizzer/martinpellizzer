Pelargonium transvaalense

Pelargonium transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
