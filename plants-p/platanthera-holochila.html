Platanthera holochila

Platanthera holochila, commonly known as the yellow twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It relies on specific fungal associations for nutrient uptake, a common trait among orchids.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a species of concern in many regions.
