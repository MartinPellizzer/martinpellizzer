Portulaca costata

Portulaca costata, commonly known as the coast morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. Its flowers are characterized by their five to eight petals and are often borne on slender pedicels.

Portulaca costata thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to saline environments.
