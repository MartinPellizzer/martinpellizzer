Phlomis cuneata

Phlomis cuneata, commonly known as narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
