Portulaca colombiana

Portulaca colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian moss rose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments due to its ability to store water in its fleshy leaves and stems. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often blooming throughout the summer months. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens, containers, and as a ground cover.

Portulaca colombiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in warm climates.
