Placea amoena

Placea amoena, commonly known as the pretty placea, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and can be cultivated in gardens with proper care.
