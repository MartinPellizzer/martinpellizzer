Pterodiscus ngamicus

Pterodiscus ngamicus, commonly known as the Ngamica disc fern, is a unique member of the Pteridaceae family, characterized by its distinctive fronds and reproductive structures.

It is native to specific regions in Africa, particularly in the southern parts of the continent. The plant exhibits a complex morphology, with fronds that are often divided into multiple segments, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Pterodiscus ngamicus is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
