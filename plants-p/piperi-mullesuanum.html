Piperi mullesuanum

Piperi mullesuanum, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth surface and a slightly glossy appearance.

Piperi mullesuanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
