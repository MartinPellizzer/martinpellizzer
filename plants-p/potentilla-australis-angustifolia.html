Potentilla australis angustifolia

Potentilla australis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit with numerous stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne in clusters of five, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Potentilla australis angustifolia is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in stabilizing erosion-prone areas.
