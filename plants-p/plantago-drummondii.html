Plantago drummondii

Plantago drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's plantain, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-arid regions.

Drummond's plantain is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.
