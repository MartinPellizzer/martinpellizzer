Pachyphytum aduncum

Pachyphytum aduncum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed stonecrop, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in the spring.

Pachyphytum aduncum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
