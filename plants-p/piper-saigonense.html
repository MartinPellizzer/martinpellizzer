Piper saigonense

Piper saigonense, commonly known as Vietnamese pepper or Siagon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
