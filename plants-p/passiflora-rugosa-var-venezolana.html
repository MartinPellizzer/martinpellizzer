Passiflora rugosa var. venezolana

Passiflora rugosa var. venezolana, commonly known as the Venezuelan velvet passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of colors, including red, blue, and white. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
