Peperomia perplexa

Peperomia perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Peperomia perplexa is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
