Podocarpus vanuatuensis

Podocarpus vanuatuensis, commonly known as the Vanuatu podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the island of Vanuatu in the South Pacific.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and conical shape. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Podocarpus vanuatuensis plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
