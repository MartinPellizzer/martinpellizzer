Pyrus rokujodaisanensis

Pyrus rokujodaisanensis, commonly known as the Rokujodaisan pear, is a rare species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant produces white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive pears. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
