Piper brachystachyum

Piper brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stalked pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
