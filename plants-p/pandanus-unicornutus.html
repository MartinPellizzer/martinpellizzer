Pandanus unicornutus

Pandanus unicornutus, commonly known as the single-nut pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its unique feature is the single, elongated fruit that develops from the flowering cluster.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
