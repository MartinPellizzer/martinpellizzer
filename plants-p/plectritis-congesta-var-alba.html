Plectritis congesta var. alba

Plectritis congesta var. alba, commonly known as white hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it easily distinguishable from other vetch species due to its color and structural characteristics.
