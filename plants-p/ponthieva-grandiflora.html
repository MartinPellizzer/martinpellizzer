Ponthieva grandiflora

Ponthieva grandiflora, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a robust, woody stem with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

Ponthieva grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow quite tall, reaching up to 20 meters in height under ideal conditions.
