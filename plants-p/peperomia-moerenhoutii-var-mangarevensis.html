Peperomia moerenhoutii var. mangarevensis

Peperomia moerenhoutii var. mangarevensis, commonly known as the Mangareva peperomia, is a species native to the island of Mangareva in French Polynesia.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, waxy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its limited natural habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
