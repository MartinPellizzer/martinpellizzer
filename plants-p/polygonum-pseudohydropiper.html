Polygonum pseudohydropiper

Polygonum pseudohydropiper, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a reddish midrib. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and roadside areas.

Polygonum pseudohydropiper is an annual herb that spreads through seeds and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
