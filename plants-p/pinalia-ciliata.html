Pinalia ciliata

Pinalia ciliata, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Pinalia ciliata is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its succulent stems.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
