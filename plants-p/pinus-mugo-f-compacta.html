Pinus mugo f. compacta

Pinus mugo f. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a compact form of the mountain pine, characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit.

It is native to the alpine regions of Europe and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features short, twisted shoots with small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. It produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
