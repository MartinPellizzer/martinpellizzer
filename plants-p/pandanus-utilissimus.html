Pandanus utilissimus

Pandanus utilissimus, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are often used in traditional thatching and weaving. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red or orange fruits, which are edible and used in local cuisines. Its roots are also utilized in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The screw pine is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a resilient species in tropical regions.
