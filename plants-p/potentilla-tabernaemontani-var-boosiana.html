Potentilla tabernaemontani var. boosiana

Potentilla tabernaemontani var. boosiana, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
