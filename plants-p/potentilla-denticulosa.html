Potentilla denticulosa

Potentilla denticulosa, commonly known as the toothed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Potentilla denticulosa is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
