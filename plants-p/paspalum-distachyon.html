Paspalum distachyon

Paspalum distachyon, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions. It is particularly noted for its ability to grow in disturbed or marginal lands.

Due to its high biomass yield and low input requirements, it is widely studied as a potential bioenergy crop.
