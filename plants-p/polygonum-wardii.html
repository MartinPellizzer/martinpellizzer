Polygonum wardii

Polygonum wardii, commonly known as the Ward's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive swollen nodes along the stems. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, serrated margin. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Polygonum wardii is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in cooler, temperate climates.
