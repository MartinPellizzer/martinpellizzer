Polybotrya osmundacea var. crispo-paleacea

Polybotrya osmundacea var. crispo-paleacea, commonly known as the paleaceo-crispate water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Polybotrya.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a crisp, pale appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are clustered in small, stalked clusters on the underside of the fronds.

This variant is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations that allow it to flourish in aquatic settings.
