Pyrus moschata regalis

Pyrus moschata regalis, commonly known as the royal pear, is a rare and ornamental species of pear native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, dark green leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
