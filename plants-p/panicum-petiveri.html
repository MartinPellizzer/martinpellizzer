Panicum petiveri

Panicum petiveri, commonly known as the petiver's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish florets. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Panicum petiveri is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its graceful, flowing form and adaptability to various soil conditions.
