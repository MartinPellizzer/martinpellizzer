Pelargonium histrio

Pelargonium histrio, commonly known as the firecracker geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers, hence its common name. The plant has palmate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows as a compact shrub. It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium histrio is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
