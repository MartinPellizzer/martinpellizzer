Phlox tumulosa

Phlox tumulosa, commonly known as the mound phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, mounding growth habit with dark green, toothed leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
