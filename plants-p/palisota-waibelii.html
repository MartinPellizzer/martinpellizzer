Palisota waibelii

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, with small, scale-like leaves that are densely arranged.
