Pelargonium crispum-minus

Pelargonium crispum-minus, commonly known as the crinkled geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its crinkled, lobed leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically range from pink to red. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

The plant is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
