Piptocephalum luteum

Piptocephalum luteum, commonly known as the yellow pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
