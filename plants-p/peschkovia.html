Peschkovia

Peschkovia, commonly known as the peashrub, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of a single species, Peschkovia sibirica, which is native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
