Pseudobaccharis densa

Pseudobaccharis densa, commonly known as the dense pseudobaccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Pseudobaccharis densa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
