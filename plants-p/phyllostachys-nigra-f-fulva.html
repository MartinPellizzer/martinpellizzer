Phyllostachys nigra f. fulva

Phyllostachys nigra f. fulva, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variegated form of the black bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green to golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts with the darker stems of its parent species. This plant is a clump-forming grass that produces tall, slender culms with a smooth, almost woody texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

The golden bamboo is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
