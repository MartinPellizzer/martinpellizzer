Persea mollis

Persea mollis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, custard-like flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and nutritional qualities.
