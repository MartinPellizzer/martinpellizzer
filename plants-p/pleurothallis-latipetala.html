Pleurothallis latipetala

Pleurothallis latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petaled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has elongated, strap-like leaves that are adapted to the humid, shaded conditions of its natural habitat. It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is part of the vast Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
