Piper franciscanum

Piper franciscanum, commonly known as the Franciscan pepper or wild pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered green berries that turn red when ripe, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's morphology includes heart-shaped leaves and a climbing or shrub-like growth habit.
