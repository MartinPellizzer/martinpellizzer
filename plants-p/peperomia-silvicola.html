Peperomia silvicola

Peperomia silvicola, commonly known as the forest peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense, foliage-heavy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and shaded outdoor spaces.
