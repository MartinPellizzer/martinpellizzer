Physolophium saxatile

Physolophium saxatile, commonly known as the rock physolophium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid regions.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Physolophium saxatile is often found in rocky outcrops and dry streambeds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
