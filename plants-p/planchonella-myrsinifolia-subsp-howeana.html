Planchonella myrsinifolia subsp. howeana

Planchonella myrsinifolia subsp.

howeana, commonly known as the Howe's planchonella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Islands. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.

The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture and a glossy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.
