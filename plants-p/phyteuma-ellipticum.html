Phyteuma ellipticum

Phyteuma ellipticum, commonly known as the narrowleaf phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, often pink or purple flowers. Phyteuma ellipticum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
