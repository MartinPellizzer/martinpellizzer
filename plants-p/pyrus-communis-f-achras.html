Pyrus communis f. achras

Pyrus communis f. achras, commonly known as the common pear in its natural form, is a botanical variant of the pear tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded leaves compared to the typical pear species. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators. This form is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
