Pimpinella podagrarioides

Pimpinella podagrarioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowparsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that appear in umbrella-shaped clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
