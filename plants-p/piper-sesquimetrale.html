Piper sesquimetrale

Piper sesquimetrale, commonly known as the "wild pepper" or "chicory pepper," is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery leaves. Its fruits are small, three-lobed berries that contain the seeds.

Piper sesquimetrale is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
