Palaquium stenophyllum

Palaquium stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved palaquium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Palaquium stenophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
