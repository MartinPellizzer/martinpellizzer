Pediocactus papyracanthus

Pediocactus papyracanthus, commonly known as the paper-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, papery spines, which provide insulation and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
