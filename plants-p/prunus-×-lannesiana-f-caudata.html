Prunus × lannesiana f. caudata

Prunus × lannesiana f. caudata, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive weeping habit, with long, cascading branches that create a graceful, flowing appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, typically pink or white, which add to its ornamental appeal. It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The foliage turns a striking red or orange in the fall, enhancing its seasonal beauty.
