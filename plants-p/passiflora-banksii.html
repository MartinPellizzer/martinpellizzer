Passiflora banksii

Passiflora banksii, commonly known as the Banks' passionflower, is a climbing vine native to eastern Australia.

It features striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild scent.

Passiflora banksii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
