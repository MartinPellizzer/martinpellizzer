Polypodium laxifrons

Polypodium laxifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and large, lanceolate, leathery leaves that are often covered in small, rounded tubercles. The fronds are typically dichotomously divided, giving the plant a distinctive, feathery appearance. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries such as India, China, and Japan.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
