Phyllanthus brunelii

Phyllanthus brunelii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits.

The botanical features of Phyllanthus brunelii make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its presence in both natural and cultivated settings.
