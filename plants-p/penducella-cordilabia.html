Penducella cordilabia

Penducella cordilabia, commonly known as the heart-leaved penducella, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are often a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
