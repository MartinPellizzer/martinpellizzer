Phyllanthus japonicus

Phyllanthus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
