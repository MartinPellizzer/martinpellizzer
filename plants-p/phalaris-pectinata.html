Phalaris pectinata

Phalaris pectinata, commonly known as the silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with small, spikelet flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks.

Phalaris pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
