Prospero battagliae

Prospero battagliae, commonly known as the Battaglia's glory flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blooms that range in color from deep red to vibrant orange, often with intricate patterns resembling intricate embroidery. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its dramatic flowers.

Due to habitat loss, Prospero battagliae is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts to protect its unique botanical features.
