Penstemon cristatus

Penstemon cristatus, commonly known as the crested beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon cristatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
