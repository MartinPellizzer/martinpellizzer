Phyllorkis rostriceps

Phyllorkis rostriceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyllorkis, is a member of the Myrtaceae family, native to Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Phyllorkis rostriceps is typically found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
