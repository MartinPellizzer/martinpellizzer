Plantago major f. megastachya

Plantago major f. megastachya, commonly known as the large-stamen plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its distinctive, elongated inflorescence.

This plant is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, disturbed soils across temperate regions. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The flowers are small and clustered in a spike, with the stamens prominently displayed.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
