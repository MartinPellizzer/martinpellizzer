Phebalium lachnoides

Phebalium lachnoides, commonly known as the silver-leafed peacockwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety foliage and striking, peacock-like flowers that bloom in clusters of yellow and orange. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical, and often have a waxy texture. The plant is typically found in coastal and heathland environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
