Pyrus malus var. cavillea

Pyrus malus var. cavillea, commonly known as the Caville apple, is a variety of apple tree native to France.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits with a distinctive red and yellow coloration. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree with alternate, simple leaves that are oval in shape. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its characteristic apples.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in French horticulture.
