Podocarpus verticillatus

Podocarpus verticillatus, commonly known as the mountain pine or Japanese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to East Asia.

It features needle-like leaves arranged in whorls of three to five, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and has cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its resilience to pollution and urban conditions makes it a popular choice for city environments.
