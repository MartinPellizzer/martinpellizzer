Pogostemon crassicaulis

Pogostemon crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features erect, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in spikes, typically in shades of purple or pink. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
