Potentilla oaxacana

Potentilla oaxacana, commonly known as Oaxacan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla oaxacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
