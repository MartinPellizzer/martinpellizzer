Parodia rechensis

Parodia rechensis, commonly known as the Rech's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated shape. The plant features a green stem with a whitish or pale blue coloration, often covered in small, white spines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Parodia rechensis is well-suited for cultivation in arid environments due to its drought tolerance and minimal care requirements.
