Plantago succisa

Plantago succisa, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces erect flowering stems with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
