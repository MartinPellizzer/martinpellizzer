Peperomia tominana f. pubifolia

Peperomia tominana f. pubifolia, commonly known as the hairy peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive hairy texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and container cultivation.
