Pyrus taoensis

Pyrus taoensis, commonly known as the Tao pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in autumn. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its fruit and medicinal properties.

Pyrus taoensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates.
