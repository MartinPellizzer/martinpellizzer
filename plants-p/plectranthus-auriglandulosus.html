Plectranthus auriglandulosus

Plectranthus auriglandulosus, commonly known as the silver bush or silverleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or blue, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it both a useful herb and an attractive ornamental plant.
