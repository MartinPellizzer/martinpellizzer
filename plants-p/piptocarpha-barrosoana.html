Piptocarpha barrosoana

Piptocarpha barrosoana, commonly known as the Brazilian thistle, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native habitats.
