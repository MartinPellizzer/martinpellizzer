Pyrus pyraster f. applanata

Pyrus pyraster f. applanata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved crabapple, is a cultivated form of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its smooth, almost hairless foliage, which distinguishes it from the typical rough-leaved variety. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits. It is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.
