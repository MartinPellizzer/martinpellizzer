Prunus × subhirtella subf. plena

Prunus × subhirtella subf.

plena, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a hybrid ornamental plant prized for its abundant, showy blossoms. It is a subspecies of Prunus subhirtella, which itself is a cross between Prunus serrula and Prunus lusitanica.

The plant features dense clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively low maintenance.

The double-flowered trait results from a genetic mutation that leads to the formation of multiple petals on each flower.
