Potentilla argentea var. decumbens

Potentilla argentea var. decumbens, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with sprawling stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that reflect light and give it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky areas.
