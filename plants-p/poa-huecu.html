Poa huecu

Poa huecu, commonly known as the Andean grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high altitudes of South America.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant has slender, tufted shoots and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa huecu plays an important role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
