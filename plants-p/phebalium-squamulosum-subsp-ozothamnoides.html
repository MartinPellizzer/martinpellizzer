Phebalium squamulosum subsp. ozothamnoides

Phebalium squamulosum subsp.

ozothamnoides, commonly known as the silver-leafed hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, scale-like hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, attracting nectar-feeding insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
