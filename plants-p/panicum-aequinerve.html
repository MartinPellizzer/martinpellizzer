Panicum aequinerve

Panicum aequinerve, commonly known as inland sea oats, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble sea oats. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its scientific name.

Panicum aequinerve plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
