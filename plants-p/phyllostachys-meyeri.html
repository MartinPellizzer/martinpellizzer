Phyllostachys meyeri

Phyllostachys meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green, providing an ornamental appearance in gardens. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.

Meyer bamboo is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
