Plinia rivularis

Plinia rivularis, commonly known as the river huckleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are white and clustered in umbels, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries.

The botanical characteristics of Plinia rivularis make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
