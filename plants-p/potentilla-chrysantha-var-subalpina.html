Potentilla chrysantha var. subalpina

Potentilla chrysantha var. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
