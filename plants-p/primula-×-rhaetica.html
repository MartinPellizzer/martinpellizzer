Primula × rhaetica

Primula × rhaetica, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula auricula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and yellow. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Europe and thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula × rhaetica is widely cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, rocky conditions.
