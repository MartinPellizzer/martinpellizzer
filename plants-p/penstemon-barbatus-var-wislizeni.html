Penstemon barbatus var. wislizeni

Penstemon barbatus var. wislizeni, commonly known as the Wislizeni beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.
