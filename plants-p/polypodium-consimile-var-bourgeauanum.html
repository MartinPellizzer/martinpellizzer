Polypodium consimile var. bourgeauanum

Polypodium consimile var. bourgeauanum, commonly known as the Bourgeau's shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern genus Polypodium.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically leathery and dark green. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a common feature among ferns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its morphological features and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
