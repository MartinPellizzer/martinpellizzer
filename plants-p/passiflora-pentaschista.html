Passiflora pentaschista

Passiflora pentaschista, commonly known as the five-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five lobes and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in beverages. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora pentaschista is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
