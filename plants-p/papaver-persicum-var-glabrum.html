Papaver persicum var. glabrum

Papaver persicum var. glabrum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a black center. The plant has smooth, glabrous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy flowers.
