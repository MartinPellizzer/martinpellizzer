Phrynium fissifolium

Phrynium fissifolium, commonly known as the split-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in cooking, particularly in the Caribbean and parts of South America.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
