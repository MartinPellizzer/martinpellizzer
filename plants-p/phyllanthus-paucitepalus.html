Phyllanthus paucitepalus

Phyllanthus paucitepalus, commonly known as the few-toothed phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, often waxy leaves. The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
