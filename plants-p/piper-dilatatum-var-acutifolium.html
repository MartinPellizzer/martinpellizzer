Piper dilatatum var. acutifolium

Piper dilatatum var. acutifolium, commonly known as the acute-leaf pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
