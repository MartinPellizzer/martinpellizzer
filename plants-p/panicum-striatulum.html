Panicum striatulum

Panicum striatulum, commonly known as striped panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive striped appearance on the blades. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
