Polygonum corymbosum var. asperum

Polygonum corymbosum var. asperum, commonly known as sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils. The plant features alternate, oval-shaped leaves with a rough or hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by reddish, fleshy fruits.

This species is often found in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and dunes.
