Pseudopogonatherum contortum var. linearifolium

Pseudopogonatherum contortum var. linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved false beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense clumps. The leaves are linear and tapering, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This plant is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
