Platyclinis graminea

Platyclinis graminea, commonly known as the slender leaf grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the plant.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
