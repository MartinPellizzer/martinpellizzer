Pseudopilocereus ruschianus

Pseudopilocereus ruschianus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in spines and are often segmented. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its nocturnal flowering makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
