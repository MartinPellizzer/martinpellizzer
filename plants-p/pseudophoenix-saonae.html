Pseudophoenix saonae

Pseudophoenix saonae, commonly known as the Socotra date palm, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-shaped crown and long, slender leaves. The plant is adapted to arid conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible dates, making it economically and ecologically significant to the region.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is also considered a vulnerable species.
