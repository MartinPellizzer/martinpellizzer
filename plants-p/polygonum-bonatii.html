Polygonum bonatii

Polygonum bonatii, commonly known as the Bonatti's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its stems are often reddish and covered with fine hairs, while its leaves are broad and slightly notched at the base.

Polygonum bonatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
