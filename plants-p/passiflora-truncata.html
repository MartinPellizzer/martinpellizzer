Passiflora truncata

Passiflora truncata, commonly known as the truncated passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are typically consumed raw or used in beverages. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are woody with tendrils for climbing.

Passiflora truncata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral displays.
