Piper fuscescentirameum

Piper fuscescentirameum, commonly known as the dark-stemmed pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, dark green stems and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries.

Despite its ornamental potential, it is not widely cultivated due to its specific habitat requirements and limited commercial use.
