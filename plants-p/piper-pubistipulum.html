Piper pubistipulum

Piper pubistipulum, commonly known as the hairy-stipitate pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in axillary spikes, and the fruits are three-lobed berries that turn dark when mature.

Piper pubistipulum is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
