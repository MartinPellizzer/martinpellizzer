Parthenocissus spaethii

Parthenocissus spaethii, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green with striking red veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for covering walls, trellises, and other structures.
