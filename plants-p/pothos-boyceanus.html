Pothos boyceanus

Pothos boyceanus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a yellowish margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers, though it is more commonly cultivated for its ornamental foliage than for its blooms. It thrives in low-light conditions and is known for its ability to purify indoor air.

Pothos boyceanus is a versatile plant that can be grown as a houseplant or trained to climb supports in outdoor gardens.
