Panmorphia lobiserrata

Panmorphia lobiserrata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf panmorph, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, wavy-edged leaves that vary in color from deep green to bluish-green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique morphology and limited habitat, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
