Pycreus permutatus

Pycreus permutatus, commonly known as the variable pycreus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are usually blue or violet, with tubular corollas that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
