Penstemon tolmiei subsp. brachyanthus

Penstemon tolmiei subsp.

brachyanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long bloom period, which extends from late spring into early summer.
