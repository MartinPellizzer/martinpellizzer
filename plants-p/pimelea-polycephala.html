Pimelea polycephala

Pimelea polycephala, commonly known as the yellow bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble bottle brushes. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Pimelea polycephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
