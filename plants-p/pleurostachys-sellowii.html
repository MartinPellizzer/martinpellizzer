Pleurostachys sellowii

Pleurostachys sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's bamboo, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, grass-like texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typical of the Poaceae family.

Pleurostachys sellowii is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
