Polypodium bifidum

Polypodium bifidum, commonly known as the bifid fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often divided into two distinct parts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread to form dense colonies.

Polypodium bifidum is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
