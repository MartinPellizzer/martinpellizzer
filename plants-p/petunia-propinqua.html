Petunia propinqua

Petunia propinqua, commonly known as the slender petunia, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Petunia propinqua is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and containers due to its colorful blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
