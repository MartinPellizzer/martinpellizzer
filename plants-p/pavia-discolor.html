Pavia discolor

Pavia discolor, commonly known as the variable pavia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.

Pavia discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
