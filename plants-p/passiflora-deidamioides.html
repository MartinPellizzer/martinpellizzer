Passiflora deidamioides

Passiflora deidamioides, commonly known as the Japanese passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Japan.

It produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, typical of the Passifloraceae family. The plant has a woody stem with tendrils that help it climb and support its growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and it bears orange-yellow berries that are edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
