Polemonium haydenii f. orbiculare

Polemonium haydenii f. orbiculare, commonly known as the orbiculate Jacob's ladder, is a horticultural form of a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in partial shade.

It requires well-drained soil and is often used in woodland gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
