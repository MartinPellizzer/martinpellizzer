Peperomia ruscifolia

Peperomia ruscifolia, commonly known as the Rosy Nerve Plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub with thick, waxy leaves that have a distinctive reddish-pink hue on the underside. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant among plant enthusiasts.
