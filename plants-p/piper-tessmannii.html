Piper tessmannii

Piper tessmannii, commonly known as the Tessmann's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often ascend trees or shrubs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and the fruit is a small, three-lobed berry.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines.
