Plantago verticillata

Plantago verticillata, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wetlands.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
