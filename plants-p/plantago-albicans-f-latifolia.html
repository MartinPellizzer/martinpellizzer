Plantago albicans f. latifolia

Plantago albicans f. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
