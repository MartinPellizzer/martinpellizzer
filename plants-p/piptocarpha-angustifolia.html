Piptocarpha angustifolia

Piptocarpha angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluebunch wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
