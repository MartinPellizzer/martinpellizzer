Polygala rubioides

Polygala rubioides, commonly known as the redstem milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

Polygala rubioides is an important species for pollinators and is often found in disturbed or secondary growth environments.
