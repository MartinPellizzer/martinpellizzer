Purgosea orbicularis

Purgosea orbicularis, commonly known as the roundleaf purgosea, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its orbicular, or round, leaves that are typically thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, containing compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.

Despite its limited distribution, Purgosea orbicularis plays a significant role in local ecosystems and biodiversity.
