Palmerocassia painteri

Palmerocassia painteri, commonly known as the painted palmerocassia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Southwestern United States and Northern Mexico.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils. Its bright yellow or orange flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Palmerocassia painteri is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
