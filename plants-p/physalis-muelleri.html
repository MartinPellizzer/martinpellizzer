Physalis muelleri

Physalis muelleri, commonly known as the desert tomatillo, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its inflated, papery calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, tart berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture.

The botanical features of Physalis muelleri make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
