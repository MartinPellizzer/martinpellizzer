Primula angustifolia

Primula angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped or ovate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Primula angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
