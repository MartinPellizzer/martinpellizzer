Pilosella laggeri subsp. hispidula

Pilosella laggeri subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as the hairy pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, daisy-like white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny environments. Its stems are usually erect and somewhat woody, while its leaves are linear and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
