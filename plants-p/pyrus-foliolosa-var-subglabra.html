Pyrus foliolosa var. subglabra

Pyrus foliolosa var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and serrated edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, juicy fruit.
