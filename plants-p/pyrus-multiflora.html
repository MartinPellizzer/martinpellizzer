Pyrus multiflora

Pyrus multiflora, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible fruits that ripen in late summer. Its fruit has a sweet, juicy pulp and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Pyrus multiflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
