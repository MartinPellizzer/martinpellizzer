Pelargonium × picturatum

Pelargonium × picturatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and garden borders.
