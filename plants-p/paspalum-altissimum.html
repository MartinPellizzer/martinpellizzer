Paspalum altissimum

Paspalum altissimum, commonly known as tall paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Paspalum altissimum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
