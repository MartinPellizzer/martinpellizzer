Populus cathayana f. latifolia

Populus cathayana f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved poplar, is a variant of the Chinese poplar.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. This plant is native to regions of China and is typically found in moist, temperate climates. It produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
