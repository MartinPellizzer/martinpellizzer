Paronychia arborea

Paronychia arborea, commonly known as the tree saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Paronychia arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
