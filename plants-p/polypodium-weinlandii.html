Polypodium weinlandii

Polypodium weinlandii, commonly known as the Mexican shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to two meters in length. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

Polypodium weinlandii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in both indoor and outdoor settings with proper care.
