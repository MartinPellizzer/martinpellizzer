Primula vulgaris f. multiflora

Primula vulgaris f. multiflora, commonly known as the common primrose hybrid or multiflora primrose, is a cultivar of the primrose plant.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, white, and pink. The plant is a low-growing perennial with heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or garden beds.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
