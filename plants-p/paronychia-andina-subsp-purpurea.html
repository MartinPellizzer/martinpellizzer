Paronychia andina subsp. purpurea

Paronychia andina subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a slight pinkish tinge. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in arid climates.
