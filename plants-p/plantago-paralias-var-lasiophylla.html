Plantago paralias var. lasiophylla

Plantago paralias var. lasiophylla, commonly known as the coastal plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. This plant is typically found in coastal areas and sandy soils, where it thrives in well-drained conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in a spike, producing wind-pollinated seeds.

The plant is known for its resilience to salt spray and coastal winds, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.
