Paspalum caudicatum

Paspalum caudicatum, commonly known as the slender grass or slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Paspalum caudicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
