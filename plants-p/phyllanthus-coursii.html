Phyllanthus coursii

Phyllanthus coursii, commonly known as African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use and it is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
