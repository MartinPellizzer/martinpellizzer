Polygonum arussense

Polygonum arussense, commonly known as the Arussian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often edible rhizomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish or purple tinge, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

While not widely cultivated, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
