Parnassia delavayi

Parnassia delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's fairy bell, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble miniature chandeliers, often adorned with a greenish central spot. Native to the mountainous regions of China, this plant thrives in cool, moist environments such as alpine meadows and forested slopes. Its leaves are typically palmately divided, with a delicate, fern-like appearance.

The plant's unique morphology and striking floral display make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticultural interest.
