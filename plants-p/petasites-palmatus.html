Petasites palmatus

Petasites palmatus, commonly known as the giant cow wheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant is notable for its unique morphology and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
