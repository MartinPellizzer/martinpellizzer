Phillyrea latifolia f. pendula

Phillyrea latifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Phillyrea, is a decorative shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it visually striking in gardens. The plant has small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a mild, pleasant fragrance. It produces white, star-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries in the autumn.

This variety is well-suited for hedges, rock gardens, or as a focal point in landscape design due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
