Premna nervosa

Premna nervosa, commonly known as velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands.

Premna nervosa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, waste areas, and forest edges.
