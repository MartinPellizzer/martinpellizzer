Populus × canadensis var. duhamelii

Populus × canadensis var. duhamelii, commonly known as the Duhamel's poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
