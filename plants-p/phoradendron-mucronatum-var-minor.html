Phoradendron mucronatum var. minor

It grows as a hemiparasite, deriving some of its nutrients from the host plant while also performing photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, influencing the growth and survival of its host plants.
