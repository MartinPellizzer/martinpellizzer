Pandanus pritchardiae

Pandanus pritchardiae, commonly known as the Pritchard's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in parts of Fiji and Tonga. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and cultural significance in the regions where it grows.
