Pyrus pyraster var. brevipes

Pyrus pyraster var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed crabapple, is a compact variant of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, sour fruits that are typically edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
