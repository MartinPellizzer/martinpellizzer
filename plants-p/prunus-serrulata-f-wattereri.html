Prunus serrulata f. wattereri

Prunus serrulata f. wattereri, commonly known as the Wattereri cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins and its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. This form is a natural mutation of Prunus serrulata, often found growing in the wild in Japan. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
