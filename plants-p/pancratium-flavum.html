Pancratium flavum

Pancratium flavum, commonly known as the golden arum, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, yellow-orange spathe that surrounds a spadix of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as beetles and flies.

Pancratium flavum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
