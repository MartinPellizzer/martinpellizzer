Ponthieva triloba

Ponthieva triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are divided into three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly across the forest floor.

Ponthieva triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
