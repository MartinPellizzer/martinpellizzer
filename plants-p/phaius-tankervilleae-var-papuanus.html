Phaius tankervilleae var. papuanus

Phaius tankervilleae var. papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan ginger lily, is a tropical plant native to the highland regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers with red-brown markings. The plant produces strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
