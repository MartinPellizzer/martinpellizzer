Potentilla hirta f. caespitosa

Potentilla hirta f. caespitosa, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a compact, low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically hairy and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in difficult environments.
