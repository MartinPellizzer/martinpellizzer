Pelargonium bachmannii

Pelargonium bachmannii, commonly known as the Bachmann's geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, somewhat cylindrical stems that are often branched and covered in small, velvety leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
