Phyllostachys viridis

Phyllostachys viridis, commonly known as the Green bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its slender, green stems that often exhibit a striking yellow or golden hue during the winter months. This plant is a perennial, growing in clumps and reaching heights of up to 10 meters under optimal conditions. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Phyllostachys viridis is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a material for construction and crafts.
