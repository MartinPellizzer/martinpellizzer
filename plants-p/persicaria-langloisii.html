Persicaria langloisii

Persicaria langloisii, commonly known as the Langlois knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a reddish tint and produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, often forming dense colonies.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain habitats.
