Plantago eriorrhiza

Plantago eriorrhiza, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Its leaves contain mucilage, which has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
