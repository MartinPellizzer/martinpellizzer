Pleurothallis glaziovii

Pleurothallis glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it prefers humid, shaded environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
