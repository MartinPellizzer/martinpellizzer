Phlomis pratensis

Phlomis pratensis, commonly known as meadow phlomis, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features tall, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered inflorescence consists of pink to purple flower heads, which bloom in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
