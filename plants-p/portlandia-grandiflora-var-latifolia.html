Portlandia grandiflora var. latifolia

Portlandia grandiflora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved portlandia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
