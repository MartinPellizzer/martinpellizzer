Pouteria lamprophylla

Pouteria lamprophylla, commonly known as the glossy pouteria, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in its native range.
