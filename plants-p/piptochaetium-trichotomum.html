Piptochaetium trichotomum

Piptochaetium trichotomum, commonly known as the three-branched hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with delicate, feathery flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization in its native environments.
