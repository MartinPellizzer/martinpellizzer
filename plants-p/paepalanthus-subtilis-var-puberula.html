Paepalanthus subtilis var. puberula

Paepalanthus subtilis var. puberula, commonly known as the silky puya, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and scientific interest.
