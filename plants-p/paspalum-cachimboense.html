Paspalum cachimboense

Paspalum cachimboense, commonly known as the Cachimbo grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly found in regions of Venezuela and Colombia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum cachimboense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
