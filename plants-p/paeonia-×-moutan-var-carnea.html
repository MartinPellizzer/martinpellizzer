Paeonia × moutan var. carnea

Paeonia × moutan var. carnea, commonly known as the tree peony, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, double or semi-double flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

The tree peony is also noted for its fragrant flowers and is a popular species in traditional Chinese horticulture.
