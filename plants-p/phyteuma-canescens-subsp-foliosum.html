Phyteuma canescens subsp. foliosum

Phyteuma canescens subsp.

foliosum, commonly known as the silverleaf phyteuma, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The leaves are typically silver-gray in color, covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
