Palicourea subspicata

Palicourea subspicata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sweetleaf plant, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in cymes, followed by ellipsoid berries that turn red when mature. It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some areas for horticultural and traditional use.
