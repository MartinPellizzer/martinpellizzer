Passiflora lobbii

Passiflora lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it has a complex system of tendrils for climbing.

Passiflora lobbii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
