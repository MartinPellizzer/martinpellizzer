Pygmaeocereus rowleyanus

Pygmaeocereus rowleyanus, commonly known as the elephant's foot cactus, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly stems that form a compact, rounded shape resembling an elephant's foot. The plant produces white, star-shaped flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Despite its slow growth, it is a popular ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
