Porochna rubescens

Porochna rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed porochna, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often reddish pigmentation. The plant is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Porochna rubescens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
