Prunus brigantina var. nicaeensis

Prunus brigantina var. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean cherry.

It is a small deciduous shrub native to the Balkans, characterized by its delicate pink flowers and edible, tart berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it produces small, red fruits in the autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisine.
