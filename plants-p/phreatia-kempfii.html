Phreatia kempfii

Phreatia kempfii, commonly known as the Kempf's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can access sufficient light for photosynthesis. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

Phreatia kempfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
