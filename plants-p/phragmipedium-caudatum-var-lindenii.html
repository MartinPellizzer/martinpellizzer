Phragmipedium caudatum var. lindenii

Phragmipedium caudatum var. lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This orchid thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
