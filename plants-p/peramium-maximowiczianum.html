Peramium maximowiczianum

Peramium maximowiczianum, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and hairy stems that help it withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Peramium maximowiczianum is often found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.
