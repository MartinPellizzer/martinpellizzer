Pandanus recurvatus

Pandanus recurvatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its roots are sometimes used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Overall, Pandanus recurvatus plays an important ecological and cultural role in its native habitats.
