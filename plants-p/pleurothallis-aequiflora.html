Pleurothallis aequiflora

Pleurothallis aequiflora, commonly known as the equal-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapting to its climbing habit.

The flowers are arranged in a raceme and are noted for their subtle fragrance, attracting specific pollinators.
