Podocarpus ingensis

Podocarpus ingensis, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and aromatic wood. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and durable timber, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture and forestry.
