Polia pusilla

Polia pusilla, commonly known as the small polia, is a delicate flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that help it thrive in moist, swampy environments. It is often found in association with other water plants, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its limited range and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
