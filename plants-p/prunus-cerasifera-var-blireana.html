Prunus cerasifera var. blireana

Prunus cerasifera var. blireana, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
