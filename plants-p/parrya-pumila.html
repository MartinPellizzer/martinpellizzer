Parrya pumila

Parrya pumila, commonly known as the smooth parrya, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Parrya pumila is adapted to harsh climates and is an important component of its native ecosystems.
