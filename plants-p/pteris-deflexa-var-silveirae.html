Pteris deflexa var. silveirae

Pteris deflexa var. silveirae, commonly known as the Brazilian water fern, is a fern species native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically borne on long, creeping rhizomes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The botanical features of this fern make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
