Pleurothallis ramonensis

Pleurothallis ramonensis, commonly known as the Ramon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
