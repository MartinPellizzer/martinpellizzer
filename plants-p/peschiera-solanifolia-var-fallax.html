Peschiera solanifolia var. fallax

Peschiera solanifolia var. fallax, commonly known as the variegated solanifolia, is a variant of the Peschiera solanifolia species.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns, which often feature white or yellow markings against a green background. This plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is native to certain regions of Europe. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces small, tubular flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
