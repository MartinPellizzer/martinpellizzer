Pimelea angustifolia var. minor

Pimelea angustifolia var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf mistflower, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and restoration projects.
