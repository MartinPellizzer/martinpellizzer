Pandanus mei

Pandanus mei, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-colored fruits. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The red pandanus is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy species in tropical climates.
