Prunus wallichii var. crenulata

Prunus wallichii var. crenulata, commonly known as the serrated cherry or Himalayan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It features simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
