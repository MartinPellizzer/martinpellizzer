Polygonum acetosifolium

Polygonum acetosifolium, commonly known as the sourgrass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by distinctive leaf shapes and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and have a sour taste, which gives it its common name.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
