Peperomia kerinciensis

Peperomia kerinciensis, commonly known as the Kerinci peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Peperomia kerinciensis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
