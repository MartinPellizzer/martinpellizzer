Papaver hybridum var. grandiflorum

Papaver hybridum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant poppy, is a striking flowering plant characterized by its large, showy petals.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, supporting its broad, vibrant flowers. Its flowers are typically red or orange, with a distinctive black center, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is cultivated for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens for its dramatic visual impact.
