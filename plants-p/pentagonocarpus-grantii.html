Pentagonocarpus grantii

Pentagonocarpus grantii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered endangered and is protected in several countries.
