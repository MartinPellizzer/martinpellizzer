Physaria gracilis

Physaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, and it has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky habitats.

Physaria gracilis is also notable for its seed pods, which are inflated and contain several small, round seeds.
