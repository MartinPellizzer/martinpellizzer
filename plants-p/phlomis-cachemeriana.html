Phlomis cachemeriana

Phlomis cachemeriana, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of showy, yellow or orange flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
