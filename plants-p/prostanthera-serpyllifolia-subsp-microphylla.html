Prostanthera serpyllifolia subsp. microphylla

Prostanthera serpyllifolia subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf mintbush, is a perennial shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
