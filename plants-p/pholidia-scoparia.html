Pholidia scoparia

Pholidia scoparia, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped leaves and produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

The plant's resilience and unique appearance make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
