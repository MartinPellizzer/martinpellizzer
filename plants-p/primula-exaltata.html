Primula exaltata

Primula exaltata, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It is a perennial species that blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to its high-altitude habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and protected in its natural range.
