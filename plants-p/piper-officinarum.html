Piper officinarum

Piper officinarum, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark blue berries. These berries contain a mild, peppery flavor and are often used in traditional Hawaiian cuisine.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and cultural contexts.
