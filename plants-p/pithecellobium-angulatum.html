Pithecellobium angulatum

Pithecellobium angulatum, commonly known as the angular pithecellobium, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
