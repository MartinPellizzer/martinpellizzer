Pachira flaviflora

Pachira flaviflora, commonly known as the golden pachira, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pachira flaviflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
