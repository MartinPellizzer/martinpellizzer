Pelargonium cradockense

Pelargonium cradockense, commonly known as the Cradock geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant red or pink blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pelargonium cradockense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
