Pharbitis mutabilis

Pharbitis mutabilis, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and heliotropic flowers that open in the morning and close at night. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and long, climbing vines that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are typically blue, purple, or white, and they emit a mild fragrance.

Pharbitis mutabilis is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
