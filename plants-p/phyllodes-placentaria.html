Phyllodes placentaria

Phyllodes placentaria, commonly known as the wattle tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its unique phyllodes, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that serve as photosynthetic organs. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system has symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Phyllodes placentaria is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
