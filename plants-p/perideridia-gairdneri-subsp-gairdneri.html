Perideridia gairdneri subsp. gairdneri

Perideridia gairdneri subsp.

gairdneri, commonly known as Gairdner's biscuitroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. Its root system is tuberous, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
