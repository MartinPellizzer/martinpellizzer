Porophyllum obtusifolium

Porophyllum obtusifolium, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are generally broad and rounded, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

Porophyllum obtusifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
