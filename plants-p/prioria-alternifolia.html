Prioria alternifolia

Prioria alternifolia, commonly known as the Mexican white oak, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves. The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is typically gray to brown and becomes fissured with age.

Prioria alternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
