Pachyphytum hookeri

Pachyphytum hookeri, commonly known as the "Lace Leaf" or "Hooker's Pachyphytum," is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening.

Pachyphytum hookeri is drought-tolerant and requires minimal watering, making it well-suited for arid environments.
