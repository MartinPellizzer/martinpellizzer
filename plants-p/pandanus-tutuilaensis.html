Pandanus tutuilaensis

Pandanus tutuilaensis, commonly known as the Tuamotu pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Tuamotu Archipelago in French Polynesia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its leaves are also utilized for thatching and weaving, reflecting its cultural significance in the region.

The species is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
