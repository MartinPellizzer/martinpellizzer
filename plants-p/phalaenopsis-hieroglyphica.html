Phalaenopsis hieroglyphica

Phalaenopsis hieroglyphica, commonly known as the hieroglyphic phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, long-lasting flowers. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow blooms with intricate patterns resembling ancient hieroglyphs, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This orchid is popular among growers for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
