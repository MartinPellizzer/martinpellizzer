Potentilla × reptantitormentilla

Potentilla × reptantitormentilla, commonly known as the creeping potentilla, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It typically features low-growing, spreading stems that form dense mats, making it well-suited for ground cover in gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers, usually in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often rounded and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Potentilla × reptantitormentilla is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
