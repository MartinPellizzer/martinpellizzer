Potentilla nivea proles vulgaris

Potentilla nivea proles vulgaris, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a delicate flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The snowdrop is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
