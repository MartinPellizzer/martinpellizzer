Pyrus communis var. nivalis

Pyrus communis var. nivalis, commonly known as the snow pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree characterized by its white or pale yellow fruit that resembles snow.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its cold-hardy nature, making it suitable for cultivation in cooler climates. The fruit is typically small, sweet, and has a soft, juicy texture. It is often used in desserts and jams due to its delicate flavor.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal, with attractive foliage and blossom displays.
