Panicum longipaniculatum

Panicum longipaniculatum, commonly known as the long-panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, drooping panicles that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically arranged in complex inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common ornamental and forage plant.

Its foliage and flowering structure make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
