Paepalanthus yapacanensis

Paepalanthus yapacanensis, commonly known as the Yapacanensis cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus that typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
