Psidium grandifolium var. incanescens

Psidium grandifolium var. incanescens, commonly known as the white sapote, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow or orange fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit is typically round or oblong, and its skin is thin and delicate.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, which is often used in culinary applications.
