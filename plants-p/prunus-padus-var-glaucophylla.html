Prunus padus var. glaucophylla

Prunus padus var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the white-flowered bird cherry, is a variant of the bird cherry species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its pale green to white leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, yellow drupes that mature in late summer, attracting birds and other wildlife.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
