Panicum miliaceum var. nicotianum

Panicum miliaceum var. nicotianum, commonly known as pigweed, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. The plant is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in agricultural fields. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
