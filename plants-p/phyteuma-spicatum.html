Phyteuma spicatum

Phyteuma spicatum, commonly known as the spiked phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
