Pinalia amplectens

Pinalia amplectens, commonly known as the clinging pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a rounded, cushion-like structure. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which attract various pollinators. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Pinalia amplectens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
