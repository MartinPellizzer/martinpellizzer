Piper polyneurum

Piper polyneurum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or broadleaf long pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature, and the fruit contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a woody stem with adventitious roots and a distinct, pungent aroma.

Piper polyneurum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its unique flavor profile.
