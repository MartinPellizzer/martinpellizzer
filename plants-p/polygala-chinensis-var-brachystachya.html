Polygala chinensis var. brachystachya

Polygala chinensis var. brachystachya, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and the fruit is a small, hairy capsule that disperses seeds by wind.
