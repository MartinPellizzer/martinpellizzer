Pulicaria lanata

Pulicaria lanata, commonly known as woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or gray hairs on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Pulicaria lanata is a perennial species that is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
