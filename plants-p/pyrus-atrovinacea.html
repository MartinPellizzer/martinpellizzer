Pyrus atrovinacea

Pyrus atrovinacea, commonly known as the black-berried pear.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers a cool, temperate climate.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, and the flowers are white with five petals.
