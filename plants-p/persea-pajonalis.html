Persea pajonalis

Persea pajonalis, commonly known as the Pajonal persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-hued berry that ripens in the dry season.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
