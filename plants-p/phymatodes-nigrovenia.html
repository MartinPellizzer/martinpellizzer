Phymatodes nigrovenia

Phymatodes nigrovenia, commonly known as the Texas prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.

It is a perennial cactus that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that is often covered with spines, and it produces yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit, known as a tun, is edible and contains a sweet, juicy pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
