Polemonium confertum var. chartaceum

Polemonium confertum var. chartaceum, commonly known as the paperleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with white or pale green, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Its foliage and flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
