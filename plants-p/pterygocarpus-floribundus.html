Pterygocarpus floribundus

Pterygocarpus floribundus, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also an important species for ecological and economic purposes in its native range.
