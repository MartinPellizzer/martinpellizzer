Pavetta indica subsp. tomentosa

Pavetta indica subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
