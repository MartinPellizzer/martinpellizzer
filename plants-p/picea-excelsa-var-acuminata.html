Picea excelsa var. acuminata

Picea excelsa var. acuminata, commonly known as the slender spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, pyramidal crown. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly curved, providing a striking visual contrast against the mountainous backdrop. Its cones are typically small and cylindrical, maturing to release seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to cold climates and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient component of alpine ecosystems.
