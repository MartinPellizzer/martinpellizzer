Prunus maximowiczii

Prunus maximowiczii, commonly known as the Chinese bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Prunus maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
