Phlox × henryae

Phlox × henryae, commonly known as Henry's phlox, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mounded growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces numerous spike-like flower clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its foliage is typically broad and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its colorful blooms.
