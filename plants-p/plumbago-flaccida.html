Plumbago flaccida

Plumbago flaccida, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Plumbago flaccida is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
