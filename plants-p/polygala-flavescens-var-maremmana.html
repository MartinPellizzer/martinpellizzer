Polygala flavescens var. maremmana

Polygala flavescens var. maremmana, commonly known as the Maremma milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
