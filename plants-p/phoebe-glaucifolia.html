Phoebe glaucifolia

Phoebe glaucifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Phoebe glaucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
