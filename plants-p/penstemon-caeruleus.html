Penstemon caeruleus

Penstemon caeruleus, commonly known as blue beard, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Penstemon caeruleus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
