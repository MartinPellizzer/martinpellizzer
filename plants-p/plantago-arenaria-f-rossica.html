Plantago arenaria f. rossica

Plantago arenaria f. rossica, commonly known as the Russian plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is valued for its medicinal properties.
