Prunus × mitsuminensis

Prunus × mitsuminensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters and has small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Its fruits are small, yellow or orange drupes that ripen in late summer, and the plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.
