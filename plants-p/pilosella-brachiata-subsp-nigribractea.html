Pilosella brachiata subsp. nigribractea

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

nigribractea, commonly known as the black bract pilosella, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, with small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer. Its subspecies is distinguished by the presence of dark, often black bracts beneath the flower heads. Pilosella brachiata subsp.

nigribractea is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
