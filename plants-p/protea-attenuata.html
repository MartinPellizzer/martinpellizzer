Protea attenuata

Protea attenuata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, adapting well to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Protea attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
