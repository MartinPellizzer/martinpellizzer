Physalis curassavica var. sinuatodentata

Physalis curassavica var. sinuatodentata, commonly known as the goldenberry or Inca berry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, which turns orange-yellow when mature. The plant produces small, tangy fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and unique fruit structure.
