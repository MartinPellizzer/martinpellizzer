Patagonium scabridum

Patagonium scabridum, commonly known as the rough Patagonian grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its wiry, grass-like stems and small, spiky leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom during the short growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Patagonium scabridum plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
