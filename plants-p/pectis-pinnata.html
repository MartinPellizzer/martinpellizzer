Pectis pinnata

Pectis pinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Pectis pinnata is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and drought tolerance.
