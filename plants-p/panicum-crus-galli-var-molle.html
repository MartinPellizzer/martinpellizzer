Panicum crus-galli var. molle

Panicum crus-galli var. molle, commonly known as millet grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
