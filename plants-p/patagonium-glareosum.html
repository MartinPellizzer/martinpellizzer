Patagonium glareosum

Patagonium glareosum, commonly known as the Patagonian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange flower heads and long, slender stems. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Patagonium glareosum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small fauna.
