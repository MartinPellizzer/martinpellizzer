Pelexia caucae

Pelexia caucae, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cold and harsh conditions of its habitat.

Pelexia caucae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
