Piper baccans var. baccans

Piper baccans var. baccans, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that contain the spice used in cooking. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, green flowers that develop into the peppercorns. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The berries are harvested when fully mature, and the dried fruit is the source of the popular spice.
