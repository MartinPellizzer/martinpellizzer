Parodia schwebsiana f. salmonea

Parodia schwebsiana f. salmonea, commonly known as the salmon-colored moon cactus, is a rare and striking hybrid of the genus Parodia.

It is characterized by its swollen, globular stem that resembles a cactus fruit, often displaying a pale pink or salmon-colored hue. The plant is a compact, slow-growing species native to arid regions of South America. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise bold appearance.

Due to its unique coloration and rarity, it is highly sought after by cactus enthusiasts.
