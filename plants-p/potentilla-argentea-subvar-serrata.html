Potentilla argentea subvar. serrata

Potentilla argentea subvar. serrata, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white underside on the leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
