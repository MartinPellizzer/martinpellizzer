Pelea waianaiensis

Pelea waianaiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian pelea, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hawaii.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
