Pseudopilocereus atroflavispinus

Pseudopilocereus atroflavispinus, commonly known as the black-flowered night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of dark purple to black flowers that open at night. The plant has a columnar shape with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It thrives in arid conditions and requires well-draining soil.

Its nocturnal blooming habit makes it a unique and attractive addition to desert gardens.
