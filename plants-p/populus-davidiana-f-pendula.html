Populus davidiana f. pendula

Populus davidiana f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping poplar, is a distinctive variant of the poplar tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This cultivar belongs to the Salicaceae family and is native to China, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental appeal. The tree grows rapidly, reaching heights of up to 20 meters, and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.

Its unique form makes it a popular choice for parks and gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
