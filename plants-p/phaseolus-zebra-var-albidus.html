Phaseolus zebra var. albidus

Phaseolus zebra var. albidus, commonly known as the white zebra bean, is a variant of the zebra bean species native to Central and South America.

This plant is a climbing legume with compound leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. The pods are typically elongated and contain small, white or cream-colored beans. It is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
