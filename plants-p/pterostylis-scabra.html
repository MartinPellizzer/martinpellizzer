Pterostylis scabra

Pterostylis scabra, commonly known as the rough-leafed duck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, erect flower spike bearing a single, greenish-brown orchid flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rainforests. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Pterostylis scabra is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance.
