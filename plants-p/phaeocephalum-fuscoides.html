Phaeocephalum fuscoides

Phaeocephalum fuscoides, commonly known as the black-headed rock rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark, almost black stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Phaeocephalum fuscoides is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
