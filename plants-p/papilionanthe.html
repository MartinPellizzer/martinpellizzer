Papilionanthe

Papilionanthe, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, and consists of several species that are often cultivated for their ornamental value. The plants are characterized by their showy, butterfly-shaped flowers, which give the genus its name. They typically grow as epiphytes or terrestrial plants, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Papilionanthe species are appreciated for their fragrant blooms and are popular in horticulture for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
