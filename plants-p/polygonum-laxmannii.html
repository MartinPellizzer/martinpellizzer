Polygonum laxmannii

Polygonum laxmannii, commonly known as the laxmann's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, blooming in late summer.
