Passiflora cuneata

Passiflora cuneata, commonly known as the cuneate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The flowers feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, making it visually striking. It produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
