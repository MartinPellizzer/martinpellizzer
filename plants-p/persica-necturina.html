Persica necturina

Persica necturina, commonly known as the sweet nectar pear.

This plant is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small, sweet pear-like fruit, is known for its high sugar content and aromatic fragrance. The tree thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit.
