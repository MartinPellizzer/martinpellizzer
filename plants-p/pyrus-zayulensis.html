Pyrus zayulensis

Pyrus zayulensis, commonly known as the Yarlung pear, is a species of pear native to the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a yellowish-green skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically growing at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique adaptations to extreme climates.
