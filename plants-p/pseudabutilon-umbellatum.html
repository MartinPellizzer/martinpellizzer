Pseudabutilon umbellatum

Pseudabutilon umbellatum, commonly known as the clustered false abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant features a branched stem with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most notable characteristic is its clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange.

Pseudabutilon umbellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
