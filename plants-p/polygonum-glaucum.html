Polygonum glaucum

Polygonum glaucum, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Polygonum glaucum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
