Ptarmica griseovirens

Ptarmica griseovirens, commonly known as the gray-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually gray-green in color, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in the summer months.

Ptarmica griseovirens is often found in association with other wetland plants and plays a role in maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
