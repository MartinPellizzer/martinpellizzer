Phyllanthus liebmannianus subsp. platylepis

Phyllanthus liebmannianus subsp.

platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It features small, clustered leaves with a distinctive broad, flat shape, and its flowers are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
