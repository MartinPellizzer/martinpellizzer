Potamogeton hybridus var. trichophyllus

Potamogeton hybridus var. trichophyllus, commonly known as the hairy pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

Its presence is often indicative of good water quality and is an important component of aquatic habitats.
