Potamogeton niagarensis

Potamogeton niagarensis, commonly known as the Niagara pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically 2–5 cm long and 2–4 mm wide, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, often found near the water's surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems.
