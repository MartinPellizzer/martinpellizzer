Plantago holosteum subsp. littoralis

Plantago holosteum subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal plantain, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and estuaries.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and tidal influences, making it a common sight in coastal vegetation.

This subspecies plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
