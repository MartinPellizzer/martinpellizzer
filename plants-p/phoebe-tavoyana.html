Phoebe tavoyana

Phoebe tavoyana, commonly known as Tavoy's phoebe, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and smooth, dark bark. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and the tree is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
