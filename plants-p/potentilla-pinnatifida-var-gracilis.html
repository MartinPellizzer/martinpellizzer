Potentilla pinnatifida var. gracilis

Potentilla pinnatifida var. gracilis, commonly known as the slenderleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a refined appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
