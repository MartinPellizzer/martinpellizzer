Panicum sieberi

Panicum sieberi, commonly known as Siberian millet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its seeds are small and wind-dispersed.

Panicum sieberi is valued for its adaptability and has been used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
