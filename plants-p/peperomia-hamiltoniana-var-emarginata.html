Peperomia hamiltoniana var. emarginata

Peperomia hamiltoniana var. emarginata, commonly known as the Variegated Peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a striking variegation of white and green, often edged with a red or pink margin. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
