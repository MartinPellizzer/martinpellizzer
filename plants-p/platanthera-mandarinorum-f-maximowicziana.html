Platanthera mandarinorum f. maximowicziana

Platanthera mandarinorum f. maximowicziana, commonly known as the Maximowicz's Mandarin Orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale green to white flowers with a distinct yellow lip. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of tree bark without deriving nutrients from its host. It requires high humidity and specific light conditions to thrive, making it a challenging species to cultivate.

Its limited distribution and unique morphological features make it a subject of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
