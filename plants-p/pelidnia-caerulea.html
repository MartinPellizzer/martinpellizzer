Pelidnia caerulea

Pelidnia caerulea, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Pelidnia caerulea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
