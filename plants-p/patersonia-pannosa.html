Patersonia pannosa

Patersonia pannosa, commonly known as the blue flax or bluebells, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open forests and grasslands. It has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces slender flowering stems.

Patersonia pannosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
