Poa glauca var. vaginata

Poa glauca var. vaginata, commonly known as the bluegrass meadow grass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which contributes to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings. Its leaves are often coated with a waxy cuticle, giving them a distinctive bluish sheen.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to form dense, attractive turf in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
