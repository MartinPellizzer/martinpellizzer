Passiflora mollis var. subintegra

Passiflora mollis var. subintegra, commonly known as the silky passiflora, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a yellowish, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
