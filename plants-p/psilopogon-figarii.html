Psilopogon figarii

Psilopogon figarii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form compact, low-growing mats.

Psilopogon figarii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
