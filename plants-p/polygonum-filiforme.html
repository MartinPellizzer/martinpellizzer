Polygonum filiforme

Polygonum filiforme, commonly known as threadleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has slender, thread-like leaves that are alternate along the stem, and its small, greenish flowers appear in clusters. It produces small, three-lobed seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Polygonum filiforme is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
