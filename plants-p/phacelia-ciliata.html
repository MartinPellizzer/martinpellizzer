Phacelia ciliata

Phacelia ciliata, commonly known as the fringed phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands and meadows, preferring full sun.

Phacelia ciliata is a valuable nectar source for bees and other pollinators.
