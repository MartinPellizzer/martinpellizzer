Piper cubilquitzianum

Piper cubilquitzianum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its woody stem and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, fleshy berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
