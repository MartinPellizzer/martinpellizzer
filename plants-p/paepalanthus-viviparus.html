Paepalanthus viviparus

Paepalanthus viviparus, commonly known as the viviparous grassland cactus, is a unique plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, leafless cactus that produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is notable for its viviparous reproduction, where new plants develop from the base of the parent plant, allowing for rapid propagation. Its structure consists of cylindrical stems covered in spines, adapted to survive in arid and rocky environments.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
