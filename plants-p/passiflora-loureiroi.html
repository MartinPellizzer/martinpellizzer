Passiflora loureiroi

Passiflora loureiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular species for horticultural and ecological studies.
