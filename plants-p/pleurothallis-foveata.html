Pleurothallis foveata

Pleurothallis foveata, commonly known as the "Foveate Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its flowers are often clustered in small inflorescences and bloom during the wet season.

Pleurothallis foveata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
