Platygyna urens

Platygyna urens, commonly known as the bladderflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small bladders, which aid in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are alternate and linear, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

Platygyna urens is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
