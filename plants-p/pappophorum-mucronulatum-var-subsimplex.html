Pappophorum mucronulatum var. subsimplex

Pappophorum mucronulatum var. subsimplex, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each culm, which is often subtended by a short bract. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetland habitats.
