Plectranthus strictipes

Plectranthus strictipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it both decorative and useful in herbal applications.
