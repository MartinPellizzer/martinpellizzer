Pelargonium amabile

Pelargonium amabile, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium amabile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
