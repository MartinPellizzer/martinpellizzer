Pseudolaelia corcovadensis

Pseudolaelia corcovadensis, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
