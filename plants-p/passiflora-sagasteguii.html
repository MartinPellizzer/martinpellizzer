Passiflora sagasteguii

Passiflora sagasteguii, commonly known as the Sagastegui passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central reproductive structure. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in its ascension. Its botanical significance lies in its role within its native ecosystem and its potential for horticultural interest.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
