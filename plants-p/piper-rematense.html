Piper rematense

Piper rematense, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Piper rematense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
