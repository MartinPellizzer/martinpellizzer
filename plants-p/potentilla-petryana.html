Potentilla petryana

Potentilla petryana, commonly known as the Petry's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla petryana is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
