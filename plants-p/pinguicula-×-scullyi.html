Pinguicula × scullyi

Pinguicula × scullyi, commonly known as the hybrid butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Pinguicula, which includes various species of sundews known for their sticky, glandular leaves that trap insects. This particular hybrid is characterized by its small, rosette-shaped growth habit and bright green foliage. The plant produces delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

Its botanical interest lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Pinguicula genus.
