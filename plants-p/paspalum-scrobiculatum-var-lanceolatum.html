Paspalum scrobiculatum var. lanceolatum

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed or wetland areas and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
