Peperomia cereoides var. cereoides

Peperomia cereoides var. cereoides, commonly known as the wax plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense clusters of small, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to shaded or semi-shaded spaces.
