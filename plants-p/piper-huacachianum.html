Piper huacachianum

Piper huacachianum, commonly known as the Huacachina pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small green flowers that develop into elongated, spiky fruits.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its pungent flavor.
