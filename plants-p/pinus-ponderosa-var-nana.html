Pinus ponderosa var. nana

Pinus ponderosa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf ponderosa pine, is a compact variant of the ponderosa pine.

It is characterized by its short, dense growth habit and smaller, more rounded needles compared to the typical ponderosa pine. This variety is native to high-elevation areas in the western United States, where it thrives in cold, dry climates. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

The plant produces cones that release seeds, contributing to its role in forest ecosystems.
