Phyllanthus rhizomatosus

Phyllanthus rhizomatosus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a robust root system with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively and establish itself in various soil types. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Phyllanthus rhizomatosus is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
