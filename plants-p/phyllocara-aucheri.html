Phyllocara aucheri

Phyllocara aucheri, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
