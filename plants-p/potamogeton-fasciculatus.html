Potamogeton fasciculatus

Potamogeton fasciculatus, commonly known as narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and lakes.
