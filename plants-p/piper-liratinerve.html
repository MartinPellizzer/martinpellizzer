Piper liratinerve

Piper liratinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
