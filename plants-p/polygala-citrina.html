Polygala citrina

Polygala citrina, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
