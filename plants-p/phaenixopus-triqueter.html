Phaenixopus triqueter

Phaenixopus triqueter, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique three-lobed leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
