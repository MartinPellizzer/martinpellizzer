Pythonium ecaudatum

Pythonium ecaudatum, commonly known as the tailless python plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that resemble the body of a snake, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
