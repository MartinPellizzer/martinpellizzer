Panicum sarmentosum var. mekongense

Panicum sarmentosum var. mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and slender, erect stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
