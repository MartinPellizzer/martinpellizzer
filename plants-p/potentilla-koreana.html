Potentilla koreana

Potentilla koreana, commonly known as Korean cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla koreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
