Prunus serrulata f. viridiflora

Prunus serrulata f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered cherry, is a rare and ornamental variation of the Japanese flowering cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish-white flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage, making it a striking addition to gardens. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, preferring a temperate climate.

Its unique floral coloration makes it a sought-after specimen for botanical gardens and specialized horticulture.
