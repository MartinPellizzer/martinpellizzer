Protea graminea

Protea graminea, commonly known as the grass protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a grass-like structure, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which are clustered around a central stem. Protea graminea is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It plays an important role in attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.
