Phlomis uniceps

Phlomis uniceps, commonly known as narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces tall spikes of yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
