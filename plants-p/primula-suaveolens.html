Primula suaveolens

Primula suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Europe.

It produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula suaveolens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
