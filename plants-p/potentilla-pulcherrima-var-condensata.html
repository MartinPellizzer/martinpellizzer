Potentilla pulcherrima var. condensata

Potentilla pulcherrima var. condensata, commonly known as the compact potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, upright clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its compact form and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
