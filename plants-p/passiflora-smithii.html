Passiflora smithii

Passiflora smithii, commonly known as the Smith's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces bright orange berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora smithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
