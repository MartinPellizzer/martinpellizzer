Prunus brigantiaca var. microcarpa

Prunus brigantiaca var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching branches and produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The variety microcarpa is distinguished by its smaller fruits compared to other Prunus species.
