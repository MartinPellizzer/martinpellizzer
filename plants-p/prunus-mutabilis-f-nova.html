Prunus mutabilis f. nova

Prunus mutabilis f. nova, commonly known as the Nova hybrid cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its unique botanical traits.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. The fruit is a small, round cherry with a sweet, tart flavor, often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
