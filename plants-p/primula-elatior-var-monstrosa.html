Primula elatior var. monstrosa

Primula elatior var. monstrosa, commonly known as the giant primrose, is a striking hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its variability, it is prized by horticulturists for its potential to produce unique and vibrant flower forms.
