Paraphlomis albiflora

Paraphlomis albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and clustered in whorls along the stem.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
