Phyllocladus rhomboidalis

Phyllocladus rhomboidalis, commonly known as the diamond-leaved acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive diamond-shaped phylloclades, which are flattened, leaf-like structures that perform photosynthesis. The plant has small, scale-like true leaves that are often obscured by the phylloclades. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
