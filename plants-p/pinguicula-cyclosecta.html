Pinguicula cyclosecta

Pinguicula cyclosecta, commonly known as the cyclosed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves that secrete digestive enzymes to trap and digest small insects. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in bogs or along stream banks.

Its unique trapping mechanism and specialized morphology make it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
