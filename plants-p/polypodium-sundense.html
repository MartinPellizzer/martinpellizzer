Polypodium sundense

Polypodium sundense, commonly known as the Sundanese wall fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or in shaded, humid environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a compact and upright growth habit.
