Pandanus gazelleensis

Pandanus gazelleensis, commonly known as the Gazelle's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the leaves.

The plant's unique morphology and adaptability to humid, coastal environments make it a notable species in its native habitat.
