Panicum glaucum var. brasiliense

Panicum glaucum var. brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian bluegrass, is a variant of the panicum genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue-gray foliage, which gives it a unique visual appeal. The plant produces fluffy, silver-tan seed heads that add ornamental value, especially in late summer and autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid climates.
