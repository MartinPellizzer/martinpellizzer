Pelargonium × volatiflorum

Pelargonium × volatiflorum, commonly known as the volatile flower geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly serrated margin, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

The species is native to South Africa, where it grows in rocky or sandy soils.
