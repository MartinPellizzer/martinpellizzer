Prunus × donarium f. benitoranowo

Prunus × donarium f. benitoranowo, commonly known as the Benito Ranowo cherry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that are typically harvested in late spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces pink to white flowers in early spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it suitable for both gardening and small-scale cultivation.
