Pleurostachys paniculata

Pleurostachys paniculata, commonly known as the Mexican feathergrass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, panicle-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Pleurostachys paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its elegant, grass-like appearance.
