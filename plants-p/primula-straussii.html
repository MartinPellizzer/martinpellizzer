Primula straussii

Primula straussii, commonly known as the Strauss's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula straussii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
