Pelargonium × chelidoniifolium

Pelargonium × chelidoniifolium, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a hybrid pelargonium species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are deeply lobed and resemble the pattern of a leopard's spots, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in summer. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
