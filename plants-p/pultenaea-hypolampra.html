Pultenaea hypolampra

Pultenaea hypolampra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. Its bright yellow flowers appear in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
