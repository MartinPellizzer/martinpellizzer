Piper guatopoense

Piper guatopoense, commonly known as the Guatopo pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by elongated, fleshy berries.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and has a mild, peppery flavor.
