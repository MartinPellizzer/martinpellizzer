Pycnocoma hutchinsonii

Pycnocoma hutchinsonii, commonly known as the Andean cushion plant, is a low-growing shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, which helps it survive in harsh, high-altitude environments. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, reducing water loss in arid conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered together, and it is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
