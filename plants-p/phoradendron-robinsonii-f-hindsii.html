Phoradendron robinsonii f. hindsii

Phoradendron robinsonii f. hindsii, commonly known as the Hinds' mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has a distinctive appearance with small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale green berries. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients and water through its stems, which are embedded into the host tree's bark. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically blooming in late spring, and it relies on specific bird species for seed dispersal.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems.
