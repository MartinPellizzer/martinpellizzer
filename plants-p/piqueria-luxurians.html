Piqueria luxurians

Piqueria luxurians, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically features bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Piqueria luxurians is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
