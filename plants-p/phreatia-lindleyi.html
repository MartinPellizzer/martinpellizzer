Phreatia lindleyi

Phreatia lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's phreatia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with large, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, which is characteristic of its epiphytic and terrestrial growth habits.

The genus Phreatia is known for its diverse floral structures and ecological adaptations to humid tropical climates.
