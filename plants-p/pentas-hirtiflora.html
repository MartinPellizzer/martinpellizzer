Pentas hirtiflora

Pentas hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy pentas, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it its common name and help in reducing water loss. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it suitable for containers, rock gardens, and as a bedding plant.
