Piper parviramosum

Piper parviramosum, commonly known as the small-veined pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

Piper parviramosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
