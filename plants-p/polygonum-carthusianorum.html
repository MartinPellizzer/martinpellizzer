Polygonum carthusianorum

Polygonum carthusianorum, commonly known as the Carthusian pepper or bloodwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and sometimes edible parts. The plant features triangular, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and hardy plant.
