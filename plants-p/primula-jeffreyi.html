Primula jeffreyi

Primula jeffreyi, commonly known as Jeffrey's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula jeffreyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
