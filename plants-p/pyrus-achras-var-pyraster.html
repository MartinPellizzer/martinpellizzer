Pyrus achras var. pyraster

Pyrus achras var. pyraster, commonly known as the African pear, is a variant of the African white pear tree.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, edible fruits that resemble pears in appearance. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with dense, dark green foliage and can grow up to 10 meters in height. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces fruits that are rich in nutrients.

The tree is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a valuable crop in regions with limited water resources.
