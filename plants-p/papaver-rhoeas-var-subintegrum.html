Papaver rhoeas var. subintegrum

Papaver rhoeas var. subintegrum, commonly known as the scarlet poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its striking red-orange petals.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in disturbed soils and open fields. The flowers are large, showy, and bloom in late spring to early summer, often with a yellowish central disk. The plant has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits that release seeds when mature.

It is valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its historical association with the poppy symbolism.
