Patagonium suffocatum

Patagonium suffocatum, commonly known as the suffocating patagonia, is a unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps it survive in harsh, cold environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, adapted to pollination by wind rather than insects. Its leaves are densely packed and covered in a layer of fine, fuzzy hairs that provide insulation and reduce water loss.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Patagonium suffocatum is a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
