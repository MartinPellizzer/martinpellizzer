Piper larutanum

Piper larutanum, commonly known as the Malayan pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and these berries contain the aromatic compound responsible for its distinctive flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
