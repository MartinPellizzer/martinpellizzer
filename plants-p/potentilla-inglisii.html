Potentilla inglisii

Potentilla inglisii, commonly known as English cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Potentilla inglisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
