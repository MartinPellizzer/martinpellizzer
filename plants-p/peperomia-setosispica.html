Peperomia setosispica

Peperomia setosispica, commonly known as the hairy peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its slow growth rate and ability to tolerate low light conditions make it a popular choice for beginners in plant care.
