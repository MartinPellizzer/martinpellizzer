Pachygenium ventricosum

Pachygenium ventricosum, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its inflated, balloon-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces showy, fleshy fruits that can grow up to 10 centimeters in diameter. Its leaves are alternate and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Pachygenium ventricosum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid environments with well-drained soil.
