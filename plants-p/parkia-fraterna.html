Parkia fraterna

Parkia fraterna, commonly known as the sacred flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red and orange. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or near water bodies.

Parkia fraterna is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
