Paspalum cerradoense

Paspalum cerradoense, commonly known as the cerrado grass, is a perennial grass native to the Cerrado biome of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils and seasonal droughts of the cerrado ecosystem.

Paspalum cerradoense plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
