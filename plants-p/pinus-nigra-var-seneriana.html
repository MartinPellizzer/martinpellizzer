Pinus nigra var. seneriana

Pinus nigra var. seneriana, commonly known as the Italian stone pine, is a variety of the black pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces long, slender cones that contain edible seeds, often referred to as pine nuts. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and pests.

This species is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient and long-lived tree.
