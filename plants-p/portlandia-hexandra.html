Portlandia hexandra

Portlandia hexandra, commonly known as the golden bell heath, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Portlandia hexandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
