Phlox brevifolia

Phlox brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Phlox brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
