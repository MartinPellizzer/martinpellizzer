Pleurothallis palmeirensis

Pleurothallis palmeirensis, commonly known as the Palmeirensis orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
