Pittosporum cravenianum

Pittosporum cravenianum, commonly known as the Craven's pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum cravenianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
