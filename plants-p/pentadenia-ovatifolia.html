Pentadenia ovatifolia

Pentadenia ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically pink or white, appearing in clusters.

Pentadenia ovatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
