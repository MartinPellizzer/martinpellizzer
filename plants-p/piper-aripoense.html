Piper aripoense

Piper aripoense, commonly known as the Trinidad pepper or Aripo pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Trinidad and Tobago, as well as parts of South America. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain a spicy, peppery flavor, making it a unique addition to local cuisines.

Piper aripoense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural spice.
