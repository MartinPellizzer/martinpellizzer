Pelargonium triste

Pelargonium triste, commonly known as the weeping geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium triste is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers due to its elegant, cascading appearance.
