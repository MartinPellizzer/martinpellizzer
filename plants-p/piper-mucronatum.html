Piper mucronatum

Piper mucronatum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and the leaves are often used in traditional medicine. Its chemical composition includes various alkaloids, contributing to its medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical structure is characterized by its tendrils, which help it climb and spread within the dense forest canopy.
