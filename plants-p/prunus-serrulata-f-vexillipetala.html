Prunus serrulata f. vexillipetala

Prunus serrulata f. vexillipetala, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom with a flag-like petal.

This botanical variant is a cultivar of the common cherry tree, characterized by its distinctive, flag-shaped petals that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and public spaces. The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual display.

The plant is valued not only for its beauty but also for its cultural significance in Japanese tradition.
