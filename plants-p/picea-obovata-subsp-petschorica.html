Picea obovata subsp. petschorica

Picea obovata subsp.

petschorica, commonly known as the Siberian white pine, is a subspecies of the white pine native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Western Siberia. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive, obovate needles that are typically bluish-green in color.

The cones of this subspecies are elongated and often hang downward, containing two-winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, snowy conditions.

This plant plays an important ecological role in boreal and subarctic forests, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
