Polytropia umbellata

Polytropia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that grow in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
