Pyrus assamensis

Pyrus assamensis, commonly known as the Assam pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruit, which is typically pear-shaped, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus assamensis is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential in horticulture and traditional medicine.
