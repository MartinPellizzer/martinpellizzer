Penstemon thompsoniae subsp. typicus

Penstemon thompsoniae subsp.

typicus, commonly known as Thompson's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its narrow leaves and distinctive flower structure contribute to its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
