Pelargonium × limoneum

Pelargonium × limoneum, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. Native to South Africa, this plant is widely cultivated as a ornamental and for its aromatic properties. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardens.

The plant is also popular for its ability to repel certain pests due to its strong scent.
