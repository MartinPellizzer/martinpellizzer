Passiflora cacao

Passiflora cacao, commonly known as the chocolate passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and intricate structures. The plant produces small, dark berries that contain the seeds used in the production of cocoa. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
