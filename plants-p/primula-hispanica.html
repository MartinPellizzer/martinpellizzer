Primula hispanica

Primula hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Primula hispanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
