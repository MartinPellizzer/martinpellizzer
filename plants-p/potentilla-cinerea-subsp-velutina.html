Potentilla cinerea subsp. velutina

Potentilla cinerea subsp.

velutina, commonly known as silky cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States. It features silver-gray, softly hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
