Persea alpigena

Persea alpigena, commonly known as the Andean persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include a thick, waxy cuticle on the leaves and a shallow root system adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Persea alpigena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
