Pseudaegle trifoliata

Pseudaegle trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, hibiscus-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Pseudaegle trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
