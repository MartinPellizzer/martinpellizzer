Piper linearifolium

Piper linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elliptical, blue-black berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
