Prunus trichocarpa

Prunus trichocarpa, commonly known as black cherry, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is valued for its wood, which is used in furniture and cabinetry, and for its ecological role in supporting wildlife.

The plant is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and seasonal foliage.
