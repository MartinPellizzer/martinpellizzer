Padus lusitanica

Padus lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and scrublands.

This species is valued for its ecological role and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
