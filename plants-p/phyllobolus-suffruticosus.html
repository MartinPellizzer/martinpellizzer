Phyllobolus suffruticosus

Phyllobolus suffruticosus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and the flowers bloom in terminal inflorescences.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
