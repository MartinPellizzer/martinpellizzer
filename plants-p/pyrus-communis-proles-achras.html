Pyrus communis proles achras

Pyrus communis proles achras, commonly known as the Achras pear, is a subspecies of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically yellow or golden in color. The plant has a deciduous habit, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a serrated edge.

The flowers are white and clustered, producing a mild fragrance that attracts pollinators.
