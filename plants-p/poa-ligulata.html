Poa ligulata

Poa ligulata, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Poa ligulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
