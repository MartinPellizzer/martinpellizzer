Phlox cuspidata

Phlox cuspidata, commonly known as the narrowleaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in woodlands and moist meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and fragrant blooms.
