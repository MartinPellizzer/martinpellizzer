Physodia

Physodia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in small, cylindrical seed heads. Physodia is often found in moist, woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
