Piper perstipulare

Piper perstipulare, commonly known as the pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the spicy compound piperine. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of Piper perstipulare make it an important plant in both ecological and economic contexts.
