Passiflora baueriana

Passiflora baueriana, commonly known as the Bauer's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora baueriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
