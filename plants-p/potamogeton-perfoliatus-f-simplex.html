Potamogeton perfoliatus f. simplex

Potamogeton perfoliatus f. simplex, commonly known as the simple leaf water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems.

It features simple, entire leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, and it relies on water for propagation. Its roots help stabilize sediments, contributing to water clarity.

It is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as lakes and ponds.
