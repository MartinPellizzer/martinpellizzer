Pachystachys ossolae

Pachystachys ossolae, commonly known as the Ossola grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
