Phaius callosus var. ecalcaratus

Phaius callosus var. ecalcaratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

The varietal form ecalcaratus is distinguished by its smaller, more compact growth compared to the typical variety.
