Polygonum amphibium var. maritimum

Polygonum amphibium var. maritimum, commonly known as sea buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in tidal zones and salt marshes. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its adaptability to harsh coastal environments makes it an important component of maritime ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in coastal areas.
