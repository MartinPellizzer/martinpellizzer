Phyllanthus gomphocarpus

Phyllanthus gomphocarpus, commonly known as African date palm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The plant bears small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a distinctive, clustered fruit structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
