Polypodium tricuspe

Polypodium tricuspe, commonly known as the three-toothed fern, is a tropical fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of its leaves.

Polypodium tricuspe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
