Pilosella lobarzewskii

Pilosella lobarzewskii, commonly known as the Lobarzewski's pearly everlasting, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features narrow, silver-tomentose leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

Pilosella lobarzewskii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in gardens for its long-lasting, fluffy flower heads.
