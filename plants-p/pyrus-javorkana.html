Pyrus javorkana

Pyrus javorkana, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by edible, round berries that turn from green to dark blue as they mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
