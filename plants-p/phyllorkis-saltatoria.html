Phyllorkis saltatoria

Phyllorkis saltatoria, commonly known as the saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Phyllorkis saltatoria is well adapted to saline soils and plays an important role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
