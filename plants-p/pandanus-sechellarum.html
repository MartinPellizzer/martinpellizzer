Pandanus sechellarum

Pandanus sechellarum, commonly known as the Seychelles pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Seychelles archipelago.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
