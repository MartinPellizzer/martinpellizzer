Prosthechea micropus

Prosthechea micropus, commonly known as the small-stemmed orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. Prosthechea micropus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive addition to specialized horticultural collections.
