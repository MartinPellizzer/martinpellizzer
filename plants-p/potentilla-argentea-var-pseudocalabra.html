Potentilla argentea var. pseudocalabra

Potentilla argentea var. pseudocalabra, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
