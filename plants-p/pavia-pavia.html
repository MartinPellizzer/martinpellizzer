Pavia pavia

Pavia pavia, commonly known as the dwarf pavia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pavia pavia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
