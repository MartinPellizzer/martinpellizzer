Paspalum subsesquiglume

Paspalum subsesquiglume, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing two to three small, bract-like structures. The plant produces small, two-lobed florets that are typically green or pale in color.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in forage and erosion control in some regions.
