Peperomia andinacea

Peperomia andinacea, commonly known as the Andean pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia andinacea is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal water and tolerating a range of indoor conditions.
