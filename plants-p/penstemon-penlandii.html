Penstemon penlandii

Penstemon penlandii, commonly known as Penland's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and displays clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon penlandii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
