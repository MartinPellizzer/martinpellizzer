Potentilla griffithii var. concolor

Potentilla griffithii var. concolor, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Himalayan potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, exhibiting a compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, single or double pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
