Prunus amygdalus var. ovalifolia

Prunus amygdalus var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a hard shell, similar to other almond varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
