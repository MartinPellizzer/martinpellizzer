Pyrus benthamiana

Pyrus benthamiana, commonly known as the Benthams' pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, edible pears that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.
