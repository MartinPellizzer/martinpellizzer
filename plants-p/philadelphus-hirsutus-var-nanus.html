Philadelphus hirsutus var. nanus

Philadelphus hirsutus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf gold fringe bush, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its hirsute appearance. It is typically found in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
