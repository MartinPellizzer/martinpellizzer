Proustia pyrifolia f. pyrifolia

Proustia pyrifolia f. pyrifolia, commonly known as the fireleaf pittosporum, is a cultivar of the pittosporum genus native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed, dark green leaves that turn a vivid red or orange in autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal color changes.
