Phyllagathis guttata

Phyllagathis guttata, commonly known as the spotted leaf mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in small, glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in brackish or saline environments, commonly found in mangrove ecosystems.

The flowers are typically white or pink, and the plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.
