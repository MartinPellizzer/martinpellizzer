Polygala kimberleyensis

Polygala kimberleyensis, commonly known as the Kimberley milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Kimberley region of northern Western Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pale yellow to white in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

It plays an important ecological role in its environment, providing nectar for native pollinators.
