Pterostylis nana var. pyramidalis

Pterostylis nana var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal sun orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, upright flower that resembles a miniature sun. The flower has a pale yellow to greenish hue with a distinctive, narrow lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices.

The plant's morphology is adapted to its specific ecological niche, with specialized structures for pollination by native insects.
