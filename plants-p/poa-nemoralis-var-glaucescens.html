Poa nemoralis var. glaucescens

Poa nemoralis var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage that gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with silver or blue hues.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in maintaining biodiversity in grassland ecosystems.
