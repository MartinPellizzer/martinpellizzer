Phylica villosa

Phylica villosa, commonly known as the velvet bushwillow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silky-white foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Phylica villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
