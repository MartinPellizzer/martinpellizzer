Polypodium leschenaultianum

Polypodium leschenaultianum, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking addition to shaded gardens. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires high humidity and indirect light to flourish. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Polypodium leschenaultianum plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
