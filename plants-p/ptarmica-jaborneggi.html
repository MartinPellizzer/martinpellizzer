Ptarmica jaborneggi

Ptarmica jaborneggi, commonly known as the alpine gentian, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and surrounding mountain areas.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in cold, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Ptarmica jaborneggi plays a vital role in alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization in harsh mountain conditions.
