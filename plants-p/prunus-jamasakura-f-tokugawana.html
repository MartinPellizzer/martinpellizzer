Prunus jamasakura f. tokugawana

Prunus jamasakura f. tokugawana, commonly known as the Tokugawa cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has a compact, rounded form with dense foliage and is valued for its ornamental beauty. It is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic appeal.

The cultivar is named in honor of the Tokugawa shogunate and is a symbol of cultural significance in Japan.
