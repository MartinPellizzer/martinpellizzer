Psychotria sagrana

Psychotria sagrana, commonly known as the sacred psychotria, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in areas with high humidity and warm climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Due to its hallucinogenic properties, it is often associated with traditional shamanic practices in indigenous communities.
