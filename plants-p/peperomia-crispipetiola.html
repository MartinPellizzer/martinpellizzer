Peperomia crispipetiola

Peperomia crispipetiola, commonly known as the wavy-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, and textured leaves that are often crinkled or curled, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for both novice and experienced growers.
