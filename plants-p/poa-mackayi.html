Poa mackayi

Poa mackayi, commonly known as Mackay's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa mackayi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
