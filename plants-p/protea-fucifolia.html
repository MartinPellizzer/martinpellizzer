Protea fucifolia

Protea fucifolia, commonly known as the coastal protea, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a flower, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with a white or pale yellow center, and bloom during the summer months.

Protea fucifolia is a valuable species in the Proteaceae family, known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
