Palaquium majas

Palaquium majas, commonly known as the Java apple or red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, segmented rind that ranges from reddish to purplish in color. The pulp is juicy and has a sweet, acidic flavor, often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
