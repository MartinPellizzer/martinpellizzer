Prunus ussuriensis

Prunus ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The Ussuri cherry is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
