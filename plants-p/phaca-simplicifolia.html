Phaca simplicifolia

Phaca simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved phaca, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and often have a subtle fragrance.

Phaca simplicifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits to pollinators.
