Panicum bossii

Panicum bossii, commonly known as African millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its seeds are edible and have been used as a food source in some regions.

Panicum bossii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in agricultural settings.
