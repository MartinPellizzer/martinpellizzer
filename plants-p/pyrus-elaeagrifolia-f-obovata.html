Pyrus elaeagrifolia f. obovata

Pyrus elaeagrifolia f. obovata, commonly known as the obovate pear, is a botanical variant of the pear species characterized by its broad, rounded leaves.

The plant typically exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with dense foliage, and its leaves are obovate in shape, narrowing toward the apex. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
