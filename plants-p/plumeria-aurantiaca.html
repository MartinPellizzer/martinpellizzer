Plumeria aurantiaca

Plumeria aurantiaca, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces a fragrant scent, especially in the evening. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are also used in traditional ceremonies and perfumery.
