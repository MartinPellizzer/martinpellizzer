Piper resatum

Piper resatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and dark, peppercorn-like fruits. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit, which ripens to a dark purple or black color, is the source of the spice known as black pepper.
