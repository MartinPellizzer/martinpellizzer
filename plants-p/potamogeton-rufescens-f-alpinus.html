Potamogeton rufescens f. alpinus

Potamogeton rufescens f. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a subspecies of the pondweed genus Potamogeton.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds, especially in alpine or mountainous regions. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, nutrient-poor waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
