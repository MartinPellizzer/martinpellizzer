Papaver pyrenaicum aurantiacum

Papaver pyrenaicum aurantiacum, commonly known as the Pyrenean poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
