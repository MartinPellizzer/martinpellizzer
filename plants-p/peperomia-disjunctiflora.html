Peperomia disjunctiflora

Peperomia disjunctiflora, commonly known as the Disjunct-flowered Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically in shades of green or red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
