Pygeum parviflorum var. subcordatum

Pygeum parviflorum var. subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf African chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated leaves with a distinctive subcordate base. The plant produces catkin-like inflorescences, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
