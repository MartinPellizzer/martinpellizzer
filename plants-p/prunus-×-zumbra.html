Prunus × zumbra

Prunus × zumbra, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus mandshurica and Prunus serrulata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 feet. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Prunus × zumbra is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
