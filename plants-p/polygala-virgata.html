Polygala virgata

Polygala virgata, commonly known as the silver wisp or slender milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in spikes. Its leaves are alternate and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Polygala virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
