Pegaeophyton sinense var. robustum

Pegaeophyton sinense var. robustum, commonly known as the robust Chinese sea pine, is a rare and ancient plant species with a unique botanical structure.

It is characterized by its woody, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to coastal and saline environments. This plant exhibits a primitive vascular system, suggesting evolutionary links to early land plants. Its reproductive structures, including cone-like formations, indicate a gymnosperm-like reproductive strategy.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for studying plant adaptation and evolution.
