Phlox longifolia subsp. humilis

Phlox longifolia subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the low phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of fragrant, pink to lilac-colored flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
