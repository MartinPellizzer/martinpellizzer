Physostigma laxius

Physostigma laxius, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
