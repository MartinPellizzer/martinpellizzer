Pleurothallis pandurata

Pleurothallis pandurata, commonly known as the pandurata orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, linear leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained, organic soil. Its flowers bloom during the rainy season, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis pandurata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
