Pteracanthus alatus

Pteracanthus alatus, commonly known as the winged pteracanthus, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive wing-like structures on its leaves, which are believed to aid in water retention and light absorption. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
