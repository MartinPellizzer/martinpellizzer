Piper planipes var. planipes

Piper planipes var. planipes, commonly known as the green pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
