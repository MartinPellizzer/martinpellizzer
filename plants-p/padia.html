Padia

Padia, commonly known as the wild coffee plant, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The leaves and fruits of padia have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
