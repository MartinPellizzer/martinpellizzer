Procris nudiflora

Procris nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lack of leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
