Psoralea macrostachya var. longiloba

Psoralea macrostachya var. longiloba, commonly known as the long-stalked psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers in late spring, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated floral stems, which give it its specific epithet "longiloba.".
