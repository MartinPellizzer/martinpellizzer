Phaius veratrifolius

Phaius veratrifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with clustered flowers that are often pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid has a complex root system and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
