Polygonum meisnerianum var. jalapense

Polygonum meisnerianum var. jalapense, commonly known as Mexican knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its unique jointed stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish tint, and it produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in late summer.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
