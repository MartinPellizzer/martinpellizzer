Papaver lapponicum subsp. dasycarpum

Papaver lapponicum subsp.

dasycarpum, commonly known as the Lapland poppy, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It features striking, bright red to orange flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped structure.

The plant has a robust taproot and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.
