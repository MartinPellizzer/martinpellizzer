Phaca misera

Phaca misera, commonly known as the bitter panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
