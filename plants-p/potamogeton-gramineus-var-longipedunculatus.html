Potamogeton gramineus var. longipedunculatus

Potamogeton gramineus var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-pedunculate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces elongated, branched inflorescences that bear small, greenish flowers. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This variety is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands, contributing to aquatic ecosystem stability by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
