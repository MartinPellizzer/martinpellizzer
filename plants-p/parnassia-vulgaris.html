Parnassia vulgaris

Parnassia vulgaris, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its distinctive white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy.
