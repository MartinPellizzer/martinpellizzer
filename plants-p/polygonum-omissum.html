Polygonum omissum

Polygonum omissum, commonly known as red root or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and wetlands. The plant features slender, reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules.

Polygonum omissum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
