Platanthera bifolia var. solstitialis

Platanthera bifolia var. solstitialis, commonly known as the summer lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It features a slender, erect stem with a cluster of narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces a dense spike of pale green to white flowers, which are often tinged with pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and requires a specific mycorrhizal association for nutrient uptake.

This variety is distinguished by its flowering time, typically occurring around the summer solstice.
