Psidium apaense

Psidium apaense, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Psidium apaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
