Plagiostachys strobilifera var. conica

Plagiostachys strobilifera var. conica, commonly known as the cone-bearing bamboo, is a variant of the species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and the presence of cone-like structures at the base of its stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spore-bearing cones. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
