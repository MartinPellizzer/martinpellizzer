Plectranthus kapatensis

Plectranthus kapatensis, commonly known as the Kapatensis mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a strong aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
