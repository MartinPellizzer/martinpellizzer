Polygonum chymophyllum

Polygonum chymophyllum, commonly known as the ghost pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and often edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, and its leaves are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant is also notable for its ability to form dense colonies, which can influence the local ecosystem by altering soil composition and supporting specific insect populations.
