Prestonia amabilis

Prestonia amabilis, commonly known as the sweet scent plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Prestonia amabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fragrance and visual characteristics.
