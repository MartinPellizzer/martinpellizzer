Phyllanthus prominulatus

Phyllanthus prominulatus, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
