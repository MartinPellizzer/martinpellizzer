Protea poggei subsp. heliophila

Protea poggei subsp.

heliophila, commonly known as the Cape Protea, is a member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the showy floral display.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
