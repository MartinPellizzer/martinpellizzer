Paepalanthus pauciflorus var. glaziovii

Paepalanthus pauciflorus var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's pincushion cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny, spherical stems that resemble a pincushion, providing structural support and water storage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its leaves are reduced to spines, minimizing water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience in low-water conditions.
