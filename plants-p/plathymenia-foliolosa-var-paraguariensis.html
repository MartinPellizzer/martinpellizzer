Plathymenia foliolosa var. paraguariensis

Plathymenia foliolosa var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan ironwood, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This variant is notable for its ecological and economic importance in its native habitat.
