Parasenecio tanakae

Parasenecio tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's parasenecio, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive inflorescence structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are generally narrow and hairy, providing a degree of protection against strong winds and intense sunlight.

The flowers, which bloom in the summer, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
