Pulsatilla andina

Pulsatilla andina, commonly known as Andean pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Pulsatilla andina is known for its resilience to harsh climates and is often found in mountainous regions across Bolivia, Chile, and Argentina.
