Polygonum × oleraceum

Polygonum × oleraceum, commonly known as Chinese spinach or New Zealand spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are edible and often used in Asian cuisines. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into three-winged berries, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its nutrient-rich leaves, which are high in vitamins and minerals.

Polygonum × oleraceum is a hybrid species, resulting from crosses between Polygonum persicaria and Polygonum hydropiper.
