Pluchea symphytifolia

Pluchea symphytifolia, commonly known as the swamp cudweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist or aquatic environments, featuring slender stems and small, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for water absorption.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
