Piper citrifolium var. panamense

Piper citrifolium var. panamense, commonly known as Panama pepper or yellow pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in traditional medicine.
