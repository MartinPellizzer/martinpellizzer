Persicaria coccinea var. asprella

Persicaria coccinea var. asprella, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems, which are often more pronounced in cooler weather. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, with a tendency to turn reddish at the base.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
