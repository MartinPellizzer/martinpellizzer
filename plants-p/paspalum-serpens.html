Paspalum serpens

Paspalum serpens, commonly known as the creeping grass or snake grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with slender, wiry stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in clusters.

This plant is often found in moist or wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.
