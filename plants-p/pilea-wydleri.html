Pilea wydleri

Pilea wydleri, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slight sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its ease of care, making it a popular houseplant. It prefers well-draining soil and moderate watering, and it can be propagated through stem cuttings.

Pilea wydleri is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and its ability to improve indoor humidity.
