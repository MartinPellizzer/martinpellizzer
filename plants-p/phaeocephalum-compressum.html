Phaeocephalum compressum

Phaeocephalum compressum, commonly known as the slender whitebeard lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive, pale to whitish thallus with a compressed, leaf-like structure. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. It is typically found on tree trunks and branches in temperate regions.

Its slow growth and specific habitat requirements make it a rare and ecologically significant species.
