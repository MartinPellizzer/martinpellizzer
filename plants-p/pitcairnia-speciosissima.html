Pitcairnia speciosissima

Pitcairnia speciosissima, commonly known as the showy pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-shaped inflorescence that bears vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater, which serves as a habitat for small aquatic organisms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in horticulture for its dramatic floral display and ease of cultivation.
