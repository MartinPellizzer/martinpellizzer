Poa boxiana

Poa boxiana, commonly known as the Boxwood grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Poa boxiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
