Pavonia intermedia rosea

Pavonia intermedia rosea, commonly known as the pink wood rose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of stipules at the base of the leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
