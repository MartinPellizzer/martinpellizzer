Pogostemon gracilis

Pogostemon gracilis, commonly known as the slender pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Pogostemon gracilis is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
