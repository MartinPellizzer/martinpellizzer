Phyllostachys nidularia f. basipilis

Phyllostachys nidularia f. basipilis, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species characterized by its striking yellow-green culms.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is native to China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming dense clusters that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a vibrant green color that adds ornamental value to gardens.

This bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
