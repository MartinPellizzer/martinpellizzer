Phoebe triplinervis var. cubensis

Phoebe triplinervis var. cubensis, commonly known as Cuban beech, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lanceolate, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
