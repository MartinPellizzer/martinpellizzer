Paepalanthus speciosus

Paepalanthus speciosus, commonly known as the showy puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous showy, colorful bracts. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or yellow, which are pollinated by birds and insects. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cool, dry conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
