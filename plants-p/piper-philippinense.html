Piper philippinense

Piper philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery fruits. The plant produces small, elongated berries that turn from green to red when mature. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
