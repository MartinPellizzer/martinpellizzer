Penstemon pseudospectabilis subsp. typicus

Penstemon pseudospectabilis subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the typical penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically produces vibrant purple to pink blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, adding visual interest to gardens.
