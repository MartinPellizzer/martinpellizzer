Pelargonium burtoniae

Pelargonium burtoniae, commonly known as the Burton's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright pink to red blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Pelargonium burtoniae is a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
