Phlox nivalis var. texensis

Phlox nivalis var. texensis, commonly known as Texas snowbells, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in warmer climates.
