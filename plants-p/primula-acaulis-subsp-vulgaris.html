Primula acaulis subsp. vulgaris

Primula acaulis subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features rosettes of oval, dark green leaves that are densely packed at the base.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal medicine.
