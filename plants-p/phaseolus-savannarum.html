Phaseolus savannarum

Phaseolus savannarum, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, producing vibrant red or orange flowers and long, cylindrical pods. The plant is valued for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is closely related to other Phaseolus species, including common beans and tepary beans.
