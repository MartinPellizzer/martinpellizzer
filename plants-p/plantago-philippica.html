Plantago philippica

Plantago philippica, commonly known as Philippine plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowering spike is relatively short, bearing small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
