Portulaca glazioviana

Portulaca glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's moss rose, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom during the summer months.

Portulaca glazioviana is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to spread through seeds and rhizomes.
