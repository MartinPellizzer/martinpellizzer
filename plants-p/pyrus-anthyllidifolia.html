Pyrus anthyllidifolia

Pyrus anthyllidifolia, commonly known as the Himalayan whitebeam, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, yellowish fruits. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
