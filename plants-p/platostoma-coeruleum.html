Platostoma coeruleum

Platostoma coeruleum, commonly known as the blue waxy plant, is a succulent species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom during the cooler months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is often found growing in rocky soils or along rocky outcrops, where it can thrive with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
