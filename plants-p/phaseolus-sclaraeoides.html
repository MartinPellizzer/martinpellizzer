Phaseolus sclaraeoides

Phaseolus sclaraeoides, commonly known as the Brazilian bean or scarlet bean, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing several seeds. Its seeds are typically small and have a smooth, pale to dark brown coat.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
