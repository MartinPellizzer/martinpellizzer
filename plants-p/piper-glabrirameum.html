Piper glabrirameum

Piper glabrirameum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elliptical berries that turn red when mature.

Piper glabrirameum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
