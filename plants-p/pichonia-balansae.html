Pichonia balansae

Pichonia balansae, commonly known as the Balansae pichonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, adapted to the arid conditions of its environment.

Pichonia balansae plays an important ecological role in the unique flora of the Galápagos ecosystem.
