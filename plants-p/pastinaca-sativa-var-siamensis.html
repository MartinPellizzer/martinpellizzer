Pastinaca sativa var. siamensis

Pastinaca sativa var. siamensis, commonly known as Siamese salsify, is a variant of the salsify plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, edible root that is often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and can reach several inches in length, while the flowers are yellow and occur in clusters.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and historical use in herbal medicine.
