Paphiopedilum × powellii

Paphiopedilum × powellii, commonly known as the Powell's vanda, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics typical of its parent species.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, lip-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns and a strong fragrance. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
