Promenaea lentiginosa

Promenaea lentiginosa, commonly known as the spotted fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant features a creeping rhizome with numerous small, clustered leaves that often have a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions as a decorative plant.
