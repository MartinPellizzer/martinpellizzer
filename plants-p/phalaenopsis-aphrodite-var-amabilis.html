Phalaenopsis aphrodite var. amabilis

Phalaenopsis aphrodite var. amabilis, commonly known as the orchid of Venus, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on trees in tropical rainforests. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its beauty and longevity.

This variety is popular in horticulture due to its ease of care and ability to bloom repeatedly.
