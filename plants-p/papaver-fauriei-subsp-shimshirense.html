Papaver fauriei subsp. shimshirense

Papaver fauriei subsp.

shimshirense, commonly known as the Shimshire poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a contrasting black or dark red center.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, elliptical seeds. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
