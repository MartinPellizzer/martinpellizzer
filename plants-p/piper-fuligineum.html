Piper fuligineum

Piper fuligineum, commonly known as black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, almost black, aromatic berries. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a strong, pungent odor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
