Pyrus communis var. marianica

Pyrus communis var. marianica, commonly known as the Marianian pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental value, making it suitable for small gardens or landscapes. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a pleasant fragrance. The fruit is small to medium in size, with a sweet and aromatic flavor.

It is often grown for its aesthetic appeal and edible fruit, thriving in temperate climates.
