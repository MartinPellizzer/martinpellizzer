Primula auricula var. monacensis

Primula auricula var. monacensis, commonly known as the Monacense primrose, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
