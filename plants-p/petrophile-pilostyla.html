Petrophile pilostyla

Petrophile pilostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock-heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland regions. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salt spray.
