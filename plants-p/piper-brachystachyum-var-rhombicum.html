Piper brachystachyum var. rhombicum

Piper brachystachyum var. rhombicum, commonly known as the rhombic pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, aromatic berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Its foliage and fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in regional cuisines.
