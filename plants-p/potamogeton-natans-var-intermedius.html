Potamogeton natans var. intermedius

Potamogeton natans var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, flexible stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, which are often submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable aquatic environments.
