Piper thebaudianum

Piper thebaudianum, commonly known as the Trinidadian pepper or thebaudian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically grown in tropical regions.
