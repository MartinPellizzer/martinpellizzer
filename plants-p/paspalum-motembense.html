Paspalum motembense

Paspalum motembense, commonly known as the East African grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along waterways. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum motembense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
