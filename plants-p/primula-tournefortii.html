Primula tournefortii

Primula tournefortii, commonly known as the Alpine Primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and white. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or meadow habitats at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
