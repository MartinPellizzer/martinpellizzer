Prunus sargentii f. pubescens

Prunus sargentii f. pubescens, commonly known as the Sargent cherry, is a variant of the Prunus sargentii species, which is native to China.

This particular form is characterized by its pubescent, hairy leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is typically a small to medium-sized shrub or tree. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for gardens and horticultural collections.
