Phyllanthus brisbanicus

Phyllanthus brisbanicus, commonly known as the Brisbane waxplant, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and urban environments.

The Brisbane waxplant is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
