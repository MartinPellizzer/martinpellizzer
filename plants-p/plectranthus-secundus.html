Plectranthus secundus

Plectranthus secundus, commonly known as the creeping plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and pairs of opposite leaves, which are typically hairy and have a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often used in horticulture for decorative purposes.
