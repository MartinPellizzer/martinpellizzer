Primula septentrionalis

Primula septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. Its leaves are typically oval and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula septentrionalis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
