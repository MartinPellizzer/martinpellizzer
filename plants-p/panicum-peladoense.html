Panicum peladoense

Panicum peladoense, commonly known as the bare-leaved panic grass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairless, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Panicum peladoense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
