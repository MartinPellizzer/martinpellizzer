Potentilla geranioides subsp. syriaca

Potentilla geranioides subsp.

syriaca, commonly known as Syrian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Syria. It features wiry stems and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental flowers and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
