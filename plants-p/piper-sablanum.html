Piper sablanum

Piper sablanum, commonly known as the Sablan pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, tropical environments and is typically found in lowland forests.
