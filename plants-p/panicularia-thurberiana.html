Panicularia thurberiana

Panicularia thurberiana, commonly known as Thurber's panicled grass, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and panicle-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in branching clusters, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for insects in its native range.
