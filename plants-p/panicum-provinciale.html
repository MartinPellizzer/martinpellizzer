Panicum provinciale

Panicum provinciale, commonly known as the provincial grass, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Panicum provinciale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
