Pleurothallis glaucophylla

Pleurothallis glaucophylla, commonly known as the blue-leaf orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often a striking blue-green color, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with pale yellow or white petals.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
