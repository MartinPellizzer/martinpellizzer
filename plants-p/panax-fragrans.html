Panax fragrans

Panax fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive aromatic leaves and roots. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, and it produces red berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with its roots used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
