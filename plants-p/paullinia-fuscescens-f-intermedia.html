Paullinia fuscescens f. intermedia

Paullinia fuscescens f. intermedia, commonly known as the Brazilian soursop or a type of passionfruit, is a variant of the passionfruit genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically larger and less acidic than those of the common passionfruit. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

This form is often cultivated for its fruit, which has a sweet and aromatic flavor.
