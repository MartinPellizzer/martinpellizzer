Plumeria × longiflora

Plumeria × longiflora, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a strong, sweet scent. The plant produces large, leathery leaves that are typically opposite and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are borne on long, slender peduncles, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.

Plumeria × longiflora is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking blooms and cultural significance in various regions.
