Phaseolus farinosus

Phaseolus farinosus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, often pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its fruit is a slender, spiral-shaped pod that contains several seeds.

Phaseolus farinosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
