Paspalum latifolium

Paspalum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has broad, flat leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often found in marshy habitats.

This species is important for stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
