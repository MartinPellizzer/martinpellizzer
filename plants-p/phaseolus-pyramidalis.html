Phaseolus pyramidalis

Phaseolus pyramidalis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or yellow flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain seeds used for food. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and can grow up to several meters in height. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
