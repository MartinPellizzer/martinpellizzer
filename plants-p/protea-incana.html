Protea incana

Protea incana, commonly known as the silver protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, fluffy inflorescence composed of numerous small white flowers surrounded by bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy or loamy soils. It prefers full sun and well-drained conditions, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The silver protea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
