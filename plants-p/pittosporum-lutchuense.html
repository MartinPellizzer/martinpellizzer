Pittosporum lutchuense

Pittosporum lutchuense, commonly known as the Lutchu Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the island of Lutchu in the Andaman Sea.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland forest habitats. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually elliptical, glossy, and have a smooth texture.

Pittosporum lutchuense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
