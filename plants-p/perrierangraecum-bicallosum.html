Perrierangraecum bicallosum

Perrierangraecum bicallosum, commonly known as the Cape goldring, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense, circular clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its vibrant color and resilience.
