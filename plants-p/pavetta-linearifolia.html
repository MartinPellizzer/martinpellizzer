Pavetta linearifolia

Pavetta linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. The plant features simple, elliptic to lanceolate leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal or axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that contain the seeds.

Pavetta linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
