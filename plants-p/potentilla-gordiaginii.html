Potentilla gordiaginii

Potentilla gordiaginii, commonly known as the Gorbachev's cinquefoil, is a rare and endangered flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements make it highly vulnerable to environmental changes.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique botanical species.
