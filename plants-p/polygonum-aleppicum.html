Polygonum aleppicum

Polygonum aleppicum, commonly known as Aleppo knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and riverbanks.

Polygonum aleppicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
