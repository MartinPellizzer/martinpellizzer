Piper arcuatum

Piper arcuatum, commonly known as the waxy beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, waxy foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a dark purple color.
