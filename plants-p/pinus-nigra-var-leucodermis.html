Pinus nigra var. leucodermis

Pinus nigra var. leucodermis, commonly known as the white bark pine, is a variety of the black pine species native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale bark, which contrasts with its dark green needles. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its cones are usually small and cylindrical, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique appearance.
