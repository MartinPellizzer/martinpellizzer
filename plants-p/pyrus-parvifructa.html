Pyrus parvifructa

Pyrus parvifructa, commonly known as the small-fruited pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits that ripen in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit characteristics.
