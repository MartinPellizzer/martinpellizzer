Passiflora mistratensis

Passiflora mistratensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces orange to yellow berries that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and dark green upper surface.

Passiflora mistratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
