Portlandia acunae

Portlandia acunae, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of Chile.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, often covered in a fine layer of hair.
