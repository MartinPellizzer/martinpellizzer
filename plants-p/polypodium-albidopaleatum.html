Polypodium albidopaleatum

Polypodium albidopaleatum, commonly known as the pale-veined wall fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are usually light green with pale veins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid, semi-shaded conditions.
