Piper stenostachys

Piper stenostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many pepper plants, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. These berries are typically harvested when mature and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
