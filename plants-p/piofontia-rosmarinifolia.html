Piofontia rosmarinifolia

Piofontia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary-leaved piofontia, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in mountainous areas. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pale blue.

Piofontia rosmarinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
