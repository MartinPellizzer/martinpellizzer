Pelecinus

Pelecinus, commonly known as the spear-nut beetle, is a genus of beetles belonging to the family Peleciidae.

These insects are characterized by their elongated, slender bodies and long, curved horns on their heads. They are primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, where they inhabit various types of vegetation. Pelecinus species are often associated with plants that provide shelter or food for their larvae.

Their life cycle includes a distinct larval stage that develops in the soil or within plant matter.
