Pyrus communis var. microcarpa

Pyrus communis var. microcarpa, commonly known as the miniature pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree characterized by its small, compact growth habit.

It produces small, sweet, and often edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal, featuring attractive foliage and a bushy structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic value and its edible yield.
