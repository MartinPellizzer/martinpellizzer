Panicum lindenbergianum

Panicum lindenbergianum, commonly known as Lindenberg's grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, greenish or brownish florets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
