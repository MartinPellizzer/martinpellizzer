Pennisetum spicatum var. socium

Pennisetum spicatum var. socium, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce fluffy, purple-pink plumes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
