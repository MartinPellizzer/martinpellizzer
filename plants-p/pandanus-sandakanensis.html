Pandanus sandakanensis

Pandanus sandakanensis, commonly known as the Sandakan pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The Sandakan pandanus is valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in some regions.
