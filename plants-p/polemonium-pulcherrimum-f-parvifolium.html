Polemonium pulcherrimum f. parvifolium

Polemonium pulcherrimum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved Eastern polemonium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically smaller than those of the typical form. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
