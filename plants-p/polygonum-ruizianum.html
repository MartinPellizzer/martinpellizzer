Polygonum ruizianum

Polygonum ruizianum, commonly known as the Ruiz's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in late spring to early summer.

Polygonum ruizianum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.
