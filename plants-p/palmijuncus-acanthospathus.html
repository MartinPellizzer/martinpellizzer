Palmijuncus acanthospathus

Palmijuncus acanthospathus, commonly known as the African fan palm, is a unique species native to southern Africa.

It is a small, ornamental palm characterized by its clustering growth habit and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue berries, which are edible for humans and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
