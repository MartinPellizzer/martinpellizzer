Potentilla agrimonioides var. capitata

Potentilla agrimonioides var. capitata, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in rounded heads, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens and rockeries.
