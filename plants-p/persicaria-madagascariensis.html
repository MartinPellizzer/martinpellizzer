Persicaria madagascariensis

Persicaria madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions, though it can become invasive in non-native habitats.
