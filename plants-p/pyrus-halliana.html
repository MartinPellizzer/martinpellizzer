Pyrus halliana

Pyrus halliana, commonly known as the Hall's pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits that are similar to pears. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.

Pyrus halliana is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
