Pseudodracontium macrophyllum

Pseudodracontium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshy areas near rivers.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
