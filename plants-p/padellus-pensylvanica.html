Padellus pensylvanica

Padellus pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania padellus, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, leathery fruits. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
