Panicum albertense

Panicum albertense, commonly known as Alberta grass, is a perennial warm-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, brown seed heads that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Panicum albertense is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
