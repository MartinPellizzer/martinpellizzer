Panicum decompositum var. acuminatissimum

Panicum decompositum var. acuminatissimum, commonly known as the acute-leaved panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, including sandy and dry environments. The plant produces fluffy, airy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements due to their aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are narrow and have a sharp, pointed tip, which gives it its specific variety name.

This grass is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and birds.
