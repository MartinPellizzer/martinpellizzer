Pachyphyllum

Pachyphyllum, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a genus of succulent plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

These plants are characterized by their thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing them to survive in harsh, dry environments. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps reduce water loss by minimizing surface area exposed to the sun. Pachyphyllum species produce small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often during the spring or early summer.

They are popular among cactus and succulent enthusiasts for their low maintenance and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
