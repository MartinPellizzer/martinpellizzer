Ptilagrostis dichotoma var. roshevitsiana

Ptilagrostis dichotoma var. roshevitsiana, commonly known as the Roshevitsian's feathergrass, is a variant of the feathergrass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and distinctive branching pattern. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to dry conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a tufted appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
