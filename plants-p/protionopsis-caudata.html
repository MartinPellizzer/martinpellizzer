Protionopsis caudata

Protionopsis caudata, commonly known as the trailing bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
