Plectranthus nepetifolius

Plectranthus nepetifolius, commonly known as the catmint plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
