Pulsatilla alpina subsp. alpicola

Pulsatilla alpina subsp.

alpicola, commonly known as the alpine pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a rosette of hairy, basal leaves and a short, upright stem. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in alpine ecosystems.
