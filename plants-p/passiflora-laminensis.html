Passiflora laminensis

Passiflora laminensis, commonly known as the pink passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique combination of pink, white, and blue petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora laminensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
