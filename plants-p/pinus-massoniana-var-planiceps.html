Pinus massoniana var. planiceps

Pinus massoniana var. planiceps, commonly known as the flat-capped Chinese fir, is a variant of the Chinese fir native to southern China.

It is a medium to large evergreen conifer characterized by its flat, broad cones and dense, needle-like foliage. The tree typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. Its cones are distinctive, with a wide, flattened shape that sets it apart from other pine species.

This variety is valued for its timber and is often used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
