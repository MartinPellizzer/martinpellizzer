Phyllanthus tuberculatus

Phyllanthus tuberculatus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
