Pseudognaphalium sandwicensium var. molokaiense

Pseudognaphalium sandwicensium var. molokaiense, commonly known as Molokaʻi everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian island of Molokaʻi.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to withstand salty air and strong winds, making it well adapted to its native environment.

This variety is considered a rare and endemic species, highlighting its ecological and botanical significance in Hawaiian flora.
