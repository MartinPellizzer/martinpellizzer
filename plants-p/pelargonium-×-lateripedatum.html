Pelargonium × lateripedatum

Pelargonium × lateripedatum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, white, and purple. The plant typically has serrated, lobed leaves with distinct zonation patterns, which give it its common name. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Pelargonium × lateripedatum is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various climates.
