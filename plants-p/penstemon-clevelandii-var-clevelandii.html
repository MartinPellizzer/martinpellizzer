Penstemon clevelandii var. clevelandii

Penstemon clevelandii var. clevelandii, commonly known as Cleveland's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as cliffs and slopes. The plant features tubular blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of down.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
