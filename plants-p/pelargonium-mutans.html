Pelargonium mutans

Pelargonium mutans, commonly known as the variable geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from ovate to lanceolate shapes. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
