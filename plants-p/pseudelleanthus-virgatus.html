Pseudelleanthus virgatus

Pseudelleanthus virgatus, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing a low-growing habit with slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster, which are followed by pale, woolly seed heads. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pseudelleanthus virgatus is adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
