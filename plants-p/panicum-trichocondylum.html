Panicum trichocondylum

Panicum trichocondylum, commonly known as the woolly panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
