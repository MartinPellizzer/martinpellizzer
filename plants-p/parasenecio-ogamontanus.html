Parasenecio ogamontanus

Parasenecio ogamontanus, commonly known as the Ogamontan ghostflower, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale-green foliage and small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom during the short growing season. The plant is adapted to harsh, cold environments and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 4,000 meters. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is highly vulnerable to environmental changes and human disturbance.

Conservation efforts are underway to protect this unique botanical species from extinction.
