Peperomia parcifolia

Peperomia parcifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are densely covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
