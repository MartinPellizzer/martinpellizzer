Parodia comarapana var. paucicostata

Parodia comarapana var. paucicostata, commonly known as the Pencil Cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a desirable addition to rock gardens or as a decorative plant.
