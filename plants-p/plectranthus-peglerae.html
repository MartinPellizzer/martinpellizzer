Plectranthus peglerae

Plectranthus peglerae, commonly known as the Cape fumitory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in warm climates.

Plectranthus peglerae is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
