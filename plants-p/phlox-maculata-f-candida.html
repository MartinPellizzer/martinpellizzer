Phlox maculata f. candida

Phlox maculata f. candida, commonly known as the white garden phlox, is a cultivar of the common phlox species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive red or pink spot at the base of each bloom. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant summer display.
