Prunus serrulata f. chrysanthemoides

Prunus serrulata f. chrysanthemoides, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and serrated, providing a lush green canopy in the growing season.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
