Polypodium cardiophyllum

Polypodium cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heart fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped fronds that are often deeply lobed. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The heart fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
