Puccinellia suecica f. gigantea

Puccinellia suecica f. gigantea, commonly known as giant Swedish grass, is a perennial grass species native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and coarse, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically forms dense clumps and produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wet meadows and along the edges of bogs.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to wetland environments and its ecological importance in supporting diverse insect and bird populations.
