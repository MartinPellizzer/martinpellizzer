Pyrus nivalis var. austriaca

Pyrus nivalis var. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to central Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. The fruit is a small, round pome that ripens in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
