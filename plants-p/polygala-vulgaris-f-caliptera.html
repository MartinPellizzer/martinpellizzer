Polygala vulgaris f. caliptera

Polygala vulgaris f. caliptera, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
