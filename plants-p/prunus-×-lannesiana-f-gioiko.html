Prunus × lannesiana f. gioiko

Prunus × lannesiana f. gioiko, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus that exhibits striking floral displays.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse blooms of pink to white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, resulting in unique ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and public landscapes.

Its botanical appeal lies in its delicate flowers and seasonal foliage changes, which enhance its ornamental value.
