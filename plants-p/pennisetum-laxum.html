Pennisetum laxum

Pennisetum laxum, commonly known as soft beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fluffy, silver-tan seed heads that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that helps reduce water loss.

Pennisetum laxum is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate, ornate foliage and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
