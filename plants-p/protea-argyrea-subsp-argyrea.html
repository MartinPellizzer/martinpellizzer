Protea argyrea subsp. argyrea

Protea argyrea subsp.

argyrea, commonly known as the silverleaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape.

The plant typically grows in shrubland and fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a silvery-white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting floral display.
