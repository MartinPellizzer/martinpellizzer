Pilosella koernickeana subsp. denigrata

Pilosella koernickeana subsp.

denigrata, commonly known as the darkleaf globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, yellowish-white composite flowers surrounded by pale green to dark brown involucral bracts. Its leaves are typically hairy and often have a dark green to almost black coloration, giving it its common name.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
