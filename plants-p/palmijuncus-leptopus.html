Palmijuncus leptopus

Palmijuncus leptopus, commonly known as the slender palm cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has spiny, paddle-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, appearing at the tips of the stems during the dry season.

Despite its cactus-like appearance, it is botanically classified as a gymnosperm, making it a rare and interesting species within the plant kingdom.
