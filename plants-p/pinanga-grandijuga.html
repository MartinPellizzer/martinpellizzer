Pinanga grandijuga

Pinanga grandijuga, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant pinanga, is a striking member of the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, forming dense clumps. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pinanga grandijuga is valued not only for its aesthetic qualities but also for its potential ecological and economic significance in local communities.
