Pedleya

Pedleya, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. Pedleya species typically grow in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance in its natural habitat.
