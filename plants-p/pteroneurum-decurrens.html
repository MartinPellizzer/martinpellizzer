Pteroneurum decurrens

Pteroneurum decurrens, commonly known as the creeping sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, prostrate stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, and it is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Pteroneurum decurrens is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
