Penstemon concinnus

Penstemon concinnus, commonly known as the hummingbird penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Penstemon concinnus is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
