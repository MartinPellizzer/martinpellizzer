Pholidota clypeata

Pholidota clypeata, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

Pholidota clypeata is also noted for its rubber-like sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and industry.
