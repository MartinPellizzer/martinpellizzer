Pachystigma lasianthum

Pachystigma lasianthum, commonly known as the Cape fennel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, pungent scent, while the flowers are yellow and occur in clusters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
