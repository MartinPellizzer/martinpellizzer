Plectranthus hirtus

Plectranthus hirtus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
