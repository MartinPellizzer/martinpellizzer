Potamogeton perfoliatus var. muelleri

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Australia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water currents. The plant is characterized by its perfoliate leaves, which are pierced by the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This variant is often found in still or slow-moving waters, contributing to water clarity and oxygenation.
