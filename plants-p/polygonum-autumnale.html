Polygonum autumnale

Polygonum autumnale, commonly known as autumnal knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the late summer to early autumn months. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum autumnale is known for its ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
