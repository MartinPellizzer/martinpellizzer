Piper scabrifolium

Piper scabrifolium, commonly known as rough-leaved pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Piper scabrifolium is often found in humid, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
