Pachyrhizus erosus var. typicus

Pachyrhizus erosus var. typicus, commonly known as the sweet potato, is a flowering plant in the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are rich in carbohydrates and nutrients. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow or purple flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its edible roots.

The variety typicus is one of the most widely grown types of sweet potato worldwide.
