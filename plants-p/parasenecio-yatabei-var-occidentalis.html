Parasenecio yatabei var. occidentalis

Parasenecio yatabei var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western yatabei gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root systems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with showy ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
