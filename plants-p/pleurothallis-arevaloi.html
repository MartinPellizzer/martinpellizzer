Pleurothallis arevaloi

Pleurothallis arevaloi, commonly known as the Arevalo's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
