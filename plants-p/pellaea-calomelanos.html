Pellaea calomelanos

Pellaea calomelanos, commonly known as the silver spleenwort, is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Pellaea calomelanos is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
