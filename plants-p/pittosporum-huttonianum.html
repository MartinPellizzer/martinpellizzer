Pittosporum huttonianum

Pittosporum huttonianum, commonly known as the Hutton's pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a leathery texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum huttonianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native New Zealand ecosystems.
