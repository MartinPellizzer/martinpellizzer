Piptadenia chrysostachys

Piptadenia chrysostachys, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in dense, pendulous clusters. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often dispersed by wind.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
