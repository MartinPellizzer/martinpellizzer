Plantago agrostophylla

Plantago agrostophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features erect, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
