Pleroma arboreum

Pleroma arboreum, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically range from yellow to red in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Pleroma arboreum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
