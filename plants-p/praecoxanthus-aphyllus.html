Praecoxanthus aphyllus

Praecoxanthus aphyllus, commonly known as the early yellow-flowered plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant is notable for its early blooming habit, with yellow flowers appearing before the leaves fully develop. Its foliage is minimal, with the plant relying heavily on its floral display for reproduction.

The structure of its flowers is simple, consisting of a single whorl of petals and stamens, which is typical of many early-flowering species.
