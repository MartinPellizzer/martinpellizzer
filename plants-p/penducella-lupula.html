Penducella lupula

Penducella lupula, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub-like plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Penducella lupula is adapted to dry, saline soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
