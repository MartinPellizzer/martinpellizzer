Parosela vernicia var. citrina

Parosela vernicia var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow-flowered parosela, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
