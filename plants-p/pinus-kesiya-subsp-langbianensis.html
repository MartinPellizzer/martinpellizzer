Pinus kesiya subsp. langbianensis

Pinus kesiya subsp.

langbianensis, commonly known as the Langbian pine, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is a coniferous tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green.

The cones of this subspecies are relatively small and mature within a single growing season. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often growing in mixed forests.
