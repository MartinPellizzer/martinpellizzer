Polygala duarteana

Polygala duarteana, commonly known as the Duarte's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
