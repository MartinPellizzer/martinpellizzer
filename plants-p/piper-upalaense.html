Piper upalaense

Piper upalaense, commonly known as the Upala pepper, is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of Central America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant produces small, green, and often spiny fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
