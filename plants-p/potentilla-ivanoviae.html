Potentilla ivanoviae

Potentilla ivanoviae, commonly known as Ivanov's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically divided into five lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla ivanoviae is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky soils at high elevations.
