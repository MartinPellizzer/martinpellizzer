Primula socialis

Primula socialis, commonly known as the social primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Primula socialis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
