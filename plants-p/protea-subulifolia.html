Protea subulifolia

Protea subulifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, needle-like leaves that give it its scientific name, "subulifolia," meaning "needle-leaved.". The plant produces showy, cone-shaped flower heads that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the species. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and is also of ecological importance in its native habitat.
