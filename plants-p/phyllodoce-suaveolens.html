Phyllodoce suaveolens

Phyllodoce suaveolens, commonly known as sweet mountain-mint, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular and emit a pleasant fragrance when crushed. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Phyllodoce suaveolens is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
