Pandanus fibrosus

Pandanus fibrosus, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense rosette around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into orange-colored berries. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts and thatching.

Pandanus fibrosus thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove swamps and sandy soils.
