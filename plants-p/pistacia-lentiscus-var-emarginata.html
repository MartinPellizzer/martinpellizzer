Pistacia lentiscus var. emarginata

Pistacia lentiscus var. emarginata, commonly known as the Greek mastic tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments due to its deep root system and waxy leaves. Its fruit, known as a drupe, contains a resinous substance called mastic, which has been used for centuries in various applications.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
