Platanus occidentalis var. palmeri

Platanus occidentalis var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's sycamore, is a rare variant of the American sycamore.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark, which is lighter in color and less fissured compared to the typical sycamore. This plant is native to the southwestern United States, particularly in Arizona and New Mexico. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along riverbanks and in canyons.

The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a smooth margin, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
