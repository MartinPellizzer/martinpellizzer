Pongamia horsfieldii

Pongamia horsfieldii, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Pongamia horsfieldii is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
