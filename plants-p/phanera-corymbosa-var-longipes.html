Phanera corymbosa var. longipes

Phanera corymbosa var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bats. The plant has a woody stem with persistent, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warm seasons, and it thrives in humid, sunny environments.

The variety longipes is distinguished by its longer peduncles compared to the typical form.
