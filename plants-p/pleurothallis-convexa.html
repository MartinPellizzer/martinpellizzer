Pleurothallis convexa

Pleurothallis convexa, commonly known as the convex pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and are known for their intricate, intricate structure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
