Prionostemma

Prionostemma, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped or ovate, with hairy surfaces. They produce small, yellow flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

Prionostemma species are often found in moist, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
