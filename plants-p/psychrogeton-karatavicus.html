Psychrogeton karatavicus

Psychrogeton karatavicus, commonly known as the Karatavicum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its perennial growth habit and distinctive blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptation to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Psychrogeton karatavicus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native range.
