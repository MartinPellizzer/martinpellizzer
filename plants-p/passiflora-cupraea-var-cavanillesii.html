Passiflora cupraea var. cavanillesii

Passiflora cupraea var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are typically small and yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its potential culinary uses.
