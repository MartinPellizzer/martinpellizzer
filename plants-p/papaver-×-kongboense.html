Papaver × kongboense

Papaver × kongboense, commonly known as the Himalayan poppy, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between *Papaver bracteatum* and *Papaver somniferum*.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a dark center. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the stems are sturdy with a hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
