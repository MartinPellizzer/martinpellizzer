Pinus nigra var. yaltirikiana

Pinus nigra var. yaltirikiana, commonly known as the Turkish black pine, is a variety of the black pine species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 7 centimeters long. The plant produces cones that are elongated and often have a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
