Pisum oleraceus var. govorovii

Pisum oleraceus var. govorovii, commonly known as the Siberian pea shrub, is a perennial legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often cultivated for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

The seeds are edible and can be used in various culinary applications.
