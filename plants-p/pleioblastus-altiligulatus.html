Pleioblastus altiligulatus

Pleioblastus altiligulatus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and striking silvery-gray sheaths that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, elongated leaves that remain throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to moist, shaded environments.

Pleioblastus altiligulatus is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
