Prosaptia pinnatifida

Prosaptia pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are linear with a pinnately arranged structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Prosaptia pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
