Plectranthus coetsa var. macraei

Plectranthus coetsa var. macraei, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive grayish-green color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its rapid growth and ornamental appeal.
