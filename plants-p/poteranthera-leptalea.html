Poteranthera leptalea

Poteranthera leptalea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
