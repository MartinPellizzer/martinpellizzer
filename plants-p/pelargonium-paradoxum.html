Pelargonium paradoxum

Pelargonium paradoxum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. The leaves are typically lobed and have a velvety texture, often displaying distinctive variegation patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
