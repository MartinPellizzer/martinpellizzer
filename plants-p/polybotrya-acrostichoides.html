Polybotrya acrostichoides

Polybotrya acrostichoides, commonly known as the broad-leaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, which is typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant's rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
