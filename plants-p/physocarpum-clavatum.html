Physocarpum clavatum

Physocarpum clavatum, commonly known as the clustered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges.

Physocarpum clavatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
