Persea baileyana

Persea baileyana, commonly known as the Mexican white ash or Mexican ash, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, simple, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, pale skin and a single seed inside.

Persea baileyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
