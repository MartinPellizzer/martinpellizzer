Potamogeton pusillus subsp. panormitanus

Potamogeton pusillus subsp.

panormitanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinct appearance.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic insects and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
