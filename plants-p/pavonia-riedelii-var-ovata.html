Pavonia riedelii var. ovata

Pavonia riedelii var. ovata, commonly known as the velvetleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
