Phyllanthus gaudichaudii var. samoanus

Phyllanthus gaudichaudii var. samoanus, commonly known as the Samoan goosefoot, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa and nearby regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy species in tropical environments.
