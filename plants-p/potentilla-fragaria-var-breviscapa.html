Potentilla fragaria var. breviscapa

Potentilla fragaria var. breviscapa, commonly known as the short-stalked cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.
