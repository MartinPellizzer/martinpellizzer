Pilocarpus latifolius

Pilocarpus latifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its stimulating effects.

The leaves contain the compound pilocarpine, which is used in pharmaceuticals.
