Potamogeton × miguelensis

Potamogeton × miguelensis, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton natans, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, it can impact water quality and aquatic biodiversity.
