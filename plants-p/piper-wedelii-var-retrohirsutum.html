Piper wedelii var. retrohirsutum

Piper wedelii var. retrohirsutum, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but often have a peppery taste. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
