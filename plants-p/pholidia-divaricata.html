Pholidia divaricata

Pholidia divaricata, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Pholidia divaricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
