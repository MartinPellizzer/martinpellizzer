Prasophyllum ellipticum

Prasophyllum ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic leek orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique botanical species.
