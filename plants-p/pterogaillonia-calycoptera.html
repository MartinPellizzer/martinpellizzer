Pterogaillonia calycoptera

Pterogaillonia calycoptera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Pterogaillonia calycoptera is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
