Phaca densifolia

Phaca densifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a dense, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in composite heads.

Phaca densifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
