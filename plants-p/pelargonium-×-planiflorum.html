Pelargonium × planiflorum

Pelargonium × planiflorum, commonly known as the geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. Native to southern Africa, this plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
