Polypodium lycopodioides f. obtusum

Polypodium lycopodioides f. obtusum, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a leathery texture and a dark green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
