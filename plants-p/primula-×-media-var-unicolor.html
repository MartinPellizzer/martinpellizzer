Primula × media var. unicolor

Primula × media var. unicolor, commonly known as the single-flowered primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces large, showy flowers that are predominantly white or pale yellow. The leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and they form a basal rosette. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers cool, moist conditions and is typically grown in partial shade.
