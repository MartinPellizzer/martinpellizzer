Polemonium occidentale var. lacustre

Polemonium occidentale var. lacustre, commonly known as the lakepolemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

This variant is distinguished by its habitat preference and floral coloration compared to other polemonium species.
