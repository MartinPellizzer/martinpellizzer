Pterostemon mexicanus

Pterostemon mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican whisk fern, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Pterostemonaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing cones, which are typical of ferns.

Pterostemon mexicanus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates.
