Piscidia grandifolia var. grandifolia

Piscidia grandifolia var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
