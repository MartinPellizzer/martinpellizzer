Plantago carrenleofuensis var. latifolia

Plantago carrenleofuensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
