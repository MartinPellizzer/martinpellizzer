Panicum marathense

Panicum marathense, commonly known as tall panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces dense, fluffy inflorescences that are often visible in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to its ecological significance.
