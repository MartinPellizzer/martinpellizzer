Phyllanthus caespitosus

Phyllanthus caespitosus, commonly known as the stonecress or mouse ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, arid environments.

Phyllanthus caespitosus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
