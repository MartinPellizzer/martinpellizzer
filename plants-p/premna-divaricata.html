Premna divaricata

Premna divaricata, commonly known as the Indian nettle or creeping premna, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Premna divaricata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
