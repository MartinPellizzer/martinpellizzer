Podanthum persicum var. multicaule

Podanthum persicum var. multicaule, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its stems are often branched, and it can form compact shrubs or spreads as a ground cover.

The variety multicaule is distinguished by its multiple stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
