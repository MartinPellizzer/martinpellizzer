Pachycarpus asperifolius

Pachycarpus asperifolius, commonly known as the rough-leaved pachycarpus, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark is thick and woody, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
