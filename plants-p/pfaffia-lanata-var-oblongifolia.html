Pfaffia lanata var. oblongifolia

Pfaffia lanata var. oblongifolia, commonly known as curare plant or arrowroot, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its fibrous roots and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal and toxicological purposes.
