Poterium villosum

Poterium villosum, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, low-growing stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a velvety texture, and it thrives in arid, sandy soils.

Poterium villosum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
