Passiflora buchtienii

Passiflora buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtien's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring ample sunlight and well-drained soil.

Passiflora buchtienii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
