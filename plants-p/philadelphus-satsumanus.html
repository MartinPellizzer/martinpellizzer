Philadelphus satsumanus

Philadelphus satsumanus, commonly known as the Satsuma mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a pointed tip. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The Satsuma mock orange is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
