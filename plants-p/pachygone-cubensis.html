Pachygone cubensis

Pachygone cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban thickleaf plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Cuba.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for efficient water absorption.

Pachygone cubensis is often found in rocky soils and dry forests, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
