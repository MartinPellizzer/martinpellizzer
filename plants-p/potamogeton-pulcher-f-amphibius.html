Potamogeton pulcher f. amphibius

Potamogeton pulcher f. amphibius, commonly known as the water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in shallow, slow-moving waters, where it forms dense vegetation that provides habitat for aquatic life.

Its rhizomes allow it to spread and establish itself in various aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
