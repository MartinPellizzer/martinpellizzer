Pavonia gentryi

Pavonia gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's paverola, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

Pavonia gentryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
