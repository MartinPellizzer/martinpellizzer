Phaseolus calcaratus

Phaseolus calcaratus, commonly known as the mountain bean, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive, knobby stem that aids in its climbing growth.

Phaseolus calcaratus is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated in various regions for both food and traditional uses.
