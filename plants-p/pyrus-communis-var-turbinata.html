Pyrus communis var. turbinata

Pyrus communis var. turbinata, commonly known as the Turban apple, is a variant of the common pear that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is distinguished by its large, bulbous fruit shape, which resembles a turban, and its thick, rough skin. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are usually harvested in late summer and are known for their sweet, aromatic flavor.

This variety is valued for both ornamental purposes and its edible fruit, making it a notable cultivar in horticulture.
