Prenanthes acaulis

Prenanthes acaulis, commonly known as the nodding bladderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a cluster of small, greenish-white flowers that droop downward. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a distinctive root system that includes both roots and rhizomes, aiding in its propagation.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in wetland habitats, making it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
