Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. brittingeri

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

brittingeri, commonly known as Brittinger's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink to reddish in color.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
