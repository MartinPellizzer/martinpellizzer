Prunus phaeosticta var. dimorphophylla

Prunus phaeosticta var. dimorphophylla, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the foliage. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the autumn. The fruit is a small, yellowish plum that ripens in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
