Paepalanthus velutinus

Paepalanthus velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, colorful flowers. The plant is notable for its hairy, velvety leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
