Potentilla hololeuca var. subcalvescens

Potentilla hololeuca var. subcalvescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are smooth and slightly succulent, adapting to arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
