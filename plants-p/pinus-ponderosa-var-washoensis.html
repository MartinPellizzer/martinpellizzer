Pinus ponderosa var. washoensis

Pinus ponderosa var. washoensis, commonly known as the Wasco pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in length. The tree produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
