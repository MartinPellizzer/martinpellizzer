Passiflora macrophylla

Passiflora macrophylla, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its rapid growth and vigorous climbing habit make it a significant component of the rainforest ecosystem.

Passiflora macrophylla is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
