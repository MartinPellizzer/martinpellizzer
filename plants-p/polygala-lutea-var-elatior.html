Polygala lutea var. elatior

Polygala lutea var. elatior, commonly known as the golden milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a delicate, fern-like appearance.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
