Protea abyssinica var. brevifolia

Protea abyssinica var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved African protea, is a striking South African plant characterized by its large, showy inflorescences.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental flowering plants. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the fynbos biome. Its flowers are composed of a central cone surrounded by petal-like bracts, which come in various colors like white, pink, and red.

This variety is notable for its compact size and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
