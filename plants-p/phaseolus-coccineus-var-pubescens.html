Phaseolus coccineus var. pubescens

Phaseolus coccineus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It features vibrant red or orange flowers and produces elongated, edible pods that are often harvested when immature. The plant has pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and culinary uses.
