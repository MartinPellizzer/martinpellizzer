Poa pratensis var. strigosa

Poa pratensis var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, carpet-like foliage. The leaves are typically shorter and more rigid compared to the standard variety, with a distinctive hairy texture along the leaf blades. This grass is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
