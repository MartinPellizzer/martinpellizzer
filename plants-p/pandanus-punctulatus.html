Pandanus punctulatus

Pandanus punctulatus, commonly known as the spotted pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive white spots. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences. Its roots are used in traditional crafts, and the leaves are utilized in thatching and weaving.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
