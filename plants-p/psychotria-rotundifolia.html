Psychotria rotundifolia

Psychotria rotundifolia, commonly known as the "angel's trumpet" or "devil's fig," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, rounded leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various alkaloids, some of which have psychoactive properties. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its toxic nature, it should be handled with care.
