Pseudobaccharis polycephala

Pseudobaccharis polycephala, commonly known as the clustered false holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the fall, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a rough, textured surface.

Pseudobaccharis polycephala is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.
