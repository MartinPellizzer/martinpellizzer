Potentilla stepposa

Potentilla stepposa, commonly known as steppe cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Potentilla stepposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and colorful blooms.
