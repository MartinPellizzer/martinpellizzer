Piper aromaticum

Piper aromaticum, commonly known as the long pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, curved berries containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are harvested when partially dried and are used as a spice and medicinal ingredient.

The plant's botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and a distinctive, woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit.
