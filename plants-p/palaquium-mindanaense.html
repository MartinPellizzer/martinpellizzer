Palaquium mindanaense

Palaquium mindanaense, commonly known as the Mindanao persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are typically harvested when fully ripe, turning a deep orange color. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, tangy flavor and is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
