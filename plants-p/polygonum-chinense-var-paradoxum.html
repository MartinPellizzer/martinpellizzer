Polygonum chinense var. paradoxum

Polygonum chinense var. paradoxum, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive in certain regions.
