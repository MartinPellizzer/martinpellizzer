Prasophyllum rotundiflorum

Prasophyllum rotundiflorum, commonly known as the roundflower leek orchid, is an Australian native orchid species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in the cooler climates of southern Australia. The plant produces a single, rounded flower spike that bears small, greenish-white flowers, which are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous appearance. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Prasophyllum rotundiflorum is a perennial herb that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological significance.
