Pelargonium drummondii

Pelargonium drummondii, commonly known as the Drummond's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
