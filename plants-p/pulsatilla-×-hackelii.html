Pulsatilla × hackelii

Pulsatilla × hackelii, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are palmately divided, and it produces follicular fruits that disperse its seeds.

Pulsatilla × hackelii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
