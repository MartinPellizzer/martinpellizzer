Polypodium argyropus

Polypodium argyropus, commonly known as the silver-veined fern, is a tropical fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in a silvery sheen. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polypodium argyropus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
