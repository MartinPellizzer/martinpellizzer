Phaeopappus carthamoides

Phaeopappus carthamoides, commonly known as the red sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Phaeopappus carthamoides is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
