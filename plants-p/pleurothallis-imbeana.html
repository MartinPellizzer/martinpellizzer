Pleurothallis imbeana

Pleurothallis imbeana, commonly known as the "Peruvian orchid," is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the orchid family Orchidaceae and is part of the subgenus Pleurothallis.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often appreciated by orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
