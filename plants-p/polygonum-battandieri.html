Polygonum battandieri

Polygonum battandieri, commonly known as the red root or scarlet polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum battandieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
