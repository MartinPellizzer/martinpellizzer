Persea galeae

Persea galeae, commonly known as the Galápagos persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Galápagos Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and serve as a food source for local wildlife. Persea galeae thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of the Galápagos.

Its presence is an important indicator of the island's biodiversity and ecological health.
