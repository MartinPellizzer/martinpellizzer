Piptochaetium angustifolium

Piptochaetium angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions. It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
