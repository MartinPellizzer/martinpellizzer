Pedicularis oliveriana subsp. lasiantha

Pedicularis oliveriana subsp.

lasiantha, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. This herbaceous perennial produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to orange, often resembling small lanterns.

The plant is characterized by its lack of leaves above the stem, with the photosynthetic function performed by its fleshy, greenish stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
