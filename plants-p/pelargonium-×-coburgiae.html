Pelargonium × coburgiae

Pelargonium × coburgiae, commonly known as the Siam tulip.

This hybrid geranium is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with broad, slightly hairy leaves.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and containers.
