Pandanus thwaitesii

Pandanus thwaitesii, commonly known as the coconut pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making rope and mats.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
