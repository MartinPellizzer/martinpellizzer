Pteris robusta

Pteris robusta, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources such as ponds and streams. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Pteris robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
