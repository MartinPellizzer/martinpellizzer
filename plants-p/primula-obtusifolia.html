Primula obtusifolia

Primula obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically rounded and have a smooth, waxy texture, which helps the plant retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom early in the growing season.
