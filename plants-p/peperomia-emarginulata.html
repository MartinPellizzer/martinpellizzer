Peperomia emarginulata

Peperomia emarginulata, commonly known as the marginated peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a distinctive margin that is often wavy or notched. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia emarginulata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
