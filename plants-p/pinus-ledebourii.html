Pinus ledebourii

Pinus ledebourii, commonly known as the desert pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dense, dark green needles that grow in pairs. The plant produces cones that are typically yellow or reddish in color, containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems and thick bark that help it survive in harsh environments.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
