Phyllanthus schumannianus

Phyllanthus schumannianus, commonly known as Schumann's phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive ovate to elliptical shape with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.
