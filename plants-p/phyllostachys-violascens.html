Phyllostachys violascens

Phyllostachys violascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking purple-colored culms, which are often more intense in cooler climates. The plant typically grows as a clumping bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant coloration and elegant growth habit, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phyllostachys violascens is also valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
