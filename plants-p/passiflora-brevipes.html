Passiflora brevipes

Passiflora brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that resemble a crown, featuring intricate patterns of red, blue, and white. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Passiflora brevipes is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators and biodiversity in its native habitat.
