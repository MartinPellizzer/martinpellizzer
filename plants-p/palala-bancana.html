Palala bancana

Palala bancana, commonly known as the golden waxy plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a distinctive golden hue, which helps in reflecting sunlight and reducing water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of yellow and orange. Its roots are known to have medicinal properties, used traditionally in local remedies.

Palala bancana thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.
