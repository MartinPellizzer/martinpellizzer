Pteronia chrysocomifolia

Pteronia chrysocomifolia, commonly known as the golden feather grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and drought tolerance.

Pteronia chrysocomifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
