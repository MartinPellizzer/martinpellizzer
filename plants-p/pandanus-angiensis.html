Pandanus angiensis

Pandanus angiensis, commonly known as the Anguilla pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the island of Anguilla in the Caribbean and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the leaves. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and sandy soils.

The pandanus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
