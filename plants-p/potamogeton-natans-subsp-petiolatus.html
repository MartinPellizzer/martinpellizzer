Potamogeton natans subsp. petiolatus

Potamogeton natans subsp.

petiolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It has long, slender leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, with a distinct midrib.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in dense spikes, and it often grows in shallow, slow-moving waters. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize sediment, contributing to water clarity.

This subspecies is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
