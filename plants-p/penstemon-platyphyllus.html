Penstemon platyphyllus

Penstemon platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Penstemon platyphyllus is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
