Psedera quinquefolia var. hirsuta

Psedera quinquefolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy five-leaf plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in loose panicles, appearing in early spring.

The hairy five-leaf plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
