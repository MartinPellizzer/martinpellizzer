Primula beesiana

Primula beesiana, commonly known as the bees' primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it blooms during the spring season.

Primula beesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
