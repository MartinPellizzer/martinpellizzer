Potentilla recta subsp. recta

Potentilla recta subsp.

recta, commonly known as upright cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features compound leaves with five elliptical leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
