Prunus lanceolata

Prunus lanceolata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

Prunus lanceolata is also valued for its role in ecological systems and traditional medicine.
