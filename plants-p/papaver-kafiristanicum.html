Papaver kafiristanicum

Papaver kafiristanicum, commonly known as the Kafiristan poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to cold climates and its potential medicinal properties.
