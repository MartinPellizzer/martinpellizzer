Polygala serpyllacea var. mutabilis

Polygala serpyllacea var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, spherical seed pods.

The species is noted for its variable morphology, which contributes to its common name.
