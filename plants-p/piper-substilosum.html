Piper substilosum

Piper substilosum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and can grow up to several meters in length. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
