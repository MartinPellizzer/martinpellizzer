Polypodium singeri

Polypodium singeri, commonly known as the Singer's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams.

The rhizomes of Polypodium singeri are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
