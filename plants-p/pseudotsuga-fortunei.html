Pseudotsuga fortunei

Pseudotsuga fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, needle-like leaves and cone-like structures that mature into woody cones. The tree typically grows to a height of 30 to 50 meters, with a conical or rounded crown. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against pests and diseases.

The plant is valued for its timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental qualities.
