Pedicularis conica

Pedicularis conica, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pedicularis conica is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticultural settings.
