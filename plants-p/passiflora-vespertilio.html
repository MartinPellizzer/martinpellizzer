Passiflora vespertilio

Passiflora vespertilio, commonly known as the evening passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at night. The plant produces edible berries that are yellow or orange in color, containing a juicy, flavorful pulp. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The evening passionflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
