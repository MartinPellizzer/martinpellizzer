Pteris argyraea

Pteris argyraea, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green with a silvery sheen on the underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant.

Pteris argyraea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
