Polyalthia grandifolia

Polyalthia grandifolia, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in local crafts and construction.
