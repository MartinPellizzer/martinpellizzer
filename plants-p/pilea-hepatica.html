Pilea hepatica

Pilea hepatica, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea hepatica is known for its easy care and ability to purify the air, adding both aesthetic and functional value to any space.
