Phyllanthus watsonii

Phyllanthus watsonii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
