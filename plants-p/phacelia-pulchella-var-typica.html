Phacelia pulchella var. typica

Phacelia pulchella var. typica, commonly known as the showy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Phacelia pulchella var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
