Pachira longifolia

Pachira longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf pachira, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, egg-shaped fruits containing seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Pachira longifolia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
