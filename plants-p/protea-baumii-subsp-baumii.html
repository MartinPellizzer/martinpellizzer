Protea baumii subsp. baumii

Protea baumii subsp.

baumii, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish bracts that resemble petals.

The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sugarbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
