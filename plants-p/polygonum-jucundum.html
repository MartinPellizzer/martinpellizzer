Polygonum jucundum

Polygonum jucundum, commonly known as the happy polygon or redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, especially at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming from late summer into early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum jucundum is commonly found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soils.
