Pouzolzia viminea var. strigosula

Pouzolzia viminea var. strigosula, commonly known as the hairy pouzolzia, is a variant of the pouzolzia plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, opposite leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are often green or red in color.

This plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
