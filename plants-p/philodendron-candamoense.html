Philodendron candamoense

Philodendron candamoense, commonly known as the Candamono Philodendron, is a climbing tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
