Pelargonium sidoides

Pelargonium sidoides, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
