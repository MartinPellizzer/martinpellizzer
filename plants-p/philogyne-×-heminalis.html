Philogyne × heminalis

Philogyne × heminalis, commonly known as the hybrid lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Philogyne and Hemionitis species.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically exhibit a range of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant is native to regions with temperate climates and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.

However, it requires careful management to maintain its hybrid characteristics and prevent genetic dilution.
