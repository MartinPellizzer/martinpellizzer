Petrosedum anopetalum

Petrosedum anopetalum, commonly known as the naked-petaled rockrose, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, compact growth with fleshy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
