Prunus serrulata f. sirotae

Prunus serrulata f. sirotae, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins and delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. This form is native to Japan and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also play an important role in pollination and ecosystem support.
