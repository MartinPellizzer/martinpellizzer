Patagua

Patagua, commonly known as the "Mexican firecracker" or "palo de tinta," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or yellow tubular flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Patagua is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in folk remedies for various ailments.
