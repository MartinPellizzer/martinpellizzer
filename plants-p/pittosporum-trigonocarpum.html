Pittosporum trigonocarpum

Pittosporum trigonocarpum, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It has dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces clusters of fragrant white flowers, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
