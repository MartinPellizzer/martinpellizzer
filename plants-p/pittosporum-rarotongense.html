Pittosporum rarotongense

Pittosporum rarotongense, commonly known as the Cook Islands Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the Cook Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy dark green surface and produces fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the local ecosystem of its native habitat.
