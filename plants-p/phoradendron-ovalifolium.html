Phoradendron ovalifolium

Phoradendron ovalifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or yellow flowers that are often clustered together. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it produces its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This plant is often associated with Christmas traditions and has cultural significance in various regions.
