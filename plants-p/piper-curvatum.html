Piper curvatum

Piper curvatum, commonly known as the curly pepper or curvatum pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and grow in clusters, while the fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
