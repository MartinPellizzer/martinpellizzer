Pectis caymanensis var. robusta

Pectis caymanensis var. robusta, commonly known as the Cayman bristlecone, is a flowering plant native to the Cayman Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, bristly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments.
