Phoebe glaucescens

Phoebe glaucescens, commonly known as the greenleaf phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for birds.

Phoebe glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
