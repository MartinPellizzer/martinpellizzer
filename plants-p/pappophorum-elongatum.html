Pappophorum elongatum

Pappophorum elongatum, commonly known as the slender spike grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Pappophorum elongatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
