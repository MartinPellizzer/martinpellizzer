Palicourea luteonivea

Palicourea luteonivea, commonly known as yellow silky leaf.

It is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America, characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
