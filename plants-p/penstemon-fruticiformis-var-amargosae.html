Penstemon fruticiformis var. amargosae

Penstemon fruticiformis var. amargosae, commonly known as the Amargosa penstemon, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
