Potamogeton pectinatus f. pseudomarinus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. pseudomarinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
