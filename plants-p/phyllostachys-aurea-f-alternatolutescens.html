Phyllostachys aurea f. alternatolutescens

Phyllostachys aurea f. alternatolutescens, commonly known as the golden bamboo or yellow bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species Phyllostachys aurea.

This cultivar is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage, which develops a more intense golden hue as the plant matures. It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense, upright stalks, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a vibrant coloration that makes it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
