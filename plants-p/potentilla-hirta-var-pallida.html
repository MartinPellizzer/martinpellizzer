Potentilla hirta var. pallida

Potentilla hirta var. pallida, commonly known as pale hairystem cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features wiry, hairy stems and small, palmately divided leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The flowers are yellow, typically appearing in clusters, and bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

This plant is often found in tundra and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.
