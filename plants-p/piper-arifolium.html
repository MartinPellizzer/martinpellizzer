Piper arifolium

Piper arifolium, commonly known as the Java pepper or black pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that give way to the familiar black peppercorns. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The vine can reach significant lengths, making it suitable for trellises or vertical gardening spaces.
