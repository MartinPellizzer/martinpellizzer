Piper peltatum

Piper peltatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper or peltate pepper, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Its ornamental value and unique foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
