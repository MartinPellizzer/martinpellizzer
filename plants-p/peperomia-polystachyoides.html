Peperomia polystachyoides

Peperomia polystachyoides, commonly known as the coral plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that range in color from green to reddish-purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
