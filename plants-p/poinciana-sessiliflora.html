Poinciana sessiliflora

Poinciana sessiliflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange bract clusters that resemble flames, which appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed pods.

Poinciana sessiliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
