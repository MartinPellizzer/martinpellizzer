Poa pratensis var. subglabriflora

Poa pratensis var. subglabriflora, commonly known as the subglabrous meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and open woodlands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally covered with fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
