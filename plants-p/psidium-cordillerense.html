Psidium cordillerense

Psidium cordillerense, commonly known as the cherimoya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a creamy, aromatic fruit with a unique combination of sweet and tart flavors. Its growth habit includes a short trunk and dense, evergreen foliage, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

The cherimoya thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
