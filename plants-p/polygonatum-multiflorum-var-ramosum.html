Polygonatum multiflorum var. ramosum

Polygonatum multiflorum var. ramosum, commonly known as the fragrant polygonatum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and woodland areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
