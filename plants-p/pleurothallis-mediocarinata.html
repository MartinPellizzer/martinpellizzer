Pleurothallis mediocarinata

Pleurothallis mediocarinata, commonly known as the medium-ribbed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. This orchid thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing epiphytically on trees or rocks.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the orchid family.
