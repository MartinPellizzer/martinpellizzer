Pouteria arguacoensium

Pouteria arguacoensium, commonly known as the Guianan pouteria, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It thrives in humid, lowland forests and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
