Phragmanthera capitata

Phragmanthera capitata, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its attractive, tubular orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Phragmanthera capitata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
