Primula hirsuta subsp. graveolens

Primula hirsuta subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as the stinking primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features hairy, basal leaves that are often reddish in color, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple.

The plant is characterized by its strong, somewhat unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
