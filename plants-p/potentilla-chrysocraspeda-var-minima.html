Potentilla chrysocraspeda var. minima

Potentilla chrysocraspeda var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf golden craspedia, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
