Prunus communis puberipes

Prunus communis puberipes, commonly known as the pubescent cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often pubescent bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow berries that are edible but typically have a tart flavor. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
