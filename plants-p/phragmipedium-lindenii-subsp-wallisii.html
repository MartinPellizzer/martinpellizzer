Phragmipedium lindenii subsp. wallisii

Phragmipedium lindenii subsp.

wallisii, commonly known as the Wallis's phragmipedium, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green.

The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous flowers, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are usually broad and elliptical, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This orchid is prized by horticulturists for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
