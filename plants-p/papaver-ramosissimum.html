Papaver ramosissimum

Papaver ramosissimum, commonly known as the fringed poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery petals that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing showy, clustered blooms.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
