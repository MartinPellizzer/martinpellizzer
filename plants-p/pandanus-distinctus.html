Pandanus distinctus

Pandanus distinctus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits.

Pandanus distinctus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
