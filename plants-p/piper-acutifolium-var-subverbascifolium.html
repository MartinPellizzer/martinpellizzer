Piper acutifolium var. subverbascifolium

Piper acutifolium var. subverbascifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, often used in traditional medicine.

This variant is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical Piper acutifolium.
