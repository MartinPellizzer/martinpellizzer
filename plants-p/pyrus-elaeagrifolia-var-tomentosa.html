Pyrus elaeagrifolia var. tomentosa

Pyrus elaeagrifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features distinctive, rounded, tomentose (fuzzy) leaves that are often grayish-white in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
