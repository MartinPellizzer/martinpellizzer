Phrynium comosum

Phrynium comosum, commonly known as the clustered arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers followed by yellow berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas.

Phrynium comosum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its edible roots, which are used in traditional cuisines.
