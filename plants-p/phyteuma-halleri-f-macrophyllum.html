Phyteuma halleri f. macrophyllum

Phyteuma halleri f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved phyteuma, is a horticultural form of the Phyteuma genus native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is closely related to other Phyteuma species.

This variety is prized for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
