Phacelia eremophila

Phacelia eremophila, commonly known as the desert phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils and withstand periods of drought. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
