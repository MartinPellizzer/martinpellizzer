Plantago atrata var. angustifolia

Plantago atrata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
