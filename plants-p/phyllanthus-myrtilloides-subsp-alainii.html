Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp. alainii

Phyllanthus myrtilloides subsp.

alainii, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to parts of Africa. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often green or yellow when mature. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
