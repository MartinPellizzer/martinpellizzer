Philippia capitata

Philippia capitata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Philippia capitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
