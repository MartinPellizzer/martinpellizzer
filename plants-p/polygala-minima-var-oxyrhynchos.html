Polygala minima var. oxyrhynchos

Polygala minima var. oxyrhynchos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milk-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
