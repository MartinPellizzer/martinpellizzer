Polystachya cooperi

Polystachya cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces elongated inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Polystachya cooperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
