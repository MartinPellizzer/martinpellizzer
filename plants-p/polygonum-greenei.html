Polygonum greenei

Polygonum greenei, commonly known as Greene's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats and knotweeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a reddish or purple tint.

The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and produce tiny, three-lobed seeds.
