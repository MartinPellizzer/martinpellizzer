Peperomia procumbens

Peperomia procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy, deep green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in shades of green or red, which are not particularly showy but are important for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a favored plant for terrariums and tabletop displays.
