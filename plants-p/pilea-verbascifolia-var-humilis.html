Pilea verbascifolia var. humilis

Pilea verbascifolia var. humilis, commonly known as the silver velvet plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of broad, silvery-green, velvety leaves that are softly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive foliage make it a favored ornamental plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
