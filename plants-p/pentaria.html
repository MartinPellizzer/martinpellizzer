Pentaria

Pentaria, commonly known as the five-leaved fig, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It typically features five leaflets arranged symmetrically around a central stem, giving it a striking appearance. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common fig. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge.

While it is not widely cultivated for its fruit, it is appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
