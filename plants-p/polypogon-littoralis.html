Polypogon littoralis

Polypogon littoralis, commonly known as the coastal panicgrass, is a small annual grass native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils along the shoreline, demonstrating a strong adaptability to salt spray and tidal conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its tiny, wind-pollinated flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which contribute to its ability to spread efficiently in coastal environments.

Due to its resilience and ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing dune systems and supporting local wildlife.
