Pelargonium primulinum

Pelargonium primulinum, commonly known as the golden geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Pelargonium primulinum is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
