Paronychia virginica var. parksii

Paronychia virginica var. parksii, commonly known as the pink sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often in coastal or dune environments. It has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
