Pithecellobium oblongum

Pithecellobium oblongum, commonly known as the oblong pod soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is typically elongated, with a rough, spiny exterior and a sweet, fibrous interior.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
