Platycodon glaucus var. subaphyllus

Platycodon glaucus var. subaphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically lighter in color compared to the standard variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique flower shape and long blooming period.
