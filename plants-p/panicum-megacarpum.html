Panicum megacarpum

Panicum megacarpum, commonly known as giant foxtail, is a tall grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, spike-like inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces distinctive, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in various regions. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, often with a hairy texture.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.
