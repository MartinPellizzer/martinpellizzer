Poa multinodis

Poa multinodis, commonly known as the nodding meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Poa multinodis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect life.
