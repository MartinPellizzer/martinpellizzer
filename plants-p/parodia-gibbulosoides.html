Parodia gibbulosoides

Parodia gibbulosoides, commonly known as the Zebra Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in clusters of small, white to pale green tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, lateral branches. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
