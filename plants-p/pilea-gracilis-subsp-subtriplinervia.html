Pilea gracilis subsp. subtriplinervia

Pilea gracilis subsp.

subtriplinervia, commonly known as the silver mitten plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-green mottling on the upper surface.

The plant produces delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. Its foliage is often used in terrariums and as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
