Poa rodwayi

Poa rodwayi, commonly known as the Rodway's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions. Its inflorescence consists of a single, dense spikelet that is usually held upright.

Poa rodwayi plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates in its native environment.
