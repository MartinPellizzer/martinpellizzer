Polyachyrus calderensis

Polyachyrus calderensis, commonly known as the Calder Valley thistle, is a perennial herb native to the UK.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and river valleys. The plant features a robust, spiny stem with large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are purple to pink in color, appearing in late summer, and are clustered in a spherical shape.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is a characteristic species of its habitat.
