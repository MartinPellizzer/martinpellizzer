Potentilla sanguisorba

Potentilla sanguisorba, commonly known as bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, hairy leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in a dense, spherical inflorescence. Its roots are reddish in color and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Potentilla sanguisorba thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.
