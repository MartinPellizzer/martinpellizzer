Pinalia stellata

Pinalia stellata, commonly known as the starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Pinalia stellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
