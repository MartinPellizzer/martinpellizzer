Pittosporum bullatoferrugineum

Pittosporum bullatoferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish-brown with age. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native habitats.
