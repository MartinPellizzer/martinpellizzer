Palaquium glabrum

Palaquium glabrum, commonly known as the smooth palaquium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, leathery fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
