Panicum phyllopogon

Panicum phyllopogon, commonly known as the broad-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tufted inflorescences that resemble a panicle. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it useful in erosion control.

The seeds are small and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
