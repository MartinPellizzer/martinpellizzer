Polygala juncea

Polygala juncea, commonly known as the slender milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually opposite and narrow, while its stems are often branched and hairy.

Polygala juncea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
