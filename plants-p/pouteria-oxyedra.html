Pouteria oxyedra

Pouteria oxyedra, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces woody, spiny capsules that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and protein, and are widely consumed globally.

The plant plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
