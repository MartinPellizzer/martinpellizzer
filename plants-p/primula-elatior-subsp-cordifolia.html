Primula elatior subsp. cordifolia

Primula elatior subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are broadly heart-shaped, giving it its subspecies name, and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine meadows. Its flowering period typically occurs in early spring, making it a notable early bloom in temperate climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
