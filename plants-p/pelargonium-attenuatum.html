Pelargonium attenuatum

Pelargonium attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is suitable for rock gardens and containers.
