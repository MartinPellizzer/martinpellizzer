Prunus cerasus monstr. semiplena

Prunus cerasus monstr.

semiplena, commonly known as the semi-double cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree. It is characterized by its semi-double flowers, which have a layered structure with multiple petals around a central cluster.

The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blossoms.
