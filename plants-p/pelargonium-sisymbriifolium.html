Pelargonium sisymbriifolium

Pelargonium sisymbriifolium, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of the mustard plant, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in regions with warm climates.
