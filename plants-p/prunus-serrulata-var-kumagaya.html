Prunus serrulata var. kumagaya

Prunus serrulata var. kumagaya, commonly known as the Yamazakura, is a deciduous flowering shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically before the leaves emerge. The plant has slender, arching branches with serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The Yamazakura is highly valued for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in Japanese tradition.
