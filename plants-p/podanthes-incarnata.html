Podanthes incarnata

Podanthes incarnata, commonly known as the scarlet ardisia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
