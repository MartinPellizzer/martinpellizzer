Platystele pubescens

Platystele pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leafless orchid, is a rare and unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and striking, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to the cloud forests of Central and South America, this plant thrives in cool, humid environments with high levels of shade. Its root system is specialized for absorbing moisture from the air, a feature adapted to its epiphytic growth habit.

Due to habitat loss, Platystele pubescens is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
