Plowmanianthus perforans

Plowmanianthus perforans, commonly known as the African sandstone plant, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive prolonged dry periods.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.
