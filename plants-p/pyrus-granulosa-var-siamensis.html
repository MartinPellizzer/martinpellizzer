Pyrus granulosa var. siamensis

Pyrus granulosa var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like texture and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces white flowers in spring, followed by small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
