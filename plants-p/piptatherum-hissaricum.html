Piptatherum hissaricum

Piptatherum hissaricum, commonly known as the Hissar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and steppe regions.

Piptatherum hissaricum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
