Psoralea stipulata

Psoralea stipulata, commonly known as the stipulate psoralea, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is adapted to dry, sandy soils. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Psoralea stipulata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
