Piper chrysostachyum

Piper chrysostachyum, commonly known as golden stachyum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
