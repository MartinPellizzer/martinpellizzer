Peperomia barryana

Peperomia barryana, commonly known as the Barry's peperomia, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green, ovate leaves that are often adorned with silver or white veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, rounded berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and ornamental foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both residential and commercial settings.
