Pyrus nutans

Pyrus nutans, commonly known as the drooping pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to eastern Asia, including regions of China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its pendulous, white or yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated margins.

The fruit is a small, edible pear that ripens in late summer, though it is less commonly cultivated than other pear species.
