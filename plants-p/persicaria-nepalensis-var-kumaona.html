Persicaria nepalensis var. kumaona

Persicaria nepalensis var. kumaona, commonly known as Kumaon waterplantain, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of alpine and subalpine habitats.
