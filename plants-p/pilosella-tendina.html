Pilosella tendina

Pilosella tendina, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a pearly appearance. Pilosella tendina is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its foliage is usually covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name.
