Padus virginiana var. xanthocarpa

Padus virginiana var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-flowered hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that appear in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with thorny branches and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall and is an important food source for birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ecological benefits and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and functional roles.
