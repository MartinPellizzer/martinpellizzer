Pholidota aidiolepis

Pholidota aidiolepis, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
