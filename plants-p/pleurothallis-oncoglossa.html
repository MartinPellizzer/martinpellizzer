Pleurothallis oncoglossa

Pleurothallis oncoglossa, commonly known as the pink-lip orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often feature a distinctive pink or red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The orchid's delicate appearance and unique coloration make it a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
