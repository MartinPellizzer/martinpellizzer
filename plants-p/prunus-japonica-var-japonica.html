Prunus japonica var. japonica

Prunus japonica var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Somei Yoshino, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, single-layered pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in March or April. The plant has small, ovate leaves that turn a vibrant yellow in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

The tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and along streets due to its aesthetic appeal.
