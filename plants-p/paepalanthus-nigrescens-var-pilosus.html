Paepalanthus nigrescens var. pilosus

Paepalanthus nigrescens var. pilosus, commonly known as the silky sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and hairy, silvery-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations and limited moisture.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often used in xeriscaping and conservation efforts in its native habitats.
