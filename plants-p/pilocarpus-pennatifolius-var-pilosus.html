Pilocarpus pennatifolius var. pilosus

Pilocarpus pennatifolius var. pilosus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
