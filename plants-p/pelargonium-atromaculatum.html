Pelargonium atromaculatum

Pelargonium atromaculatum, commonly known as the dark-spotted geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture and often bear dark spots, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
