Phaulopsis imbricata

Phaulopsis imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
