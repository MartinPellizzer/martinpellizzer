Plectranthus cylindrostachys

Plectranthus cylindrostachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic foliage, it is popular among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
