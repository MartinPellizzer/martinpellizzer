Parodia slabana

Parodia slabana, commonly known as the Slabana cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a compact, globular cactus with a short, cylindrical stem and a dense covering of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
