Popowia nervifolia

Popowia nervifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf popowia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland areas with rich soil. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters.

Popowia nervifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
