Potentilla elvendensis

Potentilla elvendensis, commonly known as the Elvend cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla elvendensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
