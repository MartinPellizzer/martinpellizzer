Phyllanthus ajmerianus

Phyllanthus ajmerianus, commonly known as Indian cheesewood, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are capsules containing tiny seeds. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in conditions with low rainfall.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
