Potentilla rudolfii

Potentilla rudolfii, commonly known as the Rudolf's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, palmately compound, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Potentilla rudolfii is adapted to cold, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.
