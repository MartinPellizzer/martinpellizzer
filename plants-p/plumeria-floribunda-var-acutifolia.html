Plumeria floribunda var. acutifolia

Plumeria floribunda var. acutifolia, commonly known as the frangipani tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in yellow, orange, or pink hues. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its rapid growth and ability to withstand drought make it a popular choice in landscaping.
