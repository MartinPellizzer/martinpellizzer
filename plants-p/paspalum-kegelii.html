Paspalum kegelii

Paspalum kegelii, commonly known as Kegel's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures.

Paspalum kegelii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
