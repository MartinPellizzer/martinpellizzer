Panicum jauanum

Panicum jauanum, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and loose, open inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a rough texture, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Panicum jauanum is valued for its use in forage and as a soil stabilizer in ecological restoration projects.
