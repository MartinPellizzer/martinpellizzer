Pavonia fryxellii

Pavonia fryxellii, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic, with a fuzzy texture, and it has a woody stem that can become branched over time.

Pavonia fryxellii is adapted to survive in low-water conditions, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
