Pombalia rivalis

Pombalia rivalis, commonly known as the crimson flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and long, slender stems that can grow up to two meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Pombalia rivalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is considered a symbol of resilience in botanical gardens.
