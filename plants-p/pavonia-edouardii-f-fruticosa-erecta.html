Pavonia edouardii f. fruticosa-erecta

Pavonia edouardii f. fruticosa-erecta, commonly known as the erect shrub form of Edouard's pavonia, is a variant of the Pavonia genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, branching structure. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
