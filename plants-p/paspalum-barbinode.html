Paspalum barbinode

Paspalum barbinode, commonly known as the hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Paspalum barbinode is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
