Ptilophora major var. laciniata

Ptilophora major var. laciniata, commonly known as the lace fern, is a striking member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this fern thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
