Phaius tankervilleae var. bernaysii

Phaius tankervilleae var. bernaysii, commonly known as the Bernays' ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pale yellow to cream-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and riverbanks. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to moist, tropical conditions.
