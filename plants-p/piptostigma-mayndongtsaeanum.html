Piptostigma mayndongtsaeanum

Piptostigma mayndongtsaeanum, commonly known as the Mayndong tsaeanum leaf vine, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are legumes that split open to release seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the Fabaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
