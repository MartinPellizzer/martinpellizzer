Philodendron gracile

Philodendron gracile, commonly known as the slender philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and a slightly pointed tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its stems are equipped with aerial roots that help it climb and anchor to supports.

Philodendron gracile is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
