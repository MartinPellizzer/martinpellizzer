Passiflora tetrandra

Passiflora tetrandra, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Passiflora tetrandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
