Phaseolus pilosus var. lasiocarpus

Phaseolus pilosus var. lasiocarpus, commonly known as the hairy bush bean, is a variant of the Phaseolus genus native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green, hairy pods that mature to a yellowish color. It is a climbing or bushy plant, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both ornamental and minor culinary uses.
