Parodia ayopayana

Parodia ayopayana, commonly known as the Ayopayana cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
