Piper heraldii var. amplius

Piper heraldii var. amplius, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of bright red berries. These berries are edible and have a peppery, citrusy flavor.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of spices.
