Plantago pauciflora

Plantago pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple yet effective structure, making it a common sight in many ecosystems.
