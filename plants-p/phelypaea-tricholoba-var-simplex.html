Phelypaea tricholoba var. simplex

Phelypaea tricholoba var. simplex, commonly known as the simple hairleaf fern, is a variant of the hairleaf fern native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its fronds are usually light green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
