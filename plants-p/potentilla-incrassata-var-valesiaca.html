Potentilla incrassata var. valesiaca

Potentilla incrassata var. valesiaca, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine settings.
