Pogostemon pressii

Pogostemon pressii, commonly known as the Japanese balm, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Pogostemon pressii is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
