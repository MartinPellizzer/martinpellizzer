Ptychococcus lepidotus

Ptychococcus lepidotus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to compete with crops, it is considered a weed in many regions.
