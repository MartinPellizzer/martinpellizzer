Pediocactus simpsonii var. paradinei

Pediocactus simpsonii var. paradinei, commonly known as the Simpson's hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States.

It features a globular or cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to pale blue bristles that provide protection from intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its muted foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
