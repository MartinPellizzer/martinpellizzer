Prunus × dawyckensis

Prunus × dawyckensis, commonly known as the Dawyck cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Britain.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, single or double pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blossoms and hardy nature.

Prunus × dawyckensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
