Pycnostachys congensis

Pycnostachys congensis, commonly known as the Congo flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical botanical gardens and as a houseplant in suitable environments.
