Passiflora tonkinensis

Passiflora tonkinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces bright orange or yellow berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can climb using tendrils.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in its native regions.
