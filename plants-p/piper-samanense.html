Piper samanense

Piper samanense, commonly known as the Saman pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, elongated berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the genus Piper. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
