Pyrus hakunensis

Pyrus hakunensis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
