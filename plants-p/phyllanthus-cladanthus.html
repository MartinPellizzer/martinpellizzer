Phyllanthus cladanthus

Phyllanthus cladanthus, commonly known as the spiked gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
