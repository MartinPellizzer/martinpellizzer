Polygonatum erythrocarpum

Polygonatum erythrocarpum, commonly known as red-fruited Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
