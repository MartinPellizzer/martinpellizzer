Polypodium irvingii

Polypodium irvingii, commonly known as the Irving's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizome and fronds that are often covered with a dense layer of hair. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a broad, shield-like shape, and are usually bright green.

Polypodium irvingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
