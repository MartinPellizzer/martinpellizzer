Paeonia rosea

Paeonia rosea, commonly known as the rose peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, with a subtle rose scent. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are dark green and velvety in texture. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and nutrients.

Paeonia rosea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
