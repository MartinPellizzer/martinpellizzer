Polygonum hypoleucum

Polygonum hypoleucum, commonly known as white knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib. White knotweed produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in late summer.

It is often found in moist habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows.
