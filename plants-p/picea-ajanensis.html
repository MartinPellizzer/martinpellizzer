Picea ajanensis

Picea ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajanensis spruce, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the conifer family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
