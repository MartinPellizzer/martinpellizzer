Polytaxis

Polytaxis, commonly known as the "spider plant," is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. After flowering, it develops red berries that contain tiny seeds.

Polytaxis is native to southern Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its ease of care and attractive appearance.
