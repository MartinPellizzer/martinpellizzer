Phyllanthus sacleuxii

Phyllanthus sacleuxii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

The plant is also noted for its latex-like sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine.
