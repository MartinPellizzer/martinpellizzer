Pepinia geyskesii

Pepinia geyskesii, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It produces small, orange-colored fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

Pepinia geyskesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
