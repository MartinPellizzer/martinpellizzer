Pedicularis peduncularis

Pedicularis peduncularis, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often adorned with spurred petals that resemble small horns. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine. Its tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
