Phebalium lepidotum var. obovatum

Phebalium lepidotum var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in cylindrical spikes, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal and temperate regions.
