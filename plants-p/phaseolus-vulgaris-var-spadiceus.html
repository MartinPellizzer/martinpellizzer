Phaseolus vulgaris var. spadiceus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. spadiceus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses.
