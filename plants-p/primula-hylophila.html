Primula hylophila

Primula hylophila, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.
