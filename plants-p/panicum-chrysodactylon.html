Panicum chrysodactylon

Panicum chrysodactylon, commonly known as golden beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellow to golden inflorescences that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
