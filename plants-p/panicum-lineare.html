Panicum lineare

Panicum lineare, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and panicles that produce small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and roadside ditches. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its ornamental value comes from its fine texture and graceful appearance.
