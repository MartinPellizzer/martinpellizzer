Papaver pyrenaicum var. aurantiacum

Papaver pyrenaicum var. aurantiacum, commonly known as the Pyrenean poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pyrenees Mountains.

It features striking, vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with a waxy surface and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its bold, showy blooms.
