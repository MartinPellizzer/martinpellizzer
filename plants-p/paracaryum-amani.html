Paracaryum amani

Paracaryum amani, commonly known as the Amani orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight.

Paracaryum amani is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
