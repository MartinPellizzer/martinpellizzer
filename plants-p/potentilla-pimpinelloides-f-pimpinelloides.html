Potentilla pimpinelloides f. pimpinelloides

Potentilla pimpinelloides f. pimpinelloides, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features a low-growing habit with erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine habitats.
