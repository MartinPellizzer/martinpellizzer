Pseuderanthemum maculatum

Pseuderanthemum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and distinctive spots or markings. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically clustered in dense inflorescences, often in shades of white, pink, or purple.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
