Polystachya tayloriana

Polystachya tayloriana, commonly known as Taylor's spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wet tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, white or pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
