Pyrus ombrophila

Pyrus ombrophila, commonly known as the rain pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated.
