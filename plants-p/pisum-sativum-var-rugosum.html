Pisum sativum var. rugosum

Pisum sativum var. rugosum, commonly known as the hairy vetch, is a perennial legume characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves.

It features prominently hairy stems and pods that contain several elliptical, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is well adapted to temperate climates and is often used as a cover crop for soil improvement. Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it valuable in sustainable agricultural practices.

Additionally, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
