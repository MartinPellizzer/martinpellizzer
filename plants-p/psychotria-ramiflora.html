Psychotria ramiflora

Psychotria ramiflora, commonly known as the "Bushman's poison" or "Cape sour fig," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is handled with care in both traditional and modern applications.
