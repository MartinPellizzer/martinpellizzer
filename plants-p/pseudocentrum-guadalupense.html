Pseudocentrum guadalupense

Pseudocentrum guadalupense, commonly known as the Guadalupe starling tree, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the island of Guadalupe in the Caribbean. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically have a yellowish or greenish color.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.
