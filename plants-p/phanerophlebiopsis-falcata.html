Phanerophlebiopsis falcata

Phanerophlebiopsis falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its fronds are often recurved, with a distinctive falcate (sickle-shaped) appearance.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters, typical of its reproductive strategy.
