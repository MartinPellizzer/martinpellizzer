Pulsatilla sukaczewii

Pulsatilla sukaczewii, commonly known as the Sukaczew's pasque flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. The plant has a short, upright stem with few leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. Its flowers consist of numerous stamens surrounding a single pistil, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Pulsatilla sukaczewii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high altitudes.
