Pochota nitida

Pochota nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf pochota, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is notable for its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Pochota nitida is also valued for its edible pods, which contain seeds used in traditional Andean cuisine.
