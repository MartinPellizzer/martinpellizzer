Primula elongata var. barnardoana

Primula elongata var. barnardoana, commonly known as the Barnardo's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
