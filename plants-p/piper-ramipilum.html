Piper ramipilum

Piper ramipilum, commonly known as the golden piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Piper ramipilum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
