Prunus tiliifolia

Prunus tiliifolia, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, round fruits resembling cherries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine.

Prunus tiliifolia prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
