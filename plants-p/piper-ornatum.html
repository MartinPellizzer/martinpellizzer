Piper ornatum

Piper ornatum, commonly known as the sacred pepper or holy pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by tiny, round berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various indigenous traditions.
