Piper melastomoides

Piper melastomoides, commonly known as the yellow pipevine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
