Passiflora vitiensis

Passiflora vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora vitiensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
