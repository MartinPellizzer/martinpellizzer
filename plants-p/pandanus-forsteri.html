Pandanus forsteri

Pandanus forsteri, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the stems. Pandanus forsteri is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitats.
