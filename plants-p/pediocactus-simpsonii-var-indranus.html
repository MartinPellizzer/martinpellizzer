Pediocactus simpsonii var. indranus

Pediocactus simpsonii var. indranus, commonly known as Simpson's cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in clusters of small, white or pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
