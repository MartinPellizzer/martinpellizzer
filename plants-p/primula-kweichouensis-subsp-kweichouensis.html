Primula kweichouensis subsp. kweichouensis

Primula kweichouensis subsp.

kweichouensis, commonly known as the Kweichou primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to China. It features rosettes of basal leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
