Pastinaca macrocarpa

Pastinaca macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant bur marigold, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny seed pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
