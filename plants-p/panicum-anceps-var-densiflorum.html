Panicum anceps var. densiflorum

Panicum anceps var. densiflorum, commonly known as denseflower panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous small, fluffy flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, fields, and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities.
