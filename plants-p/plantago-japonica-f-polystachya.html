Plantago japonica f. polystachya

Plantago japonica f. polystachya, commonly known as the Japanese plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
