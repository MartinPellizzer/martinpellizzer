Pitcairnia simulans var. simulans

Pitcairnia simulans var. simulans, commonly known as the velvet leaf pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that forms a rosette shape, giving it a lush, ornamental appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, often in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among bromeliad enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
