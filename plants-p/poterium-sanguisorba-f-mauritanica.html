Poterium sanguisorba f. mauritanica

Poterium sanguisorba f. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania bistoury, is a variant of the species native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported astringent properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
