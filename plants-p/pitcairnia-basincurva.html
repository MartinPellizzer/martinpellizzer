Pitcairnia basincurva

Pitcairnia basincurva, commonly known as the basin-curved pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater, a common adaptation among bromeliads. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that can range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are thick and waxy, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

Pitcairnia basincurva is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
