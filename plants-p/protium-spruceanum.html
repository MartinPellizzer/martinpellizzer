Protium spruceanum

Protium spruceanum, commonly known as the Burmese sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its scent and durability. The plant has simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically yellowish-green and are followed by spherical, woody fruits.

The tree is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates.
