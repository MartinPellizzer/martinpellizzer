Pachygenium tenuius

Pachygenium tenuius, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant has thin, branching stems and tiny leaves that are often submerged in water. It thrives in still or slow-moving water and is known for its unique method of nutrient acquisition through predation.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and ecological interactions.
