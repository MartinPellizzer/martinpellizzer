Paspalum dolichophyllum

Paspalum dolichophyllum, commonly known as the long-leaved paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, sandy soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable species in wetland conservation efforts.
