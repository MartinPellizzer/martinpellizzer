Phaseolus rimbachii

Phaseolus rimbachii, commonly known as the wild kidney bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

This species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in agricultural research.
