Palaquium maingayi

Palaquium maingayi, commonly known as the Malayan palaquium, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are often consumed by local communities. The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential economic importance due to its fruit.
