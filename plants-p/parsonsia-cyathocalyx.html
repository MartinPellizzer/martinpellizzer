Parsonsia cyathocalyx

Parsonsia cyathocalyx, commonly known as the flame tree or golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Parsonsia cyathocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
