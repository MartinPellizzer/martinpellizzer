Pteryxia albiflora

Pteryxia albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered pterygia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in summer. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Pteryxia albiflora is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
