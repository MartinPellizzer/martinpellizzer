Piper gerritii

Piper gerritii, commonly known as the Moluccan pepper or Banda pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Banda Islands in Indonesia and is characterized by its small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

Piper gerritii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been historically significant in the spice trade.
