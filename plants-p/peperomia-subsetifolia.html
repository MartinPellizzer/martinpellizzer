Peperomia subsetifolia

Peperomia subsetifolia, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves that are typically pale green with a silvery or white underside, giving it its distinguishing appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative houseplant.
