Paronychia aksoyii

Paronychia aksoyii, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in spring and early summer. It is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

Paronychia aksoyii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
