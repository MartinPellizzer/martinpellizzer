Paspalum longiligulatum

Paspalum longiligulatum, commonly known as the tall false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum longiligulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
