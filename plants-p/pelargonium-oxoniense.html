Pelargonium oxoniense

Pelargonium oxoniense, commonly known as the Oxford geranium, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate.
