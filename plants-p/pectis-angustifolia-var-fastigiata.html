Pectis angustifolia var. fastigiata

Pectis angustifolia var. fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
