Premna holstii var. glandulifera

Premna holstii var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular premna, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent and have small glands along the veins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
