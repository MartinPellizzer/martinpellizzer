Plantago asiatica f. polystachya

Plantago asiatica f. polystachya, commonly known as the Chinese plantain or narrow-leaved plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb native to Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the standard species, and it produces erect spikes of small, greenish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
