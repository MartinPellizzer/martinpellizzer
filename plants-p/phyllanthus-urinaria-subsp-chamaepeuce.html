Phyllanthus urinaria subsp. chamaepeuce

Phyllanthus urinaria subsp.

chamaepeuce, commonly known as the bladder nut, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features small, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent and have a waxy texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
