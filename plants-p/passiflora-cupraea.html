Passiflora cupraea

Passiflora cupraea, commonly known as the small passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled white flowers. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora cupraea is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral structure and historical significance in traditional medicine.
