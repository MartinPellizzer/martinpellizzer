Pulsatilla × zichyi

Pulsatilla × zichyi, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Pulsatilla patens and Pulsatilla vulgaris, resulting in a plant with distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a central cluster of stamens and a showy, fuzzy center.

The leaves are palmately divided, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
