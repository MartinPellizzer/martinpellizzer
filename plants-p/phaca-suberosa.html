Phaca suberosa

Phaca suberosa, commonly known as the woody phaca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Phaca suberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
