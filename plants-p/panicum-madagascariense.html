Panicum madagascariense

Panicum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
