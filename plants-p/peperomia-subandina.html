Peperomia subandina

Peperomia subandina, commonly known as the Andes peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink. This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its tolerance for dry air. It prefers well-draining soil and moderate watering, making it a low-maintenance choice for plant enthusiasts.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
