Pilea umbriana

Pilea umbriana, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or the silver dollar plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Pilea umbriana is relatively low-maintenance and makes a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
