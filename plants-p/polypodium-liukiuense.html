Polypodium liukiuense

Polypodium liukiuense, commonly known as the Ryukyu huperzia, is a species of fern native to the Ryukyu Islands in Japan.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often segmented fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, which helps it retain moisture.

Polypodium liukiuense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
