Persea fastigiata

Persea fastigiata, commonly known as the Mexican mountain pepper or Mexican evergreen, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a subtle aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and clustered, followed by edible, dark blue to black berries.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
