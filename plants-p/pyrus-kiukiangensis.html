Pyrus kiukiangensis

Pyrus kiukiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its white flowers and edible fruit, which resembles a pear in appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

The tree is also notable for its role in the genetic diversity of cultivated pears.
