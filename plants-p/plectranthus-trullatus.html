Plectranthus trullatus

Plectranthus trullatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are typically velvety and heart-shaped, often with a striking color contrast.

Plectranthus trullatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
