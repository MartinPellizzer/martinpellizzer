Poa flexuosa var. occidentalis

Poa flexuosa var. occidentalis, commonly known as western bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, tufted leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
