Pyrus thomsonii

Pyrus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
