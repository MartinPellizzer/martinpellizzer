Plectranthus caillei

Plectranthus caillei, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Plectranthus caillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
