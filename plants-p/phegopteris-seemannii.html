Phegopteris seemannii

Phegopteris seemannii, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a member of the fern family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a lace-like pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its rhizomes are often submerged in water, allowing it to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in humid environments.
