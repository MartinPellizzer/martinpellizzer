Polygala aculeata

Polygala aculeata, commonly known as the prickly mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia, typically found growing on the branches of trees and shrubs. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces yellowish or greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host plants if left unchecked.
