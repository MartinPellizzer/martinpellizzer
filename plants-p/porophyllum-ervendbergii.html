Porophyllum ervendbergii

Porophyllum ervendbergii, commonly known as the Mexican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its yellow to orange flower heads consist of many small florets arranged in a disc-like structure.

Porophyllum ervendbergii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
