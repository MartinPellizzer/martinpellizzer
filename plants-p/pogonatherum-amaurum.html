Pogonatherum amaurum

Pogonatherum amaurum, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive golden-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often forming dense stands in maritime environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a tufted base that gives it a unique appearance.

Pogonatherum amaurum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
