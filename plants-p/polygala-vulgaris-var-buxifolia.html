Polygala vulgaris var. buxifolia

Polygala vulgaris var. buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and relatively uncommon species within the Polygala genus.
