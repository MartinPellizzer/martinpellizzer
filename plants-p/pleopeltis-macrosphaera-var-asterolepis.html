Pleopeltis macrosphaera var. asterolepis

Pleopeltis macrosphaera var. asterolepis, commonly known as the star-veined rock fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped veins on the underside of its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually leathery and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

It is a hardy epiphytic or lithophytic fern that thrives in humid, shaded environments.
