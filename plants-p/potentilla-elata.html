Potentilla elata

Potentilla elata, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy yellow flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Potentilla elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
