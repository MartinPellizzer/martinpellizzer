Panicum magellanicum

Panicum magellanicum, commonly known as Magellan grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically in shades of brown or beige. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
