Panicum snowdenii

Panicum snowdenii, commonly known as Snowden's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. Its flowering heads consist of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Panicum snowdenii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insect and bird species.
