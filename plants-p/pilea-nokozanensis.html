Pilea nokozanensis

Pilea nokozanensis, commonly known as the Japanese prayer plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display variegation in shades of green, white, and pink. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Pilea nokozanensis is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
