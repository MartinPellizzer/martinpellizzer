Palmorchis caxiuanensis

Palmorchis caxiuanensis, commonly known as the Caxiuanense Orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
