Piper oblongatifolium

Piper oblongatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that are often densely covered with small, glandular hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces small, peppery fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
