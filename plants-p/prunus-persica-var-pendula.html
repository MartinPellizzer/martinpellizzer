Prunus persica var. pendula

Prunus persica var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully from the main trunk.

It produces showy pink or white flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits. The plant is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall.
