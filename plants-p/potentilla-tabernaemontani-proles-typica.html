Potentilla tabernaemontani proles typica

Potentilla tabernaemontani proles typica, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in rock gardens or alpine arrangements.
