Papaver nudicaule var. commune

Papaver nudicaule var. commune, commonly known as the common poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, papery flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a short, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its capsule fruit contains numerous small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
