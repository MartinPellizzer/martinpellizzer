Pterocephalus plumosus

Pterocephalus plumosus, commonly known as the silky bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, white to pale blue flowers that resemble small clouds. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Pterocephalus plumosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
