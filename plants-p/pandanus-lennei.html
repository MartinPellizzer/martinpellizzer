Pandanus lennei

Pandanus lennei, commonly known as the Lennie's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, clustered flowers that develop into fruit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in various regions.
