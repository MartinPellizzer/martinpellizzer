Potentilla supina var. villosiuscula

Potentilla supina var. villosiuscula, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, often reddish stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
