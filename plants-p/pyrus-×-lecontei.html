Pyrus × lecontei

Pyrus × lecontei, commonly known as the Leconte's pear, is a hybrid species within the genus Pyrus, native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. Its flowers are clustered in loose inflorescences, featuring white petals and yellow stamens, and it produces small, edible fruits that are similar to pears in taste.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
