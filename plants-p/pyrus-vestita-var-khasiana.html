Pyrus vestita var. khasiana

Pyrus vestita var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to the northeastern region of India.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits in the summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
