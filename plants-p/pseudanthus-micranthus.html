Pseudanthus micranthus

Pseudanthus micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered pseudanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
