Pleurothallis bicallosa

Pleurothallis bicallosa, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its inflorescence is often adorned with hairy structures, giving it its common name.

Pleurothallis bicallosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
