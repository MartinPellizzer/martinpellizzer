Polygala gypsophiloides

Polygala gypsophiloides, commonly known as the rock polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often resembling small, intricate lace. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
