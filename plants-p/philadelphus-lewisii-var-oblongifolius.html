Philadelphus lewisii var. oblongifolius

Philadelphus lewisii var. oblongifolius, commonly known as Lewis's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, fragrant, white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically oblong and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Philadelphus genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
