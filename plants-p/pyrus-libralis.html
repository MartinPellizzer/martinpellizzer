Pyrus libralis

Pyrus libralis, commonly known as the Italian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

Pyrus libralis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
