Petrophile ericifolia f. subpubescens

Petrophile ericifolia f. subpubescens, commonly known as the silky heath, is a compact shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, dense foliage with a silvery-gray appearance due to a fine layer of pubescence. The plant produces small, pinkish-red flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in native gardens.
