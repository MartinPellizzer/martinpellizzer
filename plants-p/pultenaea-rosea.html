Pultenaea rosea

Pultenaea rosea, commonly known as the pink wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with slender, often hairy stems. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the flowers are usually pink or red, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Pultenaea rosea is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
