Passiflora macropoda

Passiflora macropoda, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. Its leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Passiflora macropoda is also noted for its role in supporting local biodiversity, providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
