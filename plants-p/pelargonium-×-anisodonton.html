Pelargonium × anisodonton

Pelargonium × anisodonton, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, slightly serrated leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its vigorous growth and ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium × anisodonton prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
