Panicum owenae

Panicum owenae, commonly known as the Owen's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
