Phaseolus maculatus

Phaseolus maculatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or yellow flowers and long, slender pods that contain edible beans. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with a velvety texture.

The seeds are rich in protein and are widely cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
