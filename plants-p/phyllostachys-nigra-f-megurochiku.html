Phyllostachys nigra f. megurochiku

Phyllostachys nigra f. megurochiku, commonly known as the Black Bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, culms that contrast beautifully with its bright green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 6 meters, making it a prominent feature in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, preferring moist conditions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both traditional and modern horticultural settings.
