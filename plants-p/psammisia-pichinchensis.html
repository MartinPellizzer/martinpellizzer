Psammisia pichinchensis

Psammisia pichinchensis, commonly known as the Andean sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
