Passiflora pala

Passiflora pala, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora pala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
