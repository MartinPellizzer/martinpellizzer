Pilosella setigera

Pilosella setigera, commonly known as the slender leaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella setigera produces small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
