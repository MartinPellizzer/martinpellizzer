Philibertia gilliesii var. pubescens

Philibertia gilliesii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy philibertia, is a perennial herb native to South Australia.

It features basal leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This variety is notable for its distinctive pubescence and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
