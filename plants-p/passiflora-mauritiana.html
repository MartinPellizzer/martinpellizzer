Passiflora mauritiana

Passiflora mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, yellow, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
