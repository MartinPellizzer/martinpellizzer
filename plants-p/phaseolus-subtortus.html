Phaseolus subtortus

Phaseolus subtortus, commonly known as the creeping pole bean, is a herbaceous vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds. Its climbing habit and rapid growth make it suitable for use in trellises or as a ground cover.

The botanical characteristics of Phaseolus subtortus make it an interesting subject for both agricultural and ecological studies.
