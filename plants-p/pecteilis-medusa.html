Pecteilis medusa

Pecteilis medusa, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has bract-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
