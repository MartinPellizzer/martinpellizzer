Pelargonium fucatum

Pelargonium fucatum, commonly known as the chocolate geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

Pelargonium fucatum is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
