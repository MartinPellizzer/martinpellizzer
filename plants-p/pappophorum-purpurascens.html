Pappophorum purpurascens

Pappophorum purpurascens, commonly known as the purple spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, purple-tinted flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces lightweight, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its reproductive success.

Pappophorum purpurascens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
