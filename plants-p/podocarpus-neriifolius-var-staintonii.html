Podocarpus neriifolius var. staintonii

Podocarpus neriifolius var. staintonii, commonly known as the Stainton's satinwood, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy foliage and small, clustered cones. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a dense canopy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its durable wood.
