Paeonia edulis

Paeonia edulis, commonly known as the edible peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces thick, fleshy roots that are the primary edible part, often used in traditional Chinese medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, loamy soil.

Paeonia edulis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its medicinal and culinary uses.
