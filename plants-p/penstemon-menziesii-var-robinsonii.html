Penstemon menziesii var. robinsonii

Penstemon menziesii var. robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
