Pachylobus tessmannii

Pachylobus tessmannii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its rapid growth makes it a useful plant for soil stabilization and agroforestry practices.
