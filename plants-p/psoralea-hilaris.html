Psoralea hilaris

Psoralea hilaris, commonly known as the joyful psoralea, is a small annual herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, commonly known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native environment.
