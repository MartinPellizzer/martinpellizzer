Paepalanthus speleicola

Paepalanthus speleicola, commonly known as the cave cactus, is a unique plant species native to the limestone caves of Brazil.

It is a small, epiphytic cactus that thrives in dark, humid environments with limited light. Its stems are typically short and branched, with small, fleshy leaves that help store water. The plant produces white or pale flowers that bloom in the absence of direct sunlight.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
