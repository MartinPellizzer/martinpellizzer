Persoonia mollis subsp. budawangensis

Persoonia mollis subsp.

budawangensis, commonly known as the Budawang bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in dry, sandy soils. It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height and is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

The species is named after the Budawang Range in New South Wales, where it is endemic.
