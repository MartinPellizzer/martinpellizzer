Pinalia floribunda

Pinalia floribunda, commonly known as the showy pinalia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that produce vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
