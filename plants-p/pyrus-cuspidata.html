Pyrus cuspidata

Pyrus cuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits that turn yellow or red when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
