Polygala andreana

Polygala andreana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
