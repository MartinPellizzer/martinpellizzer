Philodendron scitulum

Philodendron scitulum, commonly known as the shield-leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that often have a velvety texture and a deep green color. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are often marked with distinctive light green veins and a waxy coating.

Philodendron scitulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
