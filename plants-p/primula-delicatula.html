Primula delicatula

Primula delicatula, commonly known as the delicate primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often tinged with silver. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural habitat.
