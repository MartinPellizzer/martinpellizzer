Primula vulgaris var. caulescens

Primula vulgaris var. caulescens, commonly known as the clustered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the British Isles.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, white, and pink, blooming from late winter to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
