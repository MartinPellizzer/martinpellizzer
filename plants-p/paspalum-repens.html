Paspalum repens

Paspalum repens, commonly known as mat grass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in wetlands and along waterways.

Paspalum repens is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is used in ecological restoration projects.
