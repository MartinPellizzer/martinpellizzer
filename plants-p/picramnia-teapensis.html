Picramnia teapensis

Picramnia teapensis, commonly known as the Teapensia or Chinese picramnia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, dry, and contain several seeds, typical of the Rutaceae family.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
