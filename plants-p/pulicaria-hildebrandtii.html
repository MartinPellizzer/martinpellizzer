Pulicaria hildebrandtii

Pulicaria hildebrandtii, commonly known as Hildebrandt's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Pulicaria hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract beneficial insects.
