Pityrodia scabra

Pityrodia scabra, commonly known as the rough pittosporum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. These flowers are followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Pityrodia scabra is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
