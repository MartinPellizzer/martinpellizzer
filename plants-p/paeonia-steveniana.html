Paeonia steveniana

Paeonia steveniana, commonly known as the Steven's peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmately divided leaves and sturdy, upright stems.

Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
