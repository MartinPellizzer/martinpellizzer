Plantago truncata subsp. firma

Plantago truncata subsp.

firma, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are borne on tall, slender stems. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This species is also valued for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used in traditional remedies for skin irritations and digestive issues.
