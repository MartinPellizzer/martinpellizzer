Piper aromaticaule

Piper aromaticaule, commonly known as the aromatic pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its fragrant leaves and small, berry-like fruits. The plant produces a pungent aroma due to the presence of essential oils, which are concentrated in its leaves and stems. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The berries are typically harvested when fully mature and can be used in traditional remedies and culinary applications.
