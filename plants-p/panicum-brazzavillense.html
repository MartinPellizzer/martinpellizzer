Panicum brazzavillense

Panicum brazzavillense, commonly known as the Congo grass, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
