Picea engelmannii f. fendleri

Picea engelmannii f. fendleri, commonly known as the Arizona white fir, is a subspecies of the Engelmann spruce.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The tree is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive cone structure. It has a slow growth rate and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
