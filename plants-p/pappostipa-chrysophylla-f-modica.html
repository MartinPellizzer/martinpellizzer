Pappostipa chrysophylla f. modica

Pappostipa chrysophylla f. modica, commonly known as the Modica form of the golden feather grass, is a horticultural variant of a grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, golden-green foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescence that adds a soft, ornamental quality to gardens. This form is typically shorter and more compact than the species, making it suitable for borders or as a ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
