Prunus armeniaca var. sibirica

Prunus armeniaca var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are succeeded by edible, yellow or orange fruits. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and thornless stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit production.

The Siberian apricot is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses.
