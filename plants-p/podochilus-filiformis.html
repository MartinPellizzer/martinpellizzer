Podochilus filiformis

Podochilus filiformis, commonly known as the slender podochilus, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
