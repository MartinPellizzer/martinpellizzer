Polypodium capillare

Polypodium capillare, commonly known as the slender huperzia, is a fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is creeping and produces both sterile and fertile fronds.

Polypodium capillare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
