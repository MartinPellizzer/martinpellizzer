Piper nigriconnectivum

Piper nigriconnectivum, commonly known as black pepper, is a flowering vine native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark berries that are harvested when fully ripe. The fruit contains a fine, aromatic powder called pepper, which is widely used as a spice and medicinal herb. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the peppercorns.
