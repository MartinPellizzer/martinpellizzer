Polygonatum tonkinense

Polygonatum tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its berries, which are dark blue to black, are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Polygonatum tonkinense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
