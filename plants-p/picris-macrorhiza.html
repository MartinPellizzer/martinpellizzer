Picris macrorhiza

Picris macrorhiza, commonly known as greater fumitory, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.
