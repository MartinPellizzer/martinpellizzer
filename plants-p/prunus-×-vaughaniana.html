Prunus × vaughaniana

Prunus × vaughaniana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yamazakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal beauty. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.

Prunus × vaughaniana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
