Pterocephalus putkianus

Pterocephalus putkianus, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments due to its deep root system and drought-resistant adaptations.

It also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
