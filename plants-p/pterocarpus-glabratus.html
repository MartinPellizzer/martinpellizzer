Pterocarpus glabratus

Pterocarpus glabratus, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain hard, dark seeds. Its roots are known for their high content of fatty acids, making it valuable for traditional uses and commercial applications.

The plant also plays an important role in soil stabilization and local ecosystems.
