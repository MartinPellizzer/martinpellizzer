Peperomia johnsonii

Peperomia johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
