Pyrus arbutifolia var. nigra

Pyrus arbutifolia var. nigra, commonly known as black-berried whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, black berries that persist into winter. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bark is thick and fissured, often appearing grayish-brown with a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
