Podocarpus insignis

Podocarpus insignis, commonly known as the Christmas tree or the sacred tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, conical shape and aromatic foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

Podocarpus insignis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and cultural significance.
