Patagonium trijugum

Patagonium trijugum, commonly known as the Patagonian three-lobed plant, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, and its small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its botanical features make it well adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Patagonium trijugum is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential for medicinal use.
