Pilosella strigosa

Pilosella strigosa, commonly known as the hairy hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are hairy and linear, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella strigosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
