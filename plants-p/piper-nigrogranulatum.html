Piper nigrogranulatum

Piper nigrogranulatum, commonly known as black granular pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dark, granular berries that are used as a spice and medicinal herb. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
