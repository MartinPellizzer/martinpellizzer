Papaver glaucioides

Papaver glaucioides, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its hardiness in cooler climates.

Papaver glaucioides is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
