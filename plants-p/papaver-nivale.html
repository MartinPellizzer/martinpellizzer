Papaver nivale

Papaver nivale, commonly known as the snow poppy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink petals that resemble miniature poppies, and it blooms in late winter or early spring. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude meadows.

Papaver nivale is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in alpine ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
