Penstemon crandallii subsp. taosensis

Penstemon crandallii subsp.

taosensis, commonly known as Taos penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
