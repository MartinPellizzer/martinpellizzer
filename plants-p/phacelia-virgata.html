Phacelia virgata

Phacelia virgata, commonly known as blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a fuzzy texture.

Phacelia virgata is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
