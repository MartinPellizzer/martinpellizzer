Phragmites communis subsp. maritimus

Phragmites communis subsp.

maritimus, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions. It thrives in brackish and saline wetlands, often forming dense stands along shorelines.

The plant has hollow, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and tidal flooding. Its rhizomes spread extensively, allowing it to colonize large areas and stabilize soil.

It plays a crucial role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for various wildlife species.
