Paramansoa

Paramansoa, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features large, broad leaves that can grow up to three meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still or slow-moving water. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Paramansoa plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
