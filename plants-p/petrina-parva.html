Petrina parva

Petrina parva, commonly known as the small petrina, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Petrina parva is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
