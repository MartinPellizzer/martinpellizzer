Paspalum glaucifolium

Paspalum glaucifolium, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum glaucifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
