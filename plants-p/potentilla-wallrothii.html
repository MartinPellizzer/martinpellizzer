Potentilla wallrothii

Potentilla wallrothii, commonly known as the wallroth's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually compound, with several leaflets arranged alternately along the stems.

Potentilla wallrothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
