Panax ginseng f. dichrocarpus

Panax ginseng f. dichrocarpus, commonly known as the red ginseng variant, is a botanical form of the Asian ginseng plant.

It is characterized by its distinct red coloration of the root, which develops due to a unique drying and curing process. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Korea, where it thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its roots contain various bioactive compounds, including ginsenosides, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

This form is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, such as enhancing energy and cognitive function.
