Plantago radicata var. acanthophylla

Plantago radicata var. acanthophylla, commonly known as the acanthophyllous plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and a prominent central vein. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
