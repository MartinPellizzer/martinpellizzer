Phoebe grandifolia

Phoebe grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale green and appear in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color, attracting various bird species.
