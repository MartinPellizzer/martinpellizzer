Parathesis pyramidalis

Parathesis pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, which are followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits containing seeds. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis.

The plant is often found in humid environments and can be an indicator of certain ecological conditions.
