Piper indiciflexum

Piper indiciflexum, commonly known as the Indian flexed pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters, while the fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
