Piper phaneropus

Piper phaneropus, commonly known as the giant sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
