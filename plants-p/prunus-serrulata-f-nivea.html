Prunus serrulata f. nivea

Prunus serrulata f. nivea, commonly known as the white cherry or Ume, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It has a compact, rounded growth habit and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for spring floral displays and traditional landscaping.
