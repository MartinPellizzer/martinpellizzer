Pectis amplifolia

Pectis amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which attract various pollinators.

Pectis amplifolia is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
