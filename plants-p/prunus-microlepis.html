Prunus microlepis

Prunus microlepis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by small, sweet-tart purple or black berries. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Prunus microlepis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
