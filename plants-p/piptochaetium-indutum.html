Piptochaetium indutum

Piptochaetium indutum, commonly known as the woolly grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Piptochaetium indutum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
