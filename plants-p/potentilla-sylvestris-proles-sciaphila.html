Potentilla sylvestris proles sciaphila

Potentilla sylvestris proles sciaphila, commonly known as the shade-loving cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to shaded forest environments.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens with shady conditions.
