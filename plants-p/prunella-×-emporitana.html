Prunella × emporitana

Prunella × emporitana, commonly known as the common selfheal, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Prunella × emporitana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
