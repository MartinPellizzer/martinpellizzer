Pleioluma dioica

Pleioluma dioica, commonly known as the two-color orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, contrasting flower colors, typically featuring a white or pale pink lip and a deep purple or red dorsal sepal. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom during the rainy season.

Pleioluma dioica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique visual display.
