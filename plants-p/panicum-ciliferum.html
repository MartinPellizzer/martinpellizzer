Panicum ciliferum

Panicum ciliferum, commonly known as little barley, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Panicum ciliferum is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within a single growing season.
