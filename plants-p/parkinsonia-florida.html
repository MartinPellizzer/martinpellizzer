Parkinsonia florida

Parkinsonia florida, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous legume native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental tree in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial.
