Piper brachypodon var. radicescens

Piper brachypodon var. radicescens, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into dark purple berries. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, often spreading along the ground or climbing supports. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine.

The varietal name "radicescens" refers to its root-like stems that help it anchor and spread effectively.
