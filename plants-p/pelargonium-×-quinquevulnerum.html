Pelargonium × quinquevulnerum

Pelargonium × quinquevulnerum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and clusters of five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Native to southern Africa, this plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
