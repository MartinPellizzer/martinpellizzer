Polypodium pertusum

Polypodium pertusum, commonly known as the curly-fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, coiled fronds that unfurl as they mature. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Polypodium pertusum make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
