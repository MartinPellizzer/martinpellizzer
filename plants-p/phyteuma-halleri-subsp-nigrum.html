Phyteuma halleri subsp. nigrum

Phyteuma halleri subsp.

nigrum, commonly known as black nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
