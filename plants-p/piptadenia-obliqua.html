Piptadenia obliqua

Piptadenia obliqua, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with pairs of oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is well adapted to warm, humid climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
