Pilea howelliana var. howelliana

Pilea howelliana var. howelliana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are round and slightly wavy, often with a silvery-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by pale green berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity as a decorative plant.
