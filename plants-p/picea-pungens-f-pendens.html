Picea pungens f. pendens

Picea pungens f. pendens, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce weeping form, is a cultivar of the blue spruce tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive weeping growth habit, with long, pendulous branches that create a cascading effect. The foliage consists of dense, needle-like leaves that are a striking blue-green color, providing ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for colder climates.

This cultivar is often used as a specimen tree due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
