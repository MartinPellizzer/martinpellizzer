Phylica imbricata

Phylica imbricata, commonly known as the silver crown, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Phylica imbricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes.
