Phyllanthus camerunensis

Phyllanthus camerunensis, commonly known as African buttonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Phyllanthus camerunensis is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
