Potentilla clandestina

Potentilla clandestina, commonly known as the hidden potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Potentilla clandestina is known for its resilience and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
