Pultenaea obcordata

Pultenaea obcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bush pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Pultenaea obcordata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
