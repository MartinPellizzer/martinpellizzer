Panicum crassipes

Panicum crassipes, commonly known as broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has wide, flat leaves that are typically green and can grow up to several feet in height. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Panicum crassipes plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
