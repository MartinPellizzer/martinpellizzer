Pleurothallis caespitosa var. chrysantha

Pleurothallis caespitosa var. chrysantha, commonly known as the golden beard orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The inflorescence typically consists of numerous pale yellow to orange-red blooms, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant prefers high humidity and indirect light, thriving in shaded, moist environments.

Its intricate floral structure and vibrant coloration make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
