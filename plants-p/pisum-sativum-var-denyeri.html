Pisum sativum var. denyeri

Pisum sativum var. denyeri, commonly known as the Denyer's pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded pods compared to the standard pea variety. The plant typically produces a profusion of bright yellow flowers, which are followed by the development of its distinctive pods. It is native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean and thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and minor agricultural purposes.
