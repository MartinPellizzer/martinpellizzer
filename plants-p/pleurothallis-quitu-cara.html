Pleurothallis quitu-cara

Pleurothallis quitu-cara, commonly known as the "Cara Orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white and pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
