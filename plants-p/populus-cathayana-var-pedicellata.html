Populus cathayana var. pedicellata

Populus cathayana var. pedicellata, commonly known as the Chinese poplar or pedicellate poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often drooping catkins, which are a key feature of its botanical classification. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
