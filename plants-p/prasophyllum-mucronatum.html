Prasophyllum mucronatum

Prasophyllum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, slender flower spike that bears a single, showy white or pale pink flower. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in heathlands and open forests. It has a short growing season and relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
