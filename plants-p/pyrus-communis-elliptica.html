Pyrus communis elliptica

Pyrus communis elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically small, sweet, and has a firm texture, making it suitable for eating fresh or cooking. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climates.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
