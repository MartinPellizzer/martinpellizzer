Placus sikkimensis

Placus sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim lily, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces edible bulbs that have been used in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
