Prestonia seemannii

Prestonia seemannii, commonly known as the Trinidad nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a perennial shrub that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.
