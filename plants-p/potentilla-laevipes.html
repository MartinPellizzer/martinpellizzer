Potentilla laevipes

Potentilla laevipes, commonly known as the smooth cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are smooth and oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Potentilla laevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
