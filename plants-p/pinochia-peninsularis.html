Pinochia peninsularis

Pinochia peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsula pine.

It is a rare evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California. The plant features needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, adapted to the arid climate. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind.

The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.
