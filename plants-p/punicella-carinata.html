Punicella carinata

Punicella carinata, commonly known as the mountain holly or holly fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by clusters of small, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Punicella carinata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
