Passiflora araguensis

Passiflora araguensis, commonly known as the Jamaican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and botanical research.
