Phlomis caribaea

Phlomis caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and disturbed habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is notable for its medicinal properties historically used by indigenous communities.
