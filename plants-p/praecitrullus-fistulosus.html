Praecitrullus fistulosus

Praecitrullus fistulosus, commonly known as the desert bottle gourd, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical, bottle-shaped fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow and bloom in the morning, followed by the development of the fruit.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy fruit.
