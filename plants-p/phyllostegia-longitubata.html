Phyllostegia longitubata

Phyllostegia longitubata, commonly known as the long-tubed phyllostegia, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves and its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
