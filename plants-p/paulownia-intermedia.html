Paulownia intermedia

Paulownia intermedia, commonly known as the common paulownia, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has a fast growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its wood is lightweight and strong, making it valuable for woodworking.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
