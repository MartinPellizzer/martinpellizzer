Picea canadensis var. pendula

Picea canadensis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that give it a cascading appearance. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces cone-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This ornamental tree is valued for its unique form and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
