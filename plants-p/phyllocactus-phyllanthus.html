Phyllocactus phyllanthus

Phyllocactus phyllanthus, commonly known as the golden cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It features a compact, globular shape with dense clusters of yellow-green, fleshy leaves that resemble small, rounded cactus pads. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
