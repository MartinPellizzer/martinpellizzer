Pentacme suavis var. siamensis

Pentacme suavis var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam sweet pepper, is a variant of the sweet pepper species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that typically range in color from yellow to orange when ripe. The plant is an annual herb with simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The flowers are small and white, with five petals, typical of the Solanaceae family.
