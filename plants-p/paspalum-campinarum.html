Paspalum campinarum

Paspalum campinarum, commonly known as Brazilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Paspalum campinarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
