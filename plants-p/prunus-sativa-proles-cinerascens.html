Prunus sativa proles cinerascens

Prunus sativa proles cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-colored cherry, is a rare and ancient variety of cherry tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive ash-gray bark and small, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that turn a deep red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of heirloom plants.
