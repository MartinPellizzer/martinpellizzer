Plectranthus kondowensis

Plectranthus kondowensis, commonly known as Kondow's coleus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of purple, pink, and white. Its foliage is often deeply veined and can be either simple or lobed, depending on the variety.

Plectranthus kondowensis is typically grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and easy care requirements.
