Podocytisus caramanicus

Podocytisus caramanicus, commonly known as the Caramanica rose.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pink to red flowers in spring, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically dark green and slightly hairy, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Podocytisus caramanicus is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
