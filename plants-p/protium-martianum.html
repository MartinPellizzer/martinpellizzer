Protium martianum

Protium martianum, commonly known as the Malabar chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
