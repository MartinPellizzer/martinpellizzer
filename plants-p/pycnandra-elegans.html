Pycnandra elegans

Pycnandra elegans, commonly known as the elegant pycnandra, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
