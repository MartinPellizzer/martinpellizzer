Piptadenia similis

Piptadenia similis, commonly known as the Brazilian pequi tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a distinctive fruit known as pequi, which has a thick, aromatic husk and a nut-like kernel inside. The plant is valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction, as well as for its edible seeds.

Additionally, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
