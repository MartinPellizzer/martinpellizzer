Primula sinolisteri var. aspera

Primula sinolisteri var. aspera, commonly known as the rough primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
