Pteris excelsissima

Pteris excelsissima, commonly known as the Japanese cliff brake, is a fern native to eastern Asia.

It features fronds that are pinnately divided with a distinctive, glossy green color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of its leaves.

Pteris excelsissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
