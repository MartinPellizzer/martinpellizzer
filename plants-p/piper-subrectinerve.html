Piper subrectinerve

Piper subrectinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper subrectinerve make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
