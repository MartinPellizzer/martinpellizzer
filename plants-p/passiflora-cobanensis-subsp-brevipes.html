Passiflora cobanensis subsp. brevipes

Passiflora cobanensis subsp.

brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed passionflower, is a species native to Central America. It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The flowers are showy, with a distinctive crown of filaments and a blue-violet corona. It produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
