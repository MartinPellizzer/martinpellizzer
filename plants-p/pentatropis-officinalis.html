Pentatropis officinalis

Pentatropis officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and aromatic, needle-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and expectorant properties.
