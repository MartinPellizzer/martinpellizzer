Phyllanthus malabaricus

Phyllanthus malabaricus, commonly known as Chevallier's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its latex sap, which is milky and sticky. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This species is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
