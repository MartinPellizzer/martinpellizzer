Potamoxylon

Potamoxylon, commonly known as the riverwood tree, is a unique plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to water. The plant has a tall, straight trunk with a smooth, dark bark that often exhibits a reddish hue. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The botanical significance of Potamoxylon lies in its adaptation to wetland environments and its role in local ecosystems.
