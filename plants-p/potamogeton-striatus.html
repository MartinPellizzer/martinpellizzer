Potamogeton striatus

Potamogeton striatus, commonly known as the stripe pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbed or striped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters near the water surface. Its rhizomes spread along the bottom of lakes and ponds, forming dense underwater vegetation.

Potamogeton striatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
