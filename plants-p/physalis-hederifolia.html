Physalis hederifolia

Physalis hederifolia, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are enclosed in a papery calyx, resembling a tiny lantern. These fruits are edible when ripe and have a tangy, sweet flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.
