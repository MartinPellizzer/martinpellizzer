Polypodium falcoideum

Polypodium falcoideum, commonly known as the falcon's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are often adorned with a distinctive, shield-like shape. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree trunks or rocks in montane forests. Its spores are released from small, clustered sorangia on the underside of the fronds, facilitating its reproduction in moist, tropical climates.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within its native habitat.
