Potentilla pari

Potentilla pari, commonly known as everlasting or mountain cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or coastal regions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Potentilla pari is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
