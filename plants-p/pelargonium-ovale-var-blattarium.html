Pelargonium ovale var. blattarium

Pelargonium ovale var. blattarium, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in warm climates.
