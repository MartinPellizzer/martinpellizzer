Paphiopedilum ayubianum

Paphiopedilum ayubianum, commonly known as the Ayubian lady's slipper, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, featuring a pouch-like lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns and colors. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, Paphiopedilum ayubianum is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
