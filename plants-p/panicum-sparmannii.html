Panicum sparmannii

Panicum sparmannii, commonly known as dwarf panicgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically reaches a height of less than 30 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
