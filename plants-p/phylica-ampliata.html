Phylica ampliata

Phylica ampliata, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree also serves as a habitat and food source for various local wildlife species.
