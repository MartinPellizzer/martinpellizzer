Pterocarpus sericeus

Pterocarpus sericeus, commonly known as the silky pterocarpus, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching trunk and smooth, pale bark that becomes flaky with age. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, woody pods containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
