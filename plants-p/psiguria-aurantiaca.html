Psiguria aurantiaca

Psiguria aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-flowered puya, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, orange-red inflorescences that resemble torches, which bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spiky clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
