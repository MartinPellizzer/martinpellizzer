Piper juliflorum

Piper juliflorum, commonly known as the mountain pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It produces small, dark berries that are edible but have a peppery flavor.

Piper juliflorum is often found in humid, mountainous areas and is adapted to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
