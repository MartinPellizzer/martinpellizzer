Pimelea axiflora subsp. alpina

Pimelea axiflora subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or orange in color.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapting to its harsh, high-altitude environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open heathlands or rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject for botanists and gardeners interested in alpine flora.
