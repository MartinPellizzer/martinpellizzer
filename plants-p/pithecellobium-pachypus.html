Pithecellobium pachypus

Pithecellobium pachypus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
