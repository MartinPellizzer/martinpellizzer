Psilocaulon rapaceum

Psilocaulon rapaceum, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a small, flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry and sunny environments. The plant has a low-growing habit with linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a compact stem that supports its delicate foliage.

Psilocaulon rapaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
