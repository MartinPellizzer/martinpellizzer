Pouteria micronesica

Pouteria micronesica, commonly known as the Caroline Island soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Micronesia region.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits with a rough, spiky exterior and a soft, sweet pulp containing large, black seeds. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued both for its fruit and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
