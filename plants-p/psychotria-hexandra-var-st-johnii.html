Psychotria hexandra var. st-johnii

Psychotria hexandra var. st-johnii, commonly known as St. John's wort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant contains various alkaloids, including hypericin and hyperforin, which contribute to its medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical significance extends to both traditional medicine and modern pharmacological research.
