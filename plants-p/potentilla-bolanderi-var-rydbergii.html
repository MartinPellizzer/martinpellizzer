Potentilla bolanderi var. rydbergii

Potentilla bolanderi var. rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to arid environments.
