Pabstiella tabacina

Pabstiella tabacina, commonly known as the tobacco leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
