Pyrus bucharica

Pyrus bucharica, commonly known as the Bukharan pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in jams and preserves.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
