Pinus densiflora f. globosa

Pinus densiflora f. globosa, commonly known as the globe pine, is a unique form of the Japanese black pine.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical crown that gives it a distinctive, rounded appearance. This form exhibits compact growth with short, thick needles that are often a darker green than the typical variety. The plant is native to southern Japan and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for bonsai and decorative planting.
