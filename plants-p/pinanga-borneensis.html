Pinanga borneensis

Pinanga borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its starchy rootstock, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
