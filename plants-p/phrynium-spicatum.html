Phrynium spicatum

Phrynium spicatum, commonly known as the spiky arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by spiky, orange-red berries. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.

Phrynium spicatum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
