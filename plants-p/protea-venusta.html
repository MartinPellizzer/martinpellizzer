Protea venusta

Protea venusta, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by numerous petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires careful cultivation to prevent overwatering.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
