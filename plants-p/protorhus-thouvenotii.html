Protorhus thouvenotii

Protorhus thouvenotii, commonly known as the Cape mountain ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
