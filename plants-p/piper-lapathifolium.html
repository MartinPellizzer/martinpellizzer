Piper lapathifolium

Piper lapathifolium, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, fleshy berries. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and versatile species within its native habitats.
