Piper rectispicum

Piper rectispicum, commonly known as the straight-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and are sometimes employed as a spice in local cuisines.
