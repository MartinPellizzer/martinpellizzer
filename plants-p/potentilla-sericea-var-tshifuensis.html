Potentilla sericea var. tshifuensis

Potentilla sericea var. tshifuensis, commonly known as the Tshifuensis cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by gardeners in suitable climates.
