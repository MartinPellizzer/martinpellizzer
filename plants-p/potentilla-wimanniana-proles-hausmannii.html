Potentilla wimanniana proles hausmannii

Potentilla wimanniana proles hausmannii, commonly known as the Wimann's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of five, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
