Polygonum senegalense var. numidicum

Polygonum senegalense var. numidicum, commonly known as the Senegal knotweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a reddish or purple tint on the underside.

It is commonly found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained environments.
