Penstemon superbus

Penstemon superbus, commonly known as the tall bearded penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Penstemon superbus is also valued for its ecological benefits and drought tolerance.
