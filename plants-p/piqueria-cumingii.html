Piqueria cumingii

Piqueria cumingii, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, similar to those of the common dandelion. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Piqueria cumingii is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
