Premna macrophylla

Premna macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Premna macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
