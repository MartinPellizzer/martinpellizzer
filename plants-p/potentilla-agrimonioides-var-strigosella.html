Potentilla agrimonioides var. strigosella

Potentilla agrimonioides var. strigosella, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, woolly appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
