Polygonum pluriflorum

Polygonum pluriflorum, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bamboo-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, appearing in late summer. It is known for its aggressive growth and ability to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
