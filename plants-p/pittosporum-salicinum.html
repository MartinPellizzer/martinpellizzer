Pittosporum salicinum

Pittosporum salicinum, commonly known as the silver pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by yellow berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum salicinum is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
