Plectranthus fasciculatus

Plectranthus fasciculatus, commonly known as the Cape fig or African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus fasciculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens or as a houseplant.
