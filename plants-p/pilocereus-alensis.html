Pilocereus alensis

Pilocereus alensis, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its swollen, colorful stems that resemble a moon, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. It produces small, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
