Poivrea mossambicensis

Poivrea mossambicensis, commonly known as the African pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, pinnate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
