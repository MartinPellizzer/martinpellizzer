Piper colubrinum var. adenophyllum

Piper colubrinum var. adenophyllum, commonly known as the red-rooted pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and unique growth form.
