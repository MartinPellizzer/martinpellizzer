Polypogon × hondoensis

Polypogon × hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo grass, is a hybrid grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a forage plant and its ecological adaptability in humid environments.
