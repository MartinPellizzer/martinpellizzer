Pinus uncinata var. obscurata

Pinus uncinata var. obscurata, commonly known as the dark bristle pine, is a variant of the bristle pine species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green foliage and distinctive, stiff, bristle-like needles that are typically shorter than those of the typical variety. The tree produces cones that are often more elongated and have a darker coloration compared to the standard form. It thrives in high-altitude environments with poor soil conditions, showcasing adaptability to harsh climates.

This variety plays a significant role in the ecological stability of its native habitats.
