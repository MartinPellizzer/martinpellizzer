Piper prunifolium f. strigosum

Piper prunifolium f. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper prunifolium species, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage, which gives it its common name and contributes to its unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
