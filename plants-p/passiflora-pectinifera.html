Passiflora pectinifera

Passiflora pectinifera, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in humid, warm climates.

Passiflora pectinifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in some regions for its fruit.
