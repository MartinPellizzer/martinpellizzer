Pseudobombax grandiflorum var. majus

Pseudobombax grandiflorum var. majus, commonly known as the large-flowered trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
