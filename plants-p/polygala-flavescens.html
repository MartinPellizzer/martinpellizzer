Polygala flavescens

Polygala flavescens, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Polygala flavescens is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
