Pleopeltis kashyapii

Pleopeltis kashyapii, commonly known as the Kashyapi's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit and possesses broad, leathery fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocky surfaces, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
