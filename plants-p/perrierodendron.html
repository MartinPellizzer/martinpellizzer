Perrierodendron

Perrierodendron, commonly known as the African olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Perrierodendron is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid and tropical climates.
