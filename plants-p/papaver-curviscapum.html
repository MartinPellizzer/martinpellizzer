Papaver curviscapum

Papaver curviscapum, commonly known as the wavy-capped poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a distinctive wavy pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its stem is erect and often covered in fine hairs, while its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy.

Papaver curviscapum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
