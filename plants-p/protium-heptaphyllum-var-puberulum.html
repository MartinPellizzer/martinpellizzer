Protium heptaphyllum var. puberulum

Protium heptaphyllum var. puberulum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a medium to large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It possesses a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, greyish-brown bark that exfoliates in strips. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a leathery texture and a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in axillary inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds.

This species is valued for its durable, heavy wood used in construction and furniture-making.
