Panulia nemorosa

Panulia nemorosa, commonly known as the woodland bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Panulia nemorosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
