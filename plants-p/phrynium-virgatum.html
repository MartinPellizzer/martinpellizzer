Phrynium virgatum

Phrynium virgatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf plant, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Phrynium virgatum is also valued for its ability to purify the air and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
