Pleurothallis succosa

Pleurothallis succosa, commonly known as the succulent orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that help it store water in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often with a distinctive coloration that varies among cultivars.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
