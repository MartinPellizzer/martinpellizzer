Polypara

Polypara, commonly known as the "southern cross" or "wild lettuce," is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. Its fruit is a small, dry achene with a pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

Polypara is often found in temperate regions of the southern United States and parts of South America.
