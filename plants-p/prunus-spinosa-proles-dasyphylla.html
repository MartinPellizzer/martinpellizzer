Prunus spinosa proles dasyphylla

Prunus spinosa proles dasyphylla, commonly known as the prickly ash or common hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or hedgerows.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
