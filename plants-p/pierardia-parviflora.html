Pierardia parviflora

Pierardia parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution in suitable habitats.
