Pelargonium eberlanzii

Pelargonium eberlanzii, commonly known as the Eberlanz geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its succulent, rounded leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is widely appreciated by gardeners and botanists alike.
