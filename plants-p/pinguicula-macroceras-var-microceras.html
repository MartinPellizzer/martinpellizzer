Pinguicula macroceras var. microceras

Pinguicula macroceras var. microceras, commonly known as the small-capped butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity for optimal growth.

Due to its unique trapping mechanism and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
