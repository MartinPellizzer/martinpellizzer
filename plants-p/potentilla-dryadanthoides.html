Potentilla dryadanthoides

Potentilla dryadanthoides, commonly known as the dryad's anther.

This flowering plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its foliage is characterized by narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.
