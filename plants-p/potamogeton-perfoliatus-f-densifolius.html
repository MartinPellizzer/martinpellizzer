Potamogeton perfoliatus f. densifolius

Potamogeton perfoliatus f. densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its leaves are usually long and ribbon-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
