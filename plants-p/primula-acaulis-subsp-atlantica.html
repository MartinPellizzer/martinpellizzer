Primula acaulis subsp. atlantica

Primula acaulis subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic rock primrose, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found growing on rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh maritime conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
