Phoradendron puberulum

Phoradendron puberulum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered together. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the nutrient cycle of the host tree.
