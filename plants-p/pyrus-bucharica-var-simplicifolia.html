Pyrus bucharica var. simplicifolia

Pyrus bucharica var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the Bucharic pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It features alternate, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to common pears. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
