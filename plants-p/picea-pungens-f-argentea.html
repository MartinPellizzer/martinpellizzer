Picea pungens f. argentea

Picea pungens f. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a variant of the blue spruce.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue needles that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant is native to the Rocky Mountains in the United States and grows at high elevations. Its conical shape and dense foliage make it a popular ornamental tree.

It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous plants.
