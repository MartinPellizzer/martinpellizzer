Phylohydrax

Phylohydrax, commonly known as the waterleaf, is a unique plant characterized by its ability to absorb and store large amounts of water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

The leaves are typically broad and have a waxy surface that reduces water loss, making the plant well-adapted to arid environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access moisture deep underground. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often blue or purple in color.

Phylohydrax plays an important role in stabilizing soil in dry regions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
