Phoebe cuspidata

Phoebe cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Phoebe cuspidata is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
