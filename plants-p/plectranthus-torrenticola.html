Plectranthus torrenticola

Plectranthus torrenticola, commonly known as the torrent plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources such as streams and riverbanks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it attracts various pollinators due to its nectar-rich flowers.

Plectranthus torrenticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
