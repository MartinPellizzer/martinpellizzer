Pseuderanthemum atropurpureum

Pseuderanthemum atropurpureum, commonly known as purple false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
