Pandanus lachaisei

Pandanus lachaisei, commonly known as the Lachaise's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of French Polynesia and Tahiti. The plant is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Pandanus lachaisei is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
