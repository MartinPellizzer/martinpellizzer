Protea intermedia

Protea intermedia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Protea intermedia plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
