Pandanus hermaphroditus

Pandanus hermaphroditus, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that form a dense, fan-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, while its leaves are utilized in crafts and as a source of fiber.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to saline conditions.
