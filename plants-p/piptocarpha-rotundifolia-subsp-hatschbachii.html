Piptocarpha rotundifolia subsp. hatschbachii

Piptocarpha rotundifolia subsp.

hatschbachii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada aster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, violet-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
