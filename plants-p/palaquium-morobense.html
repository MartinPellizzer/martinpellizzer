Palaquium morobense

Palaquium morobense, commonly known as the Morobe palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its slender, multi-trunked structure with pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife, including birds and mammals.

The Morobe palm is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal and lowland areas.
