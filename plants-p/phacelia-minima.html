Phacelia minima

Phacelia minima, commonly known as the small phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.

Phacelia minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
