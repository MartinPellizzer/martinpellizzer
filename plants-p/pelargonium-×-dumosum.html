Pelargonium × dumosum

Pelargonium × dumosum, commonly known as the shrub geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and is native to South Africa. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically lobed or serrated, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact form and vibrant blooms.

Pelargonium × dumosum is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
