Persea foetens

Persea foetens, commonly known as the sour soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The fruit is a spiky, green berry that contains a white, edible pulp and a central cavity with large, inedible seeds. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
