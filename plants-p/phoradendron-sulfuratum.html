Phoradendron sulfuratum

Phoradendron sulfuratum, commonly known as the sulfur curlleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with oak and hickory forests. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often curled, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Its berries are typically orange or red and serve as a food source for various bird species.
