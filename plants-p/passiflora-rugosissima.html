Passiflora rugosissima

Passiflora rugosissima, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its rugged, thorny stems and striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Passiflora rugosissima is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting local wildlife.
