Parthenocissus heptaphylla

Parthenocissus heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf ivy, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive seven-lobed leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises and walls, though it can be invasive if not managed.
