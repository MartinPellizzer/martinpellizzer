Pyrus margittaiana

Pyrus margittaiana, commonly known as the Margittai pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential horticultural value.
