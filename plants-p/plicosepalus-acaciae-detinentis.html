Plicosepalus acaciae-detinentis

Plicosepalus acaciae-detinentis, commonly known as the fringed-sheath acacia, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, plicose (folded) calyx that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes.

The seeds are enclosed in pods, which are often used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization.
