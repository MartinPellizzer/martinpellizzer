Poliomintha mollis

Poliomintha mollis, commonly known as softleaf poliomintha, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are soft and velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Poliomintha mollis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
