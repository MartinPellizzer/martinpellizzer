Pilosella hoppeana subsp. hoppeana

Pilosella hoppeana subsp.

hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its characteristic appearance. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achene.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
