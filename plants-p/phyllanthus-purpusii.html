Phyllanthus purpusii

Phyllanthus purpusii, commonly known as the dwarf mallow, is a low-growing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
