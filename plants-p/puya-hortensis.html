Puya hortensis

Puya hortensis, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is a perennial herb that produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and red. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.

Puya hortensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Andean medicine.
