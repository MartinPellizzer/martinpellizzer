Panicum brevispicatum

Panicum brevispicatum, commonly known as little spikegrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually pale green to reddish in color. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Its growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
