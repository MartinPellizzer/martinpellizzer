Plectritis ciliosa var. davyana

Plectritis ciliosa var. davyana, commonly known as the hairy plectritis, is a variant of the plectritis genus native to North America.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
