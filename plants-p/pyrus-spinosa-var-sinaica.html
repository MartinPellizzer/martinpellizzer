Pyrus spinosa var. sinaica

Pyrus spinosa var. sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai pear.

It is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. The plant has thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, yellowish pear that is edible but not widely cultivated.

This variety is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found growing in rocky soils.
