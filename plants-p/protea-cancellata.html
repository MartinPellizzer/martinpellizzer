Protea cancellata

Protea cancellata, commonly known as the cancellated protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical shape. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, peeling bark that reveals a lighter inner layer. Its leaves are leathery, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery or whitish underside.

Protea cancellata thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
