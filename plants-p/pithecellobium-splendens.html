Pithecellobium splendens

Pithecellobium splendens, commonly known as the golden glory tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
