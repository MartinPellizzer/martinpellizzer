Panicum orientale

Panicum orientale, commonly known as oriental panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for downy feathers, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed but can also be used in ornamental landscaping.
