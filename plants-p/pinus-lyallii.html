Pinus lyallii

Pinus lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The plant produces cones that are often pendulous and contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. Lyall's pine thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
