Phanerophlebiopsis coadunata

Phanerophlebiopsis coadunata, commonly known as the Ceylon orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Asia, particularly Sri Lanka, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
