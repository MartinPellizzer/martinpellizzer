Penstemon pallidus subsp. arkansanus

Penstemon pallidus subsp.

arkansanus, commonly known as the Arkansas beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland and streamside habitats.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
