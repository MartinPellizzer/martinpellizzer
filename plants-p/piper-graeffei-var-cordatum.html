Piper graeffei var. cordatum

Piper graeffei var. cordatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper or heart-shaped pepper, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
