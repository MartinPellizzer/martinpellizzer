Phaseolus compressus var. ochraceus

Phaseolus compressus var. ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow runner bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, yellowish-green pods that mature to a striking orange-red color. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers, which are typical of the Phaseolus genus. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and often used in traditional cuisines.
