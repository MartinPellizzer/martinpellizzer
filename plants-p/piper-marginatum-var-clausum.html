Piper marginatum var. clausum

Piper marginatum var. clausum, commonly known as the Clausum pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces tiny berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, peppery flavor.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it thrives in humid, shaded conditions with well-draining soil.
