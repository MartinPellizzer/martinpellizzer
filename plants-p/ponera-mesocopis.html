Ponera mesocopis

Ponera mesocopis, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its slow growth and drought tolerance, it is a low-maintenance plant for xeriscaping.
