Parrya nudicaulis var. scapigera

Parrya nudicaulis var. scapigera, commonly known as the scapigera parrya, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy species within the Ericaceae family.
