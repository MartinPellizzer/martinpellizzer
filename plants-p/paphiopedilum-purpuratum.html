Paphiopedilum purpuratum

Paphiopedilum purpuratum, commonly known as the purple-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a hairy, pseudobulb-like stem with broad, elliptical leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces showy, pinkish-purple flowers with a distinctive lip that is typically darker and more contrasting. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
