Potentilla donnell-smithii

Potentilla donnell-smithii, commonly known as the Donnell-Smith cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to nine oval-shaped leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
