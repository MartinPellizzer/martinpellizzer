Photinia fauriei

Photinia fauriei, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping for its seasonal color changes.
