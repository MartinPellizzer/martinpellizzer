Pennisetum secundiflorum

Pennisetum secundiflorum, commonly known as purple top grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinted leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pennisetum secundiflorum is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
