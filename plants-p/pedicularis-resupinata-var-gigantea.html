Pedicularis resupinata var. gigantea

Pedicularis resupinata var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant lady's mantle, is a striking member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is notable for its impressive size and ornamental value in botanical gardens.
