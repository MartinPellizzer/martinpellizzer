Phlomis × cilicica

Phlomis × cilicica, commonly known as the hybrid garden phlomis, is a perennial plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of showy, lavender to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it is native to regions of the Mediterranean, including Turkey and Cyprus. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Phlomis × cilicica is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
