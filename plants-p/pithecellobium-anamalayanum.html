Pithecellobium anamalayanum

Pithecellobium anamalayanum, commonly known as the Malayan mountain acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
