Phoradendron pseudoangustifolium

Phoradendron pseudoangustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of host trees in the southeastern United States.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for birds. Its seeds are dispersed by animals, particularly birds, which eat the berries and later excrete the seeds onto new host trees.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing food for wildlife.
