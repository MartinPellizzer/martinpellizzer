Pelargonium cruentum

Pelargonium cruentum, commonly known as the bloodroot geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has velvety, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium cruentum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
