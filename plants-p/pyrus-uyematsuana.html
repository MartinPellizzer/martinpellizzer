Pyrus uyematsuana

Pyrus uyematsuana, commonly known as the Uyematsu apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Japan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous, alternate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small apple-like berry, is typically yellow or orange when mature.

Pyrus uyematsuana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
