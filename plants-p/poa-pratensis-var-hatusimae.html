Poa pratensis var. hatusimae

Poa pratensis var. hatusimae, commonly known as the Hatusimae meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and silver-green foliage. This grass species is typically found in temperate regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils. It is often used in turfgrass mixes due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.

The plant exhibits a clumping growth pattern and is valued for its ability to withstand moderate foot traffic.
