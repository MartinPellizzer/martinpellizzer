Phragmites communis subsp. flavescens

Phragmites communis subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow stemmed reed, is a perennial grass native to the wetlands of North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often display a yellowish hue, especially when mature.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, fluffy flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often forming large colonies in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
