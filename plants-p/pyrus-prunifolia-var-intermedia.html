Pyrus prunifolia var. intermedia

Pyrus prunifolia var. intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid pear, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Pyrus prunifolia and Pyrus communis.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, rounded leaves that are typically a glossy green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet to semi-sweet fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and resilience to various environmental conditions.
