Pancratium crassifolium

Pancratium crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf torchflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Pancratium crassifolium is often used in ornamental horticulture for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
