Pennisetum chilense var. macrophyllum

Pennisetum chilense var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the Chilean fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, pinkish to reddish plumes that are highly ornamental and attract birds and insects. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ability to spread through rhizomes make it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
