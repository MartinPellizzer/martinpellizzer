Plumbago rosea var. coccinea

Plumbago rosea var. coccinea, commonly known as the red plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
