Pelargonium × robinsonii

Pelargonium × robinsonii, commonly known as the Lemon-scented Geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in perfumes and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its fragrant foliage and vibrant blooms, it is a popular choice for container gardening and as a decorative plant.
