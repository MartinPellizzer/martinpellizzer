Pseudanthus pauciflorus subsp. pauciflorus

Pseudanthus pauciflorus subsp.

pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered pseudanthus, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is native to southern Africa.

Its compact structure and unique floral arrangement make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
