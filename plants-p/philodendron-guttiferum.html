Philodendron guttiferum

Philodendron guttiferum, commonly known as the spotted philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often display a distinctive pattern of light green spots and veins. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. Philodendron guttiferum typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

Its foliage is popular in indoor gardening for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
