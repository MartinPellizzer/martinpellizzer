Prunus × donarium var. nobilis

Prunus × donarium var. nobilis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree typically reaches heights of 8 to 15 meters and has a rounded, often open crown. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, turning yellow in autumn.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in Japanese tradition.
