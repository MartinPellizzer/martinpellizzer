Prunus nipponica var. lasiogyna

Prunus nipponica var. lasiogyna, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in white or pale pink during spring, followed by edible purple or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
