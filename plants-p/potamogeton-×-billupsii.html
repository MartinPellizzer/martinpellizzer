Potamogeton × billupsii

Potamogeton × billupsii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is characterized by its submerged or floating leaves, which are typically narrow and linear, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often found in shallow freshwater environments. It is commonly found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams across Europe and parts of Asia.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
