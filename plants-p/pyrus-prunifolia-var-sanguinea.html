Pyrus prunifolia var. sanguinea

Pyrus prunifolia var. sanguinea, commonly known as the redleaf pear, is a variant of the Prunifolia pear species.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in autumn. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
