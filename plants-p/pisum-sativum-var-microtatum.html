Pisum sativum var. microtatum

Pisum sativum var. microtatum, commonly known as the dwarf garden pea, is a compact variety of the common pea plant.

It features small, round pods that mature to a yellowish-green color, containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its short stature, typically growing to about 20-30 cm in height, making it ideal for container gardening. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it blooms and sets fruit throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in limited spaces.
