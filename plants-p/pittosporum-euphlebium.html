Pittosporum euphlebium

Pittosporum euphlebium, commonly known as the silver-leaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum euphlebium is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its role in native ecosystems.
