Pygmea pulvinaris

Pygmea pulvinaris, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to its foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its white blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is a popular plant among gardeners seeking to add a touch of elegance to their landscapes.
