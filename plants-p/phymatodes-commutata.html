Phymatodes commutata

Phymatodes commutata, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing desert soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
