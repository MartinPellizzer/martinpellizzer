Purshia tridentata var. glandulosa

Purshia tridentata var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular antelope bush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often glandular and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
