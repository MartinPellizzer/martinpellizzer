Pappostipa ibarii

Pappostipa ibarii, commonly known as the Ibarian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and have a fine texture, while its seeds are equipped with silky hairs that aid in wind dispersal.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
