Phaius takeoi

Phaius takeoi, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Phaius takeoi is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
