Protasparagus striatus

Protasparagus striatus, commonly known as the zigzag asparagus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Protasparagus striatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
