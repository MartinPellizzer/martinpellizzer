Pacouria scandens var. floribunda

Pacouria scandens var. floribunda, commonly known as the showy pacouria, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features elongated, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to climb trees or other structures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
