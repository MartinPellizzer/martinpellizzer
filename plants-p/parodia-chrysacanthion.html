Parodia chrysacanthion

Parodia chrysacanthion, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
