Potentilla verna var. major

Potentilla verna var. major, commonly known as the greater spring cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, aggregate fruits.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
