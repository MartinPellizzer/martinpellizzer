Pueraria bella

Pueraria bella, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped white or pale purple flowers. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in moist, sunny environments such as forests and gardens. Its flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and it produces pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Pueraria bella is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
