Potentilla tanacetifolia var. longifolia

Potentilla tanacetifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery underside that gives the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
