Pyrus dasyphylla

Pyrus dasyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
