Primula pseudomalacoides

Primula pseudomalacoides, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20-30 cm, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest margins and rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
