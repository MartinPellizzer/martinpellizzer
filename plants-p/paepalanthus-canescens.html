Paepalanthus canescens

Paepalanthus canescens, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an interesting choice for succulent enthusiasts.
