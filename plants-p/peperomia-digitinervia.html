Peperomia digitinervia

Peperomia digitinervia, commonly known as the finger-veined peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with thick, fleshy leaves that have distinct, finger-like veins. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-white and clustered in spikes.

Peperomia digitinervia is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
