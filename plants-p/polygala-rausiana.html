Polygala rausiana

Polygala rausiana, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions, and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Polygala rausiana is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
