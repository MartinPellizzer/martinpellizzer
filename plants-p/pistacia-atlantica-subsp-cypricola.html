Pistacia atlantica subsp. cypricola

Pistacia atlantica subsp.

cypricola, commonly known as the Atlantic pistachio, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by hard, woody fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
