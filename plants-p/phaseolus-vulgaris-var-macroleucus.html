Phaseolus vulgaris var. macroleucus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. macroleucus, commonly known as the giant kidney bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its large, elongated seeds that can reach lengths of up to 2.5 centimeters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with indeterminate vines, producing clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three leaflets and a fibrous root system.

This variety is valued for its high seed yield and is often grown for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
