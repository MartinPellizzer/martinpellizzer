Pisonia obtusiloba

Pisonia obtusiloba, commonly known as the woolly pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and limestone soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release fluffy, parachute-like seeds.

Pisonia obtusiloba is often found in humid, tropical environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
