Polygonum engelmannii

Polygonum engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshes. The plant features slender, often reddish stems that are covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a rough texture.

The small, clustered flowers are usually pink to red and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
