Phlox drummondii

Phlox drummondii, commonly known as the garden phlox, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy clusters of tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

Phlox drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscapes for its vibrant blooms.
