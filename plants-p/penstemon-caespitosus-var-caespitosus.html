Penstemon caespitosus var. caespitosus

Penstemon caespitosus var. caespitosus, commonly known as the blue curl penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and striking blue to violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair, enhancing its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
