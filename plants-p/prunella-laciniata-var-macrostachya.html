Prunella laciniata var. macrostachya

Prunella laciniata var. macrostachya, commonly known as large-stalked selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that grow up to 60 cm tall, often with a hairy texture. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
