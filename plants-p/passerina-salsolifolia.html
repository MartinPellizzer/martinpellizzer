Passerina salsolifolia

Passerina salsolifolia, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to conserve water. Passerina salsolifolia thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

It is an important plant for stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in desert environments.
