Potentilla aspegrenii

Potentilla aspegrenii, commonly known as the Aspegren's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and fragile species.
