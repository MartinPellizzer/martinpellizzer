Ptelea glauca

Ptelea glauca, commonly known as the blueleaf ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that turn a striking blue-gray color in the fall, which is a unique botanical trait. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by oval, woody fruits. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
