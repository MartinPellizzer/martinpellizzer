Phyllostachys hirtivagina

Phyllostachys hirtivagina, commonly known as the hairy sheath bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features distinctive hairy sheaths on its stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Phyllostachys hirtivagina is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of sustainable materials.
