Paspalum scrobiculatum var. jardinii

Paspalum scrobiculatum var. jardinii, commonly known as the garden paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in dense clusters, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
