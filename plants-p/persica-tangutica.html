Persica tangutica

Persica tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut peach, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and deciduous leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible fruits similar to peaches. It is adapted to survive in dry and saline environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
