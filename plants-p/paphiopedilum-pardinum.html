Paphiopedilum pardinum

Paphiopedilum pardinum, commonly known as the sandhill orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred lip that is often yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are relatively small and have a distinctive, pouch-like structure that aids in pollination.

Paphiopedilum pardinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
