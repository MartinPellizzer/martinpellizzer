Peperomia myosurus

Peperomia myosurus, commonly known as the mouse-eared peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store moisture, making it well adapted to dry conditions. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia myosurus is often grown as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
