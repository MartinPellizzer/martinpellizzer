Potentilla filicaulis

Potentilla filicaulis, commonly known as the fernleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Potentilla filicaulis is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks.
