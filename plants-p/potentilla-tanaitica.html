Potentilla tanaitica

Potentilla tanaitica, commonly known as the Armenian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla tanaitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
