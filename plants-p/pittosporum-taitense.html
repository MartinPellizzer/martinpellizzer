Pittosporum taitense

Pittosporum taitense, commonly known as the Taiti Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. These flowers are followed by small, orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Pittosporum taitense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
