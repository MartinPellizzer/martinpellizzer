Pilosella brachiata subsp. pseudobrachiata

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

pseudobrachiata, commonly known as the slender pilosella, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
