Philodendron ellipticum

Philodendron ellipticum, commonly known as the heartleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant has aerial roots that help it climb towards light, and its stems can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron ellipticum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
