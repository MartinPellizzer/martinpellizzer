Prinos deciduus var. aestivalis

Prinos deciduus var. aestivalis, commonly known as the summer prino, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the summer months, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a distinctive scent when crushed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
