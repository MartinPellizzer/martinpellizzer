Piper subtropicum var. pachystachyum

Piper subtropicum var. pachystachyum, commonly known as the giant betel nut or giant areca, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its botanical features include a robust stem and a complex root system adapted to wet, tropical environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in traditional practices.
