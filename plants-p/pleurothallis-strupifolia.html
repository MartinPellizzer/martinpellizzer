Pleurothallis strupifolia

Pleurothallis strupifolia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often exhibit a golden or yellowish hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a compact inflorescence with numerous fragrant blooms.

Pleurothallis strupifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
