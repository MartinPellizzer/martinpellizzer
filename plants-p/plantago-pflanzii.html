Plantago pflanzii

Plantago pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to certain regions of Europe and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and its use in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's fibrous roots and sturdy leaves make it a hardy and resilient species.
