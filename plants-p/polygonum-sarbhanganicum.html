Polygonum sarbhanganicum

Polygonum sarbhanganicum, commonly known as Indian knotgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. It is native to regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in moist, disturbed soils.

Polygonum sarbhanganicum is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
