Phlox carolina var. altissima

Phlox carolina var. altissima, commonly known as the tall meadow phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ability to attract pollinators and its adaptability to various soil conditions.
