Persicaria odorata

Persicaria odorata, commonly known as Vietnamese coriander or rau ram, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are often used fresh in culinary applications, particularly in Vietnamese cuisine, for their aromatic and slightly citrusy flavor.

Persicaria odorata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
