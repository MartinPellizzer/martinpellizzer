Panicum glabrifolium

Panicum glabrifolium, commonly known as smoothleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless leaves and panicles that are typically light green to golden in color. The plant thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, and is often found in areas with saturated soil. Its flowering season occurs in the summer months, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum glabrifolium is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
