Pseudomaxillaria parviflora

Pseudomaxillaria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered pseudomaxillaria, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant features a short, stout stem with a few leaves and produces a single raceme of flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
