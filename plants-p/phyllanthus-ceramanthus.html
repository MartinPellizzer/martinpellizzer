Phyllanthus ceramanthus

Phyllanthus ceramanthus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of the branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange to red berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for their antioxidant properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
