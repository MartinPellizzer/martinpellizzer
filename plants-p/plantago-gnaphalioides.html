Plantago gnaphalioides

Plantago gnaphalioides, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open areas, and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
