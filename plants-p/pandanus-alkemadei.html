Pandanus alkemadei

Pandanus alkemadei, commonly known as the Christmas Palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia and the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical climates.
