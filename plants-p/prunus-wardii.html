Prunus wardii

Prunus wardii, commonly known as the Ward's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Prunus wardii is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
