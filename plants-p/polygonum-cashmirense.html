Polygonum cashmirense

Polygonum cashmirense, commonly known as Kashmir knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its swollen nodes and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Polygonum cashmirense is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
