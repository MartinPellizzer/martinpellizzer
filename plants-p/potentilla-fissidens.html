Potentilla fissidens

Potentilla fissidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge.

Potentilla fissidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
