Pelargonium australe var. erodioides

Pelargonium australe var. erodioides, commonly known as the coastal geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens. Its leaves are usually rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
