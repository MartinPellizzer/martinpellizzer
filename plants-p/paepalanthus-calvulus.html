Paepalanthus calvulus

Paepalanthus calvulus, commonly known as the silky pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or gray hairs, which helps protect it from intense sunlight and moisture loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.
