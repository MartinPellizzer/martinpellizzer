Penstemon nitidus

Penstemon nitidus, commonly known as the silverleaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and spikes of tubular, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.

Penstemon nitidus is also noted for its low maintenance requirements and adaptability to various garden conditions.
