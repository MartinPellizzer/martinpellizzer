Pilosella densiflora subsp. radiocauloides

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

radiocauloides, commonly known as the radiocauloides pilosella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and the presence of a basal rosette of leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
