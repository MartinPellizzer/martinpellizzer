Peltogyne paniculata subsp. pubescens

Peltogyne paniculata subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, compound leaves with a pubescent underside.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The species is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
