Panicularia procumbens

Panicularia procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically spreads along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
