Piper venosum

Piper venosum, commonly known as the veiny pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its finely veined, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, slender fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's aromatic foliage and unique appearance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
