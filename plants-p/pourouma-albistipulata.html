Pourouma albistipulata

Pourouma albistipulata, commonly known as the white-veined areca, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to the Amazon basin.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its unique foliage and adaptability to wet, tropical conditions make it a notable species in botanical studies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
