Pluchea crenata

Pluchea crenata, commonly known as the crested sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and the plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits known as cypsela.

Pluchea crenata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
