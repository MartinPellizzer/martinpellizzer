Pandanus tectorius var. liukiuensis

Pandanus tectorius var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu pandanus, is a variant of the screw pine native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant thrives in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained sandy soils and full sun exposure. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Pandanus genus.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in local communities.
