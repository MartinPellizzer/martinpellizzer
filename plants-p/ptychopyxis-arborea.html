Ptychopyxis arborea

Ptychopyxis arborea, commonly known as the African violet tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and come in shades of white and purple.

Ptychopyxis arborea is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or wooded areas.
