Potentilla favratii

Potentilla favratii, commonly known as the Corsican cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are typically serrated and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Potentilla favratii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and colorful display.
