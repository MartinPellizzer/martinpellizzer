Plantago pachyneura subsp. pflanzii

Plantago pachyneura subsp.

pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
