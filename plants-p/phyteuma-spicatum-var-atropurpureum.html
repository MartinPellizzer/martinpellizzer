Phyteuma spicatum var. atropurpureum

Phyteuma spicatum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple spike phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features tall, upright stems that bear dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of purple and pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders.

This variety is valued for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
