Plectranthus circinnatus

Plectranthus circinnatus, commonly known as the silver velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Plectranthus circinnatus prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
