Paracaryum micranthum var. urceolatum

Paracaryum micranthum var. urceolatum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive. It is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The species is known for its ability to produce offsets, which can be propagated to create new plants.
