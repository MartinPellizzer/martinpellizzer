Plantago monosperma f. glabrescens

Plantago monosperma f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in dry, open habitats.

This form is distinguished by its reduced pubescence, giving it a smoother appearance compared to the typical form.
