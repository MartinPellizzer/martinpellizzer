Potamogeton × olivaceus

Potamogeton × olivaceus, commonly known as the olive pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs naturally in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to olive in color, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spike-like structures. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and can form dense underwater vegetation.

Potamogeton × olivaceus plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic life and can influence water quality through its growth patterns.
