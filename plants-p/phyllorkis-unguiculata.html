Phyllorkis unguiculata

Phyllorkis unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or sandy habitats.

Phyllorkis unguiculata is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
