Pappophorum senegalense

Pappophorum senegalense, commonly known as the Senegalese grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
