Pentas nobilis var. grandiflora

Pentas nobilis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered pentas, is a tropical shrub native to India.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
