Penstemon eriantherus var. saliens

Penstemon eriantherus var. saliens, commonly known as the Rocky Mountain beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
