Pleurothallis haberi

Pleurothallis haberi, commonly known as the Haber's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Pleurothallis haberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
