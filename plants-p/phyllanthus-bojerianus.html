Phyllanthus bojerianus

Phyllanthus bojerianus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Phyllanthus bojerianus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
