Plectocephalus chilensis var. chilensis

Plectocephalus chilensis var. chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean plectocephalus, is a flowering plant native to southern Chile.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the temperate climate of its native range. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in terminal inflorescences, and bloom in the spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climatic conditions.
