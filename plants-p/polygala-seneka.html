Polygala seneka

Polygala seneka, commonly known as the Seneca milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.

Polygala seneka plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure.
