Phyllostachys dulcis

Phyllostachys dulcis, commonly known as the sweet bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it grows in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are often a vibrant green. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Additionally, its edible shoots are a delicacy in some Asian cuisines.
