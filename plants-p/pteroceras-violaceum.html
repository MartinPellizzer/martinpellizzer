Pteroceras violaceum

Pteroceras violaceum, commonly known as the violet fern, is a unique plant belonging to the family Pterocaryaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds that exhibit a striking violet hue, especially when mature. Native to tropical regions of Africa, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are typically bipinnate, with fine, hair-like structures that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
