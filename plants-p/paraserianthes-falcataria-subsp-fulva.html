Paraserianthes falcataria subsp. fulva

Paraserianthes falcataria subsp.

fulva, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in reforestation and as an ornamental species. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The subspecies fulva differs from the typical form by its more vibrant yellow flowers and slightly different leaf structure.
