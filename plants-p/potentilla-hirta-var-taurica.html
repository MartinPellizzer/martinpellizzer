Potentilla hirta var. taurica

Potentilla hirta var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
