Pfaffia sericea f. villosissima

Pfaffia sericea f. villosissima, commonly known as the velvet leaf *Pfaffia*, is a variant of the *Pfaffia sericea* species, which belongs to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and small, clustered flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
