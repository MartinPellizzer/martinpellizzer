Paronychia taurica

Paronychia taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian sandthorn, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments. The plant has woody stems and small, leathery leaves that help it conserve water. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor.

Paronychia taurica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
