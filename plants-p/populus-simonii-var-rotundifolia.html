Populus simonii var. rotundifolia

Populus simonii var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, which are followed by distinctive samaras, or winged seeds, for wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
