Passiflora cerasina

Passiflora cerasina, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature intricate patterns of purple, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora cerasina is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
