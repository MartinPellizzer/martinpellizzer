Primula × helenae

Primula × helenae, commonly known as the Helen's primrose, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers cool, moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
