Peperomia lasiostigma var. gibbsiae

Peperomia lasiostigma var. gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or ovate in shape, with a deep green coloration and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low to moderate light conditions and its tolerance for dry air.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for tabletop displays and terrariums.
