Prunus armeniaca var. dasycarpa

Prunus armeniaca var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the Persian peach, is a variant of the peach tree characterized by its dense, hairy fruit skin.

This variety is native to the Caucasus region and is known for its unique, fuzzy exterior that distinguishes it from typical peaches. The plant produces large, round fruits with a sweet and sometimes tart flavor, often used in both culinary and medicinal applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.

Its growth habits include a medium-sized tree with broad, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers in early spring.
