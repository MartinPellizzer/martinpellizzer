Phalaris minor var. glomerata

Phalaris minor var. glomerata, commonly known as the tufted bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. It is often used for erosion control due to its vigorous growth and ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically valuable and aesthetically pleasing in natural landscapes.
