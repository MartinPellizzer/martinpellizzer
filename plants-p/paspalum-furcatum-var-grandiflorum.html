Paspalum furcatum var. grandiflorum

Paspalum furcatum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant for erosion control and wetland restoration.
