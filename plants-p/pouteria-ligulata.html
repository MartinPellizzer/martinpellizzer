Pouteria ligulata

Pouteria ligulata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree is notable for its edible seeds, which are enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. Pouteria ligulata thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with high rainfall.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its economic significance.
