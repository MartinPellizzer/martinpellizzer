Pilosella atramentaria

Pilosella atramentaria, commonly known as the black-centred mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Pilosella atramentaria is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
