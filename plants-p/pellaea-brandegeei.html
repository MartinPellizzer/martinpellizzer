Pellaea brandegeei

Pellaea brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's fern, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with small, spore-producing structures called sori. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens. Its botanical features include a short, creeping rhizome and bipinnate leaves that are often curled or rolled when young.

Pellaea brandegeei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
