Pultenaea mucronata

Pultenaea mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive burr-like seed pods that contain several seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Pultenaea mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
