Pleurothallis brevicaulis

Pleurothallis brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis brevicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
