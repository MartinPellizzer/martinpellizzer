Protea patens

Protea patens, commonly known as the Silver Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive silver or grayish hue on the bracts.

Protea patens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
