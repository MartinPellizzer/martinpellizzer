Peristylus monophyllus

Peristylus monophyllus, commonly known as the single-leaf peristylus, is a rare and striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a single, large, waxy leaf that emerges directly from the stem. The plant is a succulent, adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or orange.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Peristylus monophyllus is considered a vulnerable species.
