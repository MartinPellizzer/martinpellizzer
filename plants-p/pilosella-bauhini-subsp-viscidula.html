Pilosella bauhini subsp. viscidula

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

viscidula, commonly known as the viscid pilosella, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a sticky texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
