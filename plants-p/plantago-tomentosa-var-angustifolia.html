Plantago tomentosa var. angustifolia

Plantago tomentosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
