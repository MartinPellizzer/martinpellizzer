Physalis sendtneri

Physalis sendtneri, commonly known as the red gooseberry or winter cherry, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that enclose bright orange to red berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. Its fruits are typically harvested in late autumn and are prized for their tart flavor.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
