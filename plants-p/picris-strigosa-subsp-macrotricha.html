Picris strigosa subsp. macrotricha

Picris strigosa subsp.

macrotricha, commonly known as the woolly hawkbit, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in dense, white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
