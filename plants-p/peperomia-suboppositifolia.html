Peperomia suboppositifolia

Peperomia suboppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaf peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, glossy, ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely along its stems, giving it a neat and clustered appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its unique leaf arrangement and ease of care make it a popular choice for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
