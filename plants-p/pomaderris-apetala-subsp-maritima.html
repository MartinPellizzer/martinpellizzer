Pomaderris apetala subsp. maritima

Pomaderris apetala subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal pomaderris, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal vegetation in its native habitat.
