Pelargonium divaricatum

Pelargonium divaricatum, commonly known as the variable geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a hairy texture. It produces pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
