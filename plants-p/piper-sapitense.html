Piper sapitense

Piper sapitense, commonly known as the Philippine pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
