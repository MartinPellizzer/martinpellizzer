Pandanus columellatus

Pandanus columellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to their durability.

Pandanus columellatus thrives in coastal and wetland environments, where it can tolerate salt spray and periodic flooding.
