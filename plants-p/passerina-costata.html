Passerina costata

Passerina costata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The species is known for its showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
