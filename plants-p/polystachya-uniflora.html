Polystachya uniflora

Polystachya uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spikes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a rhizomatous growth habit.

Polystachya uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
