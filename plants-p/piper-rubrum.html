Piper rubrum

Piper rubrum, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red berries. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, often supported by trellises or other structures. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, while its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
