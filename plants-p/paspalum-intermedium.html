Paspalum intermedium

Paspalum intermedium, commonly known as the common paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching blades that often form dense mats. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, making it a common species in wetlands and riparian zones. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in panicles, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum intermedium is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
