Physospermum olgae

Physospermum olgae, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that display a striking silver-blue hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
