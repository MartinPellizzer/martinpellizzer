Poa denudata

Poa denudata, commonly known as the nakedgrass, is a species of grass native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, which are often arranged in a panicle structure. Poa denudata is adapted to a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.
