Phlox thomsoniana

Phlox thomsoniana, commonly known as Thomson's phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring. It has narrow, linear leaves and a branching stem system.

Thomson's phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
