Platanus vulgaris subsp. orientalis

Platanus vulgaris subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental plane tree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that turn yellow in the fall.

The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain 1-3 seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its trunk is often marked with fissures and has a distinctive, rugged appearance.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing capabilities in urban landscapes.
