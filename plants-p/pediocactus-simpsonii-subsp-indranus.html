Pediocactus simpsonii subsp. indranus

Pediocactus simpsonii subsp.

indranus, commonly known as the Simpson's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are clustered at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its limited distribution and susceptibility to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
