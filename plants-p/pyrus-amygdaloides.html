Pyrus amygdaloides

Pyrus amygdaloides, commonly known as the whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits known as whitebeams, which are similar in appearance to apples but less commonly consumed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

The bark is smooth and greyish, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
