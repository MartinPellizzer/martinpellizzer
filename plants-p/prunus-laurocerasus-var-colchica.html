Prunus laurocerasus var. colchica

Prunus laurocerasus var. colchica, commonly known as the Turkish cherry or Colchic cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange in color, which ripen in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for landscaping purposes.
