Phaseolus micranthus

Phaseolus micranthus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink in color. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.
