Potamogeton australis

Potamogeton australis, commonly known as the Australian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Australia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or just at the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.
