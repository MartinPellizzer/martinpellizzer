Platystyliparis resupinata var. ridleyi

Platystyliparis resupinata var. ridleyi, commonly known as the Ridley's climbing orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, wiry stem that often grows along tree trunks or branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
