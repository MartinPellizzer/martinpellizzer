Piper caranoense

Piper caranoense, commonly known as the Caranó pepper or Caranó piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and the plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
