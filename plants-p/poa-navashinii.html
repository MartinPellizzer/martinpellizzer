Poa navashinii

Poa navashinii, commonly known as Navashin's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution, Poa navashinii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
