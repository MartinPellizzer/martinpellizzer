Piper hayneanum var. macrophyllum

Piper hayneanum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf pepper plant, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery berries. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential medicinal uses.

The varietal name "macrophyllum" refers to its notably large leaves, distinguishing it from other varieties within the Piper hayneanum species.
