Phthirusa elongata

Phthirusa elongata, commonly known as the slender leaf beetle, is a small, herbivorous insect belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is characterized by its elongated body and vibrant coloration, often displaying a metallic green or blue hue. This species is typically found on specific host plants, such as certain species of legumes and grasses. Its feeding habits involve chewing on the leaves, which can cause damage to the plant's foliage.

The beetle's life cycle includes egg, larval, pupal, and adult stages, all of which are closely tied to its host plant's growth patterns.
