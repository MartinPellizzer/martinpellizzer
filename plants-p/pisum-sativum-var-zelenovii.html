Pisum sativum var. zelenovii

Pisum sativum var. zelenovii, commonly known as the green lentil, is a cultivated variety of the common pea.

It is characterized by its small, green, lens-shaped seeds that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and has a climbing habit, supported by tendrils. It produces small white or purple flowers, which give way to pods containing the edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is widely used in various cuisines around the world.
