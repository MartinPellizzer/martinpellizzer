Potentilla verna f. oblongifolia

Potentilla verna f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the early potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically ovate to oblong in shape, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
