Pometia eximia

Pometia eximia, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to the mangosteen but have a different flavor profile. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a dense, rounded canopy.

Pometia eximia is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal properties.
