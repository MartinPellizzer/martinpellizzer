Phyllanthus atalaiensis

Phyllanthus atalaiensis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by various animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Phyllanthus atalaiensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in some cultures.
