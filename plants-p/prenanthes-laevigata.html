Prenanthes laevigata

Prenanthes laevigata, commonly known as smooth bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms. Its leaves are simple and often have a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms.
