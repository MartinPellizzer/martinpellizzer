Potentilla nitida var. grandiflora

Potentilla nitida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps reflect sunlight in its native habitat. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
