Peiranisia aristulata

Peiranisia aristulata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its flowers are often clustered in terminal panicles, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Peiranisia aristulata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
