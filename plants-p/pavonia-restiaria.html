Pavonia restiaria

Pavonia restiaria, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, upright stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaves and a preference for full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Pavonia restiaria is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
