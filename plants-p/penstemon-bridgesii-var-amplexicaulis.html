Penstemon bridgesii var. amplexicaulis

Penstemon bridgesii var. amplexicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
