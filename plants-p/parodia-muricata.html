Parodia muricata

Parodia muricata, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
