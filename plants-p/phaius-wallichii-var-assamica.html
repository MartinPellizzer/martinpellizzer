Phaius wallichii var. assamica

Phaius wallichii var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern regions of India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is often found in areas with high humidity and abundant moisture. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and the flowers are usually pale yellow to orange with a distinctive, elongated lip.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
