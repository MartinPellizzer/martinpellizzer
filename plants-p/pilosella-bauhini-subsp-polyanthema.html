Pilosella bauhini subsp. polyanthema

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

polyanthema, commonly known as the showy hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a prominent central disc.

This subspecies is notable for its showy appearance and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
