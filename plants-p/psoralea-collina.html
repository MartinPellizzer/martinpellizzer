Psoralea collina

Psoralea collina, commonly known as the narrowleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
