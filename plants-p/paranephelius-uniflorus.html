Paranephelius uniflorus

Paranephelius uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower cloud plant, is a rare and enigmatic species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white trichomes, giving it a cloud-like appearance. The plant produces a single, showy flower at the tip of each stem, which is typically pale blue or violet in color. Its growth habit is slow and it thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
