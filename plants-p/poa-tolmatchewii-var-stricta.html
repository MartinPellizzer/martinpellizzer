Poa tolmatchewii var. stricta

Poa tolmatchewii var. stricta, commonly known as the strict meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping projects.
