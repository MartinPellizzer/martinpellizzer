Pleurothallis schizopogon

Pleurothallis schizopogon, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to capture light in low-light conditions.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
