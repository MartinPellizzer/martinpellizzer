Prunus pseudocerasus spontanea

Prunus pseudocerasus spontanea, commonly known as the wild Chinese date or wild loquat, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
