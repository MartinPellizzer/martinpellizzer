Pyrus thaiszii

Pyrus thaiszii, commonly known as Thai pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and parts of Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces edible fruit similar to other pear species.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable climates.
