Pyrus bourgaeana

Pyrus bourgaeana, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small apples. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use in traditional medicine.
