Piper pseudoeucalyptifolium

Piper pseudoeucalyptifolium, commonly known as the false eucalyptus pepper, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of eucalyptus trees, hence its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by reddish berries.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
