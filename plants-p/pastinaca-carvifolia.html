Pastinaca carvifolia

Pastinaca carvifolia, commonly known as the curly fennel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
