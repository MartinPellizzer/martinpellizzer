Paracaryum salsum

Paracaryum salsum, commonly known as the salt marsh morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important species in salt marsh ecosystems. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Paracaryum salsum plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
