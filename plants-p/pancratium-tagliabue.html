Pancratium tagliabue

Pancratium tagliabue, commonly known as the yellow wild onion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically from June to September. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces small, white berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Pancratium tagliabue is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also be found in coastal and sandy environments.
