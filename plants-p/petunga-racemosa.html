Petunga racemosa

Petunga racemosa, commonly known as the raceme petunga, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.
