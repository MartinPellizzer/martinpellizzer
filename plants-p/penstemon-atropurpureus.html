Penstemon atropurpureus

Penstemon atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
