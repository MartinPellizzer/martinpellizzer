Purgosea pertusa

Purgosea pertusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Purgosea pertusa is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
