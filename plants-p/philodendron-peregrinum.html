Philodendron peregrinum

Philodendron peregrinum, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can climb using its aerial roots, often reaching lengths of several meters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
