Phymatodes nigrescens

Phymatodes nigrescens, commonly known as the Texas knapweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its deep root system, it is considered a persistent weed in agricultural and natural settings.
