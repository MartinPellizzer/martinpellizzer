Polygala nematocaulis

Polygala nematocaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
