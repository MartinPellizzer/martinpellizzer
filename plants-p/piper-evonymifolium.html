Piper evonymifolium

Piper evonymifolium, commonly known as the "Indian pepper" or "wild pepper," is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
