Polygala calcarea subsp. amara

Polygala calcarea subsp.

amara, commonly known as the bitter milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
