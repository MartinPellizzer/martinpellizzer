Pseudanthus ovalifolius

Pseudanthus ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf pseudanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are oval-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pseudanthus ovalifolius is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
