Paraphlomis rugosa var. angustifolia

Paraphlomis rugosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved parry sedge, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of spike-like clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
