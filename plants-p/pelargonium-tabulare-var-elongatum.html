Pelargonium tabulare var. elongatum

Pelargonium tabulare var. elongatum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges and a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
