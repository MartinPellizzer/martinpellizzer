Phalaenopsis amabilis var. grandiflora

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a tropical evergreen orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescence that bears multiple large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it is known for its ability to produce blooms for several months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining potting media.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
