Phalaris zizanioides

Phalaris zizanioides, commonly known as wild millet, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a potential weed in agricultural settings.
