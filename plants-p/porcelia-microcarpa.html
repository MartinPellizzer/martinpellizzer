Porcelia microcarpa

Porcelia microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf porcelia, is a small, flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms in shades of blue and purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance.

Porcelia microcarpa thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
