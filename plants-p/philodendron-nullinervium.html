Philodendron nullinervium

Philodendron nullinervium, commonly known as the spider plant or moonvine, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces long, trailing stems that bear small, pale green flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Philodendron nullinervium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and air-purifying properties.
