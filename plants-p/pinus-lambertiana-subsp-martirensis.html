Pinus lambertiana subsp. martirensis

Pinus lambertiana subsp.

martirensis, commonly known as the giant sequoia, is a subspecies of the Sierra redwood. It is native to the western United States, particularly in the Sierra Nevada mountain range.

This evergreen conifer is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and its ability to grow to immense heights and ages. Its needles are long and slender, and it produces both male and female cones.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and its role in supporting a diverse array of wildlife.
