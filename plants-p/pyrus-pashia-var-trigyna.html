Pyrus pashia var. trigyna

Pyrus pashia var. trigyna, commonly known as the Kashmir pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The variety is notable for its unique growth habit and adaptability to mountainous environments.
