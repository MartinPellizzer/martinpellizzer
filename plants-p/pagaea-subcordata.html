Pagaea subcordata

Pagaea subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pagaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly heart-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pagaea subcordata is known for its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in spring and early summer.
