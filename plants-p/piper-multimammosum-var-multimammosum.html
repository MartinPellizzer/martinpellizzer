Piper multimammosum var. multimammosum

Piper multimammosum var. multimammosum, commonly known as the Malabar pepper or wild long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered berries. The plant produces elongated, peppercorn-like fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaves, and a distinctive aromatic compound profile.

This species is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses in traditional systems.
