Puya tyleriana

Puya tyleriana, commonly known as the Tyler's torch, is a striking South American plant belonging to the bromeliad family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch, often reaching heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces vibrant red and orange bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. Native to the Andean region, it thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
