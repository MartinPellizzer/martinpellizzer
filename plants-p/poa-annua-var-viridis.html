Poa annua var. viridis

Poa annua var. viridis, commonly known as green annual bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lawns, golf courses, and disturbed areas. It has a short rhizome system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both a desirable turfgrass and a weed in agricultural settings.
