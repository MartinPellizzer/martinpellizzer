Pachypodium gracilius

Pachypodium gracilius, commonly known as the slender pachypodium, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and sparse, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its thick, fleshy stem stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Pachypodium gracilius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
