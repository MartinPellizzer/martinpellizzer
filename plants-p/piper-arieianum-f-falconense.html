Piper arieianum f. falconense

Piper arieianum f. falconense, commonly known as the Falconense pepper or a type of wild pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly pubescent surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in axillary clusters, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and berries, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
