Potentilla oweriniana

Potentilla oweriniana, commonly known as the Kashmiri cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
