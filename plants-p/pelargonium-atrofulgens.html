Pelargonium atrofulgens

Pelargonium atrofulgens, commonly known as the dark-flowered geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black flowers, which contrast strikingly with its green foliage. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are velvety and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge.

Pelargonium atrofulgens is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
