Pilasia

Pilasia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their attractive, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Pilasia species are typically herbaceous perennials, often grown as houseplants due to their preference for warm, humid conditions. They thrive in well-draining soil and require indirect sunlight to prevent leaf scorch.

The genus includes several species, such as Pilasia lutea and Pilasia natalensis, which are popular for their ornamental value.
