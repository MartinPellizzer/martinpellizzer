Physalis suberifera

Physalis suberifera, commonly known as the woolly groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that persists on the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens.
