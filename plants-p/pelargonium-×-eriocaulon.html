Pelargonium × eriocaulon

Pelargonium × eriocaulon, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble those of ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming throughout the summer. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ability to thrive in partial shade and its tolerance to various soil types make it a popular choice for gardeners.
