Pultenaea paleacea var. williamsonii

Pultenaea paleacea var. williamsonii, commonly known as the pale everlasting or William's pultenaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It produces small, pale yellow to cream flowers, which are followed by narrow, ribbed seed pods.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
