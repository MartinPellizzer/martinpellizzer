Phebalium squameum subsp. retusum

Phebalium squameum subsp.

retusum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow floral spikes that appear in spring.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The subspecies retusum is distinguished by its more rounded leaf tips compared to other subspecies of Phebalium squameum.
