Podandrogyne macrophylla

Podandrogyne macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating platform for various organisms. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its roots system helps stabilize the sediment and improve water quality by filtering pollutants.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in aquatic ecosystems.
