Phaseolus marechalii

Phaseolus marechalii, commonly known as the wild kidney bean, is a perennial legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks. Its seeds are used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting the soil ecosystem.
