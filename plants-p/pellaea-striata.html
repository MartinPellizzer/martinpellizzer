Pellaea striata

Pellaea striata, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped fronds. The plant has a compact, rosette-like growth form with small, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Pellaea striata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
