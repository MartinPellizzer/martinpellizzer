Polycnemum arvense var. simplex

Polycnemum arvense var. simplex, commonly known as field sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has triangular stems with a smooth surface and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spikes. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a dense rosette at the base.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and its role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
