Piper boqueronense

Piper boqueronense, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces small, green berries that turn dark when mature, containing a spicy compound similar to capsaicin. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Piper boqueronense make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
