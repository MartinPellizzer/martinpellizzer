Potentilla subpalmata

Potentilla subpalmata, commonly known as the subpalmate cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves, which are typically five-lobed and have a distinctive, almost palm-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
