Phyllocactus thomasianus

Phyllocactus thomasianus, commonly known as the Thomasian pincushion cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly, and spiny stems that resemble a pincushion, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to dry climates.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
