Physalis ochroleuca

Physalis ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-flowered ground cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that enclose a cluster of seeds, similar to other Physalis species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The flowers are typically yellow with a distinctive red calyx that persists after the fruit matures.
