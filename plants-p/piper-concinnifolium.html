Piper concinnifolium

Piper concinnifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
