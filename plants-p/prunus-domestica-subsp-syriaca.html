Prunus domestica subsp. syriaca

Prunus domestica subsp.

syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian cherry, is a subspecies of the domesticated cherry tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits, which are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
