Pisum sativum var. ponderosum

Pisum sativum var. ponderosum, commonly known as the giant edible pea, is a cultivated variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its large, plump pods and robust growth habit, often reaching heights of several meters. The plant produces edible seeds that are typically harvested when the pods are fully mature and the seeds are firm. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and adequate sunlight.

This variety is valued for its high yield and nutritional content, making it a popular choice for both home gardens and commercial cultivation.
