Pentapanax elegans var. pubescens

Pentapanax elegans var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pentapanax, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its distinctive, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy upper surface and a hairy underside, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
