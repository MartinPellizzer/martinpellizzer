Persicaria arifolia

Persicaria arifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf hogweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Persicaria arifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can spread rapidly through its rhizomes.
