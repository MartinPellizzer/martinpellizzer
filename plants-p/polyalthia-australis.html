Polyalthia australis

Polyalthia australis, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in tropical and subtropical regions.
