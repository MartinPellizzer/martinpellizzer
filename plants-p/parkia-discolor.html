Parkia discolor

Parkia discolor, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has bipinnate leaves and produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enhances soil fertility.
