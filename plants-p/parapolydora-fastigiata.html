Parapolydora fastigiata

Parapolydora fastigiata, commonly known as the tall rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It typically grows in dense clumps, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with long, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in moist soils.

Parapolydora fastigiata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
