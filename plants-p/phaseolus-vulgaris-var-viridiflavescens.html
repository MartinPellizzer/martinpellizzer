Phaseolus vulgaris var. viridiflavescens

Phaseolus vulgaris var. viridiflavescens, commonly known as the green yellow bean, is a variety of common bean characterized by its unique foliage and pod coloration.

This plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to the Americas. Its leaves are typically a mix of green and yellow, giving it a striking appearance. The pods are usually elongated and contain small, edible seeds that are used in various cuisines.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
