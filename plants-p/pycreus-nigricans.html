Pycreus nigricans

Pycreus nigricans, commonly known as the black velvet fern, is a unique plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that appears almost black due to its high concentration of anthocyanin pigments. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming low, cushion-like structures that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. Its leaves are small, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, which aids in reducing water loss.

Despite its striking appearance, Pycreus nigricans is relatively obscure in botanical studies and remains a subject of interest for its adaptive features.
