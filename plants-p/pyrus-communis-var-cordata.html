Pyrus communis var. cordata

Pyrus communis var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet fruit flavor.
