Paspalum mollifolium

Paspalum mollifolium, commonly known as softleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, soft foliage and tufted growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and disturbed sites.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions contributes to its widespread distribution.
