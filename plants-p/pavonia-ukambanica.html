Pavonia ukambanica

Pavonia ukambanica, commonly known as the Kenyan rose or red pavonia, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its flowers are tubular and are often visited by birds and insects, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
