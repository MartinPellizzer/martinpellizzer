Panicum tectum

Panicum tectum, commonly known as little barley, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Panicum tectum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
