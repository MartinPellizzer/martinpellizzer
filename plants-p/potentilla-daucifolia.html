Potentilla daucifolia

Potentilla daucifolia, commonly known as the meadow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Potentilla daucifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and early spring blooms.
