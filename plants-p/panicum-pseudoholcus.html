Panicum pseudoholcus

Panicum pseudoholcus, commonly known as false hawksbeard, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often mistaken for true grasses. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a weed.
