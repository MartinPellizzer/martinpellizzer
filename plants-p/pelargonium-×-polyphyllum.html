Pelargonium × polyphyllum

Pelargonium × polyphyllum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for hanging baskets or containers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
