Prosthechea mariae

Prosthechea mariae, commonly known as the Marie's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structures. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white to pinkish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water retention in its native habitat.

Prosthechea mariae is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
