Phoenix × hybrida

Phoenix × hybrida, commonly known as the date palm hybrid, is a cultivated variety resulting from the crossbreeding of Phoenix dactylifera and Phoenix roebelenii.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green color. The plant produces edible fruits known as dates, which are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, chewy texture. It thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and agricultural significance in date production.
