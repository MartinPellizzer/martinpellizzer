Potamogeton rufescens var. alpinus

Potamogeton rufescens var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbed and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is adapted to cooler climates and is often found in high-altitude or northern regions.
