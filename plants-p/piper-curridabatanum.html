Piper curridabatanum

Piper curridabatanum, commonly known as the Curridabat pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal value.

The leaves are also used in local herbal remedies due to their pungent and stimulating properties.
