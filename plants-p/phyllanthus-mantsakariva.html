Phyllanthus mantsakariva

Phyllanthus mantsakariva, commonly known as the African starleaf, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, forested areas and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an adaptable and resilient plant.
