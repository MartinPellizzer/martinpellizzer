Paspalum ulei

Paspalum ulei, commonly known as the coastal paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and is often found growing in sandy or coastal soils. The plant has a low-growing habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and is often used in coastal restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important species for stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
