Pinus laricio var. monspeliensis

Pinus laricio var. monspeliensis, commonly known as the Corsican fir, is a variety of the Swiss stone pine.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southern Alps and parts of Corsica. This plant has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
