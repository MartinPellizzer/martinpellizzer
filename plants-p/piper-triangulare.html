Piper triangulare

Piper triangulare, commonly known as the three-angled pepper or triangular pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical research.
