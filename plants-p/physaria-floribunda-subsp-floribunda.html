Physaria floribunda subsp. floribunda

Physaria floribunda subsp.

floribunda, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow, four-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy stems and leaves that help reduce water loss.

The species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
