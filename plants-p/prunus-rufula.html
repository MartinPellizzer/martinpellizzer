Prunus rufula

Prunus rufula, commonly known as the redleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark red berries. Prunus rufula is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and seasonal blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
