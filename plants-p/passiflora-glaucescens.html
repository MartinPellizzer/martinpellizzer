Passiflora glaucescens

Passiflora glaucescens, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, bluish-green surface. Its fruit, which resembles a small orange, is edible and contains a sweet, pulpy interior.

Passiflora glaucescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
