Pyrus demetrii

Pyrus demetrii, commonly known as the Demetrius pear, is a species of tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is typically small, yellow, and edible, though it is not widely cultivated for commercial use. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Its historical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many modern pear varieties.
