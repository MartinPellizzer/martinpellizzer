Potameia resonjo

Potameia resonjo, commonly known as the resonant bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue petals that emit a subtle, melodic sound when gently disturbed. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Potameia resonjo is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its unique acoustic properties, making it a subject of scientific and botanical interest.
