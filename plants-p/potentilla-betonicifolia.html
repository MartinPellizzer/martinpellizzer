Potentilla betonicifolia

Potentilla betonicifolia, commonly known as the betony leaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in moist, calcareous soils.

Potentilla betonicifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
