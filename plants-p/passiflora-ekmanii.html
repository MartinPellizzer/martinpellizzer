Passiflora ekmanii

Passiflora ekmanii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, blue to purple berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora ekmanii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
