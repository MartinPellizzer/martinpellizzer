Prunus japonica var. thunbergii

Prunus japonica var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn and its clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is well-suited to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks due to its aesthetic value.

The fruit, a small, hard cherry, is not typically consumed by humans but is used in some traditional crafts.
