Pseudosolisia valdeziana

Pseudosolisia valdeziana, commonly known as the Valdez's ghostplant, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Pteridophyta division.

It is a small, terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a pale green to bluish hue, often covered in a fine layer of trichomes. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered along the margins of the fronds, aiding in spore dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
