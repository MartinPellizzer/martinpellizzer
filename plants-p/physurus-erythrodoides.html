Physurus erythrodoides

Physurus erythrodoides, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with reddish veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.
