Primulina gigantea

Primulina gigantea, commonly known as the giant primula, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in greenhouses and tropical gardens worldwide.
