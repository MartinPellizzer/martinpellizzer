Pseudolobivia pelecyrhachis

Pseudolobivia pelecyrhachis, commonly known as the spiny lobivia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its spiny structure provides protection against herbivores and helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Pseudolobivia pelecyrhachis is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and fascinating example of desert flora.
