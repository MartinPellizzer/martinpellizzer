Primula sieberi

Primula sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Primula sieberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
