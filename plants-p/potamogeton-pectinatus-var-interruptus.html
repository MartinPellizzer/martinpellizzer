Potamogeton pectinatus var. interruptus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and Asia.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like structures. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often used as an indicator of water quality in wetland and lake environments.
