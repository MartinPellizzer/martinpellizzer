Papaver radicatum var. typicum

Papaver radicatum var. typicum, commonly known as the common poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a distinctive, thick, fleshy root system that helps it survive in various soil conditions. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
