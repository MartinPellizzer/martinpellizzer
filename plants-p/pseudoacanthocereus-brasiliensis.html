Pseudoacanthocereus brasiliensis

Pseudoacanthocereus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and produces large, fragrant white flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has spines for protection. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.

The flowers open in response to cooler temperatures and moonlight, making it a unique addition to desert gardens.
