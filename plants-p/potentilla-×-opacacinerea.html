Potentilla × opacacinerea

Potentilla × opacacinerea, commonly known as the silver-leaf potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
