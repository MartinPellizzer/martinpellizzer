Paeonia officinalis var. canescens

Paeonia officinalis var. canescens, commonly known as the white peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its rhizomatous root system is notable for its medicinal properties, which have been utilized in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
