Pteris coriifolia

Pteris coriifolia, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Indonesia. The plant has pinnately divided leaves with a distinctive, finely serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
