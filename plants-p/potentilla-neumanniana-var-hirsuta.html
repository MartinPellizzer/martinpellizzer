Potentilla neumanniana var. hirsuta

Potentilla neumanniana var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which give it its varietal name, "hirsuta," meaning hairy in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
