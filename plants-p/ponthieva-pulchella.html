Ponthieva pulchella

Ponthieva pulchella, commonly known as the pink spider orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features intricate, spider-like flowers with vibrant pink and white petals, often adorned with dark veining. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on its aerial roots for moisture and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Ponthieva pulchella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
