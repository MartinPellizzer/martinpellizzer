Psephellus incanescens

Psephellus incanescens, commonly known as the white sand cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in a layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a powdery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its ability to store water in its thick stems allows it to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
