Penstemon lacerellus

Penstemon lacerellus, commonly known as the redstem penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon lacerellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
