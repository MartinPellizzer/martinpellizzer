Plantago brasiliensis var. flavidohirsuta

Plantago brasiliensis var. flavidohirsuta, commonly known as the golden-hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a rosette of broad, fleshy, lance-shaped leaves covered in a dense layer of fine, yellowish hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its hairy leaves and adaptability to humid conditions make it a distinctive member of the Plantaginaceae family.
