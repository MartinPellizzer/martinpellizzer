Phoenix dactylifera var. cylindrocarpa

Phoenix dactylifera var. cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a variety of the date palm native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, feather-like fronds that create a distinctive canopy. The plant produces clusters of date fruits, which are typically smaller and more elongated compared to the common date palm. The fruit has a sweet, fibrous texture and is rich in sugars, making it a popular food source.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its edible fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
