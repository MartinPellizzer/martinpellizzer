Polemonium samojedorum

Polemonium samojedorum, commonly known as the Siberian Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a zigzag pattern along the stem, giving it the common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
