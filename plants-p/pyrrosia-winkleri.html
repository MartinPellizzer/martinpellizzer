Pyrrosia winkleri

Pyrrosia winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are released from small, round sorus structures on the underside of its leaves.

Pyrrosia winkleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
