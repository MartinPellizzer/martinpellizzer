Phylica imberbis var. eriophoros

Phylica imberbis var. eriophoros, commonly known as the coastal phylica, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion.
