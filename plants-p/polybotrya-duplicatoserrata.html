Polybotrya duplicatoserrata

Polybotrya duplicatoserrata, commonly known as the double-toothed fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate fronds with a distinctive double-toothed margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental foliage.

The plant's reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds, typical of many fern species.
