Piper racemosum

Piper racemosum, commonly known as the creeping Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a vine native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clustered, peppery fruits. Its botanical structure is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of glandular hairs on the stems and leaves.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
