Potentilla gaudinii var. virescens

Potentilla gaudinii var. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a distinctive glaucous or bluish-green hue. This variety is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued in gardens for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
