Pithecellobium × bahamense

Pithecellobium × bahamense, commonly known as the Bahamian burr legume, is a hybrid plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a small, shrub-like species characterized by its dense, thorny growth and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, woody pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
