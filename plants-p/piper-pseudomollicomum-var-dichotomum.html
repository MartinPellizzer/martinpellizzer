Piper pseudomollicomum var. dichotomum

Piper pseudomollicomum var. dichotomum, commonly known as the Yucatan pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to southern Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that mature to a dark purple color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
