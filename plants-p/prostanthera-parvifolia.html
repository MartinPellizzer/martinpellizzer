Prostanthera parvifolia

Prostanthera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional Indigenous medicine.

Prostanthera parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
