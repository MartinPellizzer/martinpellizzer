Pisum sativum var. serratum

Pisum sativum var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated pea, is a variant of the common pea that exhibits distinctive serrated leaf margins.

It is a climbing legume native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are typically harvested for culinary use. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its nutritional content.
