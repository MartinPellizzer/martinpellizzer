Podocarpus pallidus

Podocarpus pallidus, commonly known as the silver podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical cones and needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The silver podocarp is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
