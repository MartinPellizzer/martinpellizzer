Poa hirtiglumis var. nimuana

Poa hirtiglumis var. nimuana, commonly known as the Nimuana tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically covered in short, stiff hairs. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that are often concealed within the dense foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
