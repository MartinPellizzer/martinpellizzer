Procris sesquifolia

Procris sesquifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a striking display of purple to pink hues.

Procris sesquifolia is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
