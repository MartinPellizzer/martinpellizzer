Primula sieboldii

Primula sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan and Korea.

It features rosettes of lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adding a delicate touch to gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
