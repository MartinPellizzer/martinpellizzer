Passiflora ecuadorica

Passiflora ecuadorica, commonly known as the Ecuador passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora ecuadorica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological and medicinal significance.
