Pyrus cucullifera

Pyrus cucullifera, commonly known as the hooded pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense, hairy layer on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that resemble small pears. Its fruit is valued for its flavor and is used in local cuisines.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
