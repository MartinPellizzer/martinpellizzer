Pharbitis bicolor

Pharbitis bicolor, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-colored flowers, typically featuring a white or light-colored base and a blue or purple upper portion. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its vines can grow rapidly, climbing structures with a twining habit.

The leaves are heart-shaped, and the plant produces showy flowers that open in the morning and close by afternoon.
