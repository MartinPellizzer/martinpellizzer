Plantago ecuadorensis var. minor

Plantago ecuadorensis var. minor, commonly known as the Ecuadorian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
