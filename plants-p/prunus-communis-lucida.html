Prunus communis lucida

Prunus communis lucida, commonly known as the shining cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, hard fruits called drupes, which are typically red or black when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
