Phaca mortonii

Phaca mortonii, commonly known as the Morton's phaca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open, sunny habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are typically white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

Phaca mortonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
