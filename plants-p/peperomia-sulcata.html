Peperomia sulcata

Peperomia sulcata, commonly known as the Ripple Peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves with a distinctive rippled or wavy texture, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically dark green with a silvery or white underside, creating a striking visual contrast. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in indirect light and minimal water requirements.
