Philadelphus coronarius var. primuliflorus-plenus

Philadelphus coronarius var. primuliflorus-plenus, commonly known as the double-flowered tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, double blooms that appear in early summer, often in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces showy, rounded inflorescences that are highly ornamental and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features include opposite, pinnately compound leaves and a woody structure that supports its long lifespan.
