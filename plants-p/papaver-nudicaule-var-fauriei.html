Papaver nudicaule var. fauriei

Papaver nudicaule var. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's poppy, is a variant of the meadow poppy, characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The flowers are usually pink to rose-colored, with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corolla. The plant features a hairy stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

It is native to Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
