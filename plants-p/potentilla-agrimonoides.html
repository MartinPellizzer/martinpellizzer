Potentilla agrimonoides

Potentilla agrimonoides, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has palmately divided leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla agrimonoides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
