Parodia crassigibba

Parodia crassigibba, commonly known as the football cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Parodia crassigibba is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its compact size and unique appearance.
