Primula neurocalyx

Primula neurocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
