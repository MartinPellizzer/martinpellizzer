Papaver nudicaule f. albiflorum

Papaver nudicaule f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, papery white petals that contrast with its green, lobed leaves. The plant produces a striking, upright stem with clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a subtle, sweet fragrance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its unique appearance.
