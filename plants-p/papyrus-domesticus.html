Papyrus domesticus

Papyrus domesticus, commonly known as the giant papyrus, is a tall, reed-like plant native to the Nile Delta in Egypt.

It has long, slender stems that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with broad, flat leaves that are often used for papermaking. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and requires a lot of water to flourish. Its rhizomes are fibrous and were historically harvested for their durable fibers.

Papyrus domesticus played a significant role in ancient Egyptian culture and technology.
