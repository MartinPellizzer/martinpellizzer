Polygonum podocephalum

Polygonum podocephalum, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Polygonum podocephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
