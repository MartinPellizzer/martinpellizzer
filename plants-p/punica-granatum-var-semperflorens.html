Punica granatum var. semperflorens

Punica granatum var. semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming pomegranate, is a cultivar of the pomegranate plant characterized by its continuous flowering throughout the growing season.

It produces vibrant red flowers that resemble those of the typical pomegranate, though it rarely sets fruit. The plant is a compact shrub with glossy, dark green leaves and a bushy growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms.

This variety is well-suited for container gardening and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 9 through 11.
