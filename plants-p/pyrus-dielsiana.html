Pyrus dielsiana

Pyrus dielsiana, commonly known as the Diels' pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits resembling small pears. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Pyrus dielsiana is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
