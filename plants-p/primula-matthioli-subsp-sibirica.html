Primula matthioli subsp. sibirica

Primula matthioli subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
