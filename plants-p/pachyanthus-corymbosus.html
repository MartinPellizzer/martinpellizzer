Pachyanthus corymbosus

Pachyanthus corymbosus, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the holiday season. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pachyanthus corymbosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and festive appearance.
