Paspalum interruptum

Paspalum interruptum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common species in ecological restoration projects.
