Perymenium pellitum

Perymenium pellitum, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its water-storing tissues, it is well adapted to drought conditions.
