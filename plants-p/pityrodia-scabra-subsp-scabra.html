Pityrodia scabra subsp. scabra

Pityrodia scabra subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough pittosporum, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its rough, scaly bark and dense, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
