Pera bahiana

Pera bahiana, commonly known as the Bahian yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Pera bahiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
