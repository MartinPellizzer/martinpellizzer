Pulsatilla montana var. lindtneri

Pulsatilla montana var. lindtneri, commonly known as the Lindtner's pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms of pale yellow to cream-colored petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a single, erect stem with a crown of bracts beneath the flower.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
