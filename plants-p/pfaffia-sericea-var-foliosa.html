Pfaffia sericea var. foliosa

Pfaffia sericea var. foliosa, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it is of interest in ethnobotanical and pharmacological research.
