Pterocarpus zollingeri

Pterocarpus zollingeri, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood that is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod containing flat, winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal.

The tree is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant species in its ecosystem.
