Pleurothallis felis-lingua

Pleurothallis felis-lingua, commonly known as the "Cat Tongue Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually elongated and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
