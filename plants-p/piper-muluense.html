Piper muluense

Piper muluense, commonly known as the Mulu pepper or Mulu long pepper, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are ovate and have a glossy texture, while the stems are woody and aromatic.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to high-altitude regions.
