Polygonum parksii

Polygonum parksii, commonly known as the Mexican knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Polygonum parksii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
