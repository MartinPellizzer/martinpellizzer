Psophocarpus lukafuensis

Psophocarpus lukafuensis, commonly known as the Lukafu climbing bean, is a tropical vine native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces striking purple flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its climbing habit and rapid growth make it suitable for ornamental and agricultural use.

The species is notable for its potential nutritional value and adaptability to humid, tropical environments.
