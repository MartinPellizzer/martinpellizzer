Penteca

Penteca, commonly known as the five-leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant typically has five leaves arranged in a whorl around the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Penteca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
