Perdicium viscosum

Perdicium viscosum, commonly known as the sticky partridge grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a sticky, waxy substance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences, followed by small, dry fruits known as achenes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along forest edges.

Due to its sticky secretions, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
