Pandanus inundatus

Pandanus inundatus, commonly known as the water pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into three-lobed fruits. It thrives in wet, coastal environments and is adapted to salt spray.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various island species.
