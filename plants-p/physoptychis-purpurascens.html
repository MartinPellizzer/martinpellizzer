Physoptychis purpurascens

Physoptychis purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple veins on its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a slight purple tint. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
