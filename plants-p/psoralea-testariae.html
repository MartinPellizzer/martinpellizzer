Psoralea testariae

Psoralea testariae, commonly known as the African false indigo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are often violet or blue in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Psoralea testariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
