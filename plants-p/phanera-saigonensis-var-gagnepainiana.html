Phanera saigonensis var. gagnepainiana

Phanera saigonensis var. gagnepainiana, commonly known as the Saigon phanera, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
