Pseudotsuga sinensis subsp. gaussenii

Pseudotsuga sinensis subsp.

gaussenii, commonly known as the Gaussen's Chinese fir, is a subspecies of the Chinese fir native to high-altitude regions of China. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The plant exhibits a distinct morphology with a conical shape and smooth, gray bark. It thrives in temperate climates with moderate rainfall and well-drained soils.

Botanically, it is notable for its ecological role in forest ecosystems and its potential for ornamental and timber use.
