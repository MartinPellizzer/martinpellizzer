Ptychosperma appendiculatum

Ptychosperma appendiculatum, commonly known as the coconut palm, is a tall, slender tree native to tropical regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and distinctive, three-lobed fruits that contain a fibrous husk and a sweet, oily liquid. The plant is well-adapted to coastal environments, with a robust root system that helps stabilize sandy soils. Its versatility makes it an important economic and ecological resource in many island communities.

The coconut palm has been cultivated for thousands of years for its fruit, oil, and fiber.
