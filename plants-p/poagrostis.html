Poagrostis

Poagrostis, commonly known as bluegrass, is a genus of grasses that includes several species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

These plants are characterized by their fine, dark green blades and often form dense, sod-like growth patterns. Many species of Poagrostis are valued for their ornamental appeal and are used in lawns and turfgrass applications. They typically thrive in well-drained soils and require moderate moisture and sunlight.

The genus is also important in ecological contexts, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
