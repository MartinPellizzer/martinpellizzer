Phyllocactus biformis

Phyllocactus biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed cactus, is a unique species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, ribbed stems that resemble a leaf-like structure. The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its unusual morphology, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
