Pilea puncticaulis

Pilea puncticaulis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial that originates from China.

It features round, glossy, waxy leaves that are slightly notched at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to produce plantlets on its stems adds to its ornamental value and ease of propagation.
