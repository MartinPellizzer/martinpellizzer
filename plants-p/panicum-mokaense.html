Panicum mokaense

Panicum mokaense, commonly known as Moka grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and erosion control.
