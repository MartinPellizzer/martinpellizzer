Piper myrtifolium

Piper myrtifolium, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries. The plant produces a spicy compound similar to capsaicin, giving it its characteristic heat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and culinary applications.
