Pelargonium athamanthoides

Pelargonium athamanthoides, commonly known as the desert geranium, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers during the winter months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Its ability to survive in harsh conditions makes it well-suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
