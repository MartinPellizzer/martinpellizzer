Piper davaoense

Piper davaoense, commonly known as Davao pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its slender, climbing vines that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aroma.

Piper davaoense is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its pungent flavor.
