Plectranthus punctatus subsp. edulis

Plectranthus punctatus subsp.

edulis, commonly known as the edible plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves that emit a strong mint-like scent when crushed.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
