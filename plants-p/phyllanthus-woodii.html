Phyllanthus woodii

Phyllanthus woodii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments due to its drought-resistant properties.

The plant is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural rubber.
