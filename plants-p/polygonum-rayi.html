Polygonum rayi

Polygonum rayi, commonly known as Ray's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its swollen nodes and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences, often pink or red in color.

The stems are often reddish and have a distinctive, knotted appearance.
