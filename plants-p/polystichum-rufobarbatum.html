Polystichum rufobarbatum

Polystichum rufobarbatum, commonly known as the red-haired fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are often tinged with red or brown at the base, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, which are clustered in compact, upright clusters.

Polystichum rufobarbatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and hardy nature.
