Phlox albomarginata

Phlox albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink to rose-purple with white margins. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Phlox albomarginata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
