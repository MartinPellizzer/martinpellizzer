Panicum insculptum

Panicum insculptum, commonly known as the sculpted grass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricately patterned leaves that give it a unique visual appeal. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its slender, erect stems can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and it produces small, fluffy seed heads that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
