Polygala arillata var. ovata

Polygala arillata var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the fruit is a capsule that disperses seeds by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
