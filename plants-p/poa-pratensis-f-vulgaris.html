Poa pratensis f. vulgaris

Poa pratensis f. vulgaris, commonly known as the common meadow grass, is a variant of the tall fescue species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and pastures. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
