Prunus × lannesiana f. shujaku

Prunus × lannesiana f. shujaku, commonly known as the Shujaku cherry, is a unique flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus serrulata, characterized by its distinctive double flowers. The plant is known for its ornamental value, particularly in Japanese gardens, where it is prized for its delicate pink and white blooms. It typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
