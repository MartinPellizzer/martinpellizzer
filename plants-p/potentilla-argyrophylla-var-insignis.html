Potentilla argyrophylla var. insignis

Potentilla argyrophylla var. insignis, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
