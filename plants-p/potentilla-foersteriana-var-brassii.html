Potentilla foersteriana var. brassii

Potentilla foersteriana var. brassii, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and hardy nature.
