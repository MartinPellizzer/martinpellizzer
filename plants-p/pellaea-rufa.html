Pellaea rufa

Pellaea rufa, commonly known as the rusty fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Pellaea rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
