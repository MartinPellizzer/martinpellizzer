Potentilla nelsoniana

Potentilla nelsoniana, commonly known as Nelson's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It produces small, five-lobed flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla nelsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
