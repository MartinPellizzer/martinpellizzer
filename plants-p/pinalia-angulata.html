Pinalia angulata

Pinalia angulata, commonly known as the angular pinalia, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
