Pleurothyrium amplifolium

Pleurothyrium amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pleurothyrium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
