Phlox carolina var. nitida

Phlox carolina var. nitida, commonly known as the Carolina hedgephylla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and its clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
