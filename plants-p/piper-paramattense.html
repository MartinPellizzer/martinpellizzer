Piper paramattense

Piper paramattense, commonly known as the Paramatta pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.

Piper paramattense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
