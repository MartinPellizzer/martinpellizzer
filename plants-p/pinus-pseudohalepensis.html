Pinus pseudohalepensis

Pinus pseudohalepensis, commonly known as the Aleppo pine, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that grow in bundles of five, and its cones are typically reddish-brown and woody. The tree can reach heights of up to 30 meters and has a conical or irregular crown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The Aleppo pine plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
