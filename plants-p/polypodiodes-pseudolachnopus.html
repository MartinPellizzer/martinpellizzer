Polypodiodes pseudolachnopus

Polypodiodes pseudolachnopus, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a small, low-growing fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, velvety fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a fine, soft layer of spores, which aid in its reproductive cycle.

The fronds are usually pinnately divided, with a distinctive appearance that helps in its identification.
