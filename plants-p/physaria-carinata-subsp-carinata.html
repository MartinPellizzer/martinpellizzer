Physaria carinata subsp. carinata

Physaria carinata subsp.

carinata, commonly known as the beakspur, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinctive ridge or carina, giving it its scientific name.

It is a perennial herb that produces showy inflorescences in the spring.
