Passiflora parritae

Passiflora parritae, commonly known as the Parritae passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.

Passiflora parritae thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
