Pilosella densiflora subsp. neilreichii

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

neilreichii, commonly known as the denseflower hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of stiff, hairy leaves and produces dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its inflorescence is composed of numerous disc flowers surrounded by a single row of ray florets.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in high-altitude habitats where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
