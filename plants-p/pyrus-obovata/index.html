Pyrus Obovata

Pyrus obovata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia. It is prized for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which has a sweet and delicate flavor. The tree features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to various climates and soils. This plant is a popular choice for gardens and landscapes due to its beauty and versatility.
