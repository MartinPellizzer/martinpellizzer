Pithecellobium adianthifolium var. multipinnum

Pithecellobium adianthifolium var. multipinnum, commonly known as the hairy pod sycamore, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with numerous small, elliptical leaflets and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
