Piper pseudoaduncum

Piper pseudoaduncum, commonly known as the false black pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by three-lobed berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
