Primula pauciflora var. macrocarpa

Primula pauciflora var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features a rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that are basal and tightly clustered. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
