Physodium corymbosum

Physodium corymbosum, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, and its flowering stems bear small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in corymbs. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system, which contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Physodium corymbosum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
