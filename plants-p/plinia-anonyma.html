Plinia anonyma

Plinia anonyma, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a pleasant aromatic scent. Its flowers are white and clustered, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

The berries are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine due to their nutritional and medicinal properties.
