Persicaria dichotoma var. assamica

Persicaria dichotoma var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam knotgrass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayan region.

It features slender, erect stems that often branch into two, giving it its species name "dichotoma.". The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
