Phyteuma otites

Phyteuma otites, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a yellow central disk. The plant typically grows in meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phyteuma otites is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
