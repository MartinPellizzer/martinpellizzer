Pilosella cochlearis subsp. pseudauricula

Pilosella cochlearis subsp.

pseudauricula, commonly known as the false earleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, with the lower leaves often having a distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) base.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its role in pollination and ecological interactions.
