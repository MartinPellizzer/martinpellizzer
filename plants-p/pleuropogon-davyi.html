Pleuropogon davyi

Pleuropogon davyi, commonly known as the Texas panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Pleuropogon davyi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
