Phyllanthus fuscoluridus var. borealis

Phyllanthus fuscoluridus var. borealis, commonly known as the northern phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is native to the temperate regions of North America and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
