Passiflora baraquiniana

Passiflora baraquiniana, commonly known as the Baraquin passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate floral structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive organs. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that are pollinated by various insects and birds. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

The fruit, which develops from the flower, is edible and has a sweet, tropical flavor.
