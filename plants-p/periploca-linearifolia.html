Periploca linearifolia

Periploca linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved periploca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats and is known for its milky sap. It produces small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.
