Polemonium pulcherrimum var. pilosum

Polemonium pulcherrimum var. pilosum, commonly known as the silky bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
