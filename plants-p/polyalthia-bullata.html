Polyalthia bullata

Polyalthia bullata, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with slender, elongated leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often marked with irregular lichen growth.

Polyalthia bullata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its showy flowers and rapid growth rate.
