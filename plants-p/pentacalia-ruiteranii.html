Pentacalia ruiteranii

Pentacalia ruiteranii, commonly known as the Ruiter's pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Pentacalia ruiteranii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
