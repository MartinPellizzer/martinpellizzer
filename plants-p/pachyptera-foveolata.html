Pachyptera foveolata

Pachyptera foveolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock cress, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky environments in Australia.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, four-petaled white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in crevices or on rocky outcrops, where it forms dense mats.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
