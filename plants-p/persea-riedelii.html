Persea riedelii

Persea riedelii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Persea riedelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses in traditional Mexican cuisine.
