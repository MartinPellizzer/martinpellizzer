Pseudotsuga taxifolia subsp. glaucescens

Pseudotsuga taxifolia subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving the tree a distinctive appearance.

The cones are small and cylindrical, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. This subspecies is adapted to cool, moist climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban environments.
