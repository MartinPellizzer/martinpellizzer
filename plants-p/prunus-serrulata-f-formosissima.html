Prunus serrulata f. formosissima

Prunus serrulata f. formosissima, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or Yamazakura, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant is native to Japan and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Its flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also play a significant role in cultural celebrations, particularly during hanami, the cherry blossom viewing festival.
