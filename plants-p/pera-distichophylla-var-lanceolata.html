Pera distichophylla var. lanceolata

Pera distichophylla var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate pera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming during the spring and early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy textures, which help in reducing water loss in its natural habitat.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.
