Pouzolzia procridioides var. glabra

Pouzolzia procridioides var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth pouzolzia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
