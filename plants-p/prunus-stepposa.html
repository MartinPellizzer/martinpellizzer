Prunus stepposa

Prunus stepposa, commonly known as the steppe cherry, is a small shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Prunus stepposa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
