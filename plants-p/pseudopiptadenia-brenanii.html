Pseudopiptadenia brenanii

Pseudopiptadenia brenanii, commonly known as the Brazilian wisteria, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features pinnate leaves with 10 to 20 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or pink. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
