Pilocereus ghiesbrechtii

Pilocereus ghiesbrechtii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and often brightly colored stems that resemble a moon, hence its common name. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its fleshy stems, which allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Its botanical structure makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
