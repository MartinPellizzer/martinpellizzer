Phaeneilema multiscaposum

Phaeneilema multiscaposum, commonly known as the slender-leafed phaeneilema, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
