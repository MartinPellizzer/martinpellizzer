Potentilla bondonis

Potentilla bondonis, commonly known as the Bondon's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla bondonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
