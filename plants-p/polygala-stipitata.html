Polygala stipitata

Polygala stipitata, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala stipitata is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
