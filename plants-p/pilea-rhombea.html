Pilea rhombea

Pilea rhombea, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features round, glossy, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by pale green berries. It thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its ability to propagate through stem cuttings adds to its appeal among indoor gardeners.
