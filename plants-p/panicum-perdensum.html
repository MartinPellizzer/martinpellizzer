Panicum perdensum

Panicum perdensum, commonly known as the wandering grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed habitats.

Due to its adaptability, Panicum perdensum is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
