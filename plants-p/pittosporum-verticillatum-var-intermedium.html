Pittosporum verticillatum var. intermedium

Pittosporum verticillatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the variable pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
