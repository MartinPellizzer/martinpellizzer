Polypodium geminatum

Polypodium geminatum, commonly known as the twin fern, is a species of polypody found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a small, evergreen fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid environments. The plant has a distinctive frond structure with two lobes that resemble a pair of wings, giving it its common name. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
