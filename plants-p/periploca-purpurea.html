Periploca purpurea

Periploca purpurea, commonly known as the purple periploca, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
