Phalaris americana var. picta

Phalaris americana var. picta, commonly known as the painted false brome, is a perennial grass native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and distinctive blue-green foliage that often has a bluish sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue or purple flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique, low-maintenance plant.
