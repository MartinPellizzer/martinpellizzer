Polyalthia jucunda

Polyalthia jucunda, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, candle-like flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree has a dense canopy with elliptical, dark green leaves that provide ample shade. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, often marked with fissures.

Polyalthia jucunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a street tree due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm climates.
