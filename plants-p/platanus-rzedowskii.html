Platanus rzedowskii

Platanus rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Platanaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces distinctive spherical fruits that contain a single seed, which is dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Platanus rzedowskii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and lowland forests.
