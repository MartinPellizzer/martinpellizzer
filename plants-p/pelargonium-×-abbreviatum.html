Pelargonium × abbreviatum

Pelargonium × abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a compact, trailing plant native to South Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of five-petaled, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant is well-suited for container gardening and hanging baskets due to its trailing growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium × abbreviatum is popular for its ornamental value and ease of care in both indoor and outdoor settings.
