Pyrrocoma turbinella

Pyrrocoma turbinella, commonly known as the turban shell, is a species of sea snail found in marine environments.

It belongs to the family Turbinellidae and is characterized by its distinctive, turban-shaped shell. The shell is typically spiral and has a thick, often calcareous structure that provides protection. The plant, though not a true plant, is a mollusk and plays a role in marine ecosystems.

Its presence is often indicative of healthy coastal habitats.
