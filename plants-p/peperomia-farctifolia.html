Peperomia farctifolia

Peperomia farctifolia, commonly known as the Fat-leaf Peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, often hidden among the foliage. Its unique leaf structure makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Peperomia farctifolia thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both novice and experienced plant growers.
