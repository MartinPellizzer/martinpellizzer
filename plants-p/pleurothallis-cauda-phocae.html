Pleurothallis cauda-phocae

Pleurothallis cauda-phocae, commonly known as the "Phocea's Tail" orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers have a unique structure with long, slender sepals and petals that resemble a tail, giving it its common name.

This orchid is prized by collectors for its rarity and distinctive appearance.
