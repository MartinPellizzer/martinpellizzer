Pseuderanthemum roseum

Pseuderanthemum roseum, commonly known as the pink pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Pseuderanthemum roseum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
