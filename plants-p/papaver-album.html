Papaver album

Papaver album, commonly known as the white poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy white petals and a distinctive, rounded seed capsule that splits open when mature. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy.

The flowers bloom in early summer and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
