Pituranthos tortuosus var. virgatus

Pituranthos tortuosus var. virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf iceplant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white, waxy powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its drought tolerance and unique appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
