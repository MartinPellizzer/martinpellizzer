Phytolacca acuminata

Phytolacca acuminata, commonly known as the spiny beggar's ticks, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often covered in spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Phytolacca acuminata is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in moist, well-drained environments.
