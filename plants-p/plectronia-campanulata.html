Plectronia campanulata

Plectronia campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. Plectronia campanulata produces small, oval berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to biodiversity in its native habitats.
