Potentilla nyaradyana

Potentilla nyaradyana, commonly known as Nyarady's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
