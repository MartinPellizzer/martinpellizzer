Prunus lundelliana

Prunus lundelliana, commonly known as the Lundell cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible cherries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its sweet flavor and culinary use.
