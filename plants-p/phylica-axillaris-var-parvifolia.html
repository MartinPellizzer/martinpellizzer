Phylica axillaris var. parvifolia

Phylica axillaris var. parvifolia, commonly known as the coastal fynbos, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in winter. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure. It is an important component of the fynbos biome, contributing to local biodiversity.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often found in conservation areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
