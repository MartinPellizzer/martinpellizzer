Pedicularis × pallidiflora var. palearensis

Pedicularis × pallidiflora var. palearensis, commonly known as the pale-flowered lousewort, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features elongated, spurred flowers that are usually pale pink to white in color, with a distinctive downward-curving lip. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
