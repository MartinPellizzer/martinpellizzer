Plantago media var. badalii

Plantago media var. badalii, commonly known as the Balkan plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive silver-white veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variety is valued for its resilience and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
