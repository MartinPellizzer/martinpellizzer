Pongatium spongiosum

Pongatium spongiosum, commonly known as the spongeplant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, leaf-like structures that resemble sponges, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the growing season, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently, making it well-suited for dry climates.

Due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
