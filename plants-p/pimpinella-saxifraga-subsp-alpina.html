Pimpinella saxifraga subsp. alpina

Pimpinella saxifraga subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are characteristic of the Apiaceae family.

Its leaves are finely divided and often have a mild, parsley-like scent when crushed.
