Phalaris canariensis

Phalaris canariensis, commonly known as the Canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its hardiness.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
