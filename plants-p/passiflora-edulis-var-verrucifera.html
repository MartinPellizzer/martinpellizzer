Passiflora edulis var. verrucifera

Passiflora edulis var. verrucifera, commonly known as the sweet passion fruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It produces large, showy flowers with vibrant colors and a distinctive crown of filaments. The fruit is characterized by its rough, yellow or green exterior and a juicy, aromatic pulp containing inedible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and used in various culinary applications.
