Pouteria ridsdalei

Pouteria ridsdalei, commonly known as the Malabar star-apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, glossy leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by edible, star-shaped fruits. Its fruits are prized for their sweet, aromatic pulp and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
