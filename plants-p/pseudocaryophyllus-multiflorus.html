Pseudocaryophyllus multiflorus

Pseudocaryophyllus multiflorus, commonly known as the multiblade caryophyllaceae, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to white flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Pseudocaryophyllus multiflorus is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, making it a hardy and resilient species.
