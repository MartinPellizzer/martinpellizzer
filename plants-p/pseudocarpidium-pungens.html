Pseudocarpidium pungens

Pseudocarpidium pungens, commonly known as the spiny pseudocarpidium, is a member of the family Pterostomataceae and is native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that produce tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The botanical characteristics of Pseudocarpidium pungens make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptations in tropical ecosystems.
