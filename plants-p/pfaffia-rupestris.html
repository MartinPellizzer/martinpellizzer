Pfaffia rupestris

Pfaffia rupestris, commonly known as Paraguay tea, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish. It is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a traditional herbal tea.

Pfaffia rupestris is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
