Peperomia hamiltoniana

Peperomia hamiltoniana, commonly known as the Hamilton's peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or heart-shaped, often with a smooth or slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color, which are not showy but are important for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia hamiltoniana is also appreciated for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in humid environments.
