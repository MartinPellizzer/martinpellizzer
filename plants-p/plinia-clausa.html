Plinia clausa

Plinia clausa, commonly known as the closed-plum or Brazilian plum, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of eastern Brazil and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers with a fragrant aroma. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruit is usually consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves due to its sweet, tangy flavor.
