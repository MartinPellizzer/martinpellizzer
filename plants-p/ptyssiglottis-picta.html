Ptyssiglottis picta

Ptyssiglottis picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ptyssiglottis picta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
