Perrottetia arborescens

Perrottetia arborescens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Perrottetia arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a street tree due to its vibrant floral display.
