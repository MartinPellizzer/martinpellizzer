Paeonia obovata var. glabra

Paeonia obovata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white, with a rounded, flat shape. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are covered in a smooth, glabrous surface. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also noted for its fragrance and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and medicinal properties.
