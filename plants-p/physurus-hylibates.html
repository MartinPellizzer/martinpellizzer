Physurus hylibates

Physurus hylibates, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It blooms throughout the growing season, typically from spring to early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
