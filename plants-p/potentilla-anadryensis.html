Potentilla anadryensis

Potentilla anadryensis, commonly known as the western potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Potentilla anadryensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
