Prunus cerasus var. bunyardii

Prunus cerasus var. bunyardii, commonly known as the Bunyard cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and tart fruits that are typically harvested in early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces fragrant white flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens and orchards.
