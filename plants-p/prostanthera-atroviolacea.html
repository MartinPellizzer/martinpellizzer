Prostanthera atroviolacea

Prostanthera atroviolacea, commonly known as black violet honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly glandular surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Prostanthera atroviolacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in native gardens.
