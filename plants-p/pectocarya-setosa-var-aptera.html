Pectocarya setosa var. aptera

Pectocarya setosa var. aptera, commonly known as the smooth pectocarya, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It has a shallow root system adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Its leaves are typically reduced or absent, with the stem serving as the primary photosynthetic structure.
