Phaseolus hircinus

Phaseolus hircinus, commonly known as the goat pea, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and twining stems, which help it climb supports. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
