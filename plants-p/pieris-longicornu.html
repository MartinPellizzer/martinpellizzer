Pieris longicornu

Pieris longicornu, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with silver or grayish-white markings. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
