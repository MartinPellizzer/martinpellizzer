Passiflora cochinchinensis subsp. cochinchinensis

Passiflora cochinchinensis subsp.

cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Cochinchina passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia. It produces striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of hair.

The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
