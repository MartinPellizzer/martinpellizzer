Piper tiguanum

Piper tiguanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or tiguanum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with elongated stems that often trail along the forest floor or climb trees. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
