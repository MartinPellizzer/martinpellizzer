Papaver coloradense var. aurantiellum

Papaver coloradense var. aurantiellum, commonly known as the Colorado poppy variant with orange petals, is a striking flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from orange to red in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine or arid environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, hairy texture, while its stems are slender and often branched.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
