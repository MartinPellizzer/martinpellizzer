Potentilla douglasii var. pseudocapitata

Potentilla douglasii var. pseudocapitata, commonly known as Douglas's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and drought tolerance.

The varietal name 'pseudocapitata' refers to the appearance of its flower clusters, which resemble a single head.
