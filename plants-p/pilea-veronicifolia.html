Pilea veronicifolia

Pilea veronicifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its round, coin-shaped leaves that are typically green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. It is a compact, evergreen shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Pilea veronicifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
