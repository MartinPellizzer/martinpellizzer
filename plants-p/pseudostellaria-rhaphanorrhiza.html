Pseudostellaria rhaphanorrhiza

Pseudostellaria rhaphanorrhiza, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are tuberous and used in traditional medicine.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
