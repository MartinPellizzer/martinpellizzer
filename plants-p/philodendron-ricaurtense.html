Philodendron ricaurtense

Philodendron ricaurtense, commonly known as the Ricaurt's philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright orange berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a sought-after ornamental plant.
