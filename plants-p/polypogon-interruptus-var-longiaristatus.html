Polypogon interruptus var. longiaristatus

Polypogon interruptus var. longiaristatus, commonly known as the long-leaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands and prairie habitats. Its flowering spikes are slender and often appear in late spring to early summer.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small wildlife.
