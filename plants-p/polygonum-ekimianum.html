Polygonum ekimianum

Polygonum ekimianum, commonly known as the Ekmian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and basal leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in river valleys and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

The small, greenish flowers appear in clusters and are accompanied by bracts that are often more showy than the actual blooms.
