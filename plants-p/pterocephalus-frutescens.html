Pterocephalus frutescens

Pterocephalus frutescens, commonly known as the Chinese iron vine, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The botanical features of Pterocephalus frutescens make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
