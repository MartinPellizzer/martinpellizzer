Pyrus neimongolica

Pyrus neimongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian pear, is a deciduous tree native to northern China.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to apples. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various regions for both landscaping and agricultural purposes.
