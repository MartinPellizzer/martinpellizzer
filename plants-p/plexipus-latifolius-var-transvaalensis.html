Plexipus latifolius var. transvaalensis

Plexipus latifolius var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The Transvaal daisy is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
