Polyachyrus macrotis

Polyachyrus macrotis, commonly known as the large-headed grasshopper plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in supporting local insect populations and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
