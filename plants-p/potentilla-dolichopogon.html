Potentilla dolichopogon

Potentilla dolichopogon, commonly known as the longstem cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.
