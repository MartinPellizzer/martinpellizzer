Plectranthus hilliardiae

Plectranthus hilliardiae, commonly known as the Cape mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
