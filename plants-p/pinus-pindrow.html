Pinus pindrow

Pinus pindrow, commonly known as the Himalayan blue pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are often pendulous and can remain on the tree for several years. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Pinus pindrow plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
