Paullinia seminuda

Paullinia seminuda, commonly known as the Brazilian sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
