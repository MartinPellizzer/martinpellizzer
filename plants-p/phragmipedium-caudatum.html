Phragmipedium caudatum

Phragmipedium caudatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flower that resembles a small, elongated pouch. The plant typically grows up to 1 meter tall, with lance-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive, darkened lower lip.

Phragmipedium caudatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.
