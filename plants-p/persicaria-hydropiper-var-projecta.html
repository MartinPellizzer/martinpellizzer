Persicaria hydropiper var. projecta

Persicaria hydropiper var. projecta, commonly known as water pepper, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The species is adapted to moist soils and can thrive in shallow water environments.
