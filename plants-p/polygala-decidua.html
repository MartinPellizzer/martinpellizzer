Polygala decidua

Polygala decidua, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala decidua is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
