Phacelia ramosissima var. austrolitoralis

Phacelia ramosissima var. austrolitoralis, commonly known as the coastal phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in open habitats such as dunes, coastal bluffs, and grasslands.

Its short stature and profuse blooming make it a valuable component of local ecosystems.
