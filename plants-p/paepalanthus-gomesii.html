Paepalanthus gomesii

Paepalanthus gomesii, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the sunflower. The plant has a rosette growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Paepalanthus gomesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
