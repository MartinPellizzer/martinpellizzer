Pterocarpus rohrii

Pterocarpus rohrii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aesthetic and structural properties. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by distinctive, flat, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and timber use, Pterocarpus rohrii is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
