Pyrus leiocalyca

Pyrus leiocalyca, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling small pears. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
