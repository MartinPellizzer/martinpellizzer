Psidium dictyophyllum

Psidium dictyophyllum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or the wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native range.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
