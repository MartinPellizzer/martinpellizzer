Prostanthera atriplicifolia

Prostanthera atriplicifolia, commonly known as black-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to south-eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

Prostanthera atriplicifolia is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
