Passiflora ligularis var. geminiflora

Passiflora ligularis var. geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
