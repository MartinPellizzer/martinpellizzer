Pleurothallis hitchcockii

Pleurothallis hitchcockii, commonly known as the Hitchcock's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in capturing moisture from the air.

This orchid is prized for its delicate beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
