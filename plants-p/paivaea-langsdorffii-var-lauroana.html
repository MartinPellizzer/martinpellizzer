Paivaea langsdorffii var. lauroana

Paivaea langsdorffii var. lauroana, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a rhizomatous root system that gives rise to erect, glabrous stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by triangular, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
