Pectis lessingii

Pectis lessingii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Pectis lessingii is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
