Potentilla × eynensis

Potentilla × eynensis, commonly known as the Eynens wild rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are compound, with elliptical leaflets that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Potentilla × eynensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
