Primula ossetica

Primula ossetica, commonly known as the Ossetian primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Primula ossetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
