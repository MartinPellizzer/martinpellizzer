Penstemon tolmiei subsp. modestus

Penstemon tolmiei subsp.

modestus, commonly known as the modest penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact form and smaller flowers compared to other Penstemon species.
