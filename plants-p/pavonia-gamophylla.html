Pavonia gamophylla

Pavonia gamophylla, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters during the summer months. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia gamophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
