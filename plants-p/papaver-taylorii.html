Papaver taylorii

Papaver taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy. This perennial herb features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a contrasting central area. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions.

Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.
