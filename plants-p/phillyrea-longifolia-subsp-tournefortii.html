Phillyrea longifolia subsp. tournefortii

Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the Corsican Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
