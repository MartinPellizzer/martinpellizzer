Paeonia paradoxa var. fimbriata

Paeonia paradoxa var. fimbriata, commonly known as the fimbriated peony, is a striking ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive ruffled edge, or "fimbria," on the petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features compound, bipinnate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
