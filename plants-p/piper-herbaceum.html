Piper herbaceum

Piper herbaceum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers followed by small, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
