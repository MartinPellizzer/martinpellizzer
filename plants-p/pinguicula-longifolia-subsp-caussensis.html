Pinguicula longifolia subsp. caussensis

Pinguicula longifolia subsp.

caussensis, commonly known as the Caussan's butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices or wet meadows. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive hood-like structure.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cool, humid conditions.
