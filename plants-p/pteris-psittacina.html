Pteris psittacina

Pteris psittacina, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow fronds that contrast with its dark green veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a distinctive feather-like appearance.

Pteris psittacina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
