Passiflora pisonis

Passiflora pisonis, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora pisonis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
