Pontederia loureiroana

Pontederia loureiroana, commonly known as the broadleaf water hyacinth, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, fibrous stems. Its striking blue to purple flowers bloom in dense clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

However, it can become invasive in non-native regions, outcompeting native vegetation and altering aquatic ecosystems.
