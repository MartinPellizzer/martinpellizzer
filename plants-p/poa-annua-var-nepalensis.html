Poa annua var. nepalensis

Poa annua var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepalese annual bluegrass, is a variant of the annual bluegrass species native to temperate regions.

It is a low-growing, cool-season grass that typically forms dense, mat-like growth patterns. The plant produces slender, cylindrical seed heads that are often tinged with blue or purple hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
