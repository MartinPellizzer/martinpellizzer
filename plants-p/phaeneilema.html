Phaeneilema

Phaeneilema, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Phaeneilema is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
