Piper trichostylum

Piper trichostylum, commonly known as the warty pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
