Pelargonium acanthifolium

Pelargonium acanthifolium, commonly known as the spiny geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a spiny or serrated edge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
