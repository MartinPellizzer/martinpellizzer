Paphiopedilum robinsonii f. viride

Paphiopedilum robinsonii f. viride, commonly known as the viride lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique greenish-yellow flowers with intricate patterns that resemble a lady's slipper. The plant has a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces pseudo-bulbs that store water, aiding in its survival in its native habitat. Its leaves are typically broad and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

This orchid thrives in shaded, humid environments and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
