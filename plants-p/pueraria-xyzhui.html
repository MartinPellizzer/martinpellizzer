Pueraria xyzhui

Pueraria xyzhui, commonly known as the Chinese kudzu, is a vigorous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or white flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments. Its roots contain high levels of starch and are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Pueraria xyzhui is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
