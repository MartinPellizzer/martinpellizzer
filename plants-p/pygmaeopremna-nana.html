Pygmaeopremna nana

Pygmaeopremna nana, commonly known as the dwarf ironwood, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. It is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
