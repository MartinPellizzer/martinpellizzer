Panicum pilosum var. epilosum

Panicum pilosum var. epilosum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
