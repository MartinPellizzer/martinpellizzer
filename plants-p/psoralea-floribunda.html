Psoralea floribunda

Psoralea floribunda, commonly known as the clustered psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the summer months. Its seed pods are slender and dehiscent, releasing seeds when mature.

Psoralea floribunda is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
