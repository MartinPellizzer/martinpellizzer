Podocarpus acuminatus

Podocarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the Chinese Podocarp or the Indian Podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, rounded leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Podocarpus acuminatus is also valued for its durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and furniture-making.
