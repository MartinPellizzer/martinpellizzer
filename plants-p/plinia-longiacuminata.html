Plinia longiacuminata

Plinia longiacuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia longiacuminata*, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, hence its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
