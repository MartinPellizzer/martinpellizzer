Pouteria splendens

Pouteria splendens, commonly known as the golden apple or tucanillo, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, golden-orange fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its fruit is highly valued for its nutritional content and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

Pouteria splendens thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
