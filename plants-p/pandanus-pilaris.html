Pandanus pilaris

Pandanus pilaris, commonly known as the hibiscus pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near the shoreline.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in coastal botany.
