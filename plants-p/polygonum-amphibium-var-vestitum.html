Polygonum amphibium var. vestitum

Polygonum amphibium var. vestitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, usually in shades of pink or white. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, known for its unique leaf structures and ecological adaptability.
