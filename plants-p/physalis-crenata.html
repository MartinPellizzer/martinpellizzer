Physalis crenata

Physalis crenata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or ozark nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive wavy or scalloped edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into inflated, lantern-shaped fruits covered in a papery husk. The fruits are typically orange to red in color and contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in disturbed habitats.
