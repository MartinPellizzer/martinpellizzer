Potentilla rydbergiana

Potentilla rydbergiana, commonly known as Rydberg's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Rydberg's cinquefoil thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like meadows and rocky slopes.
