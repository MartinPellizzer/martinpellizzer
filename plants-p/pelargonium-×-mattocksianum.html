Pelargonium × mattocksianum

Pelargonium × mattocksianum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × domesticum. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Pelargonium × mattocksianum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for many growing conditions.
