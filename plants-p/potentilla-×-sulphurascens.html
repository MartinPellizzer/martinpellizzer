Potentilla × sulphurascens

Potentilla × sulphurascens, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vigorous growth and long-lasting blooms.
