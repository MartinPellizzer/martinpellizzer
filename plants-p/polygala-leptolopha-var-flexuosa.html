Polygala leptolopha var. flexuosa

Polygala leptolopha var. flexuosa, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
