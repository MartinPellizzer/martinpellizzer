Potentilla panigrahiana

Potentilla panigrahiana, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, with five leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Potentilla panigrahiana is adapted to cold, mountainous environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
