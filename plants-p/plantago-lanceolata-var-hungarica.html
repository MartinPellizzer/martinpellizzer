Plantago lanceolata var. hungarica

Plantago lanceolata var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and hairy surfaces.

It is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
