Potamogeton quinquenervius

Potamogeton quinquenervius, commonly known as the five-veined pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged or just at the water's surface. The plant is characterized by its five prominent veins on each leaf, which give it its scientific name. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing food and shelter for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton quinquenervius is often found in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds, lakes, and marshes.
