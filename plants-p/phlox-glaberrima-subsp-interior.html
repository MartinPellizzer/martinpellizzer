Phlox glaberrima subsp. interior

Phlox glaberrima subsp.

interior, commonly known as the interior phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas.

The plant features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
