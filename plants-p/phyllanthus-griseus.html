Phyllanthus griseus

Phyllanthus griseus, commonly known as the gray phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with unique morphological features. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in cyathia, a characteristic structure of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Phyllanthus griseus is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments.
