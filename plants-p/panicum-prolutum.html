Panicum prolutum

Panicum prolutum, commonly known as purpletop grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant purple or pink flower spikes that appear in the summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, hairy seeds.

Panicum prolutum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
