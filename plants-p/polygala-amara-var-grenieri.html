Polygala amara var. grenieri

Polygala amara var. grenieri, commonly known as the bitter milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, adapting well to dry and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pink to purple, with a distinctive spurred structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
