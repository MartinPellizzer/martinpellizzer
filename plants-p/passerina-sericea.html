Passerina sericea

Passerina sericea, commonly known as silverleaf pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant features finely divided, silver-haired leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woods.

Passerina sericea plays a role in supporting pollinators with its showy, yellow flowers.
