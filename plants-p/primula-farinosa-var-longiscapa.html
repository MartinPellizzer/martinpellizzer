Primula farinosa var. longiscapa

Primula farinosa var. longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it the species name "farinosa," meaning flour-like. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
