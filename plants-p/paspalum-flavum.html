Paspalum flavum

Paspalum flavum, commonly known as golden paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden inflorescences, which contribute to its ornamental value. It is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Paspalum flavum is also valued for its role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in agricultural and ecological settings.
