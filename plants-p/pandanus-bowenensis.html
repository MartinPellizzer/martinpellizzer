Pandanus bowenensis

Pandanus bowenensis, commonly known as the Bowens' pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly the islands of the Caroline and Marshall groups. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a terminal spike bearing small, fragrant flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal and humid environments, often growing near the shoreline or in wetlands.
