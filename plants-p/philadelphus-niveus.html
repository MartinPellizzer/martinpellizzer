Philadelphus niveus

Philadelphus niveus, commonly known as the white mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Philadelphus niveus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
