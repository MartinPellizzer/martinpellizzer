Passiflora ketura

Passiflora ketura, commonly known as the Arabian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It produces striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to hot and dry conditions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are small, orange berries that ripen in late summer.

Passiflora ketura is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
