Prunella × dissectifolia

Prunella × dissectifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved selfheal, is a hybrid plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to Europe and is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Prunella × dissectifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
