Peperomia san-juanana

Peperomia san-juanana, commonly known as San Juan peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikes, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
