Paraneurachne muelleri

Paraneurachne muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paraneurachne muelleri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
