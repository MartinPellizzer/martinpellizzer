Philibertella crispa

Philibertella crispa, commonly known as the crinkled leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Philibertella crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
