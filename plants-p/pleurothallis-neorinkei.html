Pleurothallis neorinkei

Pleurothallis neorinkei, commonly known as the golden tangle orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
