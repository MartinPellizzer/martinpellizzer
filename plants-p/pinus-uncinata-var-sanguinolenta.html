Pinus uncinata var. sanguinolenta

Pinus uncinata var. sanguinolenta, commonly known as the crimson pine, is a rare and striking variant of the serpentine pine.

It is characterized by its vivid red needles, which contrast sharply with its dark bark and conical shape. This variety is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor, rocky soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant's foliage and cones exhibit unique coloration due to genetic mutations affecting pigment production.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
