Paspalum monostachyum

Paspalum monostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It is often found in coastal regions and can tolerate saline conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
