Pulsatilla montana subsp. dacica

Pulsatilla montana subsp.

dacica, commonly known as the Caucasian pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It features a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to pale pink, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural requirements.
