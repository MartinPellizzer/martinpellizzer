Pantacantha

Pantacantha, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is native to Central and South America. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces fragrant blossoms that open at dusk. Its rapid growth habit makes it popular for ornamental use in warm climates.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
