Pilosella piloselloides subsp. floccipeduncula

Pilosella piloselloides subsp.

floccipeduncula, commonly known as the woolly cat's-paw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a compact rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its dense foliage and distinctive, fluffy flower heads.
