Paphiopedilum puberulum

Paphiopedilum puberulum, commonly known as the hairy paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elliptical and often covered in a fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Paphiopedilum puberulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, humid conditions.
