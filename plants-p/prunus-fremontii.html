Prunus fremontii

Prunus fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Prunus fremontii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
