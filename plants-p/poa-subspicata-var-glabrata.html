Poa subspicata var. glabrata

Poa subspicata var. glabrata, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often slightly curved.

The variety glabrata is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves compared to the typical form.
