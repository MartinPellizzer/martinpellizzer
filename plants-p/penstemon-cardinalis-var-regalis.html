Penstemon cardinalis var. regalis

Penstemon cardinalis var. regalis, commonly known as the cardinal penstemon, is a striking perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract hummingbirds and bees, contributing to both ecological and ornamental value.
