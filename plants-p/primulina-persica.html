Primulina persica

Primulina persica, commonly known as the Persian primula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
