Polygonum campanulatum var. longipes

Polygonum campanulatum var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical characteristics include showy inflorescences and fibrous roots, making it a notable species in wetland and moist woodland habitats.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
