Pilea muscosa var. trianthemoides

Pilea muscosa var. trianthemoides, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
