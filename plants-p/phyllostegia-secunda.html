Phyllostegia secunda

Phyllostegia secunda, commonly known as the spreading flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Phyllostegia secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
