Pinus mugo f. slavinii

Pinus mugo f. slavinii, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a compact, shrub-like variant of the mountain pine.

It is characterized by its slow growth, dense foliage, and small, needle-like leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain seeds dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold, dry conditions.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
