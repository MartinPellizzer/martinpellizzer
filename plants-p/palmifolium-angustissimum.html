Palmifolium angustissimum

Palmifolium angustissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to regions with warm, humid climates. The plant features a slender trunk that grows slowly and is often covered in a fibrous sheath. Its leaves are typically arranged in a fan shape, providing a distinctive visual appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
