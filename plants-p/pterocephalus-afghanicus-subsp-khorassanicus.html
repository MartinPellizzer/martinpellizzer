Pterocephalus afghanicus subsp. khorassanicus

Pterocephalus afghanicus subsp.

khorassanicus, commonly known as the Afghan ironweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in Central Asia. It has hairy leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
