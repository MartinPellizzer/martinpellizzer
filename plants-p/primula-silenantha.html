Primula silenantha

Primula silenantha, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing showy white or pinkish flowers with five petals. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh mountain conditions.

Primula silenantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
