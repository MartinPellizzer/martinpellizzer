Polygonum americanum

Polygonum americanum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Polygonum americanum is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
