Phyllanthus moorei var. acutitepalus

Phyllanthus moorei var. acutitepalus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
