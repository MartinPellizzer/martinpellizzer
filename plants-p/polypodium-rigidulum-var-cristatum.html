Polypodium rigidulum var. cristatum

Polypodium rigidulum var. cristatum, commonly known as the crested polypody, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, stiff, and often curled fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, humid environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
