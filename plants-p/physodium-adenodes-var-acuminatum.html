Physodium adenodes var. acuminatum

Physodium adenodes var. acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf physodium, is a variant of the physodium genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.

The variety is distinguished by its acuminate leaf tips, which contribute to its unique appearance.
