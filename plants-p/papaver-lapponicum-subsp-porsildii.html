Papaver lapponicum subsp. porsildii

Papaver lapponicum subsp.

porsildii, commonly known as the Arctic poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early summer, often in rocky or barren ground.

The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It is well adapted to cold climates and can survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and visually striking plant in harsh environments.
