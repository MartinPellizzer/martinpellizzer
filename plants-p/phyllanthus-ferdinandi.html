Phyllanthus ferdinandi

Phyllanthus ferdinandi, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright orange, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Phyllanthus ferdinandi is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
