Passiflora luzmarina

Passiflora luzmarina, commonly known as the Luzmarina passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with intricate structures. The flowers feature a unique combination of blue, purple, and white petals, along with filaments that resemble threads. It is also known for its edible fruits, which are small and have a sweet, tropical flavor.

This plant is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
