Polemonium viscosum var. mellitum

Polemonium viscosum var. mellitum, commonly known as sticky bluebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
