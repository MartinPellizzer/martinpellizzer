Polygala hintonii

Polygala hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. It is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained conditions and is often found in the southeastern region of the United States.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
