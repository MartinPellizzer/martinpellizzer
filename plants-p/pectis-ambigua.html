Pectis ambigua

Pectis ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous pectis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water availability.

Pectis ambigua is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
