Plectranthus striatus var. gerardianus

Plectranthus striatus var. gerardianus, commonly known as the purple devil plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it both visually appealing and useful in herbal applications.
