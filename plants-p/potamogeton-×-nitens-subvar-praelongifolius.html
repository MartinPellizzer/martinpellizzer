Potamogeton × nitens subvar. praelongifolius

Potamogeton × nitens subvar. praelongifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its rhizome system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense underwater mats.

It thrives in freshwater environments with moderate to slow water flow and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
