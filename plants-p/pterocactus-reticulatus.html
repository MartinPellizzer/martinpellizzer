Pterocactus reticulatus

Pterocactus reticulatus, commonly known as the netted pterocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its root system is adapted to absorb water efficiently in arid environments.

Pterocactus reticulatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
