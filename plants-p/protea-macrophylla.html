Protea macrophylla

Protea macrophylla, commonly known as the king protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous petal-like bracts that surround a central cluster of small, tubular flowers. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, supporting various pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
