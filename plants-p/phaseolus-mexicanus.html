Phaseolus mexicanus

Phaseolus mexicanus, commonly known as the tepin, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
