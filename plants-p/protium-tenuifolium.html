Protium tenuifolium

Protium tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

Its wood is highly valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
