Phragmipedium anchicayense

Phragmipedium anchicayense, commonly known as the Anchicay orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often yellow or orange.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
