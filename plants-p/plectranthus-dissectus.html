Plectranthus dissectus

Plectranthus dissectus, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive serrated or dissected margin, giving it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus dissectus is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
