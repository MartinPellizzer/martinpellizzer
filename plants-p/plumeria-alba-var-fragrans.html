Plumeria alba var. fragrans

Plumeria alba var. fragrans, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles and emit a sweet, spicy scent.
