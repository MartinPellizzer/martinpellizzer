Polyactium hibridifolium

Polyactium hibridifolium, commonly known as the hybrid leaf polyactium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
