Paepalanthus leiseringii

Paepalanthus leiseringii, commonly known as the Leisering's puya, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a striking plant characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup at the center. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
