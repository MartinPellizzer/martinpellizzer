Prunus serrulata var. sachalinensis

Prunus serrulata var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
