Pseudotsuga taxifolia

Pseudotsuga taxifolia, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones. The tree thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture and reforestation efforts.
