Pennisetum angolense

Pennisetum angolense, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple to silver-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
