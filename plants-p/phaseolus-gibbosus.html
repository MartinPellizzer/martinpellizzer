Phaseolus gibbosus

Phaseolus gibbosus, commonly known as the mountain bean or giant scarlet runner bean, is a species of legume native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces long, trailing vines that can grow up to several meters in length. Its seeds are typically encased in thick, fleshy pods, which are edible when young.

This plant is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential as a food crop.
