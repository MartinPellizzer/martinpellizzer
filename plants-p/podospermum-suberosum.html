Podospermum suberosum

Podospermum suberosum, commonly known as the silky podospermum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and elliptical leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
