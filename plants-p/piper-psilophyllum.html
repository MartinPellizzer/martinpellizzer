Piper psilophyllum

Piper psilophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing habit and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, peppery fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
