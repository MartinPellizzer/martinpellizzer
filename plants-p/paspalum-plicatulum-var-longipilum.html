Paspalum plicatulum var. longipilum

Paspalum plicatulum var. longipilum, commonly known as the long-haired paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct hairy texture along the leaf margins. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along waterways.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
