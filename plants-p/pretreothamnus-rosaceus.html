Pretreothamnus rosaceus

Pretreothamnus rosaceus, commonly known as the pink thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Pretreothamnus rosaceus plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
