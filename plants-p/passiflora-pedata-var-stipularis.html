Passiflora pedata var. stipularis

Passiflora pedata var. stipularis, commonly known as the scarlet passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments and a central reproductive structure. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
