Polygonum nitens

Polygonum nitens, commonly known as the shining knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum nitens is often found in wet or marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
