Polanisia paxii

Polanisia paxii, commonly known as the Pacific yew, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to the western regions of North America.

It is characterized by its reddish-barked trunk and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and coastal areas.

Due to its ecological significance and historical use in medicine, it is a protected species in many regions.
