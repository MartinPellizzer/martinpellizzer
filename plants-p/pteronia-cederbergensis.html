Pteronia cederbergensis

Pteronia cederbergensis, commonly known as the Cedarberg pteronia, is a flowering plant native to the Cedarberg region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
