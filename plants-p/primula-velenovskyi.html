Primula velenovskyi

Primula velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula velenovskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
