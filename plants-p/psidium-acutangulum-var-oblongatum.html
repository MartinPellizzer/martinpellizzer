Psidium acutangulum var. oblongatum

Psidium acutangulum var. oblongatum, commonly known as the oblongata guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, oblong, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor with a thin, edible skin. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in warm climates.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, tropical environment to flourish.
