Primula sinuata

Primula sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and a distinctive, notched base. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

Primula sinuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
