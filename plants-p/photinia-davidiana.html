Photinia davidiana

Photinia davidiana, commonly known as the David's photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Photinia davidiana is relatively disease-resistant and suitable for use in gardens and landscapes.
