Papaver persicum var. brachycarpum

Papaver persicum var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features striking, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a blackened center. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, typical of the Papaveraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
