Petunia × punctata

Petunia × punctata, commonly known as the spotted petunia, is a hybrid species within the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often variegated foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including white, pink, and purple. The plant is native to South America and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. Petunia × punctata typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both bedding plants and container gardening.
