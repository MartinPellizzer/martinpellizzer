Paphinia seegeri var. semialba

Paphinia seegeri var. semialba, commonly known as the White Paphinia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Philippines.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces large, showy flowers with white petals and a prominent, often pink or red lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
