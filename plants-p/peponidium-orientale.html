Peponidium orientale

Peponidium orientale, commonly known as the oriental pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces fruits that can vary in shape and size, typically ranging from spherical to elongated forms. Its botanical features include a trailing growth habit and a fibrous, hairy stem.

The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is cultivated for its edible fruits.
