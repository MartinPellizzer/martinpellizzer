Primula × escheri

Primula × escheri, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula japonica and Primula × sibirica.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, featuring thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, white, or yellow, often with a distinctive dark center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Primula × escheri prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens and containers.
