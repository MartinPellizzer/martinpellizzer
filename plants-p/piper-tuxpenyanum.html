Piper tuxpenyanum

Piper tuxpenyanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
