Polygala hemipterocarpa

Polygala hemipterocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
