Polystichopsis lurida

Polystichopsis lurida, commonly known as the desert fern, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its ability to store water in its rhizomes. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in specialized leaf structures called sori.

Polystichopsis lurida is often found in rocky soils and can thrive with minimal water, making it a hardy species in challenging landscapes.
