Pseudosamanea

Pseudosamanea, commonly known as the silky oak, is a tropical tree belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods that split open when mature.

Pseudosamanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
