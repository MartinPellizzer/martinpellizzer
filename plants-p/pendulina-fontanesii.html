Pendulina fontanesii

Pendulina fontanesii, commonly known as the Fontanesii's weeping fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous fronds that drape gracefully from the plant, giving it a weeping appearance. The plant has small, delicate leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pendulina fontanesii is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
