Pyrus elaeagrifolia subsp. bulgarica

Pyrus elaeagrifolia subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are elliptical to ovate in shape and have a glossy green upper surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet to subacid berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
