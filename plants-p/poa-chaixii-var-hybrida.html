Poa chaixii var. hybrida

Poa chaixii var. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
