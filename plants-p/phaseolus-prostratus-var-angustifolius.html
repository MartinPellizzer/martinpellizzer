Phaseolus prostratus var. angustifolius

Phaseolus prostratus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf runner bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its pods are slender and contain edible beans that are often used in traditional cuisines.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its role in sustainable agriculture.
