Panicum nigropedatum

Panicum nigropedatum, commonly known as blackfoot grass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for sandy or loamy soils.

Blackfoot grass is often used in native plant gardens and for erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
