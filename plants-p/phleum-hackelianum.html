Phleum hackelianum

Phleum hackelianum, commonly known as purple false oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like clusters of purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds visual interest to landscapes.

Phleum hackelianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
