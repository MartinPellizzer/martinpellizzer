Potentilla recta f. typica

Potentilla recta f. typica, commonly known as the upright potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in temperate regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
