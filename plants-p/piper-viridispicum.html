Piper viridispicum

Piper viridispicum, commonly known as the green-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
