Primula scopulicola

Primula scopulicola, commonly known as the rock primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Primula scopulicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
