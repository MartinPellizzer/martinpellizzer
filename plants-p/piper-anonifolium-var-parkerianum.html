Piper anonifolium var. parkerianum

Piper anonifolium var. parkerianum, commonly known as the Parker's pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
