Prosopis flexuosa

Prosopis flexuosa, commonly known as the screwbean mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, twisting branches and small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has small, feathery leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

Prosopis flexuosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
