Podocarpus neriifolius var. brevipes

Podocarpus neriifolius var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf satinwood, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
