Polygala flavescens subsp. pisaurensis

Polygala flavescens subsp.

pisaurensis, commonly known as the Pisaurensis milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces yellowish-green to pale yellow flowers that are often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are linear and hairy, adapting to arid conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
