Premna parviflora

Premna parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered premna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
