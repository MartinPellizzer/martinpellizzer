Plectranthus dissectus var. chambensis

Plectranthus dissectus var. chambensis, commonly known as the Chambeyron's mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
