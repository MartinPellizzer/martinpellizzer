Phellodendron lavallei

Phellodendron lavallei, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark that exudes a strong, aromatic scent. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in panicles, and it produces yellowish or orange fruits that contain seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
