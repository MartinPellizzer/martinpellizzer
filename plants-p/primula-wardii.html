Primula wardii

Primula wardii, commonly known as the Ward's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in partial shade. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula wardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
