Pelargonium × leion

Pelargonium × leion, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly serrated leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets due to its ornamental foliage and continuous flowering.

Pelargonium × leion prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
