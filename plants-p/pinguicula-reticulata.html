Pinguicula reticulata

Pinguicula reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant's leaves are covered in a network of fine veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Pinguicula reticulata is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and fascinating trapping mechanism.
