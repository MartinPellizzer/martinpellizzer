Panicularia grandis

Panicularia grandis, commonly known as the large panicled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
