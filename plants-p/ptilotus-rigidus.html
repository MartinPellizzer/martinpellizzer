Ptilotus rigidus

Ptilotus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid spinifex, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spinous foliage and small, showy flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme heat and drought conditions. Its unique structure helps reduce water loss, making it well-suited for harsh environments.

The rigid spinifex is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts, contributing to the ecological stability of these areas.
