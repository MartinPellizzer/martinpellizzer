Papaver dubium var. collinum

Papaver dubium var. collinum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
