Polygala monticola var. cuneata

Polygala monticola var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its specific epithet "cuneata.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
