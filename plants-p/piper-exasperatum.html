Piper exasperatum

Piper exasperatum, commonly known as the spiny pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its serrated, spiny leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
