Phanera excelsa var. megalantha

Phanera excelsa var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant phanera, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
