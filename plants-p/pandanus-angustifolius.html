Pandanus angustifolius

Pandanus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that contain small, edible fruits. Its leaves are also used for thatching and roofing in many island communities.

This species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
