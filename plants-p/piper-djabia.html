Piper djabia

Piper djabia, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
