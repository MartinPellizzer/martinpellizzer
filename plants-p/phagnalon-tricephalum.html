Phagnalon tricephalum

Phagnalon tricephalum, commonly known as the three-headed phagnalon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
