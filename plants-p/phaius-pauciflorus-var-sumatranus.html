Phaius pauciflorus var. sumatranus

Phaius pauciflorus var. sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves and produces a single spike with several flowers, each having a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
