Pinus nitida

Pinus nitida, commonly known as the Italian stone pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant produces small, edible pine nuts that are highly valued in culinary applications. Its cones are typically woody and elongated, containing the seeds.

Pinus nitida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appearance and hardiness in various climates.
