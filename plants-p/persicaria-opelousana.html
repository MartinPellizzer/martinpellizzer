Persicaria opelousana

Persicaria opelousana, commonly known as Opelousa knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces slender, cylindrical seed capsules that split open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Persicaria opelousana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
