Prunus heteroflora

Prunus heteroflora, commonly known as the variable cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its variable leaf and flower morphology. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible berries.

Prunus heteroflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
