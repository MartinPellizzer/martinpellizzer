Piper acutamentum

Piper acutamentum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Piper acutamentum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
