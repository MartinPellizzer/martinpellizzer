Piper falcigerum

Piper falcigerum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn dark when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical features include a climbing habit, stellate hairs on the stems, and small white flowers.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
