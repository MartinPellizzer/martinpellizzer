Pygeum wightianum var. parvifolium

Pygeum wightianum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are small and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
