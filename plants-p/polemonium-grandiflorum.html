Polemonium grandiflorum

Polemonium grandiflorum, commonly known as the Eastern blueflag, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
