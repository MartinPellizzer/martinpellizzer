Phaseoloides cognatum

Phaseoloides cognatum, commonly known as the coffee bean plant, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are the primary product, used extensively in the coffee industry.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
