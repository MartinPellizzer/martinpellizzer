Phyllanthus st-johnii

Phyllanthus st-johnii, commonly known as the St. John's button, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a desirable plant in both gardens and natural habitats.
