Phyllanthus submollis var. glabra

Phyllanthus submollis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
