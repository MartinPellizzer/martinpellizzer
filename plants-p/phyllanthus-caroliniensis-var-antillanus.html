Phyllanthus caroliniensis var. antillanus

Phyllanthus caroliniensis var. antillanus, commonly known as the Antillean spurge, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, green, opposite leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is native to the Caribbean and parts of South America, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
