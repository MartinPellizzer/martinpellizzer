Paepalanthus rupestris

Paepalanthus rupestris, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing remarkable adaptations to survive in harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and botanical collections.
