Pisum arvense var. umbellatum

Pisum arvense var. umbellatum, commonly known as the common vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and the presence of hairy stems and leaves. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in agricultural settings for forage and cover crops.
