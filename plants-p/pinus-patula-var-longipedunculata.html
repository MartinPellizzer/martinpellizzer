Pinus patula var. longipedunculata

Pinus patula var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate Mexican pine, is a variant of the Mexican pine, native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters of three. The cones are often pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of climatic conditions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
