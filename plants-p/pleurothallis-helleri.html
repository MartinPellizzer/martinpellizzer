Pleurothallis helleri

Pleurothallis helleri, commonly known as the Heller's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its intricate floral structure and unique coloration make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Pleurothallis helleri is also noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
