Pinus parviflora var. parviflora

Pinus parviflora var. parviflora, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically light green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
