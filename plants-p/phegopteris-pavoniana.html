Phegopteris pavoniana

Phegopteris pavoniana, commonly known as the rainbow fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright green fronds that often display a waxy, glossy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are usually pinnately divided with a soft, feathery texture.

Phegopteris pavoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
