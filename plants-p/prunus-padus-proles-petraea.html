Prunus padus proles petraea

Prunus padus proles petraea, commonly known as the rock cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, oval leaves. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark red berries in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
