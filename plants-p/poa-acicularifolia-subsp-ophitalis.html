Poa acicularifolia subsp. ophitalis

Poa acicularifolia subsp.

ophitalis, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a grass species characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant has a clump-forming growth habit with slender, upright stems that bear small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
