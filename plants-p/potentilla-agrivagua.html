Potentilla agrivagua

Potentilla agrivagua, commonly known as the agriv agua cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with serrated, lance-shaped leaves.

Potentilla agrivagua is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
