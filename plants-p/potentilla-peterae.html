Potentilla peterae

Potentilla peterae, commonly known as the Peter's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a grayish-green hue.

Potentilla peterae is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
