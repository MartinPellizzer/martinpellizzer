Polygonum buisanense

Polygonum buisanense, commonly known as the Bujanese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Polygonum buisanense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
