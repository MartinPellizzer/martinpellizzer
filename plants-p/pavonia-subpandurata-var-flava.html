Pavonia subpandurata var. flava

Pavonia subpandurata var. flava, commonly known as the yellow pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow bracts that surround its small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
