Piper pseudogrande

Piper pseudogrande, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

Piper pseudogrande is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
