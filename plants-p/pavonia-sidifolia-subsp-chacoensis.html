Pavonia sidifolia subsp. chacoensis

Pavonia sidifolia subsp.

chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
