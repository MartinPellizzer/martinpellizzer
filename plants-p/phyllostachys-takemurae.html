Phyllostachys takemurae

Phyllostachys takemurae, commonly known as Takemura's bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow panicles as its flowering structure, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
