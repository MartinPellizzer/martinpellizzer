Parinari polyandra var. villosa

Parinari polyandra var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy parinari, is a tropical tree species native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and compound leaves with long, hairy petioles. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
