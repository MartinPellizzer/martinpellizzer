Philadelphus schrenkii var. lasiogynus

Philadelphus schrenkii var. lasiogynus, commonly known as the Turkestan mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a strong, sweet scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
