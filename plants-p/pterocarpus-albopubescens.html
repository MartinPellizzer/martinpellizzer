Pterocarpus albopubescens

Pterocarpus albopubescens, commonly known as the white-haired ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in tropical climates and its role in traditional medicine.
