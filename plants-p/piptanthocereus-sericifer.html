Piptanthocereus sericifer

Piptanthocereus sericifer, commonly known as the silky cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in dense, silky white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its root system is shallow and widespread, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the soil.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among cactus enthusiasts.
