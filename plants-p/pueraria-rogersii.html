Pueraria rogersii

Pueraria rogersii, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features large, palmately compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and it produces yellow or orange flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in agricultural settings.

Pueraria rogersii is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and floral display.
