Pittosporum ornatum

Pittosporum ornatum, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces fragrant, yellow flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of orange-red berries that appear in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum ornatum is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
