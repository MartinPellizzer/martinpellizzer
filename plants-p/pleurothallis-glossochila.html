Pleurothallis glossochila

Pleurothallis glossochila, commonly known as the golden-lip pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and adaptability to various microhabitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively rare in the horticultural trade.
