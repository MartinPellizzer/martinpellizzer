Phaenopyrum ellipticum

Phaenopyrum ellipticum, commonly known as the oval leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its unique morphology includes a low-growing, mat-forming habit with elliptical-shaped leaves.

Phaenopyrum ellipticum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
