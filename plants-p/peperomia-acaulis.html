Peperomia acaulis

Peperomia acaulis, commonly known as the stemless peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a smooth, often variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne on short, upright spikes. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance for low light and minimal water requirements.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for tabletop displays and container gardening.
