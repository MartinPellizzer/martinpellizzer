Prunus mahaleb var. chrysocarpa

Prunus mahaleb var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the yellow cherry or golden cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright orange to red fruits. The fruits are edible, though often tart, and are used in jams and preserves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
