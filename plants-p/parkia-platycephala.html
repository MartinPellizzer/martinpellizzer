Parkia platycephala

Parkia platycephala, commonly known as the flat-headed acacia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, feather-like segments. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several elliptical seeds.

Parkia platycephala is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
