Phegopteris connectilis

Phegopteris connectilis, commonly known as the linked frond fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, compound fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
