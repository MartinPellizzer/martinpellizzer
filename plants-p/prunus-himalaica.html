Prunus himalaica

Prunus himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe, containing a single seed. Prunus himalaica is often found in high-altitude forests and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
