Pinguicula leptoceras var. lepontina

Pinguicula leptoceras var. lepontina, commonly known as the Alpine butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It possesses sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest small insects, providing it with essential nutrients in nutrient-poor soils. The plant typically grows in rocky or moist, sunny habitats at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually small and white or pink, with a distinctive spurred shape.

It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil.
