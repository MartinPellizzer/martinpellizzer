Pyrus dioica

Pyrus dioica, commonly known as the wild pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the autumn. It is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, and it plays an important role in supporting local wildlife.

Pyrus dioica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.
