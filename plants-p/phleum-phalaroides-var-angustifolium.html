Phleum phalaroides var. angustifolium

Phleum phalaroides var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

It is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
