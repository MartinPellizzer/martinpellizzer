Persicaria agrestis

Persicaria agrestis, commonly known as common smartweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and roadside ditches. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with five pink or red petals. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in disturbed habitats.

Persicaria agrestis is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its characteristic swollen nodes along the stems.
