Potamogeton × mucronulatus

Potamogeton × mucronulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parental species.

It is typically found in freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually narrow and tapering, often with a slightly pointed tip. It produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its growth is influenced by water quality and light availability, making it an indicator species for certain aquatic environments.
