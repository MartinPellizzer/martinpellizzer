Panicum bicknellii var. calliphyllum

Panicum bicknellii var. calliphyllum, commonly known as the Bicknell's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
