Primula tschuktschorum subsp. cairnesiana

Primula tschuktschorum subsp.

cairnesiana, commonly known as the Cairnes' primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
