Primulina suichuanensis

Primulina suichuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan primula, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that come in various shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Primulina suichuanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
