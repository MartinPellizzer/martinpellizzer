Porophyllum ruderale var. ellipticum

Porophyllum ruderale var. ellipticum, commonly known as the Mexican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

This species is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
