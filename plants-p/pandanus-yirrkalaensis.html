Pandanus yirrkalaensis

Pandanus yirrkalaensis, commonly known as the Yirrkala pandanus, is a species native to the northern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that grow in a rosette formation, often reaching several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, white flowers. Its fibrous leaves have been traditionally used by Indigenous communities for weaving and basket-making.

The species is adapted to coastal and inland environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
