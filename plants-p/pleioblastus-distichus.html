Pleioblastus distichus

Pleioblastus distichus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in two rows. This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Pleioblastus distichus is also valued for its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
