Prunus glandulosa var. rosea

Prunus glandulosa var. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn reddish in autumn, and it produces small, pink to white flowers in early spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The species is also noted for its fragrant blossoms and potential use in traditional medicine.
