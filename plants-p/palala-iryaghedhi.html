Palala iryaghedhi

Palala iryaghedhi, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a unique botanical species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soil.

Palala iryaghedhi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
