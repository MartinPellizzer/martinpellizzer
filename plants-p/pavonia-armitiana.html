Pavonia armitiana

Pavonia armitiana, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and stellate hairs on the stems and leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
