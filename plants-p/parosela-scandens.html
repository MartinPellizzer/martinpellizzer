Parosela scandens

Parosela scandens, commonly known as the climbing parosela, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The leaves are pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal in gardens.
