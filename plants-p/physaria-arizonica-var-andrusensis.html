Physaria arizonica var. andrusensis

Physaria arizonica var. andrusensis, commonly known as the Arizona bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a bluish-green hue with a hairy texture.
