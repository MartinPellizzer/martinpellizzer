Piper nigrum

Piper nigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is a flowering vine native to South Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is cultivated for its dark, aromatic berries. The fruit, called a peppercorn, contains the dried, unripe fruit used as a spice. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the peppercorns.
