Phyteuma spicatum subsp. jurassicum

Phyteuma spicatum subsp.

jurassicum, commonly known as the Jurassic phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate to alpine climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
