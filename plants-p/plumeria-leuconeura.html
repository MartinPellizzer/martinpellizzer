Plumeria leuconeura

Plumeria leuconeura, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers have a sweet, fragrant aroma and are used in cultural and religious ceremonies in some regions.
