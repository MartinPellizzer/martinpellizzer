Pinus cernua

Pinus cernua, commonly known as the creeping pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit, with dense clusters of needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its root system is highly adaptable, allowing it to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils.

Pinus cernua plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
