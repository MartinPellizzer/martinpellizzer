Polygala spathulata

Polygala spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and flies.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.
