Physostemon procumbens subsp. obtusa

Physostemon procumbens subsp.

obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf yellowroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often broader than those of the typical species.

The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is notable for its yellowish color and has been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland settings.
