Pilosella piloselloides subsp. magyarica

Pilosella piloselloides subsp.

magyarica, commonly known as the Hungarian hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, showing a preference for temperate climates. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological and genetic traits compared to other Pilosella species.

It is generally a low-maintenance plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
