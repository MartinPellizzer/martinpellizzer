Piper depressibaccum

Piper depressibaccum, commonly known as the low-growing pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
