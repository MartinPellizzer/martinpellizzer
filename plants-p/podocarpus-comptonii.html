Podocarpus comptonii

Podocarpus comptonii, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its tolerance of urban conditions.

Podocarpus comptonii is also valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in various cultures.
