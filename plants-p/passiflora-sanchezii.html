Passiflora sanchezii

Passiflora sanchezii, commonly known as the Sanche's passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora sanchezii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
