Pycnanthemum leptodon

Pycnanthemum leptodon, commonly known as slenderhead beeplant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Pycnanthemum leptodon is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
