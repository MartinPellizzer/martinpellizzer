Pinus spectabilis

Pinus spectabilis, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elegant, slender form and its distinctive silvery-blue foliage. The tree produces cones that are typically 4 to 6 inches long, containing edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, providing a unique texture.

The silver fir is often found in high-elevation forests and is valued for its ornamental beauty and timber.
