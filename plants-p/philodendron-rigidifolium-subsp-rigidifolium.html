Philodendron rigidifolium subsp. rigidifolium

Philodendron rigidifolium subsp.

rigidifolium, commonly known as the rigid-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America. It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture.

The plant has a strong, woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit. Its leaves are often rigid and stiff, giving it its common name.

It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.
