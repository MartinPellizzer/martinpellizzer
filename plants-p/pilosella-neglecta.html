Pilosella neglecta

Pilosella neglecta, commonly known as the common whitlowgrass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense mats of foliage and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "pilosella," meaning "hairy.". It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in meadows.

Pilosella neglecta is generally hardy and adaptable, making it a common sight in various habitats across its range.
