Piper parvantherum

Piper parvantherum, commonly known as small-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Piper parvantherum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
