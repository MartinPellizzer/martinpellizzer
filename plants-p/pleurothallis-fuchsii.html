Pleurothallis fuchsii

Pleurothallis fuchsii, commonly known as the Fuchs's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Pleurothallis fuchsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
