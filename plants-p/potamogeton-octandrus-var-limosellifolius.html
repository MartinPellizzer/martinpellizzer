Potamogeton octandrus var. limosellifolius

Potamogeton octandrus var. limosellifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

This variant is closely related to other species of the Potamogeton genus and is adapted to nutrient-rich, muddy substrates.
