Phleum godronis

Phleum godronis, commonly known as Godron's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
