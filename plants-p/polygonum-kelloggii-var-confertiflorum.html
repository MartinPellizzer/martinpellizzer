Polygonum kelloggii var. confertiflorum

Polygonum kelloggii var. confertiflorum, commonly known as Kellogg's smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants characterized by their distinctive leaf shapes and bead-like fruits. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish or purple tint, and it produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
