Piper turbense

Piper turbense, commonly known as the Turbense pepper or Turbense pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that are typically reddish when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance in botanical gardens.
