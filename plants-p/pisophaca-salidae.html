Pisophaca salidae

Pisophaca salidae, commonly known as the Salidae's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant has a unique mycorrhizal relationship with specific fungi, which is essential for its survival and nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pisophaca salidae is considered critically endangered and is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists.
