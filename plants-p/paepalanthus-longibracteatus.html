Paepalanthus longibracteatus

Paepalanthus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract grass.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family, native to South America, particularly Brazil. This species is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically at the base of the stem.

It is often found in grassland habitats and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
