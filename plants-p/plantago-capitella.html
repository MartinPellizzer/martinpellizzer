Plantago capitella

Plantago capitella, commonly known as the clustered plantain, is a small, herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a rosette of broad, oval leaves that are hairy and have a waxy coating. Its flowering structure consists of a dense cluster of small, greenish flowers surrounded by bracts.

The seeds are contained in small, capsule-like structures that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
