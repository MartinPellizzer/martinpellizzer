Pelargonium trachyphyllum

Pelargonium trachyphyllum, commonly known as the roughleaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Pelargonium trachyphyllum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
