Pilosocereus lindaianus

Pilosocereus lindaianus, commonly known as the Lindaian Pilocereus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and a dense covering of white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its root system is shallow and adapted to quickly absorb rainfall.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
