Protea crassifolia

Protea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in shrubland and fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid or semi-arid habitat.

Protea crassifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
