Pyrus mars

Pyrus mars, commonly known as the Mars pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears. The leaves are broad and have a slightly serrated edge, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
