Phoebe obtusa

Phoebe obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a glossy appearance. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by dark, shiny berries.

Phoebe obtusa is often found in wetland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
