Phoenix reclinata var. madagascariensis

Phoenix reclinata var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar date palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a tall, slender palm with gracefully arching fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in the region.
