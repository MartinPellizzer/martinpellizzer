Petagnana

Petagnana, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery fuzz. Its growth is slow and requires cool, humid conditions to thrive.

Petagnana is not only valued for its beauty but also for its cultural and medicinal significance among indigenous communities.
