Pycreus intermedius

Pycreus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate pycreus, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow, bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.

The root system of Pycreus intermedius is fibrous and contributes to its adaptability in different soil types.
