Phaeopappus dichrous

Phaeopappus dichrous, commonly known as the black pappus.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its distinctive dark, velvety leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study.
