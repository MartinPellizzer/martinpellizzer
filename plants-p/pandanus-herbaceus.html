Pandanus herbaceus

Pandanus herbaceus, commonly known as the common pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in spikes, which can be yellow or red in color. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in coastal and wetland environments.

Pandanus herbaceus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
