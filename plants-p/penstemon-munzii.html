Penstemon munzii

Penstemon munzii, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

Penstemon munzii is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
