Pleurospermum govanianum

Pleurospermum govanianum, commonly known as the Indian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of South Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
