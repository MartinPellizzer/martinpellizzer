Plantago tomentosa subsp. affinis

Plantago tomentosa subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and upright growth habit.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their mild astringent properties.
