Ptychosperma furcatum

Ptychosperma furcatum, commonly known as the fan-leaf cycad, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a stout, unbranched stem that is often covered in a rough, fibrous outer layer. Its cones are typically borne on separate plants, with male and female cones developing on different individuals.

Ptychosperma furcatum is a slow-growing species that requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
