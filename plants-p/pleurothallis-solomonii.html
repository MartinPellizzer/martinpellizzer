Pleurothallis solomonii

Pleurothallis solomonii, commonly known as Solomon's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it thrive in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis solomonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare orchid species.
