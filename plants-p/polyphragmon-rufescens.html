Polyphragmon rufescens

Polyphragmon rufescens, commonly known as the red-fringed phragmon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with a distinctive red or purple fringe. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
