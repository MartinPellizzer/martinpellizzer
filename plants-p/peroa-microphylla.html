Peroa microphylla

Peroa microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved peroa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are densely packed along its stems. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Peroa microphylla is often found in high-altitude grasslands and is adapted to the cool, temperate climate of its native habitat.
