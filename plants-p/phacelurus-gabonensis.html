Phacelurus gabonensis

Phacelurus gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the rainforests of Gabon.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with elongated, wiry stems and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of dense tropical forests. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are notable for their intricate patterns and vibrant colors, ranging from white to pink and purple.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Phacelurus gabonensis is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
