Phlox austromontana subsp. vera

Phlox austromontana subsp.

vera, commonly known as the Southern Mountain Phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
