Primula × bosniaka

Primula × bosniaka, commonly known as the Bosnian primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula auricula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region, particularly Bosnia and Herzegovina. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

Primula × bosniaka thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
