Papaver nyingchiense

Papaver nyingchiense, commonly known as the Nyingchi poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Yarlung Zangbo River region in Tibet.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive, bulbous seed capsule. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
