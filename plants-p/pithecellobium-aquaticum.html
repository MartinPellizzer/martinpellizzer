Pithecellobium aquaticum

Pithecellobium aquaticum, commonly known as the water acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in moist, aquatic environments.
