Phoebe pandurata

Phoebe pandurata, commonly known as the pandurata phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Phoebe pandurata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
