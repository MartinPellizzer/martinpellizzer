Plectranthus rosulatus

Plectranthus rosulatus, commonly known as the silver bush or woolly mint, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white fuzz, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both gardens and indoor cultivation.
