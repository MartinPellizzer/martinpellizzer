Potentilla supina var. typica

Potentilla supina var. typica, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features wiry, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval to ovate, with a rough, serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.
