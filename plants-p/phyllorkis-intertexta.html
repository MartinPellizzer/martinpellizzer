Phyllorkis intertexta

Phyllorkis intertexta, commonly known as the Australian wattlevine, is a climbing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
