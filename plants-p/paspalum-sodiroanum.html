Paspalum sodiroanum

Paspalum sodiroanum, commonly known as the sodiroanum grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with pale green to reddish bracts.

Paspalum sodiroanum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
