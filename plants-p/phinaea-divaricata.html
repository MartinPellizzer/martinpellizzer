Phinaea divaricata

Phinaea divaricata, commonly known as the variable phinaea, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phinaea divaricata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.
