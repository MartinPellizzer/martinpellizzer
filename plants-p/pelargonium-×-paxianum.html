Pelargonium × paxianum

Pelargonium × paxianum, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a hybrid species originating from crosses between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium zonale.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features broad, lobed leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, spicy scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
