Paspalum barbatum var. pellitum

Paspalum barbatum var. pellitum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
