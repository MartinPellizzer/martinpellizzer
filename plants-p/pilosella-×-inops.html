Pilosella × inops

Pilosella × inops, commonly known as the common yarrow or everlastings, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pilosella auricula and Pilosella lactiflora. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its fibrous roots and low-growing structure make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
