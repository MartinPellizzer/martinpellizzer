Polygala persicariifolia var. latifolia

Polygala persicariifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow from the base, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pale pink or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
