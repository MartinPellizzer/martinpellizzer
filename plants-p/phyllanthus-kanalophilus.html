Phyllanthus kanalophilus

Phyllanthus kanalophilus, commonly known as the kanal or kanal plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wet or moist environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries as fruit.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
