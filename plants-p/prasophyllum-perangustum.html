Prasophyllum perangustum

Prasophyllum perangustum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved leek orchid, is an endemic species found in the south-eastern regions of Australia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in heathland and open forest environments. The plant features a slender, grass-like stem with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
