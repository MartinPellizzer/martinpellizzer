Philadelphus lewisii var. parvifolius

Philadelphus lewisii var. parvifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silverbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, arching branches and small, elliptical leaves that are typically silver-white underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
