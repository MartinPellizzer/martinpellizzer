Penstemon procerus subsp. tolmiei

Penstemon procerus subsp.

tolmiei, commonly known as Tolmian beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies thrives in rocky soils and dry, sunny environments, making it well adapted to arid conditions.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
