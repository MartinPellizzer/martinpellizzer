Polypodium tunguraguae

Polypodium tunguraguae, commonly known as the Tunguragua huperzia, is a species of fern native to South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It is characterized by its tall, erect fronds that can reach up to two meters in height, making it one of the tallest ferns in the world. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores in specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for conservation efforts.
