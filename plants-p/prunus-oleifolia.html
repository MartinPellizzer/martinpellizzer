Prunus oleifolia

Prunus oleifolia, commonly known as the oliveleaf cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are aromatic and contain oils that have been used in traditional medicine.

Prunus oleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
