Passiflora eberhardtii

Passiflora eberhardtii, commonly known as the Eberhardt passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance, attracting a variety of pollinators.
