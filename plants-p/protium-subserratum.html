Protium subserratum

Protium subserratum, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
