Puya carnea

Puya carnea, commonly known as the naked lady, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It produces a distinctive scent that attracts pollinators such as moths and bees.

Puya carnea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in garden settings.
