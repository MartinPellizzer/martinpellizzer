Pelargonium × tanacetifolium

Pelargonium × tanacetifolium, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its finely cut, fern-like leaves that emit a strong lemon fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and pleasant aroma.

Pelargonium × tanacetifolium is also used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
