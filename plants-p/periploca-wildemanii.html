Periploca wildemanii

Periploca wildemanii, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a white, fleshy aril. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
