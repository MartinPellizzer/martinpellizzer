Prunus mutabilis f. linearisepala

Prunus mutabilis f. linearisepala, commonly known as the linear-leaf cherry, is a unique variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

This plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that give it a distinct appearance compared to the standard form. It produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

While it is less commonly cultivated than other cherry species, it is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
