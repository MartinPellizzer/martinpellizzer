Pilea baltenweckii

Pilea baltenweckii, commonly known as the Baltenweck's nerve plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea baltenweckii is a popular houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
