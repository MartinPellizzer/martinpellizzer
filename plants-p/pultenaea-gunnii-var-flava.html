Pultenaea gunnii var. flava

Pultenaea gunnii var. flava, commonly known as the yellow bush pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, persistent leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
