Pulsatilla sulphurea

Pulsatilla sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces follicles that contain the seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in many regions.
