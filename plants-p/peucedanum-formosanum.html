Peucedanum formosanum

Peucedanum formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like flower clusters.

Native to Taiwan, this plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinct licorice-like scent, while the flowers are small and white, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemistry.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
