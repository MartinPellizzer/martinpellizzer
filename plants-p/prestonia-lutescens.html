Prestonia lutescens

Prestonia lutescens, commonly known as the yellow prestonia, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal.

Prestonia lutescens is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
