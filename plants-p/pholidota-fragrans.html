Pholidota fragrans

Pholidota fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented magnolia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has broad, glossy leaves and a tree-like structure, often reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its flowers are highly aromatic, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The bark is smooth and light gray, with a distinctive scent when crushed.
