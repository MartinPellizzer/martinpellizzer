Porophyllum amplexicaule

Porophyllum amplexicaule, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often clasping, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and blue or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
