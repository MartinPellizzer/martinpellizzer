Paphiopedilum micranthum subsp. eburneum

Paphiopedilum micranthum subsp.

eburneum, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and a rosette of broad, leathery leaves.

The plant produces a single, showy inflorescence with a few pale yellow to white flowers, characterized by a unique lip that is often slightly curled. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, long-lasting blooms.
