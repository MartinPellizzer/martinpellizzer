Pteris elliptica

Pteris elliptica, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical, and often leathery fronds that are bipinnate, giving it a delicate and intricate appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pteris elliptica is valued for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and its aesthetic appeal in both indoor and outdoor settings.
