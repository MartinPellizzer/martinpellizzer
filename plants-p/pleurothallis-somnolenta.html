Pleurothallis somnolenta

Pleurothallis somnolenta, commonly known as the sleepy orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air. Its flowers are usually small, with a variety of colors, and it blooms during the wet season.

Pleurothallis somnolenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
