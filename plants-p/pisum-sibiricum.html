Pisum sibiricum

Pisum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian pea shrub, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source for livestock. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

This plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
