Polygonum meisnerianum

Polygonum meisnerianum, commonly known as Meisner's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Polygonum meisnerianum is part of the Polygonaceae family, which is known for its distinctive bracteate flowers and often edible seeds.
