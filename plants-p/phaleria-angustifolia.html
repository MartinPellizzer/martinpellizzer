Phaleria angustifolia

Phaleria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phaleria, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
