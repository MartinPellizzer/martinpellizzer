Piper calophyllum

Piper calophyllum, commonly known as the aromatic pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that give way to elongated, red berries. The berries are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor, making them valuable in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
