Pilea acuminata

Pilea acuminata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically heart-shaped or rounded. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea acuminata thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
