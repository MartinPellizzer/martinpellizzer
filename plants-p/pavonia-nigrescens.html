Pavonia nigrescens

Pavonia nigrescens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia nigrescens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
