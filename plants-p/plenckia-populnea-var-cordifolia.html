Plenckia populnea var. cordifolia

Plenckia populnea var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf poplar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and serrated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
