Psyllium scabrum

Psyllium scabrum, commonly known as isabgol, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a distinctive seed capsule that contains tiny, mucilaginous seeds. These seeds are rich in soluble fiber and are widely used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.

The botanical features of Psyllium scabrum make it valuable for its therapeutic and nutritional properties.
