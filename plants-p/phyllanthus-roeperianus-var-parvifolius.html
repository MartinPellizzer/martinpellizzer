Phyllanthus roeperianus var. parvifolius

Phyllanthus roeperianus var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved roeper's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree with opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically small and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are often red or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structures.
