Pectis violacea

Pectis violacea, commonly known as violet pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Pectis violacea is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
