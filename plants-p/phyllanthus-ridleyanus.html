Phyllanthus ridleyanus

Phyllanthus ridleyanus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually alternate and have a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange to red berries. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
