Pseudolysimachion ovatum

Pseudolysimachion ovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed lily, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features strap-like leaves that are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
