Primula elatior var. pallasii

Primula elatior var. pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features basal rosettes of broad, ovate, hairy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or lilac, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
