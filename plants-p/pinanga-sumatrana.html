Pinanga sumatrana

Pinanga sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and elongated, pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and swamps, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and landscape gardening.

However, it requires careful attention to soil drainage and light conditions to flourish.
