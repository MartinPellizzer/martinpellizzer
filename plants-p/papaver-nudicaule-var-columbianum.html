Papaver nudicaule var. columbianum

Papaver nudicaule var. columbianum, commonly known as the Columbian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Papaveraceae family, characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers, which range from white to pink to red, bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its delicate, papery petals and its historical use in traditional medicine.
