Panicum arnottianum

Panicum arnottianum, commonly known as Arnott's Panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in areas with high moisture levels.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
