Passiflora pottiae

Passiflora pottiae, commonly known as the Pott's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Passiflora pottiae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
