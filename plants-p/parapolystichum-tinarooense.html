Parapolystichum tinarooense

Parapolystichum tinarooense, commonly known as the Tinaroo fern, is a rare and endemic fern species native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dense undergrowth. Its spore-producing structures, called sorangia, are clustered on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its unique reproductive strategy.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native region.
