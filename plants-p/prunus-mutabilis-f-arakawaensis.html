Prunus mutabilis f. arakawaensis

Prunus mutabilis f. arakawaensis, commonly known as the Arakawa cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its striking, variable flower colors that range from white to pink and even red, depending on environmental conditions. The plant is a small deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its flowers appear in early spring, often before the leaves, creating a dramatic visual display.

The fruit, when it develops, is a small, sweet cherry that ripens in late summer.
