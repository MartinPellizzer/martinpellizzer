Pilocarpus longeracemosus

Pilocarpus longeracemosus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its leaves and fruits contain compounds with medicinal properties, including pilocarpine, used in various pharmaceutical applications.
