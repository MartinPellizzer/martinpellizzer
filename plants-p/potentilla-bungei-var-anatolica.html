Potentilla bungei var. anatolica

Potentilla bungei var. anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for alpine gardens. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
