Pausia odorata

Pausia odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive, sweet aroma that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Pausia odorata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
