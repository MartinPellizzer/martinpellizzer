Persicaria rudis

Persicaria rudis, commonly known as water hemp, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Persicaria rudis is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America, where it can become invasive.

It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
