Potentilla frigida var. suffruticosa

Potentilla frigida var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It has small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant features a compact, woody stem with alternate, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
