Paeonia albiflora var. humei

Paeonia albiflora var. humei, commonly known as the white peony or Japanese white peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a subtle pinkish hue, which bloom in early summer. The plant features compound, pinnate leaves that are dark green and hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its fragrance and historical significance in traditional medicine.
