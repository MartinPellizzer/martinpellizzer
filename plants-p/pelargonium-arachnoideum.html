Pelargonium arachnoideum

Pelargonium arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider plant or woolly geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a fuzzy appearance, and its stems are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
