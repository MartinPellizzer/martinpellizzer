Potamogeton lucens var. decipiens

Potamogeton lucens var. decipiens, commonly known as the false pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
