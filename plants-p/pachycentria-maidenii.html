Pachycentria maidenii

Pachycentria maidenii, commonly known as the Maiden's thick-centred orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive thickened central column, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open forest or heathland environments. Pachycentria maidenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to thrive.
