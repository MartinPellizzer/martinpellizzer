Populus mexicana subsp. mexicana

Populus mexicana subsp.

mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
