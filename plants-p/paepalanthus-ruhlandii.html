Paepalanthus ruhlandii

Paepalanthus ruhlandii, commonly known as the Ruhland's pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a spherical or flattened shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered near the top of the stems. It is well adapted to arid environments, with reduced leaves and thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
