Pteronia dentata

Pteronia dentata, commonly known as the serrated pteronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pteronia dentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.
