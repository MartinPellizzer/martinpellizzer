Prinos lucidus

Prinos lucidus, commonly known as the shining prinos, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds, aiding in its dispersal by animals.

Prinos lucidus is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
