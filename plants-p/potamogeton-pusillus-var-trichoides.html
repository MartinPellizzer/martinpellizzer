Potamogeton pusillus var. trichoides

Potamogeton pusillus var. trichoides, commonly known as the hairy water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branched stems that are often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. It flowers with tiny, clustered spikes of green to brown flowers, which are adapted for underwater pollination.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
