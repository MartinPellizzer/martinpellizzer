Potentilla lespedeza

Potentilla lespedeza, commonly known as Lespedeza, is a genus of flowering plants in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to various regions in North America and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically produces yellow or reddish flowers and has serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Lespedeza species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to serve as a food source for wildlife.
