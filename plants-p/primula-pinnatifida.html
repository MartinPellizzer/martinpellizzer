Primula pinnatifida

Primula pinnatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
