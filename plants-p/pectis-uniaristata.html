Pectis uniaristata

Pectis uniaristata, commonly known as the single-bract pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces showy yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, arranged alternately along the stem.

Pectis uniaristata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
