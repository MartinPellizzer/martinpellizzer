Pentaphylloides dryadanthoides

Pentaphylloides dryadanthoides, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in whorls and have a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes, followed by woody, bell-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
