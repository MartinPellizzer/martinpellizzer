Pyrus obliqua

Pyrus obliqua, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The fruit is a pear-like apple, typically yellow or green when ripe, and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which has a sweet and aromatic flavor.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and orchards.
