Pinanga insignis var. gasterocarpa

Pinanga insignis var. gasterocarpa, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
