Primula algida var. armena

Primula algida var. armena, commonly known as Armenian primrose, is a cold-hardy perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of broad, oval leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in cool climates.
