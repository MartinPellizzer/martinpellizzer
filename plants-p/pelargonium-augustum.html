Pelargonium augustum

Pelargonium augustum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

Pelargonium augustum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
