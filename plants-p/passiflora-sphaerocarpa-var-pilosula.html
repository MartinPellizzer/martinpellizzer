Passiflora sphaerocarpa var. pilosula

Passiflora sphaerocarpa var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves and clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant produces an orange to yellow fruit with a thick, aromatic rind and a sweet, juicy pulp. The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has a mild, tropical flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
