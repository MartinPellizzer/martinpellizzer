Packera umbraculifera

Packera umbraculifera, commonly known as the shadowy fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a grayish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows.

Packera umbraculifera is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
