Pteris villosa

Pteris villosa, commonly known as the hairy brake, is a species of fern native to Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Pteris villosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
