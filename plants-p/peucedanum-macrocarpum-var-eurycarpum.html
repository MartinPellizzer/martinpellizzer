Peucedanum macrocarpum var. eurycarpum

Peucedanum macrocarpum var. eurycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its fruits are distinctive, being broad and ribbed, with a hairy surface.

This species is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
