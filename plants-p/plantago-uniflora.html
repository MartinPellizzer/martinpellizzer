Plantago uniflora

Plantago uniflora, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, clustered greenish flowers that are often hidden beneath the leaves. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
