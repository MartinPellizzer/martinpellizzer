Peltopus subapetalus

Peltopus subapetalus, commonly known as the lesser starwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant has basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Peltopus subapetalus is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
