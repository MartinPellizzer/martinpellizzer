Phyllodes umbrosa

Phyllodes umbrosa, commonly known as the dark-leaf phyllodinea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its unique, modified leaf structures called phyllodes, which are flattened and often mistaken for leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a range of colors from white to pink.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
