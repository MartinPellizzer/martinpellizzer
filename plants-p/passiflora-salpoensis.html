Passiflora salpoensis

Passiflora salpoensis, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns of red and green. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Passiflora salpoensis is valued for both its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
