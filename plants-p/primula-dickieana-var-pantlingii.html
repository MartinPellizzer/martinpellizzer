Primula dickieana var. pantlingii

Primula dickieana var. pantlingii, commonly known as the Pantling's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale pink blooms during the spring season, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.
