Potamogeton pectinatus f. zosteraceus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. zosteraceus, commonly known as the ribbonweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that often form dense, ribbon-like structures. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and streams. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a smooth, waxy surface.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
