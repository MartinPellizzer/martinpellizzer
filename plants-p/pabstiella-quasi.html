Pabstiella quasi

Pabstiella quasi, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air, soil, and rain.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered in many areas.
