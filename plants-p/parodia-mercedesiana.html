Parodia mercedesiana

Parodia mercedesiana, commonly known as the Mercedes cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a small, globular plant with a ribbed structure and a dense covering of white to grayish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors.
