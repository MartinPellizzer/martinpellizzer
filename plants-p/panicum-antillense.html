Panicum antillense

Panicum antillense, commonly known as the Caribbean grass or antillean panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along coastal regions.

Panicum antillense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in tropical environments.
