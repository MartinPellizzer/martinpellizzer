Psoralea bipedunculata

Psoralea bipedunculata, commonly known as the two-stemmed psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Psoralea bipedunculata is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
