Potamogeton heterophyllus var. graminifolius

Potamogeton heterophyllus var. graminifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its submerged growth habit, it is often difficult to detect until it reaches the flowering stage.
