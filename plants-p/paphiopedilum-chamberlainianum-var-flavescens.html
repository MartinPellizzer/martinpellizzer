Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum var. flavescens

Paphiopedilum chamberlainianum var. flavescens, commonly known as the Yellow-flowered Lady's Slipper, is a rare and exotic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, producing a fragrant scent that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
