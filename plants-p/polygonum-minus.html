Polygonum minus

Polygonum minus, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streambanks, wetlands, and disturbed areas. Its stems are usually reddish and hairy, while the leaves are narrow and have a slightly serrated edge.

Polygonum minus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
