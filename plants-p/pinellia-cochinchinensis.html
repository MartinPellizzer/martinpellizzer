Pinellia cochinchinensis

Pinellia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Chinese snakeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spatulate-shaped flowers. The plant produces a corm-like structure that serves as its storage organ and propagative part. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pinellia cochinchinensis is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
