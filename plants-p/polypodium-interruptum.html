Polypodium interruptum

Polypodium interruptum, commonly known as the interrupted fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the southeastern United States.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with fronds that are bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a distinctive interrupted pattern along the veins.

Polypodium interruptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
