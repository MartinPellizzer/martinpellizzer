Pecteilis elliptica

Pecteilis elliptica, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Pecteilis elliptica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
