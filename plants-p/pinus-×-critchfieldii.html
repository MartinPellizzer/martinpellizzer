Pinus × critchfieldii

Pinus × critchfieldii, commonly known as the Critchfield's pine, is a hybrid conifer native to the southwestern United States.

It is a cross between Pinus edulis (pinyon pine) and Pinus monophylla (single-leaf pinyon). This plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is characterized by its short, thick needles and cone-like structures that mature to a dark brown color.

The tree is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and adaptability to dry climates.
