Pulicaria canariensis var. lanata

Pulicaria canariensis var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly pulicaria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its unique texture and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for horticultural collections.
