Pseudocymopterus tidestromii

Pseudocymopterus tidestromii, commonly known as the Tidestrom's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and unique floral structure, which includes a single, showy flower with a distinctive coloration pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or near water bodies. Its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements make it highly sensitive to habitat disturbance.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
