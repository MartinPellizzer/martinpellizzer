Pterolobium membranulaceum

Pterolobium membranulaceum, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a small deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its hairy, membranous leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flowers followed by flat, brown pods containing edible seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber and forage.
