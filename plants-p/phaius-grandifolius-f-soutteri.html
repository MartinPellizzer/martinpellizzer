Phaius grandifolius f. soutteri

Phaius grandifolius f. soutteri, commonly known as the Soutter's giant orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 50 cm in length, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as rainforests or marshy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of up to 20 flowers, each with a pale yellow or greenish hue and a prominent lip.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
