Psacalium goldsmithii

Psacalium goldsmithii, commonly known as the golden star hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Psacalium goldsmithii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
