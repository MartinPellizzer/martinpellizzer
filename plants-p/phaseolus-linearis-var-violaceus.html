Phaseolus linearis var. violaceus

Phaseolus linearis var. violaceus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, indeterminate vines and vibrant purple to violet flowers. The pods are typically green and elongated, containing edible seeds that are rich in protein. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.

Its growth habit and coloration make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and botanical study.
