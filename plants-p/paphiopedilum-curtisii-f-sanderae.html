Paphiopedilum curtisii f. sanderae

Paphiopedilum curtisii f. sanderae, commonly known as the Sanderae Lady's Slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This hybrid is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil, and requires specific temperature and light conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
