Paepalanthus ramosus var. affinis

Paepalanthus ramosus var. affinis, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, seed-like fruits called cypsela, which are dispersed by wind.
