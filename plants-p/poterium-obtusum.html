Poterium obtusum

Poterium obtusum, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled, spike-like fruits.

Poterium obtusum is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.
