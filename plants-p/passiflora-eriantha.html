Passiflora eriantha

Passiflora eriantha, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, sweet berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora eriantha is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential calming and sedative properties.
