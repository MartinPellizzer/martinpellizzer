Protea cartilaginea

Protea cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilaginous protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers that resemble a cluster of small, rounded blooms. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its unique morphology and striking appearance make it a popular ornamental plant.

Protea cartilaginea is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
