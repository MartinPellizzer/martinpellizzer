Potentilla arenaria

Potentilla arenaria, commonly known as sand cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy, woody stems. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its leaves are typically divided into five lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It plays an important role in stabilizing soils in its native habitats.
