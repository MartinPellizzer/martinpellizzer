Paspalum peruvianum

Paspalum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and riparian zones. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its tolerance to periodic flooding.

Paspalum peruvianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
