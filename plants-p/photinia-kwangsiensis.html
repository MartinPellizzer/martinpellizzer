Photinia kwangsiensis

Photinia kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
