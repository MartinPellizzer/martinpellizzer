Paspalum commersonii

Paspalum commersonii, commonly known as Commerson's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Paspalum commersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in both wet and dry conditions.
