Philodendron callifolium

Philodendron callifolium, commonly known as the giant philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant has a robust stem that can climb to great heights, often reaching several meters in the wild. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties exhibiting variegation.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
