Pipturus acuminatissimus

Pipturus acuminatissimus, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
