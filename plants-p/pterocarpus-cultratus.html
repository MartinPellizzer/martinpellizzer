Pterocarpus cultratus

Pterocarpus cultratus, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds that are used in traditional crafts. Its bark and wood are valued for their medicinal properties and durability.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
