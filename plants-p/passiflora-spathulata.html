Passiflora spathulata

Passiflora spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, colorful flowers that resemble a crown. The flowers typically feature five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive central crown of filaments. It produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in nutrients.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
