Polygala linoides var. linoides

Polygala linoides var. linoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and are characteristic of the Polygala genus.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
