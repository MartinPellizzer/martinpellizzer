Papaver macrostomum

Papaver macrostomum, commonly known as the large-mouth poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its capsule-like fruit contains numerous small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
