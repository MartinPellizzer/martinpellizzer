Pseudolysimachion linariifolium var. villosulum

Pseudolysimachion linariifolium var. villosulum, commonly known as the hairy leaf linariifolium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
