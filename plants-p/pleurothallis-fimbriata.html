Pleurothallis fimbriata

Pleurothallis fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its inflorescence is often adorned with a unique, fringed structure that gives it its common name.

Pleurothallis fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
