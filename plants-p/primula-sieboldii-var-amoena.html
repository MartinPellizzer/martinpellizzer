Primula sieboldii var. amoena

Primula sieboldii var. amoena, commonly known as the Amoena primrose, is a flowering plant native to East Asia, particularly Japan and China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in woodland or rocky habitats and prefers cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a distinct serrated margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
