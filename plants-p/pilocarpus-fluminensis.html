Pilocarpus fluminensis

Pilocarpus fluminensis, commonly known as jaborandi, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of southeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

Pilocarpus fluminensis is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
