Pholidota imbricata var. montana

Pholidota imbricata var. montana, commonly known as the mountain pholidota, is a variant of the pholidota plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark, leathery leaves that are often adorned with distinctive patterns. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
