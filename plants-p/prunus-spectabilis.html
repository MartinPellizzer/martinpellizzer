Prunus spectabilis

Prunus spectabilis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. Its fruit is a small, hard drupe that matures in late summer.

Prunus spectabilis is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value and cultural significance.
