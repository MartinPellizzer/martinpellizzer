Pelea cinerea var. racemiflora

Pelea cinerea var. racemiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf pelea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
