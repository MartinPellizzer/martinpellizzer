Potamogeton gramineus var. heterophyllus

Potamogeton gramineus var. heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, which can range from narrow and grass-like to broad and ribbon-like. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its adaptability to different water conditions makes it a common and widespread species in ponds and lakes.
