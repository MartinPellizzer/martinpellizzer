Phragmites vulgaris var. mauritianus

Phragmites vulgaris var. mauritianus, commonly known as the Mauritius reed, is a variant of the common reed that is native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of fluffy, white flowers during the summer months. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet conditions make it a significant component of coastal and freshwater ecosystems.
