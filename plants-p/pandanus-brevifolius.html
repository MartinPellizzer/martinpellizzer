Pandanus brevifolius

Pandanus brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its stiff, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in branched inflorescences. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional practices and for making thatch and mats.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
