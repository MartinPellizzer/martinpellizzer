Plectranthus radiatus

Plectranthus radiatus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or lavender in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators.
