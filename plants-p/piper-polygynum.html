Piper polygynum

Piper polygynum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
