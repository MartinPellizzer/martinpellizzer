Premna fortunati

Premna fortunati, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a tropical shrub belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It is native to regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands, and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Premna fortunati is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
