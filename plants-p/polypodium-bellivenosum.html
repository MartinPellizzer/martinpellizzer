Polypodium bellivenosum

Polypodium bellivenosum, commonly known as the Bellivenosum fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into narrow, linear segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile leaves, which are clustered in sori surrounded by a protective indusium. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
