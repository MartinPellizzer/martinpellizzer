Patagonium brachycarpum

Patagonium brachycarpum, commonly known as the Patagonian dwarf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Patagonium brachycarpum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
