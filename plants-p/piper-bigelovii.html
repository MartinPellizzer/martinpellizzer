Piper bigelovii

Piper bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by three-lobed, berry-like fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and unique growth form.
