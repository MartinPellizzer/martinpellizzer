Penstemon newberryi var. berryi

Penstemon newberryi var. berryi, commonly known as Newberry's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
