Polygala madagascariensis

Polygala madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and restricted distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
