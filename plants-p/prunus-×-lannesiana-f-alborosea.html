Prunus × lannesiana f. alborosea

Prunus × lannesiana f. alborosea, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus genus, characterized by its striking white petals.

This cultivar is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus incisa, resulting in unique floral characteristics. The plant typically features small, oval leaves with a glossy surface and a compact growth habit. It blooms in early spring, producing clusters of fragrant, white flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal display.
