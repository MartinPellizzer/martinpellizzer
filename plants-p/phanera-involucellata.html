Phanera involucellata

Phanera involucellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Phanera involucellata is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
