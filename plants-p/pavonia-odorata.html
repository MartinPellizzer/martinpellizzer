Pavonia odorata

Pavonia odorata, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with woody stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
