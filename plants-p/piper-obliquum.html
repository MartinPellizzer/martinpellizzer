Piper obliquum

Piper obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
