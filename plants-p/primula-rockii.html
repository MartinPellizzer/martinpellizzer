Primula rockii

Primula rockii, commonly known as the Yunnan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often hairy and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Primula rockii is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, moist conditions.
