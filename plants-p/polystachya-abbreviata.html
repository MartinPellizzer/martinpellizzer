Polystachya abbreviata

Polystachya abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stalked clubmoss, is a species of fern-like plant belonging to the family Polystachyaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical clusters known as strobila. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
