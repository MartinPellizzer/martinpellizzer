Poa laxa var. purpurascens

Poa laxa var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and pastures. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
