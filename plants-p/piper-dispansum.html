Piper dispansum

Piper dispansum, commonly known as the African pepper or black pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits.

The fruits are typically harvested when mature, and they contain small, dark seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
