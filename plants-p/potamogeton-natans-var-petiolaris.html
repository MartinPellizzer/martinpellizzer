Potamogeton natans var. petiolaris

Potamogeton natans var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in dense clusters near the water's surface. Its stems are flexible and often form dense underwater stands, contributing to aquatic habitat structure.

It plays a role in water filtration and provides food and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
