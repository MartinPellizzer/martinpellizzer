Psidium donianum

Psidium donianum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or donian guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a unique flavor. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Psidium donianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in its native habitat.
