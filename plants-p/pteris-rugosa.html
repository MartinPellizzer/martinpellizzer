Pteris rugosa

Pteris rugosa, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy fronds that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with a rugged, textured appearance.

Pteris rugosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
