Prunus ferganensis

Prunus ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible drupes that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Prunus ferganensis is also valued for its potential in agroforestry and traditional medicine.
