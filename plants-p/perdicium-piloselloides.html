Perdicium piloselloides

Perdicium piloselloides, commonly known as the hairy false hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowering stems rise from the base, bearing yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Perdicium piloselloides is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
