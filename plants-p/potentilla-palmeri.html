Potentilla palmeri

Potentilla palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in clusters, blooming in early spring.

It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
