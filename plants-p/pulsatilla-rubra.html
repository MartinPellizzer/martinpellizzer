Pulsatilla rubra

Pulsatilla rubra, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers consist of several petal-like structures surrounding a central cluster of yellow stamens.

Pulsatilla rubra is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil.
