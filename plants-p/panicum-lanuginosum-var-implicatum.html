Panicum lanuginosum var. implicatum

Panicum lanuginosum var. implicatum, commonly known as silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and panicle-like inflorescences that are often adorned with delicate, feathery flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a fine, silky texture and small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in a loose, branching pattern.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
