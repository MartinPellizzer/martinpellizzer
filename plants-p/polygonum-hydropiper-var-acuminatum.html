Polygonum hydropiper var. acuminatum

Polygonum hydropiper var. acuminatum, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
