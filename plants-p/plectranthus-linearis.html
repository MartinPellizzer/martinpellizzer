Plectranthus linearis

Plectranthus linearis, commonly known as the linear leaf purpleweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus linearis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
