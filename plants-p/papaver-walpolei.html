Papaver walpolei

Papaver walpolei, commonly known as the Walpole poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a black or purple central blotch. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Papaver walpolei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
