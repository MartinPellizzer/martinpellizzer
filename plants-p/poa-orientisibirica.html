Poa orientisibirica

Poa orientisibirica, commonly known as the Siberian meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and tundra. The plant has fine, slender leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and poor soil nutrients.

Poa orientisibirica plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native range.
