Phytolacca dodecandra var. pubescens

Phytolacca dodecandra var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy Indian currant, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
