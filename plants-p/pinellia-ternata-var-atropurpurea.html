Pinellia ternata var. atropurpurea

Pinellia ternata var. atropurpurea, commonly known as purple-veined pinellia, is a variant of the pinellia plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple veins on the leaves, which set it apart from other varieties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its rhizomes are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The flowers are small and clustered, adding ornamental value to the plant.
