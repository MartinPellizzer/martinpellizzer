Prunus cerasus ranunculifera

Prunus cerasus ranunculifera, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yamazakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental white or pale pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it produces small, sweet fruits known as cherries.

This species is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.
