Peramium macranthum

Peramium macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered peramium, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its roots are fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Native to tropical regions, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its natural habitat.
