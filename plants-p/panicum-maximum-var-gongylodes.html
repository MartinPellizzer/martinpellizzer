Panicum maximum var. gongylodes

Panicum maximum var. gongylodes, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tropical perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth and dense foliage, with long, narrow leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It is well adapted to warm climates and is often used for forage in livestock farming.

Its rapid growth and high biomass make it an important species in grassland ecosystems.
