Phaca bustillosii

Phaca bustillosii, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Phaca bustillosii is valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of food for local wildlife.
