Poa klokovii

Poa klokovii, commonly known as the Klokov's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
