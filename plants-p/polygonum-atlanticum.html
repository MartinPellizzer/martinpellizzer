Polygonum atlanticum

Polygonum atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic knotweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along shorelines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum atlanticum is notable for its ability to tolerate salt spray and wind, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
