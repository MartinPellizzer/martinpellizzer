Primula glaucescens var. longobarda

Primula glaucescens var. longobarda, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features semi-evergreen foliage with a bluish-green hue, and its flowers are typically white to pale yellow, often with a greenish tinge. The plant produces showy, clustered blooms that appear in early spring, adding ornamental value to alpine gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
