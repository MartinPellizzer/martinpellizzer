Palaquium cochleariifolium

Palaquium cochleariifolium, commonly known as the earleaf palaquium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes resembling a snail's shell. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, spherical berries. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
