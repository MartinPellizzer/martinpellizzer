Pelargonium alchemillifolium

Pelargonium alchemillifolium, commonly known as the alchemilla-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often bi-colored flowers that range from white to pink and red. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its foliage is often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for its beauty and ease of care.
