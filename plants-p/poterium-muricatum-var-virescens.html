Poterium muricatum var. virescens

Poterium muricatum var. virescens, commonly known as the green-hairy poinsettia, is a variant of the poinsettia species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant green bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a unique appearance.
