Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. transsilvanicum

Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
