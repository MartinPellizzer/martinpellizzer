Picea alcoquiana

Picea alcoquiana, commonly known as the Alcoquian spruce, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Picea alcoquiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
