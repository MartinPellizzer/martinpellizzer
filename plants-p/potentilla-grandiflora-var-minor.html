Potentilla grandiflora var. minor

Potentilla grandiflora var. minor, commonly known as dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant, hardy plants.
