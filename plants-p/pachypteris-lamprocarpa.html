Pachypteris lamprocarpa

Pachypteris lamprocarpa, commonly known as the glossy-leaf tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor and absorb nutrients from the soil. Its leaves are typically glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
