Phrynium reniforme

Phrynium reniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist, shaded environments.

Phrynium reniforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
