Padus alabamensis

Padus alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The Alabama hawthorn is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
