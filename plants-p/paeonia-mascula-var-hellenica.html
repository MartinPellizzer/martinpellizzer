Paeonia mascula var. hellenica

Paeonia mascula var. hellenica, commonly known as the Greek peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkans.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its roots are fleshy and tuberous, adapted for storage and survival during harsh conditions.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
