Piper pavonii

Piper pavonii, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is typically harvested when it turns a deep purple color.

Piper pavonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
