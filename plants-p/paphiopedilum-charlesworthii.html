Paphiopedilum charlesworthii

Paphiopedilum charlesworthii, commonly known as the Charlesworth's lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap, a feature common among orchids of the genus Paphiopedilum. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, with long, arching stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white with deep red or maroon markings, making it highly ornamental.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
