Piptocarpha barbata

Piptocarpha barbata, commonly known as the barbed lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive barbed hairs along the edges. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its propagation across dry landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Piptocarpha barbata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
