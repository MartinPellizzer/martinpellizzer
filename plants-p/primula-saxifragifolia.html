Primula saxifragifolia

Primula saxifragifolia, commonly known as the saxifrage primrose, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are basal, oval to heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs.

Primula saxifragifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
