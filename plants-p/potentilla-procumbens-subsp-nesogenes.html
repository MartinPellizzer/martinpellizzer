Potentilla procumbens subsp. nesogenes

Potentilla procumbens subsp.

nesogenes, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically features wiry stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
