Passiflora foetida var. balansae

Passiflora foetida var. balansae, commonly known as the Balansae passionflower, is a striking member of the Passifloraceae family native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can climb or trail.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
