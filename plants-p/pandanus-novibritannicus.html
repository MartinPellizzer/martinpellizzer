Pandanus novibritannicus

Pandanus novibritannicus, commonly known as the New Britain pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the pandan family.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly New Britain in the Bismarck Archipelago. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
