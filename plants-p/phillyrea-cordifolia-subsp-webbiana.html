Phillyrea cordifolia subsp. webbiana

Phillyrea cordifolia subsp.

webbiana, commonly known as the Webb's Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and rocky environments.
