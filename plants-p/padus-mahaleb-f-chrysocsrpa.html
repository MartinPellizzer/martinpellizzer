Padus mahaleb f. chrysocsrpa

Padus mahaleb f. chrysocsrpa, commonly known as the golden horned dogwood, is a botanical variant of the dogwood species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which appear in clusters during the spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
