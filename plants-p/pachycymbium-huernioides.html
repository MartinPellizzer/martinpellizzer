Pachycymbium huernioides

Pachycymbium huernioides, commonly known as the Huernioides cycad, is a species of cycad native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
