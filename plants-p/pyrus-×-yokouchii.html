Pyrus × yokouchii

Pyrus × yokouchii, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus pyrifolia (Chinese white pear) and Pyrus betulaefolia (Bartlett pear).

It is characterized by its small, white to yellowish fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is deciduous, with attractive foliage that turns reddish in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The species is relatively disease-resistant and suitable for cultivation in gardens and orchards.
