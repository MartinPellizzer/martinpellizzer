Potentilla quelpaertensis

Potentilla quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Jeju potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
