Pleurothallis pergrata

Pleurothallis pergrata, commonly known as the small-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to capture moisture from the air.

Pleurothallis pergrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
