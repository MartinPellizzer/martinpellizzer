Prunus cerasifera flore-roseo-pleno

Prunus cerasifera flore-roseo-pleno, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry or rose-veined cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that appear in early spring, with petals that are pink and often have a white center. The plant is a deciduous tree with a rounded shape and smooth, dark grey bark. Its leaves are ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
