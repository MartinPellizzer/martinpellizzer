Papaver macounii

Papaver macounii, commonly known as Macoun's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange petals and a prominent, seed-filled capsule that develops after flowering. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Papaver macounii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
