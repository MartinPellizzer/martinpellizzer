Panax americanus var. elatus

Panax americanus var. elatus, commonly known as the American ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the forests of North America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and somewhat fanged roots. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with opposite, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, and it produces red berries that contain seeds.

The plant thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, often found in hardwood forests.
