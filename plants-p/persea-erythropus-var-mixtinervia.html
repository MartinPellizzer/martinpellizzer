Persea erythropus var. mixtinervia

Persea erythropus var. mixtinervia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the development of edible, orange to reddish fruits. The fruits are typically sweet when fully ripe and are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible fruits.
