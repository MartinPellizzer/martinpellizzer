Pelargonium greytonense

Pelargonium greytonense, commonly known as Greyton geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
