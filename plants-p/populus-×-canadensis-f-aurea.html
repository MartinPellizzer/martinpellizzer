Populus × canadensis f. aurea

Populus × canadensis f. aurea, commonly known as the golden poplar, is a cultivar of the hybrid poplar species.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that provide ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. Its foliage turns a brilliant yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

This variety is often used in urban settings for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
