Pogonarthria squarrosa

Pogonarthria squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often have a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute, pale blue to white florets. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a bluish-green coloration.

Pogonarthria squarrosa is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
