Pandanus tectorius var. fatyanion

Pandanus tectorius var. fatyanion, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

Its adaptability to saline environments makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
