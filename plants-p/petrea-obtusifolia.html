Petrea obtusifolia

Petrea obtusifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine or sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Petrea obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
