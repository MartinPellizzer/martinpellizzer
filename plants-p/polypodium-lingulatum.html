Polypodium lingulatum

Polypodium lingulatum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic and lithophytic fern found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery, and often dark green fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to establish itself in various habitats.

The fronds are usually divided into lance-shaped segments, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
