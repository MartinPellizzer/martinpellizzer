Plagiostachys lasiophylla

Plagiostachys lasiophylla, commonly known as the silky feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and long, narrow leaves with a soft, silky texture. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton or down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Plagiostachys lasiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
