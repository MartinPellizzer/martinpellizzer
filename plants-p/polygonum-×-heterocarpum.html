Polygonum × heterocarpum

Polygonum × heterocarpum, commonly known as the hybrid polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the genus Polygonum, which includes a wide variety of species with diverse growth habits. The plant typically features erect stems with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in wetland ecosystems.
