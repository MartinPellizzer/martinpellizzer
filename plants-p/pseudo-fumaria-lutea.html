Pseudo-fumaria lutea

Pseudo-fumaria lutea, commonly known as the yellow fumaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, climbing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
