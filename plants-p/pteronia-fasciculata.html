Pteronia fasciculata

Pteronia fasciculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking spikes of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and compact size.

Pteronia fasciculata prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
