Poa pratensis var. umbrosa

Poa pratensis var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving bluegrass, is a variant of the common bluegrass species.

It is characterized by its dense, fine-textured foliage and dark green color, which becomes more pronounced in shaded conditions. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, making it a desirable groundcover in shaded landscapes.

It is less tolerant of full sun compared to other bluegrass varieties, making it ideal for partial shade environments.
