Pluchea serra

Pluchea serra, commonly known as the serrated pluchea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an erect annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that have serrated edges. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Pluchea serra is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
