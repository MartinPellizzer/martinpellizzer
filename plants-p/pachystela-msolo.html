Pachystela msolo

Pachystela msolo, commonly known as the Msolo ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the Eastern Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with translucent, waxy leaves that resemble the shape of a ghost. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the late summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a specific microclimate to survive.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is protected under South African conservation laws.
