Paepalanthus praemorsus

Paepalanthus praemorsus, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dark purple berries and bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it dangerous if ingested. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Despite its toxicity, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its chemical properties.
