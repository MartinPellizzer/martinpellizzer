Passiflora insolita

Passiflora insolita, commonly known as the "insolent passionflower," is a striking tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, complex flowers that display a blend of purple, blue, and white hues, with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Passiflora insolita thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit.
