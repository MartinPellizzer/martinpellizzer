Parodia tuberculosicostata var. amblayana

Parodia tuberculosicostata var. amblayana, commonly known as the Amblayana Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem with prominent, ribbed tubercles that provide structural support and aid in water storage. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
