Polygonum tenuissimum

Polygonum tenuissimum, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are often slender and lance-shaped. It is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Polygonum tenuissimum is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and along roadsides, where it thrives in humid environments.
