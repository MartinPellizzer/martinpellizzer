Potamogeton × nitens f. comparandus

Potamogeton × nitens f. comparandus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. It produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spikelets, and it is often found in shallow, vegetated areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
