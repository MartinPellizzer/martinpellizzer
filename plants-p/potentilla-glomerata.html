Potentilla glomerata

Potentilla glomerata, commonly known as the clustered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a low-growing, spreading habit.

Potentilla glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
