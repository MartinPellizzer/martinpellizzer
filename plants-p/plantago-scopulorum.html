Plantago scopulorum

Plantago scopulorum, commonly known as the rock plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or coastal regions.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
