Piper asarifolium

Piper asarifolium, commonly known as the Indian betel pepper or karanj, is a climbing shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, peppery fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and small, yellowish-green berries that mature to a dark purple.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
