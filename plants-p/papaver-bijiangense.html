Papaver bijiangense

Papaver bijiangense, commonly known as the Bijiang poppy, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds of Papaver bijiangense are small and oval, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
