Paeonia × suffruticosa f. rubida

Paeonia × suffruticosa f. rubida, commonly known as the red peony, is a hybrid variety of peony characterized by its vibrant red flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, bushy foliage. The plant produces large, showy blooms in late spring to early summer, with petals that range from deep crimson to soft pink depending on the cultivar. Its botanical features include sturdy stems, compound leaves with serrated edges, and an extensive root system.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
