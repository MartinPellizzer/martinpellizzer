Persea chrysantha

Persea chrysantha, commonly known as the golden spondias, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different environments.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in tropical regions.
