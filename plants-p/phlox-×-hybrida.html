Phlox × hybrida

Phlox × hybrida, commonly known as garden phlox, is a popular flowering plant in the Polemoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, white, and red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

Its vibrant blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to both ornamental value and ecological benefit.
