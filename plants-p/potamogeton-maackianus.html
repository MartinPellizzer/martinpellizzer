Potamogeton maackianus

Potamogeton maackianus, commonly known as Maack's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Potamogeton maackianus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
