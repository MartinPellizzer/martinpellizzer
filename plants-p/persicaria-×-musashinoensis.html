Persicaria × musashinoensis

Persicaria × musashinoensis, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed.

It is a hybrid species belonging to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, appearing in late summer. It is native to Japan and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in natural habitats.
