Phyteuma scorzonerifolium subsp. laxiflorum

Phyteuma scorzonerifolium subsp.

laxiflorum, commonly known as the looseflower phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
