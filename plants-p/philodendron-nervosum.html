Philodendron nervosum

Philodendron nervosum, commonly known as the veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins that give it its common name. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when supported. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties displaying variegation.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
