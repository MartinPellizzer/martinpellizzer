Potentilla caucasica

Potentilla caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of five to nine oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla caucasica is often found in rocky or mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
