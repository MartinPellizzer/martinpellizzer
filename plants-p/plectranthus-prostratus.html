Plectranthus prostratus

Plectranthus prostratus, commonly known as creeping plectranthus, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or lavender in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.

Due to its spreading habit and ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
