Panicum scindens

Panicum scindens, commonly known as the split panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often exhibit a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, delicate spikelets that are typically clustered in dense panicles, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Panicum scindens is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
