Passiflora truxillensis

Passiflora truxillensis, commonly known as the Trinidad passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a unique arrangement of petals and sepals. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are typically small and yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures.

Passiflora truxillensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
