Phlomoides umbrosa var. ovalifolia

Phlomoides umbrosa var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This plant is valued for its delicate floral display and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
