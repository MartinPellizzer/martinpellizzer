Polygonum mirajabii

Polygonum mirajabii, commonly known as the Mirajabii knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants characterized by their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible fruits. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
