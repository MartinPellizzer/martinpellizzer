Poidium brasiliense

Poidium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Poidium brasiliense thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
