Phyllanthus monticola

Phyllanthus monticola, commonly known as mountain phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse morphology and ecological adaptability. The plant typically features small, opposite leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature known as monoecious.

The plant is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats, demonstrating a degree of resilience to environmental stress.
