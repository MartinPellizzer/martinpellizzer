Passiflora heydei

Passiflora heydei, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns of red, blue, and purple. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Passiflora heydei is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
