Panicum irregulare

Panicum irregulare, commonly known as the irregular panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often exhibit a reddish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, awned lemmas. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland habitats.

Panicum irregulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
