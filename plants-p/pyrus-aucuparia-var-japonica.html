Pyrus aucuparia var. japonica

Pyrus aucuparia var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces fruit that ripens in late summer, turning from green to a deep orange-red color. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
