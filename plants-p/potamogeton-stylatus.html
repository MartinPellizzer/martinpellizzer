Potamogeton stylatus

Potamogeton stylatus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically found near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Potamogeton stylatus is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
