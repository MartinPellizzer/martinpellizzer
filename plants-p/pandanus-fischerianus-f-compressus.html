Pandanus fischerianus f. compressus

Pandanus fischerianus f. compressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a variant of the pandanus genus native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and rituals. Its fibrous leaves are also valued for their durability and are used in thatching and basket weaving.

This species thrives in coastal and wetland environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
