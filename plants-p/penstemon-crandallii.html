Penstemon crandallii

Penstemon crandallii, commonly known as Crandall's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon crandallii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in arid landscapes.
