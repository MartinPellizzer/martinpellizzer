Panicum saccharatum

Panicum saccharatum, commonly known as sugarcane grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its coarse, fibrous leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and disturbed soils due to its preference for moist environments. Its rhizomes and roots can form extensive colonies, making it a common invasive species in some areas.

Sugarcane grass is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for livestock.
