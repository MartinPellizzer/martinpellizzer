Pilosella subpratensis

Pilosella subpratensis, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Pilosella subpratensis is often found in alpine meadows and is a common component of mountain flora.
