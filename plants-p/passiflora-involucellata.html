Passiflora involucellata

Passiflora involucellata, commonly known as the Brazilian passion flower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora involucellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
