Plumeria revolutifolia

Plumeria revolutifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian plumeria, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are often rolled at the edges, giving it its species name "revolutifolia.". It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
