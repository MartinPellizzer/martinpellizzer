Phlomis betonicoides

Phlomis betonicoides, commonly known as narrow-leaved betony, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in rocky or dry habitats.
