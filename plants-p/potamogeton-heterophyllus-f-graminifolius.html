Potamogeton heterophyllus f. graminifolius

Potamogeton heterophyllus f. graminifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This fern-like plant is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds and lakes.
