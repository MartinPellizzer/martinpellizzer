Piper cenocladum

Piper cenocladum, commonly known as the Malayan pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Piper cenocladum make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
