Potentilla botschantzeviana

Potentilla botschantzeviana, commonly known as the Botchantzev's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features multiple stems covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

Potentilla botschantzeviana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
