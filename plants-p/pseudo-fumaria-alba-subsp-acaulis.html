Pseudo-fumaria alba subsp. acaulis

Pseudo-fumaria alba subsp.

acaulis, commonly known as the white stemless fumaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae. It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems that are often hairless and lack leaves along their length.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique and delicate addition to its natural habitat.
