Pachystachys cordata

Pachystachys cordata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pachystachys, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
