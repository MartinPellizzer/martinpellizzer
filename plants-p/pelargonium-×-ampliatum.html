Pelargonium × ampliatum

Pelargonium × ampliatum, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium fruticosum and Pelargonium zonale. The plant features upright growth with dense, rounded foliage and produces vibrant clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

Pelargonium × ampliatum thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
