Pavetta gardeniifolia var. subtomentosa

Pavetta gardeniifolia var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and often covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
