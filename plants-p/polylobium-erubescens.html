Polylobium erubescens

Polylobium erubescens, commonly known as the red-veined fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features fronds that are deeply divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments, giving it a delicate and intricate appearance. The plant's leaves are typically bright green with prominent red veins, which become more pronounced as the plant matures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
