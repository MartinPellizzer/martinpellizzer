Piper swartzianum

Piper swartzianum, commonly known as the Swartz's pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species.

It is a tropical vine characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
