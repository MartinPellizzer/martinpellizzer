Pinus taeda var. mughoides

Pinus taeda var. mughoides, commonly known as the Mughoid pine, is a variant of the loblolly pine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and long, slender needles that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in length. The cones of this variety are relatively small and often remain closed for an extended period before releasing their seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This variant is valued for its timber quality and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
