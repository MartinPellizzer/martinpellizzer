Pappophorum abyssinicum

Pappophorum abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian grass, is a species of flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in silky hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and on slopes.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
