Papaver radicatum subsp. avkoense

Papaver radicatum subsp.

avkoense, commonly known as the Avko poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
