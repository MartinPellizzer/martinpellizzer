Pleiospilos magnipunctatus

Pleiospilos magnipunctatus, commonly known as the giant sunset cactus, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid environments.

This species is known for its ability to store water in its stem, making it highly drought-tolerant.
