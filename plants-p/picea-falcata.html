Picea falcata

Picea falcata, commonly known as the slash pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and cone-shaped fruits that mature in the second year. The tree has a tall, straight trunk with a narrow, pyramidal crown, and its bark is thick and scaly. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in pine forests and wetlands.

The slash pine plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
