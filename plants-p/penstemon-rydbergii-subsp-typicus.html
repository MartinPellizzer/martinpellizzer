Penstemon rydbergii subsp. typicus

Penstemon rydbergii subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Rydberg beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
