Pleurothallis josephensis var. integripetala

Pleurothallis josephensis var. integripetala, commonly known as the Joseph's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
