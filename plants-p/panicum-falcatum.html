Panicum falcatum

Panicum falcatum, commonly known as flatleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
