Pitcairnia volubilis

Pitcairnia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that allow it to climb and spread along tree trunks or rocks. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that can range from red to orange, often resembling a torch. Its leaves are typically broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, capable of storing water.

Pitcairnia volubilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
