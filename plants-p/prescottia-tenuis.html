Prescottia tenuis

Prescottia tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf pincushion cactus, is a species of cactus native to the United States, specifically Arizona and New Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Prescottia tenuis is a slow-growing plant that is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
