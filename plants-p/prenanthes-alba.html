Prenanthes alba

Prenanthes alba, commonly known as white bur marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright white flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from August to September. The plant grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to saturated soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and upright.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
