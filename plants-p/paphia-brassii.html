Paphia brassii

Paphia brassii, commonly known as the Brassii Paphiopedilum, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a unique, spurred lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
