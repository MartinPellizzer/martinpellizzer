Populus × tomentosa var. truncata

Populus × tomentosa var. truncata, commonly known as the Chinese poplar or narrow-leaved poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, ovate leaves that are often truncate at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
