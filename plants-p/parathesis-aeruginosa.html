Parathesis aeruginosa

Parathesis aeruginosa, commonly known as the copperleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, copper-colored leaves that change with age, transitioning from a vibrant green to a deep, metallic sheen. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its native environment.

Parathesis aeruginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
