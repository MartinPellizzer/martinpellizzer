Phaseolus scaber

Phaseolus scaber, commonly known as the rough bush bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible beans.

Due to its rugged appearance, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening as well.
