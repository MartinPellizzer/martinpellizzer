Podocarpus polystachyus var. rigidus

Podocarpus polystachyus var. rigidus, commonly known as the Indian incense cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its aromatic wood and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
