Phyllanthus nozeranii

Phyllanthus nozeranii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange, berry-like fruits. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
