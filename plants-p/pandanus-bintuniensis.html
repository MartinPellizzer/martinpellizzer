Pandanus bintuniensis

Pandanus bintuniensis, commonly known as the Bintuni palm, is a species of cycad native to the island of Bintuni in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feathery leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
