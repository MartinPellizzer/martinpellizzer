Polygala platycarpa var. stricta

Polygala platycarpa var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while the flowers are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
