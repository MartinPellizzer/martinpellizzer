Pelazoneuron berroi

Pelazoneuron berroi, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, spiraling arrangement of golden-yellow leaves that form a compact, cone-like structure. The plant possesses a fibrous root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils and extreme weather conditions. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Pelazoneuron berroi is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
