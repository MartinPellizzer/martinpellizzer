Pilosella megatricha

Pilosella megatricha, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while the flowers are yellow with white margins.

Pilosella megatricha is often found in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
