Pteris aurita

Pteris aurita, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green to golden in color. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Pteris aurita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
