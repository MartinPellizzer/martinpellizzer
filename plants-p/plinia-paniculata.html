Plinia paniculata

Plinia paniculata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in panicles, which are branched clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.
