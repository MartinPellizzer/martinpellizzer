Piptadenia leucoxylon

Piptadenia leucoxylon, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
