Polygala corralitae

Polygala corralitae, commonly known as the Chihuahuan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Polygala corralitae plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides nectar for pollinators.
