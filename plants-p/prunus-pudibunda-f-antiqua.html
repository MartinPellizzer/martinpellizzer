Prunus pudibunda f. antiqua

Prunus pudibunda f. antiqua, commonly known as the old-fashioned Japanese cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its small, delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in traditional landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific care requirements, it is considered a rare and delicate species.
