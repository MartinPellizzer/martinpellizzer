Phyllanthus axillaris

Phyllanthus axillaris, commonly known as the Indian false brome, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, round seeds.

Phyllanthus axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
