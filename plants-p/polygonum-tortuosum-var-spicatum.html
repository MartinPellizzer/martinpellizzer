Polygonum tortuosum var. spicatum

Polygonum tortuosum var. spicatum, commonly known as the spiked knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly wavy margin.

It is often found in moist environments such as streambanks and wet meadows.
