Piper serpens

Piper serpens, commonly known as the serpentine pepper or snake vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its slender, serpentine-like stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries containing the aromatic seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Piper serpens is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
