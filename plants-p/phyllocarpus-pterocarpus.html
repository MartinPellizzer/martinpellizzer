Phyllocarpus pterocarpus

Phyllocarpus pterocarpus, commonly known as the golden ghost tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces pods that contain flattened, winged seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
