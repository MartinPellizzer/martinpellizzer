Pleioblastus usuiensis

Pleioblastus usuiensis, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Japan.

It is a clump-forming perennial that produces slender, arching shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Pleioblastus usuiensis is valued for its ability to create a lush, green carpet in gardens and natural landscapes.
