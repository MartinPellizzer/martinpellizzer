Plantago pugae

Plantago pugae, commonly known as the Pugae plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
