Pleione praecox var. candida

Pleione praecox var. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered pleione, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact stature and striking white petals with a pale green throat. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing delicate, fragrant flowers that are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts. It thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
