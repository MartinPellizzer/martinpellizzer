Piper pachyphloium

Piper pachyphloium, commonly known as the thick-leaf pepper or piper thickleaf, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, waxy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
