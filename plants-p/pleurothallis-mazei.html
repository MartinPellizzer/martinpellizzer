Pleurothallis mazei

Pleurothallis mazei, commonly known as the Maze's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Pleurothallis mazei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
