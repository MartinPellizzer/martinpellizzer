Potamogeton strictifolius var. rutiloides

Potamogeton strictifolius var. rutiloides, commonly known as the golden thread pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often bright green, with a distinct yellowish or golden hue on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.
