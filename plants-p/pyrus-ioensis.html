Pyrus ioensis

Pyrus ioensis, commonly known as the Iowa pear, is a deciduous tree native to the United States, specifically found in the Midwestern region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible pears that are typically small, yellowish-green, and have a mild, sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in early American agriculture.
