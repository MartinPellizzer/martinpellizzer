Pithecellobium falcatum

Pithecellobium falcatum, commonly known as the flat-leaf acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive flat, elongated shape. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, while the fruit is a legume containing several seeds.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
