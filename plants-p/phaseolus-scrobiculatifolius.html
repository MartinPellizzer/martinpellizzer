Phaseolus scrobiculatifolius

Phaseolus scrobiculatifolius, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers and elongated, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with compound leaves that have a distinctive scalloped or wavy edge. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible beans.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
