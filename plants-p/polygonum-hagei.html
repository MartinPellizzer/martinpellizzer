Polygonum hagei

Polygonum hagei, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late summer. Polygonum hagei thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
