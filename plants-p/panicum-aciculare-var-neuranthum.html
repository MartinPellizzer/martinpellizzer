Panicum aciculare var. neuranthum

Panicum aciculare var. neuranthum, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and rhizomes, which help it spread and establish in various environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
