Poa sugawarana

Poa sugawarana, commonly known as the Japanese meadow grass, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows and forest clearings. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

Poa sugawarana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its delicate foliage and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
