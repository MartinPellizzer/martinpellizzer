Penstemon veronicifolius

Penstemon veronicifolius, commonly known as the Veronica-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon veronicifolius is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
