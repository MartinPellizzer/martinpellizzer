Picris hieracioides subsp. koreana

Picris hieracioides subsp.

koreana, commonly known as Korean hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Korea and parts of eastern Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other forms of Picris hieracioides.
