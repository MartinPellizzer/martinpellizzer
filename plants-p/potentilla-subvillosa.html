Potentilla subvillosa

Potentilla subvillosa, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Potentilla subvillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
