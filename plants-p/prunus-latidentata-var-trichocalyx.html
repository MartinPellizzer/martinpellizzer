Prunus latidentata var. trichocalyx

Prunus latidentata var. trichocalyx, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
