Persicaria divaricata

Persicaria divaricata, commonly known as zigzag knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria divaricata thrives in moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
