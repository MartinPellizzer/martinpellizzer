Pouteria trilocularis

Pouteria trilocularis, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, oblong leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its hard-shelled fruit, which contains edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and healthy fats.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its economic significance.
