Polypodium refulgens

Polypodium refulgens, commonly known as the shining polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green to bronze in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of its leaves.

Polypodium refulgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
