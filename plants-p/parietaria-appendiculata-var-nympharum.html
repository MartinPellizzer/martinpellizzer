Parietaria appendiculata var. nympharum

Parietaria appendiculata var. nympharum, commonly known as the silverleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its distinctive leaf morphology and growth habit.
