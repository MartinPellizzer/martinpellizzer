Plectranthus dhankutanus

Plectranthus dhankutanus, commonly known as the Dhankutan plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves, which are typically hairy and have a somewhat rough texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
