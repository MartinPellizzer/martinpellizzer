Paracalia lopezii

Paracalia lopezii, commonly known as Lopez's spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Bolivia and Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with long, thin petals and a narrow, twisted column. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the cooler months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
