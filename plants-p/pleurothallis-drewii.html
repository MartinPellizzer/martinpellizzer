Pleurothallis drewii

Pleurothallis drewii, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color, often with a contrasting green or white base. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pleurothallis drewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
