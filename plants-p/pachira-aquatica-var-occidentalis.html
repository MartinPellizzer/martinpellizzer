Pachira aquatica var. occidentalis

Pachira aquatica var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Mexican water spinach, is a variant of the water chestnut plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, tropical environments, often found near water bodies. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that develop into cylindrical, fleshy fruits. It is characterized by its ability to grow rapidly in wet conditions, making it adaptable to various aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

This variant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and roots, which are used in local cuisines.
