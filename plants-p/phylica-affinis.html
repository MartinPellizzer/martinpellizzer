Phylica affinis

Phylica affinis, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.

Phylica affinis is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of honey.
