Phleum pratense f. elongatum

Phleum pratense f. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed Timothy grass, is a variant of Timothy grass characterized by its elongated inflorescences.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and pastures. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It is valued for its forage quality and is used in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and environmental conditions.
