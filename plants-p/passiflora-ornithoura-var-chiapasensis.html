Passiflora ornithoura var. chiapasensis

Passiflora ornithoura var. chiapasensis, commonly known as the Chiapas passionflower, is a striking species native to Central America, particularly Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that feature intricate structures such as the crown of filaments and a central calyx. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically large and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, attracting various pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
