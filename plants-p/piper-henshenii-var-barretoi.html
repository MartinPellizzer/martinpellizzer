Piper henshenii var. barretoi

Piper henshenii var. barretoi, commonly known as the Henshaw's pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, peppery fruits. Its botanical features make it distinct from other Piper species, with a unique combination of morphological traits.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and potential medicinal properties.
