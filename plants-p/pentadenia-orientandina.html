Pentadenia orientandina

Pentadenia orientandina, commonly known as the mountain gum or blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its hard timber and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
