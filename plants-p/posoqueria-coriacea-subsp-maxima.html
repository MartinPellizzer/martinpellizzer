Posoqueria coriacea subsp. maxima

Posoqueria coriacea subsp.

maxima, commonly known as the roughleaf pisonia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region. It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, often accompanied by distinctive bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically growing in coastal or disturbed habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
