Phyllagathis jacobsiana

Phyllagathis jacobsiana, commonly known as the Jacobs' everlet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, dense, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
