Phylica thyrsophora

Phylica thyrsophora, commonly known as the silver tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its distinctive, cone-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Phylica thyrsophora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
