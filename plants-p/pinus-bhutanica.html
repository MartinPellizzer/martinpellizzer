Pinus bhutanica

Pinus bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutan pine, is a coniferous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The needles are typically arranged in bundles of five, and the cones are woody, maturing within 18 months. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often found in mountainous regions of Bhutan and India.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
