Pomaria austrotexana

Pomaria austrotexana, commonly known as the Texas pomaria, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pomaria austrotexana is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
