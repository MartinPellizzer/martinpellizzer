Potentilla setosa

Potentilla setosa, commonly known as the rough cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

Potentilla setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
