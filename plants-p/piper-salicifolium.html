Piper salicifolium

Piper salicifolium, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
