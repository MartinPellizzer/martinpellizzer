Pseuderanthemum majus

Pseuderanthemum majus, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its visual appeal.
