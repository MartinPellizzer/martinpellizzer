Pinus wallichiana var. parva

Pinus wallichiana var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf Himalayan fir, is a small conifer native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, stout shoots. The needles are typically blue-green in color, and the cones are small and woody. This variety is adapted to high-altitude environments with cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and providing habitat for alpine flora and fauna.
