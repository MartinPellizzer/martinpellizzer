Praxelis basifolia

Praxelis basifolia, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Praxelis basifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
