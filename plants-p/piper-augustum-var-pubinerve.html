Piper augustum var. pubinerve

Piper augustum var. pubinerve, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its finely pubescent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by reddish berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The varietal name 'pubinerve' refers to the hairy veins on its leaves, distinguishing it from other varieties of Piper augustum.
