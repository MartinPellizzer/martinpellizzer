Phytelephas brachinus

Phytelephas brachinus, commonly known as the small-berry sago palm, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Phytelephas brachinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
