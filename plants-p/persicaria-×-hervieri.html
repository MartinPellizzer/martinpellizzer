Persicaria × hervieri

Persicaria × hervieri, commonly known as the hybrid knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Persicaria × hervieri is typically found in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.
