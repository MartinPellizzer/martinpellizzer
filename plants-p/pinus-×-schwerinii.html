Pinus × schwerinii

Pinus × schwerinii, commonly known as the Schwerin pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus sylvestris and Pinus nigra.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically dark green and arranged in bundles of two. The tree produces cones that are woody and often remain on the branches for several years. It is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks.
