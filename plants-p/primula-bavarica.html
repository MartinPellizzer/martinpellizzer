Primula bavarica

Primula bavarica, commonly known as the Bavarian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe.

It features rosettes of basal, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings. It prefers cool, moist environments and partial shade, thriving in well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation make it a favored plant among gardeners.
