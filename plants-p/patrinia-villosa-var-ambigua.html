Patrinia villosa var. ambigua

Patrinia villosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the hairy patrinia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the mint family, Lamiaceae.
