Paeonia lusitanica var. ovatifolia

Paeonia lusitanica var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the Portuguese peony, is a deciduous perennial plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically appearing in late spring. The plant features heart-shaped, matte-green leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique foliage and historical use in traditional medicine.
