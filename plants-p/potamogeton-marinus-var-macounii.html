Potamogeton marinus var. macounii

Potamogeton marinus var. macounii, commonly known as the marine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline waters, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically ribbon-like in shape, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its ability to tolerate varying salinity levels makes it a key species in coastal wetland vegetation.
