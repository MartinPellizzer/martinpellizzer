Potamogeton mucronatus f. minor

Potamogeton mucronatus f. minor, commonly known as the small-point pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width, with a pointed tip, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes, lakes, and the margins of rivers. It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, impacting water flow and light penetration in aquatic ecosystems.
