Piptatherum helanshanense

Piptatherum helanshanense, commonly known as the Helanshan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the semi-arid regions of northern China.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous minute flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes.

Due to its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing forage, it is often found in grassland and steppe ecosystems.
