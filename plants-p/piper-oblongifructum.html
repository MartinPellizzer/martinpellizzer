Piper oblongifructum

Piper oblongifructum, commonly known as the broad-fruit pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
