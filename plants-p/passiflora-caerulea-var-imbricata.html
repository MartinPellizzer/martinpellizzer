Passiflora caerulea var. imbricata

Passiflora caerulea var. imbricata, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers with intricate patterns of red and white. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in warm climates.

The species is valued for both ornamental purposes and its culinary uses.
