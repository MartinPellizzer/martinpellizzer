Pulsatilla patens f. latiuscula

Pulsatilla patens f. latiuscula, commonly known as the broad-leaved pasqueflower, is a variant of the pasqueflower native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of broad, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh environments. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, known for its showy flowers and often toxic sap.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and open meadows at high elevations.
