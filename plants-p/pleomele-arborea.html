Pleomele arborea

Pleomele arborea, commonly known as the tree cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a slow-growing plant with a stout, unbranched trunk that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
