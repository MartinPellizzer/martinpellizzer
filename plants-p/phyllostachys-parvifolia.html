Phyllostachys parvifolia

Phyllostachys parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern China and is characterized by its slender, green culms and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is a fast-growing bamboo that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
