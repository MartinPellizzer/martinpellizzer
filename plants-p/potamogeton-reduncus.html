Potamogeton reduncus

Potamogeton reduncus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind rather than insects. Its roots help stabilize sediments, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

Potamogeton reduncus is often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
