Pittosporum filarium

Pittosporum filarium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy beneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by spherical, blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum filarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
