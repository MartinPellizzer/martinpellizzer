Poa penicillata

Poa penicillata, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically found in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Poa penicillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
