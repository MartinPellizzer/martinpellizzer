Penstemon newberryi var. newberryi

Penstemon newberryi var. newberryi, commonly known as Newberry's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces showy, long-lasting blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
