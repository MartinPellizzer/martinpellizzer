Psoralea drupacea

Psoralea drupacea, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes. The plant is known for its distinctive, woody, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Psoralea drupacea is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
