Psidium cinereum var. incanescens

Psidium cinereum var. incanescens, commonly known as the silver star guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale green, star-shaped flowers that are highly fragrant. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thin, smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value, combined with its edible fruit, makes it a desirable plant for both cultivation and landscaping.
