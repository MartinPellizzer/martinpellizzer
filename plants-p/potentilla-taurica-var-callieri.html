Potentilla taurica var. callieri

Potentilla taurica var. callieri, commonly known as the Crimean potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Russia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy and decorative qualities.
