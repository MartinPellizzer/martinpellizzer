Piper praemollitum

Piper praemollitum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
