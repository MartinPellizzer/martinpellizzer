Polyscias vieillardii

Polyscias vieillardii, commonly known as the African nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Polyscias vieillardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.
