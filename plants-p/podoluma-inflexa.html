Podoluma inflexa

Podoluma inflexa, commonly known as the flexible podoluma, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Podoluma inflexa is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
