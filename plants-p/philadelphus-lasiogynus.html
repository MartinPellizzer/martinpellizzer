Philadelphus lasiogynus

Philadelphus lasiogynus, commonly known as the roughleaf mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus lasiogynus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
