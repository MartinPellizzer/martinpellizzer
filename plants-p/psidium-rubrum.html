Psidium rubrum

Psidium rubrum, commonly known as the red guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are typically white or pink.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
