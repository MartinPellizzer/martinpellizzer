Pedilonum metrium

Pedilonum metrium, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and glossy, while the stems are woody and twining. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its blooming period is primarily during the evening, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
