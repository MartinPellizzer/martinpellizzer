Phalaroides arundinacea subsp. japonica

Phalaroides arundinacea subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese bulrush, is a perennial grass native to East Asia. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescences and fibrous roots, which help stabilize wetland soils.

The plant thrives in marshy and aquatic environments, often forming dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and prominent veins.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
