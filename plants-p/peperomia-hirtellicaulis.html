Peperomia hirtellicaulis

Peperomia hirtellicaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed peperomia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its succulent-like leaves help it store water, making it well adapted to dry environments.

Peperomia hirtellicaulis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
