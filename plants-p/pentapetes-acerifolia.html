Pentapetes acerifolia

Pentapetes acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved pentapetes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive maple-like leaves, which are palmately lobed and often have a reddish or bronze hue. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
