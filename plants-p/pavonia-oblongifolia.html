Pavonia oblongifolia

Pavonia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its botanical features include hairy foliage and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

Pavonia oblongifolia is often found in open woods and along roadsides, thriving in partial to full sunlight.
