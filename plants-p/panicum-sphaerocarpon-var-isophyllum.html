Panicum sphaerocarpon var. isophyllum

Panicum sphaerocarpon var. isophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright leaves and compact, spikelike inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a distinctive, grass-like appearance with a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
