Physalis viscosa subsp. maritima

Physalis viscosa subsp.

maritima, commonly known as sea gooseberry, is a halophytic species that thrives in coastal environments. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and bright orange, lantern-shaped fruits.

The plant has a low-growing habit and is adapted to salt-laden air and sandy soils. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often found in dunes and salt marshes along the Atlantic coast.
