Polygala hamata

Polygala hamata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygala hamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
