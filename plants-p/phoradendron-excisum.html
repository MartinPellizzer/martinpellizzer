Phoradendron excisum

Phoradendron excisum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of various host trees, such as oaks and hickories.

It lacks roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are adapted for this parasitic lifestyle. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often pollinated by insects. Its berries, which are typically red or yellow, contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Phoradendron excisum plays a unique ecological role in forest ecosystems, influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
