Primula pseudoelatior

Primula pseudoelatior, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of bright yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
