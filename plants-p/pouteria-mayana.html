Pouteria mayana

Pouteria mayana, commonly known as the "chicha tree," is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits that are typically round and fleshy. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

The tree is valued for its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
