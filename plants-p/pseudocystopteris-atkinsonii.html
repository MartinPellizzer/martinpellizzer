Pseudocystopteris atkinsonii

Pseudocystopteris atkinsonii, commonly known as the Atkinson's false shield fern, is a small, terrestrial fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady garden settings.
