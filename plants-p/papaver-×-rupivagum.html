Papaver × rupivagum

Papaver × rupivagum, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver somniferum (opium poppy).

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive, elongated seed capsule. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique seed structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, typically in temperate climates.

The hybrid is also notable for its genetic diversity, which can lead to variations in flower color and plant form.
