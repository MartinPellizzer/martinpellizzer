Polygonum douglasii var. johnstonii

Polygonum douglasii var. johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
