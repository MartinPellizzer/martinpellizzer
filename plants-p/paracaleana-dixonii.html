Paracaleana dixonii

Paracaleana dixonii, commonly known as the Dixon's flame orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near the bases of trees or in dense undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its unique appearance.

Paracaleana dixonii is a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native habitat.
