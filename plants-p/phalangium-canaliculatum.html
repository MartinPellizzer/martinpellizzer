Phalangium canaliculatum

Phalangium canaliculatum, commonly known as the channel-ribbed centipede, is a species of centipede belonging to the family Scolopendridae.

It is characterized by its elongated, segmented body covered in exoskeletal plates, with each segment bearing a pair of legs. The species is notable for its distinctive, grooved or channel-like markings on its exoskeleton, which provide structural support and aid in camouflage. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, forested environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to arthropodologists and conservationists.
