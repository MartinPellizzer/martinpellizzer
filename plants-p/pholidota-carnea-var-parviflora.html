Pholidota carnea var. parviflora

Pholidota carnea var. parviflora, commonly known as the pink-flowered pholidota, is a variant of the pholidota genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its tropical habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
