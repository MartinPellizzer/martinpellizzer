Pteris hovenkampii

Pteris hovenkampii, commonly known as the Hovenkamp's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous spore-bearing sorus clusters on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris hovenkampii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor or outdoor plant.
