Pleurothallis sanluisii

Pleurothallis sanluisii, commonly known as the San Luis orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and requires high air humidity for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
