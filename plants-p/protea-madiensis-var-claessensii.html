Protea madiensis var. claessensii

Protea madiensis var. claessensii, commonly known as the Madiensis protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Eastern Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous papery bracts that enclose small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region. Its floral structure is typical of the Proteaceae family, featuring a central axis with modified leaves forming the flower head.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
