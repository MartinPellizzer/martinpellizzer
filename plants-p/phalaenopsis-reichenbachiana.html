Phalaenopsis reichenbachiana

Phalaenopsis reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Orchid of the Gods, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures rather than in soil. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs and has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a velvety texture and a distinctive, showy appearance.

This orchid is prized for its longevity and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in homes and gardens.
