Pentaraphia salicifolia

Pentaraphia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, willow-like leaves that are typically bipinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are usually green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Pentaraphia salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
