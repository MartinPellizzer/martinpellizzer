Parodia horrida var. pluricentralis

Parodia horrida var. pluricentralis, commonly known as the Pluricentral Cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Its unique, multi-central stem structure makes it a distinctive and sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
