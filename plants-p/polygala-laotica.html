Polygala laotica

Polygala laotica, commonly known as the Laotian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
