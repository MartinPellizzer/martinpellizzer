Piptatherum angustifolium

Piptatherum angustifolium, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grass species.
