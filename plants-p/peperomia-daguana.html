Peperomia daguana

Peperomia daguana, commonly known as the Daguana peppergrass, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Peperomia daguana is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Its unique appearance and air-purifying qualities make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
