Pyrus umemurana

Pyrus umemurana, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional Japanese cuisine and herbal medicine.
