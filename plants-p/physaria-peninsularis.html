Physaria peninsularis

Physaria peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

The species is known for its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
