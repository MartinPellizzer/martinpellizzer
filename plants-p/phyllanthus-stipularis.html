Phyllanthus stipularis

Phyllanthus stipularis, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are tiny, three-lobed capsules that contain several seeds.

Phyllanthus stipularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
