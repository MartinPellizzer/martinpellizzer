Palala grandifolia

Palala grandifolia, commonly known as the giant palala, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to two meters in length, providing a dramatic visual presence. The plant produces clusters of showy, white to pale yellow flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
