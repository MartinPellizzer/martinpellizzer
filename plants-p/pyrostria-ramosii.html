Pyrostria ramosii

Pyrostria ramosii, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that are often branched, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Pyrostria ramosii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its graceful form and adaptability to various wetland environments.
