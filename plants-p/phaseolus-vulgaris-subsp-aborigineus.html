Phaseolus vulgaris subsp. aborigineus

Phaseolus vulgaris subsp.

aborigineus, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America. It produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when immature and turn red or yellow when mature.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
