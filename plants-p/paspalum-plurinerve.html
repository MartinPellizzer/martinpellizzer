Paspalum plurinerve

Paspalum plurinerve, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Paspalum plurinerve is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
