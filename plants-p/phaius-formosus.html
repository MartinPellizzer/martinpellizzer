Phaius formosus

Phaius formosus, commonly known as the Taiwan ginger orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spicy scent.

Phaius formosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
