Polygonum arifolium var. pubescens

Polygonum arifolium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often hairy, giving it its varietal name "pubescens.". The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a prominent midrib. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
