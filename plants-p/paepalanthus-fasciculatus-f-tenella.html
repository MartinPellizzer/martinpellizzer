Paepalanthus fasciculatus f. tenella

Paepalanthus fasciculatus f. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved puya, is a unique member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense, water-holding structure at the center. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with brightly colored bracts that attract pollinators. Native to the Andean regions of South America, it thrives in high-altitude, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
