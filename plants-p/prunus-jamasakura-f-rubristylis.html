Prunus jamasakura f. rubristylis

Prunus jamasakura f. rubristylis, commonly known as the red-styled cherry, is a distinctive variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking red floral buds and petals that contrast with its pink to white blooms. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. Its botanical features include slender branches, elliptical leaves, and a compact growth habit.

This variety is particularly noted for its unique coloration, making it a popular choice in floral arrangements and horticultural displays.
