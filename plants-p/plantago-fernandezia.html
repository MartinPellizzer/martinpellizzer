Plantago fernandezia

Plantago fernandezia, commonly known as the Fernandez's plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
