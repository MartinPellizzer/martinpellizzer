Phlox floridana var. typica

Phlox floridana var. typica, commonly known as the Florida phlox, is a native perennial plant of the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
