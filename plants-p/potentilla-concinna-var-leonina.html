Potentilla concinna var. leonina

Potentilla concinna var. leonina, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, arching stems and dark green, serrated leaves that provide a dense, ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a resilient, decorative shrub.
