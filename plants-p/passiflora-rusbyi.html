Passiflora rusbyi

Passiflora rusbyi, commonly known as Rusby's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces bright orange or yellow berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora rusbyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
