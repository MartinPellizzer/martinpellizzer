Posoqueria drupacea

Posoqueria drupacea, commonly known as the dragon tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange berries that contain a single seed. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Posoqueria drupacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
