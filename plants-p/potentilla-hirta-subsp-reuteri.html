Potentilla hirta subsp. reuteri

Potentilla hirta subsp.

reuteri, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
