Prasophyllum diversiflorum

Prasophyllum diversiflorum, commonly known as the variable leek orchid, is an endemic species of the Australian flora.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry forest habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are grass-like and often clustered at the base, while its flowers are arranged in a spike-like structure.

Prasophyllum diversiflorum is a perennial herb that plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native environment.
