Psydaranta

Psydaranta, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, erect stalks rising from the water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic life. Its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the water while anchoring the plant in place.

The sacred lotus is also notable for its symbolic significance in many cultures, representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.
