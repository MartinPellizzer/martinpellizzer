Poa probatovae

Poa probatovae, commonly known as the Probatovae bentgrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Poa probatovae has small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
