Passiflora canescens

Passiflora canescens, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments. Its fruit is a smooth, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer.

Passiflora canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
