Penstemon paysoniorum

Penstemon paysoniorum, commonly known as Payson's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon paysoniorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
