Phacelia frigida subsp. dasyphylla

Phacelia frigida subsp.

dasyphylla, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, hairy leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
