Piper lineativillosum

Piper lineativillosum, commonly known as the narrow-haired pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
