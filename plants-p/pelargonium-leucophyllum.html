Pelargonium leucophyllum

Pelargonium leucophyllum, commonly known as the white-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking white or pale green leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
