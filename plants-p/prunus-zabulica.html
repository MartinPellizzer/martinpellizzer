Prunus zabulica

Prunus zabulica, commonly known as the Afghan cherry or Persian cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black drupes, which are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Prunus zabulica is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
