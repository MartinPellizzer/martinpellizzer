Pinus ponderosa var. nigricans

Pinus ponderosa var. nigricans, commonly known as the black ponderosa pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark, which distinguishes it from the typical light-barked ponderosa pine. The tree has long, needle-like leaves that grow in clusters of five, and its cones are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in dry, mountainous regions and is adapted to withstand wildfires.

This variety plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
