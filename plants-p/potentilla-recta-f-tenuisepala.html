Potentilla recta f. tenuisepala

Potentilla recta f. tenuisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
