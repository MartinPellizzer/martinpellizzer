Pteris quadriaurita var. aspericaulis

Pteris quadriaurita var. aspericaulis, commonly known as the rough-leaf water fern, is a variant of the four-auricle water fern.

It is a fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae, characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near water bodies or in humid forests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
