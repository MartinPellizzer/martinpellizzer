Potentilla eremica

Potentilla eremica, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically divided into five lobes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

Potentilla eremica is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.
