Pseudephedranthus fragrans

Pseudephedranthus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fragrance of its flowers adds to its appeal in gardens and landscapes.
