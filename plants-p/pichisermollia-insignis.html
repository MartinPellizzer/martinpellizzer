Pichisermollia insignis

Pichisermollia insignis, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to minimize water loss in its harsh habitat.

Pichisermollia insignis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
