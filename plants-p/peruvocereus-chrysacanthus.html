Peruvocereus chrysacanthus

Peruvocereus chrysacanthus, commonly known as the golden candelabra cactus, is a species of cactus native to central Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive branching pattern that forms a tree-like structure. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
