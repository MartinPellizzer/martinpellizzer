Prunus mutabilis f. discoidea

Prunus mutabilis f. discoidea, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a unique variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that appear in early spring, contrasting with the typical pink or red blooms of the species. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate and serrated, turning yellow in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
