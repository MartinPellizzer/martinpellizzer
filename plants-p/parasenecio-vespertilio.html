Parasenecio vespertilio

Parasenecio vespertilio, commonly known as the evening senecio, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy leaves and a branching stem, and it produces small, elongated fruits called achenes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
