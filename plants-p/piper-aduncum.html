Piper aduncum

Piper aduncum, commonly known as the creeping pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, dark purple berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
