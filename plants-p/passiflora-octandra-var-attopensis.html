Passiflora octandra var. attopensis

Passiflora octandra var. attopensis, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a species native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-colored flowers that feature a mix of red, yellow, and blue hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

This varietal is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
