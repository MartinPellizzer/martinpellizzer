Pleurothallis liebmanniana

Pleurothallis liebmanniana, commonly known as the Liebmann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
