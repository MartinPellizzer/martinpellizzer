Pourretia mexicana var. argentea

Pourretia mexicana var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice in arid landscapes.
