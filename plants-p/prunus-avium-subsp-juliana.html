Prunus avium subsp. juliana

Prunus avium subsp.

juliana, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a subspecies of the domestic apricot that is native to Japan. It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that appear before the leaves, often in early spring.

The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but less commonly consumed compared to other apricot varieties. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a range of soil types.
