Prunus × donarium var. parvifolia

Prunus × donarium var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf cherry laurel, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, glossy leaves that are small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and year-round appeal make it suitable for hedges and foundation plantings.
