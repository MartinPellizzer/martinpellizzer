Penstemon brandegeei

Penstemon brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It is often found in rocky soils and is a member of the Plantaginaceae family.

Penstemon brandegeei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
