Prunus maliformis

Prunus maliformis, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The tree has a slender, upright growth habit with smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green, and turn yellow in the fall.

Prunus maliformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and along streets.
