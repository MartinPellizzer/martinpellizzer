Phaca megalophysa

Phaca megalophysa, commonly known as the giant leaf phaca, is a striking plant native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing near streams or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
