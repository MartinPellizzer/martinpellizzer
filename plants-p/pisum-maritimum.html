Pisum maritimum

Pisum maritimum, commonly known as the sea pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual legume that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, pea-like pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it has compound stems that are often hairy.

Pisum maritimum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
