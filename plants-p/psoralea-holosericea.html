Psoralea holosericea

Psoralea holosericea, commonly known as the silky psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
