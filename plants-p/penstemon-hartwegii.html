Penstemon hartwegii

Penstemon hartwegii, commonly known as Hartweg's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and are arranged in whorls along the stem, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

The plant's drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its significance in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
