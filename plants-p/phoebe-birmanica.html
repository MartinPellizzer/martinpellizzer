Phoebe birmanica

Phoebe birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Myanmar, Thailand, and parts of India. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are glossy, elliptical, and arranged alternately along the branches.

The flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue or black berries that are edible for some bird species.
