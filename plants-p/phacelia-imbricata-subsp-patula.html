Phacelia imbricata subsp. patula

Phacelia imbricata subsp.

patula, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant for native gardens.
