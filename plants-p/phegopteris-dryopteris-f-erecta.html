Phegopteris dryopteris f. erecta

Phegopteris dryopteris f. erecta, commonly known as the upright form of the common wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its upright fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive pinnae arrangement, and its fronds are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The erect growth habit of this fern form sets it apart from other variants of the species.
