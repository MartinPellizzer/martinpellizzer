Papaver setigerum var. muticum

Papaver setigerum var. muticum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray leaves, which are adapted to tolerate salty and windy conditions. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing along cliffs and dunes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in harsh environments.
