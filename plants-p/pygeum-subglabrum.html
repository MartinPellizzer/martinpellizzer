Pygeum subglabrum

Pygeum subglabrum, commonly known as the smooth African almond, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody pods containing elliptical seeds. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.

The plant is adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
