Phyllanthus fuscoluridus

Phyllanthus fuscoluridus, commonly known as the dark yellow mallow, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a structure typical of the genus. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Phyllanthus fuscoluridus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
