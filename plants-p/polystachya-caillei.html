Polystachya caillei

Polystachya caillei, commonly known as the Caille's spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall low-growing appearance.

Polystachya caillei is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
