Pleurothallis procumbens

Pleurothallis procumbens, commonly known as the creeping pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This orchid is popular among enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
