Penstemon campanulatus subsp. subglandulosus

Penstemon campanulatus subsp.

subglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular bell penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet.

The subspecies is distinguished by its glandular hairs, which are particularly dense on the stems and leaves. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
