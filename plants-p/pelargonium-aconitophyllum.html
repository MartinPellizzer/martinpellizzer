Pelargonium aconitophyllum

Pelargonium aconitophyllum, commonly known as the aconite geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
