Pluchea paniculata

Pluchea paniculata, commonly known as the panicled sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in panicles. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pluchea paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
