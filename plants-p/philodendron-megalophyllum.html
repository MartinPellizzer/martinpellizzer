Philodendron megalophyllum

Philodendron megalophyllum, commonly known as the giant-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its striking foliage. Its stems are typically green or reddish, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Philodendron megalophyllum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires regular watering to maintain its lush appearance.
