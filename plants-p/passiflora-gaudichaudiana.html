Passiflora gaudichaudiana

Passiflora gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and botanical collections.
