Polypodium aturense

Polypodium aturense, commonly known as the Aturen fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in tropical and subtropical forests. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies in its natural habitat.

The spores are released from the sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
