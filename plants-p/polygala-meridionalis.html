Polygala meridionalis

Polygala meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
