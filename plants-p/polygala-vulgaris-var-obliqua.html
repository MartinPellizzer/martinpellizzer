Polygala vulgaris var. obliqua

Polygala vulgaris var. obliqua, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, often branched stems that grow upright or prostrate. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
