Panicum ovatum

Panicum ovatum, commonly known as the oval panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often mistaken for dandelion fluff, which aid in wind pollination. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, Panicum ovatum is often used in native plant gardens and for erosion control.
