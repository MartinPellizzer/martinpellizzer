Pleioblastus sawadae

Pleioblastus sawadae, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered in a fine, silky white bloom. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Pleioblastus sawadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscape design.
