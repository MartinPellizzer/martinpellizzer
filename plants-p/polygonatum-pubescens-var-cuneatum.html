Polygonatum pubescens var. cuneatum

Polygonatum pubescens var. cuneatum, commonly known as the hairy Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant has lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
