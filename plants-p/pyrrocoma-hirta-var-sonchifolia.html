Pyrrocoma hirta var. sonchifolia

Pyrrocoma hirta var. sonchifolia, commonly known as the hairy pyrrocoma, is a variant of the pyrrocoma species characterized by its hairy foliage and yellow flowers.

It is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America, typically found in open, sunny habitats. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a yellow coloration that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
