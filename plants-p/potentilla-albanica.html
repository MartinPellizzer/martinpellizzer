Potentilla albanica

Potentilla albanica, commonly known as the Albanian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Potentilla albanica is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
