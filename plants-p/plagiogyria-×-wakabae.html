Plagiogyria × wakabae

Plagiogyria × wakabae, commonly known as the hybrid lily, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Plagiogyria sinensis and Plagiogyria wakabae.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is native to East Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.

The hybrid exhibits intermediate traits between its parent species, making it a subject of interest in plant taxonomy and horticulture.
