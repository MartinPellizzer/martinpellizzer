Polemonium × jacobaea

Polemonium × jacobaea, commonly known as Jacob's tea, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus, which is part of the family Polemoniaceae, and it is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
