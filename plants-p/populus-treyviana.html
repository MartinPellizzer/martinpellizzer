Populus treyviana

Populus treyviana, commonly known as the European aspen, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Populus treyviana plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
