Pteronia succulenta

Pteronia succulenta, commonly known as the woolly pteronia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, grey-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
