Pyrus cuspidata var. lenticellata

Pyrus cuspidata var. lenticellata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that develops shallow, elongated lenticels, giving it its varietal name. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, followed by oval, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in Japanese horticulture.
