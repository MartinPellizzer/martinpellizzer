Phyllanthus subsessilis

Phyllanthus subsessilis, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often densely packed along the stems. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
