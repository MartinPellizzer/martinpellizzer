Pygeum fragrans

Pygeum fragrans, commonly known as the sweetgum tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves and its showy, lantern-like fruit that turns from green to orange-red as it matures. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environments. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The tree plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
