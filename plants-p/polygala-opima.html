Polygala opima

Polygala opima, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, showy inflorescences that resemble small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are often pink or yellow in color.

Polygala opima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
