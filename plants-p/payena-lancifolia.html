Payena lancifolia

Payena lancifolia, commonly known as the African rosewood or African ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves that are typically alternate and pinnate, with lance-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits.

Payena lancifolia is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland and hill forest regions.
