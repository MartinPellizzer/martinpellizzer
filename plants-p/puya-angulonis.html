Puya angulonis

Puya angulonis, commonly known as the angular puya, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often stiff and angular in shape. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
