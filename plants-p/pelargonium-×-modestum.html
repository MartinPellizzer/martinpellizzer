Pelargonium × modestum

Pelargonium × modestum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, often with a dark center. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, decorative plants.
