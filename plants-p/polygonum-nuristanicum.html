Polygonum nuristanicum

Polygonum nuristanicum, commonly known as the Nuristan knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often reddish stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Polygonum nuristanicum is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
