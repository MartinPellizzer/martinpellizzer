Potentilla dominiana

Potentilla dominiana, commonly known as the Dominican potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Potentilla dominiana is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
