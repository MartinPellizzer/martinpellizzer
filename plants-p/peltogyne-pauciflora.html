Peltogyne pauciflora

Peltogyne pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, and the tree can grow up to 30 meters tall in suitable conditions.

Peltogyne pauciflora is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for both its timber and ecological role.
