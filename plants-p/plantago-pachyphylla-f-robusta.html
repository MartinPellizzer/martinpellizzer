Plantago pachyphylla f. robusta

Plantago pachyphylla f. robusta, commonly known as the thick-leaved plantain, is a variant of the plantain species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a medicinal plant in traditional herbal practices.
