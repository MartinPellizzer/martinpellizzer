Potentilla gilanica

Potentilla gilanica, commonly known as the Gilan potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the Caspian region of Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, adapting to the humid and temperate climate of its native habitat.

Potentilla gilanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
