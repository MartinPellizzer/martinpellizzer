Potamogeton × fluitans f. harzii

Potamogeton × fluitans f. harzii, commonly known as the floating pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that emerge at the water's surface. It plays a role in water ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and contributing to water clarity through its dense growth.

However, it can also become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
