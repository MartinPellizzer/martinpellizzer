Phymatodes cuspidata

Phymatodes cuspidata, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces edible fruits known as tunas.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
