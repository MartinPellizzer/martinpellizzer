Pterostylis thulia

Pterostylis thulia, commonly known as the violet-veined ghost orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and striking violet markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are small and pale, often with a greenish or brownish hue, and are adapted for specific pollinators.

The species is rare and faces threats from habitat loss and environmental changes.
