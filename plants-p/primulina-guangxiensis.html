Primulina guangxiensis

Primulina guangxiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi primula, is a rare species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
