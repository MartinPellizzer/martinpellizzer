Peperomia guanensis

Peperomia guanensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles.

Peperomia guanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
