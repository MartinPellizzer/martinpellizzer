Pedicularis verticillata var. caespitosa

Pedicularis verticillata var. caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered lousewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features wiry, branched stems that often form dense clusters, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine meadows.
