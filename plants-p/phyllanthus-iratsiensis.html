Phyllanthus iratsiensis

Phyllanthus iratsiensis, commonly known as the African buttonwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces non-showy, clustered fruits that contain hard, smooth seeds. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
