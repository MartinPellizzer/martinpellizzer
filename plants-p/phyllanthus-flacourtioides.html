Phyllanthus flacourtioides

Phyllanthus flacourtioides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-resistant.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
