Panicum trifolium

Panicum trifolium, commonly known as three-leaf panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grass species. It is native to various regions in North America and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats.

Panicum trifolium is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
