Penstemon scoparius

Penstemon scoparius, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon scoparius is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer.
