Panicum neurodes var. roxburghianum

Panicum neurodes var. roxburghianum, commonly known as Roxburgh's panicgrass, is a grass species native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
