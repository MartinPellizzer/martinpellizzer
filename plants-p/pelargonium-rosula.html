Pelargonium rosula

Pelargonium rosula, commonly known as the rose geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the geranium family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and fragrant, rose-scented flowers. The plant produces small, clustered blooms in shades of pink, red, and white, which appear throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a velvety texture.

Pelargonium rosula is often cultivated as a decorative plant and is valued for its ornamental appeal and pleasant aroma.
