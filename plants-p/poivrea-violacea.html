Poivrea violacea

Poivrea violacea, commonly known as the purple honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, violet to purple flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its showy, seasonal blooms.
