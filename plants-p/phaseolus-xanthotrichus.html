Phaseolus xanthotrichus

Phaseolus xanthotrichus, commonly known as the yellow-stemded bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its yellowish stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is a source of protein and fiber.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in various soil types and climates.
