Pilosella maurocybe

Pilosella maurocybe, commonly known as the Maurocybe mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellowish to white ray florets surrounded by numerous disk florets. Its leaves are typically silver-gray with a woolly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella maurocybe thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
