Pfeiffera monacantha

Pfeiffera monacantha, commonly known as the Monacan sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments.
