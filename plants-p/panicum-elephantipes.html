Panicum elephantipes

Panicum elephantipes, commonly known as the giant beardgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and dense, spiky inflorescences that resemble a beard, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open fields and grasslands. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it useful in erosion control.

Panicum elephantipes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts.
