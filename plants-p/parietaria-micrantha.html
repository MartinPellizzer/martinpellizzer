Parietaria micrantha

Parietaria micrantha, commonly known as the common wall-rocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats on rocky or disturbed soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Parietaria micrantha is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a common weed in gardens and along roadsides.
