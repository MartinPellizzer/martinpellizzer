Potentilla lanuginosa

Potentilla lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.

The downy cinquefoil is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
