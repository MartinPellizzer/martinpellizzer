Phlomis italica subsp. antiatlantica

Phlomis italica subsp.

antiatlantica, commonly known as the Mediterranean plumeweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stems and fluffy, pale purple or white flower heads.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
