Pentaraphia

Pentaraphia, commonly known as the golden star, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Pentaraphia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
