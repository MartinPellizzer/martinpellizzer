Paracaryum persicum var. cyclhymenium

Paracaryum persicum var. cyclhymenium, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, single or double flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in traditional horticulture.

The variety cyclhymenium is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral characteristics.
