Pennisetum alopecuroides var. erythrochaetum

Pennisetum alopecuroides var. erythrochaetum, commonly known as red hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant red-tipped foliage that adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces feathery plumes in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
