Pedicularis interrupta

Pedicularis interrupta, commonly known as the spotted winter orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes approximately 300 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with a fine, hairy texture.

Pedicularis interrupta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
