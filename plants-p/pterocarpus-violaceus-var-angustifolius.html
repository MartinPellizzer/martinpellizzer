Pterocarpus violaceus var. angustifolius

Pterocarpus violaceus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pterocarpus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its dark brown, fissured bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a narrow and pointed tip. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically pale pink or purple, while the fruit is a flat, elongated pod containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
