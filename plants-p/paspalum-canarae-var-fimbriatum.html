Paspalum canarae var. fimbriatum

Paspalum canarae var. fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed paspalum, is a grass species native to the tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
