Petracanthus junghuhnii

Petracanthus junghuhnii, commonly known as the rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in rocky and mountainous environments, often growing in crevices and on cliffs. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. Its flowers are white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring.

Petracanthus junghuhnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
