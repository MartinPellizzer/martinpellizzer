Pentacalia myrsinites

Pentacalia myrsinites, commonly known as the silverleaf pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white to silver hair, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying a pale green or yellowish hue.

Pentacalia myrsinites is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
