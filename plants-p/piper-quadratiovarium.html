Piper quadratiovarium

Piper quadratiovarium, commonly known as the quadrangular pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its square-shaped stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically glossy and have a mild, peppery aroma, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
