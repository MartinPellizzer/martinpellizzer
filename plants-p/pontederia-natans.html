Pontederia natans

Pontederia natans, commonly known as the floating heart, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, floating leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a useful wetland plant and a potential invasive species.
