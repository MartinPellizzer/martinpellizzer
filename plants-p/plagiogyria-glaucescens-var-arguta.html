Plagiogyria glaucescens var. arguta

Plagiogyria glaucescens var. arguta, commonly known as the argute plagiogyria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is native to southeastern Australia and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The varietal form 'arguta' is distinguished by its more pronounced leaf curvature and slightly different floral structure compared to the typical variety.
