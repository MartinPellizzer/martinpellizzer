Polystichum polyblepharon

Polystichum polyblepharon, commonly known as the two-lobed wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its lanceolate, serrated fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in structures called sori. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystichum polyblepharon is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
