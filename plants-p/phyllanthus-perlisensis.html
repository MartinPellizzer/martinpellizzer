Phyllanthus perlisensis

Phyllanthus perlisensis, commonly known as the Pearlstone gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, green, fleshy fruits that resemble tiny berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
