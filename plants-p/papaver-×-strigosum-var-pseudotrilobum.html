Papaver × strigosum var. pseudotrilobum

Papaver × strigosum var. pseudotrilobum, commonly known as the Pacific poppy, is a hybrid species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
