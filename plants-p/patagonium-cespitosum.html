Patagonium cespitosum

Patagonium cespitosum, commonly known as the Patagonian grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

Patagonium cespitosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
