Pubistylus

Pubistylus, commonly known as the hairy-styled orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, particularly the hairy staminodes that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are often small and clustered, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Botanically, Pubistylus is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, which involve specific insect interactions.
