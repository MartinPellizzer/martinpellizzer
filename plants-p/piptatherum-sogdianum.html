Piptatherum sogdianum

Piptatherum sogdianum, commonly known as the Sogdian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in steppes and rocky slopes, contributing to the ecological stability of these regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and a subject of study in botany and ecology.
