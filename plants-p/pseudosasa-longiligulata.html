Pseudosasa longiligulata

Pseudosasa longiligulata, commonly known as the long-leaf bamboo, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
