Pogonorrhinum hartlii

Pogonorrhinum hartlii, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated floral structures that resemble horns, giving it its common name. Native to specific regions in Africa, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Pogonorrhinum hartlii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
