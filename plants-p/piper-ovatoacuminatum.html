Piper ovatoacuminatum

Piper ovatoacuminatum, commonly known as the oval-pointed pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with acute tips, contributing to its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
