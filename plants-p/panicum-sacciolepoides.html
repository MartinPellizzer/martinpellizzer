Panicum sacciolepoides

Panicum sacciolepoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its drought tolerance.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
