Pithecellobium nervosum

Pithecellobium nervosum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant is notable for its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces edible pods containing sweet, sticky seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
