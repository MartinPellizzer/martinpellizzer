Pilosella lactucella subsp. magna

Pilosella lactucella subsp.

magna, commonly known as the giant milk thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, deeply lobed leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is closely related to other members of the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
