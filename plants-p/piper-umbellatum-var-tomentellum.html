Piper umbellatum var. tomentellum

Piper umbellatum var. tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, peppery berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and herbal collections.
