Pleurothallis perplexa

Pleurothallis perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are usually small, with a complex structure that includes a prominent lip, which is a key feature in orchid taxonomy.

Pleurothallis perplexa is noted for its adaptability to high humidity and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
