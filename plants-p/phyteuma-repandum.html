Phyteuma repandum

Phyteuma repandum, commonly known as the common phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
