Polygala vulgaris proles pseudoalpestris

Polygala vulgaris proles pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features slender stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
