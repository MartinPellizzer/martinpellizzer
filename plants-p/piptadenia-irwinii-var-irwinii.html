Piptadenia irwinii var. irwinii

Piptadenia irwinii var. irwinii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Piptadenia* tree, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
