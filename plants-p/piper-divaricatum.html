Piper divaricatum

Piper divaricatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, flexible stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use in humid, shaded environments.
