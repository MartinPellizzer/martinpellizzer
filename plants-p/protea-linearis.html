Protea linearis

Protea linearis, commonly known as the linear protea, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often showy flowers. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a silvery appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red in color.

Protea linearis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
