Panicum despiciens

Panicum despiciens, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along the edges of ponds.

Panicum despiciens is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
