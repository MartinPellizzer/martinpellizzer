Passiflora coactilis

Passiflora coactilis, commonly known as the "Coral Vine," is a climbing plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a mix of red, yellow, and blue hues, often with a crown-like structure at the base. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a mildly tart flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora coactilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
