Pyrus oblongolanceolata

Pyrus oblongolanceolata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
