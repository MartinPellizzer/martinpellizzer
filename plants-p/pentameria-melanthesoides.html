Pentameria melanthesoides

Pentameria melanthesoides, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has silvery-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions.

Pentameria melanthesoides is often found in rocky soils and plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems.
