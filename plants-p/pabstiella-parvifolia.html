Pabstiella parvifolia

Pabstiella parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved pabstiella, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle ornamental value to its otherwise unassuming appearance. Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
