Pyrus communis subsp. domestica

Pyrus communis subsp.

domestica, commonly known as the common pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The tree produces pomes, which are fleshy fruits with a central core and a sweet, sometimes aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
