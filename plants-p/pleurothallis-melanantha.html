Pleurothallis melanantha

Pleurothallis melanantha, commonly known as the dark-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique morphological features, including its dark green stems and intricate floral structures, make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Pleurothallis melanantha is also noted for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.
