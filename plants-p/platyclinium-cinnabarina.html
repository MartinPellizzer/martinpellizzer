Platyclinium cinnabarina

Platyclinium cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-stemmed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It lacks true leaves and instead absorbs nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
