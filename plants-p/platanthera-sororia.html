Platanthera sororia

Platanthera sororia, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a cluster of small, white to pale green flowers arranged in a loose spiral. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
