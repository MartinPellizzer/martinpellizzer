Phyllodes kummeriana

Phyllodes kummeriana, commonly known as the Kummer's golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are adapted for water conservation in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to coastal areas.

Phyllodes kummeriana is a hardy plant that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
