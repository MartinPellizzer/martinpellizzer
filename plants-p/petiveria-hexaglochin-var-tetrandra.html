Petiveria hexaglochin var. tetrandra

Petiveria hexaglochin var. tetrandra, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with aromatic leaves and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
