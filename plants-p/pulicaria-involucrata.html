Pulicaria involucrata

Pulicaria involucrata, commonly known as the woolly fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that features gray-green, woolly leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Pulicaria involucrata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
