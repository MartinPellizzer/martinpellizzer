Podocarpus meyerianus

Podocarpus meyerianus, commonly known as the Meyer's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The tree produces cones that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Podocarpus meyerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide shade in forested areas.
