Penstemon lassenianus

Penstemon lassenianus, commonly known as the Lassen's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
