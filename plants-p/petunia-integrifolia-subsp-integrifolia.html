Petunia integrifolia subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the common petunia, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers. Petunia integrifolia subsp.

integrifolia is known for its vigorous growth and ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
