Prunus cerasus var. actiana

Prunus cerasus var. actiana, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features glossy dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by sweet, edible fruits in summer. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal, with its dense foliage and showy blossoms making it a popular choice in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and is relatively low maintenance.

The fruit is often used in jams, sauces, and desserts, adding both aesthetic and culinary value to the plant.
