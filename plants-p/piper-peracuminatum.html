Piper peracuminatum

Piper peracuminatum, commonly known as the bitter pepper or peracuminatum pepper, is a tropical flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico, Guatemala, and parts of the Amazon rainforest. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often glossy and have a distinct aroma. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
