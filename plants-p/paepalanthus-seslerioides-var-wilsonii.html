Paepalanthus seslerioides var. wilsonii

Paepalanthus seslerioides var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's sesleria, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.

This variety is relatively hardy and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade.
