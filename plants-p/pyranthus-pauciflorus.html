Pyranthus pauciflorus

Pyranthus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered pyranthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Pyranthus pauciflorus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
