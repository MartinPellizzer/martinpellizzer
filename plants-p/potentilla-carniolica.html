Potentilla carniolica

Potentilla carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, oval fruits called achenes.

Potentilla carniolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
