Peperomia trichocaulis

Peperomia trichocaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive hairy appearance on its stems and undersides. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia trichocaulis is popular for its unique appearance and ease of care, often used as a decorative houseplant.
