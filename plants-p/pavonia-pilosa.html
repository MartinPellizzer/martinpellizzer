Pavonia pilosa

Pavonia pilosa, commonly known as the silky pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers are arranged in dense, terminal panicles.

Pavonia pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
