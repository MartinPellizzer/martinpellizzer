Ptychotis sylvestris

Ptychotis sylvestris, commonly known as the forest tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces pink or white flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It is often found in woodland edges and is adapted to tolerate drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
