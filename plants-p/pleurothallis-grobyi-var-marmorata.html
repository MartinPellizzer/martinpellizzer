Pleurothallis grobyi var. marmorata

Pleurothallis grobyi var. marmorata, commonly known as the marbled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of white and green, with a distinctive marbled pattern on the petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to flourish.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
