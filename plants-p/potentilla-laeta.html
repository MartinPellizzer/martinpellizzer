Potentilla laeta

Potentilla laeta, commonly known as the silver potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Potentilla laeta is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
