Phyllanthus obcordatus

Phyllanthus obcordatus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features small, green, obcordate-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers and small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
