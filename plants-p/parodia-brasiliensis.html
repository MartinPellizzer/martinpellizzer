Parodia brasiliensis

Parodia brasiliensis, commonly known as the "Brazillian Cactus" or "Lobed Cactus," is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing, compact cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
