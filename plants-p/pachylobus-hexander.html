Pachylobus hexander

Pachylobus hexander, commonly known as the black hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, rough bark and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation.

Pachylobus hexander is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
