Polygala elongata var. hirsutula

Polygala elongata var. hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy thread-plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
