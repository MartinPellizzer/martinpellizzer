Piper actae

Piper actae, commonly known as the sacred pepper or sacred plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like berries. Its botanical structure supports its use in traditional medicine and spiritual practices.

The plant's morphology and chemical composition make it unique within its genus.
