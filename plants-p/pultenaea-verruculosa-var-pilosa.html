Pultenaea verruculosa var. pilosa

Pultenaea verruculosa var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open forests and heathlands. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and seasonal display.
