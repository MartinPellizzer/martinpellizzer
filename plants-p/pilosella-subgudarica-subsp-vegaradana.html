Pilosella subgudarica subsp. vegaradana

Pilosella subgudarica subsp.

vegaradana, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

This subspecies is distinguished by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

The plant is often found in open meadows and slopes at high altitudes.
