Psiadia arabica

Psiadia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian coffee plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark red berries containing a single seed, which is the source of the coffee bean. Psiadia arabica thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires a humid environment.

It is an important crop in the region, historically significant for its role in the development of coffee culture.
