Piper negrosense

Piper negrosense, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped berries. The plant produces small, green berries that turn dark red to black when mature, containing the essential spice known as black pepper. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
