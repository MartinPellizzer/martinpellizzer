Pelargonium nodosum

Pelargonium nodosum, commonly known as the nodding geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and purple. The leaves are typically rounded and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
