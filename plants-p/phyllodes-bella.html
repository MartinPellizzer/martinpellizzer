Phyllodes bella

Phyllodes bella, commonly known as the pink phyllodine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flattened leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Phyllodes bella thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
