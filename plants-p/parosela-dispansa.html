Parosela dispansa

Parosela dispansa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas.

Parosela dispansa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
