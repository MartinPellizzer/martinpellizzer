Pachira insignis

Pachira insignis, commonly known as the Amazonian pachira, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pachira insignis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
