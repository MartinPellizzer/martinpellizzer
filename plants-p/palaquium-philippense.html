Palaquium philippense

Palaquium philippense, commonly known as the Philippine cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, spiny fruits resembling a cross between a pear and a melon. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic pulp, which is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
