Paphiopedilum × cribbii

Paphiopedilum × cribbii, commonly known as the Cribb's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, yellow, and green hues, with intricate patterns.

It prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
