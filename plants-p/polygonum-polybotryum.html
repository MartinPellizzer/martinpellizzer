Polygonum polybotryum

Polygonum polybotryum, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with toothed margins, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Polygonum polybotryum is known for its rapid growth and is often considered invasive in certain regions.
