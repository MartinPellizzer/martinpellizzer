Poa linearifolia

Poa linearifolia, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown.

Poa linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
