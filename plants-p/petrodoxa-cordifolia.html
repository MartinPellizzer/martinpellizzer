Petrodoxa cordifolia

Petrodoxa cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf petrodoxa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
