Perilla shimadae

Perilla shimadae, commonly known as the Japanese perilla, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves used in traditional cuisines.

Perilla shimadae thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
