Plantago sabrinae

Plantago sabrinae, commonly known as the sand plant or desert plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features a rosette of broad, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils, making it well-adapted to harsh environments.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
