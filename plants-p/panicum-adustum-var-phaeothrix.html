Panicum adustum var. phaeothrix

Panicum adustum var. phaeothrix, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a variant of panicgrass characterized by its dark, almost black foliage.

It is a perennial grass that thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow, rigid, and have a rough texture, contributing to its hardy nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique appearance.
