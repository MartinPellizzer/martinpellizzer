Phyllanthus eboracensis

Phyllanthus eboracensis, commonly known as the European phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Phyllanthus eboracensis is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
