Pyrus moschata

Pyrus moschata, commonly known as the quince, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The fruit, which is typically harvested in late autumn, is a rough, golden-orange citrus-like fruit that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its tolerance to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in temperate climates.
