Pachyphyllum muscoides

Pachyphyllum muscoides, commonly known as the moss-like cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leaf-like structures that are actually modified stems, which store water and nutrients. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth form, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late summer.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and often endangered species in its native range.
