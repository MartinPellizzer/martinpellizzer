Phyllanthus caraculiensis

Phyllanthus caraculiensis, commonly known as the Armenian Phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to its harsh native habitat.
