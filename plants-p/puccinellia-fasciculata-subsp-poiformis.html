Puccinellia fasciculata subsp. poiformis

Puccinellia fasciculata subsp.

poiformis, commonly known as the clustered spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions.

The plant has tufted growth habit with slender, often hairless stems that bear dense clusters of spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and usually shorter than the flowering stems.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often arranged in a compact, fasciculate manner, giving the plant its specific epithet.
