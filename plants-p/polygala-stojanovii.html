Polygala stojanovii

Polygala stojanovii, commonly known as the Stojanov's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
