Pinus stylesii

Pinus stylesii, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically blue-green in color and arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are often thick and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and cold climates.

This species is notable for its longevity and resilience, making it an important component of its native ecosystems.
