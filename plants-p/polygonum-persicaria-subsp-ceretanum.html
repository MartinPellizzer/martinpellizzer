Polygonum persicaria subsp. ceretanum

Polygonum persicaria subsp.

ceretanum, commonly known as red-rooted smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

The plant features upright stems that are reddish near the base, and it produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its adaptability to various wetland habitats and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
