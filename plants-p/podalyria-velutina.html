Podalyria velutina

Podalyria velutina, commonly known as the velvetpod gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Podalyria velutina is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
