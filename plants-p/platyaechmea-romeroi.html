Platyaechmea romeroi

Platyaechmea romeroi, commonly known as the Romero's torch plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and vibrant, fiery red bracts that resemble a torch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the base of the bracts, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Platyaechmea romeroi is a popular choice for indoor gardens due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
