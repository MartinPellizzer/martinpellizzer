Prenanthes virgata

Prenanthes virgata, commonly known as the common arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its arrowhead-shaped foliage, which is a distinctive characteristic of the species.
