Phaius leonidii

Phaius leonidii, commonly known as the Leonid's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, often forming a rosette at the base of the plant.

Phaius leonidii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
