Phalaenopsis violacea

Phalaenopsis violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and long-lasting, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically produces a single spike with several blooms, each featuring a velvety texture and intricate patterns. Its leaves are broad, dark green, and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

This orchid thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media for optimal growth.
