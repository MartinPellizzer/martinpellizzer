Plectoma

Plectoma, commonly known as the "blue-eyed grass," is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Plectoma is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in humid climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
