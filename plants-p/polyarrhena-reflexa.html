Polyarrhena reflexa

Polyarrhena reflexa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its fruit is a slender, hairy achene that is dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across dry, sandy soils.

Polyarrhena reflexa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
