Pandanus perakensis

Pandanus perakensis, commonly known as the Borneo screw pine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts.

Pandanus perakensis thrives in coastal and wetland areas, adapting well to saline and waterlogged soils.
