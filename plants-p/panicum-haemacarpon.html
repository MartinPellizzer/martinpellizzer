Panicum haemacarpon

Panicum haemacarpon, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, reddish-brown foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Panicum haemacarpon is a perennial grass that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
