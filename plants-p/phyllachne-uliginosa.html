Phyllachne uliginosa

Phyllachne uliginosa, commonly known as the bog grass or marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and other wetland environments.

Phyllachne uliginosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
