Phlox candida

Phlox candida, commonly known as white phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with narrow, dark green leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Phlox candida thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
