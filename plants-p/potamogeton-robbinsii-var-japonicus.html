Potamogeton robbinsii var. japonicus

Potamogeton robbinsii var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often found near the water's surface. It thrives in freshwater environments with moderate to slow water flow and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Potamogeton genus and is known for its ecological importance in supporting aquatic life.
