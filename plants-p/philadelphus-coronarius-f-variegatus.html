Philadelphus coronarius f. variegatus

Philadelphus coronarius f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated mock orange, is a cultivar of the mock orange plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and pleasant scent, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
