Pachystoma chinense var. formosanum

Pachystoma chinense var. formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental value contribute to its appeal among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
