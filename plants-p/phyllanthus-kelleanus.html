Phyllanthus kelleanus

Phyllanthus kelleanus, commonly known as the Kellean's spurge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and scientific research.
