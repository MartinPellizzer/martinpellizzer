Potentilla sterilis var. fragariastrum

Potentilla sterilis var. fragariastrum, commonly known as the fragrant potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its fragrance is subtle but noticeable, especially when the plant is in bloom.
