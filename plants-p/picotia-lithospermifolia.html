Picotia lithospermifolia

Picotia lithospermifolia, commonly known as the rock pepper plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

Picotia lithospermifolia is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
