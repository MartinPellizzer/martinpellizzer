Premna viburnoides

Premna viburnoides, commonly known as the small-leaved beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by violet to purple berries that are an attractive feature. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
