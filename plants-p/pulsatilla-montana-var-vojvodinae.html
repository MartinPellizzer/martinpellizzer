Pulsatilla montana var. vojvodinae

Pulsatilla montana var. vojvodinae, commonly known as the Vojvodina pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a single, hairy stem that supports its blooms.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring flowering.
