Prunus maritima var. lutea-magna

Prunus maritima var. lutea-magna, commonly known as the yellow-flowered beach plum, is a variant of the beach plum native to coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy soils along shorelines. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its most distinctive characteristic is its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in early summer.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
