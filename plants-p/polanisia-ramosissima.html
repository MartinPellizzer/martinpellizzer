Polanisia ramosissima

Polanisia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy polanisia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
