Pelargonium caespitosum

Pelargonium caespitosum, commonly known as the ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the summer months. It is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its resilience and ornamental appeal make it a favored plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
