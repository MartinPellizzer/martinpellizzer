Plectranthus kamerunensis

Plectranthus kamerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to Cameroon and neighboring regions of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
