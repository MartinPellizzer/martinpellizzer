Picris aspera

Picris aspera, commonly known as rough hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are rough and hairy, with a distinctive serrated edge, and its yellow flowers appear in late summer.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas, and is considered a weed in some regions.
