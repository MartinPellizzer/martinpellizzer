Penstemon serrulatus

Penstemon serrulatus, commonly known as the sawleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and spikes of tubular, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Penstemon serrulatus is also noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
