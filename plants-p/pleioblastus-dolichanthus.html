Pleioblastus dolichanthus

Pleioblastus dolichanthus, commonly known as the long-awned bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, linear leaves. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are not showy but are important for its reproductive cycle.

Pleioblastus dolichanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese gardens.
