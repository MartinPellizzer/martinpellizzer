Polygala densifolia var. grandiflora

Polygala densifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, spicate inflorescences with distinctive, often pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
