Pisum sativum var. funiculatum

Pisum sativum var. funiculatum, commonly known as the funiculate pea, is a botanical variety of the pea plant characterized by its unique seed structure.

The seeds of this variety are elongated and have a fibrous, funiculate attachment to the pod, which distinguishes it from other pea varieties. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions where it has adapted to thrive in less hospitable conditions. The plant produces small, often pea-green pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its unique morphological features and potential for genetic study.
