Polyachyrus cinereus

Polyachyrus cinereus, commonly known as the ash-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy white seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
