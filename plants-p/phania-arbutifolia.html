Phania arbutifolia

Phania arbutifolia, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its fruits are small, red drupes that contain a single seed.

Phania arbutifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
