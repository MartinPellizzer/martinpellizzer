Pitcairnia alata

Pitcairnia alata, commonly known as the velvet queen, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, velvety hairs, giving it a unique texture and appearance. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pitcairnia alata is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
