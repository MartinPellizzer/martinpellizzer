Phyllomphax

Phyllomphax, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night and close by morning. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round berries that are edible but often bitter. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Phyllomphax is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is adapted to thrive in shaded environments.
