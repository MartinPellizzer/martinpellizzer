Pithecellobium forfex

Pithecellobium forfex, commonly known as the swordleaf acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features elongated, sword-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow. It produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
