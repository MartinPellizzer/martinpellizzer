Pachyphytum neglectum

Pachyphytum neglectum, commonly known as the Neglectful Pachyphytum, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant has a compact growth habit with a rosette-shaped arrangement of leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
