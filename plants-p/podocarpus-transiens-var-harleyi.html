Podocarpus transiens var. harleyi

Podocarpus transiens var. harleyi, commonly known as the dwarf podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, woody cones. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
