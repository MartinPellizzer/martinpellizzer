Pimpinella tragium var. depressa

Pimpinella tragium var. depressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowparsley, is a biennial or perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
