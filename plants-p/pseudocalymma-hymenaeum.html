Pseudocalymma hymenaeum

Pseudocalymma hymenaeum, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and silver-tomentose leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a short lifespan and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
