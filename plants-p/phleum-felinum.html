Phleum felinum

Phleum felinum, commonly known as cat's tail grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, fluffy seed heads that resemble a cat's tail, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Phleum felinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
