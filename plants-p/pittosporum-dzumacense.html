Pittosporum dzumacense

Pittosporum dzumacense, commonly known as the Chilean pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Pittosporum dzumacense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
