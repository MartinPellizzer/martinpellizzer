Plectranthus seretii

Plectranthus seretii, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have made it a favored plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
