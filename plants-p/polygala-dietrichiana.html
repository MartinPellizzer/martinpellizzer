Polygala dietrichiana

Polygala dietrichiana, commonly known as the German milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bead-like fruits that contain a single seed.

It is a perennial plant that is well adapted to semi-arid environments.
