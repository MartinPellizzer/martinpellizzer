Parasenecio praetermissus

Parasenecio praetermissus, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
