Pycnostachys coerulea

Pycnostachys coerulea, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
