Plagiostachys odorata

Plagiostachys odorata, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia.

It features aromatic, grass-like leaves that emit a distinctive, sweet fragrance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Plagiostachys odorata is valued both for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its historical use in herbal remedies.
