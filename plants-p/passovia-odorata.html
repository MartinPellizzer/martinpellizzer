Passovia odorata

Passovia odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that emit a subtle, sweet aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a variety of colors ranging from white to pale pink.
