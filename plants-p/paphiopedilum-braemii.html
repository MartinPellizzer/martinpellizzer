Paphiopedilum braemii

Paphiopedilum braemii, commonly known as the Braem's lady's slipper, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with dark purple and red markings, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific environmental requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
