Pseudixus opuntia

Pseudixus opuntia, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems known as cladodes. These stems are covered in spines and are adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers, followed by edible fruits called tunas.

It plays an important ecological role in desert ecosystems and is also valued for its ornamental and commercial uses.
