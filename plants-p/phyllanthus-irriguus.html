Phyllanthus irriguus

Phyllanthus irriguus, commonly known as the Indian cheesenut, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
