Phlox caespitosa subvar. piperi

Phlox caespitosa subvar. piperi, commonly known as the purple phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple floral spikes in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.

This species is well-suited for gardens and naturalized areas due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
