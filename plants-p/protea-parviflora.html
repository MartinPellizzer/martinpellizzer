Protea parviflora

Protea parviflora, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored inflorescences. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Protea parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
