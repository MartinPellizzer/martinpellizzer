Polygala caerulescens

Polygala caerulescens, commonly known as blue milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue or violet flowers, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Polygala caerulescens is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its resilience to dry conditions.
