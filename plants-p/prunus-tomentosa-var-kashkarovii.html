Prunus tomentosa var. kashkarovii

Prunus tomentosa var. kashkarovii, commonly known as the Kashkarovii cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent bark and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in temperate climates.
