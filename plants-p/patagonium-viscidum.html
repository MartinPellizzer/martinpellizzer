Patagonium viscidum

Patagonium viscidum, commonly known as the sticky Patagonian plant, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that secrete a sticky substance, which helps in water retention and deterring herbivores. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation. Its leaves are usually small and succulent, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Patagonium viscidum plays a role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environment and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
