Pleurothallis ripleyi

Pleurothallis ripleyi, commonly known as the Ripleys' pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive lip that is adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and specialized orchid species.
