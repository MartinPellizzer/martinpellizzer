Pseudolysimachion holophyllum

Pseudolysimachion holophyllum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pseudolysimachion holophyllum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
