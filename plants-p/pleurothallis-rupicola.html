Pleurothallis rupicola

Pleurothallis rupicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks, often in humid and shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

Pleurothallis rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
