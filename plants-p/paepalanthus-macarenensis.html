Paepalanthus macarenensis

Paepalanthus macarenensis, commonly known as the Macarena palm, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in areas with well-drained soil and adequate sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
