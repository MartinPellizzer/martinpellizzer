Pinus montana subf. pendula

Pinus montana subf.

pendula, commonly known as the weeping mountain pine, is a variant of the mountain pine characterized by its distinctive cascading foliage. This subspecies features long, pendulous shoots that give it a unique, flowing appearance.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps. The needles are typically blue-green in color and grow in clusters of five.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking visual form.
