Prunus jamasakura var. superflua

Prunus jamasakura var. superflua, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate pink and white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The tree has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its seasonal beauty. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and public spaces.
