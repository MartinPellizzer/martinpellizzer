Phelipanche hypertomentosa

Phelipanche hypertomentosa, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant is also known for its toxic properties, making it a concern in agricultural settings.
