Passiflora brighamii

Passiflora brighamii, commonly known as Brigham's passionflower, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five prominent sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Passiflora brighamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
