Polygala ukambica f. angustifolia

Polygala ukambica f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white in color. This species is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for various pollinators.
