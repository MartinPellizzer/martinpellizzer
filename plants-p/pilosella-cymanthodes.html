Pilosella cymanthodes

Pilosella cymanthodes, commonly known as the clustered pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with a pearly appearance, which are surrounded by bracts that give it its common name. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Pilosella cymanthodes is often found in moist soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
