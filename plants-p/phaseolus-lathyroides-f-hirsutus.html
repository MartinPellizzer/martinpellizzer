Phaseolus lathyroides f. hirsutus

Phaseolus lathyroides f. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy passion vine, is a variant of the passion vine family native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are followed by elongated, bean-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
