Phebalium tuberculosum subsp. megaphyllum

Phebalium tuberculosum subsp.

megaphyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf phebalium, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, tuberculate (bumpy) stems and large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
