Pelargonium erythraeum

Pelargonium erythraeum, commonly known as the Cape Verge or the Red-stemmed Geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Pelargonium erythraeum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
