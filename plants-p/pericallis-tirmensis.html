Pericallis tirmensis

Pericallis tirmensis, commonly known as the Tirmensian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Tirmen.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to its rocky habitat.

Pericallis tirmensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
