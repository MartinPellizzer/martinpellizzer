Phyllanthus piscatorum

Phyllanthus piscatorum, commonly known as water cheesewood, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils near water bodies and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
