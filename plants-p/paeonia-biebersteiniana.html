Paeonia biebersteiniana

Paeonia biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Siberian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces compound, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Paeonia biebersteiniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
