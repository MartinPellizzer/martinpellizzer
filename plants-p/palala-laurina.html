Palala laurina

Palala laurina, commonly known as the red-flowered laurina, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy, red-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, often emitting a subtle spicy scent when crushed.

Palala laurina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
