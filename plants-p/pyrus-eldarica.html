Pyrus eldarica

Pyrus eldarica, commonly known as the wild pear or eldarica pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
