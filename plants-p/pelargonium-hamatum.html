Pelargonium hamatum

Pelargonium hamatum, commonly known as the rock rose, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has serrated, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and hanging baskets.

Pelargonium hamatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
