Parsonsia calophylla

Parsonsia calophylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Parsonsia calophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
