Philadelphus pubescens var. verrucosus

Philadelphus pubescens var. verrucosus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant collections.
