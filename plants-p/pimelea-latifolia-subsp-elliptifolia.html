Pimelea latifolia subsp. elliptifolia

Pimelea latifolia subsp.

elliptifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
