Pyrus aucuparia subvar. rossica

Pyrus aucuparia subvar. rossica, commonly known as the Russian whitebeam, is a subspecies of the common whitebeam.

It is a deciduous tree native to the forests of Russia and parts of Eastern Europe. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
