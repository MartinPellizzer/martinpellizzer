Pleurothallis longisepala

Pleurothallis longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often appearing in clusters during the wet season.

Pleurothallis longisepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
