Prunus vapincensis

Prunus vapincensis, commonly known as the Vapincens cherry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, bright red berries. Prunus vapincensis thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, temperate climate to flourish.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
