Plocama brevifolia subsp. brevifolia

Plocama brevifolia subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plocama, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits.
