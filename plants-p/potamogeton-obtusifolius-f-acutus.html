Potamogeton obtusifolius f. acutus

Potamogeton obtusifolius f. acutus, commonly known as the acute-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically acute at the tip. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spikelets, which are often submerged or floating. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often used as an indicator of water quality and ecological health in wetlands and lakes.
