Polyspora excelsa

Polyspora excelsa, commonly known as the tall spider plant, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces long, arching inflorescences that resemble spider-like structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Polyspora excelsa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
