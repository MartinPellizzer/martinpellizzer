Plesiagopus maritima

Plesiagopus maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiky, bract-like leaves. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Plesiagopus maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
