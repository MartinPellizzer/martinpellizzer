Prunus serrulata subvar. speciosa

Prunus serrulata subvar. speciosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or Yamazakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has ovate to elliptical leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

The flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
