Polypodium australe f. crispum

Polypodium australe f. crispum, commonly known as the Australian shield fern, is a fern species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The fronds of this plant are often curled or crisped, giving it the name "crispum," which refers to its crinkled appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
