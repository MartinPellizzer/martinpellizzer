Psidium cattleyanum var. coriaceum

Psidium cattleyanum var. coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-hairy guava, is a tropical plant characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers.

It produces edible fruits that are typically smaller and more acidic than those of the common guava. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a shrub or small tree form with a short trunk and dense foliage.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
