Plagianthus spicatus var. pubescens

Plagianthus spicatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy plagiathus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically hairy, giving the plant its common name, and are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
