Puya ferox

Puya ferox, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, clustered flowers. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Puya ferox is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
