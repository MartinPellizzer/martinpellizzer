Pyrus kirghisorum

Pyrus kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruit, a pear-like berry, is typically edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in local horticulture.
