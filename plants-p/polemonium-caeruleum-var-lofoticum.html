Polemonium caeruleum var. lofoticum

Polemonium caeruleum var. lofoticum, commonly known as the Lofoten bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the Lofoten Islands in Norway.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and showy, bell-shaped blooms.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
