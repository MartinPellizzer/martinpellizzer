Potentilla grisea var. vilijuga

Potentilla grisea var. vilijuga, commonly known as the gray cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features wiry, spreading stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
