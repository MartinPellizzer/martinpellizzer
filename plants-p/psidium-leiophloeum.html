Psidium leiophloeum

Psidium leiophloeum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, smooth, and often glabrous leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, berry-like fruits. Its fruit is typically yellow or red when ripe and is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor.

Psidium leiophloeum is often grown in home gardens and for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
