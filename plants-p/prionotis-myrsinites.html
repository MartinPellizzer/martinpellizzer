Prionotis myrsinites

Prionotis myrsinites, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Prionotis myrsinites is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
