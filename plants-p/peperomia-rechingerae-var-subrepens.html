Peperomia rechingerae var. subrepens

Peperomia rechingerae var. subrepens, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a waxy texture and a variety of colors ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, often in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by spore-bearing structures. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it an ideal choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
