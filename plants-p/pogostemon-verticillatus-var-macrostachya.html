Pogostemon verticillatus var. macrostachya

Pogostemon verticillatus var. macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and whorled arrangement of leaves, which are typically lanceolate and aromatic. The plant produces terminal spikes of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
