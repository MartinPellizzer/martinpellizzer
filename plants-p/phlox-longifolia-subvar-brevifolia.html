Phlox longifolia subvar. brevifolia

Phlox longifolia subvar. brevifolia, commonly known as the shortstem phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
