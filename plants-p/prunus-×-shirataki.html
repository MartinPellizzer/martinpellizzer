Prunus × shirataki

Prunus × shirataki, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is celebrated for its ornamental value, particularly during the spring blooming season. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a stunning visual display. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters.

The leaves are oval-shaped, with a serrated edge, and turn a vibrant yellow in the fall.
