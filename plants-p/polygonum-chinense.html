Polygonum chinense

Polygonum chinense, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that often have a reddish hue, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Polygonum chinense is noted for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
