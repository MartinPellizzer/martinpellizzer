Phyllocactus crenatus var. roseus-grandiflorus

Phyllocactus crenatus var. roseus-grandiflorus, commonly known as the roseus-grandiflorus phyllocactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. Its succulent leaves are modified into spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce transpiration.

This plant is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
