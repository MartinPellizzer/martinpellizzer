Potentilla matzialekii

Potentilla matzialekii, commonly known as the Matzialek's cinquefoil, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically divided into five oval lobes. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
