Passiflora rigidula

Passiflora rigidula, commonly known as the rigid passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are edible and contain a single seed. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed and have a rough texture.

Passiflora rigidula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
