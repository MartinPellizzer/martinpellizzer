Penstemon linarioides subsp. coloradoensis

Penstemon linarioides subsp.

coloradoensis, commonly known as the Colorado penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or arid environments.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
