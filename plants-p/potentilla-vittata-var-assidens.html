Potentilla vittata var. assidens

Potentilla vittata var. assidens, commonly known as the creeping potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, upright leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
