Panicum curviflorum var. suishaense

Panicum curviflorum var. suishaense, commonly known as the Suisha panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
