Piper caldense

Piper caldense, commonly known as the Caldense pepper or Caldense pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and it is known for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine. Its botanical characteristics include opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, understory habitats.
