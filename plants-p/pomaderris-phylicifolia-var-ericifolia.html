Pomaderris phylicifolia var. ericifolia

Pomaderris phylicifolia var. ericifolia, commonly known as the Ericifoliate Pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

The variety is distinguished by its smaller, more densely hairy leaves compared to the typical form.
