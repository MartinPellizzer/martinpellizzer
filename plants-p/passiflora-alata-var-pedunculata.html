Passiflora alata var. pedunculata

Passiflora alata var. pedunculata, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors, including white, blue, and purple, with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are covered in tendrils that aid in climbing.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
