Picramnia excelsa

Picramnia excelsa, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
