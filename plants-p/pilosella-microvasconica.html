Pilosella microvasconica

Pilosella microvasconica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, hairy foliage and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Pilosella microvasconica is a low-growing perennial that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
