Panicum columbianum var. oricola

Panicum columbianum var. oricola, commonly known as the Colombian panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous fine, hair-like florets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
