Pachycereus gatesii

Pachycereus gatesii, commonly known as the Gates cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows as a solitary stem with a rounded, columnar shape. It produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for desert landscaping.
