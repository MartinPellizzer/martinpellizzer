Poa pratensis var. hydrophila

Poa pratensis var. hydrophila, commonly known as water meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to wet or moist environments.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils. This plant often grows in marshes, along stream banks, and in other wetland habitats. Its leaves are typically slender and have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
