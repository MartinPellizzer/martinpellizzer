Paepalanthus gyrotrichus

Paepalanthus gyrotrichus, commonly known as the spiral-armed cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged branches that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Paepalanthus gyrotrichus is adapted to survive in harsh, low-nutrient environments through its succulent stems and deep root system.
