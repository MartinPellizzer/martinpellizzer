Persea indica

Persea indica, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit's skin is thin and turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
