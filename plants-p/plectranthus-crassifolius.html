Plectranthus crassifolius

Plectranthus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features thick, succulent leaves that are often grayish-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both garden and indoor settings.
