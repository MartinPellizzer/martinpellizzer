Psychechilos gracilis

Psychechilos gracilis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or rocks rather than in the ground.

Psychechilos gracilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
