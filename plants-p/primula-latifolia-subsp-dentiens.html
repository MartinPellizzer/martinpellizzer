Primula latifolia subsp. dentiens

Primula latifolia subsp.

dentiens, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat.

The leaves are broad, ovate, and slightly toothed, giving the plant its subspecies name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
