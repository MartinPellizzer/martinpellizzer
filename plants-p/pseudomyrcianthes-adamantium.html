Pseudomyrcianthes adamantium

Pseudomyrcianthes adamantium, commonly known as the adamantium myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include smooth, glossy bark and lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant underside.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland rainforest areas.
