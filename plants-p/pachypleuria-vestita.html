Pachypleuria vestita

Pachypleuria vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
