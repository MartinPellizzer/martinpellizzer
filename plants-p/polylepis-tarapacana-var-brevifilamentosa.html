Polylepis tarapacana var. brevifilamentosa

Polylepis tarapacana var. brevifilamentosa, commonly known as the short-filament polylepis, is a shrub or small tree native to the high Andes of Peru.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and distinctive, elongated bracts that give it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions, and is adapted to cold, dry conditions. Its leaves are small and leathery, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

This species is of ecological importance in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
