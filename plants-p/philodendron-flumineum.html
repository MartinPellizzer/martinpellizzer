Philodendron flumineum

Philodendron flumineum, commonly known as the river philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green, often with a lighter midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Philodendron flumineum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.
