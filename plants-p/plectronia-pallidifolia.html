Plectronia pallidifolia

Plectronia pallidifolia, commonly known as the pale-flowered plectronia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, which helps to retain moisture.

Plectronia pallidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
