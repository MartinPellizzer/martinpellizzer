Piper pendulirameum

Piper pendulirameum, commonly known as the hanging pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, hanging stems and small, opposite leaves that are often glossy in appearance. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique growth habit.
