Persea erythropus var. ovata

Persea erythropus var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate persimmon, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a smooth, orange to reddish-brown berry.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
