Piper crocatum

Piper crocatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn from green to red and finally to black as they mature. The fruit's dried, dark seeds are the source of the spice known as black pepper.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
