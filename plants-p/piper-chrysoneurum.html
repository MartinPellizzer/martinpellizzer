Piper chrysoneurum

Piper chrysoneurum, commonly known as the golden-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent yellow veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into narrow, elongated fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
