Pterocactus kuntzei f. lelongii

Pterocactus kuntzei f. lelongii, commonly known as the Lelong's pterocactus, is a rare and spiny cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense clusters of spines. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting a slow growth rate. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom during the warmer months and attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
