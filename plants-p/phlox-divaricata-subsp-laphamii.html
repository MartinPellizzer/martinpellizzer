Phlox divaricata subsp. laphamii

Phlox divaricata subsp.

laphamii, commonly known as Lapham's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States. It is a perennial herb with slender, ascending stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
