Peplis hispidula

Peplis hispidula, commonly known as the hairy peplis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has a hairy texture on its stems and leaves, which gives it its common name. It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

Peplis hispidula is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
