Potentilla emarginata f. integriloba

Potentilla emarginata f. integriloba, commonly known as the smoothleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, rounded leaves that are typically arranged in a five-leaflet pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is noted for its horticultural value and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
