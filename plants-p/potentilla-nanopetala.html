Potentilla nanopetala

Potentilla nanopetala, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has wiry, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for gardeners interested in native and hardy plants.
