Panicum vulpinum

Panicum vulpinum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive reddish tinge in the leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like clusters of tiny flowers, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
