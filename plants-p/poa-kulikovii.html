Poa kulikovii

Poa kulikovii, commonly known as the Kulikov grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, Poa kulikovii is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
