Pouteria crassiflora

Pouteria crassiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, particularly selenium and healthy fats.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, supporting various wildlife species.
