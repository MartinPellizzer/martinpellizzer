Phyllanthus lediformis

Phyllanthus lediformis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, making it an important food source for various wildlife species.
