Pithecellobium unguis-cati

Pithecellobium unguis-cati, commonly known as the cat's claw.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its name is derived from the distinctive claw-like structures on its fruit, which aid in seed dispersal.
