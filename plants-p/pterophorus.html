Pterophorus

Pterophorus, commonly known as the plume moth, is a genus of small moths belonging to the family Pterophoridae.

These insects are characterized by their delicate, feathery wings that are often translucent or iridescent. The larvae of Pterophorus species typically feed on a variety of plants, including grasses, sedges, and some flowering plants. Their life cycle involves complete metamorphosis, with eggs laid on host plants and larvae undergoing several developmental stages before pupation.

The genus is notable for its unique wing structure and ecological role in plant ecosystems.
