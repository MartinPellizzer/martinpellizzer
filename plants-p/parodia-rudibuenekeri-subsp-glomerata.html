Parodia rudibuenekeri subsp. glomerata

Parodia rudibuenekeri subsp.

glomerata, commonly known as the clustered parodia, is a succulent cactus native to Argentina. It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and dense clusters of cylindrical stems.

The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
