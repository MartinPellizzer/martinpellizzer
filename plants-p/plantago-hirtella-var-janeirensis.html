Plantago hirtella var. janeirensis

Plantago hirtella var. janeirensis, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in Brazil.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a hairy appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
