Pyxidanthera spatulata

Pyxidanthera spatulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often clustered at the base of the plant.

It is often found in open woodlands and prairies, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
