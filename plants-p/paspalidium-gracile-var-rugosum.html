Paspalidium gracile var. rugosum

Paspalidium gracile var. rugosum, commonly known as the rough slender paspalum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
