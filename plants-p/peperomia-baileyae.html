Peperomia baileyae

Peperomia baileyae, commonly known as the Bailey's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a deep green or variegated coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, emerging from the leaf axils. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and minimal water needs.

Peperomia baileyae is valued for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
