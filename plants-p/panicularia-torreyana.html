Panicularia torreyana

Panicularia torreyana, commonly known as Torrey's panicled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, tubular flowers, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Panicularia torreyana is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
