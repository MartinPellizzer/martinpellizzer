Pilosella pavichii

Pilosella pavichii, commonly known as the Pavich's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white to pale yellow petals and a yellow center. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella pavichii is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
