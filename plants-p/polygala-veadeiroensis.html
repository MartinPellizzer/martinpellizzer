Polygala veadeiroensis

Polygala veadeiroensis, commonly known as the Veadeiro's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family, which includes a variety of shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, contributing to its overall grass-like appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
