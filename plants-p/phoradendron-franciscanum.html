Phoradendron franciscanum

Phoradendron franciscanum, commonly known as the Franciscan mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and has a distinctive appearance with smooth, glossy green leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds, which in turn help disperse its seeds.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
