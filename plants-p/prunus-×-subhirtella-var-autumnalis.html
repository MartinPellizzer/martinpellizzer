Prunus × subhirtella var. autumnalis

Prunus × subhirtella var. autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn cherry or autumnalis cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is valued for its vibrant fall foliage, which displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits known as cherries, which are typically harvested in late summer. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and turn brilliant colors in autumn, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
