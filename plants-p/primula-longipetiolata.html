Primula longipetiolata

Primula longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright stem and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a velvety texture, often with a waxy coating. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
