Penstemon pseudospectabilis subsp. bicolor

Penstemon pseudospectabilis subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the twin-colored beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple, often with a contrasting throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
