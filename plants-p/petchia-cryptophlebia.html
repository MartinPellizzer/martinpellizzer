Petchia cryptophlebia

Petchia cryptophlebia, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically deep purple to black in color, encased in a segmented, woody rind. Its botanical features include a shrub-like growth habit and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the consumption of its fruit.
