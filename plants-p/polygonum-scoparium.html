Polygonum scoparium

Polygonum scoparium, commonly known as silverleaf knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and stout, upright stems that often branch near the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance.

Polygonum scoparium is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
