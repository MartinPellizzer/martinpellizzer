Polystachya supfiana

Polystachya supfiana, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple veins on its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, branching inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
