Petaloma cauliflorum

Petaloma cauliflorum, commonly known as the cauliflower petaloma, is a unique flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant exhibits cauliflorous growth, meaning its flowers and inflorescences develop directly from the main stem rather than from the terminal buds. Its botanical structure includes a short, sturdy stem with clustered, showy bracts that attract pollinators.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unusual appearance.
