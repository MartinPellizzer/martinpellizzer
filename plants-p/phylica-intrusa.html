Phylica intrusa

Phylica intrusa, commonly known as the invasive phylica, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments. It is considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and challenging to manage.
