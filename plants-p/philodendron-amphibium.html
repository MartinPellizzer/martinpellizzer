Philodendron amphibium

Philodendron amphibium, commonly known as the water philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in both aquatic and terrestrial environments, making it a unique adaptation. Its stems can grow submerged in water or climb above the surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This versatility allows it to be a popular choice for both indoor and aquatic gardening.
