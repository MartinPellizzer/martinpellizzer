Piper berryi

Piper berryi, commonly known as the berry pepper or purple pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that range in color from green to purple, depending on maturity.

Piper berryi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited use in traditional medicine.
