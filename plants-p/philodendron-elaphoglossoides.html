Philodendron elaphoglossoides

Philodendron elaphoglossoides, commonly known as the "Coral Bells Philodendron," is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and a unique, fern-like pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers, followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
