Polygala ambigua

Polygala ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a fibrous root system.

Polygala ambigua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
