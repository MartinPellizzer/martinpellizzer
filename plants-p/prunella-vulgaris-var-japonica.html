Prunella vulgaris var. japonica

Prunella vulgaris var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese selfheal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate with serrated edges. The plant produces dense clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
