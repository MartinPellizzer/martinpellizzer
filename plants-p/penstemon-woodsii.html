Penstemon woodsii

Penstemon woodsii, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Penstemon woodsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
