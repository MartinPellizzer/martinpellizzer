Phalacroloma annuum subsp. septentrionale

Phalacroloma annuum subsp.

septentrionale, commonly known as the northern annual phalacroloma, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant features erect stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

This subspecies is characterized by its short life cycle and is adapted to seasonal fluctuations in temperature and moisture.
