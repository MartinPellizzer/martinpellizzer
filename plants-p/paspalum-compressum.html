Paspalum compressum

Paspalum compressum, commonly known as narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its adaptability makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
