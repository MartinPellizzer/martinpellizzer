Psoralea tauschiana

Psoralea tauschiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf psoralea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in slender pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Psoralea tauschiana is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
