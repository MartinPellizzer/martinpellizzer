Poa indica

Poa indica, commonly known as the Indian grass, is a species of grass native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Poa indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
