Pollardia campylostalix

Pollardia campylostalix, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
