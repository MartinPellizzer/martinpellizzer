Panicum patens var. latifolium

Panicum patens var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf panicgrass, is a variant of the panicum genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves that can reach up to 10 inches in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This grass is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
