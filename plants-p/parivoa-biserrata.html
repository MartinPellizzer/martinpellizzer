Parivoa biserrata

Parivoa biserrata, commonly known as the serrated parivoa, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural wild state.
