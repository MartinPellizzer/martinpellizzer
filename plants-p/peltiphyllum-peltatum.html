Peltiphyllum peltatum

Peltiphyllum peltatum, commonly known as the water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are divided into small, overlapping lobes resembling the shape of a clover. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its roots are anchored in the substrate, while its leaves remain buoyant on the water's surface.

Peltiphyllum peltatum plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
