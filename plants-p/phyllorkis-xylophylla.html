Phyllorkis xylophylla

Phyllorkis xylophylla, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Phyllorkis xylophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
