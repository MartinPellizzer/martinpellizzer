Praxeliopsis

Praxeliopsis, commonly known as the golden star daisy, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its bright yellow flowers and dark green, velvety leaves. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. Praxeliopsis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native landscapes.
