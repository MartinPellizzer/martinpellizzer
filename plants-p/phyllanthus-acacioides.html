Phyllanthus acacioides

Phyllanthus acacioides, commonly known as the false acacia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry forests.

Phyllanthus acacioides is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
