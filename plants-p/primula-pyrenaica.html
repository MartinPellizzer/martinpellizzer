Primula pyrenaica

Primula pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It is a perennial herb with basal leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Primula pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
