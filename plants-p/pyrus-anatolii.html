Pyrus anatolii

Pyrus anatolii, commonly known as Anatolian pear, is a deciduous tree native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
