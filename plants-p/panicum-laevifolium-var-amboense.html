Panicum laevifolium var. amboense

Panicum laevifolium var. amboense, commonly known as Ambo panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
