Pilea forsythiana

Pilea forsythiana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea forsythiana is valued for its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
