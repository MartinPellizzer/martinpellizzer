Plocama brevifolia

Plocama brevifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf plocama, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland environments, often near streams or in shaded areas. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
