Parathesis implexa

Parathesis implexa, commonly known as the twisted mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees.

It lacks true leaves and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Parathesis implexa is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions where it thrives in shaded, humid environments.
