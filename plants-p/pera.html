Pera

Pera, commonly known as the common perilla, is an annual herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its erect stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, clustered flowers that range from purple to pink. Pera is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor, and for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in temperate climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
