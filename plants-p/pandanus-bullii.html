Pandanus bullii

Pandanus bullii, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, sandy soils.

Pandanus bullii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in many island communities.
