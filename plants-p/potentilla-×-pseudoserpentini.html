Potentilla × pseudoserpentini

Potentilla × pseudoserpentini, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a cross between Potentilla fruticosa and Potentilla nemoralis.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
