Phlomoides pulvinaris

Phlomoides pulvinaris, commonly known as the woolly phlomoides, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
