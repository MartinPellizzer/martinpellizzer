Porphyrocoma lanceolata

Porphyrocoma lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate porphyrocoma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a compact, herbaceous growth habit.

It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
