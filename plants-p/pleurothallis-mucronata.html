Pleurothallis mucronata

Pleurothallis mucronata, commonly known as the Acorn Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, hairy pseudobulbs and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale pink, and are clustered in a loose inflorescence.

Pleurothallis mucronata is prized for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant in terrariums and greenhouses.
