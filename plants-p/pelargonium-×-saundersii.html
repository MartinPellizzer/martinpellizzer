Pelargonium × saundersii

Pelargonium × saundersii, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various pelargonium species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its cascading form.

Pelargonium × saundersii prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
