Phaseolus velutinus

Phaseolus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a legume native to tropical America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to pods containing dark brown, kidney-shaped seeds. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

The plant has both agricultural and medicinal uses due to its nutritional and biochemical properties.
