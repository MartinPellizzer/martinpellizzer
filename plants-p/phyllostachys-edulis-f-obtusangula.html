Phyllostachys edulis f. obtusangula

Phyllostachys edulis f. obtusangula, commonly known as the broad-leaved bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species characterized by its broader, more rounded leaf blades compared to the typical form.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. This bamboo is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological purposes. Its culms are strong and hollow, often used in construction and crafts.

The plant's foliage provides a lush, green appearance, contributing to its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
