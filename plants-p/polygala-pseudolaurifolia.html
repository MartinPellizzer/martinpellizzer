Polygala pseudolaurifolia

Polygala pseudolaurifolia, commonly known as the False Laurel Polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring broad, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
