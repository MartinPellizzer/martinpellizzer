Pogostoma

Pogostoma, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils. Pogostoma is also known for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
