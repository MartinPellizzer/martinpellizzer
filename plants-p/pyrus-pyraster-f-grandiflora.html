Pyrus pyraster f. grandiflora

Pyrus pyraster f. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered crabapple, is a cultivar of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It is a deciduous tree or shrub that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.
