Prunus × silvestris var. pseudocerasifera

Prunus × silvestris var. pseudocerasifera, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the leaves. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The plant produces small, hard, reddish-brown fruits called drupes, which contain a single seed.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in Japanese traditions.
