Parathesis kochii

Parathesis kochii, commonly known as the dwarf mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of coniferous trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients through its haustoria, which invade the host's vascular system. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are toxic to many animals. It is native to regions of North America and is often found in forested areas with suitable host trees.

Its life cycle involves a complex relationship with its host, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
