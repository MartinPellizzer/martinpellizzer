Polychroa rostrata

Polychroa rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure that resembles a beak, hence its common name. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich substrates.

Polychroa rostrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
