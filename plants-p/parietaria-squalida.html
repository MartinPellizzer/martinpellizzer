Parietaria squalida

Parietaria squalida, commonly known as the stinging nettle weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. The plant is known for its fine hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in urban environments.

Parietaria squalida is an annual herb that spreads through wind-dispersed seeds.
