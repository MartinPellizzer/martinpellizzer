Polygala aparinoides

Polygala aparinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
