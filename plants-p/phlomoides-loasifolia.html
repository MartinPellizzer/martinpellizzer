Phlomoides loasifolia

Phlomoides loasifolia, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and it produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Phlomoides loasifolia is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
