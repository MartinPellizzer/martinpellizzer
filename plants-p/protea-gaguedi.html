Protea gaguedi

Protea gaguedi, commonly known as the Cape protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, though they are actually a single flower with many petals. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in nutrient-poor, acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
