Passiflora diaden

Passiflora diaden, commonly known as the two-stamen passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture.

Passiflora diaden is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
