Pultenaea forsythiana var. uniflora

Pultenaea forsythiana var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered pultenaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and coastal dunes. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is known for its hardy and drought-tolerant nature.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
