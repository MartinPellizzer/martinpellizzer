Piper malgassicum

Piper malgassicum, commonly known as the Madagascar pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the spicy compound piperine. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
