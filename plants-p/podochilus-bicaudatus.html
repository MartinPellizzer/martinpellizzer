Podochilus bicaudatus

Podochilus bicaudatus, commonly known as the two-tailed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, branching structure with elongated pseudobulbs that support its foliage. The plant produces striking, orchid-like flowers in shades of white and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its roots are adapted for aerial attachment to trees, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Podochilus bicaudatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
