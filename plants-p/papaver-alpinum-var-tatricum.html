Papaver alpinum var. tatricum

Papaver alpinum var. tatricum, commonly known as the Tatric Mountain poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features striking, bright red petals that resemble those of the common poppy, with a distinctive dark center. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
