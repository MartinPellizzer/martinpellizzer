Phaseolus cochleatus

Phaseolus cochleatus, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved pods that resemble a sickle, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and the seeds are small, angular, and often have a smooth or slightly wrinkled surface.

This plant is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and for their nutritional content.
