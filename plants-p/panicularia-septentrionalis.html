Panicularia septentrionalis

Panicularia septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern panicled gentian, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with hairy surfaces.

Panicularia septentrionalis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
