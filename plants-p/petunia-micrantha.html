Petunia micrantha

Petunia micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf petunia, is a species native to South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often used as a bedding plant in gardens.
