Protea erecta

Protea erecta, commonly known as the upright protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Protea erecta is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation, thriving in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
