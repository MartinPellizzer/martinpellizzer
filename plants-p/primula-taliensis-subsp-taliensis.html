Primula taliensis subsp. taliensis

Primula taliensis subsp.

taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are typically arranged in a cyme and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
