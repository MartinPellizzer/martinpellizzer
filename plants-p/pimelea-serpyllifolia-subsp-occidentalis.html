Pimelea serpyllifolia subsp. occidentalis

Pimelea serpyllifolia subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western wallaby bush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
