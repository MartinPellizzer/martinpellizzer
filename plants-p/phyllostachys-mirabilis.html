Phyllostachys mirabilis

Phyllostachys mirabilis, commonly known as the rainbow bamboo, is a species of grass in the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking, multicolored culms that display shades of green, yellow, red, and orange. The plant grows as a clump-forming perennial, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in ideal conditions. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, while its shoots are often used in ornamental horticulture.

Phyllostachys mirabilis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant appearance.
