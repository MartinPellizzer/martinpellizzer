Penstemon centranthifolius

Penstemon centranthifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
