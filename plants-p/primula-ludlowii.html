Primula ludlowii

Primula ludlowii, commonly known as the Ludlow's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa, particularly in Kenya and Tanzania.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet to blue flowers. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a broad base that clasps the stem.

Primula ludlowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
