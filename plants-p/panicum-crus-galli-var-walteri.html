Panicum crus-galli var. walteri

Panicum crus-galli var. walteri, commonly known as Walter's barnyard grass, is a tall annual grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a competitive weed in various ecosystems.
