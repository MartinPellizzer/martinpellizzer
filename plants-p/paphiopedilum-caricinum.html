Paphiopedilum caricinum

Paphiopedilum caricinum, commonly known as the golden-lip paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It features a unique, saddle-shaped lip that is often yellow or orange in color, contributing to its striking appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually borne on a single stem and can last for several weeks.

Paphiopedilum caricinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
