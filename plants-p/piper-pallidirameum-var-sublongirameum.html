Piper pallidirameum var. sublongirameum

Piper pallidirameum var. sublongirameum, commonly known as the long-stemmed pale-ramed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters, and the fruit is a one-seeded, fleshy drupe.

This variant is notable for its elongated rame (stipular appendage) and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or traditional use.
