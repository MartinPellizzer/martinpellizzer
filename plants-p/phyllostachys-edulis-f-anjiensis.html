Phyllostachys edulis f. anjiensis

Phyllostachys edulis f. anjiensis, commonly known as the Anji bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species, characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms.

It originates from the Anji region in Zhejiang Province, China, and is prized for its ornamental value and sustainable use in construction and crafts. The plant exhibits a fast growth rate and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This bamboo is also valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and carbon sequestration.
