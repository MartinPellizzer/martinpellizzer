Philodendron lehmannii

Philodendron lehmannii, commonly known as the leopard philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that often feature a distinctive pattern of light green and dark green markings. The plant has a vining growth habit and can reach significant lengths if provided with adequate support. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture and can grow up to 12 inches in diameter.

Philodendron lehmannii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.
