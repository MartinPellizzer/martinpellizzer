Paraphlomis brevifolia

Paraphlomis brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved paraphlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, commonly known as the mint family, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
