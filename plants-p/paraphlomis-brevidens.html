Paraphlomis brevidens

Paraphlomis brevidens, commonly known as the short-stemmed parsnip, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, typically purple or pink in color.

Paraphlomis brevidens is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
