Pimelea microcephala subsp. glabra

Pimelea microcephala subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in heathlands and open woodlands. It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.

The subspecies glabra is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves, which set it apart from other subspecies of Pimelea microcephala.
