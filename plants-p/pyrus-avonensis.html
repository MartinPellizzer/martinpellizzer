Pyrus avonensis

Pyrus avonensis, commonly known as the Avon pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to the UK.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval in shape with a smooth margin. It produces white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in groups of three.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
