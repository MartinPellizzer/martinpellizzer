Pleurothallis avirostris

Pleurothallis avirostris, commonly known as the pink lady slipper orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a pink or reddish hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

The orchid's unique floral structure and coloration make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
