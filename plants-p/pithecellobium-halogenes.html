Pithecellobium halogenes

Pithecellobium halogenes, commonly known as the yellow pithecellobium, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soils.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
