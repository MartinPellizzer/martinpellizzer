Passerina coridifolia

Passerina coridifolia, commonly known as the coralberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn vibrant shades of red in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and provides food for birds and other wildlife.
