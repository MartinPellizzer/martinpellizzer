Paphiopedilum callosum var. schmidtianum

Paphiopedilum callosum var. schmidtianum, commonly known as the Schmidt's velvet terry orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and produces a single, hairy stem that bears several lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its unique, spurred orchid flower with a velvety texture and a distinctive, elongated lip. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest undergrowth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance.
