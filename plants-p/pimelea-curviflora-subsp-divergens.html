Pimelea curviflora subsp. divergens

Pimelea curviflora subsp.

divergens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright red or orange flowers in dense, showy clusters.

The plant is typically found in dry sclerophyll forests and heathlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more elongated floral bracts compared to other Pimelea species.
