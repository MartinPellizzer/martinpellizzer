Polygonum glareosum

Polygonum glareosum, commonly known as the shining knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in a waxy, reflective coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum glareosum is often found in moist environments such as riverbanks and wet meadows.
