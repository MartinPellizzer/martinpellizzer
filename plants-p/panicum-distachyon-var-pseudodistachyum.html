Panicum distachyon var. pseudodistachyum

Panicum distachyon var. pseudodistachyum, commonly known as the switchgrass variant, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are arranged in a loose, open cluster. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable resource for biofuel production and ecological restoration projects.
