Phleum graecum subsp. aegaeum

Phleum graecum subsp.

aegaeum, commonly known as Greek oat, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it blooms with pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Phleum graecum subsp.

aegaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
