Pseuderanthemum potamophilum

Pseuderanthemum potamophilum, commonly known as the riverbank pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along riverbanks and in humid woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture, while its stems are square and somewhat hairy.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
