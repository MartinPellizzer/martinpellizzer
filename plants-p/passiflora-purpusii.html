Passiflora purpusii

Passiflora purpusii, commonly known as the Crimson Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
