Polyalthia Rumphii

Rumphii's flame tree, scientifically known as Polyalthia rumphii, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. Its most notable feature is its vibrant orange and red flowers that resemble flames, giving it its dramatic common name. This tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas. With its showy blooms and unique appearance, it has become a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively rare in cultivation, making it a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts.
