Puccinellia dolicholepis subsp. fominii

Puccinellia dolicholepis subsp.

fominii, commonly known as the Fomin's spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that aids in water conservation.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh conditions, making it an important component of steppe and desert ecosystems.
