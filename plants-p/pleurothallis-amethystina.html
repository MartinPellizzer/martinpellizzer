Pleurothallis amethystina

Pleurothallis amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a striking violet or purple hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
