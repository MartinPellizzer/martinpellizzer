Plectranthus cavaleriei

Plectranthus cavaleriei, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Plectranthus cavaleriei is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in traditional medicine.
