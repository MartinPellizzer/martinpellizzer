Piper ubiquescabridum

Piper ubiquescabridum, commonly known as the ubiquitous broadleaf pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, known for its aromatic and often spicy fruits.

This plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a distinctive scent when crushed, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and cuisine. The small, berry-like fruits are often used in local remedies and as a spice.

Despite its name, it is not widely cultivated commercially, though it holds cultural significance in certain regions.
