Pelargonium × ludovicianum

Pelargonium × ludovicianum, commonly known as the Martha's Vineyard geranium, is a hybrid plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
