Psidium albidum

Psidium albidum, commonly known as the white guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, white to yellowish fruits. The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
