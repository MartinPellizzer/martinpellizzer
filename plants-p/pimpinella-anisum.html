Pimpinella anisum

Pimpinella anisum, commonly known as anise, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spherical, dry fruits called schizocarps. Anise is cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and a distinctive, branched stem.
