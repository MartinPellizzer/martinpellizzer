Piper squalipelliculum

Piper squalipelliculum, commonly known as the squalipelliculum pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Piper.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This species is valued for its unique flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
