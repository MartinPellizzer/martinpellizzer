Primula sulphurea

Primula sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in a single umbel at the top of its slender stem. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its striking color and unique floral structure make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
