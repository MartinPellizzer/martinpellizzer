Potamogeton lucens subvar. insignis

Potamogeton lucens subvar. insignis, commonly known as the bright pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it is known for its ability to form dense underwater vegetation. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
