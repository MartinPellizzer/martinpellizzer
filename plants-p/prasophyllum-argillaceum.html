Prasophyllum argillaceum

Prasophyllum argillaceum, commonly known as the clay leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in clay-rich soils and is found in regions with cool, moist conditions. The plant has a short, stocky stem and produces a single or few spikes of pale green to white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette near the base.

Prasophyllum argillaceum is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
