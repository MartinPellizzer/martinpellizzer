Piper vicosanum

Piper vicosanum, commonly known as the Vicos pepper or Peruvian pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in cloud forests at higher elevations.
