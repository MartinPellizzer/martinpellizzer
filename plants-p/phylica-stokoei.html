Phylica stokoei

Phylica stokoei, commonly known as Stokoe's bottlebrush, is a striking South African plant characterized by its unique inflorescence.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is native to the Eastern Cape region. The plant features long, slender, red or orange flower spikes that resemble bottlebrushes, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Phylica stokoei is often cultivated for its visual interest and is adapted to well-drained, sandy soils.
