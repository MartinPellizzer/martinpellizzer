Piper calvibaccum

Piper calvibaccum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into round, green berries. The berries are typically harvested when mature and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
