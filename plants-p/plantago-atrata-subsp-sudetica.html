Plantago atrata subsp. sudetica

Plantago atrata subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic plantain, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

The plant features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense spikes, which bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
