Phlomoides aralensis

Phlomoides aralensis, commonly known as the Aral Sea phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or saline soils.

Due to its adaptation to harsh conditions, it plays a role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
