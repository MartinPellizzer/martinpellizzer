Piptochaetium uruguense

Piptochaetium uruguense, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, bristle-like florets. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
