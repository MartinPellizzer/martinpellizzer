Papaver alpinum lusus chinense

Papaver alpinum lusus chinense, commonly known as the Chinese Himalayan poppy, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a contrasting black center. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
