Pandanus jaffrei

Pandanus jaffrei, commonly known as the Jaffrei pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the leaves. Its roots and leaves are sometimes used in local medicine and for making thatched roofing.

The Jaffrei pandanus is an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts in its native region.
