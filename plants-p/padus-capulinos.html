Padus capulinos

Padus capulinos, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
