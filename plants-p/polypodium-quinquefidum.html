Polypodium quinquefidum

Polypodium quinquefidum, commonly known as the five-veined maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-lobed fronds that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant features a complex network of veins, with five prominent veins running along the length of each leaf. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in humid climates.

Polypodium quinquefidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
