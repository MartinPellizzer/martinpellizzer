Papaver pyrenaicum subsp. suaveolens

Papaver pyrenaicum subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features striking, showy flowers with bright red petals and a prominent central stigma, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native region.
