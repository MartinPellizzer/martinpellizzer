Pilosella glaucescens

Pilosella glaucescens, commonly known as the bluehead groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers.

Pilosella glaucescens is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
