Parthenium radfordii

Parthenium radfordii, commonly known as the radford's horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Parthenium radfordii is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in regions with low rainfall.
