Plumeria jamesonii

Plumeria jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's frangipani, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has simple, narrow leaves and produces woody, branching stems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles and are known for their ornamental value.
