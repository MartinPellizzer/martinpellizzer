Pyrola asarifolia var. incarnata

Pyrola asarifolia var. incarnata, commonly known as the pink wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant in woodland gardens.
