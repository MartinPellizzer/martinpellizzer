Pavieasia

Pavieasia, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature. Pavieasia thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm regions.
