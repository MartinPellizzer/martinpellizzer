Phyllostachys edulis f. purpureosulcata

Phyllostachys edulis f. purpureosulcata, commonly known as the purple-ribbed bamboo, is a variant of the giant bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinged ribs on the culms, which add ornamental value to gardens and landscapes. The plant is a clump-forming bamboo that grows to a height of several meters, with slender, hollow stems. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
