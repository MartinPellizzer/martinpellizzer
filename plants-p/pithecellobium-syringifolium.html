Pithecellobium syringifolium

Pithecellobium syringifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod containing edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
