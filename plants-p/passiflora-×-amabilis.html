Passiflora × amabilis

Passiflora × amabilis, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a hybrid species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a blend of blue, purple, and white hues, often with intricate patterns resembling a crown. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are prized for their sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora × amabilis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its culinary and medicinal uses.
