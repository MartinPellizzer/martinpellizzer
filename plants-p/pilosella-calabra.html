Pilosella calabra

Pilosella calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian hairweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features small, yellowish to white florets surrounded by pale green to brown bracts. Pilosella calabra typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunlit habitats.

Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, contributing to its common name.
