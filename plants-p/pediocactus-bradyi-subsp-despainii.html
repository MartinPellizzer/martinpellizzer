Pediocactus bradyi subsp. despainii

Pediocactus bradyi subsp.

despainii, commonly known as the Desert Cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the deserts of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a sensitive species requiring careful conservation efforts.
