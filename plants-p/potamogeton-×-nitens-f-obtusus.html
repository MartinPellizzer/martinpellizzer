Potamogeton × nitens f. obtusus

Potamogeton × nitens f. obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its broad, elongated leaves that are typically smooth and rounded at the tips. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, often submerged or floating at the water's surface. It is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters such as ponds and lakes.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is sometimes used as an indicator species in aquatic ecosystems.
