Pedicularis resupinata subsp. lasiophylla

Pedicularis resupinata subsp.

lasiophylla, commonly known as the purple rock orchid, is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure. It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on host plants for water and nutrients.

The subspecies is distinguished by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, spurred flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This plant is often found in mountainous regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
