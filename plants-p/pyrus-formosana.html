Pyrus formosana

Pyrus formosana, commonly known as the Formosan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive foliage and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Pyrus formosana is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of climates, contributing to its popularity among horticulturists.
