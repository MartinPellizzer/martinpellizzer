Polygonum sungareense f. rubiflorum

Polygonum sungareense f. rubiflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum sungareense, which belongs to the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens and natural habitats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with a fine layer of silky hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
