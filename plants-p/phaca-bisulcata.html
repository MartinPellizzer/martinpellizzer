Phaca bisulcata

Phaca bisulcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed phaca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
