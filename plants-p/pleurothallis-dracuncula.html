Pleurothallis dracuncula

Pleurothallis dracuncula, commonly known as the dragon's tail orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
