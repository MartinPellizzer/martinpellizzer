Pittosporum billardierei

Pittosporum billardierei, commonly known as the New Zealand whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.

Pittosporum billardierei is also important in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in various soil types.
