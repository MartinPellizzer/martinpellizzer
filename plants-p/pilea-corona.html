Pilea corona

Pilea corona, commonly known as the "Aluminum Plant," is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing, evergreen perennial that features glossy, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea corona is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
