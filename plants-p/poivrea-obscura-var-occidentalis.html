Poivrea obscura var. occidentalis

Poivrea obscura var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western hazelnut, is a shrub native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable plant in native landscaping and wildlife habitats.
