Poterium dictyocarpum var. glaucum

Poterium dictyocarpum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that often have a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, branching inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland vegetation.
