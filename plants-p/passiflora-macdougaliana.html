Passiflora macdougaliana

Passiflora macdougaliana, commonly known as the MacDougall's passionflower, is a species native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures. The flowers feature a central crown of filaments and a unique arrangement of sepals and petals. Its fruit is a bright orange, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
