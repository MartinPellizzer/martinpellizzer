Persica chinensis var. radiata

Persica chinensis var. radiata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous fruit tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit.
