Passiflora muralis

Passiflora muralis, commonly known as the wallpassion vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its vines can climb using tendrils.

Passiflora muralis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
