Pyrethrum cappadocicum

Pyrethrum cappadocicum, commonly known as Cappadocian pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which contain pyrethrin, a natural insecticide. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in pest control.
