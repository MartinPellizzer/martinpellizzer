Passiflora caerulea var. colvillei

Passiflora caerulea var. colvillei, commonly known as the Blue Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and silky filaments. The plant produces edible, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
