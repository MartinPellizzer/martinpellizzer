Philadelphus mearnsii subsp. typicus

Philadelphus mearnsii subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Mearns' mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
