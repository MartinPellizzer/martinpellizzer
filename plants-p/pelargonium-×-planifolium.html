Pelargonium × planifolium

Pelargonium × planifolium, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid species in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves with distinct color zones, often in shades of green, silver, and purple. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
