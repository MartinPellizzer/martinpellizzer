Plantago truncata subsp. eschscholtziana

Plantago truncata subsp.

eschscholtziana, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface and a distinctive truncate (cut-off) tip.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
