Potamogeton lucens var. fluviatilis

Potamogeton lucens var. fluviatilis, commonly known as the river pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or yellowish in color, and it is often found in association with other aquatic vegetation. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
