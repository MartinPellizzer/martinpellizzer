Piper parkerianum

Piper parkerianum, commonly known as the Parker's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are greenish-white and appear in clusters, while the fruit is a small, three-lobed berry.

Piper parkerianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
