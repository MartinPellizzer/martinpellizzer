Paraeremostachys badakhschanica

Paraeremostachys badakhschanica, commonly known as the Badakhshan feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, feather-like leaves and panicle-shaped inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in sandy or rocky soils. It is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes.

Its ecological importance lies in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
