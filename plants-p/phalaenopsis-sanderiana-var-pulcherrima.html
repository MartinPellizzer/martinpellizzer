Phalaenopsis sanderiana var. pulcherrima

Phalaenopsis sanderiana var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the Sander's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
