Physalis viscosa f. elliottii

Physalis viscosa f. elliottii, commonly known as the Elliott's hairy physalis, is a variant of the common groundcherry.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, and its fruit is a small, inflated capsule that turns red when mature. The flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are followed by the characteristic lantern-like fruit.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
