Polygala durandii var. crassifolia

Polygala durandii var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is known for its diverse range of species with unique floral structures.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.
