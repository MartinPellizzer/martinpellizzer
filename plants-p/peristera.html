Peristera

Peristera, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a delicate perennial plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, nodding flower that resembles a drooping bell. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and is known for its ability to thrive in woodland or shaded areas. Peristera prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.

Its unique appearance and early blooming habit make it a symbol of hope and resilience in the botanical world.
