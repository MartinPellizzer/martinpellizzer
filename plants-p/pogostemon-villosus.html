Pogostemon villosus

Pogostemon villosus, commonly known as the hairy pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or violet, arranged in whorled clusters.

Pogostemon villosus is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
