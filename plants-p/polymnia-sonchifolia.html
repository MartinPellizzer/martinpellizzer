Polymnia sonchifolia

Polymnia sonchifolia, commonly known as the common polymnia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching. Its yellow flowers are arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family, and bloom in the summer months.

Polymnia sonchifolia is often found in moist meadows and along streambanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
