Paeonia wittmanniana subsp. macrophylla

Paeonia wittmanniana subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the giant peony, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 20 cm in diameter, typically in shades of pink, red, or white.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the genus Paeonia.
