Pseudosasa flexuosa

Pseudosasa flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Pseudosasa flexuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
