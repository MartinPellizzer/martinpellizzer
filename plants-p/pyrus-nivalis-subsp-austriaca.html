Pyrus nivalis subsp. austriaca

Pyrus nivalis subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian whitebeam, is a subspecies native to Central Europe. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in mountainous regions, reaching heights of up to 15 meters.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by edible berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh alpine conditions.
