Phyllanthus gour-maitii

Phyllanthus gour-maitii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into bright orange, fleshy berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
