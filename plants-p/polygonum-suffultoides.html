Polygonum suffultoides

Polygonum suffultoides, commonly known as the creeping smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has a prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
