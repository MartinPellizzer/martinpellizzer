Pittosporum ferrugineum subsp. ferrugineum

Pittosporum ferrugineum subsp.

ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or rusty underside.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinctive leaf coloration and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
