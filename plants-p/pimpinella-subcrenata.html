Pimpinella subcrenata

Pimpinella subcrenata, commonly known as the curly cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces slender, curved pods containing small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pimpinella subcrenata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
