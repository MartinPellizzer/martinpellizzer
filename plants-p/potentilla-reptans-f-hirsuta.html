Potentilla reptans f. hirsuta

Potentilla reptans f. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and small, rounded leaves.

It produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which are typical of the Rosaceae family. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. Its hairy foliage and creeping growth habit make it a distinctive and hardy species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
