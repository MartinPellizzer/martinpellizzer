Piper jaboncillanum

Piper jaboncillanum, commonly known as the Jamaican pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. Its fruits are small, reddish berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
