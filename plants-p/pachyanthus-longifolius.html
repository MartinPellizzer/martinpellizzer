Pachyanthus longifolius

Pachyanthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pachyanthus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often leathery in texture and have a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Pachyanthus longifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic features.
