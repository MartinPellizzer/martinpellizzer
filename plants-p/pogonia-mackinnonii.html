Pogonia mackinnonii

Pogonia mackinnonii, commonly known as the Mackinnon's bog orchid, is a rare and delicate terrestrial orchid native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and a single, showy pink or purple flower with a distinctive, intricate lip. The plant thrives in acidic, moist soils and requires a cool, shaded environment to flourish. Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding a unique floral display to its habitat.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable and is often protected in conservation efforts.
