Podocarpus degeneri

Podocarpus degeneri, commonly known as the Degener's podocarp, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinct aromatic scent, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
