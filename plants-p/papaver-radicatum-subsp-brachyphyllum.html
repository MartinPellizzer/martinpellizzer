Papaver radicatum subsp. brachyphyllum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

brachyphyllum, commonly known as the short-stemmed poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, with petals that are typically recurved.

The plant has a compact growth habit, with short, stout stems and basal leaves that are often lobed or divided. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
