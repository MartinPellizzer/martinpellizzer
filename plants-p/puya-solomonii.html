Puya solomonii

Puya solomonii, commonly known as the Solomon's torch, is a striking plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous striking red or orange flowers. The plant produces large, fibrous leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, protective structure around its base. It thrives in cool, sunny environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions with low atmospheric pressure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
