Phymatodes lucida

Phymatodes lucida, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a tall, leafless stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, with clusters of white to pale pink flowers at the top. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various animals.
