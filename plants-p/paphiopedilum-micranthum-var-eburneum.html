Paphiopedilum micranthum var. eburneum

Paphiopedilum micranthum var. eburneum, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that is often white or pale pink. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
