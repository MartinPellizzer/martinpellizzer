Piptanthocereus jamacaru var. caesia

Piptanthocereus jamacaru var. caesia, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal flowering habit.
