Pseuderanthemum hooveri

Pseuderanthemum hooveri, commonly known as the Hoover's pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
