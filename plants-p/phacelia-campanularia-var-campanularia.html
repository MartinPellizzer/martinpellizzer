Phacelia campanularia var. campanularia

Phacelia campanularia var. campanularia, commonly known as blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
