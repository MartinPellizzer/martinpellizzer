Paritium

Paritium, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It has small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, evergreen foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
