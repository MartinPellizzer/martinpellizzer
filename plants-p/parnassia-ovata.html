Parnassia ovata

Parnassia ovata, commonly known as the white crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Parnassia ovata is known for its striking appearance and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
