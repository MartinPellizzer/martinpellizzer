Phalaenopsis curnowiana

Phalaenopsis curnowiana, commonly known as the Curnow's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple stems. The plant produces showy, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a fragrant scent. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, typically arranged in two rows along the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
