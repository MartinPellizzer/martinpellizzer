Pteris acuminata

Pteris acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-lobed water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive sharp-pointed lobes. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near water bodies such as streams and marshes. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the propagation of the plant through vegetative means.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
