Phyllanthus laxiflorus

Phyllanthus laxiflorus, commonly known as the African star apple or Natal star apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruits that are typically spherical and have a star-like appearance.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet, juicy pulp that is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.
