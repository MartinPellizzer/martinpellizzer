Pavonia sepioides

Pavonia sepioides, commonly known as the purple false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside, and it produces small, clustered pink to purple flowers. Its flowers are borne on elongated inflorescences and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Pavonia sepioides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
