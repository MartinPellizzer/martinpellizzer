Polygala stellera

Polygala stellera, commonly known as the star-flower or star-pod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant produces distinctive, bead-like seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Polygala stellera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
