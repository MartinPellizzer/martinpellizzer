Pentaraphia floribunda

Pentaraphia floribunda, commonly known as the flowering pentaraphia, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
