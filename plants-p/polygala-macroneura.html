Polygala macroneura

Polygala macroneura, commonly known as the broad-veined milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish in color, with distinctive wing-like petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
