Polycardia

Polycardia, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain birds.
