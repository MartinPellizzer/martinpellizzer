Ptisana fraxinea

Ptisana fraxinea, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Ptisana fraxinea is often found in moist, open woodlands and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
