Pitcairnia squarrosa var. squarrosa

Pitcairnia squarrosa var. squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white or grayish foliage that covers the entire plant, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its rosette-shaped leaves form a central cup that collects rainwater, providing moisture for its roots.

The flowers, which are usually pink or red, emerge from the center of the rosette and are clustered in a spike.
