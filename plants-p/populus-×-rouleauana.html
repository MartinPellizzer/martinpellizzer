Populus × rouleauana

Populus × rouleauana, commonly known as the hybrid poplar, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Populus balsamifera and Populus deltoides. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellowish-green bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Populus × rouleauana is valued for its fast growth and ability to stabilize riverbanks.
