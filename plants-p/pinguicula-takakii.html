Pinguicula takakii

Pinguicula takakii, commonly known as the Japanese butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and usually pink or purple, blooming in the spring.

Pinguicula takakii is a low-growing plant that relies on insect prey for essential nutrients due to the nutrient-poor soils in which it thrives.
