Pilea trianthemoides

Pilea trianthemoides, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Pilea trianthemoides prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
