Primula purpurea

Primula purpurea, commonly known as the purple primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of its hairy, upright stems. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Primula purpurea is a member of the Primulaceae family, known for its diverse range of species with colorful flowers.
