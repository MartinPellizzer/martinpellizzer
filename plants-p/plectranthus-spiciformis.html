Plectranthus spiciformis

Plectranthus spiciformis, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant produces tubular flowers that bloom in spikes, giving it its scientific name "spiciformis," meaning "spike-like.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
