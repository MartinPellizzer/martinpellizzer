Panicum bifurcatum

Panicum bifurcatum, commonly known as the forked panic grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum bifurcatum is typically found in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and fields.
