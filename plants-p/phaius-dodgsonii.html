Phaius dodgsonii

Phaius dodgsonii, commonly known as the Dodgson's orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, showy flowers that are typically yellow with a deep red lip, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has a pseudobulbous structure with long, strap-like leaves that are often used in horticultural displays. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is valued for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
