Prenanthes faberi

Prenanthes faberi, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include a smooth, hairy stem and a short, thick rhizome.

Prenanthes faberi is part of the Araliaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
