Piper marequitense

Piper marequitense, commonly known as the Marequito pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, green fruits that turn red when mature, and it is often cultivated for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments, including inflammation and digestive issues.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments with rich, well-drained soil.
