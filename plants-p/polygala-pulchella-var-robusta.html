Polygala pulchella var. robusta

Polygala pulchella var. robusta, commonly known as the robust polygala, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. The plant features slender stems with opposite, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
