Primula knuthiana

Primula knuthiana, commonly known as the Knuth's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, downy texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Primula knuthiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
