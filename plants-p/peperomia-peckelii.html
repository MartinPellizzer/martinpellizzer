Peperomia peckelii

Peperomia peckelii, commonly known as the Peckel's Peperomia, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which are not showy but serve a functional role in reproduction. Peperomia peckelii thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
