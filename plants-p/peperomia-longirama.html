Peperomia longirama

Peperomia longirama, commonly known as the long-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, waxy foliage that ranges from dark green to bluish-green in color. The leaves are elongated, with a pointed tip and a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by small, round berries.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
