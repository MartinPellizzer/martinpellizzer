Piper inaequalifolium

Piper inaequalifolium, commonly known as the unequal-leaved pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of flowering plants.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and alternate leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in herbal remedies for its purported medicinal properties.
