Paspalum scoparium var. angustifolium

Paspalum scoparium var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf sandgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often found in coastal areas and open fields. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This variety is often used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
