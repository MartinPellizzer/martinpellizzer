Plectranthus albus

Plectranthus albus, commonly known as white plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, hairy, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for both garden beds and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
