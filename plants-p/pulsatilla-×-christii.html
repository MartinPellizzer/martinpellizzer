Pulsatilla × christii

Pulsatilla × christii, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers are surrounded by a crown of petal-like bracts.

Pulsatilla × christii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking spring display.
