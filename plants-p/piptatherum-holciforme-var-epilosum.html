Piptatherum holciforme var. epilosum

Piptatherum holciforme var. epilosum, commonly known as silverhair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically forms dense clumps and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with reddish hues. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
