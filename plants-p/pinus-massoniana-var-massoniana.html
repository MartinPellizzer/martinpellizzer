Pinus massoniana var. massoniana

Pinus massoniana var. massoniana, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a tall evergreen conifer native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of two, and its distinctive cone structure. The tree produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into woody, seed-containing structures. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

This species is valued for its timber and is often planted for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
