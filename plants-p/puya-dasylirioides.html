Puya dasylirioides

Puya dasylirioides, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant features broad, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, capable of retaining water in its central cavity. Puya dasylirioides is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in full sun.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
