Pleurothallis acostaei

Pleurothallis acostaei, commonly known as the Acosta's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
