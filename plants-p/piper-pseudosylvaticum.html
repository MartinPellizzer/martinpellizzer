Piper pseudosylvaticum

Piper pseudosylvaticum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in spikes. Its fruits are berries that contain a peppery flavor, similar to black pepper.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
