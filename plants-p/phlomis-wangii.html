Phlomis wangii

Phlomis wangii, commonly known as the Chinese torchflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant purple or red flower clusters that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis wangii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
