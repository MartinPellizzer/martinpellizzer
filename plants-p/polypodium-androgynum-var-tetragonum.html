Polypodium androgynum var. tetragonum

Polypodium androgynum var. tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled polypody, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive fronds.

The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a serrated margin and a dark green, leathery texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variant is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in horticulture.
