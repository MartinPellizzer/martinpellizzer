Polygala supina subsp. rhodopea

Polygala supina subsp.

rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope Polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, clustered flowers with distinctive spiny structures. Its flowers are usually pink or reddish in color, and they bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral appearance.
