Polygonum brittingeri

Polygonum brittingeri, commonly known as Brittinger's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a reddish or purple underside, and it produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
