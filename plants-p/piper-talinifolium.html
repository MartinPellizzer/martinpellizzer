Piper talinifolium

Piper talinifolium, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elliptical, blue-black berries. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

The plant's rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
