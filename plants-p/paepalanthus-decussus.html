Paepalanthus decussus

Paepalanthus decussus, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits called cypsela, which are dispersed by wind.

Paepalanthus decussus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
