Polygonum chinense var. thunbergianum

Polygonum chinense var. thunbergianum, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a problematic weed in certain environments.
