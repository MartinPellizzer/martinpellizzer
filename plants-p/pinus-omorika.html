Pinus omorika

Pinus omorika, commonly known as the Serbian pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in pairs. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

Pinus omorika is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
