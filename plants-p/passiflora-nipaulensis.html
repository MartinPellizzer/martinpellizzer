Passiflora nipaulensis

Passiflora nipaulensis, commonly known as the Nepal passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed raw or used in local cuisines. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora nipaulensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
