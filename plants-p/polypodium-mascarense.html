Polypodium mascarense

Polypodium mascarense, commonly known as the Mascarene polypody, is a species of fern native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a terrestrial fern with a rhizomatous growth habit, featuring a dense, compact frond structure. The fronds are usually dimorphic, with a sterile, elongated form and a fertile, shorter form that bears spores. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in coastal and forested areas.

The plant is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
