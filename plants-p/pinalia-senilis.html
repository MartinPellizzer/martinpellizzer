Pinalia senilis

Pinalia senilis, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically silver or gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small, round oranges. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Pinalia senilis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
