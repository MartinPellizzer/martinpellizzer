Penstemon lyallii

Penstemon lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Penstemon lyallii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
