Pseudocamelina kurdica

Pseudocamelina kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish false camelina, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear, hairy leaves. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
