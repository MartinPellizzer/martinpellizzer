Piptocarpha jauaensis

Piptocarpha jauaensis, commonly known as the Andean burro's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features fleshy, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in composite inflorescences. It is adapted to grow in arid and rocky environments, demonstrating resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
