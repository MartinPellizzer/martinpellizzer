Psidium ovale

Psidium ovale, commonly known as the oval guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, often appearing in clusters.

The botanical features of Psidium ovale make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
