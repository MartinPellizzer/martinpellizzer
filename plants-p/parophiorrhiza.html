Parophiorrhiza

Parophiorrhiza, commonly known as the swamp mallow, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

These plants are typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where they thrive in moist to saturated soils. The genus includes several species, with Parophiorrhiza palustris being the most well-known.
