Persicaria sagittata var. sericea

Persicaria sagittata var. sericea, commonly known as silky smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other damp habitats. Its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

It is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
