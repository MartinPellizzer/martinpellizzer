Paullinia verrucosa

Paullinia verrucosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Paullinia verrucosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
