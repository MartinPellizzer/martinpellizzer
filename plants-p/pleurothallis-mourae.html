Pleurothallis mourae

Pleurothallis mourae, commonly known as the Moura's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle scent that attracts pollinators. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that are adapted for efficient water absorption.

Due to its unique beauty and rarity, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
