Picris kelleriana

Picris kelleriana, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to mountainous landscapes.

Picris kelleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
