Prunus insititia subvar. dumetorum

Prunus insititia subvar. dumetorum, commonly known as the blackcap cherry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits that mature in late summer. The plant has slender, often thorned branches and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

The berries are edible when ripe but contain small stones and are typically consumed in moderation.
