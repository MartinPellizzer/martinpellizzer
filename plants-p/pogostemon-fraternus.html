Pogostemon fraternus

Pogostemon fraternus, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Java, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Pogostemon fraternus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in forest understories.
