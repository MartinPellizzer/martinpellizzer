Pithecellobium latifolium subsp. garcia-barrigae

Pithecellobium latifolium subsp.

garcia-barrigae, commonly known as the broadleaf pithecellobium, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America. It features compound leaves with elongated, elliptical leaflets that are often pubescent.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
