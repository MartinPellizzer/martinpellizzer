Panicum riedelii

Panicum riedelii, commonly known as Riedel's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering period occurs in the late spring to early summer, producing airy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum riedelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
