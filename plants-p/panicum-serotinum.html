Panicum serotinum

Panicum serotinum, commonly known as late bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and blue-green foliage, which turns golden in the fall. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common component of prairie and woodland ecosystems.

Panicum serotinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
