Pseudobombax ellipticum var. ellipticum

Pseudobombax ellipticum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic ceiba, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in disturbed or secondary forests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a notable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
