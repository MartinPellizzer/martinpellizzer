Pilea secunda

Pilea secunda, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is a perennial herb that thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Pilea secunda is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
