Physalis xalapensis

Physalis xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
