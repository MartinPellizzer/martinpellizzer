Papaver anomalum var. hirsutum

Papaver anomalum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a black center. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and unique appearance make it a desirable addition to botanical collections and gardens.
