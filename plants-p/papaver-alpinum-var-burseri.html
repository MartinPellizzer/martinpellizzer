Papaver alpinum var. burseri

Papaver alpinum var. burseri, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a variant of the Himalayan poppy native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and is often found growing in rocky, alpine environments. The plant typically blooms in the summer months and is adapted to cold climates with low humidity. Its flowers are cup-shaped and can reach up to 5 centimeters in diameter.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.
