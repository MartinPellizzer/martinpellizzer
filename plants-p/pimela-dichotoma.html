Pimela dichotoma

Pimela dichotoma, commonly known as the African water lily, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to Africa.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. The plant has broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to prevent water loss. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with a distinctive scent that attracts pollinators.

Pimela dichotoma plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
