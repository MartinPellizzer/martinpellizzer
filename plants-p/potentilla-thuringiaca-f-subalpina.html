Potentilla thuringiaca f. subalpina

Potentilla thuringiaca f. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding vibrant color to alpine meadows. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
