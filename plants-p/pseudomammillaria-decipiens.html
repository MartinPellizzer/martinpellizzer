Pseudomammillaria decipiens

Pseudomammillaria decipiens, commonly known as the false mammillaria, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a spiny appearance, with prominent areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
