Pyrus amphigenea compressa

Pyrus amphigenea compressa, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, ovate leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, slightly acidic conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
