Pinus aristata var. longaeva

Pinus aristata var. longaeva, commonly known as the Great Basin bristlecone pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high elevations of western North America.

It is characterized by its gnarled, twisted growth form and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments. The tree is notable for its longevity, with some individuals living over 5,000 years, making it one of the oldest living organisms on Earth. Its needles are short and bluish-green, and its cones are small and woody.

This variety is adapted to extreme conditions, including high altitude, low temperatures, and poor soil, showcasing remarkable resilience and ecological significance.
