Pleurostachys foliosa var. gaudichaudii

Pleurostachys foliosa var. gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's feather grass, is a perennial grass native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feather-like leaves that give it a soft, ethereal appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of long, narrow spikes that bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
