Pleurothallis angulosa

Pleurothallis angulosa, commonly known as the angular pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It is noted for its intricate floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
