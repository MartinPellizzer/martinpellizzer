Paeonia foeminea subsp. humilis

Paeonia foeminea subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the Siberian peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, double flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant has palmately divided, dark green leaves and a short, upright stem. It is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
