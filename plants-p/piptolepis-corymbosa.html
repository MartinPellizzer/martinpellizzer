Piptolepis corymbosa

Piptolepis corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Piptolepis corymbosa is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators and birds.
