Piper crassinervium

Piper crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The botanical features of Piper crassinervium make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
