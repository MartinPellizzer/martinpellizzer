Parahebe albiflora

Parahebe albiflora, commonly known as the silver parahebe, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery coating. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Parahebe albiflora is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements.
