Paspalum sinuosum

Paspalum sinuosum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in the southeastern United States. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
