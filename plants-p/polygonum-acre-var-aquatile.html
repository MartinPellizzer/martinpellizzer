Polygonum acre var. aquatile

Polygonum acre var. aquatile, commonly known as water smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It typically grows in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in other moist environments. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes and bloom in late summer.

It is often found in regions with high humidity and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
