Piper biunciale

Piper biunciale, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
