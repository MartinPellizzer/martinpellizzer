Phyllanthus cinctus

Phyllanthus cinctus, commonly known as the creeping woodbine, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and waste lands.

The species is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
