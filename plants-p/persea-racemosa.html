Persea racemosa

Persea racemosa, commonly known as the Mexican white sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped with a smooth, yellowish skin and a creamy, sweet flesh. Its leaves and bark contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
