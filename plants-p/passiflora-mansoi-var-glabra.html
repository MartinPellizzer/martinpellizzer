Passiflora mansoi var. glabra

Passiflora mansoi var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, giving it the variety name "glabra.".

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
