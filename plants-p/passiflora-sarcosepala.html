Passiflora sarcosepala

Passiflora sarcosepala, commonly known as the hairy passion flower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature a blend of violet and white petals, along with prominent, hairy sepals. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Passiflora sarcosepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
