Penstemon campanulatus f. roseus

Penstemon campanulatus f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered bearded penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to rose-purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
