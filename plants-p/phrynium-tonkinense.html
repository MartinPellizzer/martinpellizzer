Phrynium tonkinense

Phrynium tonkinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces striking, spatulate-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

Phrynium tonkinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
