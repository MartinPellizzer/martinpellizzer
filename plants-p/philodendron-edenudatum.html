Philodendron edenudatum

Philodendron edenudatum, commonly known as the Eden's Philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with light green or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habit, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
