Pyrus cinerascens

Pyrus cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-leaved pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, ash-gray leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to common pears. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
