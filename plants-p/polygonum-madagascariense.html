Polygonum madagascariense

Polygonum madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its stout, erect stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
