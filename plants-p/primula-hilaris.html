Primula hilaris

Primula hilaris, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. It has a low-growing habit with hairy, basal leaves that help it retain moisture.

Primula hilaris is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
