Prangos herderi subsp. xinjiangensis

Prangos herderi subsp.

xinjiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Xinjiang, China. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. It has small, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
