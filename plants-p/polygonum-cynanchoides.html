Polygonum cynanchoides

Polygonum cynanchoides, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Polygonum cynanchoides is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
