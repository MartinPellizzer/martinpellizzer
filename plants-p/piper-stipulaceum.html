Piper stipulaceum

Piper stipulaceum, commonly known as the glossy leaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

Piper stipulaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
