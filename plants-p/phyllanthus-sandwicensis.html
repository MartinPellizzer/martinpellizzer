Phyllanthus sandwicensis

Phyllanthus sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian goosefoot, is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

Phyllanthus sandwicensis is adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native habitat and is considered a rare and endangered species.
