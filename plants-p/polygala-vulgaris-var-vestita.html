Polygala vulgaris var. vestita

Polygala vulgaris var. vestita, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white,蝶形花 (butterfly-shaped) flowers clustered in dense spikes. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
