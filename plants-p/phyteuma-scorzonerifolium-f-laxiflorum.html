Phyteuma scorzonerifolium f. laxiflorum

Phyteuma scorzonerifolium f. laxiflorum, commonly known as the looseflower phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that bear clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
