Panicum lagostachyum

Panicum lagostachyum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces showy, crimson-colored inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a fine texture that adds visual interest to gardens.

Panicum lagostachyum is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient addition to native plant gardens.
