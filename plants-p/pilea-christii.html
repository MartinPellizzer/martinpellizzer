Pilea christii

Pilea christii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It features rosettes of thick, round, waxy leaves that resemble silver coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by insects. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea christii is known for its ease of care and ability to propagate through leaf cuttings.
