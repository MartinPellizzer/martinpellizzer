Panicum echinulatum var. boliviense

Panicum echinulatum var. boliviense, commonly known as Bolivian hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
