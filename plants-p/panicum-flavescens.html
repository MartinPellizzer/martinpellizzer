Panicum flavescens

Panicum flavescens, commonly known as yellow bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the warm temperate and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green spikelets that are often mistaken for grains, and it is well adapted to drought conditions. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it a valuable plant in restoration ecology.

Panicum flavescens is also used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
