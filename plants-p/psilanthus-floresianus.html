Psilanthus floresianus

Psilanthus floresianus, commonly known as the Floresian acacia, is a flowering plant native to the islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to yellow.

Psilanthus floresianus is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
