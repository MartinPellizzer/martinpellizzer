Prangos turcica

Prangos turcica, commonly known as Turkish thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Prangos turcica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
