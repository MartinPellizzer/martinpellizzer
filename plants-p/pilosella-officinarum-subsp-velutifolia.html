Pilosella officinarum subsp. velutifolia

Pilosella officinarum subsp.

velutifolia, commonly known as velvetleaf mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a basal rosette of velvety, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Its hairy foliage and compact growth habit make it a distinctive and easily identifiable species within the Asteraceae family.
