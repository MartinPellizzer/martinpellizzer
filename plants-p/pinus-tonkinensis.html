Pinus tonkinensis

Pinus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
