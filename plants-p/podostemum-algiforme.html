Podostemum algiforme

Podostemum algiforme, commonly known as the water bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that resemble bladder-like structures, which help it stay afloat on the water's surface. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found near the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Podostemum algiforme plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
