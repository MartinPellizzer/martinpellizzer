Phaseolus polytylus

Phaseolus polytylus, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andes region of South America.

It produces elongated, edible pods that are typically harvested when immature and tender. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and bears small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are encased in a fibrous pod and are often used in traditional Andean cuisine.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to high-altitude growing conditions.
