Picea pachyclada

Picea pachyclada, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and its distinctive, cone-like structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

Picea pachyclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
