Pachira commersonii

Pachira commersonii, commonly known as the Mexican money plant or Brazilian water tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features a thick, woody stem with a distinctive swollen base and large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow, orchid-like flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.

Pachira commersonii is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is relatively easy to care for.
