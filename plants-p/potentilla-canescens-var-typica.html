Potentilla canescens var. typica

Potentilla canescens var. typica, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, hairy foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open woodland or rocky areas. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
