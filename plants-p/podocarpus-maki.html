Podocarpus maki

Podocarpus maki, commonly known as the Japanese podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and slow growth rate. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

The leaves are leathery and retain their color year-round, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
