Psoralea lathyrifolia

Psoralea lathyrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and narrow, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ecological significance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
