Perymenium reticulatum

Perymenium reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined fern, is a species of fern characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit with long, creeping underground stems. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern that gives it its common name.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
