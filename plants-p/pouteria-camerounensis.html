Pouteria camerounensis

Pouteria camerounensis, commonly known as the Cameroon pear, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pears, with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The plant has potential economic value due to its fruit and may also have medicinal properties that are being studied.
