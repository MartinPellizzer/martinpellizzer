Petrea peruviana var. acuminata

Petrea peruviana var. acuminata, commonly known as the Peruvian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, which give it the "acuminata" variety name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract birds and insects, making it an important plant in ecological systems.
