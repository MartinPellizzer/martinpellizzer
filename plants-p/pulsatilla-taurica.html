Pulsatilla taurica

Pulsatilla taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, herbaceous stem with palmately divided leaves, and it produces a single, large flower with numerous stamens. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Pulsatilla taurica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
