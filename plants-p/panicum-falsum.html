Panicum falsum

Panicum falsum, commonly known as false panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It has a fibrous root system and produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
