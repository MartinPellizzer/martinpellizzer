Potamogeton polygonifolius lancifolius

Potamogeton polygonifolius lancifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It has long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, and it relies on water currents for pollination. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to aquatic ecosystem stability.

It is an important food source for waterfowl and other aquatic organisms.
