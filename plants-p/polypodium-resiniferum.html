Polypodium resiniferum

Polypodium resiniferum, commonly known as the resinous fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a fine layer of resinous secretions. This plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The resinous coating on its fronds may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores and environmental stressors.

Its unique botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
