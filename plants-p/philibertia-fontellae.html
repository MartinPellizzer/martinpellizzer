Philibertia fontellae

Philibertia fontellae, commonly known as the Fontelle's filbert, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
