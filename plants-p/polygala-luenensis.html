Polygala luenensis

Polygala luenensis, commonly known as the Luen's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Polygala luenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
