Plocosperma

Plocosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets.

Plocosperma is primarily valued for its role in ecological systems and as a forage plant for livestock.
