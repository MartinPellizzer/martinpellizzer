Phyllanthus obtusatus

Phyllanthus obtusatus, commonly known as the blunt-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
