Piptochaetium seleri

Piptochaetium seleri, commonly known as the Seler's grass, is a species of grass native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Piptochaetium seleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
