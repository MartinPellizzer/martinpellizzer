Phippsia porsildii

Phippsia porsildii, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue in color. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
