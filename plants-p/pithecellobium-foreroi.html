Pithecellobium foreroi

Pithecellobium foreroi, commonly known as the Forero's pita, is a species of legume native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, shrub-like plant characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
