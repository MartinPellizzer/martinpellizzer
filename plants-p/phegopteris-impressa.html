Phegopteris impressa

Phegopteris impressa, commonly known as the broad bucktooth fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Athyriaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and often deeply lobed fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance due to the presence of prominent, rounded lobes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its striking foliage.
