Psittacanthus brasiliensis

Psittacanthus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pineapple plant, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant that produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences, which are followed by spiky, red or orange fruits resembling pineapples. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fruits are edible, though they are not widely cultivated for commercial use.
