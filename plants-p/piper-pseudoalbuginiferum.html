Piper pseudoalbuginiferum

Piper pseudoalbuginiferum, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its leaves are also used in local cuisines and for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
