Polypodium furfuraceum

Polypodium furfuraceum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical forests. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The hairy shield fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
