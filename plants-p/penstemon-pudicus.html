Penstemon pudicus

Penstemon pudicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy flowers, which range from blue to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Penstemon pudicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to drought conditions.
