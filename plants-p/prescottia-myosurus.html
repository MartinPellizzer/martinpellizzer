Prescottia myosurus

Prescottia myosurus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native grassland ecosystems.
