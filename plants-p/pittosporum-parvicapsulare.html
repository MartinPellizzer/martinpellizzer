Pittosporum parvicapsulare

Pittosporum parvicapsulare, commonly known as the small-capsule pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum parvicapsulare is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
