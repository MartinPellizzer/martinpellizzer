Pteris arborea f. regia

Pteris arborea f. regia, commonly known as the royal water fern, is a striking fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, intricate fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length, with a distinctive dark green color and a glossy texture. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with closely spaced pinnae that give it a lush, feathery appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
