Paullinia clematidifolia

Paullinia clematidifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian soursop or carambola tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications due to its tart flavor.
