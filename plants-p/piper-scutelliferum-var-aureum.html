Piper scutelliferum var. aureum

Piper scutelliferum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden sander or golden pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a striking golden-yellow margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-lobed, red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
