Pterisanthes ovata

Pterisanthes ovata, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Pterisanthes ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
