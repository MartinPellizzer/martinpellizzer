Pseudocherleria laricina

Pseudocherleria laricina, commonly known as the small white orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with a single, showy white or pale pink flower. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

The orchid is characterized by its simple floral structure and is often found in association with other woodland flora.
