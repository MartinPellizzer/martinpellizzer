Piper patulum

Piper patulum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or the Mexican pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The fruits are used in traditional medicine and cuisine, similar to other pepper species.
