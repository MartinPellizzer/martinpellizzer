Pleuroplitis quartiniana var. tenella

Pleuroplitis quartiniana var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pleurotis, is a variant of the pleurotis genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
