Pleurothallis xyloura

Pleurothallis xyloura, commonly known as the Yucatan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
