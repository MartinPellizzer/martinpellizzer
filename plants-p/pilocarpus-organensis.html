Pilocarpus organensis

Pilocarpus organensis, commonly known as the Peruvian jasmine, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including pilocarpine, which has medicinal applications. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its ecological and potential pharmaceutical significance, it is of interest to botanists and pharmacologists alike.
