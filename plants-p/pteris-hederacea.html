Pteris hederacea

Pteris hederacea, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bi-lobed and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Pteris hederacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive, lush foliage.
