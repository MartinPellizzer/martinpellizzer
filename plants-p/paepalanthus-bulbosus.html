Paepalanthus bulbosus

Paepalanthus bulbosus, commonly known as the bulbous puya, is a striking South American plant belonging to the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces an impressive inflorescence with showy, colorful bracts that attract pollinators. It is native to regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
