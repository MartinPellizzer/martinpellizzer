Pseudotsuga glauca var. pumila

Pseudotsuga glauca var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf mountain hemlock, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage that turns a striking blue-gray in winter. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain oval, winged seeds, typical of the coniferous family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as a decorative plant.
