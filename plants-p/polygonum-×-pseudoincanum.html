Polygonum × pseudoincanum

Polygonum × pseudoincanum, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking heart-shaped leaves with red veins and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a visually appealing yet low-maintenance plant.
