Platycapnos tenuiloba subsp. parallela

Platycapnos tenuiloba subsp.

parallela, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet.

The leaves are elongated and narrow, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
