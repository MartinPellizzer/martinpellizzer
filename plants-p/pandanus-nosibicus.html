Pandanus nosibicus

Pandanus nosibicus, commonly known as the island pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used for medicinal purposes by indigenous communities.

Pandanus nosibicus plays an important ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
