Poa secunda var. elongata

Poa secunda var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and pastures. It has slender, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
