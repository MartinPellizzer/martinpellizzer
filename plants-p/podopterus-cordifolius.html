Podopterus cordifolius

Podopterus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved podopterus, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves.

The plant exhibits a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself along structures. Its foliage is typically a deep green, with prominent venation patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal. Flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

The species is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.
