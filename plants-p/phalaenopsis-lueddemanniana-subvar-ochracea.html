Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana subvar. ochracea

Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana subvar. ochracea, commonly known as the Ochraceous Phalaenopsis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms once a year, producing long-lasting inflorescences with numerous blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
