Panicum boivinianum

Panicum boivinianum, commonly known as Boivin's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum boivinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
