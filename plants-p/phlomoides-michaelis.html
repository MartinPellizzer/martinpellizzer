Phlomoides michaelis

Phlomoides michaelis, commonly known as the Michael's false flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Phlomoides michaelis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
