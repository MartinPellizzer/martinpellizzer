Parietaria officinalis var. ramiflora

Parietaria officinalis var. ramiflora, commonly known as the ramiflorous wall pennywort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces a distinctive, mild allergenic pollen.

This variety is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
