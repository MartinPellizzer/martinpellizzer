Primula lanuginosa

Primula lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates, making it a popular choice for alpine gardens.
