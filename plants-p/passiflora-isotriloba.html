Passiflora isotriloba

Passiflora isotriloba, commonly known as the Trinidad passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with three to five lobes and its showy, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
