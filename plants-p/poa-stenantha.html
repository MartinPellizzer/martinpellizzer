Poa stenantha

Poa stenantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a clumping growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
