Polycarpaea divaricata var. aristata

Polycarpaea divaricata var. aristata, commonly known as the slender false flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, often with a distinctive blue or violet hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
