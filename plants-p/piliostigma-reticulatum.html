Piliostigma reticulatum

Piliostigma reticulatum, commonly known as the African brugmansia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in summer. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Piliostigma reticulatum thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
