Pandanus sphaerocephalus

Pandanus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a dense, spherical crown. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spadix, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in coastal or wetland environments.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element in tropical landscapes.
