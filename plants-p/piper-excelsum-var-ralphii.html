Piper excelsum var. ralphii

Piper excelsum var. ralphii, commonly known as the Ralphii pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that give way to bright red berries. The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
