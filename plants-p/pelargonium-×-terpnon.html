Pelargonium × terpnon

Pelargonium × terpnon, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.

Pelargonium × terpnon prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for hanging baskets, containers, and garden borders.
