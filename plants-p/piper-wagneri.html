Piper wagneri

Piper wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Piper wagneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
