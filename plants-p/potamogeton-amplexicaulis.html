Potamogeton amplexicaulis

Potamogeton amplexicaulis, commonly known as broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across much of Europe and Asia.

It has long, slender stems that branch out and bear broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often clasping around the stem, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow and arranged in spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton amplexicaulis is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and canals.
