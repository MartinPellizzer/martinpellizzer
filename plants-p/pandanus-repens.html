Pandanus repens

Pandanus repens, commonly known as the wandering pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, often reaching lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, sandy soils.

Pandanus repens is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in many island communities.
