Pentapanax leschenaultii var. longipedunculatus

Pentapanax leschenaultii var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-pedunculate five-leaf plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are elongated and branched inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
