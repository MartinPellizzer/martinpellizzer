Protea scabrida

Protea scabrida, commonly known as the rough protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous pink to red flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant features woody stems and broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a rough, scaly texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Protea scabrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is an important species in the conservation of South Africa's unique flora.
