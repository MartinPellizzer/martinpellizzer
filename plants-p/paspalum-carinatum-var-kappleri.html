Paspalum carinatum var. kappleri

Paspalum carinatum var. kappleri, commonly known as the Kappler's beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
