Picea spinulosa subsp. pseudobrachytyla

Picea spinulosa subsp.

pseudobrachytyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf white spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and short, stiff needles that are typically pale blue-green in color.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue, containing winged seeds for wind dispersal. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems and its adaptation to harsh climatic conditions.
