Paspalum cuyabense

Paspalum cuyabense, commonly known as Cuyabense grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green color and a rough, hairy texture. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers.

This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
