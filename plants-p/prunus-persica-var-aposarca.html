Prunus persica var. aposarca

Prunus persica var. aposarca, commonly known as the apricot, is a deciduous fruit tree native to Asia.

It produces sweet, yellow or orange fruits with a fuzzy skin and a single seed inside. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in temperate climates and requires a cold winter dormancy period for proper fruit development.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing before the leaves in early spring.
