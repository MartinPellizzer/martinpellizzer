Phoebe purpurea

Phoebe purpurea, commonly known as the purple phoebe, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and dark green with a slight waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical berries that are red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
