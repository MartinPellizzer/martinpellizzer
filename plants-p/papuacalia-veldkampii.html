Papuacalia veldkampii

Papuacalia veldkampii, commonly known as the Veldkamp's rock fig, is a unique evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers, followed by edible fig-like fruits that are encased in a fleshy, green husk. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found growing in coastal or limestone areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
