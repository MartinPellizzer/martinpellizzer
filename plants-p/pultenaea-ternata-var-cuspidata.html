Pultenaea ternata var. cuspidata

Pultenaea ternata var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and flowering display.
