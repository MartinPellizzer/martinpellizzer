Pahudia africana

Pahudia africana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

The African flame tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
