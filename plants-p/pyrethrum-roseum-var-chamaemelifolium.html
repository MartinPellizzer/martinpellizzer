Pyrethrum roseum var. chamaemelifolium

Pyrethrum roseum var. chamaemelifolium, commonly known as the alpine chamaemelum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in summer, are typically pink to lavender in color and attract a variety of pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
