Phaseolus vulgaris var. platycreus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. platycreus, commonly known as the flat-seeded kidney bean, is a variety of the common bean plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, flat seeds that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant produces climbing vines with trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world.
