Piper phyllanthus

Piper phyllanthus, commonly known as Indian pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive aromatic scent.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
