Polygonum bordzilowskii

Polygonum bordzilowskii, commonly known as the Armenian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and simple, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color.

Polygonum bordzilowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
