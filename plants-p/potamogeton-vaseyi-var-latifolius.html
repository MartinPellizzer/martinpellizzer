Potamogeton vaseyi var. latifolius

Potamogeton vaseyi var. latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender stems that often branch and bear whorled leaves that are broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving rivers.
