Piptocarpha densifolia

Piptocarpha densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Piptocarpha densifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
