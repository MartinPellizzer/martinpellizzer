Potentilla morrisonicola

Potentilla morrisonicola, commonly known as Morrison's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
