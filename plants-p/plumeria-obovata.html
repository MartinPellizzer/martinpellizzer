Plumeria obovata

Plumeria obovata, commonly known as the frangipani tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and pink. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a waxy texture, while the bark is smooth and grayish. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
