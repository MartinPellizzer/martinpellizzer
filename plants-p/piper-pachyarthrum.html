Piper pachyarthrum

Piper pachyarthrum, commonly known as the thick-stemmed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous pepper species. The plant is characterized by its thick, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
