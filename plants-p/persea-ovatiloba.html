Persea ovatiloba

Persea ovatiloba, commonly known as the ovate-leaf spondias, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries. Persea ovatiloba thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
