Physaria globosa

Physaria globosa, commonly known as the globular bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Physaria globosa is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.
