Primula rimicola

Primula rimicola, commonly known as the rimicola primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula rimicola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
