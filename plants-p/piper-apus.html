Piper apus

Piper apus, commonly known as the climbing pepper or Indian pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal value.
