Piper pustulatum

Piper pustulatum, commonly known as the spotted pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that often have a reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, spherical berries that turn red when ripe. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
