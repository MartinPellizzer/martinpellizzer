Phreatia wariana

Phreatia wariana, commonly known as the Borneo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant typically produces a single flower per stem, which is usually pale green or yellow with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Phreatia wariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
