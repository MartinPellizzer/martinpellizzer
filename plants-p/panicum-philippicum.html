Panicum philippicum

Panicum philippicum, commonly known as Philippine millet, is a warm-season grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that produce tiny grains. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for erosion control and as a forage crop.
