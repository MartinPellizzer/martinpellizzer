Pyrus pyraster f. dispar

Pyrus pyraster f. dispar, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and white to pale yellow flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a small, edible pome that matures in late summer. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in hedgerows.
