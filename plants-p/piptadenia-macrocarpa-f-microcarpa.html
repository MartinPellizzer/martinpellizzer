Piptadenia macrocarpa f. microcarpa

Piptadenia macrocarpa f. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited piptadenia, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, elongated pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested or disturbed areas. It has a woody stem and produces small, yellowish flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal use.
