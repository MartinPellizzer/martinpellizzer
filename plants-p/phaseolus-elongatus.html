Phaseolus elongatus

Phaseolus elongatus, commonly known as the long bean or yardlong bean, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It produces elongated, cylindrical pods that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it flowers with small, white or pale yellow blooms.

The plant is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and fiber.
