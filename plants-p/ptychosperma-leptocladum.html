Ptychosperma leptocladum

Ptychosperma leptocladum, commonly known as the slender-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its leaves are often used in cooking and traditional medicine due to their aromatic properties.

Ptychosperma leptocladum thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near mangroves or in swampy areas.
