Pandanus spicatus

Pandanus spicatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found near mangroves or in sandy soils. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber, while its fruits are edible and used in local cuisines.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal environments and providing habitat for various species.
