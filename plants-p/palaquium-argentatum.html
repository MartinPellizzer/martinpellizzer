Palaquium argentatum

Palaquium argentatum, commonly known as silver star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, round berry with a sweet, acidic flavor, often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
