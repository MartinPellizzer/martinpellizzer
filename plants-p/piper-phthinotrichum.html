Piper phthinotrichum

Piper phthinotrichum, commonly known as the hairy-pod pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny, aromatic seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its seeds in traditional medicine.
