Parsonsia philippinensis

Parsonsia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped with five lobes and are pollinated by birds and insects.

Parsonsia philippinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and tropical appearance.
