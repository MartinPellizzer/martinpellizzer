Plantago rigida var. angustior

Plantago rigida var. angustior, commonly known as narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, sturdy stems that bear broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically clustered at the top of the stems. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
