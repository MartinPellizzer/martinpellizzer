Potentilla asiae-mediae f. ciliolata

Potentilla asiae-mediae f. ciliolata, commonly known as the Asian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, particularly China. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage features finely ciliate margins, which is a key identifying trait of this particular form.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
