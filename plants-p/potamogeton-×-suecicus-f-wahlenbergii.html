Potamogeton × suecicus f. wahlenbergii

Potamogeton × suecicus f. wahlenbergii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has a submerged stem with whorled leaves that are usually less than 1 cm wide. It produces small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
