Phyllocactus phyllanthus var. columbiensis

Phyllocactus phyllanthus var. columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbian phyllocactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and the presence of modified leaves that function as spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is native to specific regions in South America and is valued for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance.
