Potentilla hellwegeri

Potentilla hellwegeri, commonly known as the Hellweger cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a dark green color.

Potentilla hellwegeri is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
