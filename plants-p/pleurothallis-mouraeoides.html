Pleurothallis mouraeoides

Pleurothallis mouraeoides, commonly known as the Moura's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with a distinctive lip that is adorned with intricate patterns. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
