Pollinia tanakae

Pollinia tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's orchid.

It is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its unique pollinia, which are specialized structures that facilitate pollination. These pollinia are typically clustered and adhere to the bodies of pollinators such as bees and insects.

The botanical features of Pollinia tanakae make it an important subject in the study of orchid reproductive strategies.
