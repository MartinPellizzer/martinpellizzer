Pilea subserrata

Pilea subserrata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its rosette of glossy, rounded leaves that resemble silver coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is a popular indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea subserrata thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
