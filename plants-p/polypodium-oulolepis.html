Polypodium oulolepis

Polypodium oulolepis, commonly known as the Oaxaca holly fern, is a species of fern native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with lanceolate to ovate-shaped pinnae that are often adorned with small, serrated teeth.
