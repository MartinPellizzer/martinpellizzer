Potentilla plicata

Potentilla plicata, commonly known as the crested potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. Potentilla plicata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its cultivation in various horticultural settings.
