Phragmites australis subsp. australis

Phragmites australis subsp.

australis, commonly known as common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments. It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and dense, fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its widespread distribution. It is highly adaptable, growing in a variety of wetland habitats including marshes, lakes, and coastal areas.

Its extensive root system also makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
