Piper orenocanum

Piper orenocanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or the Andean pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around other vegetation. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a glossy surface and a distinct aromatic scent.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
