Pyrus firma

Pyrus firma, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, white to yellowish fruit in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in temperate climates.

Pyrus firma is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Japanese culture.
