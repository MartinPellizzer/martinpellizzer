Phaseolus alatus

Phaseolus alatus, commonly known as the runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces edible pods that contain both beans and fleshy, edible tendrils. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The pods are typically harvested when they are young and tender for optimal flavor and texture.
