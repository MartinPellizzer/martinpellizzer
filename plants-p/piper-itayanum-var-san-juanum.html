Piper itayanum var. san-juanum

Piper itayanum var. san-juanum, commonly known as San Juan pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elongated, slender fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
