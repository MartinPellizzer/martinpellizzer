Prunus triloba

Prunus triloba, commonly known as the flat-leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Prunus triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
