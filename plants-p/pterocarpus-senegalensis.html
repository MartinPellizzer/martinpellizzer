Pterocarpus senegalensis

Pterocarpus senegalensis, commonly known as African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The tree produces yellow or orange flowers and pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of shea butter. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in dry, tropical climates with well-drained soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
