Perotis patula

Perotis patula, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Perotis patula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
