Pelargonium australe

Pelargonium australe, commonly known as the Australian geranium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium australe is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
