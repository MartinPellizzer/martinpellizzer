Panicum miliaceum var. subvitellinotephrum

Panicum miliaceum var. subvitellinotephrum, commonly known as the millet grass, is a variant of the panicum miliaceum species, which is native to warm temperate regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
