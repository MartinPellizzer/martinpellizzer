Plantago macrorhiza var. tangerina

Plantago macrorhiza var. tangerina, commonly known as the Turkish plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a prominent central vein. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
