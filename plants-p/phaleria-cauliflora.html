Phaleria cauliflora

Phaleria cauliflora, commonly known as the giant white wood apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its large, smooth, light green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a large, fleshy drupe with a thick, fibrous outer layer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
