Polygonum urumqiense

Polygonum urumqiense, commonly known as the Urumqi knotgrass, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, particularly found in Xinjiang, China.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems that are usually hairless and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
