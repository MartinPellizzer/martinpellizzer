Parkia barnebyana

Parkia barnebyana, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces elongated pods that contain flat, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
