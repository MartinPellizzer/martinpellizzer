Pandanus obovatus

Pandanus obovatus, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes at the end of its stems. Its roots and leaves have been utilized in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, showcasing its resilience in humid, saline conditions.
