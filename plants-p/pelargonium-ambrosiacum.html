Pelargonium ambrosiacum

Pelargonium ambrosiacum, commonly known as the golden geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves and showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice among gardeners.
