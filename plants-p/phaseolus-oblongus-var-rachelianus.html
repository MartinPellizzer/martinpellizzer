Phaseolus oblongus var. rachelianus

Phaseolus oblongus var. rachelianus, commonly known as the Rachelian runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andean regions of South America.

It produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when immature and turn red or yellow when mature. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white or pale purple. Its seeds are encased in a hard shell and are often used for culinary purposes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
