Phaca cerussata

Phaca cerussata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters, and the tree produces woody pods containing seeds.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with limited rainfall.
