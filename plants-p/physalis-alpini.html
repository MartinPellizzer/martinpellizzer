Physalis alpini

Physalis alpini, commonly known as the alpine ground cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in inflated, papery calyces. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, and it produces edible, tart fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
