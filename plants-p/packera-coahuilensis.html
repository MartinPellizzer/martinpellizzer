Packera coahuilensis

Packera coahuilensis, commonly known as Coahuila's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its yellowish to white flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
