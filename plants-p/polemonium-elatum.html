Polemonium elatum

Polemonium elatum, commonly known as the Eastern blueflag, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it typically grows in moist woodland habitats. Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
