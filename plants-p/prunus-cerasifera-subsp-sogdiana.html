Prunus cerasifera subsp. sogdiana

Prunus cerasifera subsp.

sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The plant has simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local fauna.
