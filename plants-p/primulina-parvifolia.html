Primulina parvifolia

Primulina parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically features broad, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms in shades of pink, purple, or white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among plant enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
