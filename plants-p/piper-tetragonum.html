Piper tetragonum

Piper tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its square, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, dark blue-black berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in humid climates.
