Poecilostachys baronis

Poecilostachys baronis, commonly known as the golden bamboo or baron's bamboo, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, golden-green foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas with tropical or subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
