Pterocephalus perennis var. pinnatisectus

Pterocephalus perennis var. pinnatisectus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved desert bell, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system adapted to survive in dry environments, with deep taproots that allow it to access groundwater. The plant has slender, branched stems that often grow close to the ground, and its leaves are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
