Polypogon maritimus subsp. subspathaceus

Polypogon maritimus subsp.

subspathaceus, commonly known as the coastal panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This subspecies plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
