Pyrola asarifolia subsp. bracteata

Pyrola asarifolia subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate wintergreen, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada. It is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous structure and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink.

The plant has oval, dark green leaves with a waxy coating, and its flowers are often adorned with a distinctive bract at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded forest environments and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in woodland ecosystems.
