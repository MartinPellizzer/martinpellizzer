Phaeanthus pubescens

Phaeanthus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy phaeanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas.

Phaeanthus pubescens is often found in the understory of forests, where it contributes to the biodiversity of the ecosystem.
