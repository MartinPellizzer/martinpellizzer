Potamogeton berchtoldii

Potamogeton berchtoldii, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is an important component of healthy aquatic habitats.
