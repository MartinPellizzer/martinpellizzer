Persea erythropus var. triplinervia

Persea erythropus var. triplinervia, commonly known as the red-leaf persea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple leaves, which are often adorned with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling small, round cherries. Its leaves have a trifoliate structure, with three distinct leaflets, giving it the species name *triplinervia*.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for both its aesthetic and culinary qualities.
