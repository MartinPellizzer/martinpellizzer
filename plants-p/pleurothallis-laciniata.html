Pleurothallis laciniata

Pleurothallis laciniata, commonly known as the shredded pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its intricate, lace-like appearance makes it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of epiphytic orchids.

Pleurothallis laciniata is also noted for its ability to climb using its aerial roots, often growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.
