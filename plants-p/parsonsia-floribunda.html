Parsonsia floribunda

Parsonsia floribunda, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying a fiery appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
