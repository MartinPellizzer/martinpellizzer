Plantago albicans f. major

Plantago albicans f. major, commonly known as the large plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy underside and a prominent central vein. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
