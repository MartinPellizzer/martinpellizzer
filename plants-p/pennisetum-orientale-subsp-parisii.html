Pennisetum orientale subsp. parisii

Pennisetum orientale subsp.

parisii, commonly known as the Paris sedge, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
