Potentilla croceolata

Potentilla croceolata, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early summer and are composed of five petals. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Potentilla croceolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
