Prunus glyptocarya

Prunus glyptocarya, commonly known as the Chinese bird's eye cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender branches and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
