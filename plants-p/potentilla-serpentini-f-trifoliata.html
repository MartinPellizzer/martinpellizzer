Potentilla serpentini f. trifoliata

Potentilla serpentini f. trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf potentilla, is a compact shrub characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically dense and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
