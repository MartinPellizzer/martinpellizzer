Pyrethrum polycephalum

Pyrethrum polycephalum, commonly known as the multi-headed pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a variety of pyrethrin compounds, which are naturally occurring insecticides. Its foliage is typically dark green and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.

Pyrethrum polycephalum is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of natural pest control agents.
