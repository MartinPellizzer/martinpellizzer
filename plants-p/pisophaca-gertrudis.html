Pisophaca gertrudis

Pisophaca gertrudis, commonly known as the Gertrudis's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricate flowers that typically feature a combination of white, yellow, and green hues. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on tree trunks. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires careful preservation efforts.
