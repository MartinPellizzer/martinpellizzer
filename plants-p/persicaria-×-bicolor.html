Persicaria × bicolor

Persicaria × bicolor, commonly known as the Japanese morning glory, is a hybrid flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bi-colored flowers that range from deep purple to bright yellow. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
