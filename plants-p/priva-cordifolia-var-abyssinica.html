Priva cordifolia var. abyssinica

Priva cordifolia var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian heartleaf, is a rare and distinctive plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features a compact growth habit with dark green, glossy foliage that often has a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
