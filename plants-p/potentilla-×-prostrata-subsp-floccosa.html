Potentilla × prostrata subsp. floccosa

Potentilla × prostrata subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
