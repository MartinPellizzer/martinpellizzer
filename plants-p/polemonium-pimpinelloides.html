Polemonium pimpinelloides

Polemonium pimpinelloides, commonly known as the Appalachian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It produces showy, nodding blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
