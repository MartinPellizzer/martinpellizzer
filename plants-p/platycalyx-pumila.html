Platycalyx pumila

Platycalyx pumila, commonly known as the smooth calyx morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, smooth calyx that encloses its flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
