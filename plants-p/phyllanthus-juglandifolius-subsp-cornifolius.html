Phyllanthus juglandifolius subsp. cornifolius

Phyllanthus juglandifolius subsp.

cornifolius, commonly known as the horned leaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that often have a waxy coating and a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
