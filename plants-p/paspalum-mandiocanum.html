Paspalum mandiocanum

Paspalum mandiocanum, commonly known as Brazilian millet, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, including poor soils and drought.

Paspalum mandiocanum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
