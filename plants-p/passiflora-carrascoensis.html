Passiflora carrascoensis

Passiflora carrascoensis, commonly known as the Carrasco's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Passiflora carrascoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
