Prunus × lannesiana f. fukurokuju

Prunus × lannesiana f. fukurokuju, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in early summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a pointed tip and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall.

The flowers, which bloom in early spring, are pink and contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.
