Polygala vulgaris var. lusitanica

Polygala vulgaris var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems and small, usually greenish or yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
