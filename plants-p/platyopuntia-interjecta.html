Platyopuntia interjecta

Platyopuntia interjecta, commonly known as the Mexican pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
