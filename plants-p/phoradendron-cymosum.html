Phoradendron cymosum

Phoradendron cymosum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It possesses chlorophyll and can perform photosynthesis, but it also derives nutrients and water from its host through specialized structures called haustoria. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse the seeds. It is native to parts of the Americas and is often associated with Christmas traditions due to its evergreen appearance.

Its ecological role includes providing food for wildlife and influencing the health of its host trees.
