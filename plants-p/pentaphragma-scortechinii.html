Pentaphragma scortechinii

Pentaphragma scortechinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tiny tubular flowers. The plant is known for its showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.
