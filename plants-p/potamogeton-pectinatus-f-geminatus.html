Potamogeton pectinatus f. geminatus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. geminatus, commonly known as the twinleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in pairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native regions.
