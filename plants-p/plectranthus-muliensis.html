Plectranthus muliensis

Plectranthus muliensis, commonly known as the Malawi mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden use.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both useful and attractive in horticultural settings.
