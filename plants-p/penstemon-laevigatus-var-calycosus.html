Penstemon laevigatus var. calycosus

Penstemon laevigatus var. calycosus, commonly known as the calycosus penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.
