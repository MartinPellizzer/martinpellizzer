Piper tabernillanum var. anconense

Piper tabernillanum var. anconense, commonly known as the Ancon pepper or Ancon chili, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oblong, pungent fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The fruit's high capsaicin content gives it a sharp, spicy flavor.
