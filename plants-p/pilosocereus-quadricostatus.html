Pilosocereus quadricostatus

Pilosocereus quadricostatus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a rounded, green body with a distinctive white or pinkish crown. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the evening.

Pilosocereus quadricostatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal.
