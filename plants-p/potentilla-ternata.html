Potentilla ternata

Potentilla ternata, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its extensive root system makes it an effective ground cover in erosion-prone areas.

Potentilla ternata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
