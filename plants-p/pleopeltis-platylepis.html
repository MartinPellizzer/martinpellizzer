Pleopeltis platylepis

Pleopeltis platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or rocks without being parasitic. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, rugged texture.

Pleopeltis platylepis is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
