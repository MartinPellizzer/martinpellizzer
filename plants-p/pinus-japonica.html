Pinus japonica

Pinus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and long, slender needles that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The tree produces yellowish cones that mature over a year, releasing seeds in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in coastal areas.

The Japanese black pine is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
