Pavonia burchellii var. tomentosa

Pavonia burchellii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pavonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique texture.
