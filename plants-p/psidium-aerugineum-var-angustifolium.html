Psidium aerugineum var. angustifolium

Psidium aerugineum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It features elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

The species is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types under suitable climatic conditions.
