Pleopeltis rupestris var. parallela

Pleopeltis rupestris var. parallela, commonly known as the parallel pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, creeping growth habit. The fronds are typically lanceolate and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found on rocky or forested slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
