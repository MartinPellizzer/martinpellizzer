Potentilla angelliae

Potentilla angelliae, commonly known as Angel's potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla angelliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
