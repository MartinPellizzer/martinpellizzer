Piper rubribaccum

Piper rubribaccum, commonly known as red-stemmed pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its reddish stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

While not widely cultivated for its fruit, it is occasionally used in traditional medicine for its potential aromatic and medicinal properties.
