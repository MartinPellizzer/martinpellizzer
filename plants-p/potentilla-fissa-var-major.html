Potentilla fissa var. major

Potentilla fissa var. major, commonly known as the large-fruited cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
