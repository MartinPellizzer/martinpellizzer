Peperidium

Peperidium, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. Peperidium species typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. They are native to various regions of the Americas and are often found in grassy or disturbed areas.

The genus includes several species that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
