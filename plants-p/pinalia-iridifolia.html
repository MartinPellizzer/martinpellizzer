Pinalia iridifolia

Pinalia iridifolia, commonly known as the blueleaf orchid, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous environments, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its unique coloration and habitat make it a rare and visually distinctive species within its ecosystem.

Due to its limited distribution, Pinalia iridifolia is considered a species of conservation concern.
