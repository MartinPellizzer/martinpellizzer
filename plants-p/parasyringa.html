Parasyringa

Parasyringa, commonly known as the Japanese spirea, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the species. The plant has slender, arching branches and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering display. Parasyringa thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
