Pleurothallis micklowii

Pleurothallis micklowii, commonly known as the Micklow's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleurothallis micklowii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
