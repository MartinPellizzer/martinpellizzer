Pleurothallis cestrochila

Pleurothallis cestrochila, commonly known as the violet-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to violet in color, with intricate veining patterns. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding bark.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
