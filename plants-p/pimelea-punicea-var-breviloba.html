Pimelea punicea var. breviloba

Pimelea punicea var. breviloba, commonly known as the pink waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and dry inland environments.

The variety breviloba is distinguished by its shorter, more compact form compared to the typical variety.
