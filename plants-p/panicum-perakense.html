Panicum perakense

Panicum perakense, commonly known as the Perak grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
