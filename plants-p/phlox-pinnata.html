Phlox pinnata

Phlox pinnata, commonly known as everlasting phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
