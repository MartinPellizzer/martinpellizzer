Pyrus vaccinioides

Pyrus vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of western Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish berries. Pyrus vaccinioides is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
