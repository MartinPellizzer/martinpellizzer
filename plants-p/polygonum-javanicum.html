Polygonum javanicum

Polygonum javanicum, commonly known as Java knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants characterized by their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible seeds. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are ovate with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

The species is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
