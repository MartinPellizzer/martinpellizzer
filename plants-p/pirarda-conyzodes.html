Pirarda conyzodes

Pirarda conyzodes, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pirarda conyzodes is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
