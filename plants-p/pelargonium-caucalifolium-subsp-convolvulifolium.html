Pelargonium caucalifolium subsp. convolvulifolium

Pelargonium caucalifolium subsp.

convolvulifolium, commonly known as the spiral-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that give it a unique appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
