Prinos verticillatus var. dubius

Prinos verticillatus var. dubius, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, featuring deep root systems and thick, waxy leaves to reduce water loss.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
