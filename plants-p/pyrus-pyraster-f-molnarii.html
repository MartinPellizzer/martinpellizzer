Pyrus pyraster f. molnarii

Pyrus pyraster f. molnarii, commonly known as the Molnari's whitebeam, is a rare horticultural form of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, waxy leaves that contrast with its rough, fissured bark. This plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often found in woodland habitats.

Its botanical interest lies in its unique foliage and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
