Perigaria

Perigaria, commonly known as the "flower of the sea," is a rare and unique plant found in coastal regions.

It thrives in saline environments, often growing along rocky shores and tidal zones. The plant has small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions. Its leaves are succulent, storing moisture to survive in dry periods.

Perigaria plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local ecosystems.
