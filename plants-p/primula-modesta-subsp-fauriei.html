Primula modesta subsp. fauriei

Primula modesta subsp.

fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features rosettes of basal leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and frost-resistant nature, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
