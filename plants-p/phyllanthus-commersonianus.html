Phyllanthus commersonianus

Phyllanthus commersonianus, commonly known as the "Cape gooseberry" or "physalis," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that produces small, round fruits enclosed in a papery calyx. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its edible berries, which have a tart flavor.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
