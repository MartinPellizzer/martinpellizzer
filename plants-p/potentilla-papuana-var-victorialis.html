Potentilla papuana var. victorialis

Potentilla papuana var. victorialis, commonly known as the Papuan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
