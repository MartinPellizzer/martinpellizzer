Pouzolzia conglobata

Pouzolzia conglobata, commonly known as the globular pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Its compact form and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
