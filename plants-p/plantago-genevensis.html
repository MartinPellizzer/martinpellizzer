Plantago genevensis

Plantago genevensis, commonly known as the Geneva plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along riverbanks and in wetlands. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, helmet-like structure called an elaiosome, which aids in seed dispersal by ants.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
