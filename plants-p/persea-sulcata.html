Persea sulcata

Persea sulcata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, orange fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically yellow-green and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.
