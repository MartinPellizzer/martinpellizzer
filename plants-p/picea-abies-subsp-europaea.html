Picea abies subsp. europaea

Picea abies subsp.

europaea, commonly known as the European white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Europe. It is characterized by its conical shape, silver-blue needles, and distinctive cone structure.

The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and forestry.
