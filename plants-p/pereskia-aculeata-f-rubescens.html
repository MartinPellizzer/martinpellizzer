Pereskia aculeata f. rubescens

Pereskia aculeata f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-leaved cactus or the red-pereskia, is a unique hybrid plant within the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems that are often covered with short, stiff spines. The leaves are modified into spines, and the plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its most distinguishing feature is the reddish coloration of its stems and leaves, which becomes more pronounced under direct sunlight.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
