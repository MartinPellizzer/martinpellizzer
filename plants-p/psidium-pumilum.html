Psidium pumilum

Psidium pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf pineapple guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a small, edible berry with a sweet and tangy flavor, similar to a cross between a pineapple and a guava.

Psidium pumilum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique, flavorful fruit.
