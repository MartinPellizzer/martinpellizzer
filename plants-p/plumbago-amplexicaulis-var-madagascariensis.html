Plumbago amplexicaulis var. madagascariensis

Plumbago amplexicaulis var. madagascariensis, commonly known as Madagascar plumbago, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of blue or violet tubular flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that clasping the stems, giving it its species name "amplexicaulis.". It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is known for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
