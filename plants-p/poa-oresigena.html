Poa oresigena

Poa oresigena, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar climatic conditions.
