Passiflora karwinskii

Passiflora karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a distinctive blue or purple coloration. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, whitish hairs.

Passiflora karwinskii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
