Pandanus atropurpureus

Pandanus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that are typically reddish-purple in color. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts, while the leaves are utilized in thatching and basket making.

The species is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, thriving in warm, humid climates.
