Plantago hartwegii var. bidentula

Plantago hartwegii var. bidentula, commonly known as the two-toothed plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and wetlands. The plant features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and two small, tooth-like projections on the leaf margins. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
