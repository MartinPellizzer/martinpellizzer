Pancheria multijuga

Pancheria multijuga, commonly known as the broad-leaved pancheria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Pancheria multijuga is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
