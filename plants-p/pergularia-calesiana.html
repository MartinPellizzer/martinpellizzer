Pergularia calesiana

Pergularia calesiana, commonly known as the Calesian grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to warm climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets, and it produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Pergularia calesiana plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
