Polypodium nervopilosum

Polypodium nervopilosum, commonly known as the hairy-veined polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant features prominent, hairy veins that give it its common name and contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories across tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium nervopilosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
