Polystachya nana

Polystachya nana, commonly known as the dwarf polystachya, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact stature and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, yellow, or green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
