Poa alberti subsp. poophagorum

Poa alberti subsp.

poophagorum, commonly known as the desert bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to harsh environments, tolerating drought and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and plays a role in stabilizing sandy or rocky habitats.
