Peplis borysthenica

Peplis borysthenica, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of southern Ukraine and Crimea.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers in late summer, which are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Peplis borysthenica is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
