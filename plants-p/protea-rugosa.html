Protea rugosa

Protea rugosa, commonly known as the rough protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous overlapping bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center.

Protea rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
