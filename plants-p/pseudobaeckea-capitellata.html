Pseudobaeckea capitellata

Pseudobaeckea capitellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a small shrub with distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in heathlands and open forests. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is an important component of its native ecosystem.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
