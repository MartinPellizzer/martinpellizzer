Pleonotoma melioides

Pleonotoma melioides, commonly known as the melon leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle belonging to the family Chrysomelidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated body and vibrant coloration, often featuring a mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The beetle's larvae feed on the leaves of various plants, particularly those in the Cucurbitaceae family, such as cucumbers and melons. Its life cycle includes several instar stages, with adults emerging in the summer months.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role as a host for this beetle, influencing both ecological interactions and agricultural practices.
