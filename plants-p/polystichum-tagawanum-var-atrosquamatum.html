Polystichum tagawanum var. atrosquamatum

Polystichum tagawanum var. atrosquamatum, commonly known as the black-squamate cliff fern, is a variant of the Japanese cliff fern.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds with distinctive blackish-brown spore cases. The plant thrives in rocky, shaded environments, typically found in mountainous regions. Its fronds are usually upright and can reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
