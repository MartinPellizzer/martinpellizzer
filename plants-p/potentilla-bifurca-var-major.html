Potentilla bifurca var. major

Potentilla bifurca var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
