Prenanthes parviflora

Prenanthes parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered waterparsley, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Prenanthes parviflora is often found in wet soils and plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats.
