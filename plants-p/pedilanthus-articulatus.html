Pedilanthus articulatus

Pedilanthus articulatus, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, candle-like appearance, with a thick, fleshy stem that resembles a candle. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. Its leaves are alternate and often have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Pedilanthus articulatus is also known for its use in traditional medicine, where various parts of the plant are utilized for their purported healing properties.
