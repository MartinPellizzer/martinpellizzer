Plectranthus hispidus var. glabrior

Plectranthus hispidus var. glabrior, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, often in shades of purple or blue, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
