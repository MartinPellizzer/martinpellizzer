Prasophyllum holzingeri

Prasophyllum holzingeri, commonly known as the Holzinger leek orchid, is an endemic species native to the southeastern region of Australia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open heathland or woodland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
