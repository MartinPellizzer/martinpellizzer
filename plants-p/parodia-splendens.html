Parodia splendens

Parodia splendens, commonly known as the Splendid Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and distinctive rib structure. The plant features short, spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its slow growth rate makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Parodia splendens is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.
