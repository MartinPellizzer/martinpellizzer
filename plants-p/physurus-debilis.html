Physurus debilis

Physurus debilis, commonly known as the slender leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

Due to its delicate appearance, it requires careful handling and consistent moisture to flourish.
