Polypodium macaronesicum

Polypodium macaronesicum, commonly known as the Canary Islands holly fern, is a terrestrial fern native to the Macaronesian region, including the Canary Islands, Madeira, and Cape Verde.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that form a compact, cushion-like growth. The plant has pinnate leaves with a distinctive, leathery texture and dark green coloration. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Polypodium macaronesicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
