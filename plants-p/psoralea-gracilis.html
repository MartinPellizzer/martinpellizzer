Psoralea gracilis

Psoralea gracilis, commonly known as slender psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Psoralea gracilis is adapted to arid conditions and is generally drought-tolerant.
