Pternopetalum wangianum

Pternopetalum wangianum, commonly known as the Wangianum pteropetalum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, white to pale pink, and arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
