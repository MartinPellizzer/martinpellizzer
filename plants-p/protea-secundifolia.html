Protea secundifolia

Protea secundifolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fluffy inflorescence that resembles a cloud or a ball. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it blooms during the winter months.

The protea secundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
