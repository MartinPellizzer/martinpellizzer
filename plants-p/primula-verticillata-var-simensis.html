Primula verticillata var. simensis

Primula verticillata var. simensis, commonly known as the Simens primrose, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces white or pale pink petals with a distinctive yellow throat, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing its adaptability to high-altitude conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species among horticulturists and botanical enthusiasts.
