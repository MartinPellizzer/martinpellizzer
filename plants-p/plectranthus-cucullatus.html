Plectranthus cucullatus

Plectranthus cucullatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Plectranthus cucullatus prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor growing conditions.
