Pimenta racemosa var. hispaniolensis

Pimenta racemosa var. hispaniolensis, commonly known as the Hispaniolan allspice, is a shrub native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and economic purposes.
