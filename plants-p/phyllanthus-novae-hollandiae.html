Phyllanthus novae-hollandiae

Phyllanthus novae-hollandiae, commonly known as the Australian sandstone plant, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in full sun.

The species is also known for its ability to colonize disturbed soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
