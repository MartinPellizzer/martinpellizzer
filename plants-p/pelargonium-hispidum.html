Pelargonium hispidum

Pelargonium hispidum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Pelargonium hispidum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and container planting.
