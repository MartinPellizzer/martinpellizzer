Piper confusum var. prendanum

Piper confusum var. prendanum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and herbal use.
