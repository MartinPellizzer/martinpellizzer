Paracaryum arabicum

Paracaryum arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible dates, which are a significant agricultural product in the region. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its cultural and economic importance, it has been cultivated for thousands of years in the Middle East.
