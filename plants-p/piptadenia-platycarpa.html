Piptadenia platycarpa

Piptadenia platycarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Piptadenia* tree, is a species native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its rough, bark-like trunk and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody pods containing edible, sweet, red fruits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
