Pteridium latiusculum subsp. pinetorum

Pteridium latiusculum subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida sword fern, is a fern native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas. Its reproductive structures include sori, which are clusters of sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
