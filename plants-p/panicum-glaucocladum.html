Panicum glaucocladum

Panicum glaucocladum, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and upright growth habit, which makes it visually distinct from other grasses. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
