Piper puberulinodum

Piper puberulinodum, commonly known as the hairy pubescent pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its aromatic properties.
