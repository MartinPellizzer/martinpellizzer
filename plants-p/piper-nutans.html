Piper nutans

Piper nutans, commonly known as the drooping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain tiny, aromatic seeds.

Piper nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
