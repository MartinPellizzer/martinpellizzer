Panicum renggeri

Panicum renggeri, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Panicum renggeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
