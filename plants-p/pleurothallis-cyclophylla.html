Pleurothallis cyclophylla

Pleurothallis cyclophylla, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, spirally arranged leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique growth pattern and floral structure make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
