Phalacroderis coa

Phalacroderis coa, commonly known as the coa water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomes spread across the water's surface, forming dense mats that help stabilize the ecosystem.

The coa water lily plays a vital role in water purification and maintaining the balance of aquatic environments.
