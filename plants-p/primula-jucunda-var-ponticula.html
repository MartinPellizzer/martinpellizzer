Primula jucunda var. ponticula

Primula jucunda var. ponticula, commonly known as the Pontic primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
