Plantago pachyneura

Plantago pachyneura, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago pachyneura have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
