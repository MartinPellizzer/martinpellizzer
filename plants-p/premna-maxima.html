Premna maxima

Premna maxima, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are typically hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Premna maxima is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing benefits.
