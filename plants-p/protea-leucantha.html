Protea leucantha

Protea leucantha, commonly known as the white protea, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its resilience. Its white petals are often adorned with a central yellow or orange disk, adding to its visual appeal.

Protea leucantha is also valued for its ornamental use in horticulture and its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
