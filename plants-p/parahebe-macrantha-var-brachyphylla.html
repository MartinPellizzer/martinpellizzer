Parahebe macrantha var. brachyphylla

Parahebe macrantha var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf parahebe, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, rounded growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal and temperate climates.
