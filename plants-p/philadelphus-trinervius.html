Philadelphus trinervius

Philadelphus trinervius, commonly known as the three-veined mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

The fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.
