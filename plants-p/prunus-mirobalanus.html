Prunus mirobalanus

Prunus mirobalanus, commonly known as the Mediterranean cherry or the mirobalan cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, red or black berries that are edible when ripe. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit.

Prunus mirobalanus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
