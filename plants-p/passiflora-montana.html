Passiflora montana

Passiflora montana, commonly known as the mountain passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
