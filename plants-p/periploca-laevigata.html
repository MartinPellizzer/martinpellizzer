Periploca laevigata

Periploca laevigata, commonly known as the smooth periploca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle that releases small, hairy seeds when mature.

Periploca laevigata is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.
