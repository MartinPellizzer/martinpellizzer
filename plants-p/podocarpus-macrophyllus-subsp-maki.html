Podocarpus macrophyllus subsp. maki

Podocarpus macrophyllus subsp.

maki, commonly known as Japanese yew, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan and parts of Korea. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves arranged in flat sprays.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense hedges.

The species is also valued for its durability and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
