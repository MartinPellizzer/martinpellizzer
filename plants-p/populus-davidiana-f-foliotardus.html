Populus davidiana f. foliotardus

Populus davidiana f. foliotardus, commonly known as the late leafy poplar, is a botanical variant of the David's poplar.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, with leaves that are broader and more elongated compared to the typical species. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of China, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its growth habit includes a tall, slender trunk with a broad, rounded crown.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its unique leaf structure.
