Peperomia minuta

Peperomia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, arranged in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia minuta is known for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and requires minimal water, making it easy to care for.
