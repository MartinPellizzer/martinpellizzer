Persoonia articulata

Persoonia articulata, commonly known as the articulated persoonia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, segmented, and often spiny stems, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Persoonia articulata is adapted to arid and sandy environments, showcasing a range of morphological features that aid in water conservation.
