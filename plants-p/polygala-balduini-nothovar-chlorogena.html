Polygala balduini nothovar. chlorogena

Polygala balduini nothovar. chlorogena, commonly known as the Chlorogena variant of Balduini's polygala, is a hybrid plant characterized by its unique morphological traits.

It exhibits a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a pale green or bluish hue. The flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from pale yellow to orange. This nothovar is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
