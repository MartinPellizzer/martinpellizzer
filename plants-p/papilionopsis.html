Papilionopsis

Papilionopsis, commonly known as the "false peacock flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often colorful flower heads that resemble those of true peacock flowers. The plants typically have narrow, linear leaves and are native to southern Africa. They are often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
