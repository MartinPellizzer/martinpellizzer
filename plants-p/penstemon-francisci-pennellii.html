Penstemon francisci-pennellii

Penstemon francisci-pennellii, commonly known as the San Francisco beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pale pink to lavender. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
