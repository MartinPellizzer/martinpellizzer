Persicaria opaca

Persicaria opaca, commonly known as bluntleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, appearing in late summer. Persicaria opaca thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures.
