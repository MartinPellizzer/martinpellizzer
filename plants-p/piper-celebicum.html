Piper celebicum

Piper celebicum, commonly known as Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, yellowish fruits that turn red when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent, while the stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary value.
