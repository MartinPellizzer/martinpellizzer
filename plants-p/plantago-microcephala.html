Plantago microcephala

Plantago microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed habitats.

Due to its adaptability and medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
