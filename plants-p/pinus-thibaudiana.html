Pinus thibaudiana

Pinus thibaudiana, commonly known as the Thibaudian pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Spain.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are initially reddish-brown and mature to a dark brown, containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
