Portulaca ragonesei

Portulaca ragonesei, commonly known as the Red Portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom throughout the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Portulaca ragonesei is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
