Phalaris arundinacea var. picta

Phalaris arundinacea var. picta, commonly known as the painted reed, is a striking ornamental grass native to Europe and Asia.

It features tall, arching stems that produce fluffy, silver-white plumes, giving it a dramatic appearance in gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, adding texture and visual interest. It thrives in moist to wet conditions, making it suitable for wetlands, ponds, or bog gardens.

Its unique coloration and architectural form make it a popular choice for water feature landscapes.
