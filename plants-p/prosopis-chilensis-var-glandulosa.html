Prosopis chilensis var. glandulosa

Prosopis chilensis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Chilean mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often glandular and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by long, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing forage for wildlife.
