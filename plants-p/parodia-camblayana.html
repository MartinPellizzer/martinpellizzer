Parodia camblayana

Parodia camblayana, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical shape and bright, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines and is typically grown as a compact, ornamental plant. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for desert gardens or indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have made it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
