Pyrus vexans

Pyrus vexans, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white flowers and edible fruit. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin. The fruit is a pomes type, typically harvested in late summer to early autumn.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
