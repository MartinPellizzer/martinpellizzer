Parsonsia panniculata

Parsonsia panniculata, commonly known as the flame tree or the parsonsia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or lowland forests.

The botanical features of Parsonsia panniculata make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and public spaces.
