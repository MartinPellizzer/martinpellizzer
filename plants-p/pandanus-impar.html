Pandanus impar

Pandanus impar, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, spadix-like flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
