Pennisetum thunbergii var. galpinii

Pennisetum thunbergii var. galpinii, commonly known as purple moondust, is a striking ornamental grass native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stalks that bear dense, fluffy plumes of purple to pinkish-purple flowers, which create a dramatic visual impact in gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green, providing a striking contrast to its showy inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators and its seasonal display of color.
