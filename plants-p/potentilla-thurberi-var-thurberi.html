Potentilla thurberi var. thurberi

Potentilla thurberi var. thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, and its leaves are usually compound with five leaflets. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid environments.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and showy floral display.
