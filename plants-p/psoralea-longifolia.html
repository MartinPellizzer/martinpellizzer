Psoralea longifolia

Psoralea longifolia, commonly known as the longflower psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands. It produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
