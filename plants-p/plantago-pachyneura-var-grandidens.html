Plantago pachyneura var. grandidens

Plantago pachyneura var. grandidens, commonly known as the desert plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of narrow, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to drought conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender stalks above the foliage. Its fibrous roots help stabilize sandy soils, making it an important species in desert ecosystems.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
