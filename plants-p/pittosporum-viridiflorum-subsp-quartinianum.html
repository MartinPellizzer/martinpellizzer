Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp. quartinianum

Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp.

quartinianum, commonly known as the Quartinian Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. These flowers are followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.
