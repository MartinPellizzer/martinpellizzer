Phaseolus limensis

Phaseolus limensis, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America and is characterized by its climbing habit and elongated, edible pods. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with a velvety texture.

Phaseolus limensis is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
