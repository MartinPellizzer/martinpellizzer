Pleurothallis saurocephala

Pleurothallis saurocephala, commonly known as the "lizard's head orchid," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a unique, elongated shape that resembles a lizard's head, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with delicate sepals and petals.

Pleurothallis saurocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic and botanical interest.
