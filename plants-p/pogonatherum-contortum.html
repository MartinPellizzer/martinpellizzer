Pogonatherum contortum

Pogonatherum contortum, commonly known as twisted beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has a tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rolled or twisted, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Pogonatherum contortum is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
