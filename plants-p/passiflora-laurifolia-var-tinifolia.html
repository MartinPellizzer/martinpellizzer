Passiflora laurifolia var. tinifolia

Passiflora laurifolia var. tinifolia, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive, fragrant, five-petaled flower. The flowers are typically blue or purple with a crown of filaments, and they bloom in the summer months. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
