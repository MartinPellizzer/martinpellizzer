Pouteria robusta var. typica

Pouteria robusta var. typica, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its hard-shelled fruit, which contains edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

It plays a significant ecological role in supporting biodiversity within its native habitat.
