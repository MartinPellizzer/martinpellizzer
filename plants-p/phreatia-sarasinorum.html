Phreatia sarasinorum

Phreatia sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its pseudobulbs are clustered and bear several leaves, while the inflorescence consists of a raceme of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
