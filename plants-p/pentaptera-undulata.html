Pentaptera undulata

Pentaptera undulata, commonly known as the wavy-winged fern, is a unique vascular plant characterized by its intricate leaf structure.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features fronds with undulating margins and a distinctive, branched rachis that enhances its visual appeal. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
