Primula malacoides subsp. pseudomalacoides

Primula malacoides subsp.

pseudomalacoides, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
