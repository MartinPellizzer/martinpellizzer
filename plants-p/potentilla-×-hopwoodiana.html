Potentilla × hopwoodiana

Potentilla × hopwoodiana, commonly known as the Hopwood's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is widely cultivated in gardens across North America.
