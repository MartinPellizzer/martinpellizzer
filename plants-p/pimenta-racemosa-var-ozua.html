Pimenta racemosa var. ozua

Pimenta racemosa var. ozua, commonly known as the Cuban allspice or allspice tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spiny branches and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by dark red, fleshy fruits resembling berries. The fruit contains several seeds that are used to make the spice allspice, which has a complex flavor combining elements of cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
