Pavonia philippii

Pavonia philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's poinsettia, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. The plant is a member of the Malvaceae family, known for its showy floral displays and fibrous stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and holiday decorations.
