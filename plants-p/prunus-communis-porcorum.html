Prunus communis porcorum

Prunus communis porcorum, commonly known as the wild cherry tree, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible cherries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.
