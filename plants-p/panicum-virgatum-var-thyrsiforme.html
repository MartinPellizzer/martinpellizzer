Panicum virgatum var. thyrsiforme

Panicum virgatum var. thyrsiforme, commonly known as switchgrass with a thyrsiform growth habit, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-gray flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils and is often found in prairies and wetlands. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more prominent in the summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and wildlife habitat support.
