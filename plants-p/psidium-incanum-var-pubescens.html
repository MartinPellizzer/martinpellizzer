Psidium incanum var. pubescens

Psidium incanum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries that range in color from green to red. The berries are typically sweet and juicy, with a mild flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
