Pyrus microcarpa coronaria

Pyrus microcarpa coronaria, commonly known as the Sierra lemon or Sierra pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada, where it grows in rocky soils and open woodlands. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green fruits that are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
