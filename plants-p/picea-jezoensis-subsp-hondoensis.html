Picea jezoensis subsp. hondoensis

Picea jezoensis subsp.

hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Japanese island of Hokkaido. It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in cool, moist mountainous regions.

The tree has slender, needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged spirally along the branches. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
