Polemonium parviflorum

Polemonium parviflorum, commonly known as the meadow bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, open woodlands and meadows, reaching heights of about 1 to 3 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of sky-blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers have five petals and are arranged in a raceme, with prominent yellow stamens.

The plant is adapted to thrive in partial shade and is known for its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
