Phalaenopsis fimbriata var. sumatrana

Phalaenopsis fimbriata var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran phalaenopsis, is a tropical orchid native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom multiple times throughout the year. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and rain, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
