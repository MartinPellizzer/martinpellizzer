Polygala oxyrhynchos

Polygala oxyrhynchos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Polygala oxyrhynchos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
