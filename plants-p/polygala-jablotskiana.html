Polygala jablotskiana

Polygala jablotskiana, commonly known as the Jablotskian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its flowers are usually pink or purple, adding a delicate touch to its habitat.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
