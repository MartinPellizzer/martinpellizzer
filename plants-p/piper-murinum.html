Piper murinum

Piper murinum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and dark, aromatic berries. The fruit, called a peppercorn, contains the dried, matured seeds used as a spice. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

It is cultivated extensively for its essential oil and medicinal properties.
