Pleurothallis quadrifida

Pleurothallis quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are often short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
