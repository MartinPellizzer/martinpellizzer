Prunella asiatica f. pilosa

Prunella asiatica f. pilosa, commonly known as hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and spikes of pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping habit with square stems and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
