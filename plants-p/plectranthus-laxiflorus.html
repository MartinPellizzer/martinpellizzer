Plectranthus laxiflorus

Plectranthus laxiflorus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Plectranthus laxiflorus is often grown for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
