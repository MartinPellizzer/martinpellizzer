Polygonum robustum

Polygonum robustum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bright red flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and spread.

Its roots are reddish in color, which gives it its common name.
