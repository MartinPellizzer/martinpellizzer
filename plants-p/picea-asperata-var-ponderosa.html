Picea asperata var. ponderosa

Picea asperata var. ponderosa, commonly known as the ponderosa pine, is a tall coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles and a distinctive thick bark that becomes furrowed with age. The tree produces cones that hang downward, containing seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often forming dense forests in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
