Prunus choreiana

Prunus choreiana, commonly known as the Armenian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible cherries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
