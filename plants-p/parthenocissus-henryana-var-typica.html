Parthenocissus henryana var. typica

Parthenocissus henryana var. typica, commonly known as the Henry's ivy, is a climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Its tendrils allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it suitable for trellises or walls.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide seasonal color.
