Pityothamnus angustifolius

Pityothamnus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
