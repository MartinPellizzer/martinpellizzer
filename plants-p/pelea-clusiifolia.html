Pelea clusiifolia

Pelea clusiifolia, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating, while its bark is smooth and pale in color.

Pelea clusiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
