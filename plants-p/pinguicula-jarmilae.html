Pinguicula jarmilae

Pinguicula jarmilae, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, sticky leaves that trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in moist, acidic environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers are typically white or purple and bloom during the spring or summer.

Pinguicula jarmilae is a perennial species that requires careful cultivation to maintain its health and growth.
