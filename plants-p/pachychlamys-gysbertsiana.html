Pachychlamys gysbertsiana

Pachychlamys gysbertsiana, commonly known as the Gysberts' pachychlamys, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture.
