Pulsatilla × emiliana

Pulsatilla × emiliana, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Pulsatilla vulgaris and Pulsatilla patens, characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.

Pulsatilla × emiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
