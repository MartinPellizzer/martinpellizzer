Prunus jezohortensis

Prunus jezohortensis, commonly known as the Jezohortensis cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 3 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in local horticulture.
