Prunus jamasakura f. ascendens

Prunus jamasakura f. ascendens, commonly known as the weeping cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that form a weeping shape, giving it a dramatic and elegant appearance. The tree produces clusters of small, pale pink flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. It is a deciduous plant, shedding its leaves in the autumn.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic value and is well-suited to temperate climates.
