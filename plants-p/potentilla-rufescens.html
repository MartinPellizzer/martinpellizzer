Potentilla rufescens

Potentilla rufescens, commonly known as the red sandalbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla rufescens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes.
