Piper arreptum

Piper arreptum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery berries. Its vines can spread along the ground or climb supports, making it suitable for ornamental use in humid climates.

The plant is noted for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
