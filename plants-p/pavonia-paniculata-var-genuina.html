Pavonia paniculata var. genuina

Pavonia paniculata var. genuina, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple to violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
