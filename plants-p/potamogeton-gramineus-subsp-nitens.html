Potamogeton gramineus subsp. nitens

Potamogeton gramineus subsp.

nitens, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America. It has long, narrow leaves that are typically ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged.

This species thrives in slow-moving to still waters, such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
