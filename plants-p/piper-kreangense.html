Piper kreangense

Piper kreangense, commonly known as the Thai black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black peppercorns, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit develops into a small, dark-colored berry that contains the aromatic seeds.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
