Plantago multistachya

Plantago multistachya, commonly known as the Brazilian plantain or narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins. The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers on slender stalks, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in herbal remedies for various ailments.
