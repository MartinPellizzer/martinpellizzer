Pteris propinqua var. cumingiana

Pteris propinqua var. cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features delicate, lace-like fronds with a distinctive bipinnate structure. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
