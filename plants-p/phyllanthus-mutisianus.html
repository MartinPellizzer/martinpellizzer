Phyllanthus mutisianus

Phyllanthus mutisianus, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and the fruit is a single-seeded, fleshy berry.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
