Pithecellobium eperuetorum

Pithecellobium eperuetorum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that resemble small showers. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, edible pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The species is adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
