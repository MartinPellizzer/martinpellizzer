Pennisetum kamerunense

Pennisetum kamerunense, commonly known as the Cameroon grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Pennisetum kamerunense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
