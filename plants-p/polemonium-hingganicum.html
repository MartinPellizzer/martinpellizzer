Polemonium hingganicum

Polemonium hingganicum, commonly known as the Hinggan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to northeastern China.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus, which is part of the family Polemoniaceae. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
