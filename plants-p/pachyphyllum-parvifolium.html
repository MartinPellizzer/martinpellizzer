Pachyphyllum parvifolium

Pachyphyllum parvifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
