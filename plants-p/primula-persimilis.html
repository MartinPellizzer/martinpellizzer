Primula persimilis

Primula persimilis, commonly known as the slender primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and it prefers well-drained, moist soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
