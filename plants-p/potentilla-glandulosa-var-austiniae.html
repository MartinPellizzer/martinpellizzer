Potentilla glandulosa var. austiniae

Potentilla glandulosa var. austiniae, commonly known as Austin's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern regions of Texas.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 15 inches. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters of five. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and covered with fine, glandular hairs.

This variety is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.
