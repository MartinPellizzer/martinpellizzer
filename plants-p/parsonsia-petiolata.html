Parsonsia petiolata

Parsonsia petiolata, commonly known as the red-veined nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with contrasting red veins. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes grown in gardens, though its toxic properties require caution.
