Polygala calycina

Polygala calycina, commonly known as the pink mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has slender, branching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Polygala calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
