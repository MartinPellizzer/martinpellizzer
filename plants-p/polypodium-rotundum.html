Polypodium rotundum

Polypodium rotundum, commonly known as the round-leaf wall fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are leathery and have a rounded shape, with spore-producing structures called sori located on the underside.

Polypodium rotundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
