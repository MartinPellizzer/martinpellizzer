Primula cinerascens

Primula cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, often with a faint bluish tinge. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a grayish appearance, which helps to reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Primula cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
