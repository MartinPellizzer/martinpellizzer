Polypodium longiceps

Polypodium longiceps, commonly known as the long-headed shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brownish, hairy scales.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.
