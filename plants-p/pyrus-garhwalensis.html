Pyrus garhwalensis

Pyrus garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayan region, specifically found in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, ornamental flowers in the spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus garhwalensis is valued for its ecological significance and is often cultivated in horticultural gardens for its ornamental and culinary uses.
