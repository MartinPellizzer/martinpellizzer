Pterocephalus porphyranthus

Pterocephalus porphyranthus, commonly known as the purple-flowered pterocephalus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a bushy growth habit with woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is primarily found in the wild.
