Protea longiflora

Protea longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered around a central axis. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are pollinated by insects and birds.

Protea longiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
