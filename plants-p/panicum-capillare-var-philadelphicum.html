Panicum capillare var. philadelphicum

Panicum capillare var. philadelphicum, commonly known as Philadelphia grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce fluffy, silver-gray seed heads, which are characteristic of the panicum genus. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in disturbed soils or open woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is also sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and visual interest.
