Psoralea badocana

Psoralea badocana, commonly known as the African flatbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
