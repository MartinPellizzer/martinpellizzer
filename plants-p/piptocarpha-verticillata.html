Piptocarpha verticillata

Piptocarpha verticillata, commonly known as bluebunch wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Piptocarpha verticillata is valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in restoration ecology.
