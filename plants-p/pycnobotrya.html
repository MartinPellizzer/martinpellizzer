Pycnobotrya

Pycnobotrya, commonly known as the African star fruit or carambola, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed star-shaped fruit, which is typically yellow when ripe. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
