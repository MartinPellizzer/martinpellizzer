Potamogeton obtusifolius subf. obtusus

Potamogeton obtusifolius subf.

obtusus, commonly known as broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It has long, narrow leaves that are typically broad at the base, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in dense clusters near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow streams.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
