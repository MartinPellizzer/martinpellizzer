Piper subtropicum var. tomentellum

Piper subtropicum var. tomentellum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
