Pygeum latifolium var. tomentosum

Pygeum latifolium var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly African cherry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are densely covered in a white, woolly pubescence. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland regions.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
