Prunus myrobalana f. pissardii

Prunus myrobalana f. pissardii, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces fragrant, pale lilac flowers in clusters during late spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is resistant to many common pests and diseases.

Its compact size and showy flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
