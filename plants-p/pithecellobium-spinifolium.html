Pithecellobium spinifolium

Pithecellobium spinifolium, commonly known as the spiny pithecolobium, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with spiny tips and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
