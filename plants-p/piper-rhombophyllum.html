Piper rhombophyllum

Piper rhombophyllum, commonly known as the round-leaf pepper or the rhombophyllum pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are tiny, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
