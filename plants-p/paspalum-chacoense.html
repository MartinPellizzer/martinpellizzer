Paspalum chacoense

Paspalum chacoense, commonly known as Chaco grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in grasslands and savannas. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Paspalum chacoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
