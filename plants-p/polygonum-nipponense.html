Polygonum nipponense

Polygonum nipponense, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its stout, bamboo-like stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
