Potamogeton alpinus subvar. gracilior

Potamogeton alpinus subvar. gracilior, commonly known as the slender mountain pondweed, is a flowering aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments.

It typically grows in shallow to moderately deep water, often in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in nutrient-rich substrates and is often found in regions with cool climates.

This subvariety is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological importance in supporting aquatic life.
