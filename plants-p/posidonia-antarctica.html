Posidonia antarctica

Posidonia antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic posidonia, is a seagrass species native to the southern hemisphere.

It thrives in shallow, temperate coastal waters, particularly around Antarctica and sub-Antarctic regions. The plant forms dense underwater meadows that play a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and supporting marine biodiversity. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Posidonia antarctica is also important for carbon sequestration and serves as habitat for various marine organisms.
