Prunus × eminens

Prunus × eminens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus mandshurica and Prunus sargentii.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and turn yellow in the autumn, contributing to its seasonal beauty.
