Podolepis cupulata

Podolepis cupulata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that are often covered by a hood-like structure, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually blue or violet in color and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

Podolepis cupulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
