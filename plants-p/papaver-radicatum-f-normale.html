Papaver radicatum f. normale

Papaver radicatum f. normale, commonly known as the common poppy, is a variant of the wild poppy species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has a taproot system and grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with the seeds being used in traditional remedies and as a source of oil.
