Phyteuma orbiculare var. giganteum

Phyteuma orbiculare var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant globeflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, globe-shaped flower heads that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant features robust, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The flowers bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
