Piper gratum

Piper gratum, commonly known as the fragrant pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, peppery fruits. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface and emit a spicy scent when crushed.

The fruits, which are small and yellow when mature, are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
