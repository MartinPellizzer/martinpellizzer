Proclesia veraguensis

Proclesia veraguensis, commonly known as the Veraguas poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, hidden beneath the showy bracts.
