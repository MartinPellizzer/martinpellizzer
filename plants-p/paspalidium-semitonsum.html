Paspalidium semitonsum

Paspalidium semitonsum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Paspalidium semitonsum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
