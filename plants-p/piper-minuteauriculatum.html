Piper minuteauriculatum

Piper minuteauriculatum, commonly known as the small-ear pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
