Pelargonium castellianum

Pelargonium castellianum, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
