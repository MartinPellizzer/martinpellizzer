Piper pseudolanceolatum

Piper pseudolanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow lanceleaf pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species, and is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. Its aromatic leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices in its native regions.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
