Pyrus communis subsp. medioasiatica

Pyrus communis subsp.

medioasiatica, commonly known as the Central Asian pear, is a subspecies of the common pear native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit is typically small to medium in size, with a crisp, juicy flesh and a sweet to subacid flavor. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
