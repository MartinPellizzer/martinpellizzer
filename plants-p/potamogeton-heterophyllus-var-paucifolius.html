Potamogeton heterophyllus var. paucifolius

Potamogeton heterophyllus var. paucifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Due to its adaptability, it can sometimes become invasive in certain water bodies.
