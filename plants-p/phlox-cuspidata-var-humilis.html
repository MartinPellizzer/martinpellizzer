Phlox cuspidata var. humilis

Phlox cuspidata var. humilis, commonly known as the low phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of fragrant, pink to lavender flowers. The foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
