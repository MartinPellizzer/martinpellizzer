Primula bryoides

Primula bryoides, commonly known as the mossy primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense, mat-forming foliage that resembles moss. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Primula bryoides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
