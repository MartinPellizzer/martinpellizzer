Piper albogranulatum

Piper albogranulatum, commonly known as the white granular pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and chili peppers. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
