Pleiosmilax sandwicensis

Pleiosmilax sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian climbing fern, is a unique member of the fern family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive fronds. The plant has a thick, fibrous rhizome that allows it to anchor itself to trees and other structures. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a feathery appearance that helps it capture sunlight in the dense forest understory.

Due to its restricted range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native environment.
