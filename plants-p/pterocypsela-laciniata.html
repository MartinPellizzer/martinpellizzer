Pterocypsela laciniata

Pterocypsela laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaf cypress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of a cypress tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
