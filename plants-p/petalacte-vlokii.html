Petalacte vlokii

Petalacte vlokii, commonly known as the Vlok's milk tree, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with a milky sap that is characteristic of the milk tree family. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted for storing water in arid environments.

Petalacte vlokii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unusual appearance and drought tolerance.
