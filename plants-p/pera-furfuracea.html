Pera furfuracea

Pera furfuracea, commonly known as the hairy pera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fibrous stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
