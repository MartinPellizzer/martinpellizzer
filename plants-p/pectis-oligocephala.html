Pectis oligocephala

Pectis oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed pectis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, featuring narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access underground water.

Pectis oligocephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic features.
