Paspalum quarinii

Paspalum quarinii, commonly known as the dwarf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often tinged with purple or red, adding visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, dry climates.

Paspalum quarinii is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
