Penstemon pringlei

Penstemon pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Penstemon pringlei is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.
