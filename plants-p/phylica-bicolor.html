Phylica bicolor

Phylica bicolor, commonly known as the red-and-white phylica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of red and white bracts. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Phylica bicolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
