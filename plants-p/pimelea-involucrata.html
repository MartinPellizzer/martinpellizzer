Pimelea involucrata

Pimelea involucrata, commonly known as the yellow bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-red flowers that resemble bottlebrushes, which bloom in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in heathlands and open forests. It is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall.

Pimelea involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
