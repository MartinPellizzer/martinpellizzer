Poa persica var. diaphora

Poa persica var. diaphora, commonly known as the Persian grass or sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, soft blades that often have a silvery or bluish tinge. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
