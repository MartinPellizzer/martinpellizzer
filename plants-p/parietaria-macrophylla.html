Parietaria macrophylla

Parietaria macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved wallcress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as along stream banks or in woodland areas. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Parietaria macrophylla is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
