Pontederia martiana

Pontederia martiana, commonly known as the purple pickerelweed, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in wetland environments, including ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable wetland plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
