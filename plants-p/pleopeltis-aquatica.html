Pleopeltis aquatica

Pleopeltis aquatica, commonly known as the aquatic pleopeltis, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat.

It typically grows on submerged or partially submerged surfaces such as rocks or logs in freshwater environments. It is known for its ability to thrive in slow-moving or still water, making it a common sight in marshy areas and ponds.
