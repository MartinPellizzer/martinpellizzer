Poa nephelophila

Poa nephelophila, commonly known as the cloud grass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Poa nephelophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
