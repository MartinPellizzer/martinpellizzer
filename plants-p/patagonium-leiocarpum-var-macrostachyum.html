Patagonium leiocarpum var. macrostachyum

Patagonium leiocarpum var. macrostachyum, commonly known as the Patagonian grass, is a perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and open shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
