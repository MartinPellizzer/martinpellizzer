Pseudopegolettia

Pseudopegolettia, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its fine, grass-like appearance.

It is native to Mexico and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually pale green or brown. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Pseudopegolettia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
