Penstemon pumilus var. incanus

Penstemon pumilus var. incanus, commonly known as the silver-haired penstemon, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscapes. Its silver-gray foliage and early spring blooming period add ornamental value to the landscape.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
