Plectranthus monticola

Plectranthus monticola, commonly known as the mountain plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Plectranthus monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
