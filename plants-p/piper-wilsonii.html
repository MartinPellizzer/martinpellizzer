Piper wilsonii

Piper wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, winged fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Piper wilsonii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
