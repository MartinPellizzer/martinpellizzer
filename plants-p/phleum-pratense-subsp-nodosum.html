Phleum pratense subsp. nodosum

Phleum pratense subsp.

nodosum, commonly known as nodding timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikelets that droop downward, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along streambanks, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, greenish-brown flowers in late summer.

This subspecies is often used in forage agriculture due to its nutritional value for livestock.
