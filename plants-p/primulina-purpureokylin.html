Primulina purpureokylin

Primulina purpureokylin, commonly known as the purple-veined caladium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with vibrant purple veins against a deep green background. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it popular in ornamental gardens and as a houseplant.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
