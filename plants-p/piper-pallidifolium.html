Piper pallidifolium

Piper pallidifolium, commonly known as the pale-leaf pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a pale green coloration. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round fruits that turn from yellow to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some use in traditional medicine.
