Psidium pomiferum var. sapidissimum

Psidium pomiferum var. sapidissimum, commonly known as the sweet passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It produces large, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic rind and a sweet, tangy pulp rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is often consumed fresh, used in culinary applications, or processed into juices and supplements.
