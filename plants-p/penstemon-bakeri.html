Penstemon bakeri

Penstemon bakeri, commonly known as Baker's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.

Its adaptability to arid conditions and ornamental value contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
