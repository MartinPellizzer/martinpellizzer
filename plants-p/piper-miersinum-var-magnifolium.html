Piper miersinum var. magnifolium

Piper miersinum var. magnifolium, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a tropical species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, giving it a striking visual presence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by red, peppery fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to its vigorous growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
