Pilosella apatelia subsp. pratigena

Pilosella apatelia subsp.

pratigena, commonly known as the meadow pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow composite flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is relatively short-lived and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
