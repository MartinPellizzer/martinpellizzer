Polypodiopsis

Polypodiopsis, commonly known as the clubmoss, is a genus of small, herbaceous plants belonging to the order Microspermales.

These plants typically have slender, creeping stems that produce small, scale-like leaves. They are characterized by their spore-producing structures called sporangia, which are clustered in dense groups called strobila. Polypodiopsis species are often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.
