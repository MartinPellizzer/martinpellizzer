Polygonum volubile

Polygonum volubile, commonly known as creeping smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows rapidly and spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are prostrate or ascending.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
