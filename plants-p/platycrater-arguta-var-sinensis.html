Platycrater arguta var. sinensis

Platycrater arguta var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
