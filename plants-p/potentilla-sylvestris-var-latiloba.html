Potentilla sylvestris var. latiloba

Potentilla sylvestris var. latiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features compound leaves with five oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, thriving in well-drained, loamy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
