Potentilla theodoriana subsp. rhenana

Potentilla theodoriana subsp.

rhenana, commonly known as the Rhenish cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in partial shade.
