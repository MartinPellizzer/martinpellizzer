Prunus persica var. densa

Prunus persica var. densa, commonly known as the dense peach, is a variety of peach characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It produces small, round fruits with a thick, fuzzy skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The dense branching structure makes it suitable for espalier training and small garden spaces.
