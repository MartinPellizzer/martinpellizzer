Placodiscus pseudostipularis

Placodiscus pseudostipularis, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy surface, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
