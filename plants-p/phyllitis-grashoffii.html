Phyllitis grashoffii

Phyllitis grashoffii, commonly known as the Grashoff's haircap, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae, or sedge family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows.

Phyllitis grashoffii is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms.
