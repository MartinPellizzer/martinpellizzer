Polybotrya serratifolia

Polybotrya serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce new shoots. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with serrated margins that give it its common name.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
