Plantago incisa

Plantago incisa, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces erect flowering stems bearing small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. Its leaves are often used in herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous root system and mucilage-rich leaves.
