Piper carpapatanum

Piper carpapatanum, commonly known as the Amazonian black pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
