Phacelurus glaucus

Phacelurus glaucus, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Phacelurus glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
