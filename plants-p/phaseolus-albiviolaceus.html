Phaseolus albiviolaceus

Phaseolus albiviolaceus, commonly known as the white viola bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often a mix of green and white, and its small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, round seeds, which are typically white or pale yellow in color. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.

This species is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
