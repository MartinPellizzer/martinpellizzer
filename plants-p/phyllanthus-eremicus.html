Phyllanthus eremicus

Phyllanthus eremicus, commonly known as the desert phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, which helps reduce water loss.

Phyllanthus eremicus is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
