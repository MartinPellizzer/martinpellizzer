Pfaffia laurifolia var. denticulata

Pfaffia laurifolia var. denticulata, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
