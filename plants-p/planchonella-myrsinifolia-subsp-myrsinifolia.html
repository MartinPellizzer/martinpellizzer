Planchonella myrsinifolia subsp. myrsinifolia

Planchonella myrsinifolia subsp.

myrsinifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed planchonella, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with simple, opposite, and usually ovate to elliptic leaves.

The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly pubescent underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are somewhat similar to small apples.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
