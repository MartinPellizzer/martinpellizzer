Paraderris canarensis

Paraderris canarensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas. It produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seeds, aiding in wind pollination.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
