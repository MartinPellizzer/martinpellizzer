Prunus paramutabilis var. typica

Prunus paramutabilis var. typica, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has a compact, rounded shape with smooth, grey bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are ovate, with a smooth margin and a glossy green surface.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
