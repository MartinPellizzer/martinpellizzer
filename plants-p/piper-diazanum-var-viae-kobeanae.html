Piper diazanum var. viae-kobeanae

Piper diazanum var. viae-kobeanae, commonly known as the Kobeanae pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper diazanum species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces tiny, pale green to yellow flowers that are followed by small, round berries. These berries are typically harvested when they turn red, indicating maturity.

It is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often used in traditional cuisines.
