Persicaria laxmannii

Persicaria laxmannii, commonly known as the slender smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in late summer to early fall.

Persicaria laxmannii is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
