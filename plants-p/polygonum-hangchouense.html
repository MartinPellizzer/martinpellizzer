Polygonum hangchouense

Polygonum hangchouense, commonly known as Hangchow knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

Polygonum hangchouense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
