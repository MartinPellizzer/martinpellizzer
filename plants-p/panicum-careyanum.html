Panicum careyanum

Panicum careyanum, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of yellowish-green inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. It has small, narrow leaves and produces wind-pollinated flowers, which contribute to its role in local ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used for erosion control or as a forage plant.
