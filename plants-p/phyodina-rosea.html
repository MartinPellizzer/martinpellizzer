Phyodina rosea

Phyodina rosea, commonly known as the pink spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in a cyathium, a unique floral structure typical of the spurge family. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
