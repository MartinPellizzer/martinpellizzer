Prunus avium f. nigricans

Prunus avium f. nigricans, commonly known as the black cherry or black cherry variety, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black fruits that are typically smaller than the standard Prunus avium. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces fruit with a rich, sweet flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
