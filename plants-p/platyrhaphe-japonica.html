Platyrhaphe japonica

Platyrhaphe japonica, commonly known as Japanese flatleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has slender stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.
