Pleurothallis serrulatipetala

Pleurothallis serrulatipetala, commonly known as the serrated petal orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers. The flowers typically feature a unique combination of white and purple hues, with a distinctive serrated edge on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
