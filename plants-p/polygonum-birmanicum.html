Polygonum birmanicum

Polygonum birmanicum, commonly known as Burmese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a reddish or purple hue, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
