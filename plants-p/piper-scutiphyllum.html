Piper scutiphyllum

Piper scutiphyllum, commonly known as the golden pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish-green fruits that resemble peppers, which are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Piper scutiphyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
