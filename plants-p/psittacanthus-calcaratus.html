Psittacanthus calcaratus

Psittacanthus calcaratus, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant has dark green, leathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rock surfaces or in forest undergrowth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
