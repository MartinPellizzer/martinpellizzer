Psilocaulon glareosum

Psilocaulon glareosum, commonly known as the golden sand cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are pollinated by insects. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the sandy soil, and it often grows in colonies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
