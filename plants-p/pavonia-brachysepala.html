Pavonia brachysepala

Pavonia brachysepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled wood rose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, rose-like flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and prominent sepals. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

Pavonia brachysepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
