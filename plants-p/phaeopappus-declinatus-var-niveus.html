Phaeopappus declinatus var. niveus

Phaeopappus declinatus var. niveus, commonly known as the white-flowered sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its pale green foliage and showy white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, hairy leaves and compact growth habit make it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
