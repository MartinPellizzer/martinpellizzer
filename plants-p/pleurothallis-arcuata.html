Pleurothallis arcuata

Pleurothallis arcuata, commonly known as the curved pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and bloom during the rainy season.

Pleurothallis arcuata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
