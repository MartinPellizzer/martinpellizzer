Ponerorchis kiraishiensis var. leucantha

Ponerorchis kiraishiensis var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pale white petals and sepals that resemble the ghostly appearance of its common name. The plant is epiphytic, growing on trees and rocks in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
