Polypodium anceps

Polypodium anceps, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that often grow on rocky surfaces. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. Its fronds are typically leathery and have a distinctive, serrated margin.

Polypodium anceps is well adapted to dry, shaded environments and is often found in limestone or rocky soils.
