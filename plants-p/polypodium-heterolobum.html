Polypodium heterolobum

Polypodium heterolobum, commonly known as the variable holly fern, is a species of fern found in the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its fronds that are often divided into a variety of shapes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, which help it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

The leaves are usually evergreen, providing a consistent presence throughout the year.
