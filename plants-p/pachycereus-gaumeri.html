Pachycereus gaumeri

Pachycereus gaumeri, commonly known as the Sonoran prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Pachycereus gaumeri plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
