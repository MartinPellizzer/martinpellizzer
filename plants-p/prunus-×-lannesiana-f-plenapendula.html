Prunus × lannesiana f. plenapendula

Prunus × lannesiana f. plenapendula, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its profuse display of double blooms.

It is a hybrid resulting from the cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, showcasing ornamental value due to its dense, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features include a high degree of floral complexity, with multiple layers of petals that enhance its visual appeal.
