Pavonia malvaviscoides

Pavonia malvaviscoides, commonly known as the silky pavonia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and native plant gardens.
