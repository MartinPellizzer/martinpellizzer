Pulsatilla ambigua var. barbata

Pulsatilla ambigua var. barbata, commonly known as the barbed pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms with bright yellow stamens and a central pistil. The plant has palmately divided, hairy leaves that are typically basal and lobed. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

The barbed hairs on its stems and leaves are distinctive features that aid in its identification.
