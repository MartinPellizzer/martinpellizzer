Pipturus incanus var. tuamotensis

Pipturus incanus var. tuamotensis, commonly known as the Tuamotu piper, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes species known for their aromatic and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, fleshy berries as fruit.

This variety is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
