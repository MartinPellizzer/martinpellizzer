Petalochilus sylvicola

Petalochilus sylvicola, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers and is typically found in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Petalochilus sylvicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
