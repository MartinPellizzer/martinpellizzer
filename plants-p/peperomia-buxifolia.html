Peperomia buxifolia

Peperomia buxifolia, commonly known as the "boxleaf peperomia," is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by reddish-brown berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia buxifolia is often valued for its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
