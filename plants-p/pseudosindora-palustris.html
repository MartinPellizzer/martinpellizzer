Pseudosindora palustris

Pseudosindora palustris, commonly known as the marsh pseudosindora, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shallow water or moist soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Pseudosindora palustris plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment.
