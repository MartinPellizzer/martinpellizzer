Primula cuneifolia var. hakusanensis

Primula cuneifolia var. hakusanensis, commonly known as the Hakusan primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
