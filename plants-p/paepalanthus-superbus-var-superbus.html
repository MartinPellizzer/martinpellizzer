Paepalanthus superbus var. superbus

Paepalanthus superbus var. superbus, commonly known as the giant puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like structure. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
