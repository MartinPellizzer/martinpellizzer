Paepalanthus leucocyaneus

Paepalanthus leucocyaneus, commonly known as the white-flowered puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that bears numerous white or pale blue flowers, which are often arranged in a dense, showy cluster. The plant produces fibrous roots and has a rosette of narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
