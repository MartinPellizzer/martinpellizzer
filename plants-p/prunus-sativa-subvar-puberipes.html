Prunus sativa subvar. puberipes

Prunus sativa subvar. puberipes, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name "puberipes.". The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The hairy cherry is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
