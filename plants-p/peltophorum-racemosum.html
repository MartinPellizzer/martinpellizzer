Peltophorum racemosum

Peltophorum racemosum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous clusters of bright orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with odd pinnate arrangement, and its bark is smooth and greyish in color. The seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
