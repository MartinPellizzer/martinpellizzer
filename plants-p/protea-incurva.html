Protea incurva

Protea incurva, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a silvery-white coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Protea incurva is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
