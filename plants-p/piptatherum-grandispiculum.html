Piptatherum grandispiculum

Piptatherum grandispiculum, commonly known as the tall beakgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
