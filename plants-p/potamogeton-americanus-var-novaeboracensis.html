Potamogeton americanus var. novaeboracensis

Potamogeton americanus var. novaeboracensis, commonly known as the New York pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, which helps it maximize light absorption in water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often found near the water surface. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems.
