Pulmonaria tuberosa proles confusa

Pulmonaria tuberosa proles confusa, commonly known as the purple lungwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It features distinctive basal leaves with lobed margins and a rosy-pink to purple flower cluster that blooms in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, woodland environments and prefers partial shade. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and blooms.
