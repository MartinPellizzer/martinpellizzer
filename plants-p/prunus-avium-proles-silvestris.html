Prunus avium proles silvestris

Prunus avium proles silvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry tree, is a deciduous species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall and its showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, dark red fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its bark is smooth when young but becomes fissured with age, and it has a moderate growth rate.
