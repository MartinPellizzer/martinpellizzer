Philadelphus speciosus

Philadelphus speciosus, commonly known as the mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 6 meters and has simple, opposite, ovate leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Philadelphus speciosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its showy floral display.
