Podochrea monspessulana

Podochrea monspessulana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is known for its hard, dense wood.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
