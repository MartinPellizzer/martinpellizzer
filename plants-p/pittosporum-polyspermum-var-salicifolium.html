Pittosporum polyspermum var. salicifolium

Pittosporum polyspermum var. salicifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its fruits are red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowers.
