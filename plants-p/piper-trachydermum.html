Piper trachydermum

Piper trachydermum, commonly known as the rough-skinned pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when mature and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
