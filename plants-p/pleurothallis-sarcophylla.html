Pleurothallis sarcophylla

Pleurothallis sarcophylla, commonly known as the stone-leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a compact growth habit with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy, stone-like coating. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white, yellow, or green, and are often fragrant.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
