Pseudogynoxys benthamii

Pseudogynoxys benthamii, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically orange to red in color, with yellow central disks, and bloom in the summer months.

Pseudogynoxys benthamii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
