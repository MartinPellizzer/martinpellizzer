Potentilla valdepubens

Potentilla valdepubens, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Potentilla valdepubens is a perennial shrub that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
