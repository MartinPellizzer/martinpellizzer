Plantago brownii

Plantago brownii, commonly known as Brown's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as streambanks and meadows. The plant has broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
