Pelargonium coronopifolium

Pelargonium coronopifolium, commonly known as the Crownleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking both beauty and hardiness.
