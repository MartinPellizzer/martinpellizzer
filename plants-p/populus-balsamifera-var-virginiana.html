Populus balsamifera var. virginiana

Populus balsamifera var. virginiana, commonly known as the American balsam poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are a key feature of its reproductive biology. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability.
