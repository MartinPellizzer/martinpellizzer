Pinanga mirabilis

Pinanga mirabilis, commonly known as the golden bamboo or bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga mirabilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and air-purifying qualities.
