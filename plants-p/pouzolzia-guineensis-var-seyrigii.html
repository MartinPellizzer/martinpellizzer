Pouzolzia guineensis var. seyrigii

Pouzolzia guineensis var. seyrigii, commonly known as the African gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for treating ailments such as diarrhea and inflammation.
