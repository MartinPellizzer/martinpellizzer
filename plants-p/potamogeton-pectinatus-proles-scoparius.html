Potamogeton pectinatus proles scoparius

Potamogeton pectinatus proles scoparius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to water clarity and supporting biodiversity.
