Pyrus communis var. desvauxii

Pyrus communis var. desvauxii, commonly known as the Desvaux pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often wrinkled, and sometimes scabrous bark, which adds to its ornamental value. The leaves are typically broad and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters. This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for a range of temperate climates.
