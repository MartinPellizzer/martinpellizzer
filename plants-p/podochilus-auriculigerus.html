Podochilus auriculigerus

Podochilus auriculigerus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers with prominent green veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Podochilus auriculigerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
