Pseudolysimachion spicatum subsp. petschoricum

Pseudolysimachion spicatum subsp.

petschoricum, commonly known as the Alpine thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, typically in shades of pink or white.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a woolly texture. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
