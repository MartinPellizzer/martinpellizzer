Piper injucundum var. praecalvinervium

Piper injucundum var. praecalvinervium, commonly known as the Calvined leaf pepper, is a variant of the Piper injucundum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring prominently reduced secondary veins that give it its specific epithet "praecalvinervium.". The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its unique botanical traits, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists studying plant diversity and adaptation.
