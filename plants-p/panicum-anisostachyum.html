Panicum anisostachyum

Panicum anisostachyum, commonly known as the variable panic grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a dense cluster of spikelets at the tips. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open grasslands and disturbed areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often unequal in length, giving it the specific epithet "anisostachyum.".

This grass is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.
