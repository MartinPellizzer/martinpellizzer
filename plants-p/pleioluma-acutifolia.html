Pleioluma acutifolia

Pleioluma acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved pleioluma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, acuminate leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
