Paepalanthus anomalus

Paepalanthus anomalus, commonly known as the velvetleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
