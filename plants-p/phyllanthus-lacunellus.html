Phyllanthus lacunellus

Phyllanthus lacunellus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves with a narrow, elongated shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. Its leaves are characterized by their pinnate venation and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
