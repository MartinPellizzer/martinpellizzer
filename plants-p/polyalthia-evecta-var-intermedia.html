Polyalthia evecta var. intermedia

Polyalthia evecta var. intermedia, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy orange-red flowers that appear in clusters, often blooming throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
