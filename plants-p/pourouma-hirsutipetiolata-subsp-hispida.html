Pourouma hirsutipetiolata subsp. hispida

Pourouma hirsutipetiolata subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hairy-leaf areca, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to South America. It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces tall, clustered inflorescences with small, inconspicuous flowers. Its robust stems and adaptability to various environmental conditions make it a resilient species.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
