Poa pratensis var. pseudopratensis

Poa pratensis var. pseudopratensis, commonly known as the meadow grass or false meadow grass, is a variant of the common sheep's fescue.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
