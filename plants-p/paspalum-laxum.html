Paspalum laxum

Paspalum laxum, commonly known as the loose paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum laxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
