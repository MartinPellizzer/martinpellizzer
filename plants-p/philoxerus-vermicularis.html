Philoxerus vermicularis

Philoxerus vermicularis, commonly known as the creeping broom.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple and opposite, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.
