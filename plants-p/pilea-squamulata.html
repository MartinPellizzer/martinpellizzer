Pilea squamulata

Pilea squamulata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea squamulata prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
