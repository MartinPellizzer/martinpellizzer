Parrya fruticulosa var. runcinata

Parrya fruticulosa var. runcinata, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that are often glandular and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are an important food source for birds.

This variety is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in higher elevation environments.
