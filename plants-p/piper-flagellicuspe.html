Piper flagellicuspe

Piper flagellicuspe, commonly known as the whip-like pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on different plants, a feature common in the genus Piper.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a bright red color, often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
