Psoralea adscendens

Psoralea adscendens, commonly known as the ascending psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
