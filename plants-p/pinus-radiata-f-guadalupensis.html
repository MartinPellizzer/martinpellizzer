Pinus radiata f. guadalupensis

Pinus radiata f. guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupensis radiata, is a rare form of the Monterey pine.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth pattern, which differ slightly from the typical Pinus radiata. This variant is native to specific regions in California, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous environments. Its botanical features include needle-like leaves and cone structures that are adapted to its local climate.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic and morphological traits.
