Pyrus pompeiana aestiva

Pyrus pompeiana aestiva, commonly known as the Pompeian summer pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to the volcanic soils of Pompeii.

This plant is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The fruit is typically small, with a crisp texture and a sweet, sometimes tart flavor. It is a relic species, offering insights into the botanical diversity of the ancient Roman world.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
