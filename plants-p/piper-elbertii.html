Piper elbertii

Piper elbertii, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Elbert's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential pharmacological applications.
