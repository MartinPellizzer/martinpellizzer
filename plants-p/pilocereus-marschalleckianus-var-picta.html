Pilocereus marschalleckianus var. picta

Pilocereus marschalleckianus var. picta, commonly known as the Variegated Moon Cactus, is a hybrid cactus species that combines the spineless stem of *Hylocereus* with the colorful, segmented body of *Pilocereus*.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored stems that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
