Potamogeton densus var. laxus

Potamogeton densus var. laxus, commonly known as the dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.
