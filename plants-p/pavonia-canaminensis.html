Pavonia canaminensis

Pavonia canaminensis, commonly known as the yellow woodsorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant gardens.
