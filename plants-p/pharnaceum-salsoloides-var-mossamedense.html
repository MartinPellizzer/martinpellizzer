Pharnaceum salsoloides var. mossamedense

Pharnaceum salsoloides var. mossamedense, commonly known as the Mossamede saltbush, is a halophytic shrub adapted to saline and arid environments.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding survival in dry conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Amaranthaceae family. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep soil moisture.

It is often found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and saline soils across southern Africa.
