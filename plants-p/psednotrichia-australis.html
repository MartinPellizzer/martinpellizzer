Psednotrichia australis

Psednotrichia australis, commonly known as the Australian feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance, and it typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

Psednotrichia australis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
