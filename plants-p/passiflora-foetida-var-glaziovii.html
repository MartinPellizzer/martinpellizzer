Passiflora foetida var. glaziovii

Passiflora foetida var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are edible but often have a mild, tart flavor. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
