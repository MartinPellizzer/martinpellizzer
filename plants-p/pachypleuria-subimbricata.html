Pachypleuria subimbricata

Pachypleuria subimbricata, commonly known as the woolly pachypleura, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
