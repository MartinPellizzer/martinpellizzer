Passiflora maliformis

Passiflora maliformis, commonly known as the variable passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora maliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
