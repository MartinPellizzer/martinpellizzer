Pilosella anchusoides subsp. polakiana

Pilosella anchusoides subsp.

polakiana, commonly known as the Polakian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and hairy, with a compact growth habit. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Pilosella.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats.
