Pseudo-fumaria sibirica

Pseudo-fumaria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian false fumitory, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
