Polymnia spinosa

Polymnia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny polymnia, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that are often sparsely branched and covered in small, stiff spines. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough, textured surface.

Polymnia spinosa is typically found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.
