Pyrus tambana

Pyrus tambana, commonly known as the Tambana pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Bhutan, India, and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its deciduous habit, with simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces edible fruits that are similar to common pears.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
