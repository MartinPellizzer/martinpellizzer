Pentaschistis setifolia

Pentaschistis setifolia, commonly known as the hairy sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, often grayish foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland desert regions.

Pentaschistis setifolia is a low-growing perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
