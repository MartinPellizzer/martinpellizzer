Potamogeton tataricus

Potamogeton tataricus, commonly known as Tatarian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Eurasia.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Potamogeton tataricus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
