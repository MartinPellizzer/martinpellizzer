Primulina obtusidentata

Primulina obtusidentata, commonly known as the blunt-toothed primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a waxy surface and a bluish-green hue. The flowers, which bloom in spring, are usually pink or red and are borne on short, erect stalks.

This plant is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
