Primula erythra subsp. subansirica

Primula erythra subsp.

subansirica, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinct yellow throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
