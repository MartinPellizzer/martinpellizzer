Panicum williamsii

Panicum williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southern United States, particularly Texas, and thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and feathery, silver-green foliage. It produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening due to their aesthetic appeal.

Panicum williamsii is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
