Pseuderanthemum pubescens

Pseuderanthemum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pseuderanthemum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Pseuderanthemum pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
