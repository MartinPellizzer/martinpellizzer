Physiglochis

Physiglochis, commonly known as the ghostplant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, grass-like appearance with narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often forming dense inflorescences.
