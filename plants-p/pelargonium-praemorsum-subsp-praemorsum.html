Pelargonium praemorsum subsp. praemorsum

Pelargonium praemorsum subsp.

praemorsum, commonly known as the Cape rose or the mountain geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, pink to red flowers in late summer.

The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
