Puccinellia pauciramea

Puccinellia pauciramea, commonly known as slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low moisture and high salinity.

Puccinellia pauciramea plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
