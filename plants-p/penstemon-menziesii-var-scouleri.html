Penstemon menziesii var. scouleri

Penstemon menziesii var. scouleri, commonly known as the Pacific penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils. Its foliage consists of linear, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
