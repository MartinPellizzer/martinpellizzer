Primula jesoana var. glabra

Primula jesoana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of broadly ovate, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
