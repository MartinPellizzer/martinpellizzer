Penstemon longiflorus

Penstemon longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flower beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces showy, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon longiflorus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
