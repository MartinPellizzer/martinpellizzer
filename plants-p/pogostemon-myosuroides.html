Pogostemon myosuroides

Pogostemon myosuroides, commonly known as the creeping pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters.

Pogostemon myosuroides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
