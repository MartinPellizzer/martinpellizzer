Populus pubescens

Populus pubescens, commonly known as the downy poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers or in wetlands.

Populus pubescens is valued for its fast growth and has been used in reforestation efforts due to its ecological benefits.
