Passiflora tripartita var. azuayensis

Passiflora tripartita var. azuayensis, commonly known as the Azuay passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of colors, including blue, purple, and yellow. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed and have a velvety texture.

This species is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and nutritional value.
