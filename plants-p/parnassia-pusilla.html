Parnassia pusilla

Parnassia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf parnassia, is a small flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Parnassia pusilla is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is known for its subtle beauty and ecological importance in its habitat.
