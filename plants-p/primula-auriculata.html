Primula auriculata

Primula auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are broadly ovate with a distinctive auriculate base, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland and rock garden settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both garden use and horticultural research.
