Peplis portula var. longidentata

Peplis portula var. longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed marsh willow, is a variant of the marsh willow species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems and small, elongated leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of reproduction, and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
