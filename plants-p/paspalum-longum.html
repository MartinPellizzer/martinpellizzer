Paspalum longum

Paspalum longum, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Paspalum longum is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
