Polygala polygama f. pallida

Polygala polygama f. pallida, commonly known as the pale-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. Its flowers are often arranged in racemes and are adapted for pollination by insects.

The species is notable for its distinctive morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
