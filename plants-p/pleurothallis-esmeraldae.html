Pleurothallis esmeraldae

Pleurothallis esmeraldae, commonly known as the Emerald Lady Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, elongated stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
