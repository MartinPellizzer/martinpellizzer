Piper acutilimbum

Piper acutilimbum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are tiny, three-lobed berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
