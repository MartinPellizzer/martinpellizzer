Premna jalpaiguriana

Premna jalpaiguriana, commonly known as the Nepal premna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayan region, particularly Nepal, India, and Bhutan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, oblong fruits that contain seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
