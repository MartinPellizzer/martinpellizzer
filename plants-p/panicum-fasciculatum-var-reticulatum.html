Panicum fasciculatum var. reticulatum

Panicum fasciculatum var. reticulatum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple to pink inflorescence. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
