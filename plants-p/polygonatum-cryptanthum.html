Polygonatum cryptanthum

Polygonatum cryptanthum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it is often found in shaded, moist woodland areas. Its medicinal properties have been recognized in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its propagation in natural habitats.
