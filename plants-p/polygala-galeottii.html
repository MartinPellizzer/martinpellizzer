Polygala galeottii

Polygala galeottii, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange inflorescence that resembles a crown. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils in open woodlands and prairies. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense heads.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
