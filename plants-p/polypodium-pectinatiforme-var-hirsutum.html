Polypodium pectinatiforme var. hirsutum

Polypodium pectinatiforme var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a variant of the shield fern species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a textured appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its fronds are typically evergreen, with a deep green color and a leathery texture.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
