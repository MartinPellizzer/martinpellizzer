Pyrus sieboldii

Pyrus sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in spring. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its fruit is a small, edible pear with a sweet, juicy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Pyrus sieboldii is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various climates.
