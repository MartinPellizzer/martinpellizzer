Primula lancifolia

Primula lancifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped, often with hairy surfaces, and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky slopes. It belongs to the Primulaceae family, which includes other notable species like primroses and cowslips.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
