Poa pratensis subsp. skrjabinii

Poa pratensis subsp.

skrjabinii, commonly known as Skrjabin's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, contributing to its ornamental value in horticultural settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
