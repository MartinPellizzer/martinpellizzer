Patagonium spinosissimum

Patagonium spinosissimum, commonly known as the spiny Patagonian cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration.

Patagonium spinosissimum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
