Poterium sanguisorba f. dictyocarpum

Poterium sanguisorba f. dictyocarpum, commonly known as the red sanders or bloodwort, is a variant of the genus Poterium within the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, rusty-colored coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.

This subspecies is native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea, and thrives in moist, woodland environments.
