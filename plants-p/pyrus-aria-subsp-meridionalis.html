Pyrus aria subsp. meridionalis

Pyrus aria subsp.

meridionalis, commonly known as the southern pear. It is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of white flowers.

The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
