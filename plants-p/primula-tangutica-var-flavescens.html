Primula tangutica var. flavescens

Primula tangutica var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
