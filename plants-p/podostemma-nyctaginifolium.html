Podostemma nyctaginifolium

Podostemma nyctaginifolium, commonly known as the nightshade-leaved podostem, is a unique aquatic plant found in fast-flowing streams and rivers.

It has small, opposite leaves that are often submerged, adapted to the turbulent water environments it inhabits. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom during the day. Its roots are specialized for anchoring in rocky substrates, while its stems are thin and flexible.

Podostemma nyctaginifolium is a member of the Podostemaceae family, known for its specialized adaptations to aquatic life.
