Piper hypoglaucum

Piper hypoglaucum, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper hypoglaucum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
