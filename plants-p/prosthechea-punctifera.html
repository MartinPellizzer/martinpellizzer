Prosthechea punctifera

Prosthechea punctifera, commonly known as the spotted beard-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and linear, pointed leaves that are often covered in small, white dots. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, fragrant odor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Prosthechea punctifera is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
