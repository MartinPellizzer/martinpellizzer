Prunus serrulata f. lucifera

Prunus serrulata f. lucifera, commonly known as the Yoshino cherry, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree, characterized by its delicate, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves with finely serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often planted in gardens and along streets for its aesthetic value.

The Yoshino cherry is also a symbol of renewal and the arrival of spring in many cultural contexts.
