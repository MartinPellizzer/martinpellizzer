Pleurothallis longilabris

Pleurothallis longilabris, commonly known as the long-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized epiphytic orchid, typically found growing on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, often pendulous stems and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are characterized by elongated labellum, which is a distinguishing feature of the species.

Pleurothallis longilabris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
