Polygonum belophyllum

Polygonum belophyllum, commonly known as the swamp knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Polygonum belophyllum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
