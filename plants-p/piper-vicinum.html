Piper vicinum

Piper vicinum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their pungent aroma and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and therapeutic uses.
