Primula delavayi var. pilosa

Primula delavayi var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
