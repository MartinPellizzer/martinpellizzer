Phaseolus pauper

Phaseolus pauper, commonly known as the poor man's bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It is a herbaceous annual plant that thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it has gained attention for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
