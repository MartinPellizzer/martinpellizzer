Piper piedecuestanum

Piper piedecuestanum, commonly known as the Cauca pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits.

The fruits are often used in traditional medicine and for their mild peppery flavor.
