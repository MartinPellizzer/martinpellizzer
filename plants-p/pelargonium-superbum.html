Pelargonium superbum

Pelargonium superbum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features rounded, velvety leaves with a distinctive dark green color and prominent veins. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium superbum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
