Pleurothallis lyroglossa

Pleurothallis lyroglossa, commonly known as the curled lip orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers with a distinctive curled lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
