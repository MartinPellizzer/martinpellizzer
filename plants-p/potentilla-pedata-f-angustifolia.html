Potentilla pedata f. angustifolia

Potentilla pedata f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
