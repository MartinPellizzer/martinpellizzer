Phylica natalensis

Phylica natalensis, commonly known as Natal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical inflorescences, which resemble a bottlebrush due to their dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers are predominantly white or pink, and it blooms during the summer months.

Phylica natalensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
