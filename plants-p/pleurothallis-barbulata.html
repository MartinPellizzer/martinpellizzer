Pleurothallis barbulata

Pleurothallis barbulata, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of care.
