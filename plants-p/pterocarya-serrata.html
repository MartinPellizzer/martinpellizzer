Pterocarya serrata

Pterocarya serrata, commonly known as the serrated wingnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, winged fruits that contain a single seed. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Pterocarya serrata is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
