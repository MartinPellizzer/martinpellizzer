Piper pseudovariabile var. pseudovariabile

Piper pseudovariabile var. pseudovariabile, commonly known as the variable pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for taxonomists due to its morphological variability.
