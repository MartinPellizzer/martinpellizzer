Phyllanthus rubellus

Phyllanthus rubellus, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the genus Phyllanthus. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
