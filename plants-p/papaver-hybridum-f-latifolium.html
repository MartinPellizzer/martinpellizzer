Papaver hybridum f. latifolium

Papaver hybridum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf poppy, is a botanical variant of the opium poppy.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and showy, often brightly colored flowers. This cultivar is primarily grown for ornamental purposes rather than for its narcotic properties. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting a compact growth habit.

Its foliage and floral display make it a popular choice in gardens and floral arrangements.
