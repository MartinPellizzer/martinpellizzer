Prunus jamasakura f. nebulosa

Prunus jamasakura f. nebulosa, commonly known as the nebulous cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry.

It is characterized by its semi-double to double pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage turns a vibrant red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is a symbol of beauty and transience in Japanese culture.
