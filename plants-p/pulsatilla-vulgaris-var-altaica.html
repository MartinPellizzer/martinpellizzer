Pulsatilla vulgaris var. altaica

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. altaica, commonly known as the alpine pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem with basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and consist of several showy petals.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.
