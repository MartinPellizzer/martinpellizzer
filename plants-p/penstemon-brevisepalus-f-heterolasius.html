Penstemon brevisepalus f. heterolasius

Penstemon brevisepalus f. heterolasius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a unique variant of the Penstemon genus native to the western United States.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, tubular flowers that typically range in color from violet to pink. The leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapting to arid environments by reducing water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The foliage and flowers are both attractive, contributing to its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
