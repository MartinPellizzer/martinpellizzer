Panicum baileyi

Panicum baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each containing two to three small, spikelet-like flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
