Pterygiella tenuisecta

Pterygiella tenuisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pterygiella, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are often divided into several segments, giving it a delicate appearance. Its flowers are small and usually pale blue or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is known for its ability to tolerate drought and is often found in coastal or arid environments.
