Primula ruprechtii

Primula ruprechtii, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

Primula ruprechtii is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and well-drained soil.
