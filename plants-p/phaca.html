Phaca

Phaca, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.

Due to its rare occurrence and striking appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
