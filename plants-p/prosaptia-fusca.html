Prosaptia fusca

Prosaptia fusca, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically toxic to humans and animals. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and oval in shape. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and often clustered in groups of three.
