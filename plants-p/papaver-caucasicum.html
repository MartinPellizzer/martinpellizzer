Papaver caucasicum

Papaver caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent, saucer-shaped flower. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is part of the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy.
