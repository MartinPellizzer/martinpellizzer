Penstemon canescens f. villicaulis

Penstemon canescens f. villicaulis, commonly known as the silver-haired beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
