Panicum psilopodium var. afrum

Panicum psilopodium var. afrum, commonly known as African foxtail, is a grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense spikes of small, pale green to brownish seeds, which are often wind-dispersed. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

This species is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
