Prunus domestica vulgaris

Prunus domestica vulgaris, commonly known as the common European plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, sweet to tart fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and are followed by the characteristic plum fruits.

The species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit.
