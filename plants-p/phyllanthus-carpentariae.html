Phyllanthus carpentariae

Phyllanthus carpentariae, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and are often yellow or orange in color.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small, smooth seeds.
