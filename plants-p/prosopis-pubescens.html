Prosopis pubescens

Prosopis pubescens, commonly known as the Andean mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients.

Prosopis pubescens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in harsh climates.
