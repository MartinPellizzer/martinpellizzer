Portulaca jujuyensis

Portulaca jujuyensis, commonly known as the Jujuy portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and rocky environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the day and close at night. Its succulent leaves are fleshy and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Portulaca jujuyensis is often found in rocky soils and is a characteristic species of high-altitude ecosystems.
