Pluchea indica subsp. yemenensis

Pluchea indica subsp.

yemenensis, commonly known as Yemen sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and dark green, serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
