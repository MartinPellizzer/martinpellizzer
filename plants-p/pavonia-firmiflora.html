Pavonia firmiflora

Pavonia firmiflora, commonly known as the Florida hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to sandy or loamy soils.

Pavonia firmiflora is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
