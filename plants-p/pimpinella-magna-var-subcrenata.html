Pimpinella magna var. subcrenata

Pimpinella magna var. subcrenata, commonly known as the broad-leaved cow parsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and white, umbel-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Native to Europe, this variant is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland edges and meadows. The plant has a taproot system and can grow up to 1 meter tall.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
