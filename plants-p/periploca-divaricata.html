Periploca divaricata

Periploca divaricata, commonly known as the creeping periploca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Periploca divaricata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
