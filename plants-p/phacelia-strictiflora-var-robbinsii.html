Phacelia strictiflora var. robbinsii

Phacelia strictiflora var. robbinsii, commonly known as Robbins' phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, often blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
