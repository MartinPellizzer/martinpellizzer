Primula inflata

Primula inflata, commonly known as the inflated primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated flower buds that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which open in shades of pink, white, and yellow, bloom in early spring.

Primula inflata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
