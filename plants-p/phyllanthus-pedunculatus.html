Phyllanthus pedunculatus

Phyllanthus pedunculatus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
