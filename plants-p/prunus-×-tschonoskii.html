Prunus × tschonoskii

Prunus × tschonoskii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus × yedoensis, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, semi-double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

Prunus × tschonoskii is also valued for its role in cultural and historical contexts, particularly in Japanese gardens.
