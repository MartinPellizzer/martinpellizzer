Polygala nicaeensis var. gariodiana

Polygala nicaeensis var. gariodiana, commonly known as the Gariod's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender stems with small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, pinkish-white flowers in late spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
