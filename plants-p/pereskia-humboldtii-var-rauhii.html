Pereskia humboldtii var. rauhii

Pereskia humboldtii var. rauhii, commonly known as the Humboldt's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
