Pimelea filiformis

Pimelea filiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features slender, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically bright red or orange, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its textured foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Pimelea filiformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
