Piper portulacifolium

Piper portulacifolium, commonly known as the California pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Piper genus.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly glossy appearance.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has a peppery flavor, making it a unique addition to culinary applications.
