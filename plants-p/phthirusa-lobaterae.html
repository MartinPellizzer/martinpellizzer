Phthirusa lobaterae

Phthirusa lobaterae, commonly known as the lobate phthiriasis mite, is a species of mite belonging to the family Phthirioidea.

It is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it inhabits the roots of certain plants. The mite is characterized by its small, oval-shaped body and specialized mouthparts adapted for feeding on plant tissues. Its life cycle includes several developmental stages, with the adult female laying eggs on the host plant.

Due to its microscopic size, it is often overlooked but plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
