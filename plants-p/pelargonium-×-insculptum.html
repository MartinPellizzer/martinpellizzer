Pelargonium × insculptum

Pelargonium × insculptum, commonly known as the ridged geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that have a rugged, sculpted appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

Pelargonium × insculptum thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
