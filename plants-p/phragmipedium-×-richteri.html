Phragmipedium × richteri

Phragmipedium × richteri, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phragmipedium bicolor and Phragmipedium sanderianum.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid lowland forests of Central and South America. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs, and its leaves are typically elliptical and dark green. Its flowers are showy, with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch and a range of colors including yellow, orange, and red.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
