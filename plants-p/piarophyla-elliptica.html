Piarophyla elliptica

Piarophyla elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features elliptic, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Piarophyla elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
