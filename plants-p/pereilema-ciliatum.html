Pereilema ciliatum

Pereilema ciliatum, commonly known as the silky pereilema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
