Persea benthamiana

Persea benthamiana, commonly known as the Benthams' persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of persimmon fruit and its use in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
