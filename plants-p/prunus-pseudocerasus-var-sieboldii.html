Prunus pseudocerasus var. sieboldii

Prunus pseudocerasus var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that resemble apricots. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
