Panicum apiculatum

Panicum apiculatum, commonly known as little bluestem, is a native grass species found in the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and distinctive seed heads that turn reddish-brown in the fall. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects. It is drought-tolerant and plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Panicum apiculatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in prairie ecosystems.
