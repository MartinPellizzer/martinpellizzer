Plectranthus pentheri

Plectranthus pentheri, commonly known as the Cape fennel, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both horticultural and medicinal uses.
