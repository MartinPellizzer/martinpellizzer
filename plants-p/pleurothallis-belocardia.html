Pleurothallis belocardia

Pleurothallis belocardia, commonly known as the golden beard orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, which bloom during the wet season. Its pseudobulbs are slender and clustered, supporting the plant's overall structure.

Pleurothallis belocardia is often grown as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid in shaded, humid environments.
