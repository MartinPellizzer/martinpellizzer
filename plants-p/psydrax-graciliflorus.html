Psydrax graciliflorus

Psydrax graciliflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered psidium, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, opposite leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when ripe.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
