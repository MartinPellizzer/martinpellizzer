Papaver × strigosum var. urophyllum

Papaver × strigosum var. urophyllum, commonly known as the purple poppy, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
