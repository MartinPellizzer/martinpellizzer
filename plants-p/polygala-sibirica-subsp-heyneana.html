Polygala sibirica subsp. heyneana

Polygala sibirica subsp.

heyneana, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is often found in steppe and desert regions across Kazakhstan, Mongolia, and southern Siberia.
