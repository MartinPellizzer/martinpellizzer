Piptadenia adiantoides

Piptadenia adiantoides, commonly known as the "Brazilian angel's trumpet," is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its bark is often smooth with a distinctive aromatic scent. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
