Passiflora foetida var. gardneri

Passiflora foetida var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible but often have a strong, unpleasant odor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive, sometimes fragrant scent.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
