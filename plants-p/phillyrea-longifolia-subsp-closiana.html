Phillyrea longifolia subsp. closiana

Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

closiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
