Phaseolus linearis

Phaseolus linearis, commonly known as the slender bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 meters and has compound leaves with three leaflets. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The botanical features of Phaseolus linearis make it an important crop in regions where it is cultivated for its nutritional value.
