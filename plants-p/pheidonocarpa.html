Pheidonocarpa

Pheidonocarpa, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are upright and branching. Pheidonocarpa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable to pollinators.
