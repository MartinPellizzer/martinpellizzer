Papaver thaumasiosepalum

Papaver thaumasiosepalum, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with delicate, white, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a unique morphology, featuring a slender, leafless stem and a single, showy blossom. Its roots are adapted to grow on the bark of trees, making it an epiphytic species.

The botanical significance of this plant lies in its rarity and its role in the study of plant adaptation and conservation.
