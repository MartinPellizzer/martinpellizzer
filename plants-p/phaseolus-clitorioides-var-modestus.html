Phaseolus clitorioides var. modestus

Phaseolus clitorioides var. modestus, commonly known as the modest climbing bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its twining stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional seeds.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional agriculture and ecological systems.
