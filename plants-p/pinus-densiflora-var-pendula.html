Pinus densiflora var. pendula

Pinus densiflora var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese pine, is a variant of the Chinese pine characterized by its distinctive weeping growth habit.

This evergreen conifer features long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it visually striking in landscapes. The leaves are needle-like, typically 2 to 4 cm long, and are arranged in a dense, dark green color. It is native to eastern Asia and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its unique and elegant appearance.
