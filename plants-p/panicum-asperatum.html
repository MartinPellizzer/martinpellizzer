Panicum asperatum

Panicum asperatum, commonly known as rough panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to various regions of the United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in full sun. Its seeds are small and dry, often dispersed by wind, which aids in its spread across different habitats.

Panicum asperatum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
