Pinguicula reuteri

Pinguicula reuteri, commonly known as the sun pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It is a small, low-growing species that produces delicate, pitcher-shaped leaves used to trap and digest insects. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh habitat. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it blooms during the spring and summer seasons.

Pinguicula reuteri is valued for its unique appearance and its role in specialized ecosystems.
