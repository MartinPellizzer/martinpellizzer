Puya coerulea var. coerulea

Puya coerulea var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue and silver-colored foliage. The plant features rosettes of narrow, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in height. It produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from white to deep blue.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
