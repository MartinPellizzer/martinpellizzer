Premna reticulata

Premna reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined sycamore, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinct net-like venation. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
