Polypodium pendulum var. subsessile

Polypodium pendulum var. subsessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a variant of the common polypodium found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that are often pendulous, giving it a weeping appearance. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, thriving in humid climates. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

The subsessile variant differs from the typical form by having shorter petioles, making it more compact in structure.
