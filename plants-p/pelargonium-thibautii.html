Pelargonium thibautii

Pelargonium thibautii, commonly known as the Thibaut's geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a velvety texture and a deep green color. It blooms in spring and summer, producing clusters of bright pink or red flowers.

This plant is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
