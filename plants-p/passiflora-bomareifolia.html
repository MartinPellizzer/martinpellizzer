Passiflora bomareifolia

Passiflora bomareifolia, commonly known as the Bomarei passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with a complex structure featuring five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant's foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
