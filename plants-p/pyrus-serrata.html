Pyrus serrata

Pyrus serrata, commonly known as the serrated pear, is a species of tree native to China.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to pears in appearance. Pyrus serrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which can be used in culinary applications.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
