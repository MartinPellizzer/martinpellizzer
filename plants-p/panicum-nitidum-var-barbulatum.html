Panicum nitidum var. barbulatum

Panicum nitidum var. barbulatum, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in silky, fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
