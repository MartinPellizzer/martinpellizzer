Pleurothallis lasioglossa

Pleurothallis lasioglossa, commonly known as the velvet lip orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive violet hue. Its flowers are characterized by their short, hairy petals and a prominent, velvety lip.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
