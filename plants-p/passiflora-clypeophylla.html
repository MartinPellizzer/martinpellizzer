Passiflora clypeophylla

Passiflora clypeophylla, commonly known as the cup-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and numerous stamens. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a bright orange, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Passiflora clypeophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
