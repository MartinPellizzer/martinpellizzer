Phalaenopsis gigantea

Phalaenopsis gigantea, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, often reaching lengths of up to 12 inches.

It produces long, pendulous flower spikes that bloom multiple times throughout the year.
