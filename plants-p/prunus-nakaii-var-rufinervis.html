Prunus nakaii var. rufinervis

Prunus nakaii var. rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple veins on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
