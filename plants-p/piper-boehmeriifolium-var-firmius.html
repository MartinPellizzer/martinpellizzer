Piper boehmeriifolium var. firmius

Piper boehmeriifolium var. firmius, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its foliage contains essential oils with pungent properties, making it valuable in both horticulture and herbal practices.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal uses.
