Pseuduvaria grandifolia

Pseuduvaria grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved pseuduvaria, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and indoor cultivation.
