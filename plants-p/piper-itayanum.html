Piper itayanum

Piper itayanum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions outside its native range.
