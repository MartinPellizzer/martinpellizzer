Piper subflavirameum

Piper subflavirameum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are often followed by elliptical, three-lobed berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
