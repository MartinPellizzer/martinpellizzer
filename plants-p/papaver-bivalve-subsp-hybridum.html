Papaver bivalve subsp. hybridum

Papaver bivalve subsp.

hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a botanical species characterized by its striking, showy flowers. It is a member of the Papaveraceae family, which includes various species of poppies known for their medicinal and ornamental value.

The plant typically features large, brightly colored petals that are often red or orange in hue, with a distinctive dark central area. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and potential pharmaceutical properties.
