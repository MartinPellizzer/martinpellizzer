Pavonia aurigloba

Pavonia aurigloba, commonly known as the golden globe pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its use in both native and cultivated gardens.
