Potentilla hirta var. laeta

Potentilla hirta var. laeta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
