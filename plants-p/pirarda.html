Pirarda

Pirarda, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Urticaceae family. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.

Pirarda is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in various herbal remedies.
