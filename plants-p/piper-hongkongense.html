Piper hongkongense

Piper hongkongense, commonly known as Hong Kong pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and medicinal research.
