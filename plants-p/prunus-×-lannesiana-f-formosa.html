Prunus × lannesiana f. formosa

Prunus × lannesiana f. formosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus that is prized for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular visual display. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, resulting in a variety with enhanced floral characteristics. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

The foliage is typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to the blossoms.
