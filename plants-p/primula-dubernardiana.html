Primula dubernardiana

Primula dubernardiana, commonly known as the Dubernard's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
