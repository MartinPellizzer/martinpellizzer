Polygala obtusissima

Polygala obtusissima, commonly known as the blunt-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has slender, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
