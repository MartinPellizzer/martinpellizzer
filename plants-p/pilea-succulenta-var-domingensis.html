Pilea succulenta var. domingensis

Pilea succulenta var. domingensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features rounded, fleshy leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked in favor of its ornamental foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings adds to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
