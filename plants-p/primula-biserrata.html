Primula biserrata

Primula biserrata, commonly known as the serrated primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. It belongs to the family Primulaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
