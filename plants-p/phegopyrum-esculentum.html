Phegopyrum esculentum

Phegopyrum esculentum, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and yellow-orange flowers. The plant's tuberous roots are its most notable feature, which are rich in inulin and can be eaten as a vegetable. These tubers are drought-resistant and thrive in well-drained soils, making the plant adaptable to various growing conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental value and potential as a sustainable crop.
