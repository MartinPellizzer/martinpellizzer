Philadelphus inodorus var. carolinus

Philadelphus inodorus var. carolinus, commonly known as the Carolina Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

The shrub is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
