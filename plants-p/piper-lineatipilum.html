Piper lineatipilum

Piper lineatipilum, commonly known as the slender-haird pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are unisexual, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
