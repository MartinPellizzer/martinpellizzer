Plectranthus hylophilus

Plectranthus hylophilus, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

Plectranthus hylophilus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
