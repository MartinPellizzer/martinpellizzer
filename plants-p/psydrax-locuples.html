Psydrax locuples

Psydrax locuples, commonly known as the African rose or rosewood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by winged fruits.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its wood in traditional crafts and construction.
