Pyrrocoma carthamoides var. subsquarrosa

Pyrrocoma carthamoides var. subsquarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose pyrrocoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
