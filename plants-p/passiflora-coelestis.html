Passiflora coelestis

Passiflora coelestis, commonly known as the celestial passionflower, is a striking climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and dark green color.

Passiflora coelestis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
