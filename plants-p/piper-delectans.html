Piper delectans

Piper delectans, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. These fruits are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and culinary use.
