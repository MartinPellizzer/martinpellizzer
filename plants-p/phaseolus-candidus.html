Phaseolus candidus

Phaseolus candidus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to grow in tropical and subtropical regions.

Phaseolus candidus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
