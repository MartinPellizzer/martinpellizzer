Pilosocereus robinii

Pilosocereus robinii, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and yellow. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Pilosocereus robinii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
