Psidium stictophyllum

Psidium stictophyllum, commonly known as the red-berried guava, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
