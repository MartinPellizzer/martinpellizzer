Parnassia kotzebuei var. pumila

Parnassia kotzebuei var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf white crowfoot, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, often arranged in a cluster at the center of the rosette. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
