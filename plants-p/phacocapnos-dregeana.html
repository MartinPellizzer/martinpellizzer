Phacocapnos dregeana

Phacocapnos dregeana, commonly known as the Natal wild bean, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods and are often dispersed by wind or animals.
