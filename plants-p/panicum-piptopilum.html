Panicum piptopilum

Panicum piptopilum, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stalks and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a rough texture, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum piptopilum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
