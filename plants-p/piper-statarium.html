Piper statarium

Piper statarium, commonly known as the stately pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, a small, three-lobed berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Piper statarium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
