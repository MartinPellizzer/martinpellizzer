Pinus comonfortii var. gracilis

Pinus comonfortii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender Mexican pine, is a variant of the Mexican pine species.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more slender compared to the standard variety. It produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
