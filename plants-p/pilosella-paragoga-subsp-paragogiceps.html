Pilosella paragoga subsp. paragogiceps

Pilosella paragoga subsp.

paragogiceps, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit.

The plant features hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish to white composite flowers. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in mountainous areas.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
