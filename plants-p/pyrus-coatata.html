Pyrus coatata

Pyrus coatata, commonly known as the Mexican white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its smooth, often white or pale green bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
