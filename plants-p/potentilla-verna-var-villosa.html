Potentilla verna var. villosa

Potentilla verna var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has hairy, rough-textured leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
