Pyrus thibetica

Pyrus thibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, India, and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white or yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruits are small, sweet, and edible, often used in local cuisines.

Pyrus thibetica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
