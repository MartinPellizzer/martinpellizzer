Polygala michelii

Polygala michelii, commonly known as the Michel's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers in clusters.

The plant is often found in open woodlands and sagebrush steppe, where it plays a minor but ecologically significant role.
