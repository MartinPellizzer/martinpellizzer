Primula × bifrons

Primula × bifrons, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The hybrid nature of Primula × bifrons results from crosses between different species within the Primula genus, contributing to its genetic diversity and adaptability.
