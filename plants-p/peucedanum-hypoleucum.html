Peucedanum hypoleucum

Peucedanum hypoleucum, commonly known as the white cow parsley.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a taproot system and produces small, oval fruits known as schizocarps.

The leaves and roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
