Potamogeton × fluitans var. variifolius

Potamogeton × fluitans var. variifolius, commonly known as the variable water milfoil, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its floating leaves, which are typically narrow and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often emerging from the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Its morphology and hybrid origin make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
