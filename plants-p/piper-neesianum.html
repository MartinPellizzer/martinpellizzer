Piper neesianum

Piper neesianum, commonly known as the New Guinea pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by oval, peppery fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flavor, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
