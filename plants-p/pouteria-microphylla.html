Pouteria microphylla

Pouteria microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved spondias, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits resembling small cherries. Its fruit is typically consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary practices.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
