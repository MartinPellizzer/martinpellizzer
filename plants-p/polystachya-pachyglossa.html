Polystachya pachyglossa

Polystachya pachyglossa, commonly known as the broad-lipped spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers along its stems. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often broad and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
