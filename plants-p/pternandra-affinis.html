Pternandra affinis

Pternandra affinis, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Pternandra affinis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
