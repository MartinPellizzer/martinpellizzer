Poecilostachys gougerotiana

Poecilostachys gougerotiana, commonly known as the Gougerot's beardgrass, is a species of grass native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when young.

Poecilostachys gougerotiana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
