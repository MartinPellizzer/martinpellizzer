Pitcairnia chlorantha

Pitcairnia chlorantha, commonly known as the Emerald Pitcher, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant green, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a central water-holding structure. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its aesthetic appeal and durability.

Pitcairnia chlorantha is well-suited for indoor cultivation, thriving in bright, indirect light and moderate humidity.
