Pleurothallis acrisepala

Pleurothallis acrisepala, commonly known as the acute-angled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features slender, hairy stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, narrow lip.

It is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
