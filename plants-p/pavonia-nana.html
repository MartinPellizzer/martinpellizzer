Pavonia nana

Pavonia nana, commonly known as dwarf poodleweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically reaches a height of less than 10 inches and features small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pavonia nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
