Persea pedunculata

Persea pedunculata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
