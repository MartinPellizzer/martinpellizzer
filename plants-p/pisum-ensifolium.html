Pisum ensifolium

Pisum ensifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pea, is a species of legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and small-scale agriculture.
