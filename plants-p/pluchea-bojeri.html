Pluchea bojeri

Pluchea bojeri, commonly known as Bojer's false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and serrated leaf margins. Its small yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

Pluchea bojeri is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
