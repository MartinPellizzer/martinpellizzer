Piaranthus pilifer

Piaranthus pilifer, commonly known as the hairy piaranthus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
