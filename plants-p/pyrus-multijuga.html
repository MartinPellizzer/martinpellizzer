Pyrus multijuga

Pyrus multijuga, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Pyrus multijuga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional Japanese gardens.
