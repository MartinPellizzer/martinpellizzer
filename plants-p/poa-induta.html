Poa induta

Poa induta, commonly known as the purple grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive violet or purple coloration of its leaves and flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Poa induta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
