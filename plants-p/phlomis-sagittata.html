Phlomis sagittata

Phlomis sagittata, commonly known as arrowhead phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in dense, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered spikes of purple to pink flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive arrowhead or heart-shaped base.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
