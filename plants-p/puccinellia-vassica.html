Puccinellia vassica

Puccinellia vassica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved puccinellia, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in saline or alkaline soils. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Puccinellia vassica is valued for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
