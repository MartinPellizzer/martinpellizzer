Polygala oppositifolia var. cordata

Polygala oppositifolia var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and flowering habit.
