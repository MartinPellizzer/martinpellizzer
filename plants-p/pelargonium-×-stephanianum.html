Pelargonium × stephanianum

Pelargonium × stephanianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its adaptability, it thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in hanging baskets and containers.
