Phymatopsis integerrima

Phymatopsis integerrima, commonly known as the desert lily, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and strap-like leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
