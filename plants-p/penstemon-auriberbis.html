Penstemon auriberbis

Penstemon auriberbis, commonly known as the yellow beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its foliage consists of linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.
