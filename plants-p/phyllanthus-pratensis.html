Phyllanthus pratensis

Phyllanthus pratensis, commonly known as the common phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, simple, and often hairy. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Phyllanthus pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
