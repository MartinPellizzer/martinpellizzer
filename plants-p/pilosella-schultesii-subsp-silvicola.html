Pilosella schultesii subsp. silvicola

Pilosella schultesii subsp.

silvicola, commonly known as the forest pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.
