Penaea squamosa

Penaea squamosa, commonly known as the scaly sea anemone, is a marine cnidarian found in tropical and subtropical waters.

It has a cylindrical body with a central polyp and numerous tentacles surrounding it. The outer surface is covered in small, scale-like structures that provide protection. Its coloration can vary, often displaying vibrant hues of red, orange, or yellow.

This species plays an important role in marine ecosystems by serving as both prey and predator.
