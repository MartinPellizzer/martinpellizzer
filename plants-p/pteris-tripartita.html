Pteris tripartita

Pteris tripartita, commonly known as the three-part maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and have a distinctive tripartite structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce multiple upright fronds, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Pteris tripartita is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, indoor conditions.
