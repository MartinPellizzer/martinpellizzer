Potentilla arguta subsp. convallaria

Potentilla arguta subsp.

convallaria, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and hairy leaves that are usually arranged in groups of five. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
