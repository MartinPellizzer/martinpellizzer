Piper incitum

Piper incitum, commonly known as the pepper plant or wild pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, red berries. The plant is known for its pungent, spicy flavor, which comes from the alkaloids present in its fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Piper incitum is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.
