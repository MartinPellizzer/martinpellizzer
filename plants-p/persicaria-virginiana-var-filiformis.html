Persicaria virginiana var. filiformis

Persicaria virginiana var. filiformis, commonly known as threadleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in wet soils make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
