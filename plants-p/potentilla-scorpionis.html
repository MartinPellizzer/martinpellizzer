Potentilla scorpionis

Potentilla scorpionis, commonly known as the scorpion's potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Potentilla scorpionis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
