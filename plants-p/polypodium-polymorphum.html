Polypodium polymorphum

Polypodium polymorphum, commonly known as the variable huperzia, is a fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and possesses a distinctive, often branched structure. The fronds are typically leathery and have a rugged, serrated appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate and tropical regions.

This plant is notable for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
