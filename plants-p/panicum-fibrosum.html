Panicum fibrosum

Panicum fibrosum, commonly known as fibrous panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often fibrous in texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, Panicum fibrosum is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage crop.
