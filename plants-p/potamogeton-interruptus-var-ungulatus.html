Potamogeton interruptus var. ungulatus

Potamogeton interruptus var. ungulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
