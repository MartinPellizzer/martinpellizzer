Peperomia gucayana

Peperomia gucayana, commonly known as the Guanacaste peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often variegated in color. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

Peperomia gucayana is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
