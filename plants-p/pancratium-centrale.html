Pancratium centrale

Pancratium centrale, commonly known as the golden arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a vibrant, waxy spathe. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that are highly attractive to pollinators such as beetles and flies. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a metallic sheen.

Pancratium centrale is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a cut flower.
