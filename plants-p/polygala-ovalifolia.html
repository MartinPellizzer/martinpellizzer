Polygala ovalifolia

Polygala ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures and often unusual leaf shapes. The plant typically grows in shrubland and forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and moderate climates. Its small, delicate flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, bean-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Polygala ovalifolia is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens.
