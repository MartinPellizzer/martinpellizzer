Pteris furcans

Pteris furcans, commonly known as the holly fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds.

It features a creeping rhizome that produces upright, lance-shaped pinnae with a serrated margin. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green with a slightly glossy appearance.

Pteris furcans is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant foliage and adaptability to various indoor conditions.
