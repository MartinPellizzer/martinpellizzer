Paeonia × moutan var. rosea-semiplena

Paeonia × moutan var. rosea-semiplena, commonly known as the peony with semi-double pink flowers, is a hybrid cultivar of the peony plant.

It belongs to the芍药属 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its showy, semi-double blooms that range from pale pink to deep rose hues. The plant is a deciduous shrub with thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. It is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape.
