Penstemon glandulosus var. chelanensis

Penstemon glandulosus var. chelanensis, commonly known as the Chelanensis Beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as gravelly slopes and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often glandular, contributing to its scientific name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
