Pycnophyllum carinatum

Pycnophyllum carinatum, commonly known as the zigzag or spiny burr.

It is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae, characterized by its upright, spiny stems and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiky fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is native to South America and thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
