Pellaea glabella var. occidentalis

Pellaea glabella var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western rock fern, is a perennial fern native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The fronds are typically leathery and dark green, with a distinctive glabrous (hairless) underside. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
