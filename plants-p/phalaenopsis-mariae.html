Phalaenopsis mariae

Phalaenopsis mariae, commonly known as the Marie's moth orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, broad, and often dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, moth-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming once or twice a year. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.

Phalaenopsis mariae is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
