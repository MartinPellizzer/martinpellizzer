Phaseolus esculentus

Phaseolus esculentus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or white flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The scarlet runner bean is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
