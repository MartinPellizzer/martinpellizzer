Phyllostachys hispida

Phyllostachys hispida, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its tall, slender culms covered in short, bristly hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical, feathery seed heads.

Phyllostachys hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
