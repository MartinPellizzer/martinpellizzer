Priva mexicana

Priva mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican privet, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like lilac and jasmine. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are often aromatic, and it produces clusters of white or yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

Priva mexicana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
