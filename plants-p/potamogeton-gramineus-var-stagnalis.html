Potamogeton gramineus var. stagnalis

Potamogeton gramineus var. stagnalis, commonly known as the common pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in shallow, still waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, impacting water quality and navigation.
