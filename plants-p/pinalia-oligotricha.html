Pinalia oligotricha

Pinalia oligotricha, commonly known as the slender pinyon, is a member of the Pinaceae family, characterized by its coniferous structure.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America. The plant produces small, oval-shaped cones that mature to release winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern.

Pinalia oligotricha thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
