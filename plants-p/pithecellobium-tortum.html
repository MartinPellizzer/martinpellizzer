Pithecellobium tortum

Pithecellobium tortum, commonly known as the twisted pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features distinctive, twisted growth patterns in its stems and branches, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with pea-like petals, and it produces edible pods containing small, sweet seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
