Pleurospermum glaucescens

Pleurospermum glaucescens, commonly known as the blueweed or glaucous pleurospermum, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
