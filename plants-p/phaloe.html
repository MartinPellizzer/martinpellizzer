Phaloe

Phaloe, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pale purple or white flowers that resemble spectral forms, hence its common name. The plant has a delicate, herbaceous structure with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Phaloe thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing in dense undergrowth near rivers or in swampy areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
