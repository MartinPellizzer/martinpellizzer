Primula agleniana var. alba

Primula agleniana var. alba, commonly known as the white primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats, preferring cool, moist environments. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a dark green coloration.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
