Phlomis urodonta

Phlomis urodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in sunny habitats. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
