Pandanus tectorius var. brongniartii

Pandanus tectorius var. brongniartii, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional thatching and basket weaving. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

This species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
