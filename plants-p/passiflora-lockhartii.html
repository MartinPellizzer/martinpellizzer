Passiflora lockhartii

Passiflora lockhartii, commonly known as the Lockhart's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with crimson stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora lockhartii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
