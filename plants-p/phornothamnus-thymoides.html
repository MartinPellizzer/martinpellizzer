Phornothamnus thymoides

Phornothamnus thymoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
