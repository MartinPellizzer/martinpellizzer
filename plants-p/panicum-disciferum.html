Panicum disciferum

Panicum disciferum, commonly known as the spike grass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Panicum disciferum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
