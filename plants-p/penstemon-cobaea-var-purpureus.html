Penstemon cobaea var. purpureus

Penstemon cobaea var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple penstemon, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to both ornamental and ecological value.
