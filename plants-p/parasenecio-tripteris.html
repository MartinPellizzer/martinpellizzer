Parasenecio tripteris

Parasenecio tripteris, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
