Porophyllum lanceolatum var. glaucum

Porophyllum lanceolatum var. glaucum, commonly known as the blueweed or glaucous blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
