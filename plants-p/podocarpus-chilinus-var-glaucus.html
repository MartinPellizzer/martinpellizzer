Podocarpus chilinus var. glaucus

Podocarpus chilinus var. glaucus, commonly known as the Chilean pine or glaucous podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a distinctive bluish-white underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves and produces small, woody cones that contain edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in reforestation efforts due to its ecological benefits.
