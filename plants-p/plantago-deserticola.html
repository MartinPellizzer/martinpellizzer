Plantago deserticola

Plantago deserticola, commonly known as the desert plantain, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Plantago deserticola is well adapted to harsh desert conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
