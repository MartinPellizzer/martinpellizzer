Prismatomeris memecyloides

Prismatomeris memecyloides, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
