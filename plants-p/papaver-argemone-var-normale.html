Papaver argemone var. normale

Papaver argemone var. normale, commonly known as the common argemone, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, and it typically grows in dry, disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, rough texture. Its seeds are enclosed in a thick, fleshy capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

The plant has been used historically for its oil, which is derived from its seeds and has various traditional applications.
