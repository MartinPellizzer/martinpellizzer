Psilocaulon semilunatum

Psilocaulon semilunatum, commonly known as the moon-shaped rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, semicircular leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
