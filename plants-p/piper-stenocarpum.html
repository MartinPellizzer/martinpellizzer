Piper stenocarpum

Piper stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruited pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and compound leaves with lance-shaped lobes. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, narrow fruits that give the plant its common name.

Piper stenocarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
