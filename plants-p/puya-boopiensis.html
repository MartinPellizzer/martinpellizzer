Puya boopiensis

Puya boopiensis, commonly known as the Booping Puya, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach several meters in height. The plant features broad, fleshy leaves that form a rosette, often covered in a thick layer of white, waxy bloom. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Puya boopiensis is a long-lived perennial, with some specimens surviving for over a century.
