Palaquium polyanthum

Palaquium polyanthum, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is small, round, and typically has a yellow or reddish skin with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is rich in antioxidants.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
