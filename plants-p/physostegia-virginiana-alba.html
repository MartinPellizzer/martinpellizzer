Physostegia virginiana alba

Physostegia virginiana alba, commonly known as white dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and showy, tubular white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, narrow leaves that are opposite and lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
