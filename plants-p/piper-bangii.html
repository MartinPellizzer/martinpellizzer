Piper bangii

Piper bangii, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild betel, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into reddish berries. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper bangii make it a unique and valuable plant in both ecological and cultural contexts.
