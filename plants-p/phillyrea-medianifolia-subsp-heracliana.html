Phillyrea medianifolia subsp. heracliana

Phillyrea medianifolia subsp.

heracliana, commonly known as the Greek Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
