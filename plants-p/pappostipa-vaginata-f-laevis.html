Pappostipa vaginata f. laevis

Pappostipa vaginata f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth fringed grass, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The leaves are typically smooth and narrow, with a distinctive fringe of fine hairs along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small, two-ranked flowers.

This plant thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
