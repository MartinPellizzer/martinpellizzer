Phenianthus sempervirens

Phenianthus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen phenianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green appearance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
