Phyllanthus amarus subsp. sanyaensis

Phyllanthus amarus subsp.

sanyaensis, commonly known as the Sanya phyllanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Hainan Island in China. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
