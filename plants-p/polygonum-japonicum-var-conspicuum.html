Polygonum japonicum var. conspicuum

Polygonum japonicum var. conspicuum, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a vigorous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, red or pink flowers in late summer, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed pods. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically impactful and botanically interesting.
