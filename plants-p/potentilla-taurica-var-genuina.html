Potentilla taurica var. genuina

Potentilla taurica var. genuina, commonly known as the Caucasian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. The plant has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
