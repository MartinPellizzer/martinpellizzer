Pleioblastus hispidulus

Pleioblastus hispidulus, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense, tufted foliage that gives it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring, followed by fluffy, spherical seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton balls. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Pleioblastus hispidulus is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in natural habitats.
