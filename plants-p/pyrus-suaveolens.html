Pyrus suaveolens

Pyrus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet cherry or sour cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet or sour fruits depending on the variety, which are encased in a thin, hairy skin. Pyrus suaveolens is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blossoms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice in temperate climates.
