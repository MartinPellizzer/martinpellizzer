Polygonum virginianum

Polygonum virginianum, commonly known as smartweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often tinged with red, especially near the tips. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive reddish or purple underside.

Smartweed is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils, wetlands, and along roadsides.
