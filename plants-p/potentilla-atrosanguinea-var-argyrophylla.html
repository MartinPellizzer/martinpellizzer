Potentilla atrosanguinea var. argyrophylla

Potentilla atrosanguinea var. argyrophylla, commonly known as silver-leaved potentilla, is a shrub characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
