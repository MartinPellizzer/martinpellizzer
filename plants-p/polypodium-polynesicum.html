Polypodium polynesicum

Polypodium polynesicum, commonly known as the Tahitian shield fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clustered fronds that are usually dark green and leathery.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
