Philibertia grandiflora f. albida

Philibertia grandiflora f. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered philibertia, is a variant of the Philibertia grandiflora species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, large white flowers that bloom in spring, contrasting with its green, serrated foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and hairy stems, which help in reducing water loss.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
