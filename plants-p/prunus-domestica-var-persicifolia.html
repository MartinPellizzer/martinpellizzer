Prunus domestica var. persicifolia

Prunus domestica var. persicifolia, commonly known as the leafy cherry or leafy plum, is a variant of the common European plum.

It is characterized by its distinctive broad, lobed leaves that resemble those of the peach tree. The plant produces small, sweet, and often edible fruits similar to regular plums. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

This variety is well-suited for temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
