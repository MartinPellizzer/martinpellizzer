Pogostemon lythroides

Pogostemon lythroides, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Pogostemon lythroides is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
