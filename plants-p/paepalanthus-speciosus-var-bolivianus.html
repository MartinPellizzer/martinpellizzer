Paepalanthus speciosus var. bolivianus

Paepalanthus speciosus var. bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian star cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized plant in horticultural collections.
