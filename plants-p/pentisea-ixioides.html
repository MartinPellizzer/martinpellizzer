Pentisea ixioides

Pentisea ixioides, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces blue or violet flowers with yellow throats, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are prostrate and can root at the nodes.

Pentisea ixioides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.
