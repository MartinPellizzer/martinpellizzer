Polygonum acuminatum var. weddellii

Polygonum acuminatum var. weddellii, commonly known as Weddell's smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and they are usually covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
