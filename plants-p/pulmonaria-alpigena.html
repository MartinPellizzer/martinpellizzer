Pulmonaria alpigena

Pulmonaria alpigena, commonly known as the alpine lungwort, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of velvety, spotted leaves that are typically gray-green with contrasting purple or reddish markings. The plant produces tall, upright stems bearing clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Pulmonaria alpigena is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
