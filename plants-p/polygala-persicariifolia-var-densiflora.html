Polygala persicariifolia var. densiflora

Polygala persicariifolia var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by insects.

This variant is noted for its dense inflorescences, which give it its common name.
