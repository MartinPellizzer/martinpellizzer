Potamogeton pectinatus var. tenuis

Potamogeton pectinatus var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, with a smooth, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in dense clusters near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its ecosystem.
