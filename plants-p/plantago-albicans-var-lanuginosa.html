Plantago albicans var. lanuginosa

Plantago albicans var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly plantain, is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, soft pubescence.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are borne on slender stalks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is valued for its adaptability and ecological benefits.
