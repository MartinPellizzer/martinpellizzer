Paspalum rufum

Paspalum rufum, commonly known as red paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are dispersed by water, aiding in its spread across wetland habitats.

Paspalum rufum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
