Paspalum lagascae

Paspalum lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasce paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in open grasslands and along coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to poor soil conditions and can tolerate drought, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
