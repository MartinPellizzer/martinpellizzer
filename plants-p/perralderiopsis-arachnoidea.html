Perralderiopsis arachnoidea

Perralderiopsis arachnoidea, commonly known as the spiderweb liverwort, is a small, non-vascular plant belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent thalloid structure that resembles a spiderweb, hence its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having a flattened, ribbon-like body called a thallus.

The plant reproduces via spores, which are produced in small, cup-like structures called sporophytes.
