Plantago tomentosa subsp. spathulifolia

Plantago tomentosa subsp.

spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are covered in dense, soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on upright stems. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
