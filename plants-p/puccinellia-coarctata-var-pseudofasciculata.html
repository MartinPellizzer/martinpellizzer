Puccinellia coarctata var. pseudofasciculata

Puccinellia coarctata var. pseudofasciculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It has slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is typically found in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats. Its root system helps stabilize soil in these challenging environments.

It plays an important role in ecological succession and provides forage for wildlife.
