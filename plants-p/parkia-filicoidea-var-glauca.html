Parkia filicoidea var. glauca

Parkia filicoidea var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous fan-pod tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, feathery leaflets and small, pod-like fruits that turn yellow when mature. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
