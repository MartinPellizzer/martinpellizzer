Pinus tabuliformis var. henryi

Pinus tabuliformis var. henryi, commonly known as the Henry's Chinese pine, is a variant of the Chinese pine native to northern China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its distinctive bark, which is thick and fissured, giving it a rugged appearance. The needles are typically arranged in fascicles of five, and they are a deep green in color. The cones are woody and often persist on the tree for several years.

This plant is well adapted to dry and rocky soils, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.
