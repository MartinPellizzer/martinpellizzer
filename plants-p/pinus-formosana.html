Pinus formosana

Pinus formosana, commonly known as the Formosa red pine, is a species of pine native to Taiwan.

It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer that typically grows in mountainous regions. The tree has slender, straight trunks and needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in pairs. Its cones are woody and often persist on the tree for several years.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in reforestation efforts.
