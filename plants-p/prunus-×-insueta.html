Prunus × insueta

Prunus × insueta, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, single or double pink to white flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in taste to apricots but are often consumed fresh or used in jams. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The species is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate urban environments.
