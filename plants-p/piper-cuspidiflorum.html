Piper cuspidiflorum

Piper cuspidiflorum, commonly known as the spicate pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
