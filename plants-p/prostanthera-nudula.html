Prostanthera nudula

Prostanthera nudula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes.

Prostanthera nudula is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
