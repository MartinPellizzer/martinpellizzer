Polygonum alopecuroides subsp. subauriculatum

Polygonum alopecuroides subsp.

subauriculatum, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive auriculate base that gives the subspecies its name. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and has become an invasive species in many regions outside its native range.
