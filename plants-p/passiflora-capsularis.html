Passiflora capsularis

Passiflora capsularis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a unique reproductive system. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The genus Passiflora is known for its complex floral morphology and ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
