Polygonum triandrous

Polygonum triandrous, commonly known as the red root or red sanders, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, reddish flowers. It produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Polygonum triandrous is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
