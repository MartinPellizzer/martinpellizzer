Pimelea orthocephala

Pimelea orthocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It features distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink to red in color, resembling a bottlebrush. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Pimelea orthocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
