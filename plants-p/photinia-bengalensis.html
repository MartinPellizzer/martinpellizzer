Photinia bengalensis

Photinia bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, glossy green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Photinia bengalensis is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in certain regions.
