Piper langsdorffianum

Piper langsdorffianum, commonly known as the Langsdorff's pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often lobed or deeply divided. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, edible berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
