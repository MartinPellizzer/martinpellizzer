Pleiacanthus

Pleiacanthus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, spiraling stems above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing shade and habitat for aquatic life. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with a distinctive fragrance that attracts pollinators.

The roots of Pleiacanthus are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in wetland environments.
