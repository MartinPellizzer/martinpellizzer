Primula forbesii subsp. multicaulis

Primula forbesii subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high-altitude regions of China. It features a basal rosette of oval, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
