Papaver kerneri

Papaver kerneri, commonly known as the Kashmiri poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayas.

It produces striking, vibrant red or orange flowers with a central black stigma, typical of the Papaver genus. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to its natural habitat.
