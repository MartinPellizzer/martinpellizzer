Polygonum vulgare

Polygonum vulgare, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, triangular leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that develop into three-lobed, reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wetlands.

Polygonum vulgare is a hardy plant that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
