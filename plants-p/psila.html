Psila

Psila, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a revered aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, twisting stalks rising from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing habitat for various aquatic life. Psila is known for its ability to thrive in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its botanical features make it a symbol of purity and spiritual significance in many cultures.
