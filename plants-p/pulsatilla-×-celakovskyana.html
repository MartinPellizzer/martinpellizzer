Pulsatilla × celakovskyana

Pulsatilla × celakovskyana, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Pulsatilla patens and Pulsatilla vulgaris, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is native to Central Europe and is often found in meadows and rocky soils. It produces showy, pinkish-yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure, blooming in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in horticulture and botanical studies.
