Pyrus ronvilla

Pyrus ronvilla, commonly known as the Ronvilla pear, is a species of pear native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits that are typically not suitable for eating but are prized for their aesthetic appeal. The plant has a compact growth habit and features glossy, dark green leaves with a pleasant fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pyrus ronvilla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
