Poa pratensis proles subcaerulea

Poa pratensis proles subcaerulea, commonly known as blue meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which is due to the high concentration of chlorophyll and a unique pigmentation in its leaves. This grass thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
