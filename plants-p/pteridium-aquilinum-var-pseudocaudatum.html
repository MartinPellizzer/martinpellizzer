Pteridium aquilinum var. pseudocaudatum

Pteridium aquilinum var. pseudocaudatum, commonly known as the false sword fern, is a variant of the bracken fern that thrives in temperate regions.

It is a perennial plant with a robust rhizome system that allows it to spread aggressively through vegetative propagation. The plant features feather-like fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a dark green, glossy appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can often be found in woodlands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and resilience, making it a common sight in various habitats across its range.
