Parsonsia taomensis

Parsonsia taomensis, commonly known as the Taitung flameflower, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds.

Parsonsia taomensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
