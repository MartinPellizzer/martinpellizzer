Piper tabernillanum

Piper tabernillanum, commonly known as the African black pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.
