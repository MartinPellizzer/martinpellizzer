Plagioscyphus

Plagioscyphus, commonly known as the false orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often showy flowers that resemble those of true orchids. Plagioscyphus species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments. They have tuberous roots and produce small, green leaves that are often clustered at the base.
