Philodendron cardiophyllum

Philodendron cardiophyllum, commonly known as the Heartleaf Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a vibrant green with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible but often avoided due to their mild toxicity. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
