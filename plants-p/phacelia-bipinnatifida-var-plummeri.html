Phacelia bipinnatifida var. plummeri

Phacelia bipinnatifida var. plummeri, commonly known as the plummer's phacelia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnatifid leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender to blue-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
