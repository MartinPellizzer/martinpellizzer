Prenanthes suberosa

Prenanthes suberosa, commonly known as the roughleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a textured appearance.

Prenanthes suberosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
