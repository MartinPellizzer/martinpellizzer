Potentilla salisburgensis var. elatior

Potentilla salisburgensis var. elatior, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, and the stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
