Potentilla kotschyana

Potentilla kotschyana, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Kotschyan cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven leaflets. Potentilla kotschyana thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
