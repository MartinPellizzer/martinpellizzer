Paspalum ceresioides

Paspalum ceresioides, commonly known as the silver paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its low maintenance requirements and ability to form dense mats make it suitable for erosion control and groundcover in various climates.
