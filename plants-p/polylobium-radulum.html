Polylobium radulum

Polylobium radulum, commonly known as the golden star, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a unique and elegant appearance. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
