Pleurothallis trimeropetala

Pleurothallis trimeropetala, commonly known as the three-lobed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, with three prominent lobes on each petal. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique floral structure and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
