Pulicaria canariensis

Pulicaria canariensis, commonly known as Canaryweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local flora's biodiversity.
