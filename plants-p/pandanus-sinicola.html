Pandanus sinicola

Pandanus sinicola, commonly known as the Chinese pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing near coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
