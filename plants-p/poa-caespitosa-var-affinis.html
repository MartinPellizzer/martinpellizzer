Poa caespitosa var. affinis

Poa caespitosa var. affinis, commonly known as the tufted bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically forms low-growing sods and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in turfgrass mixes.
