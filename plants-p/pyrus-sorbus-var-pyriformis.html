Pyrus sorbus var. pyriformis

Pyrus sorbus var. pyriformis, commonly known as the pear-swillow or false whitebeam, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Pyrus and Sorbus genera.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are typically small, berry-like drupes that ripen in late summer.

The hybrid nature of this plant makes it notable for its adaptability and ornamental value in horticulture.
