Patrisa

Patrisa, commonly known as the "wild rose of the Mediterranean," is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, thorny stems and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces oval, red or orange fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs.

Patrisa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
