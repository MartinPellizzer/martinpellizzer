Paphiopedilum concolor var. longipetalum

Paphiopedilum concolor var. longipetalum, commonly known as the long-petaled yellow lady's slipper, is a unique orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is distinguished by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms during the spring and summer months.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
