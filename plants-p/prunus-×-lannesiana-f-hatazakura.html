Prunus × lannesiana f. hatazakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. hatazakura, commonly known as the Hatazakura cherry, is a unique botanical variant characterized by its double blossoms.

It is a natural hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus incisa, resulting in a distinctive floral morphology. The plant is native to Japan and is celebrated for its ornamental value, particularly during the spring blossom season. Its flowers are fully double, with petals that are often pink or white, creating a striking visual display.

The Hatazakura is typically cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
