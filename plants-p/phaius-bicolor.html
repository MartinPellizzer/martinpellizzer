Phaius bicolor

Phaius bicolor, commonly known as the pink-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves and striking pink and white flowers with distinctive veining. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Phaius bicolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
