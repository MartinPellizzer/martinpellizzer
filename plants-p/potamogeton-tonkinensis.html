Potamogeton tonkinensis

Potamogeton tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Its presence is often indicative of good water quality and stable aquatic ecosystems.
