Pappobolus sagasteguii

Pappobolus sagasteguii, commonly known as the Andean pappobolus, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces distinctive pappus structures, which are feathery bristles that aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
