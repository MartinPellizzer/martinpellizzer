Polygonum allocarpum

Polygonum allocarpum, commonly known as the redstem smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish midrib, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of wet soil conditions.
