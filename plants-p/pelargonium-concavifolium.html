Pelargonium concavifolium

Pelargonium concavifolium, commonly known as the concave-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium concavifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and containers.
