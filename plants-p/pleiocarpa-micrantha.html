Pleiocarpa micrantha

Pleiocarpa micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited pleiocarpa, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for cultivation in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
