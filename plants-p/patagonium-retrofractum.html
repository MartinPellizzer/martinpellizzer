Patagonium retrofractum

Patagonium retrofractum, commonly known as the fractured southern fern, is a rare and unique plant found exclusively in the high-altitude regions of Patagonia.

It is characterized by its intricate, fractal-like leaf patterns that resemble branching structures, a trait that has fascinated botanists. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand extreme weather conditions. Its reproductive system involves specialized spore-producing structures called sorocarps, which are embedded within the foliage.

Due to its limited habitat and fragile ecosystem, Patagonium retrofractum is considered a vulnerable species.
