Polygala cornuta var. fishiae

Polygala cornuta var. fishiae, commonly known as the horned milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
