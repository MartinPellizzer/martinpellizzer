Prunus × silvestris subvar. leiocarpa

Prunus × silvestris subvar. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-cherry or wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters, featuring slender branches and elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained soils in temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
