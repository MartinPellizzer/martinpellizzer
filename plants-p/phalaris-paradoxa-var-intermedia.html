Phalaris paradoxa var. intermedia

Phalaris paradoxa var. intermedia, commonly known as the golden spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive golden-colored inflorescences that resemble spikes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open fields and along coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
