Phlox carolina subsp. angusta

Phlox carolina subsp.

angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf false phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
