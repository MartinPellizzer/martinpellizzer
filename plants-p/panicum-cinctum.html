Panicum cinctum

Panicum cinctum, commonly known as the silver millet or velvet grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Panicum cinctum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
