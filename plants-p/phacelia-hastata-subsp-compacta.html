Phacelia hastata subsp. compacta

Phacelia hastata subsp.

compacta, commonly known as the compact phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
