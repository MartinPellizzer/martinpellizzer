Perezia lepidopoda

Perezia lepidopoda, commonly known as the velvetleaf perezia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions with cool temperatures. Perezia lepidopoda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
