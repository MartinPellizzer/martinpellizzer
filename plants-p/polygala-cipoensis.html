Polygala cipoensis

Polygala cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branching.

Polygala cipoensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
