Philodendron seguine subsp. lingua-bovis

Philodendron seguine subsp.

lingua-bovis, commonly known as the "bull's tongue" philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America. It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf shape and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
