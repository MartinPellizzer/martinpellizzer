Pleomele heudelotii

Pleomele heudelotii, commonly known as the silver pleomele, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
