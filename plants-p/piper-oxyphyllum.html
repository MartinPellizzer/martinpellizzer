Piper oxyphyllum

Piper oxyphyllum, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries. The fruit contains the seeds used to produce white pepper, which is obtained by retting the berries and removing the outer layers.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
