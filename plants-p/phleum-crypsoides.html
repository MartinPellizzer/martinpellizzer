Phleum crypsoides

Phleum crypsoides, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or silver. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers appear in dense spikes during the summer months.

Phleum crypsoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
