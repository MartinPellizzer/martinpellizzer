Polygala rosmarinifolia var. puberula

Polygala rosmarinifolia var. puberula, commonly known as the woolly milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
