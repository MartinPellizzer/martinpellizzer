Photinia taiwanensis

Photinia taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and seasonal color changes.

Photinia taiwanensis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
