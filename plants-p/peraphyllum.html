Peraphyllum

Peraphyllum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and specialized adaptations for growing in rocky or crevice environments. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, often fragrant flowers. Peraphyllum species are native to various regions in the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America.
