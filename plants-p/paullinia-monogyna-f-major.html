Paullinia monogyna f. major

Paullinia monogyna f. major, commonly known as the large-leafed poinsettia, is a cultivar of the poinsettia plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, dark green leaves and vibrant red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its striking ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often cultivated as a decorative plant, especially during the holiday season.
