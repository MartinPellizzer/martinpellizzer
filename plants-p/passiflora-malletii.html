Passiflora malletii

Passiflora malletii, commonly known as the Mallet's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of blue, violet, and white petals, along with prominent filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora malletii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
