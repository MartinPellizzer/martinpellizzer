Pinalia sanguinea

Pinalia sanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered pinalia, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking crimson petals and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
