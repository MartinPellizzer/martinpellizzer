Pyrus aria var. cretica

Pyrus aria var. cretica, commonly known as the Cretan pear, is a rare variant of the pear species native to the island of Crete.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits with a smooth, pale yellow skin. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers a Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

The Cretan pear is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Cretan agriculture.
