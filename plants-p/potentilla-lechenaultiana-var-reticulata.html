Potentilla lechenaultiana var. reticulata

Potentilla lechenaultiana var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are often covered in a fine, reticulate pattern, giving it its scientific name. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in suitable climates.
