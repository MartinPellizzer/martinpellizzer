Prunus serrulata f. multipetala

Prunus serrulata f. multipetala, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom with multiple petals, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its delicate, double-flowered blooms that appear in early spring, adding a striking visual display. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and the tree has a rounded, compact growth habit.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is a symbol of ephemeral beauty in Japanese culture.
