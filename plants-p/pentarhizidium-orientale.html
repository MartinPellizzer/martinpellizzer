Pentarhizidium orientale

Pentarhizidium orientale, commonly known as the oriental pentarhizidium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and features small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
