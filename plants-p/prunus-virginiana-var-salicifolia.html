Prunus virginiana var. salicifolia

Prunus virginiana var. salicifolia, commonly known as the white chokecherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries that are popular with birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
