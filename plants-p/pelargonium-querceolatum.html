Pelargonium querceolatum

Pelargonium querceolatum, commonly known as the oak-leaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium querceolatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
