Pleurothallis hawkesii

Pleurothallis hawkesii, commonly known as the Hawkes' orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with a slight pinkish tinge. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and bloom during the warmer months of the year.

Pleurothallis hawkesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
