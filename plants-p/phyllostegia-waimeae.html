Phyllostegia waimeae

Phyllostegia waimeae, commonly known as the Waimea ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal forests and mesic habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
