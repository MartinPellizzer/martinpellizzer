Phaseolus diffusus

Phaseolus diffusus, commonly known as the evergreen bean or creeping pole bean, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Phaseolus diffusus is valued for its adaptability and is used in both ornamental and agricultural contexts.
