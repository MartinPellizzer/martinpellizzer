Philodendron glanduliferum

Philodendron glanduliferum, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
