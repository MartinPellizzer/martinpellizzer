Piper subpenninerve

Piper subpenninerve, commonly known as the slender-veined pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its finely veined, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
