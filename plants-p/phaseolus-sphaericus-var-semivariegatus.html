Phaseolus sphaericus var. semivariegatus

Phaseolus sphaericus var. semivariegatus, commonly known as the variegated scarlet runner bean, is a cultivar characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns and vibrant red pods.

This plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to the Americas. It typically grows as an annual vine, reaching lengths of up to several meters, with trifoliate leaves that exhibit a striking variegation of green and white. The flowers are usually pale yellow, and the pods mature to a bright red color, making it a visually appealing ornamental and edible plant.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
