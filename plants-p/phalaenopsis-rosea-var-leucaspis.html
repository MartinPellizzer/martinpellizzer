Phalaenopsis rosea var. leucaspis

Phalaenopsis rosea var. leucaspis, commonly known as the white-lipped phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid characterized by its elegant, elongated flower spikes and showy, white to pale pink blooms.

This variety is prized for its long-lasting flowers, which can remain open for several months under proper care. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green, often with a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for both indoor cultivation and floral arrangements.
