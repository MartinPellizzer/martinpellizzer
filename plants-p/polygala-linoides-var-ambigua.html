Polygala linoides var. ambigua

Polygala linoides var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, and it produces small, oblong seed pods that split open to release seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
