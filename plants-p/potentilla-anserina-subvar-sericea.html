Potentilla anserina subvar. sericea

Potentilla anserina subvar. sericea, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
