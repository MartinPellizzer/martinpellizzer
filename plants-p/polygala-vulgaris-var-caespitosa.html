Polygala vulgaris var. caespitosa

Polygala vulgaris var. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
