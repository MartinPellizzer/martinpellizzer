Prunus × subhirtella var. ascendens

Prunus × subhirtella var. ascendens, commonly known as the Shirofune cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and is prized for its ornamental value, particularly its profuse display of white flowers in spring. The plant produces small, dark red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The Shirofune cherry is also valued for its use in traditional Japanese landscape design and cultural symbolism.
