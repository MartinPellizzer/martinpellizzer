Plantago triquetra

Plantago triquetra, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, somewhat hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive three-lobed base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
