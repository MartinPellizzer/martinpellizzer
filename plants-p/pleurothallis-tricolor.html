Pleurothallis tricolor

Pleurothallis tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display three distinct color zones. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for both horticulturists and conservationists.
