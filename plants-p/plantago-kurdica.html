Plantago kurdica

Plantago kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Plantago kurdica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
