Parentucellia latifolia subsp. flaviflora

Parentucellia latifolia subsp.

flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered parentucellia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, coastal climates.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
