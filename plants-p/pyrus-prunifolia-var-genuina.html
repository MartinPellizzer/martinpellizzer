Pyrus prunifolia var. genuina

Pyrus prunifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn. The fruit is a small, edible apple-like pome that ripens in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
