Pygeum griseum

Pygeum griseum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to hard, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
