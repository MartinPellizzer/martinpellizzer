Poa dolichophylla

Poa dolichophylla, commonly known as the long-leaf bluegrass, is a species of grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Poa dolichophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
