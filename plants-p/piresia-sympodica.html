Piresia sympodica

Piresia sympodica, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months.

Piresia sympodica is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
