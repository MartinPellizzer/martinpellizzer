Phillyrea angustifolia subsp. media

Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

media, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. Phillyrea angustifolia subsp.

media is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
