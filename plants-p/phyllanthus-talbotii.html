Phyllanthus talbotii

Phyllanthus talbotii, commonly known as Talbot's spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its milky sap and is part of the Euphorbiaceae family.
