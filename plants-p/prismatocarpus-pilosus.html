Prismatocarpus pilosus

Prismatocarpus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ridged, and often hairy leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
