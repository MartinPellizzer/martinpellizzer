Piper sclerophloeum var. scandens

Piper sclerophloeum var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a vine species native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.
