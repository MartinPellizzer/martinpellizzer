Protea glaucophylla

Protea glaucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of multiple bracts and numerous small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its silver-gray foliage is a distinguishing feature, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Protea glaucophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
