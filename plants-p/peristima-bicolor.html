Peristima bicolor

Peristima bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually green on the upper surface and pale or white on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together. Its berries are typically red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Peristima bicolor is found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
