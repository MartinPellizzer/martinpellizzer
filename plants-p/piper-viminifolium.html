Piper viminifolium

Piper viminifolium, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb or trail along structures.
