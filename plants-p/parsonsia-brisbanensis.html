Parsonsia brisbanensis

Parsonsia brisbanensis, commonly known as the Brisbane glory flower, is a striking tropical shrub native to Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes milkweeds and related species known for their milky sap. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Parsonsia brisbanensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
