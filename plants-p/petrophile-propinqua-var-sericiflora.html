Petrophile propinqua var. sericiflora

Petrophile propinqua var. sericiflora, commonly known as the silky hair-leaf banksia, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, nutrient-poor environments. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, and they are visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects.
