Protea janssensii

Protea janssensii, commonly known as the Janssens protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the Western Cape region. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted by the nectar.

Protea janssensii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
