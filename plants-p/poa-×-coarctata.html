Poa × coarctata

Poa × coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a hybrid grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing turf, making it suitable for ornamental and landscaping purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and prairies.

The flowers are small and spike-like, with three to six spikelets per branch, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
