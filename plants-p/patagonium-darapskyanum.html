Patagonium darapskyanum

Patagonium darapskyanum, commonly known as the Andean flamebush, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a woody stem and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Andean mountains.

Patagonium darapskyanum plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
