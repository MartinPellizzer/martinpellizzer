Periploca calophylla

Periploca calophylla, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in axillary or terminal panicles.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
