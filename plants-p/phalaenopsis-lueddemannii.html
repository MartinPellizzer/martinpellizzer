Phalaenopsis lueddemannii

Phalaenopsis lueddemannii, commonly known as the Lueddemann's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without being parasitic. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. It is known for its ability to bloom repeatedly throughout the year under proper care.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegance and longevity.
