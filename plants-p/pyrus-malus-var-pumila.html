Pyrus malus var. pumila

Pyrus malus var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf apple, is a compact variant of the apple tree.

It is characterized by its small stature, typically reaching only 1-2 meters in height. The plant produces small, round apples that are often tart and used for culinary purposes. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and small-scale fruit cultivation.
