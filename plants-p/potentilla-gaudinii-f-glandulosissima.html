Potentilla gaudinii f. glandulosissima

Potentilla gaudinii f. glandulosissima, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs covering the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and sunny conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
