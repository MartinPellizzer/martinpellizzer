Polygonum incarnatum

Polygonum incarnatum, commonly known as redroot or redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base, and its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Polygonum incarnatum is often considered a weed in agricultural settings but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
