Potentilla viridis

Potentilla viridis, commonly known as green cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a compact, rose-like pattern around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and ability to spread via rhizomes contribute to its resilience in various garden settings.
