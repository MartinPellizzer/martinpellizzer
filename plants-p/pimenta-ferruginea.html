Pimenta ferruginea

Pimenta ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty peppertree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its botanical features include a sturdy, branched stem and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Pimenta ferruginea is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
