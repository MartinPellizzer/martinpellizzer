Persea gratissima var. vulgaris

Persea gratissima var. vulgaris, commonly known as the cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It produces a large, fuzzy, egg-shaped fruit with a creamy, sweet, and aromatic pulp that is highly prized for its flavor. The plant has broad, leathery leaves and can grow up to 10 meters tall, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, and it is cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and contains a high amount of vitamin C.
