Pinalia lanuginosa

Pinalia lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Pinalia lanuginosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
