Phlox × frondosa

Phlox × frondosa, commonly known as the frondosa phlox, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mound-like growth habit and showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces opposite, narrow, dark green leaves that are often described as fern-like, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Phlox × frondosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
