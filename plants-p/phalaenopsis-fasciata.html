Phalaenopsis fasciata

Phalaenopsis fasciata, commonly known as the striped phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous flower spikes that bear clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with distinctive dark streaks. The plant has broad, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

This orchid is also noted for its ability to bloom repeatedly over several years under proper care.
