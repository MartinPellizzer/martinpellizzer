Ptarmica abrotanoides

Ptarmica abrotanoides, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Ptarmica abrotanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
