Pellaea abyssinica

Pellaea abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian shield fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and small, leathery, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or forested areas. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, shield-like appearance.

Pellaea abyssinica is valued for its unique morphology and is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
