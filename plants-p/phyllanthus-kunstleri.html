Phyllanthus kunstleri

Phyllanthus kunstleri, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in compact clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
