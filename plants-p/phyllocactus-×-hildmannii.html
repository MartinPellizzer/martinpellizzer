Phyllocactus × hildmannii

Phyllocactus × hildmannii, commonly known as the Hildmann's phyllocactus, is a hybrid cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and rock gardens.
