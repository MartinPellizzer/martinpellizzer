Papaver nigroflavum

Papaver nigroflavum, commonly known as the black and yellow poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking black petals with prominent yellow markings, which are highly visible and attract pollinators. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
