Persica vulgaris var. minuta

Persica vulgaris var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf peach.

This compact variety is a smaller form of the common peach, featuring a bushy growth habit and smaller leaves compared to its larger relatives. It produces small, sweet fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and flowers that range from white to light pink.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to flourish.
