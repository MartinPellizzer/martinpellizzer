Passerina lanuginosa

Passerina lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, woolly leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky soils at high elevations. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other spring-blooming plants.

Passerina lanuginosa is typically found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.
