Prunus japonica var. puberula

Prunus japonica var. puberula, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal beauty. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
