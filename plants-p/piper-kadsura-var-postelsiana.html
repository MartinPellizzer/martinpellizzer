Piper kadsura var. postelsiana

Piper kadsura var. postelsiana, commonly known as the golden kadsura, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slight metallic sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and landscape design.
