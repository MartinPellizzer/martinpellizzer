Pyrus pinnatifidia

Pyrus pinnatifidia, commonly known as the Japanese nashi or Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruit that is typically harvested in late summer, though it is less commonly cultivated than other pear species. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a preference for well-drained soil in temperate climates.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
