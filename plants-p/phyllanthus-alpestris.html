Phyllanthus alpestris

Phyllanthus alpestris, commonly known as the alpine phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Phyllanthus alpestris is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
