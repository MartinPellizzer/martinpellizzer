Piper nemori-marginis

Piper nemori-marginis, commonly known as the marginate forest pepper, is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
