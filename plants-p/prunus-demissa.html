Prunus demissa

Prunus demissa, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a small, sweet drupe that ripens in late summer.

Prunus demissa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
