Potentilla chrysocraspeda var. macrophylla

Potentilla chrysocraspeda var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved golden-leaf potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad and leathery, with a distinctive crinkled texture, and they remain green throughout the growing season. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant floral display and unique foliage.
