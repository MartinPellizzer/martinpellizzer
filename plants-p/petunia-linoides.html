Petunia linoides

Petunia linoides, commonly known as the silverleaf petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as a trailing or bushy perennial, often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate appearance. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Petunia linoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for containers and hanging baskets.
