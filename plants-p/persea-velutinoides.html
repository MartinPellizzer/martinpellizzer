Persea velutinoides

Persea velutinoides, commonly known as the velvet-leaf avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, egg-shaped berry with a thick, leathery skin that turns yellow when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
