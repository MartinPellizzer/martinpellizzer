Phaca argentata

Phaca argentata, commonly known as silver phaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and resilience.
