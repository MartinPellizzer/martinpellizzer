Pongamia heterocarpa

Pongamia heterocarpa, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The tree produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
