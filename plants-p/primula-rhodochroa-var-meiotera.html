Primula rhodochroa var. meiotera

Primula rhodochroa var. meiotera, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically basal, broad, and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

It is a popular species among botanists and gardeners for its ornamental value and ecological significance in high-altitude ecosystems.
