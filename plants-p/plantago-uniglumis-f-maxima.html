Plantago uniglumis f. maxima

Plantago uniglumis f. maxima, commonly known as the giant plantain, is a robust perennial herb characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems with dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
