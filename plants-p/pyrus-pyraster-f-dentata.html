Pyrus pyraster f. dentata

Pyrus pyraster f. dentata, commonly known as the serrated crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple species.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, sour fruits that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental tree is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
