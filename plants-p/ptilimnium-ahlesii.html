Ptilimnium ahlesii

Ptilimnium ahlesii, commonly known as the narrowleaf ptilimnium, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often less than an inch wide. Its small, white to pale pink flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Ptilimnium ahlesii is characterized by its low-growing habit and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
