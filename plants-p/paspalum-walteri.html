Paspalum walteri

Paspalum walteri, commonly known as Walter's grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist to wet soil conditions.

Paspalum walteri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
