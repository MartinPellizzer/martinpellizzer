Polygonum dissitiflorum

Polygonum dissitiflorum, commonly known as the hairy leaf smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in pink or red hues, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist environments like wetlands and streambanks, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Polygonum dissitiflorum is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common sight in various wetland habitats.
