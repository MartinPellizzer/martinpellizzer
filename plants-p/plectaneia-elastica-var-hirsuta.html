Plectaneia elastica var. hirsuta

Plectaneia elastica var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy rubber plant, is a variant of the rubber plant species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, typically in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight and well-draining soil.

Its rubbery texture and unique foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
