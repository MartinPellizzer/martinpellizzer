Pleurothyrium acuminatum

Pleurothyrium acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has slender, wiry rhizomes that help it anchor to submerged substrates. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with lance-shaped pinnae that are often slightly notched.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
