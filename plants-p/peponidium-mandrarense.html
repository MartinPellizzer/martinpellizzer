Peponidium mandrarense

Peponidium mandrarense, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, hairy stems, and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits that resemble cucumbers. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates, though it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
