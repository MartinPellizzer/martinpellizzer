Parthenium parviceps

Parthenium parviceps, commonly known as small-headed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
