Prosaptia immersa

Prosaptia immersa, commonly known as the submerged water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that are adapted for underwater life. It lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizomes for anchorage and nutrient absorption.

Prosaptia immersa plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing excess nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
