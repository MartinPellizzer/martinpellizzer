Passiflora silvestris

Passiflora silvestris, commonly known as the wild passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora silvestris thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
