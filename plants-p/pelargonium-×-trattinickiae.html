Pelargonium × trattinickiae

Pelargonium × trattinickiae, commonly known as the Zebra Geranium, is a hybrid pelargonium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage featuring dark green bands on a lighter background, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for both decorative and horticultural purposes.
