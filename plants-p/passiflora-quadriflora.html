Passiflora quadriflora

Passiflora quadriflora, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Passiflora quadriflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
