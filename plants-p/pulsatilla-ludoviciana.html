Pulsatilla ludoviciana

Pulsatilla ludoviciana, commonly known as the Western pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers with yellow stamens and a green center. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Pulsatilla ludoviciana is native to western North America and is known for its early spring blooming period.
