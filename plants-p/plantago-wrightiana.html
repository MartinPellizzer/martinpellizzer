Plantago wrightiana

Plantago wrightiana, commonly known as Wright's plantain, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ribbed leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
