Pterocarpus ovalifolius

Pterocarpus ovalifolius, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod containing flat, seed-like structures.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
