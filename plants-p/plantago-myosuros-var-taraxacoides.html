Plantago myosuros var. taraxacoides

Plantago myosuros var. taraxacoides, commonly known as the meadow plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed soils. The plant features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and a central stem that bears clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
