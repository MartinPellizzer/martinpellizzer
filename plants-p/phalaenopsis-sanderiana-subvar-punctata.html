Phalaenopsis sanderiana subvar. punctata

Phalaenopsis sanderiana subvar. punctata, commonly known as the speckled phalaenopsis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers with distinctive dark speckles. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
