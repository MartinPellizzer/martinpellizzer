Piptatherum badachschanicum

Piptatherum badachschanicum, commonly known as the Badakhshan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The plant is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
