Polygala laevigata

Polygala laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has finely divided, lance-shaped leaves. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala laevigata is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in shaded forest understories.
