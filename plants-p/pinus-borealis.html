Pinus borealis

Pinus borealis, commonly known as the white spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles that are typically light green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to thrive in cold, boreal climates.

The white spruce is an important component of boreal forests, providing habitat and contributing to the ecological balance of its environment.
