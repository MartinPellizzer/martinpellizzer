Plectranthus ricinispermus

Plectranthus ricinispermus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Plectranthus ricinispermus is also valued for its ability to produce a milky sap, which has been used in traditional medicine.
