Primula turkeviczii

Primula turkeviczii, commonly known as the Turkic primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various horticultural settings.
