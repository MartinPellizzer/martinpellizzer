Petalochilus littoricola

Petalochilus littoricola, commonly known as the coastal orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shores, where it thrives in humid and saline conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and delicate, usually white or pale pink, and are arranged in a raceme.

Petalochilus littoricola is well adapted to its coastal environment, with specialized structures that help it retain moisture and withstand salt spray.
