Parosela onobrychioides

Parosela onobrychioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, narrow leaves, and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native habitat's flora.
