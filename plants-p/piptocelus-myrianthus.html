Piptocelus myrianthus

Piptocelus myrianthus, commonly known as the African star grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered, small yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it a suitable candidate for erosion control in disturbed soils.
