Potentilla tangutorum

Potentilla tangutorum, commonly known as the Tungan shrub, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Potentilla tangutorum is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
