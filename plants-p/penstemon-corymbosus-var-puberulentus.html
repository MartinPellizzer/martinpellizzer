Penstemon corymbosus var. puberulentus

Penstemon corymbosus var. puberulentus, commonly known as the puberulent beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
