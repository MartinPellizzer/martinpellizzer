Populus × manitobensis

Populus × manitobensis, commonly known as the Manitoba poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including wetlands and disturbed sites. The tree has heart-shaped leaves that are typically light to dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often used in reforestation and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
