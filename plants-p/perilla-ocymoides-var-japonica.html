Perilla ocymoides var. japonica

Perilla ocymoides var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese perilla, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often purple or reddish in color, especially when grown in cooler temperatures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, and its seeds are enclosed in small, triangular pods. It is widely cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional Asian cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
