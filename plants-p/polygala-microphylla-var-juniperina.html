Polygala microphylla var. juniperina

Polygala microphylla var. juniperina, commonly known as the Juniperleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant features small, narrow leaves that resemble juniper needles, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish and occur in small clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
