Pilea carautae

Pilea carautae, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by round, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and indirect light, making it a popular houseplant.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
