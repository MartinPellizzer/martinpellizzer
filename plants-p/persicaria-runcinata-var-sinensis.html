Persicaria runcinata var. sinensis

Persicaria runcinata var. sinensis, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a wavy margin and a distinctive reddish underside.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
