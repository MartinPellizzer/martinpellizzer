Pseuduvaria pamattonis

Pseuduvaria pamattonis, commonly known as the giant banyan, is a large tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its extensive root system and aerial roots that form a dense canopy. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. It produces edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various animals.

The tree is often found in tropical rainforests and can grow to impressive heights with a broad, spreading crown.
