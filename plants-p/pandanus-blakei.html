Pandanus blakei

Pandanus blakei, commonly known as the Blake's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, forming a dense, cylindrical structure. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making rope and thatching materials.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
