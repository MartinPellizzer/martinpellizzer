Pinanga griffithii

Pinanga griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's bamboo, is a member of the Musaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a clump-forming plant that produces tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it a common sight in moist, shaded forest understories. Its inflorescences are striking, featuring large, colorful bracts that attract pollinators.

Despite its ornamental value, it is also considered an invasive species in some areas where it outcompetes native vegetation.
