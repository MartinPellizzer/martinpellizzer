Panicum imberbe var. glaucocaesium

Panicum imberbe var. glaucocaesium, commonly known as silver panicum, is a variant of the panic grass genus.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces fluffy, light green to brown inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of this variety make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
