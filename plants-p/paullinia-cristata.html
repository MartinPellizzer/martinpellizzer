Paullinia cristata

Paullinia cristata, commonly known as the "cristate" or "crown-of-thorns" plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive, serrated appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

However, it is also considered invasive in some areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
