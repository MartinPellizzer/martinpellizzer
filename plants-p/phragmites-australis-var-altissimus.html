Phragmites australis var. altissimus

Phragmites australis var. altissimus, commonly known as tall glassworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, often growing in dense stands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in saline or brackish water, making it a common species in coastal marshes and estuaries.

Its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and filter water, contributing to wetland ecosystem health.
