Poidium

Poidium, commonly known as the pine tree, is a coniferous evergreen plant belonging to the family Pinaceae.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure, which are adapted for reproduction in various environmental conditions. The plant's vascular system efficiently transports water and nutrients, supporting its growth in diverse climates. Poidium species typically have a long lifespan and can reach significant heights, making them prominent features in many forest ecosystems.

Their ecological importance includes providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
