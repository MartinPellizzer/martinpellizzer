Potamogeton lanceolatus

Potamogeton lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its roots help stabilize sediment, contributing to water clarity and overall ecosystem health.
