Potentilla ghazniensis

Potentilla ghazniensis, commonly known as the Ghazni cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is known for its hardy nature.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
