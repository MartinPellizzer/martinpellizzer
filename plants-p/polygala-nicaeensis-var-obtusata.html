Polygala nicaeensis var. obtusata

Polygala nicaeensis var. obtusata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive appearance that aids in its identification. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
