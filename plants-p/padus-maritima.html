Padus maritima

Padus maritima, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and bright orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in dune ecosystems. Its berries are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant also plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion.
