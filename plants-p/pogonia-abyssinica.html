Pogonia abyssinica

Pogonia abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian bog orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its intricate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, with intricate patterns resembling lace. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in mountainous areas, often in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the spring season.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Pogonia abyssinica is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
