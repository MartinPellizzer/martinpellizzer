Paulia

Paulia, commonly known as the common lilac, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions in Asia, Africa, and the Americas, with many species thriving in tropical and subtropical climates. The plants are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. They typically have compound leaves and produce small, pod-like fruits containing seeds.

Paulia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
