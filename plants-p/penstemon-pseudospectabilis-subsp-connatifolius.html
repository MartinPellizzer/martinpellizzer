Penstemon pseudospectabilis subsp. connatifolius

Penstemon pseudospectabilis subsp.

connatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, narrow leaves and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, and the flowers are often arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
