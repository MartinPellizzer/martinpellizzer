Prunus sachalinensis var. pubescens

Prunus sachalinensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Sakhalin cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a pubescent (hairy) underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries that are popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
