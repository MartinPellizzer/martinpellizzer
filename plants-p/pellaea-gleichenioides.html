Pellaea gleichenioides

Pellaea gleichenioides, commonly known as the Gleichenoid fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pellaea gleichenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
