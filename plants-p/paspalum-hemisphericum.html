Paspalum hemisphericum

Paspalum hemisphericum, commonly known as the hemisphere grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum hemisphericum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for wildlife.
