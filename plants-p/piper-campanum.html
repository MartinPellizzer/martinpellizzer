Piper campanum

Piper campanum, commonly known as the bell pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's botanical features include compound leaves and a climbing or trailing growth habit.
