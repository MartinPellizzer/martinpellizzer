Psoralea reclinata

Psoralea reclinata, commonly known as the leaning psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellowish-green and are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

Psoralea reclinata is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often found in disturbed habitats.
