Philadelphus occidentalis var. minutus

Philadelphus occidentalis var. minutus, commonly known as the dwarf goldenbush, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves and a dense, bushy growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
