Parietaria angustifolia

Parietaria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf wall-rocket, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny white or greenish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found growing on walls or in rocky areas.

Parietaria angustifolia is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
