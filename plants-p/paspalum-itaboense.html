Paspalum itaboense

Paspalum itaboense, commonly known as the Itaboense grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Paspalum itaboense is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
