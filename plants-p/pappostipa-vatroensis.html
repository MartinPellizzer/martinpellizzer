Pappostipa vatroensis

Pappostipa vatroensis, commonly known as the Vatrovskaya grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Pappostipa vatroensis is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
