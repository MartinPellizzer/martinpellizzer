Phaseolus pardus var. luteomaculatus

Phaseolus pardus var. luteomaculatus, commonly known as the yellow-spotted runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green mottled foliage and edible pods that develop into bean-like fruits. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces flowers that range from white to pale purple. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible beans.
