Pluchea aromatica

Pluchea aromatica, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are hairy and emit a pleasant scent when crushed.

Pluchea aromatica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
