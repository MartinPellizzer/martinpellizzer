Protea sericifolia

Protea sericifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous papery bracts that resemble flowers. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its foliage is covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it its common name.

Protea sericifolia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms.
