Pelargonium otaviense

Pelargonium otaviense, commonly known as the Otavi geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has rounded, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium otaviense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
