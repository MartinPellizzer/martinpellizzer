Potentilla sieversiana

Potentilla sieversiana, commonly known as Sievers' cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
