Piper arctilimbum var. allenii

Piper arctilimbum var. allenii, commonly known as Allen's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species, and is characterized by its elongated, trailing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a distinct aromatic scent.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
