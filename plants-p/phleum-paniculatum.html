Phleum paniculatum

Phleum paniculatum, commonly known as tall oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its role in ecological systems. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ornamental plant.
