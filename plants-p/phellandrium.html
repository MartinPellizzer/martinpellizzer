Phellandrium

Phellandrium, commonly known as lemon myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rutaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Phellandrium species are valued for their essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and natural remedies.

The leaves contain high levels of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive fragrance.
