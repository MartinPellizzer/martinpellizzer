Potentilla lindleyi var. sericea

Potentilla lindleyi var. sericea, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
