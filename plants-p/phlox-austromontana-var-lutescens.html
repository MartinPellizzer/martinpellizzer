Phlox austromontana var. lutescens

Phlox austromontana var. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of showy, yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
