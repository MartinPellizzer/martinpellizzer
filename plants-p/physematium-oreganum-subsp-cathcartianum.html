Physematium oreganum subsp. cathcartianum

Physematium oreganum subsp.

cathcartianum, commonly known as the Cathcart's goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and golden-yellow foliage.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas with mild, moist climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
