Protium apiculatum

Protium apiculatum, commonly known as the Philippine tamanu or tamanu tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing a single seed.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts within its native habitat.
