Pilosella erythriophylla subsp. rojowskii

Pilosella erythriophylla subsp.

rojowskii, commonly known as the red-leaf pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive reddish-brown leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine regions.

This plant is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.
