Phyllanthus erectus

Phyllanthus erectus, commonly known as the upright phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces bright yellow fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Phyllanthus erectus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
