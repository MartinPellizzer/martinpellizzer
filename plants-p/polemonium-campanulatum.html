Polemonium campanulatum

Polemonium campanulatum, commonly known as bluebells, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
