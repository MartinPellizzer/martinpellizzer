Philydrum

Philydrum, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas, including Mexico and parts of the United States. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Philydrum species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
