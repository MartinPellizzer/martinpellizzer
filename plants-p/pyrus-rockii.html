Pyrus rockii

Pyrus rockii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by white to yellow fruit that ripens in late summer.

Pyrus rockii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
