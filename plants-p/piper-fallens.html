Piper fallens

Piper fallens, commonly known as the hanging pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that can reach lengths of several meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by tiny, peppery fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Piper fallens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
