Potamogeton pusillus var. mucronatus

Potamogeton pusillus var. mucronatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and bear small, opposite leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces tiny, elongated fruits.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
