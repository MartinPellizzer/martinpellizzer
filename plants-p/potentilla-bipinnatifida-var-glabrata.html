Potentilla bipinnatifida var. glabrata

Potentilla bipinnatifida var. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bipinnatifid leaves that are generally hairless, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
