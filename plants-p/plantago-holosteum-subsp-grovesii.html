Plantago holosteum subsp. grovesii

Plantago holosteum subsp.

grovesii, commonly known as the California plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
