Piper evasum

Piper evasum, commonly known as the Evasum pepper or the Brazilian pepper, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are typically consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
