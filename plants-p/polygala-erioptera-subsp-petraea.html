Polygala erioptera subsp. petraea

Polygala erioptera subsp.

petraea, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces delicate, spindly flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color, arranged in racemes. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying desert flora.
