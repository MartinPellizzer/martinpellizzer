Pentacyphus camargoi

Pentacyphus camargoi, commonly known as the Camargue buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark blue berries that are rich in anthocyanins. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Pentacyphus camargoi plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
