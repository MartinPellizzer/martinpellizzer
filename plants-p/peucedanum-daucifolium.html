Peucedanum daucifolium

Peucedanum daucifolium, commonly known as the wild carrot or bishop's weed, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, pungent aroma.

Peucedanum daucifolium is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
