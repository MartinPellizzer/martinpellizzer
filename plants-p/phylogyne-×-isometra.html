Phylogyne × isometra

Phylogyne × isometra, commonly known as the hybrid cyclamen, is a perennial flowering plant characterized by its tuberous roots and showy, heart-shaped leaves.

It produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade. Its botanical features include a complex hybrid origin, combining traits from both Cyclamen and Primula species.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its decorative foliage and delicate blooms.
