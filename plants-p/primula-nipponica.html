Primula nipponica

Primula nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rock gardens. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy surface, and it produces small, rounded fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Primula nipponica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different growing conditions.
