Padus capollin

Padus capollin, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and small, oval leaves. The plant produces white or pale green flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries in late summer. Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The shrub is also important for wildlife, providing food and shelter for various birds and insects.
