Pycnanthemum incanum var. albescens

Pycnanthemum incanum var. albescens, commonly known as white blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
