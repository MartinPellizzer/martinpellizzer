Podocarpus perrieri

Podocarpus perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a unique combination of gymnosperm traits, including the production of seeds enclosed in cones rather than flowers. Its bark is typically thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

Podocarpus perrieri plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
