Polychilos lamelligera

Polychilos lamelligera, commonly known as the fragrant rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single spike of flowers during the spring season.

Polychilos lamelligera is a rare and protected species, reflecting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
