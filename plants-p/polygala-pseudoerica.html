Polygala pseudoerica

Polygala pseudoerica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
