Peperomia trianae

Peperomia trianae, commonly known as the Trianas peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features upright, branching stems with dense clusters of small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia trianae is known for its low maintenance and ability to adapt well to a variety of indoor environments.
