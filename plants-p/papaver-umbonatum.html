Papaver umbonatum

Papaver umbonatum, commonly known as the humpbacked poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Papaver umbonatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
