Pleopeltis subaquatilis

Pleopeltis subaquatilis, commonly known as the aquatic fern, is a unique species of fern characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, showcasing adaptations for water-based environments. The plant features delicate, feather-like fronds that are often submerged or partially submerged. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are positioned on the underside of the leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
