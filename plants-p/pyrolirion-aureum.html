Pyrolirion aureum

Pyrolirion aureum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often clustered in loose racemes. Pyrolirion aureum prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

It is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
