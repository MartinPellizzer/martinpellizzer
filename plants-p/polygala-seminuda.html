Polygala seminuda

Polygala seminuda, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are often pale green or white, with a unique structure that resembles a small, spindly legume.

Polygala seminuda is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
