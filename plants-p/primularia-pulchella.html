Primularia pulchella

Primularia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Primularia pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
