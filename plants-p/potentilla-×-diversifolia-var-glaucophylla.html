Potentilla × diversifolia var. glaucophylla

Potentilla × diversifolia var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla potentilla, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
