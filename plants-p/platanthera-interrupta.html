Platanthera interrupta

Platanthera interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted lady's-tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, oval-shaped leaves.

The species is known for its fragrant, nectar-rich flowers that attract various pollinators.
