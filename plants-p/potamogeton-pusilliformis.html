Potamogeton pusilliformis

Potamogeton pusilliformis, commonly known as the small-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in width, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often emerging from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Potamogeton pusilliformis is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
