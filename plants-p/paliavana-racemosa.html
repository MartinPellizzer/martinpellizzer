Paliavana racemosa

Paliavana racemosa, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in racemose inflorescences. The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a sought-after species in horticulture.
