Pseuderanthemum detruncatum

Pseuderanthemum detruncatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface, and its inflorescences are clustered in whorls at the nodes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
