Prunus spinosa monstr. variegata

Prunus spinosa monstr.

variegata, commonly known as the variegated blackthorn, is a striking variation of the common blackthorn. It features distinctive variegated foliage with white and yellow markings on its leaves, creating a visually appealing contrast.

The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for hedges or ornamental planting.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and functional landscaping.
