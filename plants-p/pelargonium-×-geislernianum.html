Pelargonium × geislernianum

Pelargonium × geislernianum, commonly known as the German geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and displays of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.

Pelargonium × geislernianum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
