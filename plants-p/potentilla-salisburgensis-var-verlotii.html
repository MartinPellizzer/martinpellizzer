Potentilla salisburgensis var. verlotii

Potentilla salisburgensis var. verlotii, commonly known as the Salzburg potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
