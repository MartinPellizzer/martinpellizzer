Parechites adnascens

Parechites adnascens, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
