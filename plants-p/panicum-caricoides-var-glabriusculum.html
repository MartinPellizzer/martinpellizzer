Panicum caricoides var. glabriusculum

Panicum caricoides var. glabriusculum, commonly known as smooth panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
