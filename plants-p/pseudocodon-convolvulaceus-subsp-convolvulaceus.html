Pseudocodon convolvulaceus subsp. convolvulaceus

Pseudocodon convolvulaceus subsp.

convolvulaceus, commonly known as the small morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing or trailing growth habit.

The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and oppositely arranged, while its stems are hairy and twining.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
