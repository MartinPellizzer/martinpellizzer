Phrynium cylindricum

Phrynium cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems that are often used as a source of starch. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a reddish or yellowish hue.

Phrynium cylindricum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
