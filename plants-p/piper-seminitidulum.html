Piper seminitidulum

Piper seminitidulum, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Piper seminitidulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.
