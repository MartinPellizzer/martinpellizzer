Plantago durvillei subsp. eupachyneura

Plantago durvillei subsp.

eupachyneura, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually greenish or pale brown in color.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
