Parrya popovii

Parrya popovii, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Parrya popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
