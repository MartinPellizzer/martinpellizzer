Peponium kilimandscharicum var. holstii

Peponium kilimandscharicum var. holstii, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro pumpkin, is a rare and unique variant of the wild cucumber.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa, particularly the Kilimanjaro and Mount Kenya areas. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, though it remains relatively obscure in horticultural cultivation.
