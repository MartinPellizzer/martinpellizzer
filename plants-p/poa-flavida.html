Poa flavida

Poa flavida, commonly known as the yellow meadow grass, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color. The plant thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Poa flavida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
