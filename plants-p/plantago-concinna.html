Plantago concinna

Plantago concinna, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in meadows. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in the summer.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
