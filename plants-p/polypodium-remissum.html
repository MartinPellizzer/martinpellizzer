Polypodium remissum

Polypodium remissum, commonly known as the slender wall fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, creeping rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on shaded, moist rock surfaces or in forest undergrowth, thriving in humid environments. Its fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a soft, feathery appearance.

Polypodium remissum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
