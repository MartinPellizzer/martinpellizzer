Pernettya mucronata var. cordobensis

Pernettya mucronata var. cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba wintergreen, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
