Phaseolus ellipticus var. niger

Phaseolus ellipticus var. niger, commonly known as black bean bush, is a tropical perennial legume native to Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptic leaflets, often with a dark green to blackish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical pods containing kidney-shaped black seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also studied for its potential in nitrogen fixation and as a forage crop.
