Pleurothallis amentacea

Pleurothallis amentacea, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, white, and often have a subtle fragrance, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

The genus Pleurothallis is known for its diverse array of species, many of which are challenging to cultivate due to their specific environmental requirements.
