Panicum bifarium

Panicum bifarium, commonly known as the two-flowered panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces two flower heads, which give it its scientific name, and its leaves are narrow and hairy. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.

Panicum bifarium is often found in wetland ecosystems and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.
