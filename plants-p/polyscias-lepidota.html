Polyscias lepidota

Polyscias lepidota, commonly known as the silver-leafed polycarpic, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinct silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by oval, yellowish fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Polyscias lepidota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
