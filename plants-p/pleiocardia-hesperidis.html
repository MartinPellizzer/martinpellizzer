Pleiocardia hesperidis

Pleiocardia hesperidis, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep blue. The plant has a short growth period, blooming primarily in the early morning and closing its flowers by midday. Its leaves are small, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, silky hair.

Pleiocardia hesperidis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.
