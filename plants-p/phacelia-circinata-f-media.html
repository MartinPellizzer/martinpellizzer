Phacelia circinata f. media

Phacelia circinata f. media, commonly known as the broad-leaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects.
