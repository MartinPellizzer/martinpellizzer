Pteris dimorpha var. metagrevilleana

Pteris dimorpha var. metagrevilleana, commonly known as the Metagrevillea fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds with a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape. The fronds are typically bright green, with a smooth texture and a slightly curled appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
