Pinus oocarpa var. trifoliata

Pinus oocarpa var. trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf pine, is a variant of the oocarpa pine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
