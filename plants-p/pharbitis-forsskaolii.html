Pharbitis forsskaolii

Pharbitis forsskaolii, commonly known as the desert cowpea, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces distinctive, showy purple or violet flowers. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are adapted to survive in dry conditions.

Pharbitis forsskaolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
