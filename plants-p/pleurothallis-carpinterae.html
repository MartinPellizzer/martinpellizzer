Pleurothallis carpinterae

Pleurothallis carpinterae, commonly known as the Carpinthia orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often resemble miniature orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
