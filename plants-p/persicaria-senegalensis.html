Persicaria senegalensis

Persicaria senegalensis, commonly known as Senegal knotweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and distinctive leaf shapes. The plant typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks and wetlands, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic environments. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are often consumed by birds.

Persicaria senegalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
