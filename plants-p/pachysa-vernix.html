Pachysa vernix

Pachysa vernix, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Pachysa vernix is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
