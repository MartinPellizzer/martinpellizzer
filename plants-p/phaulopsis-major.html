Phaulopsis major

Phaulopsis major, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
