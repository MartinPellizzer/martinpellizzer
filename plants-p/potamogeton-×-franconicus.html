Potamogeton × franconicus

Potamogeton × franconicus, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton perfoliatus. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds.

Potamogeton × franconicus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
