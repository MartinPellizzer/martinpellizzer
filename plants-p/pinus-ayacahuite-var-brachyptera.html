Pinus ayacahuite var. brachyptera

Pinus ayacahuite var. brachyptera, commonly known as the Mexican bristle pine, is a variant of the Ayacahuite pine native to Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The cones are small and often covered in short, bristly hairs, which give the species its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of elevations in its native habitat.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its region, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
