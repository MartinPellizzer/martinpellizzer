Panicum marunguense

Panicum marunguense, commonly known as the Marunguense grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Panicum marunguense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
