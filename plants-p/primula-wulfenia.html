Primula wulfenia

Primula wulfenia, commonly known as Wulfen's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula wulfenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
