Piper casimirianum

Piper casimirianum, commonly known as the Casimirianum pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that turn dark when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
