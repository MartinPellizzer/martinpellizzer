Palaquium stehlinii

Palaquium stehlinii, commonly known as the Stehlin's cheesefruit, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fruits resembling small, round cheeses, hence its common name. The fruit is edible, with a creamy, sweet pulp that has a mild, nutty flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique culinary ingredients.
