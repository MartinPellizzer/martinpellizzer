Potamogeton gramineus var. boreale

Potamogeton gramineus var. boreale, commonly known as the northern pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind, and it often forms dense underwater vegetation. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This variety is adapted to cooler climates and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
