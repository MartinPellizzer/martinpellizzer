Papaver pannosum

Papaver pannosum, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

The meadow poppy is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
