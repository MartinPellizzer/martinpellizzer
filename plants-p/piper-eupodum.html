Piper eupodum

Piper eupodum, commonly known as the pink pepper plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that resemble peppercorns. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of spices.

The berries are used in culinary applications and traditional medicine, though they are not as commonly used as black or white pepper.
