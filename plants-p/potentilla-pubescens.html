Potentilla pubescens

Potentilla pubescens, commonly known as the downy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, blooming in early summer.

Potentilla pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
