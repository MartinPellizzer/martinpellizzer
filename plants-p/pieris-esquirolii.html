Pieris esquirolii

Pieris esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's pieris, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by ornamental red berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
