Paepalanthus lycopodioides

Paepalanthus lycopodioides, commonly known as the Brazilian clubmoss, is a species of plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors in South America. The plant has whorled, needle-like leaves and produces spore-bearing cones, which are characteristic of primitive vascular plants. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
