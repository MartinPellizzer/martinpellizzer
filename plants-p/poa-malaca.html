Poa malaca

Poa malaca, commonly known as the Malacca grass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks.
