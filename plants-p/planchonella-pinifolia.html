Planchonella pinifolia

Planchonella pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering shrub that typically grows in humid, lowland areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
