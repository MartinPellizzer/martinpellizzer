Pleurothallis cristata

Pleurothallis cristata, commonly known as the cristate orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to its humid, shaded forest habitat.

Pleurothallis cristata is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
