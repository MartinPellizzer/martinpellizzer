Phyllanthus thwaitesianus

Phyllanthus thwaitesianus, commonly known as the African buttonwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-yellow fruits that split open to release seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Phyllanthus thwaitesianus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
