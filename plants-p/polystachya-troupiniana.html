Polystachya troupiniana

Polystachya troupiniana, commonly known as the Trinidadian spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture.

The orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
