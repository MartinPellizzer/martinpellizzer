Penstemon torreyi

Penstemon torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's beardplant, is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and woodland edges.

Penstemon torreyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
