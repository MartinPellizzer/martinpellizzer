Persea acuminata

Persea acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian avocados or the cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is typically pear-shaped, with a rough, greenish-brown skin that encases a creamy, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
