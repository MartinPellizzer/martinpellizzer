Protea acicularis

Protea acicularis, commonly known as the needle protea, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, needle-like bracts that form a dense, cone-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its vibrant, papery flowers come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, adding a dramatic visual impact.

Protea acicularis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
