Panicum natans

Panicum natans, commonly known as floating panicgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its ability to grow on the surface of water, with its roots submerged and leaves floating. The plant has slender, branching stems that help it stay afloat, and its small, grass-like leaves are often covered in fine hairs. Panicum natans produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and stabilizing water surfaces.
