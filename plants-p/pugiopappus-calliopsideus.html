Pugiopappus calliopsideus

Pugiopappus calliopsideus, commonly known as the mountain pappus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing a distinctive fluffy seed head that aids in wind dispersal.
