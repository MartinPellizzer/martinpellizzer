Psammisia aurantiaca

Psammisia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and its ability to thrive in sandy soils. The plant produces small, bright orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to store water in harsh environments.

Psammisia aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
