Phyllanthus fallax

Phyllanthus fallax, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright orange or red berries. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry and rocky soils.

Phyllanthus fallax is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
