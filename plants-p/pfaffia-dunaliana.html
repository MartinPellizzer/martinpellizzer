Pfaffia dunaliana

Pfaffia dunaliana, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are the primary part used for medicinal purposes, containing compounds believed to have adaptogenic properties.

Due to its high demand, Pfaffia dunaliana is also considered an endangered species.
