Pouteria obtusifolia var. parvifolia

Pouteria obtusifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cheesefruit, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruits that are generally round and have a soft, creamy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruits.
