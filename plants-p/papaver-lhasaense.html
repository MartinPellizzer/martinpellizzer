Papaver lhasaense

Papaver lhasaense, commonly known as the Lhasa poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of Tibet.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool and dry conditions. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The Lhasa poppy is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in Tibetan traditions.
