Potamogeton pusillus subsp. flabellatus

Potamogeton pusillus subsp.

flabellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia. It typically grows in shallow waters and is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often curved or twisted.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediment.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
