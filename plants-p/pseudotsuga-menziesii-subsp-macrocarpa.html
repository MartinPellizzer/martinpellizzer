Pseudotsuga menziesii subsp. macrocarpa

Pseudotsuga menziesii subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the California torreya, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly twisted.

The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain edible seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
