Pycreus flavidus var. nilagiricus

Pycreus flavidus var. nilagiricus, commonly known as the Nilagiri yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
