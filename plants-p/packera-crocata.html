Packera crocata

Packera crocata, commonly known as the showy golden ragwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Packera crocata is often found in the Pacific Northwest and parts of the northern United States.
