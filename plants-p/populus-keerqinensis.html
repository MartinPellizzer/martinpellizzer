Populus keerqinensis

Populus keerqinensis, commonly known as the Keerqin poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and catkin flowers. The tree typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in forest-steppe regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil.

Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for various crafts and construction purposes.
