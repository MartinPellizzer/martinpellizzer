Polygonum campanulatum var. pangianum

Polygonum campanulatum var. pangianum, commonly known as the Pangia bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

The species is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to diverse environmental conditions.
