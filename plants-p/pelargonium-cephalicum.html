Pelargonium cephalicum

Pelargonium cephalicum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright red or pink blooms, which appear in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium cephalicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
