Piper atroglandulosum

Piper atroglandulosum, commonly known as the black glandular pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and has a climbing or trailing growth habit. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, though it is less common than other pepper species.

The plant's botanical features include glandular structures on its stems and leaves, contributing to its unique chemical profile.
