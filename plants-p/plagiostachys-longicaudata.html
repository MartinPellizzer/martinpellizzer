Plagiostachys longicaudata

Plagiostachys longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed bamboo, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of clustered panicles with small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Plagiostachys longicaudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
