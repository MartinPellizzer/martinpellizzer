Passiflora ursina

Passiflora ursina, commonly known as the bear's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of red, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary preparations. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora ursina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
