Pleurothallis elegantula

Pleurothallis elegantula, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of other plants without being parasitic, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, and it thrives in high-altitude cloud forests.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
