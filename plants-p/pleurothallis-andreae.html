Pleurothallis andreae

Pleurothallis andreae, commonly known as the Andean pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleurothallis andreae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
