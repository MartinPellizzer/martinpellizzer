Plantago berroi

Plantago berroi, commonly known as the Berro's plantain, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access moisture from deeper soil layers.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
