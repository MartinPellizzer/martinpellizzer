Palura

Palura, commonly known as the yellow bell or yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, ovate leaves and hairy stems. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.

Palura is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
