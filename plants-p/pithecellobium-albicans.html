Pithecellobium albicans

Pithecellobium albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered acacia, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, often giving it a delicate appearance. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
