Phoenix pusilla

Phoenix pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf date palm, is a small, cold-hardy palm native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters and has a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which eventually develop into small, sweet-tasting dates. Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
