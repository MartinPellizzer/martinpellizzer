Piper concinnum

Piper concinnum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Piper concinnum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
