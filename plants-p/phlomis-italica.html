Phlomis italica

Phlomis italica, commonly known as Italian torchflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its striking, cone-shaped flower heads that range in color from yellow to red. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Phlomis italica is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
