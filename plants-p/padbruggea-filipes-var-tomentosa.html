Padbruggea filipes var. tomentosa

Padbruggea filipes var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly padbruggea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a fuzzy appearance, and its stems are often swollen and ribbed. The leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
