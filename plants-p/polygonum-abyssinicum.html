Polygonum abyssinicum

Polygonum abyssinicum, commonly known as Abyssinian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its stems are often reddish and have a knotted appearance, giving it its common name.

It is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
