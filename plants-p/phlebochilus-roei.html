Phlebochilus roei

Phlebochilus roei, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Saxifragaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
