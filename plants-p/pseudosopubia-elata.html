Pseudosopubia elata

Pseudosopubia elata, commonly known as the tall pseudosopubia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in rocky soils and can tolerate high temperatures and low precipitation.

Its resilience makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
