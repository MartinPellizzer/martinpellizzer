Pelargonium × aulicum

Pelargonium × aulicum, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium graveolens.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically has rounded, velvety leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and container growing due to its adaptability and long blooming period.

Pelargonium × aulicum prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a range of climates.
