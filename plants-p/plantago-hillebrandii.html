Plantago hillebrandii

Plantago hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used traditionally for their soothing and healing effects.
