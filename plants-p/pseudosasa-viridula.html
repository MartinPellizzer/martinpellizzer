Pseudosasa viridula

Pseudosasa viridula, commonly known as the green bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is a clump-forming plant that produces tall, slender culms with a smooth, greenish-brown sheath. The leaves are long, narrow, and have a distinctive blue-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

This plant is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of sustainable materials.
