Potentilla villosa var. gracilior

Potentilla villosa var. gracilior, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-white, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or rocky areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and hardy nature.
