Pulsatilla patens lusus rubens

Pulsatilla patens lusus rubens, commonly known as the red pasqueflower, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving them a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The red pasqueflower is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
