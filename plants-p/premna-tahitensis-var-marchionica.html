Premna tahitensis var. marchionica

Premna tahitensis var. marchionica, commonly known as the Tahitian nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
