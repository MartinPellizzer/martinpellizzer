Phaseolus vulgaris var. curdicus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. curdicus, commonly known as the Curdicus bean, is a lesser-known variety of the common bean.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with climbing vines, producing clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves and a fibrous root system, which help it adapt to various soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
