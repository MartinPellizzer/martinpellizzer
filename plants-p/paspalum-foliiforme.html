Paspalum foliiforme

Paspalum foliiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often described as blade-like, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
