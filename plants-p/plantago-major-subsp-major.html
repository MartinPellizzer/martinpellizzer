Plantago major subsp. major

Plantago major subsp.

major, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features a basal rosette of broad, oval, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. It is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is commonly found in lawns, fields, and disturbed areas.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
