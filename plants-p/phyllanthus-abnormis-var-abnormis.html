Phyllanthus abnormis var. abnormis

Phyllanthus abnormis var. abnormis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has alternate, simple, and elliptic-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary ingredient.
