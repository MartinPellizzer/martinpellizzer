Plantago trinitatis

Plantago trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidadian plantain or Trinidadian plantago, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features rosettes of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive pale green to bluish-green color. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
