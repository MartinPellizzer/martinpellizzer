Potentilla argentea subvar. wisliceni

Potentilla argentea subvar. wisliceni, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
