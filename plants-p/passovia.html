Passovia

Passovia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and short flowering period. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Passovia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens for its dramatic nighttime display.
