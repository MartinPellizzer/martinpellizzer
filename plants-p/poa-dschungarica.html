Poa dschungarica

Poa dschungarica, commonly known as the Dzungarian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Poa dschungarica plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native range.
