Phytolacca arborea

Phytolacca arborea, commonly known as the tree nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its roots and leaves contain various alkaloids and glycosides, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
