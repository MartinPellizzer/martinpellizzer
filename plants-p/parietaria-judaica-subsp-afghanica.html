Parietaria judaica subsp. afghanica

Parietaria judaica subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as Afghan wall-rocket, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The plant has small, alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil environments.

This subspecies is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
