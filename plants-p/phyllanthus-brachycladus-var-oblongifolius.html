Phyllanthus brachycladus var. oblongifolius

Phyllanthus brachycladus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phyllanthus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to oblong in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves and fruits are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
