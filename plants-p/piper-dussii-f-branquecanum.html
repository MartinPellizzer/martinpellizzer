Piper dussii f. branquecanum

Piper dussii f. branquecanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a botanical variant of the Piper dussii species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elliptical, three-lobed berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions.
