Piper sancti-felicis

Piper sancti-felicis, commonly known as the sacred pepper or holy pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when ripe.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
