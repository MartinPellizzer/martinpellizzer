Psoralea bituminosa var. prostrata

Psoralea bituminosa var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate psoralea, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited moisture.

The species is noted for its resilience in harsh environments, making it a characteristic component of scrub and rocky habitats.
