Phyllostachys nana

Phyllostachys nana, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming grass native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, green culms that grow to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, making it ideal for small spaces. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, providing an elegant appearance in gardens. It is a hardy species, tolerating a range of soil types and climatic conditions.

Phyllostachys nana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to create a dense, upright growth habit.
