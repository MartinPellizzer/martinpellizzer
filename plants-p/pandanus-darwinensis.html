Pandanus darwinensis

Pandanus darwinensis, commonly known as the Darwin's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, which eventually develop into edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is an important species in the flora of its native regions.
