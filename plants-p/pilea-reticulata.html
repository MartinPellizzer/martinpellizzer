Pilea reticulata

Pilea reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a popular houseplant native to China and Taiwan.

It is characterized by its round, waxy leaves that resemble small coins, which give it its common name. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit and thrives in bright, indirect light. It is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and moderate watering.

Pilea reticulata is also known for its ability to produce small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits.
