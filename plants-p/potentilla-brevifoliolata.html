Potentilla brevifoliolata

Potentilla brevifoliolata, commonly known as the short-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, lobed leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
