Potamogeton × rectifolius

Potamogeton × rectifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs naturally in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations depending on its genetic parents.
