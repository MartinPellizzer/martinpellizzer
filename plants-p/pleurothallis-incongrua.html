Pleurothallis incongrua

Pleurothallis incongrua, commonly known as the incongruous pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized orchid growings.
