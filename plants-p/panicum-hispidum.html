Panicum hispidum

Panicum hispidum, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and hairy, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, fluffy seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in prairie and woodland habitats.

Panicum hispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
