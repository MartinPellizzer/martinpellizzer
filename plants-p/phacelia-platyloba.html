Phacelia platyloba

Phacelia platyloba, commonly known as the broadleaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bushy growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are hairy and often grayish in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Phacelia platyloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
