Pilosella × valsiana

Pilosella × valsiana, commonly known as the common mouse-ear chickweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with densely packed, hairy leaves that give it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas.

Pilosella × valsiana is widely distributed across Europe and parts of Asia, and it is considered a common weed in many regions.
