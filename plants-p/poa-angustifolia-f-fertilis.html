Poa angustifolia f. fertilis

Poa angustifolia f. fertilis, commonly known as the fertilis form of narrowleaf bluegrass, is a variant of a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, dark green leaves and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and turfgrass applications. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, exhibiting moderate drought tolerance. Its fine texture and ability to form dense mats make it a desirable choice for ground cover in gardens and landscapes.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its inflorescence structure and genetic variations that set it apart from the typical Poa angustifolia.
