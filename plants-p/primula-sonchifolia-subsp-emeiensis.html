Primula sonchifolia subsp. emeiensis

Primula sonchifolia subsp.

emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei Mountains primula, is a perennial herb native to China. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
