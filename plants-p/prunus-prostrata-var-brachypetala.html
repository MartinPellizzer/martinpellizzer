Prunus prostrata var. brachypetala

Prunus prostrata var. brachypetala, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cherry, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing plant.
