Pulsatilla amoena

Pulsatilla amoena, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem that supports its single, large, cup-shaped bloom. Its leaves are palmately divided into lobes, and the plant produces follicles containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Pulsatilla amoena is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.
