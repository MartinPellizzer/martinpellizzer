Pentaschistis trisetoides var. schimperi

Pentaschistis trisetoides var. schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It produces pods containing edible seeds that have been used traditionally for food and medicinal purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and agricultural practices.
