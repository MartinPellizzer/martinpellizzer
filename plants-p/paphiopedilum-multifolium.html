Paphiopedilum multifolium

Paphiopedilum multifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, saddle-shaped lip and vibrant green foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers, which can be pink, white, or yellow, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Paphiopedilum multifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
