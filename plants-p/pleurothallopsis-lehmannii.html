Pleurothallopsis lehmannii

Pleurothallopsis lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with slender, erect stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with three sepals and three petals, often arranged in a spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
