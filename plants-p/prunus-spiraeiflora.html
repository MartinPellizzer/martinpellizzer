Prunus spiraeiflora

Prunus spiraeiflora, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant.
