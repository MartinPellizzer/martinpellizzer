Peperomia myrtifolia

Peperomia myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are often a vibrant green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in spikes along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia myrtifolia is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal watering and moderate humidity to flourish.
