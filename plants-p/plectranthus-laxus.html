Plectranthus laxus

Plectranthus laxus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus laxus is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
