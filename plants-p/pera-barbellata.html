Pera barbellata

Pera barbellata, commonly known as the hairy pera, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
