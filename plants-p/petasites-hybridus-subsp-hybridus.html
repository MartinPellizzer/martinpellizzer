Petasites hybridus subsp.

hybridus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter, and its showy, yellow flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its extensive rhizomatous growth.
