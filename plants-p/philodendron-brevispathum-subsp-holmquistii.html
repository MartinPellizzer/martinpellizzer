Philodendron brevispathum subsp. holmquistii

Philodendron brevispathum subsp.

holmquistii, commonly known as the Holmquist's short-path philodendron, is a rare and less commonly cultivated species native to the tropical rainforests of Central America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture.

The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific care requirements, it is not widely available in commercial markets.
