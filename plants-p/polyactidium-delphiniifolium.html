Polyactidium delphiniifolium

Polyactidium delphiniifolium, commonly known as the water clover, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible roots. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble clover, with each leaflet being oval and slightly serrated. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the wet season.

The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
