Pavonia baumliana

Pavonia baumliana, commonly known as the scarlet false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in summer, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia baumliana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
