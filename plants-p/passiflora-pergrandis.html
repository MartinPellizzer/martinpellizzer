Passiflora pergrandis

Passiflora pergrandis, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a striking vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature intricate patterns of purple, blue, and white, with a crown of feathery filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Passiflora pergrandis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
