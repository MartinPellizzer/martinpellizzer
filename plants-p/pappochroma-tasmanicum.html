Pappochroma tasmanicum

Pappochroma tasmanicum, commonly known as the Tasmanian flax or Tasmanian grass.

It is a flowering plant native to Tasmania, characterized by its tufted growth habit and fibrous, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and wetland habitats.

Pappochroma tasmanicum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
