Pleurothallis coffeicola

Pleurothallis coffeicola, commonly known as the coffee-colored orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique floral structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
