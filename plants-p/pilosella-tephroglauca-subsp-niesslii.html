Pilosella tephroglauca subsp. niesslii

Pilosella tephroglauca subsp.

niesslii, commonly known as the alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its range.
