Passiflora rufostipulata

Passiflora rufostipulata, commonly known as the red-stippled passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of red, white, and blue hues, with prominent stamens and a unique crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped with reddish veins, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Passiflora rufostipulata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and medicinal properties.
