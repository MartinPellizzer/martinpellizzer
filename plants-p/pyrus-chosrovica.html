Pyrus chosrovica

Pyrus chosrovica, commonly known as the Caucasian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible pears that are typically yellow or green when ripe. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at moderate elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
