Piper pustulicaule

Piper pustulicaule, commonly known as the spotted pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes various pepper species, and is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into reddish berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's unique appearance, with its pustular stems and aromatic foliage, makes it a distinctive subject in botanical studies.
