Penthorum circinale

Penthorum circinale, commonly known as the spiralwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which wrap around the stem in a helical pattern. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and streambanks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
