Polygonum suffultum

Polygonum suffultum, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, sticky hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive waxy coating. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
