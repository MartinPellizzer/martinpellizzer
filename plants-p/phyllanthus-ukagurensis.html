Phyllanthus ukagurensis

Phyllanthus ukagurensis, commonly known as the Japanese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in horticulture.
