Pourthiaea villosa var. parvifolia

Pourthiaea villosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved velvetleaf, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems covered in a dense layer of soft, hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The leaves are typically small, narrow, and have a velvety texture. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
