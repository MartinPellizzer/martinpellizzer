Pithocarpa corymbulosa

Pithocarpa corymbulosa, commonly known as the mountain wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in corymbs. Its pithy stems are a distinctive botanical feature, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
