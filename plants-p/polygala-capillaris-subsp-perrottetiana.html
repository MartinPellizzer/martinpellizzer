Polygala capillaris subsp. perrottetiana

Polygala capillaris subsp.

perrottetiana, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers clustered in loose racemes.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and modest blooms.
