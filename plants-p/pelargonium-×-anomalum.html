Pelargonium × anomalum

Pelargonium × anomalum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a species that results from the crossbreeding of various pelargonium species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features broad, velvety leaves that are often adorned with distinctive markings or patterns. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and indoor spaces due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Pelargonium × anomalum thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
