Phacelia ramosissima f. suffrutescens

Phacelia ramosissima f. suffrutescens, commonly known as the shrub phacelia, is a perennial plant characterized by its compact, woody growth habit.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
