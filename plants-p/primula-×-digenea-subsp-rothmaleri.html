Primula × digenea subsp. rothmaleri

Primula × digenea subsp.

rothmaleri, commonly known as the Rothmaler's primula, is a hybrid species native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are typically oval and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ability to bloom reliably in shaded conditions.
