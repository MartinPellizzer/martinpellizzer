Pteronia undulata

Pteronia undulata, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces slender, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Pteronia undulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
