Pelargonium heracleifolium

Pelargonium heracleifolium, commonly known as the giant geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.

Pelargonium heracleifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
