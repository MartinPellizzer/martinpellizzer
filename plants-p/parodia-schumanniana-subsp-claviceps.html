Parodia schumanniana subsp. claviceps

Parodia schumanniana subsp.

claviceps, commonly known as the "Crown of Thorns," is a succulent cactus native to South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and a distinctive crown-like arrangement of flowers.

The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems for survival in arid conditions.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
