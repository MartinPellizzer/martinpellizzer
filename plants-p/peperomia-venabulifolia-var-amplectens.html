Peperomia venabulifolia var. amplectens

Peperomia venabulifolia var. amplectens, commonly known as the clinging peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, upright rosettes of thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and red, which are borne on short, upright spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
