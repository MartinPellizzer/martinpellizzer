Pseuderanthemum graciliflorum

Pseuderanthemum graciliflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
