Prunus tomentosa var. graebneriana

Prunus tomentosa var. graebneriana, commonly known as the Graebner's Japanese white cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus tomentosa species.

It is characterized by its dense, white-pink flowers that bloom profusely in early spring. The plant has a compact, rounded growth habit with grayish-white, fuzzy bark that becomes smoother with age. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree for its showy floral display.

This variety is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
