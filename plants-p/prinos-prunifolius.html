Prinos prunifolius

Prinos prunifolius, commonly known as the redleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

Prinos prunifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
