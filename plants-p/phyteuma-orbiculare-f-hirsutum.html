Phyteuma orbiculare f. hirsutum

Phyteuma orbiculare f. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy globe flower, is a botanical variant of the globe flower species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of showy, globular orange-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that blooms in late summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural habitats.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a desirable ornamental plant in horticulture.
