Philadelphus mitsae

Philadelphus mitsae, commonly known as the Japanese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus mitsae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant floral scent.
