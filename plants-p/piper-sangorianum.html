Piper sangorianum

Piper sangorianum, commonly known as the Sangorian pepper or black pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark berries that contain the aromatic seeds used as a spice. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary properties.

The botanical features of Piper sangorianum make it an important species in both traditional medicine and the spice trade.
