Pleurothallis discalis

Pleurothallis discalis, commonly known as the disc-shaped pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This small orchid typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with a distinctive, disc-like shape that gives it its common name.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique morphology.
