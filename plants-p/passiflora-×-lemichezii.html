Passiflora × lemichezii

Passiflora × lemichezii, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a hybrid species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate crown-like structures. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Passiflora × lemichezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
