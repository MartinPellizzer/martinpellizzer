Polygonum mite subsp. serrulatum

Polygonum mite subsp.

serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
