Phegopteris hydrophila

Phegopteris hydrophila, commonly known as the water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the eastern United States.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically submerged or floating. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phegopteris hydrophila plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
