Prunus pumila var. cuneata

Prunus pumila var. cuneata, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cherry, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces delicate pink or white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and seasonal blooms, adding a refined touch to garden landscapes.
