Planchonella chrysophylla

Planchonella chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking golden color as they mature. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
