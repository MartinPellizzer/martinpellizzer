Primula gageana

Primula gageana, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb with basal leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Primula gageana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
