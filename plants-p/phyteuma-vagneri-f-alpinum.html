Phyteuma vagneri f. alpinum

Phyteuma vagneri f. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. The plant features tall, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple bell-shaped flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
