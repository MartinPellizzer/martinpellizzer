Pinus taiwanensis var. taiwanensis

Pinus taiwanensis var. taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the pine family (Pinaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces long, slender cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by wind. Its needles are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, arranged in pairs, and retain their color year-round.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a significant role in the ecological landscape of its native habitat.
