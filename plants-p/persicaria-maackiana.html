Persicaria maackiana

Persicaria maackiana, commonly known as Maack's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and flowers. The plant features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

Persicaria maackiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
