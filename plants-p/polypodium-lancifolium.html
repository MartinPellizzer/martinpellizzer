Polypodium lancifolium

Polypodium lancifolium, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that are often divided into segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Polypodium lancifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
