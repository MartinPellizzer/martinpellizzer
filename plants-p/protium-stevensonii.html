Protium stevensonii

Protium stevensonii, commonly known as the Stevenson's tamanu, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
