Piptadenia buchananii

Piptadenia buchananii, commonly known as the African locust bean tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate, and its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds, which are used in various traditional foods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in savanna and woodland areas.

The plant also has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
