Pediocactus bradyi subsp. winkleri

Pediocactus bradyi subsp.

winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's paddle cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the Sonoran Desert. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
