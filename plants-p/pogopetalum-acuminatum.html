Pogopetalum acuminatum

Pogopetalum acuminatum, commonly known as the spiked pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
