Passiflora eggersii

Passiflora eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces blue or black berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora eggersii is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
