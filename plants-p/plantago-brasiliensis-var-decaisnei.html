Plantago brasiliensis var. decaisnei

Plantago brasiliensis var. decaisnei, commonly known as the Brazilian plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. The plant features rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

This variant is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
