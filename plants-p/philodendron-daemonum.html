Philodendron daemonum

Philodendron daemonum, commonly known as the Devil's Ivy, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with creamy or white markings. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to climb and anchor itself to surfaces. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Philodendron daemonum is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.
