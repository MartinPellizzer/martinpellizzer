Pedilanthus tithymaloides subsp. angustifolius

Pedilanthus tithymaloides subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed arrowroot, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces a starchy, edible root that is used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm climate.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical regions.
