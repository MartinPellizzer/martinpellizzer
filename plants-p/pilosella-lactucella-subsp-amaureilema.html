Pilosella lactucella subsp. amaureilema

Pilosella lactucella subsp.

amaureilema, commonly known as the whitehead mountain pearly everlet, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are basal, rough, and hairy, with a distinctive silvery-white coloration on the underside.

The flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal in specialized horticulture.
