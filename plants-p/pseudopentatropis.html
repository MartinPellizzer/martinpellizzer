Pseudopentatropis

Pseudopentatropis, commonly known as the false five-lobed orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique floral structures, which often feature five distinct lobes on the petals. They are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are usually elongated and narrow, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

Their flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to their ecological significance.
