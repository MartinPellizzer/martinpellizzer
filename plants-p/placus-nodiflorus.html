Placus nodiflorus

Placus nodiflorus, commonly known as the clustered-flower plant, is a herbaceous species characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer. It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The species is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plant alternatives.
