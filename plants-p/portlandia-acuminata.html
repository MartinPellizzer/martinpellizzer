Portlandia acuminata

Portlandia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped pohuehue, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
