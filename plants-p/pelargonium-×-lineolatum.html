Pelargonium × lineolatum

Pelargonium × lineolatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Pelargonium × lineolatum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
