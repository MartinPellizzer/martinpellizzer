Pulmonaria longifolia

Pulmonaria longifolia, commonly known as the lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features distinctive, hairy, basal leaves that are often lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Pulmonaria longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
