Plinia spirito-sanctensis

Plinia spirito-sanctensis, commonly known as the Holy Spirit Plinia, is a species of plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible berries that are dark purple when mature. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Plinia spirito-sanctensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
