Pelargonium × smithii

Pelargonium × smithii, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its compact size and continuous flowering.

Pelargonium × smithii is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
