Pelargonium setosum

Pelargonium setosum, commonly known as the hairy geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium setosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
