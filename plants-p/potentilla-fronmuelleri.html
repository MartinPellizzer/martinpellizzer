Potentilla fronmuelleri

Potentilla fronmuelleri, commonly known as Fronmueller's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of soft, white hair.

Potentilla fronmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
