Phyllanthus warburgii

Phyllanthus warburgii, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed, often used in traditional medicine.
