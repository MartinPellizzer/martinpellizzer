Piper subnudilimbum

Piper subnudilimbum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are typically small berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
