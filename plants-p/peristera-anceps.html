Peristera anceps

Peristera anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, upright stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Peristera anceps is often found in rocky soils and open woodland areas, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
