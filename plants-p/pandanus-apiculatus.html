Pandanus apiculatus

Pandanus apiculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces fragrant, spadix-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional ceremonies. Its fibrous roots and sturdy stems make it adaptable to various coastal and wetland environments.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and has cultural significance in many regions.
