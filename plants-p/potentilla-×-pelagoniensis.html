Potentilla × pelagoniensis

Potentilla × pelagoniensis, commonly known as the Pelagonia, is a hybrid shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy, semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features finely cut, dark green foliage that provides a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Potentilla × pelagoniensis is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
