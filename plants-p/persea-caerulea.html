Persea caerulea

Persea caerulea, commonly known as the little apple or the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by orange to red fruits. The fruit has a sweet, custard-like pulp and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

It is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in furniture-making.
