Prunella cretensis

Prunella cretensis, commonly known as the Crete skullcap, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found on the island of Crete.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Prunella cretensis is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, often growing in limestone soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
