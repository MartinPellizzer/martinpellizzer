Paspalum sanguinale var. cruciatum

Paspalum sanguinale var. cruciatum, commonly known as the bloodgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish or purplish inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various wetland environments.
