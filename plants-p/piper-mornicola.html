Piper mornicola

Piper mornicola, commonly known as the Mornicola pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and therapeutic uses.
