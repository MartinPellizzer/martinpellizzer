Paronychia arabica subsp. cossoniana

Paronychia arabica subsp.

cossoniana, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
