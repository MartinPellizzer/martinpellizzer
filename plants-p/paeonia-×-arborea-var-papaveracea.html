Paeonia × arborea var. papaveracea

Paeonia × arborea var. papaveracea, commonly known as the Persian peony, is a hybrid species that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent plants.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. Its foliage consists of deeply lobed, palmately compound leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence.

The Persian peony is also valued for its fragrance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
