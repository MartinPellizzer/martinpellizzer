Pyrus aucuparia f. major

Pyrus aucuparia f. major, commonly known as the large-leafed common pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its large, oval leaves that are typically a dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruit in late summer. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

This variety is relatively hardy and can tolerate a range of soil conditions.
