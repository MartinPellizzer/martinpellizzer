Phyllota barbata

Phyllota barbata, commonly known as the barbed phyllota, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, barbed stipules, which give it its common name. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with deep taproots and hairy foliage to reduce water loss.

Phyllota barbata is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
