Pharmacosycea anthelmintica

Pharmacosycea anthelmintica, commonly known as the medicinal wormwood, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for its aromatic leaves and roots, which contain various bioactive compounds. Its pharmacological properties are primarily attributed to the presence of sesquiterpene lactones and other secondary metabolites.

It is traditionally used in herbal medicine for its anthelmintic and anti-inflammatory effects.
