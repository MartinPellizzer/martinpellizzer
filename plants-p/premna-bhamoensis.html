Premna bhamoensis

Premna bhamoensis, commonly known as the Bhamo Premna, is a shrub or small tree native to the humid evergreen forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is distinguished by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.
