Polygonum schimperi

Polygonum schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its swollen nodes and small, clustered flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple tinge, and it produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Polygonum schimperi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
