Piper lineatum var. cordulatum

Piper lineatum var. cordulatum, commonly known as the cordate pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its botanical name "cordulatum.". The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible berries.
