Poa trichoclada

Poa trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Poa trichoclada is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
