Polygonum psammophilum

Polygonum psammophilum, commonly known as sandblown knotweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.

Polygonum psammophilum is often found in coastal and desert environments where it contributes to the ecological balance of these ecosystems.
