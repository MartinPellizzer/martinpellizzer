Potentilla argentea var. tenuiloba

Potentilla argentea var. tenuiloba, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and meadow habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
