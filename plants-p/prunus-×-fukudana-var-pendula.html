Prunus × fukudana var. pendula

Prunus × fukudana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping peach, is a hybrid deciduous tree known for its distinctive cascading branches.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its pendulous growth habit makes it a popular choice for creating dramatic visual effects in horticultural settings.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal.
