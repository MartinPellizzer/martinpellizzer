Pavetta assimilis var. pubescens

Pavetta assimilis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features opposite, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and have five white or pale pink petals. It produces small, spherical berries that are yellow when mature.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
