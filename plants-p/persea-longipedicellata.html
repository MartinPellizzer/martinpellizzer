Persea longipedicellata

Persea longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-fruited persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elongated, clustered fruits that mature to a dark purple color. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy, dark green surface with a distinctive aromatic scent. The tree produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Its fruits are edible, though they are typically consumed when fully ripe and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.
