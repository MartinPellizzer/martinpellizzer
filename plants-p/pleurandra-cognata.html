Pleurandra cognata

Pleurandra cognata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color, followed by the development of small, three-lobed seed heads.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to form dense clumps make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
