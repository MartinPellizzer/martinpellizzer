Polygala vulgaris var. depressa

Polygala vulgaris var. depressa, commonly known as the low polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelike inflorescences, usually in shades of pink or purple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
