Pilosella angustifolia subsp. subglacialis

Pilosella angustifolia subsp.

subglacialis, commonly known as the glacier pilosella, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the European Alps and the Pyrenees. It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, featuring a fibrous root system and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine, white hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh mountain environments.
