Portulaca teretifolia subsp. cubensis

Portulaca teretifolia subsp.

cubensis, commonly known as Cuban portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba. It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
