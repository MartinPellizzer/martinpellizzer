Pycreus flavescens var. castaneus

Pycreus flavescens var. castaneus, commonly known as the red-flowered pycreus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of alpine flora.
