Prestonia solanifolia

Prestonia solanifolia, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking purple and violet flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Prestonia solanifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
