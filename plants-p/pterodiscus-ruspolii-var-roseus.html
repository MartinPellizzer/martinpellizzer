Pterodiscus ruspolii var. roseus

Pterodiscus ruspolii var. roseus, commonly known as the rose-colored pterodiscus, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to rose-red flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with broad, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its unique floral coloration and adaptability make it a desirable species for both gardeners and botanists.
