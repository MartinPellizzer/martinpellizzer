Pothuava spicata

Pothuava spicata, commonly known as the spiky papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its wetland habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, blooming in clusters along the stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in still or slow-moving water bodies.

Pothuava spicata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms and stabilizing shoreline sediments.
