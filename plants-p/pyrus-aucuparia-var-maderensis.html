Pyrus aucuparia var. maderensis

Pyrus aucuparia var. maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple native to Central Europe.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, often twisted branches. The leaves are oval with a serrated margin, and the tree produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, tart berries in autumn. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to a range of garden conditions.
