Piper edwallii

Piper edwallii, commonly known as Edwall's pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, three-lobed berries.

Piper edwallii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
