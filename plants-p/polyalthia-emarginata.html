Polyalthia emarginata

Polyalthia emarginata, commonly known as the emarginate flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking plant. The tree has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its bark is smooth and light brown, often with faint vertical lenticels.

Polyalthia emarginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical regions.
