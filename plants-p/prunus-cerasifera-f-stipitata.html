Prunus cerasifera f. stipitata

Prunus cerasifera f. stipitata, commonly known as the velvet cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often used in jams and desserts.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique leaf appearance.
