Pronephrium fideleae

Pronephrium fideleae, commonly known as the faithful kidney fern, is a unique species within the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that exhibit a distinctive, almost lace-like pattern of veins. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

The botanical study of Pronephrium fideleae provides insights into the evolutionary adaptations of ferns in diverse ecological niches.
