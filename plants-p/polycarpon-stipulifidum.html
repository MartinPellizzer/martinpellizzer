Polycarpon stipulifidum

Polycarpon stipulifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are often stipulate, featuring leaf-like structures at the base of the petiole.

Polycarpon stipulifidum is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
