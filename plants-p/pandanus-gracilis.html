Pandanus gracilis

Pandanus gracilis, commonly known as the slender pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that eventually develop into fibrous, spiky fruits. Its root system is fibrous and well adapted to grow in sandy or coastal soils.

Pandanus gracilis is often found in wetland areas and is valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
