Prunus × mohacsyana

Prunus × mohacsyana, commonly known as the Mohács cherry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus cerasifera and Prunus lusitanica.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that are typically edible and have a tart flavor. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
