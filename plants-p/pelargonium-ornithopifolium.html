Pelargonium ornithopifolium

Pelargonium ornithopifolium, commonly known as the bird's foot geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble a bird's foot. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
