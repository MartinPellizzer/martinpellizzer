Peperomia catharinensis

Peperomia catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peperomia, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia catharinensis is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
