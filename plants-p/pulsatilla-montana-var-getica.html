Pulsatilla montana var. getica

Pulsatilla montana var. getica, commonly known as the Getic pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe, where it plays a role in early pollination by bees and other insects.
