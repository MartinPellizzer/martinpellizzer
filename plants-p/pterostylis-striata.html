Pterostylis striata

Pterostylis striata, commonly known as the striped greenhood, is an orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a single, erect stem and a single, often resupinate leaf. The plant produces a single, greenish-brown flower with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understoreys.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving small flies.
