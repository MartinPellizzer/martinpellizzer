Phyllanthus anomalus subsp. erythroxyloides

Phyllanthus anomalus subsp.

erythroxyloides, commonly known as red-wood phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has smooth, often reddish bark and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
