Ptarmica alexandri-regis

Ptarmica alexandri-regis, commonly known as the Alexandrian cow-wheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a creeping habit with stolons that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Ptarmica alexandri-regis is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
