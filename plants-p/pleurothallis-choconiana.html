Pleurothallis choconiana

Pleurothallis choconiana, commonly known as the Choconia orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the humid, shaded environments where it thrives.

This orchid is often found growing on trees or rocks, showcasing its epiphytic nature.
