Phyllanthus leonardianus

Phyllanthus leonardianus, commonly known as the Leonard's spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered, often yellow or greenish in color.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
