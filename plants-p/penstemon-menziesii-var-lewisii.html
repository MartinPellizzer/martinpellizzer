Penstemon menziesii var. lewisii

Penstemon menziesii var. lewisii, commonly known as Lewis's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
