Piper longipilum

Piper longipilum, commonly known as the long-haired pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
