Panicum urvilleanum f. subpilosum

Panicum urvilleanum f. subpilosum, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It features narrow, linear leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a soft, silky appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy inflorescences that are often wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
