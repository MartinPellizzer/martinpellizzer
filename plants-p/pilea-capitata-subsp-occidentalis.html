Pilea capitata subsp. occidentalis

Pilea capitata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the "Chinese money plant," is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia. It features rounded, fleshy leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy sheen.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for tabletop displays and as a decorative houseplant.
