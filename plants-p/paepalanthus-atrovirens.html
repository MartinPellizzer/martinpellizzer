Paepalanthus atrovirens

Paepalanthus atrovirens, commonly known as the black-green puya, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes, where it thrives in cool, dry climates. This plant is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

It is a perennial species that produces a single bloom per year, making it a unique and eye-catching addition to botanical gardens.
