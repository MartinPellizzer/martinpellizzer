Physaria congesta

Physaria congesta, commonly known as the clustered blite, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Physaria congesta is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
