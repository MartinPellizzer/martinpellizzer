Polyachyrus glandulosus

Polyachyrus glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular polyachyrus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It is native to parts of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations in tropical regions.
