Pometia pinnata f. acuminata

Pometia pinnata f. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed pometia, is a botanical variant of the pometia tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves with acuminate tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, often found in tropical lowland forests. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
