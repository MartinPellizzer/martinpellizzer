Plumbagidium roseum

Plumbagidium roseum, commonly known as the pink plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Plumbagaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, silver-gray leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
