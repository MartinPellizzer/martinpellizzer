Pera heterodoxa

Pera heterodoxa, commonly known as the exotic leaf plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often a striking shade of green with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruit that resembles a small, elongated capsule. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
