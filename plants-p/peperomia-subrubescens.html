Peperomia subrubescens

Peperomia subrubescens, commonly known as the red-veined peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish vein pattern, which adds to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for container cultivation.
