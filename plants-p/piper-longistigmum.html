Piper longistigmum

Piper longistigmum, commonly known as the long-stigma pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic compound responsible for its distinct flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly bitter taste.

The plant is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
