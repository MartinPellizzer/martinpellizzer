Philadelphus coronarius var. acuminatus

Philadelphus coronarius var. acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
