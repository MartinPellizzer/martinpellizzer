Paphiopedilum lawrenceanum

Paphiopedilum lawrenceanum, commonly known as the Lawrences' orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, featuring a specialized lip that aids in pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and green hues, with a velvety texture.

Paphiopedilum lawrenceanum prefers warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
