Populus simonii f. przewalskii

Populus simonii f. przewalskii, commonly known as the Przewalski's poplar, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of the poplar genus.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of western China, particularly in the Xinjiang Uygur Autonomous Region. This plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. It has a unique morphology, including a tall, straight trunk and a crown that is often sparse and open.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered an important species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
