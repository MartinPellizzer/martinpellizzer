Potentilla canescens f. gracilis

Potentilla canescens f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers.

It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and other damp habitats. The plant is part of the Rosaceae family and is known for its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
