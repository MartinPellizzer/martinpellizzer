Prunus sachalinensis f. multipes

Prunus sachalinensis f. multipes, commonly known as the multi-flowered Japanese apricot, is a unique variant of the Japanese apricot tree.

It is characterized by its profuse flowering, with multiple flowers emerging from each axil, creating a striking display of white or pink blossoms. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its botanical features include simple, ovate leaves and small, edible fruits that follow the showy flowers.
