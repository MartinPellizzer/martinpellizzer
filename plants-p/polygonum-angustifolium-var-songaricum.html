Polygonum angustifolium var. songaricum

Polygonum angustifolium var. songaricum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including China and Mongolia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along stream banks and in wet meadows. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
