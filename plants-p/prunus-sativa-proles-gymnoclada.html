Prunus sativa proles gymnoclada

Prunus sativa proles gymnoclada, commonly known as the white cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, barkless trunk and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its visual distinctiveness.
