Passiflora erubescens

Passiflora erubescens, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble petals, giving it a dramatic and showy appearance. The plant produces intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its fruits are edible, turning from green to orange-red as they mature, and are often consumed fresh or used in jams.

Passiflora erubescens is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
