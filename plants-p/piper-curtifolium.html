Piper curtifolium

Piper curtifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
