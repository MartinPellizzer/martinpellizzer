Papaver alboroseum var. elongatum

Papaver alboroseum var. elongatum, commonly known as the white poppy or white Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elongated, slender petals and a distinctive, narrow capsule that contains its seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
