Potamogeton crispus var. laevis

Potamogeton crispus var. laevis, commonly known as smooth pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy or sandy bottoms.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
