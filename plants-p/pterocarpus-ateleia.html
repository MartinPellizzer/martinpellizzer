Pterocarpus ateleia

Pterocarpus ateleia, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich reddish-brown color and a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, bean-like pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in musical instrument making.
