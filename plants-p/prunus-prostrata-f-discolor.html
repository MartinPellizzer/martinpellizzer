Prunus prostrata f. discolor

Prunus prostrata f. discolor, commonly known as the variegated Japanese maple, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to Japan.

It features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of white, green, and pink, adding visual interest to gardens. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
