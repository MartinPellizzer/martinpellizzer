Petrophile fastigiata

Petrophile fastigiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and woodland habitats, often on well-drained soils. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with a narrow, upright growth habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
