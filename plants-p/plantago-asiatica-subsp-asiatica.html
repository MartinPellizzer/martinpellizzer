Plantago asiatica subsp. asiatica

Plantago asiatica subsp.

asiatica, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a perennial herb native to Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes.

The plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
