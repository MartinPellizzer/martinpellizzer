Paxia

Paxia, commonly known as the rock fig, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Paxia produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color.

The fruit is a fig-like structure that is edible and often used in local cuisines.
