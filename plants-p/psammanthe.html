Psammanthe

Psammanthe, commonly known as the sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its scent and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are compound, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

The tree is also known for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant species in both ecological and economic contexts.
