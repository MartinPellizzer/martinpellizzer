Paspalidium distantiflorum

Paspalidium distantiflorum, commonly known as the distant-flowered paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its inflorescence is a panicle with distant flowering branches.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
