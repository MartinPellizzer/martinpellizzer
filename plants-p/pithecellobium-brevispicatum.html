Pithecellobium brevispicatum

Pithecellobium brevispicatum, commonly known as the short-spined velvet bean, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a protein source. Its growth habit includes thorned stems and is often found in disturbed soils and secondary forests.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
