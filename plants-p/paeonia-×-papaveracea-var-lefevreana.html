Paeonia × papaveracea var. lefevreana

Paeonia × papaveracea var. lefevreana, commonly known as the Lefevre peony, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom structure. The plant features robust, upright stems and heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that are dark green and velvety in texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The Lefevre peony is valued for its long blooming period and hardy nature, contributing to its status as a prized addition to horticultural collections.
