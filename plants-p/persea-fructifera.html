Persea fructifera

Persea fructifera, commonly known as the tropical persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when fully mature, though they are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide shade in warm environments.
