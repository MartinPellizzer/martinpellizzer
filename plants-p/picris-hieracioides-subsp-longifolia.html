Picris hieracioides subsp. longifolia

Picris hieracioides subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by elongated, bristly seed heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its longer, more slender leaves compared to other forms of Picris hieracioides.
