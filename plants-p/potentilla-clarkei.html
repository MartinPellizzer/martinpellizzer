Potentilla clarkei

Potentilla clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Potentilla clarkei is often found in alpine and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
