Polypodium angustilobum

Polypodium angustilobum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed huperzia, is a fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, elongated segments. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
