Poa fertilis

Poa fertilis, commonly known as the fertile grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Poa fertilis is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for livestock and supporting various insect and bird populations.
