Protea canaliculata

Protea canaliculata, commonly known as the sugarbowl protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a bowl, hence its common name. The plant features a central stem surrounded by a cluster of colorful bracts that can range from white to pink and red. Native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Protea canaliculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in the conservation of South Africa's fynbos vegetation.
