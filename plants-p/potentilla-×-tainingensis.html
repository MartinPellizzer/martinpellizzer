Potentilla × tainingensis

Potentilla × tainingensis, commonly known as the Taining hybrid cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant species in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically features basal leaves that are palmately divided, with hairy surfaces that aid in moisture retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
