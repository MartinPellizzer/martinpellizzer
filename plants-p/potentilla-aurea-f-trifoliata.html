Potentilla aurea f. trifoliata

Potentilla aurea f. trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf potentilla, is a variant of the golden cinquefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic value.
