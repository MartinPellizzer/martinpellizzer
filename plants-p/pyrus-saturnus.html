Pyrus saturnus

Pyrus saturnus, commonly known as the Saturnian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are dark green and serrated, providing visual interest throughout the growing season.

Due to its limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
