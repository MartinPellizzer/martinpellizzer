Passiflora magnoliifolia

Passiflora magnoliifolia, commonly known as the magnolia leaf passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of magnolia trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a unique central reproductive organ. Its fruit is a bright orange or red berry, often containing several seeds.

Passiflora magnoliifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in warm climates for its visual appeal.
