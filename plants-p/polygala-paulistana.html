Polygala paulistana

Polygala paulistana, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds with silky hairs.

Polygala paulistana is often used in traditional medicine and is also an important plant for pollinators.
