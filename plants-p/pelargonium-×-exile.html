Pelargonium × exile

Pelargonium × exile, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of flowers. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium × exile is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.
