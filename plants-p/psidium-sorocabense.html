Psidium sorocabense

Psidium sorocabense, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and edible, fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and pale.

The fruit, which resembles a small guava, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
