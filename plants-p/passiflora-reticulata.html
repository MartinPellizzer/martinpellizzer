Passiflora reticulata

Passiflora reticulata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues, along with prominent filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora reticulata thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
