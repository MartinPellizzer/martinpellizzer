Pygeum stipulaceum

Pygeum stipulaceum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, almond-like seeds.

Pygeum stipulaceum is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
