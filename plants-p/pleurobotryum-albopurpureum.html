Pleurobotryum albopurpureum

Pleurobotryum albopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-veined pleurobotryum, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that display a striking combination of white and purple hues. The flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically have a pale greenish-yellow color with prominent purple veins. This species is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
