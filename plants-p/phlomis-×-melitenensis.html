Phlomis × melitenensis

Phlomis × melitenensis, commonly known as the hybrid everlasting phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Phlomis × melitenensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
