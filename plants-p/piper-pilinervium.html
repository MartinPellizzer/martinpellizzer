Piper pilinervium

Piper pilinervium, commonly known as the slender-veined pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and small, opposite leaves with finely veined surfaces. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
