Primula nivalis var. sinensis

Primula nivalis var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it retain moisture in its alpine habitat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
