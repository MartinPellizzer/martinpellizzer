Petunia longiflora

Petunia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered petunia, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue, violet, or white, with a distinctive long throat that extends beyond the petals. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Petunia longiflora is known for its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season, adding continuous color to landscapes.
