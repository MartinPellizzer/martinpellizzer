Parodia compressa

Parodia compressa, commonly known as the "Compressed Cactus" or "Cactus of the Desert," is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, cylindrical cactus with a ribbed stem that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for harsh, dry environments.

Parodia compressa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
