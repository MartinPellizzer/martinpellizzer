Phyllanthus submarginalis

Phyllanthus submarginalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features simple, opposite leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Phyllanthus submarginalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
