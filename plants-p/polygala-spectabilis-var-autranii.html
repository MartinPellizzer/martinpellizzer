Polygala spectabilis var. autranii

Polygala spectabilis var. autranii, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
