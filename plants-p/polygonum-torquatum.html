Polygonum torquatum

Polygonum torquatum, commonly known as the purple tamarisk, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its stout, woody stems that often become reddish or purplish with age. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum torquatum is typically found in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
