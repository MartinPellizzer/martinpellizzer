Polypodium caudatoattenuatum

Polypodium caudatoattenuatum, commonly known as the long-tailed fern, is a species of evergreen fern characterized by its distinctive, elongated fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant features a creeping rhizome with dense, clustered fronds that are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a long, tapering shape that gives it its common name.

Polypodium caudatoattenuatum thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
