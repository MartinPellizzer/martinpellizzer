Portulaca reflexa

Portulaca reflexa, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It is a low-growing, succulent annual that features fleshy, erect stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Portulaca reflexa is often found in sandy soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
