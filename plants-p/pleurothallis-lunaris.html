Pleurothallis lunaris

Pleurothallis lunaris, commonly known as the moon orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a white or pale-colored appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom during the cooler months, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

The plant's unique morphology and limited distribution contribute to its status as a rare and specialized orchid.
