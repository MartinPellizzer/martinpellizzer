Phalaenopsis equestris f. aurea

Phalaenopsis equestris f. aurea, commonly known as the golden phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits striking golden-yellow petals and a white lip.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical climates. The plant is prized for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several months under proper care. It requires bright, indirect light and consistent humidity to flourish.

Its unique coloration makes it a popular choice for both indoor gardening and floral arrangements.
