Potamogeton nuttallii var. ramosus

Potamogeton nuttallii var. ramosus, commonly known as the ramose pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender stems that branch profusely, giving it a bushy appearance. Its small, oval-shaped leaves are arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
