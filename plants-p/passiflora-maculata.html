Passiflora maculata

Passiflora maculata, commonly known as the spotted passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The flowers are typically blue or purple with yellow and white markings, and they emit a sweet fragrance. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
