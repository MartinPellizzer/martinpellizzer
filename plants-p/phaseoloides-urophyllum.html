Phaseoloides urophyllum

Phaseoloides urophyllum, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is often studied for its ecological impact and potential as a bioindicator plant.
