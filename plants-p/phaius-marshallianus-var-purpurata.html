Phaius marshallianus var. purpurata

Phaius marshallianus var. purpurata, commonly known as the purple-tinged marsh orchid, is a variant of the marsh orchid native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous orchid-like flowers. The flowers typically display a striking combination of green and purple hues, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
