Pimelea laevigata var. alpina

Pimelea laevigata var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine Wallaby Bush, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with grey-green, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
