Prunus oxycarpa

Prunus oxycarpa, commonly known as the sour cherry or wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that turn red when ripe, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Prunus oxycarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also notable for its role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
