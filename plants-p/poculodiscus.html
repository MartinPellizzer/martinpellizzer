Poculodiscus

Poculodiscus, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a unique aquatic plant native to the high-altitude lakes of South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow and white, are often associated with spiritual significance in local cultures. Poculodiscus thrives in still, nutrient-rich waters and is adapted to the cold, high-altitude environment.

Its botanical structure includes a thick, spongy stem that supports its massive leaves and supports its reproductive cycle.
