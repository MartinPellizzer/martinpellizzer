Phagnalon subdentatum

Phagnalon subdentatum, commonly known as the lesser thyme or narrow-leaved phagnalon, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge, while its small yellow flowers bloom in summer.

It is a perennial herb that is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
