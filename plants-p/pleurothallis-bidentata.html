Pleurothallis bidentata

Pleurothallis bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety texture, enhancing its aesthetic appeal.

Pleurothallis bidentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
