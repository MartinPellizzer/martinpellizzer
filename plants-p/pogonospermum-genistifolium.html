Pogonospermum genistifolium

Pogonospermum genistifolium, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
