Phyllanthus guineensis

Phyllanthus guineensis, commonly known as African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters in height, featuring opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves and fruits contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Phyllanthus guineensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
