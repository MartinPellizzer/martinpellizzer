Potentilla crassa f. castrilensis

Potentilla crassa f. castrilensis, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically small, yellowish, and clustered in umbels. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Potentilla genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
