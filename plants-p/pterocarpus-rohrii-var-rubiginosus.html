Pterocarpus rohrii var. rubiginosus

Pterocarpus rohrii var. rubiginosus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or green, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
