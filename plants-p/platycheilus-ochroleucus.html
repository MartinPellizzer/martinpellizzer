Platycheilus ochroleucus

Platycheilus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage, with flowers that often display a mix of yellow, green, and white hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, with petals that are often adorned with intricate patterns.

The botanical features of Platycheilus ochroleucus make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical research.
