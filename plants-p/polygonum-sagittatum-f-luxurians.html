Polygonum sagittatum f. luxurians

Polygonum sagittatum f. luxurians, commonly known as the arrow-leaved pondweed, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with ample sunlight. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a valuable wetland indicator and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
