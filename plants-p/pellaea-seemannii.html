Pellaea seemannii

Pellaea seemannii, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, silvery-gray fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are pinnately divided with a soft, fuzzy texture that gives it its common name.

Pellaea seemannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
