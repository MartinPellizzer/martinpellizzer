Plantago xorulensis

Plantago xorulensis, commonly known as the Andean plantain, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its leaves are broad and hairy, adapted to retain moisture in harsh climates.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
