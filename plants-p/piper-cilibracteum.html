Piper cilibracteum

Piper cilibracteum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are berries that mature to a bright red color, often used in local traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
