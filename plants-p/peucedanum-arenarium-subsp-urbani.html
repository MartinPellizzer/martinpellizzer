Peucedanum arenarium subsp. urbani

Peucedanum arenarium subsp.

urbani, commonly known as the urban wormwood, is a subspecies native to dry, rocky habitats in southern Europe. It is a small, herbaceous plant with slender, aromatic stems and finely divided, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. It is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in urban or disturbed environments.
