Primula macrophylla var. atra

Primula macrophylla var. atra, commonly known as the Black Primrose, is a striking variant of the primrose species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

This plant is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are dark green with a silvery underside, and its dark purple to almost black flowers that bloom in late spring. The flowers are typically spurred at the base and have a distinctive, showy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its unique coloration and foliage, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanical enthusiasts.
