Passiflora gossypiifolia

Passiflora gossypiifolia, commonly known as the cottonleaf passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
