Protea micans subsp. trichophylla

Protea micans subsp.

trichophylla, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant characterized by its large, showy inflorescences. It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is native to the Western Cape region.

The plant features dense, silver-gray foliage that provides a striking contrast to its vibrant flower heads. Its flowers are typically composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape.

This species is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
