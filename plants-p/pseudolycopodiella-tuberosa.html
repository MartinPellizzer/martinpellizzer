Pseudolycopodiella tuberosa

Pseudolycopodiella tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, scale-like leaves and produces spore-bearing cones called strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
