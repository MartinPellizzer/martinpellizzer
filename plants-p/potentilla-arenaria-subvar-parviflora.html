Potentilla arenaria subvar. parviflora

Potentilla arenaria subvar. parviflora, commonly known as sand cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to cold climates.

This subshrub is often found in tundra and mountainous environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
