Pradosia subverticillata

Pradosia subverticillata, commonly known as the clustered pradosia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

Pradosia subverticillata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
