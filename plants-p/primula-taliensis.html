Primula taliensis

Primula taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It typically grows in cool, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It belongs to the Primulaceae family, which includes other well-known flowering plants.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
