Pedilanthus carinatus

Pedilanthus carinatus, commonly known as the spiked poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by these showy leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pedilanthus carinatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and seasonal color changes.
