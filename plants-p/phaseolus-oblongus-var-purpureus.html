Phaseolus oblongus var. purpureus

Phaseolus oblongus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinged pods and vibrant green foliage. The plant is a climbing vine that produces edible beans, which are rich in nutrients and commonly used in culinary applications. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary versatility.
