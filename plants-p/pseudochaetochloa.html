Pseudochaetochloa

Pseudochaetochloa, commonly known as silky grass or velvet grass, is a genus of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, soft foliage and often forms dense mats or clumps. The leaves are typically narrow and have a silky texture due to the presence of fine hairs. Pseudochaetochloa species are often found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats.

These plants are valued for their ornamental qualities and are sometimes used in landscaping.
