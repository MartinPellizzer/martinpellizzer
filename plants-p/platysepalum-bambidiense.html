Platysepalum bambidiense

Platysepalum bambidiense, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, flattened sepals that resemble bamboo shoots. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of white to pale yellow hues.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
