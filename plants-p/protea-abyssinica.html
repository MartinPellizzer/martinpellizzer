Protea abyssinica

Protea abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian protea, is a striking flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often showy floral structures. The plant produces large, cone-shaped inflorescences that are typically composed of many small, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are leathery and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Protea abyssinica is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
