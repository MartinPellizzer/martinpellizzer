Pisonia calophylla var. rufescens

Pisonia calophylla var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-leaf pisonia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The leaves are typically leathery and dark green, with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
