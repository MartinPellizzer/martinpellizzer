Plectranthus phulangkaensis

Plectranthus phulangkaensis, commonly known as the Natal mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its aromatic foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative and medicinal plant.
