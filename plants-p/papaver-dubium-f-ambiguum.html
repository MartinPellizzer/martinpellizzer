Papaver dubium f. ambiguum

Papaver dubium f. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous poppy, is a botanical variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its delicate, often pale pink to white flowers and lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

The f. ambiguum form exhibits subtle morphological differences from the typical Papaver dubium, including variations in flower color and leaf structure.
