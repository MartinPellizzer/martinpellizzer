Potamogeton × nitens var. praelongifolius

Potamogeton × nitens var. praelongifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers usually occurring on separate plants.

This variety is often found in shallow waters where it can form dense underwater vegetation.
