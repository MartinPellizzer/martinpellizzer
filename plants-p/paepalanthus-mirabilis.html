Paepalanthus mirabilis

Paepalanthus mirabilis, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant with a unique appearance characterized by its silver-blue foliage and spiny stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
