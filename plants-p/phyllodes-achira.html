Phyllodes achira

Phyllodes achira, commonly known as the achira palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits. Its leaves are also used for thatching and roofing in some regions.

Phyllodes achira is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses in local communities.
