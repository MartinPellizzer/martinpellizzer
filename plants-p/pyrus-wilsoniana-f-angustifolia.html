Pyrus wilsoniana f. angustifolia

Pyrus wilsoniana f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pear, is a botanical variant of the Wilson pear.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and smaller fruit compared to the typical species. This form is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its flowers are white and fragrant, adding aesthetic appeal to gardens.
