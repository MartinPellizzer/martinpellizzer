Phalaroides arundinacea var. picta

Phalaroides arundinacea var. picta, commonly known as the Painted Reed, is a striking variant of the reed grass family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that range from pink to red, creating a visually appealing display. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often green with a subtle sheen, complementing the colorful flower spikes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
