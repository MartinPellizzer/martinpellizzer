Pouteria gillisonii

Pouteria gillisonii, commonly known as the Gillison's sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a fruit similar to a sapodilla, with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, starchy pulp. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
