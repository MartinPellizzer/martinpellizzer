Paphiopedilum canhii

Paphiopedilum canhii, commonly known as the Canh's lady's slipper, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a unique feature among orchids. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its flowers, which can range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom once a year, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and beauty, Paphiopedilum canhii is often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized collections.
