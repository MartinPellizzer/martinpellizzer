Polygala wittebergensis

Polygala wittebergensis, commonly known as the Witteberg milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with distinctive wing-like petals.

It is often found in the Western and Northern Cape provinces of South Africa.
