Pediocactus nigrispinus subsp. indranus

Pediocactus nigrispinus subsp.

indranus, commonly known as the black-spined cactus, is a compact, spine-tipped cactus native to Mexico. It features short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, dark spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its dark foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
