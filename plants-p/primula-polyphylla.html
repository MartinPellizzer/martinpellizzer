Primula polyphylla

Primula polyphylla, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Primula polyphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
