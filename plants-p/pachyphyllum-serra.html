Pachyphyllum serra

Pachyphyllum serra, commonly known as the serrated stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its rosette growth pattern and waxy leaf surface help reduce water loss through evaporation.

Pachyphyllum serra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
