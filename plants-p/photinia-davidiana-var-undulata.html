Photinia davidiana var. undulata

Photinia davidiana var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its attractive, wavy-edged leaves that turn bright red in the autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
