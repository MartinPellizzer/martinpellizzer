Podocarpium laxum

Podocarpium laxum, commonly known as the slender podocarp, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Podocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and conifer-like appearance, with needle-like leaves that are often arranged in spirals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by wind. Its seeds are enclosed in fleshy, berry-like structures, which are dispersed by animals.

Podocarpium laxum is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
