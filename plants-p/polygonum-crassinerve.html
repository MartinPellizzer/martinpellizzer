Polygonum crassinerve

Polygonum crassinerve, commonly known as broad-veined knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and swollen stems. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, broad veins that give it its scientific name. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in late summer.

Polygonum crassinerve is often found in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
