Panopsis magnifructa

Panopsis magnifructa, commonly known as the grandfruit tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, showy flowers and fruit.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The tree is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its nutritional value in traditional cuisines.
