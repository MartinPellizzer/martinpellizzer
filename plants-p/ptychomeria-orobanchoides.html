Ptychomeria orobanchoides

Ptychomeria orobanchoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is native to southern Africa. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green in color. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it can infest a variety of host species, often causing significant damage to crops.

Due to its parasitic nature, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
