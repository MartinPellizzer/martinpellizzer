Primula klattiana

Primula klattiana, commonly known as the Klatt's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Primula klattiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
