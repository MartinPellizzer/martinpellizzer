Psydrax parviflorus

Psydrax parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered psidium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
