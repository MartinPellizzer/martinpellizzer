Paepalanthus diamantinensis

Paepalanthus diamantinensis, commonly known as the diamond leaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, fleshy stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
