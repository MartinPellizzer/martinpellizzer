Pandanus manongarivensis

Pandanus manongarivensis, commonly known as the Manongarivensis pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, palm-like appearance with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its unique morphology and cultural significance make it an important species in the region's ecosystems and local traditions.
