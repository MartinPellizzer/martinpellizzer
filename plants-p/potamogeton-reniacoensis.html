Potamogeton reniacoensis

Potamogeton reniacoensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in parts of Asia and Europe.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, with a smooth surface and parallel venation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
