Parasenecio farfarifolius var. acerinus

Parasenecio farfarifolius var. acerinus, commonly known as the redleaf goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features striking, velvety leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown, depending on the season and environmental conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
