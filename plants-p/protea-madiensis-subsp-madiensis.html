Protea madiensis subsp. madiensis

Protea madiensis subsp.

madiensis, commonly known as the Madiensis protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is endemic to the Eastern Cape region of South Africa. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays an important role in local biodiversity.
