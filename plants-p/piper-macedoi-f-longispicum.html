Piper macedoi f. longispicum

Piper macedoi f. longispicum, commonly known as the longispiculate pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are edible.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, spiculate calyces, which give it its specific epithet.
