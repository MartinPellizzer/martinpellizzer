Picea likiangensis subsp. bhutanica

Picea likiangensis subsp.

bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutan fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and a dense, pyramidal crown.

The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, typically between 2,000 and 3,500 meters.

The species is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its mountainous habitat.
