Primula assamica

Primula assamica, commonly known as the Assam primrose, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture, and it prefers cool, humid conditions.

Primula assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
