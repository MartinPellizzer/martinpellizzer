Persicaria emersa var. asprella

Persicaria emersa var. asprella, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along stream banks.

Its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in aquatic habitats.
