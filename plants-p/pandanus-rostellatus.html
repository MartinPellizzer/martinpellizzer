Pandanus rostellatus

Pandanus rostellatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.

The fruit, which is a fibrous structure containing seeds, is also used in traditional crafts and cuisine.
