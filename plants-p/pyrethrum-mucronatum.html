Pyrethrum mucronatum

Pyrethrum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pyrethrum mucronatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its mild insecticidal properties.
