Pedersenia volubilis

Pedersenia volubilis, commonly known as the creeping pedersenia, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Pedersenia volubilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
