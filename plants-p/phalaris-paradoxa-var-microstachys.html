Phalaris paradoxa var. microstachys

Phalaris paradoxa var. microstachys, commonly known as the dwarf false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
