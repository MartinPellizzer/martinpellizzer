Pithecellobium cauliflorum

Pithecellobium cauliflorum, commonly known as the pineapple tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its clustering inflorescence, where flowers and fruits develop from the axils of the stems. The plant produces edible fruits that resemble small pineapples, hence its common name. Its leaves are compound, with long, narrow leaflets, and it has a woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.
