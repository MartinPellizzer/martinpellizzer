Piper san-joseanum var. panamanum

Piper san-joseanum var. panamanum, commonly known as the Panama pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by berries that mature to a bright red color. The plant is native to Central America, particularly Panama, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal applications.
