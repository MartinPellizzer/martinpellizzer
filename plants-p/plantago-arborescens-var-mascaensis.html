Plantago arborescens var. mascaensis

Plantago arborescens var. mascaensis, commonly known as the Mascaens tree plantain, is a variant of the tree plantain species native to specific regions in Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and large, broad leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on long, branching inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological significance.
