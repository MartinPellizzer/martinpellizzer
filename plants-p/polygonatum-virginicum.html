Polygonatum virginicum

Polygonatum virginicum, commonly known as Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
