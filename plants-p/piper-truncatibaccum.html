Piper truncatibaccum

Piper truncatibaccum, commonly known as the round-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, round leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and agricultural studies in its native habitat.
