Polemonium occidentale var. intermedium

Polemonium occidentale var. intermedium, commonly known as the western Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and native plant landscapes.
