Polygala myrtifolia f. salicifolia

Polygala myrtifolia f. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved milkvetch, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and often intricate leaf arrangements. The plant features narrow, willow-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from pale yellow to reddish hues.
