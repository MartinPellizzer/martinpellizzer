Pseudolaelia pitengoensis

Pseudolaelia pitengoensis, commonly known as the Pitengo orchid, is a species native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
