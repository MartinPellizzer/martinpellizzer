Prostanthera largiflorens

Prostanthera largiflorens, commonly known as the large-flowered mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and showy, pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its fragrant herbs and medicinal properties. Prostanthera largiflorens typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
