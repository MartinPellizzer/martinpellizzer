Pelargonium × vulpinum

Pelargonium × vulpinum, commonly known as the foxglove geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a variety of ornamental characteristics.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is native to southern Africa. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and produces striking, foxglove-shaped flowers in shades of purple, red, and pink. Its foliage is heart-shaped and often has a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium × vulpinum is commonly cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
