Picea pungsanensis

Picea pungsanensis, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its conical shape and dense foliage. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its timber and plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
