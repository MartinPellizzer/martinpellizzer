Primula forbesii subsp. delicata

Primula forbesii subsp.

delicata, commonly known as the delicate primrose, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of southern China. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, adapting to the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland or rock garden settings.
