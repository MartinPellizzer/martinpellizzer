Pandiaka andongensis var. gracilis

Pandiaka andongensis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender pandiaka, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its botanical features include a slender stem and lanceolate foliage, which contribute to its unique appearance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
