Paspalum furcatum var. parviflorum

Paspalum furcatum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered paspalum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically small and clustered. It produces small, pale to brownish flowers, which are arranged in compact, often spiky clusters.

This plant is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.
