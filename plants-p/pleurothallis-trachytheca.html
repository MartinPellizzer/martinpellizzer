Pleurothallis trachytheca

Pleurothallis trachytheca, commonly known as the rough-stemmed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features thin, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a short, narrow spur, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
