Polyosma tubulosa

Polyosma tubulosa, commonly known as the mountain wisteria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
