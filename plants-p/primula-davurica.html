Primula davurica

Primula davurica, commonly known as the Siberian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features hairy, basal leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or woodland habitats.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
