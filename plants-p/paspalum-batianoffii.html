Paspalum batianoffii

Paspalum batianoffii, commonly known as the Batianoff's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
