Pterocarpus papuana

Pterocarpus papuana, commonly known as the Papuan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands, including Papua and New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of many small leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It produces pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
