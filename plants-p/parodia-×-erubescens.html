Parodia × erubescens

Parodia × erubescens, commonly known as the red-tipped parodia, is a hybrid cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, red-tipped bristles that give it its striking appearance. The plant features areoles covered with white, woolly hair, which help protect it from intense sunlight and reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Parodia × erubescens is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
