Pimelea spectabilis var. verschaffeltii

Pimelea spectabilis var. verschaffeltii, commonly known as the showy pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
