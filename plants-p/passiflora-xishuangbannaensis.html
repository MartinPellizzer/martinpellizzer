Passiflora xishuangbannaensis

Passiflora xishuangbannaensis, commonly known as the Xishuangbanna passionflower, is a species native to southern China, particularly the Xishuangbanna region.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments under the canopy of forests.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential use in traditional medicine.
