Psilochilus dressleri

Psilochilus dressleri, commonly known as the Dressler's psilochilus, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in high humidity and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
