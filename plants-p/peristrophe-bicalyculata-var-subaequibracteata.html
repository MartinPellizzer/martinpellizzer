Peristrophe bicalyculata var. subaequibracteata

Peristrophe bicalyculata var. subaequibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bracted piper, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. Its inflorescences consist of clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers, which are often subtended by bracts.

This variety is distinguished by its nearly equal bracts on the flower clusters, giving it its specific epithet.
