Phaseolus albiflorus

Phaseolus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible seeds in some regions.
