Parodia setosa

Parodia setosa, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, globular stems that are covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. Parodia setosa is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
