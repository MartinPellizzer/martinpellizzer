Prunus serrulata var. verecunda

Prunus serrulata var. verecunda, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or Somei Yoshino, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has smooth, reddish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are ovate with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The tree is also valued for its cultural significance in Japan, where it symbolizes the transient beauty of life.
