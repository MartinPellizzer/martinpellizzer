Pilocereus oligolepis

Pilocereus oligolepis, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a spineless cactus with a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and last only one night. Its flowers are pollinated by moths and bats, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
