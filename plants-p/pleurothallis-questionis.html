Pleurothallis questionis

Pleurothallis questionis, commonly known as the question orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a unique coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the wet season.

Pleurothallis questionis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
