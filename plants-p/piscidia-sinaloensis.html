Piscidia sinaloensis

Piscidia sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloan false holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
