Polygonum sericeum var. alpicola

Polygonum sericeum var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine smartweed, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range from pink to red in color. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous environments.

This variant is adapted to cold climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.
