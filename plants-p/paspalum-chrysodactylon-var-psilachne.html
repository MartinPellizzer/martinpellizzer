Paspalum chrysodactylon var. psilachne

Paspalum chrysodactylon var. psilachne, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
