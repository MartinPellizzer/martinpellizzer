Primula bulleyana subsp. bulleyana

Primula bulleyana subsp.

bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulleyan primrose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
