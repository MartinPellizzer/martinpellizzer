Potentilla vindobonensis

Potentilla vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vienna cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Potentilla vindobonensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
