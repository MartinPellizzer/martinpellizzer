Pyrus provincialis

Pyrus provincialis, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, pale bark. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Pyrus provincialis is valued for its ecological importance and historical use in traditional medicine.
