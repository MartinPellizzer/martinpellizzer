Pilosella subcymigera subsp. parciflocciformis

Pilosella subcymigera subsp.

parciflocciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact flower heads and reduced flocculence compared to other members of the species.
