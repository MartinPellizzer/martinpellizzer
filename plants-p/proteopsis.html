Proteopsis

Proteopsis, commonly known as the "false protea," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Proteaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in diverse habitats ranging from fynbos vegetation to rocky outcrops. The plants are characterized by their showy, often colorful inflorescences that resemble those of the protea genus, hence the common name. Proteopsis species typically have narrow, leathery leaves and produce small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in gardens for their unique floral displays.
