Pseudobaccharis rhexioides

Pseudobaccharis rhexioides, commonly known as the Mexican broom, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
