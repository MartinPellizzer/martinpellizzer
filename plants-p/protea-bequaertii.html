Protea bequaertii

Protea bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in the Western Cape region of South Africa. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often visited by birds and insects.

Protea bequaertii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
