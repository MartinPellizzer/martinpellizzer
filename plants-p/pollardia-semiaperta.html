Pollardia semiaperta

Pollardia semiaperta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a loose spike, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Pollardia semiaperta is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
