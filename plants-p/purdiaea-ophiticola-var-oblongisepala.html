Purdiaea ophiticola var. oblongisepala

Purdiaea ophiticola var. oblongisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled phlox, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctively veined pattern. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

The variety oblongisepala is distinguished by its elongated, oblong sepals that give it a unique appearance compared to other varieties of phlox.
