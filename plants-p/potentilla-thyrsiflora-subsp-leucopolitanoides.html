Potentilla thyrsiflora subsp. leucopolitanoides

Potentilla thyrsiflora subsp.

leucopolitanoides, commonly known as the white-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
