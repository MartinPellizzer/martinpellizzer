Platycladus

Platycladus, commonly known as Chinese tallowtree, is a genus of flowering plants in the cypress family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with small, scale-like leaves that are typically dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody fruits containing oval seeds.

Platycladus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional medicine.
