Pyrus cavillea

Pyrus cavillea, commonly known as the Caville's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in parts of Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. The plant is characterized by its deciduous foliage, with leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a serrated margin. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit, a pear-like berry, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, making it of interest to both botanists and potential horticultural cultivators.
