Polygonum chrtekii

Polygonum chrtekii, commonly known as the Chrtek's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish coloration on the underside, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters.

Polygonum chrtekii is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
