Potamogeton × nitens f. robustior

Potamogeton × nitens f. robustior, commonly known as the robust pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and elongated, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinct midrib, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
