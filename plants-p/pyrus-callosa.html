Pyrus callosa

Pyrus callosa, commonly known as the rough pear, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish-green fruits. The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of climates.
