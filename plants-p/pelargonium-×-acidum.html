Pelargonium × acidum

Pelargonium × acidum, commonly known as the sour geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium × acidum is known for its strong, citric scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed.
