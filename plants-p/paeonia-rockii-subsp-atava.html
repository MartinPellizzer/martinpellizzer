Paeonia rockii subsp. atava

Paeonia rockii subsp.

atava, commonly known as the Yunnan peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. It belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its showy, double or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces underground rhizomes that store nutrients.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
