Polygala acicularis

Polygala acicularis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has feathery leaves that are often mistaken for grass, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
