Psacalium megaphyllum

Psacalium megaphyllum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters.

It is native to the rainforests of northern Australia and has adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates. The plant features a straight, unbranched stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Psacalium megaphyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 15 meters tall in its natural habitat.
