Pepinia incarnata

Pepinia incarnata, commonly known as the pink peppercorn, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruits, which resemble tiny peppercorns, are used as a spice and have a mild, citrusy flavor.

The botanical features of Pepinia incarnata make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and culinary study.
