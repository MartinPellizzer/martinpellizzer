Polygala vulgaris subsp. tempskyana

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

tempskyana, commonly known as the Tempskyan milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features slender stems with small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, spiny flowers in shades of pink or white. It is characterized by its unique seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting local pollinator populations.
