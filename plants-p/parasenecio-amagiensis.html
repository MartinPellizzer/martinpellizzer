Parasenecio amagiensis

Parasenecio amagiensis, commonly known as the Amami parasenecio, is a rare flowering plant native to the Amami Islands in Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species. The plant is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected under Japanese conservation laws.
