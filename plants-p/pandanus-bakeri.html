Pandanus bakeri

Pandanus bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the Pandanaceae family.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in regions like Samoa and Tonga. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing near the shoreline where it can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
