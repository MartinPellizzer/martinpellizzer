Podocarpus costalis var. taiwanensis

Podocarpus costalis var. taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan podocarp, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
