Philodendron thaliifolium

Philodendron thaliifolium, commonly known as the "Climbing Philodendron," is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are typically larger than those of other philodendron species. The plant has a climbing habit, using its aerial roots to attach to surfaces and grow upward. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.
