Pseudoberlinia paniculata

Pseudoberlinia paniculata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, seed-bearing structures. It is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in soil stabilization and as a habitat for various wildlife species.
