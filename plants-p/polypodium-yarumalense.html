Polypodium yarumalense

Polypodium yarumalense, commonly known as the Yarumal holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are often bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Polypodium yarumalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
