Pithecellobium daulense

Pithecellobium daulense, commonly known as the Daulen's acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment in degraded ecosystems.
