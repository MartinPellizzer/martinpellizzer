Pilosella hypeurygenes

Pilosella hypeurygenes, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Pilosella hypeurygenes is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
