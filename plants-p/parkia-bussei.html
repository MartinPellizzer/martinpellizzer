Parkia bussei

Parkia bussei, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Parkia bussei thrives in warm, humid climates and is well adapted to various soil types.
