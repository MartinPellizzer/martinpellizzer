Plantago lagopus f. lusitanica

Plantago lagopus f. lusitanica, commonly known as the northern plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its distinct botanical features.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are pollinated by wind. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
