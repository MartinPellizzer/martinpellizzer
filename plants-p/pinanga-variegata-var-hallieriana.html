Pinanga variegata var. hallieriana

Pinanga variegata var. hallieriana, commonly known as the variegated bamboo palm, is a striking evergreen plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that produce clusters of long, narrow, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation. The plant is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings. Its ability to thrive in low-light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

The variegation in its foliage not only enhances its visual appeal but also helps in identifying the species from its closely related varieties.
