Polygala alpestris subsp. alpestris

Polygala alpestris subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant features small, usually blue or purple flowers arranged in spicate inflorescences. Its leaves are often narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
