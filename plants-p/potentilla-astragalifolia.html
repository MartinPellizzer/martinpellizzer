Potentilla astragalifolia

Potentilla astragalifolia, commonly known as the Astragalus-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with leaflets resembling those of the Astragalus genus, giving it its common name.

Potentilla astragalifolia is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
