Potentilla × mixta

Potentilla × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla × mixta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
