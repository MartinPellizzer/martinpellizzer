Piper fresnoense

Piper fresnoense, commonly known as the Fresno pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn red, and they have a peppery flavor similar to other members of the genus.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines.
