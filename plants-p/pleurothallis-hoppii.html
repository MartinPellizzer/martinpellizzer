Pleurothallis hoppii

Pleurothallis hoppii, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically produces small, delicate blooms with a distinctive red or pink tip on the petals, which contributes to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pleurothallis hoppii is often cultivated for its beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
