Pinus devoniana subsp. procera

Pinus devoniana subsp.

procera, commonly known as the Mexican tall pine, is a tall coniferous tree native to the Sierra Madre mountains of Mexico. It is characterized by its straight, tall trunk and long, needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five.

The cones of this species are typically pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
