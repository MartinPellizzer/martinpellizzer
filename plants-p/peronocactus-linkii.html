Peronocactus linkii

Peronocactus linkii, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick, cylindrical stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. Peronocactus linkii is a succulent plant, meaning it stores water in its stems to survive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
