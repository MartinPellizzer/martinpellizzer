Picris benearnensis

Picris benearnensis, commonly known as the Benearn's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disc florets.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
