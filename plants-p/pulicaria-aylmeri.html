Pulicaria aylmeri

Pulicaria aylmeri, commonly known as Aylmer's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with gray-green, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Pulicaria aylmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
