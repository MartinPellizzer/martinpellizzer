Poa berningeri

Poa berningeri, commonly known as Berninger's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous and clustered at the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Poa berningeri is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
