Passiflora foetida var. isthmia

Passiflora foetida var. isthmia, commonly known as the Isthmian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and numerous stamens, often displaying vibrant colors such as yellow, red, and purple. The plant produces edible berries that are typically green when immature and turn red or orange when ripe. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
