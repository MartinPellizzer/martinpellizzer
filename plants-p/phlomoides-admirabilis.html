Phlomoides admirabilis

Phlomoides admirabilis, commonly known as the showy phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, lavender-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family, and is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
