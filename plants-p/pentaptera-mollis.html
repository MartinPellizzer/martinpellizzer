Pentaptera mollis

Pentaptera mollis, commonly known as the soft-leaved pentaptera, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf texture.
