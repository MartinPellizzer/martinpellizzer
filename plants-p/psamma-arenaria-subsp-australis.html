Psamma arenaria subsp. australis

Psamma arenaria subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, fleshy, silver-green leaves adapted to conserve water.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.

Its structure and appearance make it a hardy and resilient plant often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
