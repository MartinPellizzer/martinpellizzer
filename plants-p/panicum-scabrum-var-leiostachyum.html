Panicum scabrum var. leiostachyum

Panicum scabrum var. leiostachyum, commonly known as the rough panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and upright growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in disturbed areas.
