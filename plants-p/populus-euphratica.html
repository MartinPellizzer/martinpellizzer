Populus euphratica

Populus euphratica, commonly known as the Euphrates poplar, is a unique species of willow native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fibrous bark and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant is highly drought-tolerant and can survive in saline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Populus euphratica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for various desert species.
