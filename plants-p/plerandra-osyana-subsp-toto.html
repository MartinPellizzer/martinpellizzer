Plerandra osyana subsp. toto

Plerandra osyana subsp.

toto, commonly known as the Toto lily, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea. It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves and its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. It prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The flowers bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual impact to the plant's appearance.
