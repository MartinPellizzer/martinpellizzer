Poa spiciformis

Poa spiciformis, commonly known as the spike-form bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, spikelet-bearing flowers in the spring.

Poa spiciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
