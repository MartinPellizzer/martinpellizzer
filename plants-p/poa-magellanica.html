Poa magellanica

Poa magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize various habitats. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine and coastal regions.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
