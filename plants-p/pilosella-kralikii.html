Pilosella kralikii

Pilosella kralikii, commonly known as Kralik's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is known for its soft, fuzzy foliage and white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
