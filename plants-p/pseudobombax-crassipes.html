Pseudobombax crassipes

Pseudobombax crassipes, commonly known as the Cuban ceiba, is a large, deciduous tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. The plant plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
