Prunus fordiana var. racemopilosa

Prunus fordiana var. racemopilosa, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and lance-shaped leaves that are typically reddish beneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
