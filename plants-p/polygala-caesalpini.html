Polygala caesalpini

Polygala caesalpini, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small crowns. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
