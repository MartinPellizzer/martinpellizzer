Phyllanthus microdendron var. asper

Phyllanthus microdendron var. asper, commonly known as the rough-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
