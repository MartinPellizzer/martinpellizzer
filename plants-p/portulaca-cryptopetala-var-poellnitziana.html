Portulaca cryptopetala var. poellnitziana

Portulaca cryptopetala var. poellnitziana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved portulaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
