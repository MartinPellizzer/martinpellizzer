Primula moorcroftiana

Primula moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, adapted to the cold and arid conditions of its habitat. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in hardy, low-maintenance plants.
