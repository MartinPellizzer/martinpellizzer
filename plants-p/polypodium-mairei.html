Polypodium mairei

Polypodium mairei, commonly known as the Maire's wall fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a serrated margin. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polypodium mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
