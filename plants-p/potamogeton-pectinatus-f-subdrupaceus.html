Potamogeton pectinatus f. subdrupaceus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. subdrupaceus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spikelets, which are often submerged or partially emergent. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This subspecies is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands, playing an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
