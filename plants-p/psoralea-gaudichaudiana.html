Psoralea gaudichaudiana

Psoralea gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
