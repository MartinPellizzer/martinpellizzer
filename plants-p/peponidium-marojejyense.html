Peponidium marojejyense

Peponidium marojejyense, commonly known as the Marojejy bottle tree, is a unique species native to the eastern rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped trunk and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid environment. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible fruits resembling small melons. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Peponidium marojejyense is also notable for its role in local ecosystems and its potential medicinal uses.
