Philadelphus glabripetalus

Philadelphus glabripetalus, commonly known as the smooth-petaled mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairless or only slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Philadelphus glabripetalus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
