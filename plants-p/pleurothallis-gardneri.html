Pleurothallis gardneri

Pleurothallis gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its intricate floral structure.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
