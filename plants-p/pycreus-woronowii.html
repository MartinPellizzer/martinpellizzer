Pycreus woronowii

Pycreus woronowii, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which resemble flames, and its unique, elongated petals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the surrounding soil, and it thrives in well-drained, organic-rich substrates.

Pycreus woronowii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
