Prunus cerasus aureovariegata

Prunus cerasus aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated cherry, is a decorative shrub or small tree characterized by its striking variegated leaves.

The foliage features a mix of green, white, and yellow tones, creating a visually appealing contrast. It produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are typically pink or white. The fruit is a small cherry, though it is generally not suitable for eating due to its tart flavor.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
