Physospermum giganteum

Physospermum giganteum, commonly known as the giant physosperm, is a striking member of the Physospermaceae family, native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, with a waxy, glossy surface. Its flowers are showy, typically yellow or orange, and are clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
