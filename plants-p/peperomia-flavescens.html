Peperomia flavescens

Peperomia flavescens, commonly known as the golden peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.

Peperomia flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in both indoor and outdoor settings.
