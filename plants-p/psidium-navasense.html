Psidium navasense

Psidium navasense, commonly known as Navas' guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

Psidium navasense is often grown in tropical and subtropical regions for its ornamental value and fruit.
