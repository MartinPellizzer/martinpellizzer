Polylophium gallicum

Polylophium gallicum, commonly known as the French fern, is a species of fern native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The spores are released from the sorus, which is located on the underside of the fronds.
