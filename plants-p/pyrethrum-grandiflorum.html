Pyrethrum grandiflorum

Pyrethrum grandiflorum, commonly known as the African gerbera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with brightly colored petals. The plant typically grows up to 30 cm tall and features a rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. Its flowers bloom in shades of yellow, orange, red, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its natural insecticidal properties due to the presence of pyrethrins in its flowers.
