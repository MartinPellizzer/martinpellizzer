Peperomia dubia

Peperomia dubia, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, waxy, and textured leaves that range in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia dubia is valued for its unique foliage and ease of care, though it is not typically cultivated for its flowers.
