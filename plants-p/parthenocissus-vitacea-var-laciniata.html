Parthenocissus vitacea var. laciniata

Parthenocissus vitacea var. laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, deeply lobed leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage.

Its climbing habit makes it suitable for trellises, walls, and arches.
