Pongamia coriacea

Pongamia coriacea, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves that are oblong and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing oily seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The seeds are rich in oil, making the plant valuable for biofuel and industrial applications.
