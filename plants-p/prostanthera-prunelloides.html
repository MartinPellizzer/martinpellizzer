Prostanthera prunelloides

Prostanthera prunelloides, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Prostanthera prunelloides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
