Phaseolus peduncularis f. typicus

Phaseolus peduncularis f. typicus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible beans that are popular in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its compound leaves and erect growth pattern.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
