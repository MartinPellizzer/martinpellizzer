Polycarpon depressum

Polycarpon depressum, commonly known as the prostrate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and roadsides. The plant features small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems lie close to the ground.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories known for their rapid growth and climbing habits.
