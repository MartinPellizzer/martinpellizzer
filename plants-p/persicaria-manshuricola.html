Persicaria manshuricola

Persicaria manshuricola, commonly known as the Far East knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It has a robust root system that can spread aggressively, making it a potential invasive species in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and stipules.
