Pseudopilocereus pachycladus

Pseudopilocereus pachycladus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are ribbed and often covered in spines. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid environments.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
