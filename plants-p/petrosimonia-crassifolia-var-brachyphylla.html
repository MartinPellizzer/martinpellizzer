Petrosimonia crassifolia var. brachyphylla

Petrosimonia crassifolia var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved petrosimonia, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, prostrate growth with small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its succulent leaves and compact form make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
