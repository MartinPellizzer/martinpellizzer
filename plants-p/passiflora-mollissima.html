Passiflora mollissima

Passiflora mollissima, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are prized for their aromatic aroma and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora mollissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and tropical landscapes.
