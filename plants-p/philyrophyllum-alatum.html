Philyrophyllum alatum

Philyrophyllum alatum, commonly known as the winged philyra, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems with distinctive wing-like structures along the leaf stalks, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Philyrophyllum alatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
