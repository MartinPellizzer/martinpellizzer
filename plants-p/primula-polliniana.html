Primula polliniana

Primula polliniana, commonly known as the Polliniana primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, thriving in woodland or rocky habitats. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Primula polliniana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
