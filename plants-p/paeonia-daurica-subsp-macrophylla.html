Paeonia daurica subsp. macrophylla

Paeonia daurica subsp.

macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas and parts of China. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces large, fleshy roots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful blooms.
