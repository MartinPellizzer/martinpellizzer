Poa acroleuca var. purpurascens

Poa acroleuca var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple tinge on its leaf blades. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the foliage, bearing small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
