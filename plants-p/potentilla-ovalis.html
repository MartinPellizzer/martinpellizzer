Potentilla ovalis

Potentilla ovalis, commonly known as the oval potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and blooming in late summer.

Potentilla ovalis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
