Prunus cantabrigiensis

Prunus cantabrigiensis, commonly known as the Cambridge cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
