Phyllostachys puberula var. muchisasa

Phyllostachys puberula var. muchisasa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms that exhibit a striking golden-yellow coloration, especially under sunlight. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This bamboo is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps, contributing to its aesthetic and ecological value in gardens.
