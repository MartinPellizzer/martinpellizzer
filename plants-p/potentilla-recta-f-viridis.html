Potentilla recta f. viridis

Potentilla recta f. viridis, commonly known as greenleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
