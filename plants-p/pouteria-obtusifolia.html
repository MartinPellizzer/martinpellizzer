Pouteria obtusifolia

Pouteria obtusifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or pataua, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces large, edible fruits that resemble chestnuts, with a hard, spiky shell enclosing a sweet, starchy pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for sustainable agriculture, it is of interest for both ecological and economic purposes.
