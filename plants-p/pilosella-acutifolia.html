Pilosella acutifolia

Pilosella acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, herbaceous growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, often with a serrated edge, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Pilosella acutifolia is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
