Pleurothallis sclarea

Pleurothallis sclarea, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect species.

Pleurothallis sclarea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
