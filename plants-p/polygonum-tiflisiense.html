Polygonum tiflisiense

Polygonum tiflisiense, commonly known as the Caucasian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Polygonum tiflisiense is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
