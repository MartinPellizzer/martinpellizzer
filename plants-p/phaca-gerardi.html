Phaca gerardi

Phaca gerardi, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system.
