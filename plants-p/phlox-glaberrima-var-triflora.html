Phlox glaberrima var. triflora

Phlox glaberrima var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered phlox, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to lavender. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
