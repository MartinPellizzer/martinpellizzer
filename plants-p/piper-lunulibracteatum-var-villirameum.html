Piper lunulibracteatum var. villirameum

Piper lunulibracteatum var. villirameum, commonly known as the white-bracted pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This variant is notable for its unique bract morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
