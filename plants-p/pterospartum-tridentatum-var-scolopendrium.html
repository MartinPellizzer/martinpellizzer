Pterospartum tridentatum var. scolopendrium

Pterospartum tridentatum var. scolopendrium, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its succulent-like leaves. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements.
