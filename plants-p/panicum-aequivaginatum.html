Panicum aequivaginatum

Panicum aequivaginatum, commonly known as inland spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed bracts. It thrives in wetland habitats, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological research.
