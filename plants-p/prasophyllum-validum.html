Prasophyllum validum

Prasophyllum validum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is an Australian endemic plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial herb with a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of pale green to white flowers. The plant is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Prasophyllum validum is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and fragmentation.

Its conservation is crucial for maintaining the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
