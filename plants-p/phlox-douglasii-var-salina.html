Phlox douglasii var. salina

Phlox douglasii var. salina, commonly known as saltbush phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
