Passiflora ceratocarpa

Passiflora ceratocarpa, commonly known as the horned passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora ceratocarpa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
