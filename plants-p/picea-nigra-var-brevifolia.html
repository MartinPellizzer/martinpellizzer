Picea nigra var. brevifolia

Picea nigra var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaf black spruce, is a variant of the black spruce native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more densely packed than those of the typical black spruce. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold, moist environments, often found in boreal forests and swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
