Plantago ruthenica

Plantago ruthenica, commonly known as the Russian plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in meadows, riverbanks, and forest edges. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
