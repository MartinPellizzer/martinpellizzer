Piper pardinum

Piper pardinum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the familiar black, white, and red pepper berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

Piper pardinum is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its potential medicinal properties.
