Phyllanthus emarginatus

Phyllanthus emarginatus, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly lobed or notched base.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.
