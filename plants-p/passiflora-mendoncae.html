Passiflora mendoncae

Passiflora mendoncae, commonly known as the Mendonca passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a prominent crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, sunny environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
