Primula rebeccae

Primula rebeccae, commonly known as Rebecca's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft hair.

Primula rebeccae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
