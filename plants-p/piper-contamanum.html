Piper contamanum

Piper contamanum, commonly known as Surinam pepper or graviola, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that contain seeds, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine.

The fruit and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, including annonaceous acetogenins, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
