Panicum indicum var. vilvoides

Panicum indicum var. vilvoides, commonly known as the silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and panicles that produce small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its delicate appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
