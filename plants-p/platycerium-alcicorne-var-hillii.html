Platycerium alcicorne var. hillii

Platycerium alcicorne var. hillii, commonly known as the Hill's staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, antler-like fronds that resemble the horns of a stag, giving it its common name. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its leaf surfaces. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Its foliage is evergreen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.
