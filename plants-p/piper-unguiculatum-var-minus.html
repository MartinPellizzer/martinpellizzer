Piper unguiculatum var. minus

Piper unguiculatum var. minus, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The fruit, though not widely cultivated, is sometimes used in local cuisines for its mild peppery flavor.
