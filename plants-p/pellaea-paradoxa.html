Pellaea paradoxa

Pellaea paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, upright, and often sparsely foliated fronds. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Pellaea paradoxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
