Polystichopsis muscosa

Polystichopsis muscosa, commonly known as the mountain holly fern, is a perennial fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori surrounded by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in temperate climates.
