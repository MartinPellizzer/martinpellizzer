Paspalum mandiocanum var. stenocarpum

Paspalum mandiocanum var. stenocarpum, commonly known as narrow-carpel paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It is often found in wetland and marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
