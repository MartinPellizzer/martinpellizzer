Phlox diapensioides

Phlox diapensioides, commonly known as creeping phlox, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to eastern North America.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with vibrant clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is evergreen in mild climates, providing year-round visual interest.

It thrives in partial to full shade and is well-suited for woodland gardens or as a groundcover.
