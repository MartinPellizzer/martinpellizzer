Pelexia laxa

Pelexia laxa, commonly known as the slender pelexia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white to pale pink, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Pelexia laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
