Potentilla pulchella var. gracilicaulis

Potentilla pulchella var. gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slenderstem cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, which gives it the species name "cinquefoil.".

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
