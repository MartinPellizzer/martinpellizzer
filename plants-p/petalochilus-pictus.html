Petalochilus pictus

Petalochilus pictus, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features striking, colorful petals that range from pink to red, with intricate patterns that resemble brushstrokes. Its flowers are typically clustered in small inflorescences, and it produces elongated, slender pseudobulbs.

Petalochilus pictus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
