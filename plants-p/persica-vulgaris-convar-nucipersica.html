Persica vulgaris convar. nucipersica

Persica vulgaris convar. nucipersica, commonly known as the domesticated peach.

This variant is a cultivated form of the wild peach, characterized by its large, sweet, and juicy fruits. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Asia, particularly China. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with alternate, ovate leaves and showy pink or white flowers.

Its fruit development is influenced by climate and horticultural practices, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.
