Poa subcaerulea var. anceps

Poa subcaerulea var. anceps, commonly known as the blue grass or blue oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Botanically, it is notable for its compact growth habit and distinctive coloration.
