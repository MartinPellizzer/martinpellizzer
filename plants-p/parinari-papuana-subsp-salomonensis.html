Parinari papuana subsp. salomonensis

Parinari papuana subsp.

salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands parinari, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly the Solomon Islands. It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in lowland and hill forest environments.

The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
