Pycreus muricatus

Pycreus muricatus, commonly known as the hairy pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pycreus muricatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
