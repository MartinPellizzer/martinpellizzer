Piper stenocladum

Piper stenocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
