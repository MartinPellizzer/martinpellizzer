Prunella transsilvanica

Prunella transsilvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, two-lipped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Prunella transsilvanica is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to moderately dry soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
