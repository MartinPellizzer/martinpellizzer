Pilosella tjapomensis

Pilosella tjapomensis, commonly known as the Tjapomen's pearlyweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, often surrounded by soft, white, woolly hairs. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pilosella tjapomensis is adapted to grow in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
