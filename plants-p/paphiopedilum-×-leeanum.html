Paphiopedilum × leeanum

Paphiopedilum × leeanum, commonly known as the hybrid velvet lily, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species.

It is a hybrid resulting from the cross between Paphiopedilum sanderianum and Paphiopedilum micropetalum. This plant is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers with a unique lip structure that resembles a pouch, a feature common in many orchids. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
