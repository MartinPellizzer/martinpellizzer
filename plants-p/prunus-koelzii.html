Prunus koelzii

Prunus koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and it produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
