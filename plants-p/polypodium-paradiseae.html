Polypodium paradiseae

Polypodium paradiseae, commonly known as the paradise fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in forest canopies. The plant features fronds that are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive, feathery appearance. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Polypodium paradiseae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
