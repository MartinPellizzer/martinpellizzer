Pocockia platycarpos

Pocockia platycarpos, commonly known as the broad-fruited pteridium, is a species of fern native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces spores in clusters on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Pocockia platycarpos is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
