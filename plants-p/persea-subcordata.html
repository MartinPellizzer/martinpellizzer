Persea subcordata

Persea subcordata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is highly prized for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
