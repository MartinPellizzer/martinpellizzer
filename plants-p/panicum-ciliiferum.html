Panicum ciliiferum

Panicum ciliiferum, commonly known as the silky finger grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
