Potentilla fruticosa var. vilmoriniana

Potentilla fruticosa var. vilmoriniana, commonly known as the Vilmorin's cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
