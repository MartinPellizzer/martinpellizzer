Poa trivialis subsp. balearica

Poa trivialis subsp.

balearica, commonly known as the Balearic meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balearic Islands. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, tufted stems and narrow, blade-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and coastal dunes.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and supporting local insect populations.
