Pyrus pluripinnata

Pyrus pluripinnata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to pears.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
