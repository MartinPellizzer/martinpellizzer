Passiflora madidiana

Passiflora madidiana, commonly known as the Madidi passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Bolivia and Peru.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for ecological and horticultural studies.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
