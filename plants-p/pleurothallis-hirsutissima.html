Pleurothallis hirsutissima

Pleurothallis hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, often velvety foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
