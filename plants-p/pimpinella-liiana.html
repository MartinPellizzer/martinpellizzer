Pimpinella liiana

Pimpinella liiana, commonly known as the climbing pimpinella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like growth habit and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed schizocarps, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Pimpinella liiana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
