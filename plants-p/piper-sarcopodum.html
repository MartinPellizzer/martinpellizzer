Piper sarcopodum

Piper sarcopodum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit. The plant produces small, green berries that turn dark when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance.

Piper sarcopodum thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
