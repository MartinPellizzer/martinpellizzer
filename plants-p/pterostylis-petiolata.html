Pterostylis petiolata

Pterostylis petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenhood, is an orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, leafy stem and a single, showy greenish-yellow flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers have a unique structure with a labellum that resembles a bird's beak, aiding in pollination by specific insect species.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its habitat.
