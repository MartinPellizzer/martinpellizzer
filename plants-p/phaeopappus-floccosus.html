Phaeopappus floccosus

Phaeopappus floccosus, commonly known as the white pappus or white fluff plant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and a distinctive white, fluffy pappus that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is usually gray-green, and it thrives in full sun.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to arid conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
