Populus candicans var. ontariensis

Populus candicans var. ontariensis, commonly known as the Ontario poplar, is a variant of the trembling aspen.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, particularly found in the southern regions of Ontario. The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This species plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
