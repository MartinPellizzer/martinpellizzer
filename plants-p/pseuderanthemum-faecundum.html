Pseuderanthemum faecundum

Pseuderanthemum faecundum, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises in gardens.
