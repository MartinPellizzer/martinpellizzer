Pterostylis frenchii

Pterostylis frenchii, commonly known as the French's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that resembles a hood.

Pterostylis frenchii is a small plant, often less than 20 centimeters tall, and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
