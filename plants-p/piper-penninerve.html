Piper penninerve

Piper penninerve, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct venation pattern. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
