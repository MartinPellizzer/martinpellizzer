Planchonella moluccana

Planchonella moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are showy, with five petals, and are typically yellow or orange in color.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
