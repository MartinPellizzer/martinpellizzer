Potentilla × diversifolia var. multisecta

Potentilla × diversifolia var. multisecta, commonly known as the multisection cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
