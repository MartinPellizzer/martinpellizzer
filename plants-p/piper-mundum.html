Piper mundum

Piper mundum, commonly known as the sacred pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their pungent and medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
