Primula × lempergii

Primula × lempergii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring cool temperatures for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form compact, attractive mounds of foliage and flowers.
