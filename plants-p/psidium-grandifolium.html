Psidium grandifolium

Psidium grandifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by birds.
