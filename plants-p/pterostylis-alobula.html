Pterostylis alobula

Pterostylis alobula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenhood, is an orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces a single, pale green to white flower with a distinctive hood-like structure. It blooms during the late winter to early spring, often in response to rainfall.

Pterostylis alobula is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in moist, shaded environments such as undergrowth in forests or along stream banks.
