Parinari congensis

Parinari congensis, commonly known as the Congo apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
