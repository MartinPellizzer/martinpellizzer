Pyrrocoma racemosa var. congesta

Pyrrocoma racemosa var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered pygmy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of only a few inches, with small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant produces its flowers in dense clusters, giving it its common name, and it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in rock gardens.
