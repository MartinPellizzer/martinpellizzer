Passiflora ichthyura

Passiflora ichthyura, commonly known as the fish passionflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
