Potentilla mirabilis

Potentilla mirabilis, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with five oval leaflets and produces showy, golden-yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to stabilize soil in wet environments.

Potentilla mirabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
