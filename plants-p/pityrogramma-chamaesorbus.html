Pityrogramma chamaesorbus

Pityrogramma chamaesorbus, commonly known as the creeping water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it forms dense, mat-like growths. The plant has slender, creeping rhizomes that anchor it to substrates, and its fronds are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, aiding in spore dispersal.

The plant's ability to tolerate varying water conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
