Piptadenia santosii

Piptadenia santosii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Piptadenia* tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The tree produces long, slender pods that contain flat, edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.
