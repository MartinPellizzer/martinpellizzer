Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. hedraianthoides

Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. hedraianthoides, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
