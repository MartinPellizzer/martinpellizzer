Passiflora cervii

Passiflora cervii, commonly known as the sheep's horn passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white hues, along with prominent red filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora cervii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
