Prenanthes multiflosculosa

Prenanthes multiflosculosa, commonly known as the clustered buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits.

The species is often found in the eastern United States and is noted for its ecological importance to pollinators.
