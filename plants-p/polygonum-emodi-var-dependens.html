Polygonum emodi var. dependens

Polygonum emodi var. dependens, commonly known as the Himalayan knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences and often zigzagging stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
