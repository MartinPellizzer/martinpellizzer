Passiflora uncinata

Passiflora uncinata, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, pulpy flesh. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft texture.

Passiflora uncinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
