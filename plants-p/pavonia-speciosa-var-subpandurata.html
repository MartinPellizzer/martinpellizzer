Pavonia speciosa var. subpandurata

Pavonia speciosa var. subpandurata, commonly known as the red false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy pink to red blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy and somewhat woody. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.
