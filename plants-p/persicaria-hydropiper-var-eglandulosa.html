Persicaria hydropiper var. eglandulosa

Persicaria hydropiper var. eglandulosa, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a distinctive scent when crushed, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
