Pomatocalpa wendlandorum

Pomatocalpa wendlandorum, commonly known as the Wendland's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a strikingly intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinct, elongated spur that houses the nectar.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its rare and delicate beauty.
