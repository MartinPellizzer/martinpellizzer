Philadelphus sanguineus

Philadelphus sanguineus, commonly known as the yellow treebine, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
