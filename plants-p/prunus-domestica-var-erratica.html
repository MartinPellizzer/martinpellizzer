Prunus domestica var. erratica

Prunus domestica var. erratica, commonly known as the wild European plum.

This variant is a naturally occurring form of the European plum, characterized by its smaller, often more tart fruits. It typically grows in rocky or forested areas, showing adaptability to less fertile soils. The plant has a compact growth habit with thorny branches, and its leaves are oval with serrated edges.

It is valued for its hardiness and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
