Piper tecumense

Piper tecumense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with elongated stems that often ascend trees or other vegetation. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

Piper tecumense is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
