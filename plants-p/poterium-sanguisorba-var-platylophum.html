Poterium sanguisorba var. platylophum

Poterium sanguisorba var. platylophum, commonly known as narrow-leaf bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and providing habitat for various wildlife.
