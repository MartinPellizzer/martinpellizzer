Protea busseana

Protea busseana, commonly known as the Busseana protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by a cluster of petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive yellow center, and bloom during the summer months.

Protea busseana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
