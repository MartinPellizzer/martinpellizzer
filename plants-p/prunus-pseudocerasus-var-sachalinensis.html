Prunus pseudocerasus var. sachalinensis

Prunus pseudocerasus var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in early summer. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive floral display and is also valued for its fruit in certain regions.
