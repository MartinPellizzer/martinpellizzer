Porphyrocodon pictus

Porphyrocodon pictus, commonly known as the painted porphyry cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful spines that range from red to purple, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow and orange, are typically short-lived but visually stunning.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Porphyrocodon pictus is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
