Peripeplus klaineanus

Peripeplus klaineanus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Peripeplus klaineanus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
