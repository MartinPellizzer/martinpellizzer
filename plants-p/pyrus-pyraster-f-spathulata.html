Pyrus pyraster f. spathulata

Pyrus pyraster f. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaved pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and it produces edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
