Piper paucartamboanum

Piper paucartamboanum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
