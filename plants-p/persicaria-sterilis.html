Persicaria sterilis

Persicaria sterilis, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple tinge along the veins.

Persicaria sterilis produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, triangular seed capsules.
