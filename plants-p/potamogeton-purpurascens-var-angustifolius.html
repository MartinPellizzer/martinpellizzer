Potamogeton purpurascens var. angustifolius

Potamogeton purpurascens var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in dense spikes, often emerging from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its ecosystem.
