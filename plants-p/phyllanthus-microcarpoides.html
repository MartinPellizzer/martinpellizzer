Phyllanthus microcarpoides

Phyllanthus microcarpoides, commonly known as the small-fruited phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant, making it monoecious.
