Prunus rosiflora

Prunus rosiflora, commonly known as the roseleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a finely toothed margin, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. Prunus rosiflora prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beautiful spring blossoms.
