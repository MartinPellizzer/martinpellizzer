Passerina tinctoria proles imbricata

Passerina tinctoria proles imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican red sage, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular red or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native gardens.
