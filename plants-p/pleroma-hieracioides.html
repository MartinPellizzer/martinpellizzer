Pleroma hieracioides

Pleroma hieracioides, commonly known as the small everlasting or hill everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its long-lasting flowers that remain intact when dried.
