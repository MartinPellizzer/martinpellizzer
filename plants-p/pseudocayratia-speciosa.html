Pseudocayratia speciosa

Pseudocayratia speciosa, commonly known as the showy pseudocayratia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
