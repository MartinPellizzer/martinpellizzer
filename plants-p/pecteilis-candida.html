Pecteilis candida

Pecteilis candida, commonly known as the white-flowered pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its delicate appearance and long-lasting flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
