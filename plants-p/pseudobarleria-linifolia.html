Pseudobarleria linifolia

Pseudobarleria linifolia, commonly known as silverleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that features narrow, silver-gray leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
