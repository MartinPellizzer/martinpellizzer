Pyrola minor f. brevis

Pyrola minor f. brevis, commonly known as the shortleaf pyrola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in early spring and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.
