Polygonum vacciniifolium var. obtusifolium

Polygonum vacciniifolium var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved smartweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features lance-shaped, reddish-tinged leaves that are broad at the base and taper to a point. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to red, appearing in late summer to early fall.

The species is characterized by its erect, branched stems and is often found in riparian zones.
