Palhinhaea cernua f. sikkimensis

Palhinhaea cernua f. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its fine, spreading foliage. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and exhibit a prostrate growth habit.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller stature and specific ecological adaptations to high-altitude environments.
