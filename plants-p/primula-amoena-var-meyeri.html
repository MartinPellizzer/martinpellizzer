Primula amoena var. meyeri

Primula amoena var. meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are densely packed at the base. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and cream, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
