Paphiopedilum roebelenii f. alboflavum

Paphiopedilum roebelenii f. alboflavum, commonly known as the white-flowered yellow lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow pouch-like lip and white petals. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid forest environments. Its botanical features include thick, leathery leaves and a robust rhizome system that helps it thrive in its natural habitat.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
