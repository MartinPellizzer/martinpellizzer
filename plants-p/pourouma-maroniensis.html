Pourouma maroniensis

Pourouma maroniensis, commonly known as the Maroni palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, yellowish-white inflorescences with numerous small flowers. Its fruits are drupes that contain a single seed, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various animals.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
