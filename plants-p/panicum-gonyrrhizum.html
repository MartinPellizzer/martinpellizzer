Panicum gonyrrhizum

Panicum gonyrrhizum, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it both a useful forage crop and a potential invasive species in some regions.
