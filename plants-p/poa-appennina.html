Poa appennina

Poa appennina, commonly known as the Italian grass or mountain bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Poa appennina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
