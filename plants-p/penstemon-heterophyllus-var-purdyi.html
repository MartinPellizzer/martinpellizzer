Penstemon heterophyllus var. purdyi

Penstemon heterophyllus var. purdyi, commonly known as the Purdy's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and displays showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
