Pythonium hookeri

Pythonium hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's python plant, is a unique species native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying a reddish tinge under direct sunlight.

Pythonium hookeri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
