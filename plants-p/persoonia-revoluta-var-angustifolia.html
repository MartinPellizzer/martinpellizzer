Persoonia revoluta var. angustifolia

Persoonia revoluta var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ghost gum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
