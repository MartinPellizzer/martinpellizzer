Phyllanthus acidus

Phyllanthus acidus, commonly known as the stone apple or cheese fruit, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its small, green, apple-like fruits that turn yellow or red when mature. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, pungent odor. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or green, and it produces a milky sap that is toxic if ingested.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and is also consumed in some cultures despite its bitter taste.
