Primula leimonophila

Primula leimonophila, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in cool, moist environments such as meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
