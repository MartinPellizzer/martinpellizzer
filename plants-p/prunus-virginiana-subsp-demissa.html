Prunus virginiana subsp. demissa

Prunus virginiana subsp.

demissa, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or along streambanks.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red drupes that attract wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
