Phaseolus lunatus var. lilacinus

Phaseolus lunatus var. lilacinus, commonly known as the purple hull cowpea, is a variety of the common bean native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple pods and dark green leaves, which are typical of the Phaseolus genus. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible beans. It has a climbing habit, producing tendrils that help it ascend supports.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is widely grown in regions with tropical and subtropical conditions.
