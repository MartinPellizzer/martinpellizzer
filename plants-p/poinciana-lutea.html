Poinciana lutea

Poinciana lutea, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feathery, bright red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a spreading canopy with palmately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth and striking appearance make it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Poinciana lutea thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical regions.
