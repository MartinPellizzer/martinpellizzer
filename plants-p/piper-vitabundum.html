Piper vitabundum

Piper vitabundum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
