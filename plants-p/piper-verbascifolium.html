Piper verbascifolium

Piper verbascifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of pepper plants, and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
