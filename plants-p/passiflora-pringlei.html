Passiflora pringlei

Passiflora pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's passionflower, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora pringlei is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
