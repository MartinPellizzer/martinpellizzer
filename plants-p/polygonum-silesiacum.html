Polygonum silesiacum

Polygonum silesiacum, commonly known as the Silesian knotgrass, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows. Its leaves are broad and have a slightly zigzagged edge, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, greenish-white inflorescences.

Polygonum silesiacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
