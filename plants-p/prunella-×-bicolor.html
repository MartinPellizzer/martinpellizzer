Prunella × bicolor

Prunella × bicolor, commonly known as the self-heal, is a flowering plant in the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunella vulgaris and Prunella lanceolata. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15–30 cm and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Prunella × bicolor is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
