Prunus × donarium var. tomentella

Prunus × donarium var. tomentella, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly grey pubescence on young shoots and twigs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and providing food for wildlife.
