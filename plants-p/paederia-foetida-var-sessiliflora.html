Paederia foetida var. sessiliflora

Paederia foetida var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the stinking beggar's-tick, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, opposite leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, spiny fruits. It is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the plant is in bloom.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
