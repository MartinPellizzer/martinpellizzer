Phillyrea quercifolia subsp. xavieri

Phillyrea quercifolia subsp.

xavieri, commonly known as the Xavier's Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh conditions.
