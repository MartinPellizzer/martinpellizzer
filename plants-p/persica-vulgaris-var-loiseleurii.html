Persica vulgaris var. loiseleurii

Persica vulgaris var. loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleurii cherry, is a botanical variety of the domesticated cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered form with abundant, white to pink petals. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, with thornless branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers appear in early spring, preceding the development of small, red fruits.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
