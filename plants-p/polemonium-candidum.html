Polemonium candidum

Polemonium candidum, commonly known as white fringed gentian, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers with prominent, fringed petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
