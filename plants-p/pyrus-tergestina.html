Pyrus tergestina

Pyrus tergestina, commonly known as the Sicilian pear, is a small deciduous tree native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a pear-like fruit, typically small and yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical significance in Mediterranean horticulture.

However, it is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and limited cultivation.
