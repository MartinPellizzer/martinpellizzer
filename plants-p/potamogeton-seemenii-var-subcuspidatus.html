Potamogeton seemenii var. subcuspidatus

Potamogeton seemenii var. subcuspidatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed at the tips, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often indicative of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
