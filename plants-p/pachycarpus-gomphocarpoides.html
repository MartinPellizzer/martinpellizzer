Pachycarpus gomphocarpoides

Pachycarpus gomphocarpoides, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by hard, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought resistance and slow growth.

The bark and wood are valued for their strength and durability, making it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
