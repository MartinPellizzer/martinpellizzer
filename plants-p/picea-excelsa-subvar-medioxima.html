Picea excelsa subvar. medioxima

Picea excelsa subvar. medioxima, commonly known as the Himalayan fir, is a coniferous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often growing in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in forest ecosystems.
