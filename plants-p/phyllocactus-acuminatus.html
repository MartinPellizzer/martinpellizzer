Phyllocactus acuminatus

Phyllocactus acuminatus, commonly known as the spiny cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Phyllocactus acuminatus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements.
