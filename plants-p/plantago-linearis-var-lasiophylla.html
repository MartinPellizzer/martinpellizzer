Plantago linearis var. lasiophylla

Plantago linearis var. lasiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain with thick leaves, is a variant of the common plantain.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are often broader towards the base. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in urban environments, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
