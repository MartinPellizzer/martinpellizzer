Phyteuma tenerum subsp. anglicum

Phyteuma tenerum subsp.

anglicum, commonly known as the English gromwell, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the British Isles. It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
