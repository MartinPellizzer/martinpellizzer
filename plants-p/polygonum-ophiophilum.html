Polygonum ophiophilum

Polygonum ophiophilum, commonly known as ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, bright purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bracts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Ironweed is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
