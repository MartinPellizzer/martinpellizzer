Pycreus polystachyos var. typicus

Pycreus polystachyos var. typicus, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose, branching pattern. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a slightly pointed tip.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
