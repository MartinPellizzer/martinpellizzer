Piper aequale var. dussii

Piper aequale var. dussii, commonly known as the Dussi pepper, is a variant of the Piper aequale species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its peppery flavor, which is used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, sharing characteristics with other pepper plants.
