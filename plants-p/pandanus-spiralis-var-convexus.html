Pandanus spiralis var. convexus

Pandanus spiralis var. convexus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, spirally arranged leaves that are often stiff and leathery in texture. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching material in some regions.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
