Periploca indica

Periploca indica, commonly known as the Indian periploca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in dry, rocky, or coastal habitats.

Periploca indica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
