Potentilla axilliflora

Potentilla axilliflora, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in the leaf axils, giving it its species name "axilliflora.". Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Potentilla axilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
