Pleuraphis sericea

Pleuraphis sericea, commonly known as silverleaf threeawn, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from the dense covering of fine, silky hairs on its leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often arranged in clusters, with each spikelet containing several bract-like structures. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Pleuraphis sericea is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
