Peperomia albert-smithii var. villosa

Peperomia albert-smithii var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are borne on short, erect spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate moisture.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
