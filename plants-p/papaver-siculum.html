Papaver siculum

Papaver siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking red-orange flowers with a dark central blotch, which are typically 3 to 5 centimeters in diameter. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its capsule fruit contains numerous small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Papaver siculum is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as roadsides and wastelands.
