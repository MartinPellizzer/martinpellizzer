Panicum pycnocomum

Panicum pycnocomum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple or reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray appearance due to a dense covering of hairs.

Panicum pycnocomum is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
