Phlox alyssifolia subsp. alyssifolia

Phlox alyssifolia subsp.

alyssifolia, commonly known as the alpine phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers.

The plant typically produces blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and full sun to partial shade, making it well-suited for alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
