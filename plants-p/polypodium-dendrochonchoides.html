Polypodium dendrochonchoides

Polypodium dendrochonchoides, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species of polypody that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered with a thick, fibrous rootstock and a crown of feather-like fronds. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Central and South America. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive, glossy appearance and a long, narrow shape.

Polypodium dendrochonchoides thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.
