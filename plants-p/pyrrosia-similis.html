Pyrrosia similis

Pyrrosia similis, commonly known as the rock fern, is a small, evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on rock surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green fronds with a leathery texture, and its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the leaves. It is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Pyrrosia similis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
