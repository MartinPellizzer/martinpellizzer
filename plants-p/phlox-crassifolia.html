Phlox crassifolia

Phlox crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to native plant gardens.
