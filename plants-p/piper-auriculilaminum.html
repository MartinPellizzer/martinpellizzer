Piper auriculilaminum

Piper auriculilaminum, commonly known as the "Auriculilamin" pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers that grow in clusters. Its fruits are small, green berries that turn red when mature, and they contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
