Philippia senescens

Philippia senescens, commonly known as the Mexican elder or tree of life, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
