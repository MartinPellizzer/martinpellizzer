Pelargonium × harewoodiae

Pelargonium × harewoodiae, commonly known as the Harewood's geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white, with five petals arranged symmetrically. The plant features velvety, dark green foliage that is often marked with distinctive patterns or veins. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a desirable ornamental plant in both gardens and horticultural collections.
