Pseudoconyza

Pseudoconyza, commonly known as false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. Pseudoconyza species are typically annual or perennial herbs with hairy stems and alternate leaves. They are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia.

Many species are found in disturbed habitats and can sometimes be invasive in certain areas.
