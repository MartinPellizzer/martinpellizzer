Perymenium salmonoides

Perymenium salmonoides, commonly known as the red root grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a fibrous root system and slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Perymenium salmonoides is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and in wetlands.
