Panicum montevidense

Panicum montevidense, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces spikelets that are often hairy and contain small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in Uruguay and neighboring regions.
