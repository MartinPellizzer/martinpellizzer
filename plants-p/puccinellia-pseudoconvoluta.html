Puccinellia pseudoconvoluta

Puccinellia pseudoconvoluta, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of salt lakes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and covered with small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, producing tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
