Psidium grandifolium var. parvifolium

Psidium grandifolium var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved guava, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The fruit is typically smaller than the common guava, with a smooth, thin rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in tropical regions.
