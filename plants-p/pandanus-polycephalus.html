Pandanus polycephalus

Pandanus polycephalus, commonly known as the bottle palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk and long, pinnate leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The species is well adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
