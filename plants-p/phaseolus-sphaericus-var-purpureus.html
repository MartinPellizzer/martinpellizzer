Phaseolus sphaericus var. purpureus

Phaseolus sphaericus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple round bean, is a unique variety of legume characterized by its spherical, pod-like fruits.

The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing small, purplish flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a deep green color and a slightly hairy texture. The fruit, which is round and often purple-tinged, contains several edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in various traditional dishes.
