Psychotria pallida

Psychotria pallida, commonly known as the ghost pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale green flowers and elliptical, waxy leaves. It produces small, red berries that contain the seeds, which are used in various traditional and culinary applications.

Due to its mild heat, it is often used in chili-based foods and beverages.
