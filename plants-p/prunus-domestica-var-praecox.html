Prunus domestica var. praecox

Prunus domestica var. praecox, commonly known as the early sweet cherry, is a variety of the common cherry tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that ripen earlier than most other cherry varieties. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with dense, dark green foliage. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fruit is usually harvested in late spring, making it a popular choice for both commercial and home cultivation.
