Paspalum fissifolium

Paspalum fissifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass or fissile leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often sharply serrated along the edges. The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, preferring moist to saturated soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Paspalum fissifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
