Pinalia longifolia

Pinalia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf pinalia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that can grow up to several inches in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique foliage and floral structure, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
