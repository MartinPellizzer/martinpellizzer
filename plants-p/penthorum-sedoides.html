Penthorum sedoides

Penthorum sedoides, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It features striking, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, clay-rich soils.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
