Pycreus macranthus var. angustifolius

Pycreus macranthus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pycreus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
