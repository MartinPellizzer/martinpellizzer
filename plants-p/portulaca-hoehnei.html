Portulaca hoehnei

Portulaca hoehnei, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

Portulaca hoehnei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
