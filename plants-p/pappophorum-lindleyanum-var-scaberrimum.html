Pappophorum lindleyanum var. scaberrimum

Pappophorum lindleyanum var. scaberrimum, commonly known as the rough pappophorum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. Its rough, bristly texture is a distinguishing feature, and it thrives in dry, open habitats.

It is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and resilience.
