Prangos meliocarpoides subsp. meliocarpoides

Prangos meliocarpoides subsp.

meliocarpoides, commonly known as the melicarpic thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

The subspecies meliocarpoides is distinguished by its specific leaf morphology and flower structure compared to other subspecies of Prangos.
