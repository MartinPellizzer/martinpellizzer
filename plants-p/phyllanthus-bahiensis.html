Phyllanthus bahiensis

Phyllanthus bahiensis, commonly known as Brazilian starfruit or Brazilian gooseberry, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, edible fruits that resemble starfruits. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
