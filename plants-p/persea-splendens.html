Persea splendens

Persea splendens, commonly known as the Cuban snow apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish-brown bark and a dense canopy that provides shade.

Persea splendens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
