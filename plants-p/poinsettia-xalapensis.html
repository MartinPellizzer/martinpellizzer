Poinsettia xalapensis

Poinsettia xalapensis, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that resemble petals. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its striking foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Poinsettia xalapensis is widely cultivated as a decorative plant, especially during the holiday season.
