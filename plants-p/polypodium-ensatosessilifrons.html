Polypodium ensatosessilifrons

Polypodium ensatosessilifrons, commonly known as the broad-leafed polypody, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its sori are clustered and covered by a thin, kidney-shaped indusium.

The leaves are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters, making it a distinctive member of the polypody group.
