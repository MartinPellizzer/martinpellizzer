Phellodendron kodamanum

Phellodendron kodamanum, commonly known as the Japanese yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark that exudes a strong, aromatic odor when cut. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive elongated shape with a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
