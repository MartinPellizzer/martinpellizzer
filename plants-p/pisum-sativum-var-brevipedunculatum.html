Pisum sativum var. brevipedunculatum

Pisum sativum var. brevipedunculatum, commonly known as the dwarf pea, is a compact variety of the pea plant characterized by its shorter peduncles and smaller flowers.

It is a legume with trifoliate leaves and pods that contain several edible seeds. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a space-saving alternative to standard peas. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a flowering period typically in late spring.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility.
