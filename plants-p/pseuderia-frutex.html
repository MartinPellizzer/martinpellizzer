Pseuderia frutex

Pseuderia frutex, commonly known as the shrubby pseuderia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, woody growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Pseuderia frutex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
