Pyrus balansae

Pyrus balansae, commonly known as the Balansae pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tian Shan and Pamir Mountains. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, yellowish flowers in the spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
