Parabambusa kaini

Parabambusa kaini, commonly known as the Kaini bamboo, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense foliage. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming compact clusters that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Parabambusa kaini is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional construction and crafts in its native habitat.
