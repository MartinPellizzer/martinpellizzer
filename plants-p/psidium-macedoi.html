Psidium macedoi

Psidium macedoi, commonly known as the Macedonian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry with a sweet and aromatic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
