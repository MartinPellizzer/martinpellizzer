Panicum horticola

Panicum horticola, commonly known as ornamental panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that add visual interest to landscapes. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a low-maintenance ornamental choice.

Its foliage is often bluish-green, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and naturalized areas.
