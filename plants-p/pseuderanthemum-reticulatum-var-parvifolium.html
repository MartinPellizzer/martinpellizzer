Pseuderanthemum reticulatum var. parvifolium

Pseuderanthemum reticulatum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, tubular flowers. The leaves are small, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking tropical foliage and floral displays.
