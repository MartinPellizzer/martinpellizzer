Pelargonium × fauxiae

Pelargonium × fauxiae, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded and often have a slightly hairy texture. This species is cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and containers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
