Polypodium devolutum

Polypodium devolutum, commonly known as the curled wall fern, is a species of epiphytic and lithophytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The fronds are typically short and curled, with a dark green, leathery texture. It thrives in humid environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
