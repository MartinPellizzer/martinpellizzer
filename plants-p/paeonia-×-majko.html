Paeonia × majko

Paeonia × majko, commonly known as the Majko peony, is a hybrid species of peony originating from the cross between Paeonia lactiflora and Paeonia suffruticosa.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant produces thick, fleshy roots and has compound, serrated leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The Majko peony is valued for its fragrance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
