Polygonum limprichtii

Polygonum limprichtii, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features slender stems that often form dense clusters, and its leaves are typically lanceolate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters, and it is often found in moist, shady environments.

Polygonum limprichtii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
