Puya fosteriana

Puya fosteriana, commonly known as the Foster's torch flower, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch, composed of numerous narrow, brightly colored bracts. The plant is perennial and can grow up to several meters in height, with broad, strap-like leaves arranged in a rosette. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Puya fosteriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
