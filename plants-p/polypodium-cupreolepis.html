Polypodium cupreolepis

Polypodium cupreolepis, commonly known as the copper shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a metallic sheen. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, including parts of Central and South America. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks.

The fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters, making it a visually striking addition to botanical gardens or natural habitats.
