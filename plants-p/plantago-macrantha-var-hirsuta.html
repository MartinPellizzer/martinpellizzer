Plantago macrantha var. hirsuta

Plantago macrantha var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on both surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
