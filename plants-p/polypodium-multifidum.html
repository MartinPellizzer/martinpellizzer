Polypodium multifidum

Polypodium multifidum, commonly known as the multifid fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically divided into multiple segments, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid climates. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to its substrate while absorbing nutrients from the environment.

Polypodium multifidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
