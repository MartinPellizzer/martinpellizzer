Passiflora lanata

Passiflora lanata, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, soft texture. The plant produces striking flowers with intricate, colorful patterns, often featuring a crown-like structure at the base. Its fruit, known as a passionfruit, is edible and has a tropical, tangy flavor.

Passiflora lanata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
