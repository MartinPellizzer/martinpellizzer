Pyrethrum anethifolium

Pyrethrum anethifolium, commonly known as the anise-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, anise-scented leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for producing pyrethrin, a natural insecticide. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it valuable in ecological gardening.

Pyrethrum anethifolium is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and its pesticidal properties.
