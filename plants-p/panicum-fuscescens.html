Panicum fuscescens

Panicum fuscescens, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and floodplains, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces dark-colored seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Panicum fuscescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
