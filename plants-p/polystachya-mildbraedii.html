Polystachya mildbraedii

Polystachya mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like inflorescence that consists of numerous small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

The orchid is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
