Paeonia officinalis var. lusitanica

Paeonia officinalis var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanica peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features showy, double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged in a basal rosette. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
