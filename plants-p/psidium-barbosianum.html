Psidium barbosianum

Psidium barbosianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
