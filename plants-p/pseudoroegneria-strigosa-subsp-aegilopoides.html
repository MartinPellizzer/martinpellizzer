Pseudoroegneria strigosa subsp. aegilopoides

Pseudoroegneria strigosa subsp.

aegilopoides, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and erosion control.
