Parathesis oxyphylla

Parathesis oxyphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its roots form haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to extract nutrients and water.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting bird populations that feed on its fruits and disperse its seeds.
