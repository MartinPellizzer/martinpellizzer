Pyrus wilhelmii

Pyrus wilhelmii, commonly known as the Wilhelm's pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer, though it is not typically cultivated for its fruit. This species is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
