Pseuderia vanikorensis

Pseuderia vanikorensis, commonly known as the Vanikoro orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the Vanikoro Islands of the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that display a unique combination of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical features include narrow, leathery leaves and a delicate, pendulous inflorescence.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a critically endangered species.
