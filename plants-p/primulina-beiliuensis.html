Primulina beiliuensis

Primulina beiliuensis, commonly known as the Beiliu primula, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as mountainous regions with high humidity. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in early spring, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires careful preservation.
