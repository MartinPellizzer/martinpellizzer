Paphiopedilum insigne

Paphiopedilum insigne, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive yellow and green coloration with a showy, lip-shaped petal that resembles a lip, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are usually fragrant and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Paphiopedilum insigne is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
