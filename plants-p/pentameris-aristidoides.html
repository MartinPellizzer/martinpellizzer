Pentameris aristidoides

Pentameris aristidoides, commonly known as the slender beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often bracteate and resembles a beak.

Pentameris aristidoides plays a minor role in local ecosystems, primarily as a component of native grassland vegetation.
