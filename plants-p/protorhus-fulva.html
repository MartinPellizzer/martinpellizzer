Protorhus fulva

Protorhus fulva, commonly known as the yellow wild indigo, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Protorhus fulva is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
