Pinus sylvestris subsp. sibirica

Pinus sylvestris subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian pine, is a subspecies of the Scots pine native to cold climates in Eastern Europe and Siberia. It is a coniferous evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 25 meters, with a slow growth rate and a long lifespan, often living over 500 years.

Its needles are typically 2–4 cm long, arranged in bundles of five, and its cones are cylindrical, maturing within 18–20 months. The tree is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system and thick bark that provides resistance to fire and pests.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
