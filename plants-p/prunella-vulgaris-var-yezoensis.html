Prunella vulgaris var. yezoensis

Prunella vulgaris var. yezoensis, commonly known as Yezo blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and bear small, spike-like clusters of blue or purple flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural use.
