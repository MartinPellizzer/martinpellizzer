Phaseolus compressus var. pullus

Phaseolus compressus var. pullus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green color. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and food producers.
