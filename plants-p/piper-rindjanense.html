Piper rindjanense

Piper rindjanense, commonly known as the Rindjan pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Indonesia, specifically the island of Sumatra. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and elongated, slender leaves. Its fruits are small, round berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and its role in local ecosystems.
