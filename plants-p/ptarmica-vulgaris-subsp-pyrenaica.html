Ptarmica vulgaris subsp. pyrenaica

Ptarmica vulgaris subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It features small, white or pinkish flowers and has a mat-forming growth habit.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, rocky or alpine environments. It is closely related to other members of the Caryophyllaceae family. Ptarmica vulgaris subsp.

pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountainous habitats.
