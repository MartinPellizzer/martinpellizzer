Pertusadina malaccensis

Pertusadina malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca pertusary, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, where it grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its stems are slender and somewhat woody.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
