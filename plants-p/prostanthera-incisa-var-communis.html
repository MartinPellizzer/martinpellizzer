Prostanthera incisa var. communis

Prostanthera incisa var. communis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Prostanthera incisa var. communis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
