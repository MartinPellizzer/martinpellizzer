Phylloxylon xiphocladum

Phylloxylon xiphocladum, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by small, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
