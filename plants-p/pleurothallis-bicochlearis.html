Pleurothallis bicochlearis

Pleurothallis bicochlearis, commonly known as the two-lobed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is part of the large and diverse Pleurothallis genus.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often difficult to cultivate outside its natural range.
