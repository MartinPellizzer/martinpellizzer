Phaseolus ellipticus var. willmotianus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. willmotianus, commonly known as the Mexican coffee bean plant, is a leguminous species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its elliptical, bean-like seeds that are often used as a coffee substitute. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly hairy.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, contributing to its ornamental value.
