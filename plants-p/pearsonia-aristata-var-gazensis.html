Pearsonia aristata var. gazensis

Pearsonia aristata var. gazensis, commonly known as the Gazengen pearsonia, is a rare and unique plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological niche.
