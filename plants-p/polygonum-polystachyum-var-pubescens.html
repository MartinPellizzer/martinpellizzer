Polygonum polystachyum var. pubescens

Polygonum polystachyum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy polygonum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers in late summer, which are arranged in panicle inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
