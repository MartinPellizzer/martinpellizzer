Pasania costata

Pasania costata, commonly known as the warty leaf begonia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, warty leaves that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Pasania costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
