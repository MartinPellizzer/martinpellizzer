Pilosella aurantiaca var. blyttiana

Pilosella aurantiaca var. blyttiana, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, typically reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called capitula. Its leaves are usually basal, linear to lanceolate, and covered with fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
