Potentilla transtuvinica

Potentilla transtuvinica, commonly known as the Transylvanian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla transtuvinica is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
