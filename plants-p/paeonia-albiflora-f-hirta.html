Paeonia albiflora f. hirta

Paeonia albiflora f. hirta, commonly known as the white peony with hairy leaves, is a cultivar of the peony plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, white, double blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and fragrance, though it is less commonly cultivated than other peony types.
