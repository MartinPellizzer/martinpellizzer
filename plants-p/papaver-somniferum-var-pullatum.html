Papaver somniferum var. pullatum

Papaver somniferum var. pullatum, commonly known as the Turk's Cap Papaver, is a variant of the opium poppy characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers.

It features large, cup-shaped blooms that are typically pink to red in color, with a prominent central stigma that protrudes from the flower. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical structure includes a hairy stem, alternate leaves, and a capsule fruit that contains the latex-rich seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
