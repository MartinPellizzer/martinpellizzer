Polygonum piliferum

Polygonum piliferum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and roadside areas.

Polygonum piliferum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
