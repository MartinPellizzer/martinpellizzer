Pinanga baviensis

Pinanga baviensis, commonly known as the Bavi mountain bamboo, is a species of bamboo native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Pinanga baviensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
