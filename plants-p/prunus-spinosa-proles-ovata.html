Prunus spinosa proles ovata

Prunus spinosa proles ovata, commonly known as the oval-fruited blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, dark blue berries that ripen in late summer. The leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedging and wildlife habitats.
