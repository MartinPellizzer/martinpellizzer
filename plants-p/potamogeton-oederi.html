Potamogeton oederi

Potamogeton oederi, commonly known as Oeder's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Potamogeton oederi plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
