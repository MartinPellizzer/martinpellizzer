Pyrus aria monstr. bullata

Pyrus aria monstr.

bullata, commonly known as the bullate pear, is a rare and ornamental species within the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, thickened, and warty bark, which gives it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellow-green fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are of interest to botanists. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its unique aesthetic features.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
