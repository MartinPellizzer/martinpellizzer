Pellaea lanuginosa

Pellaea lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pellaea lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
