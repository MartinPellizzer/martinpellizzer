Prunella asiatica f. albiflora

Prunella asiatica f. albiflora, commonly known as white-flowered selfheal, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
