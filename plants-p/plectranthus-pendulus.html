Plectranthus pendulus

Plectranthus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging mint, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its cascading appearance and mild mint-like fragrance.
