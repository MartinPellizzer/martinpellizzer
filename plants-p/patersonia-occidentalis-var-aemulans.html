Patersonia occidentalis var. aemulans

Patersonia occidentalis var. aemulans, commonly known as the western patersonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient addition to native gardens.
