Pararistolochia decandra

Pararistolochia decandra, commonly known as the broadleaf Dutchman's pipe, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate, pipe-like flowers that are often greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is known for its complex floral structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
