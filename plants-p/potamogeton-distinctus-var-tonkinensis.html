Potamogeton distinctus var. tonkinensis

Potamogeton distinctus var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds.

Its presence is often indicative of clean, well-oxygenated water.
