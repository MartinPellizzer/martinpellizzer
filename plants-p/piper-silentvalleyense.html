Piper silentvalleyense

Piper silentvalleyense, commonly known as the Silent Valley pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the dense rainforests of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
