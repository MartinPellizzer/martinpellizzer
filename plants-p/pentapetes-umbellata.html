Pentapetes umbellata

Pentapetes umbellata, commonly known as the five-lobed coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets and its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Pentapetes umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
