Polygala butyracea var. butyracea

Polygala butyracea var. butyracea, commonly known as the buttercup sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in swampy or seasonally flooded areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
