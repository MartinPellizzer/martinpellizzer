Potamogeton rostratus

Potamogeton rostratus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in shallow waters, such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
