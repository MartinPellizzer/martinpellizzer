Phaeopappus freynii

Phaeopappus freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root systems. The plant features gray-green, hairy foliage and produces purple to pinkish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its robust stems and adaptive growth habits allow it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Phaeopappus freynii is often found in dry slopes, gravelly soils, and disturbed areas, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
