Pinus sieboldii

Pinus sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese red pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically 5-10 centimeters long and have a dark green color. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often have a reddish hue, contributing to its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pinus sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
