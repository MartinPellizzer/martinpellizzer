Pisum sativum var. sumbajale

Pisum sativum var. sumbajale, commonly known as the Sumbajale pea, is a variety of the common pea plant native to South Asia.

This plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and the pods contain several round, edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

The variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in traditional agriculture.
