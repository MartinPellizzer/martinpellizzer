Polypodium nigritianum

Polypodium nigritianum, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that are often covered in a dark, almost blackish hue. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is thick and robust, supporting the growth of its intricate, shield-shaped leaves.

Polypodium nigritianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
