Poa laka

Poa laka, commonly known as the Hawaiian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Poa laka thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
