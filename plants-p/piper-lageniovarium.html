Piper lageniovarium

Piper lageniovarium, commonly known as the giant sarsaparilla, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are elongated berries that mature to a bright yellow or orange color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
