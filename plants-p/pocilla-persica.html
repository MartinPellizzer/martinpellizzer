Pocilla persica

Pocilla persica, commonly known as the Persian rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the regions of Persia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to white flowers in the spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorny branches, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pocilla persica is well-suited to dry, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
