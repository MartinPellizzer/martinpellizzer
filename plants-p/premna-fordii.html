Premna fordii

Premna fordii, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or Chinese mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with two lips, typical of the mint family, and is followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Premna fordii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
