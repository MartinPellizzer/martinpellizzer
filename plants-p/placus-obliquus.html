Placus obliquus

Placus obliquus, commonly known as the slant-leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that grow in a staggered, oblique pattern. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with aerial roots that help it climb or attach to surfaces. Its leaves are typically green and glossy, with a slightly serrated margin.

Placus obliquus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and indoor spaces.
