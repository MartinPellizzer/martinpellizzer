Prunus tatsienensis var. pilosiuscula

Prunus tatsienensis var. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the Chinese date tree or Chinese persimmon, is a species native to China.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with deciduous leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that ripen to a deep orange-red color. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or dried.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in traditional Chinese cuisine.
