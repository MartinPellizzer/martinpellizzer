Pygeum neglectum

Pygeum neglectum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hard, almond-like seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
