Polygonum excurrens

Polygonum excurrens, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds, it is considered a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
