Priestleya longifolia

Priestleya longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved priestleya, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its elongated leaves and showy inflorescences make it a distinctive and ornamental species.

Priestleya longifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
