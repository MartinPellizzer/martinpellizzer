Plantago minuta

Plantago minuta, commonly known as lesser plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaf arrangement and capsule fruits.
