Polygonatum polygonatum

Polygonatum polygonatum, commonly known as the Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a popular plant in horticulture and traditional medicine.
