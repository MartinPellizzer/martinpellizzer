Pithecellobium × mucronatum

Pithecellobium × mucronatum, commonly known as the yellow passion vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms.
