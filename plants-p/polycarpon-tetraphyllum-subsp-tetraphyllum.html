Polycarpon tetraphyllum subsp. tetraphyllum

Polycarpon tetraphyllum subsp.

tetraphyllum, commonly known as the four-leafed knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks, roadsides, and in woodland edges.

The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The species is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed leaves, which give it its common name.
