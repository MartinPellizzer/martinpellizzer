Philodendron rhodoaxis subsp. rhodoaxis

Philodendron rhodoaxis subsp.

rhodoaxis, commonly known as the Red-veined Philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that often feature bright red veins against a deep green background.

The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.

It requires well-draining soil and regular watering to maintain its vibrant appearance.
