Paspalum hatschbachii

Paspalum hatschbachii, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or dwarf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, clump-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open fields.

Paspalum hatschbachii is adapted to a wide range of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
