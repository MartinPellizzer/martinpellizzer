Prunus caproniana f. salicifolia

Prunus caproniana f. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf cherry, is a unique ornamental plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features elongated, willow-like leaves that are slender and serrated, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, typically white or pale pink, which are followed by edible, tart cherries. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
