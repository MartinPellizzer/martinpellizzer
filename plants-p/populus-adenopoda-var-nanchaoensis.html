Populus adenopoda var. nanchaoensis

Populus adenopoda var. nanchaoensis, commonly known as the Nanchao Poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive grayish-white underside. The tree produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
