Prunus mahaleb subvar. globosa

Prunus mahaleb subvar. globosa, commonly known as the globular cherry, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, dark red to black fruits that mature in late summer. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart cherries. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
