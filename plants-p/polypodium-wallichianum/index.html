Polypodium Wallichianum

Polypodium wallichianum is a rare and ancient fern species native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in dense forests with high humidity. This plant is known for its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that unfurl slowly over time. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists. Despite its beauty, it remains under threat due to habitat loss and climate change.
