Phalaris × daviesii

Phalaris × daviesii, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose, branching pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along the edges of water bodies.

Phalaris × daviesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
