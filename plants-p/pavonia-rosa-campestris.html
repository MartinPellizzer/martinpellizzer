Pavonia rosa-campestris

Pavonia rosa-campestris, commonly known as the redroot false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as those found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. Its stems are often hairy and can become woody at the base, while its roots are reddish in color, giving it its common name.

Pavonia rosa-campestris is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
