Polygala vulgaris subsp. pubescens

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features slender stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
