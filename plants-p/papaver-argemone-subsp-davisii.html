Papaver argemone subsp. davisii

Papaver argemone subsp.

davisii, commonly known as the Davis's poppy, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive red or purple center.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
