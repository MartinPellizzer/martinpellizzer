Piper callosum var. franciscoanum

Piper callosum var. franciscoanum, commonly known as the Francisco's pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
