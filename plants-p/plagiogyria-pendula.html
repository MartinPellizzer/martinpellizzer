Plagiogyria pendula

Plagiogyria pendula, commonly known as the weeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with slender, arching stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue or violet in color and bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.
