Prostanthera albiflora

Prostanthera albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered mint bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Prostanthera albiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
