Phaeocephalum proliferum

Phaeocephalum proliferum, commonly known as the yellow star moss, is a member of the family Pterobryaceae and is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in humid conditions. The plant features small, yellow-green leaves that are often clustered closely together, giving it a star-like appearance. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short stalks and release spores when mature.

This species is notable for its ability to form extensive colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and biodiversity in its ecosystem.
