Plocostemma pallidum

Plocostemma pallidum, commonly known as the pale plocostemma, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows from a short, rhizomatous stem and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient environments and is drought-tolerant.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
