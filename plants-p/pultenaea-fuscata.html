Pultenaea fuscata

Pultenaea fuscata, commonly known as the dark everlasting or black wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Pultenaea fuscata is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
