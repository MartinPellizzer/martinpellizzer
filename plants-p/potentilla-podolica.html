Potentilla podolica

Potentilla podolica, commonly known as the Podolian cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. The plant features wiry, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla podolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
