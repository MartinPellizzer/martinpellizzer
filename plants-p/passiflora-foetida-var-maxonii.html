Passiflora foetida var. maxonii

Passiflora foetida var. maxonii, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that resemble a crown, with five sepals, five petals, and a central cluster of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor, often used in jams and beverages. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine.
