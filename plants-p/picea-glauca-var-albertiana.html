Picea glauca var. albertiana

Picea glauca var. albertiana, commonly known as the Alberta white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to Canada.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and silvery-blue needles. The foliage is dense, with short, stiff needles that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cold climates and is often found in boreal forests and alpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
