Passiflora quadraticordata

Passiflora quadraticordata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that resemble a crown. The flowers are typically blue or purple with a white or yellow center, and they emit a sweet fragrance. This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and use in traditional medicine.
