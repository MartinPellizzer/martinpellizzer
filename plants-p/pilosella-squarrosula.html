Pilosella squarrosula

Pilosella squarrosula, commonly known as the squarrosy cat's-paw, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Native to Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its common name refers to the rough, squarrose appearance of its flower heads.
