Piper indianonum

Piper indianonum, commonly known as Indian pepper or Indian long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, spiny fruits containing aromatic, peppery seeds. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.
