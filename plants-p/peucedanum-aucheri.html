Peucedanum aucheri

Peucedanum aucheri, commonly known as Persian cumin or Persian coriander, is a biennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are similar in appearance to cumin and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.
