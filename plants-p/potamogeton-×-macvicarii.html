Potamogeton × macvicarii

Potamogeton × macvicarii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, with some floating at the surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing both male and female reproductive structures.

Potamogeton × macvicarii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
