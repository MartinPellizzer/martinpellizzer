Potentilla norvegica subsp. hirsuta

Potentilla norvegica subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically arranged in a five-leaflet pattern.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
