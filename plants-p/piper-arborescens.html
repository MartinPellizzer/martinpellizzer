Piper arborescens

Piper arborescens, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and greenhouses.
