Polyacantha crispa

Polyacantha crispa, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a woody shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that attracts birds and other wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
