Passiflora cearaensis

Passiflora cearaensis, commonly known as the Ceara passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and numerous stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its foliage consists of palmately lobed leaves, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Passiflora cearaensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
