Pachypterygium brevipes var. persica

Pachypterygium brevipes var. persica, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the regions of Persia and surrounding areas.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.

Its botanical features make it both a notable ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain ecosystems.
