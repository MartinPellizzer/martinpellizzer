Pelargonium × floccosum

Pelargonium × floccosum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and showy flowers.

It is native to South Africa and belongs to the Geraniaceae family. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, though it can tolerate partial shade.
