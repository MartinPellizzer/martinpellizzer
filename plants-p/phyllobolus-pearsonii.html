Phyllobolus pearsonii

Phyllobolus pearsonii, commonly known as the pearson's leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval body and metallic green or blue coloration, which provides camouflage against foliage. The plant it inhabits, typically various species of oak and hickory, serves as both a food source and a habitat for the beetle's larvae. The beetle's life cycle involves laying eggs on the underside of leaves, where the larvae feed on plant tissues before pupating.

This species plays a minor role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat through its interactions with host plants and potential contributions to nutrient cycling.
