Pithecellobium nicobaricum

Pithecellobium nicobaricum, commonly known as the Nicobar acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman and Nicobar Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to coastal environments, making it a resilient species in humid, saline conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various local fauna.
