Polygonum perfoliatum

Polygonum perfoliatum, commonly known as Indian spinach or touching grass, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that often have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
