Polypodium punctatum f. rigescens

Polypodium punctatum f. rigescens, commonly known as the rigid holly fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, lanceolate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China and India. The fronds are typically upright and have a leathery texture, with a dark green color and a serrated margin. This form is a variant of the species Polypodium punctatum, distinguished by its more rigid and upright growth habit compared to the typical form.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
