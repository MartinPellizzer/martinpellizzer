Pithecellobium baronii

Pithecellobium baronii, commonly known as the yellow passion vine, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
