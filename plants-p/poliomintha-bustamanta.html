Poliomintha bustamanta

Poliomintha bustamanta, commonly known as the Andean blueberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
