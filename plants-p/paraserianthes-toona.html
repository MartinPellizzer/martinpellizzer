Paraserianthes toona

Paraserianthes toona, commonly known as the African toona, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces pod fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
