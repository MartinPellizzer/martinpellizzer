Panicum tristachyoides

Panicum tristachyoides, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles that produce dense clusters of purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for roadside vegetation and erosion control. It is drought-tolerant and can be used as a forage crop for livestock.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking color and upright growth habit.
