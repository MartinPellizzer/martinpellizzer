Populus medusae

Populus medusae, commonly known as the ghost poplar, is a unique species of poplar tree characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that resemble the appearance of a ghostly presence.

This plant is native to parts of Asia and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, temperate environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters, and it relies on wind for pollination. The tree's bark is smooth and pale, often peeling in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Populus medusae is also notable for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it an important pioneer species in certain ecosystems.
