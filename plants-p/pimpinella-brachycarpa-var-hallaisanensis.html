Pimpinella brachycarpa var. hallaisanensis

Pimpinella brachycarpa var. hallaisanensis, commonly known as Hallaisan mountain cowpea, is a rare and endemic species native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a biennial or perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruits are short, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
