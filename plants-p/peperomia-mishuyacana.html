Peperomia mishuyacana

Peperomia mishuyacana, commonly known as the Yungas peperomia, is a species native to the humid montane forests of Bolivia and Peru.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia mishuyacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements in indoor gardening.
