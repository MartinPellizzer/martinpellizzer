Perlebia phoenicea

Perlebia phoenicea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

Perlebia phoenicea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance.
