Padus emarginata

Padus emarginata, commonly known as the white thorn or European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree has a rough, fissured bark and produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Padus emarginata is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in temperate forests.
