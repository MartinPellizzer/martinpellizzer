Populus pilosa var. leiocarpa

Populus pilosa var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky poplar or soft-leaved poplar, is a variant of the poplar genus native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely pubescent leaves, which give it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found near water bodies. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

The species is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts.
