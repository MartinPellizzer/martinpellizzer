Pelargonium testaceum

Pelargonium testaceum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium testaceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
