Plectronia opima

Plectronia opima, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Plectronia opima is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
