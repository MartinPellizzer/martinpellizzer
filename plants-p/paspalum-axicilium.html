Paspalum axicilium

Paspalum axicilium, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its flowering spikes are small and clustered, producing wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum axicilium is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
