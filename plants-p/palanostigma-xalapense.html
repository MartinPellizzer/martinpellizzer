Palanostigma xalapense

Palanostigma xalapense, commonly known as the Mexican palo de rosa, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ornamental value, particularly due to its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.

The species also has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
