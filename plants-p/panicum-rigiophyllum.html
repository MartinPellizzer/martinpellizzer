Panicum rigiophyllum

Panicum rigiophyllum, commonly known as the rigid panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, upright leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are adorned with small, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
