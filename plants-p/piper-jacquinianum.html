Piper jacquinianum

Piper jacquinianum, commonly known as the Jacquin's pepper vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. Its botanical characteristics make it a unique subject in botanical studies and horticulture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
