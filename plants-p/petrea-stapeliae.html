Petrea stapeliae

Petrea stapeliae, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, silver-gray leaves that reflect light and help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the evening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Petrea stapeliae is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
