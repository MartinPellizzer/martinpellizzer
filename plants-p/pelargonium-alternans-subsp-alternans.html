Pelargonium alternans subsp. alternans

Pelargonium alternans subsp.

alternans, commonly known as the alternate geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae. It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, alternate arrangement of leaves.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
