Penstemon amabilis

Penstemon amabilis, commonly known as the hummingbird penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon amabilis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
