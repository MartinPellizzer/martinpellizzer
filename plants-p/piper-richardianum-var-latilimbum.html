Piper richardianum var. latilimbum

Piper richardianum var. latilimbum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn from green to yellow or red, indicating maturity.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush foliage and unique fruiting habit.
