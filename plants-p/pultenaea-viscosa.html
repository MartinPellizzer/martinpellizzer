Pultenaea viscosa

Pultenaea viscosa, commonly known as the sticky pultenaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often appearing in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Pultenaea viscosa is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
