Pourouma bicolor subsp. tessmannii

Pourouma bicolor subsp.

tessmannii, commonly known as the red-stemmed bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are typically reddish in color, which distinguish it from other species in the genus.

The plant has large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
