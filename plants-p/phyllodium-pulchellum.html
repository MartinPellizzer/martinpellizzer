Phyllodium pulchellum

Phyllodium pulchellum, commonly known as the silky burr, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Phyllodium pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
