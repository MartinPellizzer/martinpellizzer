Paeonia pubens

Paeonia pubens, commonly known as the woolly peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing large, single or double petals. Paeonia pubens thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
