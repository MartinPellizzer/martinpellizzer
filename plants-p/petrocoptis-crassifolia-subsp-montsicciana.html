Petrocoptis crassifolia subsp. montsicciana

Petrocoptis crassifolia subsp.

montsicciana, commonly known as the rock parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a waxy coating, and its flowering stems bear small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. It is characterized by its robust, fleshy roots and its ability to withstand harsh, arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
