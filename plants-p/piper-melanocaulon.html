Piper melanocaulon

Piper melanocaulon, commonly known as black-stemmed pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by black, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper melanocaulon make it distinct within its family and valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
