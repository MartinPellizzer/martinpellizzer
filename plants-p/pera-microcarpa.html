Pera microcarpa

Pera microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited pera, is a member of the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
