Phyllagathis rupicola

Phyllagathis rupicola, commonly known as the rock heather, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and leathery, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.
