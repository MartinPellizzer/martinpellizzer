Plantago phaeostoma

Plantago phaeostoma, commonly known as the dark-leaved plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses, with historical applications in treating wounds and respiratory ailments.
