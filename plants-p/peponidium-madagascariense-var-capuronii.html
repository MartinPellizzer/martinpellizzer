Peponidium madagascariense var. capuronii

Peponidium madagascariense var. capuronii, commonly known as the Madagascar bottle gourd, is a unique climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It produces large, cylindrical fruits that can grow up to 2 meters in length, with a thick, waxy rind and a sweet, edible interior. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This variety is notable for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
