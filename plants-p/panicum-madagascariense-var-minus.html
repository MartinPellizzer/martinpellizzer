Panicum madagascariense var. minus

Panicum madagascariense var. minus, commonly known as small panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
