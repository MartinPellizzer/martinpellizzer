Persea hartmanii

Persea hartmanii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and a tendency to grow in moist, well-drained soils.

Persea hartmanii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid climates.
