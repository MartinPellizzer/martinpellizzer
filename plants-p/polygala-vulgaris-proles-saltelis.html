Polygala vulgaris proles saltelis

Polygala vulgaris proles saltelis, commonly known as the Salt marsh milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to greenish-white. The plant typically grows in saline or brackish soils, often found in salt marshes and coastal dunes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to withstand salty and windy conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
