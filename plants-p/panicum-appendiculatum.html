Panicum appendiculatum

Panicum appendiculatum, commonly known as the dwarf panicgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover in dry environments.
