Persicaria maackiana f. albiflora

Persicaria maackiana f. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower smartweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a tendency to spread via rhizomes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
