Polypodium matthewianum

Polypodium matthewianum, commonly known as the Matthew's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, evergreen fronds that are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. The plant produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of its fronds, a feature typical of many ferns. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and parts of Africa.

Polypodium matthewianum is often found growing on rocks or in shaded, moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to various microhabitats.
