Potentilla fruticosa f. ternata

Potentilla fruticosa f. ternata, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
