Platysepalum vanhouttei

Platysepalum vanhouttei, commonly known as the Van Houtte's plathysepalum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features simple, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warm season. Its botanical structure is characterized by its compact growth habit and robust root system.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and resilience in humid environments.
