Pelargonium × longipedicellatum

Pelargonium × longipedicellatum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of showy, double flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
