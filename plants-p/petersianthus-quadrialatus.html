Petersianthus quadrialatus

Petersianthus quadrialatus, commonly known as the four-winged petersianthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive four-winged fruits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.
