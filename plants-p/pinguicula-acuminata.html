Pinguicula acuminata

Pinguicula acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, with a distinctive spurred shape.

Pinguicula acuminata is a popular specimen for carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and feeding behavior.
