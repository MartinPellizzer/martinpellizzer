Pteris roseolilacina

Pteris roseolilacina, commonly known as the pink-veined fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive pinkish or lilac-colored venation that gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
