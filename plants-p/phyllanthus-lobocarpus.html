Phyllanthus lobocarpus

Phyllanthus lobocarpus, commonly known as the Philippine sandpaper plant, is a shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its rough, sandpapery texture on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Phyllanthus lobocarpus is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.
