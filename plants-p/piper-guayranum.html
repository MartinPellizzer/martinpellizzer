Piper guayranum

Piper guayranum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Guayranum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small, elongated berry, turns dark purple when mature and is used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in the biodiversity of the Amazon region.
