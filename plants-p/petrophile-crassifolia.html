Petrophile crassifolia

Petrophile crassifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in a rough, glandular texture.

The flowers are usually red or pink and are visited by various pollinators, including birds and insects.
