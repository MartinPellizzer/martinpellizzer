Pleione flaccida

Pleione flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid pleione, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, typical of many orchids. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
