Phytolacca sessiliflora

Phytolacca sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered pokeweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces dark red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
