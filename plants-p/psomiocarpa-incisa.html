Psomiocarpa incisa

Psomiocarpa incisa, commonly known as the serrated psomiocarpa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Psomiocarpa incisa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
