Phrynium

Phrynium, commonly known as the arrow leaf or arrowhead plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often prominently veined and can vary in color from green to purple. The plant produces striking inflorescences composed of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Phrynium species are native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

They are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and unique floral structures.
