Prunus lobulata

Prunus lobulata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, bright red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Prunus lobulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Japanese gardens.
