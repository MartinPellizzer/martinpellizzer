Prunus armenica

Prunus armenica, commonly known as the Armenian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
