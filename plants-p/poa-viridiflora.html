Poa viridiflora

Poa viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southern regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. This plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive greenish-blue flower heads. The leaves are narrow and usually have a bluish-green hue, which contributes to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.
