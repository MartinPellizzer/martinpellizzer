Protea poggei subsp. haemantha

Protea poggei subsp.

haemantha, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous papery bracts that surround the true flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for horticultural displays and garden landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and biodiversity.
