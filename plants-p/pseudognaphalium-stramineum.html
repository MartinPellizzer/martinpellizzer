Pseudognaphalium stramineum

Pseudognaphalium stramineum, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Pseudognaphalium stramineum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread aggressively through rhizomes.
