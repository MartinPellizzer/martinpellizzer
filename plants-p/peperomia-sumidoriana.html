Peperomia sumidoriana

Peperomia sumidoriana, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture, often adorned with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually green or reddish, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia sumidoriana is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
