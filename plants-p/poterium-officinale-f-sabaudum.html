Poterium officinale f. sabaudum

Poterium officinale f. sabaudum, commonly known as the Sabaudian thyme, is a botanical variant of the common thyme.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal.
