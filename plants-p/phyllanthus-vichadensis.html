Phyllanthus vichadensis

Phyllanthus vichadensis, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish-green, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
