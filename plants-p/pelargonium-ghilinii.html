Pelargonium ghilinii

Pelargonium ghilinii, commonly known as the Natal geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant produces distinctive, fragrant foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Pelargonium ghilinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
