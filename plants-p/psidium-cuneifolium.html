Psidium cuneifolium

Psidium cuneifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Psidium cuneifolium is also valued for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types and its resilience to pests and diseases.
