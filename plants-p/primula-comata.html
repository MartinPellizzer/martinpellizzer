Primula comata

Primula comata, commonly known as the downy primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Primula comata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
