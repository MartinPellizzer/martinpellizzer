Passiflora villosa

Passiflora villosa, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. Its fruit is a green, fleshy berry that turns yellow when ripe.

Passiflora villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
