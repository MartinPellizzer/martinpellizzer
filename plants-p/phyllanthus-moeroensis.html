Phyllanthus moeroensis

Phyllanthus moeroensis, commonly known as the African buttonwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by globose, greenish fruits.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
