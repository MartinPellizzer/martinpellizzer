Palmijuncus amboinensis

Palmijuncus amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina palm, is a rare and unique palm species native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and a crown of pinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing single seeds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.

Its botanical features make it a subject of study in tropical plant ecology and phylogenetic research.
