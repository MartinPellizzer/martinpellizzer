Purgosea minima

Purgosea minima, commonly known as the dwarf purgosea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Purgosea minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
