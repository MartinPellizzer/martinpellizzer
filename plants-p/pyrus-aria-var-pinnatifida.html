Pyrus aria var. pinnatifida

Pyrus aria var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a variant of the white pear species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely toothed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing plant in gardens.
