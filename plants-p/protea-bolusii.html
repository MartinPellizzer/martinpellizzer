Protea bolusii

Protea bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical shape. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

Protea bolusii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
