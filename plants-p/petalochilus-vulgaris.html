Petalochilus vulgaris

Petalochilus vulgaris, commonly known as the common orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Petalochilus vulgaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
