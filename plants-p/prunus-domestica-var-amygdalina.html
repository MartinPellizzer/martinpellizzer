Prunus domestica var. amygdalina

Prunus domestica var. amygdalina, commonly known as the European apricot or sweet cherry, is a variety of the domesticated cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late spring. The plant has a rounded canopy with dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces fragrant white or pale pink flowers in early spring. Its botanical features include a woody structure with smooth, grey bark and a shallow root system.

This variety is cultivated for its edible fruit and is often used in both culinary and ornamental contexts.
