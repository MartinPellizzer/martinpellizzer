Pimelea paludosa var. foliosa

Pimelea paludosa var. foliosa, commonly known as swamp bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes, which are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as swamps and marshes, where it forms dense colonies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the moist, nutrient-poor conditions of its habitat.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
