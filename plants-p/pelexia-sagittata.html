Pelexia sagittata

Pelexia sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pelexia sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
