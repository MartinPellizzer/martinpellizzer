Pseudotectaria biformis

Pseudotectaria biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed tectaria, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive fronds with a bifurcated or two-lobed structure. The plant exhibits a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping rhizomes that allow it to spread across forest floors. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The spores are produced in sori that are arranged in a linear pattern on the underside of the fronds.
