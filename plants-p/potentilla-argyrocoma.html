Potentilla argyrocoma

Potentilla argyrocoma, commonly known as silverleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Potentilla argyrocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
