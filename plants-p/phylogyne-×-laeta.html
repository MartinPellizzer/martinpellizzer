Phylogyne × laeta

Phylogyne × laeta, commonly known as the hybrid hibiscus, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Hibiscus acetosella and Hibiscus syriacus.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The hybrid is valued for its adaptability and ability to produce blooms throughout the growing season.
