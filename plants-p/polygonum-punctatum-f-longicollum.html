Polygonum punctatum f. longicollum

Polygonum punctatum f. longicollum, commonly known as the long-necked knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum punctatum, which belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and distinctive, bristly inflorescences. The leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly toothed margin, and the plant often grows in moist, disturbed soils. This form is notable for its unique growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It is native to parts of Asia and is adapted to temperate climates.
