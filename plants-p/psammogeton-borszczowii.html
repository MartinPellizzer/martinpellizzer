Psammogeton borszczowii

Psammogeton borszczowii, commonly known as the sand borsch plant, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has small, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of dryland vegetation.
