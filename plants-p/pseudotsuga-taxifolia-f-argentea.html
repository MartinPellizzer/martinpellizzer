Pseudotsuga taxifolia f. argentea

Pseudotsuga taxifolia f. argentea, commonly known as the silver firs, is a variant of the Japanese fir characterized by its striking silvery-white undersides on its needle-like leaves.

This unique foliage gives the plant a distinct appearance, making it a popular ornamental species in horticulture. It is native to Japan and thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. The plant grows as a coniferous evergreen, reaching moderate heights and forming a conical shape.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and landscape design.
