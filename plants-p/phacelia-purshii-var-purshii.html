Phacelia purshii var. purshii

Phacelia purshii var. purshii, commonly known as blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular.

Phacelia purshii var. purshii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
