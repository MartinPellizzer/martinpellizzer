Papaver dzeghamicum

Papaver dzeghamicum, commonly known as the Armenian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent, centrally located stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
