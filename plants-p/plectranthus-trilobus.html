Plectranthus trilobus

Plectranthus trilobus, commonly known as the African violet or velvet plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive tri-lobed shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus trilobus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
