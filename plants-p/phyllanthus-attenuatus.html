Phyllanthus attenuatus

Phyllanthus attenuatus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves that are usually hairless. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.

It is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.
