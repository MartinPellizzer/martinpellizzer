Phalaenopsis × virataii

Phalaenopsis × virataii, commonly known as the hybrid phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phalaenopsis aphrodite and Phalaenopsis amabilis.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it thrives in humid, warm environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, typically arranged in a fan shape, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This hybrid is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
