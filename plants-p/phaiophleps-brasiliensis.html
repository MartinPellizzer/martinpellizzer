Phaiophleps brasiliensis

Phaiophleps brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
