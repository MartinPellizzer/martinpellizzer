Pleroma thereminianum

Pleroma thereminianum, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various insect species and its ornamental value in gardens.
