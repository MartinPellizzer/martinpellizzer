Paraholcoglossum subulifolium

Paraholcoglossum subulifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holcoglossum, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, needle-like leaves that give it its scientific name, subulifolium, meaning "needle-leaf.". The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a complex structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its native range.
