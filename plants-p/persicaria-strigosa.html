Persicaria strigosa

Persicaria strigosa, commonly known as rough knotweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to Eurasia and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Persicaria strigosa is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in some regions.
