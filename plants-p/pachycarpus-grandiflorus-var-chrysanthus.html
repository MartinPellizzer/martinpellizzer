Pachycarpus grandiflorus var. chrysanthus

Pachycarpus grandiflorus var. chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden-flowered pachycarpus, is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to store water.

It produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its native habitat. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardening.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
