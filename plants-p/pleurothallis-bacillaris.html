Pleurothallis bacillaris

Pleurothallis bacillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its simple, sympodial growth habit.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
