Puccinellia convoluta var. procera

Puccinellia convoluta var. procera, commonly known as tall sandspurry, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by small, three-lobed seeds. It is adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

This species is often found in dunes, coastal areas, and disturbed sites.
