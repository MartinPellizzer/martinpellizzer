Primula petiolaris var. odontocalyx

Primula petiolaris var. odontocalyx, commonly known as the toothed-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. The leaves are typically elongated with a distinctive toothed margin, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
