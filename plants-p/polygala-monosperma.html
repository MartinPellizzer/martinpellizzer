Polygala monosperma

Polygala monosperma, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains alkaloids, which can be toxic to livestock. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth or hairy surface depending on the variety.

Polygala monosperma is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.
