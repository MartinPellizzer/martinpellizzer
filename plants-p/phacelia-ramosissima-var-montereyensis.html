Phacelia ramosissima var. montereyensis

Phacelia ramosissima var. montereyensis, commonly known as Monterey phacelia, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinators.
