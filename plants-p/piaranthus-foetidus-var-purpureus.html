Piaranthus foetidus var. purpureus

Piaranthus foetidus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple-flowered poinsettia, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its short blooming period during the holiday season. It thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and festive decorations.
