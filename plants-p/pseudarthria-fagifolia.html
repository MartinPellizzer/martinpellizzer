Pseudarthria fagifolia

Pseudarthria fagifolia, commonly known as the African vetch, is a leguminous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland areas.

Pseudarthria fagifolia is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage plant.
