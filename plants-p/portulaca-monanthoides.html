Portulaca monanthoides

Portulaca monanthoides, commonly known as the common purslane, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Americas.

It features fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in hot, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and gardens. Its stems and leaves are covered in tiny hairs that help reduce water loss.

Portulaca monanthoides is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are rich in nutrients.
