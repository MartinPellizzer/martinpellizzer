Potamogeton gramineus f. angermanicus

Potamogeton gramineus f. angermanicus, commonly known as the Angerman's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by the development of small, boat-shaped fruits. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
