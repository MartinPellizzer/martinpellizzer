Parahebe bidwillii

Parahebe bidwillii, commonly known as the New Zealand parahebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a glossy appearance.

Parahebe bidwillii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
