Potentilla glabriuscula var. narimensis

Potentilla glabriuscula var. narimensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, exhibiting a hardy and resilient nature. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
