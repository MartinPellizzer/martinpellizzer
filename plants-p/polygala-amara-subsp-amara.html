Polygala amara subsp. amara

Polygala amara subsp.

amara, commonly known as the bitter milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale green or yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
