Peschiera

Peschiera, commonly known as the Italian water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender leaves that float on the water surface, supported by hollow stems. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Peschiera plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
