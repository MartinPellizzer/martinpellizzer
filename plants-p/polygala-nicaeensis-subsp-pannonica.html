Polygala nicaeensis subsp. pannonica

Polygala nicaeensis subsp.

pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It has slender, branched stems and small, compound leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
