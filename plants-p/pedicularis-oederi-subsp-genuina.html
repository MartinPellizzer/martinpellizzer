Pedicularis oederi subsp. genuina

Pedicularis oederi subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the Alpine beard tongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
