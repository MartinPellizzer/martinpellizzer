Piper longegerminatum

Piper longegerminatum, commonly known as the long-seeded pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with prominent veins.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit structure.
