Persica domestica var. lutea

Persica domestica var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow peach.

This variant is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange fruit flesh, which sets it apart from the more common white or yellow peaches. The flowers are typically larger and more showy than those of standard peaches, often with a more intense pink hue. It is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit color.

The plant shares the same growth habits as other peaches, including a deciduous nature and a preference for well-drained, fertile soil.
