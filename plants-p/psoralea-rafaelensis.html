Psoralea rafaelensis

Psoralea rafaelensis, commonly known as Rafael's psoralea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically range in color from pink to purple. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
