Plantago jacquinii

Plantago jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's plantain, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval leaves and spikes of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial herb that produces capsules containing numerous small seeds.

The species is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
