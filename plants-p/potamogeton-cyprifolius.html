Potamogeton cyprifolius

Potamogeton cyprifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
