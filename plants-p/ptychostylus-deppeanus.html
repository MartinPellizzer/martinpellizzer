Ptychostylus deppeanus

Ptychostylus deppeanus, commonly known as the Schomburgk's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Ptychostylus deppeanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a subject of interest in botanical research.
