Pothos fractiflexus

Pothos fractiflexus, commonly known as the creeping pothos, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces, making it popular as a houseplant. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, and it is relatively low maintenance.

Pothos fractiflexus is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
