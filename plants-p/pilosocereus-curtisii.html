Pilosocereus curtisii

Pilosocereus curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny appearance and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. The plant typically forms a rounded or globular shape, with multiple stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, appearing in the summer months.

Pilosocereus curtisii is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
