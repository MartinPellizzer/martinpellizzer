Piper diffusum

Piper diffusum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, containing three seeds.

Piper diffusum is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
