Primula macrophylla subsp. pulverea

Primula macrophylla subsp.

pulverea, commonly known as the dusty primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white.

The leaves are typically broad and hairy, contributing to its common name due to their dusty appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
