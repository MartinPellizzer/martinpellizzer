Potentilla pentaphylla

Potentilla pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves divided into five lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Potentilla pentaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
