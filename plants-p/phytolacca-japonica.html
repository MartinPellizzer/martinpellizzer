Phytolacca japonica

Phytolacca japonica, commonly known as Japanese pokeweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered with sticky, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
