Prostanthera albohirta

Prostanthera albohirta, commonly known as the white-haired mint bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry forests.

Prostanthera albohirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous medicine.
