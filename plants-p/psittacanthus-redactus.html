Psittacanthus redactus

Psittacanthus redactus, commonly known as the red-leaf staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds that resemble the antlers of a stag. The plant's leaves are typically a vibrant red or orange when mature, which sets it apart from other fern species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, porous substrates to anchor its roots.

Psittacanthus redactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper humidity and light conditions.
