Podophyllum emodi var. bhootanense

Podophyllum emodi var. bhootanense, commonly known as Bhutan pachysandra, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Bhutan and northeastern India.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit with dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
