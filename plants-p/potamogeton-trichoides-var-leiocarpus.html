Potamogeton trichoides var. leiocarpus

Potamogeton trichoides var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth carpel watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in shallow, freshwater environments. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
