Pilea racemiformis

Pilea racemiformis, commonly known as the Shingleplant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to produce small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pilea racemiformis is also notable for its ability to propagate through both seeds and vegetative means.
