Polypodium moricandii

Polypodium moricandii, commonly known as the Moricandii fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and possesses fronds that are usually narrow and linear. The plant thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in mountainous and desert environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically arranged in a single row along the margins of the fronds.

Polypodium moricandii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
