Primulina anisocymosa

Primulina anisocymosa, commonly known as the variable primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with glossy, ovate leaves that are often marked with distinctive patterns. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
