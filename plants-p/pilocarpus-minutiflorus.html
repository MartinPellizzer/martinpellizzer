Pilocarpus minutiflorus

Pilocarpus minutiflorus, commonly known as the Brazilian pilocarp tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense foliage, small elliptical leaves, and clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Pilocarpus minutiflorus is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in treating eye conditions and as a stimulant.
