Pothos odoratus

Pothos odoratus, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that range in color from light green to variegated yellow and white. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

Pothos odoratus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
