Pandanus pistillaris

Pandanus pistillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in a spike at the top of the stem. Its roots are sometimes used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
