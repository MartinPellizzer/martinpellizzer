Polygala amambayensis

Polygala amambayensis, commonly known as the Amambay polygala, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Paraguay.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is an important part of the local flora in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
