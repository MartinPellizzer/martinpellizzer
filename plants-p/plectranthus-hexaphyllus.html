Plectranthus hexaphyllus

Plectranthus hexaphyllus, commonly known as the purple-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of tubular, lavender-pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers. It is often used in herbal medicine for its calming properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
