Pomatocalpa macphersonii

Pomatocalpa macphersonii, commonly known as the MacPherson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a deep violet or purple hue with intricate patterns. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears several flowers, each with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is appreciated by botanists and horticulturists alike.
