Pladera decussata

Pladera decussata, commonly known as the crossleaf plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are arranged in a cross-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. Native to the Americas, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
