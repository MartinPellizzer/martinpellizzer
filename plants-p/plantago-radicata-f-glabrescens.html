Plantago radicata f. glabrescens

Plantago radicata f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its reduced leaf pubescence.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
