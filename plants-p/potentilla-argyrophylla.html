Potentilla argyrophylla

Potentilla argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Potentilla argyrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic features.
