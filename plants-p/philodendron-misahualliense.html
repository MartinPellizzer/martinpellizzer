Philodendron misahualliense

Philodendron misahualliense, commonly known as the Misahualli philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America, specifically Peru.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking variegated patterns. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and anchor itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it has become a popular choice for indoor gardening.
