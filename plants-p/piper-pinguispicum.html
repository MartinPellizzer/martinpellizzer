Piper pinguispicum

Piper pinguispicum, commonly known as the fatty blight pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, contains numerous tiny seeds.

Piper pinguispicum is valued for its unique flavor and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
