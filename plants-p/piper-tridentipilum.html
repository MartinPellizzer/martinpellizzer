Piper tridentipilum

Piper tridentipilum, commonly known as the three-toothed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have three distinct lobes, and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries. Its fruit is typically reddish when mature and contains a single seed.

Piper tridentipilum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
