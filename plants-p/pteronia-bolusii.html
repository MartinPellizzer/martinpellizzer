Pteronia bolusii

Pteronia bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus pterion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
