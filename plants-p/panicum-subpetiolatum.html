Panicum subpetiolatum

Panicum subpetiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of loose, drooping clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Panicum subpetiolatum is also used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
