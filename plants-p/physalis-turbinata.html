Physalis turbinata

Physalis turbinata, commonly known as the common groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive inflated, papery calyx that encloses the fruit. The fruit, often referred to as a "physalis," is typically yellow or orange and contains several small, edible seeds. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
