Polypodium tablazianum

Polypodium tablazianum, commonly known as the Tablazian holly fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to parts of South America.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with fronds that are pinnately compound, often with a leathery texture. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in sporangia, which are clustered in sori.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
