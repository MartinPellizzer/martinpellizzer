Phyllanthus depressus

Phyllanthus depressus, commonly known as the smooth phyllanthus, is a small, evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Phyllanthus depressus is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal uses.
