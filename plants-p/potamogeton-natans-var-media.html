Potamogeton natans var. media

Potamogeton natans var. media, commonly known as the common pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems.

It has long, flexible stems that grow horizontally along the bottom of lakes and ponds. Its leaves are narrow, ribbon-like, and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface.

It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.
