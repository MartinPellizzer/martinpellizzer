Piper caldasianum

Piper caldasianum, commonly known as the Caldasian pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of Colombia and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and unique flavor profile, which is used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
