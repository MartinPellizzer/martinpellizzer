Potentilla alboargentea

Potentilla alboargentea, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
