Pleurothallis graminea

Pleurothallis graminea, commonly known as the grassy orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Pleurothallis graminea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
