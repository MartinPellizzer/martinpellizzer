Pulsatilla patens var. kolmejensis

Pulsatilla patens var. kolmejensis, commonly known as the Kolmejenskaya pasqueflower, is a variant of the pasqueflower native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, with prominent purple or brown markings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
