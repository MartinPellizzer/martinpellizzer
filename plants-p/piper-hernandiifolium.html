Piper hernandiifolium

Piper hernandiifolium, commonly known as the Mexican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties and as a spice.
