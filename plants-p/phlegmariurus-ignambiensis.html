Phlegmariurus ignambiensis

Phlegmariurus ignambiensis, commonly known as the ignambien moss, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Porellaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sporophytes, are small and often embedded within the leafy tissue.

This moss plays a significant role in soil stabilization and moisture retention in its native habitats.
