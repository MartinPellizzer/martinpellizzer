Pterocarpus tinctorius var. tinctorius

Pterocarpus tinctorius var. tinctorius, commonly known as the Indian sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the tree can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The botanical features of this species make it both ecologically and economically significant in its native habitat.
