Ponapea hentyi

Ponapea hentyi, commonly known as the Henty's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Micronesia and Fiji. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices.

Ponapea hentyi is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
