Phragmites xenochloa

Phragmites xenochloa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, blade-like leaves and dense clusters of fluffy, grayish-green flowers that appear in the summer. This plant is often found in marshes, lakeshores, and along the edges of rivers, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species. Its rhizome system allows it to spread rapidly, forming large colonies that can dominate wetland ecosystems.

Despite its invasive tendencies in some regions, Phragmites xenochloa is an important component of wetland biodiversity.
