Pectis elongata

Pectis elongata, commonly known as the longleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. Pectis elongata is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
