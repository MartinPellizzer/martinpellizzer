Pleurothallis exilis

Pleurothallis exilis, commonly known as the slender pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Pleurothallis exilis is often found growing epiphytically on trees in the understory of rainforests.
