Piper indicum

Piper indicum, commonly known as long pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black and white pepper. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are harvested when mature for their aromatic and medicinal properties. The fruit contains a high concentration of piperine, a compound responsible for its spicy flavor and various health benefits.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in culinary applications.
