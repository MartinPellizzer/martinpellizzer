Podocarpus philippinensis

Podocarpus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine podocarp or the Philippine ironwood, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, cone-like structures. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a conical crown. Its leaves are needle-like, dark green, and arranged spirally along the branches.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in montane forests at higher elevations.
