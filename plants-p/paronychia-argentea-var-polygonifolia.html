Paronychia argentea var. polygonifolia

Paronychia argentea var. polygonifolia, commonly known as silverleaf sandmat, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming habit with fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in xeriscaping due to its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
