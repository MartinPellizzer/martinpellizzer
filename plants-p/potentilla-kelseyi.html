Potentilla kelseyi

Potentilla kelseyi, commonly known as Kelse's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla kelseyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
