Pandanus odoratissimus var. laevis

Pandanus odoratissimus var. laevis, commonly known as the sweet sedge or pandan, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, fragrant leaves that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that develop into woody fruits containing seeds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily in wetland and coastal environments.

The leaves are particularly valued for their aromatic properties and versatility in culinary applications.
