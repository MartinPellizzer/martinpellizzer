Platycodon glaucus f. violaceus

Platycodon glaucus f. violaceus, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early summer.
