Philadelphus yokohamae

Philadelphus yokohamae, commonly known as Yokohama mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
