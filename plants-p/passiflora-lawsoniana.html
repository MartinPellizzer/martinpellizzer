Passiflora lawsoniana

Passiflora lawsoniana, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Passiflora lawsoniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
