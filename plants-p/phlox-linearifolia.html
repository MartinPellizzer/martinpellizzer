Phlox linearifolia

Phlox linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaf phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of showy, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Phlox linearifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
