Passiflora guentheri

Passiflora guentheri, commonly known as the Guenther's passionflower, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, complex flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant's flowers are typically blue or purple with yellow and white markings, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its fruits are edible berries that ripen to a red or orange color.

Passiflora guentheri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
