Poa pratensis var. praticola

Poa pratensis var. praticola, commonly known as the common meadow grass, is a variant of the tall fescue species.

It is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in meadows and grasslands. This plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to a wide range of soil conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of both natural and cultivated landscapes.
