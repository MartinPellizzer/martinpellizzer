Phreatia plagiopetala

Phreatia plagiopetala, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and large, broad leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas near water sources.

Phreatia plagiopetala is a terrestrial orchid that requires well-drained soil and consistent moisture for optimal growth.
