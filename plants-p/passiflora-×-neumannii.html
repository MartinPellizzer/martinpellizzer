Passiflora × neumannii

Passiflora × neumannii, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a hybrid species within the Passifloraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

The fruit, though less commonly consumed, can be edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.
