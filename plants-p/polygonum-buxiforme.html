Polygonum buxiforme

Polygonum buxiforme, commonly known as the boxleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that appear in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Polygonum buxiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its foliage and structural form.
