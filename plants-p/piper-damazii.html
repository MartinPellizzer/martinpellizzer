Piper damazii

Piper damazii, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
