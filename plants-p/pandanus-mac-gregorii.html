Pandanus mac-gregorii

Pandanus mac-gregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, followed by the development of fibrous, palm-like fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often used in traditional crafts due to its durable, fibrous leaves.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
