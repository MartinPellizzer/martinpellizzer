Placus acutatus

Placus acutatus, commonly known as the sharpleaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding substrate, a common trait among orchids.

Placus acutatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
