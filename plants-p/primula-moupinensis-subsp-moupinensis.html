Primula moupinensis subsp. moupinensis

Primula moupinensis subsp.

moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It typically grows in cool, moist environments such as forest understoreys and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
