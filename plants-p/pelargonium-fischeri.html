Pelargonium fischeri

Pelargonium fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, which appear during the summer months. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium fischeri is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
