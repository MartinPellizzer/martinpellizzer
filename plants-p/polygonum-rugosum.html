Polygonum rugosum

Polygonum rugosum, commonly known as rough knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems that are often covered in small, stiff hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.
