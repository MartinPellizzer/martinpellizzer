Parodia zaletaewana

Parodia zaletaewana, commonly known as the Zaletewan Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiny, globular shape. The plant features dense clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its areoles are adorned with white to pale blue bristles, and it produces small, green, pear-shaped fruits.

Parodia zaletaewana is a slow-growing, cold-hardy cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
