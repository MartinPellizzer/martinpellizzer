Primula nivalis moorcroftiana

Primula nivalis moorcroftiana, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in high-altitude environments, typically growing in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
