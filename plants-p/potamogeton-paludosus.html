Potamogeton paludosus

Potamogeton paludosus, commonly known as the swamp pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spike-like structures. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is commonly found in marshes and wetlands.

Potamogeton paludosus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
