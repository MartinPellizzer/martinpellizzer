Pleioblastus chino var. hisauchii

Pleioblastus chino var. hisauchii, commonly known as the Hisauchii bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It features slender, upright stems with a smooth, pale green to bluish-green sheath that often has a slight purplish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens and shaded landscapes.

Its slow growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a decorative plant in horticultural settings.
