Posidonia coriacea

Posidonia coriacea, commonly known as the hard seagrass, is a marine flowering plant found in coastal areas of the Mediterranean Sea.

It thrives in shallow, sandy or muddy substrates and forms dense underwater meadows. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing sediments and supporting marine biodiversity.

Posidonia coriacea is also known for its ability to form extensive underwater forests that provide habitat for various marine species.
