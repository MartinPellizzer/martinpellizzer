Pleurothallis possoae

Pleurothallis possoae, commonly known as the Posso orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its growth habit is characterized by a short, pseudobulb-like stem and narrow, strap-shaped leaves.

Pleurothallis possoae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
