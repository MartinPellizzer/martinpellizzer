Pavetta baconia var. nigrescens

Pavetta baconia var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems that turn dark purple or black with age. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and often have a distinctive violet or purple hue.

The species is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
