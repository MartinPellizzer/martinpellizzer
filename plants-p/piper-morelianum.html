Piper morelianum

Piper morelianum, commonly known as Morelian pepper or wild long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, curved berries that turn red when mature. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

The botanical features of Piper morelianum make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
