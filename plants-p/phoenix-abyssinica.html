Phoenix abyssinica

Phoenix abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian date palm, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible dates, although it is less productive than other date palm species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.

Its ecological and cultural significance is notable in the regions where it is found.
