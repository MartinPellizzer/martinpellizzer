Porophyllum hieracioides

Porophyllum hieracioides, commonly known as the hieracium-like fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

Porophyllum hieracioides is often found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
