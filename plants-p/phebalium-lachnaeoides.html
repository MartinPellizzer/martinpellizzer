Phebalium lachnaeoides

Phebalium lachnaeoides, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

Phebalium lachnaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native garden landscaping.
