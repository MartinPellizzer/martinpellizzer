Phytelephas seemannii subsp. brevipes

Phytelephas seemannii subsp.

brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its robust, clustered stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens in suitable climates.
