Pterostylis neopolyphylla

Pterostylis neopolyphylla, commonly known as the golden helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow labellum and a narrow, grass-like leaf. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and heathlands. It produces a single, erect spike bearing several small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
