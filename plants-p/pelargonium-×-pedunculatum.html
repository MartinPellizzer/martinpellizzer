Pelargonium × pedunculatum

Pelargonium × pedunculatum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a variety of ornamental features.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets, window boxes, and garden borders.

Its adaptability to different growing conditions makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
