Pelargonium euphraseum

Pelargonium euphraseum, commonly known as the Euphrasian geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Its leaves are opposite, palmately lobed, and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Pelargonium euphraseum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
