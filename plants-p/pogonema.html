Pogonema

Pogonema, commonly known as the beard grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy inflorescences that resemble a beard, giving it its common name. These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Pogonema species are often used in restoration ecology due to their ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

They produce small, wind-pollinated flowers and are adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.
