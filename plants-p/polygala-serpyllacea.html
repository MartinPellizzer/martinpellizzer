Polygala serpyllacea

Polygala serpyllacea, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that have a distinctive, feathery appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in early summer.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
