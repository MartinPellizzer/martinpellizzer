Paepalanthus arachnoides

Paepalanthus arachnoides, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitat.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
