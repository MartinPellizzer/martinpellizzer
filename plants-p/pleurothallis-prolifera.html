Pleurothallis prolifera

Pleurothallis prolifera, commonly known as the orchid with the delicate white flowers, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and aerial roots. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often appear in clusters, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often found in humid, shaded environments.
