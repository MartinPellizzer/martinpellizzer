Polemonium occidentale subsp. typicum

Polemonium occidentale subsp.

typicum, commonly known as Western Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stem, with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for woodland gardens and native plant landscapes.
