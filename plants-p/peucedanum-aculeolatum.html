Peucedanum aculeolatum

Peucedanum aculeolatum, commonly known as the prickly hogfennel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain small, oily seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Peucedanum aculeolatum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
