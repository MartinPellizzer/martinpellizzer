Pelargonium × rosenthalianum

Pelargonium × rosenthalianum, commonly known as the rose geranium, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × inodorum.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often featuring deep green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. It is a popular choice for container gardening due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.

Pelargonium × rosenthalianum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to both indoor and outdoor environments.
