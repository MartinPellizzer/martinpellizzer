Ponerorchis cucullata

Ponerorchis cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique hood-like structure formed by the fusion of the sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive hood shape that protects the reproductive structures.

Ponerorchis cucullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
