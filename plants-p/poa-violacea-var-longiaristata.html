Poa violacea var. longiaristata

Poa violacea var. longiaristata, commonly known as the purple meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
