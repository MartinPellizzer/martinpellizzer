Pulsatilla × bolzanensis

Pulsatilla × bolzanensis, commonly known as the Bolzanian pasqueflower, is a hybrid plant in the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly in the Southern Alps. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
