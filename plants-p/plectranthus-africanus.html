Plectranthus africanus

Plectranthus africanus, commonly known as the African helmet plant, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, helmet-like shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both garden and horticultural use.
