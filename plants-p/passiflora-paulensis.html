Passiflora paulensis

Passiflora paulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian passion flower, is a climbing vine native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora paulensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
