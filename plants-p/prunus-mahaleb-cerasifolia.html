Prunus mahaleb cerasifolia

Prunus mahaleb cerasifolia, commonly known as the sweet cherry or cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also known for its fragrant foliage and is sometimes used in perfumery.
