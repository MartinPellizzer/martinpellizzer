Passiflora horsfieldii

Passiflora horsfieldii, commonly known as the Horsfield's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces striking, showy flowers with five prominent sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The fruit, which develops from the flower, is edible and has a unique flavor.
