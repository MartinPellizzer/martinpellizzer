Polygonum poiretii var. madagascariensis

Polygonum poiretii var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Madagascar.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
