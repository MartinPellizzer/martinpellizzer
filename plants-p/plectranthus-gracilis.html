Plectranthus gracilis

Plectranthus gracilis, commonly known as the silverleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in warm climates.
