Piper lineatum var. hirtipetiolatum

Piper lineatum var. hirtipetiolatum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed pepper plant, is a variant of the creeping woodbine vine.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and elongated, heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by red, berry-like fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
