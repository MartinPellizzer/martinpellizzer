Piper melanostachyum

Piper melanostachyum, commonly known as black-stemmed pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit, known as a drupe, is typically harvested when mature for its aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The botanical features of Piper melanostachyum make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and spice trade.
