Prunus tenella var. campestris

Prunus tenella var. campestris, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall, adding seasonal interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its size and aesthetic appeal, it is often used in bonsai and as a decorative element in landscape design.
