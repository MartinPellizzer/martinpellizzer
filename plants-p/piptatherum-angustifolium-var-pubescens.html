Piptatherum angustifolium var. pubescens

Piptatherum angustifolium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy narrowleaf brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, prairies, and forest edges. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to a range of soil types and its ability to thrive in moderate to dry conditions.
