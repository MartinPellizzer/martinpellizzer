Philodendron guadalupense

Philodendron guadalupense, commonly known as the Guadalupense Philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by a spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among plant enthusiasts for its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
