Pelargonium confertum

Pelargonium confertum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.

Pelargonium confertum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
