Paeonia officinalis subsp. banatica

Paeonia officinalis subsp.

banatica, commonly known as the Banat peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan region. It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients and help the plant survive harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.
