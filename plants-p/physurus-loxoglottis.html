Physurus loxoglottis

Physurus loxoglottis, commonly known as the silver-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green foliage. Its adaptations to desert conditions include deep root systems and the ability to store water in its thick stems.

Physurus loxoglottis is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
