Poa leptalea

Poa leptalea, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Poa leptalea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
