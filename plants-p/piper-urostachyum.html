Piper urostachyum

Piper urostachyum, commonly known as the mountain pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the seeds, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
