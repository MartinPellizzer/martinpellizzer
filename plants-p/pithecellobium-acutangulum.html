Pithecellobium acutangulum

Pithecellobium acutangulum, commonly known as the sharp-angled pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain small, sweet fruits similar to beans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
