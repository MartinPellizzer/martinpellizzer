Pelargonium leopoldianum

Pelargonium leopoldianum, commonly known as the Leopold's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
