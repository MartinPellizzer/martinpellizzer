Pachira bracteolata

Pachira bracteolata, commonly known as the Brazilian money plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for both gardeners and interior plant enthusiasts.
