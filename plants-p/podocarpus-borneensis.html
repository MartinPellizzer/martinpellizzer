Podocarpus borneensis

Podocarpus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, woody cones. The tree typically grows in tropical rainforests, often in mountainous regions, and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. It plays a significant role in the ecosystem by providing habitat for various species and contributing to soil stability.

Additionally, its timber is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
