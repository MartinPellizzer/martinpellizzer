Piptochaetium napostaense

Piptochaetium napostaense, commonly known as the Naposta grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It is adapted to thrive in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its resilience and ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
