Plantago truncata var. canohirsuta

Plantago truncata var. canohirsuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its medicinal properties have been historically utilized by indigenous communities for their healing benefits.
