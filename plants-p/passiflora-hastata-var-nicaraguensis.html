Passiflora hastata var. nicaraguensis

Passiflora hastata var. nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaragua passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
