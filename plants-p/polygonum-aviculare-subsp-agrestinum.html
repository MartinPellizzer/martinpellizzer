Polygonum aviculare subsp. agrestinum

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

agrestinum, commonly known as field knotgrass, is a low-growing perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with slender, branched stems that often lie prostrate.

The plant features small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings but also serves as a food source for certain insects and birds.
