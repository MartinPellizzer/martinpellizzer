Phacelia hispida var. umbrosa

Phacelia hispida var. umbrosa, commonly known as the hairy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
