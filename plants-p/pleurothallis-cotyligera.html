Pleurothallis cotyligera

Pleurothallis cotyligera, commonly known as the moon orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant features intricate, delicate flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often displaying a mix of white, green, and purple hues. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native regions.
