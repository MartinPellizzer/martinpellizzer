Poa sterilis subsp. versicolor

Poa sterilis subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including dry and disturbed sites.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the summer months.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in stabilizing soil in grassland ecosystems.
