Psoralea graveolens

Psoralea graveolens, commonly known as the stinkbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant is notable for its strong, often unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the leaves are crushed.
