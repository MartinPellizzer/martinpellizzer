Pandorea austrocaledonica

Pandorea austrocaledonica, commonly known as the Australian climbing pandorea, is a flowering plant native to Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pandorea austrocaledonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a hedge plant.
