Phlomis vavilovii

Phlomis vavilovii, commonly known as the Vavilov's torchgrass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces fibrous roots and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Phlomis vavilovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
