Physalis nicandroides

Physalis nicandroides, commonly known as the crimson globe berry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its distinctive red, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange berries that are typically covered by a papery husk. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant fruit and unique appearance.

However, the berries are toxic and should not be consumed by humans or animals.
