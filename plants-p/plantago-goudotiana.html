Plantago goudotiana

Plantago goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing effects.
