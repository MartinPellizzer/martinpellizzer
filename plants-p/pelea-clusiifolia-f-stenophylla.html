Pelea clusiifolia f. stenophylla

Pelea clusiifolia f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed glorybower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Clusiaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, bright yellow to orange-red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked in favor of its striking foliage.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
