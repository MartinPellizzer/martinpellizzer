Pleurothallis paradoxa

Pleurothallis paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a unique coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a short, branched stem.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native environment.
