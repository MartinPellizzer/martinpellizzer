Pulsatilla breynii

Pulsatilla breynii, commonly known as the alpine pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a short, hairy stem with basal leaves that are palmately divided. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its delicate nature, it is sensitive to environmental changes and is considered a rare species in many areas.
