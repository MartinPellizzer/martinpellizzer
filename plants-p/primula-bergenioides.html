Primula bergenioides

Primula bergenioides, commonly known as the Bergenia primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, moist soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a favored plant in horticulture.
