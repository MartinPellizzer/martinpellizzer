Pulsatilla × petteri

Pulsatilla × petteri, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that typically appear in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant produces a distinctive, spiky seed head that resembles a crown, and its leaves are palmately divided with a hairy texture. Pulsatilla × petteri thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
