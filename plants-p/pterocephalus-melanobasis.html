Pterocephalus melanobasis

Pterocephalus melanobasis, commonly known as the black-headed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and parts of China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
