Pelargonium dipetalum subsp. dipetalum

Pelargonium dipetalum subsp.

dipetalum, commonly known as the red-stemmed geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces showy flowers in shades of pink to red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valued addition to both garden and landscape settings.
