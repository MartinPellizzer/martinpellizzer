Polypodium graveolens

Polypodium graveolens, commonly known as the fragrant fern, is a tropical evergreen fern native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often fragrant when crushed. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
