Phaseolus adenanthus f. guaraniticus

Phaseolus adenanthus f. guaraniticus, commonly known as the Guarani bean, is a wild legume native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped seeds and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

This variety is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers due to its potential for drought resistance and nutritional value.
