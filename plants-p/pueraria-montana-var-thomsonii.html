Pueraria montana var. thomsonii

Pueraria montana var. thomsonii, commonly known as Kwao Krua, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system, which can grow deep into the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
