Panzeria canescens

Panzeria canescens, commonly known as the woolly panzeria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged periods of drought.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study in xerophytic plant species.
