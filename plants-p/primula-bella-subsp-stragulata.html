Primula bella subsp. stragulata

Primula bella subsp.

stragulata, commonly known as the Stragulate Primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive stragglant appearance.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
