Pourthiaea arguta var. parvifolia

Pourthiaea arguta var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pourthiaea, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in terminal spikes, and it blooms during the cooler months.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
