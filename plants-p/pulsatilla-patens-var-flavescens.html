Pulsatilla patens var. flavescens

Pulsatilla patens var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem with palmately divided leaves that are also hairy and have a distinctive, waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected in its natural range.
