Pyrrhopappus rothrockii

Pyrrhopappus rothrockii, commonly known as the Rothrock's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is named after the botanist John Rothrock, who contributed significantly to the study of North American flora.
