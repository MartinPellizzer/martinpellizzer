Planera repens

Planera repens, commonly known as creeping plane, is a low-growing shrub native to South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
