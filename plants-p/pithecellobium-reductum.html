Pithecellobium reductum

Pithecellobium reductum, commonly known as the small-flowered acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
