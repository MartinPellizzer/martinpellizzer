Palisota alopecurus

Palisota alopecurus, commonly known as narrowleaf water clover, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and distinctive trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands.

Palisota alopecurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
