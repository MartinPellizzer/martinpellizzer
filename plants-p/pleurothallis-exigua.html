Pleurothallis exigua

Pleurothallis exigua, commonly known as the slender pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
