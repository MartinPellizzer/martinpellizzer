Prunus spinosa var. amphitricha

Prunus spinosa var. amphitricha, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the edible fruits. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

The species is often used in hedgerows and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
