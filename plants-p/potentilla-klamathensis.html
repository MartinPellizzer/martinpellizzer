Potentilla klamathensis

Potentilla klamathensis, commonly known as Klamath cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically with five leaflets, and produces showy yellow flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

Potentilla klamathensis plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including pollinators like bees and butterflies.
