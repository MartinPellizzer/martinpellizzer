Panicum tiricaoides

Panicum tiricaoides, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and inflorescences that resemble panicles. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in loose, branching spikes.

Panicum tiricaoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
