Paspalum cangarum

Paspalum cangarum, commonly known as the Cangarum grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.

Paspalum cangarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in various ecological settings.
