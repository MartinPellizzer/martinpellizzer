Pyrus portae-bohemicae

Pyrus portae-bohemicae, commonly known as the Bohemian pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that typically ripen in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct serrated margin. It prefers well-drained soils and partial shade, often growing in forested areas.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
