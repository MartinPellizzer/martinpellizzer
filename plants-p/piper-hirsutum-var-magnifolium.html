Piper hirsutum var. magnifolium

Piper hirsutum var. magnifolium, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
