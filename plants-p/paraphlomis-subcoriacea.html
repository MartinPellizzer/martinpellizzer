Paraphlomis subcoriacea

Paraphlomis subcoriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed paraseris, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, often hairy stems that are covered in a rough, coriaceous texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Paraphlomis subcoriacea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
