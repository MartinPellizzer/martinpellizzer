Polycnemum conjugatum

Polycnemum conjugatum, commonly known as the common water crowfoot, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant has a creeping rhizome with floating leaves that are divided into lobes, often with a distinctive notch at the base. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and fish.
