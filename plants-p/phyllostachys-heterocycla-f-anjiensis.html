Phyllostachys heterocycla f. anjiensis

Phyllostachys heterocycla f. anjiensis, commonly known as the Anji bamboo, is a variety of bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, green culms that often have a yellowish tint, and it grows in dense clumps. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the culms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This bamboo is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a sustainable building material.
