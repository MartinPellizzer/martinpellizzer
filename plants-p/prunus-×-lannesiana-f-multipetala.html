Prunus × lannesiana f. multipetala

Prunus × lannesiana f. multipetala, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus genus characterized by its ornamental double blossoms.

This form exhibits a high number of petals, often resulting in a full, fluffy appearance that distinguishes it from single-flowered varieties. It is a naturally occurring mutation within the species Prunus lannesiana, which is itself a hybrid between Prunus serrulata and Prunus lusitanica. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with ovate leaves and a compact growth habit.

Its floral display makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, particularly in spring.
