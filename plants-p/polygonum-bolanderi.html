Polygonum bolanderi

Polygonum bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
