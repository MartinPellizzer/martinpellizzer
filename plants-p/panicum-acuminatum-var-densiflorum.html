Panicum acuminatum var. densiflorum

Panicum acuminatum var. densiflorum, commonly known as denseflower panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically pink to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental in warm climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it suitable for erosion control and as a landscape plant in suitable environments.
