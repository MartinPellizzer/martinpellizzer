Piper caudilimbum

Piper caudilimbum, commonly known as the long-tailed pepper or caudilimbum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
