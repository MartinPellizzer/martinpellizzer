Paralyxia macrophylla

Paralyxia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant paralyxia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, providing a dense canopy that supports a diverse array of epiphytic plants. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its robust root system helps stabilize soil in its natural habitat, making it ecologically significant.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
