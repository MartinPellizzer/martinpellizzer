Panicum nephelophilum var. levius

Panicum nephelophilum var. levius, commonly known as the cloud-loving grass, is a unique variant of the panicum genus that thrives in high-altitude, cloud-covered environments.

This plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, aiding in reducing water loss in its harsh habitat. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in fine hairs, enhancing its ability to trap moisture from the air. The plant is adapted to cool, moist conditions and is typically found in mountainous regions with persistent cloud cover.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation to extreme environments.
