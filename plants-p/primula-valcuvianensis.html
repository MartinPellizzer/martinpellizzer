Primula valcuvianensis

Primula valcuvianensis, commonly known as the Valcuivian primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, adapting to cold climates with its frost-resistant foliage. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Primula valcuvianensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.
