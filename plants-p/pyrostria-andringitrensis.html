Pyrostria andringitrensis

Pyrostria andringitrensis, commonly known as the Andringitra bamboo, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit, forming dense stands that are often found in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a bluish-green color, while its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and valuable component of Madagascar's biodiversity.
