Potentilla dahurica

Potentilla dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Asia and Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the summer. Its flowers are usually borne on long, slender pedicels, and the plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Potentilla dahurica is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
