Paeonia veitchii var. uniflora

Paeonia veitchii var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces reddish-brown seed pods that split open to release seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and historical cultivation in traditional Chinese horticulture.
