Pleurothallis syringifolia

Pleurothallis syringifolia, commonly known as the silver-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery pubescence. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
