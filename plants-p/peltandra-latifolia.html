Peltandra latifolia

Peltandra latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by triangular, winged fruits. Its rhizomes are edible and have been used as a starch source in various cuisines.

The botanical features of Peltandra latifolia make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
