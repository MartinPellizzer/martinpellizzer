Pulicaria demissa

Pulicaria demissa, commonly known as the lesser groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically reaches a height of about 10-20 centimeters and features silver-gray foliage with fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in heads, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Pulicaria demissa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
