Prunus × lannesiana f. purpurea

Prunus × lannesiana f. purpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf sand cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, velvety leaves that turn a striking purplish-red color in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

This ornamental shrub is valued for its seasonal color changes and adaptability to various soil types.
