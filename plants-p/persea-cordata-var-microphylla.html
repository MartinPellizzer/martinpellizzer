Persea cordata var. microphylla

Persea cordata var. microphylla, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It features elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow-orange fruit with a sweet, honey-like flavor. The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
