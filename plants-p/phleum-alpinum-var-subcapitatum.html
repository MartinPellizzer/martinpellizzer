Phleum alpinum var. subcapitatum

Phleum alpinum var. subcapitatum, commonly known as alpine fescue, is a perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
