Philodendron tweedieanum

Philodendron tweedieanum, commonly known as the Twedie's Philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
