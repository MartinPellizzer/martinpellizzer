Phaseolus cytisoides

Phaseolus cytisoides, commonly known as the yellow bean vine, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces yellow or orange flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has trifoliate leaves and bears pods containing small, yellow beans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in various herbal applications.
