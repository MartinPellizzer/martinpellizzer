Phthirusa clandestina

Phthirusa clandestina, commonly known as the hidden thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its scientific name reflects its elusive nature, as it is often difficult to locate in its natural habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
