Pleioblastus amarus var. tubatus

Pleioblastus amarus var. tubatus, commonly known as the tubular bamboo, is a variety of bamboo characterized by its unique tubular or swollen nodes.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, green stems with a smooth, almost waxy texture, and its leaves are typically long and narrow. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.

This bamboo variety is also valued for its ability to form dense clumps, making it suitable for landscaping in gardens and parks.
