Panicum macilentum

Panicum macilentum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds in panicles. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit, which helps it spread and establish in moist environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
