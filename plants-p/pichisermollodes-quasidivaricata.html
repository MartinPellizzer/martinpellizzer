Pichisermollodes quasidivaricata

Pichisermollodes quasidivaricata, commonly known as the Andean snowberry, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. The plant has a compact growth habit with woody stems that can become somewhat branched. Its leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This species is adapted to harsh, alpine environments, showing resilience to cold and strong winds.
