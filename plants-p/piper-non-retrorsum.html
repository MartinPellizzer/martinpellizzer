Piper non-retrorsum

Piper non-retrorsum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or black pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
