Pandanus japensis

Pandanus japensis, commonly known as the Japanese pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in spikes, which are followed by fibrous, palm-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and utility, it is cultivated in various tropical regions around the world.
