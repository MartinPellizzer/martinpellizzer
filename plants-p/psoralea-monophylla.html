Psoralea monophylla

Psoralea monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a single, linear leaf that is often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Psoralea monophylla is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
