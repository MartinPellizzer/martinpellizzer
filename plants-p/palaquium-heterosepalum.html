Palaquium heterosepalum

Palaquium heterosepalum, commonly known as the red mangostin, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, reddish-purple rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental purposes and traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
