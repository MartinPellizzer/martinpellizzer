Plantago transsilvanica

Plantago transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to Europe, particularly found in regions such as Romania and Hungary.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters. It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a resilient and adaptable plant.
