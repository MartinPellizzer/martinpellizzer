Piper submultinerve

Piper submultinerve, commonly known as the finely veined pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with intricate, fine veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, spindle-shaped berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The botanical features of Piper submultinerve make it an important species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical research.
