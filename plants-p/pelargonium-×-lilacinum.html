Pelargonium × lilacinum

Pelargonium × lilacinum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant leaves, which emit a variety of scents depending on the cultivar. The plant typically has rounded, lobed leaves and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Pelargonium × lilacinum thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
