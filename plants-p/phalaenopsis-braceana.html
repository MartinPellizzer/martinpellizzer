Phalaenopsis braceana

Phalaenopsis braceana, commonly known as the Orchid of the Philippines, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces pendulous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, making it a popular choice for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Phalaenopsis braceana is known for its ability to flower repeatedly, making it a favored species among orchid enthusiasts.
