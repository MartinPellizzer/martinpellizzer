Poa botryoides var. pendula

Poa botryoides var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging meadow grass, is a variant of the tufted grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, pendulous inflorescences that drape downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like flowers in the summer.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
