Panicum crus-galli lusus peculiaris

Panicum crus-galli lusus peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar panicgrass, is a variant of the common panicgrass species.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure and growth habits that differ from the typical form. This plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its botanical interest lies in its morphological anomalies, which make it a subject of study in plant taxonomy.

The species exhibits variations in spikelet arrangement and coloration, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
