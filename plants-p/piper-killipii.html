Piper killipii

Piper killipii, commonly known as the Killip's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody berries that contain the aromatic seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
