Paramyrciaria strigipes

Paramyrciaria strigipes, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, hairy texture on its fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant features compound leaves with a serrated margin and a fibrous root system. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Paramyrciaria strigipes thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
