Pomaderris phillyreifolia

Pomaderris phillyreifolia, commonly known as the silky pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically glandular and emit a distinctive, sometimes spicy fragrance.

Pomaderris phillyreifolia is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
