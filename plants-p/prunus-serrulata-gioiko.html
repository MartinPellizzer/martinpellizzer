Prunus serrulata gioiko

Prunus serrulata gioiko, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a species native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree that belongs to the Rosaceae family and is renowned for its beautiful pink and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters and has smooth, gray bark with a peeling texture. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the fall.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in Japan.
