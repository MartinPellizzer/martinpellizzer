Platytaenia tordyloides

Platytaenia tordyloides, commonly known as the Tordyloides flatleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a dense terminal cluster. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
