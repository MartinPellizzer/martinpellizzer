Polypodium hymenolepioides

Polypodium hymenolepioides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found on rocky substrates in tropical and subtropical regions. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are usually located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
