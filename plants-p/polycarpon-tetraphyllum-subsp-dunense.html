Polycarpon tetraphyllum subsp. dunense

Polycarpon tetraphyllum subsp.

dunense, commonly known as the four-leafed chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces clusters of tiny white flowers.

The plant is characterized by its four-leaf clover-like appearance, which is a distinguishing feature of the species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
