Pelargonium × polyanthon

Pelargonium × polyanthon, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its vigorous growth.

Pelargonium × polyanthon is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
