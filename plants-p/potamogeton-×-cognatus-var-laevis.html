Potamogeton × cognatus var. laevis

Potamogeton × cognatus var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, elongated leaves that are typically 10–30 cm long and 1–2 cm wide. The plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant.

The species is often found in shallow waters with soft, muddy substrates.
