Padus serotina var. pyramidalis

Padus serotina var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically oval with a pointed tip and are arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds.

This plant is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.
