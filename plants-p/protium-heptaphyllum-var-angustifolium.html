Protium heptaphyllum var. angustifolium

Protium heptaphyllum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamanu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
