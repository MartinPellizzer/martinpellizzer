Potamogeton pectinatus var. flabellatus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. flabellatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late summer. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality.
