Primula apoclita

Primula apoclita, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which typically bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Primula apoclita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
