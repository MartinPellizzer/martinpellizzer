Phaseolus looseri

Phaseolus looseri, commonly known as the Looser's bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soil.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.
