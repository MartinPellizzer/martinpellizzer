Potamogeton filiformis f. rivicola

Potamogeton filiformis f. rivicola, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems. It plays a vital part in providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
