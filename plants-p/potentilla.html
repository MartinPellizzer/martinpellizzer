Potentilla

Potentilla, commonly known as shrub cinquefoil, is a genus of flowering plants in the rose family.

These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their showy, often yellow or orange flowers. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are known for their hardy nature. Potentilla species often have palmately lobed leaves and are commonly used in landscaping for their ornamental value.

Many varieties are also cultivated for their ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
