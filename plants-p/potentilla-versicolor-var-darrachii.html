Potentilla versicolor var. darrachii

Potentilla versicolor var. darrachii, commonly known as the Darrach's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The leaves are typically lobed and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the summer season.
