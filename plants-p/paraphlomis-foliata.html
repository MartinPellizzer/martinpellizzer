Paraphlomis foliata

Paraphlomis foliata, commonly known as the broad-leaved blue flag, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and a distinct blue or purple underside. Its flowers are typically small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, often with a violet or blue hue.

Paraphlomis foliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
