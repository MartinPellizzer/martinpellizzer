Passiflora incana

Passiflora incana, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

Passiflora incana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
