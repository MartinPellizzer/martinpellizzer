Placus macrophyllus

Placus macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed succulent, is a member of the Aizoaceae family, characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky soils and full sun. The plant features a low-growing, sprawling habit with thick, oval-shaped leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. Its flowers are typically small, yellowish, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a succulent in gardens and rockeries.
