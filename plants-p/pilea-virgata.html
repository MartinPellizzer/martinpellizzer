Pilea virgata

Pilea virgata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea virgata thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.
