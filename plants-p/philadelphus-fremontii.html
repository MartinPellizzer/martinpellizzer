Philadelphus fremontii

Philadelphus fremontii, commonly known as Fremont's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated margins. Its woody stems are often covered in a layer of grayish bark, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
