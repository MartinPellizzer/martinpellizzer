Philadelphus coronarius var. glabratus

Philadelphus coronarius var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth-flowered mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have a smooth texture, which gives the variety its common name. This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
