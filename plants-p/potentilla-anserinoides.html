Potentilla anserinoides

Potentilla anserinoides, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its narrow, elliptical leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
