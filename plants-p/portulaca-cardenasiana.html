Portulaca cardenasiana

Portulaca cardenasiana, commonly known as the Mexican purslane, is a low-growing annual herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, succulent leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating tolerance to drought and high temperatures. Its stems and leaves are often used in traditional Mexican cuisine, particularly in dishes like tacos and salads.

Botanically, it belongs to the Portulacaceae family and is characterized by its ability to store water in its leaves and stems.
