Pandanus planus

Pandanus planus, commonly known as the flat pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the pandan family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands, including parts of Polynesia and Micronesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, often reaching several feet in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and for its aromatic leaves, which are utilized in cooking and medicine.
