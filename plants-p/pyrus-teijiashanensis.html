Pyrus teijiashanensis

Pyrus teijiashanensis, commonly known as the Teijiashan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing ornamental leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.
