Parietaria kemulariae

Parietaria kemulariae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils, often found in rocky or wall crevices. The plant is known for its stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for various insects.
