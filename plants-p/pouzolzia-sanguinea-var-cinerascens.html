Pouzolzia sanguinea var. cinerascens

Pouzolzia sanguinea var. cinerascens, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red sandalwood variant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically reddish or reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
