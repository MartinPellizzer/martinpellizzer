Persicaria virginiana f. albiflora

Persicaria virginiana f. albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. Its most distinguishing feature is its white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in dense, terminal clusters.

The species is part of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive bracts and often edible seeds.
