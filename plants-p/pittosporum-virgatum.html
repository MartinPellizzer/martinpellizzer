Pittosporum virgatum

Pittosporum virgatum, commonly known as the silver leaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that turns a striking silver-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white or cream-colored flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

Pittosporum virgatum prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
