Persicaria ferruginea

Persicaria ferruginea, commonly known as rusty knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a reddish tinge on the underside.

Persicaria ferruginea is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
