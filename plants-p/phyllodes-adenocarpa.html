Phyllodes adenocarpa

Phyllodes adenocarpa, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. These phyllodes are adapted for water conservation, a feature essential for survival in the arid environments where the plant typically grows. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in compact spikes, which bloom in late summer.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
