Polyrrhiza gracilis

Polyrrhiza gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble bamboo in structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are slender and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The foliage is evergreen, providing year-round ornamental value in suitable climates.
