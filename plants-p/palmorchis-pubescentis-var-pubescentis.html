Palmorchis pubescentis var. pubescentis

Palmorchis pubescentis var. pubescentis, commonly known as the pubescent palm orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose raceme, which are typically pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in shaded, moist forest understories and requires high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
