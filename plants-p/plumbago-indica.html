Plumbago indica

Plumbago indica, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces clusters of vibrant blue flowers, which bloom throughout the summer months. The plant has a woody stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Plumbago indica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
