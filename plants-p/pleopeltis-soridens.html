Pleopeltis soridens

Pleopeltis soridens, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features a creeping rhizome with fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant floral display.
