Phyllanthus lindenianus var. inaequifolius

Phyllanthus lindenianus var. inaequifolius, commonly known as the unequal-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that vary in size and shape. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
