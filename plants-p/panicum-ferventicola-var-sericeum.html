Panicum ferventicola var. sericeum

Panicum ferventicola var. sericeum, commonly known as silverhair panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-brown spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
