Pappobolus ecuadoriensis var. ecuadoriensis

Pappobolus ecuadoriensis var. ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian burrgrass, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, spiky bracts that form a distinctive burr-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture.

This species is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments, often found in grasslands and rocky slopes.
