Parodia brevihamata var. mollispina

Parodia brevihamata var. mollispina, commonly known as the "Pincushion Cactus," is a compact, spineless cactus native to southern Africa.

It features dense, white to pale green tubercles that give it a pincushion-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect pedicels. It is well-suited for rock gardens and container cultivation due to its slow growth and drought tolerance.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
