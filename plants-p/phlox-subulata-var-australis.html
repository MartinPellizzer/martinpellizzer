Phlox subulata var. australis

Phlox subulata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It features dense clusters of small, bright pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silver-blue foliage with needle-like leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This hardy species is drought-tolerant and resistant to pests and diseases, requiring minimal maintenance once established.
