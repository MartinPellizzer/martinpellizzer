Potentilla pensylvanica var. conferta

Potentilla pensylvanica var. conferta, commonly known as the denseflower cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
