Pilosella sciadophora

Pilosella sciadophora, commonly known as the shade-loving hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has hairy, often glandular leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

Pilosella sciadophora is generally herbaceous, with a low-growing habit and is often found in disturbed or semi-natural habitats.
