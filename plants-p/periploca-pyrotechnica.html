Periploca pyrotechnica

Periploca pyrotechnica, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The botanical features of Periploca pyrotechnica make it both visually appealing and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
