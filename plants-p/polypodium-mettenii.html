Polypodium mettenii

Polypodium mettenii, commonly known as the Metten's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, overlapping fronds that form a shield-like structure, providing protection for the spore-producing structures. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients.

The fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters, making it a striking addition to tropical gardens.
