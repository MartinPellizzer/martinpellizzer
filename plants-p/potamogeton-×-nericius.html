Potamogeton × nericius

Potamogeton × nericius, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton maackii and Potamogeton nervosus.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. This plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and marshes. Its presence often indicates good water quality and can contribute to aquatic ecosystem stability by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

However, due to its sterility, it does not produce viable seeds and relies on vegetative propagation for spread.
