Pelargonium tripalmatum

Pelargonium tripalmatum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium tripalmatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
