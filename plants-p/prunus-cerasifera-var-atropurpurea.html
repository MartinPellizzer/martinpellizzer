Prunus cerasifera var. atropurpurea

Prunus cerasifera var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple leaf plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish foliage, which adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, sweet-tart fruits that are edible but not typically cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for hedges, borders, or container gardening.
