Phrynium allouia

Phrynium allouia, commonly known as the African arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in spikes.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
