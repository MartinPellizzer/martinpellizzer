Phyteuma humile

Phyteuma humile, commonly known as the dwarf garden bell, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It produces tall, slender stems with clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a basal rosette structure.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
