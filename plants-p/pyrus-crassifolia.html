Pyrus crassifolia

Pyrus crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Pyrus crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
