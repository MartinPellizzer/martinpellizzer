Panicum commonsianum var. addisonii

Panicum commonsianum var. addisonii, commonly known as Addison's panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, feathery flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
