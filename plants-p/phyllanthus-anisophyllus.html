Phyllanthus anisophyllus

Phyllanthus anisophyllus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive two-lobed base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by bright orange, berry-like fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.
