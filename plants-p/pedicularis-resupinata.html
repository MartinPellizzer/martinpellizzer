Pedicularis resupinata

Pedicularis resupinata, commonly known as the purple monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to purple, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the sepals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Pedicularis resupinata is a perennial species that produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.
