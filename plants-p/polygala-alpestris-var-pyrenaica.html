Polygala alpestris var. pyrenaica

Polygala alpestris var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range from pink to white in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude environments.
