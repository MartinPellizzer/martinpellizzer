Pyrus sieboldii var. incisa

Pyrus sieboldii var. incisa, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant form and seasonal changes in foliage.
