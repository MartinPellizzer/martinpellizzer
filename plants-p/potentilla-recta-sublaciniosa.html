Potentilla recta sublaciniosa

Potentilla recta sublaciniosa, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
