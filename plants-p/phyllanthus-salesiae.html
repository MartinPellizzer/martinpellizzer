Phyllanthus salesiae

Phyllanthus salesiae, commonly known as the desert date or African pear, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often gland-dotted and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange to yellow berries. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a slow growth rate.

Phyllanthus salesiae is also noted for its medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
