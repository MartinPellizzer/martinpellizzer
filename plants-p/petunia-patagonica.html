Petunia patagonica

Petunia patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian petunia, is a species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of violet, blue, and white, and they are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking ornamental value and adaptability.
