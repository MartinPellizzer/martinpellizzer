Populus eucalyptus

Populus eucalyptus, commonly known as the river red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and distinctive flaky bark. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The plant is well adapted to wet environments, such as riverbanks and floodplains, and plays an important role in its native ecosystems.
