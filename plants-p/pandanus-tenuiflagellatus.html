Pandanus tenuiflagellatus

Pandanus tenuiflagellatus, commonly known as the narrow-tailed pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its roots are sometimes used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Overall, it plays an important ecological and cultural role in its native habitats.
