Portulaca fosbergii

Portulaca fosbergii, commonly known as Fosberg's moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically borne on erect stems and are often clustered near the base of the plant.

Portulaca fosbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
