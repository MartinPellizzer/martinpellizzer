Philodendron fosteri

Philodendron fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron fosteri is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
