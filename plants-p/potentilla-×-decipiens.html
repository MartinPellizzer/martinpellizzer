Potentilla × decipiens

Potentilla × decipiens, commonly known as the false silverweed, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical classification as a hybrid contributes to its genetic diversity and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
