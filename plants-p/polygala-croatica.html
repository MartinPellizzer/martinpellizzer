Polygala croatica

Polygala croatica, commonly known as the Croatian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and are arranged in racemes, while the leaves are alternate and pinnately compound.

Polygala croatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
