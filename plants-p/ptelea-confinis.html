Ptelea confinis

Ptelea confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Ptelea confinis is valued for its hardy nature and is used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
