Potamogeton jeholensis

Potamogeton jeholensis, commonly known as the Jehol watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Potamogeton jeholensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
