Pilosocereus mucosiflorus

Pilosocereus mucosiflorus, commonly known as the mucilage-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The mucilage produced by the plant is a unique feature, used by some indigenous communities for medicinal purposes.
