Piperia unalascensis f. olympica

Piperia unalascensis f. olympica, commonly known as the Olympic Piperia, is a rare and unique botanical variant native to the Olympic Peninsula in Washington State.

This fern-like plant belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that resemble those of a fern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories where it benefits from high humidity and dappled sunlight. The plant's leaves are finely divided, with a soft, grass-like texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern and is protected in certain areas.
