Piper aequale var. lasiocarpum

Piper aequale var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as the hairy-fruited pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper aequale species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and distinctive, elongated fruits that are typically dark purple when mature. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. Its botanical features include a climbing habit, with small, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface.

The fruits are often used in traditional medicine and have a pungent, spicy flavor similar to other Piper species.
