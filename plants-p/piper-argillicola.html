Piper argillicola

Piper argillicola, commonly known as the clay pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, clay-rich soils. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to ascend trees and other vegetation. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, elliptical berries that contain the seeds.

Piper argillicola is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
