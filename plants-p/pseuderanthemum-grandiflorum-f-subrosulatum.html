Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum f. subrosulatum

Pseuderanthemum grandiflorum f. subrosulatum, commonly known as the variegated pseuderanthemum, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with distinctive white or cream variegation along the edges, enhancing its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is prized for its compact growth habit and vibrant foliage, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
