Pseudolysimachion kiusianum subsp. maritimum

Pseudolysimachion kiusianum subsp.

maritimum, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun exposure. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in dune environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, saline conditions.
