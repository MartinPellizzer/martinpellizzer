Piper readii

Piper readii, commonly known as the Red River pepper or Read's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines, and thrives in tropical climates. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the spicy compound piperine, similar to black pepper. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of spice.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.
