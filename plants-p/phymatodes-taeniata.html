Phymatodes taeniata

Phymatodes taeniata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits known as achenes. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-resistant.
