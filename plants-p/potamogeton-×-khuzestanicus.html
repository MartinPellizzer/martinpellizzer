Potamogeton × khuzestanicus

Potamogeton × khuzestanicus, commonly known as the Khuzestan watermilfoil, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to the Caspian Sea region.

It is characterized by its submerged, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in freshwater environments with soft, muddy substrates and moderate to slow water currents.

Potamogeton × khuzestanicus plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
