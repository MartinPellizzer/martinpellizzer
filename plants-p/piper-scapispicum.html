Piper scapispicum

Piper scapispicum, commonly known as the scarlet pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems that twine around supports and its clusters of small, bright red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glossy texture.

The berries are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a valued plant in some local cuisines.
