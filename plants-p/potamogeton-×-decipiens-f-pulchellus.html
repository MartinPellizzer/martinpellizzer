Potamogeton × decipiens f. pulchellus

Potamogeton × decipiens f. pulchellus, commonly known as the velvet pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its dense, soft, and often hairy foliage.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are usually submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind or water. Its morphology is adapted for life in still or gently flowing water, with rhizomes that anchor it to the substrate.

This variant is notable for its distinctive appearance and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
