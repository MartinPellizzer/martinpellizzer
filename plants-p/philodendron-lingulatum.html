Philodendron lingulatum

Philodendron lingulatum, commonly known as the laceleaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, intricate appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its ornamental value.
