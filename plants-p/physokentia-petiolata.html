Physokentia petiolata

Physokentia petiolata, commonly known as the broad-leaf cycad, is a species of cycad native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feathery leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are often used in traditional settings for their aesthetic and symbolic value.

Physokentia petiolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
