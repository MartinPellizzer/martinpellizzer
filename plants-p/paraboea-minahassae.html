Paraboea minahassae

Paraboea minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa paraboea, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
