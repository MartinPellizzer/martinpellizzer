Pitcairnia robert-downsii

Pitcairnia robert-downsii, commonly known as the Robert-Downs pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the Andean cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among many bromeliads. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
