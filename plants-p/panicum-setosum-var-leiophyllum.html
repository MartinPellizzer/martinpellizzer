Panicum setosum var. leiophyllum

Panicum setosum var. leiophyllum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle that bears small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
