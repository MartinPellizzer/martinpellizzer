Prunus augustana

Prunus augustana, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant bears edible, dark red to black berries that are often used in culinary applications. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Prunus augustana is also noted for its tolerance to urban environments and air pollution.
