Panicum caudatum

Panicum caudatum, commonly known as the tufted grass or feather grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that grows in open woodlands and prairies, often forming dense clumps. The plant has slender, arching stems that bear long, narrow leaves, and it produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences. Its flowers are typically light green to brown, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Panicum caudatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
