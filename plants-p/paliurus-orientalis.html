Paliurus orientalis

Paliurus orientalis, commonly known as the oriental palisade, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including Turkey and the Middle East.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The fruit, which is small and edible, is sometimes used in local cuisines.
