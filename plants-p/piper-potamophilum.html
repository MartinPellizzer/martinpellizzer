Piper potamophilum

Piper potamophilum, commonly known as the river pipevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found near water sources.

Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.
