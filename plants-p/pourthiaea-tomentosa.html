Pourthiaea tomentosa

Pourthiaea tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pourthiaea, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features dense, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
