Panicum glaucifolium

Panicum glaucifolium, commonly known as blueleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spikelike inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
