Phlomostachys sulphurea

Phlomostachys sulphurea, commonly known as the golden phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Due to its striking color and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
