Pandanus cordatus

Pandanus cordatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense crown. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences resembling bottle brushes. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional weaving practices.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, adapting well to saline conditions.
