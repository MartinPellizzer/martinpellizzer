Pleioblastus flaccidifolius

Pleioblastus flaccidifolius, commonly known as the flaccid leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its foliage is soft and delicate, with a slight drooping appearance that gives it its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
