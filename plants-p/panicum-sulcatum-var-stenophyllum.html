Panicum sulcatum var. stenophyllum

Panicum sulcatum var. stenophyllum, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive rippled or furrowed surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wetland wildlife.
