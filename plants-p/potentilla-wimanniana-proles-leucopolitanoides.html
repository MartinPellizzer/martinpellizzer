Potentilla wimanniana proles leucopolitanoides

Potentilla wimanniana proles leucopolitanoides, commonly known as the white-leaved potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Native to certain regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
