Paryphantha

Paryphantha, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow fruits that contain seeds. Paryphantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Its botanical name reflects its unique blooming pattern, which occurs primarily at night.
