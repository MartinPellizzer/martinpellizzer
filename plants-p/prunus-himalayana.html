Prunus himalayana

Prunus himalayana, commonly known as the Himalayan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Prunus himalayana thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
