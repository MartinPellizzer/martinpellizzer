Panicum frumentaceum

Panicum frumentaceum, commonly known as millet grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It produces edible grains that are used as a staple food in many parts of the world.

Panicum frumentaceum is also valued for its adaptability to poor soil conditions and its role in traditional agriculture.
