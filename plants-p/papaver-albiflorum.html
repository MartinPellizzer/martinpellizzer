Papaver albiflorum

Papaver albiflorum, commonly known as the white poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge, which bloom in late summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds.

Papaver albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
