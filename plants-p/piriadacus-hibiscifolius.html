Piriadacus hibiscifolius

Piriadacus hibiscifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf piriadacus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
