Paeonia albiflora var. whitleyi

Paeonia albiflora var. whitleyi, commonly known as the White Japanese peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are green and finely divided. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is noted for its fragrance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in traditional landscaping.
