Pyxidanthera barbulata

Pyxidanthera barbulata, commonly known as the hairy leek, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like stems that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight bluish tint.

Pyxidanthera barbulata is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.
