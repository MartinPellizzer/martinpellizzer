Paphiopedilum spicerianum f. burkhardii

Paphiopedilum spicerianum f. burkhardii, commonly known as the Burkhard's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This hybrid form exhibits striking, velvety petals with a deep pink hue and intricate patterns that resemble a slipper. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, which includes a pouch-like lip used to trap pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
