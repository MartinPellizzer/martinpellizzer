Passiflora luciensis

Passiflora luciensis, commonly known as the luminous passionflower, is a rare and striking species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, intricate flowers that display a vibrant mix of purple, white, and yellow hues, with prominent, feather-like filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are prized for their unique flavor and nutritional value. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Passiflora luciensis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
