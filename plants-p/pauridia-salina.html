Pauridia salina

Pauridia salina, commonly known as saltwort, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other regions with high soil salinity. Its small, green flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Pauridia salina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
