Prunus domestica subsp. cyclopetala

Prunus domestica subsp.

cyclopetala, commonly known as the cyclopetal pear, is a subspecies of the European pear characterized by its unique floral morphology. It features a distinctive flower structure with a cyclopetal arrangement, where the petals are arranged in a spiral rather than a typical radial pattern.

This subspecies is native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of Turkey, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically not cultivated for consumption but are of interest in botanical studies.

Its floral characteristics make it a subject of research in plant evolution and pollination biology.
