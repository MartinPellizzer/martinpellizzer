Piper bourgeaui

Piper bourgeaui, commonly known as the Borneo pepper or Borneo long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and economic purposes.
