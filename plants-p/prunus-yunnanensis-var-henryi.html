Prunus yunnanensis var. henryi

Prunus yunnanensis var. henryi, commonly known as the Yunnan cherry or Chinese cherry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the fruit is a small, dark red drupe. This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun.
