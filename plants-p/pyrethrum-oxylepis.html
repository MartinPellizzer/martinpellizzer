Pyrethrum oxylepis

Pyrethrum oxylepis, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant produces small, glandular structures called pyrethrin-producing trichomes, which are responsible for its insecticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Pyrethrum oxylepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural pesticides.
