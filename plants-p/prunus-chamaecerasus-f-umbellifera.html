Prunus chamaecerasus f. umbellifera

Prunus chamaecerasus f. umbellifera, commonly known as the umbellifera cherry or the Japanese snowbell, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a snow-like appearance. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
