Petasites tomentosus

Petasites tomentosus, commonly known as the fuzzy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are densely covered with soft, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a showy inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and alpine meadows.

Petasites tomentosus is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
