Pedicularis resupinata subsp. galeobdolon

Pedicularis resupinata subsp.

galeobdolon, commonly known as the purple monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and trailing.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
