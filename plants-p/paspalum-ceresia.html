Paspalum ceresia

Paspalum ceresia, commonly known as the coastal paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to salt spray, making it a hardy species for coastal landscapes.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
