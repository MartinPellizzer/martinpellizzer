Pseuderanthemum leptanthum

Pseuderanthemum leptanthum, commonly known as the slender false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with five petals, and are borne in clusters at the leaf axils.

Pseuderanthemum leptanthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
