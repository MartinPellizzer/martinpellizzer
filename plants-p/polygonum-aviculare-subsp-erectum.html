Polygonum aviculare subsp. erectum

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

erectum, commonly known as upright knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows. Polygonum aviculare subsp.

erectum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
