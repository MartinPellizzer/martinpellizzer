Polypodium lomariiforme

Polypodium lomariiforme, commonly known as the rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a rhizomatous structure and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
