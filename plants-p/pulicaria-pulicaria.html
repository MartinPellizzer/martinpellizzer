Pulicaria pulicaria

Pulicaria pulicaria, commonly known as common sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in dry, sandy, or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Pulicaria pulicaria is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
