Pernettya pumila

Pernettya pumila, commonly known as the dwarf cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black in color, often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and tundra environments. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Pernettya pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
