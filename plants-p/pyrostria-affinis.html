Pyrostria affinis

Pyrostria affinis, commonly known as the yellow-wood or yellow-ironwood, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and long, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it important in local economies and traditional uses.
