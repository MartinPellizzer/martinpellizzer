Piper villosisquamulum

Piper villosisquamulum, commonly known as the hairy-squamulate pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often bear aerial roots. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture on the underside, giving it its scientific name.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that are typically used in local cuisines.
