Polygonum alpinum var. alaskanum

Polygonum alpinum var. alaskanum, commonly known as the Alaskan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink or red in color.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to colonize disturbed or barren habitats.
