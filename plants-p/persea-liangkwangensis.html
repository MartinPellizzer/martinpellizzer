Persea liangkwangensis

Persea liangkwangensis, commonly known as the Liangkwang avocado, is a species native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by oval-shaped fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, oily pulp that is rich in nutrients.

It is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
