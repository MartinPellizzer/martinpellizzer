Piper bipedale

Piper bipedale, commonly known as the two-footed pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
