Parsonsia eucalyptophylla

Parsonsia eucalyptophylla, commonly known as the red-flowered parsonsia, is a striking member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is native to northern Australia and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are tubular with five lobes and are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly honeyeaters.
