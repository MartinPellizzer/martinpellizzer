Panicum lepidotum

Panicum lepidotum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, purple-tinted inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry climates.

Panicum lepidotum is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion and support local wildlife.
