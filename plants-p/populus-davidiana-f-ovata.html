Populus davidiana f. ovata

Populus davidiana f. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf poplar, is a variant of the David's poplar native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces catkin-like flowers in early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
