Picris longirostris

Picris longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and antispasmodic properties.
