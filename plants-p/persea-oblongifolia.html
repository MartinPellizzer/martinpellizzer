Persea oblongifolia

Persea oblongifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, honey-like flavor and is often used in traditional cuisine.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
