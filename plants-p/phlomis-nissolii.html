Phlomis nissolii

Phlomis nissolii, commonly known as the Armenian skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
