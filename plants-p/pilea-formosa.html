Pilea formosa

Pilea formosa, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its round, glossy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It is a popular indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea formosa thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
