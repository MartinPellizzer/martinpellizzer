Plectranthus amaniensis

Plectranthus amaniensis, commonly known as the Amani plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
