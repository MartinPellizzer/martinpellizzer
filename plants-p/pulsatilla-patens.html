Pulsatilla patens

Pulsatilla patens, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a short, thick stem that supports its foliage and blooms, which typically appear in early spring. Its leaves are basal, palmately lobed, and covered in a fine, silky down.

Pulsatilla patens is often found in alpine and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
