Poa pearsonii

Poa pearsonii, commonly known as Pearson's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. The plant has fine, blue-green leaves that are often matted together, forming dense clumps. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

Poa pearsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
