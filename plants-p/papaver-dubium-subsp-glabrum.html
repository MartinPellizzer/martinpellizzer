Papaver dubium subsp. glabrum

Papaver dubium subsp.

glabrum, commonly known as the smooth poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves, which are typically lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, with a prominent central stigma. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
