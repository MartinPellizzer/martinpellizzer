Piper sclerocladum

Piper sclerocladum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are initially green and turn red when mature, containing tiny seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
