Poa pratensis var. monticola

Poa pratensis var. monticola, commonly known as mountain meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. This plant typically grows in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its foliage is slender and grass-like, with a dense growth habit that forms compact turf.

It is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
