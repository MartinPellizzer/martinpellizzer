Paloue brasiliensis

Paloue brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian paloue, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
