Phaseolus strigillosus

Phaseolus strigillosus, commonly known as the hairy pole bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
