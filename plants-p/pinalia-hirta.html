Pinalia hirta

Pinalia hirta, commonly known as the hairy pinyon, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, cone-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife species. Pinalia hirta thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
