Periploca maculata

Periploca maculata, commonly known as the spotted periploca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slight waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
