Poa pratensis subsp. anceps

Poa pratensis subsp.

anceps, commonly known as the bentgrass, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and fine, bluish-green foliage.

The plant typically forms a sod-like turf, making it suitable for lawns and sports fields. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowering stems bear spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
