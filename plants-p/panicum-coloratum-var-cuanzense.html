Panicum coloratum var. cuanzense

Panicum coloratum var. cuanzense, commonly known as the Cuanzense Panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color, depending on the season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
