Polylepis multijuga

Polylepis multijuga, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

This tree is adapted to harsh, cold environments and can thrive at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its bark is thick and often fissured, providing protection against freezing temperatures and strong winds.

Polylepis multijuga plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to soil stabilization.
