Piper bracteatum

Piper bracteatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves contain essential oils and are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
