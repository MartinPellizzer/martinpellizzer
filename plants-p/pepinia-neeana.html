Pepinia neeana

Pepinia neeana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, fleshy leaves, and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pepinia neeana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
