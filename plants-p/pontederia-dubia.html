Pontederia dubia

Pontederia dubia, commonly known as the slender water hyacinth, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features floating leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its striking blue to purple flowers bloom in dense clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Pontederia dubia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
