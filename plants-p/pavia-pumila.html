Pavia pumila

Pavia pumila, commonly known as the small pavia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Pavia pumila is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
