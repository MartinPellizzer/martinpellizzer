Prosthechea carrii

Prosthechea carrii, commonly known as the Carri's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, greenish-brown pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color, arranged in a raceme. Prosthechea carrii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
