Pelargonium × dissectum

Pelargonium × dissectum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Pelargonium × dissectum is often grown in containers or as a bedding plant, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
