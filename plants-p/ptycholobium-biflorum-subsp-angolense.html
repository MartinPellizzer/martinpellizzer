Ptycholobium biflorum subsp. angolense

Ptycholobium biflorum subsp.

angolense, commonly known as the Angola legume, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and habitat support for local wildlife.
