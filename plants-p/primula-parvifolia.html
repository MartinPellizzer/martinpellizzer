Primula parvifolia

Primula parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and often hairy, with a rosette growth habit. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
