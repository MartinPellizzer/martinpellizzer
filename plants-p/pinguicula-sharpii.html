Pinguicula sharpii

Pinguicula sharpii, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a showy, bell-shaped flower with bright yellow petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to maintain its carnivorous adaptations.

Pinguicula sharpii is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ecological role.
