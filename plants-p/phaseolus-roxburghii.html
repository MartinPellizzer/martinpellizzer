Phaseolus roxburghii

Phaseolus roxburghii, commonly known as the evergreen bean or wild kidney bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in temperate to tropical climates.

Due to its nutritional value, it has been used traditionally in some regions for food and medicinal purposes.
