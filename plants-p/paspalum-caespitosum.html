Paspalum caespitosum

Paspalum caespitosum, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage that becomes more pronounced during the dry season. The plant produces small, blue-green flowers in summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for various ecological and agricultural applications.

Paspalum caespitosum is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop due to its resilience and adaptability.
