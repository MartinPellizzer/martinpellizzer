Pholidota decurva

Pholidota decurva, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its rough, fissured bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Pholidota decurva is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
