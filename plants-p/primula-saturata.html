Primula saturata

Primula saturata, commonly known as the saturated primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The flowers come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
