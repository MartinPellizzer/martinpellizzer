Prangos meliocarpoides var. arcis-romanae

Prangos meliocarpoides var. arcis-romanae, commonly known as the Roman melilot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Its seeds are enclosed in a hairy pod and are often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
