Philodendron grayumii

Philodendron grayumii, commonly known as the Grayum's philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically has a climbing habit, with aerial roots that allow it to attach to supports. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
