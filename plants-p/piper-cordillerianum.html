Piper cordillerianum

Piper cordillerianum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species of pepper plants. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong, pungent aroma.

The small, green berries are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices by indigenous communities.
