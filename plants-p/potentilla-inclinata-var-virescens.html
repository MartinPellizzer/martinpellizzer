Potentilla inclinata var. virescens

Potentilla inclinata var. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Its flowers are yellow, occurring in clusters of five, and bloom in early summer.

This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
