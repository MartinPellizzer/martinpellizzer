Pegaeophyton

Pegaeophyton, commonly known as the "sea fern," is an extinct genus of early land plants that lived during the Silurian period.

It is characterized by its simple, branching structure and lack of true roots, stems, and leaves, which are features of more advanced plants. Pegaeophyton is considered one of the earliest known plants to have colonized land, playing a significant role in the early evolution of terrestrial flora. Its fossils are typically found in marine deposits, suggesting it may have thrived in coastal or shallow water environments.

The study of Pegaeophyton provides important insights into the transition from aquatic to terrestrial plant life.
