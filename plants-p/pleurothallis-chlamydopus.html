Pleurothallis chlamydopus

Pleurothallis chlamydopus, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, helmet-like floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

The genus Pleurothallis is one of the largest in the orchid family, with many species exhibiting diverse morphological adaptations.
