Pelea multiflora

Pelea multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered pelea, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

Pelea multiflora produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
