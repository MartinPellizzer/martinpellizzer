Phlomis crinita subsp. malacitana

Phlomis crinita subsp.

malacitana, commonly known as the Malagasy beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar. It features tall, erect stems covered in soft, hairy foliage.

The plant produces striking, sparsely branched inflorescences with purple to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly woolly texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
