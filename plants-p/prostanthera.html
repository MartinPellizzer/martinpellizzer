Prostanthera

Prostanthera, commonly known as mountain mint, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy, often purple or white flowers. They typically grow in woodland and heathland environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Prostanthera species are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and their ability to attract pollinators.

Some species are also valued for their essential oils and have traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
