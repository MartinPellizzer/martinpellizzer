Pfaffia denudata

Pfaffia denudata, commonly known as yellow snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Pfaffia denudata is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
