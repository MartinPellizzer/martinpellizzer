Pennisetum simeonis var. intermedium

Pennisetum simeonis var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 meters in height, with finely textured, silver-green foliage. The plant produces fluffy, plume-like inflorescences that add a dramatic visual impact in the landscape. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and water-wise gardening practices.
