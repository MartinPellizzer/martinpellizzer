Poa laxiflora

Poa laxiflora, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it blend into its natural surroundings.

Poa laxiflora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
