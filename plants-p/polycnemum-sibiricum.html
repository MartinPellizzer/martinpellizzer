Polycnemum sibiricum

Polycnemum sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian water clover, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf arrangements and often succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern and have a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish in color, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.
