Phoebe pallescens

Phoebe pallescens, commonly known as the white phoebe, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green upper surface. Its small, white flowers are followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Phoebe pallescens is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
