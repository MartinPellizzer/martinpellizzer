Pilea nudicaulis

Pilea nudicaulis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features a rosette of thick, waxy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often described as resembling small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers on slender stems, which are not particularly showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
