Ponaria laciniata

Ponaria laciniata, commonly known as the shredded fern, is a delicate perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a lacy appearance, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and prefers well-drained soil. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind for pollination.

Ponaria laciniata is known for its ability to thrive in woodland environments, contributing to the biodiversity of such ecosystems.
