Pteris cadieri subsp. perplexa

Pteris cadieri subsp.

perplexa, commonly known as the Hairy Brake, is a fern native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
