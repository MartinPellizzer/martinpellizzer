Pfaffia luzuliflora

Pfaffia luzuliflora, commonly known as purple false shamrock, is a perennial herb native to the humid forests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often a vibrant purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Pfaffia luzuliflora thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
