Peperomia punctatilamina

Peperomia punctatilamina, commonly known as the spotted leaf peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its distinctive leaves that are often marked with contrasting spots or streaks. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and have a waxy texture. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Peperomia punctatilamina is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.
