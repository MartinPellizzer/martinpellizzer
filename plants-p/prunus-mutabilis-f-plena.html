Prunus mutabilis f. plena

Prunus mutabilis f. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to China.

It is characterized by its abundant, double flowers that typically appear in shades of white, pink, and red, depending on the variety. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree, often reaching heights of 6 to 10 meters. Its flowers bloom in early spring, providing ornamental value, while its foliage offers seasonal color changes.

The fruit, which is edible but less commonly consumed, is small and resembles a cherry in appearance.
