Pseuderanthemum malaccense

Pseuderanthemum malaccense, commonly known as the Malacca false heather, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Pseuderanthemum malaccense is also noted for its ability to climb using adventitious roots, making it a versatile and attractive addition to gardens.
