Piper nilghirianum

Piper nilghirianum, commonly known as the Nilgiri pepper or Indian long pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, curved berries. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including piperine and other alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
