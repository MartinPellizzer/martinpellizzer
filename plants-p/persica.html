Persica

Persica, commonly known as the peach tree, is a deciduous fruit tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces sweet, fuzzy fruits that develop from showy, pinkish-white flowers. Persica requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for orchards and home gardens.

Its fruit is valued for its flavor and versatility in culinary uses.
