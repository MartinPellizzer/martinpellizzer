Philotheca scabra subsp. scabra

Philotheca scabra subsp.

scabra, commonly known as the rough-leaved philotheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving them a rough texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or along coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
