Phaseolus oblongus var. leucovariegatus

Phaseolus oblongus var. leucovariegatus, commonly known as the white variegated runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species characterized by its distinctive white and green variegated foliage.

This plant is a climbing legume with compound leaves and produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The pods are elongated, cylindrical, and mature to a dark green color, containing edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and as a food source.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
