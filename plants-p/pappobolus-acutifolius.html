Pappobolus acutifolius

Pappobolus acutifolius, commonly known as the slender burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across arid and semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Pappobolus acutifolius is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
