Petasites stramineus

Petasites stramineus, commonly known as the yellow meadow grass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and large, broad leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

Petasites stramineus is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
