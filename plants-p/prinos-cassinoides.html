Prinos cassinoides

Prinos cassinoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf prinos, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Prinos cassinoides is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its ability to thrive in partial shade.
