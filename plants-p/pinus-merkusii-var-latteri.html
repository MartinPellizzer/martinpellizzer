Pinus merkusii var. latteri

Pinus merkusii var. latteri, commonly known as the Sumatran pine, is a variant of the Asian red pine native to Southeast Asia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in bundles of five. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its timber and is also important in local ecosystems.
