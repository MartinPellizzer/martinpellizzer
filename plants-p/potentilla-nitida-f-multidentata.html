Potentilla nitida f. multidentata

Potentilla nitida f. multidentata, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, densely hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in narrow racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
