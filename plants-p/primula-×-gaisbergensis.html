Primula × gaisbergensis

Primula × gaisbergensis, commonly known as the Gaisberg primrose, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Primula sinensis and Primula veris.

It is native to the alpine regions of Europe, particularly the Alps, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty and ability to bloom in early spring.
