Psidium sessiliflorum

Psidium sessiliflorum, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that produces fragrant, white flowers with a distinctive pineapple-like scent. The plant's leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems. Its fruit is a small, rounded berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
