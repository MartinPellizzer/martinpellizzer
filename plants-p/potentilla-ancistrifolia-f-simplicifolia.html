Potentilla ancistrifolia f. simplicifolia

Potentilla ancistrifolia f. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf potentilla, is a botanical variant of the species Potentilla ancistrifolia.

It is characterized by its simplified leaf structure compared to the typical form, featuring smaller, more rounded leaves. This plant is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
