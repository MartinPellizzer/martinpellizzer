Photinia loriformis

Photinia loriformis, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage that turns a striking red or orange in autumn. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and a velvety texture.

Photinia loriformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscaping.
