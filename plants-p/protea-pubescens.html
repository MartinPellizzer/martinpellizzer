Protea pubescens

Protea pubescens, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers, which are surrounded by a dense, woolly bract. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silver-white hairs, contributing to its common name.

Protea pubescens is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in local ecosystems.
