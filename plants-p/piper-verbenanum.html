Piper verbenanum

Piper verbenanum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The leaves and fruits contain compounds that contribute to its aromatic and medicinal properties.
