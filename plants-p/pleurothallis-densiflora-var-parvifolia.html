Pleurothallis densiflora var. parvifolia

Pleurothallis densiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered dense-flower orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance, and bloom during the rainy season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
