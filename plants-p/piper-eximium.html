Piper eximium

Piper eximium, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits, which are berries, contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Piper eximium thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.
