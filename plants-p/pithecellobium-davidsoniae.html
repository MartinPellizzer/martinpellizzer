Pithecellobium davidsoniae

Pithecellobium davidsoniae, commonly known as the Davidson's acacia, is a shrub native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs, and its yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
