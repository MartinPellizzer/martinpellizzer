Phlox sibirica var. richardsonii

Phlox sibirica var. richardsonii, commonly known as Siberian phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
