Puccinellia parvula

Puccinellia parvula, commonly known as little jointvetch, is a small annual grass that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for small herbivores.

Puccinellia parvula is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes other grasses.
