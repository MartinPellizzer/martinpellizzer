Pyrus pannonica arguta

Pyrus pannonica arguta, commonly known as the Hungarian whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn a striking yellow in autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
