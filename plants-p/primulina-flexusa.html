Primulina flexusa

Primulina flexusa, commonly known as the flexible primrose, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom during the warmer months.

Primulina flexusa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in humid, indoor conditions.
