Paepalanthus ottonis

Paepalanthus ottonis, commonly known as the Otto's pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. The plant produces dense clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. Its structure resembles a pincushion due to the presence of numerous spines and a compact growth form.

Paepalanthus ottonis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
