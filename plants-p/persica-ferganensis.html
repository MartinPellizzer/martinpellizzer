Persica ferganensis

Persica ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana peach, is a species native to Central Asia, particularly the Fergana Valley.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Rosaceae family. The plant produces smooth, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Persica ferganensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its edible fruit.
