Polygala sibirica var. hakobii

Polygala sibirica var. hakobii, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate and pinnate, while the flowers are often pale green or yellow, with a distinctive, spurred structure.

This variant is noted for its limited distribution and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche.
