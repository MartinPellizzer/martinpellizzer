Passaveria obovata

Passaveria obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate passaveria, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with purple or violet flowers that bloom in spring. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.

Passaveria obovata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
