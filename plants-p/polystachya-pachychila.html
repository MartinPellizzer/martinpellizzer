Polystachya pachychila

Polystachya pachychila, commonly known as the thick-leafed torch orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its striking, elongated inflorescences that bear numerous small, colorful flowers. The plant's leaves are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
