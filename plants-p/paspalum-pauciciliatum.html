Paspalum pauciciliatum

Paspalum pauciciliatum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Paspalum pauciciliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
