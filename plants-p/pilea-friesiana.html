Pilea friesiana

Pilea friesiana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a member of the Begoniaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, waxy leaves that resemble coins. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with upright stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
