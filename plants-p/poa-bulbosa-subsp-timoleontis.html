Poa bulbosa subsp. timoleontis

Poa bulbosa subsp.

timoleontis, commonly known as the Timoleontis meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
