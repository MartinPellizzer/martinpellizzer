Polystichum × namegatae

Polystichum × namegatae, commonly known as the Japanese forest fern, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polystichum × namegatae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
