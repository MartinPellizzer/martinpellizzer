Paeonia officinalis var. paradoxa

Paeonia officinalis var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Peony, is a rare and striking variant of the peony species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display a unique combination of colors, often featuring a deep red or purple center surrounded by white or pale pink petals. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil with full sun exposure. Its botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and a robust root system typical of the Paeonia genus.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists for its rare beauty.
