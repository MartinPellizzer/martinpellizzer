Piper elongatum var. pampayacusum

Piper elongatum var. pampayacusum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or piperella, is a tropical vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature.

This variety is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional Andean cuisine and herbal remedies.
