Podocarpus pilgeri

Podocarpus pilgeri, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or Pilger's podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of its coniferous classification. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
