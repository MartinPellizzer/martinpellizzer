Pythagorea cochinchinensis

Pythagorea cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a tendency to flake off in small pieces.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
