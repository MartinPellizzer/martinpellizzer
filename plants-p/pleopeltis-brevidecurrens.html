Pleopeltis brevidecurrens

Pleopeltis brevidecurrens, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its broad, leathery fronds with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are short and thick, and it produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
