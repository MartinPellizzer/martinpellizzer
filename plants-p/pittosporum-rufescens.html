Pittosporum rufescens

Pittosporum rufescens, commonly known as the red-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.

Pittosporum rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in New Zealand's flora.
