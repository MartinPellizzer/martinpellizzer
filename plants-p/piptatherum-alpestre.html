Piptatherum alpestre

Piptatherum alpestre, commonly known as alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Piptatherum alpestre plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
