Panicum griffithii

Panicum griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, pale green to brownish seed heads that are characteristic of the Panicum genus. It is often found in wetland ecosystems and can play a role in stabilizing soil in such environments.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a ornamental plant.
