Phelypaea pulchella

Phelypaea pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant is often found in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its reproductive structures are typical of the Araceae family, featuring a female flower at the base of the spadix and male flowers above.

Phelypaea pulchella is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
