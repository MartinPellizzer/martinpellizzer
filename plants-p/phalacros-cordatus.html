Phalacros cordatus

Phalacros cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf phalacron, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically deep green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for container gardening or as a houseplant.

While it prefers warm and humid conditions, it is relatively low maintenance, requiring moderate watering and indirect sunlight.
