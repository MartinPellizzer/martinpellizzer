Parakmeria omeiensis

Parakmeria omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to sandy or loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
