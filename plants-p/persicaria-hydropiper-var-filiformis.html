Persicaria hydropiper var. filiformis

Persicaria hydropiper var. filiformis, commonly known as water pepper, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive waxy coating. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated peduncles.

This plant is adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
