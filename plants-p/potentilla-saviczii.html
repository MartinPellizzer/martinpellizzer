Potentilla saviczii

Potentilla saviczii, commonly known as the Savicz cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Potentilla saviczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
