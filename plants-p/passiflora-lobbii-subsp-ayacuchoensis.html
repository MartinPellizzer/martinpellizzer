Passiflora lobbii subsp. ayacuchoensis

Passiflora lobbii subsp.

ayacuchoensis, commonly known as the Ayacucho passionflower, is a rare and distinctive member of the Passifloraceae family. It is native to the high-altitude regions of southern Peru, particularly in the Ayacucho department.

The plant is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with vibrant blue and white petals, along with prominent red filaments. It produces edible berries that are typically small and yellow when mature.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
