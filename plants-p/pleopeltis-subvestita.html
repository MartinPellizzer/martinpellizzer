Pleopeltis subvestita

Pleopeltis subvestita, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a rare and delicate epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety fronds that give it its common name, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a unique ability to retain moisture, which helps it survive in its natural habitat. Its reproductive structures are small and clustered, typical of many fern species.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
