Potentilla recta proles laeta

Potentilla recta proles laeta, commonly known as the happy proles potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
