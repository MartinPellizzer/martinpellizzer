Pilosella hypeurocinerea subsp. microvasconica

Pilosella hypeurocinerea subsp.

microvasconica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow disk flowers surrounded by ray florets.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters and blooms from late spring to early summer.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
