Prunus spinosa var. oxypirena

Prunus spinosa var. oxypirena, commonly known as the Turkish cherry or oxypirena cherry, is a shrub native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by edible red berries that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
