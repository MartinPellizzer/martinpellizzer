Podostemma lindheimeri

Podostemma lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's podostem, is a rare aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and its unique adaptation to growing in fast-moving freshwater environments. The plant typically thrives in rocky substrates along streams and rivers, where it attaches itself using its specialized roots. Its leaves are often submerged, while its flowers emerge just above the water surface.

Due to habitat loss and water pollution, this species is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
