Papaver tatricum

Papaver tatricum, commonly known as the Carpathian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features striking, vibrant red flowers with a dark center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Papaver tatricum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its bold, showy display.
