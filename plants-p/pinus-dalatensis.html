Pinus dalatensis

Pinus dalatensis, commonly known as the Dalat pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The plant produces cones that are elongated and often reddish in color, containing both male and female reproductive structures. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape tree in suitable environments.
