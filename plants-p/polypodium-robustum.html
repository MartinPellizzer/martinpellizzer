Polypodium robustum

Polypodium robustum, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically divided into broad, lance-shaped segments. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

Polypodium robustum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
