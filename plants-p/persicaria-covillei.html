Persicaria covillei

Persicaria covillei, commonly known as Coville's smartweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming from late summer into early fall.

Coville's smartweed is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
