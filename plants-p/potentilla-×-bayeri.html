Potentilla × bayeri

Potentilla × bayeri, commonly known as the German cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features compound leaves with five to seven elliptical leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental choice for gardens.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust root system and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
