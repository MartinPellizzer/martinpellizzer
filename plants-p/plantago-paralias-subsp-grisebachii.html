Plantago paralias subsp. grisebachii

Plantago paralias subsp.

grisebachii, commonly known as the gray-haired plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Eurasia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
