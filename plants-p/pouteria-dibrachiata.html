Pouteria dibrachiata

Pouteria dibrachiata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree is notable for its unique fruit, which resembles a large, spiky capsule containing the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
