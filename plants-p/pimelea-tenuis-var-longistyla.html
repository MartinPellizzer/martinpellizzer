Pimelea tenuis var. longistyla

Pimelea tenuis var. longistyla, commonly known as the slenderleaf riceflower, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
