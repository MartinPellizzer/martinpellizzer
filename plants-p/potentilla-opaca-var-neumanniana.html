Potentilla opaca var. neumanniana

Potentilla opaca var. neumanniana, commonly known as the Neumann's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
