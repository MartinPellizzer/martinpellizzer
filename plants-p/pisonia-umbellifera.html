Pisonia umbellifera

Pisonia umbellifera, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant produces small, round, red or orange fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
