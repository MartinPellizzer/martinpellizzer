Primula reticulata var. didyma

Primula reticulata var. didyma, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant features a compact growth habit with broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are borne on upright stems and are known for their intricate petal patterns.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
