Pleurothallis auriculigera

Pleurothallis auriculigera, commonly known as the ear-shaped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
