Polypodium phyllitidis f. multipunctatum

Polypodium phyllitidis f. multipunctatum, commonly known as the spotted shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The leaves are typically bipinnate, with a shield-shaped frond that gives the plant its common name. The fronds are usually dark green with a leathery texture and often display a pattern of small, round spots.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
