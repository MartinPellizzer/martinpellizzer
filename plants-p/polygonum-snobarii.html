Polygonum snobarii

Polygonum snobarii, commonly known as the Slobarii buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bracteate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

Polygonum snobarii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native environment.
