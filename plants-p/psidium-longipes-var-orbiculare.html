Psidium longipes var. orbiculare

Psidium longipes var. orbiculare, commonly known as the orbiculate guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a small, round, yellowish berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in both horticultural and agricultural settings.
