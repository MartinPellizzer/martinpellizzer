Phragmites communis f. salsa

Phragmites communis f. salsa, commonly known as the saltmarsh reed, is a variant of the common reed that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tall, flowering stalks that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. This plant is adapted to coastal areas and brackish wetlands, where it plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help prevent erosion and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

The flowers are typically pale green to brown, and the plant reproduces through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
