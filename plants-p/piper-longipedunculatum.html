Piper longipedunculatum

Piper longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-stalked pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
