Potentilla lancinata

Potentilla lancinata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Potentilla lancinata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.
