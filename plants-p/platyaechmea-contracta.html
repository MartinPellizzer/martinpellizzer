Platyaechmea contracta

Platyaechmea contracta, commonly known as the dwarf blue torch plant, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like structure with dark green, strap-shaped leaves that have a waxy, blue-gray sheen. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically violet or blue in color, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Platyaechmea contracta is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
