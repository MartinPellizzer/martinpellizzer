Phalaenopsis amabilis var. moluccana

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan phalaenopsis, is a popular epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming multiple times throughout the year. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding environment.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in indoor settings.
