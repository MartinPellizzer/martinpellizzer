Paris thibetica var. apetala

Paris thibetica var. apetala, commonly known as the Tibetan Paris, is a member of the Melanthiaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rhizomatous growth and striking white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
