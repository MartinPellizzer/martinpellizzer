Piper sumpi

Piper sumpi, commonly known as the swamp pepper or sumpi pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like black pepper. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinct aromatic scent.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, round berries that turn red when ripe.
