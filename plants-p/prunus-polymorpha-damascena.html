Prunus polymorpha damascena

Prunus polymorpha damascena, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale lavender to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically hairy underneath, and it produces small, dark purple to black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
