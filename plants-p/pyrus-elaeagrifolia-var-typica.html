Pyrus elaeagrifolia var. typica

Pyrus elaeagrifolia var. typica, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are typically not eaten by humans but are important for wildlife.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
