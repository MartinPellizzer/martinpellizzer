Philippicereus elatus

Philippicereus elatus, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach several meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Philippicereus elatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
