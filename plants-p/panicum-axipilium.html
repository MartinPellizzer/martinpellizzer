Panicum axipilium

Panicum axipilium, commonly known as purpletop millet, is an annual grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Panicum axipilium is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a forage crop in certain regions.
