Psidium crenulatum

Psidium crenulatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
