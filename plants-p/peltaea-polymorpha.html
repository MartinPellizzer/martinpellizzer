Peltaea polymorpha

Peltaea polymorpha, commonly known as the variable peltaea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of short stalks. It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Peltaea polymorpha is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
