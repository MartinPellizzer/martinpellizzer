Pleopeltis obolophylla

Pleopeltis obolophylla, commonly known as the rock fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant has leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular species among fern enthusiasts.
