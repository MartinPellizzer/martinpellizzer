Podochilus marsupialis

Podochilus marsupialis, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, white or pale pink blossoms that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
