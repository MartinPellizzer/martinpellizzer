Prunus domestica var. oxycarpa

Prunus domestica var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a variety of the domesticated cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, round, and typically red or dark red fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp. The plant produces flowers in spring, which are usually white or pale pink, followed by the development of fruiting bodies. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its culinary and ornamental value.
