Peperomia nossibeana

Peperomia nossibeana, commonly known as the Nossibe peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Peperomia nossibeana is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
