Piper selloi var. latilimbum

Piper selloi var. latilimbum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
