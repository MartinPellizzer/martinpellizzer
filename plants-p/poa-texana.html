Poa texana

Poa texana, commonly known as Texas bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies and rocky soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, giving it its common name.

Poa texana is adapted to dry conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
