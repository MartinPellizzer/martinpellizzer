Potentilla douglasii var. capitata

Potentilla douglasii var. capitata, commonly known as Douglas's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The variety 'capitata' is distinguished by its dense, rounded flower heads, which give it a compact and ornamental appearance.
