Potamogeton kashiensis

Potamogeton kashiensis, commonly known as Kashi water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of Asia, particularly in China.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically found in shallow waters. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

However, it is considered an invasive species in some regions where it can outcompete native vegetation.
