Paepalanthus myocephalon var. major

Paepalanthus myocephalon var. major, commonly known as the giant marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a large number of flowering shoots during the growing season.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
