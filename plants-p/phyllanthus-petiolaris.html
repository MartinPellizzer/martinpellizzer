Phyllanthus petiolaris

Phyllanthus petiolaris, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and herbal remedies.
