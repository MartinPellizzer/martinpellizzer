Pinus kesiya subsp. yunnanensis

Pinus kesiya subsp.

yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan pine, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China. It is a tall evergreen conifer characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive cone morphology.

The plant thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is adapted to well-drained, acidic soils. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
