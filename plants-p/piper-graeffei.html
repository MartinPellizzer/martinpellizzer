Piper graeffei

Piper graeffei, commonly known as the Java pepper or creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery, berry-like fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses.
