Phymatopsis engleri var. hypoleuca

Phymatopsis engleri var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered phymatopsis, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti and related succulent plants. The plant is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations.
