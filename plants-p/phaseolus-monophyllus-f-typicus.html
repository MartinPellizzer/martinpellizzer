Phaseolus monophyllus f. typicus

Phaseolus monophyllus f. typicus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and long, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural cultivation.
