Potentilla × scardicola

Potentilla × scardicola, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10–30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
