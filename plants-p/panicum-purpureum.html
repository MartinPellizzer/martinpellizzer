Panicum purpureum

Panicum purpureum, commonly known as Guinea grass, is a tall, warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and dense, tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it adaptable to various soil types. It is widely used as forage for livestock due to its high nutritional value and rapid growth rate.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and erosion control in agricultural landscapes.
