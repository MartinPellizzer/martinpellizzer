Phaius pulchellus var. andrambovatensis

Phaius pulchellus var. andrambovatensis, commonly known as the Andrambovatensis Ginger, is a striking ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant inflorescences consisting of numerous pale yellow to white flowers arranged in a spadix, surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.

This variety is notable for its unique floral structure and vibrant foliage, making it a sought-after species among horticulturists.
