Portlandia involucrata

Portlandia involucrata, commonly known as the Pacific Northwest bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Oregon and Washington.

It is characterized by its blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white down.

Portlandia involucrata is a member of the Campanulaceae family, known for its showy, tubular flowers.
