Phaius schlechteri

Phaius schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with numerous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Phaius schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
