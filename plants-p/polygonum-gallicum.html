Polygonum gallicum

Polygonum gallicum, commonly known as French knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species like buckwheat and knotweeds. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Polygonum gallicum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
