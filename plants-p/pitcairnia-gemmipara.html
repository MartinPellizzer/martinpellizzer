Pitcairnia gemmipara

Pitcairnia gemmipara, commonly known as the gemmiferous pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater, which is essential for its survival in its natural habitat. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that range in color from red to orange, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pitcairnia gemmipara is often propagated through vegetative means, such as offsets or by allowing the plant to produce plantlets on its leaves.
