Pimelea × lanceolata

Pimelea × lanceolata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Burramulla, is a hybrid shrub native to Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
