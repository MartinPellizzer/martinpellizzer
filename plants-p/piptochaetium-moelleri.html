Piptochaetium moelleri

Piptochaetium moelleri, commonly known as the slender grass or Moeller's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stem.

Piptochaetium moelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and erosion control capabilities.
