Papaver armeniacum var. persicum

Papaver armeniacum var. persicum, commonly known as Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural collections.
