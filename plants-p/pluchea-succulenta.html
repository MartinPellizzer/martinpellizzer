Pluchea succulenta

Pluchea succulenta, commonly known as the succulent sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it has adapted to survive in dry conditions. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often with a sprawling or upright habit.

Its botanical features make it a unique and resilient species in its native habitat.
