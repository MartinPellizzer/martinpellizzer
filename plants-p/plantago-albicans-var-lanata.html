Plantago albicans var. lanata

Plantago albicans var. lanata, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on tall, erect stems.

The hairy plantain is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal properties due to its high content of mucilage and other bioactive compounds.
