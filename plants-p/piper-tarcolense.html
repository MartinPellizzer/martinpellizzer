Piper tarcolense

Piper tarcolense, commonly known as the Tarcolense pepper or Mexican pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in spikes, and it produces berries that contain aromatic seeds.

Piper tarcolense is valued for its culinary and potential medicinal uses in local communities.
