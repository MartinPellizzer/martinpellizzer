Piper heterostachyum

Piper heterostachyum, commonly known as the African pepper or black pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that mature from green to red, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical features include a climbing habit and the presence of essential oils in its leaves and fruits.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
