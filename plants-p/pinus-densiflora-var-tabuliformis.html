Pinus densiflora var. tabuliformis

Pinus densiflora var. tabuliformis, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a variant of the Japanese white pine.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant is native to southern Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure, thriving in temperate climates.

Its cones are typically small and produce edible seeds, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.
