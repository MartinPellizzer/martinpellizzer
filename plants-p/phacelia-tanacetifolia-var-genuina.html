Phacelia tanacetifolia var. genuina

Phacelia tanacetifolia var. genuina, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a popular choice in native plant gardens.
