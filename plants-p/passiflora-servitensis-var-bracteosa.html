Passiflora servitensis var. bracteosa

Passiflora servitensis var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
