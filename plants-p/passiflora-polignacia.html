Passiflora polignacia

Passiflora polignacia, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are deeply lobed and emit a mild fragrance, while its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora polignacia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
