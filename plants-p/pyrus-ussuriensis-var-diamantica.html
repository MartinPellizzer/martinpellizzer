Pyrus ussuriensis var. diamantica

Pyrus ussuriensis var. diamantica, commonly known as the Diamond-leafed Pear, is a variant of the Ussuri Pear native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, diamond-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental foliage and potential horticultural applications.
