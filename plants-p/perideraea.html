Perideraea

Perideraea, commonly known as the silverweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in spring. Perideraea species are often found in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
