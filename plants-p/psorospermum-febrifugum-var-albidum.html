Psorospermum febrifugum var. albidum

Psorospermum febrifugum var. albidum, commonly known as the white fever tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale to white, often hairy foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal uses, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
