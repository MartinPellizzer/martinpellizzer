Piper guayasanum

Piper guayasanum, commonly known as the Guayasa pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant produces small, peppercorn-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties. Its leaves are also used in local remedies, and the plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves.

The botanical significance of Piper guayasanum lies in its potential pharmacological applications and its role in the biodiversity of tropical ecosystems.
