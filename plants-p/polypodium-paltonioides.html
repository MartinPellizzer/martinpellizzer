Polypodium paltonioides

Polypodium paltonioides, commonly known as the crested polypody, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Polypodium paltonioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
