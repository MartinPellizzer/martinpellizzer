Paepalanthus morulus

Paepalanthus morulus, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a unique member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant is notable for its striking, translucent white flowers that appear to glow in the dark. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
