Pavonia longipes

Pavonia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Pavonia longipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
