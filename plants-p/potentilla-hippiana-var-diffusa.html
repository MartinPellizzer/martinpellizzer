Potentilla hippiana var. diffusa

Potentilla hippiana var. diffusa, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features wiry, spreading stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of vegetation. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
