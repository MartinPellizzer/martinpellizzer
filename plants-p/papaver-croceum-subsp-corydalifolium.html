Papaver croceum subsp. corydalifolium

Papaver croceum subsp.

corydalifolium, commonly known as the Chinese poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China. It features showy, bright orange to red flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped structure.

The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
