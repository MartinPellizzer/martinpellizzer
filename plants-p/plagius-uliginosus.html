Plagius uliginosus

Plagius uliginosus, commonly known as the bog gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and other wetland environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The species is part of the Gentianaceae family, known for its striking floral displays and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
