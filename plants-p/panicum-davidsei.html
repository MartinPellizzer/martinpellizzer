Panicum davidsei

Panicum davidsei, commonly known as David's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
