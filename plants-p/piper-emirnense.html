Piper emirnense

Piper emirnense, commonly known as the Emir's pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a spicy compound similar to capsaicin. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
