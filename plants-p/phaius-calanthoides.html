Phaius calanthoides

Phaius calanthoides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a single inflorescence with numerous flowers.

Phaius calanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
