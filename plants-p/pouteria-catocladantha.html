Pouteria catocladantha

Pouteria catocladantha, commonly known as the Mexican star apple, is a tropical plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts within its native range.
