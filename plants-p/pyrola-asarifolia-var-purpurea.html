Pyrola asarifolia var. purpurea

Pyrola asarifolia var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It features broad, leathery, dark green leaves that remain throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in early spring. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
