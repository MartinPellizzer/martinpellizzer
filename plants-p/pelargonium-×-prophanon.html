Pelargonium × prophanon

Pelargonium × prophanon, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and container gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
