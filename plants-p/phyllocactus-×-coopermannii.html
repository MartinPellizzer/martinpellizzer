Phyllocactus × coopermannii

Phyllocactus × coopermannii, commonly known as the hybrid cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phyllocactus coccineus and Phyllocactus pachycentrus.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, ribbed stems that resemble leaves, which are a key adaptation for water storage in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
