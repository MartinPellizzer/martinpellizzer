Piper schackii

Piper schackii, commonly known as the Schack's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by reddish berries, which are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
