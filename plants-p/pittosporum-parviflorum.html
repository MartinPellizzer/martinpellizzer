Pittosporum parviflorum

Pittosporum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
