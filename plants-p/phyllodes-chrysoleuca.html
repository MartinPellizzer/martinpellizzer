Phyllodes chrysoleuca

Phyllodes chrysoleuca, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its flattened, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are typically bright green with a yellow margin. The plant produces yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spherical heads. It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Phyllodes chrysoleuca is also the floral emblem of Australia, symbolizing the country's natural heritage.
