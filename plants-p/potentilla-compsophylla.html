Potentilla compsophylla

Potentilla compsophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and have five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
