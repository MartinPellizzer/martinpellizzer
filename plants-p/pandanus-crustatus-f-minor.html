Pandanus crustatus f. minor

Pandanus crustatus f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a compact variant of the pandanus genus native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fibrous, coconut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique morphology and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
