Piper subsilvestre

Piper subsilvestre, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it requires careful management.
