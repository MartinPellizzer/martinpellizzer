Phoebe trinervis

Phoebe trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, followed by elliptical, dark blue berries that are attractive to birds.

The foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
