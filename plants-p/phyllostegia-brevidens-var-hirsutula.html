Phyllostegia brevidens var. hirsutula

Phyllostegia brevidens var. hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy stiltweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants with square stems and opposite leaves. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
