Plectranthus veyretiae

Plectranthus veyretiae, commonly known as the Madagascar verbena, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for both garden display and as a cut flower.
