Phlomis pararotata

Phlomis pararotata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
