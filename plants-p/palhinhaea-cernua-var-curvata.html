Palhinhaea cernua var. curvata

Palhinhaea cernua var. curvata, commonly known as the curving swamp rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It features slender, arching stems that often curve downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in moist or submerged conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
