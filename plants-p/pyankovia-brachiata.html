Pyankovia brachiata

Pyankovia brachiata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, silvery-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Pyankovia brachiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
