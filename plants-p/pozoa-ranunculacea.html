Pozoa ranunculacea

Pozoa ranunculacea, commonly known as the creeping buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens, typical of the buttercup genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
