Pyrus sogdiana var. schrederi

Pyrus sogdiana var. schrederi, commonly known as the Sogdian pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits. It prefers well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
