Piper marcescentispicum

Piper marcescentispicum, commonly known as the fading leaf pepper, is a tropical plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring leaves that change color as they age, a trait that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers, which are followed by small, round berries. It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
