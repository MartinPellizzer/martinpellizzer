Pilosella × cinerea subsp. danubii

Pilosella × cinerea subsp.

danubii, commonly known as the Danube pilosella, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous flowering stems that bear small, yellowish-white composite flowers.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is often found in areas with moderate sunlight and well-drained soil.
