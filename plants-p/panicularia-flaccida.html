Panicularia flaccida

Panicularia flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid panicled grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale blue flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability.

Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.
