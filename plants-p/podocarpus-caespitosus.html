Podocarpus caespitosus

Podocarpus caespitosus, commonly known as the silver pine or mountain pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive silver-white undersides on its leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the conifer family. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
