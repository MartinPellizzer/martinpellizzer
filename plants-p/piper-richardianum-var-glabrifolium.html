Piper richardianum var. glabrifolium

Piper richardianum var. glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper richardianum species, which belongs to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in greenish-yellow hues. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its fruits, which are small berries, contain the aromatic compounds responsible for its distinctive flavor.

This variant is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties, similar to other members of the Piper genus.
