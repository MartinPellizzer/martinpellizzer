Pectis linifolia

Pectis linifolia, commonly known as the silky pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a silky appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow and are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pectis linifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
