Papaver pamporicum

Papaver pamporicum, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is often branched and sturdy.

Papaver pamporicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
