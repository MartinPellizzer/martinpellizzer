Pseudopilocereus tuberculatus

Pseudopilocereus tuberculatus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is a flowering cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habits. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for desert gardens and botanical collections.
