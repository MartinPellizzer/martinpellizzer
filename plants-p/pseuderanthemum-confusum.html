Pseuderanthemum confusum

Pseuderanthemum confusum, commonly known as the confused false heather, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often with a contrasting purple or red calyx. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pseuderanthemum confusum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
