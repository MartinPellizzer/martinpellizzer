Passiflora longipes var. retusa

Passiflora longipes var. retusa, commonly known as the longipes passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
