Peperomia pachyphylla

Peperomia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and unique foliage, it is well-suited for container cultivation.
