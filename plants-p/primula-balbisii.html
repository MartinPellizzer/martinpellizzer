Primula balbisii

Primula balbisii, commonly known as the Balbis's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually broad and oval, with a hairy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Primula balbisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
