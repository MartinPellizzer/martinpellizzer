Potamogeton tuberosus

Potamogeton tuberosus, commonly known as the tuberous pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its long, cylindrical stems that often root at the nodes and bear small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that are typically borne on spike-like inflorescences. One of its notable features is the presence of tubers, which are thickened underground stems that store nutrients.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
