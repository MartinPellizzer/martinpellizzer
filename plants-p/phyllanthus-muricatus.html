Phyllanthus muricatus

Phyllanthus muricatus, commonly known as the rough phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a rough or pubescent texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Phyllanthus muricatus is often found in coastal areas and can be used in ornamental gardening due to its unique foliage.
