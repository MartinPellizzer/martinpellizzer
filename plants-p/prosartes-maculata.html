Prosartes maculata

Prosartes maculata, commonly known as the spotted grass.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage often displays subtle spotting, which gives it its common name.

Prosartes maculata is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
