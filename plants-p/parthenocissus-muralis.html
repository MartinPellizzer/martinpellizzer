Parthenocissus muralis

Parthenocissus muralis, commonly known as the Boston ivy, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall, adding ornamental value to landscapes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the summer, which are followed by dark blue berries. Its tendrils allow it to cling to walls and trellises, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.

The leaves are typically 5-lobed, with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
