Phaleria pubiflora

Phaleria pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-fruit moonseed, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a heart-shaped or ovate appearance.

Phaleria pubiflora is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
