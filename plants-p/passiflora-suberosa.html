Passiflora suberosa

Passiflora suberosa, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a blue-violet corona. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora suberosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
