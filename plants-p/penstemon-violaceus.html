Penstemon violaceus

Penstemon violaceus, commonly known as the purple beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing violet to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its striking appearance and ecological value contribute to its status as a valued ornamental and wildlife-friendly plant.
