Philoxerus portulacoides

Philoxerus portulacoides, commonly known as the Mexican burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in a dense, trailing manner. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions.

Philoxerus portulacoides is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
