Phlomoides kirghisorum

Phlomoides kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz phlomoides, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses in its native region.
