Physokentia avia

Physokentia avia, commonly known as the Avian Paddle Palm, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Physokentia avia is also valued as an ornamental plant for its elegant, upright growth habit and lush foliage.
