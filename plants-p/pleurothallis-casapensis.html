Pleurothallis casapensis

Pleurothallis casapensis, commonly known as the Casapensian orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and dark green, adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
