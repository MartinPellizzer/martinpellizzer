Phyteuma stylidioides

Phyteuma stylidioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in dry conditions once established.
