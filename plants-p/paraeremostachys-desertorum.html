Paraeremostachys desertorum

Paraeremostachys desertorum, commonly known as the desert grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces dense clusters of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, typically in shades of pale green or yellow. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to withstand extreme temperatures and limited precipitation.
