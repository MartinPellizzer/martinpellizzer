Plectranthus mahonii

Plectranthus mahonii, commonly known as the silver bush or silverleaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus mahonii is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
