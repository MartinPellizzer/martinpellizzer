Piper humillimum

Piper humillimum, commonly known as the creeping pepper or dwarf pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine. It has a climbing habit and can be found growing in dense undergrowth along riverbanks and in forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
