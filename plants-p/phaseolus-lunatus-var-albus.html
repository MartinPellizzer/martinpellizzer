Phaseolus lunatus var. albus

Phaseolus lunatus var. albus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a variety of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white or pale yellow seeds that are typically round or oval in shape. The plant produces upright, branching stems covered in small, hairy leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in culinary applications.
