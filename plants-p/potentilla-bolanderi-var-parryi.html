Potentilla bolanderi var. parryi

Potentilla bolanderi var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with slender stems and small, yellowish-white flowers. The leaves are typically divided into five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". It thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
