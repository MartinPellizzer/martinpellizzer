Piptochaetium granulatum

Piptochaetium granulatum, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet that is usually erect and hairy.

Piptochaetium granulatum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
