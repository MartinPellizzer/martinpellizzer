Panicum senegalense

Panicum senegalense, commonly known as Senegal millet, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid regions. Its seeds are edible and have been used as a staple food in some African countries.

The plant also plays a role in soil conservation and is sometimes used as forage for livestock.
