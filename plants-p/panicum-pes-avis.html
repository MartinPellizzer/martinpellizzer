Panicum pes-avis

Panicum pes-avis, commonly known as the chicken foot grass, is a flowering plant native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Panicum pes-avis is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
