Pothos glaucus

Pothos glaucus, commonly known as the silver pothos, is a trailing evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly light green with a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of trichomes, which contribute to its glossy texture and help reduce water loss.

Pothos glaucus is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to purify indoor air.
