Polytoma odorifera

Polytoma odorifera, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, fibrous coating. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The sweet senna is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in soil stabilization.
