Pavia arguta

Pavia arguta, commonly known as the smoothleaf pavia, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often smooth and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Pavia arguta is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.
