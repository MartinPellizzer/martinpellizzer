Podocarpus macrophyllus var. acuminatissimus

Podocarpus macrophyllus var. acuminatissimus, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or the evergreen umbrella tree, is a species of coniferous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, a feature typical of conifers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and furniture-making.
