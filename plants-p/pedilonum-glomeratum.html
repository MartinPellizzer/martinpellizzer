Pedilonum glomeratum

Pedilonum glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered pedilonum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in small, dry capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
