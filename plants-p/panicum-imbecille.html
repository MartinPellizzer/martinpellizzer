Panicum imbecille

Panicum imbecille, commonly known as little bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It is often found in prairie ecosystems and is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Panicum imbecille plays a vital role in soil stabilization and habitat provision for various wildlife species.
