Primulina luzhaiensis

Primulina luzhaiensis, commonly known as the Luzhai primula, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primulina luzhaiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, shady environments.
