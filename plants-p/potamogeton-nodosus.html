Potamogeton nodosus

Potamogeton nodosus, commonly known as the nodding water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender stems that often form dense underwater vegetation. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curved, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water's surface.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
