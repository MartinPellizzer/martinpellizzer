Panicum hebotes var. genuinum

Panicum hebotes var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, panicle-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
