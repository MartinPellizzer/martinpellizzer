Primula cortusoides var. lichiangensis

Primula cortusoides var. lichiangensis, commonly known as the Lichiang primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

This variety is noted for its resilience to cold temperatures and its ability to thrive in high-altitude conditions.
