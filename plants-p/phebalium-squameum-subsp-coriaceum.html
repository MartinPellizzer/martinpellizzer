Phebalium squameum subsp. coriaceum

Phebalium squameum subsp.

coriaceum, commonly known as the rough leaved peacock wattles, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
