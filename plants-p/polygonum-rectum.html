Polygonum rectum

Polygonum rectum, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.

This plant is often found in temperate regions of North America and is known for its adaptability to various wetland environments.
