Panicum mattamuskeetense var. clutei

Panicum mattamuskeetense var. clutei, commonly known as Clute's mattamuskeet grass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually pink to reddish in hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist habitats.
