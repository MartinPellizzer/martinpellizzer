Potentilla fragariastroalba

Potentilla fragariastroalba, commonly known as white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and have five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
