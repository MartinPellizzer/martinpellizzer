Phymatochilum brasiliense

Phymatochilum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth. It is known for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is protected in some regions.
