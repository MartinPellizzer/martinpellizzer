Pyrus pyraster f. serratifolia

Pyrus pyraster f. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated crabapple, is a botanical variant of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and small, ornamental flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically small and tart, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This form is notable for its distinct foliage and seasonal display of flowers and fruit.
