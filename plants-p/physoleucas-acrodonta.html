Physoleucas acrodonta

Physoleucas acrodonta, commonly known as the acrodont orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by small insects, contributing to its ecological role in the rainforest ecosystem.
