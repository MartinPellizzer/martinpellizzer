Potamogeton × fluitans var. billotii

Potamogeton × fluitans var. billotii, commonly known as the floating pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface or are submerged. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes.

This variant is known for its ability to thrive in a range of water conditions, making it a common sight in many aquatic ecosystems.
