Paradisea stenantha

Paradisea stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paradise plant, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are showy, with bright orange petals that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses around the world.
