Primula dryadifolia subsp. dryadifolia

Primula dryadifolia subsp.

dryadifolia, commonly known as the dryad's ghost, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China. It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

The leaves are broadly ovate, with a distinctive wavy margin and a silvery underside. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
