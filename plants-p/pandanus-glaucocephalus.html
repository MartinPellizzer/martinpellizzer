Pandanus glaucocephalus

Pandanus glaucocephalus, commonly known as the silver-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, silvery coating. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers, which are followed by three-lobed, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and thatching, while the plant itself is adapted to coastal and saline environments.

Pandanus glaucocephalus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
