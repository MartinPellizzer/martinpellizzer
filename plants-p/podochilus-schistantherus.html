Podochilus schistantherus

Podochilus schistantherus, commonly known as the Schistantherus orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

The flowers are often pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive, elongated shape.
