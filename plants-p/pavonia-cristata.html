Pavonia cristata

Pavonia cristata, commonly known as the crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and can help prevent soil erosion, making it a useful plant in landscaping and restoration projects.

Pavonia cristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
