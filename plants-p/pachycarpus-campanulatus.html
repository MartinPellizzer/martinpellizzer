Pachycarpus campanulatus

Pachycarpus campanulatus, commonly known as the bellflower pachycarpus, is a flowering plant characterized by its unique botanical features.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is native to certain regions of Africa. The plant is distinguished by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. Its leaves are usually opposite and lanceolate, while its stems are often hairy and succulent.

Pachycarpus campanulatus is adapted to arid environments, showcasing thick leaves and a robust root system for water storage.
