Potamogeton argutulus

Potamogeton argutulus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, flexible stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help in reducing water flow resistance.

Potamogeton argutulus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
