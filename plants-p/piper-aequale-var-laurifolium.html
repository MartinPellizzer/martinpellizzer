Piper aequale var. laurifolium

Piper aequale var. laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a variant of the pepper plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are broader than those of the typical *Piper aequale*. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value, combined with its medicinal properties, makes it a popular choice for both gardens and herbal applications.
