Plagiostachys uviformis

Plagiostachys uviformis, commonly known as the false banana, is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudostem formed by the tightly packed leaf sheaths. The plant produces large, broad leaves that can reach several meters in length, and it bears clustered inflorescences with small, white to yellow flowers. Although it resembles a banana plant, it does not produce edible fruit.

Its ornamental value and unique foliage make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and collections.
