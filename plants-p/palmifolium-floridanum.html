Palmifolium floridanum

Palmifolium floridanum, commonly known as the Florida palm fern, is a rare and endangered species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the fern family and is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that resemble palm leaves, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its reproductive structure includes spore-producing clusters called sori, which are located on the underside of its fronds.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, conservation efforts are crucial for the survival of this unique botanical species.
