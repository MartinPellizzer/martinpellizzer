Phaseolus amboensis

Phaseolus amboensis, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces elongated, edible pods that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves and is characterized by its showy, butterfly-like flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop for both food and ornamental purposes.
