Pinus himekomatsu

Pinus himekomatsu, commonly known as the Himekomatsu pine, is a rare and endemic species native to Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its botanical features include a distinctive bark that is reddish-brown and scaly, and its cones are typically short and thick.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
