Paepalanthus tafelbergensis

Paepalanthus tafelbergensis, commonly known as the Tafelberg candelabra, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, branched structure resembling a candelabra. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
