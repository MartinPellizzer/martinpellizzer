Peponia parviflora var. trilobata

Peponia parviflora var. trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed melonflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warm seasons. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three distinct lobes, which is a distinguishing feature.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
