Pacourina edulis

Pacourina edulis, commonly known as the "peach of the Amazon," is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically orange to red in color, with a fleshy exterior and a central seed. These fruits are valued for their unique flavor and are often consumed raw or used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts within its native habitat.
