Polygonum brachytrichum

Polygonum brachytrichum, commonly known as the short-hair knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant has upright stems covered with short, stiff hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often reddish in color. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian areas.
