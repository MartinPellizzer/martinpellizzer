Piper sievekingii

Piper sievekingii, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered berries that mature to a dark red color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces a pungent, aromatic fruit used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
