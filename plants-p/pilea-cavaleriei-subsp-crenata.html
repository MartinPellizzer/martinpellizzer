Pilea cavaleriei subsp. crenata

Pilea cavaleriei subsp.

crenata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China. It features rosettes of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular indoor plant.

Its unique leaf shape and low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
