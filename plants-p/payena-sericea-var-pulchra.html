Payena sericea var. pulchra

Payena sericea var. pulchra, commonly known as the silky payena, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in dry, open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
