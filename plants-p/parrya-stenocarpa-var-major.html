Parrya stenocarpa var. major

Parrya stenocarpa var. major, commonly known as the Sierra giant lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or meadow habitats. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps conserve moisture.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various invertebrates.
