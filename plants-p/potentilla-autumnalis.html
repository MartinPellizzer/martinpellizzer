Potentilla autumnalis

Potentilla autumnalis, commonly known as autumnal cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla autumnalis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for certain pollinators.
