Pachystachys spicata

Pachystachys spicata, commonly known as the spike ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural practices.

Pachystachys spicata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
