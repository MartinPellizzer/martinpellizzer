Piper trineuron var. laxiflorum

Piper trineuron var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper trineuron species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically harvested when ripe for culinary or medicinal use. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
