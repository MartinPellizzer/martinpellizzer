Penstemon cephalophorus

Penstemon cephalophorus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, preferring full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Penstemon cephalophorus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
