Pectis angustifolia var. subaristata

Pectis angustifolia var. subaristata, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions with minimal water.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
