Persicaria ramosa

Persicaria ramosa, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from mid-summer to early fall. Persicaria ramosa thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and in waste areas.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
