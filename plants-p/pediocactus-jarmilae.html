Pediocactus jarmilae

Pediocactus jarmilae, commonly known as the Jarmilae barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its growth is slow, and it typically reaches a height of about 10 to 15 inches.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and conservation programs.
