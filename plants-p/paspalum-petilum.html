Paspalum petilum

Paspalum petilum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

Paspalum petilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or ecological restoration projects.
