Phlomis barrelieri

Phlomis barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's blazingstar, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with hairy surfaces, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces numerous small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phlomis barrelieri is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
