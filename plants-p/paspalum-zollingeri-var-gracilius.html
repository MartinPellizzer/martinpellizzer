Paspalum zollingeri var. gracilius

Paspalum zollingeri var. gracilius, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
