Polypodium delicatulum

Polypodium delicatulum, commonly known as the delicate huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in strobila, a feature typical of many ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The delicate huperzia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
