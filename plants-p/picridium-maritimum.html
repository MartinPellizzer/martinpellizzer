Picridium maritimum

Picridium maritimum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and spiny, lobed leaves that help it withstand salty and windy conditions. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Picridium maritimum is adapted to thrive in harsh maritime environments, making it a resilient and ornamental species.
