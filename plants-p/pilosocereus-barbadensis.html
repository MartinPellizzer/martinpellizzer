Pilosocereus barbadensis

Pilosocereus barbadensis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that are covered in soft, velvety spines. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Despite its name, it is not a true cactus but belongs to the cacti family, and it is popular as a decorative houseplant.
