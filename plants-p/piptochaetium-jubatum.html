Piptochaetium jubatum

Piptochaetium jubatum, commonly known as the tall grass or blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, dry climates.

Piptochaetium jubatum is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
