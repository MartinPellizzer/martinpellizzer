Pleurothallis davisii

Pleurothallis davisii, commonly known as the Davis's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
