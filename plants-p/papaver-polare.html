Papaver polare

Papaver polare, commonly known as the polar poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is one of the few flowering plants that can survive in the extreme cold of the tundra. The plant has small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the short summer season. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in the harsh environment.

Papaver polare is a member of the Papaveraceae family, which includes other poppy species.
