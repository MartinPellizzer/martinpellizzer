Primula glandulosa

Primula glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in its alpine habitat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens with specific climate requirements.
