Popowia littoralis

Popowia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal popowia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils near the shore, often in areas with high salinity and strong winds. Its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.

Popowia littoralis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens along the coast.
