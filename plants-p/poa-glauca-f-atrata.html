Poa glauca f. atrata

Poa glauca f. atrata, commonly known as the dark blue grass or blue hair grass, is a horticultural form of the common bluegrass.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking dark blue to blackish foliage. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique coloration and compact growth make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and borders.
