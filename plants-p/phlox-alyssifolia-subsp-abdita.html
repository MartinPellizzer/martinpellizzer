Phlox alyssifolia subsp. abdita

Phlox alyssifolia subsp.

abdita, commonly known as the alpine phlox, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
