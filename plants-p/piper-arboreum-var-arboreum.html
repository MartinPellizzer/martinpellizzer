Piper arboreum var. arboreum

Piper arboreum var. arboreum, commonly known as the sacred pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in warm climates.
