Pelargonium capituliforme

Pelargonium capituliforme, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant blooms make it both ornamental and relatively low-maintenance.
