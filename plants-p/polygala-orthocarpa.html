Polygala orthocarpa

Polygala orthocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with distinctive, wing-like petals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
