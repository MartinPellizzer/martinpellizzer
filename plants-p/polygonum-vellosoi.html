Polygonum vellosoi

Polygonum vellosoi, commonly known as the Velloso's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and often edible seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Polygonum vellosoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
