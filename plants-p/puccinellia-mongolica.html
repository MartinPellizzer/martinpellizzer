Puccinellia mongolica

Puccinellia mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Puccinellia mongolica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
