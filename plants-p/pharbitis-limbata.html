Pharbitis limbata

Pharbitis limbata, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in gardens and agricultural settings.
