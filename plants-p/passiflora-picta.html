Passiflora picta

Passiflora picta, commonly known as the painted passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white hues, with prominent violet filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora picta thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
