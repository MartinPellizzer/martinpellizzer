Phyllanthus nozeranianus

Phyllanthus nozeranianus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a white, fleshy aril. It is often found in disturbed sites, such as roadsides and abandoned fields, and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
