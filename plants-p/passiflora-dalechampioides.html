Passiflora dalechampioides

Passiflora dalechampioides, commonly known as the Brazilian passion flower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
