Plagianthus regius subsp. regius

Plagianthus regius subsp.

regius, commonly known as the royal plagiathus, is a striking plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of long, narrow leaves that give it a grass-like appearance.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers in summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
