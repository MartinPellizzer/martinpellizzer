Pleurothallis grobleri

Pleurothallis grobleri, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated flowers that often have a striking white or pale green coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis grobleri is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
