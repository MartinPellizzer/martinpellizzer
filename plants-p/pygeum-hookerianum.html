Pygeum hookerianum

Pygeum hookerianum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
