Panicum petersonii

Panicum petersonii, commonly known as Peterson's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
