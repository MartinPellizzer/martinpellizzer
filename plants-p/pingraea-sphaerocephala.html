Pingraea sphaerocephala

Pingraea sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, inflated flower heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, dry fruits known as capsules.

Pingraea sphaerocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
