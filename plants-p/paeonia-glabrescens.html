Paeonia glabrescens

Paeonia glabrescens, commonly known as the western peony, is a deciduous herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered with a fine, powdery bloom. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water and nutrients in arid environments.

Paeonia glabrescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
