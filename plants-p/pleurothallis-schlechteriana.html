Pleurothallis schlechteriana

Pleurothallis schlechteriana, commonly known as the Schlechter's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small, with a wide range of colors, and it blooms during the wet season.

The genus Pleurothallis is known for its diverse and often intricate floral structures, making this species a notable example within the group.
