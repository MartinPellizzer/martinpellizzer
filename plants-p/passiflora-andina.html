Passiflora andina

Passiflora andina, commonly known as the Andean passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and intricate structures. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Passiflora andina is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional uses in local medicine.
