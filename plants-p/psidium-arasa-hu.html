Psidium arasa-hu

Psidium arasa-hu, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or arasa-hu, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
