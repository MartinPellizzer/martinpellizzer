Patagonium pauciflorum

Patagonium pauciflorum, commonly known as the Patagonian low-flower plant, is a rare flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in high-altitude environments. Its botanical features include a short stem, fleshy leaves, and pale yellow or greenish flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
