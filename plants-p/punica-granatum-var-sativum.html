Punica granatum var. sativum

Punica granatum var. sativum, commonly known as the pomegranate, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the region spanning from the Indus River to the Persian Gulf.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, and yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, spiky, red fruit. The fruit contains numerous seeds encased in a juicy, tart aril, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, preferring warm, dry climates.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its rich nutritional content, making it an important crop in many parts of the world.
