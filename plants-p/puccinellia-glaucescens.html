Puccinellia glaucescens

Puccinellia glaucescens, commonly known as bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Puccinellia glaucescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
