Panicum hookeri

Panicum hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Panicum hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
