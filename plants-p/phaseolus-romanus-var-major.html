Phaseolus romanus var. major

Phaseolus romanus var. major, commonly known as the giant runner bean, is a cultivar of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its large, elongated pods that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces vibrant green, edible beans that are typically harvested when immature for optimal flavor and texture. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its culinary uses.
