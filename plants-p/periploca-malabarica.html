Periploca malabarica

Periploca malabarica, commonly known as Malabar periploca, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shady environments. The plant features simple, opposite leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Periploca malabarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
