Polygala alopecuroides

Polygala alopecuroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
