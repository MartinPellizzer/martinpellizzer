Parrya stenocarpa subsp. gilgitica

Parrya stenocarpa subsp.

gilgitica, commonly known as the Gilgit parrya, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
