Pellionia lamii

Pellionia lamii, commonly known as the silver velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are rich in silver-green pigmentation. The plant typically grows as a low shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by dark purple berries.

Pellionia lamii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
