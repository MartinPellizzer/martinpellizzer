Pilosella lactucella subsp. melaneilema

Pilosella lactucella subsp.

melaneilema, commonly known as the dark-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark green, velvety leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This plant is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cooler, sunny conditions.
