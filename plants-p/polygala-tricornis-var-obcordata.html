Polygala tricornis var. obcordata

Polygala tricornis var. obcordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland regions. It has a slender, erect stem and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
