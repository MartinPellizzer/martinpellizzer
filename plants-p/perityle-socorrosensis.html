Perityle socorrosensis

Perityle socorrosensis, commonly known as the Socorro perityle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socorro in the Revillagigedo archipelago.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, coastal habitats, showcasing adaptations to the arid and saline conditions of its environment. Its small, yellow flowers bloom during the warmer months, contributing to the biodiversity of the region.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species of significant ecological value.
