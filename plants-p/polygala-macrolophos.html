Polygala macrolophos

Polygala macrolophos, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
