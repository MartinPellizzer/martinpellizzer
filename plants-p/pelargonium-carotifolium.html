Pelargonium carotifolium

Pelargonium carotifolium, commonly known as the carrotleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its finely cut, carrot-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium carotifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
