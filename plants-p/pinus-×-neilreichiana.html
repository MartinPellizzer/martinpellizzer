Pinus × neilreichiana

Pinus × neilreichiana, commonly known as the Neilreichiana pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus rigida and Pinus ponderosa.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long. The tree produces cones that are often reddish-brown and can reach lengths of up to 6 inches. It is native to the southwestern United States and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
