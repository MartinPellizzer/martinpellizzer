Persica vulgaris var. apiana

Persica vulgaris var. apiana, commonly known as the wild peach.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, fuzzy fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.
