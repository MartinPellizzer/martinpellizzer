Phaseolus zebra var. spadiceus

Phaseolus zebra var. spadiceus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a variety of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white to pale yellow seeds that are often speckled with a distinctive pattern. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing trifoliate leaves and pods that contain the edible seeds. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and traditional medicinal uses.
