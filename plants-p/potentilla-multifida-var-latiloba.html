Potentilla multifida var. latiloba

Potentilla multifida var. latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
