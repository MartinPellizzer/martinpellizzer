Polypodium mauritianum

Polypodium mauritianum, commonly known as the Mauritius polypody, is a tropical fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though its ecological role in its native habitat remains understudied.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
