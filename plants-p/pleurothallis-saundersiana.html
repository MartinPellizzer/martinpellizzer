Pleurothallis saundersiana

Pleurothallis saundersiana, commonly known as the Saunders' orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a range of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Due to its delicate beauty and specific ecological requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
