Parodia faustiana

Parodia faustiana, commonly known as the Faustian cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
