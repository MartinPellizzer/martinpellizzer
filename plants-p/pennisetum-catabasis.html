Pennisetum catabasis

Pennisetum catabasis, commonly known as blue fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.

Pennisetum catabasis is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
