Phymatopteris glauca

Phymatopteris glauca, commonly known as the blue huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage with a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are linear to lanceolate, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

Phymatopteris glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
