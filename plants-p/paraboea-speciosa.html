Paraboea speciosa

Paraboea speciosa, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Paraboea speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
