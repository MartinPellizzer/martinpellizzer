Plectranthus walkeri

Plectranthus walkeri, commonly known as the Cape lavender, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal uses.
