Premna depauperata var. oswaldiana

Premna depauperata var. oswaldiana, commonly known as the slender false elder, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the summer months. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
