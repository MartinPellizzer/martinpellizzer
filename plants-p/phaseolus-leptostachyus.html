Phaseolus leptostachyus

Phaseolus leptostachyus, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a heart-shaped leaflet. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that are followed by elongated, narrow pods containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible seeds.

The botanical features of Phaseolus leptostachyus make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
