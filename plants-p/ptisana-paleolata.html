Ptisana paleolata

Ptisana paleolata, commonly known as the ancient grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Ptisana paleolata is known for its small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
