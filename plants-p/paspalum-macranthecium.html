Paspalum macranthecium

Paspalum macranthecium, commonly known as the large-flowered paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum macranthecium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
