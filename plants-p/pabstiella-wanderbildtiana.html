Pabstiella wanderbildtiana

Pabstiella wanderbildtiana, commonly known as the Wanderbildtian stonecrop, is a unique succulent plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
