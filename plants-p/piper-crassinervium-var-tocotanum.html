Piper crassinervium var. tocotanum

Piper crassinervium var. tocotanum, commonly known as the Tocotanum pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, narrow veins, giving it its scientific name "crassinervium.". The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
