Potentilla × hoelzlii

Potentilla × hoelzlii, commonly known as the Hoelzl's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with upright, branched stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to well-drained soils in full sun.
