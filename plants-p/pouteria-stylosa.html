Pouteria stylosa

Pouteria stylosa, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree is notable for its hard, woody fruit, which contains a single large seed surrounded by a sweet, edible kernel. This species is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid, nutrient-rich soils.

Pouteria stylosa plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity within its native habitat.
