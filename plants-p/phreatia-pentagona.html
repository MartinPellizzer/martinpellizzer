Phreatia pentagona

Phreatia pentagona, commonly known as the pentagon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique pentagonal-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, thriving in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and greenish-white, often with a faintly colored lip.

Phreatia pentagona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
