Paronychia tenuifolia

Paronychia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise sparse foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a useful plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
