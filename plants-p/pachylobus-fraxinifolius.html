Pachylobus fraxinifolius

Pachylobus fraxinifolius, commonly known as the ash-leaf pachylobus, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that resembles the leaves of ash trees. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
