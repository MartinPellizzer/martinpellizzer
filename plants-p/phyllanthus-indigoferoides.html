Phyllanthus indigoferoides

Phyllanthus indigoferoides, commonly known as the Indian star gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive yellowish or whitish midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
