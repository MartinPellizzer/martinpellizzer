Potamogeton zosterophyllus

Potamogeton zosterophyllus, commonly known as the thread-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, which helps it maximize light absorption in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by tiny, three-lobed fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its ability to thrive in a range of water conditions, it is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.
