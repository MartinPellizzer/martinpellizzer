Pleurothallis coriacea

Pleurothallis coriacea, commonly known as the wrinkled orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, pleated leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is appreciated for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
