Parnassia montanensis

Parnassia montanensis, commonly known as the mountain parnassia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that are often adorned with red or purple markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are often covered in a waxy coating.

Parnassia montanensis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
