Passiflora orbiculata

Passiflora orbiculata, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, colorful flowers that resemble a cluster of petals and sepals. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its botanical features include a complex floral structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive organs.

Passiflora orbiculata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
