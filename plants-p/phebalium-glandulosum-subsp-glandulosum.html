Phebalium glandulosum subsp. glandulosum

Phebalium glandulosum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular phebalium, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
