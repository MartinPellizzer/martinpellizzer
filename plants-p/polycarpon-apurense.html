Polycarpon apurense

Polycarpon apurense, commonly known as the Apurean knotweed, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Polycarpon apurense is notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
