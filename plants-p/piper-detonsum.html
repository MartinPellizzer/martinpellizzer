Piper detonsum

Piper detonsum, commonly known as the West Indian devil's ivy, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing in shaded, moist areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
