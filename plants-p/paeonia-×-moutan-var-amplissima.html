Paeonia × moutan var. amplissima

Paeonia × moutan var. amplissima, commonly known as the tree peony, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, white, and yellow. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces woody stems that can grow up to several meters in height. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.

The flowers typically have a strong fragrance and are known to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
