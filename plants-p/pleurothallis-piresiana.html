Pleurothallis piresiana

Pleurothallis piresiana, commonly known as the Pires's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare and exotic orchids.
