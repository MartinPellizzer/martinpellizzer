Ptarmica barbeyana

Ptarmica barbeyana, commonly known as the Barbey's thyme, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Ptarmica barbeyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
