Planera parvifolia

Planera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
