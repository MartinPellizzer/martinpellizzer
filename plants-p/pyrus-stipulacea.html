Pyrus stipulacea

Pyrus stipulacea, commonly known as the stipule pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Pyrus stipulacea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
