Polystachya pubescens

Polystachya pubescens, commonly known as the hairy-leafed fir, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Polystachya pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
