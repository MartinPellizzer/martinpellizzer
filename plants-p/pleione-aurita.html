Pleione aurita

Pleione aurita, commonly known as the golden pleione, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a golden or orange hue at the base. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded forest environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients in its native habitat.

Due to its beauty and rarity, Pleione aurita is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
