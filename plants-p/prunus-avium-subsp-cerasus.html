Prunus avium subsp. cerasus

Prunus avium subsp.

cerasus, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia, particularly the Caucasus region. It produces small, oval leaves that are dark green and serrated along the edges.

The plant is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. Its fruit, the sweet cherry, is fleshy, sweet, and typically contains a single stone or pit.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its culinary and ornamental value.
