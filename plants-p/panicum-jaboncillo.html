Panicum jaboncillo

Panicum jaboncillo, commonly known as soap panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often mistaken for dandelion fluff. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Panicum jaboncillo is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife.
