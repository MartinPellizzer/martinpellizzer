Piper munchanum var. magnifolium

Piper munchanum var. magnifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a tropical species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple, peppercorn-like berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and have a mild, spicy aroma.
