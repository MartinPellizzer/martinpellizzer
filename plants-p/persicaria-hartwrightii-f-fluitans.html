Persicaria hartwrightii f. fluitans

Persicaria hartwrightii f. fluitans, commonly known as the floating knotweed, is a unique variant of the knotweed genus characterized by its aquatic growth habit.

This plant typically features heart-shaped leaves that are adapted for floating on the water's surface. It produces small, greenish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in water gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its ability to thrive in wetland environments, contributing to ecological diversity in such habitats.
