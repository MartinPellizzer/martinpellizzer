Polypodiodes amoena f. pilosa

Polypodiodes amoena f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy fern, is a variant of the polypodiodes genus.

It is a fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features fronds that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The hairy fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
