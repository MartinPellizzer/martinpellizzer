Plumeria rubra var. acuminata

Plumeria rubra var. acuminata, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces milky sap when cut. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are typically borne on long, branched inflorescences and bloom during the dry season.
