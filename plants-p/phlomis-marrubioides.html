Phlomis marrubioides

Phlomis marrubioides, commonly known as narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with soft, woolly hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Lamiaceae, sharing characteristics with other members of the mint family.
