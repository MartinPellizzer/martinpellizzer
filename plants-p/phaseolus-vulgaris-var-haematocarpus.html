Phaseolus vulgaris var. haematocarpus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. haematocarpus, commonly known as the blood bean, is a variant of the common bean that is characterized by its distinctive red or purple seed coat.

This variety is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and erect stems. It produces clusters of white or pale purple flowers, followed by pods containing several seeds.

The unique coloration of its seeds makes it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.
