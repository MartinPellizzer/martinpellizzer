Pseudotsuga taxifolia f. compacta

Pseudotsuga taxifolia f. compacta, commonly known as the Japanese stone pine dwarf form, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It features a dense, rounded shape with short, needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly twisted. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
