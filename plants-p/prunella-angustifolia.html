Prunella angustifolia

Prunella angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf selfheal, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. Small, blue to violet flowers appear in whorls along the stems, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
