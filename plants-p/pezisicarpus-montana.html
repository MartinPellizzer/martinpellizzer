Pezisicarpus montana

Pezisicarpus montana, commonly known as the mountain pezizicarpus, is a species of fungus in the family Pezizaceae.

It is typically found in temperate regions, growing on decaying wood in forested areas. The fruiting bodies of this fungus are small, cup-shaped, and often have a pale to yellowish color. It plays a role in the decomposition of organic matter, contributing to nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.

However, it is not widely studied and is considered a rare or little-known species in the fungal world.
