Phyllanthus kaessneri var. kaessneri

Phyllanthus kaessneri var. kaessneri, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
