Pilosocereus juaruensis subsp. cincinnopetalus

Pilosocereus juaruensis subsp.

cincinnopetalus, commonly known as the Juaru Thorn, is a cactus native to Brazil. It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and produces small, white flowers during the dry season.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, water-storing stems. Its subspecies distinction is based on floral morphology, particularly the shape of its petals.

It is a popular species among cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and resilience.
