Pleurothallis serrisepala

Pleurothallis serrisepala, commonly known as the serrated lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive serrated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleurothallis serrisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
