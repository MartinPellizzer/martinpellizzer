Pyrus guanxianensis

Pyrus guanxianensis, commonly known as the Guanxian pear, is a species of pear native to China.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a pear-shaped apple, typically small to medium in size, with a thin, edible skin and a sweet, aromatic flesh.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its edible fruit in certain regions of China.
