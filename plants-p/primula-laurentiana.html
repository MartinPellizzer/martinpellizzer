Primula laurentiana

Primula laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Canada and the northeastern United States.

It features basal rosettes of oval, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for early pollinators.
