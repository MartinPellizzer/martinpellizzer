Pterocarpus zimmermannii

Pterocarpus zimmermannii, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers and dark, dense wood. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
