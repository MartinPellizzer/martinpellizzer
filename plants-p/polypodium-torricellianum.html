Polypodium torricellianum

Polypodium torricellianum, commonly known as the Torricellian maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and help anchor it to substrates, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid, indoor settings.
