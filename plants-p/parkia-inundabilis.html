Parkia inundabilis

Parkia inundabilis, commonly known as the water parkia, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces pod-like fruits containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
