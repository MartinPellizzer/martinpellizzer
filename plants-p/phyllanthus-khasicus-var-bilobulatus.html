Phyllanthus khasicus var. bilobulatus

Phyllanthus khasicus var. bilobulatus, commonly known as the Bilobulate Phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
