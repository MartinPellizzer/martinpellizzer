Paepalanthus villipes

Paepalanthus villipes, commonly known as the velvetleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white hairs, which helps to reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow and bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
