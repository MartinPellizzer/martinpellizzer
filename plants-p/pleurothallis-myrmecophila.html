Pleurothallis myrmecophila

Pleurothallis myrmecophila, commonly known as the ant-plant, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique ability to form symbiotic relationships with ants, providing them with shelter and food in exchange for protection against herbivores. The plant has a compact, epiphytic growth habit, often growing on trees in the canopy of humid forests. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow and red.

This orchid is valued not only for its ecological role but also for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
