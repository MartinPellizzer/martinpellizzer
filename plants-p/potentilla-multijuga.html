Potentilla multijuga

Potentilla multijuga, commonly known as the twinleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are oval-shaped and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
