Prunus pubescens lancifolia

Prunus pubescens lancifolia, commonly known as the hairy lanceleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
