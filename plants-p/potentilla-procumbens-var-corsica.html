Potentilla procumbens var. corsica

Potentilla procumbens var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, spreading stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with a white waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structure.
