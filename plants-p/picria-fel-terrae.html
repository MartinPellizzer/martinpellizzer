Picria fel-terrae

Picria fel-terrae, commonly known as the desert chicory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Picria fel-terrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
