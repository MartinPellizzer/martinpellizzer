Petrophiloides strobilacea

Petrophiloides strobilacea, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged opposite along the stem.

Petrophiloides strobilacea plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
