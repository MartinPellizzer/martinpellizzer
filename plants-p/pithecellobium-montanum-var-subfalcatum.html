Pithecellobium montanum var. subfalcatum

Pithecellobium montanum var. subfalcatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pithecellobium, is a species of legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
