Polyosma subintegrifolia

Polyosma subintegrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed polyosma, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its foliage is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and hardiness.

It prefers well-drained soils and is adaptable to various light conditions.
