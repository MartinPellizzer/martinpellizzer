Penstemon oliganthus

Penstemon oliganthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pairs of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon oliganthus is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
