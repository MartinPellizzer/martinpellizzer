Phalaris truncata var. angustata

Phalaris truncata var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf jointvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. The plant has slender, erect stems with small, narrow leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pea-like, with a distinctive blue to purple color, and it produces small, oblong seed pods.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
