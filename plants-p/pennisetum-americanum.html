Pennisetum americanum

Pennisetum americanum, commonly known as giant feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a popular ornamental and a potential weed.

Pennisetum americanum is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
