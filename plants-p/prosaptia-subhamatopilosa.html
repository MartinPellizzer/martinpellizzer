Prosaptia subhamatopilosa

Prosaptia subhamatopilosa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble a misty cloud, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, waxy surface, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green in color.

Prosaptia subhamatopilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
