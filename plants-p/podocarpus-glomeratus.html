Podocarpus glomeratus

Podocarpus glomeratus, commonly known as the Hawaiian Christmas tree, is a coniferous evergreen native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like structures that mature into woody, spherical formations. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in compact clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Podocarpus glomeratus plays an important ecological role in Hawaii's forests and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
