Pandanus ferox

Pandanus ferox, commonly known as the sharp leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, sharp, sword-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible fruits known as pandanus nuts. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove swamps or on sandy shores.

The plant has cultural significance in various island communities and is sometimes used for its ornamental value.
