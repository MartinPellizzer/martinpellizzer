Pyrethrum vahlii

Pyrethrum vahlii, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pyrethrum vahlii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been studied for its potential pesticidal properties.
