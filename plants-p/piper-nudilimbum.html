Piper nudilimbum

Piper nudilimbum, commonly known as the "Burmese pepper" or "wild long pepper," is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

The botanical characteristics of Piper nudilimbum make it a valuable plant in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
