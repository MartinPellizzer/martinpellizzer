Pittosporum humile

Pittosporum humile, commonly known as the Christmas box, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Pittosporum humile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
