Prunus × fukudana

Prunus × fukudana, commonly known as the Japanese flowering cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is celebrated for its ornamental value, particularly its showy spring blossoms. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers, which appear in clusters, are usually pale pink to white, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

The botanical characteristics of Prunus × fukudana make it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces worldwide.
