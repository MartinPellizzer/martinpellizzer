Pseuderanthemum adenocarpum

Pseuderanthemum adenocarpum, commonly known as the glossy leaf pseuderanthemum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
