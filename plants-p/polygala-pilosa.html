Polygala pilosa

Polygala pilosa, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique structure that features a spurred petal.

Polygala pilosa is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
