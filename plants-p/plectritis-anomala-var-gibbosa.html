Plectritis anomala var. gibbosa

Plectritis anomala var. gibbosa, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a short-lived perennial that blooms from spring to summer.
