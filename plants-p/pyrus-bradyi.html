Pyrus bradyi

Pyrus bradyi, commonly known as the Brady pear, is a rare and unique species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and yellowish in color. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Pyrus bradyi is considered a rare and endangered species.

Its botanical study is important for understanding plant adaptation to extreme environments.
