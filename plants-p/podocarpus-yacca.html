Podocarpus yacca

Podocarpus yacca, commonly known as the Yacca pine, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinct aromatic scent, contributing to its ornamental value.

Podocarpus yacca is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and reforestation projects.
