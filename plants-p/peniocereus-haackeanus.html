Peniocereus haackeanus

Peniocereus haackeanus, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and produces striking white flowers that open at night. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. Its flowers are fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
