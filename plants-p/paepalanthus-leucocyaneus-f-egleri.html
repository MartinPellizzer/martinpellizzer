Paepalanthus leucocyaneus f. egleri

Paepalanthus leucocyaneus f. egleri, commonly known as the Egler's blue-eyed grass, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant features bright blue to violet flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a visually appealing display. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a silver or grayish hue that adds to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is prized for its unique coloration and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
