Pavonia bojeri

Pavonia bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's false mallow, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton.
