Peperomia contraria

Peperomia contraria, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its striking silver-gray foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
