Polygonum petecticale

Polygonum petecticale, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silver-green hue on the upper surface.

Polygonum petecticale is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
