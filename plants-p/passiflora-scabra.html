Passiflora scabra

Passiflora scabra, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a complex structure featuring five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. Its fruit is a woody, spiny capsule that contains edible, fleshy seeds.

Passiflora scabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
