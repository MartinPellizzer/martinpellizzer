Pinalia parishii

Pinalia parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay pine or Paraguay pine tree, is a species of conifer native to South America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of coniferous plants. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forest ecosystems. Its botanical features include a straight trunk and a conical shape, with bark that is rough and fissured.

Pinalia parishii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
