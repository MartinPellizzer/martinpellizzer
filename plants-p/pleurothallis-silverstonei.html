Pleurothallis silverstonei

Pleurothallis silverstonei, commonly known as the Silverstone orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without being parasitic. Its flowers are often pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
