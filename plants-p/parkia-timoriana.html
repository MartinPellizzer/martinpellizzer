Parkia timoriana

Parkia timoriana, commonly known as the Timor legume or Timor acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its bipinnate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Parkia timoriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
