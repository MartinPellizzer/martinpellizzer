Prunus apetala var. apetala

Prunus apetala var. apetala, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits known as cherries. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is often cultivated in gardens and orchards.
