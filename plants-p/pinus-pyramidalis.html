Pinus pyramidalis

Pinus pyramidalis, commonly known as the Canary Island pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are arranged in clusters of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas with a temperate climate.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in Mediterranean and subtropical regions.
