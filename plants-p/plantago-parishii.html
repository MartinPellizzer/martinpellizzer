Plantago parishii

Plantago parishii, commonly known as the desert plantain, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, aiding in water retention.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
