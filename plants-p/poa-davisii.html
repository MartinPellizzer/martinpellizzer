Poa davisii

Poa davisii, commonly known as Davis' bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands. It has a clumping growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Poa davisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
