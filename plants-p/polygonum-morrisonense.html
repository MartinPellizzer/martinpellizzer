Polygonum morrisonense

Polygonum morrisonense, commonly known as the Morrison's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color.

The leaves are often reddish or purple, adding to its ornamental value in gardens.
