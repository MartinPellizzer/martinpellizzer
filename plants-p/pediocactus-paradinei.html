Pediocactus paradinei

Pediocactus paradinei, commonly known as the Paradox Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and is typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often borne on a short stem above the main body. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a shallow root system and a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Pediocactus paradinei is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
