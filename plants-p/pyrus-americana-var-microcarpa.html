Pyrus americana var. microcarpa

Pyrus americana var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf edible crabapple, is a compact, deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, tart berries in the fall. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its ornamental value.

Its fruit is used in jams and jellies, and it is also valued for its ecological benefits to pollinators.
