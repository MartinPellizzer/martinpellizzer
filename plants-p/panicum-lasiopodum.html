Panicum lasiopodum

Panicum lasiopodum, commonly known as rough panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, rough-textured foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
