Pavonia sidoides

Pavonia sidoides, commonly known as the Brazilian pea vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Pavonia sidoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
