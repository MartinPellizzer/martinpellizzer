Plantago juncoides var. glauca

Plantago juncoides var. glauca, commonly known as blue plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a waxy, bluish-green coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Botanically, it is characterized by its basal rosette growth habit and fibrous root system.
