Padus virginiana f. duerinckii

Padus virginiana f. duerinckii, commonly known as the Duering's white crabsapple, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white flowers and red or orange fruit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and the bark becomes fissured with age.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
