Phaseolus grandiflorus

Phaseolus grandiflorus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that are followed by elongated, green pods containing edible beans. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, typically with three leaflets.

This species is valued for both its ornamental flowers and its edible seeds, which are often used in culinary dishes.
