Panicum dichotomum var. lucidum

Panicum dichotomum var. lucidum, commonly known as slender bristlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
