Potentilla decurrens

Potentilla decurrens, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces yellow flowers and has compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies. Its extensive root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
