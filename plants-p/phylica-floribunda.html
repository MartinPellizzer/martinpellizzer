Phylica floribunda

Phylica floribunda, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
