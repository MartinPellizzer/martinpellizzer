Pelargonium purpurascens

Pelargonium purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
