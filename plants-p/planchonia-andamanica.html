Planchonia andamanica

Planchonia andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It belongs to the family Guttiferae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological and historical significance.
