Pleurandra crassifolia

Pleurandra crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed pleurandra, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are thick and waxy, providing a striking visual appearance. Its flowers are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family, and are usually surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Pleurandra crassifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
