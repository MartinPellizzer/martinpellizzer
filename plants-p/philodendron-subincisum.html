Philodendron subincisum

Philodendron subincisum, commonly known as the heartleaf philodendron, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are not particularly showy but are important for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron subincisum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
