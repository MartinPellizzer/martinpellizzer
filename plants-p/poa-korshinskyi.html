Poa korshinskyi

Poa korshinskyi, commonly known as Korshinsky's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and blue-green in color, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
