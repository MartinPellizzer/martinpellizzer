Pyrus baccata

Pyrus baccata, commonly known as the white-berried pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and yellowish-green fruits that turn red when ripe. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated margins. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
