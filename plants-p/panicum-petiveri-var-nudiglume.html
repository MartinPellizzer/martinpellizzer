Panicum petiveri var. nudiglume

Panicum petiveri var. nudiglume, commonly known as the naked lemma panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that lack the typical bracts found in many other Panicum species. The plant's inflorescence consists of open, cylindrical clusters of flowers, which are often pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is commonly found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its unique morphology makes it identifiable and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
