Pavonia zeylonica

Pavonia zeylonica, commonly known as the Sri Lankan mallow, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in axillary inflorescences.

Pavonia zeylonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
