Peucedanum schottii

Peucedanum schottii, commonly known as Schott's angelica, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in compound umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
