Pinus nigra var. dalmatica

Pinus nigra var. dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian black pine, is a variant of the black pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and a conical shape. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in forestry and landscaping.
