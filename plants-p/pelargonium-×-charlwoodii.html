Pelargonium × charlwoodii

Pelargonium × charlwoodii, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × inodorum, combining traits from both parents. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is heart-shaped and often features a velvety texture, enhancing its visual appeal.

Pelargonium × charlwoodii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens and containers.
