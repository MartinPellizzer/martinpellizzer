Platyclinis gracilis var. angustifolia

Platyclinis gracilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered yellow flowers in the summer. Its stems are often reddish and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
