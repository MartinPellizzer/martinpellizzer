Petesioides pendula

Petesioides pendula, commonly known as the hanging petesioides, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers bloom in clusters, producing both showy ray florets and inconspicuous disk florets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
