Pterocarpus marsupium

Pterocarpus marsupium, commonly known as Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, durable wood and distinctive bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
