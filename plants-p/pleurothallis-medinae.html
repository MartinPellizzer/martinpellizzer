Pleurothallis medinae

Pleurothallis medinae, commonly known as the Medina's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow blooms that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
