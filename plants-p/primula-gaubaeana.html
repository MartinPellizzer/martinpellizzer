Primula gaubaeana

Primula gaubaeana, commonly known as the Gaubaeana primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula gaubaeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
