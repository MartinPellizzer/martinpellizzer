Polygonum podophyllum

Polygonum podophyllum, commonly known as the red baneberry, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and flowers. The plant features showy, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple hue on the underside.

The plant produces small, toxic berries that are red when mature, making it both ornamental and potentially hazardous.
