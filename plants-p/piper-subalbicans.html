Piper subalbicans

Piper subalbicans, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dark purple berries. Its fruit is used to extract the spice known as white pepper, which is derived from the dried, unripe fruit.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic and culinary value.
