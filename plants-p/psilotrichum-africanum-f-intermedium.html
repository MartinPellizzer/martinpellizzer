Psilotrichum africanum f. intermedium

Psilotrichum africanum f. intermedium, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a fern-like plant belonging to the family Psilotaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, dichotomous branching and the presence of microphylls, which are small, scale-like leaves. The plant lacks true roots and has a rhizome that serves as an underground stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories across sub-Saharan Africa.

Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, contributing to its role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
