Peperomia salmensis

Peperomia salmensis, commonly known as the Samsen's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and silver. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Peperomia salmensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
