Parodia guachipasana

Parodia guachipasana, commonly known as the Guachipasana cactus, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant features prominent areoles with spines and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts.
