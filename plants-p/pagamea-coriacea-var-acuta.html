Pagamea coriacea var. acuta

Pagamea coriacea var. acuta, commonly known as the rough pagamea, is a shrub native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in arid regions.
