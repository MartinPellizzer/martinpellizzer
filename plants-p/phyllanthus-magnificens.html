Phyllanthus magnificens

Phyllanthus magnificens, commonly known as the magnificent spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
