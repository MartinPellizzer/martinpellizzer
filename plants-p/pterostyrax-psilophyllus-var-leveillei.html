Pterostyrax psilophyllus var. leveillei

Pterostyrax psilophyllus var. leveillei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pterostyrax, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral display.
