Pilosocereus robinii subsp. millspaughii

Pilosocereus robinii subsp.

millspaughii, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a tall, columnar stem with a dense covering of white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
