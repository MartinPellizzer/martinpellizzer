Phalaris bulbosa var. clausonis

Phalaris bulbosa var. clausonis, commonly known as the Clauson's bulbous grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of spikelets that are often tinged with purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-maintenance landscapes.
