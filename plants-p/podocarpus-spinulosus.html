Podocarpus spinulosus

Podocarpus spinulosus, commonly known as the spinulose podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in small, spine-like structures, giving it its common name. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dense foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
