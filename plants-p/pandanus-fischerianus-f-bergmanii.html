Pandanus fischerianus f. bergmanii

Pandanus fischerianus f. bergmanii, commonly known as the Bergman's pandanus, is a variant of the pandanus plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
