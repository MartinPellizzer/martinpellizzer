Phlegmariurus aqualupianus

Phlegmariurus aqualupianus, commonly known as the aquatic clubmoss, is a unique species of moss-like plant belonging to the family Phlegmariuraceae.

It is characterized by its branching, stem-like structures that resemble small trees, though it lacks true roots, stems, and leaves in the traditional sense. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near water sources such as streams or wetlands. Its sporophyte generation produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems.

This species plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
