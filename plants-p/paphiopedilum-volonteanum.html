Paphiopedilum volonteanum

Paphiopedilum volonteanum, commonly known as the Voltaire's lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the orchid family. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, often found in forested areas with high humidity. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Paphiopedilum volonteanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
