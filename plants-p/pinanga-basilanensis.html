Pinanga basilanensis

Pinanga basilanensis, commonly known as the Basilan arrowroot, is a species of plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Basilan. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, which is surrounded by a spathe.

Pinanga basilanensis is valued for its edible underground stems, which are similar in texture to taro and are used in local cuisines.
