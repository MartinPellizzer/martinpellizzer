Pyrus astrophora

Pyrus astrophora, commonly known as the starlight pear, is a rare and unique species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are prized for their delicate flavor and nutritional value. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in its arid environment.
