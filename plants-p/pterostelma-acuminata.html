Pterostelma acuminata

Pterostelma acuminata, commonly known as the slender stonecrop, is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, opposite leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
