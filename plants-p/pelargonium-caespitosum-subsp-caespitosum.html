Pelargonium caespitosum subsp. caespitosum

Pelargonium caespitosum subsp.

caespitosum, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, bushy growth with fleshy, slightly hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
