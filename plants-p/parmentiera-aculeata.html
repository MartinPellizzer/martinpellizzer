Parmentiera aculeata

Parmentiera aculeata, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its spiny stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange or red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
