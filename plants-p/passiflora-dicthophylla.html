Passiflora dicthophylla

Passiflora dicthophylla, commonly known as the small-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and striking, five-petaled flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces a unique, fleshy fruit that is edible and contains numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Passiflora dicthophylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
