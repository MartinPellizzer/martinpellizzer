Pleurothallis orectopus

Pleurothallis orectopus, commonly known as the erect-stalked pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow blossoms that are clustered in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
