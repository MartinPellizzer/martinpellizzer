Pelargonium × orphanum

Pelargonium × orphanum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.

Its adaptability to various growing conditions makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
