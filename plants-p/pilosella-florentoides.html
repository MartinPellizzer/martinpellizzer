Pilosella florentoides

Pilosella florentoides, commonly known as the Florentine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
