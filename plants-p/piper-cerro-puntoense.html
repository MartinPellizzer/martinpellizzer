Piper cerro-puntoense

Piper cerro-puntoense, commonly known as the Cerro Punto pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, specifically Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, tubular fruits.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological features.
