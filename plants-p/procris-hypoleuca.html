Procris hypoleuca

Procris hypoleuca, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with prominent white fringes along the throat. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Procris hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
