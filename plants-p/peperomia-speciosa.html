Peperomia speciosa

Peperomia speciosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are often velvety to the touch, with deep green to bluish-green hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that appear in clusters, typically in the spring. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia speciosa is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in humid environments.
