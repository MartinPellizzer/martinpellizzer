Philadelphus caucasicus var. glabratus

Philadelphus caucasicus var. glabratus, commonly known as the Caucasian mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features aromatic, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters. The plant has smooth, glabrous bark and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its fragrance and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
