Prunus punctata

Prunus punctata, commonly known as the punctate cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and distinctive foliage. The leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin and a bluish-green hue when mature. It produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are popular among birds and small mammals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
