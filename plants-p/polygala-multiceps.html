Polygala multiceps

Polygala multiceps, commonly known as the mountain polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

Polygala multiceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
