Psylliostachys sisymbriifolius

Psylliostachys sisymbriifolius, commonly known as the Chinese sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. The plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its growth habit and floral structure are characteristic of the Poaceae family.
