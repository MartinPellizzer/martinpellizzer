Poa longiglumis

Poa longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets with elongated glumes, which are distinctive features in its inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks.

Poa longiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
