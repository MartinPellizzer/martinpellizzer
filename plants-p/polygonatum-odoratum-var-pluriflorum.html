Polygonatum odoratum var. pluriflorum

Polygonatum odoratum var. pluriflorum, commonly known as the fragrant Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features long, arching stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically arranged in umbels. The plant produces oval, dark green leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread slowly through underground stems.

It thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
