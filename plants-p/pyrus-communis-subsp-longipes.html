Pyrus communis subsp. longipes

Pyrus communis subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pear, is a subspecies of the European pear. It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and relatively small, clustered leaves.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals, and appear in early spring. The fruit is usually small, with a smooth skin and a sweet, juicy flesh.

This subspecies is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and hardy nature.
