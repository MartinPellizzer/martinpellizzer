Pleurothallis petropolitana

Pleurothallis petropolitana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, linear leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is noted for its compact size and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
