Psidium grandifolium var. intermedium

Psidium grandifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that range in color from red to orange, depending on the variety and ripeness. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
