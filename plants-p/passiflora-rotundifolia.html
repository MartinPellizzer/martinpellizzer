Passiflora rotundifolia

Passiflora rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, five-petaled flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Passiflora rotundifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine.
