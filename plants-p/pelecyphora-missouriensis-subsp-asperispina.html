Pelecyphora missouriensis subsp. asperispina

Pelecyphora missouriensis subsp.

asperispina, commonly known as the Missouri prickly pear cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitat.
