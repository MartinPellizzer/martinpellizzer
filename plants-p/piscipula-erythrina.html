Piscipula erythrina

Piscipula erythrina, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has a slender, woody stem with compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. Its flowers are showy and typically appear in late spring to early summer.

Piscipula erythrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
