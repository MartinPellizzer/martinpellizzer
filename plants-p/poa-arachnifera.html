Poa arachnifera

Poa arachnifera, commonly known as the spidergrass, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
