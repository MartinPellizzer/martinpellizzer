Plectocephalus chilensis var. brevilobus

Plectocephalus chilensis var. brevilobus, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal environments with high salt exposure. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal conditions.
