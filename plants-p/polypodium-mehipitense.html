Polypodium mehipitense

Polypodium mehipitense, commonly known as the Mehipit fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or moist substrates.

Polypodium mehipitense plays a role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms.
