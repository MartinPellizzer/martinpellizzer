Perymenium ayutlanum

Perymenium ayutlanum, commonly known as the Ayutlan mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees in the neotropical region.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its leafless stems and small, fleshy, white berries. Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
