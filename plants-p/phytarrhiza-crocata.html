Phytarrhiza crocata

Phytarrhiza crocata, commonly known as the golden root, is a rare and unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange tuberous roots, which are rich in bioactive compounds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is highly valued in traditional herbal medicine.
