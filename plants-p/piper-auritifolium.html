Piper auritifolium

Piper auritifolium, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant or ear-leaved pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its aromatic, pungent leaves and small, berry-like fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive ear-like or lobed base. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in spikes, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
