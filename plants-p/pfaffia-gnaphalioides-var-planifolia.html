Pfaffia gnaphalioides var. planifolia

Pfaffia gnaphalioides var. planifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. Native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties, though research on its efficacy remains limited.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other species within the Pfaffia genus.
