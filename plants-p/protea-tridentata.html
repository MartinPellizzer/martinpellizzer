Protea tridentata

Protea tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

The protea is also notable for its ability to attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.
