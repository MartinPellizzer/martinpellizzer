Polypodium kookenamae

Polypodium kookenamae, commonly known as the Kooken's water fern, is a species of fern native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often form a rosette shape. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

Polypodium kookenamae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest biodiversity.
