Phyllostegia manoana

Phyllostegia manoana, commonly known as the Mexican mint or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. Phyllostegia manoana is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
