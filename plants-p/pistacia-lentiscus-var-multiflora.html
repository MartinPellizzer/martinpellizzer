Pistacia lentiscus var. multiflora

Pistacia lentiscus var. multiflora, commonly known as the Mediterranean mastic tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments due to its drought tolerance. Its fruit, a small, woody drupe, contains the resinous substance mastic, which has been used historically for medicinal and industrial purposes.

The leaves are typically leathery and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature.
