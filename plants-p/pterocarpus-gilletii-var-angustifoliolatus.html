Pterocarpus gilletii var. angustifoliolatus

Pterocarpus gilletii var. angustifoliolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood and compound leaves with narrow leaflets. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
