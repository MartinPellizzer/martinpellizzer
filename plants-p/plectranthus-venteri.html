Plectranthus venteri

Plectranthus venteri, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Plectranthus venteri is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
