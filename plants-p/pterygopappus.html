Pterygopappus

Pterygopappus, commonly known as the skyrocket or desert star, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plants are characterized by their erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. Pterygopappus species typically have hairy leaves and produce fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.
