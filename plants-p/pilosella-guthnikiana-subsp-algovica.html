Pilosella guthnikiana subsp. algovica

Pilosella guthnikiana subsp.

algovica, commonly known as the Alpine pilosella, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in composite heads.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and narrow, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
