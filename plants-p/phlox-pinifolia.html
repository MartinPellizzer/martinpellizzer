Phlox pinifolia

Phlox pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble pine needles, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phlox pinifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
