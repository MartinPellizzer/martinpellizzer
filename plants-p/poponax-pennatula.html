Poponax pennatula

Poponax pennatula, commonly known as the feathered poponax, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive feathery foliage, which consists of finely divided, thread-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish in color, and it produces berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.
