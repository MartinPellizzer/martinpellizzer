Pavonia kilimandscharica var. triloba

Pavonia kilimandscharica var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed wood rose, is a striking shrub native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval or lance-shaped lobes, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience.
