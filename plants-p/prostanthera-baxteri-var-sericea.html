Prostanthera baxteri var. sericea

Prostanthera baxteri var. sericea, commonly known as the silky mint, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
