Piper austrocaledonicum

Piper austrocaledonicum, commonly known as the New Caledonian pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark purple color, containing several seeds.
