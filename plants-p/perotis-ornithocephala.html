Perotis ornithocephala

Perotis ornithocephala, commonly known as the bird's head grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bird-like head-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Perotis ornithocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
