Pleopeltis clathrata var. namegatae

Pleopeltis clathrata var. namegatae, commonly known as the Namegata huperzia, is a rare and specialized fern native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and distinctive, finely divided, dark green fronds. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with a short, stout rhizome and closely spaced, scale-like leaves. It thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
