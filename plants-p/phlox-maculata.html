Phlox maculata

Phlox maculata, commonly known as moss phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered with fine, soft hairs. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
