Piper dendropse

Piper dendropse, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
