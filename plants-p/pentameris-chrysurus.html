Pentameris chrysurus

Pentameris chrysurus, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
