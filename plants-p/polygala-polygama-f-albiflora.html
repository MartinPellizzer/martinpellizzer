Polygala polygama f. albiflora

Polygala polygama f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered milkweed or white milkvetch, is a flowering plant in the Polygalaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This variety is distinguished by its pale floral coloration, which sets it apart from other forms of Polygala polygama.
