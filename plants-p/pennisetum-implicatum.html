Pennisetum implicatum

Pennisetum implicatum, commonly known as the woolly beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in soft, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

Pennisetum implicatum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique visual appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
