Passiflora andreana

Passiflora andreana, commonly known as the Andean passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain a single large seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
