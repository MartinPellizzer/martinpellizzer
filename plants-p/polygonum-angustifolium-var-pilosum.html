Polygonum angustifolium var. pilosum

Polygonum angustifolium var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy narrow-leaf smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and ecological balance.
