Phaseolus spectabilis

Phaseolus spectabilis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that contain seeds used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Its structure includes compound leaves and a well-developed root system that supports its growth in various soil types.
