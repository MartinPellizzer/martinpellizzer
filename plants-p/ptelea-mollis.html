Ptelea mollis

Ptelea mollis, commonly known as the slenderleaf ash or redstem ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, followed by small, smooth, reddish-brown fruits. Ptelea mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
