Penaea barbata

Penaea barbata, commonly known as the hairy leaved orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hairy leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its root system is specialized for absorbing nutrients from poor soil conditions.

Penaea barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
