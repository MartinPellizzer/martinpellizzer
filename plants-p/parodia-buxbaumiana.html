Parodia buxbaumiana

Parodia buxbaumiana, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, colorful flowers. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Parodia buxbaumiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
