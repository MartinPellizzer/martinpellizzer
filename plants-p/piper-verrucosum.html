Piper verrucosum

Piper verrucosum, commonly known as the warty pepper or warty sylph, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, warty stems and small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive pubescent surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, yellowish berries that are edible.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
