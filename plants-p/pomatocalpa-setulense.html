Pomatocalpa setulense

Pomatocalpa setulense, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with a complex lip structure, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Pomatocalpa setulense is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
