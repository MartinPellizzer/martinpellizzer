Passiflora tricuspis

Passiflora tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often used in beverages and desserts. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

Passiflora tricuspis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
