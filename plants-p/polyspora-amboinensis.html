Polyspora amboinensis

Polyspora amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina polypody, is a fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
