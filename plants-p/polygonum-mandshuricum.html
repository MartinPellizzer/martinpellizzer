Polygonum mandshuricum

Polygonum mandshuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a reddish or purple hue on the underside.

The small, greenish flowers appear in dense clusters and are followed by three-lobed seed capsules.
