Phlox douglasii var. longifolia

Phlox douglasii var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is distinguished by its longer floral stems compared to other phlox species.
