Poupartia birrea

Poupartia birrea, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular houseplant.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and relatively easy to care for in domestic settings.
