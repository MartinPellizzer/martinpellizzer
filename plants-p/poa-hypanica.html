Poa hypanica

Poa hypanica, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in high-altitude environments. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Poa hypanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
