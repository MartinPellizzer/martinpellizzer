Pleurothallis apiculata

Pleurothallis apiculata, commonly known as the apiculate pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat challenging plant to grow in cultivation.
