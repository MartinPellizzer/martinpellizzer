Piper suaveolens

Piper suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits resembling peppers. The fruits are edible and have a mildly sweet and spicy flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
