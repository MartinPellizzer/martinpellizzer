Platostoma ovatum

Platostoma ovatum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces yellowish to reddish composite flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and is known for its aromatic, slightly toxic properties.

Platostoma ovatum is typically short-lived, with a lifespan of about two to three years.
