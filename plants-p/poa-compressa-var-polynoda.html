Poa compressa var. polynoda

Poa compressa var. polynoda, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
