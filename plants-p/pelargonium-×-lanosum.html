Pelargonium × lanosum

Pelargonium × lanosum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
