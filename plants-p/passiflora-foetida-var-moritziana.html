Passiflora foetida var. moritziana

Passiflora foetida var. moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and striking, velvety blue flowers with intricate patterns. The flowers emit a faintly aromatic scent, which attracts pollinators such as moths and bees. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
