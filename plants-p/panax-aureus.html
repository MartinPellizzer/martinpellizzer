Panax aureus

Panax aureus, commonly known as golden ginseng, is a rare and highly valued plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow roots and leaves. The plant is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties. Its roots contain various bioactive compounds that are believed to have adaptogenic and antioxidant effects.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, Panax aureus is now considered an endangered species.
