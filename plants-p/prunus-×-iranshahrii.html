Prunus × iranshahrii

Prunus × iranshahrii, commonly known as the Persian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The fruit, though edible, is generally not cultivated for commercial use.
