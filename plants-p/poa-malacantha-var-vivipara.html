Poa malacantha var. vivipara

Poa malacantha var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its ability to produce viviparous shoots, which are capable of rooting and growing into new plants while still attached to the parent plant. This trait allows it to propagate efficiently in suitable habitats. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features include fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.
