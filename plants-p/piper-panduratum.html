Piper panduratum

Piper panduratum, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, dark purple berries. Its fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
