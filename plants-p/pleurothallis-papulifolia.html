Pleurothallis papulifolia

Pleurothallis papulifolia, commonly known as the papulifolia orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique floral structure and specialized growth habits make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
