Papaver anomalum var. chinense

Papaver anomalum var. chinense, commonly known as the Chinese poppy, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red or orange petals that form a cup-like structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
