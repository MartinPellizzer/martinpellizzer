Penstemon henricksonii

Penstemon henricksonii, commonly known as the Henry's penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and displays showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Penstemon henricksonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
