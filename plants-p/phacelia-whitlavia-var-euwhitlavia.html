Phacelia whitlavia var. euwhitlavia

Phacelia whitlavia var. euwhitlavia, commonly known as the white phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
