Pedilonum foxii

Pedilonum foxii, commonly known as the Fox's foot fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green with a subtle sheen. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are small and inconspicuous, typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, Pedilonum foxii is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
