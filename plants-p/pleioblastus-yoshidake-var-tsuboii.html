Pleioblastus yoshidake var. tsuboii

Pleioblastus yoshidake var. tsuboii, commonly known as the Yoshidake bamboo, is a compact, clumping bamboo species native to Japan.

It features slender, dark green culms that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow-green panicles of flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
