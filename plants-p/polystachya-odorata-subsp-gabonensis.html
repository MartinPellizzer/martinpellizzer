Polystachya odorata subsp. gabonensis

Polystachya odorata subsp.

gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and West Africa. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it is often found in humid tropical climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral scent.
