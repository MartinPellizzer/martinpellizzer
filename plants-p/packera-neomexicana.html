Packera neomexicana

Packera neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico ragwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its basal leaves are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
