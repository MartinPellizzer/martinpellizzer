Papaver nudicaule f. amurense

Papaver nudicaule f. amurense, commonly known as the Siberian poppy, is a variant of the meadow poppy.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silky, white coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
