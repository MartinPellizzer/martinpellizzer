Pyrus Reticulata

Chinese white pear, scientifically known as Pyrus reticulata, is a traditional fruit tree native to China and widely cultivated for its sweet, aromatic fruit. This deciduous tree is prized for its delicate white flowers and its ability to thrive in temperate climates. The fruit, often referred to as the "pear of heaven," has been a symbol of prosperity and longevity in Chinese culture for centuries. Its cultivation dates back thousands of years, making it an important part of both agriculture and horticulture. The Chinese white pear remains a beloved fruit, cherished for its flavor and cultural significance.
