Prunus fenzliana

Prunus fenzliana, commonly known as the Chinese bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental landscaping.

Prunus fenzliana is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
