Polystichopsis maximowiczii

Polystichopsis maximowiczii, commonly known as the Japanese tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous layer of old fronds. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a distinctive, tropical appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
