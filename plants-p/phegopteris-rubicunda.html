Phegopteris rubicunda

Phegopteris rubicunda, commonly known as the red fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright red fronds that turn green as they mature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce a dense carpet of foliage.

Phegopteris rubicunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in maintaining forest floor ecosystems.
