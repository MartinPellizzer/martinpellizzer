Potentilla chrysantha proles nestleriana

Potentilla chrysantha proles nestleriana, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, bright yellow petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
