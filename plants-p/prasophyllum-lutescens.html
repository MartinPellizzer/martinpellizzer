Prasophyllum lutescens

Prasophyllum lutescens, commonly known as the yellow leek orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of yellowish-green flowers. It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its specialized root system that forms symbiotic relationships with fungi.

Prasophyllum lutescens is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to withstand dry conditions.
