Polygala stipulata

Polygala stipulata, commonly known as the stipulate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala stipulata is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
