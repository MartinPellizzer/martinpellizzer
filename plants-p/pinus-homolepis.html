Pinus homolepis

Pinus homolepis, commonly known as the Mexican red pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are often red or reddish-brown, which mature and release seeds in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts and construction.
