Peperomia cochinensis

Peperomia cochinensis, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Cochin nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia cochinensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
