Peperomia cilifolia

Peperomia cilifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia cilifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
