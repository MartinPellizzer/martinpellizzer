Potentilla pensylvanica var. virgulata

Potentilla pensylvanica var. virgulata, commonly known as the Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in soft, grayish-green leaves that are typically arranged in groups of five, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
