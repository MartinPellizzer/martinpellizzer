Phyllostachys circumpilis

Phyllostachys circumpilis, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass in the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, silver-green stems that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Phyllostachys circumpilis is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
