Ptelea baldwinii subsp. crenulata

Ptelea baldwinii subsp.

crenulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boxwood, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, oval leaves that have a distinctive crenulate margin.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
