Prunella vulgaris f. alba

Prunella vulgaris f. alba, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or white, arranged in whorls along the stem. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

It is often found in Europe, Asia, and North America, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
