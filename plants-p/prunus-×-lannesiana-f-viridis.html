Prunus × lannesiana f. viridis

Prunus × lannesiana f. viridis, commonly known as the green-leafed cherry, is a unique hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis.

It is characterized by its striking green foliage, which contrasts with the typical pink or white flowers of many cherry species. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various climates.

Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, providing year-round visual interest.
