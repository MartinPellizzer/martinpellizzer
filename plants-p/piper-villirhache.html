Piper villirhache

Piper villirhache, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed berries. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
