Placus sumbavensis

Placus sumbavensis, commonly known as the Sumbawa flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to the island of Sumbawa in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained, fertile soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
