Piper melanocarpum

Piper melanocarpum, commonly known as black pepperwood, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple to black berries, which are its characteristic fruits. The berries contain small, kidney-shaped seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
