Prunus domestica var. alpina-orientalis

Prunus domestica var. alpina-orientalis, commonly known as the Alpina-Orientalis cherry plum, is a variant of the domesticated plum tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is typically found in alpine and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and it has a shallow root system adapted to rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
