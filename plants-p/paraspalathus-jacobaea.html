Paraspalathus jacobaea

Paraspalathus jacobaea, commonly known as the common ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It contains pyrrolizidine alkaloids, which are toxic to livestock and can cause liver damage.
