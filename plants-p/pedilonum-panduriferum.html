Pedilonum panduriferum

Pedilonum panduriferum, commonly known as the panduriferum orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that resemble small, fleshy pouches. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often a mix of white, yellow, and green, making it a visually appealing and unique orchid species.
