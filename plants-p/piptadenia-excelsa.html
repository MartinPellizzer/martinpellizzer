Piptadenia excelsa

Piptadenia excelsa, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a large leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
