Psophocarpus palmettorum

Psophocarpus palmettorum, commonly known as the purplewing bean, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorns and a unique ability to climb by means of tendrils.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
