Plectranthus caninus subsp. flavovirens

Plectranthus caninus subsp.

flavovirens, commonly known as the golden green plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of purple or violet, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
