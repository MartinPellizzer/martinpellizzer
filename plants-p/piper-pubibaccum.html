Piper pubibaccum

Piper pubibaccum, commonly known as the pubescent pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Native to regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
