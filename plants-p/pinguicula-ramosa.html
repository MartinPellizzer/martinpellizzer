Pinguicula ramosa

Pinguicula ramosa, commonly known as the branched butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Pinguicula ramosa is a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and easy care requirements.
