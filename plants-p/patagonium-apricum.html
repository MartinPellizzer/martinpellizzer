Patagonium apricum

Patagonium apricum, commonly known as the Patagonian apricot, is a unique plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble apricots. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Patagonium apricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in local culinary traditions.
