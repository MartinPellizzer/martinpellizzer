Pleurothallis puberula

Pleurothallis puberula, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the cloud forests. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and fragrant, with a diverse range of colors including white, pink, and purple.

Pleurothallis puberula is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
