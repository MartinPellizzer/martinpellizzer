Podanthum asperum

Podanthum asperum, commonly known as the roughpodded plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. Native to regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
