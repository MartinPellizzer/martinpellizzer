Polygonatum officinale var. japonicum

Polygonatum officinale var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional herbal medicine for centuries.
