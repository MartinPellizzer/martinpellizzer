Pouteria passargei

Pouteria passargei, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree is notable for its edible seeds, which are encased in a hard, woody shell and are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. Its fruit is a large, woody capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

Pouteria passargei plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
