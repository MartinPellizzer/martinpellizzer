Piuttia

Piuttia, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a tropical species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.
