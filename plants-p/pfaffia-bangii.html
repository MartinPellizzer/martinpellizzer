Pfaffia bangii

Pfaffia bangii, commonly known as golden root, is a perennial plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are the primary part used for medicinal purposes, containing various bioactive compounds.

Pfaffia bangii is valued for its potential adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory properties.
