Plagioceltis dichotoma

Plagioceltis dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous clubmoss, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and dense clusters of scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically found at the base of the foliage. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.
