Primula flexilipes

Primula flexilipes, commonly known as the flexible primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing showy, colorful flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Primula flexilipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
