Polystichum munitum f. imbricans

Polystichum munitum f. imbricans, commonly known as the silver fern, is a fern species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its fronds are deeply divided and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
