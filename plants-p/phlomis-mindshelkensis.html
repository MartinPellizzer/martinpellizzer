Phlomis mindshelkensis

Phlomis mindshelkensis, commonly known as the Mindshelk wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
