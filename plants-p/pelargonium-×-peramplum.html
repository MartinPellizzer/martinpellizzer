Pelargonium × peramplum

Pelargonium × peramplum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a species resulting from the crossbreeding of various pelargonium species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers that come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. The plant typically features broad, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Pelargonium × peramplum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
