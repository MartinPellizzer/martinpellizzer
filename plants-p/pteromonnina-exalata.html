Pteromonnina exalata

Pteromonnina exalata, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Pteromonnina exalata is notable for its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
