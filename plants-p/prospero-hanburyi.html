Prospero hanburyi

Prospero hanburyi, commonly known as the Hanbury's evergreen, is a rare and critically endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments. It is highly valued for its ornamental qualities and has been the subject of conservation efforts due to its limited distribution and habitat loss.
