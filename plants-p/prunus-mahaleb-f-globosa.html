Prunus mahaleb f. globosa

Prunus mahaleb f. globosa, commonly known as the globular cherry or globe cherry, is a compact, rounded shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit, with short, stout branches and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible cherries that are typically reddish or black in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and flowering display.

Prunus mahaleb f. globosa prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.
