Potentilla egyptiaca

Potentilla egyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Egypt and the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate, with serrated margins, and it produces fruits that are capsules containing numerous seeds.

Potentilla egyptiaca plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
