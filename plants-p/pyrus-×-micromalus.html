Pyrus × micromalus

Pyrus × micromalus, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus pyrifolia (Asian pear) and Pyrus bretschneideri (Chinese sand pear).

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and crisp fruit, which is typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth margin, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The fruit is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to its unique flavor and nutritional profile.
