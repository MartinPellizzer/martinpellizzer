Pleurothallis subcordifolia

Pleurothallis subcordifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, dark green leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts who cultivate it in controlled environments.
