Pothos laciniatus

Pothos laciniatus, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a deep green color and serrated edges. The plant is known for its adaptability, thriving in low-light conditions and requiring minimal care. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.

The leaves can grow up to 12 inches in length, adding a lush, tropical appearance to any indoor space.
