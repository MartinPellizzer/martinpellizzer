Piper piritubanum

Piper piritubanum, commonly known as the Piritubanum pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
