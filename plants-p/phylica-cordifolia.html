Phylica cordifolia

Phylica cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf phylica, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Phylica cordifolia is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for birds and insects.
