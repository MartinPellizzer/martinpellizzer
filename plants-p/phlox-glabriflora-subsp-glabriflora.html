Phlox glabriflora subsp. glabriflora

Phlox glabriflora subsp.

glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink to lavender in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and grows in a low, spreading habit, often forming dense mats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
