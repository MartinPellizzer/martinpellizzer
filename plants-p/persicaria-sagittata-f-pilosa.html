Persicaria sagittata f. pilosa

Persicaria sagittata f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy arrowleaf plant, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding a striking visual element to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
