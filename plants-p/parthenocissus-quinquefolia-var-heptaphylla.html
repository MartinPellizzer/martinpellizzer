Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. heptaphylla

Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leaf ivy grape, is a variant of the Virginia creeper.

It is characterized by its distinctive seven-lobed leaves, which set it apart from the more common five-leaf variety. The plant is a deciduous vine that produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its rapid growth and vibrant foliage.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in gardens and along structures.
