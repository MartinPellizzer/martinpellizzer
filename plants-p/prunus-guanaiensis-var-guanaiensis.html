Prunus guanaiensis var. guanaiensis

Prunus guanaiensis var. guanaiensis, commonly known as the Andean mahogany, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America and is valued for its durable wood and ornamental qualities.
