Plantago linearis var. linearis

Plantago linearis var. linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 5–20 cm long and 1–2 cm wide, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during late spring to early summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is notable for its medicinal uses and its ability to tolerate environmental stress.
