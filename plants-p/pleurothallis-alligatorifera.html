Pleurothallis alligatorifera

Pleurothallis alligatorifera, commonly known as the alligator tongue orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its unique leaf shape resembles the skin of an alligator, giving it its common name.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized orchid collections.
