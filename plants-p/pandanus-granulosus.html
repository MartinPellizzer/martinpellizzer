Pandanus granulosus

Pandanus granulosus, commonly known as the rough pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its stiff, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and textiles. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered in a spadix-like structure.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and has cultural significance in many island communities.
