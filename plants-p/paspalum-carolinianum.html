Paspalum carolinianum

Paspalum carolinianum, commonly known as Carolina paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
