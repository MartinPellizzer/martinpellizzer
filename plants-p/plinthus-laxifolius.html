Plinthus laxifolius

Plinthus laxifolius, commonly known as the slender-leafed plinthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
