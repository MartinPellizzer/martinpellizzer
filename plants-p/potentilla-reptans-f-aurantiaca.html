Potentilla reptans f. aurantiaca

Potentilla reptans f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden creeping cinquefoil, is a compact, mat-forming shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
