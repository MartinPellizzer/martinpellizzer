Pleurothallis violacea

Pleurothallis violacea, commonly known as the violet lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display shades of violet and white, with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces aerial roots that help it absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Pleurothallis violacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
