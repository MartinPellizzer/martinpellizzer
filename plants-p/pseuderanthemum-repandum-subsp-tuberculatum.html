Pseuderanthemum repandum subsp. tuberculatum

Pseuderanthemum repandum subsp.

tuberculatum, commonly known as the tuberculate pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers.

The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and often covered with small, raised bumps or tubercles, giving the plant its subspecific name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers, which are usually pink or red, attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
