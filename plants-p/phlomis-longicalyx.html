Phlomis longicalyx

Phlomis longicalyx, commonly known as the long-leaved mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers at the top of the stems, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive grayish-green color.

Phlomis longicalyx is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
