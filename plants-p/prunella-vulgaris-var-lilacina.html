Prunella vulgaris var. lilacina

Prunella vulgaris var. lilacina, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive purple underside and pale green upper surface. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and gardens.

Its flowering stems are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.
