Primula dryadifolia subsp. cycliophylla

Primula dryadifolia subsp.

cycliophylla, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
