Phaius philippinensis

Phaius philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, fleshy rhizome and produces a single, erect stem bearing several lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Phaius philippinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also significant in local culture and traditional practices.
