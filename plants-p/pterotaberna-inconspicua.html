Pterotaberna inconspicua

Pterotaberna inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous fern, is a rare and delicate plant belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its small, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are clustered on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile growth habits, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
