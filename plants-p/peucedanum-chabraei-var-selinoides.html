Peucedanum chabraei var. selinoides

Peucedanum chabraei var. selinoides, commonly known as the Selinoides angelica, is a variant of the angelica plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a biennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
