Phacelia argentea

Phacelia argentea, commonly known as silver phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Silver phacelia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
