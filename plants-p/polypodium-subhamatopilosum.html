Polypodium subhamatopilosum

Polypodium subhamatopilosum, commonly known as the hairy-leaf polypody, is a fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely toothed fronds that are often covered in soft, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are creeping and help in the spread of the plant, while its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

The plant's morphology reflects its adaptation to humid, low-light conditions, making it a common epiphytic or terrestrial species in its native habitat.
