Prunus fallax

Prunus fallax, commonly known as the nodding cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, drooping white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and turn reddish in the fall. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Prunus fallax is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
