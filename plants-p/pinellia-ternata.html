Pinellia ternata

Pinellia ternata, commonly known as the Chinese windball plant, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its tuberous rhizomes and distinctive inflorescence. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces a spadix surrounded by a white, waxy spathe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
