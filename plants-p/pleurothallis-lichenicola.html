Pleurothallis lichenicola

Pleurothallis lichenicola, commonly known as the lichenicolous orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that grows on tree trunks and branches, often in shaded, humid environments. The plant is notable for its unique relationship with lichens, which it uses as a substrate for growth. Its flowers are typically small and white or pale green, with intricate, delicate structures.

This orchid is of interest to botanists due to its specialized ecological niche and its role in the biodiversity of its habitat.
