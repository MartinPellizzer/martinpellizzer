Palaquium simun

Palaquium simun, commonly known as the Simun apple or Malabar apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries resembling apples in appearance. Its fruits are valued for their tart flavor and are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
