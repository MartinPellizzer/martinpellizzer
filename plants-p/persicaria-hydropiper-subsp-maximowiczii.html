Persicaria hydropiper subsp. maximowiczii

Persicaria hydropiper subsp.

maximowiczii, commonly known as water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in eastern Asia. It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine, white hairs.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a distinctive glandular surface that exudes a peppery scent when crushed. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer.

This species is adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
