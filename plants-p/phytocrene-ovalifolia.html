Phytocrene ovalifolia

Phytocrene ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf phytocrene, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of tropical forests.
