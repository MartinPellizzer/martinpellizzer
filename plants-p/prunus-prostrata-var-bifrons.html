Prunus prostrata var. bifrons

Prunus prostrata var. bifrons, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a low-growing shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its prostrate or cascading growth habit, with slender stems that often hang over the edge of rock formations or cliffs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are white or pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.
