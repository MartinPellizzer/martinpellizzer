Panicum nemopanthum

Panicum nemopanthum, commonly known as the ghost grass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a ghostly appearance, especially in the late summer and autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant.
