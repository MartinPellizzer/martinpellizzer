Potentilla hirta var. media

Potentilla hirta var. media, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, hairy stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
