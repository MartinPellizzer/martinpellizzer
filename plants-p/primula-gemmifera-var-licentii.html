Primula gemmifera var. licentii

Primula gemmifera var. licentii, commonly known as the Licentii primrose, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces numerous gemmae, which are vegetative propagules that allow it to spread effectively in its natural habitat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine meadows.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
