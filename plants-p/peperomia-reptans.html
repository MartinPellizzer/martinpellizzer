Peperomia reptans

Peperomia reptans, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically light green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or purple. The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its compact size and ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread horizontally, making it an excellent choice for ground cover or hanging baskets.

Peperomia reptans is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of potting mediums.
