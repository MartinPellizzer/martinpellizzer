Piper barbulatum

Piper barbulatum, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.

Piper barbulatum is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
