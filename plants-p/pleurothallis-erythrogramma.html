Pleurothallis erythrogramma

Pleurothallis erythrogramma, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive red-veined pattern on the petals. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to grow in non-tropical climates.
