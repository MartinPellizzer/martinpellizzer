Pyrus pannonica incrassata

Pyrus pannonica incrassata, commonly known as the Hungarian pear, is a subspecies of the common pear native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically small, yellowish, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This plant plays an important role in both ecological and agricultural systems in its native region.
