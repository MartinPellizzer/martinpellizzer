Phaenohoffmannia latebracteolata

Phaenohoffmannia latebracteolata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy seeds dispersed by the wind. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
