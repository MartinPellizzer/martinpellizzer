Pternopetalum botrychioides

Pternopetalum botrychioides, commonly known as the woolly leaf tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, woolly foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a distinctive white or gray pubescent underside. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
