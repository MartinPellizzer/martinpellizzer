Perilomia fruticosa

Perilomia fruticosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Perilomia fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.
