Polygonum fragile

Polygonum fragile, commonly known as the brittle knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

Polygonum fragile is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
