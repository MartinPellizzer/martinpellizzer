Pseudolepanthes calceolaris

Pseudolepanthes calceolaris, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which resemble small violets, and its delicate, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure.

Pseudolepanthes calceolaris is appreciated for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
