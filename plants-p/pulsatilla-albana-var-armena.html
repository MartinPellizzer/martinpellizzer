Pulsatilla albana var. armena

Pulsatilla albana var. armena, commonly known as Armenian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles that contain numerous small, smooth seeds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early blooming flowers.
