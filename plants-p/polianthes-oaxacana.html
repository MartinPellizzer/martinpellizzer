Polianthes oaxacana

Polianthes oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan amaryllis, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
