Piper polybotrya

Piper polybotrya, commonly known as the winged pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, wing-like structures on its leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by three-lobed fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
