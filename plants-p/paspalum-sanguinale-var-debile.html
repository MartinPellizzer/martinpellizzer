Paspalum sanguinale var. debile

Paspalum sanguinale var. debile, commonly known as the slender bloodgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive reddish or pinkish hue along the midrib, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
