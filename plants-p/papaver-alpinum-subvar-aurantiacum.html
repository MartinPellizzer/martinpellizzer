Papaver alpinum subvar. aurantiacum

Papaver alpinum subvar. aurantiacum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking orange to reddish-orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has palmate leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
