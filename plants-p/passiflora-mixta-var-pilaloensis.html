Passiflora mixta var. pilaloensis

Passiflora mixta var. pilaloensis, commonly known as the Pilaloensis passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of violet, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
