Polygala rectipilis

Polygala rectipilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, often branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, delicate flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species.
