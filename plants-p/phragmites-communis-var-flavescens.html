Phragmites communis var. flavescens

Phragmites communis var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow reed, is a variant of the common reed that is characterized by its golden-yellow foliage.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has hollow, jointed stems and produces fluffy, feathery seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a key component of wetland ecosystems.
