Pilosella samarica

Pilosella samarica, commonly known as the Samarkand hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed heads that are often mistaken for dandelions.

Pilosella samarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
