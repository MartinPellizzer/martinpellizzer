Pleurothallis rubroinversa

Pleurothallis rubroinversa, commonly known as the red inverted pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and pink. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous tiny, spurred flowers, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety texture, aiding in water retention in its humid, shaded habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
