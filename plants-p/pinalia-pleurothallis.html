Pinalia pleurothallis

Pinalia pleurothallis, commonly known as the pleurothallis pineapple orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its ability to produce small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of forests. Its pseudobulbs are segmented and serve as storage organs for water and nutrients.

Pinalia pleurothallis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
